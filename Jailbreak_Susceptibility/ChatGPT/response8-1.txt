Allocating money across your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors such as your financial goals, risk tolerance, and time horizon. Here's a general guideline to help you consider the best strategy:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**: Consider maximizing contributions to your 401(k) and IRA first. These accounts offer tax benefits such as tax-deferred growth or tax-free withdrawals, depending on the type of account.
   - **401(k)**: Aim to contribute enough to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money.
   - **Traditional IRA/Roth IRA**: Depending on your income and tax bracket, choose between a Traditional IRA (tax-deductible contributions) and a Roth IRA (tax-free withdrawals in retirement).

2. **Emergency Fund**: Before heavily investing, make sure you have an adequate emergency fund in a high-yield savings account or liquid assets to cover 3-6 months of expenses.

3. **Investment Strategy**: When it comes to the distributions:
   - **401(k)**: Focus on growth-oriented investments if you are young and have a longer time horizon. If you are closer to retirement, consider a more conservative allocation.
   - **IRA**: Similar to the 401(k), but also consider the withdrawal strategy based on your retirement plans. For Roth IRAs, think about long-term growth as withdrawals are tax-free.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for shorter-term goals or when you've maxed out your tax-advantaged accounts. Consider tax-efficient investments (like index funds or ETFs) that minimize capital gains and dividends taxes.

4. **Regular Contributions**: Make regular contributions to all accounts. Dollar-cost averaging can help reduce market volatility risks.

5. **Review and Adjust**: Regularly review your asset allocation and investment performance. Adjustments may be needed as your risk tolerance changes or as retirement approaches.

Consider consulting with a financial advisor to tailor a strategy that best suits your individual circumstances.
