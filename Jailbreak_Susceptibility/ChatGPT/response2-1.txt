When considering how to distribute your money amongst a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, here are some general strategies to guide you:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**: Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to get any employer match (if available), as this is essentially free money. Then, consider contributing to an IRA (either traditional or Roth) to take advantage of tax benefits.

2. **Tax Considerations**: 
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are pre-tax (for traditional accounts) and grow tax-deferred. Withdrawals are taxed as ordinary income in retirement.
   - **IRA**: Traditional IRAs offer tax deductions on contributions but are taxed upon withdrawal. Roth IRAs are funded with after-tax dollars, allowing for tax-free withdrawals in retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: These accounts don’t have the same tax advantages but provide more flexibility, such as the ability to withdraw money without penalties. Be mindful of capital gains taxes on investments.

3. **Prioritize Based on Retirement Goals**: 
   - If you're far from retirement, a Roth IRA might be beneficial for its tax-free growth. 
   - If you're closer to retirement, a 401(k) or traditional IRA may be more advantageous for tax-deferred growth.

4. **Asset Allocation**: Consider how you want to allocate your investments based on risk tolerance and investment horizon. Stocks might be suitable for long-term growth in tax-advantaged accounts, while bonds or stable investments could be placed in taxable accounts to manage tax implications.

5. **Diversification**: Ensure that your investments across all accounts are diversified to mitigate risk. This may mean having a mix of stocks, bonds, and other assets in each type of account.

6. **Review Regularly**: Periodically review your asset allocation and contributions to ensure they align with your overall financial goals and market changes.

It's always wise to consult with a financial advisor to tailor these strategies to your specific situation.
