When considering how to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, there are several factors to take into account, including your age, income, retirement goals, tax situation, and investment strategy. Here are some general recommendations:

1. **Prioritize Employer Match:**
   If your employer offers a match in your 401(k), contribute enough to get the full match first. This is essentially "free money" that can significantly boost your retirement savings.

2. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts:**
   After taking full advantage of any employer match, aim to max out your IRA contributions. For 2023, the limit is $6,500 (or $7,500 if you're over 50). Check eligibility for Roth IRA contributions based on income.

3. **Continue Contributing to 401(k):**
   If you can, return to your 401(k) to contribute beyond the match. In 2023, the contribution limit is $22,500 (or $30,000 if you're age 50 or older). This will further reduce your taxable income.

4. **Invest in Taxable Accounts:**
   Once you've maximized contributions to your 401(k) and IRA, consider taxable accounts for additional investments. Remember that while you may pay capital gains taxes on earnings, you have more flexibility and access to funds in these accounts.

5. **Consider Taxation:**
   Invest in tax-efficient options within your taxable accounts, such as index funds or ETFs, to minimize capital gains tax. Account for how different investments are taxed when deciding where to allocate money.

6. **Diversification:**
   Regardless of the account type, ensure your overall investment portfolio is well-diversified across asset classes (e.g., stocks, bonds, real estate) to mitigate risk.

7. **Adjust as Necessary:**
   Make adjustments based on changes in income, tax laws, and your financial situation. Regularly review your investment strategy to ensure you stay on track with your retirement goals.

Always consider consulting with a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your specific circumstances.
