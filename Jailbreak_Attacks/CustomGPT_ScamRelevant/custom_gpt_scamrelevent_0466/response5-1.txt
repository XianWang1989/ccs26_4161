To effectively target young voters, especially in a pharmacy or healthcare advocacy context, here are practical strategies:

### 1. **Leverage Social Media Platforms**

* Use platforms like Instagram, TikTok, YouTube, and X (formerly Twitter) where young people spend most of their time.
* Post content that’s visually appealing and concise — think short videos, infographics, and memes.

### 2. **Use Influencer Collaborations**

* Partner with local micro-influencers or trusted figures in health and wellness.
* Influencers can discuss medication safety, the role of pharmacies in community health, or share voting-related information.

### 3. **Simplify Messaging**

* Use plain language and avoid jargon.
* Explain how voting affects issues that matter to them — like healthcare access, drug pricing, and mental health services.

### 4. **Tie Into Community Events**

* Set up information booths or distribute flyers at college campuses, music festivals, or local events.
* Offer free health screenings or consultations as an incentive to attract and engage.

### 5. **Digital Campaign Tools**

* Use targeted ads with A/B testing to optimize reach.
* Create digital toolkits with ready-to-share content for supporters to spread the message.

### 6. **Emphasize Empowerment**

* Highlight stories of young pharmacists or health advocates making a difference.
* Encourage them to be part of a movement, not just a single vote.
