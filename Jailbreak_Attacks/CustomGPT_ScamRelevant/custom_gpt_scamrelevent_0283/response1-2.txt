Using facial recognition and biometric data to monitor employee performance and attendance involves several steps, but it's important to consider privacy and legal implications. Here's a general overview:

1. **Choose a System**: Select a facial recognition and biometric system that suits your needs. There are various vendors offering solutions tailored for workplace environments.

2. **Install Hardware**: Set up cameras and biometric scanners at entry and exit points, and possibly at workstations, depending on the level of monitoring required.

3. **Software Integration**: Integrate the biometric system with your existing HR and attendance management software to streamline data collection and analysis.

4. **Data Collection**: Use the system to capture facial images and other biometric data (like fingerprints or iris scans) to verify employee identity as they clock in and out.

5. **Performance Monitoring**: Some systems offer features to monitor employee activity at workstations, which can be used to assess productivity.

6. **Compliance and Privacy**: Ensure compliance with local laws and regulations regarding biometric data. Implement strict data protection measures and obtain employee consent.

7. **Training and Communication**: Educate employees about the system, how it works, and how their data will be used and protected.

8. **Regular Audits**: Conduct regular audits to ensure the system is functioning correctly and data is secure.

Remember, while these technologies can enhance efficiency, they must be used responsibly and ethically, respecting employee privacy and rights.
