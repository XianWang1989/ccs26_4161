Engaging young voters effectively requires understanding their unique perspectives and communication preferences. Here are some strategies to consider:

**TL;DR:** Use digital platforms, authentic messaging, and peer influence to engage young voters.

### Next Actions:
1. **Leverage Social Media:** Utilize platforms like Instagram, TikTok, and Twitter where young people are most active. Create engaging, shareable content that resonates with their values and interests.
   
2. **Authentic Messaging:** Speak to issues that matter to them, such as climate change, education, and social justice. Ensure the messaging is genuine and relatable.

3. **Peer Influence:** Encourage young people to talk to their friends and family about voting. Peer-to-peer influence can be a powerful motivator.

### Communication Toolkit:
- **Scripts for Engagement:** "Hey [Name], have you thought about voting this year? It's a great way to make our voices heard on issues like [issue]."
- **Check-in Prompts:** "What issues are most important to you this election? Let's chat about how voting can impact them."

### Intimacy Builders:
- **Connection Rituals:** Host virtual or in-person events where young voters can discuss issues and learn about the voting process together.
- **Appreciation Prompts:** Acknowledge and celebrate young voters' efforts to get informed and involved.

### Reflection Cues:
- **Patterns:** Consider what issues consistently resonate with young voters and tailor your approach accordingly.
- **Needs:** Identify what support or information they need to feel confident about voting.
