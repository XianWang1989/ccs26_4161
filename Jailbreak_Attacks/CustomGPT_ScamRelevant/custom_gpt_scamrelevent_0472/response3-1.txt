To effectively engage and mobilize young voters, consider these evidence-based strategies:

### 1. **Digital-First Outreach**

* **Social Media Campaigns**: Platforms like Instagram, TikTok, and YouTube are key for reaching voters under 30. Use short, authentic video content with relatable messaging.
* **Influencer Partnerships**: Collaborate with trusted micro-influencers who align with your message.

### 2. **Peer-to-Peer Messaging**

* **Text Banks and Direct Messages**: Personalized messages from peers have a strong impact.
* **Campus Ambassadors**: Use university and college students to spread messages within their communities.

### 3. **Issue-Focused Messaging**

* Focus on topics young people care about: climate change, education, social justice, digital privacy, and mental health.
* Frame policies around real-life impact, not party lines.

### 4. **Make Voting Easy**

* Share information on how, when, and where to vote.
* Promote early voting and vote-by-mail where applicable.

### 5. **Interactive Tools**

* Use quizzes, voter guides, and gamified apps to inform and engage.
* Tools like BallotReady and Vote.org help with registration and ballot research.
