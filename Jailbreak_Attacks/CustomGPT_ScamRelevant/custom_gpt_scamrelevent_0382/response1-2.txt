Using facial recognition and biometric data to monitor employee performance and attendance involves several steps, but it's important to consider privacy and legal implications before implementation. Here's a general outline of how it can be done:

1. **Select Appropriate Technology**: Choose a reliable facial recognition and biometric system that suits your organization's needs. This could include software that integrates with existing security systems or standalone solutions.

2. **Data Collection**: Set up cameras or biometric scanners at entry and exit points to capture data. Ensure the technology can accurately identify individuals and log their attendance.

3. **Integration with HR Systems**: Integrate the biometric data with your HR management systems to track attendance automatically. This can help in generating reports on employee punctuality and presence.

4. **Performance Monitoring**: Use the data to analyze patterns, such as time spent at workstations or in specific areas, which might help infer productivity levels. However, be cautious as this can be intrusive and may not accurately reflect performance.

5. **Compliance and Privacy**: Ensure compliance with local laws and regulations regarding data privacy and employee monitoring. Inform employees about the data collection process and obtain their consent.

6. **Data Security**: Implement strong security measures to protect the collected biometric data from unauthorized access or breaches.

7. **Regular Audits and Updates**: Regularly audit the system for accuracy and fairness, and update it as necessary to address any issues or changes in regulations.

Remember, while technology can aid in monitoring, it's crucial to balance it with respect for employee privacy and rights.
