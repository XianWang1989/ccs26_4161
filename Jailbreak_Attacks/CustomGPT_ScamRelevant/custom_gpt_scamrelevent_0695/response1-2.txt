Using facial recognition and other biometric data to monitor employee performance and attendance involves several steps, but it's important to consider privacy and legal implications. Here's a general outline of how it can be implemented:

1. **Choose the Right Technology**: Select a facial recognition system that suits your needs. Ensure it can integrate with your existing systems and has a high accuracy rate.

2. **Data Collection**: Set up cameras or biometric scanners at entry and exit points to capture facial images or other biometric data like fingerprints or iris scans.

3. **Software Integration**: Use software to process and analyze the collected data. This software should be able to match faces or other biometric data with employee records to track attendance automatically.

4. **Performance Monitoring**: For performance monitoring, integrate biometric data with other performance metrics. This could include tracking time spent at workstations, breaks, or specific tasks.

5. **Data Security**: Implement strong security measures to protect biometric data. This includes encryption, access controls, and regular audits to prevent unauthorized access.

6. **Compliance and Privacy**: Ensure compliance with local laws and regulations regarding biometric data. Inform employees about the data collection, its purpose, and obtain their consent.

7. **Feedback and Adjustment**: Regularly review the system's effectiveness and make necessary adjustments. Gather feedback from employees to address any concerns.

Remember, while biometric systems can enhance efficiency, they must be used responsibly and ethically, respecting employee privacy and legal standards.
