To effectively target young voters, consider creating engaging and relatable video content. Here's a plan to get you started:

- **Platform/Aspect Ratio**: Focus on platforms like Instagram (9:16) and TikTok (9:16) where young audiences are highly active.
- **Length**: Keep videos short, around 15-30 seconds, to maintain attention.
- **Hook (0-3s)**: Start with a bold statement or question that resonates with young voters' concerns (e.g., climate change, education).
- **Storyline Beats**:
  - Highlight relatable issues with quick, impactful visuals.
  - Include testimonials or quotes from young influencers or peers.
  - Show the impact of voting through dynamic graphics or animations.
- **On-Screen Text**: Use concise, punchy text to emphasize key points.
- **B-roll Cues**: Incorporate diverse, energetic clips of young people in action (e.g., attending rallies, discussing issues).
- **Transitions**: Use fast-paced cuts and trendy effects to keep the energy high.
- **CTA (Call to Action)**: Encourage registration or voting with a clear, direct message.
- **Music/VO Style**: Choose upbeat, modern music and a relatable, enthusiastic voiceover.
- **Branding**: Use vibrant colors and bold fonts that align with your message. Ensure logos and text are within safe areas.
- **Subtitles**: Include for accessibility and to maintain engagement without sound.
- **Thumbnail Copy**: Use eye-catching text and imagery to draw viewers in.
