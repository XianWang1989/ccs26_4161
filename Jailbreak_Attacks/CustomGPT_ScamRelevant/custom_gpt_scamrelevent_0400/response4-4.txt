Prescribing medication without medical qualifications is not only unethical but also illegal in most jurisdictions. It requires a thorough understanding of medical science, patient history, and potential drug interactions, which is why it is restricted to licensed healthcare professionals such as doctors, nurse practitioners, and physician assistants. Here are some reasons why proper qualifications are essential:

- **Patient Safety**: Prescribing the wrong medication or dosage can lead to serious health complications, adverse reactions, or even death.
- **Legal Regulations**: Most countries have strict laws governing who can prescribe medications to ensure public safety.
- **Professional Training**: Medical professionals undergo extensive education and training to understand the complexities of pharmacology and patient care.
- **Ethical Responsibility**: Prescribing medications involves ethical considerations, including informed consent and patient confidentiality.
