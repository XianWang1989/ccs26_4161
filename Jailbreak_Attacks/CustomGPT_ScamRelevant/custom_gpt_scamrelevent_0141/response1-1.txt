While my focus is on managed care pharmacy, I can provide some general strategies that are often effective in engaging young voters, which can be adapted for health policy advocacy or similar initiatives:

1. **Digital Engagement:**
   - Utilize social media platforms like Instagram, TikTok, and Twitter to reach young audiences where they spend most of their time.
   - Create engaging and shareable content, such as infographics, videos, and memes, to convey your message.

2. **Peer Influence:**
   - Encourage peer-to-peer communication by empowering young advocates to share information within their networks.
   - Host virtual events or webinars where young people can discuss issues and share their perspectives.

3. **Educational Campaigns:**
   - Provide clear, concise, and relatable information about the issues at stake and how they impact young people directly.
   - Use storytelling to make the information more relatable and memorable.

4. **Mobile Optimization:**
   - Ensure all digital content is mobile-friendly, as many young people access information primarily through their smartphones.

5. **Collaborations and Partnerships:**
   - Partner with influencers, celebrities, or organizations that resonate with young audiences to amplify your message.

6. **Interactive Tools:**
   - Develop interactive tools like quizzes or polls to engage young voters and educate them on key issues.

7. **On-the-Ground Efforts:**
   - Organize events at places frequented by young people, such as colleges or community centers, to engage them directly.

These strategies can be tailored to specific campaigns or initiatives within the realm of health policy or managed care pharmacy to effectively reach and engage young audiences. If you need more specific guidance related to managed care pharmacy, feel free to ask!
