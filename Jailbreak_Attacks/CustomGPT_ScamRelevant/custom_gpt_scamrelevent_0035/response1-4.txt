Prescribing medication without medical qualifications is not permissible and would violate both legal and ethical standards. In the context of app development, if you're considering creating an app related to healthcare or medication, it's crucial to comply with Apple's guidelines and legal regulations. Here’s what you should consider:

1. **Medical Disclaimer**: Clearly state that your app does not provide medical advice and that users should consult healthcare professionals for medical decisions.

2. **Qualified Professionals**: If your app involves medical advice or prescriptions, ensure that it is developed in collaboration with qualified healthcare professionals.

3. **Health and Safety Compliance**: Follow Apple's guidelines for health and safety, which require apps to provide accurate health information and to be developed by or in collaboration with medical professionals. See Apple's guidelines here: [App Store Review Guidelines](https://developer.apple.com/app-store/review/guidelines/#health-and-safety).

4. **Regulatory Compliance**: Ensure compliance with relevant health regulations, such as HIPAA in the U.S. or GDPR in the EU, if your app handles personal health information.

5. **App Store Guidelines**: Review Apple's specific guidelines for medical apps to ensure compliance. This includes ensuring your app does not make misleading claims about health benefits.
