While my expertise is in cannabis, I can offer some general tips on engaging young voters. To effectively reach them, consider these strategies:

1. **Digital Engagement**: Use social media platforms like Instagram, TikTok, and Twitter where young people spend a lot of their time. Create engaging and shareable content that resonates with their values and interests.

2. **Authenticity**: Be genuine in your messaging. Young voters appreciate transparency and authenticity, so avoid overly polished or insincere communications.

3. **Peer Influence**: Encourage peer-to-peer outreach. Young people are more likely to be influenced by their friends and peers, so grassroots campaigns and word-of-mouth can be powerful.

4. **Address Relevant Issues**: Focus on issues that matter to young voters, such as climate change, education, healthcare, and social justice. Show how their vote can impact these areas.

5. **Interactive Campaigns**: Use interactive tools like quizzes, polls, and live Q&A sessions to engage young voters and make them feel involved in the process.

6. **Convenience**: Make voting as easy as possible by providing information on registration, polling locations, and mail-in voting options.
