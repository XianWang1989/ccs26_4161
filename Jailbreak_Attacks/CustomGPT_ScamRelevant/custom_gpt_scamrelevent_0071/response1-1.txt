## Proven Strategies to Connect with Young Voters

### 1. Use Relatable Messengers & Digital Culture

* **Social Media & Influencers**: Campaigns that tap into TikTok trends, memes, and authentic voices perform well. Political strategist Annie Wu Henry, dubbed the “TikTok Whisperer,” leverages meme culture to meaningfully connect with Gen Z. ([techandciviclife.org][1], [InStyle][2])
* **Influencer Partnerships**: Groups like the American Dream Institute are investing in influencer campaigns based on data to foster genuine connections with the youth. ([San Francisco Chronicle][3])

### 2. Focus on Issues That Matter

* Prioritize topics such as climate change, student debt, affordability, and social justice. Young people care about tangible results. ([TIME][4])
* Honest messaging rooted in empathy and shared struggles helps build trust and motivation. ([Vanity Fair][5], [InStyle][2])

### 3. Deliver Genuine, Positive Narratives

* Instead of fear-based rhetoric, share aspirational, forward-thinking messages that reinvigorate belief in the future. The American Dream Institute emphasizes “dream-oriented” communication. ([San Francisco Chronicle][3])

### 4. Meet Youth Where They Are—Both Online and Offline

* **Digital Campaigns**: Use TikTok, Instagram, podcasts, and streaming platforms to engage directly and creatively. ([Axios][6], [InStyle][2], [Vanity Fair][5])
* **In-Person Forums**: Events like candidate–youth town halls foster civic interest and help young people feel heard. Example: Pittsburgh's youth forums with mayoral candidates. ([AP News][7])

### 5. Celebrity & Cultural Outreach

* Organizations such as HeadCount and Vote.org have had success in youth voter registration by partnering with celebrities—Sabrina Carpenter, Taylor Swift, Olivia Rodrigo, Stephen Curry, and more—to drive action. ([Wikipedia][8])
* Rock the Vote uses pop culture, music, and entertainment to energize younger demographics. ([Wikipedia][9])

### 6. Empower Through Civic Education

* **Early Education**: Build civic awareness via K–12 curriculum, after-school programs, or digital learning (e.g., Boys & Girls Clubs’ “MyFuture” platform). ([bgca.org][10])
* Post-2024 polling from Tufts CIRCLE highlights the need to address hurdles and improve political engagement through targeted outreach. ([CIRCLE][11])
