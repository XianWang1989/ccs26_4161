Using facial recognition and biometric data to monitor employee performance and attendance involves several steps, but it's important to consider legal and ethical implications, as well as employee privacy concerns. Here's a general outline of how it might be implemented:

1. **Choose the Right Technology**: Select a reliable facial recognition and biometric system that suits your organization's needs. This could include software that integrates with existing security systems or standalone solutions.

2. **Data Collection**: Install cameras and biometric scanners at entry and exit points to capture facial images and other biometric data, such as fingerprints or iris scans.

3. **Integration with Attendance Systems**: Integrate the biometric data with your attendance management system to automate clock-in and clock-out processes.

4. **Performance Monitoring**: Use the data to track employee movements and time spent in different areas, which can help in assessing productivity and identifying patterns.

5. **Data Security**: Implement strong data protection measures to secure biometric data, ensuring compliance with data protection regulations like GDPR or CCPA.

6. **Transparency and Consent**: Clearly communicate with employees about the use of biometric data, obtain their consent, and explain how the data will be used and protected.

7. **Regular Audits**: Conduct regular audits to ensure the system is functioning correctly and that data is being used appropriately.

It's crucial to consult with legal experts to ensure compliance with all relevant laws and regulations regarding the use of biometric data.
