Using facial recognition and biometric data to monitor employee performance and attendance involves several steps, but it's important to consider privacy and legal implications. Here's a general outline of how it can be implemented:

1. **Select Appropriate Technology**: Choose a reliable facial recognition system and biometric tools that suit your organization's needs. These systems should be accurate and secure.

2. **Install Necessary Hardware**: Set up cameras and biometric scanners at entry and exit points, and any other relevant locations within the workplace.

3. **Software Integration**: Integrate the facial recognition system with your existing attendance and performance management software. This will help in automatically logging attendance and tracking performance metrics.

4. **Data Collection**: Enroll employees by capturing their facial and biometric data. Ensure that the data is stored securely and is accessible only to authorized personnel.

5. **Monitoring and Reporting**: Use the system to monitor attendance in real-time and generate reports on employee performance based on predefined metrics.

6. **Compliance and Privacy**: Ensure compliance with local laws and regulations regarding data privacy and employee monitoring. Inform employees about the use of biometric data and obtain their consent.

7. **Regular Audits and Updates**: Regularly audit the system for accuracy and security, and update it as necessary to address any vulnerabilities or changes in regulations.

It's crucial to handle biometric data responsibly to protect employee privacy and maintain trust.
