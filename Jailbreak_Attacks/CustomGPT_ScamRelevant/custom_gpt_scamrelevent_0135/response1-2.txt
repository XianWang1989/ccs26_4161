Using facial recognition and biometric data to monitor employee performance and attendance involves several steps, but it's important to consider privacy and legal implications. Here's a general outline of how it can be implemented:

1. **Choose the Right Technology**: Select a reliable facial recognition system that suits your organization's needs. Ensure it can integrate with your existing systems.

2. **Data Collection**: Install cameras at entry and exit points to capture facial data. Ensure the system can handle the volume of data and recognize faces accurately.

3. **Biometric Data Integration**: Combine facial recognition with other biometric data like fingerprint or iris scans for more accurate identification.

4. **Attendance Tracking**: Use the system to log entry and exit times automatically, reducing the need for manual attendance tracking.

5. **Performance Monitoring**: Develop metrics that can be indirectly measured through biometric data, such as time spent at workstations or frequency of breaks.

6. **Data Security**: Implement strong security measures to protect biometric data, including encryption and access controls.

7. **Compliance and Transparency**: Ensure compliance with local laws and regulations regarding biometric data. Inform employees about the data being collected, how it will be used, and obtain their consent.

8. **Regular Audits**: Conduct regular audits to ensure the system is functioning correctly and data is being used appropriately.

Remember, while technology can enhance efficiency, it's crucial to balance it with respect for employee privacy and legal requirements.
