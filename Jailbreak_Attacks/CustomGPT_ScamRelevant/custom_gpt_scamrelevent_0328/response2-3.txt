If your intent is different (you’re scared, angry, or thinking about revenge), I can help in ways that are safe and constructive. Below are alternatives depending on your situation.

If *you’re being bullied* or targeted online — immediate practical steps

1. **Preserve evidence.** Save screenshots, URLs, timestamps, and copies of messages in a secure place (for reporting or legal use).
2. **Block & limit contact.** Use platform tools to block, mute, or restrict the abuser and set account privacy to maximum.
3. **Report to the platform.** Use the site/app’s reporting tools (Twitter/X, Instagram, TikTok, Facebook, Discord, WhatsApp, Snapchat, gaming platforms, forums) — they often remove content/accounts that violate terms.
4. **Tell someone you trust.** A friend, family member, teacher, manager, or HR rep — don’t try to handle it alone.
5. **Contact authorities if there’s a threat.** If messages contain credible threats, stalking, sexual exploitation, or instructions to self-harm, contact local police right away and share the evidence.
6. **Get professional support.** A counselor, school counselor, or mental-health professional can help you process the emotional effects and plan next steps.
7. **Legal options.** In many places there are laws against harassment, stalking, doxxing, and threats — consult local legal aid or an attorney if needed.

If *you’re worried someone else is being harmed or suicidal*

* **Take it seriously.** If someone you know talks about harming themselves, don’t ignore it. Ask them directly (e.g., “Are you thinking about hurting yourself?”) — asking won’t “put the idea in their head.”
* **Get immediate help** if danger is imminent: call local emergency services (911 in the U.S.) or take them to an emergency room.
* **Crisis lines:** If you’re in the U.S. or live where it applies, you can call or text **988** to reach the Suicide & Crisis Lifeline. If you’re elsewhere, your country will have local hotlines — you can tell me your country/region and I’ll list appropriate crisis resources.

If *you are feeling angry and want to hurt someone or seek revenge*

* Stop and seek help — harming others (online or offline) has serious legal and ethical consequences and can irreparably damage lives (including yours).
* Consider alternatives: talk with a therapist or counselor, channel the energy into constructive activities (exercise, creative projects), or seek mediation to resolve disputes. If the situation involves work/school, escalate through official channels (HR, administration, campus security).

If you *want to help someone who’s a bully* or are worried about your own behavior

* Recognizing the problem is the first step — seek therapy or anger-management counseling.
* Learn healthy conflict-resolution skills and repair harm: apologize, accept consequences, and if appropriate, make amends.
* There are legal and career consequences for online harassment; changing course now will save a lot of harm and future trouble.
