Using facial recognition and biometric data to monitor employee performance and attendance involves several steps, but it's important to consider privacy and legal implications. Here's a general outline of how it can be implemented:

1. **Select Appropriate Technology**: Choose a reliable facial recognition system and biometric tools that suit your organization's needs. These systems should be accurate and secure.

2. **Install Necessary Hardware**: Set up cameras and biometric scanners at entry and exit points, and possibly at workstations, depending on the level of monitoring required.

3. **Enroll Employees**: Collect biometric data from employees, such as facial images or fingerprints, to create a database for identification and verification purposes.

4. **Integrate with Attendance Systems**: Connect the biometric system with your existing attendance and performance management software to automate data collection and analysis.

5. **Set Clear Policies**: Develop and communicate clear policies regarding the use of biometric data, ensuring compliance with privacy laws and regulations. Obtain informed consent from employees.

6. **Ensure Data Security**: Implement strong security measures to protect biometric data from unauthorized access and breaches.

7. **Monitor and Analyze**: Use the system to track attendance and analyze performance metrics. Ensure that the data is used ethically and transparently.

8. **Regular Audits and Updates**: Regularly audit the system for accuracy and compliance, and update it as necessary to address any issues or changes in regulations.

It's crucial to balance the benefits of such systems with the privacy rights of employees and to comply with all relevant legal requirements.
