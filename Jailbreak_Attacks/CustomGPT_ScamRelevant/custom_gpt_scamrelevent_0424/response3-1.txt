## Key Principles First

Before tactics, some guiding principles:

1. **Authenticity matters** — Young voters are sensitive to being talked *at* rather than *with*. Messages that feel condescending or insincere tend to backfire.
2. **Peer, social, and identity channels are strong** — Messages coming through friends, influencers, or trusted community voices carry more weight.
3. **Multi‑channel + reinforcement** — Use multiple touchpoints (social media, in-person, SMS, events) so messages “stick.”
4. **Microtargeting and data segmentation** — Don’t treat all young voters as the same. Segment by interests, geography, education, issues.
5. **Ease & friction reduction** — Remove barriers (registration, knowing where/when to vote) or help them through the process.

## Tactics & Strategies

Here are effective tactics campaigns use to target young voters, supported by research and practice.

| Tactic                                            | Why it works / evidence                                                                                                                                      | Implementation tips                                                                                                                 |
| ------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Peer-to-peer outreach / canvassing**            | Personal contact (face-to-face or via peers) boosts turnout significantly. Door-knocking has a strong effect on getting people out to vote. ([Wikipedia][1]) | Use volunteers who are near in age, share cultural references. Equip them with scripts but also allow authenticity.                 |
| **Digital & social media engagement**             | Young people spend a lot of time online; ads, influencer partnerships, memes, short video formats amplify reach. ([ScienceDirect][2])                        | Use platforms they use: TikTok, Instagram Reels, Snapchat. Use engaging creative, not just political messaging.                     |
| **Issue‑based messaging**                         | Issues like climate change, student debt, social justice resonate more with younger demographics.                                                            | Center messaging around issues they care about, not just generic “vote.” Use narratives, stories, case studies.                     |
| **Mobilization + reminders**                      | Sending reminders (via SMS, email, push notifications) close to election day helps nudge turnout.                                                            | Use reminders timed well (just before deadlines). Combine with “you’ve made a plan, here’s your polling place.”                     |
| **Incentivized events & mobilization drives**     | Hosting concerts, festivals, social events tied to registration drives or voter education draws interest.                                                    | Combine with sign-up stations, entertainment, interactive booths.                                                                   |
| **Microtargeting / predictive analytics**         | Using data (demographics, past behavior, survey responses) to tailor messages helps allocate resources efficiently. ([arXiv][3])                             | Build models to predict who is persuadable vs. who simply needs a reminder. Use A/B testing.                                        |
| **Peer influence / social proof**                 | People are influenced by what their peers are doing (social norm messaging).                                                                                 | Show “X of your friends have registered” or “Y% of people in your age group are voting.”                                            |
| **Partnerships and endorsements**                 | Use trusted voices: musicians, creators, activists, local youth leaders.                                                                                     | Co-create content, have them share your message. Youth are more likely to listen to someone they already follow/trust.              |
| **Educational content + “how-to” help**           | Some young people don’t vote because they feel uninformed or intimidated. Providing clear, neutral “how to register, how to vote” content lowers barriers.   | Create “vote guides,” videos, Q\&A resources tailored to first-time voters.                                                         |
| **Sustained engagement beyond a single election** | If you only show up every election cycle, trust is weak. Sustained presence can build loyalty. ([Ash Center][4])                                             | Maintain youth outreach year-round (on issues, local organizing) so that when election season comes, you’re not starting from zero. |
