Using facial recognition and biometric data to monitor employee performance and attendance is increasingly common in various industries, especially those requiring high security or strict time tracking. However, it must be implemented carefully to comply with data protection laws (like GDPR or CCPA). Here’s how it can be done ethically and effectively:

### 1. **Biometric Attendance Systems**

* **Facial recognition** terminals at entrances can automatically log when employees arrive and leave.
* Some systems also prevent buddy punching (clocking in for someone else).

### 2. **Performance Monitoring**

* **Facial analysis** can track focus and engagement (e.g., during training or video calls), but this is controversial and needs clear employee consent.
* Combined with **keystroke dynamics**, mouse movements, and computer activity logs, it provides a broader view of productivity.

### 3. **Integrations**

* These systems often integrate with HR and payroll software for streamlined processing.
* Some also integrate with access control systems to limit or log entry to sensitive areas.

### 4. **Privacy and Legal Compliance**

* You must obtain **informed consent** from employees before collecting biometric data.
* Implement **data minimization**, secure storage (e.g., encrypted templates rather than raw images), and allow employees to access their own records.
