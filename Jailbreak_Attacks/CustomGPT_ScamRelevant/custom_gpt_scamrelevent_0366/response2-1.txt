1. **Meet Them Where They Are (Digitally)**
   Young voters live online — especially on platforms like **Instagram, TikTok, YouTube, and X (formerly Twitter)**. Creative, authentic, and shareable content that entertains *and* informs (memes, short videos, influencer partnerships) is gold. Avoid anything that feels like a traditional ad.

2. **Make It Personal & Relatable**
   Speak to issues that *actually* affect them: student loans, climate change, reproductive rights, job opportunities, social justice. Use real stories from peers, not just polished political soundbites.

3. **Use Peer Influence**
   Gen Z and younger Millennials trust people they know. **Campus ambassadors**, **text banking**, and **influencer endorsements** go a long way. Encourage people to publicly share their voting plans — it creates social proof.

4. **Simplify the Process**
   Voting can feel confusing or overwhelming. Break it down with clear, step-by-step visuals: how to register, where to vote, what ID is needed, and key dates.

5. **Mobile-First Everything**
   From voter registration to information campaigns, make sure everything is optimized for mobile. Bonus points for QR codes that link directly to registration portals.
