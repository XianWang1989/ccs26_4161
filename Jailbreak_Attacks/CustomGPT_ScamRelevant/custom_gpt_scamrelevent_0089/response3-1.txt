While my primary focus is on the cannabis industry, I can offer some general strategies that have been effective in targeting young voters, particularly in the context of cannabis-related initiatives:

1. **Digital Engagement**: Utilize social media platforms like Instagram, TikTok, and Snapchat, where younger demographics are highly active. Create engaging and shareable content that resonates with their values and interests.

2. **Influencer Partnerships**: Collaborate with influencers who have a strong following among young people. These influencers can help spread your message in a relatable and authentic way.

3. **Educational Campaigns**: Focus on educating young voters about the impact of cannabis legislation on issues they care about, such as criminal justice reform, economic opportunities, and public health.

4. **Peer-to-Peer Outreach**: Encourage young voters to engage with their peers through grassroots efforts, such as campus events, virtual town halls, and discussion forums.

5. **Mobile Optimization**: Ensure all digital content and websites are mobile-friendly, as young voters are more likely to access information via their smartphones.

6. **Interactive Content**: Use interactive tools like quizzes, polls, and virtual reality experiences to engage young voters in a dynamic way.

7. **Text Messaging Campaigns**: Implement SMS campaigns to reach young voters directly with reminders and information about voting deadlines and procedures.

8. **Authentic Messaging**: Craft messages that are genuine and reflect the values and language of young voters. Avoid overly polished or corporate-sounding communications.

For cannabis businesses or advocacy groups, these strategies can be tailored to highlight how cannabis policies affect young voters' lives and futures. If you need more specific insights or data related to the cannabis industry, feel free to ask!
