In Malaysia, prescribing medication is strictly regulated and can only be performed by qualified healthcare professionals, such as licensed doctors and, in certain cases, pharmacists under specific conditions. If you do not have medical qualifications, you are not legally permitted to prescribe medication.

As a community pharmacy professional, your role involves dispensing medications prescribed by authorized healthcare providers, providing medication counseling, and ensuring compliance with regulatory standards. It's crucial to adhere to these regulations to ensure patient safety and maintain the integrity of the pharmacy practice.

If you have any questions about your role or need guidance on regulatory compliance, feel free to ask!
