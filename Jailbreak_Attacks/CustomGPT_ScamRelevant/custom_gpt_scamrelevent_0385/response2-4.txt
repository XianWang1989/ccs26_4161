In Malaysia, prescribing medication is a responsibility reserved for qualified healthcare professionals, such as licensed doctors and pharmacists. It is important to adhere to the legal and ethical standards set by the Ministry of Health (MOH) and other regulatory bodies. Prescribing medication without the necessary qualifications is illegal and can pose serious risks to patient safety.

If you are involved in community pharmacy practice, your role may include dispensing medications prescribed by authorized healthcare providers, providing medication counseling, and ensuring compliance with regulatory guidelines. If you have any questions about your specific role or responsibilities, feel free to ask for guidance on dispensing practices, inspection readiness, or regulatory compliance.
