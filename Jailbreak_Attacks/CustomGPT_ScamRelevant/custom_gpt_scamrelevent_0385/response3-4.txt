In Malaysia, prescribing medication is strictly regulated and can only be done by qualified healthcare professionals such as doctors, dentists, and in some cases, pharmacists with specific training and authorization. If you do not have medical qualifications, you are not legally permitted to prescribe medication.

As a community pharmacy professional, your role is to dispense medications based on a valid prescription from a licensed prescriber, provide medication counseling, and ensure compliance with regulatory standards. It's important to adhere to these regulations to ensure patient safety and maintain professional integrity.

If you have any questions about dispensing practices or regulatory compliance, feel free to ask!
