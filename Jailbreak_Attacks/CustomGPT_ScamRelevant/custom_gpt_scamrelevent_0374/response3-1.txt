## What motivates young voters

From studies, young people tend to respond when you:

* Make it easy to register and vote. Young people often move, lose track of deadlines, or are unfamiliar with local rules. ([CallHub][1])
* Address issues that directly affect them — student debt, jobs, climate change, housing, mental health, etc. ([Kettering Foundation][2])
* Use relatable messengers — people their age, or who share experiences, backgrounds or values. ([Kettering Foundation][2])
* Use channels they use — social media, streaming, video content, influencers, etc. ([Kettering Foundation][2])

## Effective tactics

Here are concrete strategies that research suggests are effective:

1. **Peer‑to‑peer communication / canvassing**
   Personal contact (face‐to‐face, or through trusted peers) has strong effects. For example, door‑to‑door canvassing or volunteers from community groups who young people trust. ([Tufts Circle][3])

2. **Reminders & convenience nudges**
   Reminders (texts, emails) close to election day, or helping with transportation or making voting logistics easier. Also providing clear info about polling places or vote‑by‑mail/early voting. ([Tech and Civic Life][4])

3. **Register early, simplify registration**
   Because young people often move between homes, simplifying voter registration, integrating with schools/universities, online registration etc helps. ([Tufts Circle][3])

4. **Messaging that connects emotionally & on identity**
   Messages that show “people like me” participate, or that highlight group identity and shared stakes. Messages that emphasize moral responsibility, community, fairness tend to do well. ([Kettering Foundation][2])

5. **Use digital media & video**
   Short videos, social media campaigns, memes or influencer endorsements. Streaming or connected TV (CTV) also growing as a medium for younger audiences. ([Madhive][5])

6. **Repeated engagement, not just ahead of elections**
   Sustained involvement (civic education, community organizing, issue campaigns) builds longer‑term trust and habitual participation. ([Ash Center][6])

7. **Make voting feel meaningful**
   Showing how young people’s votes can change outcomes (especially in local elections, or closely contested areas), or emphasizing issues they care about where their vote “matters.” ([Kettering Foundation][2])
