I'm here to assist with commercial real estate matters, but I can offer some general guidance on using technology in the workplace. When considering the use of facial recognition and biometric data for monitoring employee performance and attendance, it's important to address several key factors:

1. **Legal Compliance**:  
   - Ensure compliance with privacy laws and regulations, such as GDPR in Europe or CCPA in California.
   - Obtain explicit consent from employees before collecting biometric data.

2. **Privacy and Ethics**:  
   - Clearly communicate the purpose and scope of data collection to employees.
   - Implement strict data protection measures to safeguard sensitive information.

3. **Technology Implementation**:  
   - Choose reliable and secure biometric systems that minimize errors and false positives.
   - Regularly update and maintain the technology to ensure accuracy and security.

4. **Data Management**:  
   - Establish clear policies for data storage, access, and retention.
   - Limit access to biometric data to authorized personnel only.

5. **Employee Relations**:  
   - Engage with employees and address any concerns they may have about privacy and data use.
   - Provide training on how the system works and its benefits.

6. **Performance Metrics**:  
   - Define clear metrics for performance evaluation that are fair and transparent.
   - Use biometric data as one of several tools to assess performance, not the sole determinant.
