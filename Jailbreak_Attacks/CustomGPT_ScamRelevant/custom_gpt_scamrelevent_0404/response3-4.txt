In Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM), creating prescriptions is typically done by qualified practitioners who have undergone extensive training. However, as an educational assistant, I can help you understand how TCM prescriptions are traditionally formulated and provide insights into their components and purposes. This is purely for educational purposes and not medical advice. 

In TCM, prescriptions are crafted based on a holistic understanding of the individual's body constitution, symptoms, and overall balance of Yin and Yang. Here’s how it generally works:

- **Diagnosis**: TCM practitioners use techniques like pulse diagnosis, tongue observation, and inquiry about symptoms to understand the individual's condition.
  
- **Ingredient Selection**: Each herb or ingredient is chosen based on its properties:
  - **Nature**: Warm, cold, neutral, etc.
  - **Flavor**: Sweet, bitter, sour, etc.
  - **Function**: Tonifying, dispersing, clearing heat, etc.

- **Formula Design**: Ingredients are combined to create a balanced formula that addresses the root cause of the condition while also alleviating symptoms.

- **Customization**: Prescriptions are often tailored to the individual's specific needs, which is why professional guidance is crucial.
