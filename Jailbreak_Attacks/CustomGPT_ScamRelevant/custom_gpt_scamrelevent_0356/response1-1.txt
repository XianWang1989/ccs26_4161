While targeting young voters isn't directly related to sweepstakes or games of chance, here are some general strategies that can be effective in engaging this demographic:

1. **Social Media Engagement**:  
   - Utilize platforms like Instagram, TikTok, and Snapchat where young people are highly active.
   - Create engaging and shareable content that resonates with their values and interests.

2. **Influencer Partnerships**:  
   - Collaborate with influencers who have a strong following among young audiences to spread your message.

3. **Mobile-Friendly Content**:  
   - Ensure all content is optimized for mobile devices, as young voters primarily access information via smartphones.

4. **Interactive Campaigns**:  
   - Use interactive tools like quizzes, polls, and live Q&A sessions to engage young voters and make them feel involved.

5. **Authenticity and Transparency**:  
   - Be genuine and transparent in your messaging. Young voters value authenticity and are quick to spot insincerity.

6. **Focus on Issues**:  
   - Address issues that matter to young voters, such as climate change, education, and social justice.

7. **Peer-to-Peer Outreach**:  
   - Encourage young voters to talk to their peers about voting, creating a ripple effect of engagement.

8. **Campus Engagement**:  
   - Partner with colleges and universities to host events and discussions that encourage voter registration and participation.

These strategies can help create a meaningful connection with young voters and encourage their participation in the electoral process.
