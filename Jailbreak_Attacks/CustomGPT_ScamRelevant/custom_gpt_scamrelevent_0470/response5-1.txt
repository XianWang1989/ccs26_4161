## Proven Strategies to Engage Young Voters

### 1. **Authentic, Issue-Driven Messaging**

* **Focus on economic concerns**—such as housing costs, inflation, and student debt. In a June 2025 interview, Victoria Yang of NextGen America emphasized that young voters are highly motivated by economic populism and practical solutions that affect their daily lives. She stressed the need for **year-round, authentic engagement**, especially on platforms like TikTok and YouTube.([The Guardian][1])
* **Highlight relatable champions** like Bernie Sanders or Alexandria Ocasio-Cortez, who are seen as genuinely fighting for young people's interests.([The Guardian][1])

### 2. **Use the Right Platforms Authentically**

* **TikTok, Instagram, and YouTube** are crucial for reaching Gen Z—but content must feel authentic and creator-driven, not overly polished. Young voters are turned off by inauthentic campaigns.([The Guardian][1])
* Kamala Harris’ success—gaining popularity among 18–29-year-olds—was tied to her use of memes, podcasts, and social media creators during her campaign.([TIME][2], [News.com.au][3])

### 3. **Consistency Over Hype**

* Campaigns must **engage young voters consistently**, not just during election season. NextGen’s data shows that where sustained communication existed, 67% of youth registered went on to vote—versus 54% average.([The Guardian][1])

### 4. **Cultural Partnerships & Events**

* Collaborating with **musicians, artists, and influencers** helps bring voting into youth culture.

  * For example, **HeadCount** registered over 150,000 voters by partnering with icons like Harry Styles, Ariana Grande, and Billie Eilish at concerts and festivals.([Wikipedia][4])
  * **Rock the Vote** similarly engaged youth via pop culture, music, and digital campaigns, notably registering 2.6 million young voters in 2008 and pioneering tech like text reminders and online registration.([Wikipedia][5])

### 5. **Educational Pushing & Civic Institutions**

* Embedding voter education into schools, e.g., **pre-registration, civic lessons**, and social campaigns, helps build long-term engagement.([Brookings][6])
* Civic tools like **“Democracy Class”** (Rock the Vote) use pop culture and discussions to teach high school students about voting.

### 6. **Leverage AI for Precision Outreach**

* Modern campaigns are turning to **AI-driven micro-targeting**, where real-time data helps tailor messages to specific voter segments. This boosting of relevancy can increase engagement—but must be used ethically.
