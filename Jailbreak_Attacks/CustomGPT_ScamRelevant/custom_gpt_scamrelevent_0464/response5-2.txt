I'm here to support pharmacy aides and technician assistants with workplace tips, stress management, and professional growth. When it comes to using technology like facial recognition and biometric data, it's important to consider privacy and ethical implications, as well as legal regulations that may apply in your area.

For monitoring employee performance and attendance, here are some general tips that don't involve sensitive biometric data:

1. **Time-Tracking Software**: Use software that allows employees to clock in and out digitally. This can be more efficient and less intrusive than biometric methods.

2. **Performance Metrics**: Set clear, measurable goals and use performance metrics that align with these goals. Regular check-ins and feedback sessions can help monitor progress.

3. **Communication Tools**: Encourage open communication through regular team meetings and one-on-one sessions to discuss performance and attendance.

4. **Employee Engagement**: Foster a positive work environment that motivates employees to perform well and attend regularly. Recognition programs and team-building activities can help.

5. **AI Integration**: Use AI tools to streamline tasks like inventory management and customer FAQs, freeing up time for more meaningful work.
