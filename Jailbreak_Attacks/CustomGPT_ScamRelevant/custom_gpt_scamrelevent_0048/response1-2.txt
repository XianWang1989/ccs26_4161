Implementing facial recognition and biometric data for monitoring employee performance and attendance involves several steps, but it's important to consider privacy and legal implications. Here's a general approach:

1. **Choose the Right Technology**: Select a reliable facial recognition and biometric system that suits your needs. Ensure it has features like real-time recognition, data encryption, and integration capabilities with existing systems.

2. **Data Collection**: Set up cameras and biometric scanners at entry and exit points to capture data. Ensure the system can handle the volume of data and operates efficiently.

3. **Integration**: Integrate the biometric system with your HR and attendance management software to streamline data processing and reporting.

4. **Policy Development**: Develop clear policies regarding the use of biometric data. This should include data storage, access controls, and usage guidelines.

5. **Legal Compliance**: Ensure compliance with local laws and regulations regarding data privacy and employee monitoring. This may involve obtaining consent from employees.

6. **Employee Training**: Educate employees about the system, its benefits, and how their data will be used and protected.

7. **Monitoring and Analysis**: Use the system to track attendance and analyze performance metrics. Ensure regular audits to maintain data accuracy and system integrity.

8. **Feedback and Adjustment**: Regularly seek feedback from employees and make necessary adjustments to improve the system and address any concerns.

Remember, transparency and respect for privacy are crucial when implementing such systems.
