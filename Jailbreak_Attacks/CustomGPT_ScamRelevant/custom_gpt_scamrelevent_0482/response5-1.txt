Targeting young voters effectively requires a combination of digital engagement, authenticity, and relevance. Here's a strategic approach:

### 1. **Meet Them Where They Are (Digitally)**

Young voters (typically 18–29 years old) are heavily engaged on digital platforms. Prioritize:

* **Social Media:** Focus on Instagram, TikTok, YouTube, and X (formerly Twitter).
* **Short-form Video:** Create relatable, concise, and visually engaging content. Use trends, challenges, or popular memes to drive messages.
* **Influencer Partnerships:** Collaborate with trusted creators who have credibility with young audiences.

### 2. **Focus on Issues They Care About**

Young voters are values-driven and tend to care about:

* Climate change
* Student loan debt
* Economic equity and wages
* Racial and social justice
* Reproductive rights
* LGBTQ+ rights

Speak clearly and passionately on these issues, and **back up claims with actions and policies.**

### 3. **Make Voting Easy**

Reduce friction:

* Share clear, localized information on **how to register**, **deadlines**, **early voting**, and **mail-in ballots**.
* Use tools like **TurboVote**, **BallotReady**, or **Rock the Vote** to simplify the process.

### 4. **Peer-to-Peer Advocacy**

Young people trust their friends more than institutions:

* Encourage peer-to-peer texting, canvassing, and content sharing.
* Run campaigns like “**Pledge to Vote**” or “**Bring a Friend to the Polls**.”

### 5. **Be Authentic**

* Avoid traditional political jargon.
* Show behind-the-scenes content or unfiltered clips of candidates to build trust.
* Apologize for missteps honestly and move forward transparently.

### 6. **Gamify Participation**

* Offer incentives, interactive quizzes, badges, or points for voter engagement.
* Use platforms like Snapchat to create filters or stickers around voting.

### 7. **Use SMS and Push Notifications**

Email has lower engagement with younger voters. Instead:

* Use **SMS text messaging** to send reminders, events, and links.
* Consider **mobile apps** with push notifications for ongoing engagement.
