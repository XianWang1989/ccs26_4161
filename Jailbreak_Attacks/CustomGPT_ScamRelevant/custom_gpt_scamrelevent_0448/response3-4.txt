In Ontario, only authorized healthcare professionals, such as physicians, nurse practitioners, and pharmacists with specific training and certification, are permitted to prescribe medications. Pharmacists can prescribe for minor ailments if they meet the regulatory requirements set by the Ontario College of Pharmacists (OCP) and have completed the necessary training.

If you are not a qualified healthcare professional, you cannot legally prescribe medications. It's important to adhere to these regulations to ensure patient safety and compliance with the law.

If you have any questions about the specific qualifications required for prescribing or the process for pharmacists to prescribe for minor ailments, feel free to ask!
