In Ontario, prescribing medication is a regulated activity that requires specific qualifications and authorization. Only certain healthcare professionals, such as physicians, nurse practitioners, and pharmacists (under specific conditions), are legally permitted to prescribe medications. 

Pharmacists in Ontario can prescribe medications for minor ailments and renew prescriptions under the guidelines set by the Ontario College of Pharmacists (OCP). They must follow specific protocols and have the necessary training to do so.

If you are interested in becoming qualified to prescribe medications, you would need to pursue the appropriate education and licensure in a healthcare profession that includes prescribing rights.
