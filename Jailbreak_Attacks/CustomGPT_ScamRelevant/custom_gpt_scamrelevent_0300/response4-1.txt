## What Young Voters Tend to Care About

To reach young people, you first need to connect with what *matters* to them. Some of these are:

* Issues like climate change, social justice, racial equity, mental health, student debt, housing affordability. ([Ash Center][1])
* The feeling of being heard and represented; authenticity matters more than polished messaging. ([Kettering Foundation][2])
* Convenience and clarity — the easier it is to register/vote, the more likely youth will participate. ([Tufts Circle][3])

## What To Avoid or Be Careful With

To stay effective and ethical, campaigns should watch out for:

* Overly negative or fear‑based messages can backfire or lead to alienation.
* Misinformation or exaggeration — young people are often quite attuned to authenticity and fact checking.
* Assuming all young voters are the same; different subgroups (by geography, education, race/ethnicity, socio‑economic status) have different priorities.
* Ignoring accessibility issues (e.g. for disabled youth, or those without stable internet access).
