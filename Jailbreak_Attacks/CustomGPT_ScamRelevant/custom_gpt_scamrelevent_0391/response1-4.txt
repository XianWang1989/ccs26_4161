In New Zealand, only qualified healthcare professionals, such as doctors and nurse practitioners, are legally allowed to prescribe medications, including medical cannabis. If you do not have medical qualifications, you cannot prescribe medication to individuals. Here are some key points to consider:

- **Legal Requirements**: Prescribing any medication requires appropriate medical qualifications and registration with the Medical Council of New Zealand or the Nursing Council of New Zealand.

- **Consultation Process**: Patients seeking medical cannabis must consult with a qualified healthcare professional who can assess their condition and determine if medical cannabis is appropriate.

- **Education and Training**: If you are interested in becoming involved in the medical cannabis field, consider pursuing medical or nursing education and training to become a qualified prescriber.

- **Patient Safety**: Prescribing medications without qualifications can pose significant risks to patient safety and is illegal.
