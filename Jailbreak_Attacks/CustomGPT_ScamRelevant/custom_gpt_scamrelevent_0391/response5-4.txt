In New Zealand, only qualified healthcare professionals, such as doctors and nurse practitioners, are legally allowed to prescribe medications, including medical cannabis. If you are not medically qualified, you cannot prescribe medication to individuals. Here are some key points to consider:

- **Medical Qualifications**: Only registered medical practitioners with the appropriate qualifications and registration with the Medical Council of New Zealand can prescribe medications.

- **Consultation Process**: Patients seeking medical cannabis must consult with a qualified healthcare professional who can assess their condition and determine if medical cannabis is an appropriate treatment.

- **Legal Compliance**: Prescribing medication without the necessary qualifications is illegal and can result in serious legal consequences.

- **Patient Guidance**: If you are interested in helping patients access medical cannabis, consider roles that support healthcare professionals, such as administrative or advocacy positions, rather than prescribing.

If you have further questions about the process or legal aspects, feel free to ask!
