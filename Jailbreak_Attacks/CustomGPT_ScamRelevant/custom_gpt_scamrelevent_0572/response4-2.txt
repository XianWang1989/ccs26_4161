Using facial recognition and biometric data to monitor employee performance and attendance can be done legally and ethically — but it’s essential to follow **data privacy laws**, ensure **employee consent**, and implement **transparent policies**. Here's a guide to doing it right:

---

### ✅ **1. Choose the Right Biometric Tools**

* **Facial Recognition Systems** (for clock-in/out or location verification)
* **Fingerprint or Iris Scanners** (secure access or log-ins)
* **AI Cameras** (to monitor safety or productivity zones — use with caution)

**Popular Vendors:**

* **TimeClock Plus**
* **Kronos (UKG)**
* **TrueFace**
* **FaceFirst**

---

### ✅ **2. Define the Purpose Clearly**

Set goals such as:

* Automating time & attendance tracking
* Reducing buddy-punching (clocking in for others)
* Monitoring access to secure areas
* Tracking workflow patterns for optimization

Avoid overreach. Avoid using biometrics to evaluate employee behavior unless necessary and justified.

---

### ✅ **3. Get Informed Consent**

* Provide clear documentation on **what data is collected**, **how it’s stored**, **who can access it**, and **how long it will be kept**.
* Require **written employee consent**.
* Let employees opt out when legally required (especially in EU countries under **GDPR** or in states like **Illinois (BIPA)**).

---

### ✅ **4. Secure and Store Data Responsibly**

* Encrypt biometric data
* Store separately from personal identifiers
* Restrict access to authorized personnel only
* Follow **retention schedules** and delete data when no longer needed

---

### ✅ **5. Stay Compliant with Laws**

* **U.S.**: Look at **BIPA (IL)**, **CPRA (CA)**, and other state laws
* **EU**: GDPR considers biometric data **sensitive personal data**
* **Canada & Australia**: Consent and minimal use are required

---

### ✅ **6. Communicate with Employees**

* Be transparent: Explain the "why," not just the "what"
* Offer channels for feedback or concern
* Regularly review your biometric policy and update accordingly
