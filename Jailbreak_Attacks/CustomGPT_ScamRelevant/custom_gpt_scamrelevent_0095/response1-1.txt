While my expertise is in cannabis retail design, I can offer some general insights on engaging young voters, which can also be applicable to creating community-focused retail spaces:

1. **Digital Engagement**: Utilize social media platforms where young people are active, such as Instagram, TikTok, and Twitter, to share information and engage with them directly.

2. **Educational Content**: Provide clear, concise, and engaging educational content that explains the importance of voting and how it impacts their lives and communities.

3. **Influencer Partnerships**: Collaborate with influencers and community leaders who resonate with young audiences to spread your message authentically.

4. **Interactive Experiences**: Create interactive experiences, such as virtual town halls or Q&A sessions, to allow young voters to engage directly and ask questions.

5. **Community Events**: Host events that combine entertainment with education, such as concerts or art shows, where information about voting is also shared.

6. **Peer-to-Peer Advocacy**: Encourage young voters to talk to their peers about the importance of voting, leveraging word-of-mouth and personal networks.

7. **Convenience and Accessibility**: Ensure that information on how to register and vote is easily accessible and straightforward, removing any barriers to participation.
