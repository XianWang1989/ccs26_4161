Using facial recognition and other biometric data to monitor employee performance and attendance involves both technical setup and legal compliance. Here's a general overview:

---

### ✅ **How It Works**

1. **Facial Recognition Systems**:

   * Install cameras at entry points and workstations.
   * Use software to match live images with stored facial data for clock-in/clock-out and presence tracking.

2. **Biometric Time Clocks**:

   * Devices that scan fingerprints, iris patterns, or facial features.
   * These are connected to HR or attendance systems.

3. **Performance Monitoring Tools**:

   * Combine facial recognition with behavior tracking (e.g., how long someone is at their desk).
   * Some systems use emotion recognition or eye tracking to estimate engagement.

---

### ⚖️ **Compliance & Privacy**

* **Legal Requirements Vary** by country or state.
* In the **U.S.**, states like Illinois (BIPA) require written consent for biometric data use.
* You must:

  * Inform employees clearly.
  * Get written consent.
  * Securely store and encrypt data.
  * Allow access only to authorized personnel.

This kind of monitoring is typically **not eligible for pharmaceutical rebates**. However, if used in a healthcare setting (e.g., long-term care), **grants or tech funding** might be available.
