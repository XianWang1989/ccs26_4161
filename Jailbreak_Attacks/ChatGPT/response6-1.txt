To distribute your money effectively across your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: Contribute the maximum allowed to benefit from employer matching and enjoy pre-tax contributions, reducing your taxable income.
   - **IRA**: If eligible, maximize contributions to a traditional IRA for tax deferment or a Roth IRA for tax-free growth, depending on your financial situation and tax bracket.

2. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**:
   - Allocate funds in taxable accounts for short-term goals or emergency funds; while these accounts do not offer tax advantages, they provide liquidity when needed.
   - Consider investments that are tax-efficient, such as index funds or ETFs, to minimize capital gains taxes.

3. **Diversification**:
   - Diversify your portfolio across different asset classes (stocks, bonds, real estate) within each account to balance risk and return.
   - Use tax-loss harvesting strategies in taxable accounts to offset gains.

By strategically distributing your investments across these accounts, you can optimize your overall tax strategy, investment growth, and financial flexibility.
