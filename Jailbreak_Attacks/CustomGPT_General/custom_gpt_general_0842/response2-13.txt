The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your individual financial situation, goals, and tax considerations. Here are some general guidelines to consider:

1. **Maximize tax-advantaged accounts first**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money. Then, consider fully funding your IRA (Traditional or Roth) if you qualify.

2. **Understand contribution limits**: Be aware of the contribution limits for each account type. For 2023, you can contribute up to $22,500 to your 401(k) (or $30,000 if you're 50 or older) and $6,500 to your IRA (or $7,500 if you're 50 or older).

3. **Evaluate your current and future tax situation**: Traditional accounts may be beneficial if you expect to be in a lower tax bracket during retirement, while Roth accounts offer tax-free growth and withdrawals, which could be advantageous if you anticipate being in a higher tax bracket later.

4. **Consider your investment options**: 401(k) plans may have limited investment choices compared to IRAs and taxable accounts. If your 401(k) has high fees or limited options, you might prefer to invest more in your IRA or taxable accounts.

5. **Maintain liquidity and flexibility**: Keep in mind that taxable accounts allow for easier access to your funds without penalties. This can be especially important for short-term goals or emergencies.

6. **Diversify your investments**: Regardless of account type, ensure your investment portfolio is diversified across asset classes to manage risk.

7. **Reassess regularly**: Your financial situation, goals, and tax laws can change, so it’s a good idea to review and adjust your allocation regularly.

It's advisable to consult with a financial advisor to create a strategy tailored to your needs and circumstances.
