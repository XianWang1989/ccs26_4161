Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts effectively depends on several factors, including your current financial situation, future income needs, tax considerations, and retirement goals. Here’s a general strategy to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a matching contribution for your 401(k), aim to contribute at least enough to maximize that match. It’s essentially free money.

2. **Funding the IRA**: If you’re eligible, consider contributing to a Traditional or Roth IRA, as these accounts offer tax advantages. The choice between the two depends on whether you prefer tax deductions now (Traditional) or tax-free withdrawals in retirement (Roth).

3. **401(k) Contributions**: After maximizing your employer match and contributing to your IRA, continue to contribute to your 401(k). This is especially relevant if you have access to a plan with high-quality investment options and low fees.

4. **Taxable Accounts**: Once you have maxed out your contributions to tax-advantaged accounts (the annual limits for 401(k)s and IRAs), consider investing in taxable accounts. These accounts provide more flexibility and easier access to funds without penalties.

5. **Asset Allocation**: Within these accounts, maintain a diversified asset allocation that aligns with your risk tolerance and investment horizon. Generally, a mix of stocks, bonds, and other investments based on your age and risk preference is advisable.

6. **Tax Efficiency**: Be mindful of where you hold different types of investments. For example, it might be better to hold higher-growth, tax-efficient investments like stocks in taxable accounts and more tax-inefficient investments like bonds in tax-advantaged accounts.

7. **Regular Review**: Periodically review your investment strategy and asset allocation to ensure they align with your financial goals and market conditions.

Consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your specific financial situation.
