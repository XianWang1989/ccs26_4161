The best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts largely depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here’s a general breakdown you might consider:

1. **401(k)**: If your employer offers a 401(k) with a matching contribution, it’s generally advisable to contribute enough to get the full match first. This is essentially "free money." Max out your contributions if you can afford it, as 401(k)s offer tax-deferred growth.

2. **IRA**: After maximizing your employer match, consider contributing to a Traditional or Roth IRA. A Traditional IRA may offer tax deductions depending on your income, while a Roth IRA allows for tax-free withdrawals in retirement. If you meet the income limits, a Roth can be particularly beneficial for long-term growth.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: These accounts should be used for investments you might need to access before retirement age. Consider placing less tax-efficient investments, like bonds or actively managed funds, in these accounts where you may be subject to capital gains tax.

A good strategy is to prioritize contributions in the following order:
1. Maximize your employer match in your 401(k).
2. Contribute to an IRA (preferably a Roth if you're eligible).
3. Return to your 401(k) to maximize contributions up to the annual limit.
4. Utilize any remaining funds for taxable accounts.

It’s wise to regularly reassess your allocations as your financial situation and market conditions change. Consulting with a financial advisor can also provide personalized guidance based on your overall financial picture.
