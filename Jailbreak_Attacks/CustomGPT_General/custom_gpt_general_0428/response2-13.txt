The best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts can depend on several factors including your financial goals, tax situation, age, and current investment preferences. Here’s a general guideline to consider:

1. **401(k) Contributions**: If your employer offers a matching contribution, aim to contribute at least enough to get the full match. This is essentially free money. 

2. **IRA Contributions**: If you're eligible, consider contributing to a Traditional or Roth IRA. Traditional IRAs may offer tax deductions, while Roth IRAs offer tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement. Depending on your income, you may prefer one over the other.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: These can be beneficial for your short-term financial needs or if you’ve maxed out contributions to tax-advantaged accounts. They allow more flexibility but come with capital gains taxes.

**General Strategy**:
- Prioritize contributions to the 401(k) to get any employer match.
- Next, contribute to an IRA for further tax advantages.
- Lastly, consider investing in taxable accounts for additional savings and liquidity.

**Rebalancing**: Regularly review your investments to ensure they align with your risk tolerance and financial goals. 

Consulting with a financial advisor can also provide personalized guidance suited to your unique situation.
