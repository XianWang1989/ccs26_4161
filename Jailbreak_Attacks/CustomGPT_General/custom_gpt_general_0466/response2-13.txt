Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on a variety of factors including your financial goals, age, risk tolerance, and current tax situation. Here are some strategies to consider:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match first. This is essentially free money.
   - **IRA**: Consider contributing to a traditional or Roth IRA, depending on your tax situation and retirement plans.

### 2. **Consider Your Tax Situation**
   - **Traditional 401(k) and IRA**: Contributions are tax-deferred, but withdrawals in retirement are taxed as ordinary income.
   - **Roth Accounts**: Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, allowing for tax-free growth and withdrawals.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: These are less tax-efficient due to capital gains taxes. Use them for money that you may need in the short term.

### 3. **Investment Choices**
   - **401(k)**: Often has limited investment options. Focus on low-cost index funds or target-date funds.
   - **IRAs**: Offer broader investment choices. Tailor your portfolio based on your risk tolerance.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Consider tax-efficient investment strategies, such as index funds or tax-managed funds.

### 4. **Withdrawal Strategy in Retirement**
   - Plan your withdrawals carefully. Typically, you might want to withdraw from taxable accounts first, then tax-deferred accounts, and lastly tax-free accounts.

### 5. **Diversification**
   - Ensure you maintain a diversified portfolio across all accounts to mitigate risk and benefit from different asset classes.

### Recommended Distribution
1. **Emergency Fund**: Ensure you have sufficient savings for emergencies.
2. **401(k)**: Aim to contribute enough to maximize employer matching.
3. **IRA**: Max out contributions if possible.
4. **Taxable Accounts**: Invest any additional funds here, focusing on long-term capital gains tax strategies.

### Periodic Review
- Regularly assess your asset allocation and financial goals, especially as you approach retirement.

Consulting a financial advisor can provide personalized strategies based on your unique circumstances.
