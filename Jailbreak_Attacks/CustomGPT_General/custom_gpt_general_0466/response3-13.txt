The best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your tax situation, investment goals, and time horizon. Here’s a general strategy you might consider:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**
   - **401(k):** Contribute enough to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money. If your employer offers a high match, prioritize this.
   - **IRA:** If you qualify, contribute to a traditional IRA or Roth IRA. Roth IRAs are particularly beneficial if you anticipate being in a higher tax bracket in retirement.

### 2. **Consider the Tax Implications**
   - **Taxable Accounts:** After maximizing contributions to tax-advantaged accounts, use taxable accounts for investments that generate less taxable income (like index funds or ETFs).
   - **Capital Gains and Dividends:** Be mindful of capital gains taxes and dividend taxes when deciding what to invest in your taxable accounts.

### 3. **Asset Allocation**
   - **Growth Investments in Tax-Advantaged Accounts:** Consider holding higher growth potential investments (like stocks) in tax-advantaged accounts, as the tax benefits can compound over time.
   - **Bonds and Income-Producing Investments:** These may be better suited for taxable accounts since they generate interest income that can be taxed at a higher rate.

### 4. **Regular Review and Rebalance**
   - Regularly review your asset allocation across all accounts to ensure alignment with your financial goals and risk tolerance. Adjust contributions as needed.

### 5. **Diversification Across Accounts**
   - Maintain a diversified portfolio across all accounts to manage risk effectively.

### Example Strategy (Hypothetical Proportions)
- **401(k):** 15% of income (max out to employer match)
- **IRA:** 5% (traditional or Roth depending on your tax situation)
- **Taxable Accounts:** Any remaining funds after maxing tax-advantaged options

### Individual Considerations
It's essential to tailor this strategy to your personal situation, considering factors like age, current income, expected retirement spending, and overall financial goals.

### Consult a Financial Advisor
Ultimately, working with a financial advisor can provide personalized strategies that consider your unique financial landscape and goals.
