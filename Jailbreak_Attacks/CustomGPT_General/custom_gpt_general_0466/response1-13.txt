Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment strategy. Here are some general guidelines to consider:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**
   - **401(k)**: If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to take full advantage of it. This is essentially "free money." If possible, aim to contribute the maximum allowed, especially if you're in a lower tax bracket.
   - **IRA**: After maximizing your 401(k) contributions, consider funding a Traditional or Roth IRA. Roth IRAs offer tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement, while Traditional IRAs may provide tax deductions upfront.

### 2. **Consider Your Tax Situation**
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Investments in taxable accounts can be less efficient for long-term growth due to capital gains taxes. However, they offer flexibility and access to funds without penalties before retirement age, unlike 401(k)s and IRAs.

### 3. **Balance Between Growth and Liquidity**
   - **401(k) and IRA**: Typically invest for long-term growth (stocks, mutual funds, ETFs) since these accounts are intended for retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: You might want to maintain more liquid assets (bonds, interest-bearing accounts) or invest in tax-efficient funds to minimize capital gains taxes.

### 4. **Diversification**
   - Ensure your overall portfolio is diversified among asset classes (stocks, bonds, real estate) and sectors, regardless of the type of account.

### 5. **Re-evaluate Regularly**
   - As your financial situation changes, periodically reassess your contributions to each account based on changes in income, tax laws, and investment performance.

### Example Allocation Strategy
- **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match, then max out contributions if possible (2023 max is $22,500 or $30,000 if age 50+).
- **IRA**: Max out IRA contributions (2023 max is $6,500 or $7,500 if age 50+).
- **Taxable Accounts**: Any remaining funds can go into a taxable account, focusing on investments that are tax-efficient.

### Conclusion
Your specific situation will dictate the best approach, so consider consulting with a financial advisor for personalized advice, especially regarding tax implications and long-term financial goals.
