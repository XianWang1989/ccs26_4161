Distributing your money amongst a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment strategy. Here’s a general approach:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match first. This is essentially "free money."
   - **IRA**: If eligible, consider contributing to a Roth IRA for tax-free growth or a Traditional IRA for immediate tax benefits.

2. **Consider Tax Implications**:
   - Funds in 401(k) and Traditional IRA are taxed upon withdrawal, while Roth IRAs offer tax-free withdrawals in retirement. Taxable accounts are subject to capital gains taxes.

3. **Balance Contributions**:
   - Aim to fully fund your 401(k) and IRA before putting significant funds into taxable accounts. If you can't max them out, consider proportionate splits based on contribution limits and your current tax bracket.

4. **Diversification**:
   - Ensure that your investments across these accounts are diversified to mitigate risk.

5. **Reassess Regularly**:
   - Review your distribution strategy annually or when your financial situation changes (e.g., job change, major expenses).

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**:
   - A professional can tailor a strategy based on your specific circumstances and goals.

By prioritizing contributions to tax-advantaged accounts, you can potentially maximize your retirement savings and minimize your tax burden.
