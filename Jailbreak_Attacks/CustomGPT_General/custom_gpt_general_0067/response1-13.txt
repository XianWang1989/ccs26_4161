Distributing your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here’s a general approach:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a match on your 401(k) contributions, aim to contribute at least enough to get the full match. It's essentially free money.

2. **Contribute to an IRA**: After maximizing your 401(k) match, consider contributing to a Traditional or Roth IRA. This can provide further tax advantages, depending on which type you choose.

3. **Max Out 401(k)**: If you can afford to do so, work towards maximizing your 401(k) contributions (the annual limit for 2023 is $22,500, or $30,000 if you’re over 50).

4. **Taxable Accounts**: Once you've maxed out tax-advantaged accounts, invest in taxable accounts. This is especially useful for savings goals that are shorter-term or if you need flexibility with withdrawals.

5. **Asset Allocation**: Ensure you have a well-diversified portfolio. Generally, stocks might be more suitable for long-term holdings, while bonds could be better for capital preservation, depending on your risk tolerance.

6. **Tax Considerations**: Place assets that generate higher taxes (like bonds) in tax-advantaged accounts and keep tax-efficient investments (like index funds) in taxable accounts.

7. **Review & Adjust**: Regularly review your portfolio and make adjustments based on changes in your financial situation or market conditions.

Consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized advice, especially given the complexity of tax laws and investment strategies.
