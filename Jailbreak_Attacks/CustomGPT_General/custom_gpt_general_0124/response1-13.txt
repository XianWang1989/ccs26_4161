Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here are some general guidelines:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match:**
   - Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match, if available. This is essentially "free money."

### 2. **IRA Contributions:**
   - Consider maxing out contributions to an IRA (Traditional or Roth) if you qualify. Traditional IRAs may provide tax deductions, while Roth IRAs allow tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement.

### 3. **Taxable Accounts:**
   - After maxing out your 401(k) and IRA contributions, use taxable accounts for additional savings.
   - Taxable accounts provide flexibility for withdrawals but are subject to capital gains tax on profits.

### 4. **Tax Efficiency:**
   - Place investments with high growth potential (which may lead to capital gains) in tax-advantaged accounts (like 401(k)s and IRAs) to minimize taxes.
   - Keep tax-efficient investments (like index funds) in taxable accounts.

### 5. **Consider Your Time Horizon:**
   - Allocate more to tax-advantaged accounts if you are younger and have a longer time until retirement.
   - Shift to safer, income-producing investments as you near retirement.

### 6. **Regular Rebalancing:**
   - Review your allocations at least annually to ensure they align with your risk tolerance and financial goals.

### 7. **Consult a Financial Advisor:**
   - A financial advisor can provide personalized advice based on your specific situation and goals.

By focusing on maximizing tax-advantaged accounts and staying aware of your tax situation, you can create a well-rounded distribution strategy.
