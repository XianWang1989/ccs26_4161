Distributing your money across a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts optimally requires considering several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment horizon. Here are some strategies to consider:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**:
   - **401(k)**: If your employer offers a 401(k) match, contribute enough to get the full match first, as this is essentially free money. After that, consider increasing your contributions as you can.
   - **IRA**: Contribute to a traditional or Roth IRA, depending on your income level and tax strategy. A Roth IRA allows for tax-free growth and withdrawals, making it ideal if you anticipate being in a higher tax bracket in retirement.

2. **Tax Efficiency**:
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Use taxable accounts for investments that generate qualified dividends or long-term capital gains, as these are taxed at a lower rate than ordinary income. Avoid holding high-turnover investments in taxable accounts to minimize capital gains taxes.

3. **Consider Asset Location**:
   - Place high-growth investments in tax-advantaged accounts (like stocks in your 401(k) or IRA) where they can grow tax-free or tax-deferred.
   - Hold bonds or other income-generating assets in taxable accounts, as they may be taxed at a lower rate than ordinary income generated in tax-advantaged accounts.

4. **Diversification**:
   - Ensure that your overall portfolio is diversified across all accounts to manage risk effectively. This means spreading out investments not just within each account but also across all accounts.

5. **Regular Review and Rebalance**:
   - Periodically review and rebalance your investments to ensure that your allocation remains aligned with your financial goals and risk tolerance.

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**:
   - Consider consulting with a financial planner to tailor your distribution strategy to your specific financial situation, tax considerations, and retirement goals.

In summary, start by maximizing contributions to tax-advantaged accounts, maintain tax efficiency, consider asset location, and regularly review your portfolio. Each individual's circumstances will vary, so personal adjustments may be necessary as you refine your strategy.
