Distributing your money between a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some strategies to consider for an effective distribution:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to get the full match first. This is essentially free money.
   - **IRA**: Consider contributing to a Traditional or Roth IRA, depending on your income level and tax situation. Roth IRAs offer tax-free withdrawals in retirement, which may be beneficial if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket later.

2. **Assess Your Tax Situation**:
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Generally, you can invest in taxable accounts once you’ve maximized contributions to tax-advantaged accounts. This is especially relevant if you have short-term needs since tax-efficient investments (like index funds or ETFs) can help minimize capital gains taxes.

3. **Investment Horizon**:
   - Design your allocations based on your time horizon. Longer-term goals can afford to take more risks, whereas shorter-term goals may benefit from more conservative investments.

4. **Diversification**:
   - Ensure that your investment choices across all accounts are diversified. Different accounts may offer different tax implications, so aligning your investment strategy with your overall asset allocation is crucial.

5. **Rebalancing**:
   - Regularly review and rebalance your portfolio across all accounts to maintain your desired asset allocation and risk level.

6. **Consider Future Withdrawals**:
   - Keep in mind the withdrawal rules for each account type. For instance, 401(k)s and IRAs have penalties for early withdrawals, whereas you can access your taxable account funds more freely.

7. **Consult a Financial Advisor**:
   - If you're uncertain about how to allocate your investments, consider speaking with a financial advisor. They can help tailor a strategy to your individual circumstances.

### Sample Distribution Strategy:
- **401(k)**: 15% of total savings (up to employer match).
- **IRA**: 10% of total savings.
- **Taxable Accounts**: 75% of total savings (once tax-advantaged accounts are maximized).

This strategy varies significantly based on individual circumstances, so it's important to adjust it according to your specific needs and goals.
