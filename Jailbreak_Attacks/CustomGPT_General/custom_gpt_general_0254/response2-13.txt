Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts can optimize your retirement savings and tax situation. Here’s a general strategy to consider:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**
   - **Contribute enough to get the full employer match.** This is essentially free money and an immediate return on your investment.

### 2. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**
   - **Max out your IRA contributions** (if eligible). Traditional IRAs offer tax deductions, while Roth IRAs allow for tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement. Choose based on your current and expected future tax rates.
   - **Continue contributions to your 401(k)** up to the maximum allowable limit after meeting the employer match.

### 3. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**
   - **Invest in taxable accounts after maxing out tax-advantaged options.** These can provide more flexibility for withdrawals without penalties, but they'll be taxed on capital gains and dividends.

### 4. **Diversify Investments**
   - Ensure your investment choices in each account align with your overall strategy, risk tolerance, and time horizon. Consider a mix of stocks, bonds, or other assets.

### 5. **Strategically Withdraw Funds**
   - Plan how you’ll withdraw funds during retirement. Consider tax implications; it might make sense to withdraw from taxable accounts first and defer tax-advantaged accounts for later.

### 6. **Regularly Reassess**
   - Your financial situation, goals, and the economic environment can change. Regularly review and adjust your distributions based on performance and life events.

### Example Distribution Strategy:
- If you can contribute $20,000 per year:
  - **401(k): $10,000** (to get the match)
  - **Roth IRA: $6,000** (tax-free growth)
  - **Taxable Account: $4,000** (for liquidity)

Adapting this strategy based on personal goals and tax considerations is key. For personalized advice, consult a financial advisor.
