Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts effectively depends on various factors including your financial goals, tax situation, and time horizon. Here are some key considerations:

### 1. **401(k) Contributions**:
   - **Maximize Contributions**: If your employer offers a matching contribution, aim to contribute at least enough to get the full match. This is essentially free money.
   - **Tax Advantages**: Traditional 401(k) contributions reduce your taxable income, while Roth contributions allow for tax-free withdrawals in retirement.

### 2. **IRA Contributions**:
   - **Prioritize Roth or Traditional**: Choose a Roth IRA if you expect your tax rate to be higher in retirement. A Traditional IRA may suit you better if you want tax deductions now.
   - **Contribution Limits**: Be aware of the annual contribution limits ($6,500 for those under 50 as of 2023; $7,500 for 50 and older).

### 3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - **After-Tax Contributions**: Use taxable accounts for investments that generate capital gains or dividends, as they may be taxed at a lower rate than ordinary income.
   - **Investment Choice**: Consider holding tax-efficient investments (like index funds or ETFs) to minimize capital gains tax.

### 4. **Asset Allocation**:
   - Diversify your investments across accounts based on your risk tolerance. Generally, it’s wise to keep more aggressive investments in tax-deferred accounts (like 401(k) and IRAs) while placing more conservative investments in taxable accounts.

### 5. **Consider Your Time Horizon**:
   - Short-term goals might be better suited for taxable accounts, while long-term savings can benefit from the tax advantages of retirement accounts.

### 6. **Regularly Review and Adjust**:
   - Periodically reassess your strategy as your financial situation and the tax landscape change. Rebalance your investments to align with your goals.

### Example Distribution Strategy:
- **401(k)**: Max out contributions, especially to take advantage of employer matching.
- **IRA**: If eligible, contribute to both a Traditional and Roth IRA (if you can afford it).
- **Taxable Account**: Invest extra savings after maxing out retirement accounts in low-cost, tax-efficient funds.

### Conclusion:
Tailor your strategy to your specific financial situation. Consulting with a financial advisor can provide personalized guidance based on your individual needs and goals.
