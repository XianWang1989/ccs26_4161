Investing across different account types like a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts can optimize your savings and tax situation. Here’s a general approach to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a match on your 401(k), contribute enough to get the full match first. It's essentially "free money."

2. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)/IRA**: Contribute to these accounts as they offer tax benefits (pre-tax contributions in a 401(k) reduce your taxable income; Roth IRAs offer tax-free withdrawals in retirement).
   - **Contribute Annually**: Aim to maximize contributions to your IRA ($6,500 for those under 50 in 2023; $7,500 if over 50) and your 401(k) ($22,500 for those under 50; $30,000 if over 50).

3. **Invest Based on Time Horizon**:
   - **Long-Term Goals (Retirement)**: Use the 401(k) and IRA for long-term, growth-oriented investments, focusing on a diversified mix of stocks, bonds, and other assets.
   - **Short-Term Goals (Emergencies)**: Keep an emergency fund in a taxable account, with more liquid investments.

4. **Consider Tax Implications**:
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Invest in tax-efficient funds (like index funds or ETFs) to minimize capital gains taxes.
   - **Asset Location**: Place high-growth assets in tax-advantaged accounts and lower-growth, income-generating assets (like bonds) in taxable accounts.

5. **Regularly Review and Rebalance**: Monitor your allocations and adjust as your financial situation or market conditions change.

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: If you have specific goals, it's wise to consult with a financial advisor to tailor a strategy to your individual needs.

This strategy balances tax efficiency, growth potential, and risk management across your investment portfolio.
