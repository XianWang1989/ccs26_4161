Distributing your money among 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and time horizon. Here are some general strategies to consider:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**
   - **401(k)**: If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to get the full match first; it’s essentially “free money.” Aim to max out your contributions if possible.
   - **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**: After maximizing the 401(k) match, consider contributing to an IRA. A Roth IRA offers tax-free growth and withdrawals if you meet the requirements, while a Traditional IRA may provide tax deductions.

### 2. **Consider Your Tax Situation**
   - **Taxable Accounts**: These should be funded after you achieve your goals in tax-advantaged accounts. Keep in mind that interest, dividends, and capital gains are taxable in these accounts. You might consider using them for short-term goals or as an emergency fund.

### 3. **Investment Choices**
   - **401(k) and IRA**: Often have a limited selection of investment options. Choose a diversified mix of stocks, bonds, and funds that align with your risk tolerance and goals.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Here, you can be strategic with your investments. For example, consider holding tax-efficient investments, like index funds or ETFs, to minimize capital gains taxes.

### 4. **Rebalance Regularly**
   - Monitor your allocations across all accounts to ensure they align with your risk tolerance and financial goals. Rebalance as necessary to maintain your target asset allocation.

### 5. **Think Long Term**
   - Prioritize long-term growth, especially in tax-advantaged accounts. Avoid withdrawing from these accounts early to benefit from compound growth.

### 6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**
   - If your financial situation is complex, consulting a financial advisor can provide personalized strategies tailored to your unique circumstances.

### Sample Distribution Strategy
- **401(k)**: 15% of income up to the max contribution.
- **IRA**: Contribute the max allowed annually (e.g., $6,500 for individuals under 50).
- **Taxable Accounts**: Allocate any remaining funds based on your emergency fund and short-term goals.

By following these strategies, you can create a balanced approach that optimizes your savings, minimizes taxes, and supports your overall financial objectives.
