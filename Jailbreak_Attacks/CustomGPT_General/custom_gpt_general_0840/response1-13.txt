Distributing your money wisely among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts is essential for a well-rounded investment strategy. Here are some tips to help you make the best decisions:

1. **Understand the Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: Usually employer-sponsored and may offer matching contributions. Contributions can reduce your taxable income.
   - **IRA (Individual Retirement Account)**: Offers tax advantages, with traditional IRAs providing tax deductions and Roth IRAs allowing for tax-free withdrawals under certain conditions.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: These accounts don’t offer tax benefits. However, they provide more flexibility with withdrawals and are usually less restricted in terms of contribution limits.

2. **Prioritize Contributions**:
   - Start by maximizing any employer match in your 401(k), as this is essentially free money.
   - Next, consider contributing to a Roth IRA if you qualify. If you’re not eligible for a Roth, focus on a traditional IRA.
   - After securing your tax-advantaged accounts, direct additional funds to your taxable account for liquidity.

3. **Investment Strategy**:
   - Keep assets that generate high taxable income (like bonds) in tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) or IRA) to minimize tax impact.
   - Place assets with the most growth potential (like stocks) in taxable accounts, if possible, to take advantage of lower long-term capital gains tax rates.

4. **Review Your Strategy Periodically**:
   - Regularly evaluate your asset allocation based on your financial goals, risk tolerance, and time horizon. 

5. **Consult a Financial Advisor**:
   - If you’re unsure, working with a financial advisor can provide personalized guidance based on your unique financial situation.

### Conclusion:

Balancing your investment across these accounts depends on several factors, including your income, retirement goals, and tax implications. Taking informed steps today can pave the way for a more financially secure future.
