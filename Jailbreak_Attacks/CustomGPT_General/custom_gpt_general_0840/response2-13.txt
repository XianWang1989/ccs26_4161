When it comes to distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, it's essential to have a strategic plan that aligns with your financial goals, tax situation, and time horizon. Let's break it down step by step.

### Understand Your Options

**1. 401(k): The Employer-Backed Retirement Account**
   - This is often the first step for many employees. If your employer offers matching contributions, make sure to take full advantage of that match—it's essentially free money!

**2. IRA: Individual Retirement Account**
   - An IRA (Traditional or Roth) offers tax advantages and is a great supplement to your 401(k). Traditional IRAs allow for tax-deductible contributions, while Roth IRAs provide tax-free withdrawals in retirement.

**3. Taxable Accounts: Flexible Investment Vehicles**
   - Taxable accounts give you flexibility with funds—no age restrictions or penalties for withdrawals. However, be mindful of capital gains taxes on any investments.

### Strategic Distribution Tips

**Start with Contributions**
- **Maximize Your 401(k) Contributions:** Before you allocate funds elsewhere, ensure you’re contributing enough to maximize any employer match.
- **Consider an IRA:** If you can still contribute after your 401(k), aim for the maximum contribution limits in your IRA as well.

**Assess Your Tax Situation**
- **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First:** Place bonds and other income-generating assets in tax-advantaged accounts to minimize tax liability.
- **Consider the Tax Implications:** If you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement, a Roth IRA might be more beneficial, as your withdrawals will be tax-free.

**Balancing Investments Across Accounts**
- **Diversify Your Investments:** Don't put all your eggs in one basket. Utilize a mix of stocks, bonds, and funds across your accounts while being mindful of your risk tolerance.

**Rebalance Periodically**
- Make it a habit to review and rebalance the asset allocation in each account periodically. As your financial situation changes, your asset distribution should reflect your current goals.

### Conclusion

Distributing your money wisely across a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts is crucial for long-term financial health. By maximizing your contributions, understanding the tax implications of your accounts, and regularly rebalancing your investments, you are setting yourself up for a more secure financial future.

### Actionable Takeaways
- Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the employer match.
- Explore opening and contributing to an IRA.
- Allocate funds across accounts based on your tax situation to minimize liabilities.
- Regularly review your investment allocations.
