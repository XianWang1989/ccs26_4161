Managing your investments across a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts can seem like a juggling act, but with a little strategy, you can keep everything balanced! Here’s a fun approach to consider:

1. **Max Out Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**: 
   - Aim to contribute enough to your 401(k) to get any employer match—it's essentially free money! Then, consider contributing to a traditional or Roth IRA, depending on your tax situation.

2. **Consider Your Tax Situations**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are pre-tax, reducing your taxable income. Later, withdrawals during retirement will be taxed.
   - **IRA**: If you opt for a Roth IRA, you pay taxes upfront, but withdrawals during retirement are tax-free. A traditional IRA works the other way.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Capital gains and dividends are taxed in the year they're realized, so it's a good spot for investments you'll want to access sooner.

3. **Asset Allocation**:
   - Keep more volatile investments (like stocks) in tax-advantaged accounts to take advantage of tax-free growth. 
   - Place bonds or stable investments in taxable accounts, as they generate income that can be taxed at ordinary rates.

4. **Rebalance Regularly**:
   - Check your allocations at least annually to ensure they reflect your risk tolerance and market changes.

5. **Consult a Professional**:
   - If you’re ever unsure, a financial planner can tailor a strategy specific to your goals and needs.

In the end, remember: it's not about having a pile of money in each account, but rather aligning your financial strategy with your retirement goals. Happy investing!
