When deciding how to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, it’s like planning a balanced diet—variety is key! Here’s a lighthearted breakdown:

1. **401(k):** Think of this as your main meal—it's great for long-term growth! Take full advantage if your employer offers a match. It’s free money, like finding extra fries at the bottom of the bag!

2. **IRA:** This is like dessert—sweet, but you want to limit the calories! Whether it's a traditional IRA or Roth IRA, consider your tax situation. If you expect your tax rate to be higher in retirement, the Roth might be the way to go. Plus, it’s tax-free growth—who doesn’t love that?

3. **Taxable Accounts:** These are your snacks—easy to access whenever you need them. Use these for short-term goals or emergency funds. Just remember, they can come with a side of taxes on gains!

**The Balance:** Start with your 401(k) (especially to grab that match), then consider maxing out your IRA for those delicious tax benefits. After that, fill up your taxable account as needed. Adjust your portions based on your goals, risk tolerance, and how soon you’ll need the cash. 

And remember, just like with food, you can always change your recipe as your life circumstances change! 🍔💰
