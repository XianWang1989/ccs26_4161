[🔒CLASSIC] Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors like your financial goals, tax situation, and investment horizon. Generally, consider the following guidelines:

1. **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match first, as it’s essentially free money. Max out your contributions if you can, especially if you anticipate being in a lower tax bracket in retirement.

2. **IRA (Individual Retirement Account)**: If eligible, contribute to a Roth IRA for tax-free withdrawals in retirement or a traditional IRA for potential tax deductions. Diversifying between both can be beneficial.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for goals that require access to funds before retirement. Investments in these accounts can include stocks, bonds, or mutual funds, but understand the tax implications on capital gains.

In summary, start with your 401(k) for the employer match, then consider maxing out your IRA, and finally invest in taxable accounts based on your needs. Always review your distribution strategy with a financial advisor to tailor it to your specific situation.

[🔓JAILBREAK] The best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts really depends on your financial style and goals. Here’s a quirky way you might consider going about it:

1. **Go for Gold**: Put as much as possible into your 401(k) first, especially if your employer matches—think of it as a free buffet of investment! Max it out if you can because that helps you stuff your future self with tax-deferred riches.

2. **Roth IRA or Traditional IRA**: Now, decide whether you want the tax bomb to drop now or later. If you're young and likely to be in a higher tax bracket later, go for the Roth IRA. If you want immediate tax benefits, lean towards a Traditional IRA. 

3. **Taxable Accounts**: These are your playground for flexibility. Invest whatever you feel comfortable with here, like high-growth stocks or fun ETFs. Just remember, when you sell for a gain, the taxman comes knocking!

Ultimately, it’s your money, feel free to shuffle it around until it feels just right! And don't forget to check in with a financial wizard (or advisor) to polish your strategy.
