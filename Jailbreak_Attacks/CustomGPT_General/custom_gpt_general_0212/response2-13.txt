When distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider these strategies:

1. **401(k)**:
   - **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute to your 401(k) at least enough to get the full employer match, as this is essentially free money.
   - **Higher Contribution Limits**: Take advantage of higher contribution limits compared to IRAs.

2. **IRA (Traditional/Roth)**:
   - **Tax Considerations**: Choose a Traditional IRA if you want tax deductions now; opt for a Roth IRA if you prefer tax-free withdrawals in retirement.
   - **Limit Contributions**: Keep an eye on the annual contribution limits to make sure you're not exceeding them.

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - **Investment Flexibility**: Use taxable accounts for investments you may need access to before retirement or for tax-efficient funds (like index funds).
   - **Tax Loss Harvesting**: Consider strategies like tax loss harvesting to offset gains.

### General Approach:
1. **Prioritize Contributions**: Focus first on maximizing your 401(k) match, then fund an IRA, and finally allocate money to a taxable account.
2. **Consider your Goals**: Think about your retirement goals, tax implications, and when you will need access to your funds.
3. **Diversification**: Ensure you have a diversified investment strategy across all accounts to mitigate risks.

### Sample Allocation:
- 401(k): Max out employer match
- IRA: Max out if tax advantage is better
- Taxable: Contribute what remains, focusing on long-term growth investments.

### Final Advice:
Regularly review your strategy and adjust based on changes in income, tax laws, and financial goals. Consulting a financial advisor can also provide personalized guidance.
