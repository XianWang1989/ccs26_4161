The optimal distribution of your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts can depend on several factors including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here’s a general approach:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**: If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to your 401(k) to take full advantage of that match. This is essentially "free money" and offers a great return on investment.

2. **Contribute to an IRA**: If you’re eligible, consider contributing to a traditional IRA or a Roth IRA. A traditional IRA might provide immediate tax deductions, while a Roth IRA offers tax-free withdrawals in retirement. If your income allows, max out contributions to your IRA after accounting for your 401(k) match.

3. **Continue Funding Your 401(k)**: Once you've maxed out your IRA contributions, return to your 401(k) and increase contributions as much as you can, ideally reaching the annual contribution limit.

4. **Invest in Taxable Accounts**: If you’ve maxed out your retirement accounts, consider investing in taxable accounts. These accounts offer flexibility and access to your funds without the penalties associated with early withdrawals from retirement accounts.

5. **Asset Allocation**: Within each account type, ensure proper diversification. Generally, higher-risk investments may be better suited for tax-advantaged accounts (like stocks), while more stable investments (like bonds) can be placed in taxable accounts for better tax management.

6. **Review Tax Implications**: Be mindful of capital gains taxes in your taxable accounts and the tax treatment of withdrawals from your retirement accounts. 

7. **Regularly Reassess**: Your financial situation may change, so regularly reassess your contributions and allocations based on performance, market conditions, and changes in your income or expenses.

It's wise to seek advice from a financial advisor for personalized strategies fitting your specific situation.
