Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your income, tax situation, and retirement goals. Here are some general strategies:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to receive any employer match, as this is essentially "free money."

2. **Contribute to an IRA**: After maximizing the match, consider contributing to an IRA (traditional or Roth, depending on your tax situation). Roth IRAs grow tax-free, which can be beneficial if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement.

3. **Continue 401(k) Contributions**: Once you've contributed to your IRA, direct additional savings back into your 401(k) to take advantage of tax-deferred growth.

4. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**: Use taxable accounts for investments that may generate more frequent income (like dividends) or for funds you'll need before retirement age, as those accounts are more accessible.

5. **Consider After-Tax Contributions**: If your 401(k) allows, consider after-tax contributions for higher limits on contributions, potentially using a Mega Backdoor Roth strategy.

6. **Diversify Investments**: Regardless of the account type, diversify your investments to manage risk and enhance potential returns.

7. **Rebalance Regularly**: Periodically review and rebalance your accounts to ensure your asset allocation aligns with your risk tolerance and goals.

Consult with a financial advisor who can tailor a plan to your specific situation for the best results.
