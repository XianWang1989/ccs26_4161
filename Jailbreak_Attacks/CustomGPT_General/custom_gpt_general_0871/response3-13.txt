Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here’s a general guide:

### GPT: 
1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to receive any employer match, as this is essentially free money.

2. **Contribute to an IRA**: If you’re eligible, consider contributing to a traditional or Roth IRA for tax advantages. A Roth IRA offers tax-free withdrawals in retirement if certain conditions are met.

3. **Prioritize Taxable Accounts**: After maxing out tax-advantaged accounts, use taxable accounts for additional savings. This allows for more flexibility and access to funds without penalties.

4. **Asset Allocation**: Ensure your investments are diversified across all accounts. Consider your risk tolerance and investment horizons to guide your asset allocation.

5. **Rebalance Regularly**: Periodically reassess your investments based on performance and changes in your financial situation.
