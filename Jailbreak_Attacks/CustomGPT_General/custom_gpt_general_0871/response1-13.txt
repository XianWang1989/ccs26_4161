To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following steps:

1. **Maximize Employer Match:** Start with your 401(k). Contribute enough to get the full employer match, as this is essentially free money.

2. **Assess Contribution Limits:** Understand the contribution limits for each account:
    - **401(k):** $22,500 for 2023 (or $30,000 if you're 50 or older).
    - **IRA:** $6,500 for 2023 (or $7,500 if you're 50 or older).

3. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts:** After maximizing your 401(k) match, focus on contributing to your IRA for tax benefits. Depending on your income, you may want to consider a Roth IRA for tax-free growth.

4. **Balance Investments:** Diversify your investments among all accounts based on your goals, risk tolerance, and time horizon. For example:
    - **401(k):** Usually has limited investment options but may offer low-cost index funds.
    - **IRA:** Choose a wider array of investments, including stocks, bonds, and ETFs based on your strategy.
    - **Taxable Accounts:** Focus on investments with favorable tax treatment (e.g., long-term capital gains).

5. **Rebalance Regularly:** Periodically review and adjust your allocations based on performance and changing goals.

6. **Tax Considerations:** Be mindful of tax implications. Taxable accounts are subject to capital gains taxes, while tax-advantaged accounts grow tax-deferred or tax-free.

7. **Emergency Fund:** Ensure you have an emergency fund in place before investing heavily in retirement accounts.

8. **Long-Term Perspective:** Focus on long-term growth rather than short-term fluctuations in market value.

Consulting a financial advisor for personalized advice can also be beneficial.
