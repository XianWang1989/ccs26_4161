Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here’s a general strategy to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match, as this is essentially free money.

2. **Prioritize High-Interest Debt**: If you have any high-interest debt, consider paying that off first before investing heavily.

3. **Contribute to an IRA**: After maximizing your 401(k) match, consider contributing to an IRA (Traditional or Roth) based on your eligibility and tax preferences.

4. **Maximize 401(k) Contributions**: Then, focus on maximizing your 401(k) contributions, taking advantage of tax-deferred growth.

5. **Use Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**: Finally, allocate funds to taxable accounts for more flexible access to your money, especially if you anticipate needing funds before retirement age.

### Considerations:
- **Tax Implications**: Assess your current and expected future tax rates to determine whether a Traditional or Roth IRA is more beneficial.
- **Investment Goals**: Align your investment strategy with your goals (retirement, buying a home, etc.).
- **Emergency Fund**: Ensure you have an adequate emergency fund before heavily investing.

### Example Allocation:
- 401(k): 15-20% of your income (up to the max)
- IRA: $6,500 (or $7,500 if over 50) annually
- Taxable accounts: Any remaining funds after retirement accounts

Always consult with a financial advisor to tailor this strategy to your personal circumstances.
