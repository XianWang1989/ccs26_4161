Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here are some general guidelines:

### Identify Your Goals
1. **Retirement Savings**: Prioritize tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) and IRA).
2. **Short-term Needs**: Use taxable accounts for more accessible funds.

### Tax Considerations
1. **401(k) and IRA**: Contributions are often tax-deductible or pre-tax, reducing taxable income now.
2. **Taxable Accounts**: Investments here can incur capital gains taxes, so be mindful of asset placement.

### Contribution Prioritization
1. **Max Out Employer Match**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get any employer match—this is free money.
2. **Max Out IRA**: If eligible, contribute to an IRA (either traditional or Roth).
3. **Return to 401(k)**: After receiving the employer match and funding your IRA, increase your 401(k) contributions.

### Asset Allocation
1. **Stocks vs. Bonds**: Consider putting more volatile investments (like stocks) in tax-advantaged accounts to defer taxes.
2. **Bonds in Taxable Accounts**: Since interest from bonds is taxable, you might want to keep them in a retirement account.

### Withdrawals
1. **Plan for Retirement Withdrawals**: Strategically withdraw from each account type based on tax implications.

### Regular Review
1. **Rebalance Your Portfolio**: Adjust contributions and allocations based on performance and changing goals over time.

### Consider Professional Advice
- Consult a financial advisor to tailor a strategy based on your unique situation.

By following these guidelines, you can optimize your investment strategy effectively.
