Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment time horizon. Here are some strategies to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**:
   - Contribute enough to your 401(k) to receive any employer match, as this is essentially free money.

2. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: Consider maxing out your contributions, especially if you're in a high tax bracket, since contributions reduce your taxable income.
   - **IRA**: If you're eligible, contribute to a Roth IRA for tax-free growth, or a Traditional IRA for potential tax deductions.

3. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**:
   - Use taxable accounts for money you might need access to before retirement. These can be more flexible, although less tax-efficient.

4. **Asset Allocation**:
   - Utilize tax-efficient investments (like index funds) in taxable accounts to minimize capital gains taxes. Allocate bonds or high-dividend stocks in tax-advantaged accounts to avoid higher tax rates.

5. **Rebalance Regularly**:
   - Regularly review and adjust your portfolio to maintain your desired asset allocation across all accounts.

6. **Consider Future Needs**:
   - If you anticipate needing access to funds before retiring, prioritize savings in taxable accounts.

Consulting a financial advisor can also help tailor these strategies to your specific situation.
