Distributing your money across a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts involves a few key considerations:

1. **401(k)**:
   - **Maximize Contributions**: Aim to contribute enough to get any employer match, as it’s essentially free money.
   - **Tax Benefits**: Contributions reduce your taxable income, and earnings grow tax-deferred.

2. **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**:
   - **Diverse Investment Options**: IRAs often provide more investment choices.
   - **Roth IRA Advantage**: If eligible, consider maxing out a Roth IRA for tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement.

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - **Flexibility**: These accounts allow easier access to funds, but capital gains taxes apply.
   - **Tax-Efficient Investments**: Focus on investments with favorable tax treatment, like index funds or municipal bonds.

### Recommended Approach:

- **Emergency Fund**: Ensure you have 3-6 months of living expenses saved.
- **Prioritize Retirement Accounts**: Max out your 401(k) with the employer match, then contribute to an IRA. 
- **Taxable Accounts for Additional Savings**: Use these for investments after maxing out tax-advantaged accounts.

### General Strategy:
1. **401(k)**: Contribute up to the employer match.
2. **IRA**: Fund your IRA (preferably Roth if eligible) next.
3. **Taxable Accounts**: Invest any additional savings here.

Adjust your percentages based on your retirement timeline, risk tolerance, and financial goals. Consulting a financial advisor can provide personalized advice.
