Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here’s a general approach:

### 1. **Assess Your Goals**
   - **Short-Term Needs:** Use taxable accounts for liquidity.
   - **Retirement Goals:** Focus on 401(k) and IRA contributions for tax advantages.

### 2. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**
   - **401(k):** Contribute enough to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money.
   - **IRA:** Consider traditional or Roth IRA based on your current and expected future tax rates.

### 3. **Tax Considerations**
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Invest in tax-efficient funds (e.g., index funds) that minimize capital gains taxes.
   - **401(k) and IRA Accounts:** Focus on growth investments, as taxes are deferred until withdrawal.

### 4. **Diversification**
   - Ensure your overall portfolio is well-diversified across asset classes like stocks, bonds, and real estate.

### 5. **Rebalance Regularly**
   - Periodically review and adjust allocations to maintain your desired risk level as market conditions change.

### 6. **Consider Your Retirement Timeline**
   - The closer you are to retirement, the more you might want to shift toward conservative investments to protect your capital.

### Example Distribution Strategy:
- **401(k):** 30%
- **IRA:** 30%
- **Taxable Account:** 40%

Make sure to tailor this general framework to fit your specific situation, and consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized advice.
