When deciding how to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider these strategies:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to maximize any employer match. This is essentially free money.

2. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: Contribute up to the maximum allowed. If you have a traditional 401(k), contributions reduce your taxable income.
   - **IRA**: If eligible, consider contributing to a Roth IRA for tax-free growth and tax-free withdrawals in retirement. If you have a traditional IRA, contributions may also be tax-deductible.

3. **Assess Tax Bracket**: Consider your current and projected future tax brackets:
   - If you expect to be in a lower tax bracket during retirement, a traditional 401(k) or IRA may be better for reducing your taxable income now.
   - If you expect to be in a higher tax bracket, a Roth account may be more advantageous.

4. **Taxable Accounts**: After maximizing retirement contributions, you can invest in taxable accounts. These are flexible and allow access to funds without penalties, but they’ll be subject to capital gains taxes on earnings.

5. **Diversify Investments**: Regardless of the account type, ensure your investments are diversified to manage risk.

6. **Rebalance Regularly**: Review your allocation periodically to reflect changes in your financial goals and market conditions.

7. **Seek Professional Advice**: If unsure, consider consulting a financial advisor who can help tailor a strategy based on your financial goals and situation.

This balanced approach helps you leverage the unique benefits of each account type while preparing for your future financial needs.
