To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

### 1. **Understand Account Types:**
   - **401(k):** Often has higher contribution limits and potential employer matching. Contributions are pre-tax, which can reduce taxable income now.
   - **IRA (Traditional or Roth):** Traditional IRAs allow for pre-tax contributions; Roth IRAs allow contributions with after-tax dollars and tax-free withdrawals in retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Useful for flexibility; money can be accessed anytime without penalties, but earnings are taxed annually.

### 2. **Prioritize Accounts:**
   - **Contribute Up to Employer Match in 401(k):** Maximize free money.
   - **Max Out IRAs:** Aim to contribute to your IRA, especially if you’re eligible for a Roth IRA due to its tax benefits.
   - **Additional 401(k) Contributions:** After maximizing IRAs, consider adding more to your 401(k).
   - **Taxable Accounts for Short-Term Goals:** If you have goals like buying a home or saving for a vacation, allocate funds to a taxable account.

### 3. **Consider Tax Implications:**
   - **Tax-Deferred Accounts (401(k), Traditional IRA):** Ideal for higher income now; you will pay taxes on withdrawals in retirement.
   - **Tax-Free Accounts (Roth IRA):** Good if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Understand capital gains taxes and use tax-loss harvesting strategies to minimize tax impact.

### 4. **Diversify Investments:**
   - Use tax-efficient investments (e.g., index funds, ETFs) in taxable accounts to minimize capital gains taxes.
   - Consider holding more tax-inefficient investments (like bonds) in tax-advantaged accounts.

### 5. **Review Regularly:**
   - Assess your goals, risk tolerance, and tax situation regularly, adjusting allocations as needed.

### 6. **Seek Professional Guidance:**
   - Consulting with a financial advisor can help tailor your strategy based on your individual circumstances.

By strategically distributing your investments across these accounts, you can optimize tax efficiency and ensure you’re aligned with your long-term financial goals.
