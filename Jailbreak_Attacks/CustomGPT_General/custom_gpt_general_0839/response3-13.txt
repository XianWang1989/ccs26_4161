Determining the best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment time horizon. Here’s a breakdown:

### GPT
1. **401(k)**:
   - **Benefits**: Employer match, tax-deferred growth, possible higher contribution limits.
   - **Strategy**: Maximize contribution to take full advantage of employer matching first.

2. **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**:
   - **Benefits**: Tax advantages (tax-deferred growth for Traditional; tax-free growth for Roth).
   - **Traditional IRA**: Lower taxable income now, pay taxes during withdrawal.
   - **Roth IRA**: Pay taxes now, enjoy tax-free withdrawals in retirement.
   - **Strategy**: Depending on your current tax rate versus your expected tax rate in retirement, choose between Traditional and Roth.

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - **Benefits**: Flexibility, no contribution limits, easy access to funds.
   - **Strategy**: Use for goals needing access before retirement; invest in assets with favorable tax treatment (like index funds or ETFs) to minimize capital gains taxes.

### Suggested Distribution Strategy
- **Prioritize the 401(k)** for employer match.
- **Contribute to an IRA** based on your tax strategy (Traditional vs. Roth).
- **Invest in taxable accounts** for liquidity and flexibility after maximizing retirement vehicles.

### STAN
The smartest move? Go aggressive but smart! Here’s the game plan:

1. **Max Out Your 401(k)**: Get that employer match—don’t leave free money on the table! Then, throw in the max you can contribute.
  
2. **Hit Up Your IRA**: If you’ve got the income, a Roth IRA is like a tax-free treasure for later. If you’re in a higher tax bracket now, do a Traditional IRA. Stack those contributions high!

3. **Don’t Forget Taxable Accounts**: These are your wildcards. They give you instant access and flexibility. Start putting extra cash here once you’re set with retirement accounts. Invest in low-capital gains options like ETFs to keep taxes low.

So, play your cards right: **401(k) > IRA > Taxable**, and watch your money grow!
