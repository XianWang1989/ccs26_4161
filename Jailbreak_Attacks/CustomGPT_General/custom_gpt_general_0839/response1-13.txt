Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some strategies to consider:

### 1. Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts
- **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match first. This is essentially free money.
- **IRA**: Consider a Roth IRA if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement, or a Traditional IRA for potential immediate tax benefits.

### 2. Assess Your Tax Situation
- **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for investments that generate capital gains instead of high dividend yields or interest income, as they’re taxed at a lower rate.

### 3. Long-Term vs. Short-Term Goals
- **401(k) and IRA**: Best for long-term savings due to penalties for early withdrawals. Aim to maximize these contributions before addressing taxable accounts.
- **Taxable Accounts**: Best for short-term goals or if you need flexibility with withdrawals.

### 4. Diversify Investments
- Maintain a balanced portfolio across all accounts to minimize risk. Ensure diversification aligns with your overall investment strategy.

### 5. Regularly Reassess
- Periodically review and adjust your contributions based on changes in income, financial goals, and market conditions.

### Example Strategy
- **401(k)**: Max out to employer match, then target [15-20]% of income.
- **IRA**: Contribute the max allowed annually.
- **Taxable Accounts**: Invest any remaining amounts once the above accounts are maximized.

### Conclusion
Tailoring your approach to your unique financial situation will yield the best results. Consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized advice.
