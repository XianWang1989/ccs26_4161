When it comes to distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, it's essential to consider a few key factors: taxes, growth potential, and your retirement timeline. Here's a general approach:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a 401(k) match, contribute enough to get the full match first. This is essentially free money and a great return on investment.

2. **Contribute to a Roth IRA**: If you're eligible, consider contributing to a Roth IRA after maximizing your 401(k) match. The tax-free growth and tax-free withdrawals in retirement make it a powerful retirement tool, especially if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement.

3. **Maximize 401(k) Contributions**: After funding your Roth IRA, go back to your 401(k) and contribute up to the maximum limit. This reduces your taxable income and allows for tax-deferred growth.

4. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**: Use taxable accounts for additional savings or investments once you hit the contribution limits for your tax-advantaged accounts. This offers more flexibility for withdrawals and investments without penalty.

5. **Asset Allocation**: Diversify your investments within each account. Consider your risk tolerance and time horizon, and allocate a mix of stocks, bonds, and other assets accordingly.

6. **Rebalance Periodically**: Regularly review and adjust your allocations based on performance and changing goals. This ensures you stay aligned with your long-term financial plan.

Always consult a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your specific financial situation and goals.
