Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment horizon. Here are some strategies to consider:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match**
   - Prioritize contributing enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match, as this is essentially free money.

### 2. **Consider Tax Implications**
   - **401(k):** Contributions are pre-tax, lowering your taxable income. Withdrawals are taxed as ordinary income.
   - **IRA:** Traditional IRAs provide tax-deferral, while Roth IRAs allow for tax-free withdrawals in retirement if certain conditions are met.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Investments in these accounts are subject to capital gains tax, so consider holding lower-turnover investments here to minimize tax liability.

### 3. **Asset Allocation**
   - Place more volatile investments (like stocks) in tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) and IRA) to shelter them from taxes.
   - Keep fixed-income investments (bonds) in taxable accounts to mitigate tax impact, as interest income is taxed at ordinary rates.

### 4. **Emergency Fund**
   - Maintain a cash reserve in your taxable accounts for immediate access in case of emergencies. 

### 5. **Long-Term Goals**
   - If saving for retirement is your primary goal, focus on maxing out your 401(k) and IRA contributions before directing funds to taxable accounts.

### 6. **Annual Contribution Limits**
   - Be aware of and adhere to the contribution limits for 401(k)s and IRAs to avoid penalties.

### 7. **Review Regularly**
   - Regularly assess your asset allocation and make adjustments based on performance, market conditions, and life changes.

Consulting with a financial advisor can provide personalized guidance tailored to your specific situation.
