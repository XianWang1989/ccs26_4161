Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some tips:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match if available. This is essentially free money.

2. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**: After securing the match, consider maxing out your IRA (Traditional or Roth, depending on your tax bracket) before contributing more to your 401(k) beyond the match.

3. **Account Types and Tax Implications**:
   - **401(k)**: Great for tax-deferred growth; consider your future tax bracket when withdrawing.
   - **IRA**: Offers tax advantages (deductions for Traditional, tax-free withdrawals for Roth).
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Use for shorter-term goals or if you’ve maxed out tax-advantaged accounts, but be mindful of capital gains taxes.

4. **Investment Strategy**: Keep in mind:
   - Place higher-risk investments in tax-advantaged accounts to benefit from tax-free growth.
   - Utilize taxable accounts for more conservative or liquid investments, as you'll have more flexibility with access and withdrawals.

5. **Diversification**: Ensure your overall investment portfolio remains diversified across all accounts based on your risk tolerance.

6. **Review Regularly**: Reassess your allocations annually or as your financial situation changes.

Consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your circumstances.
