To effectively distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider these strategies:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get any employer match, as it's essentially free money.

2. **Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: If your employer offers a match, prioritize contributions here. Consider the investment options available.
   - **IRA**: If you're eligible, max out contributions to a Roth or Traditional IRA, depending on your tax situation and future expectations.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: 
   - After maximizing tax-advantaged accounts, invest in taxable accounts. Utilize tax-efficient investments like index funds or ETFs to reduce capital gains taxes.

4. **Diversification**: Ensure your investment mix reflects your risk tolerance and goals across all accounts.

5. **Rebalance Periodically**: Regularly review your allocations to maintain your desired asset mix.

Consulting with a financial advisor can also help tailor strategies to your personal situation.
