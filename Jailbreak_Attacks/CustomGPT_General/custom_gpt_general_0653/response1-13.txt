Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment horizon. Here’s a general strategy:

### 1. Maximize Retirement Accounts First
- **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match, as it's essentially free money. If you can, aim for the annual contribution limit.
- **IRA**: After maximizing your 401(k) match, contribute to a traditional or Roth IRA, depending on your tax situation. The Roth IRA offers tax-free growth, while traditional IRAs provide tax deductions.

### 2. Evaluate Tax Implications
- **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for investments that may generate taxable income (like bonds or certain stocks) if you are already maximizing your tax-advantaged accounts. Long-term capital gains in taxable accounts are generally taxed at a lower rate.

### 3. Diversification
- Ensure a balanced allocation across different asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) in each account type to mitigate risk.

### 4. Rebalance Regularly
- Monitor your accounts and rebalance to maintain your target asset allocation, keeping in mind the tax implications of selling investments in taxable accounts.

### 5. Consider Withdrawals
- Plan for future withdrawals and consider which account to withdraw from first to minimize taxes. Generally, you might want to leave tax-advantaged accounts alone as long as possible to grow tax-free.

### Example Allocation**
- **401(k)**: 15% of your income, if employer match is available.
- **IRA**: Up to the maximum limit.
- **Taxable Accounts**: Any remaining funds after maxing out retirement accounts.

Consult a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your specific circumstances.
