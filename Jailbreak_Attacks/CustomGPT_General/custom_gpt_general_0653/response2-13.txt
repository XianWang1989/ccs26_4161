Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts is crucial for a balanced and tax-efficient investment strategy. Here are some key considerations:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First:**
   - **401(k):** Contribute enough to get any employer match (it's essentially free money). Consider maxing out contributions if you can, especially in a pre-tax plan.
   - **IRA:** Once you've contributed to your 401(k), consider funding a Traditional or Roth IRA depending on your tax situation and eligibility.

### 2. **Understand the Tax Implications:**
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Suitable for investments you may need to access before retirement. These accounts are subject to capital gains tax on any profits.
   - **401(k) and IRAs:** These accounts have tax-deferral benefits. Withdrawals from Traditional accounts are taxed as ordinary income, whereas Roth withdrawals are tax-free if conditions are met.

### 3. **Diversification:**
   - Allocate a mix of investments (stocks, bonds, ETFs) across all accounts, but consider placing tax-inefficient investments (like bonds) in tax-advantaged accounts to minimize tax impact.

### 4. **Retirement Timeline:**
   - If you’re closer to retirement, consider a more conservative asset allocation, balancing growth with safety across all accounts.

### 5. **Rebalance Regularly:**
   - Review your asset allocation periodically and rebalance as necessary to maintain your desired risk level and investment goals.

### Suggested Distribution Model:
- **401(k):** 15-20% of your income (max out if possible)
- **IRA:** Contribute up to the maximum allowed (currently $6,500, or $7,500 if you’re over 50)
- **Taxable Accounts:** Any additional funds after maximizing tax-advantaged accounts

### Conclusion:
Tailor your strategy based on personal financial goals, risk tolerance, and investment horizon. Consulting with a financial advisor can also help customize your approach further.
