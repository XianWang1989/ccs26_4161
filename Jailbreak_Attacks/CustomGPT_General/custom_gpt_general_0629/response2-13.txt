Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and time horizon. Here’s a general approach:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match (401(k))**
   - Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get any employer match. This is essentially free money!

### 2. **Consider Tax Advantages**
   - **401(k):** Contributions are often pre-tax, reducing taxable income. Good for higher earners.
   - **Traditional IRA:** Also offers tax deductions. Ideal for those who may be in a lower tax bracket in retirement.
   - **Roth IRA:** Contributions are made after-tax, allowing tax-free withdrawals in retirement. Good if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket later.

### 3. **Build an Emergency Fund**
   - Before heavily investing, ensure you have 3-6 months of expenses in a liquid savings account.

### 4. **Prioritize High-Interest Debt**
   - Pay down any high-interest debt before investing heavily.

### 5. **Diversify in Taxable Accounts**
   - Use taxable accounts for flexible investments, like stocks or mutual funds. Consider tax efficiency—invest in index funds or ETFs to minimize capital gains taxes.

### Example Distribution Strategy
1. **401(k):** Contribute enough to get the match.
2. **Roth IRA:** Max out if eligible (2023 limit: $6,500; $7,500 if 50+).
3. **Traditional IRA or Additional 401(k):** If still under contribution limits, consider maximizing these.
4. **Taxable Accounts:** Invest any surplus into diversified assets here.

### Final Thoughts
- Rebalance your portfolio regularly.
- Consider your age, risk tolerance, and investment horizon.
- Consult a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your specific situation.
