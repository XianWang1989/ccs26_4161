Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here’s a general guideline on how to approach this:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts:**
   - **401(k):** Contribute enough to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money. Aim to contribute the maximum allowed if your budget permits.
   - **IRA (Roth or Traditional):** After maxing out your 401(k) up to the match, consider funding a Roth or Traditional IRA based on your tax situation. Roth IRAs are beneficial if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement.

### 2. **Evaluate Your Tax Situation:**
   - **Pre-tax vs. After-tax:** Pre-tax contributions (401(k) and Traditional IRA) reduce your taxable income now but will be taxed in retirement. Roth contributions are made with after-tax dollars but grow tax-free.
   - **Diversification:** Having both pre-tax and post-tax accounts provides flexibility in managing taxes during retirement.

### 3. **Consider Investment Options:**
   - **401(k):** Often has limited investment options. Make sure to choose low-cost funds and diversify your portfolio.
   - **IRA:** Typically offers a broader range of investment choices, including stocks, bonds, and ETFs. Use this to further diversify.

### 4. **Manage Taxable Accounts Wisely:**
   - Fund these last, as they don’t offer the same tax advantages. Focus on investments that produce qualified dividends or long-term capital gains to minimize taxes.
   - Consider tax-loss harvesting to offset any gains.

### 5. **Regularly Reassess Your Strategy:**
   - As your income, goals, and the market change, revisit your distribution strategy at least annually or when major life events occur.

### Example Distribution:
- **401(k):** Max out up to the employer match (e.g., 6-10% of income).
- **IRA:** Contribute the max allowed ($6,500 for under 50; $7,500 for 50+ for 2023).
- **Taxable Accounts:** Invest whatever remains after maxing the above accounts.

### Conclusion:
Remember to consult with a financial advisor to tailor this strategy to your specific situation and goals.
