The best way to distribute your money amongst your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, current income, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here are some general strategies to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k):**  
   If your employer offers a matching contribution for your 401(k), aim to contribute enough to get the full match. This is essentially "free money" and can significantly boost your retirement savings.

2. **Contribute to IRA Accounts:**  
   Depending on your income level, consider contributing to a Traditional IRA or a Roth IRA. Traditional IRAs may provide a tax deduction now, while Roth IRAs allow tax-free withdrawals in retirement. If eligible, maximize contributions to these accounts.

3. **Consider Tax Efficiency:**  
   For your taxable accounts, focus on tax-efficiency. Invest in tax-efficient funds, such as index funds or ETFs, to minimize capital gains and avoid high-turnover investments that can trigger taxes.

4. **Debt Repayment:**  
   If you have high-interest debt, consider whether it's more beneficial to pay that down before maximizing retirement account contributions.

5. **Asset Allocation:**  
   Diversify your investments based on your risk tolerance and time horizon. Typically, your 401(k) and IRA hold more tax-advantaged investments, while your taxable accounts might benefit from more liquid or lower-growth investments.

6. **Rebalance Regularly:**  
   Regularly review and adjust your asset allocation based on market conditions and your changing financial situation to keep your investments aligned with your goals.

7. **Seek Professional Advice:**  
   Consider consulting a financial advisor who can provide personalized advice based on your specific circumstances and help you formulate a comprehensive investment strategy.

Ultimately, the right distribution strategy is unique to your financial situation and goals.
