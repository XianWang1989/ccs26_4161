To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Maximize Employer Match on 401(k):** Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to take full advantage of any employer matching contributions, as this is essentially free money.

2. **Contribute to IRA:** Depending on your income and eligibility, contribute to a Traditional or Roth IRA. A Roth IRA offers tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement, while a Traditional IRA may provide tax deductions upfront.

3. **Consider Tax Efficiency:** Allocate investments in a tax-efficient manner. For example, place lower-yield bonds or real estate investment trusts (REITs) in tax-advantaged accounts like IRAs and 401(k)s. Use taxable accounts for investments that benefit from long-term capital gains tax rates, such as stocks held for more than a year.

4. **Emergency Fund:** Ensure you have an adequate emergency fund outside these accounts, generally covering 3 to 6 months of living expenses.

5. **Diversify Investments:** Across all accounts, maintain a diversified portfolio based on your risk tolerance, time horizon, and retirement goals.

6. **Rebalance Periodically:** Regularly review and rebalance your portfolios to maintain your desired asset allocation, particularly as your investments grow or market conditions change.

7. **Take Advantage of Tax Loss Harvesting**: In taxable accounts, consider selling investments that have lost value to offset gains, reducing your overall tax liability.
