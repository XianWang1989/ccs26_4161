The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, risk tolerance, and investment timeline. Here are some guidelines to consider for each account type:

1. **401(k)**: Contribute enough to take advantage of any employer match, as this is essentially "free money." Traditional 401(k) contributions are made pre-tax, which lowers your taxable income. If you can, aim to max out your contributions (the limit is $22,500 in 2023 for those under 50, with a $7,500 catch-up if you're 50 or older).

2. **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**: Consider contributing to an IRA to diversify your tax exposure. Traditional IRAs may provide a tax deduction on contributions, while Roth IRAs allow tax-free withdrawals in retirement. The contribution limit for IRAs in 2023 is $6,500 (plus a $1,000 catch-up for those 50 and older). A Roth IRA is beneficial if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: Use taxable accounts for investments that you might need to access before retirement or for goals that are not suited for retirement accounts. Focus on tax-efficient investments, such as index funds or ETFs, to minimize capital gains taxes.

**General Strategy**:
- Prioritize contributions to your 401(k) plan to capture any employer match.
- If you're eligible, fund a Roth IRA for tax-free growth and withdrawals.
- Direct any additional funds to a taxable account, especially for shorter-term goals.
- Consider your tax bracket and future tax predictions when deciding where to allocate your money.

It's important to periodically review your asset allocation and adjust as needed based on changes in your financial situation or goals. Consulting with a financial advisor can also provide tailored advice.
