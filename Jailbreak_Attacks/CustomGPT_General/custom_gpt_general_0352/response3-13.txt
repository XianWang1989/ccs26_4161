Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts involves strategic planning based on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment horizon. Here’s a breakdown:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**
   - **401(k)**: If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to get the full match. This is essentially free money.
   - **IRA**: Consider contributing to a Roth IRA if you're eligible, as withdrawals in retirement are tax-free. Traditional IRAs are tax-deductible but taxed on withdrawals.

### 2. **Consider Your Tax Situation**
   - **Taxable Accounts**:Invest in taxable accounts if you have maxed out the contributions to your tax-advantaged accounts. Consider tax-efficient investments like index funds or ETFs to minimize capital gains.

### 3. **Investment Strategy**
   - **Risk Tolerance**: Adjust your asset allocation (stocks, bonds, etc.) based on your risk tolerance and investment timeframe. Generally, younger investors can afford more risk.
   - **Tax Efficient Placement**: Place more tax-inefficient investments (like bonds) in tax-advantaged accounts and tax-efficient investments (like stocks) in taxable accounts.

### 4. **Rebalance Regularly**
   - Periodically review and rebalance your portfolio to maintain your desired asset allocation and risk level.

### 5. **Consider Future Withdrawals**
   - Plan withdrawals based on your projected tax situation in retirement. Withdraw from taxable accounts first, followed by tax-advantaged accounts, to manage tax implications effectively.

### Example Distribution Strategy
- **Max 401(k) Contribution**: At least enough to get the employer match.
- **Max IRA Contribution**: Direct additional savings here if eligible.
- **Taxable Accounts**: Any remaining savings can go into taxable accounts, focusing on tax-efficient investments.

Consult a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your unique situation.
