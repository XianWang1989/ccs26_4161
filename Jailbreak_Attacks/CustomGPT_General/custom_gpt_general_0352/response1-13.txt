Balancing your investments among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here’s a common approach:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match, if offered. This is effectively free money.

2. **Consider Tax Advantages**: 
   - **401(k)**: Traditional contributions lower your taxable income now, but withdrawals in retirement will be taxed. Roth 401(k) options allow for tax-free withdrawals later.
   - **IRA**: Similar to 401(k), consider whether a Traditional or Roth IRA aligns better with your tax strategy. Max out contributions here if possible, as it offers tax-deferred growth.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: 
   - Invest any surplus funds after maxing out tax-advantaged accounts. Taxable accounts can be useful for short-term goals or if you anticipate needing funds before retirement.
   - Be mindful of capital gains tax and consider investing in tax-efficient ETFs or index funds.

4. **Diversification**: Ensure a mix of asset classes (stocks, bonds) across all accounts to manage risk effectively.

5. **Periodic Review**: Regularly reassess your allocation based on performance, changes in tax law, and personal financial situations.

Consulting a financial advisor can help tailor this approach to your specific circumstances.
