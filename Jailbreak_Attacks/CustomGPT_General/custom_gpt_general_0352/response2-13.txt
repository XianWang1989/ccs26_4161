Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts can effectively balance growth and tax efficiency. Here’s a general strategy:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**
   - **401(k):** Contribute up to the employer match, as this is essentially free money. Aim to max out contributions if possible, especially if your plan offers a variety of investment options.
   - **IRA/Roth IRA:** If you’re eligible, consider maxing out your IRA contributions next. A Roth IRA offers tax-free growth, making it ideal for those expecting to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement.

### 2. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**
   - After maximizing tax-advantaged accounts, invest in taxable accounts. These provide greater flexibility for withdrawals and investment choices. Use this account for investments you may want to access before retirement.

### 3. **Consider Your Investment Goals and Horizon**
   - Align your asset allocation with your goals and time horizon. Long-term investments (stocks) are typically held in tax-advantaged accounts to benefit from tax-deferral or tax-free growth.

### 4. **Diversification and Risk Tolerance**
   - Maintain a diversified portfolio considering your risk tolerance. Avoid overconcentration in any single account or investment type.

### 5. **Rebalancing**
   - Regularly review and rebalance your investments based on performance and changing goals.

### Conclusion
Consider consulting a financial advisor tailored to your situation for personalized guidance. They can help navigate specific considerations like tax implications and investment strategy.
