Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here’s a general approach to consider:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts:**
   - **401(k):** Contribute enough to get any employer match. This is essentially free money. Aim to maximize contributions, especially if you are in your peak earning years.
   - **IRA Contributions:** Consider a Traditional IRA for tax-deferred growth or a Roth IRA for tax-free withdrawals in retirement. Max out contributions if possible.

### 2. **Emergency Fund:**
   - Before maximizing retirement accounts, ensure you have an adequate emergency fund (3-6 months of living expenses) in a high-yield savings account or a liquid investment.

### 3. **Taxable Accounts:**
   - Use these for investments beyond tax-advantaged accounts. They offer flexibility for withdrawals but come with capital gains taxes. Consider holding assets that produce qualified dividends or long-term capital gains.

### 4. **Investment Allocation:**
   - **Risk Tolerance:** Assess your risk tolerance. More aggressive investments may be more appropriate in tax-deferred accounts, while more stable investments can be placed in taxable accounts to mitigate taxes.
   - **Asset Location:** Place investments that generate ordinary income (like bonds) in tax-advantaged accounts and those with favorable tax treatment (like stocks) in taxable accounts.

### 5. **Rebalance Regularly:**
   - Periodically reassess your allocations and rebalance to ensure you remain aligned with your investment goals and risk tolerance.

### 6. **Consider Future Tax Implications:**
   - Anticipate your tax bracket during retirement. A Roth IRA may be beneficial if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket later.

### 7. **Consult a Financial Advisor:**
   - For personalized guidance tailored to your unique circumstances, consider working with a financial advisor.

By following these strategies, you can create a balanced approach to distributing your investments in a way that maximizes growth while managing tax implications effectively.
