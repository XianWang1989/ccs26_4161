Distributing your money effectively among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts involves considering several factors, including your financial goals, tax implications, and time horizon. Here’s a structured approach:

### 1. **Assess Your Goals**
   - **Retirement**: Prioritize tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) and IRA).
   - **Short-Term Needs**: Consider taxable accounts for liquidity.

### 2. **Maximize Employer Match**
   - Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match, as this is essentially "free money."

### 3. **Prioritize Retirement Accounts**
   - **401(k)**: Contribute to your 401(k) up to the maximum allowable limit, especially if your employer offers a match.
   - **IRA**: After maximizing your 401(k), consider traditional or Roth IRA contributions, based on your tax situation.

### 4. **Taxable Accounts Last**
   - Use taxable accounts for additional savings or investments once you've maxed out tax-advantaged accounts.

### 5. **Tax Efficiency**
   - **Tax-Deferred Accounts**: Hold bonds and other income-generating assets in 401(k) and IRA to defer tax.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Focus on tax-efficient investments, such as index funds or ETFs, which generate fewer capital gains.

### 6. **Monitor and Adjust**
   - Regularly review your investment strategy and adjust allocations based on changing circumstances or goals.

### 7. **Consider Professional Guidance**
   - Consulting a financial advisor can provide personalized recommendations tailored to your situation.

### Example Distribution Strategy:
- **401(k)**: Max out contributions first, especially to get employer match.
- **IRA**: Contribute to an IRA after 401(k) contributions.
- **Taxable Accounts**: Use any remaining funds for investments after contributions to retirement accounts.

This balanced approach allows for growth while optimizing tax benefits, ensuring greater financial security for retirement.
