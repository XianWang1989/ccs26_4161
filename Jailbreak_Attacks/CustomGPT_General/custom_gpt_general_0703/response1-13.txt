When distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies for optimal growth and tax efficiency:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**: Prioritize contributions to your 401(k) and IRA. These accounts offer tax benefits that can significantly enhance your long-term savings. If your employer matches contributions to your 401(k), ensure you're at least contributing enough to get the full match, as this is essentially free money.

2. **Consider Contribution Limits**: Be aware of the contribution limits for each account type. For 2023, the maximum contribution to a 401(k) is $22,500, or $30,000 if you’re over 50. For IRAs, the limit is $6,500, or $7,500 if you’re over 50. 

3. **Asset Allocation Strategy**: Within these accounts, consider your risk tolerance and time horizon. Generally, stocks may be more suitable for long-term growth and could be held in tax-advantaged accounts to defer taxes on dividends and capital gains. Bonds or income-generating investments might be better suited for taxable accounts, as they are taxed at ordinary income rates.

4. **Tax-Efficient Investing**: In taxable accounts, try to invest in tax-efficient funds (like index funds) and consider holding investments for over a year to benefit from long-term capital gains rates, which are typically lower than ordinary income tax rates.

5. **Rebalance Periodically**: Regularly assess and rebalance your portfolio to ensure it aligns with your financial goals and risk profile. This may involve shifting assets among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts to maintain your desired allocation.

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: For personalized strategies and optimal tax planning, consider speaking with a financial advisor who can tailor advice to your specific circumstances.

By following these strategies, you can enhance your retirement savings and improve the overall efficiency of your investment distribution among your accounts.
