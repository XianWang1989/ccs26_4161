Determining the best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various factors such as your financial goals, risk tolerance, and tax situation. Here’s a general strategy to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**: If your employer offers a match for your 401(k), contribute enough to get the full match. This is essentially free money and should be prioritized.

2. **Contribute to an IRA**: If you’re eligible, contributing to an IRA (either traditional or Roth) can provide tax benefits. Depending on your income level, contributions to a Roth IRA can grow tax-free, which is advantageous for long-term growth.

3. **Continue Funding the 401(k)**: After maximizing the employer match, continue to contribute to your 401(k) to take advantage of tax-deferred growth. The contribution limit is typically higher than an IRA.

4. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**: Once you’ve maximized your retirement account contributions (401(k) and IRA), consider investing in taxable accounts. These provide more flexibility to access funds without penalties and can be beneficial for short-term goals.

5. **Consider Investment Strategy**: Within each type of account, consider your investment strategy based on the account's tax implications. For instance, place higher-growth investments in tax-advantaged accounts to minimize tax impact.

6. **Annual Review**: Regularly review your investment allocation to ensure it aligns with your goals and market conditions. Adjust contributions as needed based on changes in income and expenses.

Always consult with a financial advisor to tailor this strategy to your specific circumstances and to optimize your investment distribution.
