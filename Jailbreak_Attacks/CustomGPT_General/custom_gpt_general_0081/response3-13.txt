Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here are some guidelines:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**
   - If your employer offers a match, prioritize contributions to your 401(k) up to that match limit.

### 2. **Contribute to an IRA**
   - Depending on your income, consider putting money into a Traditional or Roth IRA.
   - Traditional IRAs offer potential tax deductions, while Roth IRAs allow for tax-free withdrawals in retirement.

### 3. **Assess Tax Implications**
   - Taxable accounts are more flexible but have different tax treatment compared to retirement accounts. Consider your expected tax rate now vs. in retirement.

### 4. **Focus on Asset Allocation**
   - Your investment strategy should align with your risk tolerance and time horizon. Stocks may be more suitable for long-term growth, while bonds can provide stability.

### 5. **Review Regularly**
   - Reassess your allocation regularly to stay on track with your financial goals.
