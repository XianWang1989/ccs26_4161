Distributing your money across a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here’s a general strategy to consider:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match (401(k))**
   - Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match, as this is essentially free money.

### 2. **Focus on Tax-Advantaged Accounts**
   - **IRA Contributions:** If you qualify, consider maxing out contributions to a traditional or Roth IRA. Roth IRAs are particularly beneficial if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement.
   - **401(k) Contributions:** After maximizing the employer match, increase contributions to your 401(k) up to the annual limit.

### 3. **Taxable Accounts**
   - Invest surplus funds in taxable accounts after maximizing contributions to your 401(k) and IRA. This is important for flexibility and liquidity.

### 4. **Asset Allocation**
   - **Tax Efficiency:** Hold tax-efficient investments (like index funds) in taxable accounts. Save higher-tax investments (like bonds or REITs) for tax-advantaged accounts.
   - **Diversification:** Ensure a balanced portfolio across all accounts, considering your risk tolerance and investment horizon.

### 5. **Review and Adjust**
   - Regularly review your investments and distribution strategy, adjusting based on changes in income, tax laws, and personal financial goals.

### Conclusion
Tailor this framework to your specific situation, potentially consulting with a financial advisor for a personalized strategy.
