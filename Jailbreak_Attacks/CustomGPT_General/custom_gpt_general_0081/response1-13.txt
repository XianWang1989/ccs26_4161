Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here’s a general approach:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match first. This is essentially free money.
   - **IRA**: Consider maximizing contributions, especially if it’s a Roth IRA, which allows for tax-free withdrawals in retirement.

### 2. **Consider Tax Implications**:
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Best for short-term goals or if you expect to need access to funds before retirement. Interest and dividends may be taxed annually.

### 3. **Investment Strategy**:
   - **401(k) & IRA**: Focus on long-term growth investments (stocks, index funds) to take advantage of compounding.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Consider tax-efficient investments (like ETFs or tax-managed funds) to minimize capital gains taxes.

### 4. **Savings Goals**:
   - Prioritize retirement savings in tax-deferred accounts, but also ensure liquidity for emergencies or short-term needs through your taxable account.

### 5. **Regularly Review and Adjust**:
   - Periodically review asset allocation based on your risk tolerance and adjust contributions as needed.

### Example Distribution:
- **401(k)**: 15% of income to max out employer match.
- **IRA**: Full annual contribution limit (if eligible).
- **Taxable Accounts**: Any remaining savings or investment funds.

Always consider consulting with a financial advisor to tailor this strategy to your specific needs and goals.
