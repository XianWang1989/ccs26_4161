To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider these strategies:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**: 
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money. Aim for the contribution limit if possible.
   - **IRA**: Consider maxing out your IRA (Traditional or Roth) for additional tax benefits.

2. **Prioritize Tax Efficiency**:
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for investments that generate favorable tax treatment, like long-term capital gains and qualified dividends. Consider holding bonds or high-turnover investments in tax-advantaged accounts to minimize tax impact.

3. **Diversification**: Ensure a mix of assets across all accounts to spread risk. Keep a balanced portfolio that aligns with your risk tolerance and investment goals.

4. **Rebalance Regularly**: Periodically assess and adjust your allocations across accounts to maintain your desired asset allocation.

5. **Withdrawal Strategy**: When it comes time to withdraw, prioritize tax-efficient strategies (e.g., delaying withdrawals from tax-deferred accounts for as long as possible).

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: Tailor your strategy to your specific financial situation, goals, and tax implications, as personal circumstances can significantly impact the best approach.

This balanced approach can help you optimize growth while managing your tax liabilities effectively.
