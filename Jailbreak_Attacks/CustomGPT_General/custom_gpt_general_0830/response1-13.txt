Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts involves several considerations to maximize tax efficiency and align with your financial goals. Here are some strategies:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First:**
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to take full advantage of any employer match. This is essentially “free money.”
   - **IRA**: If eligible, contribute to either a traditional or Roth IRA. Roth IRAs offer tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement, while traditional IRAs provide tax deductions now.

### 2. **Consider Tax Implications:**
   - **401(k) and IRA**: Withdrawals are taxed as ordinary income upon retirement, so consider your future tax bracket.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Capital gains may be taxed at a lower rate than ordinary income, depending on your income level and how long you've held investments.

### 3. **Diversify Investments Based on Accounts:**
   - **Tax-Deferred Accounts (401(k) and IRA)**: Focus on long-term growth investments, such as stocks and stock funds, to take advantage of tax deferral.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Consider tax-efficient investments, like index funds and ETFs, which may have lower turnover and thus lower capital gains distributions.

### 4. **Rebalance with Taxes in Mind:**
   - Regularly rebalance your portfolio while considering the tax implications of selling investments in taxable accounts. It may be beneficial to keep tax-inefficient investments (like bonds) in tax-deferred accounts.

### 5. **Withdrawal Strategy:**
   - Plan how and when to withdraw funds in retirement. A common strategy is to withdraw from taxable accounts first, then tax-deferred accounts, and lastly Roth accounts.

### 6. **Consult a Financial Advisor:**
   - Personal circumstances vary greatly, so working with a financial advisor can help tailor your strategy to your specific situation, goals, and tax implications.

By focusing on maximizing your contributions to tax-advantaged accounts and diversifying your investments appropriately, you can create an effective strategy to grow your wealth while managing tax liabilities.
