To effectively distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

### 1. **401(k) Contributions**
   - **Employer Match**: If your employer matches contributions, prioritize contributing enough to get the full match; it's essentially free money.
   - **Higher Growth Potential**: Since contributions are pre-tax, consider maxing out contributions (up to $22,500 for 2023, or $30,000 if over 50).

### 2. **IRA Contributions**
   - **Tax Advantages**: Depending on your income, consider a Traditional IRA (tax-deductible) or a Roth IRA (tax-free withdrawals in retirement). Roth IRAs benefit from tax-free growth; prioritize if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket later.
   - **Max Contribution**: Aim to contribute the max ($6,500 or $7,500 if over 50 in 2023).

### 3. **Taxable Accounts**
   - **Flexibility**: This account offers fewer tax advantages but provides liquidity and flexibility. Use for emergency savings or after maxing out 401(k) and IRA contributions.
   - **Tax Efficiency**: Focus on tax-efficient investments (e.g., index funds) to minimize capital gains taxes.

### General Guidelines:
- **Emergency Fund**: Ensure you have 3-6 months of expenses in cash or a high-yield savings account.
- **Asset Allocation**: Maintain a balanced asset allocation across all accounts based on your risk tolerance and investment timeline.
- **Diversification**: Avoid overlapping investments across accounts to maintain diversification.

### Example Distribution Strategy:
1. Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match.
2. Max out your IRA (starting with the Roth if eligible).
3. After that, increase contributions to your 401(k) until you reach the limit.
4. Finally, utilize any remaining funds in a taxable account.

Consult a financial advisor for personalized guidance based on your specific situation and goals.
