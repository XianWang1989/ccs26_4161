The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your age, retirement goals, tax situation, and investment strategy. Here are some general guidelines to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**: If your employer offers a matching contribution, contribute enough to get the full match. This is essentially free money and a great way to boost your retirement savings.

2. **Consider Tax Advantages**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are often pre-tax, reducing your taxable income. However, distributions in retirement are taxed as ordinary income.
   - **IRA (Traditional)**: Similar tax benefits as a 401(k). Contributions may be tax-deductible, and taxes are deferred until withdrawal.
   - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, but qualified withdrawals are tax-free. This can be beneficial if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket during retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Investments are taxed yearly on dividends, interest, and capital gains. This can be a flexible option for non-retirement goals.

3. **Investment Strategy**:
   - **Long-Term Growth**: Focus on your retirement accounts for long-term investments, choosing a diversified mix of stocks and bonds based on your risk tolerance.
   - **Short-Term Needs**: Use taxable accounts for funds you may need sooner, maintaining a more conservative investment strategy.

4. **Diversification**: Ensure your investments across all accounts are well-diversified to spread risk.

5. **Periodic Rebalancing**: Review and adjust your portfolio periodically to maintain your target asset allocation.

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: A financial professional can provide personalized advice based on your specific financial situation and goals.

Ultimately, the ideal distribution will vary for each individual, so it's important to assess your personal financial picture and goals.
