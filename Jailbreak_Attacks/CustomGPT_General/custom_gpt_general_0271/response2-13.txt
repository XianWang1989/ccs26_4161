Distributing money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here are some general guidelines:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a matching contribution on your 401(k), contribute enough to get the full match. This is essentially free money.

2. **Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**: Prioritize contributions to tax-advantaged accounts like your 401(k) and IRA because they offer tax benefits. For a traditional 401(k) and IRA, contributions may be tax-deductible, and your investments grow tax-deferred. For a Roth IRA, contributions are made with after-tax dollars, but withdrawals in retirement are tax-free.

3. **Contribution Limits**: Be aware of the contribution limits for each account type. For 2023, the 401(k) contribution limit is $22,500 (or $30,000 if you're age 50 or older), and the IRA limit is $6,500 (or $7,500 for those 50 and older). Ensure you're not exceeding these limits.

4. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**: After maxing out your tax-advantaged accounts, consider putting any additional savings into a taxable account. This offers more flexibility in terms of withdrawals, but remember that capital gains and dividends may be subject to taxes.

5. **Asset Allocation**: When investing in these accounts, consider separating your investments based on tax efficiency. For example, tax-efficient investments (like index funds) may be better suited for taxable accounts, while higher-growth, tax-inefficient investments (like bonds) may work well in tax-deferred accounts.

6. **Consider Your Tax Bracket**: If you're currently in a lower tax bracket, it might make sense to contribute to a Roth IRA for tax-free growth. Conversely, if you're in a high tax bracket, a traditional 401(k) or IRA might be preferable to lower your taxable income.

7. **Review and Adjust**: Regularly review your investment strategy as your financial situation or tax laws change. Adjust your contributions and asset allocations as necessary.

Always consider consulting with a financial advisor to create a personalized strategy that suits your specific circumstances.
