The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment strategy. Here’s a general strategy you might consider:

1. **Maximize 401(k) Contributions**: If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full match. This is essentially "free money." Try to max out your contributions if possible, as 401(k) contributions reduce your taxable income.

2. **Contribute to an IRA**: After maximizing your 401(k) match, consider contributing to a Traditional or Roth IRA. A Traditional IRA may give you tax-deductible contributions, reducing your taxable income, while a Roth IRA offers tax-free withdrawals in retirement, making it beneficial if you expect your tax rate to be higher in the future.

3. **Consider Taxable Accounts**: After you've maximized contributions to your retirement accounts, consider investing in a taxable brokerage account. These accounts offer more flexibility in terms of access to your money, especially if you plan to need funds before reaching retirement age. 

4. **Tax Efficiency**: Be mindful of the types of investments you hold in each account. Typically, higher-growth investments (like stocks) should go into tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) or IRA) to benefit from tax-free or tax-deferred growth. In taxable accounts, consider holding bonds or dividend-paying stocks, as their tax treatment can be more favorable.

5. **Revisit Your Allocation Regularly**: Periodically review your asset allocation based on changing financial needs, investment performance, and life circumstances.

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: For personalized advice, consider working with a financial advisor who can help you align your investment strategy with your overall financial goals.

By following these steps, you can effectively distribute your money to optimize your retirement savings and minimize your tax burden.
