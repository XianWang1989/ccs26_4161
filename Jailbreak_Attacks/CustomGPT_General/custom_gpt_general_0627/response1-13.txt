To effectively distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to receive the full employer match, as this is essentially free money.

2. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)** and **IRA**: These accounts provide tax benefits. Contribute to these first, aiming to max out your IRA if eligible (e.g., $6,500/year for 2023).
   - Consider a Roth IRA if you qualify; it offers tax-free withdrawals in retirement.

3. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**: After maxing out your tax-advantaged accounts, invest in taxable accounts for more flexible access to funds without penalties.

4. **Diversification**: Ensure a diversified portfolio across all accounts, balancing between stocks, bonds, and other investments according to your risk tolerance.

5. **Consideration of Tax Implications**: 
   - Place high-growth investments in tax-advantaged accounts to minimize tax exposure.
   - Use taxable accounts for investments with lower turnover to reduce capital gains taxes.

6. **Regular Reassessment**: Periodically review and adjust your allocations based on life changes or market conditions.

Consulting a financial advisor can help tailor a strategy to your specific financial goals and situation.
