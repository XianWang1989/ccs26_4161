Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here’s a general approach:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match. This is free money and a great first step.

2. **Consider Tax Advantages**:
   - **401(k)**: Pre-tax contributions can lower your taxable income. If you anticipate being in a lower tax bracket during retirement, this is advantageous.
   - **Traditional IRA**: Also offers tax deductions, but eligibility can depend on your income.
   - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are made with after-tax income, but withdrawals are tax-free in retirement. Consider this if you expect higher income in retirement.
   - **Taxable accounts**: Best for short-term goals or if you’ve maxed out other accounts. Investments here are subject to capital gains taxes.

3. **Diversification Across Accounts**: Aim for a balance across all accounts to mitigate risk and optimize tax efficiency. For example, consider holding more tax-efficient investments (like ETFs) in taxable accounts and higher-growth, tax-inefficient investments (like bond funds) in tax-advantaged accounts.

4. **Regular Rebalancing**: Reassess your distribution periodically to ensure you align with your risk tolerance and goals as markets fluctuate.

5. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: For personalized planning, consider speaking to an advisor who can tailor strategies to your specific situation.

### Sample Distribution Strategy:
- Max out 401(k) up to the match.
- Contribute to a Roth IRA if eligible.
- If there's remaining savings, consider additional contributions to your 401(k) or taxable accounts based on liquidity needs and tax impact.

By focusing on tax advantages and maximizing benefits from each account type, you can build a more efficient investment strategy.
