The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here’s a general approach:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**: If your employer offers a matching contribution, aim to contribute enough to get the full match. This is essentially free money.

2. **Contribute to IRA**: After maximizing your 401(k) match, consider contributing to a Roth IRA or a Traditional IRA. A Roth IRA offers tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement, while a Traditional IRA may provide tax deductions now.

3. **Continue with 401(k)**: If you can, continue to contribute to your 401(k) beyond the match, especially if you have a high income and want to reduce your taxable income. 

4. **Taxable Accounts**: Once you've maximized contributions to your tax-advantaged accounts, consider investing in taxable accounts for flexibility and access to funds before retirement.

5. **Consider Tax Implications**: Be mindful of the tax implications associated with each type of account. For instance, investments with high capital gains might be better suited for tax-advantaged accounts.

6. **Diversification**: Ensure your investments are diversified across various asset classes, regardless of the account type.

7. **Review Regularly**: Your financial situation may change, so review and adjust your allocations regularly.

It's often a good idea to consult with a financial advisor to tailor a strategy to your specific needs!
