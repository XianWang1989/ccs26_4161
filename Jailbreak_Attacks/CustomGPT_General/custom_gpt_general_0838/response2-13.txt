Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various factors including your financial goals, risk tolerance, and tax situation. Here are some general strategies to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a 401(k) match, contribute enough to take full advantage of this benefit before allocating funds elsewhere. It's essentially free money.

2. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**: After maximizing any employer match, consider maxing out your IRA contributions (if eligible). IRAs can provide tax advantages that are beneficial in the long term.

3. **Contribute to Your 401(k)**: Next, consider contributing to your 401(k) up to the annual limit if you haven't already. This helps reduce your taxable income and grow your retirement savings.

4. **Taxable Accounts**: After maxing out your tax-advantaged accounts, you can allocate additional funds to taxable accounts. These provide flexibility for withdrawals but are subject to different tax implications.

5. **Consider Your Tax Bracket**: If you anticipate being in a higher tax bracket in retirement, it may make sense to focus on tax-deferred accounts (like a 401(k) and traditional IRA). Conversely, if you expect to be in a lower tax bracket, a Roth IRA might be favorable since withdrawals are tax-free.

6. **Asset Allocation**: Be mindful of how you allocate assets across these accounts. For example, you might want to hold bonds in tax-advantaged accounts (where interest income is taxed at ordinary income rates) and stocks in taxable accounts (which enjoy lower long-term capital gains rates).

7. **Review Regularly**: Regularly review your asset distribution and contribution strategies as your financial situation and tax laws change.

8. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: For personalized advice based on your specific situation, consider consulting with a financial advisor.

Ultimately, the key is to create a balanced strategy that aligns with your long-term financial goals while managing risk and tax implications.
