Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment horizon. Here's a general approach:

1. **Maximize Employer Matches**: If your employer offers a match on your 401(k), contribute enough to get the full match first. This is essentially "free money."

2. **Contribute to an IRA**: If you're eligible, contribute to a Roth or traditional IRA. A Roth IRA allows for tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement, while a traditional IRA offers tax-deferred growth.

3. **Finish 401(k) Contributions**: After securing your IRA, continue contributing to your 401(k) until you reach the annual limit. The potential for tax-deferred growth in a 401(k) can be advantageous.

4. **Fund Taxable Accounts**: Once you've maximized contributions to your retirement accounts, consider taxable accounts for additional savings or investments. This is especially useful if you plan to access the money before retirement.

5. **Consider Tax Liabilities**: Invest in tax-efficient funds (like index funds) in taxable accounts to minimize capital gains taxes and consider strategies like tax-loss harvesting.

6. **Reevaluate Regularly**: Periodically review your distribution based on changes in income, expenses, and financial goals.

Consulting with a financial advisor can also help tailor a strategy based on your specific circumstances.
