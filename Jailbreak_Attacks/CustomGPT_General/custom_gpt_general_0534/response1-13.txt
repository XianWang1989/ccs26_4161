Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment horizon. Here are some general guidelines to consider:

### 1. **401(k) Accounts:**
   - **Contribute Enough to Get Employer Match:** If your employer offers a match, contribute at least enough to qualify for the full match. This is essentially free money.
   - **Tax Advantages:** Contributions are often made pre-tax, reducing your taxable income for the year. Taxes are paid upon withdrawal.

### 2. **IRA Accounts:**
   - **Consider a Roth IRA:** If you're eligible, a Roth IRA allows for tax-free withdrawals in retirement, which can be beneficial if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket later.
   - **Traditional IRA Benefits:** Contributions may be tax-deductible, particularly if you don't have access to a 401(k) or if you fall below the income limits.

### 3. **Taxable Accounts:**
   - **Flexibility:** Taxable accounts don’t have contribution limits and offer more flexibility for withdrawals, but you’ll owe taxes on dividends and capital gains.
   - **Consider Tax-Efficient Investments:** Use index funds or tax-managed funds to minimize capital gains distributions and invest in tax-efficient strategies.

### **General Strategy:**
1. **Max Out 401(k) Contributions:** Aim to contribute up to the maximum allowed, especially if you have a good plan with low fees.
2. **Contribute to IRA:** If you have additional funds available, contribute to an IRA (preferably Roth if you qualify) after maximizing your 401(k).
3. **Use Taxable Accounts Wisely:** After reaching your contributions limits in the 401(k) and IRA, consider investing in taxable accounts. Focus on investments that offer favorable tax treatment, like long-term capital gains.

### **Consider Your Goals:**
- **Retirement Goal:** Prioritize tax-advantaged accounts for long-term growth with potential tax benefits.
- **Shorter-Term Goals:** For goals within the next few years, consider holding more in taxable accounts for easier access.

### **Consult a Financial Advisor:**
A financial advisor can offer personalized strategies based on your overall financial situation, risk tolerance, and retirement plans.
