To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider these strategies:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money. Aim to maximize your contribution limit if possible.
   - **IRA**: Consider funding a Roth or traditional IRA based on your tax situation. Roths are beneficial for tax-free growth if you anticipate being in a higher tax bracket in retirement.

2. **Consider Investment Goals**:
   - Use tax-advantaged accounts for long-term growth investments (stocks, ETFs) since they benefit from tax-free growth or tax deferral.
   - In taxable accounts, hold investments that generate less taxable income, like index funds.

3. **Diversify Investments**:
   - Allocate across asset classes (stocks, bonds, cash) based on your risk tolerance and time horizon.

4. **Tax Considerations**:
   - Be mindful of capital gains taxes in taxable accounts. Sell investments with losses to offset gains.
   - Evaluate the tax impact of withdrawals from tax-advantaged accounts in retirement.

5. **Review and Rebalance**:
   - Regularly assess your allocations based on market changes and personal financial goals.

Consulting a financial advisor for tailored advice is also recommended for a comprehensive plan.
