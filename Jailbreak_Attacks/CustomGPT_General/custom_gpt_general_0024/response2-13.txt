Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some general strategies:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**:
   - Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match, as this is essentially free money.

2. **Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: Focus on high contributions here, especially if you have a good match. Consider traditional 401(k) for pre-tax benefits or a Roth 401(k) for tax-free withdrawals in retirement.
   - **IRA**: Contribute to either a Traditional or Roth IRA, depending on your income and tax situation. Roth IRAs offer tax-free growth and withdrawals.

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - Use these for additional savings and investments after maximizing contributions to tax-advantaged accounts. This can provide flexibility for short-to-medium-term goals.

4. **Asset Allocation**:
   - Adjust your investment strategy based on account type. Consider holding more tax-efficient investments (like index funds or ETFs) in taxable accounts, while using tax-inefficient ones (like bonds) in tax-advantaged accounts.

5. **Regular Rebalancing**:
   - Periodically review and adjust allocations based on performance, taxes, and changing financial goals.

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**:
   - For personalized advice tailored to your specific financial situation, consider consulting a financial advisor.

By following these strategies, you can optimize your money distribution for growth and tax efficiency.
