The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your individual financial situation, goals, and tax implications. Here are some general strategies to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k):** If your employer offers a matching contribution to your 401(k), start by contributing enough to take full advantage of that match. It’s essentially free money.

2. **Contribute to an IRA:** After maximizing your employer match, consider contributing to a Traditional IRA or Roth IRA. The choice between the two depends on your current tax situation and whether you prefer tax deductions now (Traditional) or tax-free growth (Roth).

3. **Utilize Taxable Accounts for Flexibility:** If you have additional funds, consider investing in taxable accounts for more flexibility. This is especially useful for short-term goals or if you want access to your funds without penalties.

4. **Balance Asset Allocation:** Diversifying your investments across these accounts can help manage risk. Ensure you have a balanced approach to stocks, bonds, and other asset classes based on your risk tolerance and time horizon.

5. **Tax Considerations:** Be strategic about where you hold different types of investments. For example, consider placing bonds (which generate taxable income) in tax-advantaged accounts, while holding stocks (which may benefit from lower capital gains tax rates) in taxable accounts.

6. **Regular Review and Rebalance:** Periodically review your allocations and rebalance as necessary to ensure they align with your financial goals.

7. **Consult a Financial Advisor:** A professional can provide personalized guidance tailored to your unique situation and help navigate tax implications more effectively.

By using this multi-account strategy, you can optimize your contributions while balancing growth potential and tax efficiency.
