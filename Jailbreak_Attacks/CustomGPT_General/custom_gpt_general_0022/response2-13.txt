When determining the best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA (Individual Retirement Account), and taxable accounts, consider these factors:

1. **Tax Implications**: 
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are typically pre-tax, which lowers your taxable income for the year. Taxes are paid upon withdrawal during retirement.
   - **IRA**: Traditional IRAs also offer tax-deductible contributions, while Roth IRAs provide tax-free withdrawals in retirement (if certain conditions are met).
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Any capital gains, dividends, or interest income from these accounts are subject to taxes in the year they are realized.

2. **Employer Match**: If your employer offers a match on your 401(k), prioritize contributing enough to take full advantage of it, as this is essentially free money.

3. **Retirement Goals**: Determine your retirement age and lifestyle goals. For long-term growth, prioritize accounts with tax advantages first, like your 401(k) and IRAs.

4. **Contribution Limits**: Be aware of the contribution limits for each account type, as this will influence how much you can invest annually:
   - **401(k)**: For 2023, the limit is $22,500 (or $30,000 if age 50 or older).
   - **IRA**: The limit is $6,500 (or $7,500 if age 50 or older).

5. **Investment Options**: 401(k)s can have limited investment options; IRAs often offer a broader range of investment vehicles. Consider diversifying your investments among these accounts to balance growth and risk.

6. **Time Horizon**: If you're younger and have a longer time horizon until retirement, you might be more aggressively investing in stocks in your tax-advantaged accounts. As you age, consider moving towards more conservative investments in preparation for retirement.

A common strategy is to maximize your 401(k) contributions to the employer match, then consider funding a Roth or Traditional IRA, and finally allocate any additional funds to a taxable account. It may also be beneficial to consult with a financial advisor to tailor your strategy to your specific financial situation and goals.
