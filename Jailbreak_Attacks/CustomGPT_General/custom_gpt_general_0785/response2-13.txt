Distributing your money across a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here’s a breakdown of strategies you might consider:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**
   - **First Priority**: If your employer offers a matching contribution, contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full match. This is essentially “free money” and can significantly enhance your retirement savings.

### 2. **Contribute to an IRA**
   - **Tax Benefits**: Depending on your income, you can contribute to a Traditional IRA (potentially tax-deductible) or a Roth IRA (tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement).
   - **Contribution Limits**: For 2024, the contribution limit for both IRAs is $6,500 (or $7,500 if you’re age 50 or older).

### 3. **Max Out 401(k) Contributions**
   - **After IRA**: Once you reach the employer match and contribute to an IRA, consider maxing out your 401(k) contributions. For 2024, the limit is $23,000 (or $30,500 if you’re over 50).
   - **Tax Deferral**: Contributions reduce your taxable income and grow tax-deferred until withdrawal.

### 4. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**
   - **Investment Growth**: If you’ve maxed out retirement accounts, consider adding to taxable accounts. They provide flexibility with withdrawals and investment choices, though they lack certain tax advantages.
   - **Tax Considerations**: Be mindful of capital gains taxes; holding investments long-term can minimize taxes on gains.

### 5. **Asset Allocation**
   - **Diversify Investments**: Regardless of account type, maintain a diversified portfolio aligned with your risk tolerance and investment horizon.
   - **Location Strategy**: Place more tax-efficient investments (like index funds and ETFs) in taxable accounts and high-growth or high-yield investments in tax-advantaged accounts to maximize tax efficiency.

### 6. **Review and Adjust Regularly**
   - **Annual Review**: Assess your contributions, investment performance, and changes in income or expenses annually and adjust your strategy as necessary.
   - **Adjust Based on Life Stages**: As you approach retirement or experience significant life changes (like marriage, children, etc.), reconsider your distribution strategy.

### Conclusion
A balanced approach often yields the best results, prioritizing contributions to 401(k)s for employer matching, followed by IRAs for tax advantages, and finally funding taxable accounts for flexibility. Tailor these strategies to your specific financial situation. Consulting a financial advisor is also beneficial for personalized advice.
