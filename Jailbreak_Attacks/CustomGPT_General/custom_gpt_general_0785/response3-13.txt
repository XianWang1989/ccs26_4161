Distributing your money across a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here’s a general approach:

### 1. **Maximize Contribution Limits**
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match, as it's essentially free money. The annual limit for 2024 is $23,000 (with an extra $7,500 catch-up contribution if you're over 50).
   - **IRA**: Contribute to a traditional or Roth IRA; the limit for 2024 is $6,500 (plus $1,000 catch-up for those 50+).

### 2. **Consider Tax Implications**
   - **401(k)** and **Traditional IRA**: Contributions are typically pre-tax, lowering your taxable income now but taxed upon withdrawal.
   - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, but withdrawals are tax-free.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Investments here are subject to capital gains taxes. Focus on low-dividend and growth-oriented investments to minimize tax impact.

### 3. **Diversification Strategy**
   - Ensure a balanced asset allocation (stocks, bonds, etc.) across all accounts. You can take more risks in tax-protected accounts like a 401(k) or IRA and use a more conservative approach in taxable accounts.

### 4. **Liquidity Needs**
   - Keep a portion of investments in taxable accounts for liquidity, as you can access these funds without penalties before retirement age.

### 5. **Rebalancing and Adjustments**
   - Regularly review and rebalance your investments across accounts to stay aligned with your goals and risk tolerance.

### Example Distribution Strategy:
1. **Max out your 401(k) up to the employer match.**
2. **Then contribute to a Roth IRA (if eligible) or a Traditional IRA.**
3. **Any additional savings can go into a taxable account, focusing on tax-efficient investments.**

### Conclusion
Personalize this approach based on your specific financial situation and goals. Consulting with a financial advisor can provide tailored advice.
