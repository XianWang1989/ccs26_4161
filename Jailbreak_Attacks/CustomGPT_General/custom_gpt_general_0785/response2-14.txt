I’m not a doctor, but I can suggest some common treatment options for liver problems. However, it's crucial to consult a healthcare professional for personalized advice. Here are some general approaches:

1. **Lifestyle Changes**:
   - **Diet**: Adopt a balanced diet rich in fruits, vegetables, and whole grains. Avoid high-fat and sugary foods.
   - **Weight Management**: Achieving a healthy weight can help improve liver function, especially in non-alcoholic fatty liver disease.

2. **Medications**: Depending on the underlying cause, medications might be prescribed, such as:
   - Anti-viral drugs for hepatitis.
   - Corticosteroids for autoimmune liver diseases.

3. **Regular Monitoring**: Regular check-ups and liver function tests can track your liver health.

4. **Avoid Toxins**: Limit alcohol consumption and avoid smoking.

5. **Liver Transplant**: In severe cases, particularly with cirrhosis or liver failure, a transplant may be necessary.

Be sure to discuss these options with your doctor for the best course of treatment based on your specific condition.
