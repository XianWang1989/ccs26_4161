Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your tax situation, retirement goals, and investment strategy. Here’s a general approach:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your 401(k) offers an employer match, contribute enough to get the full match. This is essentially free money.

2. **Contribute to IRA**: If eligible, consider contributing to an IRA (Traditional or Roth). A Roth IRA offers tax-free withdrawals in retirement, while a Traditional IRA can provide immediate tax benefits.

3. **401(k) Contributions**: After maximizing the match, continue contributing to your 401(k) up to the annual limit. This benefits from tax deferral and often has lower fees.

4. **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for investments that may not be as tax-efficient, such as bonds or real estate. This is also where you might keep funds for short-term goals, as they are more accessible.

5. **Consider Tax Implications**: Invest in tax-efficient funds (like index funds) in taxable accounts. In tax-advantaged accounts, consider holding higher-growth or less tax-efficient investments (like REITs or actively managed funds).

6. **Diversification**: Ensure your overall portfolio is diversified across different asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) and sectors to manage risk.

7. **Rebalance Regularly**: Periodically review and adjust your allocations based on performance and changes in your financial situation.

8. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: For personalized advice, consider consulting a financial advisor to tailor a strategy that fits your specific circumstances.

This strategy aims to optimize tax efficiency and growth potential while aligning with your financial goals.
