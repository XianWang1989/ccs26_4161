Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts effectively involves considering your financial goals, tax implications, and time horizon. Here’s a general strategy:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**:
   - **401(k)**: If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to get the full match. This is essentially free money.
   - **IRA**: After getting the match, consider contributing to a traditional or Roth IRA, depending on your eligibility and tax situation.

### 2. **Consider Current and Future Tax Brackets**:
   - **Traditional accounts (401(k) and Traditional IRA)**: Contributions are pre-tax, reducing your taxable income in the current year, but you'll pay taxes on withdrawals.
   - **Roth accounts**: Contributions are made after-tax, but withdrawals are tax-free in retirement. This can be advantageous if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket later.

### 3. **Diversify Investments**:
   - **401(k)**: Focus on low-cost index funds or target-date funds that align with your risk tolerance and retirement date.
   - **IRA**: Similar investment strategy as the 401(k) but offers a broader range of options, allowing for more customization.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Use for investments that generate long-term capital gains or qualified dividends, which are taxed at a lower rate than ordinary income. Consider tax-efficient funds here.

### 4. **Liquidity Needs**:
   - Keep some funds in taxable accounts for short-term needs or emergencies since 401(k) and IRA funds may incur penalties for early withdrawal.

### 5. **Review and Rebalance Regularly**:
   - Check your asset allocation periodically to ensure it aligns with your risk tolerance and financial goals.

### Additional Tips:
- Focus on your employer’s plan details, fees, and investment options.
- Consult a financial advisor for personalized advice based on your individual circumstances.

By strategically managing where you allocate funds, you can optimize your growth potential while minimizing taxes over time.
