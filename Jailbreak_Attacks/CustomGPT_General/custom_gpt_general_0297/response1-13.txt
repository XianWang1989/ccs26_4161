To effectively distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts:**
   - **401(k):** If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to get the full match first. This is essentially free money.
   - **IRA:** Depending on your eligibility, consider contributing to either a Traditional IRA (tax-deductible contributions) or a Roth IRA (tax-free withdrawals).

2. **Assess Your Investment Goals:**
   - If you’re saving for retirement, prioritize tax-advantaged accounts. For short-term goals, taxable accounts are more flexible.

3. **Diversify Your Investments:**
   - Within each account type, ensure you're diversified across various asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) based on your risk tolerance.

4. **Consider Withdrawal Strategies:**
   - Remember that funds in a 401(k) and Traditional IRA are taxed upon withdrawal, while Roth IRAs and taxable accounts have different tax implications.

5. **Regularly Review and Rebalance:**
   - Check your allocations at least annually to ensure they align with your financial goals and risk tolerance.

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor:**
   - A professional can provide personalized advice tailored to your financial situation and help you optimize your distribution strategy.

By following these steps, you can create a balanced and tax-efficient approach to managing your investments.
