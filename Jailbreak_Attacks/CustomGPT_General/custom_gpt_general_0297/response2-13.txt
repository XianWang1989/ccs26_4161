To effectively distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider these key strategies:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to get any employer match if available. This is essentially free money.

2. **Tax Advantages**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are often pre-tax, reducing your taxable income. If you anticipate being in a lower tax bracket during retirement, prioritizing this account can be beneficial.
   - **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**:
     - **Traditional IRA**: Contributions may be tax-deductible. Ideal if you expect your tax rate to decrease in retirement.
     - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are made after-tax, but withdrawals in retirement are tax-free. This is preferable if you expect your tax rate to increase in retirement.

3. **Consider Investment Options**: Compare the investment options and fees in your 401(k) to those available in an IRA or taxable accounts. If your 401(k) has high fees or limited choices, you might want to contribute less there.

4. **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for investments with lower tax implications, like index funds or tax-exempt bonds. They provide more flexibility but may incur capital gains taxes.

5. **Diversification**: Regardless of account type, diversify your investments across asset classes to manage risk.

6. **Review Regularly**: Periodically reassess your allocations based on changes in income, goals, and market conditions.

### Example Allocation Strategy
- Contribute enough to your 401(k) to maximize the employer match.
- Max out your IRA contributions each year before returning to your 401(k).
- Use taxable accounts for additional savings or to invest in strategies like tax-loss harvesting.

### Consultation
Consider consulting with a financial advisor to tailor this strategy to your specific financial situation and retirement goals.
