Distributing your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some recommendations for effectively allocating your funds:

### 1. **Understand Account Types**
   - **401(k)**: Typically offers employer matching contributions, tax-deferred growth, and limited investment options; often has higher contribution limits.
   - **IRA (Individual Retirement Account)**: Tax-deferred growth; traditional IRAs offer tax deductions upfront, while Roth IRAs offer tax-free withdrawals in retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Flexible investment choices without contribution limits; subject to capital gains tax on profits.

### 2. **Prioritize Contributions**
   - **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match, as this is essentially free money.
   - **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**: After the match, contribute to your IRA (preferably a Roth IRA if you qualify and expect your tax rate to be higher in retirement). This allows for tax-free growth.

### 3. **Consider Tax Implications**
   - **Tax Diversification**: A mix of tax-deferred (401(k), traditional IRA), tax-free (Roth IRA), and taxable accounts provides flexibility in retirement planning.
   - **Tax-Efficient Investing**: Place investments with high turnover (active stock funds) and interest income (bonds) in tax-advantaged accounts to minimize tax liabilities.

### 4. **Investment Strategy**
   - **Asset Allocation**: Determine a diversified asset allocation based on your risk tolerance and investment horizon. Higher-risk assets (stocks) could go into your taxable account for long-term growth, while safer investments (bonds, cash) might be better in retirement accounts.
   - **Rebalance Regularly**: Periodically review and adjust your allocations based on performance and any changes in financial goals or risk tolerance.

### 5. **Withdrawal Planning**
   - **Consider Future Withdrawals**: Plan for how you will withdraw funds in retirement. Generally, it's advisable to withdraw from taxable accounts first to allow tax-advantaged accounts to grow longer.

### 6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**
   - A professional can help tailor a strategy that fits your unique financial situation and goals, considering factors like income, retirement age, and risk tolerance.

### Conclusion
The ideal approach involves maximizing contributions to tax-advantaged accounts first, diversifying your investments across different account types, and regularly reviewing your strategy to ensure alignment with your goals. Prioritize tax efficiency and consider working with a financial advisor for personalized guidance.
