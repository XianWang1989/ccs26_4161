Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts effectively involves considering several factors including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here’s a strategic approach:

### 1. **Maximize Contributions to Tax-Advantaged Accounts**
   - **401(k):** If your employer offers a match, contribute at least enough to get the full match. This is essentially free money. In 2024, the contribution limit is $23,000 (or $30,500 if you’re over 50).
   - **IRA:** Depending on your eligibility (for Roth vs. Traditional), contribute to an IRA next. The limit for 2024 is $6,500 (or $7,500 if you’re over 50). Roth IRAs provide tax-free growth and withdrawals, while Traditional IRAs offer tax-deferred growth.

### 2. **Consider Your Tax Situation**
   - **Taxable Accounts:** After maxing out your tax-advantaged accounts, invest in taxable accounts. These accounts are more flexible but subject to capital gains taxes. Keep cash instruments or bonds in taxable accounts to minimize tax impact.

### 3. **Diversification and Asset Allocation**
   - **Investment Types:** Spread investments across various asset classes (stocks, bonds, real estate, etc.) to diversify your portfolio. Consider placing more aggressive investments (like stocks) in tax-advantaged accounts to shield them from taxes.
   - **Risk Tolerance:** Align your asset allocation with your risk tolerance and investment timeline. Younger investors might choose a more aggressive approach, while those nearing retirement may shift to conservative assets.

### 4. **Withdrawal Strategy**
   - **Order of Withdrawals:** Plan for retirement withdrawals starting with taxable accounts, followed by tax-deferred accounts (like 401(k)s and Traditional IRAs), and lastly, tax-free accounts (like Roth IRAs). This minimizes your tax burden over time.

### 5. **Revisit and Adjust Regularly**
   - **Annual Review:** Revisit your allocations annually or after significant life events (like a job change or marriage) to ensure that your investment strategy still aligns with your goals.

### Conclusion
A good starting point is to prioritize contributions to tax-advantaged accounts first before moving on to taxable investments. Always consider consulting with a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your specific financial situation.

### Example Distribution Strategy
- **40% in 401(k) (maximized for employer match)**
- **30% in IRA (maximized for tax benefits)**
- **30% in Taxable Accounts (for liquidity and flexibility)**

This general guideline will help you build a strong investment foundation while effectively managing taxes.
