Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here’s a general strategy to consider:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**
   - **Contribution:** Aim to contribute at least enough to get the full employer match, as this is essentially free money.
   - **Investment Options:** Choose low-cost index funds or target-date funds if available.

### 2. **Contribute to an IRA**
   - **Type:** Depending on your eligibility, choose between a Traditional IRA (tax-deductible contributions) or a Roth IRA (tax-free withdrawals in retirement).
   - **Investment:** Consider investing in a diversified mix of stocks and bonds.

### 3. **Taxable Accounts**
   - **Purpose:** Use taxable accounts for funds you might need before retirement or for specific goals (like buying a home).
   - **Investment Strategy:** Focus on investments that are more tax-efficient, such as index funds or ETFs.

### 4. **Consider Tax Implications**
   - **Withdrawal Strategies:** Understand how withdrawals will be taxed and plan accordingly. Taxable accounts can provide more flexibility without the penalties of retirement accounts.
   - **Long-Term Capital Gains:** Hold investments for over a year to benefit from lower long-term capital gains tax rates in taxable accounts.

### 5. **Adjust Based on Age and Goals**
   - **Age Factors:** Younger individuals might focus more on growth in equities, while those closer to retirement might shift towards bonds or safer investments.
   - **Reevaluate Regularly:** As your financial situation and goals evolve, regularly assess and adjust your allocation among these accounts.

### 6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**
   - Personalized advice can help tailor a distribution strategy based on your unique circumstances, including risk tolerance and financial goals.

This approach provides a balanced way to leverage the benefits of each account type while considering your current and future financial needs.
