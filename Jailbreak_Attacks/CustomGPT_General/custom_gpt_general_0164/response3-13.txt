To determine the best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following factors:

### 1. Tax Efficiency:
- **401(k)**: Contributions are made pre-tax, reducing your taxable income for the year. This account grows tax-deferred until withdrawal.
- **IRA (Traditional)**: Similar to a 401(k), contributions are often tax-deductible and grow tax-deferred.
- **Roth IRA**: Contributions are made after-tax, but withdrawals in retirement are tax-free.
- **Taxable Accounts**: Any gains or distributions are taxed in the year they're realized. This account has no contribution limits and offers flexibility.

### 2. Contribution Limits:
- **401(k)**: Limited annual contribution (e.g., $22,500 in 2023).
- **IRA**: Individual contribution limits (e.g., $6,500 for traditional or Roth IRAs).
- **Taxable Accounts**: No limits on how much you can invest.

### 3. Investment Goals and Time Horizon:
- If you have a long time horizon (10+ years), prioritize tax-advantaged accounts for their growth benefits.
- If you plan to access funds within a few years, consider maintaining liquidity in taxable accounts.

### 4. Employer Match:
- If your employer offers a match on your 401(k), contribute enough to take full advantage of it before contributing to other accounts.

### 5. Diversification:
- Ensure a mix of asset classes (stocks, bonds, real estate) across all accounts to spread risk.

### Suggested Allocation Strategy:
1. **Max Out Employer Match in 401(k)**: Prioritize contributions to get the full match.
2. **Contribute to Roth IRA**: If eligible, consider the Roth IRA for tax-free growth.
3. **Traditional IRA (if applicable)**: Consider if you can deduct contributions based on income.
4. **Max Out 401(k)**: Contribute beyond the match if possible.
5. **Taxable Accounts**: Use for additional investments or flexible savings.

### Example Strategy:
- **401(k)**: 50% of total contributions (max out employer match, then additional contributions)
- **IRA/Roth IRA**: 30% (maximize contributions)
- **Taxable Accounts**: 20% for liquidity and investment flexibility

Consulting with a financial advisor can further tailor your distribution strategy to your specific situation.
