When distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider these strategies:

1. **Maximize Contributions**: 
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match; this is essentially free money.
   - **IRA**: Aim to fully fund a Roth or Traditional IRA, depending on your tax situation.

2. **Tax Efficiency**: 
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Invest in tax-efficient assets (like index funds or ETFs) to minimize capital gains taxes.
   - **Tax-Deferred Accounts (401(k) & IRA)**: Focus on investments that generate higher returns but would incur significant taxes if held in a taxable account, like bonds or actively managed funds.

3. **Risk Tolerance and Time Horizon**:
   - Adjust your asset allocation based on how close you are to retirement. Younger investors might prefer more stocks for growth, while those closer to retirement may want more bonds for stability.

4. **Withdrawal Strategy**:
   - Plan your withdrawal strategy: Generally, it's advisable to withdraw from taxable accounts first, followed by tax-deferred accounts, and lastly, tax-free accounts (like Roth IRAs).

5. **Rebalancing**:
   - Regularly review and rebalance your portfolio to ensure that it meets your financial goals and risk tolerance.
