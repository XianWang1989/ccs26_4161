Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here’s a general approach:

### 1. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**
   - **401(k):** Contribute enough to get any employer match first, as it’s essentially free money.
   - **IRA:** Consider maxing out a traditional or Roth IRA next, depending on your income and tax situation.

### 2. **Assess Contribution Limits**
   - Be aware of annual contribution limits for each account type. For 2023, the limits are:
     - 401(k): $22,500 ($30,000 if age 50+)
     - IRA: $6,500 ($7,500 if age 50+)
   - Adjust your contributions based on these limits.

### 3. **Consider Tax Implications**
   - **401(k):** Contributions are pre-tax, lowering your taxable income now.
   - **Traditional IRA:** Similar tax benefits as a 401(k), but income limits apply for tax-deductible contributions.
   - **Roth IRA:** Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, allowing for tax-free withdrawals in retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Interest, dividends, and capital gains are taxable in the year they are earned.

### 4. **Invest for Growth**
   - Focus on growth-oriented investments in your taxable account, as capital gains may be taxed at a lower rate than ordinary income.
   - Use tax-efficient funds in your taxable account (e.g., index funds or tax-managed funds).

### 5. **Rebalance Regularly**
   - Regularly review and adjust your asset allocation based on your retirement horizon and risk tolerance. This includes considering shifting investments among accounts to maintain your desired allocation.

### Conclusion
A common approach might be to:
1. Fund your 401(k) up to the match.
2. Contribute to an IRA, then return to maxing out your 401(k) if funds allow.
3. Use taxable accounts for additional investing once you hit limits on tax-advantaged accounts.

### Consult a Financial Advisor
Consider consulting a financial advisor to tailor this strategy to your specific circumstances and goals.
