Determining the best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement plans. Here are some general strategies:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your 401(k) offers an employer matching contribution, contribute enough to get the full match first. This is essentially free money.

2. **Consider Tax Advantages**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are pre-tax, reducing your taxable income. Focus on this if you're in a higher tax bracket now.
   - **Traditional IRA**: Similar tax benefits as a 401(k), but consider income limits for tax-deductible contributions.
   - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are post-tax, allowing for tax-free withdrawals in retirement. Ideal if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket later.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Best for money needed in the short to medium term or for tax-efficient investments (like index funds).

3. **Diversification**: Ensure that your investments across these accounts are well-diversified to mitigate risk.

4. **Emergency Fund**: Maintain a separate emergency fund outside of these accounts to avoid early withdrawal penalties.

5. **Investment Strategy**: Your asset allocation should consider your risk tolerance and investment horizon across all accounts. For instance, you might prefer more aggressive investments in your 401(k) if you have a long time until retirement.

6. **Consult a Professional**: Speaking with a financial advisor can provide personalized guidance based on your unique situation.

### Example Allocation Approach:
1. **401(k)**: Contribute up to the employer match, then consider maxing out your IRA.
2. **IRA**: If eligible, contribute to a Roth IRA for tax-free growth, or a Traditional IRA for pre-tax contributions.
3. **Taxable Account**: Invest any additional savings here, especially for goals outside of retirement.

Regularly reassess your allocations as your financial situation, goals, and tax laws may change over time.
