Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment horizon. Here are some general guidelines to help you determine the best allocation:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a match on your 401(k), contribute enough to take full advantage of that. This is essentially free money.

2. **Consider Tax Advantages**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are made pre-tax, reducing your current taxable income. It grows tax-deferred until withdrawal.
   - **Traditional IRA**: Similar to 401(k) with tax benefits on contributions and tax-deferred growth.
   - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, but withdrawals in retirement are tax-free. 

3. **Assess Limits and Features**:
   - **Contribution Limits**: Be aware of annual contribution limits for each account type.
   - **Investment Options**: 401(k)s may have limited investment choices, while IRAs typically offer a broader range.

4. **Taxable Accounts**: 
   - Use taxable accounts for investments that are likely to generate short-term gains or dividends, which might not be ideal for tax-advantaged accounts.
   - Consider placing assets in taxable accounts that are more tax-efficient (like index funds).

5. **Diversify Investments**: Allocate investments based on your risk tolerance and investment goals. Diversification across asset classes can reduce risk.

6. **Withdrawal Strategy**: Plan for how you will withdraw funds in retirement. Generally, it's advisable to withdraw from taxable accounts first, then tax-deferred accounts, and lastly from Roth accounts to maximize tax efficiency.

7. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: A professional can offer personalized advice based on your specific circumstances.

### Sample Allocation Strategy:
- **401(k)**: 15% – Maximize company match and then assess further contributions based on remaining budget.
- **IRA**: 15% – Contribute to this account if you are eligible, focusing on tax-efficient investments.
- **Taxable Accounts**: 70% – Allocate funds here for flexibility and for potential investment growth without the limitations of retirement accounts.

Adjust this allocation based on your specific tax scenario, retirement timeline, and risk tolerance.
