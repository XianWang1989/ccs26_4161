Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment time horizon. Here’s a general strategy:

1. **Maximize Employer Matching**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match, as it’s essentially free money.

2. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **IRA**: Contribute to a traditional or Roth IRA. A Roth is ideal if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement, as withdrawals are tax-free. A traditional IRA offers tax deductions now but taxes on withdrawals later.
   - **401(k)**: Beyond the match, aim to max out contributions if you can. This reduces your taxable income.

3. **Utilize Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**: Once you've maxed out tax-advantaged accounts, consider investing in a taxable account. This will give you more access to your funds without penalties for early withdrawals.

4. **Consider Asset Allocation**: 
   - Place higher-growth investments in tax-deferred accounts (401(k) and IRA) to maximize tax efficiency.
   - More conservative investments, which might generate dividends or interest, can go in taxable accounts, where capital gains tax may be lower.

5. **Regular Review and Rebalance**: Check your allocations at least annually to ensure they align with your goals and risk tolerance.

Consult a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your situation.
