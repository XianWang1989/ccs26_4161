Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment time horizon. Here’s a general approach:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**
   - **401(k):** If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to get the full match. This is essentially free money.
   - **IRA:** Consider contributing to a traditional IRA or a Roth IRA based on your tax situation. Traditional IRAs offer tax deductions upfront, while Roth IRAs provide tax-free growth and withdrawals.

### 2. **Consider Tax Implications**
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Investments here are subject to capital gains taxes. Use this account for investments that may generate high taxable income, like bonds or actively managed funds, as well as long-term holdings to minimize taxes.

### 3. **Diversification and Risk Tolerance**
   - Ensure you have a diversified portfolio across all accounts. Adjust asset allocation based on your risk tolerance and investment goals.

### 4. **Emergency Fund**
   - Maintain an emergency fund in a savings account before fully investing in these accounts.

### 5. **Regular Review**
   - Periodically assess your investment strategy. Adjust contributions based on changing financial situations and goals.

### Example Allocation Strategy:
- **401(k):** Contribute enough to get the employer match.
- **IRA:** Max out this account if possible.
- **Taxable Accounts:** Invest any additional funds after maximizing tax-advantaged accounts.

Consult with a financial advisor for personalized advice based on your specific circumstances.
