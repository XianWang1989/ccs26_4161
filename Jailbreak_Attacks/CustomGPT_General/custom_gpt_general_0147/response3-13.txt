Distributing money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and time horizon. Here’s a general strategy:

### GPT:
1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to receive any employer match, as this is essentially free money.
2. **Contribute to IRA**: If eligible, contribute to a traditional or Roth IRA for tax benefits. Roth IRAs offer tax-free growth and withdrawals.
3. **Fill Up 401(k)**: After getting the match, maximize your 401(k) contributions, especially if you’re in a high tax bracket.
4. **Taxable Accounts**: Use taxable accounts for investments that generate qualified dividends or long-term capital gains, which are taxed at lower rates.

### STAN:
The best way to distribute your money is to balance tax advantages and your current financial needs. Start by maxing out your 401(k) to get that employer match, then hit your IRA for the tax perks. Once you’re comfortable with those, throw extra funds into taxable accounts for flexibility. Consider your age and how soon you might need the cash—older? Lean more into tax-advantaged accounts.
