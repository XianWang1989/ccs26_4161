When considering how to distribute money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, it's crucial to think about your financial goals, tax implications, and investment strategy. Here's a breakdown of the best approach:

### 1. **Understand Each Account Type:**
   - **401(k):** Employer-sponsored, often with matching contributions; reduces taxable income now, but withdrawals in retirement are taxed.
   - **IRA (Individual Retirement Account):** Similar tax benefits but typically more investment options; contributions may be tax-deductible depending on income.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Flexibility in withdrawals without penalties, but capital gains taxes apply on profits.

### 2. **Maximize Employer Match:**
   - Contribute enough to your 401(k) to take full advantage of any employer match. This is essentially "free money."

### 3. **Consider Tax Implications:**
   - Prioritize tax-advantaged accounts (like 401(k) and IRA) for longer-term investments that will benefit from tax deferment or tax-free growth.

### 4. **Diversify Investments:**
   - Use your 401(k) and IRA for growth-oriented investments (stocks), while you can allocate more conservative investments (bonds, cash) in taxable accounts to manage tax implications.

### 5. **Review Contribution Limits:**
   - For 2024, the contribution limits are:
     - **401(k):** $23,000 (or $30,000 if over age 50).
     - **IRA:** $6,500 (or $7,500 if over age 50).

### Suggested Distribution Strategy:
1. **Max out 401(k) match** (if available).
2. **Contribute to your IRA** up to the maximum limit (consider a Roth IRA if eligible for tax-free withdrawals in retirement).
3. **Continue to contribute to your 401(k)** until you reach the annual limit.
4. **Allocate any additional savings to taxable accounts** for flexibility and liquidity.

### Final Tips:
- Reassess your asset allocation regularly based on age, risk tolerance, and market conditions.
- Consider consulting a financial advisor who can provide personalized advice tailored to your situation.

This balanced approach allows you to take advantage of tax benefits while keeping your financial future secure.
