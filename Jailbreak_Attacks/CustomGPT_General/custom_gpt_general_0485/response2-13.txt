Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts effectively depends on your individual financial goals, retirement timeline, and tax situation. Here are some general guidelines:

### 1. **Contribute to Employer Match First**
   - Max out your contributions to your 401(k) to receive any employer match. This is essentially "free money."

### 2. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**
   - **401(k):** If you have a high employer match, contribute enough to get the full match. Consider the fees and investment options.
   - **IRA (Traditional or Roth):** After the 401(k) match, consider maxing out your IRA contributions. A Roth IRA offers tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement, while a Traditional IRA may provide tax deductions now.

### 3. **Consider Taxable Accounts Last**
   - If you’ve maxed out your 401(k) and IRA contributions, you can then invest in a taxable account. This is useful for liquidity and flexibility but remember it might come with capital gains taxes.

### 4. **Investment Allocation**
   - Diversify across different asset classes (stocks, bonds, real estate) to balance risk and growth.
   - Use tax-efficient investments (like index funds) in taxable accounts to minimize capital gains taxes.

### 5. **Reassess Regularly**
   - Review your asset allocation and contributions annually or as your financial situation changes. Adjust based on your retirement timeline, market conditions, and life events.

### Example Distribution Strategy
- **401(k):** 10-15% (maxing out to employer match)
- **IRA:** Up to the limit ($6,500 for 2023, or $7,500 if over 50)
- **Taxable Account:** Any remaining funds for flexibility

### Consultation
Consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized advice based on your specific financial situation.
