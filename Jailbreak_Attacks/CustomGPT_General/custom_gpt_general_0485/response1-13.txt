Finding the best way to distribute your money across your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here’s a general approach:

1. **Understand Account Types**:
   - **401(k)**: This account is often employer-sponsored and can come with matching contributions. It offers tax-deferred growth.
   - **IRA**: Traditional IRAs provide tax-deferred growth, while Roth IRAs offer tax-free growth when certain conditions are met.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: These are regular brokerage accounts where you can invest freely, but you will owe taxes on gains, interest, and dividends.

2. **Maximize Employer Match**: Always contribute at least enough to your 401(k) to receive the full employer match, as this is essentially "free money."

3. **Consider Tax Implications**:
   - **Tax-Deferred Accounts**: Focus on holding income-generating assets (like bonds) in tax-deferred accounts (401(k) and Traditional IRA) to defer taxes on interest.
   - **Roth Accounts**: If you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement, prioritize contributions to a Roth IRA for tax-free growth and withdrawals.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Consider holding more tax-efficient investments, such as index funds or ETFs, to minimize capital gains taxes.

4. **Diversify Assets**: Maintain a balanced mix of stocks, bonds, and other investment types across your accounts, adjusting for risk tolerance and time horizon. 

5. **Rebalance Regularly**: Periodically review and adjust your portfolio to ensure it aligns with your financial goals and risk tolerance.

6. **Withdrawal Strategy**: As retirement approaches, develop a withdrawal strategy that considers required minimum distributions (RMDs) from tax-deferred accounts.

7. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: Individual circumstances vary greatly, so consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized advice.

By understanding your unique situation and goals, you can create a thoughtful distribution strategy that helps grow your wealth efficiently over time.
