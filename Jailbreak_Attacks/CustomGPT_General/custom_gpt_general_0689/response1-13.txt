The best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment time horizon. Here are some guidelines to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a matching contribution to your 401(k), prioritize contributing enough to take full advantage of that match. It's essentially free money.

2. **Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**:
   - **401(k)**: Focus on maxing out your contributions, especially if you have a tax-deductible option.
   - **IRA**: If eligible, consider contributing to a Roth or Traditional IRA. A Roth IRA offers tax-free growth, while a Traditional IRA may provide immediate tax benefits.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: After maximizing contributions to your tax-advantaged accounts, use taxable accounts for additional savings and investment opportunities. This is especially useful for short-to-medium-term goals since these accounts offer flexibility in withdrawals.

4. **Asset Allocation**: Within each account type, diversify your investments based on your risk tolerance and time horizon. Consider a mix of stocks, bonds, and other assets aligned with your financial goals.

5. **Tax Efficiency**: Place tax-inefficient investments (like bonds or actively managed funds) in tax-advantaged accounts and tax-efficient investments (like index funds or long-term stocks) in taxable accounts.

6. **Regular Review**: Revisit your portfolio allocation periodically to ensure it aligns with your goals and any changes in your financial situation.

7. **Consult a Professional**: Depending on your specific circumstances, it might be beneficial to consult a financial advisor for tailored advice.

This approach will help you build a balanced investment strategy while maximizing tax advantages.
