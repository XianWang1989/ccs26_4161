Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts involves strategic planning based on factors such as your current income, tax situation, retirement goals, and investment preferences. Here are some key considerations:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**
   - **Action**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match, as this is essentially "free money."

### 2. **Prioritize Tax Advantages**
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are pre-tax, lowering your taxable income. Consider maximizing contributions (up to the limit).
   - **Traditional IRA**: Also offers tax deductions. If eligible, consider contributing here next.
   - **Roth IRA**: If you're eligible, this allows for tax-free withdrawals in retirement, making it attractive for future tax benefits.

### 3. **Assess Investment Options**
   - **401(k)**: Typically limited investment options; ensure you have diverse choices.
   - **IRA**: Usually offers broader investment options, allowing for more tailored strategies.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Useful for investments not tied to retirement; consider tax efficiency (like tax-loss harvesting).

### 4. **Consider Your Time Horizon**
   - **Long-term (retirement)**: Focus on tax-advantaged accounts.
   - **Short-term needs**: More liquidity can be found in taxable accounts.

### 5. **Account Growth and Withdrawals**
   - Plan based on when you’ll need access to funds, adjusting ratios as necessary.

### 6. **Tax Implications**
   - Keep in mind the implications of withdrawals from each type of account, especially how they affect your tax bracket in retirement.

### Example Distribution Strategy:
- **401(k)**: Contribute up to the employer match + additional if you can afford it.
- **IRA**: Max out contributions after 401(k) match.
- **Taxable Accounts**: Invest any additional savings, focusing on tax-efficient investments.

### Final Thoughts:
Consulting a financial advisor can help tailor these strategies to your specific financial situation and goals.
