To effectively distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider these key strategies:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**:
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money. Aim to max out contributions if possible.
   - **IRA**: After the 401(k), contribute to a Roth or Traditional IRA, depending on your income level and tax situation. A Roth IRA allows for tax-free withdrawals in retirement, while a Traditional IRA may provide tax deductions now.

2. **Consider Your Tax Bracket**:
   - Use tax-advantaged accounts for high-growth investments, deferring taxes until withdrawal.
   - Place income-generating investments (like bonds) in tax-deferred accounts to minimize taxable earnings in taxable accounts.

3. **Diversify Asset Allocation**:
   - Balance your portfolio across all accounts to align with your risk tolerance and investment goals. This typically includes a mix of stocks, bonds, and alternative investments.

4. **Utilize Taxable Accounts Wisely**:
   - Use these for investments with lower tax implications (like index funds) or for investments you may want to access before retirement age without penalties.

5. **Regularly Review and Rebalance**:
   - Periodically check the distribution and returns in each account to ensure alignment with your overall investment strategy and adjust as needed.

6. **Consider Future Withdrawals**:
   - Plan withdrawals strategically in retirement to minimize taxes, such as drawing down taxable accounts first to allow tax-advantaged accounts to grow longer.

This approach helps optimize your investment strategy for growth while minimizing tax liabilities over time.
