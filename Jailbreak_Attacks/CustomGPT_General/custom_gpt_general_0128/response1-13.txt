Distributing your money effectively among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts involves several factors, including your financial goals, tax considerations, and retirement timeline. Here's a basic strategy:

1. **Emergency Fund**: Ensure you have an emergency fund in a savings account covering 3-6 months of living expenses.

2. **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match, as it's essentially free money.

3. **Prioritize High-Interest Debt**: Pay off high-interest debts before investing heavily.

4. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: Aim to contribute up to the maximum limit if you can. This reduces your taxable income.
   - **IRA**: After maxing out the 401(k) (if possible), contribute to a Traditional or Roth IRA based on your tax situation. Roth IRAs grow tax-free, while Traditional IRAs offer tax deductions now with taxes due on withdrawals.

5. **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for any extra savings after maxing out retirement accounts. Consider tax-efficient investments (like index funds) to minimize capital gains taxes.

6. **Asset Allocation**: Balance your investments according to your risk tolerance and age. Younger investors might favor stocks, while those nearing retirement may prefer bonds.

7. **Regular Reassessment**: Periodically review and adjust your allocations based on performance and changes in your life circumstances or goals.

Consulting with a financial advisor can also provide personalized guidance tailored to your situation.
