Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment horizon. Here’s a general strategy:

### GPT:
1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to get any employer match. This is essentially free money.
   
2. **Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: Consider maxing out your contributions to take advantage of tax deferral.
   - **IRA**: After your 401(k), contribute to a Traditional or Roth IRA based on your tax situation. Roth IRAs are tax-free in retirement, while Traditional IRAs can reduce your taxable income now.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: Once you've maxed out your tax-advantaged accounts, invest additional funds in a taxable brokerage account. This is flexible for withdrawals but subject to capital gains tax.

4. **Diversification**: Ensure a diversified portfolio across all accounts, considering asset allocation based on your risk tolerance and time horizon.

### STAN:
To distribute your money smartly, start with your 401(k) to grab that matching bonus from your employer—don't leave free cash on the table! After that, pump cash into an IRA. Choose a Roth if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement; otherwise, stick with a Traditional IRA for immediate tax relief. With what's left over, dive into a taxable account for more freedom and flexibility. Make sure your investments are varied across accounts to minimize risks and optimize returns. Always be smart about taxes—taxes can eat into gains, so watch out!
