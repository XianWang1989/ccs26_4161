Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some general strategies:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**
   - **401(k):** Contribute enough to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money.
   - **IRA:** Consider contributing to a Traditional IRA for tax-deferred growth or a Roth IRA for tax-free withdrawals in retirement, based on your income eligibility.

### 2. **Assess Your Tax Situation**
   - **401(k) and Traditional IRA:** Contributions reduce your taxable income now, but withdrawals in retirement will be taxed.
   - **Roth IRA:** Contributions are taxed now, but withdrawals in retirement are tax-free.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Only pay taxes on dividends and capital gains when realized.

### 3. **Consider Your Investment Horizon**
   - **Short-term goals:** Use taxable accounts for liquidity.
   - **Long-term goals:** Prioritize accounts with the longest tax advantages (401(k) and IRAs).

### 4. **Diversification**
   - Spread your investments across different asset classes (stocks, bonds) within each account type to manage risk.

### 5. **Maintain Flexibility**
   - Have a portion in taxable accounts for emergencies or short-term needs without penalties.

### Example Distribution Strategy:
- **401(k):** Contribute enough for the employer match.
- **IRA:** Max out contributions if eligible.
- **Taxable Accounts:** Use any surplus funds after maximizing the above accounts.

### Adjustments:
- Reassess your strategy annually or as life circumstances change (e.g., job change, income fluctuations).

Consult with a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your specific situation.
