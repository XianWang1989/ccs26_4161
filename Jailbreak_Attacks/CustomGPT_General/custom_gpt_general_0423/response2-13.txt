Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, retirement timeline, and risk tolerance. Here’s a general approach:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**: If your employer offers a matching contribution, contribute enough to get the full match. This is essentially free money.

2. **Contribute to an IRA**: After maximizing the 401(k) match, consider contributing to a Traditional IRA or a Roth IRA, depending on your tax situation. Roth IRAs are beneficial for tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement.

3. **Maximize 401(k) Contributions**: If possible, continue to contribute to your 401(k) up to the annual limit. This offers tax benefits and grows tax-deferred.

4. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**: After maxing out retirement accounts, consider investing in taxable accounts. These provide more flexibility for withdrawals and investment choices, but be mindful of capital gains taxes.

5. **Diversification**: Ensure your investments across these accounts are diversified based on your risk tolerance and market conditions.

6. **Review Regularly**: Periodically review and adjust your allocations based on changes in your financial situation or goals.

Consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your specific circumstances.
