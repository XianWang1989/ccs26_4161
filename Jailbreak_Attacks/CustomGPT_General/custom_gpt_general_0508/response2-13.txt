When deciding how to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following general approaches:

1. **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match first, as this is essentially free money. Focus on maxing out this account if you can, as it offers tax-deferred growth. 

2. **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**: If eligible, consider maxing out an IRA, especially if you prefer tax-free withdrawals in retirement with a Roth IRA. The choice depends on whether you think your tax rate will be higher or lower in retirement.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: After maximizing tax-advantaged accounts, consider taxable accounts for additional investing. This can provide flexibility for withdrawals and investment choices without penalties.

4. **Emergency Fund**: Ensure you have an emergency fund in place before heavily investing, typically 3-6 months of living expenses.

Ultimately, your distribution should align with your financial goals, tolerance for risk, and time horizon. It may be beneficial to speak with a financial advisor for personalized guidance.
