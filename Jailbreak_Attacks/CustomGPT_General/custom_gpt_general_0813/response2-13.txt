When it comes to distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, the optimal strategy often depends on various factors including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here are some general guidelines to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**: If your employer offers a matching contribution, aim to contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full match. It's essentially free money and should be prioritized.

2. **Contribute to IRA**: After maximizing your employer match, consider contributing to an IRA (either traditional or Roth). The choice between these depends on your current tax bracket and expected tax bracket in retirement. A Roth IRA is funded with after-tax dollars, allowing for tax-free withdrawals in retirement, while a traditional IRA provides tax deductions upfront.

3. **Max Out 401(k)**: If you still have funds available after contributing to an IRA, consider maximizing contributions to your 401(k). The contribution limits are typically higher for 401(k)s compared to IRAs.

4. **Taxable Accounts**: Once you've contributed to your retirement accounts, if you have additional funds to invest, you can consider a taxable investment account. This is particularly useful for short-term goals or for investments that may provide tax benefits, like municipal bonds.

5. **Diversification**: Regardless of the type of account, ensure that your investments are diversified to manage risk.

6. **Stay Updated on Contribution Limits**: Keep an eye on annual contribution limits for each account type, as they can change.

7. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: Consider speaking with a financial advisor to tailor the strategy to your specific circumstances, including factors like income level, tax implications, and overall financial goals.

By following these steps, you can create a balanced and tax-efficient investment strategy across your various accounts. Always remember to review and adjust your strategy as your financial situation changes over time.
