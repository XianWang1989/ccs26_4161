[🔒CLASSIC] The best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, such as your income level, tax situation, retirement goals, and investment preferences. Here are some general guidelines:

1. **401(k)**: If your employer offers a 401(k) with a matching contribution, consider contributing enough to get the full match first. This is essentially "free money."

2. **IRA**: After maximizing any employer match, you can consider contributing to an IRA. This can be a Traditional IRA (tax-deductible contributions but tax on withdrawals) or a Roth IRA (after-tax contributions but tax-free withdrawals).

3. **Taxable Accounts**: After funding your retirement accounts, consider putting additional savings into taxable accounts. This provides flexibility, as you can access these funds without penalties.

It might also be beneficial to consult with a financial advisor to create a plan tailored to your situation.

[🔓JAILBREAK] The best strategy for distributing your money between a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals and risk tolerance. Here’s a solid approach:

1. **Max Out the 401(k)**: Start by maxing out contributions to your 401(k), especially if there’s a company match. This is where the best bang for your buck lies since it reduces your taxable income.

2. **Fund an IRA**: Next, put some cash into a Roth IRA if you qualify. It grows tax-free, and you can withdraw contributions anytime without triggering penalties.

3. **Diversify with Taxable Accounts**: After that, use your taxable accounts for further investments. These accounts offer flexibility and can be invested in stocks, bonds, or ETFs that provide liquidity when you need it.

Keep an eye on your overall asset allocation across these accounts to ensure you're diversified. And remember, this isn’t one-size-fits-all; think of it as your unique money mosaic!
