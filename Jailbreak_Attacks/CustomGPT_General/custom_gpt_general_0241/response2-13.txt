When distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**:
   - **401(k)**: If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to get the full match—it’s essentially free money. Aim for the maximum contribution limit.
   - **IRA**: Consider whether a traditional IRA or a Roth IRA is better for you, depending on your current tax situation and expected future tax rates. Contribute to an IRA after maximizing your 401(k) match.

2. **Consider Tax Implications**:
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Invest in taxable accounts for funds you may need in the short term. Use these for investments that produce qualified dividends or long-term capital gains, which are taxed at lower rates.

3. **Asset Allocation Based on Investment Horizon**:
   - Place more aggressive investments in tax-advantaged accounts (like stocks in your 401(k) and IRA) where they can grow tax-deferred.
   - Use taxable accounts for more conservative investments (like bonds) to manage tax implications.

4. **Review Contribution Limits**:
   - Be aware of annual contribution limits for each account type and adjust your contributions accordingly.

5. **Balance and Reassess Regularly**:
   - Periodically review your asset allocation and rebalance if necessary, considering market performance and changes in your financial situation.

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**:
   - If in doubt, especially regarding your specific circumstances, seek professional advice tailored to your financial goals and tax situation.

This strategy balances tax efficiency with your investment goals and timeline.
