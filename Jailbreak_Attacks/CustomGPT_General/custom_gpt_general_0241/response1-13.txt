Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, age, and risk tolerance. Here are some general strategies:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First:**
   - **401(k) and IRA Contributions**: If your employer offers a match for your 401(k), contribute enough to maximize that match before considering other investments. This is essentially free money. After that, aim to contribute to an IRA (if eligible), which could be traditional or Roth based on your tax situation.

### 2. **Evaluate Contribution Limits:**
   - **401(k)**: For 2023, the limit is $22,500 (or $30,000 if you're 50 or older).
   - **IRA**: The limit is $6,500 (or $7,500 if you're 50 or older).
   - Ensure you're within these limits to avoid penalties.

### 3. **Asset Location Strategy:**
   - Place more tax-inefficient investments (like bonds or high-yield assets) in tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) and IRA) to benefit from tax deferral.
   - Use taxable accounts for tax-efficient investments (like index funds or ETFs) that have lower capital gains distributions.

### 4. **Assess Your Withdrawal Strategy:**
   - If you plan to retire early or think you'll be in a higher tax bracket later, you might prefer Roth accounts for tax-free withdrawals in retirement.
   - Consider your current income and potential tax implications when deciding between traditional and Roth options.

### 5. **Diversification Across Accounts:**
   - Ensure your overall portfolio is diversified across asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) regardless of the account type to manage risk effectively.

### 6. **Review and Adjust Regularly:**
   - Monitor your investments and rebalance as needed to stick to your financial goals and risk tolerance.

### 7. **Consult a Financial Advisor:**
   - Personal circumstances can vary greatly, so consider consulting a financial advisor for tailored advice that fits your specific situation.

By following these strategies, you can create a balanced and tax-efficient investment approach that aligns with your long-term financial objectives.
