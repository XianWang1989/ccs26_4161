Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts involves several key considerations:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a match on your 401(k), contribute enough to receive the full match. This is effectively free money.

2. **Tax Efficiency**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are tax-deferred, making this a good place for higher-growth assets since you won’t pay taxes until withdrawal.
   - **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**: 
     - **Traditional IRA**: Similar tax treatment as a 401(k).
     - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, and qualified withdrawals are tax-free. Consider using this for assets you expect to appreciate significantly.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Ideal for funds you may need sooner, as they allow for more flexibility. Invest in tax-efficient funds (like index funds) to minimize capital gains taxes.

3. **Investment Goals and Time Horizon**:
   - Align your investment strategy with your goals (retirement, short-term savings, etc.) and time frame. Longer horizons may benefit from more aggressive investments.

4. **Diversification**: Ensure you diversify within each account type. Consider asset allocation based on your risk tolerance and age.

5. **Regular Contributions**: Set up automatic contributions to each account to build savings consistently.

6. **Rebalance Periodically**: Review your asset allocation and make adjustments to maintain your desired risk level.

It’s often advisable to consult with a financial advisor to tailor this distribution strategy to your specific financial situation and goals.
