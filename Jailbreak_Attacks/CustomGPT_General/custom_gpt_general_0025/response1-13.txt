Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here’s a general approach:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First:**
   - **401(k):** Contribute enough to get any employer match, as that’s essentially free money. If possible, aim to max out your contributions (the limit is $22,500 for 2024, with an additional $7,500 for catch-up contributions if you're over 50).
   - **IRA:** Consider contributing to a Traditional IRA or Roth IRA based on your tax situation. For 2024, the contribution limit is $6,500 (or $7,500 if over 50). A Roth IRA is beneficial if you anticipate being in a higher tax bracket in retirement.

### 2. **Taxable Accounts:**
   - Once you’ve maximized contributions to tax-advantaged accounts, any additional savings can go into a taxable account. This provides flexibility and access to funds without penalties (though you’ll owe taxes on dividends and capital gains).

### 3. **Asset Allocation:**
   - Diversify your investments across accounts. Consider putting more aggressive investments (like stocks) in tax-advantaged accounts, where gains grow tax-free or tax-deferred. Place more stable investments (like bonds) in taxable accounts to minimize tax impact.

### 4. **Consider Your Time Horizon:**
   - If retirement is far off, lean towards aggressive growth strategies in your 401(k) and IRA. As you get closer to retirement, gradually shift to more conservative investments to protect your capital.

### 5. **Review Regularly:**
   - Assess your financial situation and goals annually. Adjust your contributions and asset allocations based on performance and changes in your financial situation.

### 6. **Consult a Financial Advisor:**
   - For personalized guidance tailored to your specific situation, consider consulting a financial advisor.

By following these guidelines, you can effectively allocate your money across your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts to enhance savings and investment growth for the long term.
