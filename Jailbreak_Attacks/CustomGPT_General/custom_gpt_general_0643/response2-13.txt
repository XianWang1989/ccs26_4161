The best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some general guidelines:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match (401(k))**
   - Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money.

### 2. **Consider Tax Advantages**
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are tax-deferred, lowering your taxable income now. However, you'll be taxed on withdrawals in retirement.
   - **IRA**: Traditional IRAs also offer tax deferral, while Roth IRAs provide tax-free withdrawals in retirement if the account is held for 5 years and you meet certain conditions.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Investments here are taxed annually on dividends and interest, but long-term capital gains are taxed at a lower rate.

### 3. **Emergency Fund**
   - Ensure you have an emergency fund in a savings or money market account before investing heavily.

### 4. **Diversification**
   - Spread investments across asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) based on your risk tolerance and time horizon.

### 5. **Investment Timeline**
   - If you're younger and have a longer time until retirement, lean more towards stocks (growth) in the 401(k) and IRA.
   - As you near retirement, shift towards more conservative investments (bonds, stable funds).

### 6. **Tax Impact**
   - Consider the tax implications of withdrawing from taxable versus tax-deferred accounts based on your anticipated income in retirement.

### Sample Distribution Strategy:
- **Maximize 401(k) up to match**: 15% of income
- **Contribute to IRA**: 10% to a Roth or Traditional, depending on your tax situation
- **Invest in taxable accounts**: Remaining discretionary income

### Regular Review
- Reassess your strategy annually based on changes in income, expenses, goals, and tax laws.

### Conclusion
Each person's situation is unique, so consider consulting with a financial advisor for tailored advice.
