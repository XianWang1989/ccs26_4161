Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts requires considering several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here’s a strategic approach:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**:
   - **Contribute at least enough to get the full employer match**. This is free money and should be prioritized.

### 2. **Contribute to IRA**:
   - **Consider a Roth IRA or Traditional IRA**: Depending on your income and tax situation, a Roth IRA may provide tax-free withdrawals in retirement, while a Traditional IRA offers tax-deferred growth.

### 3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - **Use taxable accounts for additional savings** once you’ve maxed out contributions to tax-advantaged accounts (like 401(k) and IRA).
   - **Invest in tax-efficient funds** to minimize capital gains taxes.

### 4. **Asset Allocation**:
   - **Risk Tolerance**: Allocate investments based on your risk tolerance. For example, stocks can be heavier in tax-advantaged accounts, while bonds might be better in taxable accounts due to interest income being taxed as ordinary income.

### 5. **Tax Considerations**:
   - **Tax Bracket**: Pay attention to your current and expected future tax bracket when considering how much to contribute to each account type.

### 6. **Withdrawal Strategy**:
   - Plan how you’ll withdraw funds in retirement to minimize tax implications.

### Example Distribution:
- **401(k)**: 15% of income (to max match)
- **IRA**: Next 10% (if eligible)
- **Taxable Accounts**: Remaining savings after maxing out the above accounts

### Regular Review:
- Annually review your contributions, adjust based on changes in income, goals, or tax laws.

By strategically leveraging the benefits of each account type, you can optimize your savings and investments for retirement. Always consider consulting with a financial advisor for personalized advice.
