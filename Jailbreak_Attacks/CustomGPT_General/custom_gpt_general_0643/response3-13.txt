Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts involves balancing tax advantages, investment goals, and liquidity needs. Here’s a structured approach:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**

- **401(k)**
  - Contribute enough to get any employer match (free money).
  - Aim to maximize contributions if you can (limit is $22,500 for 2023, or $30,000 if age 50+).

- **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**
  - Consider maxing out an IRA after your 401(k) contributions if your income allows (limit is $6,500 for 2023, or $7,500 if age 50+).
  - Choose Traditional for tax deductions or Roth for tax-free withdrawals (based on your tax situation).

### 2. **Diversify Investments**

- Within each account, diversify with a mix of:
  - **Stocks**
  - **Bonds**
  - **Real estate or REITs**
  - **Cash equivalents**

### 3. **Utilize Taxable Accounts Wisely**

- Use taxable accounts for investments that may generate capital gains, as they are taxed at a lower rate compared to ordinary income.
- Hold investments for the long-term to minimize capital gains taxes.

### 4. **Regularly Rebalance**

- Monitor and adjust your allocations periodically based on performance and changing goals. Ensure you’re not overly concentrated in any one area.

### 5. **Consider Your Time Horizon and Goals**

- Align your investments with your retirement timeline:
  - Long-term (20+ years): More growth-oriented (stocks).
  - Short to medium-term (5-20 years): Balance between growth and income (bonds, dividend stocks).

### Conclusion

Start by maximizing contributions to employer-sponsored plans and IRAs, then allocate surplus funds to taxable accounts while considering your overall financial goals and tax implications. Regular rebalancing and adjusting for life changes will keep your plan aligned with your objectives.
