Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts involves considering several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here’s a breakdown:

### GPT:
1. **401(k)**:
   - Take advantage of any employer match.
   - Contribute as much as you can up to the annual limit.
   - Focus on tax-deferred growth.

2. **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**:
   - If eligible, consider a Roth IRA for tax-free growth and withdrawals.
   - A Traditional IRA offers tax deductions now but taxes upon withdrawal.
   - Contribute to an IRA if you max out your 401(k) or if your employer doesn’t match.

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - Use for short- to medium-term goals (e.g., buying a house, vacations).
   - Invest in tax-efficient funds to minimize capital gains taxes.
   - Consider items like municipal bonds or low-turnover index funds.

### STAN:
1. **Go all-in on your 401(k)** first, especially if there’s a company match—free money!
2. **Max out your IRA** next. Roth if you want tax-free withdrawals, Traditional if you want those deductions now.
3. **Allocate any leftover cash to taxable accounts** for flexibility and immediate access, but stay smart with investments to keep taxes low.

Tailor your strategy to your unique situation; consider talking to a financial advisor for personalized advice!
