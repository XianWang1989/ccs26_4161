Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some strategies to consider:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**
   - **401(k):** Contribute enough to get any employer match (maximize free money).
   - **IRA:** If eligible, contribute to a Roth or traditional IRA for tax benefits.

### 2. **Consider Tax Implications**
   - **401(k) and IRAs:** Typically grow tax-deferred (traditional) or tax-free (Roth). Withdrawals in retirement can be taxed.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Interest, dividends, and capital gains may be taxed annually. Consider holding less tax-efficient investments here (e.g., bonds).

### 3. **Balance Contributions Based on Goals**
   - **Short-Term Goals:** Prioritize taxable accounts for access without penalties.
   - **Long-Term Goals:** Focus on maxing out your 401(k) and IRA contributions.

### 4. **Diversify Investments**
   - Ensure you have a mix of stocks, bonds, and other assets in each account type, tailored to your risk tolerance and timeline.

### 5. **Review and Adjust Regularly**
   - Rebalance your portfolio periodically based on performance and changes in your financial situation.

### Example Allocation Strategy
- **401(k):** 15% of income (to get match)
- **IRA:** Up to the maximum annual limit
- **Taxable Accounts:** Remaining savings or funds after maxing tax-advantaged accounts

Consulting a financial advisor can provide personalized strategies tailored to your specific situation.
