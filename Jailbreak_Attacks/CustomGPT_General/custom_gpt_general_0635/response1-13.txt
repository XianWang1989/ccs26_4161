Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment strategy. Here are some general guidelines:

### 1. **401(k)**
   - **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to get any available employer match first, as this is effectively free money.
   - **Consider Contribution Limits**: For 2023, the limit is $22,500 per year (or $30,000 if you're age 50 or older).

### 2. **IRA**
   - **Contribute to a Roth or Traditional IRA**: Depending on your tax situation, consider contributing to a Roth IRA if you qualify (income limits apply) for tax-free withdrawals in retirement, or to a Traditional IRA for potential tax-deductible contributions.
   - **Annual Limits**: The contribution limit is $6,500 per year (or $7,500 if you're 50 or older).

### 3. **Taxable Accounts**
   - **Emergency Fund**: Ensure sufficient liquidity by keeping an emergency fund in a savings account or a conservative investment like short-term bonds.
   - **Tax-Efficient Investments**: Use tax-efficient investment vehicles (e.g., index funds, ETFs) to minimize tax liabilities on capital gains and dividends.

### **Overall Strategy**
   - **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**: Maximize contributions to 401(k) and IRAs before putting money into taxable accounts.
   - **Diversify Investments**: Maintain a mix of stocks, bonds, and other assets suitable for your risk tolerance and time horizon across all accounts.
   - **Rebalance Regularly**: Monitor and adjust your portfolio periodically to maintain your desired asset allocation.

### **Considerations**
   - **Withdrawal Strategy**: Plan how you will withdraw funds in retirement for tax efficiency (consider the order of withdrawals).
   - **Consult a Financial Advisor**: Personal advice can help tailor a strategy to your unique financial situation.

This balanced approach helps take advantage of tax benefits while ensuring liquidity for immediate needs.
