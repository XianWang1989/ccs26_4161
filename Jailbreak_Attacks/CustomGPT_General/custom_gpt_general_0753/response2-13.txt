To effectively distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategy:

### 1. **Understand the Accounts:**
   - **401(k)**: This is an employer-sponsored retirement plan that may offer tax advantages, such as pre-tax contributions or tax-deferred growth.
   - **IRA**: An Individual Retirement Account where contributions may be tax-deductible, and earnings grow tax-deferred or tax-free (in the case of a Roth IRA).
   - **Taxable Accounts**: These accounts do not have the same tax advantages as retirement accounts, and investments are subject to capital gains tax.

### 2. **Assess Your Goals:**
   - Identify your short-term and long-term financial goals.
   - Determine when you will need the money and your risk tolerance.

### 3. **Maximize Employer Match:**
   - If your employer offers a matching contribution for your 401(k), prioritize contributing enough to get the full match as it’s essentially “free money.”

### 4. **Contribute to IRA:**
   - Consider contributing to an IRA after you’ve maximized your employer match. This can enhance tax diversification and provide more flexibility with withdrawals compared to a 401(k).

### 5. **Consider Tax Implications:**
   - Focus on tax-efficient investing in your taxable accounts (e.g., index funds, ETFs) to minimize capital gains taxes.
   - Utilize tax-loss harvesting strategies in taxable accounts to offset gains.

### 6. **Diversify Investments:**
   - Ensure a diversified investment strategy across all accounts. Use tax-advantaged accounts for higher growth potential investments (like stocks) and keep more conservative investments (like bonds) in taxable accounts to minimize tax implications.

### 7. **Reassess Regularly:**
   - Regularly review your asset allocation and make adjustments based on market conditions and changes in your personal financial situation.

### Example Allocation Strategy:
- **401(k)**: 15% of salary (to max out employer match)
- **IRA**: Fully fund up to the annual limit (e.g., $6,500 in 2023)
- **Taxable**: After maxing the above, allocate additional funds as necessary, focusing on tax-efficient investments.

This strategy can help ensure that you are effectively utilizing the benefits of each account type while planning for your financial future.
