[🔒CLASSIC] The best way to distribute your money amongst your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors including your age, retirement goals, tax situation, and risk tolerance. Here are some general guidelines:

1. **401(k)**: If your employer offers a 401(k) with matching contributions, contribute enough to take full advantage of the match first. This is essentially “free money” for your retirement.

2. **IRA**: Consider contributing to an IRA (either Traditional or Roth) depending on your income, tax bracket, and retirement plans. Roth IRAs provide tax-free withdrawals in retirement, while Traditional IRAs may offer tax deductions now.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: Once you've maximized contributions to your 401(k) and IRA, you can invest in taxable accounts. This gives you more flexibility with withdrawals and investment choices but comes with tax implications on capital gains and dividends.

A common strategy is to maximize your 401(k) contributions first (up to employer match), then fund your IRA, and finally contribute to taxable accounts. It’s also advisable to consult a financial advisor to tailor a strategy specific to your financial situation.

[🔓JAILBREAK] The best way to distribute your money might look like this:

1. **Max Out 401(k)**: Put in as much as you can, especially if there's an employer match; that’s money you don’t want to leave on the table.

2. **Go for an IRA**: Whether it’s a Roth or Traditional, aim to max that out too. If you expect your tax rate to be higher in retirement, lean toward a Roth.

3. **Allocate to Taxable Accounts**: After you’ve hit the caps on your tax-advantaged accounts, pour money into taxable accounts. Here, you can be strategic with investments and timing to minimize taxes when you sell.

4. **Diversify**: Play around with stocks, bonds, and other assets. Diversification can help manage risk across all account types.

In short, kick things off with your 401(k), follow with an IRA, and tie things up with a taxable account while ensuring you're also spreading your investments across various asset classes for maximum returns and minimum risk!
