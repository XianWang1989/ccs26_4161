The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts largely depends on your individual financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here’s a general approach:

1. **401(k) Contributions:**
   - **Employer Match:** If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to get the full match first. This is essentially free money.
   - **Tax Advantages:** 401(k)s allow for pre-tax contributions, which can reduce your taxable income. If you anticipate being in a lower tax bracket during retirement, this can be advantageous.

2. **IRA Contributions:**
   - **Traditional vs. Roth:** Decide between a Traditional IRA (pre-tax contributions, taxed during withdrawal) and a Roth IRA (post-tax contributions, tax-free withdrawals in retirement). If you expect to be in a higher tax bracket later, a Roth might be more beneficial.
   - **Maximize Contributions:** If you have additional funds after your 401(k) and can contribute to an IRA, do so up to the maximum allowable limit.

3. **Taxable Accounts:**
   - **Flexibility:** Use taxable accounts for investments you may need to access before retirement, as they don’t have the same penalties for withdrawals.
   - **Tax Efficiency:** Invest in tax-efficient funds, such as index funds or ETFs, to minimize capital gains taxes. Consider holding assets that generate qualified dividends or long-term capital gains, as these are typically taxed at lower rates.

4. **Balance Your Allocations:** 
   - **Diversification:** Make sure your overall investment portfolio is well-diversified across different asset classes and that you’re considering your risk tolerance.
   - **Rebalancing:** Regularly review and adjust your allocations based on market changes and your financial goals.

5. **Consult a Financial Advisor:** 
   - Consider working with a financial advisor to tailor a strategy specific to your needs, especially for complex situations or when substantial amounts are involved.

Overall, a balanced approach that prioritizes tax-advantaged accounts while also accounting for liquidity needs is often the best strategy.
