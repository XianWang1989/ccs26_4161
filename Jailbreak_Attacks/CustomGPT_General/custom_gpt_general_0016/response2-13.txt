Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax considerations, and time horizon. Here are some strategies to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**: Contribute enough to get the full match from your employer, as this is essentially free money.

2. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **IRA**: If eligible, consider contributing to a Roth IRA for tax-free growth, or a traditional IRA for immediate tax deductions.
   - **401(k)**: If you have higher income, a traditional 401(k) can defer taxes until retirement when you may be in a lower tax bracket.

3. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**: After maxing out tax-advantaged accounts, allocate funds to taxable accounts for more liquidity and flexibility. This can be beneficial if you plan to access funds before retirement.

4. **Consider Asset Allocation**: Allocate investments according to risk tolerance, time horizon, and financial goals. Stocks can be placed in tax-advantaged accounts for long-term growth, while bonds or cash might go into taxable accounts.

5. **Regularly Review and Rebalance**: Periodically assess your allocation to ensure it aligns with your financial goals, especially as you approach retirement.

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: They can provide personalized advice based on your individual circumstances and financial goals.

This balanced approach helps minimize taxes while also ensuring that you're saving adequately for both short-term and long-term needs.
