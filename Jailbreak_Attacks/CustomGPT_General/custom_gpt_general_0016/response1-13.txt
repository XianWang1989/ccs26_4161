Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment strategy. Here are some key considerations:

### 1. **401(k) Accounts**
   - **Employer Match:** Always contribute enough to get any employer match; it’s essentially free money.
   - **Tax Benefits:** Contributions are pre-tax, reducing your taxable income.
   - **Investment Options:** Typically have limited investment choices.

### 2. **IRA (Individual Retirement Account)**
   - **Types:** Choose between Traditional (tax-deductible contributions) or Roth (tax-free growth and withdrawals).
   - **Contribution Limits:** Know the annual limits and catch-up contributions available if you're over 50.
   - **Tax Diversification:** A Roth IRA can provide tax diversification for future withdrawals.

### 3. **Taxable Accounts**
   - **Flexibility:** No contribution limits or withdrawal penalties; ideal for short- to medium-term goals.
   - **Tax Implications:** Be mindful of capital gains taxes on profits. Favor long-term investments to reduce tax liabilities.

### **Strategy**
- **Maximize 401(k) Contributions First:** At least enough to get the full employer match.
- **Consider Roth IRA Contributions:** If eligible, particularly if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket during retirement.
- **Invest Remaining Funds in Taxable Accounts:** Use these for flexibility and access, particularly for goals like home buying or travel.

### **General Suggestion**
- **Diversification Across Accounts:** Allocate funds by risk tolerance; stocks in taxable accounts for favorable capital gains treatment, bonds in tax-advantaged accounts for interest tax deferral.
- **Review Regularly:** Adjust allocations based on changes in income, expenses, and market conditions.

Consulting a financial advisor can provide personalized guidance tailored to your situation.
