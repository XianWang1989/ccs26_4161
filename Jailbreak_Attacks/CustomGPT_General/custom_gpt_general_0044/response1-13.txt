Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts effectively depends on your individual financial goals, tax situation, and investment strategy. Here are some key considerations for each type of account:

### 1. **401(k)**
- **Employer Match**: Prioritize contributions to your 401(k) up to the employer match. This is essentially free money and provides an immediate return on your investment.
- **Tax Advantages**: Contributions are made pre-tax, which can lower your taxable income. Consider maxing out contributions if you are in a higher tax bracket.

### 2. **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**
- **Tax Benefits**: Traditional IRAs provide tax-deferred growth, while Roth IRAs offer tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement.
- **Contribution Limits**: Keep in mind the annual contribution limits. If you qualify for a Roth IRA, consider contributing to take advantage of tax-free growth.
- **Investment Flexibility**: IRAs generally offer a wider range of investment options than 401(k) plans. Use this to tailor your portfolio to your risk tolerance and goals.

### 3. **Taxable Accounts**
- **Liquidity**: Funds in taxable accounts can be accessed without penalties, making them suitable for short- to medium-term goals.
- **Tax Efficiency**: Be aware of tax implications on dividends and capital gains. Consider using tax-efficient investments, such as index funds or tax-managed funds.
- **Diversification**: Utilize taxable accounts for investments that may generate capital gains or income, which can be taxed at lower rates.

### **General Strategies**
- **Emergency Fund**: Have a sufficient emergency fund before heavily investing in these accounts.
- **Investment Allocation**: Place higher-risk investments (e.g., stocks) in tax-advantaged accounts to maximize tax efficiency.
- **Rebalance Regularly**: Monitor and adjust your investments across all accounts to maintain your desired asset allocation and risk level.

### **Conclusion**
A balanced approach often involves maximizing contributions to the 401(k) first, then leveraging an IRA, and finally allocating funds to a taxable account based on your specific needs. Consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized guidance tailored to your situation.
