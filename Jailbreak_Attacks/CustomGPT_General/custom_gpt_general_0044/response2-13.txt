The best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and time horizon. Here’s a general approach you might consider:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**
   - **401(k)**: If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to get the full match. This is essentially free money and should be your first priority.
   - **IRA**: After maximizing your employer match, consider contributing to a Traditional or Roth IRA. If you think you're in a higher tax bracket now than you will be in retirement, a Roth IRA might be more advantageous. Conversely, if you expect to be in a lower bracket, a Traditional IRA could provide tax deductions now.

### 2. **Consider Your Tax Bracket**
   - Contribute to accounts based on your current and projected future tax brackets. Use tax-advantaged accounts for investments likely to appreciate significantly or generate a lot of taxable income.

### 3. **Diversify Investment Strategies**
   - **401(k) and IRA**: Focus on growth-oriented investments (like stocks) in these accounts since they offer tax-deferral or tax-free growth potential. 
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Consider holding investments that generate qualified dividends or long-term capital gains, which are taxed at a lower rate compared to ordinary income.

### 4. **Rebalance Regularly**
   - Ensure your risk tolerance aligns with your investments. Regularly rebalance your allocations to maintain your desired asset mix.

### 5. **Emergency Fund and Short-Term Needs**
   - Keep some liquidity in taxable accounts or cash equivalents to cover emergencies or short-term expenses, as withdrawing from retirement accounts can incur penalties.

### 6. **Monitor Contribution Limits**
   - Be aware of the contribution limits for each account type (e.g., for 401(k) and IRAs). Adjust your strategy yearly as required.

### Example Allocation Strategy:
- **401(k)**: 15% of income up to match
- **IRA**: Max out contributions yearly
- **Taxable Account**: Any additional savings not needed for immediate expenses

Always consider consulting a financial advisor tailored to your specific circumstances for personalized advice!
