Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, risk tolerance, and tax situation. Here are some strategies:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First:**
   - **401(k):** Contribute enough to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money. Consider maxing out contributions if your budget allows.
   - **IRA (Roth or Traditional):** After maximizing your 401(k) match, contribute to an IRA. If you qualify for a Roth IRA, consider that for tax-free growth.

### 2. **Determine Investment Goals:**
   - **Short-term Goals:** Use taxable accounts for liquidity, keeping investments more conservative.
   - **Long-term Goals:** Focus on tax-advantaged accounts for growth, investing in stocks or funds with higher potential returns.

### 3. **Consider Tax Implications:**
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Best for investments that generate qualified dividends or long-term capital gains, as these are taxed at lower rates.
   - **Tax-Deferred Accounts (401(k) and Traditional IRA):** Ideal for bonds and other fixed-income investments, deferring taxes until withdrawal.

### 4. **Maintain a Balanced Portfolio:**
   - Diversify your investments across accounts to achieve a balanced asset allocation that reflects your risk profile.

### 5. **Rebalance Regularly:**
   - Periodically review and adjust your allocations in all accounts to stay aligned with your investment strategy.

### Example Distribution:
- **401(k):** 15-20% of your income or up to the maximum limit.
- **IRA:** Max out annual contributions (e.g., $6,500 for 2023, or $7,500 if over 50).
- **Taxable Accounts:** Use remaining funds after maxing out tax-advantaged accounts.

### Conclusion:
Tailor your distribution based on your specific financial situation, retirement timeline, and risk tolerance. Consulting a financial advisor can provide personalized insights.
