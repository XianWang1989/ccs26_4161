Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts effectively requires considering several factors, including your financial goals, tax implications, and investment horizon. Here’s a general approach:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**:
   - Contribute enough to your 401(k) to take full advantage of any employer matching contributions. This is essentially "free money."

2. **Contribute to IRA**:
   - If eligible, contribute to an IRA (traditional or Roth) to benefit from tax advantages. A Roth IRA offers tax-free growth, while a traditional IRA has tax-deductible contributions.

3. **Fill 401(k) Contribution Limits**:
   - After maximizing the match, consider contributing more to your 401(k) up to the annual limit, especially if the investment options are good.

4. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**:
   - Use taxable accounts for funds that you might need in the near future. This also allows more flexibility with withdrawals and investments.

5. **Balanced Diversification**:
   - Ensure a diversified investment strategy across all accounts. Different accounts may have different tax implications, so adjust your asset allocation accordingly.

6. **Consider Tax Implications**:
   - Be mindful of the tax impacts of withdrawing from taxable versus tax-advantaged accounts based on your current and expected future income.

7. **Regularly Review and Adjust**:
   - Periodically reassess your investments and contributions based on changing goals, market conditions, and life events.

Consult a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your specific situation.
