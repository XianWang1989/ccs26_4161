To effectively distribute your money amongst your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider these strategies:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match, as it’s essentially free money.

2. **Tax Advantages**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions reduce your taxable income. Aim to maximize contributions if you're in a higher tax bracket.
   - **IRA**: Depending on the type (Traditional or Roth), you can either enjoy tax deductions now or tax-free withdrawals later. Consider contributing to a Roth IRA if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for investments that generate lower taxes, like qualified dividends or long-term capital gains. These accounts provide flexibility for withdrawals.

3. **Asset Allocation**:
   - **401(k) and IRA**: Focus on long-term growth (stocks) since withdrawals are usually for retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Use for short-term goals or investments that might incur higher taxes.

4. **Diversification**: Ensure a diversified portfolio across all accounts to manage risks effectively.

5. **Rebalance Regularly**: Monitor and adjust your investment allocations to maintain your desired risk level.

6. **Consider Fees**: Be mindful of account fees and investment expenses, which can erode returns over time.

Consulting a financial advisor can also provide personalized strategies based on your specific financial situation and goals.
