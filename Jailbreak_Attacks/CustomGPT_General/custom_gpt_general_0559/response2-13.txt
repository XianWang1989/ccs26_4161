To effectively distribute your money across a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, you should consider the following strategies:

1. **Maximize Employer Match:** Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to get any employer match. This is essentially free money and should be a priority.

2. **Consider Tax Implications:** Contribute to a Traditional IRA or Roth IRA according to your tax situation. If you anticipate being in a higher tax bracket in retirement, a Roth IRA might be more beneficial, as withdrawals are tax-free.

3. **Diversify Investments:** Use your 401(k) and IRAs for long-term investments in stocks, bonds, or mutual funds. In taxable accounts, consider more tax-efficient investments like index funds or tax-managed funds to reduce capital gains taxes.

4. **Maintain Liquidity:** Keep an emergency fund in a separate, easily accessible account. This ensures you have cash available without having to liquidate investments.

5. **Rebalance Regularly:** Review and adjust your asset allocation periodically to ensure it aligns with your risk tolerance and financial goals.

By applying these strategies, you can effectively balance your investments across these accounts while optimizing for growth and tax efficiency.
