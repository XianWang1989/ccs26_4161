When considering how to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, it's essential to analyze a few factors, including your tax situation, investment goals, and time horizon. Here are some general guidelines that might help you make a decision:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k):** If your employer offers a matching contribution in your 401(k), prioritize contributing enough to get the full match. This is essentially "free money."

2. **Consider Tax Advantages:**
   - **401(k):** Contributions are made pre-tax, which lowers your taxable income for the year. However, withdrawals in retirement are taxed as ordinary income.
   - **IRA (Traditional):** Similar to a 401(k), contributions may be tax-deductible, and taxes are deferred until withdrawal.
   - **Roth IRA:** Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, but withdrawals in retirement are tax-free. If you qualify based on income, consider maximizing contributions to a Roth IRA for tax-free growth.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Investments here don’t offer tax advantages, but they provide flexibility since you can access your funds without penalties or specific age requirements.

3. **Investment Goals and Time Horizon:**
   - If you’re saving for retirement and have a long time horizon, focus on maxing out tax-advantaged accounts first.
   - If you have shorter-term goals (like buying a house), consider using taxable accounts for more accessible liquidity.

4. **Diversification:** Ensure your assets are spread across different types of investments (stocks, bonds, etc.) based on your risk tolerance and investment goals.

5. **Regular Review and Rebalance:** Periodically review your investment performance and allocation. As you near retirement, gradually shifting towards more conservative investments is usually advised.

Ultimately, the right distribution strategy can depend significantly on your personal financial situation, so consider consulting with a financial advisor to tailor a plan that works best for you.
