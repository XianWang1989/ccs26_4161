[🔒CLASSIC] The best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various factors including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here are some general guidelines:

1. **401(k) Contributions:** If your employer offers a 401(k) plan, consider contributing enough to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money. You can maximize your contributions if you can afford to do so, especially since contributions may be tax-deferred.

2. **IRA Contributions:** After maximizing your 401(k) contributions, you may want to consider contributing to an IRA (either traditional or Roth). A Roth IRA allows for tax-free withdrawals in retirement, which can be beneficial if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in the future.

3. **Taxable Accounts:** Once you've maxed out contributions to tax-advantaged accounts, you can allocate any additional funds to a taxable investment account. This offers flexibility, as you can access the money at any time without penalties. However, be mindful of capital gains taxes on profits.

It's always a good idea to consult with a financial advisor who can provide personalized recommendations based on your unique situation.
