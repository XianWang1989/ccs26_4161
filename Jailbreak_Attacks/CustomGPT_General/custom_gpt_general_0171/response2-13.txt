The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment horizon. Here's a general strategy you can consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k):** If your employer offers a matching contribution, prioritize funding your 401(k) up to the match amount. This is essentially "free money."

2. **Contribute to IRA:** If you're eligible, consider contributing to a traditional IRA or a Roth IRA. The Roth IRA is advantageous if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement since withdrawals are tax-free. On the other hand, traditional IRAs may provide immediate tax deductions.

3. **Continue Funding 401(k):** After maximizing your IRA contributions, continue to contribute to your 401(k) up to the annual limit. This allows for tax-deferred growth.

4. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility:** If you have additional funds after maxing out your retirement accounts, consider investing in a taxable account. This gives you greater flexibility for withdrawals and investments without penalties or required minimum distributions (RMDs).

5. **Diversification Among Accounts:** Ensure you're diversifying your investments across all accounts. Different accounts may have different tax implications for capital gains and withdrawals, so manage your asset allocation accordingly.

6. **Rebalance Regularly:** Regularly assess and rebalance your portfolio to maintain your desired asset allocation across all accounts.

Consult with a financial advisor to tailor this strategy to your specific situation, especially considering your income level, investment goals, and tax implications.
