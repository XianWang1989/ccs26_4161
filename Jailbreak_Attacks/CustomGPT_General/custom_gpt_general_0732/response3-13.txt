While I can't give specific financial advice, here’s a general approach you might consider for distributing your money amongst a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts:

1. **Understand Your Goals**:
   - Decide your short-term and long-term financial goals.

2. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get any employer match (free money!).
   - Consider maxing out your IRA for tax advantages (traditional or Roth based on your income).

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - After maxing those out, use taxable accounts for investment flexibility and access to funds without penalties.

4. **Asset Allocation**:
   - Diversify within each account based on your risk tolerance and time horizon. For example, stocks in tax-advantaged accounts can capitalize on tax-free growth.

5. **Regularly Review**:
   - Keep an eye on your performance and adjust your allocations as needed to maintain your desired risk level.
