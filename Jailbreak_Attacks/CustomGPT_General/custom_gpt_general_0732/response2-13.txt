When it comes to distributing money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, here are some general strategies:

1. **Maximize Employer Match:** Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to get any employer match. It's essentially free money!

2. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts:** If you have the option, maximize your contributions to your IRA (especially if it's a Roth IRA). These accounts often provide better tax benefits.

3. **Consider Current Tax Bracket:** If you're in a lower tax bracket, contributing to a Roth IRA might be more advantageous since withdrawals in retirement are tax-free.

4. **Balance Taxable Investments:** Use taxable accounts for investments that generate capital gains or are likely to be held long-term, like index funds or ETFs.

5. **Emergency Fund First:** Ensure you have a solid emergency fund before locking away significant amounts in retirement accounts.

6. **Rebalance Regularly:** Keep an eye on your allocations to ensure they align with your long-term goals and risk tolerance.
