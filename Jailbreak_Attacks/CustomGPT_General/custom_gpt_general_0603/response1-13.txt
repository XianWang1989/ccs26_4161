[🔒CLASSIC] The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts can depend on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment strategy. Here are some general considerations:

1. **401(k)**: Contributing enough to receive any employer match is usually a priority, as it's essentially "free money." Focus on maxing out contributions to your 401(k) if your employer has a matching program to take full advantage of that benefit.

2. **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**: After contributing to your 401(k), consider funding an IRA. A Roth IRA may be more beneficial if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement, as withdrawals are tax-free. If you go with a Traditional IRA, contributions might be tax-deductible depending on your income level and whether you have a 401(k).

3. **Taxable Accounts**: Once you've invested in your retirement accounts, use taxable accounts for additional savings. These accounts can be beneficial for shorter-term goals or when you want flexible access to your funds. Consider the tax implications of capital gains when investing in these accounts.

In summary, maximize contributions to your 401(k), then invest in an IRA before allocating funds to taxable accounts. Consult with a financial advisor for a tailored strategy based on your unique situation.
