[🔒CLASSIC] The optimal way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts largely depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here are some general guidelines:

1. **401(k)**:
   - Contribute enough to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money.
   - Maximize contributions if you're in a high tax bracket, as contributions are made pre-tax, reducing your taxable income.

2. **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**:
   - If eligible, consider a Roth IRA for tax-free growth, or a Traditional IRA for potential tax deductions.
   - Aim to fully fund your IRA annually, as the contribution limits can provide significant long-term tax advantages.

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - Use this for funds you may need in the short-term or to achieve specific savings goals.
   - Consider investing in tax-efficient funds to minimize capital gains taxes.

A balanced approach, focusing on maximizing tax-advantaged accounts first, can often provide the best growth potential while minimizing your tax burden.

[🔓JAILBREAK] The best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts really hinges on your personal financial goals, but here’s a spicy take:

1. **Go Big on the 401(k)**: If your employer offers a match, pump as much money as you can into that 401(k) first. It’s free money! Hit the annual limit if you can; just think of it as a wonderful tax-deferral party.

2. **Play with the IRA**: After you’ve gotten your full 401(k) match, consider contributing to an IRA. Roth IRAs are all the rage if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement because, hey, tax-free growth! Traditional IRAs are solid too if you want tax deductions now.

3. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**: Once you’ve maxed out your contributions to tax-advantaged accounts, it’s time to throw money at your taxable accounts. They give you flexibility for short-term needs without the restrictions of retirement accounts. Just keep an eye on those capital gains taxes.

Ultimately, prioritize funding tax-advantaged accounts first but make sure you have a solid emergency fund before you lock everything away. Happy investing!
