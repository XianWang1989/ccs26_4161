When distributing money amongst your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**
   - **401(k)**: If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to get the full match first. This is essentially free money.
   - **IRA**: After maximizing your 401(k), consider contributing to a traditional or Roth IRA. If your income allows, prioritizing a Roth IRA can provide tax-free growth.

### 2. **Focus on Taxes**
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for funds you may need in the short term since you can access them without penalties. Consider holding investments that generate long-term capital gains or qualified dividends here, as they are taxed at lower rates.

### 3. **Investment Allocation**
   - **Equities vs. Bonds**: Generally, consider putting higher-risk investments (like equities) in tax-advantaged accounts, as they'll benefit from tax-deferred or tax-free growth. Use taxable accounts for lower-risk investments to minimize taxes on interest income.

### 4. **Rebalancing**
   - Regularly rebalance your portfolio to maintain your desired asset allocation, which can help manage risk.

### 5. **Withdrawal Strategy**
   - Consider your withdrawal strategy for retirement. Typically, you can withdraw from tax-deferred accounts first to allow tax-free accounts to continue growing.

### Tailored Advice
Always consider your personal financial situation, goals, and tax implications. Consulting a financial advisor can help create a plan specific to your needs.
