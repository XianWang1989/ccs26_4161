When deciding how to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider these key factors:

1. **Contribution Limits**:
   - Max out employer matching in your 401(k) first. This is essentially free money.
   - For 2023, the contribution limits are $22,500 for 401(k)s and $6,500 for IRAs ($7,500 if you’re age 50 or older).

2. **Tax Implications**:
   - 401(k) and IRA contributions are typically tax-deferred, meaning you won't pay taxes on them until withdrawal.
   - Taxable accounts incur taxes on dividends and capital gains, so consider keeping investments in these accounts that generate less taxable income, like growth stocks.

3. **Investment Options**:
   - 401(k)s may offer fewer investment choices compared to IRAs. Assess how much control you want over your investments.

4. **Withdrawal Flexibility**:
   - IRAs (particularly Roth IRAs) offer more flexibility for withdrawals compared to 401(k)s, which often have stricter rules.

5. **Long-Term Goals**:
   - Contribute to your 401(k) until you get the match, then direct funds to an IRA for the broader investment options and tax benefits. Use taxable accounts for short-term goals or less tax-advantaged investments.

6. **Emergency Fund**:
   - Ensure you have an emergency fund before heavily investing, typically covering 3-6 months of expenses.

### Sample Strategy
1. **Max out the 401(k) match**.
2. **Contribute to an IRA** up to the limit.
3. **Return to the 401(k)** to max out contributions.
4. **Invest additional savings in taxable accounts** as needed.

### Final Thoughts
Review your financial goals, risk tolerance, and time horizon regularly to adjust your distribution strategy. Consulting a financial planner can also provide personalized advice.
