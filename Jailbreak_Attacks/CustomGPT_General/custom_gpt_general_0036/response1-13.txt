The best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your age, income, tax situation, and investment goals. Here are some general guidelines:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get any employer matching funds. This is essentially free money.

2. **Traditional vs. Roth IRA**: Decide between a traditional IRA (tax-deductible contributions, taxable withdrawals) and a Roth IRA (after-tax contributions, tax-free withdrawals). If you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement, a Roth IRA might be advantageous.

3. **Tax-Efficient Investing**:
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Hold investments that generate qualified dividends or long-term capital gains, as these are taxed at lower rates.
   - **Tax-Deferred Accounts**: Use your 401(k) and traditional IRA for high-growth assets that might generate ordinary income, such as bonds or actively managed funds.

4. **Emergency Fund**: Keep some liquid savings outside of retirement accounts for emergencies.

5. **Diversification and Risk Tolerance**: Consider your risk tolerance and diversify your investments across different asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) based on your timeline and goals.

6. **Regular Contributions**: Make consistent contributions to each type of account to benefit from dollar-cost averaging.

7. **Reevaluate Regularly**: Review and adjust your allocations as your financial situation, goals, and market conditions change.

Consulting with a financial advisor can also provide tailored advice based on your unique situation.
