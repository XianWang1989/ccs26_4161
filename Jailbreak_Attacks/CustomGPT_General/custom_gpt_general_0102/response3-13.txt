To effectively distribute your money amongst your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

### General Guidelines
1. **Tax Efficiency**: Prioritize placing tax-inefficient investments (like bonds) in tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) and IRA).
2. **Maximize Contributions**: Aim to contribute to your 401(k) up to the employer match, then fund an IRA.
3. **Diversification**: Maintain a diversified portfolio across all account types.
4. **Retirement Goals**: Align your strategy with your retirement timeline and risk tolerance.

### Suggested Strategy
1. **401(k)**
   - **Contribute at least enough to get the full employer match** (e.g., 4% if matching 100% up to that amount).
   - **Tax-deferred growth** for long-term investments.

2. **IRA**
   - **Fund a Roth IRA if eligible**, for tax-free withdrawals in retirement, especially beneficial if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket later.
   - If your income disqualifies you from a Roth, consider a traditional IRA for tax-deferred growth.

3. **Taxable Accounts**
   - Invest in a mix of **tax-efficient funds (like index funds)** and **bonds** that generate regular income.
   - Use this for short- to medium-term goals, or as a buffer for market downturns.
