Balancing your investments across a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts can optimize growth and tax efficiency. Here are some strategies:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**
   - **401(k):** Contribute enough to get any employer match (free money).
   - **IRA:** If you qualify, contribute to a traditional or Roth IRA. Choose Roth if you expect your tax rate to be higher in retirement.

### 2. **Asset Allocation by Account Type**
   - **401(k) and IRA:**
     - Hold growth-oriented assets (stocks) in these accounts to benefit from tax-deferred growth.
     - Consider keeping lower-yield, interest-generating assets (bonds) in taxable accounts, as they incur taxes annually.
   - **Taxable Accounts:**
     - Hold tax-efficient investments like index funds or ETFs, as they generate fewer taxable events.

### 3. **Regular Contributions**
   - Automate contributions to all accounts, prioritizing 401(k) up to the match, then IRA, then taxable, balancing as needed.

### 4. **Rebalance Annually**
   - Review and adjust your asset allocations yearly based on performance and changes in financial goals.

### 5. **Emergency Fund Consideration**
   - Ensure you have liquid savings outside these accounts for emergencies before investing heavily.

### Execution Steps:
1. **Assess Current Contributions:** Evaluate how much you're currently contributing to each account.
2. **Set Goals:** Define your short-term and long-term investment goals.
3. **Adjust Contributions:** Increase contributions to max out employer match, IRA, and then allocate remaining funds to taxable accounts.
4. **Invest Wisely:** Choose appropriate investments in each account type based on tax implications and your overall risk profile.
5. **Monitor and Rebalance:** Regularly check account performance and reallocate when necessary to stay aligned with your strategy. 

This approach helps to enhance tax efficiency while targeting growth.
