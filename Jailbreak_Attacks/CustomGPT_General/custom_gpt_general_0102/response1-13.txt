Distributing your money across a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various factors like your financial goals, age, and tax situation. Here’s a general guideline:

### 1. Maximize Employer Match:
   - Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match, as this is essentially "free money."

### 2. Contribute to an IRA:
   - After the employer match, consider maxing out an IRA (Traditional or Roth) depending on your income level and tax strategy.
   - Traditional IRAs offer tax deductions; Roth IRAs provide tax-free growth.

### 3. Continue 401(k) Contributions:
   - After maxing out your IRA, go back to your 401(k) and contribute more, ideally reaching the annual contribution limit.

### 4. Build a Taxable Account:
   - Use taxable accounts for short-term goals or after-tax investments. This can provide liquidity and investment flexibility.
   - Consider using tax-efficient investments like index funds or ETFs in this account.

### 5. Consider Investment Strategy:
   - **Tax-Deferred Accounts (401(k) and Traditional IRA):** Invest in higher growth options since taxes are deferred until withdrawal.
   - **Roth IRA:** Consider holding investments that may appreciate rapidly, as these gains are tax-free.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Focus on long-term gains and dividends, which are taxed at a lower rate.

### Final Considerations:
- **Age and Risk Tolerance:** Younger investors might prioritize growth in tax-deferred accounts, while those closer to retirement may shift to more conservative investments.
- **Tax Strategy:** Regularly review how withdrawals and distributions will affect your tax situation.

Always consider consulting with a financial advisor to tailor a strategy to your specific circumstances.
