Distributing your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here’s a general approach:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**: If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to get the full match first. This is essentially free money that boosts your retirement savings.

2. **Consider IRA Contributions**: Depending on your income level and eligibility, contributing to a Traditional or Roth IRA can provide tax advantages. A Roth IRA allows for tax-free withdrawals in retirement, while a Traditional IRA may offer tax deductions now.

3. **Evaluate 401(k) Contributions**: After securing the employer match, consider maximizing your 401(k) contributions, especially if you are in a higher tax bracket. Contributions reduce your taxable income for the year.

4. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**: If you have surplus funds after maximizing retirement accounts, consider investing in taxable accounts. These provide flexibility for withdrawals without penalties and may have favorable capital gains tax rates.

5. **Consider Investment Strategies**: Tailor your investments in each account type based on the time horizon and tax implications. Use tax-efficient investments in taxable accounts and consider higher-growth, tax-inefficient options in tax-deferred retirement accounts.

6. **Monitor and Rebalance**: Regularly review your allocations and make adjustments based on performance and changing financial goals.

Consulting with a financial advisor can help you align your investments with your specific circumstances and objectives.
