The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors including your financial goals, tax situation, risk tolerance, and investment horizon. Here are some broad guidelines:

1. **Maximize Employer Contributions**: If your employer offers a match on your 401(k), try to contribute enough to get the full match. This is essentially free money.

2. **Consider Tax Advantages**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are made pre-tax, reducing your taxable income. Depending on your plan, you may also have a Roth 401(k) option for after-tax contributions.
   - **IRA**: Traditional IRAs also offer potential tax-deductible contributions, while Roth IRAs provide tax-free growth and tax-free withdrawals in retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: These do not have special tax benefits but offer flexibility in terms of withdrawals.

3. **Evaluate Investment Options**: Your 401(k) might have limited investment options compared to an IRA or taxable account. Consider where you can achieve better diversification and returns.

4. **Emergency Fund**: Ensure you have sufficient liquidity for emergencies before committing large sums to your retirement accounts.

5. **Tax Planning**: Think about your current and expected future tax situation. If you expect to be in a higher tax bracket later, maximizing Roth accounts may be beneficial.

6. **Withdrawal Strategy**: Strategize how you will withdraw funds in retirement. Some people prefer to draw from taxable accounts first to allow tax-advantaged accounts to grow.

7. **Rebalance and Review**: Regularly rebalance your portfolio according to changes in your financial situation, risk tolerance, and market conditions.

A common approach is to maximize contributions to your 401(k) up to the employer match, fully fund a Roth or traditional IRA depending on your tax situation, and then allocate any remaining funds to a taxable account. 

Ultimately, it can be beneficial to consult with a financial advisor to tailor a strategy that fits your unique circumstances.
