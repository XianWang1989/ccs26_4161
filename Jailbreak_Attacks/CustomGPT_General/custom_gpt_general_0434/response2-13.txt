Distributing your money amongst a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various factors like your age, financial goals, tax situation, and risk tolerance. Here’s a general approach:

1. **401(k) Contributions:**
   - **Maximize Employer Match:** Contribute enough to get the full employer match if available; it’s essentially free money.
   - **Consider Tax Benefits:** Contributions are often made pre-tax, reducing your taxable income. 

2. **IRA Contributions:**
   - **Traditional vs. Roth IRA:** If you expect your tax rate to be lower in retirement, a Traditional IRA may be beneficial. If you anticipate a higher rate, a Roth IRA (which offers tax-free withdrawals in retirement) could be better.
   - **Annual Contribution Limit:** Make sure you don’t exceed annual contribution limits ($6,500 for those under 50, $7,500 for 50 and older in 2023).

3. **Taxable Accounts:**
   - **Flexibility:** These accounts provide liquidity, allowing you to access funds without penalties. Consider putting investments that generate qualified dividends or long-term capital gains to minimize taxes.
   - **Emergency Fund considerations:** Ensure you have a sufficient emergency fund in these accounts before heavily investing in retirement accounts.

### A Suggested Strategy:
- **Emergency Fund:** 3-6 months of expenses in a high-yield savings account.
- **401(k):** Maximize contributions to take full advantage of employer matching.
- **IRA:** Contribute to a Roth or Traditional IRA, depending on your tax situation.
- **Taxable Accounts:** Invest any remaining funds here for additional growth, ensuring focus on tax-efficient investments.

### Periodic Review:
Reassess your strategy annually or with significant life changes. It’s often wise to consult with a financial advisor to tailor your approach to your specific situation.
