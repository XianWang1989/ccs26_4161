Distributing money across your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here are some general guidelines:

### 1. **401(k)**
   - **Maximize Contributions:** If your employer offers a matching contribution, aim to contribute enough to get the full match. This is essentially "free money."
   - **Tax Advantages:** Contributions are made pre-tax, reducing your taxable income. Consider placing higher-risk investments here, as they can grow tax-deferred.

### 2. **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**
   - **Traditional IRA:** Contributions may be tax-deductible, but withdrawals in retirement are taxed. Good for those expecting to be in a lower tax bracket in retirement.
   - **Roth IRA:** Contributions are made after-tax, but withdrawals in retirement are tax-free. Ideal if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket later.
   - **Diversify Investments:** Use the IRA for diversified investments that you might hold long-term, balancing between stocks and bonds based on your risk tolerance.

### 3. **Taxable Accounts**
   - **Flexibility:** These accounts offer more flexibility in terms of withdrawals and investment choices. They can be tapped for expenses before retirement or emergencies.
   - **Tax Considerations:** Be mindful of capital gains tax. Consider holding investments for more than a year to benefit from lower long-term capital gains rates. Use tax-efficient funds or invest in tax-exempt bonds if you're concerned about taxes.

### General Strategy
- **Emergency Fund:** Ensure you have an emergency fund before heavily investing in any of these accounts.
- **Age-Based Allocation:** Younger investors might prefer aggressive growth strategies (more stocks), while older investors may shift towards conservative strategies (bonds, income-generating assets).
- **Regular Review:** Periodically re-evaluate your asset allocation, especially as you approach retirement.

### Seek Professional Advice
Consider speaking to a financial advisor to create a personalized distribution strategy based on your specific circumstances, risk tolerance, and goals.
