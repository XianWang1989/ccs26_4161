[🔒CLASSIC] The best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts largely depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and time horizon. Here are some general guidelines:

1. **401(k)**: Maximize contributions, especially if there's an employer match. This is typically pre-tax money, reducing your taxable income.

2. **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**: Consider contributing to a Roth IRA if you're eligible. This allows tax-free withdrawals in retirement. Traditional IRAs can provide immediate tax benefits.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for short-term goals or to invest in assets that may generate qualified dividends or capital gains, which may be taxed at a lower rate.

A common strategy is to prioritize contributions to retirement accounts first (401(k) and IRA), then allocate excess funds to your taxable account. Always review your investment strategy based on risk tolerance, market conditions, and retirement plans.

Consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized advice.
