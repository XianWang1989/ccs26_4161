Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts effectively depends on various factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here are some strategies to consider:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Contributions:**
   - **401(k)**: If your employer offers a match, contribute at least enough to get the full match; this is essentially free money.

### 2. **Tax Considerations:**
   - **IRA**: If you qualify for tax-deductible contributions, prioritize this account, especially if you're in a higher tax bracket.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: This can be beneficial for investments you might need access to before retirement, as it allows for more flexibility and potentially lower capital gains taxes on long-term investments.

### 3. **Investment Goals:**
   - **401(k)**: Suitable for long-term retirement savings; choose a mix of funds appropriate for your risk tolerance.
   - **IRA**: Consider using this for traditional or Roth IRA depending on your income level and future tax expectations.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for short- to medium-term goals, and choose investments that are tax-efficient (e.g., index funds).

### 4. **Diversification:**
   - Spread your investments across asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) in all accounts to manage risk.

### 5. **Withdrawal Strategy:**
   - Plan on how and when you'll withdraw from these accounts in retirement to minimize tax impact.

### Example Allocation:
- **401(k)**: 20-30% (enough to maximize match)
- **IRA**: 10-20% (depending on deductible status)
- **Taxable**: Remainder (with focus on liquidity and tax strategy)

### Final Thoughts:
- Review your plan periodically and adjust as needed based on changes in income, goals, or tax laws.
- Consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized guidance.

These strategies can help optimize your allocation and potentially enhance your overall financial health.
