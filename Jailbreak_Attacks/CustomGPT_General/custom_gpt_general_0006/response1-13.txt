Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment strategy. Here are some key considerations:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match. This is essentially "free money."
   - **IRA**: If eligible, contribute to a Roth IRA for tax-free growth or a traditional IRA for potential tax deductions.

### 2. **Consider Your Tax Bracket**
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Investments here are subject to capital gains tax when sold. Consider allocating investments that generate qualified dividends or long-term capital gains.

### 3. **Investment Strategy**
   - **401(k) and IRA**: Focus on long-term investments such as stocks or mutual funds for potential higher returns, considering their tax-advantaged growth.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Use for more liquid assets and consider bonds or investments with lower turnover to minimize taxes.

### 4. **Emergency Fund**
   - Maintain a cash reserve in a savings account for emergencies before heavily investing.

### Example Distribution Strategy:
- **Max out 401(k)** to employer match
- **Contribute to IRA** (up to the limit)
- **Invest any remaining funds in a taxable account**, focusing on investments with favorable tax treatment.

### Review Regularly
Reassess your allocation annually to adjust for changes in income, expenses, and market conditions. Consulting a financial advisor can provide tailored advice based on your specific circumstances.
