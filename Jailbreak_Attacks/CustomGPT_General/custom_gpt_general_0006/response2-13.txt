Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your age, financial goals, tax situation, and risk tolerance. Here’s a general approach:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match if available. This is essentially free money.

2. **Emergency Fund**: Ensure you have an emergency fund (3-6 months of expenses) in a savings account before heavily investing.

3. **IRA Contributions**: If eligible, consider funding a Roth IRA (for tax-free growth) or a traditional IRA (tax-deferred), especially if you're in a lower tax bracket.

4. **401(k) Contributions**: After maxing out the IRA, continue to contribute to your 401(k) up to the contribution limit.

5. **Taxable Accounts**: Invest in a taxable account for flexibility and to access funds without penalties in case of emergencies or major purchases.

6. **Asset Allocation**:
   - **Growth Investments** (like stocks) can be placed in tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) and IRA) to defer taxes on growth.
   - **Income Investments** (like bonds) could go in taxable accounts to utilize lower capital gains tax rates.

7. **Monitor and Rebalance**: Regularly review and adjust your investments based on performance, goals, and changing life circumstances.

Consulting with a financial advisor can tailor these strategies to your specific situation.
