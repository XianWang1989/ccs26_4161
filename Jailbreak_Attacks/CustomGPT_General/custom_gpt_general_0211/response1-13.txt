Determining the best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here’s a general framework to help you decide:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money. If possible, aim to max out contributions ($22,500 for 2023; $30,000 if over 50).
   - **IRA**: Consider contributing to a Roth IRA or a traditional IRA depending on your income level and tax situation. The contribution limit for IRAs is $6,500 ($7,500 if over 50) for 2023.

### 2. **Consider Your Tax Situation**
   - **Taxable Accounts**: These can be beneficial for liquidity and short-term goals but are less tax-efficient. Prefer long-term investments in these accounts to minimize capital gains taxes.

### 3. **Diversification and Asset Allocation**
   - Allocate assets according to your risk tolerance and investment timeline. Generally, hold more aggressive investments (like stocks) in tax-advantaged accounts where they can grow tax-free or tax-deferred.

### 4. **Withdrawal Strategy**
   - Plan for when and how you will withdraw funds in retirement. Typically, you'll want to withdraw from taxable accounts first to allow tax-advantaged accounts to grow longer.

### 5. **Adjust as Needed**
   - Regularly review your allocations based on changing financial goals, retirement timelines, and tax laws.

### Example Distribution:
1. **401(k)**: Max out employer match, then aim for max contributions.
2. **IRA**: Contribute annually up to the limit, focusing on Roth if eligible.
3. **Taxable Accounts**: Use for flexibility and any short-term savings needs after maximizing retirement accounts.

Consulting with a financial advisor tailored to your specific situation can also provide personalized advice.
