The best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, current tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here are some general guidelines:

### 1. **401(k) Plans**
   - **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to get the full employer match, if offered, as it’s typically free money.
   - **Consider Contribution Limits**: For 2024, the contribution limit for 401(k) plans is $23,000 for individuals under 50 and $30,500 for those 50 and older (including catch-up contributions).

### 2. **IRAs (Traditional and Roth)**
   - **Tax Considerations**: If you expect to be in a lower tax bracket in retirement, a Traditional IRA might be beneficial for its tax-deductible contributions. If you expect to be in a higher bracket or want tax-free withdrawals in retirement, consider a Roth IRA.
   - **Contribution Limits**: For 2024, the IRA contribution limit is $6,500 for individuals under 50 and $7,500 for those 50 and older.

### 3. **Taxable Accounts**
   - **Flexibility**: These accounts provide more liquidity and flexibility for short-term goals or emergencies. 
   - **Investment Strategy**: Invest in tax-efficient funds (like index funds) to minimize capital gains taxes. Consider holding bonds or other income-generating assets in tax-advantaged accounts to defer taxes.

### **General Distribution Strategy**
- **Prioritize**: Start with employer match contributions, then max out your IRA contributions, and finally allocate funds to a taxable account.
- **Consider Fees**: Look at management fees for your 401(k) and the investment options available. Sometimes, IRAs or taxable accounts may offer better investment choices.
- **Regular Review**: Adjust your contributions annually based on your financial goals and changes in income or expenses.

### Example Distribution
1. **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get the full match.
2. **IRA**: Max out contributions to either a Traditional or Roth IRA.
3. **Taxable Account**: Allocate any remaining funds here for short-term goals or additional investment.

### Conclusion
Always consider consulting with a financial advisor to tailor your strategy based on your unique situation and goals. Regularly revisiting your allocation as your financial situation changes is also essential.
