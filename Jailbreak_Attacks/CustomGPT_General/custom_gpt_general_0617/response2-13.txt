When considering the distribution of your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, it's important to take into account your financial goals, risk tolerance, and tax implications. Here are some general strategies to think about:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**: Prioritize contributions to your 401(k) and IRA, as these accounts offer tax advantages. Contributing enough to your 401(k) to get any employer match is usually a good first step, as that's essentially free money.

2. **Consider the Type of IRA**: If you're eligible, a Roth IRA can be an excellent complement to your 401(k) because distributions in retirement are tax-free. A traditional IRA provides tax deductions now but is taxed upon withdrawal.

3. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**: Once you've maxed out your tax-advantaged accounts, consider contributing to taxable accounts. These can provide more flexibility in accessing funds without penalties or age restrictions.

4. **Asset Allocation**: Diversify your investments based on your risk tolerance within each account. Consider placing higher-risk investments in tax-advantaged accounts, where you won’t face taxes on capital gains or dividends until withdrawal.

5. **Review and Rebalance**: Regularly review your portfolio and rebalance as needed to stay aligned with your investment goals, especially as they can change over time.

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: It might be beneficial to consult a financial advisor to tailor the strategy to your specific situation, especially regarding your current income, tax bracket, and retirement timeline.
