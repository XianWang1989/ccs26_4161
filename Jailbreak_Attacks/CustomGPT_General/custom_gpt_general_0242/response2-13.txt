Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts effectively depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here’s a general strategy you might consider:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match**
   - **401(k)**: If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to get the full match. This is essentially “free money.”

### 2. **Consider Tax Efficiency**
   - **Tax-Deferred Accounts (401(k) and IRA)**:
     - **Contribute to these accounts first**. They allow your investments to grow tax-free until withdrawal, which is beneficial for long-term growth.
     - Consider using a traditional IRA if you expect to be in a lower tax bracket when you retire.
     - A Roth IRA can be advantageous if you anticipate being in a higher tax bracket in retirement, as contributions are made with after-tax dollars and withdrawals (in retirement) are tax-free.
   
   - **Taxable Accounts**: 
     - Invest in taxable accounts for additional flexibility. These are useful for short- to medium-term goals (like buying a house).
     - Focus on tax-efficient investments (e.g., index funds or ETFs) that generate fewer taxable events.

### 3. **Diversification**
   - Ensure a good mix of asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) within each account type to minimize risk. Adjust your allocation based on your risk tolerance and investment horizon.

### 4. **Withdrawal Strategy**
   - Plan how and when you will withdraw funds from each account in retirement, considering tax implications to minimize your overall tax burden.

### 5. **Regular Review**
   - Periodically review and rebalance your portfolio to maintain your desired asset allocation and adjust for any life changes.

### Example Allocation Approach:
- **401(k)**: Max out to employer match first, then contribute up to the annual limit if possible.
- **IRA**: Contribute up to the annual limit after your 401(k).
- **Taxable Accounts**: Invest any additional savings beyond tax-advantaged accounts, focusing on long-term growth and tax efficiency.

### Final Tips:
- Consult with a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your unique situation.
- Stay informed about changing tax laws and retirement account rules that could impact your strategy.

This balanced approach can help maximize growth while considering tax implications effectively.
