Distributing money amongst your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts effectively depends on various factors including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeframe. Here’s a breakdown to consider:

### 1. **Understand Account Types:**
   - **401(k):** Employer-sponsored retirement plan with potential employer matching. Contributions are pre-tax, reducing your taxable income.
   - **IRA (Individual Retirement Account):** Can be traditional (tax-deferred) or Roth (post-tax contributions, tax-free withdrawals). Contribution limits are lower than 401(k).
   - **Taxable Accounts:** No contribution limits or tax advantages; however, you pay taxes on dividends and capital gains.

### 2. **Allocation Strategy:**
   - **Maximize Contributions:**
     - First, contribute enough to your 401(k) to get any employer match—this is essentially “free money.”
     - If you have room in your budget after that, contribute to an IRA, focusing on Roth if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement.
     - Consider taxable accounts for additional savings or if you need flexibility for mid-term goals.

### 3. **Tax Considerations:**
   - **Tax Timing:** Use tax-deferred accounts for high-growth investments to defer taxes on gains. For investments generating dividends, consider placing them in tax-advantaged accounts to avoid immediate tax liabilities.
   - **Withdrawal Strategy:** In retirement, plan to draw from taxable accounts first (due to lower tax implications) and leave tax-deferred accounts to grow longer.

### 4. **Investment Goals:**
   - Align your investments with your goals (e.g., retirement, home purchase) and risk tolerance.
   - Reassess and adjust your allocations as your financial situation changes or as you approach your goals.

### 5. **Regular Review:**
   - Review your asset allocation regularly to ensure you’re on track with your financial goals and make adjustments as necessary.

### Example Breakdown:
- **401(k):** Max out to employer match (e.g., 5-15% of salary).
- **IRA:** Max out limit (e.g., $6,500 annually, or $7,500 if over 50).
- **Taxable Account:** Any remaining funds, adjusted based on your liquidity needs or market conditions.

### Conclusion:
Tailor your strategy to fit personal circumstances, seeking advice from a financial advisor as needed. Regularly revisit your allocations to adapt to life changes and market dynamics.
