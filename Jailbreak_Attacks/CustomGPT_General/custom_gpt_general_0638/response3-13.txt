I'm not a financial advisor, but here’s a general approach to consider for distributing your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match**
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get the full employer match. This is essentially free money.

### 2. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**
   - **Traditional IRA/Roth IRA**: Contribute to an IRA after maximizing your 401(k) match. A Roth IRA is beneficial if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement, while a Traditional IRA offers immediate tax benefits.

### 3. **Assess Investment Goals**
   - **Long-Term Growth**: Prioritize tax-advantaged accounts for long-term growth investments (e.g., stocks).
   - **Short-Term Needs**: Use taxable accounts for funds you may need sooner, as they offer more flexibility for withdrawals.

### 4. **Consider Tax Efficiency**
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Invest in tax-efficient assets (e.g., index funds) to minimize capital gains taxes. 
   - **401(k) and IRAs**: Focus on higher-growth investments which can grow tax-deferred or tax-free.

### 5. **Review and Rebalance Regularly**
   - Monitor your asset allocation and rebalance periodically to align with your risk tolerance and financial goals.

### Example Allocation:
- **401(k)**: 15% to 20% of your income (up to the match)
- **IRA**: Max out contributions if possible ($6,500 for under 50; $7,500 for over 50 in 2023)
- **Taxable Accounts**: Any remaining savings, focusing on diversification and tax efficiency.

### Conclusion
This strategy varies based on individual financial situations, risk tolerance, and long-term goals. Consulting with a financial advisor can provide personalized advice tailored to your circumstances.
