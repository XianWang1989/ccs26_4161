Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment horizon. Here are some strategies to consider:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**
   - **Contribute enough** to get the full employer match, as this is essentially free money.

### 2. **Focus on Tax-Advantaged Accounts**
   - **Max out IRA contributions** if you're eligible, especially if you have a Roth IRA option. Contributions are tax-deductible (traditional) or tax-free at withdrawal (Roth).

### 3. **Consider Early Withdrawals**
   - If you might need access to funds before retirement, prioritize taxable accounts for easier access.

### 4. **Invest Based on Tax Efficiency**
   - Place **high-growth investments** (stocks) in tax-advantaged accounts (like a 401(k) or IRA) to defer taxes on gains.
   - Keep **income-generating assets** (bonds, REITs) in taxable accounts for potential tax benefits from lower capital gains rates.

### 5. **Rebalance Regularly**
   - **Monitor your allocations** yearly. Adjust based on performance, changing goals, and tax implications.

### 6. **Consider Your Current and Future Tax Rates**
   - If you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement, a Roth IRA can be beneficial. Conversely, traditional accounts might be better if you expect lower taxes later.

### 7. **Diversify Investments**
   - Ensure a mix of stocks, bonds, and other assets across all accounts to manage risk.

### Summary
A balanced approach is key. Start by maximizing employer contributions in your 401(k), then focus on IRAs, and use taxable accounts for additional investments. Adjust based on your financial situation and goals. Always consult with a financial advisor for personalized advice.
