To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money. Aim to max out contributions if possible.
   - **IRA**: Consider a Roth IRA for tax-free withdrawals during retirement, or a traditional IRA for immediate tax deductions.

2. **Evaluate Your Tax Situation**:
   - Higher-income years may warrant more contributions to tax-deferred accounts (like a 401(k) or traditional IRA) to reduce taxable income.
   - In lower-income years, consider contributing to a Roth IRA, as the tax burden will be lighter.

3. **Asset Allocation**:
   - Place assets with high growth potential (like stocks) in tax-advantaged accounts where they can grow without immediate taxation.
   - Keep income-generating assets (like bonds) in taxable accounts, as they may be taxed at a lower capital gains rate.

4. **Diversification**:
   - Ensure your overall portfolio is diverse across all accounts to manage risk effectively.

5. **Withdrawal Strategy**:
   - Plan for a strategic withdrawal strategy in retirement to minimize taxes, often tapping taxable accounts first, then tax-deferred, and finally tax-free accounts.

6. **Regular Review**:
   - Regularly assess your portfolio and contributions, adjusting based on changes in income, tax laws, and retirement goals.

Consulting with a financial advisor can also provide personalized guidance based on your specific situation.
