When distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies for an optimized approach:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match, if available. This is essentially free money.

2. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: If you have a high-income tax rate, prioritize contributions here for tax deferral. Focus on maxing out the annual contribution limits.
   - **IRA**: Depending on your income, traditional IRA contributions may be tax-deductible. Consider a Roth IRA if you expect your tax rate to be higher in retirement.

3. **Evaluate Diversification**:
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Invest in opportunities that may provide favorable capital gains treatment. Consider holding investments like index funds or ETFs that typically produce fewer taxable events.
   - **Asset Location**: Place higher-growth investments (like stocks) in tax-advantaged accounts to minimize taxable gains and interest-bearing investments (like bonds) in taxable accounts to take advantage of lower capital gains rates.

4. **Adjust for Withdrawals**: As you approach retirement, think about your withdrawal strategy. Withdrawals from tax-advantaged accounts may be taxed at ordinary income rates, while capital gains from taxable accounts may be taxed at a potentially lower rate.

5. **Review Regularly**: Periodically reassess your contributions based on changes in income, financial goals, and tax laws.

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: A professional can provide personalized advice based on your specific financial situation and goals.

Balancing these accounts effectively can help maximize your long-term wealth while minimizing tax implications.
