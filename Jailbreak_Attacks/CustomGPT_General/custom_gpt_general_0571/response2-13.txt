To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match:**
   - **401(k):** Contribute at least enough to get the full employer match, if offered. This is essentially free money.

### 2. **Tax-Advantaged Accounts First:**
   - **IRA:** If you're eligible, prioritize contributions to a Traditional or Roth IRA. These accounts offer tax benefits that can enhance long-term growth.
   - **401(k) Contributions:** After maximizing your IRA contribution, direct funds to your 401(k) until you reach the contribution limit.

### 3. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility:**
   - **Taxable Investments:** After maximizing contributions to tax-advantaged accounts, consider investing in taxable accounts. These provide more flexibility with withdrawals and can be helpful for mid-term financial goals.

### 4. **Asset Allocation:**
   - Ensure diversified asset allocation based on your risk tolerance and time horizon. 
   - **Individual Account Strategies:**
     - **401(k):** Consider a mix of stock and bond funds, but be mindful of fees associated with your plan.
     - **IRA:** Use tax-efficient investments as gains are tax-deferred (Traditional) or tax-free (Roth).
     - **Taxable Accounts:** Favor investments that generate qualified dividends or long-term capital gains to minimize tax implications.

### 5. **Regularly Rebalance:**
   - Review your portfolio annually to ensure your asset allocation aligns with your goals and adjust contributions accordingly.

### Conclusion:
Prioritize funding your accounts based on employer contributions, tax benefits, and your financial goals. Regularly review and adjust your investment strategies to maximize growth and tax efficiency.
