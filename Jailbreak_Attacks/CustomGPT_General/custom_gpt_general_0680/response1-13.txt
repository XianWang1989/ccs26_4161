To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match. Aim for the annual contribution limit.
   - **IRA**: After maxing your 401(k), contribute to a traditional or Roth IRA, depending on your income and tax situation.

2. **Investment Strategy**:
   - **Tax-Deferred Accounts (401(k), Traditional IRA)**: Focus on growth-oriented investments since taxes on gains are deferred.
   - **Roth IRA**: This is ideal for tax-free growth; consider holding investments you expect to appreciate significantly.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Utilize for short-term goals or investments that produce lower tax burdens (like index funds or tax-efficient funds).

3. **Withdrawal Strategy**:
   - Plan how you will withdraw from these accounts in retirement to optimize tax implications. Generally, withdraw from taxable accounts first, then tax-deferred accounts, with Roth IRAs as a last resort.

4. **Consider Costs and Fees**:
   - Be mindful of account fees, fund expenses, and any tax implications when making investment choices across accounts.

5. **Rebalance Regularly**:
   - Periodically review and adjust your asset allocation based on performance and your retirement timeline.

Consult with a financial advisor for personalized advice based on your unique financial situation.
