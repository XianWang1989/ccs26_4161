Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here’s a general strategy:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match, as it’s essentially free money.

2. **Contribute to an IRA**: If you qualify, consider fully funding a traditional or Roth IRA for tax advantages. Roth IRAs allow tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for additional savings or investments once you’ve maximized tax-advantaged accounts. They offer flexibility and no contribution limits.

4. **Consider Tax Implications**: 
   - **401(k)** and **Traditional IRA**: Contributions are pre-tax, reducing current taxable income. Withdrawals in retirement are taxed.
   - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are after-tax, but withdrawals are tax-free.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Capital gains and dividends may be taxed, so consider holding investments long-term to minimize taxes.

5. **Investment Strategy**: Diversify your investments across accounts to manage risk, focusing on growth assets in tax-advantaged accounts and more stable investments in taxable accounts.

6. **Review and Adjust**: Regularly review your allocation and adjust as needed based on changing goals, market conditions, and tax laws.

Consulting with a financial advisor can provide personalized insights tailored to your specific situation.
