Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here’s a general approach:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match. This is essentially "free money."

2. **Contribute to an IRA**: After the employer match, consider contributing to an IRA (Traditional or Roth, depending on your eligibility and tax strategy). A Roth IRA offers tax-free growth and withdrawals, while a Traditional IRA provides tax deductions now but taxes withdrawals later.

3. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**: Once you’ve maximized contributions to tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) and IRA), consider investing in taxable accounts. Taxable accounts offer more flexibility for accessing funds without penalties, making them suitable for short- to medium-term goals.

4. **Diversification**: Ensure your investments across all accounts are well-diversified. This includes a mix of stocks, bonds, and other assets based on your risk tolerance and time horizon.

5. **Tax Efficiency**: Place tax-inefficient investments (like bonds or REITs) in tax-advantaged accounts, while keeping tax-efficient investments (like index funds) in taxable accounts.

6. **Rebalance Regularly**: Periodically review and rebalance your portfolio to maintain your target asset allocation and reflect any changes in your financial situation.

Consider consulting with a financial advisor for tailored advice specific to your situation!
