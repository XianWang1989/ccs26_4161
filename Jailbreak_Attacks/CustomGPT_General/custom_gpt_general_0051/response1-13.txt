Determining the best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and time horizon. Here are some general guidelines:

### 1. **401(k) Contributions:**
   - Take full advantage of any employer match in your 401(k). This is essentially free money.
   - Aim to contribute enough to maximize match contributions, typically around 3-6% of your salary.
   - Consider your tax bracket: 401(k) contributions are pre-tax, which can lower your taxable income now.

### 2. **IRA Contributions:**
   - If you're eligible, consider contributing to a Roth IRA or a Traditional IRA. Roth offers tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement, while Traditional IRAs give you tax deferral.
   - The annual contribution limits for 2023 are $6,500 ($7,500 if you’re 50 or older).
   - If you're in a lower tax bracket currently, a Roth may be beneficial.

### 3. **Taxable Accounts:**
   - Use taxable accounts for additional savings or investments after maxing out tax-advantaged accounts.
   - Focus on tax-efficient investments (like index funds or ETFs) to minimize capital gains taxes.
   - This account may be useful for shorter-term goals since you can access it without penalties.

### 4. **General Strategy:**
   - **Prioritize Contributions**: Max out your 401(k) match first, followed by your IRA contributions. Then, invest in a taxable account.
   - **Diversify Investments**: Use tax-advantaged accounts for higher-growth investments (which can be taxed heavily when sold), while keeping more stable, lower-growth investments in taxable accounts.
   - **Rebalance Periodically**: Review and adjust your allocations based on market performance and changing goals.

### Conclusion:
Tailor your distribution strategy to your unique financial situation, and consider speaking to a financial advisor for personalized advice. This strategy balances growth potential with tax efficiency and prepares you for both short-term and long-term financial goals.
