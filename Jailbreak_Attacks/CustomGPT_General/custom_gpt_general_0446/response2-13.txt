Determining the best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts involves considering several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, investment options, and time horizon. Here are some general guidelines:

1. **Maximize Employer Matching**: If your employer offers a matching contribution for your 401(k), aim to contribute at least enough to maximize this benefit before allocating funds to other accounts. This is essentially free money.

2. **Contribute to an IRA**: If you qualify for a Roth IRA, consider funding it after maximizing your 401(k) match. Roth IRAs offer tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement, making them a powerful long-term investment vehicle.

3. **Tax Efficiency**:
   - **Tax-Deferred Accounts**: Prioritize tax-deferred contributions (like 401(k) and traditional IRA). This allows your investments to grow without immediate tax consequences.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Once you’ve maximized contributions to your tax-advantaged accounts, you can allocate funds to a taxable account. Here, consider strategies to minimize tax impact, such as investing in tax-efficient funds or holding onto investments for over a year to benefit from long-term capital gains rates.

4. **Investment Strategy**: Ensure your investment approach aligns with your goals and risk tolerance. Consider asset allocation strategies that balance high-growth investments with stable ones according to when you'll need access to the funds.

5. **Monitor and Adjust**: Regularly review your investment strategy and distribution as your financial situation, goals, and market conditions change.

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: For personalized advice tailored to your specific circumstances, it’s often beneficial to speak with a financial advisor.

By allocating your funds wisely across these accounts, you can maximize growth potential while minimizing tax burdens, paving the way for a more comfortable retirement.
