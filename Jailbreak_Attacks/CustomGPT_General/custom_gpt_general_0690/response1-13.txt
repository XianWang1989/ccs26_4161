Distributing your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts efficiently depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some key strategies:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to take full advantage of any employer match, as this is essentially free money.

2. **Fund Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**:
   - **IRA**: Consider contributing to a Traditional or Roth IRA. A Traditional IRA may reduce your taxable income, while a Roth IRA allows for tax-free withdrawals in retirement.
   - **401(k)**: After maximizing your IRA contributions, continue contributing to your 401(k) up to the annual limit.

3. **Utilize Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**: If you have additional funds after maxing out tax-advantaged accounts, use taxable accounts for investments that can provide liquidity.

4. **Asset Location Strategy**:
   - Place higher-tax investments (like bonds or high-yield assets) in tax-advantaged accounts to minimize taxes.
   - Keep tax-efficient investments (like index funds) in taxable accounts, as they generate fewer taxable events.

5. **Regularly Reassess**: Reevaluate your distribution strategy annually based on changes in income, tax laws, and financial goals.

Consulting a financial advisor can provide personalized guidance based on your individual circumstances.
