The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and time horizon. Here are some general guidelines:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: If your employer offers matching contributions, contribute enough to take full advantage of the match first. This is essentially free money.
   - **IRA**: Consider contributing to a Traditional IRA or Roth IRA, depending on your income and tax situation. A Roth IRA allows for tax-free withdrawals in retirement.

2. **Consider Your Tax Bracket**:
   - Contribute to pre-tax accounts (like a Traditional 401(k) or Traditional IRA) if you're in a higher tax bracket now and expect to be in a lower bracket during retirement.
   - Use Roth accounts if you believe your tax rate will be higher in retirement, as withdrawals from Roth accounts are tax-free.

3. **Diversify into Taxable Accounts**:
   - After maximizing contributions to tax-advantaged accounts, focus on taxable accounts. This is useful for short-term goals or liquidity needs.

4. **Rebalance Regularly**:
   - Periodically review your asset allocation across accounts to ensure it aligns with your risk tolerance and financial goals.

5. **Consult a Financial Advisor**:
   - A professional can provide personalized strategies based on your unique financial situation and help optimize your overall investment approach.

By following these guidelines, you can effectively allocate your funds to maximize growth and minimize tax liabilities over the long term.
