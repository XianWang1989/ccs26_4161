When considering the distribution of your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, it’s essential to recognize the characteristics of each:

1. **401(k):**
   - **Tax Advantages:** Contributions are typically made pretax, reducing your taxable income.
   - **Employer Match:** If available, maximize any employer match, as this is essentially free money.
   - **Contribution Limits:** Be mindful of annual contribution limits.

2. **IRA (Individual Retirement Account):**
   - **Types:** Traditional IRAs offer tax-deferred growth, while Roth IRAs allow for tax-free withdrawals in retirement.
   - **Contribution Limits:** Each type has different limits and eligibility, especially for Roth IRAs based on income.

3. **Taxable Accounts:**
   - **Flexibility:** These accounts allow for withdrawals at any time without penalty.
   - **Tax Implications:** Understand capital gains tax and the implications of investment income.

### General Strategies:
- **Emergency Fund:** Ensure you have an adequate emergency fund before investing.
- **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts:** Focus on contributing to 401(k) and IRAs first due to tax benefits.
- **Asset Allocation:** Diversify investments based on risk tolerance and timeline. Generally, stocks for growth and bonds for stability.
- **Rebalance Regularly:** Adjust your allocations periodically based on performance and changes in financial goals.
