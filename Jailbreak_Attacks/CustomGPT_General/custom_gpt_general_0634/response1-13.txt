Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here’s a general approach:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**
   - Contribute enough to receive the full employer match, as it’s essentially "free money."

### 2. **Contribute to an IRA**
   - Consider contributing to a traditional IRA or a Roth IRA, depending on your income level and tax situation.
   - Traditional IRAs can offer tax deductions, while Roth IRAs provide tax-free withdrawals in retirement.

### 3. **Further Contribute to 401(k)**
   - After maxing out the match, continue contributing to your 401(k), especially if you have high contribution limits.

### 4. **Focus on Taxable Accounts Last**
   - Use taxable accounts for investments that provide liquidity or for short-term goals.
   - Be mindful of capital gains taxes on investments sold in these accounts.

### 5. **Consider Tax Efficiency**
   - Place highly taxed investments (like bonds) in tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) or IRA).
   - Keep investments with lower tax implications (like index funds) in taxable accounts.

### 6. **Balance Based on Your Goals**
   - Adjust contributions based on your retirement timeline, emergency fund needs, and other financial goals.
   - Review your allocations annually and rebalance as needed.

### Additional Factors
- **Risk Tolerance:** Ensure your investment choices reflect your risk tolerance.
- **Investment Horizon:** Longer horizons can take on more risk; shorter ones may need more conservative options.
- **Consult a Financial Advisor:** A professional can tailor a strategy specific to your circumstances.

By considering these factors, you can craft a balanced approach to distributing your money effectively.
