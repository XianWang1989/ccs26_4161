Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, age, and risk tolerance. Here’s a general approach:

### 1. **Maximize Contributions**
   - **401(k):** Start by contributing enough to get any employer match—this is free money.
   - **IRA:** Contribute to a Traditional or Roth IRA, depending on your tax situation. Aim for the maximum contribution limit.

### 2. **Tax Considerations**
   - **Tax-Deferred Accounts (401(k) and Traditional IRA):** Contributions reduce your taxable income now, but withdrawals in retirement are taxed.
   - **Roth IRA:** Contributions are made with after-tax money, allowing tax-free withdrawals in retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Capital gains, dividends, and interest are taxable in the year they're earned.

### 3. **Investment Strategy**
   - **401(k):** Invest in diversified options, focusing on long-term growth and risk tolerance.
   - **IRA:** Consider a mix of assets based on your retirement timeline and risk tolerance.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Use for more flexible investments, focusing on tax efficiency (e.g., index funds, ETFs).

### 4. **Emergency Fund and Debt**
   - Maintain an emergency fund outside of these accounts.
   - Pay off high-interest debt before maxing out retirement contributions.

### 5. **Withdrawal Strategy**
   - Plan how you'll withdraw from each account in retirement. Consider your tax situation each year.

### 6. **Adjust Over Time**
   - Regularly review your asset allocation and adjust based on market conditions, changes in your life, and retirement goals.

### Example Allocation
- **401(k):** 50%–60%
- **IRA:** 20%–30%
- **Taxable Accounts:** 10%–20%

### Consult a Financial Advisor
Consider speaking with a financial planner to tailor a strategy based on your specific circumstances.
