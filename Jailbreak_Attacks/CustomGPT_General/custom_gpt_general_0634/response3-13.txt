Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here are some general strategies:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match, as it's essentially free money. Aim to max out contributions if possible.
   - **IRA**: Consider a Roth IRA or a Traditional IRA based on your current and expected future tax rates.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for additional savings or short-term goals after maximizing tax-advantaged accounts.

### 2. **Consider Your Tax Situation**
   - **Tax-deferred Accounts (401(k), Traditional IRA)**: Good for reducing current taxable income. Withdrawals will be taxed as income in retirement.
   - **Roth Accounts**: Contributions are taxed, but withdrawals in retirement are tax-free.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Ideal for capital gains treatment on investments held long-term, as you'll pay lower rates on qualified dividends and long-term capital gains.

### 3. **Risk Tolerance and Investment Horizon**
   - Younger investors may prioritize aggressive growth in tax-advantaged accounts, while closer-to-retirement individuals might focus on preserving capital and generating income.

### 4. **Diversification**
   - Maintain a diversified investment strategy across all accounts, adjusting asset allocation based on the specific account type's tax implications.

### Example Allocation Strategy:
1. **401(k)**: Maximize employer match, then consider max contributions.
2. **IRA**: Contribute to a Roth IRA if eligible; otherwise, fund a Traditional IRA.
3. **Taxable Account**: After maxing out the above, invest in low-cost index funds or ETFs for tax efficiency.

### 5. **Rebalance Regularly**
   - Review your allocations at least annually to ensure they align with your goals and risk tolerance.

### Conclusion
This strategy is highly customizable. Consulting with a financial advisor can provide tailored advice based on your specific situation.
