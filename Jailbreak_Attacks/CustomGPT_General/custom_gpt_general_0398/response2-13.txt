Distributing your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and time horizon. Here’s a general strategy:

1. **Maximize 401(k) Contributions**:
   - If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to get the full match first. This is essentially "free money."

2. **Contribute to an IRA**:
   - Consider maxing out a traditional or Roth IRA for additional tax advantages. A Roth IRA can be particularly beneficial if you expect your tax rate to be higher in retirement.

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - After maxing out tax-advantaged accounts, consider investing in taxable accounts. This is useful for flexible access to funds and can provide tax diversification. Focus on tax-efficient investments (like index funds or ETFs) to minimize capital gains tax.

4. **Consider Your Tax Bracket**:
   - If you're in a lower tax bracket, prioritize Roth accounts. In higher brackets, traditional accounts might yield immediate tax benefits.

5. **Asset Allocation**:
   - Diversify your investments according to your risk tolerance and time horizon. Generally, stocks are better suited for long-term accounts (like retirement accounts), while bonds might be better for stable returns in taxable accounts.

6. **Regularly Review and Rebalance**:
   - Periodically check your asset allocation to ensure it aligns with your goals, adjusting as needed.

7. **Consult a Financial Advisor**:
   - Consider consulting with a financial advisor for personalized guidance tailored to your financial situation.

By following these steps, you can create a balanced approach to your investment distribution.
