Determining the best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment time horizon. Here are some general guidelines:

### 1. **Prioritize Employer Match**:
   - If your employer offers a matching contribution for your 401(k), contribute enough to take full advantage of that match first. It's essentially free money.

### 2. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: Contribute as much as you can, ideally up to the limit ($22,500 for those under 50 in 2023, $30,000 for those 50 and older).
   - **IRA**: If you’re eligible, consider contributing to a Traditional or Roth IRA as well (limit is $6,500 per year, $7,500 if you’re over 50).

### 3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - After maximizing contributions to tax-advantaged accounts, consider investing in taxable accounts. These can provide more liquidity and flexibility, though you'll be subject to capital gains taxes on any profits.

### 4. **Investment Strategy**:
   - Consider your risk tolerance and investment philosophy. Typically, long-term investments (like stocks) are better suited for tax-advantaged accounts, while more stable assets (like bonds or cash) might go into taxable accounts.

### 5. **Tax Considerations**:
   - Funds in a 401(k) and Traditional IRA are tax-deferred; you’ll pay taxes upon withdrawal.
   - Roth IRAs allow for tax-free withdrawals, making them ideal for income in retirement.
   - Taxable accounts will incur taxes on dividends and capital gains, making them less tax-efficient for growth investments.

### 6. **Emergency Fund**:
   - Ensure you have an adequate emergency fund (usually 3-6 months of expenses) before heavily investing in these accounts.

### 7. **Review Regularly**:
   - Monitor and adjust your allocations based on changes in income, expenses, and overall financial goals.

### Conclusion
A balanced approach that takes advantage of tax benefits while considering your liquidity needs is key. Consulting with a financial advisor can also provide personalized recommendations tailored to your specific situation.
