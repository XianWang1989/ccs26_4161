To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**:
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to maximize any employer match. This is essentially free money.
   - **IRA**: If eligible, consider contributing to a traditional or Roth IRA. Roth IRAs allow for tax-free growth and withdrawals, while traditional IRAs offer tax-deferral on contributions.

2. **Assess Your Tax Situation**:
   - **Taxable Accounts**: These are subject to capital gains taxes, so consider using them for investments with lower turnover to minimize tax liabilities. Use tax-loss harvesting strategies to offset gains.

3. **Diversify Investments**:
   - Ensure that your investment choices across these accounts complement each other and align with your risk tolerance and time horizon.

4. **Consider Withdrawal Strategies**:
   - Plan for how and when you will withdraw funds from each account type, considering factors like required minimum distributions (RMDs) from your 401(k) and traditional IRA.

5. **Rebalance Regularly**:
   - Monitor your asset allocation and make adjustments based on performance and changing financial goals.

Engaging a financial advisor can also provide personalized guidance based on your unique financial situation and goals.
