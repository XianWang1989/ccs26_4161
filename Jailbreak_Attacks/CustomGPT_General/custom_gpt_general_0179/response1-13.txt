Determining the best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and time horizon. Here’s a general approach to consider:

### 1. **Understand Account Types:**
   - **401(k):** Typically offers pre-tax contributions, meaning you pay taxes when you withdraw funds. Often has employer matching, which is beneficial.
   - **IRA (Traditional and Roth):** Traditional IRAs also offer pre-tax contributions, while Roth IRAs allow for post-tax contributions with tax-free withdrawals in retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** These allow for more flexibility and can be accessed without penalties. However, you'll pay capital gains taxes on any investments sold for a profit.

### 2. **Maximize Employer Match:**
   - Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to receive the full employer match, as this is effectively free money.

### 3. **Consider Tax Implications:**
   - If you expect your income to be higher in retirement, prioritize Roth IRAs for tax-free growth. If you expect a lower income, you might favor Traditional IRAs and pre-tax contributions to your 401(k).
   - Invest interests and dividends in tax-advantaged accounts (like IRAs) to minimize taxable income from these sources in taxable accounts.

### 4. **Diversify Your Investments:**
   - Within each account type, ensure a diversified investment portfolio that aligns with your risk tolerance and time until retirement.
   - Consider bonds and lower-risk investments in taxable accounts to minimize tax impacts.

### 5. **Long-Term Strategy:**
   - Focus on long-term growth, placing high-growth investments (like stocks) primarily in tax-advantaged accounts, where they can grow tax-free or tax-deferred.

### 6. **Regularly Rebalance:**
   - Periodically review and adjust your asset allocation based on performance and changes in your financial goals.

### Example Distribution:
- **401(k):** Max out employer match + additional contributions up to IRS limits.
- **IRA:** If eligible, contribute to a Roth IRA for tax-free withdrawals or a Traditional IRA for tax-deferred growth.
- **Taxable Accounts:** Invest any surplus funds, focusing on tax-efficient investments.

### 7. **Consult a Financial Advisor:**
   - For personalized advice tailored to your specific situation, consider consulting a financial advisor who can provide guidance based on current regulations and market conditions.

Balancing contributions across these types of accounts will help optimize growth and minimize your tax burden during retirement.
