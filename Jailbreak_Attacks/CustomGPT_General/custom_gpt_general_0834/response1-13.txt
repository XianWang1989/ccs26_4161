The distribution of your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here are some general guidelines to consider:

1. **401(k) Contributions**:
   - If your employer offers a matching contribution, make sure to contribute at least enough to get the full match. This is essentially free money and a great way to boost your retirement savings.
   - 401(k)s typically have higher contribution limits compared to IRAs, so contributing beyond the match may be beneficial if you can afford it.

2. **IRA Contributions**:
   - Traditional IRAs and Roth IRAs have different tax implications. If you expect to be in a higher tax bracket during retirement, consider prioritizing a Roth IRA for tax-free growth and withdrawals.
   - If you're eligible for tax deductions, prioritizing contributions to a traditional IRA might be advantageous, especially if you're currently in a high tax bracket.

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - After maximizing contributions to your 401(k) and IRA, consider taxable accounts for additional investment flexibility. 
   - Taxable accounts can be beneficial for investing in assets with favorable long-term capital gains rates or for funding goals before retirement. They also offer more liquidity compared to retirement accounts.

4. **Consider Your Goals**:
   - If your focus is on retirement, prioritize tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) and IRA).
   - If you have short- to medium-term financial goals (like buying a home), more funds may be allocated to taxable accounts.

5. **Diversification and Asset Allocation**:
   - Ensure that your investments are diversified across different asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) and that your asset allocation aligns with your risk tolerance and investment horizon.

In general, a balanced approach that considers both immediate financial needs and long-term retirement goals will help you effectively distribute your money across these accounts. It's also prudent to review your strategy periodically and adjust based on changes in income, expenses, and tax laws. Consulting a financial advisor can provide personalized advice tailored to your specific situation.
