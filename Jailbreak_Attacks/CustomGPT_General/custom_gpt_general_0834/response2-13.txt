Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various factors including your financial goals, tax situation, and time horizon. Here’s a general approach to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match if available. This is essentially “free money.”

2. **Prioritize High-Interest Debt**: If you have any high-interest debts (like credit cards), focus on paying those off before heavily investing, as the interest can often exceed investment returns.

3. **Emergency Fund**: Ensure you have an adequate emergency fund (typically 3-6 months' worth of expenses) in a liquid, accessible account before committing significant funds to retirement accounts.

4. **401(k) Contributions**: If you have maxed out the employer match, consider putting additional funds into your 401(k). This account has tax advantages and can help lower your taxable income.

5. **IRA Contributions**: Max out an IRA next (traditional or Roth depending on your tax situation). A Roth IRA offers tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement if you meet certain criteria, making it ideal for younger investors or those expecting to be in a higher tax bracket in the future.

6. **Taxable Accounts**: After maximizing contributions to your tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) and IRA), you can direct funds to a taxable account. This is useful for short-term goals or if you’ve reached contribution limits in retirement accounts.

7. **Diversification**: Regardless of the account types, ensure your investments are diversified across asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) to manage risk.

8. **Review and Adjust**: Regularly review your investment strategy as your circumstances, goals, and market conditions change.

It’s wise to consult with a financial advisor to ensure that your specific situation and goals are taken into account when making these decisions.
