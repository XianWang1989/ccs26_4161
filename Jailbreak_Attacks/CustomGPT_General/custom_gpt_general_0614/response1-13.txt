Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts can depend on various factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and time horizon. Here’s a general approach:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a match on your 401(k), contribute at least enough to get the full match. This is essentially free money.

2. **Max Out Tax-Advantaged Accounts**: 
   - **401(k)**: Consider maxing out your 401(k) contributions (up to the annual limit) if you haven't reached this limit yet, especially if you are in the higher tax brackets.
   - **IRA**: If eligible, contribute to a Roth IRA or a Traditional IRA. A Roth IRA offers tax-free growth and withdrawals, while Traditional IRAs provide tax-deferred growth.

3. **Balance Risk and Liquidity**:
   - **Taxable Accounts**: These are useful for money you might need access to before retirement. While they don’t offer the same tax benefits, they can provide flexibility and liquidity.

4. **Asset Allocation**: Consider how to allocate your investments based on risk tolerance and time horizon. Stocks generally provide higher returns over the long term but come with higher risk.

5. **Tax Considerations**: Think about the tax implications of each account type. Taxable accounts may incur capital gains taxes on investment gains, while tax-advantaged accounts can grow tax-deferred or tax-free.

6. **Continuous Review**: Regularly review and adjust your contributions based on changes in your income, expenses, and investment performance.

Consulting with a financial advisor can provide personalized guidance tailored to your specific situation.
