Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment horizon. Here are some strategies to consider:

1. **Max Out Tax-Advantaged Accounts**: 
   - **401(k)**: If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to get the full match before putting money in other accounts. This is essentially "free money."
   - **IRA**: Consider funding a traditional or Roth IRA after maximizing your 401(k) contributions. Traditional IRAs may give you a tax deduction, while Roth IRAs provide tax-free withdrawals in retirement.

2. **Tax Considerations**:
   - Funds in a 401(k) and traditional IRA grow tax-deferred, meaning you won't pay taxes until you withdraw them, usually in retirement.
   - Roth IRAs allow for tax-free withdrawals in retirement, but you pay taxes on contributions upfront.
   - Taxable accounts are subject to capital gains taxes when you sell investments for a profit. Consider these implications when making investment choices.

3. **Investment Choices**:
   - Use tax-advantaged accounts for investments that generate high taxable income (e.g., bonds or REITs) to minimize tax impacts.
   - Consider using taxable accounts for investments with lower turnover and capital gains (like index funds or ETFs).

4. **Rebalancing and Withdrawals**:
   - As your investment grows, regularly rebalance to maintain your desired asset allocation.
   - Plan your withdrawals strategically in retirement to minimize tax burdens.

5. **Emergency Fund**:
   - Ensure you have enough liquidity outside these accounts for emergencies or unexpected expenses.

6. **Consult a Professional**: A financial advisor can provide personalized guidance based on your unique situation, including tax implications and future goals.

Regularly reviewing your strategy and adjusting as needed is key to optimizing your investment portfolio over time.
