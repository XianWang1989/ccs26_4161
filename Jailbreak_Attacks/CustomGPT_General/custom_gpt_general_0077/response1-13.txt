Distributing your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various factors, including your financial goals, risk tolerance, and tax situation. Here’s a general strategy to consider:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First:**
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money. Aim to contribute the maximum if possible, especially if you’re in a lower tax bracket.
   - **IRA**: If eligible, consider making contributions to a traditional or Roth IRA based on your income level and tax situation. Roth IRAs can be particularly beneficial for tax-free growth.

### 2. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility:**
   - Use taxable accounts for investments you might need access to before retirement or to hold investments that generate capital gains. It's often strategic to hold tax-efficient investments here, such as index funds or ETFs.

### 3. **Consider Investment Types:**
   - **Tax-Deferred Accounts (401(k) and traditional IRA)**: Best for high-growth investments, as you won’t pay taxes on the gains until withdrawal.
   - **Roth Accounts**: Ideal for investments you expect to grow significantly over time, allowing tax-free withdrawals later.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Good for short-term needs, or for more conservative investments that generate income (keep in mind the tax implications of dividends and capital gains).

### 4. **Monitor and Adjust:**
   - Regularly review your asset allocation and reallocate as needed based on market performance and changes in your personal circumstances.

### 5. **Consult a Financial Advisor:**
   - Given the complexities of taxes and investment strategies, a financial advisor can provide personalized recommendations tailored to your specific situation.

By carefully considering these elements, you can create a balanced and tax-efficient investment strategy.
