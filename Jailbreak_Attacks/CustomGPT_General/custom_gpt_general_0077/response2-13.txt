Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here are some general strategies to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match, as this is essentially "free money."

2. **Consider Tax Implications**:
   - **401(k) and IRA**: Contributions are made pre-tax (traditional) or post-tax (Roth), which can affect your current and future tax situations.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Interest and dividends are taxed annually, while capital gains are taxed when realized.

3. **Retirement Goals**:
   - If you plan to retire early or have specific targets, prioritize Roth accounts (like a Roth IRA) for tax-free withdrawals later.
   - For short-term goals, keep more liquid assets in taxable accounts.

4. **Investment Strategy**:
   - Use tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) and IRA) for investments expected to appreciate significantly, since you won't owe taxes on gains until withdrawal (or ever, in the case of Roth).
   - Consider holding more tax-efficient investments (like index funds) in taxable accounts to minimize tax impact.

5. **Emergency Fund**: Ensure you have an emergency fund separate from your retirement accounts, typically covering 3-6 months of expenses.

6. **Evaluate Contribution Limits**: Keep in mind the annual contribution limits for each account type and adjust your strategy accordingly.

7. **Diversification and Asset Allocation**: Spread your investments across different asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) while considering the risk tolerance and timelines for each account.

It’s often beneficial to consult with a financial advisor to tailor a distribution strategy to your specific situation.
