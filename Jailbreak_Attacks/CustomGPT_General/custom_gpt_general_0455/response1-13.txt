Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, time horizon, and tax situation. Here’s a general strategy:

1. **Maximize Contributions**: First, contribute enough to your 401(k) to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money.

2. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: If your employer offers a match, prioritize this. Consider maxing out contributions if possible.
   - **IRA**: After contributing to your 401(k), consider an IRA (Traditional or Roth), which can provide additional tax advantages based on your income.

3. **Consider Taxable Accounts**: 
   - Once you’ve maximized contributions to your tax-advantaged accounts, use taxable accounts for any additional investments. This is especially useful for emergency funds or short-term goals.

4. **Investment Strategy**:
   - **Tax Efficiency**: Hold stocks and other growth investments in tax-advantaged accounts to defer taxes. Utilize taxable accounts for tax-efficient investments, such as index funds or municipal bonds.
   - **Diversification**: Maintain a diversified portfolio across all accounts for better risk management.

5. **Review & Adjust**: Periodically review your allocations based on changes in income, tax laws, and investment performance.

Consulting with a financial advisor can provide tailored advice based on your specific situation.
