Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your income, tax situation, retirement goals, and investment strategy. Here are some general guidelines to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match. This is essentially free money and should be a priority.

2. **Traditional vs. Roth IRA**: If eligible, consider contributing to a Roth IRA for tax-free withdrawals in retirement. If you prefer immediate tax benefits, a Traditional IRA or 401(k) might be better. Aim to maximize contributions to whichever IRA option aligns with your strategy.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: Use taxable accounts for additional investments once you've maximized the tax-advantaged accounts. These accounts offer flexibility and can be advantageous for investments that generate lower taxable capital gains, such as index funds or municipal bonds.

4. **Asset Allocation**: Consider your investment horizon and risk tolerance. Typically, more volatile (higher-risk) investments might be better suited for tax-deferred accounts (like a 401(k) or IRA), while more stable investments could go in taxable accounts.

5. **Diversification and Rebalancing**: Maintain a diversified portfolio across all accounts to manage risk. Regularly rebalance your investments to stay aligned with your financial goals.

6. **Tax Considerations**: Keep tax implications in mind for withdrawals. 401(k) and Traditional IRA distributions are taxed as ordinary income, while qualified Roth IRA withdrawals are tax-free.

7. **Holistic Approach**: View your financial situation as a whole. Coordinate contributions and withdrawals to minimize your overall tax burden and align with your retirement timeline.

Consulting with a financial advisor can provide personalized insights and strategies based on your unique circumstances.
