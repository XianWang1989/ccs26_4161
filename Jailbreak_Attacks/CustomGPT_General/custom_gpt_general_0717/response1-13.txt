Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here’s a general strategy to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match, if available. This is essentially "free money" and can significantly boost your retirement savings.

2. **Contribute to an IRA**: Once you’ve maximized any employer match, consider contributing to an IRA (Roth or Traditional). A Roth IRA offers tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement, while a Traditional IRA provides tax deductions upfront.

3. **Continue 401(k) Contributions**: If you still have funds to invest, return to your 401(k) to continue making contributions. This is especially beneficial if you have a high-income year, as it allows you to reduce your taxable income.

4. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**: After maxing out the above options, consider putting excess funds into taxable accounts. These accounts offer flexibility and can be used for shorter-term goals or emergencies.

5. **Taxes and Asset Allocation**: Be mindful of the tax implications of each account type. For instance, it may be beneficial to hold tax-efficient investments (like index funds) in taxable accounts, while placing higher-yield investments (like bonds) in tax-advantaged accounts.

6. **Regular Review and Adjustments**: Your financial situation may change over time, so regularly review your allocation and adjust as necessary to align with your goals.

Consider consulting with a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your specific financial situation.
