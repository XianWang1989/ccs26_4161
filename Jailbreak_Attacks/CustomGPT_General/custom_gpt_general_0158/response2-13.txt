Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts effectively requires considering several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment time horizon. Here’s a general strategy to help you allocate your funds:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**:
   - **401(k)**: If your employer offers matching contributions, prioritize contributing enough to take full advantage of this match. It’s essentially free money. Aim for the contribution limit if possible.
   - **IRA**: After maximizing the 401(k) match, contribute to a traditional or Roth IRA. The choice between them depends on your current vs. expected future tax rates.

2. **Taxable Accounts**: Once you’ve maximized contributions to tax-advantaged accounts, allocate any additional funds into taxable accounts. This is particularly important for short-term savings goals, as tax-advantaged accounts usually have penalties for early withdrawal.

3. **Consider Investment Options**:
   - **401(k)**: Typically has limited investment choices. Choose low-cost index funds or target-date funds based on your time horizon.
   - **IRA**: Usually offers a broader range of investment options. Consider a mix of stocks, bonds, and perhaps real estate ETFs based on your risk tolerance.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Focus on investments that are more tax-efficient, like index funds or municipal bonds, to reduce tax liability.

4. **Monitor and Rebalance**: Regularly review your allocations to ensure they align with your risk tolerance and financial goals, rebalancing as necessary to maintain your desired asset allocation.

5. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: If you’re unsure, consider speaking with a financial advisor who can tailor advice to your specific situation.

### Example Strategy:
- **Age 30**: 15% of income to 401(k), 5% to IRA, remaining 80% to taxable accounts.
- **Age 40**: Increase 401(k) contributions as salary increases, maintain IRA contributions, and be more strategic with taxable account investments.

This approach helps you build wealth efficiently while minimizing tax implications.
