It's great that you're thinking about how to allocate your money! Here's a breakdown of how you might consider distributing your assets among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts:

### 1. **401(k)**
   - **Maximize Contributions**: Start by contributing enough to get any employer match (if available) as it’s essentially free money.
   - **Tax Advantages**: Contributions are pre-tax (traditional) or post-tax (Roth). Consider your current tax situation and retirement plans.

### 2. **IRA (Individual Retirement Account)**
   - **Supplement Retirement Savings**: If you can, max out your IRA contributions. Choose between traditional or Roth based on tax expectations.
   - **Diverse Investment Options**: IRAs often provide a wider range of investment choices than 401(k)s.

### 3. **Taxable Accounts**
   - **Liquidity and Flexibility**: Use taxable accounts for short-term goals or if you want more access to your money without penalties.
   - **Tax Efficiency**: Focus on investments like index funds or municipal bonds to minimize tax impact.

### General Tips
- **Maintain an Emergency Fund**: Before investing, ensure you have savings for unexpected expenses.
- **Diversify Investments**: Spread investments across various asset classes (stocks, bonds) to manage risk.
- **Consider Your Goals**: Align your investments with your short-term and long-term goals.

### Example Strategy
1. **401(k)**: Max out to employer match.
2. **IRA**: Contribute to a traditional or Roth IRA.
3. **Taxable Accounts**: Invest additional cash for flexibility and liquidity.

Adjust based on your unique financial situation, risk tolerance, and retirement goals! Consulting a financial advisor can also provide personalized strategies.
