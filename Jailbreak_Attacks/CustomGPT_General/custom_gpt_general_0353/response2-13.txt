To effectively distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider these strategies:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match first. This is essentially free money.

2. **IRA Contributions**: Consider contributing to a Traditional or Roth IRA. A Roth IRA is especially beneficial if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket later, while a Traditional IRA offers tax deductions now.

3. **401(k) Contributions**: After maximizing the match, aim to contribute the maximum allowable amount to your 401(k). This reduces your taxable income and can grow tax-deferred.

4. **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for short-term savings or investments that you may need to access before retirement. Consider tax-efficient investments like index funds or ETFs to minimize capital gains taxes.

5. **Rebalance Annually**: Regularly assess your investment allocations to maintain your desired risk level, especially as you approach retirement.

6. **Emergency Fund**: Ensure you have an emergency fund in place before heavily investing in any account.

Consulting a financial advisor tailored to your personal situation can also provide additional insights based on your goals and risk tolerance.
