To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

### 1. **Understand Account Types:**
   - **401(k)**: Pre-tax contributions with potential employer matching. Tax-deferred growth.
   - **IRA**: Traditional IRAs offer tax-deductible contributions; Roth IRAs provide tax-free withdrawals in retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Flexible, but gains are taxed annually.

### 2. **Prioritize Contributions:**
   - **Maximize 401(k) Contributions**: Especially if your employer matches. Take full advantage of the match before contributing to other accounts.
   - **Contribute to an IRA**: Consider whether a Traditional or Roth IRA suits your tax situation best.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for additional savings and investment goals beyond retirement.

### 3. **Diversify Investments:**
   - **401(k)**: Focus on a mix of stocks and bonds based on your risk tolerance.
   - **IRA**: Similar strategy but consider more aggressive investments if you have a longer time horizon.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Use for tax-efficient investments like index funds or tax-managed funds to minimize capital gains taxes.

### 4. **Consider Tax Implications:**
   - **Withdrawals**: Plan for taxes on withdrawals from taxable accounts, and understand tax implications of withdrawing from 401(k) and IRAs.
   - **Asset Location**: Hold more tax-efficient investments (like bonds) in tax-deferred accounts and tax-inefficient investments (like higher dividend stocks) in taxable accounts.

### 5. **Review and Adjust:**
   - Regularly assess your portfolio and adjust contributions based on changes in income, tax laws, and financial goals.

### Bonus Tip:
Seek guidance from a financial advisor to tailor strategies to your unique financial situation and goals!

By following these principles, you can create a balanced and tax-efficient investment strategy that grows your wealth over time. Stay focused on your long-term objectives, and adjust as necessary!
