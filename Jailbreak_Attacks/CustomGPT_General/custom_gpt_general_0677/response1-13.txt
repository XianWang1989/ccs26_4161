Distributing your money effectively among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment horizon. Here are some key strategies to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**:
   - If your employer offers a matching contribution on your 401(k), contribute enough to get the full match first. This is essentially free money and provides a solid foundation for your retirement savings.

2. **Tax Considerations**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are typically pre-tax, which lowers your taxable income now. However, withdrawals in retirement will be taxed as ordinary income.
   - **IRA**: Traditional IRAs are similar to 401(k)s, while Roth IRAs allow for tax-free withdrawals in retirement since contributions are made with after-tax dollars. If you anticipate being in a higher tax bracket in retirement, prioritize Roth contributions.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: These accounts are great for short to medium-term goals or if you need more flexibility. Be mindful of capital gains taxes, and consider holding investments for over a year to qualify for long-term capital gains rates.

3. **Diversify Investments**:
   - Ensure your asset allocation (stocks, bonds, cash) aligns with your risk tolerance and time horizon. Generally, you want more aggressive investments (like stocks) in tax-advantaged accounts where they can grow tax-deferred (401(k) and IRA) and allocate more conservative investments (like bonds) in taxable accounts to minimize taxes on interest income.

4. **Regular Contributions**:
   - Implement a dollar-cost averaging strategy by making regular contributions to all accounts. This reduces the impact of market volatility over time.

5. **Review and Adjust**:
   - Periodically assess your investment strategies and allocations as your goals and market conditions change. Rebalance your portfolio to maintain your desired risk level.

6. **Consider a Financial Advisor**:
   - If you’re unsure about your strategy or need personalized guidance, consulting a financial advisor can provide insights tailored to your specific situation.

By strategically allocating your investments and regularly reviewing your approach, you can optimize your savings and tax efficiency over time.
