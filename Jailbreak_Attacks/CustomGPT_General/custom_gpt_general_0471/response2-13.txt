To effectively distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**:
   - **401(k)**: Aim to contribute at least enough to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money. If you're under 50, the annual limit is $22,500 (for 2023), and $30,000 if you’re over 50.
   - **IRA**: After maximizing your 401(k) contribution, consider contributing to a Traditional or Roth IRA (annual limit is $6,500 or $7,500 if you're over 50). Choose a Roth IRA if you expect your tax rate in retirement to be higher than now.

2. **Diversify Investments**:
   - Within each account, diversify your investments across various asset classes (stocks, bonds, real estate) based on your risk tolerance and investment horizon.

3. **Use Taxable Accounts Strategically**:
   - After maxing out tax-advantaged accounts, use taxable accounts for investments that generate qualified dividends or long-term capital gains, which are taxed at a lower rate. Consider holding bonds in tax-advantaged accounts to avoid higher ordinary income tax rates.

4. **Consider Your Time Horizon**:
   - If you have long-term goals, prioritize retirement accounts for their tax benefits. For short-term goals (e.g., buying a house), you might keep those funds in a taxable account to maintain liquidity.

5. **Monitor and Adjust**:
   - Regularly review and adjust your allocations based on performance, changes in your financial situation, and life goals.

Planning your contributions based on your current tax situation and future goals can significantly enhance your overall return. Consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your specific circumstances.
