When it comes to distributing your money amongst your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, the optimal strategy often involves considering several factors such as your financial goals, tax brackets, and retirement timeline. Here’s a general guideline:

### GPT:
1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a matching contribution for your 401(k), contribute at least enough to get the full match, as this is essentially free money.

2. **Roth vs. Traditional**: Assess whether to use a Roth IRA or a Traditional IRA. Roth IRAs allow for tax-free growth and tax-free withdrawals in retirement, but contributions are made with after-tax dollars. Traditional IRAs offer tax deductions upfront.

3. **Tax Efficiency**: 
   - **Taxable Accounts**: These are suitable for short-term goals since you can access funds without penalties. Use them for investments that generate qualified dividends and long-term capital gains, which are taxed at lower rates.
   - **Tax-Advantaged Accounts**: Focus on growth-oriented investments in your 401(k) and IRAs, as growth is not taxed until withdrawal. 

4. **Diversification**: Make sure your overall portfolio is well-diversified across asset classes in all accounts to manage risk.

5. **Withdrawal Strategy**: Plan for withdrawals in retirement. Generally, you might want to withdraw from taxable accounts first to allow tax-advantaged accounts to continue growing.
