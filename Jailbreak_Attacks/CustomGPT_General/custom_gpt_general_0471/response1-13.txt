When deciding how to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following factors to optimize your investment strategy:

### 1. **Understand Each Account's Advantages**

- **401(k):**
  - **Tax Benefits:** Contributions are pre-tax, reducing your taxable income.
  - **Employer Match:** Take full advantage of any employer match, as this is essentially free money.
  - **Higher Contribution Limits:** Generally, allows for larger contributions compared to IRAs.

- **IRA (Traditional or Roth):**
  - **Traditional IRA:** Offers tax-deferred growth; contributions may be tax-deductible.
  - **Roth IRA:** Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, but qualified withdrawals are tax-free.
  - **Flexibility in Investments:** Often more investment options compared to a 401(k).

- **Taxable Accounts:**
  - **Liquidity and Flexibility:** Easy to access cash when needed. Ideal for short-term goals.
  - **Tax Considerations:** Capital gains taxes apply when you sell investments, but you can offset gains with losses.

### 2. **Investment Goals and Time Horizon**

- **Long-Term Goals:** Prioritize tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) and IRA) for retirement savings to benefit from tax deferral or tax-free growth.
- **Short-Term Goals:** Use taxable accounts for funds you may need sooner, such as for a house down payment or travel.

### 3. **Tax Considerations**

- **Tax Bracket:** Consider your current and expected future tax rate. If you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement, a Roth IRA could be more beneficial.
- **Withdrawal Strategy:** Plan how you'll withdraw funds in retirement to minimize tax impact. For instance, consider withdrawing from taxable accounts first.

### 4. **Diversification and Risk Tolerance**

- **Asset Allocation:** Determine your risk tolerance and allocate investments accordingly across all accounts.
- **Tax-Efficient Investing:** Place more tax-inefficient investments (like bonds) in tax-advantaged accounts and keep tax-efficient investments (like index funds) in taxable accounts.

### 5. **Regular Review and Rebalancing**

- **Monitor Performance:** Periodically review your accounts to ensure alignment with your goals, risk tolerance, and market changes.
- **Rebalance as Necessary:** Adjust your allocations to maintain your desired level of risk and diversification.

### Sample Distribution Strategy

- **Max out the 401(k) up to the employer match**: 15% of salary.
- **Contribute to a Roth IRA**: Up to contribution limits if eligible.
- **Invest in taxable accounts**: Use any remaining funds for flexible, short-term investments.

This approach will balance tax advantages while providing flexibility for future financial needs. Always consult with a financial advisor to tailor the strategy to your specific circumstances.
