To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Tax Considerations**: Different accounts have varying tax implications. Contributions to a 401(k) and traditional IRA are tax-deferred, meaning you won't pay taxes until withdrawal, typically during retirement. Roth IRAs, however, use after-tax dollars, and qualified withdrawals are tax-free. Taxable accounts may incur capital gains tax on profits.

2. **Employer Match**: If your 401(k) offers an employer match, contribute at least enough to receive the full match as it’s essentially free money. Prioritize maximizing this benefit before directing funds elsewhere.

3. **Contribution Limits**: Be mindful of the annual contribution limits for each account type. For 2023, the 401(k) limit is $22,500 (or $30,000 if you’re age 50 or older), and the IRA limit is $6,500 (or $7,500 if you’re age 50 or older).

4. **Investment Goals and Time Horizon**: Consider your investment goals and how long you plan to invest. 401(k) plans often have limited investment options, while IRAs and taxable accounts typically offer more flexibility. Choose investments that align with your risk tolerance and time frame.

5. **Withdrawal Strategy**: Plan your withdrawal strategy for retirement. Taxable accounts can provide more flexibility for early withdrawals, while tax-advantaged accounts have penalties for early access before age 59½.

6. **Rebalancing**: Regularly review and rebalance your portfolio to maintain your desired asset allocation across all accounts.

A common approach is to prioritize contributions as follows: 
1. Max out the employer match in your 401(k).
2. Contribute to a Roth IRA (if eligible) for tax-free growth and withdrawals.
3. Continue to contribute to your 401(k) up to the limit.
4. Use taxable accounts for additional investments, focusing on tax-efficient investment strategies.

Consulting with a financial advisor to tailor a strategy based on your financial situation, goals, and tax bracket could enhance your approach.
