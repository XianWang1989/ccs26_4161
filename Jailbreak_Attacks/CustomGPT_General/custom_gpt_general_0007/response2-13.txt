The best way to distribute your money amongst your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some general strategies to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k):** If your employer offers a matching contribution, ensure you contribute enough to your 401(k) to take full advantage of this free money first.

2. **Tax Advantages of Accounts:**
   - **401(k):** Contributions are typically made pre-tax, which lowers your taxable income. Consider maxing out contributions to benefit from this.
   - **IRA (Traditional or Roth):** Traditional IRAs may provide tax-deductible contributions, while Roth IRAs offer tax-free growth and tax-free withdrawals in retirement. Decide based on your current and expected future tax brackets.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** These provide flexibility to access funds without penalties but will be taxed on capital gains and dividends. They can be a good vehicle for savings goals outside of retirement.

3. **Diversification of Tax Strategies:** Aim for a mix of tax-deferred (401(k), Traditional IRA), tax-free (Roth IRA), and taxable accounts to optimize your tax situation both now and in retirement.

4. **Investment Timeline:** 
   - For long-term investments (e.g., retirement), prioritize tax-advantaged accounts. 
   - For short-term goals (e.g., buying a home), consider taxable accounts for greater flexibility.

5. **Withdrawal Strategy Planning:** Consider how you plan to withdraw funds in retirement. Having a mix of account types can provide tax-efficient strategies for withdrawals.

6. **Consider Your Risk Tolerance:** Ensure that your asset allocation within each account aligns with your risk tolerance and investment goals.

7. **Rebalance Regularly:** Monitor your investment allocations periodically and rebalance as necessary to maintain your desired asset allocation.

Consulting with a financial advisor can provide personalized guidance based on your unique financial situation.
