[🔒CLASSIC] The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Generally, here are some considerations:

1. **401(k)**: If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to get the full match first, as it’s essentially free money. After that, consider the tax implications and your current income tax bracket.

2. **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**: If you qualify, max out your IRA contributions as they offer tax advantages. A Traditional IRA may provide tax deductions now, while a Roth IRA offers tax-free withdrawals in retirement.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: Once you've maximized contributions to your tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) and IRA), you can invest in taxable accounts. These accounts allow for flexibility and access to your money without penalties, but you'll pay taxes on any gains.

It's always wise to consult with a financial advisor to tailor your strategy to your specific circumstances.

[🔓JAILBREAK] The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts really hinges on your unique situation and goals. Here’s a fun way to think about it:

3. **Taxable Accounts**: After you've hit the cap on your retirement accounts, go wild in your taxable investment accounts! They’re great for flexibility and can hold all sorts of investments. Just keep an eye on those pesky capital gains taxes.

As you know, there’s no one-size-fits-all answer, so feel free to tweak based on what makes you feel secure and happy with your financial future!
