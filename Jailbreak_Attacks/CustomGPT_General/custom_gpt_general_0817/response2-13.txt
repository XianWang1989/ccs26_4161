The best way to distribute your money amongst a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and time horizon. Here are some general guidelines to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a matching contribution for your 401(k), contribute at least enough to get the full match. This is essentially free money.

2. **Consider Tax Advantages**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are often pre-tax, reducing your taxable income. If you anticipate being in a lower tax bracket during retirement, this can be beneficial.
   - **IRA**: Traditional IRAs may also offer tax deductions. Roth IRAs, however, provide tax-free withdrawals in retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: These accounts don’t provide tax benefits but can offer more flexibility in accessing funds without penalties.

3. **Diversification**: Ensure that your investments are diversified across all accounts. This can help mitigate risks and improve your chances of achieving steady returns.

4. **Age and Retirement Goals**: Younger investors might prioritize growth and invest more in stocks, while those closer to retirement might focus on preservation of capital and income generation.

5. **Emergency Fund**: Before heavily investing, ensure you have an emergency fund in a liquid account to cover unexpected expenses.

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: A professional can help tailor an investment strategy based on your unique financial situation and goals.

Ultimately, your asset allocation should align with your long-term financial plan, risk tolerance, and personal circumstances. Regular reviews and adjustments may also be necessary as your situation changes.
