The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, age, and risk tolerance. Here are some general guidelines:

### 1. **Focus on Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**
   - **401(k)**: If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to get the maximum match. This is essentially free money.
   - **IRA**: Contribute to a Traditional or Roth IRA, depending on your income level and tax situation. Roth IRAs are especially beneficial for younger investors, as withdrawals during retirement are tax-free.

### 2. **Consider Your Tax Bracket**
   - If you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement, focus more on Roth accounts (like a Roth IRA or after-tax contributions to your 401(k)).
   - If you expect to be in a lower bracket, prioritize Traditional accounts (like a Traditional IRA or 401(k)) that allow tax deductions.

### 3. **Diversify Holdings**
   - Allocate different investments (stocks, bonds, mutual funds) across accounts according to your risk tolerance and investment strategy.
   - Consider holding tax-efficient investments (like index funds) in taxable accounts, as they generate fewer taxable events.

### 4. **Review Contribution Limits**
   - Maximize contributions within annual limits for 401(k)s and IRAs.
   - Keep an eye on the income thresholds that might impact your ability to contribute to a Roth IRA.

### 5. **Balance Withdrawals**
   - Consider tax implications when planning withdrawals in retirement. Take from tax-deferred accounts first to minimize tax impact, and strategically withdraw from taxable accounts to manage tax liabilities.

### 6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**
   - Tailor your strategy based on your specific circumstances, and consider periodic reviews of your asset allocation as your financial situation changes.

### Example Distribution Strategy
1. **401(k)**: Contribute to employer match, then max out IRA contributions.
2. **IRA**: Choose a Roth IRA if eligible, otherwise a Traditional IRA.
3. **Taxable Accounts**: Invest in long-term earnings products with minimal turnover.

This approach balances growth potential with tax efficiency while aligning with your retirement goals.
