Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here’s a balanced approach to consider:

1. **401(k) Contributions**:
   - **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a match, contribute at least enough to get the full benefit.
   - **Tax Advantage**: Contributions are often made pre-tax, reducing your taxable income.

2. **IRA Contributions**:
   - **Traditional vs. Roth IRA**: If you qualify for a Roth IRA, consider contributing to it for tax-free growth; otherwise, a Traditional IRA can provide immediate tax benefits.
   - **Annual Contribution Limits**: Be mindful of the contribution limits set by the IRS.

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - **Flexibility**: Use these accounts for money you may need access to soon, as they don’t have withdrawal penalties.
   - **Tax Efficiency**: Invest in tax-efficient assets like index funds, ETFs, or municipal bonds to minimize tax implications.

### General Strategy:
- **Emergency Fund**: Ensure you have 3-6 months’ worth of living expenses saved in an easily accessible account.
- **Age and Risk Tolerance**: Younger investors might prioritize 401(k) and IRA contributions, focusing on growth assets, while those nearing retirement may shift towards more conservative investments in taxable accounts.
- **Review Periodically**: Regularly assess your portfolio and adjust based on performance and changes in income or financial goals.

### Conclusion:
Consult with a financial advisor to tailor this strategy to your specific circumstances, ensuring you optimize tax advantages while aligning with your long-term financial goals.
