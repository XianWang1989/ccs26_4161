Distributing your money effectively among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts involves balancing tax efficiency, growth potential, and your financial goals. Here are some guidelines to consider:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First:**
   - **401(k):** Aim to contribute at least enough to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money. Consider maximizing contributions if you're in a high tax bracket since contributions are tax-deferred.
   - **IRA:** If eligible, contribute to a traditional or Roth IRA. Roth IRAs offer tax-free growth, which is beneficial if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement.

2. **Assess Your Tax Situation:**
   - **Traditional Accounts (401(k) and Traditional IRA):** Contributions lower your taxable income now, but withdrawals in retirement are taxed. This is advantageous if you expect to be in a lower tax bracket in retirement.
   - **Roth Accounts:** Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, but withdrawals are tax-free. This is ideal if you anticipate higher taxes in the future.

3. **Use Taxable Accounts Wisely:**
   - Invest in lower-turnover, tax-efficient funds (like index funds or ETFs) to minimize capital gains taxes.
   - Consider holding investments that generate tax-advantaged income, such as municipal bonds.

4. **Diversify Investments:**
   - Allocate your investments across different asset classes (stocks, bonds, cash) based on your risk tolerance and time horizon. Generally, younger investors can take on more risk, while those nearing retirement should emphasize stability.

5. **Regularly Review and Adjust:**
   - Periodically reassess your financial situation, market conditions, and investment performance. Rebalance as needed to maintain your target asset allocation.

6. **Consider Future Withdrawals:**
   - Think about your withdrawal strategy in retirement. A well-planned order of withdrawals can minimize taxes (e.g., withdrawing from taxable accounts first to let tax-advantaged accounts grow).

By following these guidelines, you can create a balanced, tax-efficient investment strategy tailored to your financial goals. Consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized advice based on your specific circumstances.
