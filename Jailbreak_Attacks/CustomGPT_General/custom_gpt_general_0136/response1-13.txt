Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment horizon. Here’s a general approach:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match, as it's essentially free money.
   - **IRA**: If eligible, consider a Roth IRA for tax-free growth or a Traditional IRA for current tax deductions.

### 2. **Assess Your Tax Situation**
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Invest in tax-efficient funds (like index funds), as you'll pay taxes on dividends and capital gains.

### 3. **Consider Your Time Horizon**
   - **Short-Term Goals**: Use taxable accounts for accessibility.
   - **Long-Term Goals**: Focus on maxing out tax-advantaged accounts.

### 4. **Investment Strategy**
   - Use tax-advantaged accounts for higher-growth, more volatile investments.
   - Hold more conservative investments in taxable accounts to minimize tax impact.

### 5. **Diversification**
   - Ensure a diverse portfolio across all accounts to manage risk effectively.

### 6. **Rebalance Regularly**
   - Adjust your allocations based on performance, changing goals, or life events.

### Conclusion
Start with the employer match in your 401(k), then maximize contributions to an IRA, and use taxable accounts for remaining investments. Tailor your strategy based on your unique financial situation and goals.
