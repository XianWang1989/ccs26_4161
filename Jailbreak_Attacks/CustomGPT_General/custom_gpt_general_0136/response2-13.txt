The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment time horizon. Here are some general guidelines:

1. **401(k) Contributions**:
   - **Employer Match**: Maximize contributions to receive any employer match, as this is essentially free money.
   - **Contribution Limits**: Stay within the annual contribution limits.

2. **IRA Contributions**:
   - **Tax Benefits**: Consider a traditional IRA for tax-deferred growth or a Roth IRA for tax-free withdrawals in retirement, depending on your income level and tax strategy.
   - **Diversification**: Use IRAs to hold a diverse range of investments, including stocks, bonds, and mutual funds.

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - **Flexibility**: Use taxable accounts for investments you may want to access before retirement. 
   - **Tax Efficiency**: Focus on tax-efficient investments, such as index funds or tax-managed funds, to minimize capital gains taxes.

### Suggested Distribution Strategy:
- **Emergency Fund**: Ensure you have a sufficient emergency fund separate from these accounts.
- **Prioritize**: Focus on maximizing employer match first, then consider contributing to IRAs, and allocate any remaining funds to taxable accounts.
- **Review and Adjust**: Periodically review your allocations based on your financial situation, market conditions, and retirement goals.

### Consultation:
Consider consulting with a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your specific circumstances and goals.
