Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here are some general guidelines:

### 1. **401(k)**
   - **Maximize Employer Match:** Contribute enough to get the full employer match—it’s free money.
   - **Tax Benefits:** Contributions reduce your taxable income. Prioritize if in higher tax brackets.

### 2. **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**
   - **Tax Considerations:** 
     - **Traditional IRA:** Contributions may be tax-deductible; taxes are owed on withdrawals.
     - **Roth IRA:** Contributions are made after-tax, but withdrawals are tax-free in retirement.
   - **Contribution Limits:** Ensure you contribute up to the maximum limit, especially to a Roth if you're eligible.

### 3. **Taxable Accounts**
   - **Flexibility:** Funds in taxable accounts can be accessed anytime without penalties.
   - **Investment Gains:** Be mindful of capital gains tax; consider tax-efficient investments.

### **General Strategy**
1. **Prioritize contributions** to the 401(k) up to the match, then focus on IRA contributions.
2. After maximizing tax-advantaged accounts, allocate additional funds to taxable accounts.
3. Regularly rebalance your investments based on performance and changing financial goals.

### **Considerations**
- **Risk Tolerance:** Adjust your asset allocation based on your comfort with risk.
- **Investment Horizon:** Longer time frames can tolerate more risk; shorter time horizons may need conservative investments.

### **Consult a Financial Advisor**
It's wise to discuss your specific situation with a financial advisor for tailored advice.

### Summary
1. **401(k)** (up to match) → 2. **IRA** → 3. **Taxable Accounts**. Adjust for personal goals and tax situations.
