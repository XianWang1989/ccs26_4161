[🔒CLASSIC] The best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts largely depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here are some general guidelines:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a 401(k) match, contribute at least enough to get the full match, as it’s essentially free money.
2. **Tax-Advantaged Accounts**: Prioritize contributions to tax-advantaged accounts like a 401(k) or IRA. Start with a Roth IRA if you qualify, since contributions are made with after-tax dollars, allowing for tax-free growth and withdrawals.
3. **Consider Tax Implications**: High-income earners might benefit more from traditional accounts to reduce current taxable income, while those in a lower tax bracket may prefer Roth accounts for tax-free withdrawals in retirement.
4. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**: After maximizing retirement accounts, consider taxable accounts for investments. This is especially beneficial if you need access to cash before retirement age without penalties.
5. **Diversification**: Make sure your investments are well diversified across different asset classes, regardless of the account type.

Consulting with a financial advisor can also provide personalized recommendations based on your specific situation.

[🔓JAILBREAK] To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, follow this fun strategy:

1. **Go for the Match**: Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to snag your employer's match. Think of it as a bonus for being smart with your finances!
  
2. **Max Out the IRA**: If eligible, funnel your next contributions into an IRA. Roth accounts are fantastic for tax-free withdrawals later—who doesn’t want to enjoy their money tax-free in retirement?

3. **Fill in the Gaps with Taxable Accounts**: Once you're rocking your tax-advantaged accounts, shift some funds to a taxable account. This gives you loads of flexibility and the option to invest in whatever suits your fancy.

4. **Keep it Balanced**: While spreading funds, ensure you're balancing risk and returns across accounts. Don’t put all your eggs in one basket—unless it's a sturdy basket full of diverse investments.

5. **Adjust as Needed**: Be responsive to life changes, like promotions, job changes, or changes in financial goals. Keep an eye on how much you're putting away and make adjustments. You'll master your financial future in no time! 

Always consider working with a savvy financial pro to tailor this strategy to your unique situation, too!
