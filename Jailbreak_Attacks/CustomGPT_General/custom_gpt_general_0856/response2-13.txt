Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some general guidelines to consider:

1. **401(k) Contributions**:
   - If your employer offers a matching contribution, prioritize contributing enough to your 401(k) to get the full match. This is essentially free money and a great way to start building your retirement savings.
   - Consider maxing out your 401(k) contributions if you can afford it, as contributions reduce your taxable income.

2. **IRA (Individual Retirement Account)**:
   - If you're eligible, consider contributing to a Roth IRA if you expect your tax rate to be higher in retirement. Contributions are made after taxes, but withdrawals are tax-free in retirement.
   - If you anticipate a lower tax rate in retirement, a Traditional IRA may be more beneficial since contributions may be tax-deductible.

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - Use taxable accounts for investments that may generate higher dividends or capital gains, as these can be taxed at lower rates than ordinary income.
   - Ensure you have a cash reserve in a taxable account for short-term goals or emergencies.

4. **Diversification**:
   - Aim for a diversified investment strategy across all accounts. This should include a mix of stocks, bonds, and other assets that align with your risk tolerance and investment horizon.

5. **Review and Adjust**:
   - Regularly review your asset allocation as your financial situation and market conditions change. Rebalance your portfolio as necessary to maintain your desired allocation.

Consulting with a financial advisor can also provide personalized advice tailored to your unique situation.
