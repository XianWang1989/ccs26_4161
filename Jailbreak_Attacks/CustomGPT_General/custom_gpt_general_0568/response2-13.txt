Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment strategy. Here are some considerations for each account type:

### 1. **401(k)**
   - **Advantages**: Contributions are often pre-tax, reducing your taxable income. Many employers offer matching contributions, which is essentially free money.
   - **Considerations**: Maximize contributions up to the match first. If possible, aim to contribute the maximum allowable limit ($22,500 for 2024, or $30,000 if you're over 50).

### 2. **IRA (Traditional and Roth)**
   - **Advantages of Traditional IRA**: Contributions may be tax-deductible, and the money grows tax-deferred.
   - **Advantages of Roth IRA**: Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, but withdrawals in retirement can be tax-free.
   - **Considerations**: If you anticipate being in a higher tax bracket in retirement, a Roth IRA may be more beneficial.

### 3. **Taxable Accounts**
   - **Advantages**: Flexibility to withdraw money at any time without penalties. You can also benefit from long-term capital gains tax rates, which are typically lower than ordinary income rates.
   - **Considerations**: Use these accounts for funds you may need before retirement. Focus on tax-efficient investments here, such as index funds or ETFs.

### **General Strategy**
1. **Emergency Fund**: Ensure you have 3-6 months' worth of living expenses saved.
2. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**: Prioritize contributions to get the full match.
3. **IRA Contributions**: Consider opening a Roth IRA if eligible, or contribute to a Traditional IRA if your income allows.
4. **After Retirement Savings**: Use taxable accounts for any additional savings or investments, focusing on tax efficiency.

### **Example Distribution**
- **401(k)**: 50% of your total retirement savings
- **IRA (Roth or Traditional)**: 25%
- **Taxable Accounts**: 25%

This distribution might vary based on your risk tolerance and investment objectives. Regularly review and adjust your allocation based on changes in circumstances and market conditions. Consulting with a financial advisor can also provide personalized insights.
