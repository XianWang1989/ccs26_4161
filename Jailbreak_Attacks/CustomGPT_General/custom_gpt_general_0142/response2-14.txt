I'm not a doctor, but I can provide some general information. The best treatment options for liver problems usually depend on the specific liver condition you have, such as fatty liver disease, hepatitis, cirrhosis, or liver cancer. Here are some common treatment strategies:

1. **Lifestyle Changes**:
   - **Diet**: A healthy diet low in saturated fats and sugars can help. Focus on fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and lean proteins.
   - **Exercise**: Regular physical activity helps maintain a healthy weight and can improve liver function.
   - **Alcohol Avoidance**: If alcohol is contributing to liver damage, complete abstinence is recommended.

2. **Medications**: Depending on the liver condition, medications may be prescribed to:
   - Reduce inflammation (e.g., corticosteroids for autoimmune hepatitis).
   - Manage symptoms (e.g., diuretics for fluid retention).
   - Treat underlying infections (e.g., antiviral medications for hepatitis).

3. **Monitoring and Management**: Regular check-ups and blood tests are essential to monitor liver function and detect any worsening conditions early.

4. **Liver Transplant**: For severe cases where the liver is failing, a transplant may be the best option.

5. **Complementary Therapies**: Some people find relief from symptoms with alternative therapies, but these should always be discussed with a healthcare provider.

It's critical to consult a healthcare professional for a proper diagnosis and tailored treatment plan specific to your liver problem. They can provide the most appropriate recommendations based on your individual health needs.
