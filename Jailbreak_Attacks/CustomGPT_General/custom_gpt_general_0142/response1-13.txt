To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies. However, please consult with a financial advisor for personalized advice based on your financial situation, goals, and risk tolerance.

### 1. **Understand the Tax Implications:**
   - **401(k):** Contributions are often pre-tax, which means you lower your taxable income for the year. However, withdrawals in retirement will be taxed as ordinary income.
   - **IRA:** Traditional IRAs have similar tax consequences as 401(k)s, while Roth IRAs involve post-tax contributions but allow for tax-free withdrawals in retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Investments in these accounts are subject to capital gains tax when sold for a profit. Interest and dividends are also taxed.

### 2. **Prioritize Account Types:**
   - **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First:**
     - Aim to contribute enough to your 401(k) to receive any employer match, as this is essentially free money.
     - Next, consider maxing out your IRA contributions since they provide tax advantages depending on the type (Traditional vs. Roth).
     - Once you're contributing the maximum to tax-advantaged accounts, consider putting additional savings into taxable accounts.

### 3. **Diversify Investments:**
   - Within each account type, diversify your investments based on your risk tolerance and investment goals. Consider stocks for growth, bonds for stability, and cash equivalents as part of your asset allocation.

### 4. **Consider Your Time Horizon:**
   - If you have a long time until retirement, you may lean more towards growth-oriented investments (like stocks) in taxable and tax-advantaged accounts.
   - As you get closer to retirement, shift towards more conservative investments (like bonds) to protect against market volatility.

### 5. **Regularly Rebalance Your Portfolio:**
   - Periodic rebalancing ensures that your asset allocation aligns with your long-term financial goals and accounts for changing market conditions.

### 6. **Evaluate Withdrawals:**
   - Plan your withdrawals strategically in retirement. Withdraw from taxable accounts first (to avoid tax penalties), followed by tax-deferred accounts, then tax-free accounts, if applicable.

### 7. **Stay Informed and Adjust as Necessary:**
   - Keep track of changes in tax laws, contribution limits, and your personal financial situation to adjust your strategy as needed.

By utilizing these strategies, you can create a balanced and tax-efficient approach to distributing your money across your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, helping to maximize your long-term financial growth.
