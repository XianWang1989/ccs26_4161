When considering the best distribution of your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, it’s essential to think about your overall financial strategy, investment goals, tax efficiency, and liquidity needs. Here are some guidelines to help you make informed decisions:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k):** If your employer offers a matching contribution, prioritize contributing enough to your 401(k) to take full advantage of that match. This is essentially free money and can significantly boost your retirement savings.

2. **Consider Tax Advantages:**
   - **401(k) and Traditional IRA:** Contributions to these accounts are often made pre-tax, reducing your taxable income in the year you contribute. Use these for investments you expect to grow significantly, deferring taxes until withdrawal.
   - **Roth IRA:** If you qualify, consider maxing out a Roth IRA as well. Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, but withdrawals (including earnings) are tax-free in retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Use these for investments that may generate capital gains or dividends, as you'll pay taxes on those in the year they occur. It's also suitable for non-retirement goals since it provides more flexibility.

3. **Diversify Asset Types:** Allocate assets among these accounts based on the investment type:
   - Growth stocks and high-yield investments: Best kept in tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) or IRA) to benefit from deferred taxes.
   - Bonds and income-generating assets: Bonds might perform better in taxable accounts if held in tax-efficient forms, as they're often taxed at ordinary income rates in tax-advantaged accounts.

4. **Balance Risk and Time Horizon:** Assess your risk tolerance and investment horizon. Younger investors might lean more heavily on equities for growth, while those closer to retirement might prioritize capital preservation in safer investments.

5. **Rebalance Regularly:** Monitor your allocations and rebalance as necessary to stay in line with your investment goals. Market fluctuations can change your asset allocation over time.

6. **Seek Professional Guidance:** If in doubt, consider working with a financial advisor who can tailor a distribution strategy based on your unique financial situation, tax brackets, and retirement plans.

In summary, prioritize employer matches, utilize tax advantages appropriately, and ensure a diversified investment approach. Regular review and adjustment based on your needs and market conditions are key to maintaining an effective strategy.
