Distributing your money amongst a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here are some general guidelines to consider:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**
   - **401(k)**: If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to get the full match. This is essentially "free money." Aim to maximize your contribution if your financial situation allows it.
   - **IRA**: If you're eligible, consider contributing to a traditional IRA or a Roth IRA, depending on your income and tax strategy. Roth IRAs provide tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement, which can be beneficial if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket later.

### 2. **Consider Your Tax Situation**
   - Contributions to traditional IRAs and 401(k)s may reduce your taxable income now, which could be advantageous if you're in a higher tax bracket. Conversely, Roth contributions don’t offer upfront tax benefits, so they might be preferable if you anticipate lower taxes in retirement.

### 3. **Diversify Investments Across Accounts**
   - **Tax Considerations**: Keep tax-efficient investments (like index funds or ETFs) in taxable accounts to minimize capital gains tax. Tax-inefficient investments (like bond funds) might be better suited for tax-advantaged accounts.
   - **Asset Allocation**: Ensure your overall asset allocation (stocks vs. bonds) aligns with your risk tolerance and goals across all accounts.

### 4. **Rebalance Regularly**
   - As you contribute and the market fluctuates, revisit your asset allocation to ensure it remains aligned with your objectives. Rebalancing helps maintain your desired risk level.

### 5. **Plan for Withdrawals**
   - Your withdrawal strategy can also influence how you distribute your contributions. Certain accounts may be better to tap first depending on your tax situation in retirement.

### Conclusion
Ultimately, the right distribution will depend on your unique financial circumstances. It might be wise to consult with a financial advisor to tailor a strategy that aligns with your goals and current situation.
