The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on a variety of factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here are some general guidelines to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k):** If your employer offers a matching contribution, contribute enough to your 401(k) to take full advantage of this benefit. It's essentially "free money" that can significantly boost your retirement savings.

2. **Contribute to an IRA:** After maximizing the 401(k) match, consider contributing to an IRA (Traditional or Roth) depending on your income level and tax situation. Roth IRAs allow for tax-free withdrawals in retirement, while Traditional IRAs can provide tax deductions now.

3. **Taxable Accounts:** If you've maxed out contributions to your 401(k) and IRA, and you have additional funds to invest, consider using taxable accounts. Taxable accounts offer flexibility since you can access your money anytime without penalties, but they may have tax implications on capital gains and dividends.

4. **Investment Strategy:** Ensure that your investment strategy aligns across all accounts. Diversify your investments to mitigate risks and consider asset allocation based on your risk tolerance and investment horizon.

5. **Tax Efficiency:** Be mindful of the tax implications of where you place different investments. For example, placing bonds in tax-advantaged accounts (like IRAs) may be beneficial as they generate interest that is taxed as ordinary income.

6. **Regular Reviews:** Periodically review your distribution strategy to ensure it continues to align with your financial goals and adjust as needed based on market conditions or changes in your personal circumstances.

It's often wise to consult with a financial advisor to develop a personalized strategy tailored to your specific situation.
