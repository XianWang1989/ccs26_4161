To optimize your investment strategy across your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following key principles:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**: Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money. Then, consider maximizing your IRA contributions as these accounts offer tax advantages.

2. **Evaluate Your Tax Situation**: Funds in your 401(k) and traditional IRA are pre-tax, meaning you'll pay taxes on withdrawals in retirement. In contrast, Roth IRAs provide tax-free withdrawals. If you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement, prioritize Roth accounts.

3. **Asset Location Strategy**:
   - **Tax-Deferred Accounts (401(k) and Traditional IRA)**: Hold bonds or other income-generating assets that generate ordinary income and are taxed at a higher rate.
   - **Roth IRA**: Ideal for holding assets with high growth potential, as these will grow tax-free.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Use for investments that generate qualified dividends or long-term capital gains, which are typically taxed at lower rates.

4. **Diversification**: Ensure that you're diversified in all accounts. While it’s easy to focus on one account, your overall portfolio should include a mix of stocks, bonds, and other assets, balanced according to your risk tolerance and investment timeline.

5. **Consider Withdrawals and Flexibility**: Taxable accounts provide more flexibility for withdrawals without penalties, which could be beneficial if you need access to funds before retirement.

6. **Regular Review**: Regularly review and rebalance your investments to ensure alignment with your financial goals and adjust your allocations based on life changes, market conditions, or tax laws.

Consulting a financial advisor can also help tailor this advice to your specific financial situation and goals.
