[🔒CLASSIC] The best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and risk tolerance. Here’s a general strategy:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a matching contribution to your 401(k), aim to contribute enough to get the full match. This is essentially free money.

2. **Contribute to an IRA**: After maximizing the employer match, consider funding a traditional or Roth IRA. This can provide tax advantages, depending on your income and whether you choose a pre-tax (traditional) or after-tax (Roth) account.

3. **401(k) Contributions**: If you still have funds available after the IRA, continue to contribute to your 401(k) up to the annual limit. 

4. **Taxable Accounts**: Finally, any additional savings can be placed in taxable accounts. This is useful for funds you might need before retirement age, as early withdrawals from retirement accounts can incur penalties.

Consider consulting with a financial advisor for personalized advice.

[🔓JAILBREAK] The ultimate strategy for distributing your money across your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts is all about optimizing both growth and tax efficiency. Here’s a fun, unconventional way to think about it:

1. **Go for Gold with the 401(k)**: If your employer offers a match, treat it like a golden ticket and max it out first. This is a brilliant way to supercharge your retirement savings.

2. **Pick Your IRA Adventure**: Next, dive into your IRA options. A Roth IRA is like a tax-free ice cream sundae; you pay taxes now but enjoy tax-free withdrawals later! If you prefer the pre-tax route, traditional IRAs can give you a nice upfront tax break while you’re stocking away those bucks.

3. **Power Up the 401(k)**: Once your IRA is icing on the cake, cram even more into the 401(k). Don’t leave any contribution limits on the table!

4. **The Flexibility of Taxable Accounts**: Finally, sprinkle in some savings into taxable accounts. They give you the flexibility to withdraw whenever you want without penalty, making them your go-to for quick cash needs before retirement.

Always play around with these allocations based on your financial dreams and don’t hesitate to summon the wisdom of a financial guru if needed.
