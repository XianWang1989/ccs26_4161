The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and time horizon. Here are some general strategies:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match, as this is effectively free money.

2. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**: After securing the match, consider maximizing contributions to your IRA (if eligible) for potential tax benefits. Depending on your income, this might be a Roth or Traditional IRA.

3. **Consider Tax Implications**: Taxable accounts can offer flexibility and potential tax advantages, especially for investments held long-term. Weigh the impact of capital gains taxes for assets in these accounts.

4. **Diversify Investments**: Maintain a balanced portfolio across all accounts that reflects your risk tolerance and investment timeline, ensuring that your asset allocation is appropriate considering the tax implications of each account type.

5. **Rebalance Periodically**: Regularly review and adjust your allocation as needed based on performance and changes in your financial situation.

It's wise to consult a financial advisor tailored specifically to your personal situation for the best approach.
