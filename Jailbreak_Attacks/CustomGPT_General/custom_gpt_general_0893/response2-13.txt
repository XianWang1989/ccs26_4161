The best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors including your age, income, savings goals, tax situation, and investment preferences. Here are some general considerations to help guide your distribution:

1. **Employer Match**: If your employer offers a matching contribution for your 401(k), try to contribute enough to get the full match first. This is essentially "free money" that can significantly boost your retirement savings.

2. **Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get the employer match, and then consider maxing out your contributions if you can. Contributions are pre-tax, reducing your taxable income now.
   - **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**: Depending on your income level and tax circumstances, consider contributing to an IRA as it can provide tax advantages. A traditional IRA offers tax-deductible contributions, whereas a Roth IRA provides tax-free withdrawals in retirement.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: After maximizing contributions to your 401(k) and IRA, consider using taxable accounts for additional investments. Investments in these accounts do not have the same tax advantages, but they provide flexibility with withdrawals.

4. **Diversification**: Ensure that your investments across these accounts are well diversified to manage risk. This typically involves spreading investments across stocks, bonds, and other assets.

5. **Retirement Timeline**: Consider the timeline for your retirement. If you are closer to retirement, you may want to take less risk and focus on preserving capital, which might influence how you allocate between these accounts.

6. **Tax Implications**: Understand the tax implications of each account type. Withdrawals from a 401(k) and traditional IRA are taxable, while qualified withdrawals from a Roth IRA are tax-free.

7. **Financial Goals**: Align your distributions with your overall financial goals. If you're saving for a specific goal aside from retirement, a taxable account may be more suitable for short-term needs.

8. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: For personalized advice tailored to your specific situation, consider consulting with a financial advisor or planner.

It's crucial to reevaluate your distribution strategy regularly to ensure it continues to meet your financial needs and retirement goals.
