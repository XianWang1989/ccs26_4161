Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts can depend on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here’s a brief outline of the best practices:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**
   - Contribute enough to receive any employer match. This is essentially free money.

### 2. **Contribute to IRA**
   - After the 401(k) match, consider contributing to a traditional or Roth IRA. They offer tax advantages that can be very beneficial for retirement.

### 3. **Increase 401(k) Contributions**
   - If you have additional funds, increase your contributions to your 401(k) up to the annual limit.

### 4. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**
   - Invest in taxable accounts for goals with shorter time horizons or for more liquidity. This allows for easier access to funds without penalties.

### 5. **Consider Tax Impact**
   - Understand the tax implications of each account type. For instance, long-term capital gains in taxable accounts are taxed lower than ordinary income.

### 6. **Diversify Investments**
   - Ensure your investments across all accounts are appropriately diversified based on your risk tolerance and investment timeline.

### 7. **Rebalance Regularly**
   - Periodically review and adjust your portfolio to maintain your desired asset allocation.

### Summary
Start by maximizing your 401(k) match, then leverage tax-advantaged IRAs, and finally consider taxable accounts for flexibility. Tailor your contributions based on your specific financial circumstances and goals.
