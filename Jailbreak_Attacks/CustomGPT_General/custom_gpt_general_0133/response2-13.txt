Distributing your money between a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment horizon. Here are some general guidelines:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match if available; this is essentially "free money."

2. **Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**:
   - **401(k)**: If your employer matches contributions, prioritize this account. Contributions reduce your taxable income.
   - **IRA**: If eligible, consider contributing to a Roth IRA for tax-free growth or a traditional IRA for tax-deductible contributions.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: 
   - After maximizing contributions to tax-advantaged accounts, allocate money to taxable accounts. These accounts offer flexibility and access without penalties before retirement age.

4. **Considerations**:
   - **Time Horizon**: Younger investors might lean more toward growth-oriented assets in tax-advantaged accounts.
   - **Tax Implications**: Interest and short-term gains in taxable accounts are taxed at your ordinary income rate. Consider placing bonds or high-yield investments in tax-advantaged accounts.

5. **Diversification**: Ensure a balanced portfolio across all accounts to manage risk and tailor your investments to your risk tolerance and financial goals.

Always consult with a financial advisor to tailor these strategies to your specific circumstances!
