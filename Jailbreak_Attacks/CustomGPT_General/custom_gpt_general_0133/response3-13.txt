Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here’s a strategy to consider:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match:**
   - Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match. This is essentially "free money."

### 2. **Tax-Advantaged Accounts First:**
   - After getting the employer match, prioritize contributions to a Traditional or Roth IRA:
     - **Traditional IRA:** Contributions may be tax-deductible; you pay taxes upon withdrawal.
     - **Roth IRA:** Contributions are made with after-tax dollars; withdrawals are tax-free in retirement.

### 3. **Back to the 401(k):**
   - After maxing out your IRA contributions (up to the limit), go back to your 401(k) and increase contributions if you're not maxing it out.

### 4. **Taxable Accounts:**
   - Once you’ve maxed out your retirement accounts, consider taxable accounts. This is especially useful for:
     - Short-term goals (like buying a house)
     - Flexibility in withdrawals without penalties.

### 5. **Consider Asset Allocation:**
   - Diversify your investments across asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) according to your risk tolerance.

### 6. **Continuous Review:**
   - Periodically review and adjust your allocations based on market conditions and changes in your financial situation.

### Summary:
- **Start with 401(k) match**
- **Max out IRAs first**
- **Then increase 401(k) contributions**
- **Use taxable accounts for additional savings**

Tailor your strategy based on your unique financial situation and consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized advice.
