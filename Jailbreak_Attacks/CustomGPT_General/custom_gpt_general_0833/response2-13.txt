Distributing your money effectively among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, current income, tax considerations, and investment strategy. Here’s a general approach to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k):**
   - If your employer offers a matching contribution, prioritize your 401(k) contributions up to the match limit. This is essentially free money and a great return on your investment.

2. **Consider Tax Advantages:**
   - **401(k) and Traditional IRA:** Contributions are often tax-deductible, which can lower your taxable income in the present. However, withdrawals in retirement are taxed as ordinary income.
   - **Roth IRA:** Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, but qualified withdrawals are tax-free in retirement. If you anticipate being in a higher tax bracket in the future, consider prioritizing Roth accounts.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** These accounts do not have tax advantages, but they offer more flexibility for withdrawals without penalties. They are suitable for short-term goals or if you anticipate needing access to funds before retirement.

3. **Debt and Emergency Fund:**
   - Before heavily investing, ensure you have paid off high-interest debt and established an emergency fund covering 3-6 months of living expenses.

4. **Investment Diversification:**
   - Within each account, diversify your investments (stocks, bonds, ETFs, mutual funds) according to your risk tolerance and time horizon. Younger investors may opt for more aggressive investments, while those closer to retirement might lean towards conservative options.

5. **Rebalancing:**
   - Regularly review and adjust your allocations based on performance and changes in your financial situation or goals. This ensures that your risk level stays consistent with your preferences.

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor:**
   - If you’re unsure, consider working with a financial planner who can provide personalized advice based on your individual situation.

By leveraging tax advantages, taking advantage of employer matches, and diversifying across your accounts, you can build a robust investment strategy tailored to your needs.
