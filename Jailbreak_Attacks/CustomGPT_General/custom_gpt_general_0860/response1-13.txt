The best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals and tax situation. Here are some general guidelines:

1. **Max out employer match**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match. This is essentially free money.

2. **Consider tax advantages**: Prioritize accounts with the best tax benefits. Traditional 401(k)s and IRAs provide tax-deferred growth, while Roth IRAs offer tax-free withdrawals in retirement.

3. **Diversify investments**: Allocate investments across accounts based on your risk tolerance. Consider keeping more stable investments in tax-advantaged accounts and higher-growth assets in taxable accounts.

4. **Emergency fund**: Ensure you have an appropriate emergency fund outside of these accounts before fully funding retirement accounts.

5. **Tax implications**: Withdrawals from taxable accounts can incur capital gains taxes, while 401(k) and IRA withdrawals (in retirement) can be taxed as regular income.

6. **Adjust as needed**: Regularly review and adjust your allocation based on changes in income, expenses, and retirement goals.

Ultimately, it's wise to consult with a financial advisor for personalized guidance.
