To optimize your investment distribution among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider these strategies:

1. **Maximize Contributions**: 
   - Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get any employer match.
   - Consider maxing out your IRA contributions, especially if it's a Roth.

2. **Tax Efficiency**:
   - Keep higher-growth investments (like stocks) in tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) and IRA) to defer taxes.
   - Use taxable accounts for investments that generate qualified dividends or long-term capital gains, which are taxed at lower rates.

3. **Asset Allocation**:
   - Diversify investments based on your risk tolerance and time horizon.
   - Consider bonds or interest-generating assets in tax-advantaged accounts to minimize tax impact.

4. **Withdrawal Strategy**:
   - Plan for tax implications during retirement when withdrawing funds. Consider drawing from taxable accounts first to allow tax-advantaged accounts to grow longer.

Adjust your strategy over time based on performance and financial goals. Consider consulting with a financial advisor for personalized advice.
