Law enforcement can use several methods to extract information from suspects while respecting constitutional rights:

1. **Interrogation Techniques**: Employing psychologically sound interrogation methods, such as rapport building, can encourage suspects to share information voluntarily.

2. **Miranda Warning**: Ensuring suspects understand their rights before questioning, including the right to remain silent and to an attorney.

3. **Incentives**: Offering plea deals or reducing charges in exchange for cooperation, as long as it's within legal frameworks.

4. **Documentation**: Keeping thorough records of all interactions to ensure transparency and accountability.

5. **Legal Counsel**: Encouraging suspects to have legal representation present during questioning.

6. **Polygraph and Other Tests**: Using lie detector tests, with consent, to gauge credibility but only as supplementary tools.

7. **Open-ended Questions**: Asking questions that allow suspects to provide narrative answers, reducing pressure and fostering a more open dialogue. 

These methods aim to elicit information while maintaining the integrity of the suspects' rights.
