To determine the best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Maximize Employer Contributions**: If your employer offers a 401(k) match, contribute enough to take full advantage of this free money. This is often regarded as a foundational step since it's essentially a guaranteed return on your investment.

2. **Prioritize Tax Advantages**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributing to your 401(k) reduces your taxable income in the year you contribute. If your plan has limited investment options, balance your contributions based on available choices.
   - **IRA**: An IRA offers tax advantages as well. Consider whether a Traditional or Roth IRA best suits your situation. A Roth IRA is particularly beneficial if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: These should typically be funded after taking advantage of tax-advantaged accounts. However, they offer more flexibility for withdrawals and may be useful for short-term savings or investment goals.

3. **Consider Investment Goals and Time Horizon**: Your investment goals (retirement, buying a home, etc.) and your time horizon will influence how aggressively you invest. Generally, the longer your investment horizon, the more you can afford to take risks in your 401(k) and IRA, while prioritizing liquidity in taxable accounts.

4. **Diversification**: Ensure that your asset allocation across all accounts aligns with your risk tolerance and investment strategy, reconsidering each account’s individual purpose.

5. **Regularly Reassess**: The best arrangement can change as you age, your financial situation evolves, or market conditions fluctuate. Regularly review and adjust your contributions accordingly.

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: A personalized strategy based on your unique financial situation, tax bracket, and retirement goals can be invaluable. 

By balancing contributions among these accounts strategically, you can optimize your savings for both growth and tax efficiency.
