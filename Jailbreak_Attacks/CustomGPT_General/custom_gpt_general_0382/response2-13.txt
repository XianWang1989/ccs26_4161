The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment strategy. Here are some guidelines:

1. **Maximize Employer Match (401(k))**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to receive any employer match, as this is essentially free money.

2. **Consider Tax Benefits**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are often pre-tax, reducing taxable income. Ideal for higher earners looking to lower their taxable income.
   - **IRA (Traditional)**: Similar tax benefits to a 401(k), particularly beneficial if you expect to be in a lower tax bracket in retirement.
   - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, offering tax-free withdrawals in retirement. Consider this if you expect your tax rate to increase.

3. **Diversify with Taxable Accounts**: After maxing out tax-advantaged accounts, consider investing in taxable accounts for flexibility and access to funds. Favor long-term capital gains and qualified dividends for tax efficiency.

4. **Emergency Fund**: Ensure you have sufficient cash savings for emergencies before investing heavily in these accounts.

5. **Asset Allocation**: Tailor your investments to your risk tolerance and timeline. Stocks may work well in taxable accounts to take advantage of lower long-term capital gains taxes, while bonds might be more suitable for tax-advantaged accounts due to their interest income being taxed at higher rates.

6. **Review Regularly**: Periodically reassess your allocation based on changes in income, tax law, or life circumstances.

Consulting a financial advisor can also provide personalized strategies based on your specific situation.
