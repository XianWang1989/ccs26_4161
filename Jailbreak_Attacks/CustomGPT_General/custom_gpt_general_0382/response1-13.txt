When deciding how to allocate your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider these factors:

1. **401(k) Contributions**:
   - **Employer Match**: Contribute enough to get the full employer match if available, as this is essentially free money.
   - **Tax Advantages**: Contributions are made pre-tax (traditional) or after-tax (Roth), which can reduce your taxable income.

2. **IRA Contributions**:
   - **Tax Benefits**: Traditional IRAs provide tax-deferred growth, while Roth IRAs offer tax-free withdrawals in retirement.
   - **Contribution Limits**: Be mindful of annual contribution limits and eligibility based on income levels.

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - **Flexibility**: These accounts allow for more access to funds without penalties, making them useful for short-term goals.
   - **Capital Gains Tax**: Investments are subject to capital gains taxes when sold for a profit, but you can manage this strategically (e.g., tax-loss harvesting).

### Suggested Approach:
1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Start by contributing to your 401(k) up to the match.
2. **IRA Contributions**: Consider maxing out your IRA contributions next, depending on your tax situation and retirement goals.
3. **Additional 401(k) Contributions**: After the IRA, contribute more to your 401(k) if you have further investment capacity.
4. **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for investments beyond tax-advantaged accounts, especially for short- to medium-term goals.

### General Tips:
- **Assess Risk Tolerance**: Diversify your investments based on your risk tolerance and time horizon.
- **Rebalance**: Regularly review and rebalance your portfolio to align with your retirement goals.
- **Consult a Financial Advisor**: Consider professional advice to tailor your strategy to your specific financial situation and goals. 

This balanced approach can help you make the most of your retirement savings while providing flexibility for other financial goals.
