[🔒CLASSIC] The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, risk tolerance, and the investment options available in each account. However, here are some general guidelines:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a matching contribution for your 401(k), contribute enough to get the full match. This is essentially "free money."

2. **Contribute to IRA**: If you're eligible, consider contributing to a traditional or Roth IRA after maximizing your 401(k) match. The tax advantages of IRAs can be significant.

3. **Continue Funding 401(k)**: After contributing to an IRA, continue to maximize your contributions to your 401(k) up to the annual limit if your financial situation allows.

4. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**: Use taxable accounts for additional investments if you have extra funds after maxing out your tax-advantaged accounts. These accounts can offer more flexibility in terms of withdrawals and investment choices.

5. **Diversification**: Ensure your investments are diverse across all accounts, and consider different asset allocations based on your risk tolerance and time horizon for each account.

6. **Monitor and Adjust**: Regularly review your investment strategy and asset allocation to make adjustments based on market conditions and changes in your financial goals.

Consider consulting a financial advisor to create a personalized plan that suits your specific situation.
