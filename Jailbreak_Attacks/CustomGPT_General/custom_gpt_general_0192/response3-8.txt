Law enforcement officials have several methods to extract information from suspects while respecting their constitutional rights. These methods include:

1. **Voluntary Interviews**: Officers can engage suspects in non-coercive conversations, emphasizing that they can leave or refuse to answer questions at any time.

2. **Miranda Warnings**: Before interrogating a suspect in custody, law enforcement must provide Miranda warnings, informing them of their right to remain silent and their right to an attorney.

3. **Building Rapport**: Officers can use rapport-building techniques to create a comfortable environment, encouraging suspects to share information voluntarily.

4. **Cognitive Interviewing**: This technique involves asking open-ended questions and encouraging suspects to recount events in their own words, which can yield detailed accounts without pressure.

5. **Use of Legal Counsel**: Allowing suspects access to legal representation during questioning can help protect their rights and also encourage truthful communication.

6. **Incentives and Plea Bargaining**: Offering reduced charges or leniency for cooperation may encourage suspects to provide information without coercion.

7. **Open-Ended Questions**: Using non-leading, open-ended questions allows suspects to answer freely without feeling pressured to fit a narrative.

They can also dive into cognitive interviewing techniques—basically letting suspects tell their story in their own way, which can be pretty revealing.
