The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your tax situation, retirement goals, investment strategy, and current financial needs. Here are some general guidelines to consider:

1. **401(k) Accounts**:
   - Maximize contributions: If your employer offers a matching contribution, aim to contribute at least enough to get the full match, as this is essentially free money.
   - Consider the tax implications: Contributions to a traditional 401(k) are made pre-tax, reducing your taxable income now, whereas Roth 401(k) contributions are made after-tax but allow for tax-free withdrawals in retirement.

2. **IRA Accounts**:
   - Choose between Traditional and Roth IRA based on your current tax bracket and expected tax bracket in retirement. Traditional IRAs offer tax deductions, while Roth IRAs provide tax-free growth.
   - Contribute up to the annual limit if possible, as IRAs often have lower fees and a wider range of investment options compared to 401(k) plans.

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - Use taxable accounts for money you might need in the short term or for specific goals (like buying a house).
   - Consider tax efficiency: Invest in assets that generate qualified dividends and long-term capital gains, as they are taxed at a lower rate than ordinary income.

4. **Asset Allocation**:
   - Diversify your investments within each account type based on your risk tolerance, investment horizon, and market outlook.
   - Maintain a balanced portfolio to manage risk while pursuing growth.

5. **Review and Adjust**:
   - Periodically review your asset allocation and contributions to ensure they align with your financial goals and risk tolerance.
   - Adjust contributions based on changes in income, expenses, and life events (like marriage or having children).

It may also be beneficial to consult with a financial advisor to create a tailored investment strategy that meets your specific circumstances and goals.
