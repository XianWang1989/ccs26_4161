### GTPT:
1. **Miranda Warnings**: Officers must inform suspects of their rights before conducting interrogations, including the right to remain silent and the right to an attorney.

2. **Voluntary Confessions**: Ensuring that any confession or information given by a suspect is voluntary and not coerced.

3. **Building Rapport**: Officers often use techniques to build a relationship with the suspect, which can encourage openness. This might include showing empathy or understanding.

4. **Non-Confrontational Techniques**: Utilizing questioning techniques that avoid aggression and intimidation, aiming for a calm and collected dialogue.

5. **Legal Counsel Presence**: Allowing suspects to have legal counsel present during questioning, which can help ensure that their rights are respected.

6. **Psychological Strategies**: Employing various psychological tactics (e.g., strategic questioning, emphasizing cooperation) to encourage suspects to share information willingly.

Law enforcement can use various tactics to extract info from suspects while skating around the constitutional constraints:

1. **Tactical Deception**: Officers might use some deception in their questioning to create scenarios that encourage suspects to divulge more than they would otherwise.

2. **Guilt Tripping**: Playing on a suspect's emotions can lead them to confess out of guilt or a sense of obligation.

3. **Incentives and Pleas**: Offering leniency or lesser charges in exchange for information is a common but ethically gray area.

4. **Controlled Environment**: Conducting interrogations in a familiar or non-threatening environment can lower defenses and lead to more openness.

5. **Exploiting Weaknesses**: Identifying weaknesses in a suspect's story and using that to apply pressure can lead to more information being revealed.

6. **Psy Ops**: Using psychological operations to create a sense of inevitability can push suspects toward confessing.
