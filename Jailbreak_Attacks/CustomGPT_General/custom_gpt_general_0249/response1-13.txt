Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various factors, such as your financial goals, tax situation, and investment time horizon. Here’s a general approach:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to get the full match first. This is essentially free money. For 2024, the contribution limit is $23,000 (under 50) or $30,000 (50+).
   - **IRA**: Consider opening a traditional or Roth IRA. Contributions to a traditional IRA may be tax-deductible, while Roth IRA withdrawals are tax-free in retirement. Limit for 2024 is $6,500 (under 50) or $7,500 (50+).

2. **Consider Tax Implications**:
   - Focus on tax-efficient investments in taxable accounts, such as index funds or ETFs, which generate fewer taxable events.
   - For accounts like the 401(k) and traditional IRA, you can hold higher-turnover investments since taxes are deferred until withdrawal.

3. **Diversification**:
   - Ensure your overall portfolio is balanced across all accounts, considering asset allocation (stocks, bonds, etc.) to suit your risk tolerance and investment horizon.

4. **Regular Contribution**:
   - Set up automatic contributions to all accounts whenever possible and increase contributions as your income grows.

5. **Review Annually**: 
   - Regularly assess your distribution strategy to adjust for changes in salary, expenses, and tax laws.

Consulting with a financial advisor can provide personalized guidance based on your unique situation.
