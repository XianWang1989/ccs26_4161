Distributing your money across a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax considerations, and investment time horizon. Here’s a general strategy:

1. **401(k) Contributions**:
   - Maximize contributions to your 401(k), especially if your employer offers matching. This allows you to get "free money."
   - Focus on tax-deferred growth and potentially lower your taxable income.

2. **IRA Contributions**:
   - Contribute to a traditional IRA if you want tax-deferred growth, or a Roth IRA for tax-free withdrawals in retirement if you're eligible (based on income limits).
   - Aim to maximize the contribution limits annually.

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - Use taxable accounts for investments you may need to access before retirement or to take advantage of tax-loss harvesting.
   - Favor tax-efficient investments, such as index funds or ETFs, to minimize capital gains taxes.

4. **Diversification**:
   - Diversify your investments within each account type to manage risk effectively. Consider your age, risk tolerance, and investment knowledge.

5. **Regular Review**:
   - Periodically assess your asset allocation and reallocate as necessary to remain aligned with your financial goals and risk tolerance.

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**:
   - Consider working with a financial advisor to tailor a strategy that fits your unique circumstances.

Balancing contributions and understanding the tax implications of each account type will help you optimize your investment strategy.
