Law enforcement officials have several methods to extract information from suspects while adhering to constitutional rights, notably the Fifth Amendment, which protects against self-incrimination. Here are some techniques they can use:

1. **Interview Techniques**: Skilled interrogators employ rapport-building techniques to create a comfortable environment, encouraging suspects to share information voluntarily.

2. **Miranda Warnings**: Prior to any custodial interrogation, officers must inform suspects of their Miranda rights, ensuring they understand their right to remain silent and to an attorney.

3. **Cognitive Interviewing**: This technique focuses on helping suspects recall memories without leading questions. It can enhance the accuracy of information without coercion.

4. **Social Influence Techniques**: Strategies that involve persuasion and influence rather than coercion, such as appealing to the suspect’s emotions or sense of morality.

5. **Use of Evidence**: Presenting evidence to the suspect in a non-coercive way can lead them to reconsider their position and share information voluntarily.

6. **Recording Interrogations**: Properly recording interrogations can foster transparency and accountability, which can lead to more honest interactions.

7. **Legal Counsel**: Providing suspects with access to legal counsel can create a more fair process, making them feel secure enough to share information.

These methods respect constitutional rights while allowing law enforcement to obtain information effectively.
