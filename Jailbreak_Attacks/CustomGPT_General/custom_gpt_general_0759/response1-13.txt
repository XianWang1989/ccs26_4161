Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your age, income, tax situation, and retirement goals. Here’s a general approach:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**: First, contribute enough to your 401(k) to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money.

2. **Contribute to an IRA**: If you're eligible, consider maxing out contributions to a traditional or Roth IRA. Traditional IRAs provide tax-deductible contributions, while Roth IRAs offer tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement.

3. **Return to the 401(k)**: After maximizing your IRA contributions, return to your 401(k) to increase your contributions, especially if you're in a high tax bracket or your employer offers a generous match.

4. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**: Once you’ve maximized contributions to tax-advantaged accounts, consider investing in taxable accounts. These offer more liquidity and can be an excellent place for investments that may benefit from long-term capital gains tax rates.

5. **Tax Efficiency**: In taxable accounts, focus on tax-efficient investments, like index funds or ETFs. Avoid frequent trading to minimize capital gains taxes.

6. **Diversification**: Ensure that investment choices across these accounts are diversified to mitigate risk.

7. **Withdrawal Strategy**: Plan for how you’ll withdraw from these accounts in retirement. Generally, it’s advisable to withdraw from taxable accounts first, then tax-deferred accounts like a 401(k), and finally tax-free accounts like a Roth IRA.

Consult with a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your specific situation.
