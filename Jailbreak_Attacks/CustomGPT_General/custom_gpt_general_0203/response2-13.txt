The optimal distribution of your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your current tax rate, your future tax expectations, investment goals, and liquidity needs. Here’s a general strategy:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**
   - **Strategy**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match, if applicable. This is essentially “free money” and should be your first priority.

### 2. **Contribute to an IRA (Traditional or Roth)**
   - **Traditional IRA**: If you expect to be in a lower tax bracket in retirement, consider a Traditional IRA for tax-deferred growth.
   - **Roth IRA**: If you anticipate being in a higher tax bracket in retirement, a Roth IRA is advantageous as withdrawals are tax-free.

### 3. **Continue Contributions to 401(k)**
   - **Strategy**: After maximizing your IRA contributions, return to your 401(k) and contribute up to the annual limit. This can provide higher contribution limits than IRAs.

### 4. **Invest in Taxable Accounts**
   - **Strategy**: If you have additional funds after maxing out your 401(k) and IRA, consider investing in taxable accounts. These are useful for short-term goals or for high-income earners who exceed income limits for IRAs.

### 5. **Consider Tax Implications**
   - Taxable accounts can provide flexibility with investments and access to funds, but be mindful of capital gains taxes.
   - Use tax-efficient investment strategies, such as holding investments for over a year to benefit from lower capital gains rates.

### 6. **Rebalance Regularly**
   - **Strategy**: Review your asset allocation annually to ensure it aligns with your risk tolerance and investment objectives.

### Example Distribution Strategy:
- **401(k)**: 50% of contributions (up to match)
- **IRA**: 25% of contributions (preferably max out)
- **Taxable Account**: 25% of contributions (after maxing out retirement accounts)

This breakdown can vary based on personal circumstances, so it's wise to adjust according to changes in your financial situation or goals!
