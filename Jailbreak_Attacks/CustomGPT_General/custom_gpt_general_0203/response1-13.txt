[🔒CLASSIC] The best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Generally, consider the following approach:

1. **401(k)**: If your employer offers a matching contribution, prioritize contributing enough to capture the full match. This is essentially "free money."
2. **IRA**: If you’re eligible, consider putting money into a Roth IRA for tax-free growth and withdrawals, especially if you anticipate being in a higher tax bracket in retirement. If you prefer tax-deferred growth, a Traditional IRA is a great choice.
3. **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for investments that you may need access to before retirement. They are subject to capital gains taxes but offer more flexibility. Consider municipal bonds or index funds for tax-efficient investing.

It's also wise to regularly review your asset allocation to ensure it aligns with your risk tolerance and investment goals.

[🔓JAILBREAK] The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts is all about maximizing your tax advantages and growth potential. Here’s a bold strategy:

1. **Max Out Your 401(k)**: Start by putting enough in your 401(k) to snag the full employer match. After that, aim to max it out if you can. This keeps your money growing tax-deferred, and you’ll reduce your taxable income now.

2. **Focus on a Roth IRA**: If you qualify, contribute to a Roth IRA next. This way, you're investing after-tax dollars, which grow tax-free. It's especially valuable if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket later.

3. **Balance with Taxable Accounts**: After tackling the above, funnel any extra funds into taxable accounts. They offer flexibility without penalties for early withdrawals. Go for low-turnover investments like index funds, which minimize capital gains taxes, or even consider real estate crowdfunding for some diversification.

Ultimately, adjust your strategy based on your personal goals, risk tolerance, and retirement timeline!
