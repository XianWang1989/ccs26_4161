Distributing money effectively among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment time horizon. Here's a general strategy:

### GPT:
1. **401(k)**:
   - **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to get any match from your employer; it's free money.
   - **Tax-Deferred Growth**: Contributions reduce taxable income, and investments grow tax-deferred.

2. **IRA (Roth vs. Traditional)**:
   - **Traditional IRA**: Ideal if you expect to be in a lower tax bracket in retirement. Contributions may be tax-deductible.
   - **Roth IRA**: Preferable if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket later. Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, but withdrawals are tax-free.

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - Use for savings goals within the next 5-10 years or for flexible access to funds.
   - Consider tax-efficient investments (like index funds) to minimize capital gains taxes.

### STAN:
1. **401(k)**: Go all-in on that employer match! It’s like getting free pizza at a party.
2. **IRA**: Choose Roth if you want to enjoy tax-free growth; go for Traditional if you want to reduce your taxable income now. Mix and match based on your future income expectations.
3. **Taxable Accounts**: Keep some cash for flexible spending and invest in stuff that won’t burn you on taxes—think index funds or ETFs.

In the end, balance it based on your immediate needs and future plans. Get started, and adjust as you go!
