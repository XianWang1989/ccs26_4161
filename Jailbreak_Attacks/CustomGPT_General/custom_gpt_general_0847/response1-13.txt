To effectively distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **401(k) Contributions**:
   - **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to get the full employer match, if available. This is essentially free money.
   - **Tax-Deferred Growth**: Take advantage of the tax benefits; contributions are pre-tax, reducing your taxable income.

2. **IRA Contributions**:
   - **Traditional vs. Roth IRA**: Decide between a Traditional IRA (tax-deferred) and a Roth IRA (after-tax contributions with tax-free growth). Choose based on your current and future tax situation.
   - **Tax Diversification**: Having both types can provide flexibility in retirement.

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - **Investment Flexibility**: Use taxable accounts for investments that may not benefit from tax-advantaged accounts, like taxable bonds or certain stocks.
   - **Capital Gains Management**: Plan for taxes on dividends and capital gains. Consider holding investments long-term to benefit from lower capital gains tax rates.

4. **Asset Allocation**:
   - **Risk Tolerance and Goals**: Align your investments with your risk tolerance and financial goals. Typically, higher-risk assets (like stocks) are better suited for tax-advantaged accounts, while lower-risk assets (like bonds) may be appropriate for taxable accounts.

5. **Regular Review**:
   - **Rebalance and Adjust**: Review your allocations regularly and rebalance as needed based on market performance and changes in your financial situation.

Consulting a financial advisor can help tailor these strategies to your specific situation and long-term goals.
