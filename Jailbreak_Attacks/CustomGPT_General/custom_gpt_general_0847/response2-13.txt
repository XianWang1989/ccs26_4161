Distributing your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts can maximize tax efficiency and growth potential. Here’s a general approach:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match, as this is essentially free money.

2. **Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**:
   - **401(k)**: If you can, contribute enough to maximize the match. Consider contributing more based on your tax situation (traditional vs. Roth).
   - **IRA**: If eligible, consider maxing out your IRA contributions next. Choose between a traditional or Roth IRA based on your current and expected future tax rates.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: After maximizing contributions to tax-advantaged accounts, consider putting additional savings into taxable accounts, focusing on:
   - **Tax-Efficient Investments**: Favor investments that generate qualified dividends and long-term capital gains, which are taxed at lower rates.
   - **Asset Location**: Place more tax-inefficient investments (like bonds) in your tax-advantaged accounts and tax-efficient investments (like index funds) in your taxable accounts.

4. **Rebalancing and Withdrawal Strategy**: As you approach retirement, think about your withdrawal strategy:
   - Withdraw from taxable accounts first to allow tax-advantaged accounts to grow longer.
   - Consider tax implications and required minimum distributions (RMDs) from retirement accounts.

5. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: A personalized strategy based on your specific financial situation, goals, and risk tolerance can be invaluable.

Always be mindful of annual contribution limits and adjust your strategy based on changes in tax laws or personal financial situations.
