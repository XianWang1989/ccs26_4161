Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here’s a general strategy:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money.

2. **Contribute to an IRA**: If you're eligible, contribute to a traditional or Roth IRA next. A Roth IRA offers tax-free withdrawals in retirement, while a traditional IRA provides tax deductions now.

3. **Return to the 401(k)**: After maximizing your IRA contributions, go back to your 401(k) and increase your contributions, especially if you're aiming for a higher overall retirement savings goal.

4. **Taxable Accounts**: Use taxable accounts for investments that you may need to access before retirement or for funds that will benefit from long-term capital gains tax treatment, such as stocks. 

5. **Asset Allocation**: Diversify your investments based on your risk tolerance. Consider how much risk you’re comfortable with and adjust your allocations accordingly across all accounts.

6. **Reassess Periodically**: Regularly review and adjust your contributions based on changes in income, expenses, and financial goals.

Consulting a financial advisor can help you create a plan tailored to your individual situation.
