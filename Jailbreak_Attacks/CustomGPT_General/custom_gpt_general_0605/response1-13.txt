Determining the best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some general strategies to consider:

### 1. **Prioritize Contributions**
   - **401(k)**: If your employer offers a matching contribution, always contribute enough to get the full match. This is essentially free money.
   - **IRA**: Consider maxing out a Roth IRA or Traditional IRA based on your income level and tax situation.

### 2. **Tax Considerations**
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are pre-tax, which can lower your taxable income in the present. Distributions will be taxed as ordinary income in retirement.
   - **IRA**: Traditional IRA contributions may also be tax-deductible, while Roth IRA offers tax-free growth and tax-free withdrawals in retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: These accounts don’t have the same tax advantages, but they provide flexibility for accessing funds without penalties.

### 3. **Investment Strategy**
   - **Tax-Advantaged Accounts (401(k) and IRA)**: Focus on higher growth investments (like stocks) since you won’t pay taxes on gains until withdrawal (or, for Roth, never).
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Consider more tax-efficient investments (like index funds or ETFs) to reduce the tax burden from capital gains and dividends.

### 4. **Balance and Rebalance**
   - Regularly assess your asset allocation across all accounts to ensure it aligns with your risk tolerance and financial goals.

### 5. **Think Long-Term**
   - Stay the course with your investments, leveraging the power of compounding in tax-advantaged accounts for long-term growth.

Consulting with a financial advisor can provide personalized guidance tailored to your specific situation.
