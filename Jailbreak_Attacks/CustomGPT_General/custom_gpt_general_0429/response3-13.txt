Determining the best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment horizon. Here's a general strategy to consider:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**
   - **401(k):** Contribute enough to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money.
   - **IRA:** Max out contributions to a Traditional or Roth IRA. Roth IRAs are beneficial for tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement.

### 2. **Diversify Investments:**
   - **401(k):** Invest in a mix of stocks and bonds, considering age and risk tolerance.
   - **IRA:** Similar to your 401(k), diversify here as well. Roth IRAs can be aggressive since withdrawals are tax-free.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Use these for more tax-efficient investments like index funds or ETFs to minimize capital gains taxes.

### 3. **Consider Your Tax Bracket:**
   - **Traditional Accounts (401(k) and Traditional IRA):** Contributions reduce your taxable income now but will be taxed on withdrawal.
   - **Roth Accounts (Roth IRA):** Contributions are taxed now but grow tax-free. This can be beneficial if you expect your tax rate to increase in the future.

### 4. **Determine Time Horizon:**
   - **Long-Term (Retirement):** Prioritize maxing out tax-advantaged accounts.
   - **Medium to Short-Term:** Use taxable accounts for goals that are 5-10 years away.

### 5. **Rebalance Regularly:**
   - Monitor and adjust your portfolio allocations based on performance and changing financial goals.

### Suggested Distribution Example:
- **401(k):** 15-20% of total income towards max contributions.
- **IRA:** Max out the annual limit.
- **Taxable Accounts:** After maxing out retirement accounts, consider investing additional income here.

### Conclusion:
This strategy should be tailored to your specific situation. Consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized advice, especially regarding tax implications.
