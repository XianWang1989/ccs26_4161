Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts largely depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and time horizon. Here’s a general approach:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match. This is essentially free money.
   - **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**: If eligible, contribute to an IRA, choosing between a Traditional (tax-deductible) or Roth (tax-free growth) based on your income level and tax strategy.

### 2. **Consider Your Tax Situation**
   - **Tax-deferred accounts (401(k), Traditional IRA)** are beneficial if you expect to be in a lower tax bracket in retirement.
   - **Roth accounts (if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket)** allow for tax-free withdrawals, making them attractive.

### 3. **Investing in Taxable Accounts**
   - Use taxable accounts for investments that generate capital gains or qualified dividends, which are taxed at lower rates than ordinary income.
   - Consider municipal bonds in taxable accounts since their interest is often exempt from federal tax.

### 4. **Diversification and Risk Assessment**
   - Diversify investments across all accounts according to your risk tolerance. Ensure a mix of asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) aligned with your goals.

### 5. **Rebalance Regularly**
   - Review and adjust your asset allocation periodically to maintain your risk profile and adapt to changing markets or personal circumstances.

### Example Strategy
1. **401(k)**: Contribute up to the employer match, then max out your IRA if funds allow.
2. **IRA**: Depending on your eligibility, prioritize Roth for tax-free growth.
3. **Taxable Accounts**: Invest additional savings for flexibility, focusing on tax-efficient investments.

### Key Considerations
- **Emergency Fund**: Ensure you have sufficient liquidity outside these accounts for unexpected expenses.
- **Long-Term Goals**: Align your distributions with major financial goals (buying a home, education, retirement).

By carefully considering your current and future financial picture, you can optimize your distribution strategy among these accounts.
