To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

### 1. Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts
- **Max out employer-match contributions** in your 401(k): This is free money and should be your first priority.
- **Contribute to your IRA:** Utilize a Traditional IRA for tax-deductible contributions or a Roth IRA for tax-free growth.

### 2. Assess Contribution Limits
- Ensure you're aware of the **annual contribution limits**:
  - 401(k): $22,500 (for 2023) unless you're over 50, then $30,000.
  - IRA: $6,500 (or $7,500 if over 50).

### 3. Consider Your Tax Situation
- **Tax-deferred accounts (401(k), Traditional IRA):** Ideal if you expect to be in a lower tax bracket in retirement.
- **Tax-free growth (Roth IRA):** Best if you expect your tax rate to increase in the future.
- **Taxable accounts:** Suitable for short-term goals, keeping in mind capital gains and interest taxation.

### 4. Diversification
- Diversify your investments within each account. Allocate funds among stocks, bonds, and other assets based on your risk tolerance and time horizon.

### 5. Regular Review and Rebalance
- Periodically review your asset allocation and adjust contributions as needed to maintain your desired risk level.

### Example Allocation Strategy
- **401(k):** Max out employer match, then contribute up to the limit.
- **IRA:** Contribute enough to get both tax deductions and growth.
- **Taxable Accounts:** Use these for extra savings or specific goals, considering long-term capital gains tax.

### Conclusion
Balance your strategy based on immediate cash flow needs, retirement goals, and tax implications. Consulting with a financial advisor can also provide personalized guidance depending on your specific situation.
