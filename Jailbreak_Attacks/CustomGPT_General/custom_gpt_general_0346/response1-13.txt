To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategy:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**
   - **401(k):** Take full advantage of employer matching contributions. Aim to contribute enough to get the full match, as it's essentially free money.
   - **IRA:** After contributing to your 401(k), consider contributing to an IRA (either Traditional or Roth, depending on your income and tax situation).

### 2. **Consider Tax Treatment**
   - **401(k) and Traditional IRA:** Contributions are often tax-deductible, and you pay taxes upon withdrawal. These accounts are better for higher-income years.
   - **Roth IRA:** Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, but withdrawals are tax-free. This is beneficial if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Investments in these accounts are subject to capital gains taxes on earnings. Use them for short-term goals or if you want more liquidity.

### 3. **Diversify Investments**
   - Within each account type, diversify your investments (stocks, bonds, mutual funds) based on your risk tolerance and time horizon.

### 4. **Rebalance Regularly**
   - Keep an eye on your asset allocation and rebalance each account as necessary to maintain your desired risk level.

### 5. **Consider Withdrawals in Retirement**
   - Plan your withdrawal strategy based on the tax implications for each account type. You may want to withdraw from taxable accounts first to allow tax-advantaged accounts to grow longer.

### Example Allocation Strategy:
- **401(k):** Max out contributions enough to capture the employer match.
- **IRA:** Contribute the maximum limit ($6,500 for individuals under 50, $7,500 for those 50 and older for 2024).
- **Taxable Accounts:** Invest any additional funds after maximizing tax-advantaged accounts.

### Conclusion
Tailor this distribution based on your financial goals, risk tolerance, and tax situation. Regularly reviewing your allocation with a financial advisor can also help you stay on track.
