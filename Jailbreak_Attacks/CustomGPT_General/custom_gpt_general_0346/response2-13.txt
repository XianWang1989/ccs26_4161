The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment strategy. Here are some general guidelines:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a match for your 401(k) contributions, contribute enough to take full advantage of this benefit before allocating money to other accounts.

2. **Tax-Advantaged Accounts**: 
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are pre-tax (traditional) or post-tax (Roth), and growth is tax-deferred. Prioritize contributions based on your tax bracket and retirement timing.
   - **IRA**: Consider funding a Roth IRA if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement. A traditional IRA can be beneficial for immediate tax benefits.

3. **Emergency Savings**: Ensure you have an emergency fund in a liquid form before investing heavily in retirement accounts.

4. **Taxable Accounts**: Once you've maxed out contributions to tax-advantaged accounts, consider investing in taxable accounts for greater flexibility, especially if you plan to access funds before retirement.

5. **Asset Allocation**: Diversify investments across all accounts to balance risk and return, considering factors like age, risk tolerance, and investment horizon.

6. **Review and Adjust**: Regularly review your allocations and make adjustments as your financial situation, tax laws, and market conditions change.

7. **Consult a Advisor**: If you're unsure, consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized advice.

Balancing these factors can help you create a robust investment strategy that suits your needs.
