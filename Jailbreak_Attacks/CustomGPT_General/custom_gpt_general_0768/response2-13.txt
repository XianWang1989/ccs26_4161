To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider these strategic steps:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to receive any employer match. This is essentially "free money" and should be prioritized.

2. **Focus on Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: If your employer offers a 401(k) match, contribute enough to get the full match first. After that, think about how much additional contribution you can make based on your financial situation and tax brackets.
   - **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**: After maximizing your 401(k) match, consider contributing to an IRA. Decide between a Traditional IRA (tax-deductible contributions) or a Roth IRA (tax-free withdrawals in retirement) based on your current and expected future tax situation.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: Once you’ve maxed out contributions to your 401(k) and IRA, consider directing additional funds to taxable accounts. These can provide flexibility for early withdrawals without penalties if needed.

4. **Balance Your Investment Strategy**:
   - Ensure your investments across all accounts are well-aligned with your overall financial goals and risk tolerance.
   - For tax efficiency, consider holding less tax-efficient investments (like bonds or REITs) in tax-advantaged accounts, while placing more tax-efficient investments (like stocks) in taxable accounts.

5. **Regularly Reassess**: Financial situations and goals evolve, so regularly review your distribution strategy and adjust contributions as needed.

By following these steps, you can optimize your investment for growth while considering tax implications and future financial needs!
