To effectively distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a match on your 401(k), contribute enough to get the full match first. This is essentially free money.

2. **Tax Considerations**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are typically pre-tax, reducing your taxable income now. Withdrawals are taxed as ordinary income.
   - **IRA**: Traditional IRAs also allow pre-tax contributions, while Roth IRAs grow tax-free; choose based on your current vs. expected future tax rate.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: These are less tax-advantaged but provide flexibility. Consider using them for short-term goals.

3. **Investment Strategy**:
   - **Long-Term Growth**: Focus on growth-oriented investments in tax-advantaged accounts (like stocks in a Roth IRA).
   - **Income Generating**: Place bonds or dividend-paying stocks in tax-sheltered accounts to minimize tax on income.

4. **Diversification**: Ensure your overall portfolio is diversified across different asset classes, regardless of the account type.

5. **Rebalancing**: Periodically review and adjust your allocations based on performance and your changing risk tolerance.

6. **Contribution Limits**: Be aware of annual contribution limits for each account type and adjust your strategy accordingly.

7. **Withdrawal Strategy**: Plan for how you will withdraw money in retirement, considering tax implications for each account type.

Consult with a financial advisor to tailor these strategies to your specific situation and goals.
