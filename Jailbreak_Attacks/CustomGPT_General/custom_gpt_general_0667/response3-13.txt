Here’s a balanced approach to distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts:

### 1. **401(k)**
   - **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to get the full employer match (if offered). This is essentially free money.
   - **Tax Benefits**: Contributions are pre-tax (traditional) or after-tax (Roth), contingent on the plan.

### 2. **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**
   - **Contribution Limits**: Max out IRA contributions if you can (currently $6,500 annually, or $7,500 if over 50).
   - **Tax Treatment**: Choose between traditional (tax-deferred) or Roth (tax-free growth) based on your current tax situation and retirement goals.

### 3. **Taxable Accounts**
   - **Flexibility**: Use for savings goals that don’t require tax-advantaged space. The money can be accessed without penalties.
   - **Tax Efficiency**: Invest in tax-efficient funds (like index funds) to minimize capital gains taxes.

### **General Guidelines**
- **Emergency Fund**: Ensure you have 3-6 months of expenses saved in a liquid account.
- **Asset Allocation**: Diversify your investments across asset classes based on your risk tolerance.
- **Regular Contributions**: Dollar-cost averaging can help mitigate market volatility.

Consider consulting a financial advisor to tailor this strategy to your specific situation.
