Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors including your age, risk tolerance, and financial goals. Here’s a general guide:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**
   - Contribute enough to get any employer match, as it’s essentially free money.

### 2. **Prioritize Retirement Accounts**
   - **401(k) and IRA**: Focus on these accounts for retirement savings. They offer tax advantages.
   - Consider contributing to a Roth IRA if you qualify, for tax-free growth and withdrawals.

### 3. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**
   - Use taxable accounts for funds you may need before retirement or for short-term goals.
   - Invest in tax-efficient funds to minimize capital gains taxes.

### 4. **Diversify Investments**
   - Maintain a diversified investment portfolio across all accounts.
   - Adjust asset allocation based on the account type: more aggressive in tax-advantaged accounts and more conservative in taxable accounts.

### 5. **Consider Your Tax Situation**
   - Withdrawals from retirement accounts are taxed as income; taxable accounts may allow for capital gains tax at a lower rate.
   - Plan withdrawals strategically in retirement to minimize tax impact.

### 6. **Review Regularly**
   - Reassess your allocations annually or after major life changes to ensure they align with your goals.

### Example Strategy
- **401(k)**: Max out employer match, consider 15% of salary.
- **IRA/Roth IRA**: Contribute the maximum allowable amount.
- **Taxable Accounts**: Use residual cash after maxing out retirement accounts for flexibility.

### Conclusion
Consult a financial advisor for personalized advice based on your specific circumstances.
