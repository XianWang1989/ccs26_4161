Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here are some steps to consider:

### 1. **Understand Account Types:**
   - **401(k)**: Often has employer matching, contributing enough to get the full match is essential. Contributions reduce taxable income.
   - **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**: Traditional IRAs offer tax-deferred growth, while Roth IRAs allow tax-free withdrawals in retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: These offer flexibility to access funds without penalties but incur taxes on capital gains and dividends.

### 2. **Prioritize Contributions:**
   - **Maximize 401(k) Match**: Contribute enough to get any employer match first, as it's essentially free money.
   - **Contribute to IRA**: If you can, max out your IRA contributions. A Roth IRA is preferable if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for funds you might need before retirement or for additional investment flexibility.

### 3. **Consider Tax Implications:**
   - **Tax-Deferred Accounts**: Lower your taxable income now, deferring taxes until withdrawal.
   - **Roth IRA**: Invest in lower-growth assets here to maximize tax-free growth.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Utilize tax-loss harvesting strategies and consider holding investments long-term to minimize capital gains taxes.

### 4. **Investment Goals and Timeline:**
   - **Short-Term Goals**: Funds needed within five years might be better suited in a taxable account or a conservative 401(k) choice.
   - **Long-Term Growth**: Focus on growth-oriented investments in tax-advantaged accounts.

### 5. **Regularly Review and Adjust:**
   - Reassess your allocation annually or after significant life changes to ensure it aligns with your goals.

### General Allocation Suggestion:
- **401(k)**: Up to employer match, then max out IRA.
- **IRA**: Max out contributions based on income limits.
- **Taxable Accounts**: Allocate remaining funds for flexibility and short-term needs.

### Conclusion:
Customize your distribution based on your unique financial situation, and consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized advice.
