Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here’s a general approach:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**
   - **401(k):** Contribute enough to get any employer match first. Then, consider increasing contributions to the maximum allowed.
   - **IRA:** If eligible, contribute to a Roth IRA for tax-free growth or a Traditional IRA for potential tax deductions, depending on your income level.

### 2. **Evaluate Your Tax Situation**
   - **Taxable Accounts:** These are ideal for funds you may need before retirement or for investing in assets that benefit from capital gains tax rates. 

### 3. **Diversification and Asset Allocation**
   - Ensure assets across all accounts are diversified based on your risk tolerance. Consider placing more volatile investments (like stocks) in tax-advantaged accounts to avoid immediate capital gains taxes.

### 4. **Consider Withdrawal Strategies**
   - Plan for withdrawals in retirement. Generally, you might want to withdraw from taxable accounts first, followed by tax-deferred accounts (like 401(k)s), and finally tax-free accounts (like Roth IRAs).

### 5. **Stay Informed on Contribution Limits**
   - Keep track of annual contribution limits for 401(k)s ($22,500 for 2023, $30,000 if age 50 or older), IRAs ($6,500 for 2023, $7,500 if age 50 or older), and tax implications for your taxable accounts.

### Final Thoughts
Work with a financial advisor to tailor a strategy that fits your specific situation. They can help optimize your tax benefits and align your investments with your long-term goals.
