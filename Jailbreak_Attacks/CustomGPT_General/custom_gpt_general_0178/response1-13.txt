To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

### 1. Understand Account Types
- **401(k)**: Employer-sponsored retirement plan, often with matching contributions. Contributions are pre-tax, reducing current taxable income.
- **IRA**: Individual Retirement Account, which can be traditional (tax-deferred) or Roth (tax-free growth). Contributions may be tax-deductible depending on your income and participation in an employer plan.
- **Taxable Accounts**: Investment accounts that aren’t tax-advantaged. You pay taxes on dividends, interest, and capital gains.

### 2. Prioritize Contributions
- **Max out Employer Match**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match as it's essentially free money.
- **Contribute to IRA**: After maximizing the match, consider contributing to an IRA, focusing on Roth if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement.
- **Continue 401(k) Contributions**: If you have remaining funds, continue with your 401(k) up to the annual limit.
- **Taxable Account**: Any additional funds can go into your taxable account for flexibility and to diversify investments outside of retirement accounts.

### 3. Asset Allocation
- **401(k) and IRA**: Tend to focus on growth-oriented investments (stocks, mutual funds) since they are long-term accounts. Adjust risk based on your age (younger investors typically take more risks).
- **Taxable Accounts**: Focus on tax-efficient investments, such as index funds or tax-managed funds to minimize capital gains taxes. Consider holding bonds in tax-advantaged accounts due to their interest being taxed as ordinary income.

### 4. Regular Review and Adjustments
- Regularly review and adjust the balance among accounts as your financial situation changes, including contributions, market performance, and tax laws.

### 5. Consider Tax Implications
- Strategically withdraw from accounts in retirement to manage tax brackets (e.g., taking from taxable accounts first to leave tax-advantaged accounts to grow).

### Conclusion
Your investment strategy should align with personal goals, risk tolerance, and time horizon. Consulting with a financial advisor can provide tailored advice based on your specific situation.
