Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment time horizon. Here’s a general approach:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**: If your employer offers a matching contribution, contribute enough to take full advantage of this benefit. This is essentially free money.

2. **Contribute to an IRA**: Consider maximizing contributions to a traditional IRA or Roth IRA (if eligible). Roth IRAs allow tax-free withdrawals in retirement, while traditional IRAs provide tax-deferred growth.

3. **Pay Attention to Tax Implications**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are pre-tax, and withdrawals are taxed as ordinary income.
   - **Traditional IRA**: Similar to a 401(k) in tax treatment.
   - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, but withdrawals are tax-free if certain conditions are met.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: You'll pay capital gains taxes on any profits when you sell investments, so consider this when distributing funds.

4. **Diversify Investments**: Ensure your investment allocations align with your risk tolerance and time horizon across all accounts. Consider the following strategies:
   - **High-Growth Investments in Tax-Advantaged Accounts**: Place assets expected to appreciate significantly in tax-advantaged accounts to avoid taxes on those gains.
   - **Income-Generating Investments in Taxable Accounts**: Keep tax-efficient investments, such as index funds or ETFs that generate qualified dividends or long-term capital gains, in taxable accounts.

5. **Regularly Rebalance**: As you contribute and market conditions change, periodically review and rebalance your investments to maintain your desired asset allocation.

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: A financial advisor can help tailor a distribution strategy based on your personal circumstances, goals, and risk tolerance.

By following these steps, you can create a balanced approach to distributing your money across these accounts.
