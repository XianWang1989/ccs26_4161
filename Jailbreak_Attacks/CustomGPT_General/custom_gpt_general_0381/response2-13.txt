Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here’s a general strategy:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**: Contribute enough to get the full employer match, if available. This is essentially free money.

2. **Fund an IRA**: After maximizing your 401(k) match, consider contributing to a traditional or Roth IRA. A Roth IRA offers tax-free withdrawals in retirement, while traditional IRAs may offer immediate tax deductions.

3. **Continue Contributing to 401(k)**: If you have more to invest, continue contributing to your 401(k) up to its limit. This helps reduce your taxable income and grow your retirement savings tax-deferred.

4. **Taxable Accounts**: If you’ve maxed out retirement accounts, invest in taxable accounts. These can provide flexibility for withdrawals before retirement age. Focus on tax-efficient investments, like index funds or ETFs.

5. **Consider Your Tax Bracket**: If you're in a lower tax bracket now, prioritize Roth accounts. If you expect to be in a higher bracket during retirement, focus on traditional accounts.

6. **Emergency Fund and Short-Term Needs**: Ensure you have enough liquidity for short-term needs and emergencies before maximizing long-term investments.

7. **Adjust for Time Horizon**: Younger investors might lean more heavily on equities in taxable accounts, while those closer to retirement may prefer bonds or more conservative investments.

Regularly review your allocations as your financial situation or goals change.
