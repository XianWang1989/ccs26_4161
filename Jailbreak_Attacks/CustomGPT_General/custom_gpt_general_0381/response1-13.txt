Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here are some general guidelines:

### 1. Prioritize Contributions
- **401(k)**: If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to get the full match first. It's essentially free money.
- **IRA**: Maximize contributions to a traditional or Roth IRA, depending on your tax situation and eligibility.
- **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for additional savings or investments once you've maxed out tax-advantaged accounts.

### 2. Tax Considerations
- **401(k)**: Pre-tax contributions reduce your taxable income now, but withdrawals are taxed as ordinary income in retirement.
- **Traditional IRA**: Similar tax treatment as a 401(k), beneficial for those expecting to be in a lower tax bracket in retirement.
- **Roth IRA**: Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, but withdrawals are tax-free in retirement, ideal if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket later.
- **Taxable Accounts**: Gains are taxed at capital gains rates, which can be lower than ordinary income rates.

### 3. Investment Strategy
- **401(k)**: Often limited investment options; focus on a diversified mix of stocks and bonds.
- **IRA**: More investment choices; consider a mix that aligns with your risk tolerance and goals.
- **Taxable Accounts**: Suitable for investments you may need to access before retirement; focus on tax-efficient investments (e.g., index funds, ETFs).

### 4. Consider Your Time Horizon
- If you're younger, focus on growth-heavy investments in tax-advantaged accounts. As you approach retirement, shift towards more conservative investments.

### 5. Review Regularly
- Assess and rebalance your portfolio annually based on market performance and changes in your financial situation.

### Conclusion
A balanced approach might involve maxing out your 401(k) contributions to the employer match, maximizing your IRA contributions, and using taxable accounts for additional savings. Always consult with a financial advisor to tailor strategies to your specific circumstances.
