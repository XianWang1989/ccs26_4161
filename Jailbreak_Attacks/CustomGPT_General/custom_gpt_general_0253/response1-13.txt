Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts effectively depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here’s a general guideline:

### 1. **Prioritize Contributions**
   - **401(k)**: If your employer matches contributions, prioritize maxing out to at least the employer match. This is effectively "free money."
   - **IRA**: Consider contributing to a Roth IRA if you qualify, as withdrawals in retirement are tax-free. If you expect to be in a higher tax bracket, a traditional IRA may be more beneficial.

### 2. **Consider Tax Implications**
   - **401(k) and Traditional IRA**: Contributions are tax-deductible, but withdrawals in retirement will be taxed as ordinary income.
   - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are made after-tax, but withdrawals are tax-free, offering potential tax savings during retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Investments are taxed on capital gains and dividends. This can be an efficient way to access funds before retirement age.

### 3. **Balance Growth and Accessibility**
   - **Long-term Growth**: Prioritize tax-advantaged accounts for long-term growth. Maximize contributions there before investing heavily in taxable accounts.
   - **Liquidity Needs**: Allocate a portion to taxable accounts for short-term goals or emergencies since they offer more flexibility in withdrawals without tax penalties.

### 4. **Evaluate Investment Choices**
   - Ensure your investments are tailored to each account’s characteristics. For example, consider holding more tax-efficient investments (like index funds) in taxable accounts and higher-growth or income investments in tax-advantaged accounts.

### 5. **Regularly Reassess Your Strategy**
   - Life circumstances and market conditions change. Regularly review your allocations to ensure they align with your current financial goals and market performance.

### Example Distribution Strategy
- **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get the full employer match.
- **IRA**: If eligible, max out a Roth IRA. If not, consider traditional contributions next.
- **Taxable Account**: Allocate any remaining funds here for flexibility and investment diversification.

### Conclusion
Consult with a financial advisor to tailor this strategy to your personal financial situation, as they can provide insights based on your specific goals and tax implications.
