To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k):** Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match. This is essentially "free" money.

2. **Contribute to an IRA:** After maximizing your 401(k) match, consider contributing to a Traditional or Roth IRA. With a Roth IRA, your contributions grow tax-free, and withdrawals in retirement are tax-free, making it a good option if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket later.

3. **Continue to Fund Your 401(k):** If you're eligible, continue to contribute to your 401(k) up to the annual contribution limit. This account also benefits from tax-deferred growth.

4. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility:** Use taxable accounts for investments that may have short- to medium-term goals (like purchasing a home or funding education). You can access these funds without penalties, unlike retirement accounts, but be mindful of capital gains taxes.

5. **Asset Location Strategy:** Place investments with higher expected returns and those more sensitive to taxes (like bonds) in tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) and IRA). Keep lower-growth investments in taxable accounts to minimize tax liabilities.

6. **Consider Your Tax Bracket:** Adjust your contributions based on your current and expected future tax brackets. In higher brackets, prioritize tax-deferred accounts.

7. **Diversification:** Ensure that you maintain a diversified portfolio across all accounts to manage risk effectively.

8. **Review and Adjust Regularly:** Periodically review your allocation based on changes in your financial situation, tax laws, and retirement goals.

Consult a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your specific circumstances and goals.
