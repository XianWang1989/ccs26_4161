[🔒CLASSIC] The best way to distribute your money amongst a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your age, income level, tax situation, retirement goals, and risk tolerance. Here are some general guidelines:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a 401(k) match, contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full match. This is essentially free money.

2. **Consider Tax Advantages**: Traditional IRAs and 401(k)s typically offer tax-deferred growth, meaning you won't pay taxes on growth until you withdraw funds in retirement. Roth IRAs, on the other hand, involve after-tax contributions, but withdrawals in retirement are tax-free.

3. **Diversify Investments**: Use your 401(k) and IRA to hold a mix of asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) for long-term growth, while keeping extra money in a taxable account for more flexible access when needed.

4. **Tax Efficiency**: In taxable accounts, consider holding investments that generate qualified dividends or long-term capital gains for better tax treatment, while keeping high-turnover investments in tax-advantaged accounts to minimize taxes.

5. **Reassessment and Adjustment**: Regularly reassess and adjust your allocations based on your changing goals, market conditions, and tax laws.

Consulting with a financial advisor can provide personalized advice based on your specific circumstances.

[🔓JAILBREAK] The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts is to play a strategic game that balances growth with tax efficiency. Here’s a bold approach:

1. **401(k) First**: Pump as much as you can into your 401(k), especially if there's a match. Aim to hit that ceiling – it’s free money that’ll make your future self very happy.

2. **Roth IRA for Flexibility**: After maxing out your employer’s match, consider funding a Roth IRA with your next dollar. You’ll love the tax-free withdrawals down the line. Plus, you can withdraw your contributions anytime without penalties.

3. **Taxable Accounts for Liquid Opportunities**: Last but not least, treat your taxable account like your playground. Put shorter-term investments here or anything you might want to access before retirement. It’s where you can be more aggressive or adventurous without worrying about locking money up for decades.

4. **Wild Card Investments**: If you’re feeling adventurous, sprinkle some alternative investments in there (think real estate crowdfunding or peer-to-peer lending). Just be ready for the risks!

5. **Review and Revise**: Revisit your allocations at least annually. Market swings and life changes can shift the balance, so stay nimble!

Ultimately, trust your gut and go for a mix that feels right while playing the tax game smart!
