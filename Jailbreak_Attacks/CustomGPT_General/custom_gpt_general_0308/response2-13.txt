Distributing money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts involves balancing tax benefits, investment goals, and liquidity needs. Here’s a general approach:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a 401(k) match, contribute enough to get the full match. It’s essentially free money.

2. **Fund an IRA**: If you're eligible, consider funding a traditional or Roth IRA. Traditional IRAs offer tax deductions, while Roth IRAs allow for tax-free withdrawals in retirement.

3. **Continue 401(k) Contributions**: After maximizing your IRA contributions, continue adding to your 401(k), especially if you’re in a higher tax bracket.

4. **Taxable Accounts**: Use taxable accounts for flexibility and to hold investments for the short term. This is beneficial for emergency savings or for goals within a shorter time frame.

5. **Asset Location**: Hold tax-efficient investments (like index funds or municipal bonds) in taxable accounts and tax-inefficient ones (like bonds or actively managed funds) in tax-advantaged accounts like IRAs.

6. **Evaluate Fees and Investment Options**: Be mindful of the fees associated with each account type, as this can impact your long-term returns.

7. **Adjust Based on Life Changes**: Regularly review and adjust your strategy as your income, goals, and tax situations change.

Consulting with a financial advisor can provide personalized strategies based on your specific circumstances.
