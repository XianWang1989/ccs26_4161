Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment strategies. Here’s a general approach:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match:**
   - Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match if available. This is essentially free money.

### 2. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts:**
   - **401(k)**: If you have not maximized your contribution, consider increasing it up to the annual limit, especially if you expect your tax rate to be lower in retirement.
   - **IRA**: If eligible, contribute to a traditional IRA or Roth IRA based on your tax situation. Roth IRAs are beneficial for tax-free growth and withdrawals.

### 3. **Taxable Accounts:**
   - Once you’ve maximized contributions to the above accounts, consider investing in taxable accounts. These can provide flexibility for withdrawals but are subject to capital gains taxes.

### 4. **Investment Strategy:**
   - Different account types may have different investment focuses. For example:
     - **Tax-Deferred Accounts (401(k), IRA)**: Consider holding securities that might generate higher income (like bonds) since taxes are deferred.
     - **Taxable Accounts**: Favor tax-efficient investments like index funds or ETFs, which have lower turnover and capital gains distributions.

### 5. **Rebalance Regularly:**
   - Regardless of your initial allocation, regularly review and adjust your distributions based on performance and your changing financial goals.

### 6. **Consider Withdrawals and Benefits:**
   - Plan for how and when you intend to withdraw funds, especially from retirement accounts once you reach retirement age.

Consider consulting with a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your specific situation and objectives.
