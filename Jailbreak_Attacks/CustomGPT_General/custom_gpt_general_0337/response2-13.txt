To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to get any matching contributions from your employer. This is essentially free money.

2. **Contribute to IRAs**: If you're eligible, consider contributing to a Roth IRA (after-tax) or a Traditional IRA (pre-tax). A Roth IRA offers tax-free withdrawals in retirement, which can be advantageous for long-term growth.

3. **46-401(k) Contributions**: After maximizing your employer match and IRA contributions, continue to contribute to your 401(k) to take advantage of its higher contribution limits.

4. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**: Use taxable accounts for additional savings and investments, especially if you anticipate needing access to funds before retirement age. Invest in tax-efficient funds like index funds or ETFs to minimize tax liability.

5. **Asset Location Strategy**: 
   - Place higher-growth investments (more likely to generate short-term capital gains) in tax-advantaged accounts (like IRAs or 401(k)s).
   - Hold more stable, income-generating investments (like bonds) in taxable accounts to benefit from lower tax rates on qualified dividends and long-term capital gains.

6. **Regular Review**: Periodically reassess your allocations based on changing financial goals, tax brackets, and market conditions. 

7. **Consult a Professional**: Consider speaking with a financial advisor for personalized strategies based on your unique financial situation and goals.

This balanced approach can help optimize your growth potential while managing your tax exposure effectively.
