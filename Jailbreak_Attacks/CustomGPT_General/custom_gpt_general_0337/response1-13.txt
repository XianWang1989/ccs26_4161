Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here are some general guidelines:

1. **401(k)**:
   - **Employer Match**: Aim to contribute enough to get the full employer match if available. This is essentially free money.
   - **Contribution Limits**: Max out your contributions if possible. For 2023, the limit is $22,500 (or $30,000 if you're over 50).

2. **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**:
   - **Tax Considerations**: If you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement, consider a Roth IRA for tax-free withdrawals. If you’re in a lower bracket now, a Traditional IRA may provide immediate tax benefits.
   - **Contribution Limits**: For 2023, the contribution limit is $6,500 (or $7,500 if you're 50 or older).

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - **Flexibility**: These accounts are great for short-term goals or if you anticipate needing funds before retirement age without penalties.
   - **Invest Wisely**: Consider tax-efficient investments, like index funds or ETFs, to minimize capital gains taxes.

### Strategy
- **Emergency Fund**: Before investing, ensure you have an emergency fund covering 3-6 months of expenses.
- **Debt Management**: Pay off high-interest debt before heavily investing.
- **Diversification**: Aim for a diversified portfolio across all accounts, considering your risk tolerance and investment horizon.
- **Reassess Regularly**: Periodically review your asset allocation and adjust as needed based on market performance and life changes.

Consulting a financial advisor can provide personalized strategies based on your individual circumstances.
