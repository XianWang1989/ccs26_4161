When considering how to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, it's essential to take into account your financial goals, risk tolerance, and tax situation. Here's a general framework to guide your allocation:

1. **401(k) Contribution**:
   - **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a matching contribution, aim to contribute at least enough to get the full match. This is essentially "free money" and an excellent return on your investment.
   - **Contribution Limits**: For 2024, the contribution limit for a 401(k) is $23,000 for those under 50 and $30,500 for those 50 and older (including catch-up contributions).

2. **IRA Contributions**:
   - **Roth vs. Traditional**: Decide between a Roth IRA (tax-free growth and withdrawals) or a Traditional IRA (tax-deferred growth). If you anticipate being in a higher tax bracket in retirement, a Roth IRA might be more beneficial.
   - **Contribution Limits**: The limit for IRA contributions is $6,500 for those under 50 and $7,500 for those 50 and older in 2024.

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - **Flexible Withdrawals**: Once you've maxed out your tax-advantaged accounts, consider taxable accounts. They provide flexibility for withdrawals and can be useful for short-term goals.
   - **Tax Considerations**: Be mindful of capital gains taxes; investing in tax-efficient funds can help mitigate this.

### Suggested Allocation Strategy:

1. **Employer Match in 401(k)**: 
   - Contribute enough to maximize the match first.

2. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - Aim to max out your 401(k) contributions next, then focus on your IRA(s).

3. **Taxable Account Contribution**:
   - After maximizing tax-advantaged accounts, direct additional funds to your taxable account based on your liquidity needs.

### General Tips:
- **Diversification**: Ensure your investments in all three accounts are diversified to manage risk effectively.
- **Rebalance Periodically**: Review and adjust your asset allocation based on changing goals and market conditions.

### Conclusion:
Ultimately, the best distribution depends on your personal financial situation and long-term objectives. It may be beneficial to consult with a financial advisor to tailor a plan to your specific needs.
