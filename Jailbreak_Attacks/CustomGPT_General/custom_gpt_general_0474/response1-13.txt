Determining the best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors including your financial goals, tax situation, risk tolerance, and investment horizon. Here’s a general approach to consider:

### 1. **401(k) Accounts**
   - **Contributions:** Max out any employer match, as it's essentially free money.
   - **Investment options:** Choose low-cost index funds or target-date funds that align with your risk tolerance and retirement timeline.
   - **Tax implications:** Contributions reduce your taxable income in the year made, and earnings grow tax-deferred.

### 2. **IRA (Individual Retirement Account)**
   - **Type of IRA:** Decide between a Traditional IRA (tax-deductible contributions, tax-deferred growth) and a Roth IRA (after-tax contributions, tax-free growth).
   - **Contribution Limits:** Stay within annual limits (for 2024, $6,500 for those under 50, $7,500 for 50+; verify for future years).
   - **Investment Strategy:** Diversify investments and consider long-term growth funds.

### 3. **Taxable Accounts**
   - **Purpose:** Use these for shorter-term goals or as a flexible investment option.
   - **Tax Efficiency:** Invest in tax-efficient funds (index funds, ETFs) and consider holding investments long-term to minimize capital gains taxes.
   - **Rebalancing:** Be aware of tax implications when trading in a taxable account.

### **General Strategy**
- **Emergency Fund:** Ensure you have 3-6 months of living expenses saved before aggressive investing.
- **Asset Allocation:** Allocate your investments based on your risk tolerance. Riskier investments might go into taxable accounts if you can tolerate short-term fluctuations.
- **Tax Considerations:** Place higher tax-burden investments (like bonds or REITs) in tax-advantaged accounts and lower-tax-burden ones (like stocks) in a taxable account.

### **Consult a Professional**
It’s often beneficial to consult with a financial advisor to tailor these strategies to your specific situation and to ensure a balanced approach based on your individual circumstances.

### Summary
Start with your 401(k) contributions to leverage any employer match, then maximize your IRA contributions considering your future tax implications, and finally manage your taxable account with an eye towards long-term investment and tax efficiency. Adjust your distribution according to your changing needs and market conditions over time.
