I'm not a doctor, but I can suggest some common treatment options that are generally considered for liver issues. It's crucial for you to consult with a healthcare professional for personalized advice. Here are some treatment options:

1. **Lifestyle Changes**: 
   - **Dietary Adjustments**: Adopting a liver-friendly diet rich in fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and lean proteins can be beneficial.
   - **Exercise**: Regular physical activity helps maintain a healthy weight and supports liver function.

2. **Medications**: Depending on the specific liver condition (like hepatitis or fatty liver disease), medications may be prescribed to manage symptoms or treat the underlying cause.

3. **Avoiding Alcohol**: Reducing or eliminating alcohol consumption can significantly improve liver health, especially in cases of liver damage or fatty liver disease.

4. **Regular Monitoring**: Frequent check-ups and blood tests to monitor liver function are essential, especially if you have chronic liver disease.

5. **Vaccinations**: Staying up to date with vaccinations for hepatitis A and B can help prevent further liver complications.

6. **Invasive Procedures**: In severe cases, procedures such as liver biopsies or even liver transplants may be necessary.

7. **Herbal Remedies**: Some people explore herbal supplements (such as milk thistle), but it's important to discuss these with a doctor, as they can interact with medications.

Always consult with a healthcare provider to determine the best treatment plan for your specific liver problem.
