To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Tax Efficiency**:
   - **401(k)**: Utilize this account for higher contributions, especially if your employer offers a match. It's typically pre-tax, so it can reduce your taxable income.
   - **IRA (Traditional vs. Roth)**: 
     - **Traditional IRA**: Good for those wanting immediate tax breaks. Contributions may be tax-deductible, but withdrawals are taxed as income.
     - **Roth IRA**: Ideal for tax-free growth if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement. Contributions are made with after-tax dollars.

2. **Investment Choices**:
   - **401(k)**: Limited investment options, often in mutual funds. Consider a well-diversified mix.
   - **IRA**: More investment freedom. Combine stocks, bonds, ETFs, and mutual funds for diversification.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Use this for investments that yield capital gains or income, as you’ll pay taxes on these gains yearly.

3. **Withdrawal Strategy**:
   - Plan your withdrawals based on the tax impact; typically, tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) and IRAs) should be tapped later in retirement to minimize annual tax burdens.

4. **Keep Emergency Funds**: Maintain a liquidity strategy in taxable accounts for emergencies while maximizing tax-deferred growth in the 401(k) and IRA.

5. **Contributions According to Goals**:
   - Maximize contributions to retirement accounts first, especially if there are employer matches, before investing heavily in taxable accounts.

### Example Distribution Approach:
- **401(k)**: Max out employer match and aim to contribute the annual limit.
- **IRA**: Contribute the maximum allowable (choose Traditional or Roth based on your tax situation).
- **Taxable Accounts**: Invest any additional savings here, focusing on assets that offer favorable tax treatment.

### Final Considerations:
- **Age and Retirement Timeline**: Younger investors might prefer Roth accounts for growth, while those closer to retirement could lean towards tax-deferred accounts for immediate benefits.
- **Consult a Financial Advisor**: Tailor your strategy based on personal financial goals, income level, and market conditions.

Adjust these allocations periodically based on your financial situation, retirement timeline, and life changes.
