To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

### 1. **Understand Account Types**
   - **401(k)**: Often has employer matching, tax advantages for retirement savings, but limited investment choices.
   - **IRA**: Offers tax-deductible contributions (Traditional IRA) or tax-free withdrawals (Roth IRA) depending on the type.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: No tax advantages, but flexibility with withdrawals and investment choices.

### 2. **Maximize Contributions**
   - **401(k)**: Aim to contribute enough to get the full employer match as it’s essentially "free money." Max out contributions if possible.
   - **IRA**: Contribute the maximum to take advantage of tax benefits. Consider a Roth IRA if you expect your tax rate to be higher in retirement.

### 3. **Assess Your Tax Situation**
   - Prioritize accounts based on your current and expected future tax rates. For instance, if you’re in a lower tax bracket now than you anticipate being in retirement, a Roth IRA might be beneficial.

### 4. **Diversification of Investments**
   - Keep a mix of stocks, bonds, and other assets in all accounts, but consider tax efficiency:
     - **Tax-inefficient investments** (like bonds) can be placed in tax-advantaged accounts (401(k), IRA).
     - **Tax-efficient investments** (like index funds) can be held in taxable accounts.

### 5. **Withdrawal Strategy**
   - Plan how you’ll withdraw funds in retirement. Generally, tax-advantaged accounts (401(k), IRA) should be tapped last to allow for tax-free growth as long as possible.

### Summary
1. Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match.
2. Max out your IRA contributions based on your tax strategy.
3. Use taxable accounts for flexibility and holding tax-efficient investments.

### Additional Considerations
- Review regularly and adjust as needed based on changes in income, tax laws, and retirement goals.
- Consult with a financial advisor to tailor a strategy that fits your specific financial situation and objectives.
