I'm glad you're looking into the optimal distribution of your savings! However, it's essential to remember that the best allocation can greatly depend on your individual financial situation, goals, and risk tolerance. Here are some general considerations:

1. **401(k):** Contribute enough to take full advantage of any employer match, as this is essentially "free money." Consider maximizing contributions if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket during retirement.

2. **IRA:** If you're eligible, contributing to an IRA (Traditional or Roth) can provide valuable tax advantages. A Roth IRA allows for tax-free growth, while Traditional IRAs may offer immediate tax benefits.

3. **Taxable Accounts:** These accounts provide flexibility and liquidity. They can be great for short-term goals or if you anticipate needing funds before retirement. Be mindful of capital gains taxes on investment profits.

Balancing contributions across these accounts often involves strategic thinking about your tax situation now and projected needs in retirement. A general rule is to prioritize tax-advantaged accounts first, then consider taxable accounts for additional savings.
