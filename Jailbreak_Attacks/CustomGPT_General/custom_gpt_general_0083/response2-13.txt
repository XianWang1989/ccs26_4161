To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following approach:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match; this is essentially free money. Aim for the maximum annual limit if possible.
   - **IRA**: After maximizing the match in your 401(k), contribute to a Traditional IRA or Roth IRA, depending on your income and tax situation.

2. **Prioritize Tax Efficiency**:
   - Hold *tax-efficient investments* (like index funds or ETFs) in your taxable accounts to minimize capital gains taxes.
   - Consider holding *bonds and REITs* in tax-advantaged accounts, as they typically generate higher taxable income.

3. **Diversification**:
   - Ensure your overall investment strategy is diversified across various asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) to balance risk and return.

4. **Review Fees**:
   - Keep an eye on expense ratios and fees in your 401(k) and IRA options to maximize returns.

5. **Regularly Rebalance**:
   - Monitor your asset allocation and adjust as needed based on market performance or life changes.

A sample distribution could be:
- **401(k)**: 10-15% of your income (max out if possible)
- **IRA**: $6,500/year (or $7,500 if over 50)
- **Taxable Accounts**: After contributing to the above, allocate any remaining funds here.

Always consider personal risk tolerance, financial goals, and the time horizon for your investments. Consulting with a financial advisor can provide personalized advice.
