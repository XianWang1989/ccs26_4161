Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here’s a structured approach:

### 1. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match first, as this is essentially free money.
   - **IRA**: Max out your IRA contributions if you can (especially if it’s a Roth IRA for tax-free growth).

### 2. **Consider Your Tax Bracket**:
   - **Traditional IRA or 401(k)**: These can be beneficial if you expect to be in a lower tax bracket in retirement.
   - **Roth IRA**: Ideal if you anticipate being in a higher tax bracket in retirement, as withdrawals are tax-free.

### 3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - Use this for investments that may generate lower taxes, like index funds or ETFs, which are more tax-efficient.
   - Keep money for short-term goals here, as it’s easily accessible without penalties.

### 4. **Asset Allocation**:
   - **Stocks vs. Bonds**: Generally, place more aggressive investments (like stocks) in tax-advantaged accounts to benefit from tax-free growth, and consider more stable investments in taxable accounts to manage tax implications.

### 5. **Rebalance Regularly**:
   - Monitor and adjust your allocations based on performance and changes in your financial goals.

### Example Allocation:
- **401(k)**: 10-15% up to the match.
- **IRA**: Max contribution (currently $6,500 annually, or $7,500 if over 50).
- **Taxable Accounts**: Allocate remaining funds, focusing on long-term growth.

### Final Thoughts:
Consult with a financial advisor to tailor this strategy to your specific situation, especially regarding your investment time frame and risk tolerance.
