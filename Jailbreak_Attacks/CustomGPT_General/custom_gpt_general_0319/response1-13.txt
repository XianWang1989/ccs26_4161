To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider these strategies:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match; this is essentially "free money."
   - **IRA**: If eligible, contribute to a Roth IRA for tax-free growth and withdrawals, or a traditional IRA for tax-deductible contributions.

2. **Prioritize High-Interest Debt**: Pay off any high-interest debt before investing. 

3. **Consider Contribution Limits**:
   - Stay aware of the annual contribution limits for each account type.

4. **Tax Efficiency**:
   - Place tax-efficient investments (like index funds or stocks) in taxable accounts.
   - Hold bonds or high-turnover mutual funds in tax-advantaged accounts to minimize tax implications.

5. **Rebalance Regularly**: Adjust your asset allocation as needed, considering risk tolerance and investment goals.

6. **Emergency Fund**: Ensure you have an emergency fund separate from your investment accounts.

7. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: For tailored advice based on your specific situation and goals.

By using these strategies, you can optimize your investment distribution for growth and tax efficiency.
