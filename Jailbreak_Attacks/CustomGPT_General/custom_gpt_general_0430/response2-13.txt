Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and time horizon. Here are some general guidelines:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a match on your 401(k) contributions, aim to contribute enough to take full advantage of this benefit first.

2. **Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: Prioritize contributions to your 401(k), especially if you’re in a higher tax bracket, as contributions lower your taxable income.
   - **IRA**: Consider contributing to a Traditional or Roth IRA. A Traditional IRA provides tax-deductible contributions, while a Roth IRA allows for tax-free withdrawals in retirement.

3. **Debt Considerations**: If you have high-interest debt, it might be wise to focus on paying that down before investing significantly in retirement accounts.

4. **Diversification**: Ensure your investments are diversified across different asset classes in all accounts. This helps manage risk.

5. **Taxable Accounts**: Use taxable accounts for investments with lower turnover and tax implications (like index funds) to minimize capital gains taxes.

6. **Annual Contributions**: Keep track of the contribution limits for each account to maximize your investment potential each year.

7. **Adjustments Over Time**: Reassess your allocation periodically based on changing financial goals, market conditions, and tax laws.

Consulting with a financial advisor can provide personalized strategies tailored to your specific situation.
