Distributing your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some general strategies to consider:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**
   - **401(k):** Contribute enough to get any employer match first; this is free money. Assess if you can reach the maximum contribution limit ($22,500 for 2024, or $30,000 if you're over 50).
   - **IRA:** If eligible, also contribute to a traditional or Roth IRA. The contribution limit is $6,500 for 2024 ($7,500 if over 50). Roth IRAs are great for tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement.

### 2. **Consider Tax Implications**
   - **Taxable Accounts:** These accounts can be beneficial for flexibility and liquidity. However, you'll pay taxes on dividends and capital gains, so reserve them for investments that generate relatively low taxable income (like index funds).
   - Use tax-efficient investment strategies (like tax-loss harvesting) to manage tax liabilities.

### 3. **Diversify Investments**
   - **Tax-Deferred Accounts:** Ideal for growth-oriented investments since you won’t pay taxes on gains until withdrawal (401(k) and traditional IRA).
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Consider holding more conservative investments here to minimize potential tax bills from high turnover rates.

### 4. **Reassess Annually**
   - Regularly review your allocations and adjust as needed based on life changes, market conditions, and retirement goals.

### 5. **Consult a Financial Advisor**
   - A professional can provide personalized advice tailored to your specific financial situation and goals.

### Summary
Start by maximizing contributions to your 401(k) and IRA. After reaching those limits, allocate any additional funds to taxable accounts, keeping taxes in mind. This balanced approach can help you optimize growth while managing tax liabilities over time.
