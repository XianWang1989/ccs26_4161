Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts effectively depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here’s a general approach:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k):**
   - Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match. This is essentially free money.

### 2. **Contribute to IRA:**
   - Consider contributing to a Traditional IRA or Roth IRA. The choice depends on your income level and current vs. expected future tax rates.
   - Max out the IRA contribution limit if possible.

### 3. **Tax-Deferred Growth:**
   - Continue to prioritize contributions to your 401(k) and IRA accounts since they offer tax advantages (tax-deferred growth for Traditional accounts).

### 4. **Taxable Accounts for Extra Savings:**
   - After maxing out tax-advantaged accounts, use taxable accounts for additional investing.
   - Be mindful of capital gains taxes when investing in taxable accounts.

### 5. **Diversify Investments:**
   - Ensure a diversified portfolio across all accounts, reflecting your risk tolerance and investment goals.

### 6. **Rebalance Regularly:**
   - Review and rebalance your portfolio regularly to stay aligned with your financial goals and risk tolerance.

### 7. **Consider Withdrawal Strategy:**
   - Plan for how you’ll withdraw funds in retirement; understanding tax implications is critical.

### 8. **Consult a Financial Advisor:**
   - For personalized advice based on your specific situation and goals, it may be beneficial to consult with a financial advisor.

Focusing on maximizing tax advantages while maintaining flexibility through taxable accounts will help optimize your overall investment strategy.
