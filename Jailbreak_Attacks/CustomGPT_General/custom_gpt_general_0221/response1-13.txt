Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and timeline. Here are some general guidelines:

### 1. **Understand Each Account Type**
   - **401(k)**: Employer-sponsored retirement plan, often with matching contributions. Contributions reduce your taxable income.
   - **IRA**: Individual Retirement Account with tax advantages (traditional IRA offers tax deferral, Roth IRA offers tax-free withdrawals).
   - **Taxable Accounts**: No tax advantages but offer flexibility. You can withdraw anytime but are subject to capital gains tax on profits.

### 2. **Prioritize Contributions**
   - **Maximize 401(k) Contributions**: Aim to contribute enough to get the full employer match first, as it’s essentially free money.
   - **Contribute to an IRA**: If eligible, especially a Roth IRA for tax-free growth if you're within income limits.
   - **Use Taxable Accounts for Additional Savings**: After maxing out tax-advantaged accounts, consider investing in taxable accounts for liquidity and additional investment opportunities.

### 3. **Consider Tax Implications**
   - **Higher Tax Brackets**: Focus on tax-deferred accounts for more significant contributions.
   - **Lower Tax Brackets**: You may benefit from Roth accounts for tax-free growth.

### 4. **Balance Asset Allocation**
   - Ensure each account type has a diversified investment strategy aligned with your risk tolerance and timeline.

### 5. **Assess Your Timeline**
   - Short-term goals may warrant more savings in taxable accounts while long-term goals can leverage tax-advantaged growth.

### 6. **Regularly Review and Adjust**
   - Monitor your investments and contributions annually to adapt to changing financial situations, market conditions, and retirement goals.

Consulting with a financial advisor can provide personalized strategies that best fit your individual circumstances.
