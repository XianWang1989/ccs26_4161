To optimize your distribution among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

### 1. **Tax Efficiency**
   - **401(k)**: Max out contributions, especially if there's an employer match. This account grows tax-deferred until withdrawal.
   - **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**: If eligible, contribute to a Roth IRA for tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Use for investments that generate lower tax liabilities, such as index funds or ETFs.

### 2. **Investment Goals**
   - **Short-term Goals**: Use taxable accounts for liquidity.
   - **Long-term Goals**: Focus on tax-advantaged accounts for growth.

### 3. **Diversification**
   - Spread your investments across various asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) in each account type aligning with your risk tolerance.

### 4. **Withdrawal Strategy**
   - Plan withdrawals based on tax implications; consider withdrawing from taxable accounts first to allow tax-advantaged accounts to grow longer.

### 5. **Annual Review**
   - Regularly assess your distribution based on changes in tax laws, financial goals, and market conditions.

### Example Allocation
- **401(k)**: Max contribution (up to IRS limit).
- **IRA**: Contribute to either Traditional or Roth based on tax situation.
- **Taxable Account**: Fill gaps based on liquidity needs or additional investments.

Consulting with a financial advisor can also provide personalized insights tailored to your specific situation.
