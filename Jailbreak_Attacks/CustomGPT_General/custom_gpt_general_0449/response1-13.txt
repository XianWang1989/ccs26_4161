To effectively distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider these factors:

### 1. **Tax Benefits:**
   - **401(k):** Offers tax-deferred growth. Prioritize contributions to get any employer match.
   - **IRA:** Traditional IRAs provide tax deductions, while Roth IRAs offer tax-free withdrawals in retirement. Choose based on your current vs. expected future tax rate.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Best for flexible access to funds without penalties, but you incur taxes on capital gains and dividends.

### 2. **Investment Goals:**
   - **Long-term growth:** Maximize tax-advantaged accounts first (401(k) and IRA).
   - **Short-term needs:** Use taxable accounts for liquidity.

### 3. **Contribution Limits:**
   - Be aware of annual limits for 401(k)s and IRAs. Ensure you're maximizing these first.

### 4. **Diversification:**
   - Diversify investments across all account types to balance risk.

### 5. **Withdrawal Strategy:**
   - Plan how you will withdraw from these accounts in retirement to minimize taxes.

### Suggested Distribution Strategy:
1. Max out 401(k) contributions up to any employer match.
2. Contribute to an IRA (considering your tax situation).
3. Use any additional savings for a taxable account.

### Periodic Review:
Regularly reevaluate your allocation based on life changes, market conditions, and retirement goals. Consulting with a financial advisor can also provide tailored guidance.
