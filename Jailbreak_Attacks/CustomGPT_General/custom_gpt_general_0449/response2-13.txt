Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment horizon. Here are some strategic considerations:

### 1. **Prioritize Contributions**
   - **401(k):** Contribute enough to get any employer match. This is essentially "free money."
   - **IRA:** Max out contributions to a Traditional or Roth IRA if eligible, as they offer tax advantages.

### 2. **Tax Efficiency**
   - **401(k):** Contributions are pre-tax (Traditional) or after-tax (Roth); best for long-term growth with tax deferral.
   - **IRA:** Similar benefits as 401(k), with additional flexibility in investment choices.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Use these for investments with favorable tax treatment (like long-term capital gains), and consider tax-efficient funds to minimize capital gains distributions.

### 3. **Asset Allocation**
   - **Tax-Advantaged Accounts (401(k) and IRA):** Consider holding higher-growth investments here, as taxes are deferred.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Hold more stable, income-generating investments (like bonds) to manage tax impacts.

### 4. **Withdrawal Strategy**
   - Plan how you will withdraw funds in retirement. Taxable accounts can provide flexibility, while tax-advantaged accounts may have penalties if accessed early.

### Example Allocation
- **401(k):** 20-25% of total contributions (max out for match)
- **IRA:** 15-20% (max out if possible)
- **Taxable Accounts:** Remaining funds based on your liquidity needs and investment goals.

### Regular Review
Reassess your allocation annually based on changes in income, tax laws, and financial goals.

### Conclusion
Consult with a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your unique situation.
