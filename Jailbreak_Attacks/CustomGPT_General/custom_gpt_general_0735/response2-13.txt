Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, age, and investment strategy. Here are some general guidelines:

1. **Maximize Employer Match (401(k))**: If your employer offers a matching contribution, contribute enough to get the full match. This is essentially "free money."

2. **Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**:
   - **401(k)**: Prioritize contributions to your 401(k) up to the match limit. This reduces your taxable income.
   - **IRA**: If eligible, contribute to a traditional or Roth IRA, focusing on your long-term retirement needs. Roth IRAs offer tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement.

3. **Consider Contribution Limits**:
   - For 2024, the 401(k) limit is $23,000 ($30,000 if age 50 or older).
   - The IRA limit is $6,500 ($7,500 if age 50 or older). 

4. **Assess Your Tax Situation**:
   - Consider your current tax bracket and expected future bracket. If you anticipate being in a higher tax bracket in retirement, a Roth IRA might be beneficial.
   - Taxable accounts can be used for investments that generate capital gains, which can have favorable tax treatment.

5. **Diversification**: 
   - Ensure a diversified investment portfolio across these accounts. Different accounts can be used for different investment strategies depending on time horizon and risk tolerance.

6. **Withdrawal Strategy**: 
   - Plan how you'll withdraw funds in retirement. Generally, it might be advantageous to withdraw from taxable accounts first, followed by tax-deferred accounts (like a 401(k)) and finally tax-free accounts (like a Roth IRA) to minimize taxes.

7. **Ongoing Review and Adjustments**: 
   - Regularly review your allocations and adjust based on market performance, changes in your life situation, and retirement goals.

Consulting with a financial advisor can also provide personalized guidance based on your specific financial situation.
