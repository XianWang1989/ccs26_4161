Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some general guidelines:

### 1. **401(k) Contributions**:
   - **Employer Match**: Always contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match, as this is essentially free money.
   - **Tax Benefits**: Contributions reduce your taxable income, which can be beneficial if you're in a high tax bracket.

### 2. **IRA Contributions**:
   - **Tax Advantages**: An IRA (Traditional or Roth) offers tax advantages similar to a 401(k). A Traditional IRA may provide immediate tax benefits, while a Roth IRA allows for tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement.
   - **Flexible Investment Options**: IRAs often provide a wider range of investment options compared to a 401(k).

### 3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - **Liquidity**: If you need access to funds before retirement, consider allocating money to taxable accounts. These can be important for short-term goals.
   - **Capital Gains**: Be mindful of how capital gains tax may apply on investments held in taxable accounts. Long-term investments can be advantageous due to lower tax rates.

### 4. **General Strategy**:
   - **Emergency Fund**: Prioritize having an emergency fund before heavily investing, as this can prevent the need to pull from retirement accounts.
   - **Diversification**: Keep a diversified portfolio across all accounts to manage risk effectively.
   - **Lifecycle Goals**: Consider using your taxable account for shorter-term goals and retirement accounts for long-term growth.

### 5. **Annual Review**:
   - Regularly assess your allocation based on changes in income, expenses, and financial goals to ensure your investment strategy remains aligned with your objectives.

### Conclusion:
Ultimately, the right mix depends on your personal circumstances. If possible, consult a financial advisor to tailor a strategy that fits your specific situation and goals.
