The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts typically depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some considerations for each account type:

1. **401(k)**:
   - **Employer Match**: Contribute enough to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money.
   - **Tax Benefits**: Contributions are made pre-tax, reducing your taxable income for the year. This can be especially beneficial if you expect to be in a lower tax bracket upon retirement.

2. **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**:
   - **Tax Considerations**: With a Traditional IRA, you may get a tax deduction now, while a Roth IRA allows for tax-free withdrawals in retirement, provided certain conditions are met.
   - **Contribution Limits**: Be aware of contribution limits for both types of IRAs and consider maxing out if possible, particularly if you're eligible for a Roth IRA due to its tax-free withdrawal benefit.

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - **Flexibility**: These accounts offer the most flexibility for withdrawals without penalties. They are ideal for goals that may occur before retirement.
   - **Investing Gains**: Investments held long-term may be taxed at lower capital gains rates compared to ordinary income tax rates, making them a good option for growth investments.

### Suggested Distribution Strategy:
- Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to capture any employer match.
- Next, max out your IRA contributions (preferably maxing out a Roth IRA if eligible).
- After that, allocate any additional funds to your taxable account for greater flexibility and liquidity.

### General Tips:
- Consider your time horizon for investing; longer-term goals may lean heavier on tax-advantaged accounts.
- Regularly review and adjust your contributions as your financial situation changes.
- Consult with a financial advisor to tailor your approach based on your specific needs and goals.
