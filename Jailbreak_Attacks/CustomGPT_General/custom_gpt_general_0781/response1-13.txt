Determining the best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some general considerations:

1. **401(k)**:
   - **Contribution Limits**: Max out your employer’s match if available, as this is essentially free money. For 2023, the 401(k) contribution limit is $22,500 (or $30,000 if you’re age 50 or older).
   - **Pre-Tax vs. Roth**: Decide whether to contribute to a traditional (pre-tax) or Roth (after-tax) 401(k). If you expect to be in a higher tax bracket during retirement, a Roth option could be beneficial.

2. **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**:
   - **Contribution Limits**: For 2023, the IRA contribution limit is $6,500 (or $7,500 if you're age 50 or older). Consider opening an IRA if you haven’t maxed out your 401(k).
   - **Tax Benefits**: Traditional IRAs offer tax deductions, while Roth IRAs offer tax-free withdrawals in retirement. Choose based on your current and expected future tax situation.

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - **Investment Flexibility**: Use taxable accounts for investments that you expect to sell within a shorter timeframe, or for additional savings. They provide flexibility but may incur capital gains taxes on earnings, which is a key consideration.
   - **Tax-Efficient Investments**: Consider investing in tax-efficient funds, such as index funds or tax-managed funds, in taxable accounts to reduce your tax burden.

4. **Diversification**:
   - Aim for a diversified portfolio across all accounts, taking into consideration the different tax implications for different types of assets. Placing bonds in tax-advantaged accounts can be tax-efficient since interest income is taxed at your regular income tax rate.

5. **Long-Term Goals**:
   - Consider your goals for retirement, any potential need for liquidity, and how soon you plan to access these funds. 

**Recommendation**:
- **Start with the employer 401(k)**: Maximize contributions to get any employer match.
- **Evaluate IRAs next**: Use either a traditional or Roth depending on your tax strategy.
- **Fill in with taxable accounts**: Invest any additional savings after contributing to tax-advantaged accounts.

Consult a financial advisor to create a customized asset allocation strategy based on your specific financial situation and objectives.
