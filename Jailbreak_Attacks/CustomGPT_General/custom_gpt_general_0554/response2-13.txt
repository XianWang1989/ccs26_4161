Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here are some general strategies to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**: Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match, if available. It's essentially free money.

2. **Contribute to an IRA**: If you're eligible, consider contributing to a traditional IRA or Roth IRA. Roth IRAs offer tax-free withdrawals in retirement, which can be beneficial if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket later.

3. **Focus on Tax-Advantaged Accounts**: Prioritize contributions to tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) and IRA) before investing in taxable accounts, as these can provide tax benefits and help your investments grow tax-deferred or tax-free.

4. **Consider Your Tax Situation**: If you're in a high tax bracket now, traditional accounts (like a traditional IRA or 401(k)) may offer more immediate tax benefits. Conversely, if you expect your income to rise significantly, prioritizing Roth accounts can save on taxes in retirement.

5. **Diversify Investments**: Within each account type, ensure you're diversifying your investments to manage risk. Consider a mix of stocks, bonds, and other assets based on your risk tolerance and investment horizon.

6. **Evaluate Your Retirement Timeline**: If you're closer to retirement, you might want to be more conservative and focus on capital preservation. If you’re younger, you might opt for more aggressive investments to maximize growth.

7. **Regularly Review and Adjust**: Periodically review your investment allocations and adjust based on changes in your financial situation, market conditions, or retirement goals.

Before making any decisions, you may also want to consult with a financial advisor who can provide personalized guidance based on your unique circumstances.
