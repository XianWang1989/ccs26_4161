Distributing your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some strategies to consider:

### 1. **Maximize Tax Advantages:**
   - **401(k):** Contribute enough to get the full employer match, as this is essentially free money. Consider maxing out if you can.
   - **IRA (Traditional or Roth):** Max out contributions based on the tax benefits that suit your situation. Roth IRAs provide tax-free growth, while traditional IRAs may offer upfront tax deductions.

### 2. **Evaluate Your Tax Bracket:**
   - If you are in a higher tax bracket, prioritize tax-deferred accounts (401(k) and traditional IRA) to minimize immediate taxes.
   - If you're in a lower tax bracket now, consider contributing to a Roth IRA for tax-free withdrawals in retirement.

### 3. **Time Horizon:**
   - Short-term goals (1-5 years): Focus on taxable accounts for easier access without penalties.
   - Long-term goals (5+ years): Utilize tax-advantaged accounts to maximize growth potential.

### 4. **Investment Options:**
   - Compare the investment options available in each account. Some 401(k) plans offer limited choices, while IRAs and taxable accounts provide more flexibility.

### 5. **Diversification:**
   - Ensure your investments are diversified across all accounts. Avoid putting all your high-risk investments in just one account.

### 6. **Emergency Fund:**
   - Make sure to have sufficient liquid savings in a separate account for emergencies before heavily investing.

### Sample Allocation Strategy:
- **401(k):** 15-20% of income (max if possible to get the match)
- **IRA:** Contribute the maximum allowed (currently $6,500 or $7,500 if over 50)
- **Taxable Accounts:** Allocate any additional savings for flexible access and investment.

### Periodic Review:
Regularly reassess your allocations based on market changes, life events, or shifts in income.

### Conclusion:
A balanced approach tailored to your personal financial situation and goals will serve you best. Consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized advice.
