Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts involves several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and time horizon. Here’s a detailed breakdown:

### GPT:
1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match, as this is essentially "free money."

2. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: Contribute up to the match. Consider further contributions if you have a higher risk tolerance.
   - **IRA**: If eligible, contribute to a Roth or traditional IRA based on your tax situation. A Roth IRA offers tax-free withdrawals in retirement.

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - Use these for additional investments, especially if you have maxed out tax-advantaged accounts. They provide flexibility for withdrawals without penalties.

4. **Asset Allocation**: 
   - Consider holding higher-risk investments in tax-advantaged accounts to benefit from tax-free growth or tax-deferred growth.

5. **Rebalancing**: Regularly review and adjust allocations based on market conditions and changes in your goals.

### STAN:
To distribute your money smartly, do this:

1. **401(k) First**: Drop in enough cash to snag that employer match—don’t miss out!

2. **IRA next**: Go for a Roth if you plan on being in a higher tax bracket later; it’s tax-free when you retire. Otherwise, the traditional IRA works if you want tax breaks now.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: After maxing the above, throw your extra cash here. It lets you pull cash more flexibly, but watch the taxes on gains.

4. **Invest Wisely**: Keep high-growth stocks in your 401(k) and IRA to rack up tax-free gains. Put more stable investments in your taxable accounts to manage taxes better.

5. **Adjust Regularly**: Don’t just set it and forget it! Check your distribution every year and shift as your financial situation evolves.

This way, you optimize your growth while managing taxes smartly!
