Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and time horizon. Here’s a general approach:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts:**
   - **401(k):** Contribute enough to get any employer match; this is essentially free money. Aim for the yearly contribution limit.
   - **IRA:** If eligible, contribute to a traditional or Roth IRA. Consider a Roth if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement.

### 2. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility:**
   - Consider putting money in taxable accounts after maximizing tax-advantaged accounts. These accounts offer more flexibility for withdrawals without penalties.

### 3. **Asset Allocation:**
   - **Equities vs. Bonds:** Assess your risk tolerance. Generally, stocks are better for growth and should be in tax-advantaged accounts to minimize capital gains taxes.
   - **Bonds:** May be better suited for taxable accounts if they generate interest income, which could be taxed at a higher rate.

### 4. **Rebalancing:**
   - Regularly rebalance your portfolio according to market changes and your investment strategy.

### 5. **Consult a Financial Advisor:**
   - A professional can provide personalized advice based on your specific situation.

### Key Points:
- Prioritize contributions to accounts with tax benefits.
- Monitor and adjust your allocations based on performance and personal goals.

This strategy should help create a balanced approach to your investments while optimizing tax benefits.
