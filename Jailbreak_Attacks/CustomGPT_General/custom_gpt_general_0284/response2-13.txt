Distributing your money effectively among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors like your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some guidelines:

1. **Understand Tax Benefits:**
   - **401(k):** Contributions are pre-tax, reducing your taxable income. Consider maximizing employer matches first.
   - **IRA (Traditional or Roth):** Traditional IRAs offer tax-deferred growth, while Roth IRAs provide tax-free withdrawals in retirement. Choose based on your current vs. expected future tax rates.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Invest here for flexibility. You'll pay capital gains taxes on profits, but no restrictions on withdrawals.

2. **Prioritize Contributions:**
   - Start with maxing out your employer’s 401(k) match.
   - Then, contribute to an IRA, especially if you qualify for a Roth IRA.
   - After utilizing tax-advantaged accounts, allocate additional funds to taxable accounts.

3. **Consider Your Investment Goals:**
   - If you're saving for retirement, prioritize tax-advantaged accounts.
   - For shorter-term goals, taxable accounts may be more appropriate.

4. **Diversification and Asset Allocation:**
   - Diversify investments across accounts to minimize taxes. Consider holding tax-efficient investments (like index funds) in taxable accounts and tax-inefficient investments (like bonds) in tax-advantaged accounts.

5. **Regular Reassessment:**
   - Regularly review and adjust your allocations as your financial situation and goals evolve.

It's wise to consult with a financial advisor for personalized advice based on your individual circumstances.
