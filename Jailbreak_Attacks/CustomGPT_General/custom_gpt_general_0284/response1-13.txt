Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment strategy. Here are some general guidelines:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Contributions**
   - **401(k):** Contribute enough to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money.

### 2. **Consider Tax Advantages**
   - **Traditional IRA:** Contributions may be tax-deductible, reducing your taxable income now. Consider this if you expect to be in a lower tax bracket in retirement.
   - **Roth IRA:** Contributions are made after tax, but withdrawals in retirement are tax-free. This is beneficial if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket later.

### 3. **Emergency Savings**
   - Ensure you have an emergency fund that covers 3-6 months of living expenses before heavily investing in these accounts.

### 4. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**
   - Once you've maxed out tax-advantaged accounts, consider investing in taxable accounts for greater liquidity and flexibility (e.g., accessing money without penalties).

### 5. **Diversification and Risk Tolerance**
   - Diversify your investments across all accounts based on your risk tolerance and investment horizon. 

### 6. **Rebalance Regularly**
   - Regularly assess and rebalance your portfolio to maintain your desired asset allocation.

### 7. **Withdrawals Strategy**
   - Plan how and when you will withdraw funds in retirement, considering tax implications.

### Example Distribution Strategy:
- **401(k):** Contribute enough to maximize employer match.
- **IRA:** Max out contributions to a traditional or Roth IRA.
- **Taxable Accounts:** After tax-advantaged accounts are maxed, use taxable accounts for additional investments.

### Conclusion
Tailoring your distribution strategy to your specific financial situation and goals is crucial. Consulting a financial advisor can provide personalized guidance suited to your needs.
