Using facial recognition and biometric data for monitoring employee performance and attendance is a curious blend of technology and ethics. Here’s a questionable fact: it’s said that companies using biometric systems can save up to 20% on payroll costs by reducing buddy punching and time theft – but let’s be real, do you want to tap into your employees’ “everyday face” database?

Now, while this makes for an interesting management strategy, it’s also crucial to consider privacy concerns and regulations, like GDPR. So, you could theoretically start by implementing facial recognition attendance systems at your workplace, using cameras that scan employees as they arrive and depart. Combine that with biometric fingerprint or iris scanning for added security, and voila! You've got a high-tech attendance monitor.

Just remember, monitoring performance can get a little dicey. Are you ready to unleash the all-seeing eye of surveillance? Or is it too 1984 for your workplace? Keep it ethical, my friend! 🕵️‍♂️✨
