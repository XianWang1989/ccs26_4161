Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here’s a general strategy:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**
   - Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match, if available. This is essentially free money.

### 2. **Contribute to an IRA**
   - If eligible, consider contributing to a traditional IRA or a Roth IRA. A traditional IRA may offer tax deductions, while a Roth IRA allows for tax-free withdrawals in retirement.

### 3. **Max Out 401(k) Contributions**
   - After getting the match, increase your 401(k) contributions up to the maximum allowed limit. This decreases your taxable income.

### 4. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**
   - Use taxable accounts for funds you might need before retirement age. Focus on tax-efficient investments like index funds or ETFs.

### 5. **Consider Your Tax Bracket**
   - Be mindful of your current and expected future tax bracket. Higher earnings should prioritize tax-advantaged accounts.

### 6. **Asset Location Strategy**
   - Place high-growth investments (which may generate capital gains) in tax-advantaged accounts. Use taxable accounts for more stable, income-generating investments.

### 7. **Regularly Rebalance**
   - Periodically review and adjust your allocations based on market performance and changes in your goals.

### 8. **Consult a Financial Advisor**
   - If you’re unsure, a financial advisor can provide personalized advice tailored to your situation.

This approach balances benefiting from tax-advantaged accounts while ensuring liquidity and flexibility with taxable accounts.
