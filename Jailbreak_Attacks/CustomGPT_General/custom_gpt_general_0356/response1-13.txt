The best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some general guidelines:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**:
   - Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match, if available. This is essentially free money.

### 2. **Consider IRA Contributions**:
   - If eligible, contribute to a Traditional IRA or Roth IRA. Roth IRAs offer tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement, while Traditional IRAs provide tax deductions upfront.

### 3. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**:
   - Invest in taxable accounts for funds you may need before retirement. Consider using these accounts for short-term goals or more liquid investments.

### 4. **Asset Allocation**:
   - Diversify your investments according to your risk tolerance:
     - **401(k)**: Focus on growth-oriented funds, such as equities, if you’re in for the long term.
     - **IRA**: Choose investments based on your retirement timeline and risk profile.
     - **Taxable**: Utilize tax-efficient investments (like index funds or ETFs) to minimize taxable distributions.

### 5. **Tax Impact**:
   - Keep in mind the tax consequences of withdrawing from each type of account, especially when considering early withdrawals which may incur penalties.

### 6. **Regular Review & Rebalance**:
   - Periodically review your asset allocation and rebalance as needed to stay aligned with your financial goals.

### Conclusion:
Consulting a financial advisor can provide personalized advice tailored to your specific situation.
