To effectively distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider these strategies:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to receive any employer match, as this is free money.

2. **Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**: Prioritize contributions to your IRA (especially a Roth IRA if you're eligible), as it offers tax-free growth and tax-free withdrawals in retirement.

3. **Assess Contribution Limits**: Ensure you stay within annual contribution limits for each account:
   - 401(k): $22,500 (2023 limit for individuals under 50).
   - IRA: $6,500 (2023 limit for individuals under 50).

4. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**: After maximizing tax-advantaged accounts, consider investing in taxable accounts. This offers liquidity and flexibility, but be mindful of taxes on earnings and dividends.

5. **Diversify Investments**: Within each account, diversify your investments based on your risk tolerance and time horizon. Common allocations include stocks, bonds, and other assets.

6. **Rebalance Regularly**: Review and adjust your allocations periodically to ensure they align with your financial goals and market conditions.

7. **Consider Your Tax Situation**: Think about your current and expected future tax rates. You might prefer tax-deferred accounts if you anticipate being in a lower tax bracket during retirement.

Always consider consulting with a financial advisor to tailor a strategy to your specific needs and goals.
