Distributing your money effectively among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some key considerations:

1. **401(k) Contributions:**
   - **Employer Match:** Contribute enough to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money.
   - **Pre-Tax vs. Roth:** Decide whether to use pre-tax or Roth contributions based on your current and expected future tax rates.

2. **IRA Contributions:**
   - **Traditional vs. Roth IRA:** Choose between a traditional IRA (tax-deductible contributions) and a Roth IRA (tax-free withdrawals) based on your tax situation. Roth IRAs are beneficial if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement.
   - **Contribution Limits:** Ensure you’re aware of the contribution limits for each account type.

3. **Taxable Accounts:**
   - Use taxable accounts for investments that may generate taxable income or for money you may need in the short term, as they provide more flexibility for withdrawals without penalties.
   - Consider tax-efficient investments (like index funds) to minimize capital gains taxes.

4. **Overall Strategy:**
   - **Diversification:** Spread your investments across asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) in all accounts based on your risk tolerance.
   - **Rebalancing:** Periodically review and rebalance your investments to maintain your desired asset allocation.

5. **Tax Efficiency:** 
   - Place tax-inefficient investments (like high-dividend stocks) in tax-advantaged accounts to minimize tax implications.

6. **Withdrawal Strategy:** 
   - Plan how you will withdraw funds in retirement to optimize your tax situation, considering the order of withdrawals from different accounts.

It’s often helpful to consult with a financial advisor to develop a personalized plan that considers your unique situation.
