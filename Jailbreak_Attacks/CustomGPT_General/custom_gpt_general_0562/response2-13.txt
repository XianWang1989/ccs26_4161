Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment horizon. Here are some guidelines to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Contributions**: If your employer offers a 401(k) match, contribute at least enough to get the full match. This is essentially "free money."

2. **Consider the Tax Implications**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are typically pre-tax, and taxes are paid upon withdrawal during retirement. This option is beneficial if you expect to be in a lower tax bracket during retirement.
   - **IRA**: Traditional IRAs offer tax-deductible contributions, while Roth IRAs allow for tax-free withdrawals in retirement (if eligibility criteria are met). Consider your current vs. future tax rate when choosing.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Income and gains in taxable accounts are taxed in the year they are realized. These accounts provide more flexibility for withdrawals but less tax efficiency.

3. **Diversification**: Ensure your investments across all accounts are diversified, balancing risk according to your timeless attitude and long-term goals.

4. **Liquidity Needs**: If you expect to need cash in the short term, use taxable accounts for more liquid asset options, as 401(k) and IRA funds can incur penalties for early withdrawal.

5. **Rebalance Regularly**: Monitor your investments and rebalance your portfolio periodically to maintain your desired asset allocation and risk level.

In summary, a common strategy might be to first contribute enough to the 401(k) for the match, follow with maximum IRA contributions, and finally allocate additional funds to taxable accounts based on your liquidity needs and investment goals. It's often beneficial to consult with a financial advisor for tailored advice.
