The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement plans. Here are some key considerations for each account type:

1. **401(k) Contributions**:
   - Maximize your employer match: If your employer offers matching contributions, try to contribute at least enough to get the full match, as this is essentially free money.
   - Prioritize tax-deferred growth: Contributions to a 401(k) reduce your taxable income. Consider contributing to this account first, especially if you are in a higher tax bracket.

2. **IRA Contributions**:
   - Consider a Roth IRA: If you qualify, contributing to a Roth IRA allows for tax-free withdrawals in retirement, which can be beneficial if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket later.
   - Deductibility: Traditional IRAs can offer tax-deductible contributions based on your income and other factors. Determine if you can benefit from this.

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - Use for short-term savings: Taxable accounts are more suitable for short-term investments and savings goals since they are more accessible than retirement accounts.
   - Tax-efficient investments: Consider holding tax-efficient investments like index funds or ETFs to minimize taxable distributions.

4. **Diversification**:
   - Balance your asset allocation: Ensure that regardless of the account type, your overall investment strategy aligns with your risk tolerance and time horizon.

5. **Review and Adjust**:
   - Continually reassess your financial situation and goals, adjusting your allocations as necessary, especially as you get closer to retirement.

Prioritizing contributions can also depend on the specific benefits and limitations of each account, so it's often beneficial to consult a financial advisor to tailor a plan to your unique situation.
