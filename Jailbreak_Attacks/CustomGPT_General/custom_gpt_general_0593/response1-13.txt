Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors. Here are key considerations:

### 1. **401(k) Contributions**
   - **Employer Match**: Maximize contributions to get any employer matches, as this is free money.
   - **Contribution Limits**: For 2023, the limit is $22,500 ($30,000 if age 50+).

### 2. **IRA Contributions**
   - **Types**: Consider whether a Traditional IRA (tax-deductible) or Roth IRA (tax-free growth) suits your situation.
   - **Contribution Limits**: Limit is $6,500 ($7,500 if age 50+).

### 3. **Taxable Accounts**
   - Use these for short-term goals or needs, as they offer more flexibility.
   - Investment income will be taxed annually, unlike tax-advantaged accounts.

### Suggested Strategy:
- **Prioritize Contributions**:
  1. Max out your 401(k) up to the employer match.
  2. Max out an IRA (choose based on tax situation).
  3. Invest any additional funds in a taxable account.

### 4. **Considerations**:
   - **Tax Implications**: Consider your current and expected future tax bracket.
   - **Investment Goals**: Align investments with your time horizon (short-term vs. long-term).
   - **Diversification**: Ensure a diversified portfolio across all accounts to manage risk.

### 5. **Regular Review**:
   - Reassess your strategy annually based on changes in income, tax laws, and personal goals.

Consulting a financial advisor can also provide personalized advice tailored to your specific situation.
