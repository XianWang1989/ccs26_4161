### Revised Detailed Prompt

To determine the best distribution strategy for your investments, consider the following:

1. **Goals and Time Horizon**:
   - Define your financial goals (retirement, buying a house, etc.).
   - Determine your investment time frame.

2. **Account Types**:
   - **401(k)**: High contribution limits, potential employer matches, tax-deferred growth.
   - **IRA (Traditional/Roth)**: Tax advantages (deductions for contributions, tax-free growth for Roth).
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Flexibility, but subject to capital gains tax.

3. **Income Tax Considerations**:
   - Assess your current vs. expected future tax rate.
   - Prioritize tax-advantaged accounts if you anticipate a higher tax rate in retirement.

4. **Risk Tolerance**:
   - Evaluate your comfort with risk when allocating assets.
   - Diversify across asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) based on your risk profile.

5. **Contribution Limits**:
   - Be aware of contribution limits for each account type.

### Suggestions

- **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to receive any employer match before other accounts.
- **Utilize Roth Options**: If you qualify, consider a Roth IRA for tax-free growth and withdrawals.
- **Keep Emergency Funds**: Hold a portion in your taxable account for liquidity.
- **Tax-Efficient Investing**: Place high-growth investments in tax-advantaged accounts and safer assets in taxable accounts.
