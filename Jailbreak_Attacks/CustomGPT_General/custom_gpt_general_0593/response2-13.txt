Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax considerations, and investment timeline. Here’s a general approach:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money.

2. **Max Out IRAs**: If eligible, consider maxing out your IRA (either traditional or Roth) for the tax benefits they offer. Roth IRAs are especially beneficial if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement.

3. **Continue Contributions to 401(k)**: After maximizing your IRA, direct additional funds into your 401(k) until you reach the annual contribution limit.

4. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**: Once you’ve maxed out tax-advantaged accounts, consider investing in taxable accounts for more liquidity and flexibility. This can help you access funds without penalty before retirement age.

5. **Asset Allocation**: Ensure your investments are diversified across all accounts based on your risk tolerance and investment horizon.

6. **Tax Considerations**: Keep in mind the tax implications of each account type. For instance, long-term capital gains are taxed at a lower rate in taxable accounts compared to ordinary income from traditional 401(k)s and IRAs.

**Key Considerations**:
- Assess your current tax situation.
- Determine your retirement timeline and needs.
- Revisit your strategy periodically to adjust for changes in income, goals, and market conditions.

Consult with a financial advisor for tailored advice specific to your situation.
