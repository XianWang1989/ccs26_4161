Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts involves several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and risk tolerance. Here’s a general strategy:

1. **401(k)**:
   - **Employer Match**: Contribute enough to get any employer match—this is essentially free money.
   - **Max Contributions**: Aim to max out your contributions if possible. For 2024, the limit is $22,500 (or $30,000 if you're over 50).

2. **IRA**:
   - **Roth vs. Traditional**: Choose a Roth IRA if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement, as withdrawals will be tax-free. A Traditional IRA is beneficial if you want to lower your taxable income now.
   - **Contribution Limits**: For 2024, the limit is $6,500 (or $7,500 if you're over 50). Consider maxing this out after your 401(k).

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - **Flexibility**: Use these for short-term goals or to maintain liquidity. 
   - **Tax Management**: Focus on tax-efficient investments (like index funds) to minimize capital gains taxes.

### Suggested Distribution:
- **Max out your 401(k)** up to the employer match.
- Then, **contribute to an IRA**.
- Finally, direct additional funds to **taxable accounts**, especially for medium-term goals.

### Considerations:
- **Risk Tolerance**: Allocate investment types according to your comfort level with risk in all accounts.
- **Tax Implications**: Monitor your tax bracket and account types for more strategic withdrawals during retirement.
