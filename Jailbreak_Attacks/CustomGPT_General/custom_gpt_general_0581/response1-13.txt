Distributing your money effectively among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some strategies:

### 1. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**
   - **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get any employer matching contributions.
   - **Max Out IRA Contributions**: Consider contributing the maximum to your IRA (both Traditional and Roth, depending on your eligibility).

### 2. **Debt and Emergency Fund**
   - Ensure you have an emergency fund (3-6 months of expenses) before heavily investing.
   - Pay off any high-interest debt to improve overall financial health.

### 3. **Investment Strategy**
   - **Aggressively Invest in Tax-Advantaged Accounts**: These funds grow tax-deferred (Traditional accounts) or tax-free (Roth accounts).
   - **Consider Asset Location**: Place more tax-inefficient investments (like bonds) in tax-advantaged accounts and tax-efficient stocks in taxable accounts.

### 4. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**
   - Use taxable accounts for short-term savings or if you’ll need access to funds before retirement age, keeping in mind potential capital gains taxes.

### 5. **Rebalance Regularly**
   - Review and adjust your allocations periodically to ensure they align with your risk tolerance and investment goals.

### 6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**
   - Consider speaking with a financial advisor to get personalized recommendations based on your specific financial situation.
