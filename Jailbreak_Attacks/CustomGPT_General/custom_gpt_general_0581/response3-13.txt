To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money.
   - **IRA**: Depending on your eligibility, contribute to a Traditional or Roth IRA. Roth IRAs provide tax-free growth and withdrawals.

### 2. **Assess Your Tax Situation**
   - Invest in tax-efficient funds in taxable accounts to minimize capital gains taxes. Index funds and ETFs tend to have lower turnover and taxes.

### 3. **Consider Future Tax Implications**
   - **Traditional accounts**: You’ll pay taxes when you withdraw. If you expect to be in a lower tax bracket during retirement, these may be beneficial.
   - **Roth accounts**: Pay taxes now for tax-free withdrawals later. This is advantageous if you anticipate being in a higher tax bracket.

### 4. **Diversification Strategy**
   - Diversify your investments across the three types of accounts. Use tax-efficient investments in your taxable accounts and potentially higher-growth investments in tax-advantaged accounts.

### 5. **Regularly Rebalance**
   - Periodically assess your financial goals, risk tolerance, and market conditions to ensure your asset allocation aligns with your investment strategy.

### Example Distribution:
- **401(k)**: Max out employer match + 10-15% of your income.
- **IRA**: Contribute the maximum allowable amount annually.
- **Taxable Accounts**: Allocate any remaining funds, focusing on high-yield investments and maintaining tax efficiency.

### Seek Professional Advice
If your situation is complex, consider consulting a financial advisor to tailor a distribution strategy to your unique circumstances.
