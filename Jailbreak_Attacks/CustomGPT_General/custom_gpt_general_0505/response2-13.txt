Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your goals, tax situation, and investment horizon. Here are some strategies to consider:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**
   - **401(k):** Contribute enough to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money.
   - **IRA:** If eligible, consider maxing out your IRA contributions next. Traditional IRAs offer tax-deferred growth, while Roth IRAs provide tax-free withdrawals in retirement.

### 2. **Assess Your Tax Situation**
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Invest in taxable accounts for flexibility. They are useful for short-term goals since contributions aren't restricted like retirement accounts.
   - **Tax-Efficient Investments:** Use index funds, ETFs, or municipal bonds in taxable accounts to lower taxes on gains.

### 3. **Balance Your Investment Goals**
   - **Long-Term Growth:** Prioritize retirement accounts for long-term growth to take advantage of compounding and tax benefits.
   - **Short-Term Needs:** Keep cash or stable investments in taxable accounts for any near-term goals or emergencies.

### 4. **Review Withdrawal Rules**
   - Understand the withdrawal penalties and rules for each account type. Typically, you can withdraw from a 401(k) penalty-free after age 59½ and from an IRA under certain conditions.

### 5. **Rebalance Regularly**
   - Monitor your investments and adjust based on performance, market conditions, and any changes in your financial situation.

### 6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**
   - For personalized advice tailored to your specific financial situation, consider consulting with a financial advisor.

This balanced approach will help you optimize your investment strategy while considering tax implications and your financial goals.
