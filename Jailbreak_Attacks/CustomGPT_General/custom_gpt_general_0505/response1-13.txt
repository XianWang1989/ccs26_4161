To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

### 1. Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts
- **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money. Aim to max out contributions if possible.
- **IRA**: Consider funding a Roth IRA if eligible, as it offers tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement. If you prefer tax deductions, prioritize a traditional IRA.

### 2. Asset Allocation
- **401(k)**: Invest in a mix of equities and bonds based on your risk tolerance and time horizon. Consider target-date funds that adjust as you approach retirement.
- **IRA**: Use this account for growth-oriented investments, especially if you’re younger and can take advantage of compounding. Include options like stocks and mutual funds.
- **Taxable Accounts**: Focus on more tax-efficient investments, like index funds and ETFs, which tend to produce less taxable income.

### 3. Consider Tax Implications
- **401(k)**: Contributions are pre-tax, reducing taxable income in the present. Withdrawals are taxed as ordinary income during retirement.
- **IRA**: Traditional IRAs provide tax deductions now, while Roth IRAs allow for tax-free withdrawals later.
- **Taxable Accounts**: Be mindful of capital gains tax. Hold investments long-term to benefit from lower tax rates on long-term gains.

### 4. Reassess Regularly
- Periodically review your investments and contributions to ensure they align with your retirement goals and risk tolerance as life circumstances change.

### 5. Emergency Fund and Expenses
- Maintain an emergency fund and consider your current financial needs when deciding how much to allocate to each account.

### Conclusion
Balance contributions according to tax advantages, investment growth potential, and your overall financial strategy. Consider consulting a financial advisor for a personalized plan.
