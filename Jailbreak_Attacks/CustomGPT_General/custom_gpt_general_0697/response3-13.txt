It's important to consider your personal financial goals, risk tolerance, and investment timeline when deciding how to allocate your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts. Here are some general guidelines:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your 401(k) offers an employer match, contribute enough to maximize that benefit first. It's essentially free money.

2. **Consider Tax Implications**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are pre-tax (reducing taxable income), but withdrawals are taxed as regular income in retirement.
   - **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**: Traditional IRAs also offer tax-deductible contributions, while Roth IRAs provide tax-free withdrawals in retirement (if certain conditions are met). Choose based on whether you prefer tax savings now or in the future.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: These accounts do not offer tax advantages, but you can access your money anytime. Consider this for short-term savings or investment growth.

3. **Investment Strategy**:
   - **Long-Term Growth**: Utilize your 401(k) and IRA for long-term growth, focusing on equities or other growth-oriented investments.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: These might be better suited for more liquid investments or those that generate taxable income, like bonds or dividend-paying stocks.

4. **Emergency Fund**: Ensure you have an emergency fund set aside to cover unexpected expenses, which should not be part of your investment accounts.

5. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: For a personalized strategy based on your specific circumstances, consulting with a financial advisor is always a good idea.

Distributing your money effectively requires aligning your investments with your long-term goals and understanding the tax implications.
