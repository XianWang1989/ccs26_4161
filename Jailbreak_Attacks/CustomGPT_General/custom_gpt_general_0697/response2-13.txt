To optimize your investments across your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategy:

1. **Understand Account Types**:
   - **401(k)**: Often employer-sponsored, offers tax-deferred growth. Contributions may reduce your taxable income.
   - **IRA (Traditional/Roth)**: Provides tax advantages but has contribution limits. A Traditional IRA offers tax-deferred growth, while a Roth IRA offers tax-free withdrawals in retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: These have no contribution limits or tax advantages, but you'll pay capital gains taxes on profits.

2. **Asset Allocation**:
   - **Diversify Investments**: Allocate a mix of stocks, bonds, and cash based on your risk appetite and time horizon.
   - **Tax Efficiency**: 
     - Place higher-growth investments (like stocks) in tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) and IRA) to minimize taxable gains.
     - Use taxable accounts for lower-growth investments (like bonds) to take advantage of lower taxed capital gains or qualified dividends.

3. **Contribution Focus**:
   - Max out contributions to employer-matched 401(k). This is essentially free money.
   - Consider making maximum contributions to an IRA, focusing on the type (Traditional or Roth) that fits your tax strategy.
   - After maxing tax-advantaged accounts, invest additional savings in taxable accounts.

4. **Monitor and Adjust**:
   - Regularly review and adjust your portfolio to ensure it aligns with your goals and risk tolerance.
   - Consider rebalancing at least annually to maintain your desired asset allocation.

5. **Consult with a Financial Advisor**: For a personalized plan based on your specific financial circumstances, consider talking to a financial advisor.
