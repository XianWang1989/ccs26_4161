The best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here's a general strategy:

### 1. **Prioritize Employer Match:**
   - Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money.

### 2. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts:**
   - After getting the match, consider maxing out your IRA (traditional or Roth, depending on your income and tax strategy).
   - If you have additional funds, contribute more to your 401(k) up to the annual maximum.

### 3. **Invest in Taxable Accounts:**
   - Use taxable accounts for any funds you need for short-term goals or that exceed your tax-advantaged contributions.
   - Consider tax-efficient investments like index funds or ETFs to minimize capital gains taxes.

### 4. **Invest Based on Time Horizon:**
   - For long-term goals (retirement), focus on growth assets in tax-advantaged accounts.
   - Use taxable accounts for more liquid investments needed in the short term.

### 5. **Tax Considerations:**
   - Be mindful of tax implications when withdrawing from each account type.
   - Consider your current and future tax brackets to optimize tax efficiency.

### Example Distribution:
- **401(k):** Contribute enough for the full employer match.
- **IRA:** Max out (2023 limit: $6,500 for under 50, $7,500 for 50+).
- **Taxable Accounts:** Any leftover funds for flexibility and liquidity.

### Regular Review:
- Reassess your allocations and contributions regularly based on changes in income, tax laws, and financial goals.

Consult with a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your circumstances.
