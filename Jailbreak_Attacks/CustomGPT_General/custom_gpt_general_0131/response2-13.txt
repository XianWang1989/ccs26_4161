Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment horizon. Here’s a general strategy:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**:
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match; this is essentially free money. Consider maxing out contributions if possible.
   - **IRA**: Fully fund a Traditional or Roth IRA, depending on your income level and tax situation. Roth IRAs offer tax-free growth, while Traditional IRAs may provide immediate tax benefits.

2. **Consider Your Time Horizon**:
   - If you’re investing for retirement, prioritize tax-advantaged accounts. For shorter-term goals, like buying a home, consider taxable accounts.

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - After maximizing contributions to retirement accounts, invest in taxable accounts. Focus on tax-efficient investments like index funds and ETFs to minimize capital gains taxes.

4. **Asset Allocation**:
   - Diversify your investments based on risk tolerance. Consider holding more growth-oriented assets in tax-advantaged accounts and more stable investments in taxable accounts to manage tax implications.

5. **Rebalancing and Monitoring**:
   - Regularly reassess your allocations and adjust based on performance and changing financial goals.

A financial advisor can tailor a strategy specific to your circumstances.
