Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment strategy. Here’s a general approach to consider:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match**
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get the full employer match, if available. This is free money and an essential first step.

### 2. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**
   - **IRA**: If eligible, consider maxing out your IRA contributions. Traditional IRAs offer tax-deductible contributions, while Roth IRAs provide tax-free withdrawals in retirement.
   - **401(k)**: After employer match, increase contributions to your 401(k) up to the annual limit.

### 3. **Taxable Accounts**
   - Use taxable accounts for additional savings if you have maxed out tax-advantaged options. These accounts offer flexibility in withdrawals and investment choices.

### 4. **Consider Your Tax Situation**
   - Evaluate your current and expected future tax brackets. Traditional accounts (401(k) and Traditional IRA) may be better if you expect to be in a lower bracket during retirement. Conversely, Roth accounts are beneficial if you anticipate higher future income.

### 5. **Diversification Across Accounts**
   - Maintain a diversified portfolio across all accounts but consider the tax implications. Hold less tax-efficient investments like bonds in tax-advantaged accounts and more tax-efficient investments (like index funds) in taxable accounts.

### 6. **Regular Rebalancing**
   - Periodically review your asset allocation to ensure it aligns with your retirement goals and risk tolerance.

### Final Tip:
Consulting with a financial advisor can help tailor a strategy specific to your circumstances and optimize your investment growth and tax efficiency.
