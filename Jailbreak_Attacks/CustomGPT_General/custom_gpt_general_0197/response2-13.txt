Distributing money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and time horizon. Here are some guidelines to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to get any employer match. This is essentially "free money."

2. **Focus on Tax Advantages**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are pre-tax, reducing your taxable income. Consider maxing this out first, especially if you’re in a higher tax bracket.
   - **IRA**: Traditional IRAs also offer tax deductions, while Roth IRAs provide tax-free growth and withdrawals. Choose based on your current vs. future tax situation.

3. **Diversification**:
   - Ensure your investments are diversified across all accounts. This can help manage risk.

4. **Taxable Accounts**: Use these accounts for investments that may generate higher taxes, like bonds or actively traded stocks, as well as for short-term goals.

5. **Withdrawal Strategy**:
   - Plan for how and when you'll withdraw funds, as this can impact your tax situation in retirement.

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: A professional can help tailor a strategy to your individual circumstances.

Overall, aim for a balanced approach that aligns with your retirement goals while optimizing tax efficiency. Regularly review and adjust based on changes in financial status or tax laws.
