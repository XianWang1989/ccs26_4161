Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some strategies to consider:

1. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts:**
   - **401(k):** If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to get the full match; it's free money.
   - **IRA:** Consider maxing out a traditional or Roth IRA, depending on your tax situation.

2. **Assess Tax Implications:**
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Use these for investments that generate capital gains or dividends. This allows for better tax management.
   - **IRA and 401(k):** Ideal for long-term investments since taxes are deferred.

3. **Diversification:**
   - Ensure a mix of asset classes (stocks, bonds) across all accounts, considering the individual account's tax implications.

4. **Risk Tolerance:**
   - Align your investments with your risk tolerance. Typically, longer-term accounts (like 401(k) and IRA) can afford more risk.

5. **Withdrawal Strategy:**
   - Plan for future withdrawals. Funds in tax-advantaged accounts may be penalized if withdrawn before retirement age.

6. **Consult a Professional:**
   - Consider working with a financial advisor for personalized guidance tailored to your situation.

Review your contributions regularly and adjust as your financial situation and goals evolve.
