When considering how to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, here are some key points to evaluate:

### GPT:
1. **Tax Considerations**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are made pre-tax, offering immediate tax benefits. Consider maxing out employer matching.
   - **IRA**: Depending on the type (Traditional or Roth), contributions may provide different tax advantages. Roth IRAs allow for tax-free withdrawals in retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Great for flexibility, but capital gains tax applies when you sell investments. 

2. **Investment Goals**:
   - Define your long-term goals (retirement, buying a home, etc.) to dictate how aggressively you invest in each account.

3. **Time Horizon**:
   - Use retirement accounts (401(k) and IRA) for long-term growth. Taxable accounts can be used for short-term goals or emergencies due to their liquidity.

4. **Diversification**:
   - Ensure diversification across all accounts. This might mean holding different assets in taxable accounts compared to retirement accounts for tax efficiency.

5. **ERISA Rules**:
   - Be mindful of withdrawal restrictions and penalties associated with 401(k) and IRA accounts.

### STAN:
Distributing your money effectively means playing around with tax implications and access. Probably, you’d want to max out your 401(k) for that sweet employer match first—it’s free money! Then, think about maximizing your IRA contributions (Roth for tax-free growth if you qualify). 

Taxable accounts can handle your overflow cash, giving you flexibility but watch out for the tax bite when you cash in your investments. It’s a balancing act: hit the max contributions for tax-advantaged accounts, invest aggressively, and use taxable for quick access. Overall, the strategy should fit your unique financial picture!
