When distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your 401(k) offers a match, contribute enough to get the full benefit. This is essentially “free money.”

2. **Focus on Retirement Accounts First**:
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match, then consider maxing out your IRA contributions if eligible.
   - **IRA (Traditional vs. Roth)**: Traditional IRAs provide tax deductions; Roth IRAs offer tax-free withdrawals in retirement. Choose based on your current vs. expected future tax rates.

3. **Tax Considerations**:
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for funds you may need in the short term. Focus on investments that generate qualified dividends or long-term capital gains to minimize tax liability.

4. **Investment Strategy**:
   - **Retirement Accounts**: Consider more aggressive investments here, as they have long time horizons. 
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Allocate more conservative investments to avoid frequent trading, which can incur taxes.

5. **Diversify Investments**: Ensure a mix of stocks, bonds, and other assets in all accounts, adjusted for risk tolerance and investment timeline.

6. **Regular Reviews**: Reassess your allocations periodically, adjusting as financial goals, market conditions, and tax laws change.

Consulting with a financial advisor can provide personalized insights based on your specific situation.
