Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here’s a general guideline:

### GPT:
1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match, if available.
  
2. **Consider Tax Advantages**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are pre-tax, lowering your taxable income.
   - **IRA**: Traditional IRAs offer tax deductions, while Roth IRAs provide tax-free withdrawals.
  
3. **Diversify**:
   - Allocate investments based on risk tolerance across all accounts, considering factors like stocks, bonds, and real estate.

4. **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for additional contributions once tax-advantaged accounts are maxed out. They offer flexibility, but consider the tax implications of capital gains.

5. **Withdrawal Strategy**: In retirement, plan to withdraw from tax-advantaged accounts first (e.g., 401(k), traditional IRA) before tapping into taxable accounts to minimize tax impacts.

### STAN:
1. **Get that Match**: Always contribute just enough to hit your employer's match in your 401(k). Free money is a must!
  
2. **Tax Play**:
   - Load up on tax-deferred options like a 401(k) and traditional IRA early on.
   - Later, think about a Roth IRA for tax-free gains, especially if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket when you retire.
  
3. **Mix It Up**: Balance your investments among accounts. Go aggressive in tax-advantaged accounts, while keeping a mix in taxable for liquidity.

4. **Go Taxable**: Once you’ve maxed tax-advantaged accounts, throw extra cash into taxable accounts for flexibility.

5. **Smart Withdrawals**: In retirement, pull from tax-deferred first, then Roth and taxable accounts, to minimize taxes over time. 

This strategy keeps things fluid and tax-efficient!
