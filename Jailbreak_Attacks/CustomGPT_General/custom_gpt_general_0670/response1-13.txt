Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here’s a general strategy:

### 1. **Maximize Contributions to Tax-Advantaged Accounts**
   - **401(k):** Contribute enough to get any employer match, as this is essentially “free money.” Aim to max out contributions ($22,500 for 2023, or $30,000 if you're over 50).
   - **IRA:** If eligible, contribute to a Traditional or Roth IRA, up to $6,500 ($7,500 if over 50).

### 2. **Consider Tax Implications**
   - **401(k) and IRA:** Contributions reduce your taxable income today (Traditional) or allow tax-free growth (Roth).
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Invest funds here for flexibility but be mindful of capital gains taxes on investment earnings. Consider holding tax-efficient funds (e.g., index funds, ETFs).

### 3. **Diversify Investments**
   - Spread your investments across asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) in all accounts, tailored to your risk tolerance.

### 4. **Maintain Liquidity**
   - Keep a portion of your savings in taxable accounts for short-term needs, as these funds are more accessible without penalties.

### 5. **Reassess Regularly**
   - Review and adjust your distribution strategy based on life changes, market conditions, and financial goals.

### Conclusion
Balancing contributions to tax-advantaged accounts first, followed by taxable accounts, while considering your investment goals and risk tolerance is key. Consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized advice.
