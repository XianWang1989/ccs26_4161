The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement plans. Here are some general guidelines to consider:

1. **401(k) Plan**: 
   - If your employer offers a matching contribution, prioritize contributing enough to maximize that match, as it’s essentially "free money."
   - Contributions to a 401(k) are made pre-tax, which can reduce your taxable income, making it a beneficial option if you're in a higher tax bracket.

2. **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**:
   - **Traditional IRA**: Similar to a 401(k), contributions may be tax-deductible depending on your income, and taxes are paid upon withdrawal.
   - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are made after-tax, but qualified withdrawals are tax-free. This can be appealing if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement.
   - Depending on your eligibility, consider splitting contributions between the two types of IRAs to diversify your tax situation in retirement.

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - These can provide flexibility since you can withdraw funds without penalties at any time. They are also beneficial for long-term investments, capital gains taxes, and dividend income.
   - Investment options in taxable accounts can include stocks, bonds, and mutual funds, but you’ll want to be mindful of potential tax implications on dividends and capital gains.

4. **Asset Allocation**: 
   - Diversify your investments across different asset classes based on your risk tolerance and investment horizon, regardless of the type of account.
   - Keep in mind that typically, investments that generate higher returns (like stocks) might be better suited for tax-advantaged accounts where gains can be deferred or avoided altogether.

5. **Regular Review and Rebalancing**: 
   - Periodically review and adjust your allocation as your financial situation, market conditions, and tax laws change. 

It's always advisable to consult with a financial planner or tax advisor to tailor a strategy that fits your specific needs and circumstances.
