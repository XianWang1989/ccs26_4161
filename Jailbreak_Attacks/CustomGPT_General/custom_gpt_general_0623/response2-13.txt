Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on a variety of factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, age, and risk tolerance. Here’s a general strategy you might consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a matching contribution for your 401(k), prioritize contributing enough to get the full match. This is essentially "free money."

2. **IRAs**: Next, consider funding a traditional or Roth IRA, depending on your income levels and tax situation. Roth IRAs offer tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement, which can be beneficial if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket later on.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: After maximizing your 401(k) and IRA contributions, consider taxable accounts for additional investments. Use these accounts for more flexible investments that you can access sooner without penalties.

4. **Asset Allocation**: Within each type of account, think about your asset allocation—how you distribute investments like stocks, bonds, and cash based on your risk tolerance and investment horizon.

5. **Tax Considerations**: Understand the tax implications of each account type. For example, high-growth investments might be better suited for tax-advantaged accounts like 401(k)s and IRAs to defer taxes until withdrawal.

6. **Emergency Fund**: Ensure you have an emergency fund in place before heavily investing, as this will protect you from having to withdraw retirement funds prematurely.

Consider consulting with a financial advisor to customize a plan based on your specific circumstances. They can provide personalized advice and help you develop a comprehensive strategy for managing your investments.
