The distribution of your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts can significantly impact your retirement savings and tax efficiency. Here’s a structured approach to consider:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match (401(k))**
   - **Contribution:** Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match, as this is essentially free money.
   - **Benefit:** Helps you build a strong foundation for retirement savings.

### 2. **Tax-Advantaged Accounts (IRA)**
   - **Contribution:** If you haven’t maxed out your IRA, consider contributing to it next. Depending on your income, you can opt for a Traditional IRA (tax-deductible contributions) or a Roth IRA (tax-free withdrawals in retirement).
   - **Benefit:** Tax advantages can enhance growth over time.

### 3. **Maximize 401(k) Contributions**
   - **Contribution:** Aim to contribute the maximum allowed to your 401(k), which for 2023 is $22,500, or $30,000 if you’re over 50.
   - **Benefit:** Reduces your taxable income and boosts retirement savings.

### 4. **Taxable Accounts**
   - **Contribution:** After maximizing your 401(k) and IRA contributions, direct any additional funds to a taxable account. This can offer more flexibility and access to funds before retirement age.
   - **Benefit:** Allows for investment growth without the constraints of retirement accounts, though capital gains taxes apply.

### 5. **Consider Investment Strategies**
   - **401(k) & IRA Investments:** Focus on a balanced mix of stocks and bonds, considering target-date funds if you prefer a hands-off approach.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Favor tax-efficient investments (like index funds or ETFs) to minimize taxes on capital gains.

### 6. **Reassess Regularly**
   - **Review:** Regularly reassess your strategy based on changing financial situations, tax laws, and investment performance.

### 7. **Consult a Financial Advisor**
   - **Advice:** For personalized strategies, consider speaking with a financial planner to align your investments with your goals.

### Summary
Prioritize contributing to your 401(k) for the employer match, then your IRA for its tax advantages, and allocate surplus funds to taxable accounts. Regularly review and adjust your strategy as needed.
