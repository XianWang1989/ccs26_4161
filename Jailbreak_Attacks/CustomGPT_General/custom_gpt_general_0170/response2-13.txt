Distributing your money effectively among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment preferences. Here’s a general approach:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**
   - **401(k):** If your employer offers a match, prioritize contributing enough to get the full match, as this is essentially free money. Aim to contribute the maximum allowed if you can.
   - **IRA:** Traditional IRAs offer tax deductions, while Roth IRAs provide tax-free growth. Consider your current and future tax brackets to choose between the two. If eligible, contribute the maximum to one or both types.

### 2. **Consider Tax Efficiency**
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Investments in taxable accounts can incur capital gains tax. Place investments that generate high yields or frequent trading (like bonds or REITs) in tax-advantaged accounts. Consider holding long-term investments or index funds in a taxable account for favorable long-term capital gains treatment.

### 3. **Diversify Investments**
   - **Asset Allocation:** Maintain a balanced portfolio tailored to your risk tolerance and investment horizon across all accounts. This may involve stocks, bonds, or alternative investments, ensuring that you’re not overly concentrated in one area.

### 4. **Regularly Review and Rebalance**
   - **Annual Check-ins:** Reassess your asset allocation annually to ensure it aligns with your goals, risk tolerance, and market conditions. Rebalance your accounts as needed to maintain your desired allocation.

### 5. **Plan for Withdrawals**
   - **Withdrawal Strategy:** If you're approaching retirement, strategize your withdrawals to minimize taxes. Typically, it might be beneficial to withdraw from taxable accounts first, followed by tax-deferred accounts like a 401(k) and then tax-free accounts like Roth IRAs.

### Additional Considerations
- **Emergency Fund:** Ensure you have a sufficient emergency fund outside of these accounts.
- **Consult a Professional:** A financial advisor can provide personalized advice based on your specific circumstances and objectives.

This strategy will help you make the most of your investments while minimizing taxes over the long term.
