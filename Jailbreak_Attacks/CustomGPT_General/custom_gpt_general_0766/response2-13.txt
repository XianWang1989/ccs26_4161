When distributing money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Maximize Employer Match:** Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match. This is essentially free money.

2. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts:**
   - **401(k):** If you have a high employer match, prioritize contributions here.
   - **IRA:** If eligible, consider maxing out a Traditional or Roth IRA for tax benefits. Roth IRAs grow tax-free and allow for tax-free withdrawals in retirement.

3. **Consider Tax Implications:**
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Use these for investment vehicles that generate less taxable income, like index funds or ETFs. Capital gains taxes can be lower than ordinary income tax rates.

4. **Diversify Investments:**
   - Balancing risk and growth through diversification is essential. Consider asset allocation based on your risk tolerance and investment horizon.

5. **Emergency Fund:** Ensure you have a separate emergency fund before maximizing investments.

6. **Review and Adjust:** Regularly reassess your accounts and rebalance your portfolio based on market changes and personal financial goals.

Consulting a financial advisor can also provide tailored strategies based on your unique situation.
