Distributing your money across a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts involves considering several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment preferences. Here’s a general guide:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match, as this is essentially “free money”. Aim to maximize contributions up to the annual limit if your budget allows. This reduces your taxable income.
   - **IRA**: If eligible, consider a traditional IRA for tax-deferred growth or a Roth IRA for tax-free withdrawals in retirement. Max out your IRA contributions after your 401(k) match.

2. **Consider Investment Options**:
   - Evaluate the investment choices available in your 401(k) (often limited to mutual funds) compared to IRAs (more diverse options). If your 401(k) has high fees or poor investment choices, consider contributing less to it and more to an IRA.

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - Use taxable accounts for investments that might generate high returns or for financial goals not long-term in nature. Investments in these accounts are subject to capital gains tax on profits when sold, so consider holding tax-efficient investments (like index funds or ETFs).

4. **Emergency Fund**:
   - Ensure you have a sufficient emergency fund (3-6 months of expenses) before heavily investing. This can be in a high-yield savings or money market account.

5. **Rebalance Periodically**:
   - Regularly assess your portfolio’s performance and adjust allocations as needed to stay aligned with your investment strategy and risk tolerance.

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**:
   - For personalized advice, consider consulting with a financial planner who can tailor recommendations to your specific situation.

By strategically allocating funds, you can optimize growth while minimizing tax implications over time.
