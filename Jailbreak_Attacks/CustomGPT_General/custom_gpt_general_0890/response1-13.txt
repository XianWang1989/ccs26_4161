The best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment horizon. Here are some guidelines:

1. **401(k) Accounts:**
   - **Maximize Employer Match:** Contribute enough to get any employer match first. It's essentially free money.
   - **Tax Advantages:** Contributions are pre-tax, reducing your taxable income now. This is beneficial if you expect to be in a lower tax bracket during retirement.

2. **IRA Accounts:**
   - **Consider a Roth IRA:** If eligible, a Roth IRA allows tax-free withdrawals in retirement, which can be a good option if you anticipate being in a higher tax bracket later.
   - **Traditional IRA Contributions:** If you need to reduce taxable income, a traditional IRA can also be advantageous.

3. **Taxable Accounts:**
   - **Flexibility for Short-Term Goals:** Use taxable accounts for funds you may need before retirement, as they provide more liquidity.
   - **Tax Efficiency:** Consider investments that generate lower taxable income, such as index funds or tax-managed funds.

### Suggested Strategy:
- **Prioritize retirement accounts** (401(k) first, then IRA) for tax benefits.
- **Diversify across account types** to balance tax exposure and access to funds.
- **Adjust based on age and risk tolerance:** Younger investors can lean more towards growth in tax-advantaged accounts. Closer to retirement, consider more conservative investments.

### Final Tips:
- Regularly review and rebalance your allocations based on market conditions and personal financial goals.
- Consult with a financial advisor to tailor a plan specific to your situation.

This balanced approach helps maximize growth while considering tax implications and liquidity needs.
