Distributing money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment horizon. Here’s a general strategy:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match**
   - Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match. This is essentially "free money."

### 2. **Consider Tax Advantages**
   - **401(k) and IRA:** These accounts offer tax-deferred growth. If you expect to be in a lower tax bracket in retirement, prioritize these.
   - **Roth IRA (if applicable):** Contributions are taxed now, but withdrawals in retirement are tax-free. This is beneficial if you expect your tax rate to rise.

### 3. **Invest Beyond the Tax-Advantaged Accounts**
   - Once you maximize your contributions in tax-advantaged accounts, consider investing in taxable accounts. This gives you flexibility and access to funds without penalties.

### 4. **Asset Allocation**
   - **Tax-Efficient Investments:** Place more tax-efficient investments (like index funds) in your taxable account to minimize capital gains tax.
   - **Bonds or High-Yield Investments:** These are better suited for tax-advantaged accounts to avoid tax on interest income.

### 5. **Rebalance Regularly**
   - Regularly review and adjust your investment allocations based on performance and your changing financial goals.

### 6. **Consult with a Financial Advisor**
   - A tailored approach can be beneficial, especially considering changing laws and personal circumstances.

### Summary Example:
- **401(k):** Max out contributions to get the full match.
- **IRA:** Contribute up to the limit, prioritizing Roth IRA if you expect higher taxes in the future.
- **Taxable Account:** Invest surplus savings in low-turnover, diversified assets.

This strategy can optimize both growth potential and tax efficiency over time.
