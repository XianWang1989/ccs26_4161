To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**: Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to capture any employer matching contributions, as this is essentially free money.

2. **Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**:
   - **IRA (Individual Retirement Account)**: If eligible, consider fully funding a Roth IRA or a Traditional IRA next. Roth IRAs offer tax-free growth and withdrawals, while Traditional IRAs give you tax deductions now.
   - **401(k)**: Continue to contribute to your 401(k) beyond the employer match if you have not maxed it out, especially if you are in a high tax bracket now.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: After optimizing your contributions to retirement accounts, allocate funds to taxable investment accounts. These can provide flexibility for short- to medium-term goals. 

4. **Consider Asset Location**: 
   - Keep more heavily taxed investments (like bonds) in tax-advantaged accounts and place lower taxed investments (like index funds) in taxable accounts. 
   - This helps you minimize tax impacts and enhance after-tax returns.

5. **Regular Rebalancing**: Review your allocations periodically to adjust for changes in your financial situation, tax laws, or investment goals.

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: If you're uncertain, a financial advisor can provide personalized advice based on your specific circumstances.

This strategy balances growth potential, tax efficiency, and financial flexibility.
