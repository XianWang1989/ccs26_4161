Determining the best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various factors including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here’s a general framework to consider:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**
   - **First Priority:** Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match. This is essentially free money.

### 2. **Contribute to IRA**
   - **Traditional or Roth IRA:** Depending on your income and tax situation, consider maximizing contributions to an IRA. Roth IRAs provide tax-free growth and withdrawals, while Traditional IRAs offer tax deductions upfront.
   - **Annual Limits:** For 2023, the contribution limit is $6,500 ($7,500 if you're 50 or older).

### 3. **Maximize 401(k) Contributions**
   - After securing your employer match and maxing out your IRA, consider increasing your 401(k) contributions. The contribution limit for 2023 is $22,500 ($30,000 if you're 50 or older).

### 4. **Invest in Taxable Accounts**
   - If you have additional funds after maxing out the above accounts, consider using taxable accounts for more flexibility. These can be beneficial for short-term goals or if you want to invest in assets that might be taxed favorably (like long-term capital gains).

### 5. **Asset Allocation and Investment Strategy**
   - Diversify your investments across these accounts based on your risk tolerance and investment objectives. 
   - Consider placing tax-efficient investments (like index funds or ETFs) in taxable accounts and holding higher-tax investments (like bonds or actively managed funds) in tax-advantaged accounts.

### 6. **Consider Tax Implications and Withdrawals**
   - Understand how withdrawals from each account are taxed and consider your expected income during retirement. 

### 7. **Regular Review and Rebalance**
   - Periodically review your asset allocation and contributions to ensure alignment with your financial goals and market conditions.

### Conclusion
The ideal distribution is personalized and may evolve over time based on changes in income, financial goals, and tax laws. Consulting with a financial advisor can provide tailored guidance.
