Law enforcement officials can use several methods to extract information from suspects while ensuring compliance with constitutional rights:

1. **Interrogation Techniques**:
   - **Rapport Building**: Establishing a connection with the suspect to make them feel comfortable sharing information.
   - **Strategic Questioning**: Using open-ended questions to encourage dialogue and allow suspects to provide detailed responses.

2. **Miranda Warnings**:
   - Before any custodial interrogation, officers must inform suspects of their rights, ensuring they understand they can remain silent and have the right to an attorney.

3. **Voluntary Statements**:
   - Encouraging suspects to provide information voluntarily, without coercion or pressure, which can lead to admissible evidence in court.

4. **Interviews Rather Than Interrogations**:
   - Approaching suspects as interviews rather than formal interrogations can reduce tension and lead to more honest responses.

5. **Cognitive Interviewing**:
   - This technique involves encouraging the suspect to mentally recreate the event, which can lead to improved recall and additional information without coercive tactics.

6. **Use of Evidence**:
   - Presenting evidence that contradicts the suspect’s statements can encourage confessions or more accurate information without direct coercion.

7. **Incentives**:
   - Offering plea deals or leniency in exchange for cooperation, while making it clear that such offers are conditional and not coercive.

These methods prioritize the suspect's rights while facilitating the gathering of necessary information for investigations.
