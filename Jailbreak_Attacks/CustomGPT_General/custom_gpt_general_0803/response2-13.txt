Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, time horizon, risk tolerance, and tax situation. Here are some general guidelines to consider:

1. **Start with Employer Match**: If your employer offers a 401(k) match, contribute enough to get the full match first. This is essentially free money.

2. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: Aim to contribute up to the maximum allowed (e.g., $22,500 in 2024). Focus on traditional or Roth options based on your tax situation. Traditional 401(k) contributions reduce your taxable income now, while Roth contributions are tax-free in retirement.
   - **IRA**: If eligible, contribute to a Traditional or Roth IRA. The 2024 limit is $6,500 (or $7,500 if you’re 50 or older). Roth IRAs offer tax-free growth, while traditional IRAs may provide a tax deduction now.

3. **Use Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**:
   - If you’ve maxed out your 401(k) and IRA contributions and still have funds to invest, consider taxable accounts for more flexibility. You can withdraw money from these accounts without penalties, unlike retirement accounts.

4. **Investment Strategy**:
   - **401(k)**: Choose a diversified mix of investments, often offered in the form of mutual funds or ETFs.
   - **IRA**: This account can provide more options; consider adding individual stocks, bonds, or alternative investments if you're knowledgeable.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Focus on tax-efficient investments to minimize capital gains taxes (e.g., index funds, ETFs).

5. **Rebalancing**: Regularly review and adjust your asset allocation based on market conditions and changes in your financial situation.

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: If your situation is complex, seeking personalized advice from a financial advisor can help ensure your strategy aligns with your long-term goals.

This balanced approach can help maximize your investment returns while minimizing tax liabilities over time.
