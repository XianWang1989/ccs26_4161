Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here’s a strategy you might consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match. This is essentially free money and offers immediate returns.

2. **Contribute to an IRA**: If you're eligible, consider contributing to a traditional or Roth IRA. A Roth IRA offers tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement, while traditional IRAs provide tax-deferred growth. Choose based on your current versus expected future tax rates.

3. **Fill Up the 401(k)**: After maxing out the IRA, increase contributions to your 401(k) up to its contribution limit ($22,500 for 2023, or $30,000 if you're over 50).

4. **Invest in Taxable Accounts**: If you still have funds to invest, use taxable accounts. These offer flexibility and accessibility, but you will need to consider capital gains tax when you sell investments.

5. **Asset Allocation**:
   - **401(k)**: Consider a mix of stocks and bonds appropriate for your risk tolerance.
   - **IRA**: Your investment strategy can be more aggressive here if you’re younger and focused on long-term growth.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Use tax-efficient investments like index funds or municipal bonds to minimize tax impacts.

6. **Reassess Regularly**: Monitor your allocations at least annually and adjust based on changes in your financial situation, goals, and market conditions.

Consult with a financial advisor to tailor this strategy to your specific circumstances.
