Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts strategically depends on several factors, including your financial goals, current tax situation, and investment horizon. Here are some best practices to consider:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts:**
   - **401(k):** If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to get the full match first. Additionally, consider maxing out your contributions if possible.
   - **IRA:** After maximizing your 401(k), consider funding a traditional or Roth IRA. The choice between them depends on whether you expect your tax rate to be higher or lower in retirement.

2. **Consider Tax Implications:**
   - **Taxable Accounts:** These should be used for investments that are likely to generate capital gains or dividends, as they can be more tax-efficient and benefit from preferential rates compared to ordinary income.

3. **Investment Goals and Time Horizon:**
   - Long-term goals (like retirement) may benefit more from tax-advantaged accounts. Short-term needs might be better suited for taxable accounts to maintain liquidity.

4. **Diversification:**
   - Ensure that your investment strategy across all accounts is diversified, balancing between stocks, bonds, and other assets based on your risk tolerance.

5. **Rebalance Regularly:**
   - Periodically review your asset allocation and rebalance across accounts to maintain your desired risk profile.

6. **Withdrawal Considerations:**
   - As you approach retirement, consider the tax implications of withdrawals from each account type. Strategically plan withdrawals to minimize taxes.

Consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your specific situation.
