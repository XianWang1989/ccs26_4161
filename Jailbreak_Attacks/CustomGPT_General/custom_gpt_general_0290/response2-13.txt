When determining the best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a match for your 401(k), contribute enough to take full advantage of this benefit first. This is essentially "free money."

2. **Consider Tax Advantages**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are made pre-tax, lowering your taxable income. However, withdrawals in retirement are taxed.
   - **Traditional IRA**: Similar to the 401(k) regarding pre-tax contributions and post-retirement taxes.
   - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are made after-tax, but you won't owe tax on withdrawals during retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Money is invested post-tax, and you will pay capital gains tax on profits. 

3. **Prioritize Based on Income**:
   - If you're in a higher tax bracket, prioritize pre-tax accounts (401(k) and traditional IRA) for immediate tax benefits.
   - If you expect to be in a lower tax bracket in retirement, consider a Roth IRA. 

4. **Diversify Investments**:
   - Spread your investments across a range of asset classes (stocks, bonds, real estate) within each account to mitigate risk.

5. **Withdrawal Strategy**:
   - In retirement, strategically withdraw from different accounts to manage your tax liability. For instance, you might withdraw from taxable accounts first to allow tax-advantaged accounts to grow longer.

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: Personal circumstances vary significantly, and a professional can provide tailored advice based on your age, risk tolerance, retirement goals, and current financial situation.

**Example Allocation Strategy**:
- Contribute 10-15% of your income to the 401(k) until you hit the employer match.
- Then, consider contributing to a Roth IRA (up to the limit — $6,500 for individuals under 50 in 2023).
- Finally, direct any additional funds to taxable accounts for flexibility.

This strategic distribution should balance growth potential, tax efficiency, and future income needs.
