The distribution of your money across a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on a few factors, including your financial goals, time horizon, investment preferences, and tax situation. Here's a general overview of how to approach this:

1. **Maximize Your 401(k) Contributions**: If your employer offers matching contributions, ensure you contribute at least enough to get the full match; this is essentially free money. The 401(k) offers tax-deferred growth, so it's a great choice if you can maximize contributions there first.

2. **Consider an IRA**: After maximizing your 401(k) match, consider contributing to a Traditional or Roth IRA. Traditional IRAs may provide tax deductions now, whereas Roth IRAs allow tax-free withdrawals in retirement. Choose one based on your current tax rate and expected future tax situation.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: Once you've maximized your tax-advantaged accounts, you can use taxable accounts for additional investments. Focus here on tax-efficient investments, such as index funds or ETFs, which typically have lower turnover and generate fewer taxable events.

4. **Diversification**: Within each account, ensure you diversify your investments based on your risk tolerance, investment horizon, and financial goals. Consider different asset classes like stocks, bonds, and alternative investments.

5. **Regular Review and Rebalance**: Over time, asset values will change, so regularly review your allocation and rebalance as necessary to maintain your desired investment strategy.

It’s often beneficial to consult with a financial advisor to tailor a plan specific to your situation, especially regarding tax implications.
