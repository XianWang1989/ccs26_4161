When considering how to allocate your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, it's essential to take into account your financial goals, tax situation, and timeline. Here are some strategies:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a match for your 401(k), contribute enough to get the full match first. This is essentially free money.

2. **Tax Considerations**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions reduce your taxable income, which can be beneficial if you're in a higher tax bracket now.
   - **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**: Traditional IRAs also lower your taxable income. Roth IRAs, however, allow for tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement if certain conditions are met.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: These can be used for short-term goals or after-tax growth. They may not offer the same tax advantages but provide greater flexibility.

3. **Emergency Fund**: Ensure you have an emergency fund (3-6 months of expenses) in a liquid form (not in 401(k) or taxable accounts).

4. **Asset Allocation**:
   - Consider investing more aggressive assets (like stocks) in tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) and IRA) to maximize tax-free growth.
   - Place more tax-efficient investments (like index funds or ETFs) in taxable accounts, as they may have lower capital gains taxes.

5. **Long-Term vs. Short-Term Goals**: 
   - For retirement savings, prioritize the 401(k) and IRA. 
   - For shorter-term needs (like buying a home), focus more on taxable accounts.

6. **Regular Reassessment**: As your financial situation and tax laws change, reassess your allocations regularly to ensure they align with your goals.

Consider consulting with a financial advisor to create a personalized strategy tailored to your specific situation.
