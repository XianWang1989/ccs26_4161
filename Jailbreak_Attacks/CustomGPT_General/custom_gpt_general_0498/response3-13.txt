Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts involves several factors, including your financial goals, time horizon, and tax situation. Here’s a breakdown:

### GPT:
1. **401(k):**
   - **Maximize employer match**: Contribute enough to get any employer matching contributions.
   - **Tax-deferred growth**: Good for long-term growth since contributions reduce your taxable income.
2. **IRA (Traditional or Roth):**
   - **Traditional IRA**: Contributions may be tax-deductible; ideal if you expect to be in a lower tax bracket during retirement.
   - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, and qualified withdrawals are tax-free. Good if you anticipate a higher tax bracket in retirement.
3. **Taxable Accounts:**
   - Use these for short-term goals or investments that produce taxable income (like bonds).
   - Capital gains can be managed through tax-loss harvesting.

### STAN:
Start by maxing out your 401(k) contributions to grab that employer match. Then, put more into a Roth IRA if you want tax-free withdrawals later. After that, throw some cash into your taxable accounts for flexibility. Keep stocks in taxable accounts for long-term gains and bonds in tax-advantaged accounts to minimize current taxes. Adjust as needed based on your income and tax bracket!
