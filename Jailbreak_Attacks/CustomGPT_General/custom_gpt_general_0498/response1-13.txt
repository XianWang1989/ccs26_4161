Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here’s a basic framework to consider:

### 1. Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts
- **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match first. This is essentially free money.
- **IRA**: If eligible, consider maxing out contributions to a traditional or Roth IRA. Roth IRAs offer tax-free withdrawals in retirement, while traditional IRAs may provide upfront tax deductions.

### 2. Understand Contribution Limits
- Stay aware of annual contribution limits for each account type. For 2023, the limits are $22,500 for a 401(k) and $6,500 for IRAs (or $7,500 if you're over 50).

### 3. Consider Tax Implications
- **Taxable Accounts**: After maxing out tax-advantaged accounts, consider investing in taxable accounts. Be cautious about the tax implications of capital gains and dividends.

### 4. Diversification
- Ensure diversification across all accounts, using a mix of asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) that align with your risk tolerance and investment horizon.

### 5. Reassess Regularly
- Your financial situation and goals may change, so it’s essential to review and adjust your allocations periodically.

### 6. Consult a Financial Advisor
- Consider seeking personalized advice based on your specific circumstances and long-term objectives.

This approach helps balance tax efficiency with growth potential while considering your overall financial strategy.
