The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various factors, including your financial goals, current tax situation, retirement timeline, and investment preferences. Here are some general guidelines to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your 401(k) offers an employer match, contribute enough to get the full match. This is essentially free money and a great return on your investment.

2. **Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**: Prioritize contributions to tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) and IRA) before investing in taxable accounts. This allows your investments to grow tax-deferred or tax-free, depending on the account type.

3. **401(k) Contributions**: Contribute to your 401(k) up to the maximum limit, especially if you're in a higher tax bracket. For 2023, the limit is $22,500 ($30,000 if over age 50). Consider utilizing a Roth 401(k) option if available, as it allows for tax-free growth post-retirement.

4. **IRA Contributions**: After maximizing your 401(k) contributions, consider contributing to a Traditional or Roth IRA. The annual contribution limit for 2023 is $6,500 (or $7,500 if you’re 50 or older). A Roth IRA is beneficial if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement since withdrawals are tax-free.

5. **Taxable Account**: Once you’ve maxed out tax-advantaged accounts, use a taxable brokerage account for additional investment opportunities. Keep in mind that capital gains taxes may apply on any profits realized from investments held in taxable accounts.

6. **Asset Allocation**: Consider asset allocation based on your risk tolerance and investment horizon. Generally, equities are favored in Roth accounts for potential tax-free growth, while bonds might be more suitable for taxable accounts due to their interest income tax treatment.

7. **Tax Considerations**: Be mindful of the tax implications of withdrawals. Withdrawals from a traditional 401(k) or IRA are taxed as ordinary income, whereas Roth IRA withdrawals can be tax-free under qualifying conditions.

8. **Rebalancing**: Regularly review and rebalance your portfolio to maintain your desired asset allocation across all accounts.

9. **Consult with a Financial Advisor**: It can be beneficial to work with a financial advisor to create a personalized strategy tailored to your unique financial situation.

These steps will help you build a diversified and tax-efficient investment portfolio over time.
