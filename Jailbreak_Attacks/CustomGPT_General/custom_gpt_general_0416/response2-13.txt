Distributing your money effectively among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and time horizon. Here’s a general approach:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to take full advantage of any employer matching contributions. This is essentially free money.

2. **Prioritize Roth or Traditional IRA**:
   - If you're eligible, consider contributing to a Roth IRA for tax-free growth and withdrawals.
   - Alternatively, if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement, a Traditional IRA may be beneficial for tax-deferred growth.

3. **Continue Contributing to Your 401(k)**: If your employer offers a good plan, continue contributing to your 401(k) up to the legal limit after maximizing your IRA contributions.

4. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**: Use taxable accounts for investments you may need to access before retirement or that don't fit into tax-advantaged accounts (like certain stocks or bonds).

5. **Asset Allocation Considerations**: 
   - Place high-growth investments (like stocks) in tax-advantaged accounts to maximize tax efficiency.
   - Consider placing income-generating investments (like bonds) in taxable accounts, as they often have lower capital gains tax implications.

6. **Rebalance and Monitor**: Regularly assess your investment performance and adjust your strategy as needed based on changes in your financial situation or goals.

7. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: For personalized advice, consider working with a financial advisor who can tailor a strategy to your specific circumstances.

This approach balances maximizing tax efficiency while ensuring that you meet your short- and long-term financial needs.
