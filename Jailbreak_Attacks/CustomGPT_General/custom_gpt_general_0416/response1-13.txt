Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment strategy. Here are some general strategies to consider:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**
   - **401(k)**: If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to receive the full match; it’s essentially "free money."
   - **IRA**: Consider contributing to a Roth IRA if you qualify, as it allows tax-free withdrawals in retirement. If your income is too high, consider a traditional IRA for tax deductions.

### 2. **Consider Your Tax Situation**
   - **Taxable Accounts**: These are generally used for savings goals that may occur before retirement. They’re also useful for investments that generate qualified dividends or long-term capital gains, as these are usually taxed at lower rates than ordinary income.

### 3. **Diversify Investments**
   - Spread your investments across asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) within each account based on your risk tolerance and time horizon.

### 4. **Investment Time Horizon**
   - Short-term goals (1-5 years): Consider keeping money in taxable accounts or a high-yield savings account.
   - Long-term goals (beyond 5 years): Focus on funding retirement accounts first.

### 5. **Rebalancing and Over time**
   - Regularly review and rebalance your portfolio to maintain your desired asset allocation.

### 6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**
   - A professional can offer personalized advice based on your specific financial situation.

Balancing these accounts effectively can optimize your tax efficiency, investment growth, and readiness for retirement.
