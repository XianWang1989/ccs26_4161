Distributing your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various factors, including your financial goals, risk tolerance, and tax situation. Here’s a general strategy to consider:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**
   - **401(k):** If your employer offers a match, contribute at least enough to get the full match—this is essentially free money.
   - **IRA (Traditional/Roth):** Max out for tax benefits. Traditional IRAs can reduce taxable income, while Roth IRAs grow tax-free.

### 2. **Evaluate Contribution Limits**
   - Ensure you're aware of annual contribution limits for 401(k) and IRA accounts to avoid exceeding them.

### 3. **Consider Tax Implications**
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Use these for investments that may generate high short-term capital gains, which can be taxed at higher rates.

### 4. **Diversify Investments**
   - Within these accounts, diversify your investments according to your risk tolerance. Use a mix of stocks, bonds, and other assets.

### 5. **Rebalance Periodically**
   - Review and adjust your asset allocation periodically based on market performance and changes in your financial situation or goals.

### 6. **Plan for Withdrawals Wisely**
   - Consider the tax implications of withdrawals, especially if you plan to retire early or need access to funds before traditional retirement age.

### Example Distribution Strategy
- **401(k):** 15% of your salary up to the company match.
- **IRA:** Full contribution limit if eligible.
- **Taxable Account:** Anything above these limits or if you prefer liquidity.

### Conclusion
Tailor your strategy as needed, consult a financial advisor, and regularly assess your financial goals and tax situation.
