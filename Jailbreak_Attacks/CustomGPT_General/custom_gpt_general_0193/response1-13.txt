Distributing money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here are some general strategies to consider:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**
   - **401(k):** Contribute enough to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money.
   - **IRA:** If you're eligible, consider contributing to a Traditional or Roth IRA. Roth IRAs provide tax-free growth, while Traditional IRAs may offer immediate tax deductions.

### 2. **Consider Tax Implications**
   - **401(k):** Contributions lower your taxable income now but are taxed upon withdrawal.
   - **IRA:** Tax treatment depends on whether it’s a Traditional or Roth IRA.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Interest, dividends, and capital gains are taxed in the year they are realized.

### 3. **Assess Your Time Horizon**
   - Longer time horizons can benefit from investing in growth assets (like stocks) in tax-advantaged accounts to maximize compounding.
   - Funds needed in the short term may be better held in taxable accounts or cash equivalents.

### 4. **Diversify Investments**
   - Use tax-advantaged accounts for higher-growth investments and keep more stable, income-generating assets in taxable accounts to manage taxes efficiently.

### 5. **Regularly Rebalance**
   - Review and adjust your asset allocation periodically based on market performance, changing goals, and age.

### 6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**
   - For personalized advice tailored to your situation, it’s a good idea to work with a financial advisor.

By following these principles, you can create a balanced strategy that aligns with your retirement goals and tax situation.
