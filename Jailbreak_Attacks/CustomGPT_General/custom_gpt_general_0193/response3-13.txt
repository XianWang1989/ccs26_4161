The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various factors, including your financial goals, risk tolerance, and tax situation. Here’s a general approach:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**
   - **401(k)**: If your employer offers a match, prioritize contributing enough to get the full match, as this is essentially free money.
   - **IRA**: Contribute to your IRA (Roth or Traditional, depending on your tax situation) to take advantage of tax benefits. Many people aim for the maximum contribution if possible.

### 2. **Consider Your Tax Situation**
   - **Tax-Deferred Accounts (401(k), Traditional IRA)**: Good for individuals expecting to be in a lower tax bracket in retirement.
   - **Roth IRA**: Beneficial if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement, as withdrawals are tax-free.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Useful for funds needed in the short term, but be mindful of capital gains taxes.

### 3. **Distribute Based on Investment Goals**
   - Align your investments in each account based on the time horizon for when you’ll need the money. Longer time horizons can afford more risk (e.g., stocks), while shorter horizons may require more stability (e.g., bonds or cash).

### 4. **Review Asset Allocation**
   - Maintain a diversified asset allocation across all accounts. For example, you might hold more aggressive investments in your 401(k) and IRA, while using your taxable accounts for more conservative investments or cash for liquidity.

### 5. **Monitor and Adjust Regularly**
   - Regularly review your investments and contributions. Adjust based on market conditions, changes in your income, and evolving financial goals.

### Example Distribution Strategy
- **401(k)**: 20-30% (to maximize employer match)
- **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**: 15-25%
- **Taxable Account**: 10-20% (for liquidity needs)
- **Cash Savings**: 10% (for emergencies)

### Conclusion
Balancing contributions based on your circumstances and retirement strategy is crucial. It's often wise to consult a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your specific situation.
