Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some key strategies:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a 401(k) match, contribute enough to get the full match first—it’s free money.

2. **Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: Good for larger contributions and pre-tax benefits. Choose this for higher contributions and if you expect to be in a lower tax bracket during retirement.
   - **IRA**: A traditional IRA offers tax deductions, while a Roth IRA provides tax-free withdrawals in retirement. Choose based on your current vs. expected future tax rate.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: Use this for funds needed before retirement. It offers flexibility without penalties but be mindful of capital gains taxes.

4. **Diversification**: Spread investments across asset classes (stocks, bonds) and sectors to minimize risk.

5. **Rebalance Regularly**: Adjust your portfolio to maintain your desired asset allocation.

6. **Consider Time Horizon**: For long-term goals, higher-risk investments may be suitable. For short-term goals, stick with safer options.

Consulting a financial advisor can provide personalized strategies based on your specific circumstances.
