The best way to distribute your money amongst your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various factors such as your retirement goals, tax situation, and investment preferences. Here are some general strategies:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match. Prioritize this if your employer provides one.
   - **IRA**: Consider maxing out a Traditional or Roth IRA for additional tax benefits.

2. **Assess Your Tax Situation**:
   - Use tax-advantaged accounts for investments expected to grow significantly. This defers or avoids taxes.
   - Taxable accounts can hold investments generating lower capital gains or income (like index funds or municipal bonds).

3. **Investment Goals**:
   - Align your investment strategy with your time horizon and risk tolerance.  
   - For short-term goals, lean more towards safer, liquid assets in taxable accounts.

4. **Diversification**:
   - Ensure your overall portfolio is well-diversified across all accounts, considering asset classes and sectors.

5. **Withdrawals Strategy**:
   - Plan for tax implications when withdrawing funds in retirement, balancing between accounts to minimize taxes.

6. **Regular Review**:
   - Periodically reassess and rebalance allocations to stay aligned with your goals as markets and life circumstances change.

Consider speaking with a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your specific situation.
