Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various factors like your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here’s a strategic approach:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a matching contribution to your 401(k), start by contributing enough to take full advantage of that match. It's essentially free money.

2. **Contribute to a Roth IRA (if eligible)**: If you qualify, contribute to a Roth IRA after maxing out your 401(k) match. Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, and qualified withdrawals in retirement are tax-free.

3. **Max Out Tax-Advantaged Accounts**: Aim to contribute the maximum allowable amounts to your 401(k) and IRA. For 2023, the contribution limits are $22,500 for 401(k)s (or $30,000 if over age 50) and $6,500 for IRAs (or $7,500 if over age 50).

4. **Invest in Taxable Accounts**: After maximizing contributions to tax-advantaged accounts, consider funding taxable accounts. They offer more flexibility for withdrawals and can be advantageous for short-term goals or diversifying assets.

5. **Consider Tax Implications**: Be mindful of the tax implications of your investments. Long-term capital gains and qualified dividends are taxed at lower rates in taxable accounts. Using tax-efficient investment strategies can minimize your tax burden.

6. **Emergency Savings**: Ensure you have an emergency fund in a separate account (not included in these categories) that's easily accessible for unexpected expenses.

### Summary

- **401(k)**: Maximize the employer match first, then contribute more.
- **Roth IRA**: If eligible, contribute after tax to benefit from tax-free withdrawals.
- **Taxable Accounts**: Use for additional saving and investment after maxing tax-advantaged accounts.
- **Tax Considerations**: Focus on efficient investing to reduce tax liabilities.

### Tailor This Approach

Your specific situation and goals—like retirement age, risk tolerance, and investment preferences—should guide how you distribute your funds. Consider consulting with a financial advisor for personalized advice.
