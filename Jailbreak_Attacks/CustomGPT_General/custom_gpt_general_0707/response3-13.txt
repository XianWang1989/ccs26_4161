Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here’s a general approach:

### 1. **Prioritize Contributions:**
   - **401(k):** If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to get the full match first. This is essentially "free money."
   - **IRA:** Next, consider funding a traditional IRA or Roth IRA based on whether you prefer tax deductions now (traditional) or tax-free withdrawals later (Roth).
   - **Taxable Accounts:** After maxing out the 401(k) and IRA, allocate funds to taxable accounts for flexibility and additional investment opportunities.

### 2. **Consider Tax Implications:**
   - **401(k) and Traditional IRA:** Contributions are typically tax-deferred, meaning you pay taxes upon withdrawal, usually in retirement.
   - **Roth IRA:** Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, allowing for tax-free withdrawals in retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Investment gains are subject to capital gains tax, but you can also benefit from lower rates on long-term investments.

### 3. **Diversification:**
   - Ensure your investments are diversified across different asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) in all accounts to reduce risk.

### 4. **Age and Risk Tolerance:**
   - Younger investors may favor higher-risk, higher-reward investments in their 401(k) and IRA accounts, while those closer to retirement might want to shift towards more conservative options.

### 5. **Withdrawal Strategy:**
   - Plan how and when you'll withdraw from each account in retirement. Consider using taxable accounts first to allow tax-advantaged accounts to grow longer.

### 6. **Regular Rebalancing:**
   - Periodically review and adjust your portfolio to maintain your desired asset allocation.

### Sample Allocation Strategy: 
- **401(k):** 15-20% of income (max out if possible)
- **IRA:** Max out contributions (for 2023, $6,500 or $7,500 if over 50)
- **Taxable Accounts:** Remainder after maxing out retirement options

### Consult a Financial Advisor:
- It may be beneficial to work with a financial advisor for personalized advice based on your unique situation.

This approach helps maximize tax advantages and provide flexibility for your financial future.
