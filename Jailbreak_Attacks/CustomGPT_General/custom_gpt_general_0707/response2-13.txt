Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your age, risk tolerance, tax situation, and retirement goals. Here’s a general approach:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get any employer match. This is essentially free money.

2. **Max Out IRAs**: If eligible, consider maxing out contributions to a Traditional or Roth IRA. Roth IRAs are particularly beneficial if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement since withdrawals are tax-free.

3. **Focus on Tax Efficiency**:
   - **Tax-Deferred Accounts**: Use your 401(k) and Traditional IRA for investments that generate ordinary income, like bonds or REITs, to avoid higher taxes on these gains.
   - **After-Tax Accounts**: Use taxable accounts for investments likely to appreciate, like stocks, as they benefit from lower long-term capital gains rates.

4. **Adjust for Risk and Time Horizon**: If you’re young and have a long time until retirement, you may opt for a higher allocation to stocks in your 401(k) and IRA, while keeping more conservative investments in taxable accounts.

5. **Rebalance Regularly**: Regularly review and adjust your asset allocation based on market performance and changes in your financial situation.

6. **Consider Tax Implications**: Be mindful of the tax implications of withdrawals from each type of account when planning for retirement.

It's crucial to tailor this strategy to your personal situation, so consider consulting with a financial advisor to refine your distribution strategy.
