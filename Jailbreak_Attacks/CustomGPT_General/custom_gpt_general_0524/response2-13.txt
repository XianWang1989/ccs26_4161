Distributing your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, such as your financial goals, risk tolerance, time horizon, and current tax situation. Here’s a general strategy to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**: If your employer offers a matching contribution, aim to contribute enough to take full advantage of that match. This is essentially free money and a great start to your retirement savings.

2. **Contribute to an IRA**: After maximizing the employer match, consider contributing to an IRA (Traditional or Roth, depending on your eligibility and tax situation). Roth IRAs offer tax-free growth, while Traditional IRAs provide tax-deferred growth.

3. **Consider Tax Efficiency**: Generally, it's more tax-efficient to hold stocks and high-growth investments in tax-advantaged accounts (like 401(k)s and IRAs) since they grow tax-free or tax-deferred. Reserve bonds and income-generating assets for taxable accounts to take advantage of lower capital gains tax rates.

4. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**: Use taxable accounts for investments you may need access to before retirement. This could be a good place for short-term goals or when you want liquidity without penalties.

5. **Maintain Diversification**: Regardless of the account type, ensure your investments are well-diversified across asset classes (stocks, bonds, real estate, etc.) tailored to your risk tolerance.

6. **Review and Adjust**: Regularly review your allocation and adjust it as needed, considering changes in income, expenses, and life circumstances.

It's always a good idea to consult with a financial advisor for personalized guidance based on your situation.
