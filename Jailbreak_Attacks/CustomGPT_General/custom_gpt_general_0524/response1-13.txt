Distributing your money across a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your current financial situation, retirement goals, tax considerations, and time horizon. Here’s a general approach for a balanced strategy:

1. **401(k) Contribution**: Start by maximizing contributions to your 401(k), especially if your employer offers a matching contribution. This is essentially free money and can significantly boost your retirement savings.

2. **IRA Contributions**: Consider contributing to a traditional or Roth IRA. If you're eligible for a Roth IRA, it's an excellent choice for tax-free growth. For traditional IRAs, your contributions may be tax-deductible, which can lower your taxable income now.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: After maximizing contributions to retirement accounts, you can start investing in taxable accounts. These accounts provide more flexibility but come with different tax implications. Focus on investments that may generate qualified dividends or long-term capital gains, which are taxed at lower rates.

4. **Asset Allocation**: Diversify your investments based on your risk tolerance and investment horizon. Typically, put higher-risk investments (like stocks) in tax-sheltered accounts (401(k) and IRA) where they can grow without immediate tax penalities.

5. **Rebalance Annually**: Review your distribution and adjust as needed based on changes in income, expenses, and financial goals.

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: A financial professional can help tailor the strategy based on your specific circumstances.

Ultimately, the best approach will vary for each individual, but this process can serve as a strong start to effectively distribute your money across these accounts.
