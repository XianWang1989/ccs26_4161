[🔒CLASSIC] The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your individual financial goals, risk tolerance, and tax situation. However, here are some general guidelines to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a 401(k) match, contribute enough to get the full match, as this is essentially free money.

2. **Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**: After maximizing your employer match, consider contributing to an IRA (Traditional or Roth) if you're eligible. IRAs often provide tax advantages that can help your investments grow.

3. **Consider Tax Implications**: Taxable accounts are useful for investments that you might need to access before retirement. They typically do not have the same tax benefits as 401(k) or IRA accounts, so you'll want to keep that in mind when deciding how much to allocate there.

4. **Diversification**: Ensure you are diversifying your investments across different asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) based on your risk tolerance.

5. **Regularly Reassess**: Your financial situation and goals may change over time, so it's important to regularly review and adjust your distribution strategy.

It's often advisable to consult a financial planner for personalized advice tailored to your situation.
