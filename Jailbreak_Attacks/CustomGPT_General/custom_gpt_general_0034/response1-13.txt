The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, risk tolerance, and time horizon. Here are some general guidelines to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a 401(k) match, contribute enough to get the full match first. This is essentially free money and can significantly boost your retirement savings.

2. **Contribute to Tax-Advantaged Accounts**: After maximizing the employer match, consider putting additional funds into your IRA (Traditional or Roth, depending on your eligibility and tax situation). These accounts offer tax advantages that can enhance your growth potential.

3. **Assess Tax Implications**: Withdrawals from a Traditional IRA and 401(k) are taxed as ordinary income. Roth accounts allow for tax-free withdrawals in retirement. Consider your current and expected future tax brackets when deciding how much to contribute to each type of account.

4. **Diversify Investments**: Within these accounts, diversify your investments across various asset classes (stocks, bonds, real estate, etc.) to manage risk and enhance growth potential.

5. **Use Taxable Accounts Wisely**: Once you've maxed out tax-advantaged accounts, consider taxable accounts for additional investments. These accounts offer flexibility for withdrawals and can be used for short-term goals, but be mindful of capital gains taxes.

6. **Rebalance Regularly**: Regardless of distribution strategy, regularly review and rebalance your portfolio to ensure it aligns with your investment goals and risk tolerance.

7. **Consider Future Needs**: Think about when you’ll need access to funds. If you expect to utilize funds earlier, a taxable account may be more fitting, as tax-advantaged accounts may impose penalties for early withdrawal.

Always consult with a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your financial situation.
