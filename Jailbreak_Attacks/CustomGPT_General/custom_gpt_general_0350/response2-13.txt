Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts involves several considerations, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement plans. Here are some strategies you might consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Matching**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to receive any employer match, as it's essentially "free money."

2. **Understand Contribution Limits**: Familiarize yourself with the annual contribution limits for each account type. For 2023, the 401(k) limit is $22,500 (with an additional $7,500 catch-up contribution if you're over 50), and the IRA limit is $6,500 (with a $1,000 catch-up for those 50 and older).

3. **Tax Considerations**:
   - **Tax-Deferred Accounts (401(k) and IRA)**: Contributions are typically made pre-tax, reducing your taxable income. Withdrawals in retirement will be taxed as ordinary income.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Investments in these accounts are subject to capital gains taxes when sold, but they offer more flexibility in terms of withdrawals.

4. **Risk Tolerance and Investment Goals**: Allocate funds based on your risk tolerance. Generally, consider having a more aggressive investment strategy in accounts that have tax-deferred growth potential and a more conservative approach in taxable accounts.

5. **Diversification**: Ensure you are diversified across various asset classes in all accounts, which can help mitigate risk.

6. **Rebalance Regularly**: As your investments grow, regularly review your allocation to maintain your desired risk level and investment strategy.

7. **Consider Future Income Needs**: Think about when you will need to access these funds. Taxable accounts can provide more liquidity without penalties, but retirement accounts might be better for long-term growth.
