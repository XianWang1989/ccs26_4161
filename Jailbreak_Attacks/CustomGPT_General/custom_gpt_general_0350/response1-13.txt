The optimal distribution of your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, risk tolerance, and investment timeline. Here’s a general guideline for each account type:

1. **401(k)**: 
   - If your employer offers a matching contribution, prioritize contributing enough to maximize that match, as it’s essentially free money.
   - Contributions are pre-tax (for traditional 401(k)) or post-tax (for Roth 401(k)), so choose based on your current and expected future tax situation.

2. **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**: 
   - Consider maxing out your contribution to an IRA (as of 2023, the limit is $6,500, or $7,500 if you're over age 50) to benefit from tax advantages.
   - Choose a Traditional IRA if you expect to be in a lower tax bracket when you retire, or a Roth IRA if you think your tax rate will be higher in retirement.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: 
   - Use taxable accounts for investments that do not fit well into tax-advantaged accounts, such as certain stocks or bonds that may generate high taxable events.
   - Taxable accounts are also beneficial for shorter-term goals due to their liquidity.

### Distribution Strategy:
- **Prioritize 401(k) up to the employer match.
- Max out IRA contributions (consider a Roth or Traditional based on tax outlook).
- Fill any gaps with contributions to taxable accounts.**

### Consider your time horizon:
If you're younger and have a longer investment horizon, you may focus more on 401(k) and IRA contributions to maximize growth, while also maintaining some liquidity with taxable accounts.

### Re-Evaluate Regularly:
Market conditions, life changes, and financial goals can shift, necessitating a review and adjustment of your distribution strategy.

### Questions for further thought:
1. What are your retirement goals, and how soon do you plan to retire?
2. How do you currently allocate your investments within each account type?
3. What is your tax situation now compared to what you expect it to be in retirement?
