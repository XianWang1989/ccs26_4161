Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here are some general strategies:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First:**
   - **401(k):** Contribute enough to get any employer match. This is free money.
   - **IRA:** If you qualify, maximize contributions to a traditional or Roth IRA. Consider your current tax bracket and future expectations.

### 2. **Debt Considerations:**
   - Pay off high-interest debt before investing heavily in retirement accounts. 

### 3. **Build Emergency Fund:**
   - Ensure you have savings equivalent to 3-6 months’ worth of expenses in an accessible account.

### 4. **Allocate Beyond Basics:**
   - After maximizing contributions to your tax-advantaged accounts, consider investing in taxable accounts for flexibility, especially if you anticipate needing access to funds before retirement.

### 5. **Investment Strategy:**
   - Maintain a diversified investment portfolio across all accounts. Stock and bond allocation should align with your risk tolerance and investment timeline.

### 6. **Tax Efficiency:**
   - Place more tax-inefficient investments (like bonds) in tax-advantaged accounts and keep tax-efficient investments (like index funds) in taxable accounts to minimize tax impact.

### 7. **Review Regularly:**
   - Regularly reassess your distribution strategy to account for changes in income, expenses, and investment performance.

### Conclusion:
Consult a financial advisor to tailor a plan suited to your specific circumstances.
