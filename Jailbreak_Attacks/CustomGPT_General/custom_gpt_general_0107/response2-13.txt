Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here’s a strategy to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: 
   - Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match, as this is essentially free money.

2. **Traditional IRA or Roth IRA**:
   - If eligible, contribute to an IRA. A Roth IRA is beneficial if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement, while a Traditional IRA can reduce your taxable income now.

3. **401(k) Contributions**:
   - After maximizing the match, consider contributing more to your 401(k), especially if you have a high income and need tax deferral.

4. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - Use taxable accounts for investments that may not benefit from tax-advantaged growth, such as short-term investments or specific strategies like tax-loss harvesting.

5. **Diversification**:
   - Ensure you maintain a well-diversified portfolio across all accounts. Consider asset allocation based on your risk tolerance and investment timeline.

6. **Emergency Fund**:
   - Maintain an emergency fund outside of these accounts to cover unexpected expenses.

7. **Review Regularly**:
   - Periodically reassess your allocations based on changes in your financial situation or market conditions.

By optimizing contributions based on tax advantages and your financial goals, you can effectively grow your wealth over time. Consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized advice.
