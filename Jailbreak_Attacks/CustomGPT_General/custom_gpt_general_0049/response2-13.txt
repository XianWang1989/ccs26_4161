Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, risk tolerance, and time horizon. Here’s a general approach to consider:

1. **401(k):**
   - Maximize any employer match: If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to get the full match, as this is essentially free money.
   - Evaluate fees: Ensure that the investment options available in your 401(k) are low-cost and suit your investment strategy. 

2. **IRA:**
   - Roth vs. Traditional: Decide whether a Roth IRA (tax-free withdrawals in retirement) or traditional IRA (tax-deductible contributions) is more beneficial based on your current and expected future tax brackets.
   - Maximize contributions: Aim to contribute the maximum allowed to your IRA each year to take full advantage of tax benefits.

3. **Taxable Accounts:**
   - Use for flexibility: Invest in taxable accounts for goals that are short to mid-term (e.g., buying a house, funding education) since you can access these funds without penalties.
   - Consider tax-efficient investments: Look for investments like index funds or ETFs that generate less taxable income, and consider tax-loss harvesting strategies to offset gains.

**Overall Strategy:**
- A common strategy is to prioritize your 401(k) contributions to get the match, then fund your IRA fully, and lastly, any remaining savings can go into taxable accounts.
- Diversifying investments among these accounts can enhance growth while maintaining tax efficiency. Consider asset allocation and how each account impacts your overall tax situation.

It's essential to regularly reassess your strategy as your financial situation changes, and consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized advice.
