Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment time horizon. Here are some strategies to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: First, contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match, if available. This is essentially "free money."

2. **Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: Use this account to benefit from tax-deferred growth. Consider contributing the maximum limit if you can.
   - **Traditional IRA**: If you're eligible, this is another tax-deferred option. If you're in a higher tax bracket now but expect to be lower in retirement, this may be a good option.
   - **Roth IRA**: If you believe your tax rate will be higher in retirement, consider contributing to a Roth IRA for tax-free withdrawals.

3. **Pay Attention to Taxable Accounts**: After maximizing contributions to tax-advantaged accounts, allocate excess funds to taxable accounts. These can provide flexibility for withdrawals without penalties before retirement age. Remember to consider capital gains taxes when investing in these accounts.

4. **Diversification**: Ensure your investments are diversified across asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) in all accounts to manage risk effectively.

5. **Consider Your Time Horizon**: Align your investment choices with your timeline. For longer-term goals, more aggressive investments may be suitable, while shorter-term goals might warrant a more conservative approach.

6. **Rebalance Regularly**: Check your asset allocation periodically to ensure it aligns with your risk tolerance and investment goals.

7. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: If you're unsure, a financial advisor can provide personalized guidance based on your unique situation.

By following these guidelines, you can effectively distribute your funds to balance growth potential with tax efficiency.
