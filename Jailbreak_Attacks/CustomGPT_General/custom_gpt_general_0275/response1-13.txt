Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment time horizon. Here are some general guidelines:

### **1. 401(k):**
- **Maximize Employer Match:** Contribute enough to get the full employer match, as this is essentially free money.
- **Pre-Tax Contributions:** Focus on pre-tax contributions if you're in a higher tax bracket; this can reduce your taxable income.

### **2. IRA (Traditional/Roth):**
- **Tax Considerations:** Consider a Roth IRA if you anticipate being in a higher tax bracket in retirement since withdrawals are tax-free. With a Traditional IRA, contributions may be tax-deductible, but withdrawals are taxed.
- **Contribution Limits:** Ensure you're maximizing contributions up to the limit for either traditional or Roth IRAs.

### **3. Taxable Accounts:**
- **Flexibility:** Use taxable accounts for savings goals that are shorter-term, as they provide more liquidity.
- **Investment Choices:** Invest in tax-efficient vehicles (like index funds or ETFs) to minimize capital gains taxes.

### **General Strategy:**
1. **Emergency Fund:** Ensure you have an adequate emergency fund before investing heavily.
2. **Diversification:** Maintain a diverse portfolio across all accounts to spread risk.
3. **Regular Rebalancing:** Review and rebalance your portfolio periodically based on performance and changing goals.

### **Consult a Financial Advisor:**
Consider speaking with a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your financial situation and goals.
