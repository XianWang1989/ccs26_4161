To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money. Aim to max out contributions if possible.
   - **IRA**: Contribute to a traditional or Roth IRA based on your income level and tax situation. If eligible, prioritize a Roth IRA for tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement.

2. **Invest in a Taxable Account**:
   - Use taxable accounts for investments that might be subject to lower capital gains tax, such as long-term investments or tax-efficient funds (like index funds).

3. **Consider Your Tax Bracket**:
   - In higher income years, focus on tax-deferred accounts (401(k) or traditional IRA) to reduce your taxable income. In lower earning years, consider contributing to a Roth for tax-free growth.

4. **Diversification**:
   - Ensure your investments are diversified across different asset classes. Use tax-advantaged accounts for higher-growth investments and keep more stable or income-generating assets in taxable accounts.

5. **Withdrawal Strategy**:
   - Plan ahead for withdrawals in retirement; typically, you'll want to draw from taxable accounts first for tax efficiency, then tax-deferred, and finally tax-free accounts.

6. **Regular Review**:
   - Reassess your allocations annually to account for changes in income, tax laws, and financial goals.

It's always a good idea to consult a financial advisor to tailor a strategy specific to your circumstances.
