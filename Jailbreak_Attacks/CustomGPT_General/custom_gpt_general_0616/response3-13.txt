To determine the best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following factors:

### 1. **Contribution Limits**
   - **401(k):** Check your employer's limits (usually $20,500 for pre-tax contributions in 2023, plus a catch-up option if you're over 50).
   - **IRA:** Contribution limit is generally $6,500, with an additional $1,000 catch-up for those 50 and older.

### 2. **Tax Considerations**
   - **401(k):** Contributions are often pre-tax, reducing your taxable income. Taxes are paid upon withdrawal.
   - **IRA:** Traditional IRAs can offer tax-deductible contributions, while Roth IRAs allow tax-free withdrawals.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Investments here are taxed annually on dividends and capital gains.

### 3. **Investment Goals**
   - If your goal is long-term growth, aim to maximize tax-advantaged accounts (401(k), IRA) first. Limit contributions to taxable accounts until you're maximizing tax-advantaged options.

### 4. **Employer Match**
   - Prioritize contributing enough to your 401(k) to receive any employer match, as this is essentially "free money."

### 5. **Withdrawal Strategy**
   - Factor in when you may need access to funds. Taxable accounts offer flexibility without penalties.

### 6. **Risk Tolerance**
   - Align your asset allocation with your risk tolerance across all account types, but be mindful of tax implications.

### Example Distribution Strategy
- **Maximize 401(k) contributions:** Contribute enough to get the full employer match.
- **Contribute to an IRA (Roth or Traditional):** Depending on your tax situation and retirement strategy.
- **Supplement with taxable accounts:** Invest any remaining funds here while managing tax liabilities on gains.

Always consult with a financial advisor to tailor strategies based on your specific circumstances.
