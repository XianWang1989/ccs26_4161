To effectively distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**:
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match first, as this is essentially free money. Aim to max out contributions if possible.
   - **Traditional or Roth IRA**: After maximizing your 401(k) match, consider contributing to an IRA. Choose between a traditional IRA (tax-deductible contributions) or a Roth IRA (tax-free withdrawals in retirement) based on your current tax situation and future expectations.

2. **Consider Your Tax Situation**:
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for funds you might need sooner or for investments that generate taxable income. Long-term capital gains and qualified dividends are generally taxed at a lower rate.

3. **Investment Strategy**:
   - Diversify your investments across all accounts. For example, place higher-risk assets in tax-advantaged accounts to benefit from tax deferral.

4. **Retirement Goals**:
   - Align your account contributions with your retirement timeline. For earlier retirement, prioritize tax-advantaged accounts.

5. **Emergency Fund**:
   - Ensure you have liquid savings outside of these accounts for emergencies, preventing the need to tap into retirement funds.

6. **Rebalance Regularly**:
   - Periodically review and adjust your allocation based on performance and changing goals.

Consider speaking with a financial advisor for personalized advice based on your specific situation.
