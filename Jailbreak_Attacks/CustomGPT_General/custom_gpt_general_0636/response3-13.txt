To optimize your investment strategy across a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following guidelines:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First:**
   - **401(k):** Contribute enough to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money. Max out if possible.
   - **IRA (Roth or Traditional):** Consider maximizing contributions here as well, focusing on a Roth IRA for tax-free growth if you're eligible.

### 2. **Investment Strategy:**
   - **401(k) and IRA:** Use these for long-term growth investments (stocks, index funds) since they have tax deferral benefits. If you expect to be in a lower tax bracket during retirement, a Traditional IRA may be preferable.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Utilize these for shorter-term investments or assets that generate regular income (like bonds or dividend-paying stocks) since you’ll pay taxes on any gains annually.

### 3. **Asset Location:**
   - Keep high-growth and volatile investments in tax-advantaged accounts to maximize tax-deferred growth.
   - Place more conservative investments (bonds, income-generating assets) in taxable accounts, where capital gains can be effectively managed.

### 4. **Rebalance Regularly:**
   - Monitor and adjust your allocations annually or as your goals change to maintain your risk profile.

### 5. **Consider Emergency Funds:**
   - Ensure you have an emergency fund outside of retirement accounts for accessibility.

### 6. **Consult a Financial Advisor:**
   - Tailor your strategy to your personal financial situation and goals.

These strategies can help you benefit from tax advantages while growing your investments effectively.
