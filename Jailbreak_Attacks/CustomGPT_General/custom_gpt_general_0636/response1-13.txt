Distributing your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various factors, including your age, retirement goals, tax situation, and risk tolerance. Here are some strategies to consider:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money. Consider maximizing contributions if you're eligible for catch-up contributions (if over 50).
   - **IRA**: Consider contributing to a Traditional IRA (tax-deferred) or a Roth IRA (tax-free withdrawals in retirement) depending on your income level and tax situation.

### 2. **Understand Contributions Limits**
   - Be aware of the annual contribution limits for each account. For 2024, the contribution limit for a 401(k) is $23,000 (plus $7,500 catch-up) and $6,500 for IRAs (plus $1,000 catch-up).

### 3. **Diversify Your Investments**
   - Within each account, diversify your investments to spread risk. Consider a mix of stocks, bonds, and other assets based on your risk tolerance.

### 4. **Tax Efficiency**
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Use this for investments that generate capital gains at favorable tax rates or for money you may need before retirement. Municipal bonds or ETFs that are tax-efficient can be good choices.
   - **Tax-Deferred Accounts**: Place bonds and high-income investments here to defer taxes until withdrawal.

### 5. **Withdrawals and Distribution Strategy**
   - Plan your withdrawal strategy for retirement, keeping in mind tax implications from each account. Generally, it may be beneficial to withdraw from taxable accounts first, followed by tax-deferred, then tax-free accounts.

### 6. **Review and Adjust**
   - Regularly review your asset allocation and performance. Adjust contributions based on changes in income, expenses, and financial goals.

### 7. **Consult a Financial Advisor**
   - For personalized advice, consider consulting a financial advisor who can help tailor a strategy based on your specific circumstances.

By following these strategies, you can create a balanced and effective approach to managing your investments across your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts.
