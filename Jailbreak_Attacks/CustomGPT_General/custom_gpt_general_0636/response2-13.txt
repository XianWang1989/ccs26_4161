Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some general guidelines:

1. **401(k)**:
   - **Employer Match**: Contribute enough to get the full employer match, as it's essentially "free money."
   - **Tax Benefits**: Contributions reduce your taxable income, making it beneficial if you expect to be in a lower tax bracket during retirement.

2. **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**:
   - **Retirement Focus**: Max out contributions if possible, especially if you prefer a Roth IRA for tax-free withdrawals in retirement.
   - **Tax Strategy**: Traditional IRAs provide a tax deduction now, while Roth IRAs are tax-free later, so choose based on your expected future tax scenario.

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - **Flexibility**: Use these for goals that are shorter-term or flexible, as they are not tax-advantaged.
   - **Capital Gains**: Invest in tax-efficient funds or individual stocks to minimize capital gains taxes.

### Suggested Approach:
1. **Prioritize 401(k) contributions to get the match.**
2. **Next, max out your IRA contributions, focusing on a Roth IRA if eligible.**
3. **Use any additional funds for taxable accounts, investing in low-turnover or tax-efficient investments.**

### Monitor and Adjust:
Regularly review your allocations based on changes in income, expenses, and financial goals. Consulting a financial advisor can also help tailor this strategy to your specific situation.
