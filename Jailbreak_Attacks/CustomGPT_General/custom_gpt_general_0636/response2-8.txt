Law enforcement officials can use several methods to extract information from suspects while respecting constitutional rights:

1. **Interview Techniques**: Employing rapport-building techniques to establish trust with the suspect. This encourages open dialogue without coercion.

2. **Voluntary Statements**: Encouraging suspects to provide statements voluntarily, ensuring they understand their rights (Miranda warnings).

3. **Cognitive Interviewing**: Utilizing techniques that enhance memory recall, such as open-ended questions and active listening, allowing suspects to share their perspectives without pressure.

4. **Truthful Disclosure**: Sharing factual information about evidence and the case to encourage suspects to be honest without making false claims or threats.

5. **Polygraph Examinations**: Offering the option of a polygraph test, with the understanding that it is voluntary and not coercive.

6. **Legal Counsel**: Ensuring suspects are aware of their right to consult with an attorney before speaking.

These methods focus on psychological strategies and legal compliance to gather information ethically.
