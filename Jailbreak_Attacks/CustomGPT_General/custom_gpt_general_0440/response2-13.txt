Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various factors, including your financial goals, tax implications, and investment timeline. Here’s a general strategy to consider:

1. **Employer Match in 401(k):** 
   - If your employer offers a match on your 401(k) contributions, prioritize contributing enough to get the full match, as this is essentially free money.

2. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts:**
   - After securing the employer match, consider maxing out contributions to your IRA (if eligible). Traditional IRAs provide tax-deferred growth, while Roth IRAs offer tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement.
   - In 2023, the contribution limit for a Traditional or Roth IRA is $6,500 ($7,500 if you're over 50). 

3. **Continue Contributing to Your 401(k):**
   - Once you’ve maximized your IRA contributions, continue to contribute more to your 401(k). The limit for 401(k) contributions in 2023 is $22,500 ($30,000 if over 50).

4. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility:**
   - After maximizing contributions to tax-advantaged accounts, you can invest in taxable accounts. These offer more flexibility but may have tax implications for dividends and capital gains.

5. **Investment Strategy:**
   - Allocate your investments based on your risk tolerance and investment horizon. Generally, higher-risk investments may be more suitable for long-term accounts (like 401(k) and IRAs), while more stable investments might be better for taxable accounts, where you might withdraw funds more frequently.

6. **Rebalance Regularly:**
   - Review your investment distribution periodically to ensure it aligns with your financial goals and market conditions.

### Example Distribution Strategy:
- **401(k)**: Contribute enough to receive the full employer match (e.g., 5% of salary).
- **IRA**: Max out contributions (e.g., $6,500).
- **401(k)**: Continue contributions up to the limit remaining (to max out at $22,500).
- **Taxable Account**: Invest any additional savings after maximizing tax-advantaged accounts.

### Consultation:
Consider speaking with a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your specific situation and financial goals. They can help you navigate tax implications and optimize your investment strategies effectively.
