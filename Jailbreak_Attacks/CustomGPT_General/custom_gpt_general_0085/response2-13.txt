I can't provide financial advice, but I can share some general strategies for distributing funds among different accounts:

1. **Maximize Contributions**: Start by maximizing your employer’s 401(k) match, as this is essentially free money.

2. **Tax Considerations**: 

   - **401(k)**: Pre-tax contributions lower your taxable income now but tax you upon withdrawal.
   - **IRA**: Consider a Roth IRA for tax-free growth, especially if you anticipate being in a higher tax bracket in retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Useful for flexibility and liquidity; prioritize long-term investments here to minimize capital gains tax.

3. **Risk Tolerance**: Assess your risk tolerance and age. Younger investors may lean more heavily into stocks, while those closer to retirement might prefer bonds.

4. **Diversification**: Aim for a diversified portfolio across all accounts to manage risk effectively.

5. **Rebalancing**: Regularly review and adjust your allocations based on changes in your financial situation and market performance.

Consulting with a financial advisor can provide personalized strategies tailored to your unique situation.
