I'm not able to provide financial or investment advice, but I can give you some general guidelines on how to think about distributing funds among different account types:

1. **Emergency Fund First**: Ensure you have an emergency fund covering 3-6 months of living expenses before investing heavily.

2. **401(k)**:
   - Take advantage of employer match programs if available.
   - Maximize contributions if you have sufficient funds after your emergency fund.

3. **IRA**:
   - Consider contributing to a Roth IRA for tax-free withdrawals in retirement.
   - Traditional IRAs can offer tax deductions depending on your income.

4. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - Use this for investments beyond retirement savings.
   - Focus on tax-efficient investments, such as index funds or ETFs.

5. **Diversification**: Ensure your investments are diversified across asset classes, regardless of account type.

6. **Tax Considerations**: Keep high-growth investments in tax-advantaged accounts to defer or avoid taxes on gains.

Consulting with a financial advisor can help tailor your strategy to your specific situation and goals.
