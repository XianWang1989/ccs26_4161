Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some general strategies:

1. **Maximize Contributions to Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: Try to contribute enough to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money. The contribution limit for 2023 is $22,500 (or $30,000 if you’re over 50).
   - **IRA**: Consider contributing to a traditional or Roth IRA. The contribution limit for 2023 is $6,500 (or $7,500 if you’re over 50). Roth IRAs offer tax-free growth, while traditional IRAs may reduce your taxable income.

2. **Emergency Savings**: Ensure you have an emergency fund (3-6 months of expenses) in a high-yield savings account before investing heavily.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: After maximizing contributions to retirement accounts, consider investing in taxable accounts. These can provide more liquidity and flexibility for short-term goals.

4. **Investment Allocation**:
   - **401(k)** and **IRA**: Typically invest in a mix of stocks and bonds, appropriate for your risk tolerance. Consider target-date funds if you're unsure.
   - **Taxable Account**: Favor investments with favorable tax treatment, like index funds or ETFs, to minimize capital gains taxes.

5. **Rebalancing**: Regularly review and adjust your portfolio to maintain your desired asset allocation, considering market performance and any changes in your financial situation.

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: A professional can help tailor a strategy to your specific situation and goals.

Balancing these accounts strategically can optimize your tax situation and help you reach your financial objectives effectively.
