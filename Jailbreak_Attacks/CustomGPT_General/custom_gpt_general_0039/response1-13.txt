Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here’s a general approach to consider:

1. **Maximize Your 401(k)**: If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to take full advantage of that—it's essentially free money. Aim for the maximum contribution limit if possible.

2. **IRA Contributions**: After your 401(k) contributions, consider funding a Traditional or Roth IRA, depending on your income and tax situation. Roth IRAs allow for tax-free withdrawals in retirement, which can be advantageous.

3. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**: Use taxable accounts for money you might need before retirement. This gives you access to funds without penalty and can be advantageous if your tax situation varies.

4. **Diversification and Balance**: Ensure that your investments are diversified across different asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) in all accounts to reduce risk.

5. **Tax Considerations**: Be mindful of the tax implications of selling investments in taxable accounts versus tax-deferred accounts. Long-term capital gains tax rates are usually lower than ordinary income tax rates.

6. **Rebalance Regularly**: Periodically assess your allocations and adjust as needed based on your goals and market changes.

7. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: It can be beneficial to speak with a financial professional who understands your complete financial picture.

This strategy balances growth potential, tax efficiency, and access to funds.
