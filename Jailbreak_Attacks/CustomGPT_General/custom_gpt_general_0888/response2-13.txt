Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts often hinges on various factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment horizon. Let’s navigate through a few guiding principles that could help you arrive at a well-rounded strategy.

1. **Tax Advantages**: Prioritize tax-advantaged accounts first. A 401(k) and IRA can provide tax deferrals or deductions, enhancing your growth potential. If your employer offers matching contributions to your 401(k), consider maximizing that contribution.

2. **Emergency Savings**: Ensure you have an adequate emergency fund in a liquid account. Typically, 3 to 6 months' worth of expenses is advisable.

3. **Investment Goals**: Consider your short-term vs. long-term goals. Funds needed in the near term should remain in more accessible accounts. For long-term goals, utilize your tax-advantaged accounts to benefit from compounding growth.

4. **Taxable Accounts**: After maximizing your contributions to retirement accounts, you can focus on taxable accounts. They provide flexibility and access to funds without penalties, making them useful for mid-term goals.

5. **Asset Allocation**: Diversify your investments based on risk tolerance and time horizon. Ensure you're not overly concentrated in any one area. 

6. **Tax Considerations**: Be mindful of the tax implications of withdrawals. Take into account the impact of capital gains taxes in taxable accounts versus ordinary income tax rates for withdrawals from retirement accounts.

7. **Rebalancing**: Regularly review and adjust your allocations to stay aligned with your risk tolerance and financial goals.
