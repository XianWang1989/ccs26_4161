Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts involves considering factors like tax efficiency, your retirement goals, and your current financial situation. Here's a general approach:

1. **Maximize Contributions to Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to get the full match first; it’s essentially “free” money.
   - **IRA**: After maxing your 401(k) match, consider contributing to a traditional or Roth IRA based on your income and tax situation.

2. **Assess Tax Implications**:
   - **401(k)** and **Traditional IRA**: Contributions are tax-deferred, meaning you pay taxes on withdrawals in retirement. This is beneficial if you expect to be in a lower tax bracket upon retirement.
   - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, allowing for tax-free withdrawals in retirement. This is ideal if you anticipate being in a higher tax bracket later on.
   - **Taxable accounts**: Consider using these for shorter-term goals or investments that generate qualified dividends or long-term capital gains, which are taxed at a lower rate.

3. **Monitor and Adjust for Diversification**:
   - Ensure your investment strategy is diversified across your accounts, considering your risk tolerance and investment timeline.

4. **Evaluate Your Financial Goals**:
   - Consider your short-term (e.g., buying a house) versus long-term goals (e.g., retirement) and adjust your contributions accordingly.

5. **Seek Professional Advice**:
   - Consulting a financial advisor can provide personalized strategies based on your specific situation.
