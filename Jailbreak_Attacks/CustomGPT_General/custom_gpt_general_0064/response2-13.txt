The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some general guidelines:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a match on your 401(k), contribute enough to get the full match, as it's essentially free money.

2. **Consider Tax Advantages**: 
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are pre-tax, reducing your taxable income for the year. This is beneficial if you expect to be in a lower tax bracket in retirement.
   - **IRA (Traditional)**: Similar benefits as a 401(k) for pre-tax contributions, but there are income limits for tax-deductible contributions.
   - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, but withdrawals are tax-free in retirement. This is advantageous if you anticipate being in a higher tax bracket later.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: These provide flexibility and can be beneficial for short-term goals or when you expect to need funds before retirement.

3. **Asset Allocation**: Different accounts can hold different types of investments based on their tax implications. For instance, consider holding bonds in a tax-advantaged account (like an IRA) because they generate interest income that's typically taxed at a higher rate.

4. **Emergency Fund**: Ensure you have liquid savings outside of retirement accounts to cover any unexpected expenses.

5. **Diversification**: Spread your investments across various asset classes to manage risk effectively, regardless of the account type.

6. **Review and Adjust**: Periodically reassess your allocations based on changes in income, goals, and market conditions.

Consulting a financial advisor can help tailor a strategy specific to your situation.
