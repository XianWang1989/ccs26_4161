When considering how to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, it's essential to focus on your financial goals, investment timeline, and tax implications. Here are some general strategies:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a matching contribution to your 401(k), contribute at least enough to get the full match. This is essentially free money.

2. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: Consider maxing out your contributions to your 401(k) up to the IRS limit if not already fully funded. This reduces your taxable income and grows tax-deferred.
   - **IRA**: After maxing your 401(k) (or up to the limit you can comfortably manage), consider contributing to a Traditional IRA or Roth IRA, depending on your eligibility and future tax expectations.

3. **Consider Taxable Accounts**: Once you've maxed out contributions to your tax-advantaged accounts, you can begin investing in taxable accounts. This offers more flexibility with withdrawals but may have capital gains tax implications.

4. **Diversity Among Assets**: Ensure that your investments across these accounts are diversified and fit your risk tolerance. You might choose different asset allocations depending on the tax implications of each account.

5. **Review Regularly**: Financial markets change, and so do your needs. Regularly reviewing your strategy will help you stay on track with your financial goals.

6. **Consult a Professional**: Consider consulting with a financial advisor to tailor a strategy specific to your circumstances.

Ultimately, the right approach will vary based on your specific financial situation, age, risk tolerance, and retirement goals.
