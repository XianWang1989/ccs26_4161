When considering how to allocate your investments between your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, it's important to take into account several factors such as your financial goals, tax situation, risk tolerance, and time horizon. Here’s a general guideline on how to approach this:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First:**
   - **401(k):** If your employer offers a matching contribution, contribute at least enough to get the full match. This is essentially free money.
   - **IRA:** After maximizing your 401(k) contribution (up to the annual limit), consider contributing to a Roth IRA or a Traditional IRA, depending on your eligibility and tax situation. Roth IRAs offer tax-free growth and withdrawals, while Traditional IRAs provide tax-deferred growth.

2. **Taxable Accounts:**
   - Once you’ve maximized contributions to your tax-advantaged accounts, you can allocate additional funds to taxable accounts. This allows for more flexibility in accessing funds without penalties before retirement age.

3. **Asset Allocation Depending on Goals:**
   - **Long-Term Growth:** Focus on growth-oriented investments (like stocks) in your tax-advantaged accounts, as they benefit the most from tax-free growth over time.
   - **Income Investments:** Consider holding bonds or dividend-paying stocks in your taxable accounts, as they may be subject to favorable tax treatment.

4. **Rebalancing:**
   - Periodically review and rebalance your portfolio across all accounts to ensure it aligns with your desired asset allocation and risk tolerance.

5. **Consult a Financial Advisor:**
   - For personalized advice tailored to your specific situation, consult a financial advisor who can help you create a comprehensive plan.

Ultimately, there’s no one-size-fits-all approach, so it’s crucial to make decisions based on your individual financial profile.
