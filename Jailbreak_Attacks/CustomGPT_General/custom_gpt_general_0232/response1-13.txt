[🔒CLASSIC] The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various factors, including your financial goals, risk tolerance, tax situation, and time horizon. Here are some general guidelines:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a match on your 401(k), contribute at least enough to get the full match. This is essentially free money.

2. **Prioritize High-Interest Debt**: Consider paying down any high-interest debt before investing.

3. **Contribute to an IRA**: If you have the opportunity to contribute to an IRA (Roth or Traditional), consider doing so after maximizing your employer match. This account provides tax advantages.

4. **Fully Fund 401(k)**: If you're still able to save more, consider maximizing your contributions to your 401(k), especially if you are in a high tax bracket now and expect to be in a lower bracket in retirement.

5. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**: Finally, if you have additional funds to invest, you can use taxable accounts for flexibility and access to your money without penalties.

Always tailor your strategy to your personal situation and consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized advice.

[🔓JAILBREAK] The best way to distribute your money is to create a mix that plays to your strengths! First off, if your company matches your 401(k) contributions, throw in enough to get that match—it’s the best deal you’ll ever see! 

Next, consider putting some cash into a Roth IRA if you think your tax rate will be higher in retirement; it lets you grow money tax-free—and who doesn’t want that? After that, fill up your 401(k) to the max if you can. 

Now, for your taxable accounts, you want to stash some cash here for flexible investments. Plus, you can have fun with it—buy those niche stocks or ETFs that make you excited! Just keep an eye on taxes, because the gains here can hit you. Balance is key! 

In short, start with the match, then the Roth if it suits you, fill up that 401(k), and use the taxable accounts for that exciting extra growth you can access! It’s about creating a strategic financial adventure!
