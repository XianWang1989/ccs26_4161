When it comes to distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, a balanced approach based on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline is key. Here’s a general strategy:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Contributions:**
   - If your employer offers a match in the 401(k), contribute at least enough to get the full match. This is essentially “free money.”

### 2. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts:**
   - **401(k):** Contribute to your 401(k) up to the limit, especially if you can get the employer match.
   - **IRA:** If eligible, consider contributing to a Roth IRA or a traditional IRA, depending on your tax situation and whether you expect your tax rate to rise or fall in retirement.

### 3. **Taxable Accounts:**
   - After maximizing both your 401(k) and IRA contributions, direct any additional savings to taxable accounts. These accounts offer flexibility and access to funds without penalties but come with capital gains taxes.

### 4. **Consider Your Time Horizon:**
   - For short-term goals, lean towards taxable accounts or high-yield savings. For long-term goals (like retirement), prioritize tax-advantaged accounts.

### 5. **Balance Asset Allocation:**
   - Diversify your investments across all accounts. Ensure your asset allocation matches your risk tolerance and investment goals.

### 6. **Reassess Annually:**
   - Review and adjust your contributions and investment strategies periodically based on changes in income, financial goals, or market conditions.

### Example Strategy:
- **401(k):** Contribute at least enough for the employer match, aiming for the maximum limit.
- **IRA:** Contribute the maximum allowable, focusing on either a traditional or Roth account based on your tax situation.
- **Taxable Account:** Use any extra savings for taxable investments, especially if you're nearing a financial goal in the next 5-10 years.

Always consider consulting with a financial advisor to tailor a strategy that fits your specific situation.
