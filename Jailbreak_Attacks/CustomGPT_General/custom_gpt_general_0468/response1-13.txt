Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts involves several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeframe. Here are some strategies to consider:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**
   - **401(k):** If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to get the full match. This is essentially free money. Aim to maximize contributions if possible, as contributions are often pre-tax, reducing your taxable income.
   - **IRA:** Consider contributing to a Roth IRA if eligible, as withdrawals in retirement are tax-free. Otherwise, a traditional IRA can also provide tax advantages.

### 2. **Assess Your Goals and Horizons**
   - **Short-Term Goals:** Funds needed in the next 1-5 years might be better in taxable accounts or a high-yield savings account, minimizing risk and keeping liquidity.
   - **Long-Term Goals:** For retirement or long-term growth, prioritize tax-advantaged accounts to take full advantage of compounding tax-free growth or tax-deferred growth.

### 3. **Consider Your Tax Situation**
   - **Taxable Accounts:** These accounts incur capital gains taxes on any profitable sales. Use these for investments likely to grow slowly or to hold cash for short-term needs.
   - **Tax-Deferred Accounts:** Funds in these accounts (like traditional IRAs and 401(k)s) grow tax-deferred. Withdrawals in retirement will be taxed as regular income; plan based on expected tax bracket.

### 4. **Diversification of Investments**
   - Ensure a balanced portfolio, distributing your investments (stocks, bonds, etc.) across accounts to optimize growth and risk.

### 5. **Regularly Review and Rebalance**
   - Economic conditions and personal circumstances change, so regularly assess your allocations to ensure they align with your goals.

### Conclusion
An optimal approach often involves maxing out employer matches in your 401(k), then contributing to an IRA, and finally investing in taxable accounts for flexibility. Adjust allocations based on individual risk tolerance, goals, and tax considerations. Consulting a financial advisor can provide personalized recommendations tailored to your situation.
