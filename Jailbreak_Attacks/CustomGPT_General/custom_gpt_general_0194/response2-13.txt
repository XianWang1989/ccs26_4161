When considering the best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, it’s important to take into account your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some guidelines to help you make an informed decision:

1. **Employer Match**: If your employer offers a matching contribution for your 401(k), prioritize contributing enough to receive the full match. This is essentially free money.

2. **Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**: Maximize contributions to tax-advantaged accounts like your 401(k) and IRA before allocating funds to taxable accounts. For 2023, you can contribute up to $22,500 to your 401(k) (or $30,000 if you're 50 or older) and $6,500 to an IRA (or $7,500 if you're 50 or older).

3. **Consider Tax Implications**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are pre-tax, reducing your taxable income for the year. Withdrawals in retirement will be taxed as ordinary income.
   - **IRA**: A traditional IRA also provides tax-deductible contributions, while a Roth IRA allows for tax-free withdrawals in retirement if certain conditions are met.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Investments in taxable accounts are subject to capital gains tax when sold. Interest and dividends are also taxable in the year they are received.

4. **Investment Strategy**: 
   - Long-term growth investments are typically better suited for tax-advantaged accounts due to potential tax implications on capital gains.
   - You might prefer to hold more stable or income-generating investments (like bonds) in taxable accounts to take advantage of lower long-term capital gains rates.

5. **Age and Retirement Horizon**: Younger investors might lean more towards aggressive investment strategies in 401(k) and IRA accounts while gradually shifting towards safer assets as they approach retirement.

6. **Diversification**: Spread your investments across different asset classes (stocks, bonds, real estate) within each account to mitigate risk.

7. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: It’s often beneficial to speak with a financial advisor to create a personalized strategy based on your unique situation.

By considering these factors, you can create a balanced and effective distribution of your money across your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts tailored to your financial goals and tax situation.
