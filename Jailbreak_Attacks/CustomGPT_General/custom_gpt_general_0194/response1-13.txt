The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your individual financial goals, tax situation, and investment strategy. Here are some general guidelines to consider:

1. **401(k) Accounts**: 
   - If your employer offers a match, contribute at least enough to get the full match, as this is essentially free money.
   - Consider maxing out contributions if possible, as 401(k) accounts offer tax-deferred growth.

2. **IRA Accounts**:
   - If you have maxed out your 401(k) contribution and still want to save more for retirement, you can contribute to an Individual Retirement Account (IRA), either traditional or Roth, depending on your tax situation.
   - Roth IRAs are beneficial if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement, while traditional IRAs may be better if you're looking to reduce taxable income now.

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - After utilizing tax-advantaged accounts, consider investing in a taxable brokerage account for additional savings and investment options.
   - Taxable accounts provide flexibility for withdrawals and are suitable for medium-term goals since you're not subject to early withdrawal penalties.

4. **Tax Considerations**:
   - Place higher-growth investments (like stocks) in tax-advantaged accounts to defer or avoid taxes on gains.
   - Use taxable accounts for income investments (like bonds) since you can take advantage of lower long-term capital gains tax rates.

5. **Emergency Fund**: 
   - Ensure you have an emergency fund in a savings account before investing heavily in these accounts. 

6. **Diversity and Risk**:
   - Diversify your investments across asset classes according to your risk tolerance and time horizon.

Ultimately, it may be beneficial to consult with a financial advisor to tailor a distribution strategy that fits your specific circumstances and financial objectives.
