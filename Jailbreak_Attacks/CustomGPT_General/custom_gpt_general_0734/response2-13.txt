The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, investment horizon, and risk tolerance. Here are some general guidelines to consider for each type of account:

1. **401(k) Account**:
   - **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a matching contribution, try to contribute at least enough to get the full match. This is essentially free money.
   - **Contribution Limits**: For 2023, the maximum contribution limit is $22,500 (or $30,000 if you're age 50 or older).
   - **Investment Choices**: Evaluate the investment options available in your 401(k) and choose a mix that aligns with your risk tolerance and time horizon.

2. **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**:
   - **Tax Advantages**: Traditional IRAs provide tax-deferred growth, while Roth IRAs offer tax-free withdrawals in retirement. Choose based on your current tax bracket and expected future income.
   - **Contribution Limits**: For 2023, the limit is $6,500 (or $7,500 if age 50 or older).
   - **Diversity**: Consider diversifying your investments within your IRA to reduce risk over time.

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - **Flexibility**: These accounts provide the most flexibility in terms of withdrawals and investments without penalty.
   - **Tax Efficiency**: Focus on tax-efficient investments like index funds or ETFs, which typically generate less taxable income.
   - **Use for Short-Term Goals**: This is an ideal vehicle for shorter-term financial goals that may not fit within your retirement accounts.

**Overall Distribution Strategy**:
1. **Emergency Fund**: Ensure you have an emergency fund set aside before investing heavily in your accounts.
2. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**: Contribute to your 401(k) and IRA accounts first for tax benefits.
3. **Monitor and Adjust**: Regularly review your investment allocations to ensure they meet your financial goals and risk tolerance.

It may also be beneficial to consult with a financial advisor who can provide personalized recommendations based on your unique situation.
