Distributing your money wisely among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here’s a strategic approach:

1. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**: 
   - **401(k)**: If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to capture the full match. This is essentially free money and a guaranteed return on investment.
   - **IRA**: If you can afford to save more after maxing out the employer match, consider contributing to a Traditional or Roth IRA. The choice between these should depend on your current tax rate versus what you anticipate in retirement (Traditional IRA gives you a tax break now; Roth IRA offers tax-free growth).

2. **Assess Your Contribution Limits**:
   - In 2023, the contribution limit for a 401(k) is $22,500 ($30,000 if you're over 50).
   - For IRAs, the limit is $6,500 ($7,500 if you're over 50). Ensure you’re not exceeding these limits across accounts.

3. **Strategic Allocation**:
   - **Aggressive Growth in Tax-Advantaged Accounts**: Since you won’t be taxed on gains in the IRA and will defer taxes in the 401(k), consider putting higher-risk, high-growth investments (like stocks) in these accounts.
   - **More Conservative Investments in Taxable Accounts**: Since you’ll pay taxes on dividends and capital gains in taxable accounts, consider holding low-turnover investments or bonds here to minimize your tax burden.

4. **Diversification**:
   - Diversify your investments within each account type, balancing stock, bond, and other asset classes as per your risk tolerance and investment strategy.

5. **Review Regularly**: 
   - Monitor the performance and asset allocation regularly and rebalance as necessary to maintain your desired investment strategy.

6. **Consult with a Financial Advisor**: If you're unsure, consider consulting with a financial planner who can provide personalized advice based on your complete financial picture.
