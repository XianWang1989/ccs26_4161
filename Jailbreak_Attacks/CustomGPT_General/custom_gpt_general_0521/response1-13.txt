Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here are some general guidelines to consider:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First:**
   - **401(k):** If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to get the full match—it’s essentially free money. Aim to maximize contributions within IRS limits.
   - **IRA:** Consider contributing to a Traditional IRA for tax-deferred growth or a Roth IRA for tax-free withdrawals in retirement. Roth IRAs are particularly beneficial if you expect your tax rate to be higher in retirement.

### 2. **Tax Considerations:**
   - Place investments that generate high dividends or interest in tax-advantaged accounts (like bonds or REITs) to avoid high tax rates on these assets.
   - Keep growth-oriented investments (like stocks) in taxable accounts, as long-term capital gains are taxed at a lower rate.

### 3. **Risk Tolerance and Time Horizon:**
   - If you have a long time horizon, prioritize higher-risk investments in retirement accounts for potential growth. Allocate more conservative investments in taxable accounts for shorter-term needs.

### 4. **Liquidity Needs:**
   - Maintain some cash or liquid assets in taxable accounts for emergencies or short-term expenses, as penalties may apply to early withdrawals from retirement accounts.

### 5. **Diversification:**
   - Ensure a balanced asset allocation across all accounts to manage risk effectively.

### Recommended Strategy:
1. Contribute to your 401(k) to at least the employer match.
2. Max out IRA contributions.
3. Invest additional savings in a taxable account, focusing on tax-efficient investments.

Consulting with a financial advisor can also tailor this strategy to your specific situation.
