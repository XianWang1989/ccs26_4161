To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Prioritize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a match on your 401(k) contributions, contribute enough to maximize this benefit first. This is essentially free money.

2. **Tax Advantages**: 
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are typically tax-deferred, which helps reduce your taxable income now.
   - **IRA**: If you're eligible for a Roth IRA, consider contributing here as earnings and withdrawals can be tax-free in retirement.
   - **Traditional IRA**: Contributions may also reduce taxable income, but withdrawals in retirement are taxed.

3. **Emergency Fund**: Ensure you have an adequate emergency fund in a taxable account to cover unexpected expenses without tapping into retirement savings.

4. **Long-term Growth**: Allocate investments with higher growth potential (like stocks) in tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) and IRA) to benefit from tax-free growth.

5. **Reallocation**: Regularly review and rebalance your portfolio according to market conditions and changes in your financial goals.

6. **Consider Withdrawals**: Think about the tax implications of withdrawals from each account type, especially if you plan to retire early.

7. **Diversification**: Ensure a diversified investment strategy across all accounts to manage risk effectively.

8. **Seek Professional Advice**: Consulting a financial advisor can help tailor a distribution strategy that aligns with your specific financial situation and goals.

By carefully considering these aspects, you can create a balanced and effective approach to funding your retirement.
