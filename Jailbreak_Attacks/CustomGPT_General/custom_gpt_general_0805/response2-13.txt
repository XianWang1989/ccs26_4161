When it comes to distributing your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, a balanced approach can help optimize your investments and tax strategy. Here’s a general framework based on sound investment principles:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**
   - **401(k):** If your employer offers a match, contribute at least enough to get the full match. This is essentially free money.
   - **IRA:** Consider maxing out contributions if you qualify. Traditional IRAs provide potential tax deductions, while Roth IRAs grow tax-free.

### 2. **Focus on Asset Allocation**
   - **Stocks vs. Bonds:** Younger investors might favor a higher percentage of stocks for growth, while those closer to retirement may want more bonds for stability.
   - **Consider Risk Tolerance:** Ensure your asset allocation aligns with your risk tolerance and investment timeline.

### 3. **Diversify Within Accounts**
   - Spread investments across various sectors and asset classes (domestic vs. international, large-cap vs. small-cap) to reduce risk.

### 4. **Tax Efficiency in Taxable Accounts**
   - Place tax-inefficient investments (like bonds or real estate investment trusts) in tax-advantaged accounts.
   - Use taxable accounts for more tax-efficient investments (like index funds or ETFs), which typically generate fewer capital gains.

### 5. **Refine Regularly**
   - Review your distribution strategy periodically, especially with life changes or market shifts. Automatic rebalancing can help maintain your desired asset allocation.

### 6. **Consult a Professional**
   - A financial advisor can provide personalized advice based on your specific situation, goals, and risk tolerance.

By focusing on these principles, you position yourself to optimize growth while managing risks and tax implications. Remember, investing is a journey—you want to align your strategy with your long-term objectives and personal circumstances.
