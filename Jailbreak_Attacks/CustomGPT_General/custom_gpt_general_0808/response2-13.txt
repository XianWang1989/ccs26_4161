When distributing your money between a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider these factors:

1. **Tax Advantages**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are often tax-deferred; ideal for employer matches.
   - **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**: Traditional IRAs also provide tax-deferred growth, while Roth IRAs allow for tax-free withdrawals in retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Suitable for investments that may generate taxable income or gains, but they lack tax advantages.

2. **Contribution Limits**: Be aware of the annual contribution limits for each account type to maximize tax benefits.

3. **Investment Goals**:
   - **Long-term growth**: Prioritize tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) and IRA).
   - **Flexibility**: Use taxable accounts for short-term goals, as they allow easier access to funds without penalties.

4. **Withdrawal Strategies**: Plan for tax implications on withdrawals, especially in retirement. Prioritize withdrawing from tax-advantaged accounts to minimize tax liability.

5. **Risk Tolerance**: Adjust your asset allocation based on risk tolerance; typically, riskier investments are better suited to tax-advantaged accounts.

6. **Diversification**: Maintain a diversified portfolio across all accounts to balance risk and return.

**General Strategy**:
- Contribute to a 401(k) up to the employer match.
- Max out IRA contributions.
- Use extra funds for taxable accounts as needed.

Always consider consulting a financial advisor to tailor a strategy to your specific situation.
