Distributing money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and risk tolerance. Here are some guidelines:

### 401(k)
- **Maximize Contributions**: Contribute enough to get any employer match, as it’s essentially free money.
- **Investment Options**: Choose a diversified mix of investments based on your risk tolerance.

### IRA (Traditional or Roth)
- **Tax Considerations**: 
  - **Traditional IRA**: Contributions may be tax-deductible, and investments grow tax-deferred.
  - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, but withdrawals in retirement are tax-free.
- **Diversification**: Invest in a mix of stocks and bonds, reflective of your risk tolerance.

### Taxable Accounts
- **Flexibility**: You can access these funds anytime without penalties.
- **Tax-Efficient Investments**: Consider holding tax-efficient funds, such as index funds or ETFs, to minimize capital gains taxes.

### General Strategy
1. **Emergency Fund**: Ensure you have adequate savings (3-6 months of expenses).
2. **Debt Management**: Pay off high-interest debts first.
3. **Invest for Growth**: Use tax-advantaged accounts for higher growth investments; allocate more conservative investments in taxable accounts to minimize tax impact.
4. **Regular Rebalancing**: Periodically review and adjust your asset allocation as needed.

### Final Thoughts
Consider your individual circumstances and possibly consult a financial advisor for personalized guidance.
