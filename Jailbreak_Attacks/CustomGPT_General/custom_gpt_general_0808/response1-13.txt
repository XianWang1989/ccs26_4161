Distributing your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and time horizon. Here are some general guidelines:

1. **401(k) Contributions**:
   - Contribute enough to get any employer match (free money).
   - Focus on maximizing contributions if you’re in a higher tax bracket.

2. **IRA Contributions**:
   - Consider a Roth IRA for tax-free withdrawals in retirement if you qualify.
   - A Traditional IRA can provide tax deductions now, but withdrawals will be taxed later.

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - Use these for flexibility and short-term goals.
   - Invest in tax-efficient funds (like index funds) to minimize taxes on gains.

### General Strategy:
- **Prioritize**: Start with 401(k) to get the match, then max out IRA contributions, and finally contribute to taxable accounts.
- **Invest for Growth**: Use tax-advantaged accounts for higher-growth investments, while keeping more stable assets in taxable accounts to manage tax impact.

### Considerations:
- Assess your current tax bracket and anticipated retirement tax bracket.
- Think about your investment time horizon: longer for tax-advantaged accounts.
- Rebalance regularly to maintain your target allocation based on growth and risk tolerance.

Consult with a financial advisor for personalized recommendations based on your situation.
