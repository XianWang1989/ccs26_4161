Distributing your money across a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and time horizon. Here’s a general approach:

1. **Maximize Contributions**:
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money.
   - **IRA**: If eligible, consider maxing out a Roth or Traditional IRA for additional tax benefits.

2. **Tax Efficiency**:
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for higher-risk investments that generate capital gains, as long-term gains are taxed at a lower rate.
   - **IRA/401(k)**: Place income-generating assets (like bonds), as returns are taxed at your ordinary income rate.

3. **Time Horizon**:
   - Longer-term goals can go into more aggressive investments (stocks) in tax-advantaged accounts.
   - Short-term needs should be kept in safer, liquid investments in a taxable account.

4. **Diversification**: Ensure a diversified asset allocation within each account based on your risk tolerance.

5. **Rebalance Regularly**: Adjust your allocations to maintain your desired risk level over time.

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: Personalized strategies can help align your distribution with your specific financial situation.

Adjust this strategy based on your unique circumstances and consult a professional for tailored advice.
