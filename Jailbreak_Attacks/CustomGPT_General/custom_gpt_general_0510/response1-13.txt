The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here are some general guidelines to consider:

### 1. **Understand Account Types:**
   - **401(k):** Often has employer matching contributions and tax-deferred growth. Good for higher contribution limits.
   - **IRA (Traditional or Roth):** IRAs have tax advantages. Roth IRAs allow for tax-free withdrawals in retirement, while Traditional IRAs provide tax deductions.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Flexible for withdrawals but subject to capital gains taxes. Useful for short-term goals or if you exceed contribution limits on tax-advantaged accounts.

### 2. **Maximize Employer Match:**
   - Always contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match, as this is essentially free money.

### 3. **Prioritize Tax Advantages:**
   - If you have limited funds, prioritize contributions to tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) and IRAs) before taxable accounts.

### 4. **Account Drawdown Strategy:**
   - Plan how you'll withdraw funds in retirement. Generally, for lower tax burdens:
     - Withdraw from taxable accounts first.
     - Follow with tax-deferred accounts (401(k) and Traditional IRA).
     - Lastly, leverage Roth accounts for tax-free withdrawals.

### 5. **Consider Your Tax Situation:**
   - If you're in a high tax bracket now, max out tax-deferred accounts. If you expect to be in a lower bracket in retirement, Roth accounts may be more beneficial.

### 6. **Diversify Investments:**
   - Across all accounts, ensure your investment strategy is diversified based on your risk tolerance and time horizon.

### 7. **Regular Rebalancing:** 
   - Periodically review and adjust your allocations based on market performance and changes in your financial goals.

### Example Distribution:
- **401(k):** 20% of your total allocation for annual contributions (maximize match).
- **IRA:** 15% if you're eligible; consider Roth for tax-free growth.
- **Taxable:** Allocate remaining funds based on liquidity needs and investment strategy.

### Final Thoughts:
Consult a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your specific situation, especially regarding tax implications and investment choices.
