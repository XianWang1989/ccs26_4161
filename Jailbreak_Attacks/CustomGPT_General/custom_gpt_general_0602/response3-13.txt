Sure! Here’s a breakdown of how to effectively distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts:

### 1. **401(k) Contributions**
   - **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to get any available employer match first. This is essentially free money.
   - **Contribution Limits**: In 2023, the limit is $22,500 (or $30,000 if over 50).

### 2. **IRA Contributions**
   - **Roth vs. Traditional**: Choose based on your current tax situation. Roth IRAs offer tax-free growth and withdrawals, while Traditional IRAs provide tax-deferred growth.
   - **Contribution Limits**: For 2023, the limit is $6,500 (or $7,500 if over 50).

### 3. **Taxable Accounts**
   - **Invest for Flexibility**: Use taxable accounts for investments that you might need to access before retirement age.
   - **Tax Efficiency**: Focus on tax-efficient investments like index funds or ETFs to minimize taxable events.

### **General Strategy**
1. **Prioritize Retirement Accounts**: Fund your 401(k) to the match, then max out your IRA.
2. **Evaluate Tax Implications**: Consider where the investments will be taxed most efficiently.
3. **Diversify Investments**: Allocate assets appropriately across accounts based on risk tolerance and time horizon.

### **Example Allocation**
- **Year 1**: 
  - 401(k): 15% of income (enough to get match)
  - IRA: 10% of income
  - Taxable: Remaining for liquidity.

- **Years Following**: Adjust based on income changes and retirement goals.

### **Conclusion**
Regularly review your allocation and adjust based on market changes and your personal financial situation. Consulting with a financial advisor can also help tailor your strategy.
