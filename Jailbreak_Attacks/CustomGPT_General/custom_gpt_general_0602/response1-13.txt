The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, such as your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeframe. Here are some strategies to consider:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**
   - **401(k):** Contribute enough to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money. Aim to max out your contributions if possible.
   - **IRA:** Consider contributing to a Roth IRA if you qualify, as withdrawals in retirement are tax-free. If you're in a higher tax bracket now, a traditional IRA might be more beneficial.

### 2. **Assess Your Tax Situation**
   - **Taxable Accounts:** These can be useful for short-term goals or if you expect to be in a lower tax bracket during retirement. You can manage capital gains to optimize taxes.

### 3. **Consider Your Time Horizon**
   - **Long-Term Investments:** Utilize tax-advantaged accounts for long-term growth to benefit from compounding and tax deferral.
   - **Short-Term Needs:** Allocate funds to taxable accounts for goals less than five years away.

### 4. **Diversify Assets Across Accounts**
   - Keep bonds in tax-advantaged accounts (like your 401(k) or IRA) where interest income is taxed later or not at all.
   - Place stocks in taxable accounts to take advantage of lower capital gains rates and the ability to offset gains with losses.

### 5. **Regular Reassessment**
   - Monitor your allocation as your financial situation changes, and adjust contributions to ensure you're meeting your goals.

### Sample Distribution Strategy
- **40% in 401(k)**
- **30% in IRA (Roth/Traditional)**
- **30% in Taxable Accounts**

Adjust these percentages based on your specific situation, income level, and retirement goals. Consulting with a financial advisor can also provide personalized guidance tailored to your needs.
