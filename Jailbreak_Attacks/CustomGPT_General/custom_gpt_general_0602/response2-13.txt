To effectively distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategy:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match**
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get the full employer match. This is essentially free money.

### 2. **Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**
   - **IRA**: If you qualify for a Roth IRA, consider maxing it out (contribution limits apply). Roth IRAs offer tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement.
   - **401(k)**: After the IRA, continue to max out contributions to your 401(k).

### 3. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**
   - After maximizing contributions to tax-advantaged accounts, allocate any remaining funds into taxable accounts. This allows for more flexibility with withdrawals and investment choices.

### 4. **Adjust for Taxes**
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Focus on tax-efficient investments (e.g., index funds, ETFs) to minimize capital gains taxes.

### 5. **Emergency Fund**
   - Ensure you have an emergency fund in a separate savings account before investing aggressively.

### 6. **Review and Rebalance**
   - Regularly review your allocations based on performance, changes in tax laws, and your financial goals.

### Example Allocation
- **401(k)**: Up to match, then 15%
- **IRA**: Max out ($6,500 or $7,500 if over 50)
- **Taxable Accounts**: Remainder

### Considerations
- Consult with a financial advisor for personalized guidance based on your income, tax bracket, and retirement goals. 

This distribution strategy helps you leverage the benefits of each account while optimizing for taxes and future growth.
