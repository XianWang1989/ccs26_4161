Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment horizon. Here’s a general strategy to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match (401(k))**: If your employer offers a match, prioritize contributing enough to get the full match before allocating to other accounts.

2. **Tax-Advantaged Accounts (401(k) and IRA)**: 
   - **401(k)**: Consider maxing this out if the employer match is significant. This account reduces your taxable income in the year you contribute.
   - **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**: If you qualify for a Roth IRA, it can provide tax-free withdrawals in retirement. If you’re looking for immediate tax benefits, a Traditional IRA may be better.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: 
   - After maximizing contributions to retirement accounts, allocate funds to taxable accounts. These offer more flexibility in terms of withdrawals and investments. Consider using these for investments that produce qualified dividends or long-term capital gains, which are taxed at a lower rate.

4. **Rebalancing**: Regularly review and adjust your allocation based on performance, changes in your goals, and market conditions.

5. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: A professional can provide personalized advice based on your unique situation.

Balancing these factors will help you create a strategy that aligns with your financial goals while optimizing tax benefits.
