Distributing your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts can depend on several factors, including your tax situation, investment goals, and retirement timeline. Here are some general guidelines:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match. This is essentially free money.
   - **IRA**: If you have additional funds, consider contributing to an IRA (traditional or Roth, depending on your eligibility and tax situation).

### 2. **Consider Tax Implications**
   - **Taxable Accounts**: These should typically be funded last for long-term investments that generate capital gains, which can be taxed favorably compared to ordinary income.

### 3. **Balance Your Investments**
   - Use tax-advantaged accounts for investments that generate ordinary income (like bonds) to defer taxes.
   - Place investments with higher growth potential in taxable accounts if you're in a long-term holding pattern.

### 4. **Diversification**
   - Ensure your overall portfolio is diversified across all accounts to manage risk.

### 5. **Review Regularly**
   - Regularly assess your allocations based on performance, tax changes, and changes in your financial situation.

### Conclusion
A balanced approach often prioritizing contributions to tax-advantaged accounts like a 401(k) or IRA, followed by taxable accounts, while taking into account your investment time horizon and tax strategy, can help maximize your wealth over time. Consulting a financial advisor for personalized advice would also be beneficial.
