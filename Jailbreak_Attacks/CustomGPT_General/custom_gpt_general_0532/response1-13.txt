To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**:
   - **401(k)**: If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to get the full match first. This is essentially free money.
   - **IRA**: After your 401(k), consider funding a Traditional or Roth IRA (depending on your eligibility and tax situation). The IRA offers tax advantages that can enhance your savings.

2. **Prioritize Contributions**:
   - If you can afford it, aim to maximize your contributions to both accounts annually. For 2023, the limit is $22,500 for a 401(k) (or $30,000 if you're over 50) and $6,500 for an IRA (or $7,500 if you're over 50).

3. **Consider Tax Implications**:
   - **Taxable Accounts**: These are useful for flexibility but subject to capital gains taxes. Use these accounts for money you might need in the short term or for investing in tax-efficient funds.
   - Be strategic with investment types in these accounts. Favor investments that generate minimal taxable income (like index funds or tax-managed funds).

4. **Rebalance Regularly**:
   - Regularly review and adjust your asset allocation across accounts to maintain your intended risk profile and investment goals.

5. **Withdrawal Strategy**:
   - As you approach retirement, plan your withdrawal strategy carefully to minimize taxes. Generally, it's preferable to withdraw from taxable accounts first, followed by tax-deferred (401(k), Traditional IRA), and tax-free (Roth IRA) accounts last.

6. **Long-Term Perspective**:
   - Keep a long-term outlook. Focus on the growth potential of investments across all types of accounts rather than short-term fluctuations.

By assessing your financial goals, risk tolerance, and tax situation, you can create a balanced approach tailored to your specific needs.
