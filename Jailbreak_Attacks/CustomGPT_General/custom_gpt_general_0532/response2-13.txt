Distributing your money between a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts can depend on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some general strategies to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Contributions**: Start with your 401(k). If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to maximize that match. This is essentially free money.

2. **Contribute to an IRA**: After maximizing your 401(k) match, consider contributing to a Roth IRA or Traditional IRA. The choice here depends on whether you want tax-free withdrawals in retirement (Roth) or tax-deductible contributions now (Traditional).

3. **Consider Tax Implications**:
   - **Tax-Deferred Accounts**: 401(k) and Traditional IRA contributions lower your taxable income for the year, which can be beneficial if you're in a higher tax bracket.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Any investments here might generate taxable income. You can take advantage of long-term capital gains rates if you hold investments for over a year.

4. **Prioritize High-Interest Debt**: If you have any high-interest debt, focus on paying that down before investing heavily in these accounts.

5. **Diversify Your Investments**: Ensure that your investment choices within these accounts are diversified. Different accounts can serve different purposes depending on your withdrawal strategy in retirement.

6. **Plan for Withdrawals**: Think about how you’ll withdraw money in retirement. You may want to draw from taxable accounts first to let tax-deferred accounts continue to grow.

7. **Regularly Reassess Your Strategy**: As your financial situation changes, revisit and adjust your distribution strategy.

8. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: For personalized advice based on your situation, consider consulting a financial advisor who can tailor a strategy to your needs.

Balancing your contributions across these accounts can help optimize tax benefits and align with your long-term financial goals.
