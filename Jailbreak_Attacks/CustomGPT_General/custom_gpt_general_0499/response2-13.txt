The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, investment horizon, and risk tolerance. Here's a general strategy:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**: If your employer offers a matching contribution, contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full match. This is essentially free money.

2. **Prioritize Roth IRA (if eligible)**: If you're eligible for a Roth IRA, consider contributing to this account. Withdrawals in retirement are tax-free, which can be advantageous if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket later.

3. **Contribute to Traditional IRA**: If you still have funding capacity after maxing out the 401(k) match and the Roth IRA, consider contributing to a Traditional IRA for potential tax deductions.

4. **Maximize 401(k) Contributions**: After funding the above accounts, contribute more to your 401(k) up to the annual limit, since contributions are made pre-tax and can lower your current taxable income.

5. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**: Use taxable accounts for investments you might need access to before retirement or for short-term goals. These accounts can offer greater liquidity and may have more favorable capital gains tax treatment if you hold investments for more than a year.

6. **Consider Tax Efficiency**: Place tax-efficient investments (like index funds or ETFs) in taxable accounts to minimize tax liabilities, while placing less tax-efficient investments (like bonds) in tax-advantaged accounts.

7. **Rebalance Regularly**: Ensure your portfolio remains aligned with your long-term objectives and risk tolerance by periodically rebalancing your distribution among these accounts.

**Consult a Financial Advisor**: Given individual circumstances vary, it may be beneficial to consult a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your specific situation.
