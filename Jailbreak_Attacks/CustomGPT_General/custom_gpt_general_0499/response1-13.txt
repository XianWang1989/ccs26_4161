Optimal asset allocation among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors including your age, risk tolerance, investment goals, and the tax implications of each account type. Here are some general strategies that you might consider:

1. **Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**: Contribute to your 401(k) and IRA accounts up to the maximum or at least to where you get matching contributions from your employer in your 401(k). These accounts grow tax-deferred (401(k)) or tax-free (Roth IRA), which is beneficial in the long run.

2. **Diversification Within Accounts**: Diversify your investments within these accounts to balance risk. Ensure you have a mixture of stocks, bonds, and other asset classes based on your risk tolerance.

3. **Taxable Accounts for Specific Goals**: Use taxable accounts for short- to medium-term goals that are outside of retirement. This can include money planned for buying a home or funding education, where you may need access to funds before retirement.

4. **Consider Capital Gains**: In taxable accounts, be mindful of capital gains taxes when buying and selling investments. Long-term capital gains are taxed at a lower rate, so consider holding investments for longer periods to minimize tax implications.

5. **Withdrawal Strategy**: As you approach retirement, think about how you’ll withdraw from these accounts. It's often wise to pull from taxable accounts first to let tax-advantaged accounts continue to grow.

6. **Revisit Regularly**: Regularly review and adjust your allocations based on performance, changes in goals, tax laws, and life circumstances.

7. **Consult with a Financial Advisor**: Finally, because everyone's situation is unique, consulting with a financial advisor can help tailor a strategy that best fits your personal goals and circumstances.

This approach not only maximizes your returns but also minimizes your tax liability over time.
