Distributing your money across your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts effectively depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment horizon. Here’s a general guideline to consider:

### 1. **Understand Account Types and Tax Implications**
   - **401(k)**: Pre-tax contributions may reduce your taxable income. Taxes are due when you withdraw funds.
   - **IRA**: Traditional IRAs are similar to 401(k)s, while Roth IRAs allow for tax-free withdrawals if certain conditions are met.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Investments are taxed on capital gains and dividends, making them suitable for shorter-term goals or if you anticipate needing access to funds.

### 2. **Assess Your Goals**
   - **Long-term retirement savings**: Maximize contributions to tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) and IRA).
   - **Short-term goals**: Consider using taxable accounts for funds you may need sooner.

### 3. **Maximize Contributions**
   - Aim to contribute enough to your 401(k) to receive any employer match.
   - After the match, consider funding an IRA, prioritizing a Roth if you qualify for the tax benefits.
   - Use taxable accounts for additional investment once you reach the limits of your tax-advantaged accounts.

### 4. **Diversification and Asset Allocation**
   - Diversify your investments across all accounts, keeping an eye on each account's tax implications.
   - Consider holding tax-efficient investments (like index funds) in taxable accounts to minimize tax burdens.

### 5. **Withdrawal Strategy**
   - Plan for tax implications based on when and how you withdraw funds in retirement.
   - Typically, it's beneficial to withdraw from taxable accounts first, then tax-deferred accounts (401(k) and traditional IRA), and finally tax-free accounts (Roth IRA) last to extend tax advantages.

### Example Distribution Strategy
- **Max out your 401(k)** up to the employer match.
- **Contribute to an IRA**, prioritizing Roth if eligible.
- **Invest additional savings** in a taxable account, focusing on long-term growth investments.

### Regular Review and Rebalance
- Review your strategy annually as your financial situation and tax laws may change, and rebalance as needed based on performance and risk tolerance.

Consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your specific situation.
