Distributing your money across your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, investment horizon, and risk tolerance. Here’s a general strategy:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**
   - **Action**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match, as this is essentially free money.

### 2. **Contribute to an IRA**
   - **Action**: Max out contributions to a traditional or Roth IRA (depending on your eligibility and goals) after your 401(k) match. This can help with tax diversification.

### 3. **Continue with 401(k) Contributions**
   - **Action**: After the IRA, continue contributing to your 401(k) up to the annual limit. This allows for tax-deferred growth.

### 4. **Consider Taxable Accounts Last**
   - **Action**: Use taxable accounts for any additional savings. These can be useful for short-term goals or investments that may have favorable tax treatments (like long-term capital gains).

### 5. **Tax Efficiency**
   - **Action**: Invest tax-efficient funds (like index funds) in taxable accounts and consider holding bonds and less tax-efficient investments in tax-advantaged accounts.

### 6. **Emergency Fund**
   - **Action**: Ensure you have an emergency fund outside these accounts, typically three to six months of expenses.

### 7. **Rebalance Regularly**
   - **Action**: Review your asset allocation and adjust it periodically to stay aligned with your risk tolerance and goals.

### Conclusion
This strategy aims for a balance between tax advantages and investment growth potential. Consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your specific situation.
