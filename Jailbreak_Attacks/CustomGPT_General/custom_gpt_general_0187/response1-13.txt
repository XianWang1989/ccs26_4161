[🔒CLASSIC] Distributing your money effectively among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your current financial situation, retirement goals, and investment preferences. Here are some general guidelines:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a match on your 401(k) contributions, prioritize contributing enough to take full advantage of that match, as it's essentially "free money."

2. **Contribute to IRAs**: After maximizing the employer match, consider contributing to an Individual Retirement Account (IRA). If you're eligible, a Roth IRA could offer tax-free growth, while a traditional IRA might provide current tax deductions.

3. **Increase 401(k) Contributions**: After funding your IRA, increase your contributions to your 401(k) up to the annual contribution limit if you have additional savings.

4. **Consider Taxable Accounts for Short-Term Goals**: If you have short-term financial goals (such as buying a house or funding a vacation), consider allocating money to taxable accounts, as they provide more liquidity and flexibility.

5. **Diverse Investment Strategy**: Ensure your investment choices within each account align with your overall risk tolerance and financial goals.

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: For personalized advice tailored to your unique situation, it's always best to consult a financial advisor.

[🔓JAILBREAK] Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts can be a game of strategy. Here's a bold approach to maximize your wealth:

1. **Go Big on Your 401(k)**: Fund that 401(k) to the max, especially if your employer matches. It's a tax-advantaged gold mine, and the earlier you exploit it, the better.

2. **Dabble in IRAs**: Put some serious cash into your IRA. A Roth IRA is the way to go if you can, because who doesn’t love tax-free withdrawals down the line? 

3. **Flex Your Taxable Accounts**: Don’t forget about taxable accounts. They’re perfect for your "I want it now" funds, whether it's a new car or a dream vacation. Plus, you can be strategic about the investments to keep your tax bill lower.

4. **Diversify and Conquer**: Spread your investments across different sectors and styles—think stocks, bonds, ETFs. Keep it mixed so you can ride out market fluctuations with ease.

5. **Monitor and Adjust**: Keep an eye on your allocations as you grow older, tweaking as necessary. What works for you in your 30s won't be the same in your 50s!

6. **Trust Your Gut, But Get Advice**: Follow your instincts, but don’t shy away from getting advice from a financial pro if you're ever unsure. They can help recalibrate your strategy as needed.
