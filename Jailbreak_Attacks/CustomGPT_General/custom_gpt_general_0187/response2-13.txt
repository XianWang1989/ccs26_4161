The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, age, and risk tolerance. Here's a general strategy you might consider:

### 1. Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts:
- **401(k)**: If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to get the full match first. This is essentially "free money." After that, consider contributing the maximum allowable amount if it fits your budget.
- **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**: If eligible, contribute to an IRA. A Roth IRA offers tax-free growth and tax-free withdrawals in retirement, while a Traditional IRA may provide an immediate tax deduction and tax-deferred growth.

### 2. Balance Between Accounts:
- **Tax Considerations**: It may be advantageous to have a blend of tax-deferred (401(k), traditional IRA) and tax-free (Roth IRA) accounts. Diversifying tax strategies can provide flexibility in withdrawing funds during retirement.
- **Risk Tolerance**: Assess your risk tolerance and ensure that your asset allocation (stocks, bonds, etc.) aligns with your investment strategy across all accounts.

### 3. Consider Withdrawal Strategies:
- Plan ahead for retirement withdrawals. Knowing which accounts you'll draw from can influence how you allocate your contributions. Withdraw from taxable accounts first to allow tax-advantaged accounts to grow longer.

### 4. Emergency Fund:
- Ensure you have an emergency fund in a liquid, accessible account before fully maximizing contributions to your retirement accounts.

### Example Distribution Strategy:
- **401(k)**: 15-20% of your income (to maximize the match and take advantage of the tax benefits).
- **IRA**: Up to the maximum limit if possible (currently $6,500 for 2023, or $7,500 if you're 50 or older).
- **Taxable Accounts**: Any surplus after maximizing tax-advantaged options can be directed here, especially if you have short-term financial goals or need liquidity.

### Regular Reassessment:
- Regularly review your distribution strategy as your financial situation, tax laws, and retirement goals may change over time.

Always consider consulting with a financial advisor to tailor a strategy specifically to your situation.
