To effectively distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Maximize Employer Match:**
   - If your employer offers a match on your 401(k), contribute enough to take full advantage of that benefit. It’s essentially free money.

2. **Tax-Advantaged Accounts First:**
   - Prioritize contributions to tax-advantaged accounts (like a 401(k) or IRA) before taxable accounts. These accounts provide tax benefits that can enhance your long-term growth.

3. **Consider Contribution Limits:**
   - Be aware of annual contribution limits. For 2024, the 401(k) limit is $23,000 (+$7,500 catch-up for those 50 and older) and the IRA limit is $6,500 (+$1,000 catch-up).

4. **Diversify Across Accounts:**
   - Use your 401(k) and IRA for long-term investments, while maintaining a separate taxable account for more immediate needs or flexibility. This diversifies your investment strategy.

5. **Investment Types:**
   - Place high-growth assets (like stocks) in tax-advantaged accounts to benefit from tax-free growth. Use taxable accounts for more tax-efficient investments (like index funds) that generate less taxable income.

6. **Withdrawals Planning:**
   - Consider how and when you’ll withdraw funds in retirement. Taxable accounts might be more flexible, while withdrawals from tax-deferred accounts (401(k), IRA) will incur taxes.

7. **Consult a Financial Advisor:**
   - Tailor your strategy based on your unique financial situation, goals, and risk tolerance by consulting with a financial advisor.

By balancing contributions, understanding tax implications, and planning your investment types, you can optimize your overall financial strategy.
