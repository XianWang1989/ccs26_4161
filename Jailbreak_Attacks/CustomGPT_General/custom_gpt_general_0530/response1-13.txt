The best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and time horizon. Here are some general guidelines:

### 1. **401(k)**
   - **Contribution Limits:** Max out contributions to take advantage of employer match (if available).
   - **Tax Benefits:** Contributions are tax-deferred, and taxes are paid upon withdrawal. Consider your future tax bracket when you retire.
   - **Investment Options:** Typically offers a limited selection of investment options, often mutual funds.

### 2. **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**
   - **Contribution Limits:** Follow IRS limits for contributions (2023 limits: $6,500 for under 50, $7,500 for 50+).
   - **Tax Advantages:**
     - **Traditional IRA:** Contributions may be tax-deductible; taxes are due on withdrawals.
     - **Roth IRA:** Contributions are made post-tax, but qualifying withdrawals are tax-free.
   - **Investment Flexibility:** More options in IRAs compared to 401(k)s.

### 3. **Taxable Accounts**
   - **Flexibility:** No contribution limits or withdrawal penalties, allowing for more access to funds.
   - **Tax Considerations:** You'll pay taxes on dividends, interest, and capital gains. Consider using this account for investments that generate lower capital gains or qualified dividends.
   - **Investment Strategy:** Suitable for short-term goals or funds that you may need to access sooner.

### **Distribution Strategy**
1. **Emergency Fund:** Ensure you have a sufficient emergency fund outside these accounts.
2. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts:**
   - Prioritize maxing out your 401(k), especially to get employer matching.
   - Consider Roth IRA for tax-free growth, especially if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement.
3. **Taxable Investment Strategy:** 
   - After maxing out tax-advantaged accounts, use taxable accounts for additional investments.
   - Focus on tax-efficient investments (like index funds or municipal bonds) in these accounts.

### **Final Considerations**
- **Risk Tolerance:** Adjust your investment mix based on your ability to tolerate market fluctuations.
- **Regular Review:** Periodically reassess your allocations and adjust according to changes in your financial situation, tax laws, and retirement goals.
- **Consult a Financial Advisor:** Personalized advice can help tailor a strategy specific to your needs.

By balancing contributions across these accounts thoughtfully, you can optimize your retirement savings and tax strategy.
