The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your current financial situation, retirement goals, tax considerations, and investment preferences. Here's a general strategy to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your 401(k) offers an employer match, contribute enough to take full advantage of this benefit. This is essentially free money and a great starting point.

2. **Consider Tax Implications**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are typically made with pre-tax dollars, reducing your taxable income for the year. However, withdrawals in retirement are taxed as ordinary income.
   - **IRA (Traditional vs. Roth)**: Traditional IRAs also provide tax-deferred growth and pre-tax contributions but are taxed upon withdrawal. Roth IRAs are funded with after-tax dollars, allowing tax-free growth and tax-free withdrawals in retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: These accounts don’t have the same tax advantages but provide flexibility in terms of withdrawal. You’ll pay capital gains taxes on profits when you sell investments.

3. **Diversify Investments**: Spread your investments across different asset classes (stocks, bonds, ETFs) to minimize risk. Your risk tolerance and investment timeline should inform your specific allocations.

4. **Automate Contributions**: Set up automatic contributions to each account if possible. This ensures consistency and reduces the temptation to spend the money elsewhere.

5. **Adjust Based on Goals**: If you're closer to retirement, you might want to shift more towards safer investments within your 401(k) and IRAs. For longer-term growth, consider maintaining a heavier allocation in stocks within your taxable accounts.

6. **Regularly Rebalance**: Review your investment allocations periodically (e.g., annually) to ensure they align with your goals and make adjustments as needed.

Ultimately, it's recommended to consult with a financial advisor to tailor an investment strategy that fits your specific circumstances.
