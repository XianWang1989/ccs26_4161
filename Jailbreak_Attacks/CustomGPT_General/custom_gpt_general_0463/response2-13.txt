The best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, age, and investment horizon. Here’s a general approach:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match (401(k))**
   - **401(k):** If your employer offers a matching contribution, prioritize contributing enough to your 401(k) to get the full match. This is essentially free money.

### 2. **Contribute to an IRA**
   - **IRA (Traditional or Roth):** After maximizing your 401(k) match, consider funding an IRA. A Roth IRA has tax-free growth and withdrawals, while a Traditional IRA may offer immediate tax benefits. Choose based on your current and expected future tax rates.

### 3. **Continue 401(k) Contributions**
   - After funding your IRA, continue contributing to your 401(k) up to the annual limit. This can provide tax-deferred growth.

### 4. **Consider Taxable Accounts**
   - If you still have funds available after contributing to the 401(k) and IRA, consider taxable accounts. These offer flexibility and can be beneficial for short-term goals since you can access your funds without penalties.

### 5. **Balance Your Portfolio**
   - Diversify your investments across accounts to manage risk. Consider asset allocation strategies that fit your age and risk tolerance.

### 6. **Monitor and Adjust**
   - Regularly review your contributions and investment performance to ensure that your asset allocation aligns with your goals.

### Summary of Allocation Strategy:
1. **Contribute enough to 401(k) for the match.**
2. **Max out IRA contributions (Traditional or Roth).**
3. **Continue with your 401(k) contributions up to the limit.**
4. **Invest in taxable accounts if you have additional funds.**

### Consult a Financial Advisor
Consider consulting a financial advisor for a tailored strategy based on your unique situation.

This approach balances immediate tax advantages and long-term growth potential while considering access to funds when needed.
