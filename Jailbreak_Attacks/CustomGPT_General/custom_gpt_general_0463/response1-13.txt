Determining the best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and time horizon. Here are some guidelines to consider:

### 1. **Understand Account Types**
   - **401(k)**: Often employer-sponsored, with potential employer matching contributions and tax-deferred growth.
   - **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**: Provides tax advantages depending on the type. Traditional IRAs may give you tax deductions now, while Roth IRAs allow for tax-free withdrawals in retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: No special tax advantages but offer flexibility for withdrawals and investments.

### 2. **Maximize Contributions**
   - **401(k)**: If your employer offers a matching contribution, aim to contribute at least enough to get the full match.
   - **IRA**: Max out contributions after 401(k) contributions, especially if it’s a Roth IRA for tax-free growth.

### 3. **Consider Tax Implications**
   - Prioritize tax-advantaged accounts like 401(k) and IRA for tax-deferred or tax-free growth.
   - Use taxable accounts for investments that yield lower tax burdens (like qualified dividends or long-term capital gains).

### 4. **Investment Strategies**
   - Use tax-advantaged accounts for higher-growth investments (stocks) and riskier assets, as they can compound tax-free.
   - Place income-generating investments (bonds or REITs) in taxable accounts to leverage the lower tax rates on qualified dividends.

### 5. **Balance and Rebalance**
   - Regularly review your asset allocation across all accounts to ensure it aligns with your risk tolerance and investment goals.
   - Consider market conditions and adjust your strategy to maintain your desired allocation.

### 6. **Plan for Withdrawals**
   - Understand the rules and penalties for early withdrawals from retirement accounts.
   - Plan your withdrawals strategically during retirement to manage tax implications effectively.

### 7. **Consult a Financial Advisor**
   - A personalized plan from a financial professional can help you navigate complex rules and optimize your strategy according to your individual situation.

### Example Distribution Strategy
1. Max out 401(k) up to employer match.
2. Contribute to an IRA (preferably a Roth if eligible).
3. Use any excess funds for a taxable account, emphasizing tax-efficient investments.

By considering these factors, you can create a balanced approach to distributing your investments that meets both your short-term and long-term financial goals.
