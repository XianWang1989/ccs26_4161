The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, risk tolerance, and time horizon. Here’s a general approach:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match. This is essentially free money.

2. **Contribute to an IRA**: If you're eligible, consider maximizing contributions to a traditional or Roth IRA. Roth IRAs can be particularly beneficial for tax-free growth.

3. **Fund Taxable Accounts**: After maximizing contributions to your retirement accounts, consider putting extra funds into taxable accounts. These offer flexibility for withdrawals, but be mindful of capital gains taxes.

### Additional Considerations
- **Investment Strategy**: Consider a diversified investment strategy based on your risk tolerance. Stocks for growth, bonds for stability, etc.
- **Rebalancing**: Regularly review and rebalance your portfolio to maintain your desired asset allocation.
- **Tax Efficiency**: Place more tax-inefficient investments (like bonds) in tax-deferred accounts and tax-efficient investments (like index funds) in taxable accounts.

### Example Distribution
- **401(k)**: 15% of your income (up to the match)
- **IRA**: Max out contributions ($6,500 for under 50; $7,500 for over 50 for 2023)
- **Taxable Account**: Any remaining savings after the above contributions.

Consult a financial advisor for a personalized strategy.
