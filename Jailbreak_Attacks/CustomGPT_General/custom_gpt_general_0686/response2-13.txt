Distributing your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your age, income, financial goals, and risk tolerance. Here are some general guidelines:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**
   - Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match, as it's essentially free money.

### 2. **Consider Tax Advantages**
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are pretax, reducing taxable income. Good for higher earners looking to lower their tax bracket.
   - **Traditional IRA**: Also tax-deductible (if eligible), which can be beneficial for lowering current taxable income.
   - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, but withdrawals in retirement are tax-free.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: More flexible, but subject to capital gains tax.

### 3. **Prioritize Accounts Based on Goals**
   - **Retirement Savings**: Focus on 401(k) and IRA for long-term growth. Max out contributions if possible.
   - **Short-Term Goals**: Use taxable accounts for easier access to funds without penalties.

### 4. **Asset Allocation**
   - Diversify your investments across all accounts based on your risk tolerance. Consider index funds or ETFs for broad exposure.
   - Balance between stocks, bonds, and other assets.

### 5. **Tax Efficiency**
   - Hold tax-inefficient investments (like bonds) in tax-advantaged accounts (401(k)/IRA).
   - Keep tax-efficient assets (like index funds) in taxable accounts to minimize capital gains tax.

### 6. **Regular Rebalancing**
   - Review your allocations periodically and rebalance as needed to stay aligned with your goals.

### 7. **Consult a Financial Advisor**
   - A professional can provide personalized advice based on your unique situation.

### Conclusion
A common approach is to maximize employer matches in your 401(k), contribute to a Roth or traditional IRA depending on your tax situation, and use taxable accounts for additional investment flexibility. Always tailor your strategy to your specific financial situation.
