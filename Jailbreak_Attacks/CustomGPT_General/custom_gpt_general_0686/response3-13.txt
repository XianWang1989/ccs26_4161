To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

### 1. **Tax Advantages**
   - **401(k)**: Use for tax-deferred growth; contributions lower your taxable income.
   - **IRA**: Choose between Traditional (tax-deferred) and Roth (tax-free withdrawals) based on your tax situation.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Ideal for holding investments that generate capital gains or dividends, as you can manage your tax liability.

### 2. **Contribution Limits**
   - Maximize contributions to your 401(k) and IRAs first, as they have lower limits and tax benefits.
   - For 2023: $22,500 for 401(k) (+ $7,500 if over 50) and $6,500 for IRAs (+ $1,000 if over 50).

### 3. **Investment Strategy**
   - **401(k)**: Focus on long-term growth. Consider low-cost index funds or target-date funds.
   - **IRA**: Diversify across asset classes, potentially including ETFs or individual stocks for growth.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Use for short- to medium-term goals, focusing on more liquid investments.

### 4. **Emergency Fund**
   - Maintain sufficient cash reserves in your taxable account to cover at least 3-6 months of expenses.

### 5. **Withdrawal Strategy**
   - Strategically withdraw from taxable accounts first, then tax-deferred (401(k), Traditional IRA), and finally tax-free (Roth IRA) to manage taxes efficiently in retirement.

### 6. **Rebalancing**
   - Regularly rebalance your portfolio based on market performance and your risk tolerance.

### Summary
A balanced approach prioritizes maxing out tax-advantaged accounts while considering your financial goals, investment horizon, and tax implications.

### Consultation
Consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized advice based on your specific situation.
