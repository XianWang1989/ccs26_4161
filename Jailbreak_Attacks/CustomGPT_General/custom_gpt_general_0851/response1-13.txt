To optimize your money distribution among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

### 1. **Understand Account Types:**
   - **401(k):** Often comes with employer matching; contributions are pre-tax (traditional) or post-tax (Roth).
   - **IRA:** Individual Retirement Account can be traditional (pre-tax) or Roth (post-tax), with flexible contribution limits.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** No tax advantages, but offers flexibility for withdrawals.

### 2. **Prioritize Contributions:**
   - **Maximize Employer Match:** Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full match, as it’s essentially free money.
   - **Contribute to IRAs:** Depending on your income, you may want to contribute to a traditional or Roth IRA (max annual limit applies).
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Use these for additional savings or investments once tax-advantaged accounts are maximized.

### 3. **Consider Tax Implications:**
   - **Traditional Accounts:** Taxes due upon withdrawal; ideal for those expecting a lower tax rate in retirement.
   - **Roth Accounts:** Withdrawals are tax-free if held for five years; beneficial if you expect higher future tax rates.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Subject to capital gains tax; good for short to mid-term goals.

### 4. **Investment Strategy:**
   - **Tax-Deferred Accounts:** Consider holding higher-growth investments (stocks) for longer-term growth.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Use for bond investments or assets that generate significant dividends, as they may be taxed at a lower rate.

### 5. **Regular Reassessments:**
   - Monitor and adjust allocations based on changes in income, tax laws, and market conditions.

### Sample Distribution Strategy:
- **401(k):** Maximize employer match.
- **IRA:** Contribute up to the limit, prioritizing Roth if you expect taxes to rise.
- **Taxable Accounts:** Invest whatever remains after maximizing tax-advantaged accounts.

### Conclusion:
Tailor your strategy based on your financial goals, tax considerations, and investment timeline. Consulting with a financial advisor can provide personalized guidance.
