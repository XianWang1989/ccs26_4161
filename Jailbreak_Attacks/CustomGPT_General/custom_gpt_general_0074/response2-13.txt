When it comes to distributing your savings across a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, there are a few strategies to consider. Here’s a general approach:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**:
   - **401(k)**: If your employer offers a 401(k) match, aim to contribute enough to get the full match. This is essentially free money.
   - **IRA**: If you qualify, consider contributing to a Roth or Traditional IRA next. The tax advantages can be significant depending on your income level and future tax expectations.

2. **Assess Your Tax Situation**:
   - **Taxable Accounts**: While these accounts offer less tax efficiency compared to retirement accounts, they provide flexibility. Use them to manage your tax liabilities, especially if you're close to tax brackets that could affect your overall tax rate.

3. **Consider Your Financial Goals**:
   - If you're saving for short-term goals (e.g., a house, vacation), prioritize the taxable account since you’ll need access to those funds sooner.
   - For long-term goals (like retirement), focus on maximizing contributions to tax-advantaged accounts.

4. **Diversification Considerations**:
   - Make sure to diversify investments within each account type based on your risk tolerance, age, and financial goals. 

5. **Consult a Financial Advisor**:
   - Given the complexities of tax laws and personal financial situations, it's wise to consult a financial advisor to tailor a strategy that fits your specific needs.

Always remember that personal circumstances vary widely, so what works best for one person might not be suitable for another.
