Distributing your money across a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here’s a general strategy:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match, as this is essentially “free money.”

2. **Contribute to IRA**: If eligible, contribute to a Roth IRA or a traditional IRA, depending on your income level and tax strategy. Roth IRAs offer tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement.

3. **401(k) Contributions**: After maximizing the match and IRA contributions, continue contributing to your 401(k) up to the annual contribution limits.

4. **Taxable Accounts**: Use taxable accounts for additional savings after maximizing your tax-advantaged accounts. Consider investments that are more tax-efficient, such as index funds or ETFs.

5. **Balance Risk and Time Horizon**: Tailor your investments within each account according to your risk tolerance and when you expect to use the funds. 

6. **Review and Adjust**: Periodically reassess your strategy based on changes in income, tax laws, or personal circumstances.

Consulting with a financial advisor can help customize this approach to your specific situation.
