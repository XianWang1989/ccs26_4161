Allocating your investments among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here are some strategies to consider:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**:
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money. Consider maxing out contributions, especially if you’re in a high tax bracket.
   - **IRA**: If you're eligible, contribute to a Traditional or Roth IRA. A Traditional IRA may provide immediate tax deductions, while Roth provides tax-free withdrawals in retirement.

2. **Consider Tax Efficiency**:
   - Place tax-efficient investments (like index funds) in taxable accounts to minimize capital gains taxes.
   - Hold tax-inefficient investments (like bonds or actively managed mutual funds) in tax-advantaged accounts to shield income from taxes.

3. **Evaluate Your Investment Strategy**:
   - Maintain a diversified portfolio across all accounts, balancing risk and return based on your age, risk tolerance, and investment goals.
   - Review asset allocation regularly to ensure it aligns with changes in your financial situation.

4. **Withdrawals and Retirement Planning**:
   - Plan for how and when you'll withdraw funds from these accounts, considering tax implications and your cash flow needs in retirement.
   - Consider strategies like Roth conversions during low-income years to manage taxes in retirement.

5. **Seek Professional Advice**:
   - Consulting a financial advisor can provide personalized advice tailored to your unique circumstances, especially regarding tax strategies and retirement planning.

By balancing contributions, considering tax efficiency, and regularly reviewing your strategy, you can develop a robust plan for distributing your investments effectively.
