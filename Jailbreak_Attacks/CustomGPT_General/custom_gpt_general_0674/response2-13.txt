Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, current income, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here's a general strategy to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k):** If your employer offers a match on your 401(k) contributions, contribute enough to get the full match. This is essentially free money.

2. **Contribute to an IRA:** After maximizing your 401(k) match, consider contributing to an IRA (Traditional or Roth). A Roth IRA allows for tax-free growth and tax-free withdrawals in retirement, while a Traditional IRA may provide immediate tax deductions. The choice between them depends on your current tax rate versus your expected tax rate in retirement.

3. **Max Out 401(k):** If you're able, continue contributing to your 401(k) up to the maximum limit. It provides tax-deferred growth, and it's often beneficial to take advantage of higher contribution limits compared to IRAs.

4. **Taxable Accounts:** Once you’ve maximized contributions to your tax-advantaged accounts, consider using taxable accounts for additional investments. This allows for more flexibility, as you can withdraw funds without penalties before retirement age, but be mindful of capital gains taxes on any profits.

5. **Rebalance Based on Age and Risk Tolerance:** As you age or if your financial situation changes, adjust your investments across these accounts to align with your risk tolerance and time horizon. Typically, younger investors might prioritize growth, while those closer to retirement might shift towards more conservative investments.

6. **Consider Tax Implications:** Each account type has different tax implications, so plan your withdrawals in retirement accordingly to minimize taxes.

7. **Consult with a Financial Advisor:** If you're unsure, it's wise to speak with a financial advisor who can provide personalized advice based on your specific circumstances.

Remember, the best approach is tailored to your individual financial situation and goals.
