Distributing your money effectively among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts involves considering several factors, including your financial goals, tax implications, and investment strategies. Here are some guidelines to help you decide on an optimal distribution:

### 1. **Understand Account Types:**
   - **401(k):** Offers tax-deferred growth and may include employer matching. Contributions are typically pre-tax, reducing your taxable income.
   - **IRA (Traditional or Roth):** 
     - **Traditional IRA:** Contributions may be tax-deductible, but withdrawals in retirement are taxed.
     - **Roth IRA:** Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, but withdrawals in retirement are tax-free.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** These allow for flexibility in withdrawals but are subject to capital gains taxes on profits.

### 2. **Maximize Employer Match:**
   - Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match, as this is essentially "free money."

### 3. **Consider Tax Implications:**
   - **Short-term vs. Long-term Investment Horizons:** Utilize taxable accounts for assets you plan to hold long-term to benefit from lower capital gains taxes.
   - **Tax-Loss Harvesting:** Taxable accounts can be used to offset gains with losses, which is not possible with tax-deferred accounts.

### 4. **Emergency Fund First:**
   - Ensure you have an emergency fund in a liquid account, which should cover 3-6 months of expenses before heavily investing.

### 5. **Allocate Based on Age and Risk Tolerance:**
   - Younger investors may favor growth-oriented assets (stocks) in their tax-advantaged accounts, while older investors may prefer bonds to reduce risk in retirement accounts.
   - Consider your risk tolerance when determining asset allocation across account types.

### 6. **Diversification Across Accounts:**
   - Diversify investments across all accounts to manage risk. This includes different asset classes (stocks, bonds) and sectors.

### 7. **Regular Rebalancing:**
   - Periodically review and rebalance your portfolio to ensure your asset allocation aligns with your goals and risk tolerance.

### 8. **Consult a Financial Advisor:**
   - For personalized advice tailored to your specific circumstances, consider consulting a financial advisor.

### Example Allocation Strategy:
- **401(k):** 15% of income (enough to get full employer match)
- **IRA:** Max out contributions if eligible (currently $6,500 for those under 50, $7,500 for those 50+).
- **Taxable Account:** Any remaining funds after meeting 401(k) and IRA goals.

By tailoring your investment strategy to your financial situation and goals, you can optimize the distribution of your money across these accounts.
