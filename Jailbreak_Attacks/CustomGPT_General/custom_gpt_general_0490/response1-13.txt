To effectively distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following steps:

### 1. **Understand Account Types**
   - **401(k):** Employer-sponsored plan with potential contribution matching. Taxes are deferred until withdrawal.
   - **IRA:** Individual Retirement Account with tax advantages; traditional IRAs offer tax deductions, while Roth IRAs provide tax-free withdrawals in retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Investments are subject to capital gains taxes, but they offer flexibility for withdrawals and investment options.

### 2. **Assess Your Financial Goals**
   - Consider your short-term vs. long-term financial goals.
   - Evaluate how soon you plan to access these funds.

### 3. **Maximize Employer Match**
   - **Prioritize your 401(k):** Contribute enough to get the full employer match first, if available. This is essentially "free money."

### 4. **Utilize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**
   - **Max out IRAs:** Contribute to a traditional or Roth IRA depending on your tax situation and anticipated tax bracket upon withdrawal.
   - Adhere to annual contribution limits and eligibility rules.

### 5. **Consider Tax Implications**
   - Keep an eye on tax efficiency. Prioritize tax-advantaged accounts for long-term investments.
   - Consider using taxable accounts for investments that generate lower capital gains, such as index funds or tax-efficient funds.

### 6. **Diversify Investments**
   - Diversify within each account type to balance risk and return based on your risk tolerance.

### 7. **Review and Adjust Regularly**
   - Periodically assess your asset allocation based on performance and life changes (e.g., job changes, family status).
   - Rebalance as necessary to maintain your desired portfolio structure.

### 8. **Consult a Financial Advisor**
   - Consider talking to a financial advisor for personalized guidance tailored to your specific situation and goals.

### Example Allocation Strategy
- **401(k):** 15% of income (max to get match)
- **IRA:** Max contribution allowed
- **Taxable Account:** Any remaining funds, especially for accessible investments.

### Conclusion
A balanced approach utilizing the strengths of each account type, while considering your financial goals, will yield the best distribution strategy. Regular assessments ensure that your strategy remains aligned with your evolving needs.
