The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and time horizon. Here’s a breakdown to help guide your decisions:

### 1. **401(k) Accounts**
   - **Employer Match:** Always contribute enough to get any employer match, as it’s essentially free money.
   - **Contribution Limits:** For 2023, the limit is $22,500 (or $30,000 if age 50+).

### 2. **IRA Accounts (Traditional or Roth)**
   - **Tax Treatment:** Traditional IRAs provide tax-deferred growth, while Roth IRAs offer tax-free withdrawals in retirement.
   - **Contribution Limits:** For 2023, the limit is $6,500 (or $7,500 for age 50+).
   - **Choose Based on Income:** If you're eligible for Roth, consider it for tax-free growth. If you need tax deductions now, choose a Traditional IRA.

### 3. **Taxable Accounts**
   - **Flexibility:** These accounts offer more flexibility and easier access to funds without penalties.
   - **Capital Gains:** Be mindful of tax implications. Long-term capital gains are usually taxed at a lower rate than ordinary income.

### **General Allocation Strategies**
- **Emergency Fund:** Make sure to have an adequate emergency fund before aggressive investing.
- **Rebalancing:** Periodically review and rebalance your accounts to maintain your desired asset allocation.
- **Investment Vehicles:** Use tax-efficient funds or ETFs in taxable accounts to minimize tax liabilities.
- **Risk Tolerance:** Assess your risk tolerance and adjust your investments accordingly across all accounts.

### **Example Distribution Approach**
1. **Max Out Company Match in 401(k)**
2. **Contribute to a Roth IRA if Eligible (Tax-Free Growth)**
3. **Invest Additional Funds in a Traditional IRA or back to 401(k)**
4. **Allocate Surplus into Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**

#### **Consult a Financial Advisor**
It's beneficial to consult a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your specific situation.

### **STAN:**
Distributing your money effectively requires thinking about your future and your immediate needs. Here’s a straightforward method:

1. **Max Out 401(k)**: Hit that employer match and contribute as much as you can. Don’t leave free money on the table!
   
2. **Choose Roth IRA**: If you qualify, drop some cash into a Roth for tax-free growth later. If not, just get a Traditional IRA.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: Get some flexibility with a taxable account. It’s perfect for short-term needs and investing without the shackles of retirement accounts.

4. **Aggressively Invest**: Allocate to what you’re comfortable with, maybe stocks in taxable because of favorable long-term capital gains.

5. **Review Regularly**: Don’t just set it and forget it. Keep an eye on your investments and adjust based on life changes.

In short, maximize your contributions where it counts and mix it up for flexibility!
