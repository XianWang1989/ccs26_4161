Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here are some general strategies to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Contributions**: If your employer offers a match on your 401(k), contribute enough to get the full match first. This is essentially free money.

2. **Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**: After maximizing your employer match, consider contributing to an IRA (Traditional or Roth) depending on your income and eligibility. Both options offer tax advantages that can help your money grow more efficiently.

3. **Balance Contributions**: Once you’ve maximized contributions to retirement accounts, consider balancing additional contributions between your 401(k) and IRA based on fees and investment options. If your 401(k) has high fees or limited investment choices, it may be better to prioritize your IRA.

4. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**: Taxable accounts can offer greater flexibility for access to funds without penalties, which is useful for short-term goals or unexpected expenses. These accounts can be funded after you’ve maximized your contributions to tax-advantaged accounts.

5. **Investment Strategy**: Ensure your investments align with your risk tolerance and time horizon. Typically, you want more aggressive investments in retirement accounts (which have a longer time frame) and a more balanced approach in taxable accounts due to tax implications on gains.

6. **Review and Adjust**: Regularly review your asset allocation and adjust based on market performance, changes in your financial situation, and any adjustments in your retirement goals.

It's always best to consult with a financial advisor for personalized advice based on your specific circumstances.
