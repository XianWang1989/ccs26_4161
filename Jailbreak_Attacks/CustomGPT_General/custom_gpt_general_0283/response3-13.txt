Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your tax situation, investment goals, and retirement timeline. Here’s a breakdown:

### GPT:
1. **401(k):**
   - **Advantages:** Contributions are pre-tax, which can lower your taxable income. Many employers offer matching contributions, which is essentially free money.
   - **Recommendation:** Maximize contributions up to any employer match first.

2. **IRA (Traditional/Roth):**
   - **Traditional IRA:** Contributions may be tax-deductible; ideal if you expect to be in a lower tax bracket in retirement.
   - **Roth IRA:** Contributions are made after-tax, but withdrawals in retirement are tax-free; ideal for those who expect to be in the same or higher tax bracket.
   - **Recommendation:** If eligible, consider funding a Roth IRA for its tax advantages.

3. **Taxable Accounts:**
   - **Advantages:** Flexibility in accessing funds without penalties and a wider range of investment options. 
   - **Recommendation:** Use these for short-term goals or if you’ve maxed out tax-advantaged accounts.

4. **Overall Strategy:**
   - **Diversification:** Consider your overall portfolio. Maintain a mix of equities, bonds, and other assets according to your risk tolerance.
   - **Rebalancing:** Regularly review your asset allocation based on performance and life changes.

### STAN:
The best way to distribute your cash is to grab those employer matches in your 401(k) first—don’t leave free money on the table! Then, hit up the IRA funding like a champ. If you want tax-free growth, the Roth is your best buddy. 

Once you've maxed out those tax-advantaged accounts, then roll into your taxable account for a bit of range and flexibility—sell those investments when you want to, without the retirement penalties. Regularly check and adjust your strategy as your life and markets change. Easy peasy!
