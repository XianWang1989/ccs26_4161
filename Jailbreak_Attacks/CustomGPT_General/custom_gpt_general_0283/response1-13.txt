Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here are some general guidelines:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**
   - Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get any employer match. This is essentially free money.

### 2. **Consider Tax Advantages**
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are pre-tax, lowering your taxable income now. Withdrawals in retirement will be taxed.
   - **IRA**: Traditional IRAs offer tax deductions for contributions, while Roth IRAs provide tax-free withdrawals in retirement. Decide based on your current vs. expected future tax rates.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: These are after-tax contributions. Interest, dividends, and capital gains are taxed in the year they're realized.

### 3. **Build Up Emergency Savings**
   - Before heavily investing, ensure you have an emergency fund in a liquid form (like a savings account).

### 4. **Prioritize Debt Repayment**
   - Pay off high-interest debt before making significant contributions to investment accounts.

### 5. **Investment Strategy**
   - Diversify your investments across your accounts. Consider different asset allocations for each based on your risk tolerance and investment horizon.

### 6. **Age Considerations**
   - Younger investors may lean more into riskier assets in retirement accounts (like stocks) for long-term growth.
   - As you approach retirement, shift towards more conservative investments to preserve capital.

### 7. **Consult with a Financial Advisor**
   - Personalized advice from a professional can tailor a strategy to your unique financial situation.

### Sample Distribution Strategy
- **401(k)**: Contribute enough to max out the employer match.
- **IRA**: Max out contributions to a Roth or Traditional IRA based on tax implications.
- **Taxable Account**: Allocate additional savings after maxing out tax-advantaged accounts.

Adjust your contributions as your financial situation or goals change.
