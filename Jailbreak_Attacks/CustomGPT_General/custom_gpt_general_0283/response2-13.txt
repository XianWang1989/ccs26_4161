To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider these key strategies:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match first. This is essentially free money.

2. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: Focus on maxing out your contributions, especially if it offers a traditional option with tax-deferred growth.
   - **IRA**: If eligible, consider a Roth IRA for tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement. If not, a traditional IRA can offer tax benefits.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for additional savings once you’ve maximized contributions to tax-advantaged accounts. They provide more flexibility but are taxable annually on dividends and capital gains.

4. **Consider Your Tax Bracket**: If you’re in a higher tax bracket now, prioritize pre-tax accounts (401(k) and traditional IRA). If you expect to be in a lower tax bracket in retirement, Roth accounts may be more beneficial.

5. **Diversify Investments**: Within each account, diversify across asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) to manage risk. Consider your time horizon and risk tolerance.

6. **Rebalance Regularly**: Review your asset allocation periodically to ensure it aligns with your retirement goals and risk tolerance.

7. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: If unsure, a financial planner can provide personalized advice tailored to your situation and goals.

By following these strategies, you can create a balanced approach to growing your retirement savings.
