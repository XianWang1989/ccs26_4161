Distributing your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here are some general guidelines:

1. **401(k) Accounts**:
   - **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to get the full employer match if available. This is essentially free money.
   - **Higher Contribution Limits**: 401(k) plans typically allow for higher contributions compared to IRAs. Consider maxing out your contributions if possible.

2. **IRA Accounts**:
   - **Tax Advantages**: Traditional IRAs may provide tax-deductible contributions, while Roth IRAs allow for tax-free withdrawals in retirement. Choose based on your current tax situation and expected future tax rate.
   - **Diverse Investment Options**: IRAs often offer a broader range of investment options compared to 401(k) plans.

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - **Flexibility**: Taxable accounts offer the flexibility to withdraw funds without penalties, making them suitable for short to mid-term savings goals.
   - **Capital Gains Considerations**: Be mindful of capital gains tax when investing in taxable accounts. Consider holding investments for over a year to benefit from lower long-term capital gains rates.

### Suggested Strategy:
- **Emergency Fund First**: Ensure you have an adequate emergency fund (3-6 months of expenses) before heavily investing.
- **401(k) Up to Match**: Start by contributing to your 401(k) enough to get any employer match.
- **IRA Contributions**: Consider contributing to an IRA for additional tax benefits, especially if you qualify for a Roth IRA.
- **Max Out 401(k)**: If you have additional funds, aim to max out your 401(k) contribution next.
- **Invest in Taxable Accounts**: Finally, use any extra funds for taxable accounts, focusing on investments with favorable tax treatment.

### Assess and Adjust:
- Regularly review your allocations based on changes in income, expenses, and financial goals. Adjust contributions accordingly.

Consulting a financial advisor can provide personalized strategies tailored to your specific situation.
