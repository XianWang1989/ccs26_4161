When considering how to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, it's important to take into account your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here are some guidelines to help you make an informed decision:

1. **Maximize Employer Match:** If your employer offers a 401(k) match, contribute enough to get the full match. This is essentially free money for your retirement.

2. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts:** After maximizing your 401(k) match, consider contributing to an IRA (Traditional or Roth, depending on your eligibility and tax situation). These accounts provide tax advantages that can help your investments grow more efficiently.

3. **Evaluate Contribution Limits:** Be aware of the contribution limits for each account type. For 2023, the 401(k) limit is $22,500 ($30,000 if you’re 50 or older), and the IRA limit is $6,500 ($7,500 if you’re 50 or older).

4. **Tax Implications:** Traditional 401(k) and IRA contributions lower your taxable income now, while Roth accounts (Roth 401(k) and Roth IRA) allow for tax-free withdrawals in retirement. Consider your current tax bracket and future expectations when deciding between pre-tax and after-tax contributions.

5. **Invest in Taxable Accounts After Tax-Advantaged Accounts:** Once you've maximized your contributions to retirement accounts, consider investing in taxable accounts. This can offer more flexibility with withdrawals and a variety of investment options, though you will be taxed on any capital gains.

6. **Diversify Investments:** Don't forget the importance of asset allocation and diversification within these accounts. Aim to invest in a mix of stocks, bonds, and other assets based on your risk tolerance and investment horizon.

7. **Regularly Review:** Your financial situation and market conditions can change, so periodically review your contributions and investment strategy to ensure alignment with your goals.

It’s often beneficial to consult with a financial advisor to tailor your strategy to your individual circumstances.
