The best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your current financial situation, tax bracket, retirement goals, and the investment options available in each account. Here are some general guidelines to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**: If your employer offers a matching contribution for your 401(k), it’s generally advisable to contribute enough to get the full match. This is essentially "free money."

2. **Contribute to an IRA**: After maximizing your 401(k) match, consider contributing to a traditional or Roth IRA. A traditional IRA may provide you with tax deductions in the year you contribute, while a Roth IRA offers tax-free withdrawals in retirement. Choose based on your current vs. expected future tax rates.

3. **Continue Funding Your 401(k)**: If you still have room in your budget after contributing to your IRA, increase your contributions to your 401(k), especially if you're in a high tax bracket, since contributions are tax-deferred.

4. **Consider Taxable Accounts**: If you’ve maxed out your contributions to your retirement accounts, consider investing in a taxable brokerage account. This provides flexibility and access to funds if needed before retirement age, but keep in mind capital gains taxes.

5. **Diversification and Asset Allocation**: Ensure that your investment strategy is diversified across different asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) in each account, based on your risk tolerance and investment goals.

6. **Monitor and Adjust**: Regularly review your investments and contributions based on changes in income, market conditions, and life circumstances to make suitable adjustments.

It's always wise to consult with a financial advisor to tailor a strategy that suits your specific situation.
