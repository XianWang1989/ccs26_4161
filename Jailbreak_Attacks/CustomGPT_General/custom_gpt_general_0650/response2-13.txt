Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts involves several factors, including your financial goals, time horizon, and tax situation. Here’s a structured approach to consider:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Contributions**
   - **401(k)**: If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to get the full match first. This is essentially "free money."

### 2. **Tax-Advantaged Accounts**
   - **IRA (Traditional and Roth)**: After maximizing your 401(k) match, consider funding an IRA. Roth IRAs offer tax-free growth and tax-free withdrawals in retirement, which can be beneficial depending on your income level and anticipated tax bracket during retirement.
   - **Traditional IRA**: Contributions may be tax-deductible, lowering your tax burden now, but withdrawals in retirement will be taxed.

### 3. **Fill Up Tax-Advantaged Space**
   - Contribute to your 401(k) and IRA up to the annual limits. For 2023, these limits are $22,500 for 401(k)s (with a $7,500 catch-up for those 50+) and $6,500 for IRAs (with a $1,000 catch-up).

### 4. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**
   - After funding your retirement accounts, consider a taxable brokerage account for additional savings. They provide liquidity and flexibility since you can withdraw without penalties unlike retirement accounts.

### 5. **Asset Allocation**
   - **401(k) and IRA**: Generally, consider holding higher-growth investments (like stocks) in these accounts due to their tax-advantaged status.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: You might want to hold more tax-efficient investments, like index funds or municipal bonds, to minimize the tax impact.

### 6. **Review and Adjust Regularly**
   - Regularly review your asset allocation and contributions to ensure they align with your financial goals and adjust as needed based on market conditions or changes in your personal circumstances.

### 7. **Consult a Financial Advisor**
   - If you’re uncertain, consulting a financial advisor can provide personalized guidance tailored to your specific situation.

This structured approach can help you optimize your investment strategy effectively.
