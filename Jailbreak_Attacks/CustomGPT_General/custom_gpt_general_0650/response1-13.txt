Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, investment timeline, and risk tolerance. Here are some general guidelines:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**
   - **401(k)**: If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to get the full match. This is essentially free money. Consider the 401(k)'s investment options and fees.
   - **IRA**: After maximizing your 401(k) match, consider contributing to an IRA (Traditional or Roth). Roth IRAs offer tax-free growth on withdrawals, while Traditional IRAs may provide tax deductions.

### 2. **Consider Tax Implications**
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Use taxable accounts for investments that generate lower capital gains or dividends, as some tax advantages can be lost here. This can be a good place for investments with longer time horizons, like index funds.

### 3. **Evaluate Your Investment Goals**
   - If retirement is your primary goal, prioritize tax-advantaged accounts. If you need flexibility for short-term goals, ensure some funds are in taxable accounts.

### 4. **Assess Your Risk Tolerance**
   - Allocate investments based on your risk tolerance across all three types of accounts, adjusting for asset classes that may be more tax-efficient when held in taxable accounts (like stocks).

### 5. **Rebalance Regularly**
   - Periodically review your asset allocation across these accounts to ensure it aligns with your goals and any changes in the market.

### Example Strategy:
- **Max out 401(k)** up to the employer match.
- **Contribute to an IRA** (consider the type based on your current and expected future tax situation).
- Use a **taxable account** for extra funds or investments suitable for shorter-term goals.

### Additional Considerations:
- Stay informed about contribution limits and potential penalties for early withdrawals.
- Consult a financial advisor for personalized advice based on your specific circumstances.

By carefully considering these factors, you can create an efficient investment strategy that aligns with your financial goals.
