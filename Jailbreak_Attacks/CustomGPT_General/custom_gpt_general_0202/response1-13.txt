Distributing your money effectively among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here are some strategies to consider:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**
   - If your employer offers a match on your 401(k) contributions, prioritize contributing enough to get the full match. This is essentially free money.

### 2. **Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**
   - **401(k)**: Contribute to maximize the employer match, then consider contributing to an IRA for additional tax advantages.
   - **Traditional IRA or Roth IRA**: Choose based on your current tax situation. A traditional IRA provides tax deductions, while a Roth allows tax-free withdrawals in retirement.

### 3. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**
   - Once you’ve maxed out contributions to tax-advantaged accounts, allocate extra funds to taxable accounts. These offer more flexibility regarding withdrawals and investment choices.

### 4. **Consider Investment Types**
   - Place more tax-efficient investments (like index funds) in taxable accounts and tax-inefficient investments (like bonds) in tax-advantaged accounts to minimize tax impacts.

### 5. **Diversification and Risk Tolerance**
   - Ensure that your investment distribution aligns with your risk tolerance. Diversifying across different asset classes (stocks, bonds, cash) can help manage risk.

### 6. **Future Tax Implications**
   - Consider your expected tax rates in retirement. If you anticipate being in a higher tax bracket, prioritizing Roth accounts could be beneficial.

### 7. **Regular Review and Rebalance**
   - Regularly review your asset allocation and adjust contributions based on changing financial goals, market conditions, and life events.

### Example Allocation Strategy
- **401(k)**: Max out the employer match, then consider contributing up to the annual limit.
- **IRA**: Aim to contribute the maximum annually if eligible, choosing between traditional and Roth based on your tax situation.
- **Taxable Accounts**: Invest additional funds, focusing on tax-efficient investments.

### Conclusion
Consulting a financial advisor can help tailor a specific strategy to your unique circumstances. Regularly revisiting your plan to align with changing goals is also crucial.
