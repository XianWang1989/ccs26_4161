Distributing money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax considerations, and time horizon. Here’s a breakdown:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match (401(k))**
   - **Contribution Goal**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match, as this is essentially free money.

### 2. **Tax-Advantaged Accounts (IRA & 401(k))**
   - **Prioritize Roth vs. Traditional**: Decide between a Roth IRA (tax-free withdrawals) and traditional IRA/401(k) (tax-deferred growth).
   - **Contribution Limits**: Be aware of annual contribution limits for each account. For 2023, the limits are $22,500 for 401(k) and $6,500 for IRAs (additional $1,000 catch-up contributions if you're over 50).

### 3. **Taxable Accounts**
   - **After Tax Contributions**: Once you've maximized tax-deferred accounts, consider investing in taxable accounts for additional flexibility and liquidity.
   - **Tax Efficiency**: Focus on tax-efficient investments (like index funds) that minimize capital gains taxes.

### 4. **Asset Allocation**
   - **Risk Tolerance & Time Horizon**: Adjust your asset allocation based on your risk tolerance and how long you plan to invest before needing the money.
   - **Rebalance Regularly**: Periodically assess and rebalance your portfolio to maintain your desired allocation.

### 5. **Withdrawal Strategies**
   - **Decide on Order of Withdrawal**: When it comes time to withdraw, consider which account to draw from first to minimize taxes and maximize growth.

### Summary Strategy:
1. Contribute enough to 401(k) for the employer match.
2. Max out IRA contributions (preferably Roth if eligible).
3. Invest any additional funds in a taxable account with tax-efficient investments.

Consulting a financial advisor can provide personalized advice tailored to your specific situation.
