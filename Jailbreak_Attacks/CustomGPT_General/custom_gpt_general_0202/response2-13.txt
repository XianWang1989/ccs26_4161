Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts is essential for optimizing retirement savings and managing taxes. Here's a strategic approach to consider:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**
   - **Action:** Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match if available. This is free money and provides an immediate return on investment.

### 2. **Contribute to an IRA**
   - **Action:** If eligible, consider contributing to a Traditional or Roth IRA. A Roth IRA offers tax-free withdrawals in retirement, while a Traditional IRA provides tax-deferred growth.
   - **Contribution Limits:** Be mindful of the annual contribution limits for IRAs ($6,500 for individuals under 50 and $7,500 for those 50 and older in 2023).

### 3. **Maximize 401(k) Contributions**
   - **Action:** After securing the employer match and contributing to an IRA, increase your 401(k) contributions to the maximum limit ($22,500 for those under 50 and $30,000 for those 50 and older in 2023).
   - **Investment Choices:** Choose a diversified mix of funds within your 401(k) that aligns with your risk tolerance and time horizon.

### 4. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**
   - **Action:** If you have surplus cash after maximizing tax-advantaged accounts, allocate funds to a taxable investment account. This can provide liquidity and access to investments without early withdrawal penalties.
   - **Tax Efficiency:** Focus on tax-efficient investments, such as index funds, ETFs, or municipal bonds, to minimize tax impact.

### 5. **Maintain an Emergency Fund**
   - **Action:** Before heavily investing, ensure you have an emergency fund covering 3-6 months' worth of expenses accessible in a savings account.

### 6. **Consider Your Tax Situation**
   - **Action:** Regularly review your tax situation. Depending on your income and tax bracket, adjusting contributions between accounts might save on taxes.

### 7. **Rebalance Regularly**
   - **Action:** Periodically review and rebalance your investment portfolio to maintain your desired asset allocation and risk level.

### 8. **Seek Professional Advice**
   - **Action:** Consult a financial advisor to tailor a strategy based on your specific financial situation, goals, and risk tolerance.

By following these steps, you can effectively distribute your money across your retirement and investment accounts, maximizing growth and minimizing tax liabilities.
