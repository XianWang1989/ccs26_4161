When it comes to distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, there are several strategies you might consider based on your individual financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some general guidelines:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a 401(k) match, try to contribute enough to get the full match. This is essentially free money.

2. **Contribute to Retirement Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: Focus on maxing out your contributions if you are in a high-income bracket, as contributions are pre-tax and reduce your taxable income. In 2023, the contribution limit is $22,500, or $30,000 if you're over 50.
   - **IRA**: After maxing out your 401(k) match, consider contributing to a traditional or Roth IRA. The contribution limit for 2023 is $6,500, or $7,500 if you're over 50. A Roth IRA allows tax-free growth and tax-free withdrawals in retirement if you meet certain criteria.

3. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**: If you have money left to invest after contributing to retirement accounts, consider placing funds in taxable accounts. This gives you more flexibility for withdrawals and can help diversify your investments.

4. **Tax-Efficiency**: Keep tax-efficient investments (like stocks) in taxable accounts and less tax-efficient investments (like bonds) in tax-advantaged accounts to minimize tax liabilities.

5. **Emergency Fund**: Ensure you have an adequate emergency fund outside these accounts before heavily investing in them. Typically, 3-6 months' worth of expenses is recommended.

6. **Asset Allocation**: Allocate your investments based on your risk tolerance and investment timeline. Stocks generally offer higher returns over the long term, while bonds are typically more stable.

7. **Rebalance Regularly**: As time goes on, periodically review and adjust your asset allocation to ensure it aligns with your risk tolerance and financial goals.

By considering these factors, you can create a balanced distribution plan that supports your short-term and long-term financial objectives. Always consider consulting with a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your situation.
