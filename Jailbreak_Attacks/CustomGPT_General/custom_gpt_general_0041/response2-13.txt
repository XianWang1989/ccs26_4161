The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors including your financial goals, tax situation, and time horizon. Here’s a general strategy to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a 401(k) match, contribute at least enough to receive the full match. This is essentially free money.

2. **Prioritize High-Interest Debt**: Before investing, pay off high-interest debt (like credit card debt) as the interest on these debts often outweighs investment returns.

3. **Contribute to Your 401(k)**: After getting the match, contribute to your 401(k) up to the annual limit if you have a high income, especially if you’re in a higher tax bracket. This reduces your taxable income.

4. **Max Out IRA Contributions**: Next, consider maxing out your IRA (Traditional or Roth, depending on your eligibility and tax strategy). A Roth IRA provides tax-free growth and tax-free withdrawals in retirement.

5. **Taxable Accounts**: After maximizing contributions to your 401(k) and IRA, consider investing in taxable accounts, especially if you need flexibility with withdrawals or have maxed out retirement accounts. Look for tax-efficient investments like index funds or ETFs to minimize capital gains taxes.

6. **Rebalance Regularly**: Periodically review your asset allocation and rebalance across your accounts to stay in line with your risk tolerance and investment goals.

7. **Long-Term Strategy**: Remember to think long-term. Prioritize investments that align with your retirement timeline and risk tolerance, and take advantage of compound interest over the years.

Always consider consulting with a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your specific situation.
