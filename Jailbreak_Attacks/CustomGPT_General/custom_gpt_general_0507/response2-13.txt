The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment horizon. Here are some general guidelines to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a match for your 401(k) contributions, aim to contribute enough to take full advantage of this benefit, as it's essentially free money.

2. **Consider Tax Implications**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are typically pre-tax, which reduces your taxable income. Withdrawals are taxed as ordinary income in retirement.
   - **IRA (Traditional)**: Similar to a 401(k), contributions may be tax-deductible, and taxes are owed on withdrawals in retirement.
   - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, allowing for tax-free withdrawals in retirement. If you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in the future, prioritizing a Roth IRA can be beneficial.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: These are subject to capital gains tax when you sell investments at a profit. Consider using them for investments that you plan to hold long-term to minimize tax impact.

3. **Diversification**: Ensure that your investments are diversified across asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) regardless of the account type. This can help reduce risk while aiming for growth.

4. **Investment Goals**:
   - If you’re saving for short-term goals (like buying a house), consider keeping funds in a taxable account for flexibility.
   - For long-term goals (retirement), prioritize tax-advantaged accounts (401(k), IRA) because they offer growth potential with tax benefits.

5. **Rebalancing**: Periodically review and rebalance your accounts to ensure that your asset allocation aligns with your risk tolerance and investment goals.

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: Given the complexity of tax laws and investment strategies, consider consulting with a financial advisor who can provide personalized advice based on your specific situation.
