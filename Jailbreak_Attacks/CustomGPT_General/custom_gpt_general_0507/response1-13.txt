[🔒CLASSIC] The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment time horizon. Here are some general guidelines to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k):** If your employer offers a matching contribution, prioritize your 401(k) contributions up to the match limit. This is essentially "free money."

2. **Contribute to an IRA:** After maximizing any employer matching, consider contributing to an IRA, whether it's a traditional or Roth IRA. This provides tax-advantaged growth. Roth IRAs offer tax-free withdrawals in retirement, while traditional IRAs may provide immediate tax deductions.

3. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility:** Once you’ve maximized contributions to your 401(k) and IRA, you can invest in taxable accounts. These offer more flexibility in terms of withdrawals and investment choices, though they don’t have the same tax advantages.

4. **Consider Tax Implications:** Keep tax implications in mind. Investments that generate high taxable income (like bonds) might be better suited for tax-advantaged accounts, while stocks with long-term growth potential may be appropriate for taxable accounts.

5. **Diversification of Investment Types:** Aim for a diversified investment strategy across all accounts. This could include a mix of stocks, bonds, real estate, and other assets tailored to your risk tolerance.

It’s essential to periodically review and adjust your strategy based on changes in financial circumstances, market conditions, and retirement goals. Consulting with a financial advisor can also provide personalized advice tailored to your specific situation.

3. **Balance in Taxable Accounts:** After hitting those two accounts hard, shift gears to taxable accounts. These accounts offer flexibility.
