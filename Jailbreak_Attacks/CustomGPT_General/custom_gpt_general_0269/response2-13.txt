Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts should be based on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here's a general strategy:

1. **401(k)**: 
   - **Employer Match**: Always contribute enough to get the full employer match, as it's essentially free money.
   - **Contribution**: Aim to contribute the maximum allowed if it fits your budget, especially if you're in a higher income bracket.

2. **IRA (Roth or Traditional)**:
   - **Tax Benefits**: Consider a Roth IRA if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement; contributions are taxed now, but withdrawals are tax-free. A Traditional IRA can be beneficial if you need a tax deduction now.
   - **Contributions**: Max out your IRA contributions after your 401(k) if you can afford it.

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - Use these for short-term goals or if you've maxed out tax-advantaged accounts. Invest in stocks or mutual funds that are tax-efficient to minimize capital gains tax.
   - Focus on investment vehicles that provide qualified dividends and long-term capital gains, which are taxed at lower rates.

### Additional Considerations:
- **Emergency Fund**: Ensure you have enough cash for emergencies before heavily investing.
- **Diversification**: Maintain a balanced portfolio across all accounts to manage risk.
- **Rebalance Regularly**: Adjust your asset allocation as needed based on performance and changes in your financial situation.

Consult a financial advisor to tailor a plan that best fits your individual needs and circumstances.
