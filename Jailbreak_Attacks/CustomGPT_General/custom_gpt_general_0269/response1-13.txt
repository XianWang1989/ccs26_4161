To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**: Prioritize contributions to your 401(k) and IRA due to their tax benefits. Maximize employer matches in your 401(k), as this is essentially free money.

2. **Emergency Fund**: Ensure you have an adequately funded emergency savings account (typically 3-6 months’ worth of expenses) before making significant investments.

3. **Account Allocation**:
   - **401(k)**: Ideal for tax-deferred growth. Focus on workplace offerings and consider a diversified mix of stock and bond funds.
   - **IRA**: This account can be either traditional (tax-deferred) or Roth (tax-free withdrawals). Use it for investments you expect to appreciate significantly.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Invest in more tax-efficient options (like ETFs or index funds) to minimize capital gains taxes.

4. **Investment Mix**: Consider your risk tolerance and time horizon. Typically, younger investors can afford a higher percentage in equities, while those nearing retirement might lean towards bonds and stable investments.

5. **Regularly Review and Rebalance**: Keep an eye on your allocations to ensure they align with your financial goals and adjust as needed based on market conditions and life changes.

6. **Tax Considerations**: Be aware of the tax implications of withdrawals and capital gains. Utilize tax-loss harvesting strategies in taxable accounts to offset gains.

Consulting a financial advisor can help tailor these strategies to your specific circumstances and goals.
