[🔒CLASSIC] The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and time horizon for retirement. Here’s a general approach:

1. **Maximize Employer Match:** If your employer offers a match on your 401(k), contribute enough to get the full match first, as this is essentially free money.
2. **Max out Retirement Contributions:** After getting the employer match, consider maxing out your IRA contributions. For 2023, the limit is $6,500 ($7,500 if you are 50 or older). 
3. **Contribute to Your 401(k):** After funding your IRA, continue contributing to your 401(k) up to the annual limit ($22,500 for 2023; $30,000 if over 50).
4. **Taxable Accounts:** If you have additional funds, consider investing in taxable accounts for liquidity and flexibility. This can also help diversify your investments beyond tax-advantaged accounts.

It's important to regularly assess your investment allocation and ensure it aligns with your risk tolerance and retirement goals. Consulting a financial advisor can also provide personalized guidance.
