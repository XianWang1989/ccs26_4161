When it comes to distributing your money amongst your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, the "best" approach depends on your individual financial situation, retirement goals, and tax considerations. Here are some general strategies to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match:** If your 401(k) offers a company match, contribute enough to take full advantage of that match first. This is essentially free money!

2. **Contribute to Tax-Advantaged Accounts First:**
   - **401(k):** Contribute to your 401(k) up to the maximum allowed, especially if you benefit from tax deductions on contributions (traditional 401(k)).
   - **IRA:** If you're eligible, contribute to a Roth IRA or a traditional IRA. This allows for tax-free growth (Roth) or tax-deferred growth (traditional).

3. **Consider Tax Implications:**
   - **Taxable Accounts:** These accounts can be beneficial for short-term goals and provide more flexibility. However, any interest, dividends, or capital gains will be taxed in the year earned.
   - **Asset Location:** Consider keeping tax-inefficient investments (like bonds or certain mutual funds that generate heavy dividends) in tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) or IRA) and tax-efficient investments (like index funds) in taxable accounts.

4. **Diversification and Investment Strategy:** Ensure that your investment strategy is diversified across all accounts to reduce risk and align with your overall financial goals.

5. **Rebalancing:** Regularly review and rebalance your asset allocation across accounts to maintain your desired risk level and investment strategy.

6. **Long-Term vs Short-Term Goals:** If you have both long-term retirement goals and short-term needs, balance your investments by prioritizing long-term accounts for retirement savings, while utilizing taxable accounts for more immediate needs.

7. **Consult a Financial Advisor:** A financial planner can provide personalized advice that considers your specific financial situation, goals, and the current tax environment.

Overall, the key is to strategically distribute your money to make the most of your tax advantages while aligning with your financial goals.
