Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here's a general approach:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First:**
   - **401(k):** If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to get the full match—it’s essentially free money. Consider maxing out if your financial situation allows.
   - **IRA:** After your 401(k), consider contributing to a Traditional IRA or Roth IRA, depending on your tax strategy and eligibility. A Roth IRA is beneficial for tax-free withdrawals in retirement if you qualify.

2. **Evaluate Your Tax Situation:**
   - Prioritize tax-advantaged accounts to defer taxes and compound growth. If you think your tax rate will be higher in retirement, a Roth account can be more advantageous.
   - For taxable accounts, invest in tax-efficient vehicles like index funds or ETFs to minimize capital gains taxes.

3. **Assess Your Timeline and Goals:**
   - Short-term needs may be better served with taxable accounts for liquidity, while retirement savings (long-term) should prioritize 401(k) and IRA contributions.

4. **Diversification:** 
   - Ensure you have a diversified investment portfolio across all accounts to manage risk effectively.

5. **Review Regularly:**
   - Adjust contributions based on performance, changes in income, or life events.

Consulting a financial advisor could provide a personalized plan tailored to your individual circumstances.
