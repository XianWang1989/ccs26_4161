The distribution of your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts largely depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here’s a general approach to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match:** If your 401(k) offers an employer match, contribute enough to get the full match. This is essentially "free money" and should be prioritized.

2. **Tax-Advantaged Accounts First:** Contribute to a Traditional or Roth IRA, depending on your income level and tax situation. Roth IRAs are particularly beneficial if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement, as you pay taxes now, and withdrawals are tax-free.

3. **Continue with 401(k):** After maxing out the IRA, continue contributing to your 401(k), especially if you're close to the contribution limit. Consider potential tax benefits from pre-tax contributions in Traditional 401(k) plans versus the tax-free growth of Roth options.

4. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility:** Invest in taxable accounts for liquidity and flexibility needs. Gains here are subject to capital gains tax, but these accounts can provide additional flexibility in accessing funds without penalty.

5. **Maintain a Balanced Portfolio:** Regardless of the account type, ensure that your investment strategy aligns with your risk tolerance and investment goals. Diversification across asset classes is key.

6. **Consider Yearly Review:** Regularly assess your contributions and asset allocation, especially as your income changes or as you approach retirement.

It’s advisable to consult with a financial advisor to tailor a strategy based on your specific circumstances.
