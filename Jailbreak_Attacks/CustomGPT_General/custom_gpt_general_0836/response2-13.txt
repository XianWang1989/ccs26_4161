Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here’s a general approach:

### 1. **Tax Considerations:**
   - **401(k) and IRA:** These accounts offer tax advantages. Contributions may be tax-deductible, and investments grow tax-deferred. Consider maxing out contributions to these accounts first, especially if your employer offers matching in a 401(k).
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Investments here are subject to capital gains taxes. They can be used for short-term goals or if you anticipate needing funds before retirement.

### 2. **Investment Goals:**
   - **Long-term Growth:** Prioritize 401(k) and IRA contributions for retirement savings to benefit from tax-deferred growth.
   - **Short-term Needs:** Use taxable accounts for goals within the next 5-10 years. 

### 3. **Limitations and Rules:**
   - Contribute to a 401(k) up to the matching limit, then prioritize IRA contributions (traditional or Roth, depending on eligibility and your tax situation).
   - Consider maximum contribution limits for each account type.

### 4. **Risk Tolerance & Diversification:**
   - Diversify your investments among various asset classes (stocks, bonds, real estate) based on your risk tolerance. 

### 5. **Regular Reviews and Adjustments:**
   - Periodically reassess your distribution strategy, especially after significant life events (job change, marriage, etc.).

### Example Allocation Strategy:
- **401(k):** Max out employer match (e.g., 5-10% of salary).
- **IRA:** Contribute up to the maximum allowable amount ($6,500 for individuals under 50, $7,500 for those 50 and older in 2023).
- **Taxable Accounts:** Invest remaining funds based on your liquidity needs and financial goals.

This strategy can be tailored as your situation evolves, so consulting with a financial advisor may provide personalized recommendations.
