Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts can depend on various factors like your tax situation, investment goals, and retirement timeline. Here are some general strategies:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First:**
   - **401(k):** Contribute enough to get any employer match, as this is essentially “free money.”
   - **IRA:** Consider maxing out your IRA contributions next, especially if you can do a Roth IRA for tax-free withdrawals in retirement.

### 2. **Consider Tax Implications:**
   - **Taxable Accounts:** These are best for investments that generate lower taxes, like long-term capital gains and qualified dividends. Use this account for short-term savings or if you anticipate needing funds before retirement.

### 3. **Investment Strategy:**
   - **401(k)/IRA:** Focus on long-term growth strategies, such as stocks or index funds, since these accounts are designed for retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Utilize a mix of investments, including bonds and dividend-paying stocks, to manage tax liabilities.

### 4. **Emergency Fund:**
   - Keep a portion of your savings accessible for emergencies, usually outside of retirement accounts.

### 5. **Asset Location:**
   - Consider the types of assets you hold in each account. Place higher-growth investments in tax-advantaged accounts and income-generating assets in taxable accounts if possible.

### 6. **Review and Adjust:**
   - Regularly review your allocations as your financial situation changes, especially as you approach retirement.

### Example Allocation Strategy:
- **401(k):** 15% of your income up to the match
- **IRA:** Max out contributions
- **Taxable:** 10% for flexibility and short-term goals

Consult a financial advisor for personalized guidance, as individual circumstances can significantly influence the best strategy.
