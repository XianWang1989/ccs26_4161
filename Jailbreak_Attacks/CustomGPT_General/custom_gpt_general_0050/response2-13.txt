Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts can be strategic to maximize your tax efficiency and investment growth. Here are some key considerations:

### 1. **401(k) Accounts:**
   - **Employer Match:** Contribute enough to get the full employer match, if available. This is essentially "free money."
   - **Tax Advantages:** Contributions reduce your taxable income, which is advantageous if you expect to be in a lower tax bracket in retirement.
   - **Investment Options:** Evaluate the funds available in your 401(k); they may be limited and can have higher fees.

### 2. **Traditional or Roth IRA:**
   - **Retirement Savings:** If your income allows, consider maxing out contributions to an IRA.
   - **Roth vs. Traditional:** 
     - **Roth IRA:** Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, offering tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement. Ideal if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket later.
     - **Traditional IRA:** Contributions may be tax-deductible, providing immediate tax benefits. However, withdrawals are taxed as regular income.

### 3. **Taxable Accounts:**
   - **Liquidity:** These accounts provide flexibility for accessing funds without penalties.
   - **Investment Choices:** You have a wider range of investment options.
   - **Tax Considerations:** Be mindful of capital gains taxes on profits. Invest in tax-efficient funds or assets that produce qualified dividends to minimize tax impact.

### **General Allocation Strategy:**
- **Prioritize Matching Contributions:** Start with enough in your 401(k) for any matching contributions.
- **Maximize IRA Contributions:** Next, consider maxing out your IRA contributions. 
- **Flexible Savings in Taxable Accounts:** After you’ve funded your retirement accounts, allocate any additional funds to your taxable accounts, focusing on liquidity needs and capital gains management.

### **Investment Risk Assessment:**
- Allocate based on your risk tolerance and time horizon. Generally, longer-term and retirement-focused accounts can afford to be more aggressive in their investments (e.g., stocks), while taxable accounts might benefit from a conservative approach due to potential tax implications.

### **Annual Review:**
- Regularly assess and adjust your allocations based on changes in income, financial goals, and market conditions.

### **Consult a Financial Advisor:**
- For personalized strategies, consider consulting with a financial advisor who can help tailor your investment approach to your specific circumstances and goals. 

By balancing your investments across these accounts, you can achieve growth while managing taxes effectively.
