When distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, it's essential to consider several factors such as your financial goals, tax implications, and investment time horizon. Here’s a general approach to help guide your decision:

1. **Emergency Fund**: Before investing, ensure you have an emergency fund, typically covering three to six months of living expenses.

2. **Employer Match**: If your 401(k) offers a matching contribution, prioritize contributing enough to get the full match. This is essentially free money.

3. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: Contribute as much as you can, especially if there's a match. Consider traditional vs. Roth 401(k) based on your current and future tax rates.
   - **IRA**: If eligible, add to a traditional or Roth IRA. For 2023, the contribution limit is $6,500 ($7,500 if age 50 or older).
   - **Roth IRA**: It's beneficial if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement.

4. **Taxable Accounts**: After maximizing contributions to tax-advantaged accounts, consider taxable accounts for additional investments. These accounts allow for more flexibility in withdrawals and investments, but be mindful of capital gains taxes.

5. **Diversification**: Maintain a balanced portfolio across all accounts, including stocks, bonds, and cash. This will help mitigate risk.

6. **Investment Horizon**: Align your investments with your time horizon. For long-term goals, you might lean towards more aggressive investments (stocks), while for shorter-term goals, you could prioritize conservative investments (bonds or cash).

7. **Rebalancing**: Regularly review and rebalance your portfolio based on performance and changing life circumstances.

8. **Seek Professional Advice**: If you're unsure, consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized guidance.

By strategically distributing your investments across these accounts, you can optimize for growth while managing taxes effectively.
