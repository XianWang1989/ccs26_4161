To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider these strategies:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get any employer match first. This is essentially free money.

2. **Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**:
   - **401(k)**: If you have a high-income and tax rate, prioritize contributions to your 401(k) for tax deferral. Consider a traditional 401(k) for tax savings now or a Roth 401(k) for tax-free withdrawals later.
   - **IRA**: If eligible, contribute to a Roth IRA for tax-free growth. If you’re in a higher bracket, consider a traditional IRA for immediate tax benefits.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: After maximizing contributions to tax-advantaged accounts, allocate extra funds to taxable accounts. This can provide flexibility for withdrawals without penalties and greater access to capital.

4. **Investment Strategy**:
   - **401(k) and IRA**: Aim for long-term growth through diversified investments (stocks, bonds, ETFs). Adjust your asset allocation based on risk tolerance and age.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Focus on tax-efficient investments (index funds, ETFs) to minimize capital gains taxes.

5. **Rebalance As Needed**: Regularly review and adjust your allocations based on market performance and life changes to maintain your intended investment strategy.

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: Consider seeking personalized advice to account for your specific financial situation, goals, and risk tolerance.

Using this approach can help maximize your investment growth while managing tax implications efficiently.
