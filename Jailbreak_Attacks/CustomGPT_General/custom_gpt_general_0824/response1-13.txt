To effectively distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

### 1. Understand the Accounts:
- **401(k):** Tax-deferred; contributions lower taxable income. Employer matching may exist.
- **IRA:** Also tax-deferred or tax-free (Roth IRA). Contribution limits apply.
- **Taxable Accounts:** No tax advantages; capital gains taxes apply on profits.

### 2. Prioritize Contributions:
- **Maximize Employer Match:** Contribute enough to your 401(k) to receive the full employer match. It’s essentially free money.
- **IRA Contributions:** Next, consider fully contributing to an IRA, especially if you're eligible for a Roth IRA, which offers tax-free growth.
- **Taxable Account Contributions:** Use leftover funds for taxable accounts once retirement accounts are maxed out.

### 3. Assess Your Goals:
- **Short-Term Needs:** Keep funds in taxable accounts for easy access without penalties.
- **Long-Term Growth:** Prioritize tax-advantaged accounts for better growth potential over time.

### 4. Tax Considerations:
- **Tax-Bracket Awareness:** Be mindful of your current and anticipated future tax brackets, as it can influence whether to prioritize pre-tax (401(k), traditional IRA) vs. post-tax (Roth IRA) accounts.
- **Asset Location Strategy:** Consider holding high-growth investments in tax-advantaged accounts and lower-growth, income-generating investments (like bonds) in taxable accounts.

### 5. Monitor and Adjust:
- Regularly review your allocations based on performance, life changes, and updated financial goals. Rebalance as necessary to maintain your desired risk level.

### Example Allocation Strategy:
- **401(k):** Max out to get any employer match.
- **IRA:** Contribute up to the limit.
- **Taxable:** Allocate remaining funds while considering liquidity needs and the tax implications of investment choices.

Consult a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your specific situation.
