Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various factors including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some considerations for each type of account:

### 1. **401(k)**
   - **Tax Advantages**: Contributions are typically tax-deferred, meaning you won't pay taxes on the money until you withdraw it in retirement.
   - **Employer Match**: If your employer offers a match, aim to contribute enough to get the full match before allocating money to other accounts.
   - **Contribution Limits**: For 2023, the limit is $22,500 (or $30,000 if you're 50 or older).

### 2. **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**
   - **Tax Considerations**: Traditional IRAs also provide tax-deferred growth, while Roth IRAs offer tax-free withdrawals in retirement.
   - **Contribution Limits**: The limit for IRAs in 2023 is $6,500 (or $7,500 if you're 50 or older).
   - **Income Limits for Roth IRA**: Check your eligibility, as high earners may not be able to contribute directly to a Roth IRA.

### 3. **Taxable Accounts**
   - **Flexibility**: No restrictions on contributions or withdrawals, making them suitable for short- to medium-term investments.
   - **Tax Implications**: You’ll pay taxes on dividends, interest, and capital gains, so this account may not be the best choice for long-term growth compared to tax-advantaged accounts.

### **General Strategy**
- **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**: Start by maximizing contributions to your 401(k) to take advantage of any employer match, followed by maxing out your IRA.
- **Assess Time Horizon**: Determine how long you plan to keep the money invested. For long-term goals, prioritize retirement accounts.
- **Diversify Across Accounts**: Once you've maximized the tax-advantaged options, consider funding taxable accounts while ensuring a diversified investment strategy.
- **Adjust Based on Income**: High-income years may favor more contributions to tax-deferred accounts to lower taxable income, while lower-income years could favor Roth contributions.

### **Final Thoughts**
Consult with a financial advisor to tailor these strategies to your unique situation, considering factors like risk tolerance, retirement goals, and overall portfolio balance.
