I can't provide personalized financial advice, but I can outline some general principles that can help guide your decision-making regarding the distribution of funds among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts.

1. **401(k) Accounts**: 
   - **Employer Match**: If your employer offers a match on contributions, it’s typically wise to contribute enough to take full advantage of that match, as it's essentially free money.
   - **Tax Benefits**: Contributions to a traditional 401(k) reduce your taxable income, which can be beneficial if you expect to be in a lower tax bracket in retirement.

2. **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**:
   - **Tax Considerations**: A traditional IRA allows for tax-deductible contributions, while a Roth IRA provides tax-free withdrawals in retirement. Your decision may depend on your current and expected future tax rates.
   - **Contribution Limits**: Be aware of the annual contribution limits and the income thresholds that may restrict eligibility for Roth IRA contributions.

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - **Flexibility**: These accounts offer flexibility without the contribution limits and withdrawal restrictions associated with retirement accounts.
   - **Tax Efficiency**: Consider holding investments that generate favorable tax treatment, such as long-term capital gains or qualified dividends, in taxable accounts.

### General Strategy:
1. **Maximize Employer Contributions**: Prioritize funding your 401(k) up to the employer match.
2. **Contribute to an IRA**: Depending on your tax situation, consider funding a traditional IRA or a Roth IRA.
3. **Fund Taxable Accounts**: After maxing out retirement accounts (if possible), you can then use taxable accounts for additional investments, especially for short-term goals or if you anticipate needing liquidity.

### Diversification:
Regardless of the account type, ensure a diversified investment approach that aligns with your overall risk tolerance and financial goals. Consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your specific situation.

Always keep in mind to review the tax implications, investment options, and your financial goals when managing your investments across different types of accounts.
