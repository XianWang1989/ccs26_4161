Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here’s a general strategy:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match**
   - **401(k)**: Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to get any employer match. This is typically free money and a great start to your retirement savings.

### 2. **Focus on Tax Advantages**
   - **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**: If you qualify, contribute to an IRA. A Roth IRA offers tax-free growth and withdrawals, while a Traditional IRA provides tax deductions on contributions.
   - Consider maxing out your IRA if your 401(k) is fully funded and you have the budget to do so. 

### 3. **Assess Contribution Limits**
   - Clearly understand contribution limits for both 401(k)s and IRAs. As of 2023, the limits are:
     - **401(k)**: $22,500 (or $30,000 if you're 50 or older).
     - **IRA**: $6,500 (or $7,500 if you're 50 or older).

### 4. **Utilize Taxable Accounts Wisely**
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for investments that you might need to access before retirement or for short-term goals. 
   - They can also be beneficial for tax-efficient investments, such as index funds or ETFs, which typically generate less taxable income.

### 5. **Diversification and Risk Tolerance**
   - Balance your portfolio among these accounts based on your risk tolerance. Higher-risk investments might be better suited for tax-advantaged accounts where gains can grow tax-deferred or tax-free.

### 6. **Monitor and Rebalance**
   - Regularly review your asset allocation and rebalance as necessary to keep your investment strategy aligned with your goals.

### 7. **Consult a Financial Advisor**
   - If unsure, consulting a financial advisor can provide personalized advice based on your unique circumstances.

### Conclusion:
Prioritize contributions based on employer matches and tax benefits, and use taxable accounts for flexibility and access to funds. Each account type plays a crucial role in an overall investment strategy.
