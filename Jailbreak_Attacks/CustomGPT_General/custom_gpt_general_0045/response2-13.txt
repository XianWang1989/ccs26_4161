Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here’s a general approach to help you decide:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match on 401(k)**
   - **Strategy**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to take full advantage of any employer match, as this is essentially free money.
   - **Tip**: Start with at least the percentage that gives you the maximum match.

### 2. **Fund Roth or Traditional IRA**
   - **Strategy**: After maximizing your 401(k) match, consider contributing to an IRA. Choose between a Roth or Traditional IRA based on your tax situation:
     - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are made with after-tax dollars and grow tax-free. Good if you expect your tax rate to be higher in retirement.
     - **Traditional IRA**: Contributions may be tax-deductible, lowering your taxable income now. Good if you expect your tax rate to be lower in retirement.
   - **Contribution Limits**: Ensure you stay within the limits (e.g., $6,500 per year for individuals under 50 in 2023).

### 3. **Maximize 401(k) Contributions**
   - **Strategy**: If you still have money to invest after funding your IRA, increase your contributions to your 401(k) up to the annual limit (e.g., $22,500 in 2023, plus catch-up contributions if you're over 50).
   - **Benefit**: This also provides tax-deferred growth.

### 4. **Invest in Taxable Accounts**
   - **Strategy**: Use taxable accounts for additional investments once you have maxed out tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) and IRA).
   - **Considerations**: These accounts offer more flexibility, but they do have tax implications for dividends and capital gains.

### 5. **Consider Asset Allocation and Investment Types**
   - **Tax-Advantaged Accounts**: Focus on growth investments (stocks, mutual funds) due to tax deferment.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Consider tax-efficient investments (index funds, ETFs) to minimize tax liabilities.

### 6. **Regularly Review and Adjust**
   - **Strategy**: Periodically review your portfolio to ensure alignment with your goals and risk tolerance. Adjust contributions and asset allocation as needed.

### Final Thoughts
Balancing these accounts effectively allows for optimized tax advantages and flexibility in retirement. Consulting with a financial advisor can also tailor a strategy to your unique situation.
