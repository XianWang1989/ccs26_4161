Deciding how to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment horizon. Here’s a general approach:

### 1. **Understand Account Types:**
   - **401(k):** Often has employer matching, which is free money and should be prioritized until you receive the full match. Contributions reduce your taxable income.
   - **IRA (Individual Retirement Account):** Traditional IRAs provide tax deductions upfront, while Roth IRAs allow for tax-free withdrawals in retirement. Roth IRAs are generally preferable if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Investments here are subject to capital gains tax. These accounts provide flexibility for withdrawals without penalties, making them suitable for short- to mid-term goals.

### 2. **Prioritize Retirement Accounts:**
   - **Max out Employer Match:** Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match.
   - **Consider IRAs:** If eligible, contribute to a Roth IRA or traditional IRA up to the annual limit.
   - **Continue with 401(k):** If you can, increase your 401(k) contributions up to the annual limit after maxing out the IRA.

### 3. **Assess Tax Implications:**
   - **Utilize Tax-Advantaged Accounts:** Focus on tax-deferred growth in 401(k) and traditional IRA, and consider Roth IRAs for tax-free growth.
   - **Tax-Loss Harvesting:** In taxable accounts, consider strategies to offset gains with losses to minimize taxes.

### 4. **Diversify Investments:**
   - Maintain a mix of assets (stocks, bonds, ETFs) across all accounts tailored to your risk tolerance and time frame.

### 5. **Rebalance Periodically:**
   - Regularly review and adjust your asset allocation in all accounts based on performance and changing goals.

### Example Allocation Strategy:
- **401(k):** Max out contributions, especially to take full advantage of employer match.
- **IRA:** Fund up to the limit after 401(k) contributions.
- **Taxable Accounts:** Use for investments that may need to be accessed before retirement or for diversification, maintaining a slightly more conservative allocation.

### Additional Considerations:
- **Emergency Fund:** Ensure you have liquid savings as an emergency fund outside of these accounts.
- **Financial Goals:** Assess goals such as home purchase, travel, or education when deciding how much to allocate to taxable accounts.

Consulting a financial advisor can provide personalized insights based on your specific financial situation.
