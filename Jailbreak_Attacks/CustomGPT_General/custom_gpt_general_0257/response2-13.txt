Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some strategies to consider:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**: 
   - **401(k)**: If your employer offers a match, contribute at least enough to get the full match—this is essentially free money.
   - **IRA**: Consider contributing to a Traditional IRA for tax deductions or a Roth IRA for tax-free withdrawals in retirement, depending on your income eligibility.

2. **Determine Contributions Based on Tax Bracket**:
   - If you expect to be in a lower tax bracket in retirement, a Traditional IRA or 401(k) may be beneficial.
   - If you expect to be in a higher bracket, a Roth IRA might be more advantageous.

3. **Prioritize High-Interest Debt**: Before allocating significant funds to investments, consider paying off high-interest loans. This can effectively provide guaranteed returns equal to the interest rate you’re avoiding.

4. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**: 
   - Use taxable accounts for investments you might need access to before retirement. They also allow for tax-loss harvesting to offset gains.

5. **Asset Allocation Strategy**: 
   - Diversifying your assets based on your risk tolerance and time horizon is critical. Consider how much you want in equities, bonds, and other asset classes in each account.

6. **Revisit Annually**: Make a point to assess your financial situation and adjust contributions based on changing income, goals, and market conditions.

7. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: For personalized advice tailored to your unique financial situation, consider speaking with a financial planner.

By balancing contributions among these accounts strategically, you can optimize your savings for both growth and tax efficiency.
