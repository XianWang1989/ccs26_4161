To effectively distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider these steps:

1. **Maximize Employer Match:** Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match, as this is essentially free money.

2. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts:**
   - **401(k):** Contribute up to the limit if you have a match, or consider maxing it out if you're focused on retirement savings.
   - **IRA:** If eligible, contribute to a traditional or Roth IRA. Roth IRAs offer tax-free withdrawals in retirement, while traditional IRAs may provide tax deductions now.

3. **Emergency Fund:** Ensure you have an adequate emergency fund (3-6 months of expenses) in a liquid, easily accessible account.

4. **Taxable Accounts:** Use these for goals not tied to retirement. They provide flexibility and can be taxed at the capital gains rate, which is often lower than ordinary income tax rates.

5. **Diversification:** Within each account, diversify your investments across asset classes. Consider your risk tolerance and investment horizon when making choices.

6. **Rebalance Regularly:** Periodically review and adjust your allocations based on performance and changing objectives.

7. **Consider Fees and Taxes:** Be mindful of investment fees in your accounts and the tax implications of selling investments in taxable accounts.

It’s wise to consult a financial advisor for personalized advice based on your situation.
