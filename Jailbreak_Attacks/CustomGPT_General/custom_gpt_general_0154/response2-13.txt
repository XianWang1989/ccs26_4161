To effectively distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategy:

1. **401(k)**:
   - Max out any employer match first. This is essentially free money.
   - Focus on tax-deferred growth; choose low-cost index funds or target-date funds that align with your retirement timeline.

2. **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**:
   - If you qualify, consider a Roth IRA for tax-free withdrawals in retirement. If you expect to be in a higher tax bracket later, this is beneficial.
   - For Traditional IRAs, contributions may be tax-deductible, which can lower your taxable income now.

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - Invest in tax-efficient funds (such as index funds or tax-managed funds) to minimize capital gains taxes.
   - Consider holding investments long-term to benefit from lower capital gains rates.

### General Guidelines:
- **Emergency Fund**: Maintain 3-6 months of expenses in a liquid savings account.
- **Debt Consideration**: Pay off high-interest debt before investing aggressively.
- **Asset Allocation**: Tailor your investment mix (stocks/bonds) based on risk tolerance and time horizon.
- **Regular Contributions**: Consistently contribute to each account, adjusting for changes in income and financial goals.

### Rebalance Annually:
Keep track of your investments and rebalance your portfolio annually to maintain your desired asset allocation.

### Consult a Financial Advisor:
For personalized advice, consider consulting a financial advisor, especially for tax strategies and investment choices.
