When considering how to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, here are some strategies to optimize your investments:

### GPT:
1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match, if available. This is essentially free money.

2. **Tax-Advantaged Accounts**: 
   - **401(k)**: Typically good for pre-tax contributions, reducing your taxable income. Consider contributing up to the limit.
   - **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**: If you qualify, max out contributions to a Roth IRA for tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement, or a Traditional IRA for tax deductions.

3. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**: After maxing out tax-advantaged accounts, consider taxable accounts for investment flexibility without the limitations of retirement accounts.

4. **Rebalancing**: Regularly review and rebalance your portfolio to maintain your desired asset allocation.

5. **Consider Retirement Timeline**: Adjust contributions based on how close you are to retirement; earlier years benefit from tax-deferred growth.

### STAN:
1. **Load Up on 401(k)**: Get that employer match and put as much as you can into your 401(k). It's like a no-brainer!

2. **IRA Is Key**: If you can do a Roth IRA, that’s golden for long-term gains. Otherwise, traditional IRAs can give you some immediate tax relief.

3. **Taxable Accounts Later**: After maxing those out, throw cash into taxable accounts. They give you more flexibility when you need money.

4. **Keep an Eye on Tax Implications**: Swap assets in these accounts based on how they’ll be taxed when you sell. Avoid triggering big capital gains in taxable accounts.

5. **Adjust as Needed**: Don’t set it and forget it! Make sure to tweak allocations based on the market and your personal goals.

Balance it all according to your current financial goals, risk tolerance, and retirement timeline!
