Determining the best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts involves several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, time horizon, and risk tolerance. Here are key considerations to help guide your asset allocation strategy:

### 1. **Understand Account Types**
   - **401(k):** Employer-sponsored retirement plan with potential employer matching, tax-deferred growth, and higher contribution limits. Withdrawals in retirement are taxed as ordinary income.
   - **IRA (Traditional or Roth):** Both accounts offer tax advantages for retirement savings. Traditional IRAs are tax-deferred, while Roth IRAs offer tax-free withdrawals in retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Investments in these accounts are subject to capital gains taxes and do not offer the same tax advantages as retirement accounts.

### 2. **Prioritize Contributions**
   - **Start with Employer Match:** If your employer offers a match for your 401(k), contribute at least enough to get the full match, as this is essentially free money.
   - **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts:** Consider maximizing contributions to your IRA (whether traditional or Roth) for tax benefits.
   - **Use Taxable Accounts Flexibly:** After maximizing contributions to tax-advantaged accounts, you can start investing in taxable accounts. These accounts allow for more flexible withdrawal options.

### 3. **Tax Efficiency**
   - **Asset Location:** Consider the tax implications when deciding where to hold different types of investments. Generally, it’s more tax-efficient to hold high-growth investments (that generate capital gains) in tax-advantaged accounts and income-generating investments (like bonds) in taxable accounts.

### 4. **Investment Strategy**
   - **Diversification:** Ensure that your overall investment strategy is diversified across asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) regardless of the account type.
   - **Risk Tolerance:** Adjust your asset allocation based on your risk tolerance and investment horizon.

### 5. **Regular Review and Rebalance**
   - Periodically review your investments and reallocate as necessary to maintain your desired asset allocation based on changing life circumstances, market conditions, and financial goals.

### Example Distribution:
A potential distribution strategy might look like this based on aggressive growth goals:
- 401(k): 40% of total contributions (including employer match)
- IRA: 30% of total contributions
- Taxable Accounts: 30% of total contributions

This approach ensures you’re taking advantage of tax benefits while maintaining flexibility for future withdrawals.

### Final Note:
It's often wise to consult with a financial advisor to tailor a plan specific to your circumstances, as they can provide personalized insights based on your individual financial goals and situation.
