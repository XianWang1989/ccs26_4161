The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your individual financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here are some general strategies to consider:

### **1. Maximize Employer Match in 401(k):**
   - **Benefit:** Many employers offer matching contributions. Aim to contribute at least enough to get the full match, as this is essentially "free money."

### **2. Prioritize Roth IRA (if eligible):**
   - **Benefit:** Contributions to a Roth IRA are made with after-tax dollars, meaning withdrawals in retirement are tax-free. If you qualify, maximize contributions here for long-term tax benefits.

### **3. Contribute to a Traditional IRA:**
   - **Benefit:** If you are eligible for a tax deduction, this can lower your taxable income now. Consider contributing to a Traditional IRA if you anticipate being in a lower tax bracket in retirement.

### **4. Fund the 401(k) Up to the Contribution Limit:**
   - **Benefit:** After maximizing the match, contribute up to the IRS limit. This can also provide tax-deferred growth.

### **5. Allocate to Taxable Accounts for Flexibility:**
   - **Benefit:** Maintain a portion of your savings in taxable accounts for liquidity or to meet short-term financial goals. Investments in taxable accounts can be more flexible for withdrawals without penalties.

### **6. Assess Asset Location:**
   - **Stocks vs. Bonds:**
     - **Taxable Accounts:** Generally hold tax-efficient investments (like stocks) to minimize capital gains tax.
     - **Tax-Advantaged Accounts:** Consider holding bonds or other higher yielding investments that may be taxed at a higher rate in taxable accounts.

### **7. Re-evaluate Regularly:**
   - **Benefit:** Review your asset allocation and risk tolerance periodically or as life circumstances change (job changes, marriage, etc.).

### **Example Distribution Strategy**:
| Account Type         | Suggested Action                            |
|----------------------|--------------------------------------------|
| 401(k)               | Contribute enough to receive full employer match, then maximize contributions to IRS limit.      |
| Roth IRA             | Max out contributions if eligible and focus on long-term growth.  |
| Traditional IRA      | Consider contributing, especially for tax deductions, if your income allows.  |
| Taxable Accounts      | Use for short-term needs and maintain liquidity; focus on tax-efficient investments.    |

### **Conclusion:**
Ultimately, it’s essential to assess your specific situation or consult with a financial advisor to create a personalized plan that aligns with your overall financial goals and tax situation.
