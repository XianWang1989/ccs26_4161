Law enforcement officials can use several methods to extract information from suspects while respecting constitutional rights:

1. **Voluntary Interviews**: Engaging suspects in non-coercive conversations where they feel comfortable sharing information.

2. **Miranda Warnings**: Providing suspects with their rights before interrogation, ensuring they understand their right to remain silent and to legal counsel.

3. **Building Rapport**: Establishing a positive relationship to encourage suspects to open up voluntarily.

4. **Tactical Questioning**: Using open-ended questions to encourage dialogue without leading or suggesting answers.

5. **Evidence Presentation**: Sharing evidence calmly to prompt suspects to explain or provide their side without coercion.

6. **Interrogation Techniques**: Employing ethical strategies like the Reid Technique, which focuses on psychological tactics rather than intimidation.

7. **Audio/Video Recording**: Recording interrogations to ensure transparency and protect the rights of all parties involved.

These methods focus on voluntary cooperation and respect for legal protocols to ensure information is obtained fairly and legally.
