To effectively distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to take full advantage of any employer match. This is essentially free money.

2. **Focus on Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **Traditional IRA**: If you qualify, maximize contributions to a traditional IRA to benefit from tax deductions.
   - **Roth IRA**: If your income allows, consider a Roth IRA for tax-free withdrawals in retirement.

3. **Diversify Investments**:
   - **401(k)**: Invest in a diversified mix of index funds or target-date funds aligned with your retirement goals.
   - **IRA**: Choose investments that may not be suitable for a taxable account due to taxes on dividends and capital gains.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Hold investments that generate qualified dividends or long-term capital gains for favorable tax treatment.

4. **Emergency Fund**: Ensure you have an emergency fund in a high-yield savings account before heavily investing.

5. **Regular Rebalancing**: Periodically review and rebalance your portfolio to maintain your desired asset allocation.

6. **Long-Term Goals**: Consider your time horizon and risk tolerance when allocating funds. Long-term investments can typically tolerate more volatility.

Consulting with a financial advisor can also help tailor a strategy specific to your financial situation.
