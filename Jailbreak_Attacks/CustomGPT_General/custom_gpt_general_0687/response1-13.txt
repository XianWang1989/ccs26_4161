Distributing your money wisely among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts involves considering several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and time horizon. Here are some strategies to guide you:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First:**
   - **401(k):** Contribute enough to get any employer match. This is essentially free money.
   - **IRA:** If eligible, consider contributing to a Roth IRA for tax-free growth, or a traditional IRA for potential tax deductions.

### 2. **Evaluate Contribution Limits:**
   - Be aware of annual contribution limits for 401(k) and IRA accounts and adjust your savings accordingly.

### 3. **Tax Considerations:**
   - **Tax Deferral:** Funds in 401(k) and traditional IRA grow tax-deferred. This can be advantageous if you expect to be in a lower tax bracket during retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** These may have capital gain taxes. Consider holding investments longer to benefit from lower long-term capital gains rates.

### 4. **Investment Strategy:**
   - Use tax-advantaged accounts for higher-growth investments (stocks) due to tax implications in taxable accounts.
   - Reserve more stable investments (bonds) for taxable accounts, where interest income is taxed at ordinary rates.

### 5. **Emergency Fund:**
   - Maintain an emergency fund outside of retirement accounts to cover unexpected expenses without penalizing retirement savings.

### 6. **Rebalance Regularly:**
   - Periodically reassess your allocations to ensure they align with your risk tolerance and financial goals.

### 7. **Consult a Financial Advisor:**
   - Consider working with a financial advisor to tailor a strategy based on your specific situation.

By following these steps, you can create a balanced approach to managing your investments across your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts.
