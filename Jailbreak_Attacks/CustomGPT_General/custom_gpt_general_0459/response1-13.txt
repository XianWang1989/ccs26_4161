Distributing your money across a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some general guidelines to consider:

1. **Prioritize Tax Advantages**:
   - **401(k)**: If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to get the full match first. This is effectively free money.
   - **IRA**: Consider contributing to a Roth IRA if you're eligible, as withdrawals in retirement are tax-free. If you expect your tax rate to be higher in retirement, a Roth could be advantageous. Otherwise, a traditional IRA is good for tax deferral.
  
2. **Maximize Contributions**:
   - Contribute the maximum allowable to your 401(k) and IRA each year. In 2023, the maximum for a 401(k) is $22,500 (or $30,000 if over 50) and for IRAs it's $6,500 (or $7,500 if over 50).

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - Use taxable accounts for investments that may generate significant capital gains or income, such as stocks, because long-term capital gains are taxed at a lower rate. Consider tax-efficient funds and investments that generate qualified dividends.

4. **Assess Your Risk Tolerance**:
   - Diversify your investments within each account based on your risk tolerance and investment horizon. Younger investors might focus on growth-oriented assets (like stocks), while those nearing retirement may prefer more conservative investments.

5. **Rebalance Regularly**:
   - Ensure that your asset allocation aligns with your risk tolerance and financial goals by periodically reviewing and rebalancing your accounts.

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**:
   - A professional can help tailor a strategy specific to your situation, taking into account your overall financial plan.

By following these guidelines, you can create a strategy that maximizes your retirement savings while considering your individual financial circumstances.
