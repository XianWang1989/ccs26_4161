Distributing your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here’s a general approach:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get any employer match. This is essentially free money.

2. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**: After reaching the match, focus on fully funding your IRA (Traditional or Roth, depending on your tax situation) before adding more to your 401(k).

3. **Consider Tax Implications**:
   - **401(k)**: Pre-tax contributions lower your taxable income now, but withdrawals in retirement will be taxed.
   - **IRA**: Roth IRAs offer tax-free growth and withdrawals, while Traditional IRAs are tax-deferred.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Useful for short-term needs or specific goals; you'll pay capital gains tax on earnings, which can be avoided with tax-efficient investments.

4. **Diversify Investments**: Within each account, aim for a diversified portfolio that reflects your risk tolerance and investment horizon.

5. **Evaluate Your Tax Bracket**: If you're in a lower tax bracket now, consider a Roth IRA for tax-free withdrawals later. If you anticipate being in a higher tax bracket in retirement, focus on pre-tax options.

6. **Regular Review and Adjustments**: Periodically review your contributions and investment performance to ensure they align with your financial goals.

This strategy may vary based on individual circumstances, so it might be valuable to consult with a financial advisor for personalized advice.
