To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Maximize Contributions**: Start by maxing out your contributions to your 401(k) if your employer offers a match. This is essentially free money.

2. **Utilize IRAs**: Next, contribute to an IRA (Traditional or Roth) depending on your income level and tax situation. Roth IRAs are especially beneficial if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: Once you've maximized contributions to your retirement accounts, you can invest in taxable accounts. These can offer flexibility for both short-term and long-term goals.

4. **Tax-Efficient Investments**: In your taxable accounts, consider tax-efficient investments like index funds or ETFs. This helps minimize capital gains taxes.

5. **Asset Allocation**: Ensure your investment mix aligns with your risk tolerance and time horizon. Stocks typically go in the taxable accounts for long-term growth, while bonds might be better suited in tax-advantaged accounts.

6. **Regular Review**: Regularly review and rebalance your portfolio to ensure it aligns with your financial goals and market conditions.

Lastly, consulting with a financial advisor can provide personalized advice based on your unique situation.
