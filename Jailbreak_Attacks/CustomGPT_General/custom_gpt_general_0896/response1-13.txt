The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors including your retirement goals, tax situation, and risk tolerance. Here are some general guidelines:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a matching contribution for your 401(k), prioritize contributing enough to take full advantage of this. It’s essentially free money.

2. **Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**: Consider maxing out your contributions to tax-advantaged accounts like your 401(k) and IRA before funding a taxable account. These accounts provide tax benefits that can significantly enhance your retirement savings.

3. **Consider the Tax Treatment**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are pre-tax, reducing your taxable income now, but withdrawals in retirement will be taxed as ordinary income.
   - **IRA**: Traditional IRAs have similar tax benefits to 401(k)s, while Roth IRAs allow for tax-free withdrawals in retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: You'll pay taxes on interest, dividends, and capital gains in the year they occur, but you have greater flexibility with funds in these accounts.

4. **Diversity in Investments**: Depending on your risk tolerance, ensure that your investments are diverse across all accounts to balance risk and return.

5. **Rebalance Regularly**: Review your allocations regularly to adjust for market changes and ensure you are aligned with your financial goals.

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: Consider talking to a financial planner for advice tailored to your unique financial situation.

By keeping these strategies in mind, you can create a more effective distribution plan that aligns with your long-term financial goals.
