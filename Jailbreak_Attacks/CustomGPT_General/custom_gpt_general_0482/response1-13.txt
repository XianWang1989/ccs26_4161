Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, time horizon, and tax situation. Here’s a general approach:

1. **401(k)**: Max out any employer match first, as this is free money. After that, consider contributing enough to minimize tax burdens if you’re in a higher tax bracket.

2. **IRA (Roth or Traditional)**: Depending on your tax situation, a Roth IRA can be beneficial for tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement. A Traditional IRA might offer immediate tax deductions. Aim to contribute the maximum allowed annually.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for extra savings or investments beyond tax-advantaged accounts. Focus on tax-efficient investments, like index funds or ETFs, to minimize capital gains taxes.

### Suggested Allocation Strategy:
- **Immediate Needs**: Ensure you have an emergency fund in a highly liquid account.
- **Retirement Savings**: Prioritize your 401(k) and IRA contributions, aiming to max out as much as possible.
- **Long-Term Growth**: Allocate excess funds to taxable accounts for flexibility and potential higher returns.

### Rebalancing:
Periodically review your allocations based on performance, changes in your financial situation, and tax considerations.

Always consult with a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your specific circumstances.
