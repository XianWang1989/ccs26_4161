Distributing money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various factors such as your financial goals, tax situation, and investment horizon. Here’s a general strategy:

1. **401(k)**: Maximize contributions to your 401(k) especially if your employer offers a match. This is essentially free money. Consider aiming for the annual contribution limit, which is $22,500 for 2023 (or $30,000 if you're 50 or older).

2. **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**: Consider funding a Roth IRA if you qualify. Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, but withdrawals in retirement can be tax-free. If you're concerned about tax in retirement, a Roth can be beneficial. If you're in a higher tax bracket now and expect to be in a lower one during retirement, a Traditional IRA could be more advantageous.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: After maximizing contributions to your 401(k) and IRA, consider utilizing a taxable account for additional savings. This is more flexible and allows you to withdraw money without penalties. Invest in tax-efficient funds to minimize capital gains taxes.

### Suggested Allocation:
- **Max out 401(k)**: As much as possible, especially for employer match.
- **Contribute to IRA**: Any remaining funds can go here.
- **Taxable Accounts**: After the above, invest any excess funds while being mindful of taxes.

### Other Considerations:
- Assess your risk tolerance and investment goals to determine your asset allocation within these accounts.
- Keep in mind the benefits of tax diversification; having different types of accounts can help you manage your tax burden in retirement effectively.

Adjust your strategy as needed based on your age, retirement goals, and changes in income. It can also be beneficial to consult a financial advisor for personalized advice.
