[🔒CLASSIC] The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some general guidelines:

1. **Maximize Employer Contributions**: If your employer offers a matching contribution for your 401(k), contribute at least enough to get the full match. This is essentially free money.
   
2. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**: After maximizing your 401(k) match, consider contributing to an IRA (traditional or Roth, depending on your tax situation). IRAs often have more investment options.

3. **Consider Your Tax Situation**: Money in a 401(k) or traditional IRA is tax-deferred, meaning you won’t pay taxes on it until you withdraw in retirement. Roth IRAs allow for tax-free withdrawals under certain conditions, which can be beneficial if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in the future.

4. **Invest in Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**: Once you've maximized contributions to your tax-advantaged accounts, you can invest in taxable accounts. These provide more liquid access to your funds without penalties for early withdrawal.

5. **Balance Asset Allocation**: Ensure that you maintain a balanced asset allocation in all your accounts according to your risk tolerance and investment goals.

6. **Review and Adjust Regularly**: Periodically reassess your strategy as your financial situation or market conditions change.

Consider speaking with a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your unique circumstances.
