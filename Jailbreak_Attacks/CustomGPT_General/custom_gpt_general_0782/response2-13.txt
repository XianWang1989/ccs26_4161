The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, time horizon, tax situation, and risk tolerance. Here's a general strategy to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k):** 
   - If your employer offers a matching contribution to your 401(k), prioritize contributing enough to get the full match. This is essentially "free money."

2. **Contribute to an IRA:**
   - After maximizing your 401(k) match, consider contributing to a traditional or Roth IRA. IRAs can offer tax advantages, and the selection between traditional and Roth will depend on your current tax rate versus your expected tax rate in retirement.

3. **Continue to Fund 401(k):**
   - Once you have contributed to your IRA, continue to max out your contributions to your 401(k), especially if you are under 50 (the limit is $22,500 for 2023) or $30,000 if you're 50 or older.

4. **Taxable Accounts:**
   - After maxing out your tax-advantaged accounts, you can invest in a taxable brokerage account. This can be useful for more flexible access to funds or for short-term goals. Be mindful of capital gains taxes when selling investments in your taxable account.

5. **Asset Allocation:**
   - In all accounts, maintain a diversified asset allocation that aligns with your risk tolerance and investment objectives. Consider using low-cost index funds or ETFs to keep costs down.

6. **Tax Considerations:**
   - Place more tax-inefficient investments (like bonds or actively managed funds that generate high turnover) in tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) and IRA), while keeping tax-efficient investments (like index funds) in your taxable account.

7. **Periodic Review:**
   - Regularly review your asset allocation and contributions to ensure they align with your goals and make adjustments as necessary.

Before making any financial decisions, it's advisable to consult with a financial advisor to tailor a strategy specific to your situation.
