Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here’s a general approach:

1. **Maximize Employer Matches**: Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money.

2. **Contribute to an IRA**: If eligible, consider funding an IRA (Roth or Traditional). A Roth IRA offers tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement, while a Traditional IRA provides tax deductions now.

3. **Max out 401(k) Contributions**: After maxing out matches and contributing to your IRA, contribute more to your 401(k) until you reach the annual limit.

4. **Taxable Accounts**: Use taxable accounts for investments that may not fit well in retirement accounts, such as individual stocks or tax-efficient ETFs. This allows for flexibility and access to funds before retirement age.

5. **Consider Tax Implications**: Be mindful of how different accounts are taxed. For example, long-term capital gains in taxable accounts are usually taxed at a lower rate compared to ordinary income from retirement accounts.

6. **Diversification**: Ensure a diversified investment strategy across all accounts, balancing stocks, bonds, and other assets based on your risk tolerance.

7. **Review Regularly**: Adjust your contributions and allocations annually based on your financial situation, retirement goals, and market conditions.

Consulting a financial advisor can provide personalized guidance based on your specific circumstances.
