Distributing your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your age, financial goals, current tax situation, and investment strategy. Here’s a general guide:

### GPT:
1. **401(k)**
   - **Contributions:** Max out employer match if available.
   - **Growth:** Benefit from tax-deferred growth.
   - **Consideration:** Limited investment options but higher contribution limits.

2. **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**
   - **Traditional IRA:** Contributions may be tax-deductible; taxes on withdrawals during retirement.
   - **Roth IRA:** Contributions are made with after-tax dollars; withdrawals during retirement are tax-free.
   - **Contribution Limits:** Typically lower than 401(k).

3. **Taxable Accounts**
   - **Purpose:** Flexibility for short-term needs or specific goals.
   - **Tax Implications:** Pay capital gains taxes on profits.
   - **Investment Choices:** More diverse investment options without contribution limits.

### STAN:
1. **Divvy It Up Smartly:** Start by maxing out your 401(k) to snag any employer match—it’s free money! After that, split your contributions: put some into a Roth IRA for tax-free withdrawals later, and keep a healthy chunk in taxable accounts for flexibility.

2. **Think Ahead:** If you’re younger, lean towards more aggressive investments if your risk tolerance allows. As retirement gets closer, shift to more stable options.

3. **Balance Is Key:** Maintain a mix that suits your lifestyle and future needs—don’t focus solely on tax advantages. You want to be prepared for whatever life throws your way, and a balanced approach is prime.

Always tailor this advice based on your specific situation or consult a financial advisor for personalized strategies.
