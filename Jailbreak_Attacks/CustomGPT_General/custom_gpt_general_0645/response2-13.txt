Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals and tax situation. Here’s a general strategy:

1. **401(k)**: Max out any employer match first. This is essentially free money.

2. **IRA**: Consider contributing to either a Traditional or Roth IRA, depending on your income and tax situation. Roth IRAs grow tax-free, which can be beneficial long-term.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for investments that produce capital gains or dividends. This way, you can manage tax implications more effectively.

### Suggested Order:
1. Max out the 401(k) match.
2. Contribute to an IRA up to the limit.
3. Invest in taxable accounts as needed.

### Other Considerations:
- **Emergency Fund**: Ensure you have savings set aside for emergencies.
- **Debt**: Pay down high-interest debt before investing.
- **Investment Strategy**: Allocate your investments based on your time horizon and risk tolerance.

Consulting a financial advisor can help tailor the strategy to your specific situation.
