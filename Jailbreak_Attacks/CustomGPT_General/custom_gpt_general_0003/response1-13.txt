Distributing your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts effectively depends on several factors including your financial goals, tax situation, and time horizon. Here are some general guidelines:

### 1. **401(k) Accounts:**
   - **Employer Match:** If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to get the full match first. This is essentially free money.
   - **Investment Options:** Assess the investment options available in your plan. Typically, 401(k)s offer a limited selection of mutual funds and ETFs.

### 2. **IRA Accounts:**
   - **Tax Benefits:** IRAs (Traditional and Roth) offer tax advantages that can be beneficial for retirement savings.
   - **Roth vs. Traditional:** 
     - **Roth IRA:** Contributions are made after-tax, but withdrawals in retirement are tax-free. Ideal if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in the future.
     - **Traditional IRA:** Contributions may be tax-deductible, but withdrawals will be taxed. This is beneficial if you expect to be in a lower tax bracket during retirement.

### 3. **Taxable Accounts:**
   - **Flexibility:** These accounts provide more flexibility for withdrawals and investment choices. However, capital gains taxes apply.
   - **Investment Strategy:** Consider using taxable accounts for investments that generate qualified dividends or long-term capital gains to minimize tax impact.

### 4. **Allocation Strategy:**
   - **Emergency Fund:** Ensure you have an emergency fund separate from these accounts.
   - **Risk Tolerance:** Assess your risk tolerance to decide on the allocation of assets (stocks, bonds, etc.) across these accounts.
   - **Time Horizon:** Younger investors may benefit from higher equity exposure, while those closer to retirement might prefer more conservative investments.

### 5. **Tax Efficiency:**
   - **Place Assets Wisely:** Consider placing more tax-efficient investments (like index funds) in taxable accounts and tax-inefficient investments (like bonds) in tax-advantaged accounts.

### 6. **Regular Review:**
   - **Rebalance:** Periodically review and adjust your allocations based on performance, changing risk tolerance, and goals.

### Final Thoughts:
Consult a financial advisor to tailor this advice to your specific situation and ensure you’re making informed decisions aligned with your long-term financial goals.
