Managing the distribution of your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts can optimize your investments. Here’s a general approach:

### 1. **401(k)**
   - **Maximize Contributions:** Aim to contribute at least enough to get any employer match—this is essentially free money.
   - **Tax Advantages:** Contributions are pre-tax (or post-tax for Roth), reducing your taxable income.
   - **Investment Choices:** Check the investment options available in your plan and choose low-cost index funds if possible.

### 2. **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**
   - **Contribute Early:** Max out your contributions to take advantage of compound interest.
   - **Choose Between Traditional or Roth:** 
     - *Traditional:* Contributions may be tax-deductible; taxes are paid on withdrawals.
     - *Roth:* Contributions are taxed upfront, but withdrawals (including gains) are tax-free.
   - **Consider Your Tax Bracket:** If you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement, a Roth can be advantageous.

### 3. **Taxable Accounts**
   - **Flexibility:** These accounts offer liquidity and the flexibility to withdraw funds without penalties.
   - **Tax Efficiency:** Focus on tax-efficient investments like index funds or ETFs. Be mindful of capital gains taxes when selling investments.
   - **Income Generation:** Use this account for investments that generate taxable income, such as stocks and bonds, while keeping tax-inefficient investments (like bonds) in tax-advantaged accounts.

### **General Strategy**
- **Prioritize Contributions:** Focus on funding your 401(k) to get the match, then contribute to an IRA, and finally invest in taxable accounts.
- **Asset Allocation:** Consider your risk tolerance and time horizon. Diversify your investments across all accounts to mitigate risk.
- **Rebalance Regularly:** Periodically check and adjust your investment allocations to stay aligned with your financial goals.

### Conclusion
This distribution strategy depends on individual circumstances, so consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized advice. Balancing tax liabilities, growth potential, and liquidity is key to a successful investment strategy.
