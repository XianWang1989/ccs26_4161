Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here’s a general strategy to consider:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**
   - Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match. This is essentially free money and can significantly boost your retirement savings.

### 2. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**
   - After maximizing the employer match, focus on contributing to an IRA (Roth or Traditional) to take advantage of tax benefits. The choice between these depends on your current tax rate versus your expected tax rate in retirement.

### 3. **Consider Contribution Limits**
   - Be mindful of the contribution limits for each account:
     - For 2024, the 401(k) limit is $23,000 (or $30,500 if you're over 50).
     - The IRA limit is $6,500 (or $7,500 if you're over 50).

### 4. **Utilize Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**
   - Once you’ve maxed out tax-advantaged accounts, any additional savings can go into taxable accounts. These provide more flexibility for withdrawals but come with different tax implications on capital gains and dividends.

### 5. **Investment Strategy**
   - Consider your investment strategy based on each account type:
     - **401(k):** Focus on long-term growth through a mix of stocks and bonds. Take advantage of available low-cost index funds if available.
     - **IRA:** Depending on your tax strategy, consider a mix of growth stocks or tax-efficient ETFs, particularly in a Roth IRA to minimize tax liability on future withdrawals.
     - **Taxable Accounts:** Here, you may want to hold tax-efficient investments like index funds or municipal bonds to minimize tax exposure.

### 6. **Rebalance Regularly**
   - Regularly assess and adjust your asset allocation across all accounts to ensure it aligns with your risk tolerance and investment goals.

### 7. **Consult a Financial Professional**
   - Consider speaking with a financial advisor to tailor this strategy to your specific situation, particularly regarding tax implications and investment choices.

By leveraging the benefits of each account type and aligning your strategy with your financial goals, you can optimize your savings for the future.
