The distribution of your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some strategies to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a match on your 401(k), contribute enough to get the full match. This is free money and should be a priority.

2. **Consider Tax Implications**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are pre-tax, reducing your taxable income for the year, but withdrawals in retirement are taxed as ordinary income.
   - **IRA (Traditional vs. Roth)**: Contributions to a Traditional IRA may be tax-deductible, while Roth IRA contributions are made with after-tax dollars, allowing tax-free withdrawals in retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: These do not have tax advantages, but any capital gains and dividends are subject to taxation. Consider holding investments that generate qualified dividends in these accounts to take advantage of lower tax rates.

3. **Investment Horizon**: 
   - If you're investing for retirement and have a long time horizon, you may want to prioritize tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) and IRA) for their growth potential without immediate tax burdens.
   - For shorter-term goals, such as buying a home or funding education, taxable accounts might be better suited since you may need access to your funds sooner without penalties.

4. **Portfolio Diversification**: Ensure that your investments across all accounts align with your overall risk tolerance and goals. Avoid holding the same investment across all accounts to maintain diversification.

5. **Regular Contributions**: Make consistent contributions to each account based on your overall budget and goals. Adjust your contributions as your financial situation changes.

6. **Rebalance As Needed**: Over time, periodically review and adjust your allocations to maintain your desired asset allocation, considering market changes and personal circumstances.

Consulting with a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your circumstances is also a good decision.
