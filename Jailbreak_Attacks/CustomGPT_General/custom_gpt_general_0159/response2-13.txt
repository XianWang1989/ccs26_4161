Distributing your money amongst your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various factors such as your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here’s a general approach to consider:

1. **Max out Employer Match**: Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match, if available. This is essentially free money and can greatly enhance your retirement savings.

2. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: Aim to maximize your contributions to your 401(k), especially if your employer offers matching contributions. This account is tax-deferred, meaning you won't pay taxes on your contributions or investment gains until you withdraw them in retirement.
   - **IRA**: Next, consider contributing to a Traditional IRA or a Roth IRA (if eligible). A Traditional IRA offers tax-deductible contributions, while a Roth IRA provides tax-free withdrawals in retirement. Choose based on your current and anticipated future tax situation.
  
3. **Assess Taxable Accounts**: After maxing out your tax-advantaged accounts, consider investing in a taxable brokerage account. This allows for flexibility since there are no contribution limits or withdrawal rules, but be mindful of capital gains taxes on your earnings.

4. **Diversification**: Ensure that your investments across all accounts are diversified in terms of assets (stocks, bonds, etc.) to mitigate risk. 

5. **Rebalance Annually**: Periodically review and rebalance your portfolio to maintain your desired asset allocation and adjust for any financial changes or market conditions.

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: Finally, consulting with a financial advisor can provide personalized strategies that align with your specific financial situation and goals.

It's important to tailor these strategies to fit your unique circumstances and objectives.
