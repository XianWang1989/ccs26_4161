To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match, as it's essentially free money.

2. **Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**:
   - **IRA Contributions**: Contribute to your IRA (both Traditional and Roth) depending on your tax situation and income level. Roth accounts are especially beneficial if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement.
   - **401(k) Contributions**: After maximizing your IRA, focus on your 401(k), especially if it offers tax-deferred growth.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: Once you've maxed out your tax-advantaged accounts, consider putting additional savings into taxable accounts. This can provide more flexibility with withdrawals and is useful for shorter-term goals.

4. **Asset Allocation**: Diversify your investments based on your risk tolerance and time horizon. Typically, stocks are more appropriate for long-term growth, while bonds can provide income and stability.

5. **Tax Considerations**: Be mindful of which investments are stored in which accounts:
   - Place high-growth investments (like stocks) in tax-advantaged accounts to defer taxes on gains.
   - Use taxable accounts for investments that generate income (like bonds) to take advantage of lower capital gains tax rates.

6. **Regular Reviews**: Periodically reassess your contributions and asset allocation to adjust for changes in market conditions, your financial situation, or retirement goals.

Consulting with a financial advisor can also provide personalized insights based on your unique circumstances.
