The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment horizon. Here are some general guidelines to consider:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match first, as this is essentially "free money."
   - **IRA/Roth IRA**: If eligible, contribute to an IRA or Roth IRA after your 401(k) match. Roth accounts are particularly beneficial if you anticipate being in a higher tax bracket in retirement.

2. **Consider Your Tax Situation**:
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for investments that generate lower taxable income (like index funds or ETFs) or for short-term needs, since capital gains taxes apply when you sell assets.
   - **Tax-Deferred Accounts**: Assets that generate high taxable income (like bonds) are often better held in tax-deferred accounts, as you won't pay taxes on the earnings until withdrawal.

3. **Future Income Needs**:
   - Diversify across account types to manage withdrawals in retirement effectively. Having a mix allows flexibility in tax strategies when withdrawing funds.

4. **Investment Horizon**:
   - Longer investment horizons might be better suited to riskier investments, typically in retirement accounts (where you can ride out volatility), while shorter-term needs should be in more liquid, stable assets.

5. **Regular Review and Rebalancing**:
   - Regularly assess your portfolio distribution to ensure it aligns with your risk tolerance and financial goals, especially as markets fluctuate and your circumstances change.

It’s often beneficial to consult a financial advisor personalized to your situation for specific recommendations.
