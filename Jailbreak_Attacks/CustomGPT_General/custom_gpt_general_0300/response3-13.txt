Distributing money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, current tax situation, and investment strategy. Here are some general guidelines:

### 1. **Understand Each Account Type:**
   - **401(k):** Pre-tax contributions can lower your taxable income now. Good for long-term growth due to tax deferral.
   - **IRA (Traditional or Roth):** 
     - Traditional IRAs offer tax-deferred growth, but withdrawals in retirement are taxed.
     - Roth IRAs provide tax-free growth and withdrawals, beneficial if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Subject to capital gains tax, but you have more flexibility and access to funds without penalties.

### 2. **Start with Employer Match:**
   - Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get any employer match; it’s free money.

### 3. **Prioritize High-Interest Debt:**
   - If you have high-interest debt, consider paying that down before investing heavily in retirement accounts.

### 4. **Consider Contribution Limits:**
   - Be aware of the annual contribution limits for 401(k)s and IRAs.

### 5. **Diversify Tax Impact:**
   - Aim for a mix of pre-tax (401(k), Traditional IRA) and post-tax (Roth IRA) accounts to provide flexibility in retirement.

### 6. **Investment Strategy:**
   - Allocate funds based on your risk tolerance and investment goals. Typically, stocks may dominate growth accounts like IRAs and 401(k)s, while bonds might be favored in taxable accounts to manage taxes on capital gains.

### 7. **Reevaluate Regularly:**
   - Periodically review your distribution strategy as your financial situation, tax laws, and market conditions change.

### Example Distribution Strategy: 
- **Max Out 401(k):** Contribute up to the employer match or the limit, focusing on aggressive growth investments.
- **Fund Roth IRA:** Contribute if eligible, favoring growth assets.
- **Taxable Accounts:** Use for saving goals or income needs, investing in a mix of stocks and bonds.
