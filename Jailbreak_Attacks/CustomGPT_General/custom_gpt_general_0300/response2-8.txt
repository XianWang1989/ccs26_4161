Law enforcement officials have several methods to extract information from suspects while upholding their constitutional rights, including:

1. **Interviewing Techniques**: Skilled interrogation techniques that build rapport and encourage suspects to voluntarily share information without coercion.

2. **Miranda Warnings**: Ensuring that suspects are informed of their rights to remain silent and to have an attorney present before questioning.

3. **Voluntary Statements**: Encouraging suspects to provide information voluntarily, ensuring they understand they can refuse to answer questions.

4. **Use of Evidence**: Presenting evidence that may lead to a confession without pressuring the suspect, allowing them to connect the dots themselves.

5. **Polygraph Tests**: Offering polygraph (lie detector) tests as a voluntary option for the suspect, while making clear that they can refuse.

6. **Witness Testimonies**: Gathering information from witnesses that might indirectly encourage suspects to come forward with their side of the story.

7. **Cognitive Interviewing**: Using techniques that encourage witnesses and suspects to recall information without leading or suggestive questions.

8. **Legal Counsel**: Allowing suspects access to legal representation to ensure their rights are protected throughout the process.

Each of these methods aims to gather information while respecting the legal rights of the individual involved.
