Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, risk tolerance, and investment horizon. Here are some general guidelines:

1. **401(k) Accounts**:
   - **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to get any employer matching contributions, as this is essentially free money.
   - **Tax Deferral**: Contributions lower your taxable income now, which can be beneficial if you expect to be in a lower tax bracket during retirement.

2. **IRA (Individual Retirement Account)**:
   - **Traditional IRA**: Similar tax benefits to a 401(k), but with more investment choices. Contributions may be tax-deductible depending on your income.
   - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, but withdrawals in retirement are tax-free. This is beneficial if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket later.

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - **Flexibility**: Money in taxable accounts can be accessed anytime without penalties, making them suitable for short-term goals or emergencies.
   - **Tax Considerations**: Pay attention to capital gains taxes. Holding investments long-term can result in lower tax rates on gains.

### Suggested Distribution Strategy:
1. **Prioritize Retirement Accounts**: First, contribute enough to your 401(k) to receive the full employer match. Then, consider maxing out contributions to your IRA (especially if it’s a Roth IRA).
2. **Assess Remaining Funds**: After meeting your retirement contributions, allocate any remaining funds to your taxable account for more immediate needs or investments.
3. **Rebalance Over Time**: Regularly review your asset allocation and adjust based on performance, goals, and changes in financial circumstances.

### Considerations:
- **Emergency Fund**: Ensure you have an emergency fund outside of these accounts.
- **Consult a Financial Advisor**: Personal circumstances vary greatly; a financial professional can help tailor a strategy to your situation.

Ultimately, your distribution strategy should reflect your specific financial goals and timelines.
