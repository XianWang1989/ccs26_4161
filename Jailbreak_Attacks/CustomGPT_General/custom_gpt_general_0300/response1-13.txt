Determining the best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some general guidelines to consider:

### 1. **Understand Each Account Type**
   - **401(k)**: Often includes employer matching contributions and tax-deferred growth. Good for long-term retirement savings.
   - **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**: Offers tax advantages. A Traditional IRA provides tax-deferred growth, while Roth IRAs offer tax-free withdrawals in retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Flexibility for short-term goals and investments, though you'll owe capital gains tax on realized gains.

### 2. **Consider Your Tax Situation**
   - Use tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) and IRA) for investments that you expect to appreciate significantly, as these gains will grow tax-deferred or tax-free.
   - Allocate investments that generate regular income (like bonds) to taxable accounts to manage your tax liabilities efficiently.

### 3. **Maximize Employer Match**
   - Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match, as this is essentially "free money."

### 4. **Prioritize Contributions**
   - **401(k)**: Max out to the employer match first.
   - **IRA**: Contribute to a Traditional or Roth IRA next, depending on which offers more tax benefits based on your situation.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for any additional investments, especially if you've maxed out the above accounts.

### 5. **Assess Your Investment Horizon and Goals**
   - For long-term retirement savings, prioritize tax-advantaged accounts.
   - If you have short-term needs (like buying a house), consider more funds in taxable accounts for liquidity.

### 6. **Rebalance Regularly**
   - Periodically review and adjust your asset allocation to ensure alignment with your financial goals and risk tolerance.

### Conclusion
It’s wise to consult with a financial advisor who can tailor strategies specific to your financial circumstances. Consider creating a balanced approach that maximizes tax efficiency while aligning with your broader financial objectives.
