Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here's a general approach to consider:

1. **Employer-Match for 401(k)**:
   - Contribute at least enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match, as this is essentially free money.

2. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - After the employer match, consider maximizing your contributions to an IRA (Roth or Traditional) if you qualify. This can help with tax-free growth or tax deductions.

3. **Consider the Tax Implications**:
   - 401(k) and Traditional IRA contributions are often tax-deductible, providing immediate tax benefits. However, withdrawals in retirement will be taxed.
   - Roth IRAs allow for tax-free withdrawals in retirement, making them great for younger investors who expect to be in a higher tax bracket later on.

4. **Diversify Investments**:
   - In taxable accounts, consider investing in tax-efficient funds or strategies, since you'll pay capital gains taxes on taxable account withdrawals.

5. **Emergency Savings**:
   - Ensure you have an adequate emergency fund before heavily investing, typically 3-6 months of living expenses.

6. **Review and Adjust**:
   - Monitor your contributions and asset allocation annually, adjusting based on changes in income, goals, or market conditions.

Consulting with a financial advisor can also provide personalized advice tailored to your specific circumstances.
