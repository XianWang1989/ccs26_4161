Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts effectively requires considering your financial goals, tax implications, and investment time horizon. Here are some strategies:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**
   - **401(k):** If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to receive the full match first. It's free money.
   - **IRA:** After maximizing your 401(k) match, consider contributing to a Traditional or Roth IRA, depending on your tax situation (deductibility and potential tax-free withdrawals).

### 2. **Prioritize Taxable Accounts Last**
   - Invest in taxable accounts after maxing out your tax-advantaged accounts. This is because gains in taxable accounts are subject to capital gains tax, while retirement accounts grow tax-deferred or tax-free.

### 3. **Assess Your Tax Situation**
   - **Roth vs. Traditional:** If you anticipate being in a higher tax bracket in retirement, prioritize Roth accounts where withdrawals are tax-free. Conversely, if you expect a lower tax bracket, Traditional accounts may provide tax advantages now.

### 4. **Balance for Liquidity and Flexibility**
   - Keep some funds in taxable accounts for emergencies or short-term goals, as withdrawals from retirement accounts can incur penalties and taxes.

### 5. **Invest According to Risk Tolerance**
   - Ensure your asset allocation aligns with your risk tolerance and investment timeline, using a diversified mix of stocks and bonds across all account types.

### 6. **Review Regularly**
   - Adjust contributions and investments based on changes in your income, lifestyle, and financial goals.

### Example Allocation Strategy
1. **401(k):** Max out employer match, then contribute up to the annual limit.
2. **IRA:** Max out if possible.
3. **Taxable Accounts:** Allocate remaining funds, focusing on tax-efficient investments like index funds.

### Conclusion
The optimal strategy varies for each individual based on specific financial circumstances and goals. Consulting a financial advisor can provide personalized guidance tailored to your situation.
