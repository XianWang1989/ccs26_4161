Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, risk tolerance, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here are some general strategies:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First:**
   - **401(k):** Contribute enough to get any employer match, as it’s essentially free money.
   - **IRA:** Consider maxing out your contributions to a traditional or Roth IRA, depending on your eligibility and tax situation.

### 2. **Diversify Investments:**
   - **401(k) and IRA:** Focus on long-term growth investments (stocks, mutual funds) in these accounts since they grow tax-deferred or tax-free (Roth IRA).
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Use this for more liquid, less tax-efficient investments, like bonds or dividend-paying stocks, since you’ll be taxed on dividends and capital gains.

### 3. **Consider Your Time Horizon:**
   - If you’re far from retirement, prioritize growth in your 401(k) and IRA.
   - As you approach retirement, gradually shift towards more conservative investments.

### 4. **Tax Considerations:**
   - Taxable accounts can provide more flexibility; therefore, keep tax-inefficient investments (like REITs) in tax-advantaged accounts.
   - Be mindful of capital gains tax when reallocating in taxable accounts.

### 5. **Continuous Monitoring:**
   - Regularly review your asset allocation and adjust according to your financial goals, life changes, or market conditions.

### Example Strategy:
- **401(k):** Contribute enough to maximize the employer match.
- **IRA:** Max out contributions.
- **Taxable Account:** Use any remaining funds for short to medium-term investments or for emergency savings.

Consult with a financial advisor to tailor this distribution to your specific circumstances and goals.
