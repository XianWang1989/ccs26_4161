To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match, as this is essentially "free money."

2. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: Contribute beyond the match limit if your employer offers a good range of investment options.
   - **IRA**: Ensure you take advantage of an IRA (Roth or Traditional) for the tax benefits. Roth IRAs are great if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement.

3. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**: Use taxable accounts for investments that may be needed sooner or for income that won't fit neatly into tax-advantaged accounts. This is also useful for investments that generate capital gains rather than regular income, which can help with tax efficiency.

4. **Asset Allocation**: Consider your overall asset allocation based on your risk tolerance and investment goals. Generally:
   - **Stocks**: Great for long-term growth, ideally in retirement accounts to defer taxes.
   - **Bonds and fixed income**: Can be placed in taxable accounts to minimize tax impact on income generation.

5. **Rebalance Regularly**: Monitor and adjust your allocations periodically to ensure they align with your financial goals and risk tolerance.

6. **Consider Withdrawal Strategies**: As you get closer to retirement, think about how you'll withdraw from these accounts to minimize taxes.

7. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: For personalized advice, particularly regarding tax implications, a professional can provide tailored guidance based on your situation.

A balanced approach tailored to your financial situation and goals will yield the best results.
