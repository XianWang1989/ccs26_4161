Distributing money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts effectively depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some general guidelines:

1. **Maximize Contributions to Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match; consider maximizing contributions to take advantage of tax deferral.
   - **IRA**: If eligible, contribute to a Traditional IRA (tax-deductible) or a Roth IRA (tax-free withdrawals in retirement).

2. **Tax Considerations**:
   - **Taxable Accounts**: These are best for investments that generate capital gains or dividends taxed at advantageous rates. Consider holding investments that are tax-efficient (like index funds) here.

3. **Diversification**:
   - Spread your investments across asset classes (stocks, bonds, ETFs) to mitigate risk. Ensure that your overall portfolio reflects your risk tolerance.

4. **Withdrawal Strategy**:
   - Plan for a withdrawal strategy based on your retirement timeline, aiming to minimize taxes by withdrawing from accounts in the most tax-advantageous order.

5. **Rebalance Regularly**:
   - Periodically review and adjust your asset allocation to maintain your desired risk level.

Consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your specific situation.
