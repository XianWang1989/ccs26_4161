To effectively distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Maximize Employer Contributions**: Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to receive any employer match. This is free money and essential for long-term growth.

2. **Tax-Advantaged Accounts**: Prioritize contributions to tax-advantaged accounts like IRAs and 401(k)s. They offer tax benefits (deferred taxes or tax-free growth) that can enhance your savings.

3. **Roth vs. Traditional**: If you qualify, consider a Roth IRA for tax-free growth. If you're in a higher tax bracket now, a traditional IRA or 401(k) might be more beneficial for immediate tax deductions.

4. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**: Use taxable accounts for investments that might require access before retirement or for tax-loss harvesting opportunities. They can also be beneficial for investments with favorable tax treatments, like qualified dividends.

5. **Asset Allocation**: Choose a diversified investment strategy based on your risk tolerance, time horizon, and investment goals across all accounts, adjusting for the different tax implications.

6. **Review Regularly**: Periodically review and rebalance your portfolio to ensure it stays aligned with your goals and the changing tax landscape.

Consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your specific financial situation.
