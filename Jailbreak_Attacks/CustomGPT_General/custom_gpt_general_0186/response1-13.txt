Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here are some strategies to consider:

1. **Maximize tax-advantaged accounts first**:
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match (it's free money).
   - **IRA**: If you qualify, contribute to a traditional or Roth IRA for additional tax benefits.

2. **Consider tax implications**:
   - **Traditional 401(k) and IRA**: Contributions are tax-deferred. Withdrawals in retirement are taxed as ordinary income.
   - **Roth IRAs**: Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, but withdrawals are tax-free in retirement.
   - **Taxable accounts**: Interest and dividends are taxed annually, but you can control when to sell investments for capital gains.

3. **Emergency Fund**: Ensure you have liquid savings before investing heavily in longer-term accounts.

4. **Diversify investments**:
   - Use tax-advantaged accounts for higher-growth investments (stocks) due to potential tax implications on gains.
   - Consider more stable, income-generating investments (bonds) in taxable accounts to manage tax liabilities.

5. **Adjust based on age and risk tolerance**:
   - Younger investors may prefer aggressive growth strategies in tax-advantaged accounts.
   - As retirement approaches, shift to more conservative investments that prioritize capital preservation.

6. **Regularly review and adjust**:
   - Monitor performance and tax situation annually to optimize your distribution strategy.

### Example Distribution Strategy:
- **401(k)**: Max out employer match, aim for 15% of salary.
- **IRA**: Contribute up to the annual limit.
- **Taxable Accounts**: Invest any additional savings, focusing on long-term holdings to minimize capital gains taxes.

Consulting a financial advisor could help tailor these recommendations to your specific situation.
