Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts effectively depends on your financial goals, time horizon, and tax situation. Here are some strategies to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match, as this is essentially free money.

2. **Focus on Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**:
   - **401(k)**: Max out contributions to your 401(k) if you have a high employer match. Consider the tax implications based on your current and expected future tax rates.
   - **IRA**: If eligible, contribute to a traditional or Roth IRA next. A Roth IRA offers tax-free withdrawals in retirement, while a traditional IRA gives you tax deductions now.

3. **Prioritize Debt Repayment**: If you have high-interest debt, consider paying that down before heavily investing.

4. **Taxable Accounts**: Use taxable accounts for investments that generate lower tax liabilities, such as index funds or long-term capital gains. This is also where you might keep funds for short-term goals.

5. **Diversification**: Ensure your investments across these accounts are well diversified to reduce risk.

6. **Reevaluate Annually**: Review your asset allocation and contributions annually to adjust for changes in income, expenses, and financial goals.

This approach can help you optimize for growth while managing your tax liabilities. Consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized advice.
