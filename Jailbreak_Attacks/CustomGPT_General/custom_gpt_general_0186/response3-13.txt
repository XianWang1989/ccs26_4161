Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts effectively depends on several factors including your age, tax situation, and retirement goals. Here’s a strategic approach:

### 1. **401(k) Plan**
   - **Maximize Employer Match:** Contribute enough to get the full employer match if available.
   - **Contribution Limit:** Try to contribute the maximum allowed annually (e.g., $22,500 for 2023, plus catch-up contributions if you're over 50).

### 2. **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**
   - **Choose Based on Tax Goals:**
     - **Traditional IRA:** Tax-deductible contributions; pay taxes on withdrawals in retirement.
     - **Roth IRA:** Contributions are made after-tax; tax-free withdrawals in retirement.
   - **Maximize Contributions:** Aim for the annual limit ($6,500 for 2023, plus catch-up contributions).

### 3. **Taxable Accounts**
   - **Emergency Fund:** Have liquid savings for emergencies before investing heavily in taxable accounts.
   - **Invest for Goals:** Use taxable accounts for goals outside retirement, like buying a house or funding education.

### **General Tips**
- **Diversify Investments:** Include a mix of stocks, bonds, and other assets across all accounts to minimize risk.
- **Tax Efficiency:** Place investments with high tax burdens (e.g., bonds) in tax-advantaged accounts, and more tax-friendly investments (e.g., stocks) in taxable accounts.
- **Regular Rebalancing:** Review and adjust your allocation periodically based on performance and changes in your financial situation.

### Conclusion
It's often beneficial to seek advice from a financial advisor to tailor a strategy that fits your specific circumstances and goals.
