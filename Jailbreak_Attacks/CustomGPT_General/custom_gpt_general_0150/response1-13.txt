Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts requires consideration of several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment horizon. Here’s a general strategy:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**  
Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to take full advantage of any employer matching contributions. This is essentially "free money" and a great way to boost your retirement savings.

### 2. **Contribute to IRA**  
Next, consider contributing to a Traditional IRA or Roth IRA, depending on your income and tax situation. A Roth IRA offers tax-free withdrawals in retirement, while a Traditional IRA may allow for tax-deductible contributions now.

### 3. **Continue 401(k) Contributions**  
After maximizing your IRA contributions, redirect additional funds into your 401(k) until you reach the annual contribution limit. This not only provides tax advantages but also helps you stay disciplined in saving for retirement.

### 4. **Pay Attention to Taxable Accounts**  
Once you've optimized contributions to your retirement accounts, consider taxable accounts for additional investments. These can provide flexibility and access to funds without penalties, though they are subject to capital gains taxes.

### 5. **Diversification and Asset Allocation**  
Ensure that your investments across all accounts are diversified and aligned with your risk tolerance and time horizon. Stocks may be more suitable for long-term growth in your tax-advantaged accounts, while bonds or income-generating assets might be better for your taxable accounts to manage tax implications.

### 6. **Tax Efficiency Considerations**  
- Place investments that generate ordinary income (like bonds) in tax-advantaged accounts to avoid higher taxes on interest.
- Hold investments that generate long-term capital gains (like stocks) in taxable accounts, where the tax rate is generally lower.

### 7. **Regular Review and Adjustment**  
Finally, periodically review your allocation to ensure it aligns with your goals, especially as your income, expenses, or financial goals change.

Consulting with a financial advisor can provide personalized strategies based on your specific situation.
