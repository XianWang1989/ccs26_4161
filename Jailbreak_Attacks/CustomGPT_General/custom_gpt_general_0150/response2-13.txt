Distributing your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here are some guidelines to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**: If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to receive the full match. This is essentially free money.

2. **Prioritize Retirement Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: If you have maxed out the employer match, consider contributing more here, especially if you have a choice between pre-tax and Roth contributions. Pre-tax contributions reduce your taxable income now, while Roth contributions grow tax-free.
   - **IRA**: After the 401(k), consider contributing to an IRA. A traditional IRA can provide tax deductions, while a Roth IRA allows for tax-free withdrawals in retirement.

3. **Assess Tax Implications**:
   - Taxable accounts are usually less tax-efficient because you'll pay taxes on dividends and capital gains. Focus on holding long-term investments in these accounts to minimize taxes.
   - Consider tax-loss harvesting strategies in your taxable accounts to offset gains.

4. **Diversify Investments**: Ensure your investment strategy aligns with your risk tolerance and time horizon, diversifying across asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) to balance growth and risk.

5. **Emergency Fund**: Maintain some liquidity in a taxable account for emergencies or short-term needs, ensuring you don't have to withdraw from retirement accounts early.

6. **Adjust Based on Age**: Younger investors can typically afford to take more risks, focusing on stocks in retirement accounts for growth. As you approach retirement, consider shifting to more stable investments.

7. **Regular Review**: Reassess your distribution strategy annually or when significant life changes occur (e.g., marriage, job change) to stay aligned with your financial goals.

Consulting with a financial advisor can provide personalized strategies tailored to your specific situation.
