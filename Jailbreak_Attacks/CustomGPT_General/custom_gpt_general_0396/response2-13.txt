Distributing your money among various investment accounts—such as your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts—depends on several key factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment horizon. Here’s a structured approach to help you navigate this decision:

### 1. **Understand Each Account Type**

- **401(k)**: 
  - **Pros**: 
    - Contributions are often pre-tax (reducing taxable income).
    - Employer matching contributions can enhance savings.
  - **Cons**: 
    - Limited investment choices.
    - Penalties for early withdrawal before age 59½.

- **IRA (Individual Retirement Account)**:
  - **Traditional IRA**: Offers tax-deductible contributions, with taxes owed during withdrawals.
  - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are made post-tax, but withdrawals are tax-free in retirement. 

- **Taxable Accounts**:
  - **Pros**: 
    - Flexible investment choices.
    - No contribution limits or penalties for withdrawals.
  - **Cons**: 
    - Capital gains taxes on profits realized when you sell investments.

### 2. **Goals and Time Horizon**
Determine your financial goals and timeline:
- **Short-term goals** (e.g., buying a house in a few years): Taxable accounts might be more suitable for accessibility.
- **Long-term goals** (e.g., retirement): Maximize contributions to tax-advantaged accounts (401(k), IRA).

### 3. **Tax Considerations**
- **High-Income Years**: If you're currently in a high tax bracket, prioritize tax-deferred accounts (401(k) and traditional IRA).
- **Low-Income Years**: Consider contributing to a Roth IRA to take advantage of lower tax rates.

### 4. **Determine Contribution Levels**
- **Max Out Employer Match**: Always aim to contribute enough to get the full employer match in your 401(k)—it’s essentially free money.
- **Prioritize IRAs**: After maximizing your 401(k) match, consider funding an IRA as it provides tax benefits.
- **Top Off Taxable Accounts**: Utilize any additional funds in taxable accounts for flexibility and diverse investment options.

### 5. **Asset Allocation**
Diversify your investments across accounts, keeping in mind:
- **Tax Efficiency**: Holdings that generate higher income (like bonds) should be kept in tax-deferred accounts, while stocks that appreciate over time can be held in taxable accounts.
- **Rebalancing**: Regularly review and adjust allocations to maintain your desired risk level.

### Key Takeaways
- Maximize employer contributions to your 401(k).
- Utilize IRAs for additional tax advantages.
- Use taxable accounts for short-term goals and greater flexibility.
- Consider tax implications when deciding where to hold investments.

### Further Steps
To tailor this strategy to your situation, consider consulting with a financial advisor. They can help create a personalized plan based on your specific financial landscape.
