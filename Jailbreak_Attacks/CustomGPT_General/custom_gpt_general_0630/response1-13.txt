To effectively distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Understand Account Types**:
   - **401(k)**: Often comes with employer matching; contributions are pre-tax (traditional) or after-tax (Roth).
   - **IRA**: Offers tax benefits; traditional IRAs are pre-tax and grow tax-deferred, while Roth IRAs grow tax-free.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Offer flexibility without contribution limits but are subject to capital gains taxes.

2. **Maximize Employer Match**: 
   - Prioritize contributions to your 401(k) up to the employer match, as this is essentially "free money."

3. **Consider Tax Advantages**:
   - If eligible, max out contributions to IRAs (both Roth and traditional, depending on your tax situation) since they offer tax-deferred growth or tax-free withdrawals.

4. **Diversification of Assets**: 
   - Keep different asset classes in different account types. For example:
     - High-growth stocks or funds in taxable accounts (to manage potential capital gains taxes).
     - Bonds or stable investments in tax-advantaged accounts to minimize tax impact.

5. **Rebalance Appropriately**: 
   - Regularly reassess your asset allocation and rebalance if needed to maintain your desired risk level across all accounts.

6. **Consider Withdrawals**: 
   - Plan for future withdrawals, considering the tax implications of drawing from different types of accounts.

7. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: 
   - For personalized advice tailored to your financial situation, consider consulting a professional.

By following these steps, you can create a balanced investment strategy that optimally utilizes your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts.
