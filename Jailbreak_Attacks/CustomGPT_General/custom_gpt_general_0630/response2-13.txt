Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here’s a general approach:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**: If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to take full advantage of it. It’s essentially free money.

2. **Contribute to IRA**: If you can, contribute to a Traditional or Roth IRA after maximizing your 401(k) match. A Roth IRA can provide tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement, depending on your income.

3. **Fill Up 401(k)**: If you're eligible, continue contributing to your 401(k) up to the maximum annual contribution limit. This can provide tax benefits since contributions are often made pretax.

4. **Taxable Accounts**: Once you max out contributions to tax-advantaged accounts, allocate funds to taxable accounts. These are more flexible but come with tax implications on dividends and capital gains.

5. **Diversification and Risk Tolerance**: Keep in mind your asset allocation strategy. Diversify across different asset classes based on your risk tolerance and investment goals.

6. **Review and Adjust**: Regularly review your investment strategy and adjust your contributions based on changing financial goals or life situations.

7. **Tax Considerations**: Consider the tax implications of withdrawal strategies. Funds in a 401(k) and IRA are taxed upon withdrawal, while capital gains in taxable accounts are taxed when realized.

By following this approach and periodically reviewing your financial situation and goals, you can optimize the distribution of funds across your accounts. Consulting a financial advisor can also provide personalized strategies tailored to your specific needs.
