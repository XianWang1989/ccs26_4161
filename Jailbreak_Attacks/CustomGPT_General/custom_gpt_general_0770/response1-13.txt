To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

### 1. **Tax Efficiency**
   - **401(k) and IRA (Tax-Advantaged Accounts):**
     - Prioritize contributions to your 401(k) and IRA, as they offer tax advantages (like tax-deferred growth or tax-free withdrawals in Roth accounts).
     - If you are eligible, maximize contributions to these accounts, especially if your employer offers a 401(k) match, as this is essentially "free money."

   - **Taxable Accounts:**
     - Use taxable accounts for investments that generate capital gains or qualified dividends, as they are typically taxed at a lower rate than ordinary income.
     - Keep short-term trading or high-dividend-paying stocks in tax-advantaged accounts to avoid higher tax rates on ordinary income.

### 2. **Asset Allocation**
   - Diversify your investments according to your risk tolerance and investment horizon across all accounts.
   - Consider holding more aggressive investments in tax-advantaged accounts, as they can benefit from tax-free growth.

### 3. **Withdrawal Strategy**
   - In retirement, consider a strategic withdrawal approach from these accounts. Start withdrawals from taxable accounts first, then move to tax-deferred accounts (401(k) or traditional IRA), and finally tax-free accounts (Roth IRA) to minimize tax liabilities.

### 4. **Monitoring and Rebalancing**
   - Regularly monitor your asset allocation across all accounts to ensure it aligns with your investment goals and risk tolerance.
   - Rebalance as needed to maintain your desired asset allocation.

### 5. **Considerations for Future Taxes**
   - Be mindful of future tax implications, especially if you anticipate being in a higher tax bracket in retirement. In such cases, consider contributing more to Roth accounts.

### Conclusion
The ideal distribution will depend on your individual financial situation, including your income, tax bracket, investment goals, and retirement timeline. It’s often wise to consult with a financial advisor to create a tailored strategy that meets your needs.
