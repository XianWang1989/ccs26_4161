Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts requires a strategy that aligns with your financial goals, risk tolerance, and tax situation. Here are some best practices:

### 1. Understand Account Types:
- **401(k):** Often offers employer matching contributions; contributions are made pre-tax, which reduces taxable income.
- **IRA (Individual Retirement Account):** Traditional IRAs are tax-deferred, while Roth IRAs allow for tax-free withdrawals in retirement.
- **Taxable Accounts:** Investment earnings are subject to capital gains tax, but these accounts offer flexibility and accessibility.

### 2. Maximize Employer Match:
- Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match. This is essentially free money.

### 3. Contribute to IRAs:
- Depending on your income, you might consider maximizing contributions to an IRA. Roth IRAs can be particularly beneficial if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement.
  
### 4. Tax Efficiency:
- **Hold tax-inefficient investments** (like bonds or high-dividend stocks) in tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) or IRA).
- **Keep tax-efficient investments** (like index funds) in taxable accounts to minimize tax implications.

### 5. Diversification:
- Ensure your investments are diversified across different asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) regardless of account type.

### 6. Withdrawal Strategy:
- Plan how you will withdraw funds in retirement. Generally, you'll want to withdraw from taxable accounts first, then tax-deferred accounts (like 401(k)s) last to minimize tax impact.

### 7. Regular Review:
- Periodically review and rebalance your portfolio to adjust based on performance and changes in your goals or market conditions.

### General Allocation Considerations:
- **Younger Investors:** Focus more on growth assets (stocks) in retirement accounts.
- **Near Retirement:** Shift towards more conservative investments within tax-advantaged accounts, while still keeping some growth assets.
  
### Example Allocation Strategy:
- **Age 30:** 70% 401(k), 20% IRA, 10% Taxable.
- **Age 50:** 50% 401(k), 30% IRA, 20% Taxable.
- **Retirement:** Gradually shift towards 30% 401(k), 30% IRA, 40% Taxable for withdrawals.

### Final Notes:
Consult a financial advisor to tailor these strategies to your specific situation. They can help you navigate tax implications and adjust your plan as your financial situation evolves.
