When it comes to distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, a balanced approach that takes into consideration your financial goals, risk tolerance, and tax implications is essential. Here’s a general strategy to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k):**
   - If your employer offers a matching contribution to your 401(k), aim to contribute at least enough to take full advantage of this match. This is essentially free money.

2. **Contribute to an IRA:**
   - Depending on your eligibility, consider contributing to a traditional or Roth IRA. Traditional IRAs provide tax-deductible contributions, while Roth IRAs allow for tax-free withdrawals in retirement. Depending on your current tax bracket, one may be more favorable than the other.

3. **Taxable Accounts:**
   - After maximizing contributions to tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) and IRA), consider investing in taxable accounts for additional savings and flexibility. Taxable accounts do not have contribution limits and can provide liquidity for short-term financial goals.

4. **Diversification:**
   - Ensure your investments across these accounts are diversified. This may include a mix of stocks, bonds, real estate, and other assets to help manage risk and improve potential returns.

5. **Consider Tax Implications:**
   - Understand the tax implications of your investment choices. Tax-efficient funds may be more appropriate for taxable accounts to minimize capital gains taxes.

6. **Rebalance Regularly:**
   - Review and rebalance your investments periodically to maintain your desired asset allocation. This ensures that you do not take on more risk than intended as certain investments grow faster than others.

7. **Plan for Withdrawals:**
   - Consider your withdrawal strategy as you approach retirement. Generally, you should withdraw from taxable accounts first, then tax-deferred accounts (like 401(k)s and traditional IRAs), and finally tax-free accounts (like Roth IRAs) to minimize tax impacts.

Ultimately, the best distribution will vary for each individual based on personal circumstances. It's often advisable to speak with a financial advisor for a tailored strategy.
