The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some strategies to consider:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**
   - **401(k):** If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to get the full match, as it’s essentially free money. Consider contributing the maximum limit if you can afford it.
   - **IRA:** If you have additional funds, consider contributing to a Traditional or Roth IRA, depending on your income and tax situation. Roth IRAs offer tax-free withdrawals in retirement, while Traditional IRAs provide tax deductions on contributions.

### 2. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**
   - After maximizing contributions to retirement accounts, you can invest in a taxable account. This provides more flexibility for withdrawals and investment choices, though it comes with taxes on dividends and capital gains.

### 3. **Asset Allocation**
   - Diversify your investments across different asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) based on your risk tolerance and investment horizon.
   - Typically, stocks are better suited for retirement accounts where they can grow tax-deferred. In taxable accounts, consider holding lower-turnover investments to minimize capital gains taxes.

### 4. **Consider Your Tax Bracket**
   - If you expect to be in a higher tax bracket during retirement, prioritize tax-deferred accounts (401(k) and Traditional IRA).
   - If you expect to be in a lower bracket, Roth accounts might be more beneficial.

### 5. **Withdrawal Strategy**
   - Plan how you’ll withdraw funds in retirement. Generally, you may want to withdraw from taxable accounts first, then tax-deferred accounts, and finally tax-free accounts, to optimize tax implications.

### 6. **Review Annually**
   - Regularly review your asset allocation and contributions, adjusting as your situation (income, goals, market conditions) changes.

It's often beneficial to consult a financial advisor to tailor a strategy that fits your individual needs and circumstances.
