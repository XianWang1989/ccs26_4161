The optimal way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here are some guidelines to consider:

1. **Prioritize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a matching contribution for your 401(k), contribute enough to take full advantage of this match first. It’s essentially free money.

2. **Max Out Tax-Advantaged Accounts**: After securing the match, focus on maximizing contributions to your tax-advantaged accounts:
   - **401(k)**: Traditional or Roth options depending on your tax preference (pre-tax contributions for current tax savings, or post-tax for tax-free withdrawals in retirement).
   - **IRA**: Similarly, decide between a Traditional or Roth IRA based on your income, tax bracket, and withdrawal plans.

3. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**: Consider putting money into taxable accounts for short-term goals or if you expect to need funds before retirement. These offer more liquidity but come with capital gains taxes.

4. **Consider Diversification**: Ensure that your investments across these accounts are diversified to manage risk effectively. This might mean having a mix of stocks, bonds, and other assets in each account.

5. **Analyze Your Tax Situation**: Think about your current and expected future tax rates. For instance, if you anticipate a higher tax rate in retirement, it may be beneficial to contribute more to a Roth IRA.

6. **Rebalance Regularly**: Regularly reviewing and adjusting your asset allocation can help ensure it aligns with your risk tolerance and investment objectives.

7. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: If you're unsure about your distribution strategy, consulting a financial advisor can provide personalized advice based on your unique situation.

Balancing contributions smartly among these accounts can maximize your growth potential and minimize taxes over time.
