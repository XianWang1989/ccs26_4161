Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here’s a general approach:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**
   - Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get any employer match. This is essentially free money.

### 2. **Contribute to an IRA**
   - If eligible, consider maxing out your IRA (traditional or Roth), depending on your tax situation. A Roth IRA provides tax-free growth, while a traditional IRA offers tax deductions.

### 3. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**
   - Use taxable accounts for funds you may need sooner. These accounts also allow for more flexibility in investment choices and withdrawals without penalties.

### 4. **Consider Your Tax Bracket**
   - If you're currently in a lower tax bracket, a Roth IRA can be beneficial for tax-free growth. Conversely, if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket later, a traditional 401(k) or IRA may be better.

### 5. **Diversification Across Accounts**
   - Diversify your investments across all accounts to balance risk. Consider asset allocation that aligns with your risk tolerance and investment horizon.

### 6. **Revisit Annually**
   - Review and adjust your contributions and allocations yearly based on life changes and market performance.

This approach provides a balanced strategy, maximizing tax advantages while preparing for future financial needs. Consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized advice.
