To effectively distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following guidelines:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**:
   - **401(k)**: If your employer offers a match, prioritize contributing enough to get the full match—it’s essentially free money. Consider maxing out contributions if your budget allows.
   - **IRA**: After the 401(k), consider contributing to a traditional or Roth IRA. A Roth IRA offers tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement, while a traditional IRA may provide immediate tax benefits.

2. **Assess Tax Implications**:
   - Use taxable accounts for investments with lower expected returns or for short-term goals, as they are taxed differently (on dividends and capital gains). Tax-exempt bonds or index funds may be more suitable here.
   - Keep higher-growth investments (like stocks or mutual funds) in tax-advantaged accounts to defer taxes.

3. **Consider Your Goals and Time Horizon**:
   - For retirement, prioritize retirement accounts. If you have more immediate financial goals (like buying a home), consider a mix of taxable accounts.
   - Diversify across asset classes (stocks, bonds, cash) based on your risk tolerance and investment horizon.

4. **Review and Adjust Regularly**:
   - Regularly assess your allocation and performance. Rebalance as necessary to maintain your desired asset allocation, especially after significant market movements.

5. **Consult a Financial Advisor**:
   - If you’re unsure about the best approach, consider speaking with a financial advisor who can provide personalized advice based on your individual financial situation.

This balanced approach can help you maximize growth while managing risk and tax implications.
