The optimal distribution of your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts largely depends on your individual financial situation, goals, and tax considerations. Here are some general guidelines to consider:

1. **401(k) Account**: Contributions to a 401(k) are often made pre-tax, reducing your taxable income. Take advantage of any employer match, as this is essentially "free money". Aim to contribute enough to receive the full match. If your employer offers a Roth 401(k) option, consider contributing to that if you expect your tax rate to be higher in retirement.

2. **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**: If you have maximized your 401(k) contributions, consider contributing to an IRA. A Traditional IRA offers tax-deductible contributions, while a Roth IRA provides tax-free withdrawals in retirement. Choose based on your current and expected future tax rates. Maximum contributions can vary, so stay updated on IRS limits.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: Once you've maxed out contributions to retirement accounts (401(k) and IRA), consider investing in taxable accounts. These accounts offer more flexibility for withdrawals and can be a good option for short- to mid-term goals. Be mindful of capital gains tax on investments held in these accounts.

4. **Emergency Fund**: Ensure you have an adequate emergency fund outside of these accounts, typically covering 3-6 months of living expenses. This ensures you won't have to dip into retirement accounts for unexpected expenses.

5. **Investment Strategy**: Diversify your investments based on your risk tolerance and timeline. Consider holding more aggressive investments in tax-advantaged accounts (like a 401(k) or IRA) where you can benefit from tax-deferred growth, and more stable investments in taxable accounts for liquidity.

6. **Tax Considerations**: Review your current tax situation and plan for future tax implications of withdrawals. It may be beneficial to speak with a financial advisor or tax professional for personalized advice.

By strategically allocating your investments among these accounts, you can maximize tax efficiency and align your portfolio with your long-term financial goals. 

Consultation with a financial advisor can provide a tailored strategy based on your unique circumstances.
