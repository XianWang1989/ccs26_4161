Distributing your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your individual financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some general guidelines:

1. **Maximize Contributions**: Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to get any employer match, as this is essentially “free money.” 

2. **Consider Tax Implications**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are made pre-tax (traditional) or after-tax (Roth). Consider your current vs. expected future tax rate.
   - **IRA**: Similar to a 401(k), you can choose traditional or Roth. Traditional IRAs also offer tax-deferred growth, while Roth IRAs provide tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: These can be useful for investment flexibility and liquidity with no contribution limits, but they're subject to capital gains tax.

3. **Emergency Fund**: Ensure you have an emergency fund (3-6 months of living expenses) before heavily investing in retirement accounts.

4. **Diversify Investments**: Across all accounts, maintain a diversified investment portfolio that aligns with your risk tolerance and retirement timeline.

5. **Debt Consideration**: If you have high-interest debt, consider paying that down first before maximizing retirement contributions.

6. **Review and Adjust**: Over time, review your allocations to ensure they align with your changing goals and market conditions.

A common strategy is to fully fund your 401(k) match, then max out your IRA contributions, and finally allocate money to your taxable accounts. Consider consulting with a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your specific financial situation.
