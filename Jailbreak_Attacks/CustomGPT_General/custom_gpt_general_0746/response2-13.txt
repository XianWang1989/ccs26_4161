To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Maximize tax-advantaged accounts first**:
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match, which is essentially free money. Then consider contributing the maximum allowable limit if it fits your budget.
   - **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**: After maxing your 401(k) contributions (or at least getting the match), prioritize funding your IRA. A Roth IRA offers tax-free growth, while a Traditional IRA can provide immediate tax benefits.

2. **Consider account tax implications**:
   - Keep higher-growth investments (like stocks) in tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) and IRA) to defer taxes on capital gains.
   - Place fixed-income investments (bonds) or income-generating assets in taxable accounts, as dividends and interest may be taxed at a lower rate depending on your tax situation.

3. **Diversification**: 
   - Ensure that you don't put all your investments in one type of account. Diversifying across different asset types will help manage risk.

4. **Emergency Fund**: 
   - Maintain an emergency fund in a separate savings account. This ensures you won’t need to tap into retirement accounts for unexpected expenses.

5. **Review and Adjust**:
   - Regularly review your investment strategy and account balances, adjusting your contributions based on market performance, tax changes, and your financial goals.

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: 
   - If you're unsure about allocation or how to balance your investments, a financial advisor can tailor strategies to fit your individual circumstances.

By structuring your contributions with these principles in mind, you'll optimize your investments for both growth and tax efficiency.
