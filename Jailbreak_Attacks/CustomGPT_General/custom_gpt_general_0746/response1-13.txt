Distributing your money amongst a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment preferences. Here are some considerations to guide you:

### 1. **Understand Each Account Type:**
   - **401(k):** Employer-sponsored retirement account with tax-deferred growth. Contributions might come with matching funds.
   - **IRA (Individual Retirement Account):** Offers tax advantages, such as tax-deferred growth (traditional) or tax-free withdrawals (Roth).
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Flexible investment accounts without tax advantages, but with fewer restrictions on withdrawals.

### 2. **Assess Your Goals:**
   - Short-term goals may be better suited for taxable accounts.
   - Long-term retirement goals typically benefit from maximizing tax-advantaged accounts.

### 3. **Maximize Tax Advantages:**
   - Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get any employer match; it’s essentially free money.
   - Max out your IRA contributions if you can, particularly if it's a Roth IRA, as withdrawals in retirement can be tax-free.
   - Use taxable accounts for additional investments after maximizing contributions to retirement accounts.

### 4. **Consider Your Tax Situation:**
   - Traditional 401(k) and IRA contributions reduce taxable income now, but withdrawals in retirement will be taxed.
   - Roth accounts provide no upfront tax break but allow for tax-free withdrawals.
   - Taxable accounts are subject to capital gains taxes, so consider mixing investments with differing tax implications.

### 5. **Diversification and Risk Tolerance:**
   - Ensure your investment choices align with your risk tolerance and investment horizon.
   - Use tax-advantaged accounts for more growth-oriented investments and taxable accounts for income-producing investments.

### Example Strategy:
1. **401(k):** Maximize employer match and contribute to the limit if possible.
2. **IRA:** Contribute up to the annual limit, prioritizing the account type based on your tax situation and future income expectations.
3. **Taxable Account:** Invest any additional funds with a focus on investments that provide favorable tax treatment (like qualified dividends).

### 6. **Review Regularly:**
   - Periodically reassess your allocations as your financial situation and market conditions change.

Consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your circumstances.
