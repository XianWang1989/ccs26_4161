Distributing your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various factors including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeframe. Here are some general guidelines to help you make informed decisions:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: If your employer offers a 401(k) with matching contributions, contribute enough to get the full match first. This is essentially free money.
   - **IRA**: After maximizing the employer match, consider contributing to a traditional IRA or Roth IRA, depending on your income level and whether you prefer tax-deferred growth (traditional) or tax-free withdrawals (Roth).

2. **Consider Your Tax Situation**:
   - Contributions to a traditional IRA or 401(k) can reduce your taxable income, which might be beneficial if you are in a higher tax bracket now than you expect to be in retirement.
   - Taxable accounts may be beneficial for investments you'll need to access before retirement age, as they allow for more flexibility.

3. **Risk Tolerance and Investment Horizon**:
   - Younger investors may benefit from higher allocations in riskier assets, which could be more beneficial in tax-advantaged accounts due to potential growth.
   - If you expect to need funds soon, keeping them in a taxable account may provide better access without penalties.

4. **Diversification of Investments**:
   - Ensure a balanced distribution of your investments (stocks, bonds, etc.) across all accounts based on your risk tolerance and goals.

5. **Withdrawal Strategy**:
   - Consider how you will withdraw money in retirement. Generally, it may make sense to withdraw from taxable accounts first to allow tax-advantaged accounts to continue growing. However, this strategy can vary based on your tax situation at that time.

6. **Consult with a Financial Advisor**:
   - Given the complexity of tax codes and personal finance, consulting with a financial advisor can help tailor a strategy that best suits your individual situation.

In summary, start with maximizing contributions to tax-advantaged accounts, consider your current and future tax impacts, and think about your overall investment strategy and necessary liquidity in your planning.
