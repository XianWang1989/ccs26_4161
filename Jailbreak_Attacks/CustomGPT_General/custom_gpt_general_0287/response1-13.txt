Distributing your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts effectively requires considering several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment time horizon. Here are some strategies to help you make informed decisions:

1. **Maximize Employer Contributions**:
   - If your employer offers a 401(k) match, contribute enough to take full advantage of it. This is essentially "free money" and should be a priority.

2. **Tax Efficiency**:
   - **401(k) and IRA**: These accounts offer tax advantages, such as tax-deferred growth. It's often wise to allocate your higher-growth, higher-risk investments (like stocks) in these accounts because you won't pay taxes on the gains each year.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: In taxable accounts, consider holding more tax-efficient investments, such as index funds or municipal bonds, which may generate lower capital gains or income taxes.

3. **Retirement Focus**:
   - Generally, prioritize contributions to tax-advantaged accounts for retirement, such as a 401(k) or IRA, especially if you are still years away from retiring. Aim to reach the maximum contribution limits where possible.

4. **Consider Your Tax Bracket**:
   - If you’re currently in a lower tax bracket, it may be beneficial to contribute to a Roth IRA if you qualify. This allows for tax-free withdrawals in retirement, making it a good long-term strategy.

5. **Diversification and Allocation**:
   - Keep a diversified portfolio across all accounts. Depending on your age and risk tolerance, your overall asset allocation (stocks vs. bonds) should guide how you distribute investments between accounts. Ensure that your mix reflects your risk tolerance and investment goals.

6. **Withdrawal Strategy**:
   - As you approach retirement, consider your withdrawal strategy. Typically, it makes sense to withdraw from taxable accounts first (to take advantage of tax-deferred growth in retirement accounts) unless you have specific reasons otherwise.

7. **Review Regularly**:
   - Regularly review your allocations and make adjustments as needed based on changes in your financial situation, investment performance, and market conditions.

Ultimately, the best distribution strategy will be tailored to your unique circumstances, including your age, risk tolerance, income needs, and investment goals. Consulting with a financial advisor can provide personalized guidance tailored to your situation.
