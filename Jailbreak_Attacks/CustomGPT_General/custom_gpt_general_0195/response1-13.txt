Determining the best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment strategy. Here’s a general overview:

### 1. **401(k)**
   - **Employer Match**: Contribute enough to receive any employer matching, as it’s essentially "free money."
   - **Higher Contribution Limits**: Maximize your contributions up to the limit if possible.
   - **Tax Advantages**: Contributions are pre-tax, which lowers your taxable income now.

### 2. **IRA (Traditional/Roth)**
   - **Contributions**: Consider maxing out your IRA contributions. A Roth IRA allows for tax-free withdrawals in retirement, which can be beneficial if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket later.
   - **Tax Diversification**: Having both traditional and Roth IRAs can provide flexibility in retirement withdrawal strategies.

### 3. **Taxable Accounts**
   - **Flexibility**: These accounts offer more liquidity and no contribution limits, making them useful for short-term goals and emergencies.
   - **Tax Efficiency**: Consider investing in tax-efficient funds or strategies (like index funds) to minimize capital gains taxes.

### **General Strategy**
- Start with the 401(k) to get the full employer match.
- Then, consider maxing out your IRA for tax advantages.
- Finally, allocate any additional savings to your taxable accounts for flexibility and potential growth.

### **Considerations**
- **Time Horizon**: Longer time horizons may favor more aggressive investments in retirement accounts.
- **Income Level**: Your current and expected future income can affect whether you prioritize tax-deferred or tax-free growth accounts.
- **Investment Goals**: Align your asset allocation with your financial goals—retirement, purchasing a home, education, etc.

Consulting with a financial advisor can help you tailor this strategy to your specific circumstances.
