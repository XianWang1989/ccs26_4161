Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and timeline for withdrawals. Here's a general approach:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match; this is essentially free money.
   - **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**: Consider maxing out your IRA contributions after your 401(k) to take advantage of tax benefits.

2. **Consider Your Tax Situation**:
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for investments that yield lower tax burdens, like long-term capital gains or municipal bonds.

3. **Balance Growth and Safety**:
   - Allocate more to high-growth investments (stocks) in accounts where you won’t need immediate withdrawals, like 401(k)s and IRAs.
   - Use taxable accounts for more conservative investments (bonds or cash) for liquidity needs.

4. **Future Withdrawals**:
   - Think about when you’ll need access to your funds. Taxable accounts are more flexible for short-term needs, while retirement accounts are better for long-term growth.

5. **Rebalance Regularly**:
   - Review and adjust your allocations annually based on performance and changing needs.

6. **Consult a Professional**:
   - Personal finance is nuanced; consider consulting a financial advisor for tailored advice.

By following these guidelines, you can create a balanced strategy that aligns with your financial goals.
