Distributing your money amongst a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some strategies to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a matching contribution for your 401(k), contribute enough to get the full match. This is essentially "free money" and should be your first priority.

2. **Utilize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**: After maximizing the match, consider contributing to an IRA (traditional or Roth, depending on your eligibility and tax situation). These accounts provide tax benefits that can help grow your retirement savings more efficiently.

3. **Balance Contributions According to Tax Implications**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are made pre-tax, lowering your taxable income. Good if you expect to be in a lower tax bracket in retirement.
   - **Traditional IRA**: Contributions can also be tax-deductible, depending on income limits and participation in a workplace retirement plan.
   - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are made after-tax, but withdrawals in retirement can be tax-free. Ideal if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Consider using these for short-term goals or if you’ve maxed out your retirement accounts. They offer more flexibility but are subject to capital gains tax.

4. **Diversification**: Ensure you diversify your investments within these accounts. Have a mix of stocks, bonds, and other assets based on your risk tolerance and investment horizon.

5. **Withdrawal Strategies**: Plan how you will withdraw funds in retirement. Generally, it may be advantageous to withdraw from taxable accounts first to allow tax-advantaged accounts to continue growing.

6. **Regular Reviews**: Continually assess your asset allocation and adjust as necessary, particularly as you approach retirement.

Consider consulting with a financial advisor to customize a strategy that aligns with your specific financial situation and goals.
