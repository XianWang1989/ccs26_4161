Deciding how to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts involves several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment strategy. Here are some general guidelines:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your 401(k) offers an employer match, contribute enough to get the full match first. This is essentially "free money."

2. **Consider Tax Implications**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are generally made pre-tax, and you'll pay taxes upon withdrawal in retirement. This is good if you expect to be in a lower tax bracket in retirement.
   - **IRA**: Depending on whether it’s a Traditional or Roth IRA, you may receive tax benefits now (Traditional) or later (Roth). A Roth IRA allows tax-free withdrawals in retirement, which can be advantageous if you expect your tax rate to increase.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Funds here are accessible without penalties at any time, but you'll pay capital gains taxes on earnings. Consider keeping investments that generate less taxable income (like municipal bonds) in these accounts.

3. **Investment Goals and Time Horizon**:
   - If you're saving for retirement, prioritize tax-advantaged accounts first.
   - For shorter-term goals, use a taxable account for more liquidity and flexibility.

4. **Diversification**: Ensure your investments across all accounts are diversified to manage risk. Don’t just mirror investments across accounts, as this can lead to inefficiencies in your overall portfolio.

5. **Monitor and Adjust**: Regularly review your asset allocation and adjust contributions as needed based on performance, market conditions, and changes in your personal circumstances.

It might also be beneficial to consult a financial adviser for personalized advice tailored to your specific situation.
