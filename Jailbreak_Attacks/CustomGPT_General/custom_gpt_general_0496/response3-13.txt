Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment strategy. Here’s a general approach:

### GPT:
1. **401(k)**:
   - Contribute enough to get any employer match (it’s essentially free money).
   - Max out contributions if possible, as they reduce your taxable income.

2. **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**:
   - Consider a Roth IRA if you qualify; it offers tax-free growth and withdrawals.
   - If you're in a higher tax bracket now, a Traditional IRA can be beneficial for tax deferral.

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - Use these for short-term goals or after maxing out tax-advantaged accounts.
   - Investing in index funds or ETFs can be tax-efficient.

### STAN:
Put your money where it makes sense for your goals! Max out your 401(k) for the match first—don’t leave money on the table! If you can, hit that IRA too, especially a Roth if you're young and want tax-free gains. Afterwards, stash some cash in taxable accounts for flexibility. Diversify your investments across the accounts to minimize taxes and maximize returns. Just make sure you’re aiming for what you want down the line!
