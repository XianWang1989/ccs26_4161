Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment time horizon. Here’s a general approach:

1. **401(k):**
   - **Maximize Employer Match:** Contribute enough to get the full employer match, as it’s free money.
   - **Tax Benefits:** Contributions reduce your taxable income. Consider contributing the maximum allowed if you can.

2. **IRA (Traditional or Roth):**
   - **Traditional IRA:** If you expect to be in a lower tax bracket in retirement, this can be beneficial for tax deductions.
   - **Roth IRA:** Good if you expect to be in the same or a higher tax bracket during retirement, as withdrawals are tax-free.
   - Aim to max out contributions if possible for additional tax advantages.

3. **Taxable Accounts:**
   - **Liquidity and Access:** Ideal for short-term goals (like buying a house) since you can access funds without penalties.
   - **Tax Efficiency:** Focus on long-term investments to minimize capital gains taxes. Consider index funds or ETFs for lower turnover.

### Recommended Strategy:
- **Charge Up Your 401(k):** Prioritize contributions until you get the full match.
- **Fund Your IRA:** Next, maximize IRA contributions based on your tax strategy.
- **Allocate to Taxable Accounts:** Use any remaining funds for taxable accounts, focusing on long-term strategies.

### Periodic Review:
- Reassess your allocation annually or when your financial situation changes to ensure it aligns with your goals. Diversification is key in all account types.

Consulting with a financial advisor can help tailor this strategy to your specific circumstances.
