Determining the best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment time horizon. Here are some key considerations for each account type:

### 1. **401(k)**
   - **Advantages:** Employer match, tax-deferred growth, high contribution limits.
   - **Strategy:** Maximize contributions, especially to get the full employer match. Invest in low-fee index funds or target-date funds aligned with your retirement timeline.

### 2. **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**
   - **Traditional IRA:**
     - **Advantages:** Tax-deferred growth, potential tax deduction on contributions.
     - **Strategy:** Contribute if you expect to be in a lower tax bracket in retirement.
   - **Roth IRA:**
     - **Advantages:** Tax-free withdrawals in retirement, no RMDs (required minimum distributions).
     - **Strategy:** Contribute if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement or want tax-free income later.

### 3. **Taxable Accounts**
   - **Advantages:** Flexibility, no contribution limits, access to funds at any time.
   - **Strategy:** Use for short- to medium-term goals (like buying a home) or if you've maxed out retirement accounts. Invest in tax-efficient funds to minimize capital gains taxes.

### General Distribution Strategy:
- Prioritize contributions to your 401(k) to get any matching contributions.
- Max out an IRA (Roth or Traditional) for tax-advantaged growth.
- Use any additional funds for a taxable account, choosing investments strategically to minimize tax impacts (e.g., holding low-turnover index funds).

### Tailored Approach:
Always consider your overall financial goals, risk tolerance, and time horizon. A financial advisor can help create a personalized plan.
