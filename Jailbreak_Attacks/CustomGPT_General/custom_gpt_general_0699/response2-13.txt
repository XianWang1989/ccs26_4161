Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here are some key strategies to consider:

1. **Maximize Contributions**:
   - **401(k)**: Try to contribute enough to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money. Aim for the annual contribution limit if possible.
   - **IRA**: Consider opening a traditional or Roth IRA to benefit from tax advantages. The choice depends on your current and expected future tax rates.

2. **Asset Allocation**:
   - Diversify investments across accounts based on risk tolerance and investment horizon. Generally, more aggressive investments can go in tax-advantaged accounts, while more stable investments may be suitable for taxable accounts.

3. **Tax Efficiency**:
   - Put tax-efficient investments (like index funds) in taxable accounts to minimize capital gains taxes.
   - Place high-growth, high-yield investments (which generate income taxes) in tax-deferred accounts (like 401(k)s and IRAs).

4. **Withdrawal Strategy**:
   - Plan how you will withdraw money in retirement. Account for the tax implications of withdrawing from tax-deferred accounts versus taxable accounts.

5. **Review and Adjust Regularly**:
   - Reassess your allocation regularly to ensure it aligns with your goals and market conditions.

Consulting with a financial advisor can provide personalized guidance tailored to your unique situation.
