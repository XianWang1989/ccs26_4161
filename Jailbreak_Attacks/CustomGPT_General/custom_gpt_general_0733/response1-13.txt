Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts involves several factors, including your financial goals, tax considerations, and investment strategy. Here are some key points to consider for each account type:

### 1. **401(k)**
   - **Contribution Limits**: Maximize your contributions, especially if your employer offers a match, as this is essentially free money.
   - **Tax Benefits**: Contributions are made pre-tax (traditional 401(k)), which can lower your taxable income now. Consider a Roth 401(k) if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement.

### 2. **IRA (Individual Retirement Account)**
   - **Types of IRAs**: Choose between a traditional IRA (tax-deductible contributions) or a Roth IRA (tax-free withdrawals in retirement). 
   - **Contribution Limits**: For 2024, the limit is $6,500 per year (or $7,500 if you're over 50). Consider maxing this out for tax advantages.

### 3. **Taxable Accounts**
   - **Flexibility**: These accounts can be accessed without penalties, which is helpful for short-term goals or emergencies.
   - **Investment Choices**: You have more flexibility in choosing investments compared to retirement accounts, but be mindful of capital gains taxes when selling assets.

### **Allocation Strategy**
- **Emergency Fund**: Ensure you have a sufficient emergency fund (typically 3-6 months of expenses) before heavily investing in these accounts.
- **Tax Efficiency**: 
  - Consider storing tax-inefficient investments (like bonds) in tax-advantaged accounts (401(k)/IRA) due to their tax impacts.
  - Keep tax-efficient investments (like index funds) in your taxable account.
- **Age and Risk Tolerance**: Your age and risk tolerance should influence your asset allocation. Younger investors might focus on growth-oriented investments in tax-advantaged accounts, while older investors might prioritize income-based strategies or preservation of capital.

### **Actionable Steps**
1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match.
2. **Contribute to IRA**: After 401(k), consider maxing your IRA contributions (traditional or Roth).
3. **Fill Taxable Accounts**: Once you’ve maxed out tax-advantaged accounts, allocate funds to your taxable accounts for flexibility.
