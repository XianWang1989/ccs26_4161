Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, investment timeline, and risk tolerance. Here are some general strategies:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**: If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to get the full match first. This is essentially "free money."

2. **Contribute to IRA**: If you qualify for tax deductions on a traditional IRA or want tax-free growth with a Roth IRA, consider contributing to an IRA after maximizing your 401(k) match.

3. **Tax-efficient Investing in Taxable Accounts**:
   - Use taxable accounts for investments that generate less taxable income, such as index funds or ETFs, which typically have lower capital gains distributions.
   - Consider holding more volatile investments in tax-advantaged accounts to protect against short-term taxes.

4. **Consider Your Tax Bracket**: If you're in a higher tax bracket now than you expect to be in retirement, prioritizing tax-deferred accounts (like a 401(k) or traditional IRA) may be beneficial. Conversely, if you expect your tax rate to be higher in retirement, contributing to a Roth IRA can be a smart move.

5. **Diversification and Asset Allocation**: Ensure you are diversifying across different asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) and adjusting your allocation based on your risk tolerance and investment horizon.

6. **Rebalancing**: Regularly review and rebalance your accounts to maintain your desired asset allocation.
