Distributing your money effectively among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment horizon. Here are some strategies to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a matching contribution for your 401(k), contribute enough to get the full match first. This is essentially "free money."

2. **Consider Tax Advantages**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are pre-tax, which can reduce your taxable income. However, withdrawals during retirement are taxed as ordinary income.
   - **IRA (Traditional)**: Similar to a 401(k), contributions may be tax-deductible, but withdrawals are taxed.
   - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, but qualified withdrawals are tax-free. If you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement, prioritize the Roth IRA.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Interest and short-term capital gains are taxed as ordinary income, while long-term capital gains are taxed at a lower rate. Use this account for more liquid investments or when you expect to need funds before retirement.

3. **Investment Strategy**: Allocate investments based on your risk tolerance and time horizon. Consider keeping more aggressive investments (like stocks) in tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) and IRA) because they may benefit more from tax-deferred growth, while more conservative investments (like bonds) could be suitable for taxable accounts due to lower tax implications.

4. **Rebalance Regularly**: Monitor your allocations periodically and adjust as needed based on market performance and life changes.

5. **Emergency Fund**: Ensure you have an emergency fund outside of these accounts before heavily investing.

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: Tailored advice can be beneficial, especially considering your unique financial situation and goals.

By combining these strategies, you can create a balanced approach to distributing your investments effectively across your accounts.
