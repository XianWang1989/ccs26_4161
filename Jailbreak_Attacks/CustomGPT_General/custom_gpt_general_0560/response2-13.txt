Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some strategies to consider:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money. Aim to max out your contributions if possible ($22,500 in 2023, or $30,000 if you're over 50).
   - **Traditional IRA**: If eligible, contribute up to the annual limit ($6,500 in 2023, or $7,500 if you're 50+). This can provide tax deductions depending on your income.
   - **Roth IRA**: Consider a Roth IRA for tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement. Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, but qualified distributions are tax-free.

2. **Prioritize Debt Management**: If you have high-interest debt, prioritize paying that off before heavily investing in these accounts.

3. **Investment Strategy**:
   - **401(k) and IRAs**: Focus on long-term growth with a diversified portfolio. Consider stocks or ETFs for growth, as these accounts have tax advantages.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for more flexible access to funds. Invest in tax-efficient assets, like index funds or municipal bonds, to minimize tax liabilities.

4. **Emergency Fund**: Maintain an emergency fund in a separate account before heavily investing. This typically covers 3-6 months of expenses.

5. **Adjustments Based on Tax Bracket**:
   - In higher tax brackets, prioritizing tax-deferred accounts (like 401(k) and Traditional IRA) can be advantageous.
   - In lower tax brackets, Roth accounts may offer more benefits as you can lock in your current lower tax rate.

6. **Rebalance Periodically**: Regularly review and adjust your investments based on changing goals, market conditions, and life stages.

Consulting with a financial advisor can also help tailor a plan that's specific to your circumstances.
