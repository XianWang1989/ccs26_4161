Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts effectively depends on several factors, including your age, income, investment goals, and tax situation. Here’s a guiding strategy:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Matches in 401(k)**
   - **Contribution Strategy**: Contribute enough to get any employer matching contributions. This is essentially “free money.”

### 2. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**
   - **IRA Contributions**: If eligible, contribute to a Traditional or Roth IRA (consider tax implications). Roth IRAs offer tax-free growth, while Traditional IRAs can provide tax deductions based on your income.
   - **401(k) Contribution**: After securing employer matches, prioritize maxing out your 401(k) contributions, especially if you’re in a higher tax bracket.

### 3. **Consider Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**
   - **Taxable Investment Accounts**: These can be beneficial for short-term goals, as they offer more liquidity and access to funds without penalties. Focus on investments that potentially yield lower taxes, like index funds and tax-efficient funds.

### 4. **Asset Allocation**
   - **Risk Tolerance**: Allocate assets based on your risk tolerance. Younger investors might choose more stocks for growth, while those closer to retirement may lean towards bonds.
   - **Diversification**: Diversify across asset classes to mitigate risk.

### 5. **Regular Rebalancing**
   - **Review and Adjust**: Regularly review your investment allocations based on market performance and life changes. Adjust contributions accordingly to keep aligned with your goals.

### 6. **Long-Term Perspective**
   - **Time Horizon**: Consider your investment horizon for each account type. Longer time horizons may warrant aggressive investments, while shorter terms might require more conservative strategies.

### 7. **Consult a Financial Advisor**
   - If unsure about the best strategy for your specific situation, consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized advice.

By following these principles, you can create a balanced approach to distributing your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, maximizing growth while managing risks and taxes.
