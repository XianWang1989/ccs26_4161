The optimal distribution of your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your age, income, tax situation, and financial goals. Here’s a strategic approach:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts:**
   - **401(k):** If your employer offers matching contributions, contribute enough to get the full match—this is free money. Aim to max out your contribution if you can afford it, as it reduces your taxable income.
   - **IRA:** Consider a Roth IRA if you expect your tax rate to be higher in retirement, or a Traditional IRA if you prefer tax deductions now. Look to max out contributions here too.

2. **Taxable Accounts:**
   - Use these for funds you may need in the short to medium term, or for investments that may benefit from long-term capital gains treatment. Consider investing in tax-efficient funds or ETFs to minimize tax liabilities.

3. **Prioritize Based on Goals:**
   - If your priority is retirement, focus on maxing out the 401(k) and IRA first. If you’re saving for a short-term goal (like a home purchase), prioritize the taxable account.

4. **Diversify Investments:**
   - Within these accounts, ensure your investments are diversified across asset classes based on your risk tolerance and time horizon.

5. **Regular Rebalancing:**
   - Review your allocations periodically and rebalance if necessary to maintain your desired risk level.

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor:**
   - For personalized advice tailored to your unique financial situation, consulting with a financial advisor can be invaluable.

Ultimately, the best approach balances maximizing growth, minimizing taxes, and aligning with your financial goals.
