The ideal distribution of your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts can depend on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, investment timeline, and risk tolerance. Here are some considerations for each account type:

### 1. **401(k)**
- **Employer Match**: Contribute enough to get the full employer match if available; it’s essentially "free money."
- **Tax Benefits**: Contributions are pre-tax, reducing your taxable income for the year. Consider maxing out your 401(k) if you're in a higher tax bracket.
- **Investment Options**: 401(k)s typically have a limited range of investment options. Assess whether these align with your investment strategy.

### 2. **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**
- **Tax Strategy**: 
  - **Traditional IRA**: Contributions may be tax-deductible, which can be beneficial if you anticipate being in a lower tax bracket in retirement.
  - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, allowing for tax-free withdrawals in retirement. This is ideal if you believe your taxes will be higher in the future.
- **Contribution Limits**: Make sure you’re aware of annual contribution limits to make the most of these accounts.

### 3. **Taxable Accounts**
- **Flexibility**: These accounts provide the most flexibility for access to funds without penalties and can be a good option for shorter-term goals.
- **Tax Implications**: Be mindful of capital gains taxes; long-term investments held for a year or more typically incur lower rates.
- **Investment Strategy**: This is a good place for investments that might produce taxable income (like bonds) if managed appropriately.

### General Distribution Approach
1. **Emergency Fund**: Before allocating, ensure you have an adequate emergency fund.
2. **Max Out Matching**: Prioritize your 401(k) contributions to at least get the full employer match.
3. **Consider Tax Efficacy**: Depending on your tax bracket and future expectations, it may be wise to allocate funds between a traditional IRA and a Roth IRA.
4. **Diversify Accounts**: Invest in a mix of tax-advantaged and taxable accounts to maintain flexibility and tax efficiency.
5. **Rebalance Annually**: Reassess your allocations annually based on changing circumstances, goals, and market conditions.

### Customization
Your personal distribution will ultimately depend on your financial situation. Consider consulting with a financial advisor to tailor a strategy to your specific needs and goals.
