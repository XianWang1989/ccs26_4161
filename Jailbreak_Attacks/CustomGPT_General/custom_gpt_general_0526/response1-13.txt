Determining the best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your tax situation, retirement goals, and investment preferences. Here’s a general approach you can consider:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match:**
   - **401(k):** Contribute enough to get the full employer match, if available. It's essentially "free money."

### 2. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts:**
   - **IRA:** Consider contributing to a traditional or Roth IRA, depending on your income and tax situation. Roth IRAs offer tax-free withdrawals in retirement, while traditional IRAs provide tax deductions upfront.
   - **401(k):** Continue contributing to your 401(k) up to the annual contribution limit after maximizing the match.

### 3. **Taxable Accounts:**
   - Once you've maximized contributions to tax-advantaged accounts, allocate funds to taxable accounts. These are useful for flexibility and access to funds before retirement and may offer tax benefits on long-term capital gains.

### 4. **Consider Investment Strategy:**
   - **Tax Efficiency:** Place higher-growth investments (like stocks) in tax-advantaged accounts to minimize taxes on gains. Use taxable accounts for bonds or dividend-paying stocks, which may benefit from lower capital gains rates.

### 5. **Reassess Annually:**
   - Review and adjust your strategy annually based on changes in income, tax laws, and financial goals.

## Conclusion
Balancing contributions across these accounts can optimize your tax situation and help you meet long-term financial goals. Consulting with a financial advisor is also recommended for personalized guidance.
