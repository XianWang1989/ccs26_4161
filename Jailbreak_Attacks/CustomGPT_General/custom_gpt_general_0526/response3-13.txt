The optimal distribution of your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your tax bracket, retirement goals, and investment strategy. Here's a general approach:

### 1. **Maximize Contributions to Tax-Advantaged Accounts:**
   - **401(k):** Contribute at least enough to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money. If possible, aim for the maximum annual limit.
   - **IRA (Traditional or Roth):** Consider maxing out contributions to an IRA next. A Roth IRA offers tax-free withdrawals in retirement, while a Traditional IRA provides tax deductions now.

### 2. **Taxable Accounts:**
   - Use taxable accounts for investments that may have higher turnover or generate significant capital gains, as you will be taxed on these when realized. This can be beneficial for holding long-term investments that provide qualified dividends, which are taxed at a lower rate.

### 3. **Allocation Strategy:**
   - **Risk Assessment:** Ensure your allocation reflects your risk tolerance. Generally, younger investors can afford a more aggressive approach, while those closer to retirement may prefer a conservative strategy.
   - **Diverse Investments:** Maintain a diversified portfolio across all accounts. 

### 4. **Tax Considerations:**
   - **Taxable Investment Types:** Use tax-efficient funds or ETFs in taxable accounts. Avoid holding high-yield bonds, REITs, or actively managed funds that may generate substantial capital gains.
   - **Harvesting Tax Losses:** Consider strategies like tax-loss harvesting in taxable accounts to offset gains.

### 5. **Regular Review:**
   - Periodically assess your investment performance and adjust contributions and allocations as needed based on changes in your financial situation or goals.

By following this framework, you can strategically grow your investments while minimizing taxes and maximizing returns. Always consider consulting with a financial advisor to tailor a plan specific to your situation.
