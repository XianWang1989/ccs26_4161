To effectively distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Maximize Employer Contributions**: Start with your 401(k). Contribute enough to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money.

2. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: If your employer offers a match, prioritize this account until you reach the match limit.
   - **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**: Depending on your income level and tax situation, consider maxing out your IRA contributions next. Traditional IRAs offer tax-deferred growth, while Roth IRAs provide tax-free withdrawals in retirement.

3. **Assess Tax Implications for Investments**:
   - Use the 401(k) and IRA for investments that could generate high taxes (e.g., bonds or REITs).
   - Keep investments with favorable tax treatments (like long-term capital gains) in taxable accounts.

4. **Rebalance Regularly**: Monitor your portfolio across all accounts and rebalance to maintain your desired asset allocation.

5. **Consider Future Needs**: Think about your retirement timeline and withdrawal strategies. Taxable accounts provide flexibility for withdrawals, making them useful for near-term needs.

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: For personalized advice, consider consulting a financial advisor to align your investment strategy with your financial goals.

This approach aims for a balanced distribution, maximizing tax efficiency and aligning with your retirement objectives.
