Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts can depend on various factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment strategy. Here’s a general guideline to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**: If your employer offers a 401(k) match, contribute enough to get the full match as it is essentially "free money."

2. **Contribute to IRAs**: After maximizing your 401(k) match, consider contributing to an IRA (Traditional or Roth) depending on your tax situation. Roth IRAs offer tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement, while Traditional IRAs may provide tax deductions now.

3. **Consider Taxable Accounts**: After maximizing contributions to tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) and IRA), consider funding a taxable account for added flexibility. Taxable accounts allow for easier access to funds and can be beneficial for shorter-term goals.

4. **Diversification**: Within each account type, diversify your investments across stocks, bonds, and other assets to manage risk.

5. **Reassess Regularly**: Periodically review your asset allocation and adjust as needed based on changes in your financial situation, goals, and market conditions.

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: If unsure, consider consulting a financial advisor to help tailor a plan specific to your situation.

It's essential to evaluate your overall financial picture, including retirement age, income needs, and risk tolerance, before making decisions.
