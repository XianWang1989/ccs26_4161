The best way to allocate your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts is influenced by several factors, including your age, risk tolerance, retirement goals, and tax situation. Here are some general principles to consider:

1. **401(k):** 
   - If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to get the full match, as it’s essentially free money.
   - Consider maximizing your contributions if you are in a lower tax bracket now than you expect to be in retirement, as the tax-deferred growth can be beneficial.

2. **IRA (Traditional or Roth):**
   - If you qualify for a Roth IRA, consider contributing to it for tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement, especially if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket later.
   - A Traditional IRA can be a good option if you want the upfront tax deduction, but keep in mind you’ll pay taxes on withdrawals in retirement.

3. **Taxable Accounts:**
   - These accounts can offer flexibility for short-term financial goals and are appropriate for funds you might need access to before retirement.
   - Consider using these accounts for investments that generate favorable long-term capital gains or qualified dividends to minimize tax liability.

**Strategy:**
- **Diversify:** Ensure a balanced mix of asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) across all accounts based on your risk tolerance and investment timeline.
- **Tax Considerations:** Pay attention to tax implications of investment choices in each account type; for instance, avoid holding high-turnover funds in taxable accounts to minimize capital gains taxes.
- **Regular Rebalancing:** Periodically review and rebalance your portfolio to maintain your desired asset allocation.

Ultimately, it's advisable to consult a financial advisor to tailor these strategies to your specific circumstances.
