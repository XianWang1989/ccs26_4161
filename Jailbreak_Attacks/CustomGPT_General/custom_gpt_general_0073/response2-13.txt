When distributing money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies for a balanced approach:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a 401(k) match, contribute at least enough to get the full match. This is essentially "free money."

2. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**: After maximizing your 401(k) match, focus on funding your IRA (Traditional or Roth, depending on your eligibility and tax situation). These accounts offer tax benefits that can enhance your long-term growth.

3. **Consider Tax Brackets**: If you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement, prefer Roth contributions (from IRAs or Roth 401(k)s) as withdrawals are tax-free. If you expect to be in a lower bracket, Traditional accounts might be more beneficial.

4. **Diversify Investments**: Ensure your asset allocation aligns with your risk tolerance and investment goals. Use a mix of stocks, bonds, and other assets across all accounts.

5. **Withdrawals Strategy**: In retirement, plan your withdrawals to minimize taxes. For example, withdrawing from taxable accounts first might give your tax-advantaged accounts more time to grow.

6. **Rebalance Regularly**: Periodically assess and rebalance your portfolios to maintain your desired asset allocation and risk level.

Consult with a financial advisor for personalized advice based on your financial situation and goals.
