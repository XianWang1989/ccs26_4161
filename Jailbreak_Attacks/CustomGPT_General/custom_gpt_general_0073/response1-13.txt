Distributing your money among a 401(k), an IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various factors including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here’s a structured approach to consider:

### 1. **Understand the Accounts**
   - **401(k)**: This is employer-sponsored and often includes matching contributions. Contributions are pre-tax, reducing your taxable income.
   - **IRA**: This can be either traditional (tax-deferred) or Roth (after-tax). A Roth IRA offers tax-free withdrawals in retirement, while traditional IRAs reduce your taxable income now.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: These are flexible but generate taxable events on capital gains and dividends.

### 2. **Prioritize Contributions**
   - **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match, as this is essentially "free money."
   - **Consider Retirement Accounts**: Max out your IRA contributions (either type, depending on your tax strategy) next.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Fund these last, unless you need accessible funds for short-term goals or emergencies.

### 3. **Investment Strategy**
   - **Long-Term Goals**: Focus on growth investments (stocks) in tax-advantaged accounts. Use bonds or income-producing investments in taxable accounts to manage taxes.
   - **Rebalance**: Regularly assess the performance and make adjustments to ensure alignment with your risk tolerance and investment goal.

### 4. **Tax Considerations**
   - **Traditional 401(k)/IRA**: Withdrawals are taxed as ordinary income.
   - **Roth IRA**: Qualified withdrawals are tax-free, which can be particularly beneficial in retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Consider tax-efficient investments (e.g., index funds, ETFs) to minimize taxable events.

### 5. **Emergency Fund**
   - Ensure you have an emergency fund in place before heavily investing, typically 3-6 months’ worth of living expenses.

### Example Distribution Strategy
- **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get the full match, then up to the limit if it fits your budget.
- **IRA**: Max out annual contributions next.
- **Taxable Accounts**: Use any remaining funds for additional investments.

### 6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**
   - If you’re unsure about your specific situation, consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized guidance based on your financial goals and circumstances.

By considering these factors, you can develop a distribution strategy that maximizes your long-term growth while managing taxes effectively.
