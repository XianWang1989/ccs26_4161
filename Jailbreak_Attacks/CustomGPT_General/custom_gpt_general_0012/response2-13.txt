Distributing money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and time horizon. Here are some general strategies:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**
   - **401(k):** If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to get the full match first. This is essentially free money.
   - **IRA:** Consider maxing out your traditional or Roth IRA. Roth IRAs provide tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement, while traditional IRAs give you a tax deduction now.
  
### 2. **Assess Your Tax Situation**
   - **Higher Tax Bracket:** Focus on tax-deferred accounts (401(k) and traditional IRA) to lower your taxable income.
   - **Lower Tax Bracket:** Contributing to a Roth IRA may be beneficial for tax-free growth.

### 3. **Consider Your Time Horizon**
   - **Short-Term Needs:** Use taxable accounts for funds you might need in the next few years, as they provide more flexibility.
   - **Long-Term Growth:** Prioritize 401(k) and IRA contributions for retirement savings.

### 4. **Diversification**
   - Ensure diversification across various asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) within each account type, adjusting for risk based on your age and risk tolerance.

### 5. **Rebalance Periodically**
   - Regularly review your allocations and adjust as needed based on market performance and changes in your financial situation.

### 6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**
   - A professional can provide personalized advice tailored to your unique financial situation and goals.

By following these strategies, you can create a balanced approach to managing your accounts effectively.
