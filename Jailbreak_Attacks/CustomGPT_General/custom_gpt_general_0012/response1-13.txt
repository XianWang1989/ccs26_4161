Distributing your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment time horizon. Here are some strategies to consider:

### 1. **Tax Considerations:**
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are typically made pre-tax, reducing your taxable income. Withdrawals in retirement will be taxed at your ordinary income rate.
   - **IRA**: Traditional IRAs also offer pre-tax contributions, while Roth IRAs allow for tax-free growth and tax-free withdrawals in retirement, provided certain conditions are met.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: These accounts do not have tax advantages. You'll pay capital gains tax on investments sold for a profit and taxes on dividends and interest.

### 2. **Investment Goals:**
   - **Long-term Growth:** Max out tax-advantaged accounts first (401(k) and IRA) to maximize tax-free or tax-deferred growth.
   - **Accessible Funds:** Use taxable accounts for savings or investments you may need access to before retirement.

### 3. **Contribution Limits:**
   - Ensure you know the annual contribution limits for each account:
     - **401(k):** Up to $22,500 for individuals under 50 (as of 2023).
     - **IRA:** Up to $6,500, with catch-up contributions for those 50 and older.

### 4. **Diversification:**
   - Diversify your investments across different asset classes (stocks, bonds, real estate) within each account type to manage risk.

### 5. **Rebalancing:**
   - Periodically review and adjust your asset allocation to align with your risk tolerance and investment objectives.

### Sample Allocation Strategy:
- **Max out 401(k) up to employer match:** Fund this first to take advantage of any employer contributions.
- **Contribute to IRA:** If eligible, consider funding a Roth IRA for tax-free growth.
- **Use taxable accounts for additional investments:** After maxing out tax-advantaged accounts, invest in taxable accounts for greater flexibility.

### Final Thoughts:
Consult with a financial advisor to tailor your strategy to your specific situation, ensuring you consider your tax bracket, risk tolerance, and retirement timeline.
