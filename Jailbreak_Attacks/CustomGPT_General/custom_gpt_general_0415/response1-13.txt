Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here’s a general strategy for each type of account:

1. **401(k)**:
   - Maximize employer match: Contribute enough to get the full employer match, as this is essentially free money.
   - Consider tax implications: Contributions are typically pre-tax, reducing your taxable income now. Think about your current vs. expected future tax rate.

2. **IRA (Roth or Traditional)**:
   - Choose based on tax status: If you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement, consider a Roth IRA, as withdrawals are tax-free. A Traditional IRA can be beneficial if you need to reduce your taxable income now.
   - Max out contributions: If possible, reach the annual contribution limit.

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - Use for short-term goals: If you anticipate needing funds in the near future (such as for a house purchase or major expense), allocate money here.
   - Focus on tax-efficient investments: Choose investments that generate lower taxable income, like index funds or ETFs, to minimize tax burden.

### General Guidelines:
- **Emergency Fund**: Ensure you have a solid emergency fund (3-6 months of expenses) before heavily investing.
- **Diversification**: Diversify your investments across all account types to balance risk.
- **Rebalance as Needed**: Regularly review and adjust your asset allocation based on performance and changing goals.

Consider consulting with a financial advisor for personalized advice based on your specific financial situation and goals.
