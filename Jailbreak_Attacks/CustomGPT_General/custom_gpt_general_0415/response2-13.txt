Distributing your money effectively among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here’s a general strategy to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your 401(k) offers a matching contribution, prioritize contributing enough to get the full match. This is essentially free money.

2. **Consider Tax Implications**:
   - **401(k) and Traditional IRA**: Contributions can lower your taxable income now, and taxes are paid upon withdrawal. Ideal if you expect to be in a lower tax bracket during retirement.
   - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, but withdrawals (including earnings) are tax-free in retirement. This is advantageous if you expect higher taxes in the future.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Invest in low-turnover strategies or tax-efficient funds to minimize capital gains taxes. Use this for short-term goals or if you have already maxed out tax-advantaged accounts.

3. **Diversify Investments**: Across all accounts, ensure a diversified asset allocation that reflects your risk tolerance and timeline. Consider stocks for growth, bonds for stability, and cash equivalents for liquidity.

4. **Rebalance Regularly**: Adjust your allocations periodically to maintain your desired level of risk and adjust for changes in financial goals or market conditions.

5. **Emergency Fund**: Ensure you have accessible cash for emergencies outside of these accounts to avoid penalties for early withdrawals.

A tailored approach is essential, so consider consulting with a financial advisor to align your strategy with your personal situation.
