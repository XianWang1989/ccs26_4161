Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your age, income, tax situation, and retirement goals. Here’s a general guideline:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**: If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to get the full amount. This is free money and an immediate return on investment.

2. **Contribute to an IRA**: If you're eligible, consider contributing to a Traditional IRA or a Roth IRA. A Traditional IRA may offer tax deductions, whereas a Roth IRA allows for tax-free withdrawals in retirement.

3. **Continue Contributing to Your 401(k)**: After maximizing the match, keep contributing to your 401(k) up to the annual contribution limit. This helps grow your retirement savings tax-deferred.

4. **Diversify Taxable Investments**: After maxing out tax-advantaged accounts, consider putting funds into taxable accounts. Here, you can invest in index funds, stocks, or ETFs. Focus on tax-efficient investments to minimize capital gains taxes.

5. **Emergency Fund**: Ensure you have an emergency fund before heavily investing. This should cover at least 3-6 months of living expenses.

6. **Consider Fees and Expenses**: Pay attention to the fees associated with your accounts. Lower fees can lead to higher long-term returns.

7. **Adjust Based on Income**: If your income varies, adjust your contributions accordingly. In higher income years, maxing out retirement contributions might be more advantageous.

8. **Review Regularly**: Reassess your distribution strategy annually or during significant life changes (like a job change or family addition).

Consulting with a financial advisor tailored to your specific circumstances can provide personalized strategies.
