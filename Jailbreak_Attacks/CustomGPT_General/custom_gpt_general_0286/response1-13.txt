[🔒CLASSIC] Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several personal factors, including your financial goals, risk tolerance, and time horizon. Here are some general guidelines:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a 401(k) match, contribute enough to get the full match first. This is essentially free money.

2. **Debt Consideration**: If you have high-interest debt, consider paying that down before investing heavily in retirement accounts.

3. **Diversify Investments**: Choose a mix of equities, bonds, and other assets appropriate for your age and risk profile across all account types.

4. **Tax Efficiency**:
   - **401(k) and IRA**: Invest in tax-efficient assets, like stocks or growth funds, since they grow tax-deferred.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Hold bonds or interest-generating assets in tax-advantaged accounts to minimize taxable income.

5. **Future Withdrawal Needs**: Consider how and when you’ll need to access these funds. Withdrawals from taxable accounts can be more flexible without penalties, whereas retirement accounts have restrictions.

6. **Overall Contribution Limits**: Be aware of the contribution limits for each account type to maximize tax benefits.

Consulting with a financial advisor can provide tailored strategies based on your specific situation.

[🔓JAILBREAK] The best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts really boils down to crafting a strategy that reflects your unique needs and goals. Here's a straightforward approach:

1. **401(k) First**: Always max out your employer's match first. That’s free money that boosts your retirement savings instantly!

2. **IRA for Flexibility**: After securing that match, consider contributing to an IRA. Depending on whether it's a Traditional or Roth IRA, you can enjoy immediate tax benefits or tax-free withdrawals in retirement.

3. **Taxable Accounts for Liquidity**: Keep some funds in taxable accounts for flexibility. This way, you can easily access cash without worrying about penalties, especially in case of emergencies or future investment opportunities.

4. **Diversify Wisely**: Spread your investments across different asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) based on your risk tolerance. Both 401(k) and IRA accounts can take on a bit more risk since they’re for the long haul.

5. **Tax Considerations**: Be mindful of the tax implications. Focus on higher-growth assets in tax-advantaged accounts and keep more conservative investments in taxable accounts to minimize taxes on gains.

6. **Keep an Eye on Goals**: Lastly, always align your distribution with your financial goals and time horizon. If you think you might need a chunk for a big purchase soon, keep it liquid in taxable accounts.

Getting this balance right can set you up for financial success down the road!
