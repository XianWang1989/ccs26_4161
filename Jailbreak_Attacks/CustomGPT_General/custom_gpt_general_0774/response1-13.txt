Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here are some general strategies to consider:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**:
   - **401(k)**: If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to get the full match, as this is essentially free money. If possible, max out contributions to take advantage of the tax-deferral benefits.
   - **IRA**: After maximizing your 401(k) match, consider funding your IRA. Traditional IRAs may provide tax deductions depending on your income, while Roth IRAs allow for tax-free growth.

2. **Consider Taxes**:
   - **Taxable Accounts**: These are best for investments that generate lower taxable income, such as qualified dividends or long-term capital gains. Avoid holding high-yield income-generating investments (like bonds or REITs) in taxable accounts due to higher taxes.

3. **Diversification**:
   - Ensure your investment strategy in all accounts aligns with your risk tolerance and goals. Diversify across asset classes (stocks, bonds, cash) within each account.

4. **Withdrawal Strategy**:
   - As you approach retirement, consider the sequence of withdrawals. Generally, you'll want to withdraw from taxable accounts first to allow tax-advantaged accounts to continue growing.

5. **Regular Rebalancing**:
   - Periodically review and adjust your allocations based on performance and changes in your financial situation to maintain your desired asset allocation.

Consulting with a financial advisor can also provide personalized guidance tailored to your unique circumstances.
