Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here are some strategies to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a matching contribution for your 401(k), prioritize contributions there up to the match limit. This is essentially "free money."

2. **Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**: Focus on maxing out contributions to tax-advantaged accounts like your 401(k) and IRA before investing heavily in taxable accounts. The tax benefits can significantly enhance your investment growth.

   - **401(k)**: Contributions are pre-tax, reducing your taxable income now. Consider a Roth 401(k) if you anticipate being in a higher tax bracket during retirement.
   - **IRA**: Traditional IRAs offer tax-deferred growth, while Roth IRAs provide tax-free withdrawals if certain conditions are met.

3. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**: After maxing out tax-advantaged accounts, consider taxable accounts for additional flexibility. These accounts can be accessed without penalties before retirement age.

4. **Diversification and Asset Allocation**: Ensure your investments are diversified across asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) and aligned with your risk tolerance. Adjust the allocation based on the account type:
   - **Taxable accounts**: Favor stocks (which can benefit from long-term capital gains tax rates) and tax-efficient investments like index funds or ETFs.
   - **Tax-advantaged accounts**: You might hold more bonds or higher-growth investments since taxes are deferred or not applicable.

5. **Rebalance Periodically**: Regularly review and rebalance your portfolio to maintain your desired asset allocation and take into account any changes in your financial situation or goals.

6. **Consider Tax Implications**: Factor in your current and expected future tax rates. Shifting investments to tax-free accounts could be beneficial if you expect higher taxes in the future.

7. **Retirement Timeline**: As you get closer to retirement, you may want to adjust your allocation to become more conservative, especially in your 401(k) and IRA.

It’s often helpful to consult with a financial advisor to tailor a distribution strategy that meets your specific goals and circumstances.
