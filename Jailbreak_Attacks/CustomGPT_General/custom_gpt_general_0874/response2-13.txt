Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts is an important aspect of financial planning and depends on your individual goals, age, risk tolerance, and the specific investments available in each account. Here are some general guidelines to consider:

1. **401(k) Accounts**: 
   - If your employer offers a match, prioritize contributing enough to get the full match before allocating funds elsewhere. This is essentially free money.
   - Max out contributions if possible, especially if you're in your peak earning years, to take advantage of tax-deferred growth.

2. **IRA Accounts**: 
   - Consider contributing to a traditional IRA for tax deductions if you qualify, or a Roth IRA if you want tax-free withdrawals in retirement.
   - If eligible, maximize your contributions to take advantage of the tax benefits.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: 
   - Use taxable accounts for investments that may have a lower turnover and less frequent buying/selling, to minimize capital gains taxes.
   - If you have maxed out your retirement accounts, consider investing in taxable accounts for more immediate liquidity or specific financial goals.

### Suggested Distribution Strategy:
- **Young Investors (Under 40)**: Focus on maxing out your 401(k) and Roth IRA for long-term growth, then use taxable accounts as needed.
- **Mid-Life Investors (40-60)**: Balance between maxing out 401(k) and IRA contributions while also increasing investments in taxable accounts for flexibility.
- **Near Retirement**: Prioritize 401(k) and traditional IRA for tax-deferred growth but gradually start shifting some assets into taxable accounts for retirement spending.

### Final Tips:
- **Adjust Based on Income**: If your income fluctuates, adapt your contributions accordingly to ensure you're maximizing tax benefits throughout your working years.
- **Rebalance Regularly**: Review and adjust your allocation annually based on market performance, your risk tolerance, and changing goals.

Consulting with a financial advisor can help tailor this strategy to your specific circumstances.
