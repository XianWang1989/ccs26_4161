The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several key factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment strategy. Here’s a general approach to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k):** If your employer offers a matching contribution, aim to contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full match, as this is essentially free money.

2. **Contribute to an IRA:** If you're eligible, consider maxing out your IRA contributions next. Traditional IRAs offer tax-deferred growth, while Roth IRAs allow for tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement. Choose the type based on your current tax situation and expected tax rate in retirement.

3. **Consider Tax Efficiency:** Investments in taxable accounts are subject to capital gains taxes, so it’s often better to hold tax-efficient investments (like index funds) in these accounts. It’s wise to place higher-tax investments, such as bonds or actively managed funds, in tax-advantaged accounts.

4. **Diversify Investments:** Within each account, ensure your investments are diversified to manage risk. This includes a mix of stocks, bonds, and other asset classes based on your risk tolerance and time horizon.

5. **Rebalance Regularly:** Over time, your asset allocation may shift due to market performance. Regularly review and rebalance your portfolio to maintain your target allocation.

6. **Pay Attention to Withdrawals:** In retirement, consider which accounts to withdraw from first to minimize taxes and maximize the longevity of your investments. Generally, it’s advantageous to withdraw from taxable accounts before tax-deferred accounts.

Ultimately, consider consulting with a financial advisor to tailor a strategy to your specific situation, as they can provide personalized advice that takes into account your full financial picture.
