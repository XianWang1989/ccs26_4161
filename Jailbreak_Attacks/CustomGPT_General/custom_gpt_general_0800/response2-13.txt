When it comes to distributing your money among 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies to maximize your investment potential and tax efficiency:

1. **401(k) Contributions**: Maximize your contributions up to the employer matching limit if available. This is essentially free money and should be a priority. If you have additional funds, consider contributing the maximum limit to take advantage of tax-deferred growth.

2. **IRA Contributions**: If you're eligible, contribute to a Traditional or Roth IRA. A Traditional IRA offers upfront tax deductions, while a Roth IRA provides tax-free withdrawals in retirement. The choice depends on your current tax situation and anticipated tax bracket at retirement.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: After maximizing contributions to tax-advantaged accounts, use taxable accounts for additional investments, especially for goals in the near term. Invest in index funds or ETFs, which can be more tax-efficient due to lower capital gains distributions.

4. **Asset Location Strategy**: Consider which types of investments to hold in each type of account. For example, place high-growth investments (like stocks) in tax-advantaged accounts to shelter dividends and long-term gains from taxes. Conversely, keep more tax-efficient investments (like bonds or stable-value funds) in taxable accounts.

5. **Rebalance Portfolio**: Regularly review and adjust your asset allocation across all accounts to maintain your desired risk level and investment strategy.

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: Depending on your financial situation and goals, working with a financial advisor can provide personalized strategies tailored to your needs.

Balancing the distribution wisely between these accounts can help you optimize growth while managing tax implications effectively.
