The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some guidelines to consider:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**
   - **401(k)**: If your employer offers a match, contribute at least enough to get the full match. It's essentially "free money."
   - **IRA**: After maximizing your 401(k) match, consider contributing to a Traditional or Roth IRA, depending on your eligibility and tax situation.

### 2. **Consider Tax Implications**
   - **Tax-deferred Accounts (401(k) and Traditional IRA)**: Contributions reduce your taxable income now, but you will owe taxes upon withdrawal.
   - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, allowing for tax-free withdrawals in retirement, which can be beneficial if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket later.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: These are more flexible for withdrawals, but you’ll owe taxes on capital gains and dividends. Use these for goals that are shorter-term.

### 3. **Diversify Investments**
   - Allocate your investments across a mix of asset classes (stocks, bonds, cash) in all accounts. Typically, higher-risk assets can go in tax-advantaged accounts, while more conservative investments can be placed in taxable accounts for better tax efficiency.

### 4. **Monitor and Adjust**
   - Regularly review and adjust your asset allocation based on market conditions and personal circumstances.

### 5. **Consult a Financial Advisor**
   - For personalized advice tailored to your specific situation, working with a financial advisor can provide valuable insights and strategies.

This diversified approach can help optimize your tax situation and investment returns over time.
