To effectively distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**:
   - Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match. This is essentially "free money."

### 2. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **IRA Contributions**: If eligible, consider contributing to a Roth IRA for tax-free growth or a Traditional IRA for potential tax deductions.
   - **401(k)**: After employer match, consider maximizing contributions to your 401(k), especially if it has lower fees.

### 3. **Assess Tax Impact**:
   - Taxable accounts can be beneficial for short-term needs but may involve capital gains taxes. Keep investments that generate income (like bonds) in tax-advantaged accounts.

### 4. **Diversification**:
   - Ensure a diversified investment strategy across all accounts. Adjust allocations based on your risk tolerance.

### 5. **Emergency Funds**:
   - Maintain an emergency fund in a high-yield savings account separate from these accounts.

### 6. **Regular Review**:
   - Regularly review your asset allocation and contributions, adjusting as necessary based on changes in income, expenses, or financial goals.

### Example Allocation:
- **401(k)**: Max out employer match, then contribute up to limit.
- **IRA**: Contribute (Traditional or Roth based on tax situation).
- **Taxable Account**: Invest any remaining savings or for short-term goals.

### Conclusion:
The ideal strategy will depend on your unique financial situation, tax implications, and retirement goals. Consulting a financial advisor can also provide tailored advice.
