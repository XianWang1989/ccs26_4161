Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts effectively depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here’s a strategic approach to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Matching**: If your employer offers a matching contribution for your 401(k), contribute enough to maximize that match before putting money in other accounts. This is essentially "free money" for your retirement.

2. **Contribute to a Roth IRA**: If you're eligible, consider contributing to a Roth IRA. Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, meaning withdrawals in retirement are tax-free. This can be especially beneficial if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement.

3. **Focus on Tax Efficiency**: Generally, you want to hold investments that generate high taxable income, like bonds or REITs, in tax-advantaged accounts (401(k), IRA). Wealth-building assets like stocks may be better suited for taxable accounts if you expect long-term capital gains.

4. **Diversify Accounts**: Each account type has different tax implications. By spreading your investments among these accounts, you can optimize your tax situation. For example, you can take advantage of tax-deferred growth in a 401(k) while having tax-free growth in a Roth IRA.

5. **Consider Your Time Horizon and Risk Tolerance**: Your age and how soon you’ll need the money can influence how you allocate funds. Younger investors might lean towards more aggressive growth in stocks in their 401(k) and Roth IRA, while older investors might favor stability with bonds.

6. **Pay Down Debt**: If you have high-interest debt, consider paying that down before making large contributions to investment accounts.

7. **Rebalance and Review Regularly**: As your investments grow or your financial situation changes, periodically review your allocation to ensure it aligns with your goals.

8. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: If you're uncertain about how to allocate your investments or need personalized advice, it may be beneficial to consult with a financial advisor.

Ultimately, a balanced approach tailored to your individual situation will help you make the most of your investments across these different accounts.
