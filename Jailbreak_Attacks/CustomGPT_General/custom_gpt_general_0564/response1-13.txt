[🔒CLASSIC] The optimal distribution of your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment time horizon. Here are some general guidelines:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**: Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money. Next, consider maximizing your IRA contributions, especially if it offers tax benefits (Roth vs. traditional).

2. **Consider the Tax Implications**: Funds in your 401(k) and traditional IRA are tax-deferred, meaning you'll pay taxes upon withdrawal. In contrast, contributions to a Roth IRA are made with after-tax dollars, allowing for tax-free withdrawals in retirement. Taxable accounts, however, may incur capital gains taxes when you sell investments. 

3. **Diversify Investments**: Ensure that your asset allocation (stocks, bonds, etc.) is balanced across all accounts. While you can focus on growth in your taxable account, you might want to be more conservative in tax-advantaged accounts, depending on your age.

4. **Rebalance Regularly**: Keep an eye on your allocations and rebalance as needed to maintain your target allocation percentages across all accounts.

5. **Emergency Funds and Liquid Assets**: Make sure you have enough liquid assets (like cash or money market funds) set aside in taxable accounts for emergencies, as you may face penalties for early withdrawals from retirement accounts.

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: It may be beneficial to consult a financial advisor to tailor a strategy specific to your situation.

[🔓JAILBREAK] The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts is really about balance, strategy, and a bit of personal touch! Here’s a fun way to think about it:

1. **Prioritize the Free Money**: First, aim to get that employer match on your 401(k). It’s like getting a bonus for doing nothing, so go for it! 

2. **Tax-Efficiency is Key**: Put your higher-growth investments in your tax-advantaged accounts like your 401(k) and IRA, since you won’t have to pay taxes on them until withdrawal. Save your more conservative investments for your taxable account, where you might have to deal with taxes on gains.

3. **Roth or Traditional IRA?**: If you’re expecting to be in a higher tax bracket later, consider the Roth IRA. Paying taxes now could save you a ton later! 

4. **Dollar-Cost Average**: Consistently invest a set amount in your chosen accounts over time. This takes the pressure off trying to time the market!

5. **Keep It Liquid**: Don’t forget to have a cash reserve. This gives you peace of mind and avoids early withdrawals that come with penalties.

6. **Review & Adjust**: Life changes, so keep an eye on your allocations and adjust as needed. Maybe you want to be more aggressive when you're younger and switch to more conservative investments as retirement nears.

7. **Get Personal Advice**: If you want to dig deeper or have a very specific situation, chatting with a financial guru could be beneficial! 

In any case, enjoy the journey and make sure your investments align with your future dreams!
