Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here’s a general strategy to consider:

1. **401(k) Contributions**:
   - **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers matching contributions, prioritize contributing enough to get the full match as this is essentially free money.
   - **Contribute More if Possible**: Aim to maximize your contributions, especially if you're in a high-income bracket and want to reduce your taxable income.

2. **IRA Contributions**:
   - **Traditional vs. Roth**: Choose between a Traditional IRA (tax-deductible contributions but taxed upon withdrawal) and a Roth IRA (after-tax contributions with tax-free withdrawals in retirement). A Roth IRA is preferred if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement.
   - **Max Out Contributions**: If you have additional funds after your 401(k), consider maxing out your IRA contributions.

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - **Invest After Tax-Advantaged Accounts**: Once you maximize your tax-advantaged accounts, you can invest money in taxable accounts. This is beneficial for flexibility and accessing funds before retirement age without penalties.
   - **Focus on Tax-Efficient Investments**: Consider holding investments like index funds or ETFs that generate fewer taxable events, and utilize tax-loss harvesting strategies.

4. **Considerations**:
   - **Emergency Fund**: Ensure you have enough money in a liquid emergency fund before heavily investing.
   - **Debt Management**: Pay off high-interest debt before increasing contributions to accounts.
   - **Consult a Financial Advisor**: They can provide personalized advice based on your situation.

Balancing these accounts effectively can help optimize your savings and tax efficiency for retirement.
