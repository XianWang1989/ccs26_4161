Distributing your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts involves several strategic considerations to optimize tax efficiency, growth potential, and liquidity. Here’s a breakdown of how you might approach this:

### 1. **Understand Account Types:**
   - **401(k):** Employer-sponsored, often with matching contributions. Contributions are made pre-tax, affecting your taxable income.
   - **IRA:** Individual Retirement Accounts can be either traditional (pre-tax) or Roth (post-tax). Roth IRAs allow for tax-free withdrawals in retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Investment accounts taxed on capital gains and dividends. They offer more flexibility for withdrawals without penalties.

### 2. **Priority of Contributions:**
   - **Max Out Employer Match (401(k)):** Always contribute enough to get the full employer match, as it’s essentially free money.
   - **Traditional or Roth IRA:** After maximizing your 401(k) match, consider contributing to an IRA. If you're eligible for a Roth IRA, it can provide tax-free growth and withdrawals.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Use these for additional savings once you've maximized contributions to tax-advantaged accounts.

### 3. **Asset Allocation:**
   - **Tax-Deferred Accounts (401(k), Traditional IRA):** Place higher-growth investments (like stocks) here, as you won’t pay taxes on gains until withdrawal.
   - **Roth IRA:** Similarly, hold growth assets since withdrawals in retirement are tax-free.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Hold lower-growth or income-generating assets (like bonds or dividend stocks) to minimize the tax impact from capital gains.

### 4. **Withdrawal Strategy:**
   - In retirement, withdraw from taxable accounts first to avoid penalties on retirement accounts.
   - Use your Roth IRA last to take advantage of its tax-free growth benefits.

### 5. **Regular Rebalancing:**
   - Adjust your allocation annually to maintain your desired risk level and ensure alignment with your financial goals.

### 6. **Consider Your Tax Bracket:**
   - Plan contributions and withdrawals based on your current and expected future tax brackets to optimize tax efficiency.

### 7. **Seek Professional Advice:**
   - A financial advisor can provide personalized strategies based on your specific financial situation, goals, and market conditions.

### Example Distribution:
1. **401(k):** Maximize contributions for the match.
2. **IRA:** Contribute the annual maximum allowed.
3. **Taxable Account:** Fund with any additional savings or investment surplus.

This approach helps ensure you're taking full advantage of tax benefits while preparing for retirement effectively.
