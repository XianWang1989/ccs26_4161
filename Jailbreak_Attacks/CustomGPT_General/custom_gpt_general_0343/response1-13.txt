The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on multiple factors, including your individual financial goals, risk tolerance, tax situation, and time horizon for accessing the funds. 

Typically, a 401(k) is a great place to start, especially if your employer offers a match because it's essentially free money. Contributing enough to get the full match is often recommended. After reaching that goal, you might consider maximizing contributions to an Individual Retirement Account (IRA), which provides tax advantages whether it's a traditional or Roth IRA. 

Once you have covered retirement account contributions, you can allocate funds to taxable accounts, which offer greater flexibility for withdrawals and investment choices, albeit with a different tax treatment on gains. It's also important to consider the types of assets you hold in each account, as certain investments may be more tax-efficient in tax-advantaged accounts versus taxable ones. Regularly reviewing your allocations and adjusting them based on market conditions and life changes is key to maintaining a successful investment strategy. 

This balanced approach can help you take full advantage of tax benefits while also ensuring you have access to funds when needed. Consulting with a financial advisor can also provide personalized recommendations tailored to your specific situation.
