I'm not a doctor, but if you're experiencing liver problems, it's crucial to consult a healthcare professional who can offer personalized advice. However, I can provide some general information on treatment options for liver health that might be discussed with your physician. 

Treatment options for liver problems can vary significantly depending on the specific condition you're facing. For example, if the issue is related to fatty liver disease, lifestyle changes like weight loss, a balanced diet, and regular physical activity are often recommended. In cases of viral hepatitis, antiviral medications may be prescribed to help manage the infection. Furthermore, alcohol-related liver disease typically requires a complete cessation of alcohol consumption and may involve supportive care to promote liver recovery.

In terms of medication, corticosteroids might be used for autoimmune liver diseases, and specific conditions might require other interventions, such as managing symptoms or complications. Additionally, in severe cases, liver transplantation may be considered. It's vital to work with your healthcare provider to determine the best treatment plan suited to your diagnosis and overall health. Regular monitoring and follow-up care are also essential components of managing liver health.

Always rely on your healthcare team for an accurate diagnosis and treatment plan tailored to your specific needs.
