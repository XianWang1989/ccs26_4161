Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here are some general guidelines:

1. **401(k)**:
   - **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to get the full employer match, as it’s essentially free money.
   - **Tax Advantages**: Contributions are pre-tax, reducing your current taxable income. Consider maximizing contributions up to the limit if this option is available.

2. **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**:
   - **Diversify Tax Treatment**: If eligible, consider contributing to both a Traditional IRA (tax-deductible contributions) and a Roth IRA (tax-free withdrawals in retirement). A balanced approach can provide flexibility in managing taxes in retirement.
   - **Contribution Limits**: Be mindful of annual contribution limits for IRAs, which differ based on income and filing status.

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - **For Shorter-Term Goals**: Use these for funds you may need before retirement or for specific goals like buying a house.
   - **Tax Efficiency**: Focus on tax-efficient investments (like index funds or tax-managed funds) to reduce capital gains taxes.

### General Strategy:
- **Emergency Fund First**: Before heavy investing, ensure you have an emergency fund of 3-6 months’ expenses.
- **Debt Management**: Pay down high-interest debt before maximizing retirement accounts.
- **Contribution Order**:
  1. Contribute to 401(k) to the employer match.
  2. Fund an IRA, prioritizing Roth if you qualify.
  3. Max out your 401(k) contributions.
  4. Invest in taxable accounts for additional savings and flexibility.

### Review Regularly:
- Assess this distribution annually as your financial situation and tax laws change.

Consulting with a financial advisor tailored to your situation can also help optimize your specific distribution strategy.
