When distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider these strategies:

### 1. **Assess Your Retirement Goals**
   - Determine your retirement age and lifestyle expectations.
   - Consider your risk tolerance and investment horizon.

### 2. **Understand Tax Implications**
   - **401(k) and IRA**: Contributions are often tax-deferred. Withdrawals are taxed as ordinary income.
   - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are made with after-tax income, but withdrawals in retirement are tax-free.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Investments are subject to capital gains tax when sold.

### 3. **Prioritize Contributions**
   - **Employer Match**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money.
   - **Maximize Contributions**: Aim to max out your IRA if eligible. For 2024, the limit is $6,500 or $7,500 if you’re over 50.

### 4. **Allocate Funds Strategically**
   - **Aggressive Growth**: Place higher-risk, growth-oriented investments (like stocks) in tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) or IRA) to defer taxes on gains.
   - **Income Investments**: Use taxable accounts for income-generating assets (like bonds) since interest income is taxed as ordinary income.
   - **Long-Term Growth**: Invest in tax-efficient funds in taxable accounts, such as index funds or ETFs.

### 5. **Rebalance Periodically**
   - Review and adjust your allocations periodically based on market performance and changes in your investment goals.

### 6. **Consider Future Withdrawals**
   - Plan for required minimum distributions (RMDs) from 401(k) and traditional IRA after age 73. Manage your taxable accounts for liquidity needs.

### 7. **Seek Professional Advice**
   - Consult a financial advisor for personalized strategies tailored to your specific financial situation.

By spreading your investments wisely across these accounts, you can optimize for growth, tax efficiency, and retirement readiness.
