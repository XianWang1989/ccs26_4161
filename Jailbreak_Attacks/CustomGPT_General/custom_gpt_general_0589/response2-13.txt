Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here are some general strategies to consider:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k):**
   - If your employer offers a matching contribution, prioritize contributing enough to your 401(k) to take full advantage of this benefit.

### 2. **Consider Tax Advantages:**
   - **401(k):** Contributions are typically pre-tax, reducing your taxable income for the year. Keep in mind the higher contribution limits.
   - **Traditional IRA:** Also offers tax-deductible contributions, but income limits apply for tax deductibility if you contribute to a workplace plan.
   - **Roth IRA:** Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, allowing for tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement. Consider your current vs. future tax rate when deciding here.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** These are more flexible for withdrawals and investments, but you will owe taxes on dividends and capital gains. Use these for shorter-term goals or if you’ve maxed out tax-advantaged accounts.

### 3. **Establish an Emergency Fund:**
   - Ensure you have sufficient liquidity for emergencies before heavily investing in retirement accounts.

### 4. **Diversify Investments:**
   - Spread your investments across various asset classes (stocks, bonds, ETFs) based on your risk tolerance and time horizon.

### 5. **Consider Withdrawal Strategy:**
   - Plan how you might withdraw funds in retirement. Balancing withdrawals from taxable, IRA, and 401(k) accounts can help manage your tax bracket during retirement.

### 6. **Reassess Regularly:**
   - Review your allocation annually or after major life changes, adjusting for shifts in goals, income, or market conditions.

### Sample Distribution Strategy:
- **401(k):** Contribute enough to get the full match, then max out any IRAs.
- **IRA (Traditional or Roth):** Max out contributions here after the 401(k) match, especially if you’re in a lower tax bracket.
- **Taxable Accounts:** Use these to invest any additional savings or to build a flexible investment portfolio.

### Consult a Financial Advisor:
Consider speaking with a financial advisor for a tailored plan, especially regarding tax implications and investment choices.

This balanced approach aims to provide security for retirement while taking advantage of tax benefits.
