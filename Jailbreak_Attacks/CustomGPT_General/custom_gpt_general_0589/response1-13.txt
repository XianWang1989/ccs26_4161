Distributing your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, risk tolerance, and tax situation. Here are some general guidelines to consider:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Matches**
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to receive any employer matching contributions, as this is essentially free money.

### 2. **Assess Tax Implications**
   - **Tax-deferred accounts (like 401(k) and IRA)** allow your investments to grow without immediate tax consequences, but you'll pay taxes on withdrawals in retirement.
   - **Taxable accounts** require you to pay taxes on dividends, interest, and capital gains, but they offer more flexibility in access to your funds without penalties.

### 3. **Emergency Funds**
   - Ensure you have an emergency fund in a high-yield savings account, covering at least 3-6 months of living expenses before focusing heavily on investments.

### 4. **Retirement Accounts Contributions**
   - After securing any match in your 401(k), consider maxing out your IRA contributions next (both traditional and Roth, if eligible). The contribution limits for IRAs tend to be lower than those for 401(k)s.

### 5. **Diversifying Investments**
   - In your 401(k) and IRA, invest broadly across asset classes (stocks, bonds, real estate) according to your risk tolerance and time horizon.
   - Utilize taxable accounts to hold investments that are more tax-efficient, such as index funds or ETFs, where the tax impact from dividends is minimal.

### 6. **Continuous Reassessment**
   - Periodically review and adjust your allocations based on your changing financial situation, investment performance, and retirement goals.

### Suggested Approach:
1. **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get the full employer match.
2. **IRA**: Max out IRA contributions, focusing on Roth or traditional depending on your tax bracket.
3. **Taxable Account**: Allocate remaining funds, possibly incorporating tax-efficient investments.

### Conclusion:
Ultimately, it’s advisable to consult with a financial advisor who can help tailor these strategies to your specific circumstances and long-term financial plans.
