Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some strategies you might consider:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match**
   - Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match, as this is essentially "free money."

### 2. **Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**
   - Focus on maxing out tax-advantaged accounts (like your 401(k) and IRA) first. For 2023, the annual limits are $22,500 for the 401(k) (plus $7,500 if age 50+) and $6,500 for the IRA (plus $1,000 if age 50+).

### 3. **Consider Tax Implications**
   - With taxable accounts, you'll incur capital gains taxes on investments you sell. Ideally, hold onto stocks for the long term to minimize these taxes.

### 4. **Diversify Investments**
   - Maintain a diversified investment strategy across all accounts. Balance between equities, bonds, and other asset classes depending on your risk tolerance.

### 5. **Rebalance Regularly**
   - Regularly review and rebalance your asset allocation across accounts to ensure it aligns with your investment goals and risk tolerance.

### 6. **Withdrawals Strategy**
   - Plan your withdrawal strategy in retirement to take advantage of tax brackets and keep your taxable income manageable.

### 7. **Emergency Fund**
   - Ensure that you have sufficient liquidity in your taxable accounts for emergencies, rather than relying solely on retirement accounts.

### Conclusion
Tailor your approach to fit your specific goals and circumstances, and consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized advice. Each person’s situation is unique, so a one-size-fits-all strategy may not be effective.
