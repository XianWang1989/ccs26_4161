Distributing your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some strategies to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match—this is essentially free money.

2. **Diversify Contributions**:
   - **401(k)**: Focus on pre-tax contributions to lower your taxable income. Consider investing in a mix of stocks and bonds based on your risk tolerance.
   - **IRA**: If eligible, consider a Roth IRA for tax-free growth on qualified withdrawals. For traditional IRAs, focus on maximizing tax deductions now.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for short-term savings or goals since they offer more flexibility. Prioritize tax-efficient investments, like index funds or ETFs, to minimize capital gains taxes.

3. **Consider Tax Implications**:
   - Prioritize tax-deferred accounts (401(k), traditional IRA) for high-income years.
   - Use taxable accounts for investments that generate qualified dividends or long-term capital gains, which are taxed at lower rates.

4. **Adjust Based on Age and Goals**: As you approach retirement, shift towards more conservative investments in tax-deferred accounts and consider maintaining more growth-oriented investments in taxable accounts.

5. **Regular Rebalancing**: Periodically review and rebalance your portfolio across all accounts to maintain your desired asset allocation.

6. **Consult a Professional**: A financial advisor can provide personalized advice based on your unique situation, helping optimize your specific asset allocation strategy.

By considering these factors, you can create a balanced approach that aligns with your financial goals.
