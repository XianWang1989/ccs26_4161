To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First:**
   - Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get any employer match.
   - Consider maxing out your IRA contributions next, as they provide tax benefits.

2. **Assess Contribution Limits:**
   - For 2023, the 401(k) limit is $22,500 (plus a $7,500 catch-up if you're 50+).
   - IRA contributions are limited to $6,500 (or $7,500 if 50+).

3. **Diversify Investment Choices:**
   - Choose a mix of stocks, bonds, and other assets based on your risk tolerance within each account.
   - The tax treatment of gains also affects strategy; for example, hold bonds in tax-advantaged accounts to avoid high taxes on interest income.

4. **Consider Withdrawal Strategy:**
   - In retirement, plan to withdraw from tax-deferred accounts (401(k), IRA) first to manage your tax bracket.
   - Use taxable accounts for early retirement to minimize penalties on retirement accounts.

5. **Regularly Rebalance:**
   - Reassess your allocation annually to ensure it aligns with your risk tolerance and retirement goals.

6. **Seek Professional Advice:**
   - Consult with a financial advisor to get personalized recommendations based on your financial situation.

This approach balances the benefits of tax-advantaged growth while providing flexibility from taxable accounts.
