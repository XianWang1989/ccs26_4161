The best way to distribute your money amongst your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts largely depends on your individual financial goals, tax situation, and investment strategy. Here are some general guidelines to consider for each account:

### 1. **401(k)**
- **Maximize Contributions**: If your employer offers a match, contribute at least enough to get the full match. This is essentially free money.
- **Tax Advantages**: Contributions are made pre-tax, or Roth options allow for post-tax contributions. Consider your current and anticipated future tax rates when choosing.
- **Investment Options**: 401(k) plans often have limited investment options. Choose a diversified mix that fits your risk tolerance.

### 2. **IRA (Traditional/Roth)**
- **Contribution Limits**: For 2023, the contribution limit is $6,500 ($7,500 if you're 50 or older). If you can, maximize your IRA contributions after your 401(k).
- **Tax Benefits**: A Traditional IRA offers tax deductions, while a Roth IRA allows tax-free withdrawals in retirement. Choose based on your expected tax situation.
- **Investment Flexibility**: IRAs typically have a wider range of investment options than 401(k)s.

### 3. **Taxable Accounts**
- **Liquid Funds**: Use these for savings goals that are less than five years away, or for flexibility in withdrawals.
- **Tax Considerations**: Be mindful of capital gains taxes. Holding investments long-term can minimize tax implications.
- **Diversity**: Use your taxable account for assets that might be less tax-efficient, such as municipal bonds or certain index funds.

### Recommendations
- **Start with Retirement Accounts**: Focus on maxing out your 401(k) and IRA contributions first due to their tax advantages.
- **Consider Your Time Horizon**: Allocate funds based on when you'll need the money. Short-term funds might go into taxable accounts, while long-term investments benefit from retirement accounts.
- **Review and Rebalance**: Regularly assess your asset allocation to ensure it aligns with your risk tolerance and investment goals.

### Final Thoughts
It can be beneficial to consult with a financial advisor who can provide personalized advice tailored to your specific financial situation. The right allocation strategy can optimize your savings and enhance your financial security for retirement.
