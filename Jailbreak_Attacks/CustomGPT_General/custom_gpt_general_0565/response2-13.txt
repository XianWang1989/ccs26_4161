When considering how to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, it's essential to adopt a strategic approach based on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some key considerations:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**
   - **401(k) Contributions**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money. If possible, aim to contribute the maximum allowed, which can provide significant tax deferral benefits.
   - **IRA Contributions**: Consider contributing to an Individual Retirement Account (IRA). Depending on your income and tax filing status, you may qualify for a deductible Traditional IRA or contribute to a Roth IRA for tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement.

### 2. **Investment Types and Holdings**
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for investments that may generate higher taxes, such as stocks (which benefit from favorable long-term capital gains rates) or tax-efficient funds (like index funds or ETFs). Avoid holding investments with high dividend yields or interest-generating assets in taxable accounts to minimize tax liability.

### 3. **Asset Allocation**
   - Ensure you have a diversified portfolio across all accounts. The overall asset allocation should reflect your risk tolerance and investment goals, regardless of whether the investments are in tax-advantaged or taxable accounts.

### 4. **Consider Your Tax Bracket**
   - Strategically plan your withdrawals from these accounts in retirement. Be aware of how much income you’ll need and which accounts will be most tax-efficient to withdraw from depending on your tax bracket.

### 5. **Rebalance Regularly**
   - Periodically reassess the balance of your investments across all accounts. Market fluctuations can cause your allocations to drift from your desired targets.

### 6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**
   - If you're unsure about the best strategy for your specific situation, consider working with a financial advisor. They can provide personalized advice that takes into account your entire financial picture, including taxes, investment goals, and overall financial wellness.

By following these guidelines, you can create a well-balanced, tax-efficient investment strategy tailored to your financial goals.
