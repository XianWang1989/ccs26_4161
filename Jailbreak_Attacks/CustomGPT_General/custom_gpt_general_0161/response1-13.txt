The best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and risk tolerance. Here's a general guide:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First:**
   - **401(k):** Contribute enough to get any employer match. This is essentially "free money." Aim to maximize contributions if you can afford it.
   - **IRA (Traditional or Roth):** After maximizing your 401(k), consider contributing to an IRA. A Roth IRA offers tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement, while a Traditional IRA may provide tax deductions now.

### 2. **Balance Between Accounts:**
   - **Taxable Accounts:** These can be useful for financial goals in the near term (like buying a house). They offer flexibility but come with capital gains taxes on profits.
   - Consider that capital gains from taxable accounts are taxed differently than withdrawals from tax-advantaged accounts. If you expect to be in a lower tax bracket during retirement, you might prioritize tax-deferred accounts.

### 3. **Diversification:**
   - Within each account type, ensure that your investments are well diversified across different asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) to manage risk.

### 4. **Review Your Goals:**
   - If you plan to retire early, prioritize liquid savings in taxable accounts to avoid penalties on early withdrawals from retirement accounts.

### 5. **Consult a Financial Advisor:**
   - If you're uncertain about how to allocate your funds based on your specific circumstances, working with a financial advisor can provide personalized guidance.

### Example Allocation Strategy:
- **For Younger Investors:** Max out the 401(k) to the match, contribute to a Roth IRA, and keep some funds in taxable accounts for liquidity.
- **For Near-Retirees:** Focus on maximizing the 401(k) and IRA contributions, with less emphasis on taxable accounts unless necessary for immediate goals.

Evaluating your individual situation regularly and adjusting your distribution strategy as your financial life evolves is also crucial.
