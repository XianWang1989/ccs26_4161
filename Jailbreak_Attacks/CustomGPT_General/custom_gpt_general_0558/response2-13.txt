Distributing your money across your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts should depend on various factors including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeframe. Here are some general guidelines that can help:

1. **Maximize Employer Match:** If your 401(k) offers matching contributions from your employer, contribute at least enough to get the full match. This is essentially "free money."

2. **Consider Tax Implications:** 
   - **401(k):** Contributions are pre-tax, reducing your taxable income now but taxed upon withdrawal. Ideal for long-term retirement savings.
   - **IRA (Traditional or Roth):** Traditional IRAs may provide immediate tax benefits, while Roth IRAs offer tax-free withdrawals in retirement (as long as certain conditions are met).
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Investments here can incur capital gains taxes upon selling assets. It’s more flexible for withdrawals but less tax-efficient.

3. **Emergency Savings:** Before maxing out retirement accounts, ensure you have an emergency fund in a high-yield savings account to cover 3-6 months of expenses.

4. **Diversify Investments:** In all accounts, aim for diversification through asset allocation based on your risk tolerance and investment horizon. Stocks, bonds, and ETFs can be spread across accounts to enhance growth and mitigate risk.

5. **Prioritize High-Interest Debt:** If you have high-interest debt, consider paying that down before investing heavily in retirement accounts.

6. **Adjust Contributions Over Time:** As your financial situation changes (income increase, job changes, life events), revisit your contributions and re-balance your portfolio to align with your changing goals.

7. **Consult with a Financial Advisor:** A professional can help tailor a specific plan for you based on your unique financial situation.

Thus, the best strategy typically involves a combination of maximizing employer matches in your 401(k), considering tax implications with IRA contributions, and using taxable accounts for additional flexibility in investing.
