Distributing your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, risk tolerance, and investment time horizon. Here are some considerations for each type of account:

### 1. **401(k) Accounts**
   - **Advantages**: Tax-deferred growth, potential employer match, higher contribution limits.
   - **Considerations**: Contribute enough to get any employer match first—this is essentially free money. If you have high fees associated with your 401(k), consider investing less here after getting the match.

### 2. **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**
   - **Advantages**: Tax-deferred growth (Traditional) or tax-free growth (Roth), various investment options.
   - **Considerations**: If you’re eligible, consider maxing out a Roth IRA for tax-free withdrawals in retirement. If you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement, prioritize Roth. 

### 3. **Taxable Accounts**
   - **Advantages**: Flexibility, no contribution limits, ability to withdraw anytime without penalties.
   - **Considerations**: Invest in tax-efficient funds or strategies (like index funds) to minimize capital gains taxes. This account is good for short-term goals or if you need access to money before retirement.

### Suggested Distribution Strategy
1. **Emergency Fund**: Ensure you have enough savings in a liquid form before investing aggressively.
2. **Employer Match**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full match.
3. **Maximize IRAs**: Fund your IRA to the maximum limit if possible.
4. **Taxable Accounts**: Use taxable accounts for excess funds, especially for goals within 5-10 years.

### Example Distribution Approach
- **Up to 10%** in Taxable Accounts for short-term needs
- **Up to 50%** in 401(k) (including match)
- **Up to 40%** in IRAs (Traditional/Roth)

### Final Thoughts
Evaluate and adjust your strategy periodically based on changes in income, tax laws, and life circumstances. Also, consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your specific situation.
