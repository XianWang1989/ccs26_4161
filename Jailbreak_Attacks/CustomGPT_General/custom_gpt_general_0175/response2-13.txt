When deciding how to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Maximize Employer Match:**
   - Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to take full advantage of any employer match. This is essentially free money.

2. **Tax-Advantaged Accounts First:**
   - After getting the match, focus on your IRA (either Traditional or Roth) to further benefit from tax advantages. Roth IRAs allow for tax-free growth and tax-free withdrawals in retirement, while Traditional IRAs provide tax-deferred growth.

3. **Continuation of 401(k) Contributions:**
   - If you still have funds to invest after maxing out your IRA, continue contributing to your 401(k), but aim to max out if possible. The annual limit for 401(k) contributions is higher than that for IRAs.

4. **Taxable Accounts as a Supplement:**
   - Consider using taxable accounts for additional investments, especially for shorter-term goals or if you’ve reached the contribution limits for retirement accounts. This can offer more flexibility and liquidity.

5. **Investment Choices:**
   - Within each account, choose investments based on your risk tolerance and time horizon. Retirement accounts can often take on more risk since they are long-term.

6. **Regular Review and Rebalance:**
   - Periodically review your asset allocation and contributions to ensure they align with your financial goals and risk tolerance.

7. **Consider Future Tax Implications:**
   - Be mindful of your current and expected future tax bracket when deciding between contributing to a Traditional IRA (tax-deductible now) or a Roth IRA (tax-free withdrawals later).

8. **Consult with a Financial Advisor:**
   - Given the complexities of retirement accounts and tax implications, consulting a financial advisor can provide personalized recommendations based on your financial situation.

By prioritizing tax-advantaged accounts, taking advantage of employer matches, and considering your financial goals, you can create a balanced investment strategy.
