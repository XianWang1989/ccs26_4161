Distributing your money amongst a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors including your financial goals, tax situation, investment timeline, and risk tolerance. Here are some considerations for each account type:

1. **401(k) Plans:**
   - **Contribution Limits:** Maximize employer matching contributions as it’s essentially free money.
   - **Tax Benefits:** Contributions are often made pre-tax, reducing your taxable income. Consider this if you're in a higher tax bracket now.
   - **Investment Options:** Review the investment options available in your 401(k) and pick a diversified mix that aligns with your risk tolerance.

2. **IRA Accounts (Traditional or Roth):**
   - **Tax Considerations:** Traditional IRAs give you a tax deduction in the contribution year, while Roth IRAs allow for tax-free withdrawals in retirement (assuming conditions are met).
   - **Contribution Limits:** Be mindful of the contribution limits and consider spreading contributions over both accounts if eligible (for a tax-deductible IRA and a Roth IRA).
   - **Investment Strategy:** Similar to a 401(k), focus on diversification and alignment with your retirement timeline.

3. **Taxable Accounts:**
   - **Flexibility:** Money can be accessed at any time without penalty, making it useful for short to medium-term goals.
   - **Tax Implications:** Take into account capital gains taxes when investing in this account. Consider holding investments for longer periods to benefit from lower long-term capital gains rates.
   - **Investment Choices:** You have the flexibility to choose individual stocks, bonds, or mutual funds.

### General Strategy:
- **Prioritize Contributions:** Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match, then maximize your IRA contributions. After that, consider putting additional funds into your taxable account, especially if your tax-advantaged options are fully funded.
- **Balance Risk:** Maintain a balanced approach in investments across all accounts based on your overall risk profile.
- **Regular Review:** Annually review and adjust your asset allocation based on changes in income, expenses, life circumstances, and market conditions.

### Conclusion:
Consulting with a financial advisor can provide personalized advice tailored to your specific circumstances and goals, considering all the nuances based on tax laws and investment strategies.
