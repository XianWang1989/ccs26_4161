Determining the best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your current financial situation, long-term goals, tax considerations, and risk tolerance. Here’s a structured approach to help you:

### 1. **Understand Account Types**:
   - **401(k)**: Employer-sponsored retirement plan; contributions may reduce taxable income, and many employers offer matching contributions.
   - **IRA (Individual Retirement Account)**: Tax-advantaged account allowing for tax-deferred (Traditional IRA) or tax-free (Roth IRA) growth, depending on the type.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Investment accounts with no tax advantages; subject to capital gains taxes when assets are sold.

### 2. **Consider Contribution Limits**:
   - For 2023, the maximum employee contribution to a 401(k) is $22,500 ($30,000 if you're 50 or older).
   - The IRA contribution limit is $6,500 ($7,500 if you're 50 or older).

### 3. **Prioritize Contributions**:
   - **Maximize Employer Match**: Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to get any company match—this is essentially free money.
   - **Roth vs. Traditional**: If you anticipate being in a higher tax bracket in retirement, consider a Roth IRA for tax-free withdrawals. If you need current tax deductions, a Traditional IRA might be better.
   - **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**: Once you max out tax-advantaged accounts, consider contributing to a taxable account for liquidity and investment options. This account can be beneficial for short-term goals or emergencies.

### 4. **Asset Allocation**:
   - Use tax-efficient investments (like index funds or ETFs) in your taxable accounts to minimize tax impact.
   - Consider holding bonds in tax-advantaged accounts (like IRAs) since interest income is taxed at higher ordinary income rates compared to capital gains.

### 5. **Review and Adjust**:
   - Regularly review your asset allocation and contributions based on changes in income, financial goals, market conditions, or tax laws.

### Example Distribution Strategy:
1. **Contribute Enough to 401(k)** to get maximum employer match.
2. **Max Out IRA Contributions** (preferably a Roth if eligible).
3. **Invest Any Remaining Funds in Taxable Accounts** for short-term goals or as an emergency fund.

### Conclusion:
A balanced approach that incorporates tax efficiency and your personal financial goals will help optimize your investment across these accounts. Consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your unique circumstances.
