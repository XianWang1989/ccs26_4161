To effectively distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

### 1. **401(k): Maximize Contributions First**
   - **Employer Match:** Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get any employer match—this is essentially free money.
   - **Contribution Limits:** For 2023, the annual contribution limit for a 401(k) is $22,500 (or $30,000 if you're over 50). 

### 2. **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**
   - **Determine Type:** Decide between a Traditional IRA (tax-deductible contributions, taxed on withdrawal) and a Roth IRA (taxed on contributions, tax-free withdrawals).
   - **Contribution Limits:** For 2023, the IRA contribution limit is $6,500 (or $7,500 if you're over 50). 
   - **Roth Considerations:** If you're eligible, consider contributing to a Roth IRA for tax-free growth.

### 3. **Taxable Accounts**
   - **Liquidity Needs:** Allocate funds to taxable accounts for short-term goals or emergencies since these are more accessible.
   - **Tax Efficiency:** Use low-turnover investments (like index funds or ETFs) to minimize capital gains taxes.

### 4. **Diversification & Asset Allocation**
   - **Risk Tolerance:** Base your distribution on your risk tolerance and investment goals. Consider a mix of stocks, bonds, and other assets that align with your objectives.
   - **Rebalance:** Regularly review and rebalance your portfolio to maintain your desired asset allocation.

### 5. **Consult a Financial Advisor**
   - If unsure, consult a financial advisor to tailor a plan based on your specific financial situation, goals, and tax implications.

### Final Thoughts
Each type of account serves different purposes, so prioritize contributions in order of benefits (401(k) for match, then IRA, and finally taxable accounts). Adjust your strategy over time as your financial situation and goals evolve.
