Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts involves considering several factors, including your financial goals, tax implications, and retirement timeline. Here’s a general strategy:

### 1. **Maximize Contributions**
   - **401(k)**: If your employer offers matching contributions, start here. Contribute enough to get the full match, as it’s essentially "free money."
   - **IRA**: After maximizing your 401(k) match, consider contributing to an IRA. Depending on your income, you can choose between a traditional IRA (tax-deductible contributions) or a Roth IRA (tax-free withdrawals in retirement).

### 2. **Consider Your Tax Situation**
   - **Tax-Deferred Accounts (401(k) and Traditional IRA)**: Contributions reduce your taxable income in the year they’re made. Consider these accounts for higher income years.
   - **Roth Accounts**: If you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement, consider maxing out a Roth IRA for its tax-free growth and withdrawals.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for investments that may generate capital gains, which are often taxed at lower rates compared to ordinary income.

### 3. **Balance for Liquidity and Diversification**
   - Keep some funds in taxable accounts for short-term goals or emergencies since they are more accessible without withdrawal penalties.
   - Ensure a diversified portfolio across all accounts, considering risk tolerance and market conditions.

### 4. **Review and Adjust Annually**
   - Monitor your investments and contributions each year. As your financial situation changes (e.g., salary increases, tax law changes), adjust your distribution strategy accordingly.

### Example Allocation Approach
1. Contribute to your 401(k) to get the employer match.
2. Fund your IRA (consider maxing out a Roth IRA if eligible).
3. Use any additional funds for taxable investments, focusing on long-term growth and tax efficiency.

Always consider speaking with a financial advisor to tailor a strategy that aligns with your specific financial situation and goals.
