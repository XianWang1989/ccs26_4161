When considering how to distribute money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, it's essential to evaluate several factors, including your overall financial goals, tax situation, investment time horizon, and risk tolerance. Here are some general strategies to consider:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**: Prioritize contributions to tax-advantaged accounts. Generally, it is beneficial to:
   - Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money.
   - Consider maxing out your IRA contributions (both traditional and Roth if eligible) next, as these accounts offer tax advantages.

2. **Consider Tax Implications**: Different accounts have different tax treatments:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are pre-tax, reducing your taxable income. Taxes are paid on withdrawals in retirement.
   - **Traditional IRA**: Similar to a 401(k) with tax-deductible contributions, taxed upon withdrawal. 
   - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, but qualified withdrawals are tax-free in retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Investments here are subject to capital gains taxes. It may be best to hold investments with lower turnover or long-term growth in these accounts.

3. **Diversification of Investments**: Ensure that your investments are diversified across all accounts to manage risk effectively and capitalize on growth opportunities. Consider the types of funds or assets (stocks, bonds, index funds) in each account based on your risk tolerance.

4. **Withdrawal Strategy**: Plan for how you will withdraw from these accounts in retirement for tax efficiency. Typically, it may make sense to withdraw from taxable accounts first and defer withdrawals from tax-advantaged accounts for as long as possible.

5. **Regular Review and Adjustments**: Regularly review your asset allocation and contributions based on life changes, market conditions, and adjustments in your financial goals.

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: Given the complexity of retirement planning and tax implications, consider working with a financial advisor who can provide personalized advice based on your unique circumstances.

By taking a strategic approach to how you distribute money among your accounts, you can optimize for growth while managing risk and tax liabilities effectively.
