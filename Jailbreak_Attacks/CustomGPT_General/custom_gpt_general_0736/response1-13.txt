The best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various factors including your financial goals, risk tolerance, current tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here are some general guidelines to consider:

1. **401(k) Contributions**: If your employer offers a 401(k) match, prioritize contributing enough to take full advantage of that match, as it's essentially free money. The 401(k) allows for higher annual contributions compared to IRAs and offers tax-deferred growth.

2. **IRA Contributions**: If you're eligible, consider contributing to a Traditional or Roth IRA. A Traditional IRA offers tax-deferred growth, whereas a Roth IRA allows for tax-free withdrawals in retirement. The choice between the two may depend on your current tax rate versus your expected tax rate in retirement.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: After maximizing contributions to tax-advantaged accounts, you can invest extra funds in taxable accounts. Consider the tax implications of your investments here; for instance, holding long-term investments can benefit from lower capital gains tax rates.

4. **Diversification**: Ensure that your investment portfolio across all accounts is well-diversified to manage risk. Asset allocation (i.e., the percentage of stocks, bonds, and other investments) should align with your risk tolerance and investment horizon.

5. **Withdrawal Strategy**: Plan your withdrawals in retirement to minimize taxes. Often, withdrawing from taxable accounts first may be advantageous, followed by tax-deferred accounts, and finally tax-free accounts (like Roth IRAs).

6. **Rebalance Periodically**: As market conditions change, regularly reassess and rebalance your portfolio to maintain your desired asset allocation.

It may also be helpful to consult with a financial advisor to create a tailored strategy that suits your individual situation.
