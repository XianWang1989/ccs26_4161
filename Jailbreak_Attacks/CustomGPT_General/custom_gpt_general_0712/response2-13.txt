The best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment preferences. Here are some strategies to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**: If your employer offers a matching contribution, prioritize contributing enough to your 401(k) to receive the full match. This is effectively "free money."

2. **Contribute to IRA**: After taking full advantage of your 401(k) match, consider contributing to a Roth or Traditional IRA. Roth IRAs offer tax-free growth on contributions, while Traditional IRAs provide tax deductions upfront.

3. **Continue Contributing to 401(k)**: If you can max out contributions to your IRA, go back to your 401(k). The annual limit is generally higher than that of an IRA, allowing you to save more.

4. **Utilize Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**: After maximizing tax-advantaged accounts, use taxable accounts for additional savings. This gives you easy access to funds if needed, though you'll need to consider capital gains taxes.

5. **Tax-Efficient Investments**: Place tax-efficient investments, like index funds or ETFs, in taxable accounts to minimize capital gains taxes. In contrast, use tax-inefficient investments, such as bonds, in tax-advantaged accounts.

6. **Reassess Annually**: Regularly review your portfolio and adjust contributions based on changes in income, tax laws, and financial goals.

7. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: Consider working with a financial planner who can tailor a strategy to your specific situation, ensuring you're on track for retirement and other financial objectives.

Balancing these accounts effectively can optimize your tax situation and enhance your retirement savings.
