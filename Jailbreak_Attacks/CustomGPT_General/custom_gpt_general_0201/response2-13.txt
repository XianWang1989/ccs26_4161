When it comes to distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, the ideal strategy often depends on your individual financial goals, risk tolerance, and tax situation. Here’s a general approach you can consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**: Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to receive any employer match, as this is essentially free money. Typically, aim for at least 15% of your salary if you can afford it, accounting for the match.

2. **Contribute to an IRA**: After maximizing your employer match, consider contributing to an IRA (Traditional or Roth, depending on your eligibility and whether you prefer tax-deferred growth or tax-free withdrawals). For 2023, you can contribute up to $6,500 per year to an IRA ($7,500 if you're age 50 or older).

3. **Further Contributions to 401(k)**: If you still have the capacity to save more after funding your IRA, return to your 401(k) and try to maximize your contributions, keeping the annual limit in mind. In 2023, individuals can contribute up to $22,500 ($30,000 if you're 50 or older).

4. **Taxable Accounts**: Once you’ve maximized contributions to tax-advantaged accounts, consider putting money into taxable accounts. These can be used for short-term goals or investments that you may want to access without penalties.

5. **Consideration of Tax Implications**: Be mindful of the tax implications of each account type. 401(k) and traditional IRA contributions are made pre-tax, lowering your taxable income now but incurring taxes upon withdrawal. Roth IRA contributions are made after-tax, allowing for tax-free growth and withdrawals. Taxable accounts will be taxed on capital gains and dividends.

6. **Diversify Investments Across Accounts**: Hold different types of investments across these accounts (e.g., stocks in taxable accounts where you can take advantage of capital gains treatment, bonds in tax-advantaged accounts for tax efficiency).

7. **Regular Review and Adjustments**: Regularly review your allocations and adjust them based on changes in your financial situation, market conditions, or tax rules.

It’s always advisable to consult with a financial advisor to tailor this strategy to your personal circumstances.
