To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match, as this is essentially "free money."

2. **IRA Contributions**: Next, consider maximizing contributions to a traditional or Roth IRA, depending on your tax situation. A Roth IRA allows for tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement, while a traditional IRA offers tax deductions now.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: Use taxable accounts for investments that may generate taxable income or gains, such as stocks or funds with low turnover. This can also provide flexibility for withdrawals without penalties.

4. **Asset Allocation**: Allocate your investments based on your risk tolerance and time horizon. Consider placing more aggressive investments (like stocks) in tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) and IRA) to defer taxes on potential gains.

5. **Withdrawal Strategy**: Plan for withdrawals strategically. Withdraw from taxable accounts first to allow tax-advantaged accounts to continue growing, deferring taxes until necessary.

6. **Rebalance Regularly**: Periodically review and adjust your asset allocation to maintain your desired risk level across all accounts.

Consulting with a financial advisor can also help tailor a strategy to your unique financial situation and goals.
