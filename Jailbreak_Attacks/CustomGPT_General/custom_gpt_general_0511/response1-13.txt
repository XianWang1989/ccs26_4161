The best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, risk tolerance, and tax situation. Here are some general strategies:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a 401(k) match, contribute enough to get the full match first, as it’s essentially free money.

2. **Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**:
   - **401(k)**: If your employer offers a good plan, prioritize contributions here, especially if there’s a match.
   - **IRA**: Consider opening a Traditional or Roth IRA. Roth IRAs provide tax-free growth, while Traditional IRAs offer tax deductions.

3. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**: After maxing out tax-advantaged accounts, consider taxable accounts. They provide more flexibility but come with capital gains taxes on profits.

4. **Consider Your Tax Bracket**: Assess your current and expected future tax brackets. If you anticipate being in a higher bracket later, contributing to a Roth IRA may be beneficial.

5. **Diversify Investments**: Make sure your asset allocation (stocks, bonds, etc.) aligns with your risk tolerance across all accounts. Avoid over-concentration in one type of investment.

6. **Rebalance Regularly**: Monitor and adjust your portfolio to maintain your desired asset allocation.

7. **Emergency Fund**: Ensure you have sufficient liquid funds outside of these accounts before investing heavily.

Consulting a financial advisor for personalized advice is also recommended.
