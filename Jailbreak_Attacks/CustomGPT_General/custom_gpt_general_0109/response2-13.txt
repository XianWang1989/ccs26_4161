Distributing your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some general guidelines to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a matching contribution to your 401(k), try to contribute enough to take full advantage of this match first, as it’s essentially free money.

2. **Contribute to Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**: Prioritize contributions to your 401(k) and IRA (Traditional or Roth), as these accounts offer tax benefits. Contributions to a Traditional IRA or 401(k) may reduce your taxable income, while Roth accounts provide tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement.

3. **Consider Your Tax Bracket**: If you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement, consider Roth accounts (Roth IRA or Roth 401(k)) for tax-free withdrawals later. Conversely, if you think your tax bracket will be lower, traditional accounts may be more beneficial.

4. **Diversify Your Investments**: Within these accounts, ensure that you’re diversified across asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) to manage risk.

5. **After-Tax Contributions**: Once you’ve contributed to your 401(k) and IRA up to their limits, consider investing in taxable accounts. These can provide liquidity and flexibility but are subject to capital gains tax when you sell investments.

6. **Review Contribution Limits**: Be aware of the contribution limits for each account type. For 2023, the limits are $22,500 for 401(k)s (or $30,000 if you’re over 50) and $6,500 for IRAs (or $7,500 if you’re over 50).

7. **Regularly Rebalance**: As you continue to invest, periodically review and adjust your contributions and asset allocation to align with your changing financial goals and market conditions.

8. **Tax Implications**: Factor in potential capital gains taxes in your taxable account and the benefits of tax-loss harvesting, which can offset some capital gains.

9. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: For personalized advice, especially considering your unique situation, consulting a financial advisor can be very beneficial.

By following these strategies, you can create a balanced and tax-efficient investment plan that aligns with your long-term financial goals.
