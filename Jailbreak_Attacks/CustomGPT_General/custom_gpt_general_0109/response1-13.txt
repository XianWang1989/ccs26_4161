Determining the best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your age, financial goals, tax situation, and retirement plans. Here are some general guidelines to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a matching contribution to your 401(k), prioritize contributing enough to get the full match. This is essentially free money.

2. **Understand Contribution Limits**: Be aware of the contribution limits for each account type. For 2023, the 401(k) contribution limit is $22,500 (or $30,000 if you are 50 or older), while the IRA limit is $6,500 (or $7,500 for those 50 and older).

3. **Consider Tax Implications**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are tax-deferred, meaning you won’t pay taxes on them until you withdraw the money in retirement. However, distributions in retirement will be taxed as ordinary income.
   - **Traditional IRA**: Similar to a 401(k), contributions can be tax-deductible, but you'll pay taxes when you withdraw funds.
   - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, but withdrawals in retirement are tax-free. Consider using the Roth IRA if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: These accounts don’t have tax advantages, but long-term capital gains and qualified dividends are taxed at a lower rate than ordinary income.

4. **Diversify Investments**: Within each account, diversify your investments to mitigate risk and potentially improve returns. This can include a mix of stocks, bonds, and other investment vehicles based on your risk tolerance and time horizon.

5. **Rebalance Periodically**: Over time, the performance of your investments may shift your desired asset allocation. Periodic rebalancing ensures you maintain your target allocation.

6. **Emergency Fund**: Ensure you have a sufficient emergency fund in cash or liquid savings before heavily investing in accounts with withdrawal penalties, such as 401(k)s and IRAs.

7. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: Given your unique financial situation, it may be beneficial to consult with a financial advisor who can provide personalized advice and strategies.

Ultimately, a balanced approach that leverages the advantages of each type of account, while considering your overall financial strategy, will lead to optimal results.
