Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your tax situation, investment goals, and time horizon. Here’s a general guideline:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match, as this is free money.
   - **IRA**: If eligible, consider maxing out your contributions to a traditional or Roth IRA for tax benefits.

### 2. **Consider Tax Impact**
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for investments that generate capital gains or interest income, as they can be taxed at a lower rate than ordinary income. This account is also good for short-term goals since you can withdraw anytime.

### 3. **Diversify Investments**
   - Keep a mix of stocks, bonds, and other assets in each account type, but tailor it based on the tax implications:
     - **401(k) and IRA**: Focus on tax-efficient investments like growth stocks or index funds that minimize dividends.
     - **Taxable Accounts**: Consider holding dividend-paying stocks or municipal bonds for tax advantages.

### 4. **Rebalance Regularly**
   - Make sure your asset allocation remains aligned with your risk tolerance and goals as markets change.

### 5. **Withdrawal Strategy**
   - Plan for how and when you'll withdraw from each account to minimize taxes in retirement.

### Example Allocation Strategy
- **401(k)**: 30%
- **IRA**: 30%
- **Taxable**: 40%

This approach should fit your age, risk tolerance, and retirement strategy. Adjust as necessary, and consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized advice.
