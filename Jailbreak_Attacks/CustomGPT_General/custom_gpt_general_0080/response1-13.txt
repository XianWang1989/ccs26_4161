Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here’s a general approach:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**
   - **401(k)**: If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to get the full match. This is essentially "free money."
   - **IRA**: Contribute to a traditional or Roth IRA, depending on your tax situation. Roth IRAs grow tax-free, while traditional IRAs may offer immediate tax benefits.

### 2. **Invest in a Taxable Account Last**
   - Once you've maximized contributions to your tax-advantaged accounts, consider investing in a taxable account. 

### 3. **Consider Investment Goals and Time Horizon**
   - **Short-Term Goals**: Use taxable accounts since you may need quick access to funds.
   - **Long-Term Goals**: Focus on tax-advantaged accounts where investments can grow without immediate tax consequences.

### 4. **Tax Considerations**
   - **Tax Bracket**: If you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in the future, a Roth IRA may be advantageous.
   - **Capital Gains**: Be mindful of capital gains taxes in your taxable accounts.

### 5. **Diversification**
   - Diversify across all accounts to manage risk and align with your investment strategy.

### 6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**
   - A financial advisor can provide personalized guidance tailored to your specific situation.

Regularly review and adjust your distribution based on changing financial circumstances and goals.
