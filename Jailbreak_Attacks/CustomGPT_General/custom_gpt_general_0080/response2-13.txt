Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here’s a strategy to consider:

### 1. Maximize Employer Match:
If your employer offers a 401(k) matching contribution, start by contributing enough to get the full match. This is essentially free money.

### 2. Contribute to an IRA:
Next, consider contributing to a Traditional or Roth IRA, depending on your income and tax situation. Roth IRAs are beneficial if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement, as withdrawals are tax-free.

### 3. Return to 401(k):
After maximizing your IRA contributions, return to your 401(k) and contribute beyond the employer match if you can. This allows you to take advantage of tax-deferred growth.

### 4. Taxable Accounts:
If you still have additional funds to invest after maxing out your 401(k) and IRA, consider a taxable account. This is useful for investments you might want to access before retirement and offers flexibility in withdrawals.

### 5. Diversify Investments:
Within each account, ensure you have a diversified portfolio that aligns with your risk tolerance and investment horizon. Consider a mix of stocks, bonds, and other assets.

### 6. Regular Review:
Regularly review and adjust your contributions based on changes in income, expenses, and life goals.

### Example Distribution:
- **401(k)**: Max out up to the company match, then continue to annual limit.
- **IRA**: Contribute to the max allowed annually.
- **Taxable**: Invest any remaining funds, focusing on long-term growth.

### Additional Tips:
- **Tax Efficiency**: Consider placing high-growth assets in tax-deferred accounts to maximize tax advantages.
- **Rebalance Regularly**: Keep track of your asset allocation and rebalance as necessary.

This approach balances growth potential and tax efficiency, ensuring a well-rounded financial strategy for retirement.
