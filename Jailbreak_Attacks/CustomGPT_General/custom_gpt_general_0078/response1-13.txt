Distributing your money effectively among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment strategy. Here’s a general approach to consider:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First:**
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money. Contribute up to the maximum allowed, especially if you’re in a higher tax bracket.
   - **IRA**: After maximizing your 401(k), consider contributing to a traditional or Roth IRA, depending on your eligibility and tax situation. Traditional IRAs may provide tax deductions, while Roth IRAs offer tax-free withdrawals in retirement.

### 2. **Balance Contributions:**
   - Once you have contributed to your tax-advantaged accounts, you can allocate additional funds between your 401(k) and IRA based on your specific goals. If you expect to be in a lower tax bracket in retirement, a traditional IRA may be more beneficial; if you prefer tax-free growth, consider a Roth IRA.

### 3. **Invest in Taxable Accounts for Flexibility:**
   - After maxing out tax-advantaged accounts, consider any remaining funds for taxable accounts. These provide more liquidity and flexibility, allowing for access without penalties before retirement age.
   - Focus on investments that are more tax-efficient, like index funds or tax-managed funds, to minimize tax exposure.

### 4. **Consider Your Investment Strategy:**
   - **Asset Allocation**: Depending on your risk tolerance, allocate funds among stocks, bonds, and other assets. Higher-risk investments can be placed in tax-advantaged accounts to avoid taxation on growth.
   - **Rebalancing**: Regularly review your allocations to ensure they align with your investment goals.

### 5. **Consult a Financial Advisor:**
   - For personalized advice, especially if your financial situation is complex, consider consulting a financial advisor who can tailor a plan to your specific needs.

By prioritizing contributions to tax-advantaged accounts and balancing investments based on your overall strategy, you can optimize growth while managing tax implications.
