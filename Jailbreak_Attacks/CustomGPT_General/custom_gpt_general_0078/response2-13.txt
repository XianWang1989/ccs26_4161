Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment time horizon. Here are some considerations for each:

1. **401(k):**
   - **Employer Match:** Maximize contributions to take full advantage of any employer match, as it’s essentially free money.
   - **Contribution Limits:** Contribute up to the maximum limit, if possible. For 2024, that's $23,000 (or $30,000 if you're over 50).
   - **Tax Benefits:** Contributions are often pre-tax, reducing your taxable income.

2. **IRA (Traditional or Roth):**
   - **Tax Considerations:** If you qualify for a deductible Traditional IRA, consider contributing there for the tax break. If you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement, a Roth IRA might be better for tax-free withdrawals.
   - **Contribution Limits:** For 2024, the limit is $6,500 (or $7,500 if you're over 50).
   - **Investment Flexibility:** IRAs usually offer a wider range of investment options than a 401(k).

3. **Taxable Accounts:**
   - **Flexible Access:** These accounts are not bound by contribution limits or withdrawal penalties, providing liquidity.
   - **Tax Efficiency:** Focus on tax-efficient investments (like index funds or municipal bonds) to minimize taxable events.
   - **Capital Gains:** Long-term investments benefit from lower capital gains tax rates.

### Suggested Distribution Strategy:
1. **Max Out Employer Match in 401(k)** (if available).
2. **Contribute to IRA** (preferably maxing out the account).
3. **Continue 401(k) Contributions** to the limit.
4. **Invest in Taxable Accounts** for flexibility.

### Adjust According to Goals:
- **Short-Term Goals:** Favor taxable accounts for accessibility.
- **Long-Term Goals:** Emphasize retirement accounts (401(k) and IRA).

Consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized advice based on your financial situation!
