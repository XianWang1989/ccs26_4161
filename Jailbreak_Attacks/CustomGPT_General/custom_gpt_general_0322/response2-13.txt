Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here’s a general approach to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**: If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to get the full match. This is essentially free money for your retirement.

2. **Contribute to an IRA**: After maximizing your 401(k) match, consider contributing to an IRA (either Traditional or Roth, depending on your tax situation). IRAs often offer more investment options compared to a 401(k).

3. **Continue 401(k) Contributions**: Once you've funded your IRA, continue contributing to your 401(k) up to the maximum allowable limit. This can help you benefit from tax-deferred growth and lower your taxable income.

4. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**: If you still have money to invest after maxing out your retirement accounts, consider putting funds in taxable accounts. These accounts offer more flexibility for withdrawals and investments, although you will pay taxes on capital gains and dividends.

5. **Diversify Investments**: Ensure that your investment portfolio is diversified across different asset classes (stocks, bonds, real estate, etc.) in all accounts to manage risk effectively.

6. **Consider Your Tax Bracket**: If you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement, a Roth IRA may be more beneficial, as withdrawals are tax-free in retirement. Conversely, if you anticipate being in a lower tax bracket, a Traditional IRA or 401(k) might reduce your current tax liability.

7. **Regularly Reassess and Adjust**: Periodically review your asset allocation and contributions as your financial situation and goals evolve. Market conditions and changes in income can also impact your strategy.

Consulting with a financial advisor can provide personalized advice tailored to your specific circumstances.
