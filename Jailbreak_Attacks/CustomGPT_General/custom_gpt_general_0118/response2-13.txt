Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here are some strategies to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get any employer match. This is essentially free money.

2. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - After maximizing the 401(k) match, consider maxing out your IRA (Roth or Traditional, depending on your tax situation).
   - If you are eligible for a Roth IRA, contributions grow tax-free, which is advantageous if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement.

3. **Optimize Contributions**:
   - Contribute to your 401(k) up to the maximum annual limit after your IRA.
   - If you can contribute beyond the IRA limit, direct excess funds to your 401(k).

4. **Use Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**: 
   - Invest in taxable accounts for shorter-term goals since they offer more liquidity and flexibility.
   - Focus on tax-efficient investments (like index funds) to minimize capital gains taxes.

5. **Rebalance as Needed**: Periodically review your asset allocation and rebalance your accounts to align with your risk tolerance and investment goals.

6. **Account for Taxes**: 
   - Keep in mind the tax implications of withdrawing from each type of account. Traditional accounts are taxed upon withdrawal, while Roth accounts are tax-free.

7. **Consider Financial Goals**: Adjust your distribution strategy based on whether your focus is on retirement, buying a home, or other financial goals.

Always consult a financial advisor for personalized advice based on your specific situation.
