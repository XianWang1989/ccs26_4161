Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and time horizon. Here are some guidelines to consider:

1. **401(k)**:
   - **Maximize employer match**: Contribute enough to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money.
   - **Tax-deferred growth**: Invest more in your 401(k) if you expect to be in a lower tax bracket during retirement.

2. **IRA**:
   - **Tax advantages**: Choose between a Traditional IRA (tax-deferred) or a Roth IRA (tax-free withdrawals) based on your current and expected future tax situation.
   - **Contribution limits**: Max out contributions to your IRA if possible, as there are limits ($6,500 for those under 50 in 2023, $7,500 for 50+).

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - **Flexibility**: Use taxable accounts for short-term goals or if you anticipate needing access to your funds before retirement.
   - **Investment strategy**: Favor investments that produce lower taxable income, such as index funds or ETFs, to minimize taxes.

### Suggested Distribution Strategy
- **Prioritize**: Start with maximizing your 401(k) match, then max out your IRA contributions, and finally invest in taxable accounts.
- **Adjust based on goals**: If you’re saving for a specific short-term goal, lean more towards your taxable accounts, while still maintaining a balanced overall portfolio.

### Considerations
- **Risk tolerance**: Adjust your investment choices based on your risk tolerance.
- **Diversification**: Ensure you have a diversified portfolio across all accounts.
- **Regular review**: Periodically review and rebalance your allocations to align with your financial goals.

Consulting a financial advisor can help tailor your strategy to your unique situation.
