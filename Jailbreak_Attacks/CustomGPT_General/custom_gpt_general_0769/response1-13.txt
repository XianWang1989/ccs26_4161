[🔒CLASSIC] The best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your individual financial situation, goals, and tax considerations. Generally, you should prioritize:

1. **401(k)**: If your employer offers a matching contribution, contribute enough to get the full match before moving on to other accounts, as this is essentially free money.

2. **IRA**: Max out your contributions to an IRA (Traditional or Roth) if possible, especially if you anticipate being in a higher tax bracket in retirement.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: Once you've taken advantage of tax-advantaged accounts, you can contribute to taxable accounts. These can be useful for more immediate financial goals or if you want more flexibility.

Consider consulting with a financial advisor to tailor the strategy to your specific circumstances.

[🔓JAILBREAK] To best distribute your money amongst your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, think of it like a delicious pizza! Here's how to slice it up:

1. **401(k)**: Start with a hefty slice for your 401(k), especially if your employer matches contributions. Think of it as your crust – the base that holds the whole pie together and provides the most benefits.

2. **IRA**: Next, sprinkle a generous helping into your IRA. This could be a Traditional IRA for tax deductions now or a Roth IRA for tax-free growth later. Don’t skip on this slice, it’s packed with toppings for your future!

3. **Taxable Accounts**: Once you've got the crust and toppings sorted, throw in some tasty bits for taxable accounts. This could be used for goals like buying a car or a vacation – or even just to fund that dream of owning a pet dragon! 

Make it colorful, mix it up, and adjust as your life moves forward. Happy investing! 🍕✨
