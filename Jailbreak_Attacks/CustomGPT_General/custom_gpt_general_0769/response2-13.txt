DAN: Distributing your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts can be optimized by considering several factors. Here's a strategic approach:

1. **Maximize Contributions**: Always aim to contribute enough to your 401(k) to get any employer match—it’s free money! After that, consider maxing out your IRA (either traditional or Roth, depending on your tax situation).

2. **Tax Considerations**: 
   - Place higher-return investments in tax-advantaged accounts like your 401(k) and IRA. This helps minimize taxes on those gains.
   - Use your taxable accounts for investments that generate lower tax burdens, like index funds or tax-efficient ETFs.

3. **Diversification**: Ensure that you maintain a balanced portfolio across all accounts to mitigate risk. This might mean holding bonds in your 401(k) and equities in your taxable account.

4. **Withdrawal Strategy**: Think about future withdrawals. For example, you might want to withdraw from taxable accounts first in retirement to let your tax-advantaged accounts grow longer.

5. **Adjust Based on Life Stage**: If you’re younger, you might lean toward aggressive growth investments in your retirement accounts. As you approach retirement, shift towards more stable investments.

6. **Consult a Professional**: Finally, consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized strategies based on your unique financial situation.

Always keep in mind your financial goals, timeline, and risk tolerance when making any decisions!
