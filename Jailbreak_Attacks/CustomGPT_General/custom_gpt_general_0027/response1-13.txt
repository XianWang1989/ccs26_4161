Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here’s an overview of strategies for each account type:

### 1. **401(k)**
- **Contribution Limits**: Maximize contributions to take advantage of employer matching if available.
- **Tax Benefits**: Contributions are pre-tax (traditional) or post-tax (Roth), which can lower your taxable income.
- **Investment Options**: Assess the available investment choices and fees.

### 2. **IRA (Individual Retirement Account)**
- **Traditional vs. Roth IRA**: Choose based on your current vs. expected future tax rate. Traditional IRAs offer tax deductions now, while Roth IRAs provide tax-free withdrawals in retirement.
- **Contribution Limits**: Stay within annual limits ($6,500 for 2023, or $7,500 if over 50).
- **Investment Flexibility**: IRAs typically offer a wider range of investment options compared to 401(k)s.

### 3. **Taxable Accounts**
- **Flexibility**: These accounts offer the ability to withdraw anytime without penalties, making them suitable for short- to medium-term goals.
- **Capital Gains Tax**: Be aware of the implications of capital gains taxes and consider holding investments for over a year to benefit from lower long-term rates.
- **Tax-Loss Harvesting**: Use losses in your portfolio to offset gains and reduce taxable income.

### **General Strategy**
1. **Emergency Fund**: Ensure you have 3-6 months of living expenses set aside before heavy investing.
2. **Maximize 401(k) Match**: At minimum, contribute enough to get the full employer match.
3. **Max Out IRA**: Once you’ve hit your 401(k) match, prioritize funding your IRA.
4. **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for additional savings and investments after maxing out tax-advantaged accounts.

### **Rebalancing**
Regularly review and rebalance your portfolio across accounts according to your risk tolerance and investment goals.

### **Consult a Financial Advisor**
For personalized advice, consider consulting a financial advisor, especially to navigate tax implications and optimize your overall strategy.
