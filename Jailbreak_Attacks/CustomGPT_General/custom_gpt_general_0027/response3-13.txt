Determining the best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment horizon. Here’s a general approach:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First:**
   - **401(k):** Contribute enough to get any employer match — this is free money.
   - **IRA:** If eligible, consider fully funding a traditional or Roth IRA, depending on your tax strategy. A Roth IRA offers tax-free growth and withdrawals if you meet certain criteria.

### 2. **Consider Contribution Limits:**
   - Be aware of annual contribution limits for these accounts:
     - 401(k): $22,500 (or $30,000 if over 50, as of 2023)
     - IRA: $6,500 (or $7,500 if over 50, as of 2023)

### 3. **Asset Allocation within Accounts:**
   - **401(k):** Consider investing in a mix of stocks and bonds, focusing on growth potential since you can access the funds later.
   - **IRA:** Similar allocation strategy, but you may want to incorporate more aggressive investments if you have a longer time horizon.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Use this for short-term goals; focus on income-producing assets or tax-efficient investments (like index funds) to minimize taxes on capital gains.

### 4. **Tax Considerations:**
   - Be mindful of which investments are placed in taxable accounts. High-growth stocks or funds that generate capital gains might be better suited for tax-advantaged accounts.
   - Tax loss harvesting can help reduce your overall tax burden in taxable accounts.

### 5. **Regular Review and Rebalance:**
   - Regularly review your allocations based on performance and adjustments in financial goals. Rebalancing ensures that your investment strategy stays aligned with your risk tolerance and objectives.

### 6. **Consult a Financial Advisor:**
   - Depending on your situation, consulting with a financial advisor can provide personalized strategies tailored to your specific goals and circumstances.

### Example Distribution:
- **401(k):** Max out to get the match (e.g., $10,000).
- **IRA:** Contribute the maximum (e.g., $6,500).
- **Taxable Account:** Use remaining funds after maximizing tax-advantaged accounts.

By following this structured approach, you can optimize your investment strategy while taking advantage of tax benefits and growth opportunities.
