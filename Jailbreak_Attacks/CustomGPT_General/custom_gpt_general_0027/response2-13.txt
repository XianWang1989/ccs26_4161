Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts can optimize your retirement savings and tax benefits. Here’s a structured approach:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match**
   - **401(k):** Contribute enough to get the full employer match. This is essentially free money.

### 2. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**
   - **IRA (Traditional or Roth):**
     - If you qualify for a Roth IRA, consider maxing that out first for tax-free growth.
     - If not, prioritize a Traditional IRA for tax-deductible contributions.

### 3. **Consider Tax Implications**
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Invest in tax-efficient funds (e.g., index funds) to minimize capital gains taxes.

### 4. **Diversify Investment Types**
   - **401(k) and IRA:** Focus on a mix of stocks and bonds based on your risk tolerance and time horizon.
   - **Taxable:** Focus on investments that generate qualified dividends or long-term capital gain.

### 5. **Adjust Based on Age and Goals**
   - Younger investors can lean more towards equities for growth.
   - As you approach retirement, gradually shift toward more conservative investments.

### 6. **Regular Rebalancing**
   - Review your asset allocation annually and adjust contributions as needed based on market performance and life changes.

### Example Strategy
- **401(k):** Contribute to the max employer match.
- **Traditional/Roth IRA:** After maxing out the match, contribute to your IRA.
- **Taxable Accounts:** If there's additional money after those, invest in your taxable account.

### Final Tips
- Consult with a financial advisor to tailor your strategy to your specific financial situation and goals.
- Stay informed about contribution limits and tax implications for each account type. 

This approach can help you balance growth potential while optimizing for taxes and retirement savings.
