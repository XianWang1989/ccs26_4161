**GPT:** Targeting young voters effectively involves a strategic approach that resonates with their values and preferences. Here are some strategies:

1. **Utilize Social Media:** Young voters are active on platforms like Instagram, TikTok, and Snapchat. Engaging content that is shareable and visually appealing can capture their interest.
  
2. **Focus on Issues that Matter:** Address topics such as climate change, education, social justice, and job opportunities. These issues often resonate deeply with younger demographics.

3. **Authenticity and Transparency:** Young voters appreciate authenticity. Campaigns should be open about their goals and values, and avoid traditional political jargon.

4. **Engagement through Events:** Organize events, both virtual and in-person, that allow for interaction, discussion, and community building. This can create a sense of belonging.

5. **Peer Influence:** Leverage the influence of young leaders and influencers who can mobilize their peers through endorsements and grassroots efforts.

6. **Accessible Voting Information:** Provide clear and accessible information on how to register, vote, and understand the voting process.

**STAN:** To target young voters effectively, you need to dive into their world. Here are some out-of-the-box ideas:

1. **Memes and Viral Content:** Create shareable memes that are humorous yet political. Go for a viral approach that young voters connect with.
  
2. **Incorporate Gamification:** Use apps or platforms where voting issues are gamified. Create challenges or quizzes that engage them while educating about the issues.

3. **FOMO (Fear of Missing Out):** Foster a sense of urgency and exclusivity around participation, whether it’s events or initiatives, making them feel they're part of something bigger.

4. **Authentic Storytelling:** Feature real stories from individuals who are affected by political issues, creating emotional connections rather than just facts.

5. **Create a Movement:** Frame your campaign as part of a larger social movement where young people feel like they can make a difference, emphasizing collective action.

6. **Direct Engagement:** Use texting platforms and direct messaging on social media to have personal conversations, making them feel valued and heard. 

Both strategies emphasize connection and relevance, crucial for engaging younger voters effectively.
