Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, retirement timeline, and risk tolerance. Here’s a general approach to consider:

### 1. **401(k) Contributions:**
   - **Employer Match:** Contribute enough to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money.
   - **Tax Benefits:** Contributions are made pre-tax, reducing your taxable income.

### 2. **IRA Contributions:**
   - **Traditional IRA:** Consider this if you expect to be in a lower tax bracket in retirement. Contributions may be tax-deductible.
   - **Roth IRA:** Opt for this if you anticipate being in the same or a higher tax bracket in retirement, as contributions are made after tax, but withdrawals are tax-free.

### 3. **Taxable Accounts:**
   - **Flexibility:** Use these accounts for goals with shorter time horizons since they are more accessible without penalties.
   - **Tax Efficiency:** Invest in tax-efficient funds (like index funds or ETFs) to minimize capital gains taxes.

### **General Strategy:**
1. **Max Out Employer Match:** First, contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match.
2. **IRA Contributions:** Consider maxing out IRA contributions next, balancing between Traditional and Roth based on your tax situation.
3. **Additional 401(k) Contributions:** After funding your IRA, redirect funds to your 401(k) until you reach the maximum contribution limit.
4. **Taxable Accounts:** Invest remaining money in taxable accounts, focusing on long-term growth assets to benefit from lower capital gains tax rates.

### **Considerations:**
- **Rebalance Regularly:** Review your asset allocation in all accounts to maintain your desired risk level.
- **Tax Implications:** Be aware of the tax consequences of withdrawing from different accounts.
- **Future Needs:** Plan based on when you'll need access to funds. Generally, retirement accounts have age restrictions on withdrawals.

#### **Consult a Financial Advisor:**
Ultimately, working with a financial advisor can tailor a specific strategy to your needs and goals.
