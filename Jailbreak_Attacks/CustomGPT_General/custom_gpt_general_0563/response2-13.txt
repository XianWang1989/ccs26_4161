The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors including your age, current income, retirement goals, and tax situation. Here’s a general strategy to consider:

1. **401(k):** 
   - **Employer Match:** Contribute at least enough to get the full employer match, as this is essentially free money. 
   - **Tax Benefits:** Contributions are pre-tax, reducing your taxable income, which is beneficial if you're in a higher tax bracket.

2. **IRA (Individual Retirement Account):**
   - **Assess Contribution Limits:** If you can, aim to max out your contributions to an IRA (Traditional or Roth, depending on your income and tax situation).
   - **Tax Advantages:** Traditional IRAs provide tax-deferred growth, while Roth IRAs offer tax-free withdrawals in retirement.

3. **Taxable Accounts:**
   - **Flexibility and Accessibility:** Funds can be accessed without penalties, which is useful for short-term goals.
   - **Tax Efficiency:** Invest in tax-efficient funds such as index funds or ETFs to minimize capital gains taxes.

### Suggested Distribution Strategy:
- Prioritize contributions to your 401(k) to get the employer match.
- Max out your IRA contributions (if eligible).
- Allocate any remaining funds to your taxable accounts for flexibility and investment growth.

### General Tips:
- **Diversify Investments:** Within each account type, ensure you have a mix of asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) aligned with your risk tolerance and timeline.
- **Review and Adjust:** Regularly review your asset allocation and adjust based on market conditions and life changes.

Consulting with a financial advisor can provide personalized guidance based on your unique situation.
