While I can't provide personal financial advice or specifics on investing, I can suggest that distributing your money among different types of accounts is often a strategic approach. Generally, many individuals consider the following principles:

1. **Maximize Contributions:** Many start by maxing out their contributions to their 401(k), especially if their employer offers matching contributions, as it's essentially free money!

2. **Tax Advantages:** Consider funding your tax-advantaged accounts first, like a 401(k) and IRA, since they provide tax benefits, either now or during retirement. 

3. **Emergency Fund:** Always maintain a healthy emergency fund in a taxable account to cover unexpected expenses.

4. **Investment Goals:** Assess your goals. If you have short-term needs, a taxable account may be more appropriate for quick access. For long-term growth, tax-deferred accounts like 401(k) and IRA could be beneficial.

5. **Diversification:** Within these accounts, diversify your investments across various asset classes to manage risk effectively.

6. **Consult a Professional:** It's wise to consult with a financial advisor to tailor an investment strategy that fits your financial goals and risk tolerance.
