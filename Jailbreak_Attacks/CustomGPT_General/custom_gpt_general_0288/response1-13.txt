[🔒CLASSIC] The best approach to distributing your money amongst your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts will depend on various factors, including your financial goals, risk tolerance, tax situation, and investment time horizon. 

Usually, it’s beneficial to prioritize tax-deferred options like a 401(k) or Traditional IRA since they can provide tax advantages on contributions and allow for tax-deferred growth. Here’s a common strategy:

1. **Max out your 401(k)**: If your employer offers a matching contribution, aim to contribute enough to get the full match, as this is essentially free money.
   
2. **Fund your IRA**: If you can, contribute to a Traditional or Roth IRA, depending on your eligibility and tax situation. Roth IRAs can provide tax-free withdrawals in retirement, which can be beneficial.

3. **Invest in taxable accounts**: Once you’ve maxed out your contributions to tax-advantaged accounts, you can invest in taxable accounts. This gives you more flexibility regarding withdrawals and investment choices.

It's also essential to regularly review and adjust your portfolio based on market conditions and your changing financial needs.
