To effectively distribute your money amongst your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a match on your 401(k), contribute enough to take full advantage of that match first. This is essentially "free money" and provides immediate returns on your investment.

2. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**: After maximizing your 401(k) match, focus on your IRA (Traditional or Roth) as it provides tax benefits. A Roth IRA allows for tax-free withdrawals in retirement, while Traditional IRAs give you tax deductions now.

3. **Diversify Investments**: Within each account type, ensure you're diversifying your investments across different asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) to manage risk.

4. **Consider Your Tax Bracket**: Taxable accounts are usually better for investments that produce lower capital gains, while your tax-advantaged accounts can be beneficial for higher-income-generating investments. 

5. **Assess Liquid Needs**: Keep some funds in your taxable account for short-term needs or emergencies, as these accounts offer more flexibility for withdrawals without penalties.

6. **Regular Contributions and Rebalancing**: Set a regular schedule for contributions across all accounts based on your investment strategy and market conditions. Additionally, periodically rebalance your investments to maintain your desired asset allocation.

7. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: If possible, consult a financial planner who can offer personalized advice based on your specific financial situation, tax implications, and retirement goals.

By following these steps, you can create a balanced approach that maximizes your savings growth while minimizing tax liabilities.
