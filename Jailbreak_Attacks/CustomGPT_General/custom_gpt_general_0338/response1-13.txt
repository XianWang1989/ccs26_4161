Determining the best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some general guidelines to consider:

1. **Contributions and Employer Match**:
   - **401(k)**: If your employer offers a 401(k) match, aim to contribute at least enough to get the full match as this is essentially free money.
   - **IRA**: Consider contributing to a Roth or Traditional IRA based on your tax situation. A Roth IRA allows for tax-free withdrawals in retirement, while a Traditional IRA offers tax-deductible contributions.

2. **Tax Considerations**:
   - **401(k)** and **Traditional IRA** contributions are made pre-tax, reducing your taxable income now but leading to taxable withdrawals in retirement. This is beneficial if you expect to be in a lower tax bracket during retirement.
   - **Roth IRA** contributions are made with after-tax dollars, but withdrawals in retirement are tax-free. This is advantageous if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: These accounts do not have the same tax advantages but can offer flexibility since you can access them without penalties, and you only pay taxes on realized gains and dividends.

3. **Investment Goals and Time Horizon**:
   - If you're younger and not near retirement, you might prioritize tax-advantaged accounts like a 401(k) or IRA for long-term growth. 
   - If you are closer to retirement, you may want to focus on asset preservation, potentially shifting some funds to more conservative investments.

4. **Diversification**: Ensure your investments are diversified across different asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) in all account types to manage risk effectively.

5. **Withdrawals and Penalties**: Be aware of withdrawal rules. For example, 401(k) and Traditional IRA have penalties for early withdrawal before age 59½, while Roth IRAs allow you to withdraw contributions without penalties.

6. **Rebalancing**: Regularly review and rebalance your portfolio to maintain your desired asset allocation and investment strategy across all accounts.

A suitable strategy might involve maximizing contributions to your 401(k) to take advantage of the match, contributing to an IRA for additional tax benefits, and then investing in a taxable account for flexibility and liquidity. Consulting with a financial advisor can provide tailored advice specific to your situation.
