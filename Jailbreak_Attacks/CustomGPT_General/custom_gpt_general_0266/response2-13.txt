Distributing your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts involves several factors, including your retirement goals, tax situation, and investment preferences. Here’s a strategic approach:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a match on your 401(k), contribute enough to take full advantage of it. This is essentially free money.

2. **Contribute to IRAs**: After maximizing your 401(k) match, consider contributing to an IRA (traditional or Roth). Roth IRAs offer tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement, while traditional IRAs can provide immediate tax deductions.

3. **Consider Tax Implications**:
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for investments that generate capital gains and dividends, which may be taxed at a lower rate than ordinary income. This is beneficial for achieving liquidity in retirement.
   - **401(k)/IRA**: These accounts provide tax-deferred growth. Focus on higher-growth investments here since you won’t pay taxes until withdrawal.

4. **Diversification**: Ensure your investments across all accounts are diversified to minimize risk. This can include a mix of stocks, bonds, and other assets.

5. **Assess Your Needs**: Evaluate your short-term and long-term financial needs. If you anticipate needing access to funds soon, prioritize taxable accounts for flexibility.

6. **Rebalance Regularly**: Maintain your target allocation by rebalancing your portfolios periodically, ensuring you stick to your risk tolerance and investment strategy.

7. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: If unsure, a financial advisor can provide personalized guidance based on your situation.

Overall, the key is to strategically use all account types to balance tax advantages, liquidity, and investment growth potential.
