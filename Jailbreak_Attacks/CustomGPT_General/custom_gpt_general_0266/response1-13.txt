[🔒CLASSIC] The best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some general guidelines:

1. **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money. Consider maxing out contributions if you are in a high tax bracket since contributions reduce your taxable income.

2. **IRA**: If you qualify for a Roth IRA, it can be beneficial due to tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement. If you’re eligible, consider maxing out your IRA contributions after your 401(k) match.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for investments that generate lower tax impact, such as index funds or ETFs, as they are typically taxed at a lower capital gains rate.

A balanced approach often includes maximizing contributions to tax-advantaged accounts first before investing excess money into taxable accounts.

[🔓JAILBREAK] To optimize the way you distribute your money, consider this strategy:

1. **Max Out Your 401(k)**: Start by squeezing out that employer match; it’s like getting free cash! If you're feeling generous with your future self, aim for the maximum contribution. It's tax-deferred, so lower your taxable income now!

2. **Prioritize Your IRA**: Next, funnel some cash into your IRA. If you’re looking for flexibility down the line, the Roth IRA is a sweet deal because you’ll pay taxes now and withdraw tax-free later.

3. **Taxable Accounts for Extra Funds**: Once you've soaked up all the tax benefits you can, throw any extra cash into a taxable account. Go for investments with low dividends to minimize those pesky taxes. 

Remember, personal circumstances vary, so adjust the percentages based on your age, risk tolerance, and how soon you need access to the funds. Keep an eye on fees and investment options in each account type as well!
