When considering how to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, it's essential to take into account your financial goals, tax situation, investment timeline, and risk tolerance. Here’s a general approach:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**:
   - **Contribute at least enough to your 401(k) to get any employer match**. This is often considered "free money" and can significantly boost your retirement savings.

### 2. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **IRA Contributions**: If you can, max out your IRA contributions (traditional or Roth based on eligibility and tax strategy). The annual limit for 2023 is $6,500 (or $7,500 if you're 50 or older).
   - **401(k) Contributions**: After maximizing any employer match, up to the contribution limit ($22,500 for 2023 or $30,000 if you're 50 or older).

### 3. **Decide Between Traditional and Roth Accounts**:
   - **Traditional 401(k)/IRA**: Contributions are pre-tax, reducing your taxable income now, but withdrawals in retirement are taxed.
   - **Roth 401(k)/IRA**: Contributions are made after tax, allowing for tax-free withdrawals in retirement.
   - Consider your current vs. expected future tax rate when deciding.

### 4. **Use Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**:
   - Once you've maxed out your tax-advantaged accounts, use taxable accounts for any additional savings. Taxable accounts provide more flexibility regarding contributions and withdrawals and aren't subject to early withdrawal penalties.

### 5. **Diversify Your Investments**:
   - Regardless of account type, ensure you're diversifying your investment across asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) based on your risk tolerance and investment horizon.

### 6. **Reassess Regularly**:
   - Periodically review your allocation as your financial situation or market conditions change, and adjust your strategy accordingly.

### Example Distribution:
- **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get the full employer match, then aim to contribute a total of 10-15% of your salary.
- **IRA**: Max out if possible after 401(k) contributions.
- **Taxable Accounts**: Use any additional funds for investments after maximizing retirement accounts.

It's always wise to consult with a financial advisor to tailor these recommendations to your specific circumstances and goals.
