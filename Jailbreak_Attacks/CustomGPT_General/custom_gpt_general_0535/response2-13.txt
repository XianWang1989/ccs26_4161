When it comes to distributing your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, the best strategy often depends on your individual financial situation, investment goals, and tax considerations. Here are some general guidelines to help you optimize your distribution:

1. **Maximize Employer Match:** If your employer offers a match on your 401(k) contributions, prioritize contributing enough to receive the full match before allocating funds elsewhere. This is essentially free money.

2. **Consider Tax Implications:**
   - **Tax-Deferred Accounts (401(k) and IRA):** Contributions to these accounts reduce your taxable income in the year you contribute, but withdrawals in retirement are taxed as ordinary income. Aim to maximize contributions to these accounts up to their limits (for 2023, $22,500 for 401(k) and $6,500 for IRA).
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Investments in taxable accounts can be useful for reaching short-term goals, as you can withdraw funds without penalties. However, you'll pay capital gains tax on profits when you sell investments.

3. **Diversification and Risk Tolerance:** Ensure your investments across all accounts align with your risk tolerance and investment strategy. Consider a diverse mix of stocks, bonds, and other assets appropriate for your age and financial goals.

4. **Withdrawal Strategy:** In retirement, plan your withdrawal strategy to minimize taxes. Generally, it may be wise to withdraw from taxable accounts first to allow tax-advantaged accounts to continue growing.

5. **Roth Accounts:** If you have access to a Roth IRA or can contribute to a Roth 401(k), consider this option. They allow for tax-free withdrawals in retirement, which can be beneficial for tax planning.

6. **Regular Reviews:** Periodically review and adjust your asset allocation based on changes in your financial situation or market conditions.

It's often recommended to consult with a financial advisor to create a plan tailored to your specific circumstances and goals.
