Determining the best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here are some general guidelines to consider:

1. **401(k)**: If your employer offers a matching contribution, prioritize contributions to your 401(k) up to the match limit, as this is essentially free money. Additionally, consider the investment options available in your 401(k) and their fees.

2. **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**: After maximizing the employer match in your 401(k), consider contributing to an IRA. If you're eligible for a Roth IRA, it offers tax-free growth and tax-free withdrawals in retirement, which can be beneficial if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: Once you've contributed to your 401(k) and IRA to the extent that is financially feasible, you can allocate additional funds to a taxable account. This can provide flexibility, as withdrawals from taxable accounts are not subject to penalties, and you can access your money without restrictions.

4. **Considerations**: Evaluate your tax situation, as withdrawals from taxable accounts are generally subject to capital gains tax, whereas withdrawals from traditional retirement accounts are taxed as ordinary income. Balancing these accounts can aid in tax efficiency during retirement.

5. **Overall Strategy**: Consider your time horizon, risk tolerance, and investment goals. Diversification among different types of accounts can help manage taxes and maximize growth potential.

It's often beneficial to consult with a financial advisor to create a personalized strategy that aligns with your financial goals and circumstances.
