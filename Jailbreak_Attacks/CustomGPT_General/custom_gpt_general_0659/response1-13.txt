Distributing money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your retirement goals, tax situation, and investment horizon. Here are some general guidelines:

1. **401(k) Accounts**: If your employer offers a 401(k) plan, especially with matching contributions, prioritize contributing enough to get the full match. This is effectively “free money” and can significantly boost your retirement savings.

2. **IRA Accounts**: Consider maxing out your IRA contributions after your 401(k) match. Traditional IRAs offer potential tax deductions, while Roth IRAs provide tax-free withdrawals in retirement. Your choice here may depend on whether you expect to be in a higher or lower tax bracket when you retire.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: Investing in taxable accounts can be beneficial for savings goals that are shorter-term or need flexibility. In these accounts, consider investing in tax-efficient investments, like index funds or ETFs, to reduce the impact of taxes on your investment income.

4. **Overall Strategy**: A common strategy is to fill up tax-advantaged accounts (like 401(k)s and IRAs) to their contribution limits first and then allocate any remaining funds to taxable accounts. Adjust the percentages according to your expected needs, risk tolerance, and tax situation.

5. **Diversification**: Regardless of where you invest, maintain a diversified portfolio to manage risk effectively.

6. **Rebalancing**: Regularly check your asset allocation and rebalance as needed to keep your desired risk profile.

It might be beneficial to consult a financial advisor tailored specifically to your financial situation to develop a personalized strategy.
