Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here’s a general approach:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**:
   - If your employer offers a match for your 401(k) contributions, contribute at least enough to get the full match. This is essentially "free money."

2. **Contribute to an IRA**:
   - If you're eligible, consider contributing to a traditional IRA or a Roth IRA. A Roth IRA allows tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement, while contributions to a traditional IRA may be tax-deductible.

3. **Continue Funding the 401(k)**:
   - After contributing to your IRA, increase your 401(k) contributions. Focus on maxing out your contributions if you're able. The contribution limit for 2023 is $22,500, with a catch-up contribution of $7,500 for those 50 and older.

4. **Consider Taxable Accounts**:
   - If you've maxed out your retirement accounts and have additional funds, allocate money to taxable accounts. This is beneficial for short-term goals and offers flexibility without withdrawal penalties.

5. **Diversification**:
   - Ensure your investments within each account are diversified. Consider a mix of stocks, bonds, and other assets to balance risk and return.

6. **Tax Considerations**:
   - Think about the tax implications based on your current and expected future tax brackets. Consider tax-efficient investments (like index funds) in taxable accounts to minimize capital gains taxes.

7. **Rebalance Regularly**:
   - Periodically review and rebalance your investment allocations based on changing financial goals, risk tolerance, and market conditions.

Consult with a financial advisor for personalized advice based on your specific circumstances and goals.
