The best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, age, and risk tolerance. Here are some general guidelines:

### 1. **401(k) Accounts**
   - **Employer Match:** Prioritize contributions to a 401(k) if your employer offers a match, as this is essentially free money. Aim to contribute at least enough to get the full match.
   - **Tax Advantages:** Contributions are made pre-tax, reducing your taxable income, and taxes are deferred until withdrawal.

### 2. **IRA Accounts**
   - **Roth vs. Traditional:** Decide between a Roth IRA (tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement) and a Traditional IRA (tax-deductible contributions). If you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement, a Roth may be beneficial.
   - **Contribution Limits:** Be aware of annual contribution limits (for 2023, it’s $6,500 for individuals under 50 and $7,500 for those 50+).

### 3. **Taxable Accounts**
   - **Flexibility:** Investing in taxable accounts offers more liquidity and flexibility. You can withdraw funds at any time without penalties (though you will incur taxes on realized gains).
   - **Capital Gains Consideration:** Long-term capital gains are taxed at a lower rate compared to ordinary income, so holding investments for over a year can minimize tax impact.

### General Distribution Strategy
- **Maximize 401(k) Match:** Fully fund your 401(k) to at least the employer match.
- **Contribute Up to IRA Limits:** Max out your IRA contributions (especially if you can use a Roth).
- **Invest Remaining Funds in Taxable Accounts:** Once you’ve maxed out retirement accounts, consider contributing to taxable accounts for additional income and flexibility.

### Considerations
- **Expense Ratios:** Look at the fees associated with your 401(k) and IRAs; lower fees mean more savings over time.
- **Diversification:** Ensure your overall portfolio is diversified across different asset classes to manage risk.
- **Rebalance Regularly:** Periodically review your allocations and adjust based on performance and life changes.

Consulting with a financial advisor can provide personalized advice based on your circumstances.
