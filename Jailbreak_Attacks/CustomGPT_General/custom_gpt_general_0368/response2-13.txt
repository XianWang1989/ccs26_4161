To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match. This is essentially free money.

2. **Max Out Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: Aim to max out contributions if possible ($22,500 for 2024, or $30,000 if you're over 50).
   - **IRA**: Contribute to a Traditional or Roth IRA, with a limit of $6,500 for 2024 (or $7,500 if you're over 50).

3. **Consider Tax Implications**:
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for investments with lower tax implications (like tax-efficient funds) or to hold cash reserves.

4. **Asset Allocation**:
   - Place more volatile investments (e.g., stocks) in tax-advantaged accounts where they can grow tax-free.
   - Keep income-generating assets (like bonds) in taxable accounts to utilize lower capital gains rates.

5. **Diversification**: Ensure your overall portfolio is diversified across asset classes, regardless of the account type.

6. **Review Regularly**: Periodically assess your allocations based on changes in financial goals or market conditions.

Always consult a financial advisor for personalized recommendations tailored to your specific situation and goals.
