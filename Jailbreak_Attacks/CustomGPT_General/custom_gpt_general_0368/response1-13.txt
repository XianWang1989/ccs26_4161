Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts involves balancing tax efficiency, liquidity, and retirement goals. Here are some strategies:

### 1. **401(k)**
   - **Maximize Contributions:** Contribute enough to get any employer match first; it’s essentially free money.
   - **Invest for Growth:** If you’re in a higher income bracket, focus on growth-oriented investments to take advantage of tax-deferred growth.

### 2. **IRA (Traditional/Roth)**
   - **Consider a Roth IRA:** If you’re eligible, contribute to a Roth IRA, especially if you anticipate being in a higher tax bracket in retirement.
   - **Traditional IRA for Tax Deductions:** Use a traditional IRA if you need immediate tax deductions. This can lower your taxable income now while deferring taxes until withdrawal.

### 3. **Taxable Accounts**
   - **Tax-Efficient Investments:** Invest in tax-efficient funds (like ETFs) or tax-free bonds, as they will be taxed at favorable rates.
   - **Emergency Fund and Short-Term Goals:** Use taxable accounts for money needed within the next few years, as they offer greater liquidity.

### Allocation Tips:
- **Emergency Fund First:** Ensure you have an emergency fund in a savings account before heavily investing.
- **Diversification:** Diversify investments within each account type based on your risk tolerance and time horizon.
- **Rebalance Regularly:** Periodically review your allocations and rebalance as necessary according to your changes in risk tolerance or financial goals.

### General Advice:
Consult a financial advisor to tailor a strategy that suits your personal situation, including factors like income level, current expenses, and retirement timeline.
