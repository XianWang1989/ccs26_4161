Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts can impact your long-term retirement strategy and tax efficiency. Here are some general guidelines to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match, if available. This is essentially free money.

2. **Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**:
   - **401(k)**: If you have a plan with low fees and a good selection of investments, maxing out your contributions can be beneficial. Consider both traditional (pre-tax) and Roth (after-tax) options, depending on your current and expected future tax brackets.
   - **IRA**: If eligible, contribute to an IRA next. A Roth IRA allows for tax-free withdrawals in retirement, while traditional IRAs provide upfront tax deductions.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: After maximizing contributions to retirement accounts, direct funds to a taxable account. This provides flexibility and access to funds without penalties, though you’ll need to consider tax implications on earnings and capital gains.

4. **Diversification**: Ensure your investments are diversified across asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) to manage risk. 

5. **Consider Your Time Horizon**: If you're younger, you might favor more aggressive investments for long-term growth. As you approach retirement, gradually shift toward preservation of capital.

6. **Regular Rebalancing**: Periodically review and adjust your allocations based on market performance and changes in your financial goals.

7. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: Their expertise can provide personalized advice based on your financial situation.

By following these steps, you can create a balanced approach that aims for growth while managing tax implications effectively.
