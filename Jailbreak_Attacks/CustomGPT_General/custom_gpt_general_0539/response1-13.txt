Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here are some general guidelines:

### 1. **401(k) Accounts**
   - **Employer Match:** Contribute enough to get any employer match; it’s essentially free money.
   - **Contribution Limits:** Max out contributions if possible, considering the annual limit ($22,500 for 2023, or $30,000 if you’re over 50).
   - **Tax Advantages:** Funds grow tax-deferred until you withdraw in retirement.

### 2. **IRA Accounts**
   - **Traditional vs. Roth:** Decide whether to contribute to a Traditional IRA (tax-deductible contributions) or a Roth IRA (tax-free withdrawals in retirement).
   - **Contribution Limits:** Similar to 401(k) limits ($6,500 for 2023, or $7,500 if over 50).
   - **Tax Planning:** Consider future tax implications; a Roth can be beneficial if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement.

### 3. **Taxable Accounts**
   - **Flexibility:** Funds can be accessed anytime without penalties, making taxable accounts great for short- to mid-term goals.
   - **Tax Efficiency:** Focus on long-term investments to minimize capital gains taxes and consider tax-efficient funds.
   - **Rebalancing:** Regularly review and rebalance your portfolio to align with your investment goals.

### 4. **Considerations for Distribution**
   - **Emergency Fund:** Ensure you have an emergency fund before allocating significant funds to these accounts.
   - **Diversification:** Maintain a diversified portfolio across all accounts.
   - **Investment Horizon:** Align your investments with your risk tolerance and time until retirement.

### Conclusion
Start by maximizing any employer match in your 401(k), then consider fully funding an IRA, especially if you can take advantage of a Roth. Finally, use taxable accounts for additional savings, maintaining flexibility for short-term needs while being mindful of tax implications. Consulting a financial advisor would provide tailored advice based on your specific situation.
