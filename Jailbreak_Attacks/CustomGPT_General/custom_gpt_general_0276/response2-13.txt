Distributing money efficiently among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment preferences. Here’s a general strategy:

1. **Maximize Contributions**:
   - **401(k)**: Start by contributing enough to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money.
   - **IRA**: If you're eligible, contribute to a Roth or Traditional IRA, depending on your tax situation. Roth IRAs grow tax-free, while Traditional IRAs may provide current tax deductions.

2. **Consider Tax Implications**:
   - **Tax-Advantaged Accounts (401(k) and IRA)**: Prioritize investments that generate ordinary income (like bonds) here to minimize taxes.
   - **Taxable Account**: Focus on long-term investments, like stocks or ETFs, that qualify for lower capital gains tax rates. Use tax-loss harvesting strategies as needed.

3. **Diversification**:
   - Ensure your overall asset allocation aligns with your risk tolerance and investment goals, regardless of account type. This may require adjusting the investment mix across accounts.

4. **Rebalance Regularly**:
   - Monitor your investments and rebalance your portfolio as necessary to maintain your desired asset allocation.

5. **Withdrawal Strategy**:
   - Plan how you'll withdraw funds in retirement. Typically, it's advisable to withdraw from taxable accounts first, followed by tax-deferred accounts, and lastly tax-free accounts.

6. **Consult a Professional**:
   - Consider working with a financial advisor for personalized strategies based on your specific situation.

By following these guidelines, you can create a balanced and tax-efficient investment strategy that aligns with your long-term financial goals.
