Distributing your money amongst your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here’s a general approach:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**: Contribute enough to take full advantage of any employer match, as this is essentially free money.

2. **Contribute to IRA**: After maximizing the match, consider contributing to a traditional or Roth IRA. A Roth IRA is beneficial if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement.

3. **401(k) Contributions**: Continue contributing to your 401(k) up to the annual limit. Contributions are tax-deferred, reducing your current taxable income.

4. **Taxable Accounts**: Use taxable accounts for funds that you might need in the short term or for investments that generate tax-advantaged income (like qualified dividends or long-term capital gains).

5. **Asset Location Strategy**: Place less tax-efficient investments (like bonds or high-yield stocks) in tax-deferred accounts and more tax-efficient investments (like index funds) in taxable accounts.

6. **Rebalance Regularly**: Periodically reassess your asset allocation to ensure it aligns with your risk tolerance and financial goals.

7. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: As always, consider consulting with a financial advisor to tailor your strategy to your specific situation.

This approach can help optimize your tax situation while aligning with your financial objectives.
