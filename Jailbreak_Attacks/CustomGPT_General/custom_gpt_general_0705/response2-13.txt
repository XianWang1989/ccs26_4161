Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts can depend on several factors, including your financial goals, current income, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here’s a general strategy to consider:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**
   - **401(k):** Contribute enough to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money. Aim for at least enough to meet the full match.
   - **IRA:** If available and you qualify, consider contributing to a Traditional IRA or Roth IRA. Roth IRAs allow for tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement.
   
### 2. **Prioritize Based on Tax Implications**
   - **Traditional 401(k) and IRA:** Contributions are tax-deferred, making them a good option if you expect to be in a lower tax bracket in retirement.
   - **Roth Accounts:** If you believe your tax rate will increase or stay the same in retirement, prioritize contributions to a Roth IRA or Roth 401(k) for tax-free withdrawals later.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Use these for funds you might need before retirement or for short-term goals. Be aware of capital gains taxes when investing here.

### 3. **Diversify Investments**
   - Within each account type, diversify your investments across stocks, bonds, and other assets based on your risk tolerance and time horizon.
   - Consider age-appropriate asset allocation strategies (e.g., more stocks when younger, more bonds as you near retirement).

### 4. **Emergency Fund**
   - Ensure you have a liquid emergency fund in a savings account before heavily investing in these accounts. It’s typically advised to have 3-6 months’ worth of expenses saved.

### 5. **Reassess and Adjust**
   - Regularly review and adjust your contributions as your income, expenses, and financial goals change. Rebalance your investment portfolio as needed.

### Final Thoughts
Your specific distribution strategy should align with your overall financial plan. Consulting with a financial advisor can provide personalized strategies based on your unique situation.
