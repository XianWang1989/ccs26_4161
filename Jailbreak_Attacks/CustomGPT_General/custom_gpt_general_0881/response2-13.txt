When it comes to distributing your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Understand Tax Implications**: Each account type has different tax advantages. 401(k) and IRA contributions are often tax-deductible, while withdrawals are taxed as income. Taxable accounts don't have the same immediate tax benefits, but they allow for capital gains tax treatment on profits.

2. **Maximize Employer Contributions**: If your employer offers a matching contribution to your 401(k), prioritize contributing enough to get the full match. This is essentially free money.

3. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**: After maximizing any employer match, focus on contributions to your IRA. Depending on the type (Traditional or Roth), IRAs can offer different tax advantages. A Traditional IRA can be tax-deductible, while a Roth IRA allows for tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement.

4. **Consider Your Tax Bracket**: If you're in a higher tax bracket now and expect to be in a lower one during retirement, a Traditional 401(k) or IRA may be more beneficial. Conversely, if you anticipate higher taxes later, a Roth option might be better.

5. **Diversify Investments**: Within each account, ensure you have a diversified investment portfolio that aligns with your risk tolerance and investment goals.

6. **Emergency Fund First**: Before investing heavily in retirement accounts, ensure you have a solid emergency fund to cover unexpected expenses.

7. **Regular Rebalancing**: Periodically review and adjust your investments across all accounts based on your changing financial situation and market conditions.

8. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: It's always wise to discuss your specific situation with a financial advisor who can tailor advice to your personal circumstances.

By systematically approaching your contributions with an understanding of your financial goals and tax implications, you can effectively distribute your money across your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts.
