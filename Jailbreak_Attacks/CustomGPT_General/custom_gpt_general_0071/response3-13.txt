Balancing your investments across a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts can depend on various factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here’s a general approach:

### 1. **401(k) Contributions**
   - **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to get the full match—it's essentially free money.
   - **Consider Contribution Limits**: Aim to maximize your contributions to take advantage of tax-deferred growth. For 2023, the limit is $22,500 (or $30,000 if you’re over 50).

### 2. **IRA Contributions**
   - **Choose Between Traditional and Roth**: 
     - **Traditional IRA**: Contributions may be tax-deductible, reducing your taxable income now. Taxes are paid upon withdrawal.
     - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, but withdrawals in retirement are tax-free.
   - **Annual Contribution Limit**: For 2023, you can contribute up to $6,500 (or $7,500 if you're over 50).

### 3. **Taxable Accounts**
   - **Invest for Flexibility**: These accounts offer the most liquidity since there are no restrictions on withdrawals. 
   - **Consider Tax Efficiency**: Focus on tax-efficient investments, such as index funds or tax-managed funds to minimize capital gains taxes.

### 4. **Distribution Strategy**
   - **Asset Allocation**: Diversify your investments based on your risk tolerance across all accounts (stocks, bonds, etc.)
   - **Tax Considerations**: Generally, keep more tax-inefficient investments (like bonds) in tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) and IRAs) and tax-efficient investments (like stocks) in taxable accounts.

### Suggested Order of Contributions
1. **401(k)**: At least enough to get the full employer match.
2. **IRA**: Maximize contributions to either a Traditional or Roth IRA based on your tax situation.
3. **401(k)**: Continue to invest up to the annual limit.
4. **Taxable Account**: Invest any additional savings after maximizing the above options.

### Regular Review
- **Rebalance**: Review your allocations regularly to ensure they align with your goals and risk tolerance.
- **Adjust as Needed**: Major life changes may necessitate a reevaluation of your investment strategy.

This strategic distribution can help optimize your overall return and manage tax liabilities effectively.
