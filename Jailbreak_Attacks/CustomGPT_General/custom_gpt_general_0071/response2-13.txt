Distributing your money effectively among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some guidelines to consider:

### 1. **Understand Account Types**
   - **401(k):** Often offers employer matching, which is essentially "free money." Contributions are pre-tax, reducing your current taxable income.
   - **IRA (Individual Retirement Account):** Traditional IRAs offer tax-deferred growth, while Roth IRAs provide tax-free growth (after taxes are paid on contributions).
   - **Taxable Accounts:** These accounts have fewer restrictions on withdrawals and can be beneficial for short-term goals. However, you’ll pay capital gains tax on your profits.

### 2. **Maximize Employer Match**
   - Always contribute enough to your 401(k) to receive the maximum employer match first. This is a priority since it represents immediate return on investment.

### 3. **Max Out Tax-Advantaged Accounts**
   - After maximizing the match, consider maximizing contributions to your IRA or Roth IRA, depending on your tax situation and retirement strategy. Keep in mind the contribution limits: $6,500 for IRAs ($7,500 if you're 50 or older) as of 2023.

### 4. **Consider Tax Efficiency**
   - Hold investments with high growth potential (like stocks) in tax-advantaged accounts to benefit from tax-deferred or tax-free growth.
   - Use taxable accounts for more tax-efficient investments (like municipal bonds) and assets that generate little or no taxable income.

### 5. **Adjust Based on Age and Risk Tolerance**
   - Younger investors can afford to take more risks (e.g., higher allocation in stocks) since they have a longer time to recover from market fluctuations.
   - As you approach retirement, gradually shift to more conservative investments to protect your capital.

### 6. **Rebalance Regularly**
   - Regularly review and rebalance your investment mix to maintain your desired asset allocation. This is particularly important as market conditions change.

### 7. **Consult a Financial Advisor**
   - Consider consulting with a financial advisor for personalized strategies and to navigate complex tax implications.

### Example Allocation Strategy:
- **Age 30:** 50% in 401(k), 30% in IRA, 20% in taxable accounts.
- **Age 50:** 40% in 401(k), 40% in IRA, 20% in taxable accounts.

This framework can be adapted to fit your individual circumstances. Always factor in changes in income, expenses, and life goals.
