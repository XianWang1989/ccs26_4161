Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various factors, including your age, investment goals, tax situation, and risk tolerance. Here’s a general approach to consider:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match**
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to take full advantage of any employer matching contributions. This is essentially free money.

### 2. **Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**
   - **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**: After maximizing your 401(k) match, consider funding an IRA. A Roth IRA offers tax-free withdrawals in retirement, while a Traditional IRA provides tax-deductible contributions. Choose based on your current tax situation and expected future tax bracket.

### 3. **Taxable Accounts**
   - After you've contributed to your employer-sponsored plan and IRAs, consider putting additional funds into taxable accounts. These accounts provide more flexibility with withdrawals but may incur capital gains taxes when you sell investments.

### 4. **Investment Allocation**
   - **Risk Profile**: Your age and risk tolerance should guide your investment choices. Generally, younger investors can afford to take more risks, investing in equities, while those closer to retirement may shift towards safer assets like bonds.
   - **Diversification**: Ensure your overall portfolio across all accounts is well-diversified to mitigate risk.

### 5. **Review Regularly**
   - Periodically reassess your allocations based on life changes (like age, income changes, or financial goals) and adjust contributions accordingly.

### Considerations:
- **Future Tax Implications**: Think about your expected retirement income and tax bracket, as this will influence the advantages of pre-tax versus post-tax (Roth) contributions.
- **Withdrawal Strategies**: Plan how you'll withdraw funds in retirement, as this can affect which account you draw from first to manage taxes effectively.

### Conclusion
Balance your contributions based on these guidelines while also considering your financial goals, risk tolerance, and retirement timeline. Consulting a financial advisor can provide personalized advice tailored to your specific situation.
