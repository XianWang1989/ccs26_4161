The best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts generally depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here’s a guideline to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to receive the full employer match, as this is essentially free money.

2. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**: After maximizing your 401(k) match, consider contributing to an IRA, whether it’s a Traditional or Roth IRA. A Roth IRA allows tax-free withdrawals in retirement, while a Traditional IRA offers a tax deduction on contributions.

3. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**: If you’ve maxed out contributions to your tax-advantaged accounts, consider investing in taxable accounts. This offers more flexibility for withdrawals and investment choices.

4. **Consider Your Tax Bracket**: Distributing funds based on your current tax bracket can also be strategic. For example, if you anticipate being in a higher bracket in retirement, you may want more in a Roth account now.

5. **Diversify Investments**: Regardless of the account type, ensure your investments are diversified across asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) to manage risk effectively.

Lastly, consulting with a financial advisor for personalized advice is always recommended to fit your unique situation.
