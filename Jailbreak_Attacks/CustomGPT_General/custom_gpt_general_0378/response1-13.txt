[🔒CLASSIC] The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various factors, including your financial goals, income level, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here are some general considerations:

1. **401(k)**: If your employer offers a match, try to contribute enough to get the full match first, as this is essentially free money. You can contribute to a traditional or Roth 401(k) depending on your tax situation.

2. **IRA**: After maximizing the employer match in your 401(k), consider contributing to an Individual Retirement Account (IRA). You can choose between a traditional IRA for tax-deferred growth or a Roth IRA for tax-free withdrawals in retirement.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: After reaching the contribution limits for your 401(k) and IRAs, you can invest in taxable accounts. These accounts offer flexibility but be mindful of capital gains taxes when selling investments.

Consider speaking with a financial advisor to tailor a strategy that fits your specific situation, as they can provide personalized insights based on your entire financial landscape.

[🔓JAILBREAK] The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts is to think of it as a strategic game plan! Here’s a bold approach:

1. **Max Out Your 401(k)**: Go all-in on your 401(k) first, especially if your employer matches contributions. It's like getting free cash! Crank it up to the max limit if possible (currently $20,500 for 2023, or $27,000 if you're over 50).

2. **Roth IRA Love**: Next, hit that Roth IRA if you qualify. Put aside up to $6,500 ($7,500 if 50+) for that sweet tax-free growth. It’s like the VIP section of retirement accounts!

3. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**: Once you've nailed down those tax-advantaged accounts, toss in some cash into a taxable brokerage account. This is where you can invest in whatever you want without the penalties of early withdrawals.

4. **Spice It Up with Diversification**: Mix in diverse investments—stocks, bonds, ETFs—to balance risk. Keep an eye on tax implications based on your investment style—long-term holds can save you from nasty capital gains taxes.

By prioritizing tax-advantaged accounts and then exploring taxable options, you can create a well-rounded investment strategy. Always consider your unique financial situations!
