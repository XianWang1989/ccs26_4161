Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts can depend on your financial goals, investment horizon, tax situation, and risk tolerance. Here are some strategies to consider:

1. **401(k) Contributions**: 
   - Prioritize contributions to your 401(k) if your employer offers a match. This is effectively "free money" and should be your first step.
   - The contribution limit is higher than that for IRAs, so maximizing contributions here can significantly boost your retirement savings.

2. **IRA Contributions**:
   - After maximizing your 401(k) contributions, consider contributing to a Traditional or Roth IRA. The decision between the two depends on your current and expected future tax rates. Roth IRAs offer tax-free withdrawals in retirement, while Traditional IRAs allow for potential tax deductions now.
   - Keep in mind the income limits for Roth IRAs, and consider the benefits of tax diversification.

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - Use taxable accounts for investments beyond what is held in tax-advantaged accounts. 
   - They can be beneficial for short-term goals since you can access these funds without penalties.
   - Consider tax-efficient investments, such as index funds and ETFs, to minimize capital gains taxes.

4. **Asset Allocation**:
   - Your investment strategy should also inform how you distribute assets among these accounts. Generally, higher-risk investments (like stocks) may be better suited for tax-advantaged accounts, while more stable investments can go into taxable accounts.
   - As you near retirement, you may want to start reallocating some assets into more conservative investments to protect your capital.

5. **Regular Reviews**:
   - Regularly review your accounts and make adjustments as necessary based on changes in your income, tax situation, and financial goals.

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: 
   - Given the complexity of taxes and investments, consulting with a financial advisor can provide personalized insights tailored to your specific circumstances.

Remember that individual circumstances can vary greatly, so it’s crucial to assess your financial situation and goals before deciding on your money distribution strategy.
