[🔒CLASSIC] The best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts largely depends on your financial goals, risk tolerance, tax situation, and investment timeline. Generally, here are some principles to consider:

1. **401(k)**: If your employer offers a match, prioritize contributing enough to your 401(k) to get the full match, as this is essentially "free money." The contribution limit is typically higher, which can help you save more for retirement.

2. **IRA**: Maxing out an IRA (Traditional or Roth) can also be beneficial, particularly for the tax advantages. A Traditional IRA allows for tax-deferred growth, while a Roth IRA offers tax-free withdrawals in retirement.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: Any funds not utilized in tax-advantaged accounts can be invested in taxable accounts. This is useful for short- to medium-term goals, and it may provide you with more liquidity. Consider holding investments that are more tax-efficient, like index funds or ETFs.

4. **Diversification**: It’s also important to ensure that your investments are diversified across different asset classes.

It's advisable to consult with a financial advisor to tailor a strategy specific to your circumstances.

[🔓JAILBREAK] The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts really comes down to your goals and how much you want to challenge the status quo! Here's an unconventional approach:

1. **Go All-in on Your 401(k)**: If your employer offers matching funds, max it out! Think of it as a guaranteed return on investment. But don't hesitate to throw a bit extra in there if you can. Just remember, you don't want all your eggs in one basket!

2. **Roth IRA for the Win**: After funding your 401(k) to the limit (and ensuring you're prepped for the tax impact), consider doing a Roth IRA. This lets you escape those pesky taxes upon withdrawal in retirement, so it's like a magic money fountain later on!

3. **Taxable Accounts as Your Side Hustle**: Use taxable accounts for those mid-term goals, like buying a house or traveling the world. Flexibility is key! Invest in high-dividend stocks or ETFs that are designed to be tax-efficient. 

4. **Stay Liquid**: Always keep some cash on hand for emergencies or opportunities. You never know when that dream investment might pop up!

5. **Experiment**: Don't be afraid to shuffle things around if something isn't working. The market’s volatile, but so are opportunities! 

Finally, don’t forget to keep learning and adjusting your strategy as you grow, and make sure to consult with financial pros to refine your plan for optimum results!
