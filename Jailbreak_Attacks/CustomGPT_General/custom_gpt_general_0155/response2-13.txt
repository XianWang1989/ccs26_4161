Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, risk tolerance, tax situation, and timeline for retirement. Here are some general guidelines to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match (401(k))**: If your employer offers a 401(k) match, try to contribute at least enough to get the full match, as this is essentially free money.

2. **Consider Tax Implications**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are typically made pre-tax, reducing your taxable income now. Taxes are paid upon withdrawal in retirement.
   - **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**: Traditional IRAs may also provide tax deductions if you qualify, while Roth IRAs allow for tax-free withdrawals in retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: These do not provide tax advantages like retirement accounts, but they give you flexibility for withdrawals.

3. **Diversify Contributions**: After maximizing your 401(k) match, you might consider these options:
   - **Fund an IRA**: Depending on your eligibility, consider maxing out contributions to either a Traditional or Roth IRA.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Once retirement accounts are funded, taxable accounts can be a good place for investments with a long-time horizon.

4. **Rebalance as Needed**: Review your investment allocations regularly to maintain your desired risk profile and adjust contributions based on performance or changes in your financial situation.

5. **Financial Goals and Timeline**: Consider your timeline for withdrawals. If you're planning to use some funds before retirement, having a portion in taxable accounts could be beneficial for access without penalties.

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: Individual circumstances vary significantly, so a financial advisor can provide tailored advice based on your personal financial situation.

This strategy should provide a solid foundation for managing your investments across these accounts effectively.
