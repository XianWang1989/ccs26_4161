Distributing your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts should be tailored to your financial goals, risk tolerance, and tax situation. Here are some guidelines to consider:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**
   - Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match, as this is essentially “free money.” 

### 2. **Focus on Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**
   - If you can afford to save more after getting the match, consider maxing out your IRA contributions (traditional or Roth) next. These accounts often provide tax benefits that can enhance growth.

### 3. **Assess Your Tax Bracket**
   - Use a traditional IRA if you’re in a higher tax bracket now and expect to be in a lower one during retirement. A Roth IRA is beneficial if you expect your tax bracket to rise, as withdrawals in retirement are tax-free.

### 4. **Diversify Your Investments**
   - Spread your investments across various asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) within all accounts. Generally, place higher-risk assets (like stocks) in tax-advantaged accounts to benefit from tax-free growth.

### 5. **Allocate Funds Based on Goals**
   - Use taxable accounts for short-term savings or investments you'll need soon, as they provide more flexibility. For long-term goals, prioritize tax-advantaged accounts.

### 6. **Consider Withdrawal Strategies**
   - Plan your withdrawal strategy ahead of retirement. Typically, you might withdraw from taxable accounts first to allow tax-advantaged accounts to grow longer, potentially reducing your tax burden in retirement.

### 7. **Regular Review and Adjustments**
   - Monitor your allocations annually and adjust based on performance, changes in income, or shifts in your financial goals.

### Example Distribution Strategy:
- **401(k):** Contribute to the match, then up to the limit if possible.
- **IRA:** Max out contributions next.
- **Taxable Accounts:** Use any remaining funds for investments after maxing out tax-advantaged accounts.

By following these steps, you can create a balanced investment strategy that supports your long-term financial health while minimizing tax impact. Consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized advice based on your specific situation.
