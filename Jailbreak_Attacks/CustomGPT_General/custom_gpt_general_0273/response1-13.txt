To effectively distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following steps:

### 1. **Understand the Account Types**
   - **401(k):** Employer-sponsored retirement plan with potential employer matching. Contributions are typically pre-tax, reducing your taxable income.
   - **IRA (Individual Retirement Account):** Allows for tax-deferred growth; can be either Traditional (pre-tax contributions) or Roth (after-tax contributions).
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Investments that are taxed on capital gains and dividends; offers more flexibility for withdrawals.

### 2. **Maximize Contributions to Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**
   - **401(k):** Aim to contribute enough to maximize any employer match.
   - **IRA:** If eligible, contribute to an IRA, especially if you're considering a Roth account for tax-free growth in retirement.
  
### 3. **Consider Tax Implications**
   - **Place higher-growth investments** (like stocks) in tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) and IRA) to defer taxes on capital gains.
   - **Allocate less tax-efficient assets** (like bonds or income-generating assets) to taxable accounts where possible to minimize tax impact.

### 4. **Assess Your Retirement Timeline**
   - If retirement is far away, prioritize long-term growth in tax-advantaged accounts.
   - If you're closer to retirement, start shifting allocations towards safer investments (like bonds) to preserve capital.

### 5. **Maintain an Appropriate Asset Allocation**
   - Tailor your overall portfolio to your risk tolerance and investment goals. Diversify across asset classes to manage volatility.

### 6. **Regularly Review and Rebalance**
   - Reassess your allocations periodically (annually or semi-annually) and adjust as necessary to stay aligned with your goals and market conditions.

### 7. **Plan for Withdrawals**
   - Understand the rules for withdrawals from each account type to maximize tax efficiency in retirement.

### Example Allocation Strategy
- **401(k):** Max out contributions to get full employer match.
- **IRA:** Contribute maximum amount if under income limits.
- **Taxable Accounts:** Invest any remaining funds after maxing out tax-advantaged accounts, focusing on tax-efficient investments.

This strategy should be tailored to your individual circumstances, financial goals, risk tolerance, and tax situation, potentially with input from a financial advisor.
