Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on a few key factors including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some general guidelines to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a 401(k) match, contribute at least enough to get the full match. This is essentially free money.

2. **Prioritize Tax Advantages**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are typically pre-tax, reducing your taxable income for the year. Focus on maxing out contributions here if you're in a higher tax bracket.
   - **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**: Consider contributing to an IRA after you’ve maximized your 401(k) contributions. A Traditional IRA provides tax deductions, while a Roth IRA allows for tax-free growth after retirement.

3. **Consider Taxable Accounts**: After utilizing tax-advantaged accounts, allocate extra funds to a taxable investment account. This gives you flexibility for withdrawals and investment choices, but be mindful of capital gains taxes.

4. **Diversification**: Ensure your investments in these accounts are diverse, balancing risk and growth potential. Consider factors such as age, risk tolerance, and investment goals.

5. **Review and Adjust Regularly**: Your financial situation and market conditions can change. Regularly review your allocations to ensure they align with your current needs and long-term objectives.

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: If you're unsure, speaking with a financial planner can provide personalized guidance based on your unique situation.

Remember, the ideal balance varies for each individual, so take these points and tailor them to fit your financial landscape!
