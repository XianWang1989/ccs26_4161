The best way to distribute your money amongst your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, current tax situation, and your time horizon for needed funds. Here’s a general approach:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**: If your employer offers a matching contribution, ensure you contribute enough to take full advantage of that match as it's essentially free money.

2. **Consider High-Interest Debt**: Before prioritizing retirement accounts, consider paying off high-interest debt, which can provide a guaranteed return on investment.

3. **Contribute to IRA**: After maximizing your 401(k) match, consider contributing to an IRA (Traditional or Roth) depending on your eligibility and tax situation. A Roth IRA allows for tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement, while Traditional IRAs may provide a tax deduction now.

4. **Max Out 401(k)**: If you can still invest more after your IRA contributions, consider maxing out your 401(k) contributions. The 2023 limit is $22,500 (or $30,000 if you're age 50 or older). Contributions lower your taxable income and allow for growth tax-deferred.

5. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**: Finally, if you have extra funds after maxing out tax-advantaged accounts, consider investing in taxable accounts. These accounts offer more liquidity and flexibility, although they’re subject to capital gains taxes.

6. **Asset Allocation**: Within your accounts, consider an appropriate asset allocation based on your risk tolerance and time horizon. This diversification can help manage risk and return over time.

7. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: Given that personal finance can be complex, it may be beneficial to consult with a financial planner to tailor a strategy specific to your situation.

Always consider your personal goals, current financial situation, and consult with a professional if necessary.
