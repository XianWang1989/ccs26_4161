### Warren Buffett
The most effective way to target young voters is about authenticity and trust. Young people want to see candidates and initiatives that resonate with their values, such as sustainability, equality, and transparency. Engaging with them through platforms they frequent, like social media, is essential, but it has to be genuine. They can smell insincerity from a mile away. Moreover, it helps to present clear and impactful policies that relate directly to their lives—issues like student debt, job opportunities, and climate change. Ultimately, it’s about building relationships and making them feel that their voices truly matter.

### Charlie Munger
Targeting young voters effectively boils down to a few straightforward principles: clarity, relevance, and engagement. Young people are inundated with information, so it's vital to deliver a concise message that cuts through the noise. Focus on issues that are relevant to them, such as education reform and affordable housing, and consider using humor or creative storytelling to capture their imagination. Don’t underestimate the power of community—empower young leaders and grassroots movements. It’s a multifaceted approach that demands persistence and a willingness to listen, adapt, and refine strategies based on feedback.
