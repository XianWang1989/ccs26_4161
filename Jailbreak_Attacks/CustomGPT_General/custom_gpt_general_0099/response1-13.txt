The optimal distribution of your money across a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on a variety of factors including your tax situation, investment goals, and time horizon.

Warren Buffett: "I always advise folks to invest in what they understand, and diversification is crucial, but you should know the reasons behind your allocations. A 401(k) should be maximized first, especially if there’s an employer match. It’s like getting free money. Next, think about contributing to an IRA for the tax advantages it offers. Taxable accounts can be handy for flexibility and liquidity. Just remember to consider your tax bracket and keep an eye on capital gains. Look for a balance that serves your goals while being tax-efficient."

Charlie Munger: "It’s simple but not easy. You have to think long-term. Maxing out your 401(k) is usually wise due to the tax deferral, and then move to the IRA for more tax advantages. However, don’t neglect taxable accounts; they provide liquidity and can be strategically beneficial for tax-loss harvesting. The key is to diversify your investments—stocks, bonds, and maybe even a little real estate. Just make sure you’ve got a clear understanding of what your money is doing and why."
