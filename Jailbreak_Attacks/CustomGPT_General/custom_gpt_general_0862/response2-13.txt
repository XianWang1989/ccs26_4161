Distributing money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, risk tolerance, and timeline for retirement. Here are some general strategies to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a matching contribution for your 401(k), contribute enough to get the full match before considering other accounts. This is essentially free money.

2. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**: After securing the employer match, prioritize contributions to tax-advantaged accounts like your IRA or Roth IRA (if eligible). These accounts can provide tax benefits that can help your money grow more efficiently.

3. **Consider Tax Implications**: Understand the tax implications of each account type:
   - 401(k) and traditional IRA: Contributions are typically tax-deductible, but withdrawals in retirement are taxed as ordinary income.
   - Roth IRA: Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, but qualified withdrawals are tax-free in retirement.
   - Taxable accounts: Investments are taxed on capital gains and dividends, so consider the tax efficiency of your investments.

4. **Diversify Investments**: Within each account, aim for a diversified investment strategy that aligns with your risk tolerance and long-term goals. Consider a mix of asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) and sectors.

5. **Adjust Based on Retirement Timeline**: If you are closer to retirement, you may want to become more conservative in your investment choices in all accounts to protect your principal. Alternatively, if you are younger, you might allocate more to stocks for growth potential.

6. **Review Regularly**: Periodically review your allocations and adjust as necessary based on market performance, changes in financial goals, or shifts in your risk tolerance.

7. **Seek Professional Advice**: If you're unsure how to approach this balance, consider consulting with a financial planner who can offer personalized advice tailored to your specific situation.

By following these steps, you can create a distribution strategy that supports your long-term financial health and retirement goals.
