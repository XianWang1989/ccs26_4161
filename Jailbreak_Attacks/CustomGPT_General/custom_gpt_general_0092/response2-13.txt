The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts largely depends on your individual financial goals, time horizon, risk tolerance, and tax situation. Here are some general strategies to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k):** If your employer offers a match on your 401(k) contributions, start by contributing enough to take full advantage of that match, as it’s essentially free money.

2. **Contribute to IRAs:** Consider contributing to an Individual Retirement Account (IRA) next, especially if you qualify for a tax-deductible traditional IRA or a Roth IRA. These accounts offer tax advantages that can enhance your retirement savings.

3. **Consider Tax Efficiency:**
   - **Tax-Advantaged Accounts (401(k) and IRA):** Focus on placing investments that generate higher taxable income (like bonds or dividend-paying stocks) in these accounts to shield them from taxes until withdrawal.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Use these for investments that are more tax-efficient, like long-term stock holdings or index funds, since you can benefit from lower capital gains tax rates when sold after a year.

4. **Diversification Across Accounts:** Ensure that your asset allocation (stocks, bonds, cash) is balanced across all accounts. This approach can help manage risk while optimizing growth potential.

5. **Assess Your Tax Situation:** Depending on your income level, it might make sense to prioritize traditional accounts (401(k) and traditional IRA) for upfront tax deductions, or Roth accounts for tax-free withdrawals in retirement.

6. **Withdrawals Strategy:** Plan for how withdrawals will work in retirement. Ideally, you want to minimize tax liabilities by considering which account to draw from when.

7. **Review and Adjust:** Regularly review your asset allocation and contribution levels based on life changes (job changes, marriage, etc.) or market conditions.

Implementing a strategy that includes these considerations can help you make the most of your retirement savings across accounts. It may also be beneficial to consult with a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your circumstances.
