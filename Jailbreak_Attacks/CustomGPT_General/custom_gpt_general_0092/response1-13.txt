Deciding how to distribute money among your retirement accounts—401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts—depends on various factors, including your individual financial goals, risk tolerance, time horizon, and current tax situation. Here are some general strategies to consider:

1. **401(k) Contributions:**
   - **Maximize Employer Match:** Always contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match if available. This is essentially "free money."
   - **Tax Benefits:** Contributions are often made pre-tax, reducing your taxable income for the year.

2. **IRA Contributions:**
   - **Roth vs. Traditional:** Decide between a Roth IRA (tax-free growth and withdrawals) and a Traditional IRA (tax-deferred growth). If you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement, a Roth IRA may be more beneficial.
   - **Annual Contribution Limits:** Keep track of the annual contribution limits and prioritize maxing out your IRA if you have extra funds.

3. **Taxable Accounts:**
   - **Liquidity:** Use taxable accounts for savings goals that are shorter-term. These accounts also allow for more flexible withdrawals.
   - **Investment Options:** You can invest in a wider range of assets, including stocks, bonds, and mutual funds, which can favor your investment strategy depending on your tax situation.

4. **General Principles:**
   - **Asset Allocation:** Diversify your investments across all accounts based on your risk tolerance and investment timeline.
   - **Tax Efficiency:** Consider placing more tax-inefficient investments (like bond funds) in tax-deferred accounts (401(k) or IRA) and leaving tax-efficient investments (like stocks) in taxable accounts.
   - **Rebalancing:** Periodically review and rebalance your accounts to ensure you maintain your desired asset allocation.

5. **Financial Goals:** Align your contributions with your financial goals, like retirement, buying a house, or funding education, taking into account the time horizon and risk associated with each goal.

It may be wise to consult with a financial advisor who can help you tailor your strategy to your specific situation, ensuring you make the most of your investments and tax advantages.
