To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money. If possible, aim for the annual contribution limit.
   - **IRA**: If you qualify, contribute to a Traditional IRA or Roth IRA. Prioritize the Roth IRA for tax-free growth if you expect your income to rise.

2. **Withdrawal Strategies**:
   - Keep in mind how withdrawals from different accounts are taxed. Taxable accounts are subject to capital gains tax, whereas retirement accounts may be taxed as ordinary income upon withdrawal.

3. **Investment Allocation**:
   - Consider holding higher-risk investments in your taxable accounts where you can benefit from lower capital gains rates and holding tax-advantaged accounts for bonds or income-generating assets.

4. **Emergency Funds**:
   - Ensure you have sufficient liquidity in your taxable account for emergencies before maxing out retirement contributions.

5. **Long-Term Strategy**:
   - Evaluate your time horizon, risk tolerance, and retirement plans to adjust contributions across accounts accordingly.

Regular reviewing and rebalancing your portfolio can help achieve balance and adhere to your financial goals. Consulting a financial advisor may also provide personalized insights based on your situation.
