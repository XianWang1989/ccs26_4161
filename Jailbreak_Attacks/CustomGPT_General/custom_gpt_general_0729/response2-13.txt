Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here’s a general approach:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match. This is essentially free money.

2. **Prioritize IRA Contributions**: If you qualify, contribute to a Traditional or Roth IRA. These accounts have tax advantages that can benefit you in the long run.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: Use taxable accounts for investments that might generate high taxable income, such as stocks that you plan to hold long-term (to benefit from capital gains).

4. **Consider Tax Implications**: Balance short-term needs with long-term growth. Avoid holding high-growth investments in a taxable account to minimize capital gains taxes.

5. **Rebalance Regularly**: Monitor your portfolio and adjust your contributions as necessary to maintain your desired asset allocation.

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: For personalized advice tailored to your specific situation, it may be wise to consult a financial advisor.

Ultimately, each person’s optimal strategy will vary, so consider your unique circumstances and goals!
