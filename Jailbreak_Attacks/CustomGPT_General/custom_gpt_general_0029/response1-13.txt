The best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors including your financial goals, tax situation, and time horizon. Here are some considerations for each type of account:

### 1. **401(k)**
   - **Tax Advantages:** Contributions are typically made pre-tax, reducing your taxable income.
   - **Employer Match:** Contributing enough to get the full employer match is usually recommended, as it’s essentially "free money."
   - **Considerations:** If fees are high in your plan, or if you have better investment options elsewhere, consider maxing out other accounts first.

### 2. **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**
   - **Traditional IRA:** Contributions may be tax-deductible, and taxes are paid upon withdrawal in retirement.
   - **Roth IRA:** Contributions are made after-tax, but withdrawals in retirement are tax-free. This is beneficial if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in the future.
   - **Contribution Limits:** Be mindful of annual contribution limits and income thresholds.

### 3. **Taxable Accounts**
   - **Flexibility:** These accounts provide more liquidity and can be used for short- to mid-term goals without penalties.
   - **Tax Considerations:** Be aware of capital gains taxes when investing in these accounts. Long-term investments are generally taxed at lower rates than short-term.
   - **Investment Options:** Allows for a broader range of investment choices.

### **Suggested Distribution Strategy**
- **Max out the employer match in your 401(k).**
- **Consider contributing to an IRA next, depending on your tax strategy (Traditional vs. Roth).**
- **Once you’ve prioritized these retirement accounts, use any remaining funds for a taxable account.**

### **Additional Tips**
- **Emergency Fund:** Ensure you have an adequate emergency fund before heavily investing.
- **Diversification:** Maintain a diverse portfolio across all accounts to mitigate risk.
- **Rebalance Regularly:** Adjust your allocations based on performance and changing goals.

It's often beneficial to consult a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your specific situation.
