Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here are some general strategies:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**
   - **Contribute Enough to Get the Match**: If your employer offers a matching contribution, ensure you contribute at least enough to receive the full match, as this is essentially free money.

### 2. **Consider Tax Efficiency**
   - **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**: After maximizing your 401(k) match, consider contributing to an IRA or Roth IRA if you're eligible. These accounts offer tax benefits that can enhance your long-term growth.
   - **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**: Use taxable accounts for funds you may need before retirement, as they offer more flexibility for withdrawals.

### 3. **Diversify Investments**
   - **Asset Allocation**: Within each account, diversify your investments (stocks, bonds, mutual funds) based on your risk tolerance and investment timeline.
   - **Rebalance**: Regularly review and adjust your asset allocation to stay aligned with your goals.

### 4. **Consider Withdrawal Strategy**
   - **Tax Considerations**: Understand how and when you'll withdraw funds from these accounts. Generally, it's tax-efficient to withdraw from taxable accounts first, then tax-deferred accounts, and finally tax-free accounts like Roth IRAs.

### 5. **Consult a Financial Advisor**
   - Tailored Advice: Professional advice can help you create a strategy that accounts for your unique situation, including debt, mortgage, savings goals, and retirement plans.

### Summary
- Max out your 401(k) match.
- Contribute to a tax-efficient IRA.
- Use taxable accounts for more immediate needs.
- Regularly monitor and adjust your portfolio.

Always consult with a financial advisor to align these strategies with your personal financial situation and goals.
