When distributing money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider these key factors:

### 1. **Tax Considerations**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are typically pre-tax, reducing your taxable income. However, withdrawals will be taxed as ordinary income.
   - **IRA (Traditional)**: Similar to a 401(k) with tax benefits upon contribution but taxable upon withdrawal.
   - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, but withdrawals (including gains) are tax-free if conditions are met.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Dividends and capital gains are subject to taxes, but you can optimize for long-term capital gains rates (lower than ordinary income rates).

### 2. **Investment Goals**:
   - Consider your goals (retirement, buying a house, etc.) and time horizon. Longer-term investments may benefit from being in tax-advantaged accounts.

### 3. **Risk Tolerance**:
   - Assess your comfort with market risks and volatility. More aggressive investments might be suited for tax-advantaged accounts since capital gains won't result in taxes until withdrawal.

### 4. **Contribution Limits**:
   - Be aware of limits for 401(k) and IRA contributions. Max out employer match in a 401(k) first, then consider IRAs.

### 5. **Withdrawal Strategies**:
   - Plan for how and when you’ll need access to your money. Taxable accounts offer flexibility, while retirement accounts have restrictions and potential penalties for early withdrawal.

### Suggested Distribution Approach:
1. **Max Out Contributions**: Aim to contribute enough to your 401(k) to take full advantage of any employer match. Next, contribute to an IRA.
2. **Utilize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**: Prioritize tax-advantaged accounts (401(k), IRA, Roth IRA) for your long-term savings.
3. **Allocate Remaining Funds**: Use taxable accounts for short-term goals or for investments that generate favorable tax treatment (like qualified dividends).

Overall, it's beneficial to consult a financial advisor to tailor your strategy to your specific financial situation and goals.
