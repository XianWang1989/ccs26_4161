To effectively distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match; it’s essentially free money. Aim for the IRS contribution limit if possible.
   - **IRA (Roth or Traditional)**: After maximizing the 401(k), consider contributing to an IRA. Choose a Roth if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement, or a Traditional IRA for tax-deferred growth.

2. **Assess Your Tax Situation**:
   - Prioritize tax-efficient investments in taxable accounts (e.g., index funds or ETFs) to minimize capital gains taxes.
   - Place higher-yield investments (like bonds) in tax-advantaged accounts to defer or avoid taxes.

3. **Diversification**:
   - Ensure a diversified asset allocation across all accounts (stocks, bonds, etc.). Keep a consistent strategy for risk tolerance and investment goals.

4. **Regular Contributions**:
   - Set up automatic contributions to maintain discipline and benefit from dollar-cost averaging.

5. **Rebalance as Needed**:
   - Periodically review and rebalance your investment allocations to maintain your desired risk level.

6. **Emergency Savings**:
   - Ensure you have an emergency fund outside of these accounts, typically 3-6 months' worth of living expenses.

### Conclusion
The best approach balances maximizing tax advantages, maintaining liquidity in taxable accounts, and aligning with your long-term financial goals. Consult a financial advisor to tailor this strategy to your personal situation.
