To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**: Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to receive the full employer match, as this is essentially free money.

2. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: If your employer offers a match, prioritize this. If your income is high, consider a Roth option if available.
   - **IRA/Roth IRA**: Depending on your income level and tax situation, contributing to a traditional IRA or Roth IRA can be beneficial. Roth IRAs are especially useful for tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement.

3. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**: After maxing out tax-advantaged accounts, allocate funds to taxable accounts for flexibility and easier access to funds without penalties.

4. **Asset Location**: Place more tax-efficient investments (like index funds) in taxable accounts and less tax-efficient (like bond funds) in tax-advantaged accounts to minimize taxes.

5. **Consider Your Goals**: Tailor your distribution based on your short-term vs. long-term goals. Use taxable accounts for short-term needs and retirement accounts for long-term growth.

6. **Review and Rebalance**: Regularly assess your allocations and rebalance to maintain your desired risk level and asset allocation.

### Example Distribution:
- **401(k)**: Max employer match (up to 15% of salary, if possible).
- **IRA**: Fully fund if eligible (up to $6,500 for 2023, or $7,500 if over 50).
- **Taxable Accounts**: Invest any additional savings here.

It’s also wise to consult a financial advisor to tailor a strategy to your specific situation.
