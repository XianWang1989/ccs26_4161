The optimal way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, investment timeline, and risk tolerance. Here are some general strategies to consider:

### 1. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts:**
   - **401(k):**
     - Contribute enough to get any employer match, as this is essentially "free money."
     - If possible, maximize contributions up to the annual limit.
   - **IRA (Roth or Traditional):**
     - Depending on your income and tax situation, consider contributing to a Roth IRA for tax-free growth and withdrawals if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement.
     - Traditional IRAs offer tax deductions, which can be beneficial if you're currently in a higher tax bracket.

### 2. **Consider Your Investment Horizon:**
   - If you’re younger and have a long investment horizon, aggressive investments in your 401(k) and IRA might be appropriate, with a focus on growth assets (stocks).
   - As you approach retirement, gradually shift towards more conservative investments to preserve capital, particularly in your taxable accounts.

### 3. **Account for Tax Efficiency:**
   - Place high-growth or high-dividend-producing investments in tax-advantaged accounts to minimize taxes on growth and dividends.
   - Use taxable accounts for investments that generate lower taxable income or have favorable tax treatment (e.g., municipal bonds).

### 4. **Diversification Across Accounts:**
   - Ensure a balanced portfolio across all accounts, taking into account asset allocations, to avoid over-concentration in any one area.
   - Review your overall asset allocation regularly to align with your risk tolerance.

### 5. **Withdrawal Strategy:**
   - Consider how you plan to withdraw funds in retirement. Strategies like tax order of withdrawals can affect your tax bill significantly.

### 6. **Rebalance Regularly:**
   - Periodically review and rebalance your investment allocations across all accounts to ensure they align with your intended strategy and risk level.

### Summary
Consult with a financial advisor to tailor these strategies specifically to your individual situation. They can help create a comprehensive plan considering the nuanced aspects of your financial landscape.
