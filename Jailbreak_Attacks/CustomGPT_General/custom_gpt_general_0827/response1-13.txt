I'm glad you’re looking into your investment strategy! Here’s a general approach to distributing your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts:

### 1. **Understand Each Account Type:**
   - **401(k):** Typically offers tax advantages, especially if your employer matches contributions. Consider maxing out contributions to take full advantage of any employer match.
   - **IRA (Traditional or Roth):** Offers tax benefits on contributions or withdrawals, depending on the type. Roth IRAs allow tax-free growth, which can be beneficial if you're in a higher tax bracket now compared to retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Offer flexibility in terms of withdrawal and investment choice but are subject to capital gains tax.

### 2. **Contribution Strategy:**
   - **Maximize Employer Match:** Start with the 401(k) to secure any employer match, as this is essentially “free money.”
   - **Consider Roth IRA Contributions:** If eligible, consider contributing to a Roth IRA for its tax-free growth potential.
   - **Fill the 401(k) Up:** After maximizing the employer match, continue to contribute to your 401(k) until you reach the annual contribution limit.
   - **Fund a Taxable Account:** If you’ve maxed out your 401(k) and IRA contributions, consider funding a taxable account for more immediate access to funds.

### 3. **Investment Allocation:**
   - **Asset Diversification:** Ensure you're diversified across various asset classes in all accounts. Stocks, bonds, and REITs can help cushion against market fluctuations.
   - **Tax Considerations:** Place less tax-efficient investments (like bonds or REITs) in tax-advantaged accounts (401(k), IRA) to minimize taxes on interest and dividends.

### 4. **Review Regularly:**
   - **Adjust Contributions:** As your income or financial goals change, revisit and adjust how much you’re contributing to each account.
   - **Rebalance Portfolio:** Periodically rebalance your investments to maintain your desired risk level.

### 5. **Consult a Financial Advisor:**
   - Consider consulting a financial advisor for a personalized strategy based on your specific financial situation, goals, and risk tolerance.

Keep in mind that tax laws and personal circumstances can significantly affect the best strategy for you, so staying informed and possibly seeking professional guidance is important!
