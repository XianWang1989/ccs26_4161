When planning how to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Maximize Employer Matching**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match, as this is essentially free money.

2. **Optimize Tax Advantages**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are made pre-tax, reducing your taxable income now. Focus on maxing this out next to any employer match.
   - **IRA**: Depending on whether it's a Traditional or Roth IRA, you may benefit from tax-deferred growth or tax-free withdrawals in retirement. Prioritize Roth contributions if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for investments that generate low turnover to minimize capital gains taxes. This account is also flexible for withdrawals without penalties.

3. **Emergency Fund**: Ensure you have an emergency fund in a liquid account, separate from your investment accounts.

4. **Diversification**: Maintain a diversified portfolio across all accounts to mitigate risk. Ensure you rebalance regularly.

5. **Withdrawal Strategy**: Plan how you will withdraw funds in retirement to minimize tax liabilities. Generally, it's advisable to withdraw from taxable accounts first and defer tax-advantaged accounts as long as possible.

6. **Review and Adjust**: Regularly reassess your contributions and allocations based on your financial goals, changing tax laws, and life events.

Consider consulting with a financial advisor for personalized strategies based on your unique situation and to help manage risks.
