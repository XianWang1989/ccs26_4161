Distributing your money effectively among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, risk tolerance, and tax situation. Here are some strategies to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**: If your employer offers a match on your 401(k) contributions, prioritize contributing enough to get the full match. This is essentially free money that can significantly enhance your retirement savings.

2. **Contribute to an IRA**: After maximizing your employer match, consider contributing to a Traditional IRA or a Roth IRA. If you're eligible for a Roth IRA, it can be a great choice since withdrawals in retirement are tax-free. Consider which type of IRA aligns best with your tax situation and retirement goals.

3. **Balance Contributions**: If you have maxed out contributions to both 401(k) and IRA, consider additional contributions to your 401(k) up to the annual contribution limit. In 2023, the combined contribution limit for a 401(k) is $22,500 ($30,000 if you're over 50), while IRA contributions are limited to $6,500 ($7,500 if over 50).

4. **Use Taxable Accounts Wisely**: Taxable accounts can be beneficial for savings goals that are shorter-term, such as buying a home or funding education. You can invest in a diversified portfolio, but be mindful of capital gains taxes on sales. Consider holding tax-efficient investments like index funds or tax-managed funds in these accounts.

5. **Diversification**: Maintain a well-diversified portfolio across all accounts to manage your risk. This might involve investing in a mix of equities, bonds, and other asset classes based on your risk tolerance.

6. **Tax Considerations**: Consider the tax implications of withdrawals from each type of account. 401(k) and Traditional IRAs are taxed as ordinary income upon withdrawal, while Roth IRAs are tax-free if certain conditions are met. Taxable investments may incur capital gains tax, so be strategic about when to sell assets.

7. **Regularly Review and Rebalance**: Periodically review your asset allocation and investment performance. Rebalancing ensures that your investment strategy remains aligned with your financial goals.

8. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: If you're unsure of how to balance your investments across these accounts, seeking professional advice can help tailor a strategy to your specific situation.

Ultimately, the "best" distribution will vary based on your individual circumstances, so it's important to tailor these strategies to fit your financial plan.
