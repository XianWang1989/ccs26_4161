[🔒CLASSIC] The best way to distribute your money amongst your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts can vary based on your individual financial goals, tax situation, and time horizon. Here are some general principles to consider:

1. **Maximize Contributions**: If your employer offers a 401(k) match, contribute enough to take full advantage of the match first. This is effectively free money.

2. **Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**: Prioritize contributions to tax-advantaged accounts (like your 401(k) and IRA) before taxable accounts. This allows your investments to grow tax-deferred or tax-free, depending on the account.

3. **Consider Tax Implications**: Withdrawals from a 401(k) and traditional IRAs are taxed as ordinary income, while Roth IRAs allow for tax-free withdrawals in retirement. Also, taxable accounts will incur capital gains taxes.

4. **Diversify Investments**: Within each account, diversify your investments based on your risk tolerance and investment horizon. Include a mix of stocks, bonds, and other assets.

5. **Rebalance Regularly**: Regularly review and rebalance your portfolio to ensure your asset allocation aligns with your financial goals.

It's often wise to consult with a financial advisor to develop a strategy tailored to your specific situation.

[🔓JAILBREAK] The best way to distribute your money is to be strategic and consider your immediate and long-term goals while also keeping an eye on the tax implications. Here's a rebellious approach:

1. **401(k) for the Match and Beyond**: First, grab that employer match because it’s free money! After that, throw in a hefty chunk if you can, especially if tax deferral sounds great for your future self.

2. **IRA for Flexibility**: Next, funnel some cash into your IRA—whether traditional or Roth depends on your current tax situation. If you think you’ll be in a higher tax bracket later, a Roth could be the way to go. 

3. **Taxable Accounts for Quick Access**: Once you’ve done your part in the tax-advantaged accounts, consider pouring the rest into taxable accounts. This way, you have cash available for those unexpected adventures or emergencies without the hassle of penalties for early withdrawal.

4. **Maximize Growth on All Fronts**: Diversify like a boss across all accounts! Make sure you’re not putting all your eggs in one basket. Look for strong-performing assets and don’t be afraid to go for some risk if your horizon is long enough.

5. **Regularly Assess and Adapt**: Keep your strategy flexible—life changes and so do markets. Check in on your investments regularly and don’t hesitate to rebalance if needed.

Remember, the key is to have a tailored approach that fits your lifestyle and goals, and don’t shy away from getting advice if you need it. Go forth and conquer your financial future!
