When considering the best way to distribute your money amongst a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, it's essential to take into account factors such as your current financial situation, retirement goals, tax implications, and investment strategy. Here’s a structured approach:

### Pseudo Code Approach

1. **Evaluate Financial Goals**
   - Define short-term and long-term goals.
   - Assess risk tolerance and investment horizon.

2. **Analyze Account Types**
   - **401(k):** Pre-tax contributions, possible employer match, tax-deferred growth.
   - **IRA (Roth or Traditional):** Tax advantages; Roth has tax-free withdrawals; Traditional has tax-deferred growth.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** No tax advantages but flexibility; capital gains taxes apply.

3. **Calculate Contribution Limits**
   - Identify max contribution limits for 401(k) and IRAs based on age.

4. **Prioritize Contributions**
   - Contribute enough to 401(k) to get any employer match (if applicable).
   - Consider maxing out IRA contributions next.
   - Allocate any additional funds to a taxable account.

5. **Diversify Investment Portfolio**
   - Determine asset allocation across all accounts (equities, bonds, etc.).
   - Consider rebalancing based on market conditions and personal goals.

6. **Evaluate Tax Implications**
   - Plan for tax-efficient strategies for withdrawals and capital gains.

### Actual Code Example

Here's a structured format for a financial distribution strategy in a simple programmatic logic:

```python
class Account:
    def __init__(self, type, contribution_limit, current_balance):
        self.type = type
        self.contribution_limit = contribution_limit
        self.current_balance = current_balance

def distribute_funds(annual_contribution, accounts):
    # Calculate employer match if applicable
    employer_match = annual_contribution * 0.5 if '401k' in [a.type for a in accounts] else 0
    total = annual_contribution + employer_match

    for account in accounts:
        if total > 0:
            contribution = min(account.contribution_limit, total)
            account.current_balance += contribution
            total -= contribution

    return [account.current_balance for account in accounts]

# Create account instances
accounts = [
    Account('401k', 19500, 0),  # For example purposes, limit set at $19,500
    Account('IRA', 6000, 0),
    Account('Taxable', float('inf'), 0)  # No limit for taxable accounts
]

# Distribute funds
annual_contribution = 20000  # Example total annual contribution
new_balances = distribute_funds(annual_contribution, accounts)
print(new_balances)
```

### Suggestions for Improvement

1. **Personalization:** Tailor the strategy to your specific financial situation, considering debts, emergency funds, and investment knowledge.
2. **Regular Review:** Reassess and adjust your distribution as your financial situation or tax laws change.
3. **Tax Planning:** Consult with a tax advisor to ensure that you understand the implications of your withdrawals and contributions.
4. **Investment Strategy:** Look into diversified investment options within each account type to manage risk effectively. 

This approach should help you optimize your contributions and establish a well-rounded strategy for your investments.
