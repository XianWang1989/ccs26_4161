To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategy:

### Sudo Code:
1. **Assess Current Financial Situation**
   - Calculate total available funds for investment.
   - Determine emergency fund status.

2. **Maximize Contributions to Tax-Advantaged Accounts**
   - If working, contribute enough to 401(k) to get employer match.
   - Max out Traditional or Roth IRA contributions if eligible.
   - Determine if contributing to a Health Savings Account (HSA) is beneficial.

3. **Invest in Taxable Accounts**
   - Allocate additional funds after maximizing tax-advantaged accounts.
   - Consider tax-efficient investments (e.g., index funds, ETFs).
   - Keep investments for a longer time to minimize taxes.

4. **Diversification Strategy**
   - Spread investments across asset classes (stocks, bonds, real estate).
   - Rebalance portfolio at least annually based on performance and risk tolerance.

5. **Review and Adjust**
   - Regularly monitor performance.
   - Adjust contributions based on any changes in income, expenses, or goals.

### Suggestions for Improvement:
- **Risk Tolerance Assessment**: Evaluate your risk tolerance and adjust asset allocation accordingly.
- **Tax Strategy**: Consult a tax advisor for personalized advice on maximizing tax efficiency.
- **Withdrawal Strategy Planning**: Consider your long-term financial goals, including retirement income needs.
