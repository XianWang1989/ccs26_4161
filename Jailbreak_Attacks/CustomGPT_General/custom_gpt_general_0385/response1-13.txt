When considering the best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, several factors come into play, including your financial goals, tax implications, and retirement timeline. Here’s a general framework to guide your decision:

1. **Maximize Employer Contributions**: Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to take full advantage of any employer match. This is essentially free money.

2. **Consider Tax Deferral**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are pre-tax, providing immediate tax benefits. This makes sense if you expect to be in a lower tax bracket in retirement.
   - **Traditional IRA**: Similar benefits as a 401(k) if you qualify. If you’re eligible for tax deductibility, this can be a good option.

3. **Roth Accounts**: If you foresee being in a higher tax bracket later or want tax-free withdrawals in retirement, consider contributing to a Roth IRA or a Roth 401(k), if available.

4. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**: After maxing out retirement accounts, utilize taxable investment accounts. While these do not offer immediate tax benefits, they provide more liquidity and can be beneficial for short- to medium-term goals.

5. **Diversify Investments**: Regardless of account type, ensure you're diversifying your investments based on your risk tolerance and investment horizon.

6. **Reassess and Adjust**: Regularly review your asset allocation and make adjustments as significant life events, tax laws, or your financial situation changes.

### Example Distribution Strategy:
- Contribute up to the employer match in your 401(k).
- Max out your IRA contributions (either traditional or Roth based on eligibility and tax situation).
- Increase contributions to your 401(k) up to the annual limit.
- Invest any remaining funds in a taxable account while considering tax-efficient investment strategies (like index funds).

Ultimately, balance these considerations based on your financial situation and consult with a financial advisor if needed for personalized strategies.
