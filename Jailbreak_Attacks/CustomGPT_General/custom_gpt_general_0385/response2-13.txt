To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

### 1. Tax Efficiency:
   - **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**: Start by contributing to your 401(k) and IRA, especially if your employer offers matching contributions. This is essentially free money.
   - **Prioritize Roth Accounts for Tax-Free Growth**: If eligible, consider Roth IRA contributions for tax-free withdrawals in retirement.

### 2. Contribution Limits:
   - **Know the Limits**: For 2023, the 401(k) contribution limit is $22,500 ($30,000 if you’re 50 or older), and the IRA limit is $6,500 ($7,500 for those 50 or older). Ensure you're not exceeding these limits.

### 3. Asset Allocation:
   - **Diversify Investments**: Within each account, diversify across different asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) based on your risk tolerance and investment horizon.
   - **Consider Bonds in Taxable Accounts**: Since bond interest is taxed at the ordinary income rate, placing bonds in tax-advantaged accounts can enhance tax efficiency.

### 4. Withdrawal Strategy:
   - **Plan for Withdrawals**: Strategically plan which accounts to withdraw from first in retirement to minimize taxes. Often, withdrawing from taxable accounts first can be beneficial.

### 5. Review and Rebalance:
   - **Regularly Rebalance Your Portfolio**: Keep your asset allocation aligned with your goals and risk tolerance, adjusting as needed based on market conditions and life changes.

### 6. Consult a Financial Advisor:
   - **Get Professional Advice**: A financial advisor can provide personalized recommendations based on your specific financial situation, retirement goals, and risk tolerance.

By considering these factors, you can create a balanced approach to distributing your money that aligns with your financial goals and maximizes tax efficiency.
