Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors including your financial goals, tax situation, and the investment options available in each account. Here are some general guidelines:

1. **401(k):** Often comes with an employer match, which is essentially "free money." Contributing enough to capture the full match is usually a priority. Consider maxing out your contributions if you can, especially if your employer provides a good match.

2. **IRA (Roth or Traditional):** IRAs can provide tax advantages depending on your income and tax situation. If you're eligible, consider contributing to a Roth IRA for tax-free withdrawals in retirement. A traditional IRA may be more beneficial if you expect to be in a lower tax bracket in retirement.

3. **Taxable Accounts:** These should be used for savings goals with shorter time horizons since they do not have the same tax advantages. Use these accounts for investments that may be subject to lighter tax treatment, such as long-term capital gains or tax-efficient funds.

### Allocation Strategy:
- **Emergency Fund:** Ensure you have an emergency fund in a liquid account before maximizing investing.
- **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts:** Max out your 401(k) match first, then contribute to IRAs, and finally invest in taxable accounts.
- **Diversification:** After reaching your initial contributions limits, consider reallocating investments across all three types of accounts to ensure a well-diversified portfolio regardless of account type.

### Regular Reviews:
- Reassess your allocation periodically based on changes in financial goals, market conditions, and life circumstances.

It's often beneficial to consult a financial advisor to develop a personalized strategy that suits your unique situation.
