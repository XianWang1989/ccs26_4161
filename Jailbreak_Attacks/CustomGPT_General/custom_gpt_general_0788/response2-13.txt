Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various factors including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some general strategies to consider:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**: If your employer offers a 401(k) with a matching contribution, prioritize contributing enough to get the full match. This is essentially free money. 

2. **Contribute to an IRA**: After maximizing the 401(k) match, consider contributing to an IRA (Traditional or Roth) depending on your eligibility and tax situation. A Roth IRA offers tax-free withdrawals in retirement, while a Traditional IRA provides a tax deduction on contributions.

3. **Focus on Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**: Once you've maxed out tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) and IRA), you can allocate additional funds to a taxable brokerage account. This provides flexibility for withdrawals at any time without penalties.

4. **Consider Investment Options and Fees**: Evaluate the investment options and fees associated with each account. Sometimes 401(k) plans may have higher fees compared to IRAs or taxable accounts.

5. **Assess Your Tax Bracket**: Your current and expected future tax brackets can influence where to allocate your money. If you're in a high tax bracket now but expect to be lower in retirement, a Traditional IRA might be beneficial. Conversely, if you expect to be in the same or higher tax bracket, a Roth IRA could be more advantageous.

6. **Rebalance Regularly**: Ensure your asset allocation aligns with your risk tolerance and time horizon. Rebalance as necessary to maintain your desired distribution.

It’s always good to consult with a financial advisor tailored to your specific situation, as they can provide personalized recommendations based on your overall financial picture.
