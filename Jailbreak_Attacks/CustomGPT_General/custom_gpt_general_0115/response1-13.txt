To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Tax Optimization:**
   - **Maximize tax-advantaged accounts:** Start by contributing to your 401(k) and IRA to benefit from tax deferral or tax-free growth. Aim for at least the employer match in your 401(k).
   - **Choose the right type of IRA:** If you're eligible, consider a Roth IRA for tax-free withdrawals in retirement. If you're in a higher tax bracket now, a traditional IRA might be beneficial for immediate tax deductions.

2. **Diversification:**
   - Ensure your investments across all accounts are diversified. Balance stocks, bonds, and other assets according to your risk tolerance and investment timeline.

3. **Consider Withdrawal Strategies:**
   - As you approach retirement, think about how different accounts will be taxed upon withdrawal. Taxable accounts can be more flexible, whereas tax-advantaged accounts may have penalties for early withdrawal.

4. **Account Types for Different Investments:**
   - **401(k):** Often has limited investment choices; choose lower-cost, diversified options.
   - **IRA:** You might have broader options (stocks, ETFs, etc.) allowing for more aggressive or tailored investment strategies.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Great for investments you'll need access to before retirement; focus on tax-efficient investments (e.g., index funds).

5. **Regular Contributions:**
   - Make regular contributions to each account type based on your financial goals and cash flow. Consider increasing contributions during higher earning years.

6. **Adjust for Life Changes:**
   - Reassess your distribution strategy as your financial situation, goal timelines, or tax laws change.

Consult a financial advisor to tailor these strategies to your specific situation.
