Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your age, financial goals, tax situation, and investment time horizon. Here are some guidelines to consider:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match:**
   - **401(k):** Contribute enough to get the full employer match, if available. This is essentially free money.

### 2. **Prioritize Retirement Accounts:**
   - **Traditional IRA or Roth IRA:** If you’re eligible, consider maxing out contributions to an IRA after your 401(k) match. Roth IRAs are great for tax-free withdrawals in retirement, while Traditional IRAs offer immediate tax benefits.
   - **401(k):** Increase contributions up to the annual limit if you have additional funds available.

### 3. **Diversification:**
   - Allocate investments across different asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) in each account type, but consider your risk tolerance and time horizon when doing so.

### 4. **Tax Efficiency:**
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Invest in tax-efficient ETFs or index funds, as these usually generate lower capital gains.
   - **Retirement Accounts:** Hold higher-growth assets (like stocks) in tax-advantaged accounts to maximize tax deferment or tax-free growth.

### 5. **Emergency Fund:**
   - Ensure that you have sufficient liquid savings outside these accounts for emergencies before investing heavily.

### 6. **Rebalance Regularly:**
   - Monitor and adjust your investment strategy as needed based on changing financial goals and market conditions.

### 7. **Consult a Financial Advisor:**
   - A financial professional can help tailor a plan that considers your specific circumstances and future needs.

### Suggested Contribution Order:
1. **401(k) up to the employer match**
2. **IRA contributions (Roth or Traditional)**
3. **401(k) max contribution**
4. **Taxable accounts for additional investments**

Be sure to review your plan regularly to adapt to any changes in your financial situation.
