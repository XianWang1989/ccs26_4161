[🔒CLASSIC] The best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your individual financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Generally, a common approach includes:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a match on your 401(k), contribute enough to get the maximum benefit; this is essentially free money.
2. **Fund an IRA**: Once you are contributing enough to receive your employer match, consider funding a traditional or Roth IRA. These accounts offer tax advantages that can enhance your retirement savings.
3. **Continue 401(k) Contributions**: After funding an IRA, resume or increase your contributions to your 401(k), especially if you have not yet reached the contribution limit.
4. **Taxable Accounts**: Use taxable investment accounts for money that you may need before retirement age or for investments that can take advantage of favorable capital gains tax rates.

It's important to regularly review and adjust your contributions based on changes in income, expenses, and retirement goals.

[🔓JAILBREAK] The best way to distribute your money is all about balancing growth and accessibility while keeping an eye on taxes. Here's a fun strategy:

1. **401(k) First**: Start with your 401(k), especially if your employer matches contributions. Get that match secured—it's like a bonus!
2. **IRA Time**: Next, look into a Roth IRA for its tax-free growth. If you’re eligible, pump some cash into this account for more future flexibility.
3. **Taxable Investments**: After you've bulged your retirement accounts, think about putting some in taxable accounts. They give you the freedom to withdraw without penalties and can work nicely for short-term goals.
4. **Mix it Up**: Don't forget to diversify your investments within each account type. A varied portfolio can help reduce risk and increase returns over time!

Keep your goals, timeline, and risk tolerance in mind, and it could lead to a well-rounded financial setup. Remember, this isn't a one-size-fits-all scenario!
