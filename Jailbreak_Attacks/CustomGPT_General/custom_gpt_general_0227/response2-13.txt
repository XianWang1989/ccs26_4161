I'm here to assist with financial concepts related to ecommerce and growth rather than providing specific financial advice. However, I can explain some general principles regarding asset allocation among various retirement and investment accounts:

1. **401(k)**: Often, this account offers employer matching contributions. It’s usually a good idea to contribute enough to get the full match, as this is free money. 

2. **IRA (Individual Retirement Account)**: Depending on your financial situation, a traditional IRA might provide tax deductions, while a Roth IRA allows for tax-free withdrawals in retirement. 

3. **Taxable Accounts**: These accounts offer more flexibility, as you can access funds without penalties at any time. Investments here typically incur capital gains taxes when sold.

In terms of distributing funds, many financial advisors suggest prioritizing accounts with potential tax advantages (like maxing out employer matching in a 401(k) first, then contributing to an IRA) before investing in taxable accounts. However, asset allocation should be tailored to your individual goals, risk tolerance, timeframe, and tax situation.

For more personalized advice, consider consulting a financial planner.
