Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your specific financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here's a structured approach to consider:

### 1. **Understand Account Types**:
   - **401(k)**: Employer-sponsored, often includes matching contributions. Contributions are pre-tax (or Roth for post-tax).
   - **IRA (Individual Retirement Account)**: Offers tax advantages; Traditional IRAs provide tax-deferred growth, while Roth IRAs offer tax-free withdrawals in retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Funds are subject to capital gains taxes, but they offer flexibility for withdrawals without penalties.

### 2. **Prioritize Contributions**:
   - **Maximize 401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match, as this is essentially "free money."
   - **Fund IRAs**: If you’re eligible, contribute to IRAs (consider maxing out Roth IRAs for tax-free growth if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement).
   - **Taxable Accounts**: After maximizing tax-advantaged accounts, use taxable accounts for additional savings and investment.

### 3. **Consider Tax Implications**:
   - **Tax Diversification**: Maintaining a mix of account types can provide flexibility in retirement for managing tax liabilities.
   - **Asset Location**: Consider placing investments with higher expected returns (e.g., stocks) in tax-advantaged accounts and more tax-efficient investments (e.g., bonds) in taxable accounts.

### 4. **Investment Strategy**:
   - Choose a diversified portfolio that aligns with your risk tolerance and time horizon across all accounts, adjusting based on the specific tax treatment of each account type.

### 5. **Regular Rebalancing**:
   - Periodically review and rebalance your allocations to ensure they remain aligned with your investment goals and risk tolerance.

### Example Allocation:
1. **401(k)**: Maximize contributions to get the full employer match and invest in a mix of growth-oriented funds.
2. **IRA**: Full contribution to either a Traditional or Roth IRA based on tax strategy.
3. **Taxable Account**: Invest in low-turnover index funds or ETFs and consider tax-loss harvesting to manage capital gains.

### Key Takeaway:
Aim to maximize tax-advantaged accounts first, use taxable accounts for additional flexibility, and maintain a diversified investment strategy tailored to your financial goals.
