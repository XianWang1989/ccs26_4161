Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here's a general strategy to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match, as this is free money.

2. **Traditional vs. Roth IRA**: If eligible, consider whether a Traditional IRA or Roth IRA fits your tax situation better:
   - **Traditional IRA**: Contributions may be tax-deductible, lowering your current taxable income.
   - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, but withdrawals in retirement are tax-free.

3. **Fund Taxable Accounts**: After maximizing contributions to tax-advantaged accounts, you can use a taxable account for additional investments. This is especially useful for goals that are shorter term or if you've maxed out your IRA contributions.

4. **Consider Investment Types**: 
   - Place high-growth investments (like stocks) in tax-advantaged accounts to benefit from tax-free growth (Roth) or tax-deferral (Traditional).
   - Keep income-generating investments (like bonds) in taxable accounts, as they can be taxed at a lower capital gains rate if held long-term.

5. **Keep an Emergency Fund**: Ensure you have savings set aside for emergencies, separate from investments.

6. **Review Annually**: Reassess your allocations and contributions annually or if your financial situation changes.

Always consider consulting a financial advisor to tailor a strategy specific to your needs and minimize tax implications.
