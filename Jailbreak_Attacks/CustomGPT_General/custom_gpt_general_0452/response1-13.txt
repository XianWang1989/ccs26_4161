To effectively distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider these strategies:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match, as this is essentially free money.

2. **Tax Considerations**:
   - **Traditional 401(k) and IRA**: Contributions are tax-deferred, which can be beneficial if you expect to be in a lower tax bracket in retirement. Consider maximizing these accounts if you're in a high tax bracket now.
   - **Roth Accounts**: If you have a Roth 401(k) or Roth IRA, contributions are made with after-tax dollars, but withdrawals are tax-free in retirement, which is advantageous if you think your tax rate will be higher in the future.

3. **Emergency Fund**: Ensure you have an adequate emergency fund outside of retirement accounts. Generally, it's recommended to have 3-6 months' worth of expenses saved.

4. **Investment Strategy**:
   - Use tax-advantaged accounts (like 401(k)s and IRAs) for investments that generate higher taxable income, such as bonds or high-dividend stocks, since they’ll grow tax-deferred.
   - Place assets with lower turnover or capital gains in taxable accounts, such as index funds or long-term investments.

5. **Account Types**:
   - **401(k)**: Good for employer matching and loans; may have limited investment options.
   - **IRA**: Offers a broader selection of investments; consider whether you want a Traditional or Roth IRA based on your tax situation.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Flexible in terms of withdrawals with no penalties, but subject to capital gains taxes.

6. **Diversification**: Ensure your investments across all accounts are diversified to manage risk effectively.

7. **Periodic Review**: Regularly assess and adjust your allocations based on changes in income, tax laws, financial goals, and market conditions.

8. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: If unsure, consulting a financial advisor can help tailor a strategy based on your specific situation and retirement goals.

By balancing these accounts and strategies effectively, you can optimize your savings and growth potential for retirement.
