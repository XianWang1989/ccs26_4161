Determining the best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax considerations, and investment horizon. Here’s a general framework to guide you:

### 1. **Understand Each Account Type:**
   - **401(k):** Often comes with employer matching contributions, and contributions are pre-tax, reducing your taxable income. Consider maximizing contributions to take advantage of any match.
   - **Traditional IRA:** Similar to a 401(k), contributions may be tax-deductible, but there are income limits for deductibility. It's also subject to Required Minimum Distributions (RMDs) starting at age 73.
   - **Roth IRA:** Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, and qualified withdrawals are tax-free. There are income limits for contributions, but it doesn't have RMDs during your lifetime.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** No tax advantages, but you have flexibility in withdrawals and the ability to strategically realize gains and losses.

### 2. **Prioritization of Contributions:**
   - **Max Out Employer Match in 401(k):** Start by contributing enough to get the full employer match in your 401(k). This is essentially free money.
   - **Contribute to an IRA:** Consider contributing to an IRA next, especially if you want tax diversification (Roth vs. Traditional).
   - **Maximize 401(k) Contributions:** After meeting the IRA contributions, continue to max out your 401(k).
   - **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility:** If you have additional funds after maximizing tax-advantaged accounts, you can invest in taxable accounts for flexibility.

### 3. **Tax Considerations:**
   - **Short-term vs. Long-term Gains:** Understand how investments in taxable accounts will be taxed (short-term gains are taxed at ordinary income rates, while long-term gains have favorable rates).
   - **Location of Investments:** Consider placing more tax-inefficient investments (like bonds or actively managed funds) in tax-advantaged accounts, while holding tax-efficient investments (like index funds) in taxable accounts.

### 4. **Investment and Risk Tolerance:**
   - **Diversify Across Accounts:** Maintain a balanced asset allocation across all accounts according to your risk tolerance and investment goals.
   - **Rebalance as Necessary:** Monitor and rebalance your investments periodically to ensure alignment with your strategy and risk tolerance.

### 5. **Consult a Financial Advisor:**
   - If your situation is complex (e.g., large sums of money, specific goals), consider consulting a financial advisor. They can provide personalized advice tailored to your unique financial situation.

### General Distribution Strategy:
- **401(k):** 15-20% of your income, considering employer match.
- **IRA:** Max out contribution limits ($6,500 or $7,500 if age 50+ for 2023).
- **Taxable Accounts:** Fund based on leftover income after maximizing retirement accounts.

This strategy may vary based on your personal goals and circumstances, so assess regularly and adjust as needed.
