When distributing your money amongst a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies to optimize your investments:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k):** Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match. This is essentially "free money" and can significantly boost your retirement savings.

2. **Focus on Tax-Advantaged Accounts:**
   - **401(k):** Contribute as much as you can, especially if you are in a higher tax bracket. This lowers your taxable income. Consider a Roth 401(k) if available and you prefer tax-free withdrawals in retirement.
   - **Traditional IRA or Roth IRA:** If you're eligible, contribute to an IRA. A traditional IRA offers tax-deferred growth, while a Roth IRA provides tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement.

3. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility:** If you've maxed out your 401(k) and IRA contributions, allocate funds to a taxable brokerage account. This gives you more flexibility for withdrawals and investment types, although you'll need to manage taxes on capital gains and dividends.

4. **Diversification Across Accounts:** Ensure your investments are diversified across all accounts. For example, you might want to hold more aggressive investments (like stocks) in tax-advantaged accounts to benefit from tax-free or tax-deferred growth, while keeping safer assets (like bonds) in taxable accounts.

5. **Consider Your Time Horizon and Risk Tolerance:** Adjust your contributions based on your age, financial goals, and risk tolerance. Younger investors might prioritize growth, while those closer to retirement might focus on preservation of capital.

6. **Rebalance Regularly:** Regularly review and rebalance your portfolio across different accounts to maintain your desired asset allocation.

7. **Seek Professional Advice:** Consider consulting a financial advisor to tailor a strategy specific to your financial situation and goals.

By prioritizing your contributions strategically and considering tax implications, you can optimize your wealth over time.
