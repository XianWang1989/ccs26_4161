Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts can optimize your retirement savings and tax efficiency. Here's a general approach you might consider:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k):**
   - First, contribute enough to your 401(k) to take full advantage of any employer match. This is essentially "free money."

### 2. **Consider Tax Benefits:**
   - **401(k):** Contributions are pre-tax, lowering your taxable income. Great for high earners looking to reduce immediate tax.
   - **Traditional IRA:** Also offers tax-deferred growth, but contributions may be limited based on income if you have a workplace retirement plan.
   - **Roth IRA:** Offers tax-free withdrawals in retirement, beneficial if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket later.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Fewer tax advantages, but offer more flexibility for withdrawals. Focus on tax-efficient investments to minimize capital gains taxes.

### 3. **General Allocation Strategy:**
   - **401(k):** Max out contributions up to the employer match, then consider investing further based on your tax situation.
   - **IRA Contributions:** If eligible, consider contributing to a Roth IRA for tax-free growth. If not, max out a Traditional IRA.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Use this for funds you want to keep accessible and for longer-term investments in lower-taxed assets (like index funds).

### 4. **Invest Based on Risk Tolerance:**
   - Ensure your investment choices align with your risk tolerance and time horizon across all accounts:
     - **Aggressive Growth:** Consider stocks and ETFs in retirement accounts.
     - **Preservation:** Bonds and conservative funds in taxable accounts to manage taxes better.

### 5. **Review and Adjust Regularly:**
   - Reassess your allocations annually or when financial circumstances change. Adjust your investments based on performance and changes in your financial goals.

### 6. **Consult with a Financial Advisor:**
   - A professional can provide personalized guidance based on your entire financial picture.

By strategically distributing your money, you can maximize growth potential and minimize tax implications over the long term.
