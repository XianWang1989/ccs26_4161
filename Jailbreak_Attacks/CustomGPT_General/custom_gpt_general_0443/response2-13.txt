Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, risk tolerance, and tax situation. Here are some general guidelines to consider:

1. **401(k) Contributions:**
   - Maximize any employer match first, as this is essentially free money.
   - Choose investments with long-term growth potential, keeping in mind your age and risk tolerance.

2. **IRA (Traditional or Roth):**
   - If possible, contribute to a Roth IRA for tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement. This is particularly advantageous if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket later.
   - For a Traditional IRA, contributions may be tax-deductible, providing immediate tax benefits. This can be a good option if you expect to be in a lower tax bracket during retirement.

3. **Taxable Accounts:**
   - These accounts are beneficial for short-term goals or for investments you might want to access before retirement age without penalties.
   - Focus on tax-efficient investments, such as index funds or ETFs, to minimize capital gains taxes.

4. **Diversification:**
   - Regardless of the account type, ensure that your portfolio is diversified across asset classes (stocks, bonds, real estate) to manage risk effectively.

5. **Tax Considerations:**
   - Keep in mind your current and expected future tax rates when deciding where to invest. Long-term capital gains and qualified dividends in taxable accounts can be taxed at lower rates.

6. **Rebalancing:**
   - Regularly review and rebalance your portfolio to ensure it aligns with your investment strategy and risk tolerance.

### Example Distribution Strategy:
- **401(k):** 10-15% of your income, focusing on high-growth mutual funds or target-date funds.
- **IRA:** Max out contributions if possible (currently $6,500 or $7,500 if age 50+).
- **Taxable Account:** Use remaining funds for short-term goals or additional investments, focusing on tax-efficient options.

It might also be beneficial to consult a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your individual situation.
