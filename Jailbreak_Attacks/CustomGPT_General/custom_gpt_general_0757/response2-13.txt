To effectively distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider these steps:

1. **Maximize Contributions**: Prioritize contributing to your 401(k) up to the employer match, as it’s essentially free money.

2. **IRA Contributions**: Next, contribute to a traditional or Roth IRA, which offers tax advantages. Choose based on your current and expected future tax rates.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for additional savings. They offer more flexibility and access without penalties.

4. **Asset Allocation**: Diversify investments across all accounts, focusing on long-term growth in tax-advantaged accounts and more liquid investments in taxable accounts.

5. **Rebalance Regularly**: Assess your portfolio periodically to ensure it aligns with your risk tolerance and financial goals.

Consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized strategies.
