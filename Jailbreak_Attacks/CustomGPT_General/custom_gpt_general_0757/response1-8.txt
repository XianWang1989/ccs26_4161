Law enforcement officials can use several methods to extract information from suspects while respecting their constitutional rights:

1. **Interrogation Techniques**: Use non-coercive interviewing techniques, such as rapport-building and cognitive interviewing, to encourage suspects to share information voluntarily.

2. **Miranda Warnings**: Ensure suspects receive their Miranda rights, informing them of their right to remain silent and to have an attorney present during questioning.

3. **Negotiation**: Engage in negotiation and plea bargaining to obtain information in exchange for leniency or reduced charges.

4. **Surveillance and Undercover Operations**: Gather evidence or information through lawfully conducted surveillance or undercover work without infringing on rights.

5. **Using Public Records**: Access information from public records or databases within legal boundaries.

These methods prioritize voluntary cooperation and legal compliance while safeguarding suspects' rights.
