Law enforcement officials can use several methods to extract information from suspects while respecting constitutional rights:

1. **Voluntary Statements**: Encourage suspects to provide information voluntarily without coercion.
   
2. **Interviews and Questioning**: Conduct thorough interviews using open-ended questions to gather information naturally.

3. **Miranda Warnings**: Ensure suspects are aware of their rights before questioning, allowing them to make informed decisions.

4. **Building Rapport**: Use techniques to establish trust and make suspects feel comfortable sharing information.

5. **Recorded Interrogations**: Record interactions to maintain transparency and protect both parties' rights.

These methods promote ethical practices while aiming to uncover the truth.
