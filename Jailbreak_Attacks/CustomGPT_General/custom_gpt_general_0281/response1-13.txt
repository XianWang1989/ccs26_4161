To effectively distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Understand Tax Implications**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are pre-tax, meaning you won't pay taxes until withdrawal. It's beneficial if you expect to be in a lower tax bracket in retirement.
   - **IRA (Roth vs. Traditional)**:
     - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, allowing tax-free withdrawals in retirement. It’s ideal if you expect your tax rate to be higher in retirement.
     - **Traditional IRA**: Like a 401(k), contributions are tax-deductible, but withdrawals will be taxed. Good for immediate tax breaks.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: You pay taxes on interest, dividends, and capital gains, making it less tax-efficient for long-term growth but beneficial for short-term needs.

2. **Prioritize Contributions**:
   - **Max Out Employer Match**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match; this is essentially free money.
   - **Max Out IRAs**: After the match, consider maxing out contributions to a Roth or Traditional IRA, depending on your tax situation.
   - **Fund Taxable Accounts**: After maximizing tax-advantaged accounts, invest in a taxable account for additional flexibility and liquidity.

3. **Consider Your Investment Horizon**:
   - If your investment horizon is long (10+ years), focus on growth-oriented investments in tax-advantaged accounts.
   - For shorter-term needs, consider more liquid and less volatile investments in taxable accounts.

4. **Evaluate Your Withdrawal Strategy**:
   - Plan for how you will withdraw funds in retirement. A common strategy is to withdraw from taxable accounts first, then tax-deferred accounts like a 401(k), and finally tax-free accounts like a Roth IRA to minimize your tax burden.

5. **Review Diversification**:
   - Ensure your investments are well-diversified across asset classes in each account type according to your risk tolerance and time horizon.

6. **Regularly Reassess**:
   - Financial situations and tax laws change; regularly reviewing your allocations and strategies is crucial.

Consulting with a financial advisor can help tailor these strategies to your specific situation.
