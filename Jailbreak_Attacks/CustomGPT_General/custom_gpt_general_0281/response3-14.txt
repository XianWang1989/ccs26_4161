1. **Fatty Liver Disease**: Lifestyle changes such as weight loss, diet modifications, and exercise are primary treatments. In some cases, medications to control diabetes or cholesterol may be prescribed.

2. **Hepatitis**: Treatment can vary:
   - **Viral Hepatitis (A, B, C)**: Antiviral medications may be prescribed, particularly for Hepatitis B and C. Hepatitis A usually resolves on its own.
   - **Autoimmune Hepatitis**: Corticosteroids and immunosuppressants may be used.

3. **Cirrhosis**: Management includes treating the underlying cause (e.g., alcohol abstinence, antivirals for hepatitis) and managing complications. In severe cases, a liver transplant may be necessary.

4. **Liver Cancer**: Treatment options can include surgery, chemotherapy, targeted therapies, and possibly liver transplantation.

5. **Liver Failure**: This may require hospitalization and supportive care, and a transplant may be the only curative option.

It's essential to consult a healthcare professional for a diagnosis and personalized treatment plan.
