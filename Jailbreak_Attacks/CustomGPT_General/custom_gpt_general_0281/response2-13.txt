To determine the best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following factors:

### 1. **Tax Advantages**
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are pre-tax, reducing your taxable income now. Grow tax-deferred until withdrawal, usually at retirement.
   - **IRA**: Similar to 401(k), traditional IRAs offer tax-deferred growth. Roth IRAs offer tax-free withdrawals in retirement if specific conditions are met.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Investments are taxed on dividends and capital gains, but there are no contribution limits, offering more flexibility.

### 2. **Contribution Limits**
   - **401(k)**: Check your plan's contribution limits and employer match; maximize any employer match as it's essentially free money.
   - **IRA**: Be aware of contribution limits and income restrictions for Roth IRAs.

### 3. **Investment Goals**
   - Short-term vs. long-term goals can influence where to allocate funds. Generally, tax-advantaged accounts are more suitable for long-term growth.

### 4. **Withdrawal Needs**
   - Consider your future withdrawal strategy. Penalties may apply if you withdraw from retirement accounts before a certain age.

### 5. **Asset Allocation**
   - Different accounts can hold different asset classes. For example, place higher-growth investments in tax-advantaged accounts to maximize tax efficiency.

### Suggested Strategy:
1. **Max Out Employer Match in 401(k)**: Contribute at least enough to get the full match.
2. **Contribute to an IRA**: Depending on eligibility (traditional or Roth), ensure you're investing in tax-advantaged accounts.
3. **Any Additional Funds**: After maxing out tax-advantaged accounts, consider putting funds in a taxable account for flexibility.

### Example Allocation Strategy:
- If you can keep your tax-efficient investments in your 401(k) and IRA (like index funds), consider placing bond funds and dividend-paying stocks in taxable accounts to manage tax implications effectively.

### Regular Rebalancing:
Reassess your allocation regularly to adapt to changes in income, goals, and market conditions.

### Consult a Financial Advisor:
For personalized recommendations tailored to your specific financial situation and goals, consider consulting a financial advisor.
