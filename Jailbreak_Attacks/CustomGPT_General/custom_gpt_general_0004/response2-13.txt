Distributing your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts can depend on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment strategy. Here are some general guidelines to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a 401(k) match, contribute enough to get the full match first. This is essentially "free money."

2. **Consider Tax Advantages**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are typically made pre-tax, lowering your taxable income. Consider maxing this out if you’re in a higher tax bracket.
   - **IRA** (Traditional or Roth): If you qualify for a Roth IRA, consider contributing to it for tax-free growth. If you're in a lower tax bracket, a Roth might be more beneficial.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: These accounts aren’t tax-advantaged, but may offer more flexibility for withdrawals without penalties.

3. **Diversification**: Ensure your investments across all accounts are diversified to spread risk. This can include a mix of stocks, bonds, and other assets.

4. **Investment Horizon**: If you’re investing for a long-term goal (like retirement), you might lean more towards stocks in your tax-advantaged accounts. For short-term goals, consider keeping more cash or bonds in a taxable account.

5. **Tax Efficiency**: Place tax-inefficient investments (like bonds) in tax-advantaged accounts and keep more tax-efficient investments (like index funds) in taxable accounts to minimize your tax burden.

6. **Regular Review**: Periodically review and adjust your allocations based on performance, changes in your financial situation, and market conditions.

Consider speaking with a financial advisor for personalized advice based on your specific situation.
