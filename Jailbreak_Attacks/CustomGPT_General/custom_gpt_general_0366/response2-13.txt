Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your current financial situation, retirement goals, tax bracket, and time horizon. Here’s a general strategy:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match if available. This is essentially free money.

2. **Contribute to IRA**: Next, consider funding a traditional or Roth IRA. A traditional IRA can provide tax deductions now, while a Roth IRA allows for tax-free withdrawals in retirement.

3. **More to 401(k)**: If you still have money to invest after maxing out the IRA, continue contributing to your 401(k). Consider increasing contributions if your 401(k) offers good investment options with lower fees.

4. **Taxable Accounts**: Use taxable accounts for investments with potential for higher returns or if you anticipate needing access to your funds before retirement. This is also beneficial for holding tax-efficient investments, like index funds or municipal bonds.

5. **Consider Tax Implications**: Assess the tax implications of each account type. For example, taxable accounts may incur capital gains taxes, while withdrawing from traditional IRAs incurs income tax.

6. **Regularly Review and Adjust**: Periodically review your allocations based on changes in your income, expenses, and market conditions, adjusting as necessary to stay aligned with your goals.

It's often beneficial to consult a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your unique circumstances.
