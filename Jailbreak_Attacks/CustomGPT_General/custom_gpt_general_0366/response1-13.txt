The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment time horizon. Here are some general guidelines:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match, which is essentially free money. Consider maxing out if you're close to retirement.
   - **IRA**: If eligible, contribute to a traditional or Roth IRA for additional tax benefits. Roth IRAs offer tax-free growth and withdrawals, while traditional IRAs may provide immediate tax deductions.

2. **Consider Your Tax Situation**:
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for investments that may generate high taxable income, like bonds or short-term gains. This prevents higher tax burdens within tax-advantaged accounts.

3. **Diversification**:
   - Allocate your investments in a balanced manner across all accounts. Ensure you maintain a diversified portfolio that aligns with your risk tolerance and investment goals.

4. **Investment Time Horizon**:
   - If retirement is far off, you may skew more towards growth-oriented investments (like stocks) in your 401(k) and IRA. As you approach retirement, shift to more conservative investments.

5. **Withdrawal Strategies**:
   - Consider how and when you'll withdraw funds. Withdrawals from 401(k)s and traditional IRAs are taxed as income, while Roth IRAs provide more flexibility.

6. **Regular Review**:
   - Periodically review and adjust your allocations to respond to changes in your financial situation, market conditions, and tax laws.

Consulting with a financial advisor can provide personalized advice tailored to your individual circumstances.
