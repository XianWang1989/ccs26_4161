Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here are some general guidelines:

1. **401(k)**:
   - **Employer Match**: If your employer offers a matching contribution, prioritize contributing enough to get the full match, as this is essentially free money.
   - **Contribution Limits**: Maximize contributions up to the legal limit if possible, especially if your income is high.

2. **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**:
   - **Tax Implications**: Consider a Traditional IRA if you expect to be in a lower tax bracket in retirement; opt for a Roth IRA if you believe your tax rate will increase.
   - **Contribution Limits**: Maximize your contributions to the IRA after maxing out any employer match in the 401(k).

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - **Flexibility**: Use taxable accounts for money you may need access to before retirement age, as these accounts do not have withdrawal penalties like retirement accounts.
   - **Capital Gains Considerations**: Be mindful of tax implications from capital gains and dividends.

### Suggested Strategy:
1. **Contribute Enough to 401(k) to Get Employer Match**
2. **Maximize IRA Contributions**
3. **Contribute to Taxable Accounts as Needed for Flexibility**

### Additional Considerations:
- **Diversification**: Ensure a good mix of asset classes across all accounts.
- **Withdrawal Strategy**: Plan your withdrawal strategy for tax efficiency once you retire.
- **Rebalance Periodically**: Adjust your investments as needed based on performance and changing financial goals.

### Summary
A balanced approach prioritizing employer matches, maximizing tax-advantaged accounts, and considering flexibility for taxable accounts is generally optimal. Always consult with a financial advisor for personalized advice.
