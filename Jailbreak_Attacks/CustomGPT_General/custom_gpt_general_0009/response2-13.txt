Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts should be based on your individual financial goals, tax situation, and investment horizon. Here’s a general approach to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a matching contribution for your 401(k), prioritize contributions up to that match; it’s essentially free money.

2. **Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to take full advantage of the match. If you can afford to, contribute up to the annual limit.
   - **IRA**: If eligible, consider contributing to a Roth IRA (for tax-free growth) or a traditional IRA (for potential tax deductions). Determine which is more beneficial based on your current and expected future tax rates.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for investments that may have higher capital gains, as they may be taxed at lower rates than ordinary income. Consider holding tax-efficient investments (like index funds or ETFs) in taxable accounts.

4. **Diversification**: Ensure your portfolio across all accounts is diversified based on your risk tolerance and investment goals. 

5. **Periodic Review**: Regularly reassess your strategy based on changes in income, tax laws, or financial goals.

It's advisable to consult with a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your situation.
