Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here’s a general approach:

### 1. **Prioritize Contributions:**
   - **401(k):** Maximize employer match first. This is essentially "free money."
   - **IRA:** Contribute to a traditional or Roth IRA to benefit from tax advantages, depending on your income and tax situation.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** After maximizing contributions to tax-advantaged accounts, consider taxable accounts for additional investments.

### 2. **Tax Considerations:**
   - **401(k):** Pre-tax contributions lower your taxable income now. Consider this if you're in a higher tax bracket.
   - **IRA (Traditional vs. Roth):** A traditional IRA provides tax deductions now, while a Roth IRA offers tax-free withdrawals in retirement. Choose based on expected future tax rates.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Understand capital gains taxes and consider holding investments long-term to minimize taxes.

### 3. **Investment Options:**
   - Within each account type, select investments that align with your risk tolerance and timeline.
   - 401(k) plans may offer limited investment choices.
   - IRAs generally provide a wider range of investment options.

### 4. **Rebalance Regularly:**
   - Periodically review your allocation to ensure it aligns with your risk tolerance and market conditions.

### 5. **Evaluate Your Financial Goals:**
   - Consider short-term vs. long-term needs. Funds needed within five years may be better suited for taxable accounts to avoid early withdrawal penalties.

### General Allocation Strategy:
- **Younger Investors:** Higher allocation to stocks in 401(k) and IRA, with some cash or conservative investments in taxable accounts.
- **Closer to Retirement:** Shift towards more conservative investments, ensuring liquidity in taxable accounts for short-term needs.

### 6. **Consult with a Financial Advisor:**
   - Tailor the strategy based on your individual circumstances, as professionals can offer personalized guidance.

By following these principles, you can optimize your money distribution among the different account types to maximize growth and minimize tax burdens over time.
