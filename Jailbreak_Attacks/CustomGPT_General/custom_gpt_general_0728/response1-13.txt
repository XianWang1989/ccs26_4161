The best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts largely depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and timeline for accessing the funds. Here are some general guidelines:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match**
   - If your employer offers a 401(k) match, contribute enough to get the full match, as it's essentially free money.

### 2. **Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**
   - After maximizing the 401(k) match, consider prioritizing contributions to an IRA (either Traditional or Roth, depending on your eligibility and tax situation). These accounts provide tax benefits that can grow your investments more efficiently.

### 3. **Tax Considerations**
   - **401(k):** Contributions lower your taxable income (Traditional) or provide tax-free growth (Roth) if you meet certain conditions.
   - **IRA:** Similar benefits as a 401(k); Roth IRAs can be particularly advantageous if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Investments here are subject to capital gains taxes, so it's often better to hold more aggressive investments in tax-advantaged accounts to defer or reduce capital gains tax.

### 4. **Diversification & Rebalancing**
   - Ensure that your investments are diversified across accounts and regularly rebalance to align with your risk tolerance and investment strategy.

### 5. **Withdrawal Strategies**
   - Plan how you’ll withdraw funds in retirement. Taxable accounts may be more flexible, while tax-advantaged accounts may have age restrictions and penalties for early withdrawals.

It's often beneficial to consult with a financial advisor to create a strategy that reflects your specific financial situation and goals.
