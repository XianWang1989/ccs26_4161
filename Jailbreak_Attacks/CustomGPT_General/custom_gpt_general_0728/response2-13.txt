Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various factors, including your income, tax bracket, investment goals, and time horizon. Here are some general strategies to consider:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**
   - **Contribution**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match. This is essentially free money.

### 2. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**
   - **IRA**: If you're eligible, consider contributing to a Roth IRA or a traditional IRA, depending on your tax situation. Roth IRAs offer tax-free withdrawals in retirement, while traditional IRAs provide tax deductions on contributions.
   - **401(k)**: Continue contributing up to the contribution limit after securing the employer match.

### 3. **Consider Your Current and Future Tax Bracket**
   - **Roth vs. Traditional**: If you expect your income to increase significantly, a Roth IRA might be beneficial to lock in your current tax rate.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for funds you may need in the short term or for investments that are more tax-efficient.

### 4. **Diversify Assets**
   - **401(k)/IRA**: Typically invest in a mix of stocks, bonds, and mutual funds. Aim for a diverse portfolio that aligns with your risk tolerance.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Consider tax-efficient funds like index funds or ETFs to minimize capital gains taxes.

### 5. **Long-Term vs. Short-Term Goals**
   - **401(k)/IRA**: Use these for long-term retirement savings.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: This can be for medium- or short-term goals, such as buying a house or funding education.

### 6. **Regular Reviews and Adjustments**
   - Monitor your investment performance and adjust your allocations based on changing goals, market conditions, and life events.

### Example Allocation Strategy
- **401(k)**: 25% of total savings (up to employer match)
- **IRA**: 15% of total savings
- **Taxable Account**: 10% of total savings
- Adjust as necessary each year based on contributions and market performance.

Ultimately, it may be beneficial to consult with a financial advisor to tailor the strategy specifically to your individual financial situation.
