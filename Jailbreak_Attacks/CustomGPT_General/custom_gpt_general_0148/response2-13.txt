To optimize your investment strategy across your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**: Prioritize contributions to your 401(k) and IRA, as these accounts offer tax benefits. In a 401(k), contributions are made pre-tax, which can lower your taxable income. With a Traditional IRA, you also may receive tax deductions depending on your income, while a Roth IRA allows for tax-free withdrawals in retirement.

2. **Consider Contribution Limits**: Be aware of the contribution limits for each account type. For 2023, the 401(k) limit is $22,500 (or $30,000 if you're over 50), while IRAs allow a maximum contribution of $6,500 (or $7,500 if you're over 50).

3. **Asset Allocation**: Allocate your investments based on the type of account. For your tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) and IRA), consider holding long-term growth investments like stocks. In taxable accounts, it's often beneficial to hold more tax-efficient investments like index funds or ETFs, since they generate fewer taxable events.

4. **Tax Considerations**: Be mindful of the tax implications of your investment choices. Taxable accounts can be affected by capital gains taxes, so consider holding bonds and income-generating assets in tax-advantaged accounts, while placing more growth-oriented investments in taxable accounts to defer taxes.

5. **Rebalancing**: Regularly review and rebalance your portfolio across all accounts to maintain your desired asset allocation and respond to market changes.

6. **Future Withdrawals**: Consider your future withdrawal strategy. If you anticipate needing funds before retirement, keep funds more liquid and accessible in your taxable accounts, while maximizing tax-advantaged accounts for longer-term growth.

Each individual's financial situation is unique, so it might also be beneficial to consult a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your specific goals and risk tolerance.
