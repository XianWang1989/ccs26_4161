Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts should depend on several factors, including your income, tax situation, retirement goals, and investment strategy. Here’s a general approach:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**:
   - Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match. This is essentially free money.

2. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - After the match, consider maximizing contributions to your IRA (traditional or Roth, depending on your eligibility). Roth IRAs offer tax-free growth and withdrawals.

3. **Increase 401(k) Contributions**:
   - If you still have capacity, increase your contributions to your 401(k), especially if you can benefit from tax-deferred growth.

4. **Utilize Taxable Accounts**:
   - After fully funding tax-advantaged accounts, consider taxable accounts for investments, particularly if you want liquidity or access before retirement age.

5. **Diversification**:
   - Diversify your investments across these accounts to balance risk and return.

6. **Review and Adjust**:
   - Regularly review your asset allocation and adjust based on changes in income, expenses, and market conditions.

7. **Consult a Financial Advisor**:
   - For personalized advice and to navigate specific tax implications, working with a financial advisor is beneficial.

This balanced strategy aims to optimize growth while managing tax liabilities effectively.
