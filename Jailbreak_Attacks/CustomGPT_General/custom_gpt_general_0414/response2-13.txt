Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your current financial situation, retirement goals, and tax considerations. Here’s a strategic approach:

### 1. **Maximize Matching Contributions**
   - **401(k):** If your employer offers a matching contribution, contribute enough to get the full match. This is essentially free money.

### 2. **Contribute to IRAs**
   - **Traditional IRA or Roth IRA:** Depending on your income and tax situation, contribute to an IRA. A Roth IRA allows for tax-free withdrawals in retirement, while a Traditional IRA may provide tax-deductible contributions.

### 3. **Evaluate Tax Implications**
   - **Taxable Accounts:** After maximizing your 401(k) and IRA contributions, consider taxable accounts for additional savings. This can be beneficial for short-term goals or when you exceed contribution limits.

### 4. **Consider Investment Vehicles**
   - **Asset Allocation:** Use tax-advantaged accounts for bonds and high-growth assets in taxable accounts to minimize taxes on dividends and capital gains.

### 5. **Review and Adjust Periodically**
   - **Rebalance:** Regularly review your portfolio to ensure it aligns with your risk tolerance and investment goals.

### Example Distribution Strategy:
- **401(k):** 10-15% of your salary, enough to get the full match.
- **IRA:** Max out contributions (currently $6,500 annually, or $7,500 if over age 50).
- **Taxable Accounts:** Allocate remaining funds based on personal savings goals and timelines.

### Conclusion
Tailor your contributions based on your financial situation, and consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized advice.
