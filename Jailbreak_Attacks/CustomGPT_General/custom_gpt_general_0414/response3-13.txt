The best approach to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts involves considering various factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here’s a recommended strategy:

### 1. **Prioritize Contributions**
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match first, as this is essentially "free money."
   - **IRA**: Contribute to a traditional or Roth IRA to take advantage of tax benefits.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: After maxing out tax-advantaged accounts, use taxable accounts for additional investments.

### 2. **Diversification and Asset Allocation**
   - Ensure a mix of asset classes (stocks, bonds, real estate) based on your risk tolerance.
   - Keep higher-risk investments in tax-advantaged accounts, while using taxable accounts for more conservative investments to manage taxes.

### 3. **Tax Efficiency**
   - **Tax-Deferred Accounts (401(k), Traditional IRA)**: Focus on bonds and high-yield investments here to defer taxes.
   - **Roth IRA**: Ideal for growth investments, as qualified withdrawals are tax-free.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Use index funds or ETFs to minimize capital gains taxes.

### 4. **Withdrawal Order**
   - In retirement, start withdrawals from taxable accounts first (to take advantage of lower tax rates or capital gains), followed by tax-deferred (401(k), IRA) accounts, and finally Roth accounts.

### 5. **Review Regularly**
   - Adjust your allocation based on changes in your financial goals, market conditions, and life events.

### Example Allocation
- **401(k)**: 15-20%
- **IRA**: 10-15%
- **Taxable Accounts**: Remaining balance (adjusting monthly contributions based on returns and expenses).

### Conclusion
Regularly reassess your financial situation and make adjustments as needed, ideally with a financial advisor to tailor your strategy to your specific goals.
