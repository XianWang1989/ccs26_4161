The best way to distribute your money between a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some general guidelines to consider:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: If your employer offers a matching contribution, prioritize contributing enough to get the full match, as this is essentially free money. Consider maximizing your contributions if you can, since 401(k) contributions are pre-tax and can reduce your taxable income.
   - **IRA**: After your 401(k), consider contributing to a traditional IRA or a Roth IRA, based on your eligibility and tax situation. A Roth IRA allows for tax-free withdrawals in retirement, while a traditional IRA offers immediate tax deductions. 

2. **Prioritize Debt Reduction**:
   - If you have high-interest debt, like credit card debt, consider using available funds to pay it down before contributing heavily to investment accounts.

3. **Allocate to Taxable Accounts**:
   - Once you have maxed out your contributions to your 401(k) and IRA, consider investing in taxable accounts. This may provide more flexibility in terms of withdrawals, but remember that you will be responsible for taxes on dividends, interest, and capital gains each year.

4. **Diversify Investments**:
   - In your 401(k) and IRA, you might want to focus on a diversified mix of stocks, bonds, and other assets. In taxable accounts, consider focusing on tax-efficient investments, such as index funds or municipal bonds, to minimize tax liabilities.

5. **Regularly Review and Rebalance**:
   - Periodically review your portfolio allocation and rebalance if necessary to ensure that it aligns with your risk tolerance and investment goals.

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**:
   - Given the complexity of retirement planning and tax strategies, it might be beneficial to speak with a financial advisor who can provide personalized guidance based on your unique financial situation.

Always consider your financial goals and timeline and adjust your contributions accordingly.
