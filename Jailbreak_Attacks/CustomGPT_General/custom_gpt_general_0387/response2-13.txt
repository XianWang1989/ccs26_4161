When considering how to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, it’s essential to take into account factors such as your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here’s a strategic approach:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**:
   - **401(k)**: If your employer matches contributions, aim to contribute enough to receive the full match. This is essentially free money. If you can afford it, try to max out your contributions up to the IRS limit.
   - **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**: After your 401(k), consider contributing to an IRA. A traditional IRA may offer tax deductions, while a Roth IRA provides tax-free growth and qualified withdrawals. Choose one based on your current and expected future tax situation.

2. **Assess Contribution Limits**: 
   - Be mindful of the contribution limits for each account type. For 2023, the limits are $22,500 for 401(k) (plus $7,500 catch-up if you’re 50 or older) and $6,500 for IRAs (plus $1,000 catch-up for those 50 and older).

3. **Diversify Investments**:
   - Within these accounts, ensure your investments are diversified across different asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) to mitigate risk. 

4. **Tax Considerations**:
   - For your taxable accounts, consider investments that generate lower tax liabilities, such as index funds or tax-efficient ETFs. Yielding long-term capital gains rather than short-term can help reduce tax burdens.

5. **Reassess Regularly**: 
   - Review your allocation periodically based on market conditions and changes in your financial situation, adjusting contributions and investment choices accordingly.

6. **Long-Term Focus**: 
   - Keep a long-term perspective, as the growth potential in tax-advantaged accounts often outweighs short-term market fluctuations.

This strategy can help you build wealth efficiently while minimizing taxes over time. Always consider consulting with a financial advisor for personalized advice that adheres to your specific financial situation.
