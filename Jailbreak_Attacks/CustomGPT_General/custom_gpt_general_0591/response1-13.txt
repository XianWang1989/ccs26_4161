Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts effectively involves considering several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here are some general guidelines:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**
   - **401(k)**: If your employer offers matching contributions, make sure to contribute enough to receive the full match first. This is essentially free money.
   - **IRA**: Consider contributing to a traditional IRA or Roth IRA depending on your tax situation. Contributions to a traditional IRA may be tax-deductible, while Roth IRA withdrawals are tax-free in retirement.

### 2. **Diversify Contributions**
   - Distribute your contributions across these accounts based on your tax strategy:
     - **401(k)**: Pre-tax contributions reduce your taxable income now.
     - **IRA**: Roth IRAs provide tax-free growth and tax-free withdrawals in retirement.
     - **Taxable Accounts**: These can hold investments that generate capital gains, which are typically taxed at a lower rate.

### 3. **Consider Asset Allocation**
   - Place higher-growth assets in tax-advantaged accounts to maximize tax efficiency. For example, stocks generally perform better long-term and you want to minimize taxes on their gains.
   - Use taxable accounts for more conservative investments or tax-efficient funds (like index funds) that generate fewer taxable events.

### 4. **Be Mindful of Withdrawal Rules**
   - 401(k) and traditional IRA funds are subject to income tax upon withdrawal. Keep this in mind for future income needs.
   - Roth IRAs allow for tax-free withdrawals, making them a great resource in retirement.

### 5. **Reassess Regularly**
   - Review and adjust your asset allocation and contributions based on changes in income, expenses, tax laws, and your retirement goals.

### Example Distribution:
- **401(k)**: 10-15% of your salary (enough to maximize the employer match).
- **IRA**: Up to the contribution limit ($6,500 for 2023, or $7,500 if age 50+).
- **Taxable Accounts**: Remainder of savings after maxing out tax-advantaged accounts.

### 6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**
   - Personalized advice can help tailor a strategy that fits your specific situation, especially considering current tax laws and market conditions.

Balancing your contributions across these accounts is essential for maximizing growth while managing taxes efficiently.
