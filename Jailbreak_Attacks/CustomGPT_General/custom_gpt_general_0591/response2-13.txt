Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts requires a balanced approach based on your financial goals, risk tolerance, and time horizon. Here’s a breakdown of how to consider each type of account:

### 1. **401(k)**
   - **Employer Match:** Always contribute enough to get the full employer match, as this is essentially "free money."
   - **Tax-Deferred Growth:** Contributions are pre-tax (traditional 401(k)) or post-tax (Roth 401(k)), allowing for tax-deferred growth.
   - **Investment Options:** Typically limited to the plan's selected investment options. Choose a diversified mix of stocks, bonds, and funds.

### 2. **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**
   - **Tax Efficiency:** Traditional IRAs offer tax-deferred growth, while Roth IRAs provide tax-free withdrawals in retirement.
   - **Contribution Limits:** Be mindful of annual contribution limits (as of 2023, it’s $6,500 for those under 50, and $7,500 for those 50+).
   - **Investment Flexibility:** Generally more options than a 401(k). Invest in a wide array of assets.

### 3. **Taxable Accounts**
   - **Flexibility:** No contribution limits or withdrawal penalties, making them great for short to medium-term goals.
   - **Capital Gains Taxes:** Be aware of capital gains tax implications; holding investments for over a year can lower these taxes.
   - **Asset Location:** Consider holding investments that generate capital gains (like index funds) here, rather than high-dividend stocks, to minimize tax impact.

### **General Strategy**
- **Prioritize Contributions:**
  1. **Max out the employer match in your 401(k).**
  2. **Contribute to an IRA to benefit from tax advantages.**
  3. **Consider your taxable account for additional savings after maxing out tax-advantaged accounts.**

- **Asset Allocation:**
  - Adjust your asset allocation based on your age, risk tolerance, and investment goals. Younger investors might favor equity-heavy portfolios, while those closer to retirement may prioritize safer investments.

- **Diversification:** Ensure you have a mix of assets across all accounts to spread risk.

- **Rebalance Periodically:** Regularly review and adjust your allocations based on market performance and your financial situation.

### **Consult a Financial Advisor**
For personalized advice that considers your unique situation, it's a good idea to consult a financial advisor.

By following these guidelines, you can create a balanced approach to distributing your money across your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts based on your individual needs.
