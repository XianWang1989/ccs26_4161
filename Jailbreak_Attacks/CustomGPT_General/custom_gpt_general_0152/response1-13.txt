Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment strategy. Here’s a general approach to consider:

### 1. **Understand Account Types:**
   - **401(k):** Tax-deferred retirement account, often employer-sponsored, with possible matching contributions. Contributions reduce your taxable income.
   - **IRA:** Individual Retirement Account, which can be traditional (tax-deferred) or Roth (after-tax contributions with tax-free growth).
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Investment accounts where you pay taxes on interest, dividends, and capital gains.

### 2. **Prioritize Contributions:**
   - **Max Out Employer Match:** Contribute enough to your 401(k) to take full advantage of any employer match, as this is essentially free money.
   - **Max Out IRA Contributions:** If eligible, contribute to your IRA, especially if it’s a Roth IRA for tax-free growth.
   - **Beyond Limits:** After maximizing the above, any extra savings can go into your 401(k) or taxable accounts based on your strategy.

### 3. **Consider Tax Implications:**
   - Place higher-yield investments (like bonds) in tax-deferred accounts (401(k), IRA) to minimize tax impact.
   - Keep tax-efficient investments (like index funds) in taxable accounts to reduce capital gains taxes.

### 4. **Assess Investment Goals and Time Horizon:**
   - Align your investment strategy with your goals (retirement, down payment, etc.) and time horizon.
   - Longer timeframes allow for more risk in growth-oriented assets, while shorter timeframes may necessitate a conservative approach.

### 5. **Regularly Review and Adjust:**
   - Monitor your portfolio performance and reallocate as necessary based on changes in your life circumstances, financial goals, and market conditions.

### Example Strategy:
- **Age 30:** Contribute 15% to 401(k) (max out to employer match), $6,000 to IRA, and any excess to a taxable account.
- **Age 50:** Increase contributions to retirement accounts as income grows and focus on tax-efficient strategies.

### Conclusion:
Consulting a financial advisor to tailor a plan specific to your situation is wise, but following this framework can help guide your investment distribution effectively.
