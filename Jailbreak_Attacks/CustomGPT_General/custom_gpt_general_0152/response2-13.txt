Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here’s a general strategy you can consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k):**
   - Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match. This is essentially free money.

2. **Contribute to an IRA:**
   - If eligible, contribute to a traditional or Roth IRA. This offers tax advantages that can help grow your savings more efficiently. The choice between traditional and Roth depends on your current vs. expected future tax rate.

3. **Continue 401(k) Contributions:**
   - After maximizing your IRA contributions, keep contributing to your 401(k) up to the limit. This is particularly beneficial if you are in a high tax bracket.

4. **Use Taxable Accounts for Flexibility:**
   - If you have excess funds after maxing out tax-advantaged accounts, consider a taxable account. This provides flexibility for withdrawals and investment options, but be mindful of capital gains taxes.

5. **Consider Your Time Horizon:**
   - Invest in a mix of growth-oriented assets (stocks) in accounts where you expect to hold for a long time, and more conservative investments (bonds) in accounts where you may need liquidity sooner.

6. **Rebalance and Review:**
   - Regularly review your allocation. As your financial situation or market conditions change, be prepared to adjust your distributions accordingly.

7. **Tax-Efficiency:**
   - Consider placing investments that generate high taxable income (like bonds) in tax-advantaged accounts and holding investments with lower yields (like stocks) in taxable accounts for tax efficiency.

Each person's situation is unique, so it may be beneficial to consult with a financial advisor to tailor a strategy that meets your specific needs and goals.
