Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts can depend on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment horizon. Here are some general guidelines:

1. **401(k)**: If your employer offers a 401(k) match, prioritize contributions to at least the amount that secures the full match, as this is essentially free money. Contributions are made pre-tax, which lowers your taxable income.

2. **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**: After maximizing your 401(k) match, consider contributing to an IRA. A Traditional IRA allows for tax-deductible contributions, while a Roth IRA features tax-free withdrawals in retirement. Choose based on your current tax rate versus expected tax rate in retirement.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: Once you've made contributions to your 401(k) and IRA, you can start investing in a taxable brokerage account. This allows for more flexibility in accessing funds without penalties for early withdrawal, though you will need to pay capital gains taxes on profits.

**General Strategy**:
- Prioritize contributions to tax-advantaged accounts first (401(k) then IRA).
- If you have additional funds available after maxing out retirement accounts, invest in a taxable account.
- Consider the investment options available in each type of account and tailor your asset allocation accordingly.

**Always consider consulting with a financial advisor** to tailor a strategy that best fits your individual circumstances and goals.
