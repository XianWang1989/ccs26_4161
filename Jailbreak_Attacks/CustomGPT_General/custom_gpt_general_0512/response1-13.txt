The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, current income, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here are some general guidelines to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match:** If your employer offers a matching contribution in your 401(k), contribute enough to take full advantage of that match first. It's essentially free money.

2. **Focus on Tax-Advantaged Accounts:** After maximizing your employer match, consider maximizing contributions to your IRA (Traditional or Roth, depending on your eligibility and tax situation). Both accounts offer tax benefits that can help your money grow.

3. **Consider Your Tax Bracket:** If you're in a higher tax bracket, it might be beneficial to contribute more to tax-deferred accounts like a 401(k) or Traditional IRA. If you're in a lower bracket and expect to be in a higher one during retirement, you might benefit more from a Roth IRA.

4. **Diversify Your Accounts:** Maintain a balance between tax-deferred (401(k) and Traditional IRA), tax-free (Roth IRA), and taxable accounts. This diversification can provide flexibility in retirement when managing your income and taxes.

5. **Assess Liquidity Needs:** Funds in taxable accounts can be accessed more easily without penalties compared to retirement accounts. If you anticipate needing funds before retirement, consider maintaining a certain amount in taxable accounts.

6. **Rebalance Periodically:** Review and rebalance your portfolio within each account to ensure it aligns with your investment strategy and risk tolerance.

It's always a good idea to consult with a financial advisor to tailor a strategy that suits your individual situation and long-term objectives.
