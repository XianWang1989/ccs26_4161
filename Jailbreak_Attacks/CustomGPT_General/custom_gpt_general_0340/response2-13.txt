To effectively distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**: Prioritize contributions to your 401(k) and IRA first. These accounts offer tax benefits that can significantly enhance your savings over time. For your 401(k), aim to contribute enough to take full advantage of any employer match, as this is essentially "free money."

2. **Understand Contribution Limits**: Ensure you’re aware of the annual contribution limits for each account type. For 2023, for example, the 401(k) limit is $22,500 ($30,000 if you're age 50 or older), while the IRA limit is $6,500 ($7,500 if you're 50 or older). 

3. **Diversify Investments**: Within each account, diversify your investments across different asset classes (stocks, bonds, real estate, etc.) to manage risk. Index funds or ETFs can provide broad market exposure with lower fees.

4. **Consider Tax Implications**: 
   - **401(k)**: Traditional 401(k) contributions are pre-tax, potentially reducing your taxable income now, while withdrawals in retirement are taxed. A Roth 401(k) allows for tax-free withdrawals if conditions are met. 
   - **IRA**: Similar to the 401(k), a traditional IRA offers pre-tax benefits but taxes on withdrawal. A Roth IRA allows for tax-free growth and withdrawals.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Investments in these accounts are taxed annually on capital gains and dividends. This makes them suitable for investments that generate lower turnover or those that can be held long-term.

5. **Emergency Fund**: Ensure you have a sufficient emergency fund in a liquid savings account before maximizing contributions to retirement accounts. Generally, aim for 3-6 months of expenses.

6. **Rebalance Regularly**: Regularly review and adjust your allocation based on changes in your financial situation, retirement goals, and market conditions.

7. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: Consider consulting a financial advisor to create a tailored strategy based on your specific financial goals, risk tolerance, and time horizon.

By strategically managing contributions and investments across these accounts, you can optimize your overall financial growth while minimizing taxes.
