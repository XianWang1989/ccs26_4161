Distributing your money amongst a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here’s a general guide:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**
   - **401(k) Contribution**: If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to get the full match first. This is essentially free money.
   - **IRA Contribution**: After your 401(k), consider contributing to a traditional IRA or Roth IRA (depending on your income level and tax situation).

### 2. **Choose Investment Types Wisely**
   - **401(k)**: Best for long-term investments in equities or mutual funds. The investments grow tax-deferred.
   - **IRA**: Similar to a 401(k), but give attention to your tax situation (Roth for tax-free withdrawals in retirement vs. traditional for tax-deductible contributions).
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Use this for investments that generate capital gains or interest income. Consider tax-efficient investments like index funds, ETFs, or tax-exempt municipal bonds.

### 3. **Consider Your Time Horizon**
   - **Short-Term Goals**: Funds needed within 1-5 years may be better in a taxable account for easier access.
   - **Long-Term Goals**: Focus more on 401(k) and IRAs for tax advantages.

### 4. **Review Your Tax Situation**
   - Evaluate your current and expected future tax brackets. Tax-efficient withdrawals from tax-advantaged accounts can influence your strategy.

### 5. **Rebalance Regularly**
   - Adjust your investments periodically to ensure your asset allocation aligns with your risk tolerance and goals.

### 6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**
   - Personalized advice can be invaluable to create a strategy tailored to your specific situation.

By prioritizing contributions to tax-advantaged accounts while maintaining a flexible investment strategy in taxable accounts, you can optimize your financial growth and tax efficiency.
