Distributing your money effectively among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here’s a general approach to consider:

### 1. Prioritize Contributions
- **401(k) Contributions**: If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to get the full match first—this is effectively free money.
- **IRA Contributions**: Next, consider maxing out contributions to a traditional or Roth IRA, depending on your income level and tax situation.
- **Taxable Accounts**: After maxing out tax-advantaged accounts, allocate additional funds to taxable accounts.

### 2. Tax Considerations
- **401(k)**: Contributions reduce your taxable income, but withdrawals are taxed as ordinary income.
- **IRA**: Traditional IRAs provide tax-deferred growth, while Roth IRAs allow for tax-free withdrawals in retirement. Choose based on your current vs. expected future tax rates.
- **Taxable Accounts**: Be mindful of capital gains taxes when selling investments; holding for more than a year may result in lower tax rates.

### 3. Investment Strategy
- **401(k)**: Focus on a diversified mix of mutual funds or ETFs with options available in your plan.
- **IRA**: This account offers a wider range of investment choices, including individual stocks, bonds, and mutual funds.
- **Taxable Accounts**: Consider tax-efficient investments, such as index funds or tax-managed funds, to minimize tax liability.

### 4. Rebalancing
Regularly review and rebalance your portfolio across all accounts to ensure alignment with your risk tolerance and investment goals.

### 5. Emergency Fund
Before heavily investing, ensure you have an emergency fund of 3-6 months' worth of expenses in a liquid account.

### 6. Consult a Financial Advisor
Consider speaking with a financial advisor to tailor your strategy based on your unique financial situation.

This approach can help you effectively manage your investments while minimizing taxes and maximizing growth potential.
