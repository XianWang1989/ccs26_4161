Balancing your investments among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some strategies to consider:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match (essentially free money). If you're under 50, the 2023 limit is $22,500; if over, it's $30,000.
   - **IRA**: After the 401(k), consider maxing out an IRA (traditional or Roth). The limit for 2023 is $6,500 (or $7,500 if you're over 50).

### 2. **Diversify Investments**
   - Spread your investments across asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) in each account but consider their tax implications.
   - Place tax-efficient investments (like index funds or ETFs) in taxable accounts to minimize tax liabilities.

### 3. **Consider Your Tax Situation**
   - **Traditional 401(k)/IRA**: Contributions are pre-tax, reducing taxable income, but withdrawals are taxed. Suitable if you expect to be in a lower tax bracket during retirement.
   - **Roth Accounts**: Contributions are made post-tax, but withdrawals are tax-free in retirement. Ideal if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket later.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Useful for liquidity and flexibility but be mindful of capital gains taxes.

### 4. **Emergency Fund**
   - Ensure you have an emergency fund before heavily investing, typically 3-6 months of living expenses in a savings account.

### 5. **Revisit and Rebalance**
   - Review your allocations periodically (at least annually) to ensure they align with your goals and risk tolerance.

### Conclusion
Tailor your strategy based on your unique circumstances, considering factors like age, retirement goals, and tax brackets. Consulting with a financial advisor can provide personalized guidance.
