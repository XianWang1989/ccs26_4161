Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here’s a guideline to help you decide:

### 1. **Prioritize Contributions**  
   - **401(k)**: If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to get the full match first. This is effectively “free money.”
   - **IRA**: After maximizing the match, consider contributing to an IRA (Roth or traditional) for tax-advantaged growth.

### 2. **Consider Tax Implications**  
   - **401(k) and Traditional IRA**: Contributions are typically tax-deductible, but withdrawals in retirement are taxed as ordinary income.
   - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, but qualified withdrawals are tax-free in retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Interest, dividends, and capital gains are taxed in the year they’re realized. Consider holding long-term investments here to benefit from lower capital gains rates.

### 3. **Assess Your Investment Horizon**  
   - **Short-term Goals**: If you need the money in under five years, keep it in safer investments like cash or bonds, possibly in a taxable account for liquidity.
   - **Long-term Goals**: For goals more than five years away, consider a more aggressive allocation with stocks, potentially maximized in tax-advantaged accounts first.

### 4. **Balance Your Allocations**  
   - Maintain a diversified portfolio across all accounts. If you have a specific allocation strategy (e.g., 60% stocks, 40% bonds), apply it uniformly, but take tax implications into account.
   - Place more tax-inefficient investments (like bonds or REITs) in tax-advantaged accounts to minimize taxable income.

### 5. **Review and Adjust Regularly**  
   - Revisit your allocations periodically, especially as your income, financial goals, or market conditions change.

### Conclusion  
A balanced approach maximizing tax advantages and tailoring your asset allocation to your personal goals is typically best. Consulting with a financial advisor can provide personalized strategies tailored to your specific situation.
