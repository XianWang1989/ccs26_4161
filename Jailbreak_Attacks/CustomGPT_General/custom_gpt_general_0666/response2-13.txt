Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some strategies to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match if available. This is essentially free money.

2. **Consider Tax Benefits**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are typically pre-tax, lowering your taxable income.
   - **IRA**: Traditional IRAs may offer tax-deductible contributions, while Roth IRAs provide tax-free growth and withdrawals.

3. **Diversify Investments**: Allocate your investments based on risk tolerance:
   - **401(k)**: Choose a mix of stock and bond funds or target-date funds based on your retirement timeline.
   - **IRA**: Similar allocation as above, but you may have a wider range of investment options.
   - **Taxable accounts**: Focus on tax-efficient investments (e.g., index funds, ETFs).

4. **Emergency Fund**: Ensure you have an emergency fund outside these accounts, typically in a savings account.

5. **Tax Considerations**: When withdrawing, consider:
   - 401(k) and traditional IRA withdrawals are taxed as ordinary income.
   - Roth IRA withdrawals are tax-free if qualified.
   - Taxable accounts may incur capital gains taxes, so consider holding investments long-term to benefit from lower rates.

6. **Review Frequently**: Monitor your allocations and adjust based on life changes, market conditions, or changes in financial goals.

It's often beneficial to consult a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your specific situation.
