Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment horizon. Here are some tips:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Contributions**:
   - Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money.

### 2. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are pre-tax, reducing your taxable income. Consider maxing out contributions if you’re in a higher tax bracket.
   - **IRA**: Traditional IRAs offer tax deductions; Roth IRAs provide tax-free growth on withdrawals. Choose based on your current and expected future tax situation.

### 3. **Consider Investment Options**:
   - Determine if your 401(k) has high fees or limited investment options. If so, you might favor IRAs for their flexibility and potentially lower costs.

### 4. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**:
   - Use taxable accounts for short-term savings or investments. They offer flexibility in withdrawals and can be a strategic place for tax-efficient investments.

### 5. **Diversify Investments**:
   - Ensure your portfolio is diversified across different asset classes (stocks, bonds) to manage risk effectively.

### 6. **Rebalance Regularly**:
   - Monitor and adjust your asset allocation periodically to stay aligned with your risk tolerance and financial goals.

### 7. **Consult a Financial Advisor**:
   - Consider speaking with a financial advisor for personalized strategies based on your specific situation and long-term objectives.

By following these guidelines, you can create a balanced and efficient approach to distributing your money across these accounts.
