Distributing your money across a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and time horizon. Here are some general guidelines:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match, as this is essentially free money.

2. **IRA Contributions**: If eligible, consider contributing to an IRA (Traditional or Roth). Roth IRAs are beneficial if you expect your tax rate to be higher in retirement, while Traditional IRAs offer tax deductions now.

3. **Debt and Emergency Fund**: Ensure you have an emergency fund in a savings account and manage high-interest debt before heavily investing.

4. **Tax Considerations**:
   - **Tax-Deferred Accounts**: Use 401(k) and Traditional IRA for higher income years to reduce taxable income.
   - **After-Tax Contributions**: Roth IRA contributions grow tax-free, making them advantageous for long-term growth.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: These are flexible and useful for shorter-term goals, but be mindful of capital gains taxes.

5. **Investment Strategy**: Diversify investments based on your risk tolerance. Generally, equity investments should make up a larger portion for long-term growth, especially in tax-advantaged accounts.

6. **Adjusting Contributions**: Regularly review and adjust your contributions based on changes in your financial situation, goals, and market conditions.

7. **Seek Professional Advice**: Consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized strategies tailored to your specific situation.

This balance can maximize growth while minimizing tax liabilities over time.
