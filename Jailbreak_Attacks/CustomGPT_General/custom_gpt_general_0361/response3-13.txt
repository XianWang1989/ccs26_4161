Distributing your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your retirement goals, tax situation, and investment strategy. Here’s a breakdown of how to approach this:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**
   - **401(k):** Contribute enough to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money. Aim to maximize your contributions if you can.
   - **IRA:** After your 401(k), contribute to a Traditional or Roth IRA. A Roth IRA offers tax-free withdrawals in retirement, while a Traditional IRA provides tax deductions now.

### 2. **Emergency Fund**
   - Ensure you have a liquid emergency fund (3-6 months of expenses) in a savings account before putting too much in taxable accounts.

### 3. **Taxable Accounts**
   - Use taxable accounts for investment vehicles that may benefit from lower long-term capital gains rates. Consider holding investments like index funds, ETFs, or tax-efficient funds here for better tax treatment.

### 4. **Debt Management**
   - If you have high-interest debt (like credit cards), prioritize paying that off before maximizing investments.

### 5. **Consider Your Tax Bracket**
   - Strategize contributions based on your current vs. expected future tax brackets. If you anticipate being in a higher tax bracket in retirement, consider Roth contributions.

### 6. **Investment Strategy**
   - Align your investment goals with your time horizon. Consider more aggressive investments in tax-deferred accounts, as they can grow without immediate tax implications.

### Sample Distribution Strategy:
- **401(k):** 15-20% of your income, up to the match limit.
- **IRA:** Up to the contribution limit after maximizing your 401(k) match.
- **Taxable Accounts:** Any remaining funds after maxing these accounts will go here, particularly if you have specific short-to-medium-term goals.

Always review your strategy regularly, considering changes in income, expenses, or financial goals. Consulting with a financial advisor can also provide personalized insights.
