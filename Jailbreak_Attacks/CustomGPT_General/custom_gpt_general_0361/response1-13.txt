Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and time horizon. Here’s a general approach to consider:

### 1. **Prioritize Retirement Accounts**
   - **Maximize Employer Match**: Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to take full advantage of any employer match. This is essentially free money.
   - **IRA Contributions**: If you're eligible, contribute to a traditional or Roth IRA. Roth IRAs are especially beneficial for tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement.

### 2. **Consider Tax Implications**
   - **Tax-Deferred Accounts**: Both 401(k) and traditional IRAs allow for tax-deferred growth, which can be advantageous if you expect to be in a lower tax bracket in retirement.
   - **Roth Accounts**: Contributions occur after-tax, but withdrawals are tax-free in retirement, making them ideal if you expect higher taxes later.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: These are more flexible and useful for short- to medium-term goals, but they are subject to capital gains taxes.

### 3. **Think About Investment Options**
   - **401(k)**: Often offers a limited selection of investments. Choose low-cost index funds or target-date funds.
   - **IRAs**: Typically provide a wider array of investment choices, allowing for better diversification.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Ideal for more flexible investment strategies and accessing funds without penalties before retirement age.

### 4. **Decide on Your Risk Tolerance**
   - Allocate your investment based on your comfort with risk. Generally, younger individuals may favor equities in retirement accounts, while older individuals might shift towards bonds.

### 5. **Regularly Rebalance**
   - Monitor your investments and adjust as necessary, ensuring your allocation aligns with your financial goals.

### Sample Allocation
- **401(k)**: 15% of your income to maximize, taking into account the employer match.
- **IRA**: $6,000 annually (or $7,000 if over 50) into a Roth IRA for tax-free growth.
- **Taxable Account**: Any additional savings can go here for flexibility.

### Conclusion
Evaluate your goals, age, tax situation, and risk tolerance to tailor your allocations effectively. Consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized advice.
