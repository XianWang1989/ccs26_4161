The best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts largely depends on your individual financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here are some general guidelines to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a 401(k) match, contribute enough to take full advantage of this benefit first. It’s essentially “free money.”

2. **Consider Tax Advantages**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are typically pre-tax, reducing your taxable income now. However, you'll owe taxes on withdrawals in retirement.
   - **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**: Traditional IRAs offer tax-deferred growth, while Roth IRAs allow for tax-free withdrawals in retirement (if certain conditions are met). If you qualify, consider maxing out a Roth IRA for tax-free growth.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: These accounts are more flexible and do not have contribution limits, but you'll pay capital gains taxes on profits. They can be beneficial for short- to mid-term goals.

3. **Investment Strategy**: Allocate your investments based on your risk tolerance and investment horizon. For long-term accounts (401(k), IRA), you might invest more heavily in stocks. In taxable accounts, consider holding bonds or other income-producing assets to manage taxes efficiently.

4. **Diversification**: Ensure your overall portfolio is diversified across asset classes, sectors, and geographies to mitigate risk.

5. **Rebalance Regularly**: As market conditions change, your asset allocation may shift. Regularly review and rebalance to maintain your desired distribution.

6. **Consult with a Financial Advisor**: For personalized advice tailored to your specific situation, consider speaking with a financial advisor.

Ultimately, the ideal distribution will align with your overall financial strategy, risk tolerance, and retirement goals.
