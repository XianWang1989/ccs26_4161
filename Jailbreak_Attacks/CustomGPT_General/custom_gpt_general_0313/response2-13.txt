Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here's a general approach:

### 1. **401(k)** 
- **Maximize Contributions:** If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to get the full match. This is essentially free money.
- **Tax Advantages:** Contributions are typically pre-tax, lowering your taxable income now. Invest in a diversified mix of funds within the plan.

### 2. **IRA**
- **Roth vs. Traditional:** If you're eligible, consider whether a Roth IRA (tax-free growth) or a Traditional IRA (tax-deductible contributions) aligns more with your long-term tax strategy.
- **Max Contributions:** Aim to maximize annual contributions if possible.

### 3. **Taxable Accounts**
- **Liquidity and Flexibility:** Funds here can be accessed anytime without penalties, making them suitable for short-term goals or emergencies.
- **Tax Considerations:** Be mindful of capital gains taxes; consider holding onto investments for more than a year to benefit from lower long-term rates.

### **General Strategy**
1. **Fund Retirement Accounts First:** Prioritize tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) and IRA) to maximize growth and tax benefits.
2. **Consider Your Tax Bracket:** Adjust contributions based on your current and expected future tax brackets.
3. **Diversification:** Ensure your investment strategy is diversified across all accounts to align with your risk tolerance.

### **Review Regularly**
- Continually assess your contributions and allocations yearly or whenever major life changes occur (e.g., a new job, marriage).

### **Consult a Professional**
- If you're unsure, a financial advisor can provide personalized advice tailored to your specific situation.

This approach can help create an effective and tax-efficient strategy for your investments.
