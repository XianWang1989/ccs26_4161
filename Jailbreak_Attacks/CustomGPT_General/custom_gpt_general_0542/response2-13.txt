Balancing your investments across a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts can optimize growth and minimize taxes. Here’s a recommended approach:

1. **401(k) Contributions:**
   - **Maximize Employer Match:** Contribute at least enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match, if available; this is essentially “free money.”
   - **Tax Benefits:** Contributions are pre-tax, which can lower your taxable income.

2. **IRA Contributions:**
   - **Prioritize Roth Options:** If you qualify for a Roth IRA, consider maxing out contributions here for tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement.
   - **Traditional IRA:** Consider this if you expect to be in a lower tax bracket during retirement, allowing for tax-deferred growth.

3. **Taxable Accounts:**
   - **Invest for Flexibility:** Use taxable accounts for additional investments once you've maxed out your tax-advantaged accounts. This offers more liquidity and can be tapped into before retirement age without penalties.
   - **Focus on Tax-Efficient Investments:** Consider index funds or ETFs, which tend to generate fewer taxable events compared to actively managed funds.

4. **Diversification and Asset Allocation:**
   - Ensure that your overall investment strategy aligns with your risk tolerance and retirement timeline. Diversifying assets helps reduce risk.

5. **Considerations for Withdrawals:**
   - Understand that different accounts have different tax implications upon withdrawal. Withdraw from taxable accounts first in early retirement to allow tax-advantaged accounts to continue growing.

6. **Review Regularly:**
   - Periodically reassess your situation and adjust contributions or allocations as necessary based on performance, changes in income, and financial goals.

Consulting with a financial advisor can provide personalized insights tailored to your specific circumstances.
