Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, employer matching, and investment time horizon. Here are some general strategies:

### 1. **401(k) First (if employer matching is available)**
   - **Maximize Contributions:** Contribute enough to get the full employer match, as this is essentially "free money."
   - **Consider Investment Options:** Evaluate the investment options available within your 401(k). If they are limited or have high fees, consider focusing more on IRAs thereafter.

### 2. **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**
   - **Tax Benefits:** Choose between a Traditional IRA (tax-deductible contributions) or a Roth IRA (tax-free withdrawals in retirement).
   - **Contribution Limits:** Ensure you contribute to your IRA up to the maximum limit each year, especially if you’re using a Roth for its tax-free benefits.

### 3. **Taxable Accounts**
   - **Invest Beyond Retirement Accounts:** Use taxable accounts for investments that don’t fit in tax-advantaged accounts or if you've maxed out contributions in other accounts.
   - **Capital Gains Management:** Consider the tax implications of selling investments, focusing on long-term capital gains which are taxed at a lower rate than ordinary income.

### 4. **Diversification**
   - **Asset Allocation:** Diversify your investments across different asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) according to your risk tolerance and time horizon, regardless of account type.

### 5. **Regular Reviews**
   - **Rebalancing:** Periodically review and rebalance your portfolio to maintain your desired asset allocation and ensure you're on track to meet your financial goals.

### Conclusion
A well-rounded approach typically involves maximizing 401(k) contributions first for any employer match, then funding an IRA, and finally investing in taxable accounts. Tailoring your strategy to your specific financial situation and goals is essential, so consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized guidance.
