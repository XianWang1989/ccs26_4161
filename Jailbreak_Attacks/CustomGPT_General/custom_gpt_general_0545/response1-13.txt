Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment horizon. Here’s a general guideline:

### 1. **401(k) Accounts**
   - **Maximize Contributions**: Aim to contribute enough to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money.
   - **Tax-Deferred Growth**: Contributions lower your taxable income, allowing for growth without immediate tax implications.
   - **Investment Options**: Choose funds with lower fees and a good long-term track record.

### 2. **IRA Accounts**
   - **Traditional IRA**: Similar to a 401(k), contributions may be tax-deductible, and the investments grow tax-deferred. Consider this if you might be in a lower tax bracket in retirement.
   - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, meaning withdrawals in retirement are tax-free. This is beneficial if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket later.
   - **Contribution Limits**: Ensure you adhere to annual contribution limits ($6,500 for those under 50; $7,500 for those 50 and older for 2023).

### 3. **Taxable Accounts**
   - **Flexible Access**: These accounts don’t have the contribution limits or withdrawal restrictions of retirement accounts.
   - **Tax Efficiency**: Focus on investments that generate lower capital gains or invest in tax-efficient funds (e.g., index funds).
   - **Short-Term Goals**: Use taxable accounts for goals within the next 5-10 years, as these funds can be accessed without penalties.

### Recommended Distribution Strategy
- **Prioritize Retirement Accounts**: Start with maximizing your employer 401(k) match, then contribute to an IRA, and finally, use taxable accounts for additional savings. 
- **Evaluate Your Goals**: Adjust as necessary based on your specific targets, time frames, and risk tolerance.

### Additional Considerations
- **Consult a Financial Advisor**: A professional can provide tailored advice based on your unique circumstances.
- **Regular Review**: Monitor your allocations regularly and adjust based on changes in your financial situation or market conditions.

This strategy can optimize tax benefits and help you meet your long-term financial goals effectively.
