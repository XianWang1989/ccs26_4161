To distribute your money effectively among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **401(k)**:
   - **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to get the full employer match, as it's effectively free money.
   - **Tax-Deferred Growth**: Utilize this account for pre-tax contributions, allowing your investments to grow tax-deferred.

2. **IRA (Traditional/Roth)**:
   - **Tax Benefits**: Choose a traditional IRA for immediate tax benefits or a Roth IRA for tax-free withdrawals in retirement.
   - **Diversify Contributions**: If you qualify, consider contributing to both types to achieve tax diversification.

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - **Flexibility**: Use these accounts for more liquid investments that you might need before retirement.
   - **Tax-Efficient Investments**: Focus on tax-efficient investments like index funds or ETFs to minimize capital gains taxes.

### General Distribution Strategy
- **Emergency Fund**: Ensure you have an adequate emergency fund before heavily investing.
- **Debt Management**: Pay down high-interest debt first.
- **Risk Tolerance**: Assess your risk tolerance and age to determine the appropriate asset allocation.
- **Rebalance Regularly**: Review and rebalance your portfolio periodically to maintain your desired asset allocation.

Consulting with a financial advisor can provide personalized guidance based on your financial situation and goals.
