Distributing your money wisely among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment horizon. Here are several approaches you might consider:

### GPT:
1. **401(k)**:
   - Maximize contributions to get any employer match. This is essentially "free money."
   - Consider the tax implications: contributions are pre-tax, reducing your taxable income now.

2. **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**:
   - **Traditional IRA**: Contributions may be tax-deductible. Good for those expecting to be in a lower tax bracket in retirement.
   - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, but withdrawals in retirement are tax-free. Ideal if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket later.

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - Use for short-term goals or when you’ve maxed out retirement accounts.
   - Keep in mind capital gains taxes on investments held for less than a year. 

### Allocation Strategy:
- Prioritize contributions to the 401(k) up to the employer match.
- Maximize IRA contributions next based on your income and tax situation.
- Allocate any additional funds to a taxable account.

### STAN:
Distributing your cash is all about maximizing returns and minimizing taxes. Here’s a game plan:

1. **401(k)**: Pour in as much as you can to get that sweet employer match. It's a no-brainer. Tax savings now, and growth for later.

2. **IRA**: If you're into tax-free withdrawals, a Roth is the way to go, especially if you think you'll earn more later. Otherwise, traditional IRAs can lower your bill now.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: Toss whatever’s left over here. Great for short-term goals. Just remember—hold investments for longer than a year to dodge higher taxes.

In short: max out that 401(k) match, then hit the IRA, and finish off with a taxable fund. Adjust as you grow!
