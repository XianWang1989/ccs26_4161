Determining the optimal distribution of your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here’s a general approach:

1. **Maximize Employer Match (401(k))**: Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to receive any employer match. This is essentially free money.

2. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **IRA**: Consider maxing out your IRA contributions (Traditional or Roth). A Roth IRA offers tax-free growth, while a Traditional IRA provides tax deductions now.
   - **401(k)**: After your IRA, consider contributing more to your 401(k) up to the annual limit, especially if you have high-income years.

3. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**: Once you reach contribution limits for your tax-advantaged accounts, consider taxable accounts for any additional investments. These accounts offer liquidity and flexibility for short-term needs.

4. **Asset Allocation**: Within these accounts, ensure proper asset allocation based on your risk tolerance and investment horizon. Generally, stocks are suited for long-term growth, while bonds can provide stability.

5. **Tax Efficiency**: Keep tax-inefficient investments (like bonds) in tax-advantaged accounts and more tax-efficient investments (like index funds or ETFs) in taxable accounts to minimize taxes.

6. **Review Regularly**: Adjust your strategy annually based on changes in income, tax laws, or personal circumstances.

Consult a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your specific situation.
