Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts involves balancing growth potential, tax considerations, and your financial goals. Here are some strategies to consider:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First:**
   - **401(k):** If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to get the full benefit first. This is essentially free money.
   - **IRA:** After maximizing your 401(k) match, consider contributing to a Traditional or Roth IRA. Roth IRAs are particularly beneficial if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement.

### 2. **Assess Tax Impact:**
   - **Tax-Deferred Accounts (401(k) and Traditional IRA):** Withdrawals are taxed as regular income in retirement, so they are best for high-growth investments.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Best for investments you may want to sell in the near future or for generating income through dividends or interest, as you can hold investments long enough to minimize capital gains taxes.

### 3. **Investment Choices:**
   - **401(k) and IRA:** Focus on growth-oriented investments (like stocks) in these accounts to take advantage of tax deferral or tax-free growth (in the case of Roth).
   - **Taxable Account:** Consider holding more conservative investments (like bonds) here, as interest and dividends will be taxed annually.

### 4. **Keep an Emergency Fund:**
   - Before heavily investing, ensure you have sufficient cash reserves outside of these accounts for sudden expenses.

### 5. **Rebalance Periodically:**
   - Review your allocation yearly or after significant market changes, adjusting to maintain your desired risk level and investment mix.

### Example Allocation Strategy:
- **401(k):** 15-20% of your total investment portfolio (maximizing employer match)
- **IRA:** Up to the annual limit (currently $6,500 or $7,500 if you’re over 50)
- **Taxable Accounts:** Remaining income, focusing on balanced investments suitable for your time frame.

### Conclusion
Tailor this distribution based on your income, age, risk tolerance, and long-term goals. Adjustable contributions can further optimize your investment strategy.
