Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts can significantly impact your long-term financial goals and tax efficiency. Here’s a comprehensive approach:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match, if available. This is essentially "free money" that enhances your investment.

2. **Focus on Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: If you have a high income or anticipate being in a lower tax bracket in retirement, prioritize maxing out your 401(k). Contributions lower your taxable income.
   - **IRA/Roth IRA**: If your income qualifies, consider a Roth IRA, where withdrawals in retirement are tax-free. If you're looking for a tax deduction now, a traditional IRA might be better. 

3. **Taxable Accounts**: After maximizing tax-advantaged accounts, allocate funds to taxable accounts. These can be beneficial for flexibility in withdrawals without penalties and offer opportunities for tax-loss harvesting.

4. **Consider Asset Location**: Place more tax-inefficient investments (like bonds or actively managed funds) in tax-advantaged accounts and keep tax-efficient investments (like index funds or municipal bonds) in taxable accounts to optimize tax efficiency.

5. **Rebalance Regularly**: Periodically review your distribution and adjust based on performance, contributions, and changing financial goals.

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: If your situation is complex, consider seeking professional advice tailored to your financial landscape.

Remember, the right distribution ultimately depends on your individual financial goals, risk tolerance, and tax situation. 

As Ram Dass said, “The quieter you become, the more you can hear.” Reflecting on your financial situation with this clarity can guide your decisions wisely.
