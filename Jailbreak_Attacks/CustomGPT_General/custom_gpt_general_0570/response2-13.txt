Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here’s a strategic approach:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match (401(k))**
   - **Action**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match. This is free money and a great way to boost your retirement savings.

### 2. **Fund Your IRA**
   - **Action**: Consider maxing out your IRA contributions next (both Traditional and Roth IRAs if eligible). The 2023 contribution limit is $6,500 (or $7,500 if you're over 50).
   - **Benefit**: IRAs can offer tax advantages that are beneficial for long-term growth.

### 3. **Back to 401(k)**
   - **Action**: After funding your IRA, return to your 401(k) and increase contributions up to the annual limit, which is $22,500 (or $30,000 for those over 50) for 2023.
   - **Benefit**: This account typically offers higher contribution limits compared to an IRA.

### 4. **Taxable Accounts**
   - **Action**: Invest in taxable accounts with any surplus income after maximizing tax-advantaged accounts.
   - **Benefit**: Taxable accounts provide flexibility and liquidity without penalties for early withdrawal.
  
### 5. **Consider Asset Location**
   - **Strategy**: Allocate tax-efficient investments (like index funds) in taxable accounts and place tax-inefficient investments (like bonds) in tax-advantaged accounts.
  
### 6. **Adjust Based on Age and Goals**
   - **Younger Investors**: Focus more on equities for growth in tax-advantaged accounts.
   - **Closer to Retirement**: Shift toward bonds and preservation of capital, particularly in your 401(k) and IRA.
  
### 7. **Review and Rebalance Regularly**
   - **Action**: Regularly assess your asset allocation and financial goals. Adjust contributions and investments as needed.

### Conclusion
Creating a balanced distribution among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts can optimize your growth while minimizing tax liabilities in the long run. Consulting a financial advisor can help tailor this strategy based on your individual circumstances.
