The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here’s a general strategy:

1. **401(k)**: 
   - **Max contributions**: If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to get the full match first, as this is essentially "free money".
   - **Tax benefits**: Contributions are pretax, which lowers your taxable income.

2. **IRA (Traditional/Roth)**:
   - **Diversify tax treatment**: 
     - If you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement, consider a **Roth IRA** (after-tax contributions).
     - If you need immediate tax relief, a **Traditional IRA** might be better.
   - **Contribution limits**: Contribute up to the annual limit, especially if you qualify for catch-up contributions if you’re over 50.

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - **Liquidity and flexibility**: Ideal for short-term goals or if you might need access to funds before retirement.
   - **Tax efficiency**: Focus on tax-efficient investments, such as index funds or tax-managed funds.

### General Tips:
- **Emergency fund**: Ensure you have 3-6 months of expenses saved in a liquid account before heavily investing.
- **Asset allocation**: Diversify investments across all accounts for balanced risk.
- **Rebalance regularly**: Monitor your allocations and adjust based on market performance and personal circumstances.

Consider consulting with a financial advisor for personalized advice based on your specific situation.
