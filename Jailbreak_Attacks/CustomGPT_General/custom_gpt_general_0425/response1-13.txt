The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and time horizon. Here's a general guideline:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to maximize any employer match, as this is essentially "free money." Aim to contribute up to the annual limit if your finances allow.
   - **IRA**: If you’re eligible, contribute to a traditional or Roth IRA. Roth IRAs are especially beneficial if you expect your tax rate to be higher in retirement.

### 2. **Consider Tax Implications**
   - **Taxable Accounts**: These can be useful for shorter-term goals. Keep in mind that capital gains and dividends are taxable, so consider holding investments with lower turnover here.

### 3. **Diversification**
   - Ensure that your asset allocation aligns with your risk tolerance across all accounts. You don't want to over-concentrate in one type of investment.

### 4. **Liquid Needs**
   - Maintain some cash or liquid assets in taxable accounts for short-term needs and emergencies.

### 5. **Review and Adjust**
   - Regularly review your allocations and adjust based on changes in income, expenses, or market conditions.

### Sample Allocation Strategy
- **401(k)**: 15-20% of your salary (to the max if possible)
- **IRA**: Contribute the annual limit ($6,500 for those under 50, $7,500 for 50+)
- **Taxable Accounts**: Allocate remaining funds after maxing out tax-advantaged accounts

### Conclusion
Tailoring your strategy to fit your individual circumstances is crucial. Consulting a financial advisor may provide personalized insights based on your objectives and financial landscape.
