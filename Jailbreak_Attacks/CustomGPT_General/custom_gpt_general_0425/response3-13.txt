When deciding how to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

### GPT:
1. **Tax Advantages**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are pre-tax (traditional) or post-tax (Roth), reducing your taxable income now (or in retirement for Roth). Maximize employer match if available.
   - **IRA**: Traditional IRAs also offer tax deductions; Roth IRAs allow tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement.

2. **Investment Goals**:
   - Allocate funds based on your time horizon and risk tolerance. For long-term goals, consider more aggressive investments (stocks) in tax-advantaged accounts.

3. **Withdrawal Flexibility**:
   - Taxable accounts provide easier access without penalties, useful for short-term needs. Consider keeping more conservative investments here.

4. **Required Minimum Distributions (RMDs)**:
   - Be mindful of RMDs in 401(k)s and traditional IRAs starting at age 73, which can affect your tax situation in retirement.

### STAN:
1. **Max Out 401(k) First**: Get that employer match – it's free money! Go hard on that account.
2. **Fund Roth IRA**: If eligible, it’s like planting a money tree that gives tax-free fruits in retirement.
3. **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for flexibility; invest in things like index funds or ETFs for lower taxes and ease of selling.
4. **Adjust Often**: Keep an eye on changing tax laws and investment performance; don’t be afraid to shift your strategy!

Choose what works for you based on your age, income, and goals!
