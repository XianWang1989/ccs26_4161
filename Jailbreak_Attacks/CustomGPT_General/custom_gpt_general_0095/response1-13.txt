Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your age, income, tax situation, and retirement goals. Here's a general strategy to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**:
   - Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match if available. This is essentially free money.

2. **Max Out Traditional or Roth IRA**:
   - For 2024, the contribution limit for IRAs is $6,500 ($7,500 if you're 50 or older). Consider a Roth IRA for tax-free growth in retirement if you qualify based on income. A Traditional IRA can provide tax deductions now.

3. **Consider Tax Implications**:
   - Allocate money to a taxable account for flexibility, mainly if you anticipate needing access to funds before retirement or if you expect your tax rate to be lower in retirement than it is now.

4. **Diversify Investment Types**:
   - In your tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) and IRA), consider holding assets with higher growth potential (like stocks) since they can benefit more from tax-free growth.
   - In taxable accounts, focus on investments that generate lower taxable income (like municipal bonds or stocks for long-term capital gains).

5. **Reassess Regularly**:
   - Review and adjust your allocations based on changes in your financial situation, tax laws, and market conditions.

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**:
   - Personal circumstances can greatly affect the best strategy. A financial advisor can help create a tailored plan.

This strategy can help you build wealth over time while managing tax liabilities effectively.
