To effectively distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider these strategies:

1. **Tax Efficiency**:
   - **401(k)**: Use this account for high-income years, as contributions lower your taxable income. Opt for this if your employer matches contributions, as it’s essentially free money.
   - **IRA**: If eligible, a Roth IRA offers tax-free growth and is beneficial if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement. Conversely, traditional IRAs offer tax deductions now but are taxed upon withdrawal.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Best for investments you might need access to before retirement age. Focus on investments that generate long-term capital gains to minimize taxes.

2. **Investment Types**:
   - **401(k) and IRA**: Use for long-term investments like stocks and mutual funds, which can benefit from tax-advantaged growth.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Consider bonds or dividend-paying stocks, as you can manage realizing capital gains and losses strategically.

3. **Diversification**:
   - Ensure you have a diversified portfolio across all accounts to mitigate risk, considering your investment horizon and risk tolerance.

4. **Contribution Limits**:
   - Max out employer matching contributions in your 401(k) before funding IRAs. Be mindful of the annual contribution limits for each account type.

5. **Withdrawal Strategy**:
   - Plan how and when you’ll withdraw funds in retirement to manage tax implications effectively.

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: 
   - Tailor your strategy to your financial situation, goals, and investment preferences by seeking professional advice.

By strategically allocating your funds based on these factors, you can optimize growth and minimize tax liabilities across your investment accounts.
