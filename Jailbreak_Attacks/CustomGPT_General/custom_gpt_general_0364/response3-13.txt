Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment horizon. Here’s a general approach:

### GPT:
1. **401(k):** Prioritize contributions to your 401(k), especially if your employer offers a matching contribution. Aim to contribute enough to get the full match, as this is essentially free money.

2. **IRA:** After maximizing the employer match, consider contributing to a traditional or Roth IRA. Roth IRAs offer tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement, while traditional IRAs can provide immediate tax deductions.

3. **Taxable Accounts:** Once you've maxed out your 401(k) and IRA contributions, consider investing in taxable accounts. This offers flexibility in terms of withdrawals and investment choices, though it comes with tax implications on gains and dividends.

4. **Emergency Fund:** Ensure that you have an adequate emergency fund before heavily investing in any of these accounts, typically covering 3-6 months of expenses.

5. **Diversification:** Regardless of the account type, diversify your investment options to mitigate risk.
