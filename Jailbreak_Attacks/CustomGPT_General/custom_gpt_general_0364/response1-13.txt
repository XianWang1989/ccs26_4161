Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment horizon. Here’s a general approach:

1. **Maximize Employer Contributions**: If your employer offers a 401(k) match, contribute enough to get the full match before allocating money elsewhere. This is essentially "free money."

2. **Contribute to Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: Aim to contribute the annual limit if you're in a high tax bracket, as contributions reduce your taxable income.
   - **IRA**: Consider using a Roth IRA if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement. Otherwise, a Traditional IRA may provide immediate tax benefits.

3. **Assess Your Taxable Accounts**: Funds in taxable accounts can offer flexibility for withdrawals without penalties. They can also provide capital gains treatment on investments held long-term and the potential for tax-loss harvesting.

4. **Diversify Investments**: Among all accounts, ensure you're diversified across various asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) based on your risk tolerance.

5. **Consider Future Withdrawals**: Plan for when you'll need access to your funds. Taxable accounts offer the most flexibility, while tax-advantaged accounts often have penalties for early withdrawal.

6. **Review Regularly**: Adjust your contributions and allocations based on changes in your financial goals, investment performance, and changes in tax laws.

Consulting a financial advisor can provide personalized advice tailored to your specific situation.
