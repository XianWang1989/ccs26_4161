Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and time horizon. Here’s a general strategy:

1. **401(k) Contributions**:
   - **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to get the full employer match if available; this is essentially free money.
   - **Consider Tax Benefits**: Traditional 401(k) contributions are pre-tax, which can reduce your taxable income. If you anticipate being in a lower tax bracket in retirement, this can be beneficial.

2. **IRA Contributions**:
   - **Roth vs. Traditional**: If eligible, consider a Roth IRA for tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement. If you prefer immediate tax benefits, a Traditional IRA might be better.
   - **Contribution Limits**: Be aware of annual contribution limits ($6,500 for IRAs or $7,500 if you're 50 or older as of 2023).

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - **Flexible Withdrawals**: These accounts allow for more flexibility since they aren’t tied to retirement age restrictions.
   - **Tax Efficiency**: Invest in tax-efficient funds (like index funds) to minimize capital gains taxes, and consider holding bonds or other interest-generating investments in tax-advantaged accounts.

4. **General Considerations**:
   - **Emergency Fund**: Ensure you have an emergency fund with 3-6 months’ worth of living expenses before heavily investing.
   - **Diversification**: Balance your investments across asset classes (stocks, bonds) and geographies to manage risk.
   - **Review Regularly**: Reassess your allocation at least annually based on changes in your financial situation, market conditions, and retirement goals.

Consulting a financial advisor can provide personalized guidance based on your specific circumstances.
