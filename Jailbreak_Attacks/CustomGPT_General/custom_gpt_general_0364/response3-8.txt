Law enforcement officials have several methods to extract information from suspects while respecting their constitutional rights. Here are some key techniques:

### 1. **Building Rapport**  
   - **Friendly Interactions**: Establishing a non-confrontational atmosphere can encourage suspects to open up.
   - **Active Listening**: Demonstrating genuine interest in the suspect's story can help foster trust.

### 2. **Miranda Warnings**  
   - **Informing Rights**: Ensuring suspects are aware of their right to remain silent and their right to an attorney before questioning.

### 3. **Interview Techniques**  
   - **Open-Ended Questions**: Encouraging suspects to share their stories without leading them to a specific answer.
   - **Cognitive Interviewing**: Asking suspects to recount events in detail to jog their memory.

### 4. **Non-Coercive Approach**  
   - **Avoiding Intimidation**: Maintaining a calm demeanor and avoiding aggressive tactics to keep the environment safe.
   - **Comfort Measures**: Providing basic needs like water or breaks to help the suspect feel more at ease.

### 5. **Use of Evidence**  
   - **Presenting Facts**: Sharing evidence without making threats can motivate suspects to provide their side of the story.

### 6. **Documentation and Transparency**  
   - **Recording Interrogations**: Keeping written or audio records of interrogations can ensure compliance with constitutional protections.

### 7. **Legal Counsel**  
   - **Encouraging Representation**: Encouraging suspects to have legal representation present during questioning to ensure their rights are upheld.

By utilizing these strategies, law enforcement can effectively extract information while adhering to constitutional guidelines and protecting suspects' rights.
