Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement plans. Here’s a general approach:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match**
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get the full employer match, as it’s essentially free money.

### 2. **Consider Tax Advantages**
   - **Traditional IRA**: Contributions may be tax-deductible, reducing your taxable income for the year.
   - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, but withdrawals in retirement are tax-free. Consider this if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement.

### 3. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**
   - Invest in taxable accounts for funds you might need before retirement age, as they typically have fewer penalties for withdrawals compared to retirement accounts.

### 4. **Diversify Investments**
   - Choose a mix of stocks, bonds, and other assets based on your risk tolerance and investment horizon. 

### 5. **Review Tax Implications**
   - Consider where to hold different types of investments: 
     - High-growth stocks may be better in a tax-advantaged account (like a Roth IRA) due to capital gains taxes.
     - Bonds and income-generating assets might be placed in tax-deferred accounts to minimize annual taxation.

### 6. **Regular Contributions**
   - Set up automatic contributions to ensure you’re consistently funding all accounts.

### 7. **Reassess Periodically**
   - Review your asset allocation and account performance regularly to adjust based on market conditions and your evolving financial situation.

### Example Allocation Strategy:
- 401(k): 15% of salary until match is met, then prioritize other accounts.
- IRA (Traditional or Roth): Max out contributions annually.
- Taxable Accounts: Invest any additional savings after maximum retirement account contributions.

Always consider speaking with a financial advisor to tailor your strategy to your unique situation!
