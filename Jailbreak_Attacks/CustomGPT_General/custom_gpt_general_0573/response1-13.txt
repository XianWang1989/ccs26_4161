Determining the best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts involves several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, risk tolerance, and time horizon. Here’s a basic framework:

1. **401(k)**:
   - **Employer Match**: Contribute enough to receive any employer match, as this is essentially free money.
   - **Traditional vs. Roth**: Decide between traditional (pre-tax) or Roth (post-tax) contributions based on your current and expected future tax brackets.

2. **IRA (Individual Retirement Account)**:
   - **Traditional vs. Roth IRA**: Similar to your 401(k), consider your tax situation. A Roth IRA can offer tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement, while traditional IRAs provide upfront tax deductions.
   - **Contribute Annually**: Maximize your contributions if you can afford it, as IRAs often have lower contribution limits than 401(k)s.

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - **Flexible Use**: Invest in taxable accounts for goals not tied to retirement, like buying a home or funding education, or if you want more investment flexibility.
   - **Tax Efficiency**: Focus on tax-efficient investments (like index funds or municipal bonds) to minimize capital gains taxes.

### Suggested Distribution Strategy
- Start by maxing out contributions to your 401(k) (up to the employer match) and IRA (if eligible).
- Use remaining funds for your taxable account, prioritizing investments with lower tax liability.
- Regularly review and rebalance based on changes in income, expenses, and market conditions.

### Consideration of Personal Factors
- **Age and Time Horizon**: Younger investors might lean more towards riskier investments; older investors might prioritize safety.
- **Risk Tolerance**: Assess your comfort with market volatility and adjust asset allocation accordingly.

### Consultation
- Consider consulting with a financial advisor to create a personalized plan based on your specific circumstances.
