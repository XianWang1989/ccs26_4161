Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts effectively depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some strategies to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to take full advantage of any employer matching contributions, as this is essentially "free" money.

2. **Consider Tax Implications**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are pre-tax, which can lower your taxable income. Withdrawals are taxed as ordinary income.
   - **IRA**: Traditional IRAs offer similar tax benefits, while Roth IRAs provide tax-free withdrawals if certain conditions are met.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Investments here are subject to capital gains tax. Consider holding assets for over a year to benefit from lower long-term capital gains rates.

3. **Account Prioritization**:
   - **Short-Term Goals**: Use taxable accounts for funds you might need soon, as they provide more flexibility.
   - **Long-Term Growth**: Use tax-advantaged accounts for investments that may appreciate significantly over time, like stocks.

4. **Asset Location**: 
   - Place higher-growth investments (like stocks) in tax-advantaged accounts to defer or eliminate taxes on gains.
   - Consider putting bonds or income-generating investments in taxable accounts since they can be taxed at a lower rate than ordinary income.

5. **Diversification and Risk Management**: Ensure that your asset allocation aligns with your risk tolerance across all account types.

6. **Review and Adjust**: Regularly review your allocations based on your financial situation, market conditions, and changes in tax laws.

Consulting with a financial advisor can provide tailored advice based on your specific circumstances.
