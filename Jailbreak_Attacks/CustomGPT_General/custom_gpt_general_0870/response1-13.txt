Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here’s a general approach:

### 1. **401(k) Contributions**
   - **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to get the full employer match as it’s essentially free money.
   - **Consider Contribution Limits**: The maximum contribution limit for 2023 is $22,500 (or $30,000 if you’re 50 or older). Aim to reach this if possible.

### 2. **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**
   - **Tax Considerations**: If you expect to be in a lower tax bracket in retirement, a Traditional IRA might be beneficial. If you anticipate a higher bracket, consider a Roth IRA for tax-free withdrawals.
   - **Contribution Limits**: As of 2023, the limit is $6,500 (or $7,500 if you’re 50 or older). Max out this contribution if your budget allows.

### 3. **Taxable Accounts**
   - **After-Tax Savings**: Use these for funds you might need before retirement or for specific goals like buying a home, starting a business, etc.
   - **Diversification**: Invest in a mix of stocks and bonds to balance risk and return.

### General Strategy:
- **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**: Focus on employer matching contributions in your 401(k) and max out IRA contributions first.
- **Tax Efficiency**: Hold investments that generate high taxable income (like bonds) in tax-advantaged accounts, and consider holding long-term growth stocks in taxable accounts to benefit from lower capital gains taxes.
- **Rebalance Regularly**: Review your allocations annually to ensure they align with your financial goals and risk tolerance.

Consult with a financial advisor to personalize this structure based on your unique situation.
