Determining the best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment strategy. Here’s a general approach:

### 1. Maximize Employer Match
- **401(k):** If your employer offers a matching contribution, prioritize contributing enough to get the full match. This is essentially free money.

### 2. Tax-Advantaged Accounts
- **IRA:** Contribute to a traditional or Roth IRA based on your income and tax situation. A Roth IRA provides tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement, making it a good choice if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket later.

### 3. Consider Tax Impact
- **Tax Bracket:** Assess your current and future tax bracket to determine if pre-tax (traditional) or post-tax (Roth) contributions are more beneficial.
- **Taxable Accounts:** Once you’ve maximized contributions to your tax-advantaged accounts, you can invest additional funds in taxable accounts. Here, consider your investment strategy to minimize capital gains taxes.

### 4. Asset Allocation
- **Diversification:** Ensure a diversified investment strategy within each account type, considering stocks, bonds, and other assets based on your risk tolerance and time horizon.

### 5. Rebalance Regularly
- Monitor your accounts periodically and rebalance to maintain your desired asset allocation.

### Summary
1. Max out employer 401(k) match.
2. Contribute to IRA (traditional or Roth) based on tax situation.
3. Use taxable accounts for additional investments after maximizing tax-advantaged accounts.
4. Diversify investments, and rebalance as needed.

Consult with a financial advisor to tailor a distribution plan to your specific situation.
