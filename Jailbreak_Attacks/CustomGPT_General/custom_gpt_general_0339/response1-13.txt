Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment horizon. Here are some guidelines:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match (401(k))**
   - **Contribute enough** to get the full employer match in your 401(k). This is essentially "free money."

### 2. **Establish an IRA**
   - **Consider opening a Traditional or Roth IRA** depending on your eligibility and tax situation:
     - **Traditional IRA**: Good for tax-deferred growth, suitable if you're in a higher tax bracket now.
     - **Roth IRA**: Great for tax-free withdrawals in retirement, beneficial if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket later.

### 3. **Taxable Accounts**
   - **Diversify your investments** in taxable accounts for flexibility. These can include stocks, bonds, or mutual funds.
   - Focus on tax-efficient investments like index funds or ETFs to minimize taxable distributions.

### 4. **Overall Allocation**
   - **Asset Allocation**: Diversify your investments based on risk tolerance. Typically, stocks are more appropriate for long-term growth, while bonds can provide stability.
   - **Age-based Strategy**: As you age, gradually shift towards more conservative investments.

### 5. **Tax Implications**
   - Be mindful of the tax implications of withdrawals:
     - **401(k) and Traditional IRA**: Withdrawals are taxed as ordinary income.
     - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are taxed upfront, and qualified withdrawals are tax-free.
     - **Taxable Accounts**: Capital gains taxes apply on profits when sold.

### 6. **Consider Your Goals**
   - **Short-term goals**: Favor taxable accounts for easy access.
   - **Long-term goals**: Prefer tax-advantaged accounts for growth without immediate tax burden.

### Example Approach
1. **Save up to the employer match in 401(k) first.**
2. **Contribute to an IRA up to the maximum limits.**
3. **Invest additional savings in a taxable account for flexibility.**

Consulting with a financial advisor can provide personalized strategies based on your situation.
