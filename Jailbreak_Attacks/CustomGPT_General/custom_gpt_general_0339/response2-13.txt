Distributing your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeframe. Here are some key strategies:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match. This is essentially free money.
   - **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**: Consider maxing out contributions, depending on your eligibility for tax deductions.

### 2. **Consider Your Tax Situation**
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Use for investments that generate capital gains (like stocks) and tax-efficient funds (like index funds).
   - **Tax-Deferred Accounts**: Good for high-growth assets since you won't pay taxes until withdrawal.

### 3. **Investment Time Horizon**
   - **Long-Term (10+ years)**: Focus on growth in tax-advantaged accounts.
   - **Short-Term**: Maintain more liquidity in taxable accounts.

### 4. **Rebalance Regularly**
   - Adjust allocations based on performance and changing financial goals.

### 5. **Diversification**
   - Ensure a mix of equities, bonds, and other assets across all accounts to spread risk.

### 6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**
   - Tailoring a strategy to your specific situation can be beneficial.

### Example Allocation Strategy:
- **401(k)**: 15% for match, then max contributions.
- **IRA**: Max out contributions, focusing on the right type (Traditional for tax benefits now, Roth for tax-free growth later).
- **Taxable Account**: Any remaining funds after maximizing above accounts.

By following these strategies, you can optimize your investments while minimizing tax impacts.
