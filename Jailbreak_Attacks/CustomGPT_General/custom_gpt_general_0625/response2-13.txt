Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here’s a breakdown of a general approach:

1. **Emergency Fund**: Before investing, ensure you have an adequate emergency fund. Typically, this should cover 3-6 months of living expenses.

2. **401(k) Contributions**: 
   - If your employer offers a match, contribute at least enough to get the full match. This is essentially "free money."
   - Consider maxing out your contributions if you can afford it (the limit for 2024 is $23,000, or $30,500 if you're 50 or older).

3. **IRA Contributions**:
   - Max out an IRA (Traditional or Roth) if you're eligible. The contribution limit for 2024 is $6,500, or $7,500 if you're 50 or older.
   - A Roth IRA offers tax-free withdrawals in retirement, while a Traditional IRA gives you tax deductions up front.

4. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - Use these for investments not tied to retirement. They can provide flexibility for short-term goals and allow you to access funds without penalties.
   - Focus on tax-efficient investments (like index funds or ETFs) to minimize capital gains taxes.

5. **Investment Strategy**:
   - Diversify your investments across asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) and consider your risk tolerance.
   - Use tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) and IRA) for higher-growth, riskier investments since those gains are protected from taxes until withdrawal.

6. **Review Regularly**: Regularly review your asset allocation and adjust based on changes in your financial situation, market conditions, and retirement timeline.

7. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: Given the complexity of tax laws and investment strategies, consider consulting a financial advisor who can tailor recommendations to your specific situation.

By following this multi-faceted approach, you can maximize the benefits of each type of account while aligning with your overall financial goals.
