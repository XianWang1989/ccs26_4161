Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here's a general approach to consider:

### 1. **Prioritize Employer Match (401(k))**
   - **Maximize employer contributions:** If your employer offers a matching contribution on your 401(k), aim to contribute at least enough to take full advantage of this match. It’s essentially free money.

### 2. **Consider Tax Benefits**
   - **401(k):** Contributions are pre-tax (traditional) or after-tax (Roth), which can lower your taxable income today (traditional) or provide tax-free withdrawals in retirement (Roth).
   - **IRA:** Similar benefits as a 401(k). A traditional IRA offers tax-deferred growth, while a Roth IRA provides tax-free withdrawals in retirement.
   - **Taxable accounts:** Interest, dividends, and capital gains are taxed in the year they are realized, making them less tax-efficient for long-term savings.

### 3. **Evaluate Contribution Limits**
   - **Max out retirement accounts:** Contributing the maximum allowable amount to your 401(k) and IRA can help you grow your nest egg effectively. For 2023, the contribution limits are $22,500 for 401(k) plans and $6,500 for IRAs (plus catch-up contributions if you're over 50).
   - **Allocate remaining funds to taxable accounts:** Once you’ve maximized contributions to tax-advantaged accounts, turn to taxable accounts for additional savings or investment.

### 4. **Assess Investment Strategy**
   - **Asset allocation:** Diversify your investments based on your risk tolerance and time horizon. Generally, stocks are better for long-term growth (in tax-advantaged accounts), while more stable investments may suit taxable accounts.
   - **Bond allocation:** Consider tax-efficient bond investments in tax-advantaged accounts due to their interest income being taxed at ordinary rates.

### 5. **Monitor and Adjust**
   - **Review regularly:** Periodically reassess your asset allocation and contributions based on changes in income, expenses, financial goals, and market conditions.

### 6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**
   - If you're unsure of the best allocation for your unique situation, consider seeking personalized advice from a financial advisor who can account for your full financial picture.

By carefully considering these factors, you can create a balanced approach to distributing your money across these accounts.
