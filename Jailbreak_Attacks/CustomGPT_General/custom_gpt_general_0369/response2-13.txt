Distributing money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment strategy. Here are some general guidelines to consider:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**
   - **401(k):** Contribute enough to get any employer match. This is essentially free money. If you can afford to, aim to maximize your contributions (up to the annual limit).
   - **IRA (Traditional/Roth):** If you can, contribute to an IRA for additional tax advantages. For a Traditional IRA, you may get an upfront tax deduction. With a Roth IRA, you pay taxes upfront but enjoy tax-free withdrawals in retirement.

### 2. **Consider Tax Implications**
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Use these for investments that generate low taxable income (like index funds) to minimize taxes. Avoid holding high-turnover assets here to reduce capital gains tax.

### 3. **Investment Types**
   - **401(k) and IRA:** Focus on long-term growth with stocks and diversified funds since you won't need to access this money until retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Consider holding bonds or dividend-yielding stocks to balance your portfolio while managing tax implications.

### 4. **Diversification**
   - Spread your investments across different asset classes (stocks, bonds, real estate) to mitigate risk.

### 5. **Withdrawal Strategy**
   - Plan your withdrawals strategically in retirement. Withdraw from taxable accounts first, then tax-deferred (401(k), Traditional IRA), and finally tax-free (Roth IRA) to manage your tax bracket effectively.

### 6. **Review and Adjust Regularly**
   - Monitor your investments and asset allocation regularly to ensure they align with your financial goals and risk tolerance.

### 7. **Consult a Financial Advisor**
   - A professional can help tailor a strategy to your specific situation, including tax implications and investment choices.

These guidelines can help structure your approach, but personal circumstances vary widely, so adjust based on your individual needs!
