Distributing your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and time horizon. Here are some guidelines to help you create a balanced strategy:

### 1. **Understand Each Account Type:**
   - **401(k):** Often has higher contribution limits and may include employer matching. Contributions are pre-tax (or Roth, if offered), deferring taxes until withdrawal.
   - **IRA:** Individual Retirement Accounts also provide tax advantages. Traditional IRAs offer tax deductions, while Roth IRAs allow for tax-free growth and withdrawals.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** More flexible with no contribution limits but subject to capital gains taxes on earnings and withdrawals.

### 2. **Prioritize Contributions:**
   - **Max out employer match:** Contribute at least enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match—this is effectively free money.
   - **Fund your IRA:** Max out contributions to your IRA before further investing in taxable accounts, especially if you qualify for a Roth IRA.
   - **Contribute to taxable accounts:** After maximizing tax-advantaged options, consider investing in taxable accounts for flexibility and access to funds without penalties.

### 3. **Consider Tax Implications:**
   - **Tax-efficiency:** Place high-growth investments (e.g., stocks) in tax-advantaged accounts to maximize compounding without immediate tax burdens. 
   - **Income-generating assets (e.g., bonds):** Consider placing these in tax-advantaged accounts to defer income taxation.

### 4. **Distribution Strategy:**
   - **Regular rebalancing:** Monitor and adjust your allocations based on changes in market performance, your risk tolerance, and life circumstances.
   - **Withdrawal strategy:** In retirement, consider your withdrawal order. Generally, money from taxable accounts is taken first, followed by tax-deferred accounts, and lastly Roth accounts to minimize taxes.

### 5. **Consult a Financial Advisor:**
   - A professional can provide personalized recommendations based on your specific financial situation, goals, and risk tolerance.

### Conclusion:
The best distribution strategy requires a careful balance of maximizing tax advantages, ensuring you're on track to meet your retirement goals, and making investment choices that align with your overall financial plan. Regularly review and adjust your strategy as your situation evolves.
