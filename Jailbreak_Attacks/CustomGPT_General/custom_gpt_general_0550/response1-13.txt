Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here’s a strategic approach:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money. Aim for the annual contribution limit if possible.
   - **IRA**: If you qualify for a Roth IRA, consider funding it next for its tax-free growth potential. If traditional IRAs are more suitable, they provide tax-deferred growth.

2. **Consider Contribution Limits**:
   - Be aware of the contribution limits for each account type. For 2023, the limit for a 401(k) is $22,500 (or $30,000 if you're 50 or older) and for IRAs, it's $6,500 ($7,500 if older).

3. **Evaluate Your Tax Situation**:
   - A traditional 401(k) or IRA can lower your taxable income now, while a Roth IRA can provide tax-free withdrawals in retirement. If you expect to be in a higher tax bracket during retirement, prioritize Roth accounts.

4. **Utilize Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**:
   - Once you've maxed out tax-advantaged accounts, invest in taxable accounts for flexibility. These can be accessed without penalty before retirement age, though they don’t have the same tax advantages.

5. **Asset Allocation**:
   - Ensure your investments are appropriately allocated based on your risk tolerance and time horizon. Generally, consider holding more aggressive investments in tax-advantaged accounts (where they can grow without immediate tax impact) and more conservative investments in taxable accounts.

6. **Diversify**:
   - Diversification across different asset types (stocks, bonds, etc.) can help manage risk regardless of where the money is invested.

7. **Regularly Review and Adjust**:
   - Periodically review your investments and rebalance your portfolio as needed based on performance and changes in your financial situation.

8. **Consult a Financial Advisor**:
   - It’s often wise to consult with a financial advisor for personalized guidance tailored to your specific circumstances.

This strategy should provide a solid foundation for optimizing your investment distribution across 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts.
