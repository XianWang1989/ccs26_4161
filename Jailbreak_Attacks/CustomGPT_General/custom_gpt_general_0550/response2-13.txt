Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here’s a general strategy:

1. **401(k) Contributions**: 
   - **Maximize employer match**: Ensure you're contributing enough to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money.
   - **Tax deferral**: Contributions reduce your taxable income, which is beneficial if you expect to be in a lower tax bracket during retirement.

2. **IRA Contributions**:
   - **Roth vs. Traditional IRA**: Consider whether you want tax-free withdrawals in retirement (Roth) or tax deductions now (Traditional). If you're eligible for a Roth and expect your income to rise, it may be wise to prioritize Roth contributions.
   - **Maximize contributions**: If possible, fully fund your IRA up to the limit each year.

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - **After-tax savings**: Use this for additional savings after maximizing tax-advantaged accounts, especially for short-term goals.
   - **Invest for growth**: Focus on investments that produce qualified dividends or long-term capital gains, which are taxed at a lower rate.

4. **General Guidelines**:
   - **Diversification**: Ensure your investments across these accounts are diversified, balancing between stocks, bonds, and other assets based on your risk tolerance.
   - **Reevaluation**: Regularly review and adjust your allocations as your financial situation, goals, and market conditions change.

5. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: Consider working with a financial advisor for personalized guidance tailored to your specific circumstances.

This approach balances immediate tax benefits with long-term growth and liquidity needs.
