When distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider these key strategies:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match. This is essentially "free money."

2. **Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**:
   - **401(k) and IRA**: Focus on maxing out your contributions to these tax-advantaged accounts. For 2024, the limit for 401(k) contributions is typically $23,000 (or $30,500 if you're over 50) and for IRAs, it’s $6,500 (or $7,500 for those over 50).
   - **Roth vs. Traditional IRA**: Decide between a Roth IRA (tax-free withdrawals) and a Traditional IRA (tax-deferred growth) based on your current and expected future tax rates.

3. **Diversification in Taxable Accounts**: Once tax-advantaged accounts are maximized, allocate funds to taxable accounts. Here, focus on:
   - **Low-Turnover Investments**: Choose investments that limit capital gains taxes, such as index funds or ETFs.
   - **Tax-Loss Harvesting**: Use any losses to offset capital gains and reduce taxable income.

4. **Consider Tax Implications**: Understand that funds in tax-advantaged accounts grow tax-deferred, while taxable accounts have immediate tax implications on dividends and capital gains. 

5. **Emergency Fund**: Ensure you have an emergency fund outside these accounts to cover 3-6 months of expenses.

6. **Rebalance Annually**: Review your allocations each year to ensure you're on track with your goals and risk tolerance.

7. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: Each financial situation is unique. A professional can help tailor a specific strategy based on your income, goals, and risk profile.

This balanced approach can help optimize growth while minimizing taxes over time.
