Navigating the distribution of your money across a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts involves considering several factors such as your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here’s a streamlined approach:

### 1. **Understand Each Account Type:**
   - **401(k)**: Often has employer matching; contributions are pre-tax, growing tax-deferred until withdrawal.
   - **IRA**: Can be traditional (tax-deferred) or Roth (tax-free on qualified withdrawals). Contribution limits apply.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Funds are taxed on capital gains and dividends; more flexible in terms of withdrawal timing.

### 2. **Prioritize Contributions:**
   - **Maximize Employer Match**: Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match; it’s essentially free money.
   - **IRA Contributions**: Consider maxing out a Roth or traditional IRA next, depending on your income and tax situation.
   - **Additional 401(k) Contributions**: After funding your IRA, direct any additional savings to your 401(k) to maximize tax benefits.

### 3. **Asset Allocation Strategy:**
   - **Risk Tolerance and Time Horizon**: Allocate investments based on your risk appetite and how long you have until retirement. Younger investors might focus on growth (stocks), while those closer to retirement may lean towards income (bonds).
   - **Diversification**: Ensure a diversified portfolio to spread risk, regardless of account type.

### 4. **Tax Efficiency:**
   - **Taxable vs. Tax-Deferred Accounts**: Place higher-growth, more volatile investments in tax-deferred accounts (401(k) and IRA) to defer taxes on gains. Use taxable accounts for stable, income-producing assets, which can be efficiently managed for taxes.

### 5. **Regular Reviews and Adjustments:**
   - Continuously monitor your investment allocations and make adjustments based on performance, changes in tax laws, and shifts in your personal financial situation.

### 6. **Consult a Financial Advisor:**
   - If you're uncertain about your strategy, consider consulting a financial professional who can tailor recommendations to your specific circumstances.

By following these steps, you can create a balanced and effective investment strategy that maximizes your savings potential and prepares you for a comfortable retirement.
