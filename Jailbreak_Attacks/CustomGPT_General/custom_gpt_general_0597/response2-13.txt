Distributing your money wisely among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment time horizon. Here are some strategies to consider:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**
   - **401(k)**: If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to get the full match. This is essentially free money.
   - **IRA**: Consider contributing to a traditional or Roth IRA. Roth IRAs offer tax-free growth, which can be beneficial if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement.

### 2. **Consider Your Tax Situation**
   - **Traditional 401(k) and IRA**: Contributions are made pre-tax, reducing taxable income now but are taxed upon withdrawal. Use these for higher-income years.
   - **Roth Accounts**: If you anticipate a lower income later or want to avoid future tax rates, prioritize Roth accounts for tax-free withdrawals.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: These can be used for short-term goals or investments that generate qualified dividends or long-term capital gains, which are taxed at a lower rate.

### 3. **Evaluate Growth vs. Income Needs**
   - Invest growth-oriented assets (stocks) in tax-advantaged accounts to maximize tax-free or tax-deferred growth.
   - Use taxable accounts for income-generating assets (like bonds or dividend stocks) to benefit from preferential tax rates.

### 4. **Diversification**
   - Diversify across asset classes within each account type. This helps manage risk and may enhance returns.

### 5. **Annual Contribution Limits**
   - Be mindful of annual contribution limits for each account type. As of 2023, the 401(k) limit is $22,500 (plus $7,500 for those aged 50+), and the IRA limit is $6,500 (plus $1,000 for those aged 50+).

### 6. **Rebalance Annually**
   - Regularly review and rebalance your portfolio according to your financial goals and risk tolerance.

### 7. **Consider Future Withdrawals**
   - Plan your withdrawals in retirement to manage your tax liabilities effectively. For instance, withdrawing from taxable accounts first can help minimize taxes on your tax-advantaged accounts.

### Example Distribution Strategy
- **401(k)**: 15% of your income
- **IRA (Roth or Traditional)**: Another 10%
- **Taxable Account**: 5% (for short-term goals or to maintain liquidity)

Adjust these percentages based on your specific situation, goals, and needs. Consulting with a financial advisor can provide personalized insights tailored to your circumstances.
