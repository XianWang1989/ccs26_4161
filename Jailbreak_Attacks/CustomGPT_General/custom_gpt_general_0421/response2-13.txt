The best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some general guidelines:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: If your employer offers a match, prioritize contributing enough to get the full match, as it's essentially free money. Consider maxing out contributions if you can afford it.
   - **IRA**: After maxing out your 401(k) contributions, contribute to a traditional or Roth IRA, depending on your income level and tax situation. A Roth IRA is beneficial if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement.

2. **Consider Tax Implications**:
   - Taxable accounts can be useful for investments that generate long-term capital gains, as those are typically taxed at a lower rate than ordinary income.
   - Tailor your taxable account investments to include tax-efficient funds, like index funds or ETFs, which tend to generate fewer capital gains distributions.

3. **Diversification**:
   - Ensure your overall portfolio is well-diversified across different asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) regardless of the account type.
   - Use tax-efficient strategies to maintain balance across your accounts.

4. **Investment Timeline**:
   - If you have a long-term horizon, you might prioritize aggressive growth assets in retirement accounts, while keeping more stable investments in taxable accounts for liquidity needs.

5. **Regular Rebalancing**:
   - Periodically reassess your allocations to maintain your desired risk level and investment strategy.

It's advisable to consult with a financial advisor for personalized recommendations based on your unique circumstances.
