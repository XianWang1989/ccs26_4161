Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts involves considering several factors, including your financial goals, time horizon, and tax implications. Here’s a general strategy:

1. **Maximize Contributions**: Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to get any company match, as it’s essentially free money. Then, consider maxing out your IRA contributions.

2. **Tax Considerations**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are pre-tax, reducing your taxable income. However, withdrawals in retirement are taxed as ordinary income.
   - **IRA**: Traditional IRAs also provide tax benefits upfront, while Roth IRAs allow tax-free growth and tax-free withdrawals in retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: These won't provide tax advantages like retirement accounts, but they offer flexibility and can be beneficial for short-term goals.

3. **Asset Allocation**: Consider the types of investments:
   - Put higher-risk investments (like stocks) in tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) and IRA) to benefit from tax-deferred growth.
   - Use taxable accounts for investments with lower expected returns or that could generate high taxable income (like bonds).

4. **Withdrawal Strategy**: Plan for how you will withdraw funds in retirement. You can manage your tax liability by choosing which accounts to tap into first.

5. **Review and Adjust**: Regularly review your allocations based on performance, changes in tax laws, and personal financial goals.

Consulting with a financial advisor can help tailor a strategy specific to your situation.
