Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, current tax situation, and time horizon before retirement. Here are some general guidelines:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**
   - **401(k):** Contribute enough to get any employer match; this is essentially free money. Consider maxing out your contributions (up to $22,500 for 2024, or $30,000 if you're over 50).
   - **IRA:** If you qualify for a traditional or Roth IRA, contribute to it next. For 2024, the maximum contribution is $6,500 (or $7,500 if you’re over 50).

### 2. **Consider Your Tax Situation**
   - **Tax-Deferred Accounts (401(k) and Traditional IRA):** Contributions reduce your taxable income now but are taxed upon withdrawal. This is beneficial if you expect to be in a lower tax bracket in retirement.
   - **Roth IRA:** Contributions are made with after-tax dollars and grow tax-free. Ideal if you anticipate being in a higher tax bracket later.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Money here is taxed on realized gains and dividends, but they offer flexibility. Use this for short-term goals or emergency funds.

### 3. **Investment Strategy**
   - **Risk Tolerance:** Generally, you might hold higher-risk investments in tax-advantaged accounts (where growth is tax-deferred or tax-free) and more conservative investments in taxable accounts.
   - **Asset Location:** Place income-generating assets (e.g., bonds) in tax-advantaged accounts and growth assets (e.g., stocks) in taxable accounts to minimize taxes on dividends and interest.

### 4. **Retirement Timeline**
   - If you’re close to retirement, prioritize preserving capital and consider bond allocations.
   - If you're younger, focus on growth potential and consider more stocks in your investment mix.

### 5. **Emergency Fund**
   - Ensure you have an adequate emergency fund (typically 3-6 months’ worth of expenses) in readily accessible accounts.

### 6. **Regularly Reassess**
   - Review your allocations and contributions at least annually to adjust for changes in income, expenses, and life events.

### Conclusion
Start by maximizing contributions to tax-advantaged accounts, then diversify your savings into taxable accounts as needed. Tailor your approach based on tax situation, investment goals, and time horizon. Consulting a financial advisor can also provide personalized insights.
