When distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, several factors need to be considered to optimize your investment strategy:

1. **Tax Implications**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are typically pre-tax, which can lower your taxable income. Withdrawals in retirement are taxed as ordinary income.
   - **IRA**: Depending on the type (Traditional or Roth), you may get a tax deduction now (Traditional) or enjoy tax-free withdrawals in retirement (Roth).
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Investments here are subject to capital gains tax when you sell them. Interest and dividends are also taxed in the year they are received.

2. **Investment Goals**:
   - If you are saving for short-term goals, prioritize taxable accounts.
   - For retirement, take advantage of tax-deferred accounts like a 401(k) and IRA.

3. **Contribution Limits**:
   - Max out your 401(k) contributions up to the employer match; this is essentially free money.
   - Next, contribute to an IRA. If you're eligible for a Roth IRA, consider it for tax-free growth.
   - After maxing out the above, invest in your taxable accounts.

4. **Asset Allocation**:
   - Consider a diversified portfolio across all accounts, but tailor the types of investments based on tax efficiency. For instance:
     - Hold high-growth investments in tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) and IRA) to shield from taxes.
     - Investments generating regular income (bonds, dividend stocks) may be better in taxable accounts since they may be taxed at a lower rate.

5. **Withdrawal Strategy**:
   - Plan your withdrawals strategically in retirement to minimize taxes, taking from taxable accounts first, followed by tax-deferred accounts, and finally tax-free accounts.

6. **Review Regularly**:
   - Regularly review your investment strategy and make adjustments based on changes in income, financial goals, or tax laws.

Ultimately, consulting with a financial advisor can provide personalized advice tailored to your specific situation and goals.
