To optimize your investment strategy among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following steps:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts:** 
   - Aim to contribute the maximum allowed to your 401(k) and IRA first, as these accounts offer tax benefits. For 2023, the contribution limits are $22,500 for 401(k)s (or $30,000 if you're over 50) and $6,500 for IRAs (or $7,500 if you're over 50).

2. **Investment Allocation:**
   - Consider your risk tolerance and investment goals. Typically, you might want to hold more aggressive investments (like stocks) in tax-advantaged accounts, where gains are shielded from taxes. In taxable accounts, you may want to favor lower-turnover investments like index funds or municipal bonds to minimize tax liabilities.

3. **Asset Location Strategy:**
   - Place higher-return assets that generate significant capital gains in tax-deferred accounts (401(k), IRA) to benefit from tax deferral.
   - Use taxable accounts for income-generating investments (like bonds) or investments that you expect to hold long-term, as you pay taxes on dividends and interest each year.

4. **Diversification:**
   - Ensure that your overall portfolio is well-diversified across asset classes (stocks, bonds, cash) regardless of the account type.

5. **Regularly Review and Rebalance:**
   - Periodically assess your investment allocations to maintain your desired level of risk and adjust as necessary based on changes in financial goals, tax laws, or market conditions.

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor:**
   - If unsure, consider working with a financial advisor who can help tailor a plan specific to your financial situation.

By following these strategic steps, you can create a balanced approach to distributing your investments across your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, focusing on tax efficiency and alignment with your long-term goals.
