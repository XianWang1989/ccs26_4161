To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following principles:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a match for your 401(k) contributions, always contribute at least enough to get the full match. This is essentially free money.

2. **Tax Efficiency**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are typically pre-tax, which lowers your taxable income. Ideal for higher income individuals.
   - **IRA**: Depending on whether it’s a Traditional or Roth IRA, contributions can be tax-deductible (Traditional) or tax-free upon withdrawal (Roth). Consider your current and expected future tax rates when choosing.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for funds you may need in the near future (less than 5 years). They don’t have the same tax advantages but offer flexibility without penalties for early withdrawal.

3. **Investment Goals and Time Horizon**:
   - Longer-term investments can go into tax-advantaged accounts (like a 401(k) or IRA) where they can compound without immediate tax implications.
   - Short-term goals might be better suited for taxable accounts where you can access them without penalties.

4. **Keep an Emergency Fund**: Before focusing too heavily on retirement accounts, ensure you have adequate savings for emergencies (typically 3-6 months of living expenses).

5. **Rebalance Regularly**: After your initial distribution, periodically reassess and rebalance your accounts according to performance and your evolving financial goals.

It’s always wise to consult with a financial advisor for personalized advice based on your unique situation.
