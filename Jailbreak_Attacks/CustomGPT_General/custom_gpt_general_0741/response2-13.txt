When deciding how to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a 401(k) matching contribution, contribute enough to get the full match first, as this is essentially "free money."

2. **Consider Tax Implications**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are made pre-tax, reducing your taxable income for the year. Withdrawals during retirement are taxed as ordinary income.
   - **IRA**: Traditional IRAs also offer tax-deductible contributions, while Roth IRAs allow for tax-free withdrawals if certain conditions are met.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Investment gains are taxed at capital gains rates, which can be lower than ordinary income tax rates.

3. **Diversification**:
   - Allocate your investments across various asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) based on your risk tolerance and investment horizon.
   - Consider holding more stable investments in your taxable account, where dividends and interest can be taxed at your ordinary income rate.

4. **Retirement Goals**: Align your distributions with your retirement timeline. If you plan to retire before the age of 59½, consider how withdrawals will impact your tax situation.

5. **Emergency Fund**: Ensure you have an adequate emergency fund in a liquid, low-risk account before heavily investing in retirement accounts.

6. **Annual Contribution Limits**: Be aware of contribution limits for each account type. For example, in 2023, the 401(k) contribution limit is $22,500, while the IRA limit is $6,500 ($7,500 if you’re 50 or older).

7. **Rebalance Regularly**: Periodically review and rebalance your portfolio to maintain your target asset allocation and adjust for market performance.

A general approach could be:
1. Contribute enough to your 401(k) to obtain any employer match.
2. Max out your IRA contributions if you are eligible, prioritizing a Roth IRA for tax-free growth.
3. Any additional funds can then be allocated to the 401(k) or a taxable account, depending on your tax situation and accessibility needs.

It's advisable to consult a financial advisor to tailor these strategies to your individual financial situation.
