Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here are some general guidelines to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a 401(k) match, contribute enough to take full advantage of this "free money" first. It's a great starting point for retirement savings.

2. **Tax-Advantaged Accounts**: After maximizing the 401(k) match, prioritize contributions to your IRA (Traditional or Roth, depending on your income level and tax situation). IRAs often provide tax benefits that can help your investments grow.

3. **Consider Tax Implications**: Use taxable accounts for investments that are likely to appreciate (like stocks) and hold tax-efficient investments in your 401(k) and IRA. This strategy can minimize your tax burden.

4. **Balance Your Portfolio**: Diversify your investments across all account types. Keep in mind the different tax treatments of each account type; for instance, traditional accounts are taxed upon withdrawal, while Roth accounts grow tax-free.

5. **Age and Timeline**: As you get closer to retirement, you might lean more heavily toward the 401(k) and IRA to benefit from their tax advantages. Younger investors might prioritize Roth accounts or taxable accounts for flexibility.

6. **Rebalance Regularly**: Periodically assess and rebalance your portfolio to maintain your desired asset allocation and adjust for any changes in your financial situation or market conditions.

Consult with a financial advisor to tailor these strategies to your individual circumstances.
