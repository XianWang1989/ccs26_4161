The best way to distribute your money amongst your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment time horizon. Here are some general guidelines to consider for allocating your contributions:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**: If your employer offers a matching contribution, aim to contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full match first. This is essentially “free money” and can significantly boost your retirement savings.

2. **Contribute to an IRA**: Consider contributing to a traditional or Roth IRA next. IRAs often provide more diverse investment options compared to 401(k) plans. A Roth IRA allows for tax-free growth and tax-free withdrawals in retirement, while a traditional IRA offers tax deductions now. Choose based on your current tax situation and expected future tax rate.

3. **Diversify Investments**: Ensure your investment strategy is diversified across different asset classes (stocks, bonds, real estate, etc.) to manage risk. This is important in both retirement accounts and taxable accounts.

4. **Use Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**: Contributions to taxable accounts can be accessed at any time without penalties (though you may owe capital gains taxes on profits). This can be useful for short-term goals or emergencies.

5. **Consider Tax Implications**: Focus on tax-efficient investments in your taxable accounts, like index funds or ETFs, which typically generate fewer taxable events. Use tax-advantaged accounts for higher turnover investments.

6. **Assess Your Time Horizon**: If you have a longer time horizon (more than 10 years), aggressive growth positions in tax-advantaged accounts can yield significant growth. For shorter horizons, consider more conservative allocations.

7. **Regular Contributions**: To take advantage of dollar-cost averaging, make regular contributions rather than trying to time the market.

8. **Review and Adjust**: Regularly review and adjust your allocation based on performance, changes in income, and life events.

Consult with a financial advisor to create a personalized plan that suits your specific situation.
