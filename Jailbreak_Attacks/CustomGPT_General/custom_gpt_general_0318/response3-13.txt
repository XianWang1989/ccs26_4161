Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts largely depends on your individual financial goals, tax situation, and investment horizon. Here are some strategies to consider:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match. It’s free money!
   - **IRA**: Consider contributing to a Roth IRA if you qualify, as it offers tax-free growth.

### 2. **Consider Tax Implications**
   - **401(k)** and **Traditional IRA**: Contributions are pre-tax; withdrawals are taxed as ordinary income.
   - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are after-tax, but withdrawals are tax-free in retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Capital gains and dividends are taxed in the year they are realized, so they can be less efficient for tax management.

### 3. **Asset Allocation**
   - Hold higher-growth assets (like stocks) in tax-advantaged accounts to maximize tax-deferral benefits.
   - Use taxable accounts for bonds or income-generating investments to manage tax liability.

### 4. **Emergency Fund**
   - Keep enough liquid assets (such as cash) outside of these accounts for emergencies.

### 5. **Long-Term Goals**
   - Assess your timeline for accessing funds; the longer the timeline, the more you can prioritize growth-oriented accounts like 401(k) and IRAs.

### Example Distribution Plan
- **401(k)**: Max out to employer match.
- **IRA**: Max contributions to Roth or Traditional IRA, depending on eligibility.
- **Taxable Accounts**: Invest any remaining funds after maxing tax-advantaged accounts.

### Final Consideration
Review your portfolio regularly and adjust based on changing circumstances and financial goals. Consulting with a financial advisor can provide personalized guidance tailored to your situation.
