Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here’s a general strategy:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get any employer match; it's free money.

2. **Consider Tax Benefits**:
   - **Tax-Deferred Accounts (401(k) and IRA)**: These lower your taxable income now. Aim to max out contributions if you can ($22,500 for 401(k) and $6,500 for IRAs in 2023).
   - **Roth IRA (if eligible)**: Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, but withdrawals are tax-free in retirement.

3. **Diversify Tax Treatment**:
   - Maintain a balance of tax-deferred (401(k), traditional IRA) and taxable accounts to have flexibility in retirement for tax planning.

4. **Emergency Fund**: Ensure you have adequate savings in accessible accounts before heavily investing.

5. **Investment Choices**:
   - Hold higher-growth investments in tax-deferred accounts to maximize their growth potential.
   - Use taxable accounts for more stable investments that generate lower taxable income.

6. **Regular Review**: Reassess your investments and asset allocation regularly based on your changing goals and market conditions.

It’s often wise to consult with a financial advisor to tailor these strategies to your specific situation.
