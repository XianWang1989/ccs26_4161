Distributing your money across a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and time horizon. Here’s a general approach:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k):** Contribute enough to your 401(k) to receive the full employer match, as this is essentially free money.

2. **Contribute to an IRA:** After maximizing your 401(k) match, consider contributing to a Traditional or Roth IRA. A Roth IRA offers tax-free withdrawals in retirement, while a Traditional IRA offers tax deductions now.

3. **Increase 401(k) Contributions:** If you can still save more, consider increasing your 401(k) contributions, especially if you have a high income and are looking for tax advantages.

4. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility:** Use taxable accounts for money you might need before retirement, as they provide more liquidity and flexibility. This is also a good place for investments that are less tax-efficient, such as stocks.

5. **Diversification:** Ensure your investments across all accounts are diversified to manage risk. This includes a mix of stocks, bonds, and other assets.

6. **Periodic Rebalancing:** Regularly review and adjust your allocations to stay aligned with your risk tolerance and financial goals.

Consulting with a financial advisor can provide tailored advice based on your unique situation.
