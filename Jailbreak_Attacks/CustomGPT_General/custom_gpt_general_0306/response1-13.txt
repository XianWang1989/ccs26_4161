To effectively distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match, as it’s essentially free money. Consider maxing out your contribution if you can afford it.
   - **IRA**: After maxing the 401(k) match, consider funding a Traditional or Roth IRA, depending on your income and tax situation.

2. **Evaluate Tax Implications**:
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for investments you anticipate needing access to before retirement. Capital gains in taxable accounts are taxed differently, so consider holding investments that generate qualified dividends or long-term capital gains to minimize taxes.

3. **Diversification**:
   - Ensure your overall investment portfolio is well-diversified across asset classes and sectors. This helps manage risk.

4. **Investment Choices**:
   - Use tax-efficient investments in taxable accounts (e.g., index funds, ETFs) to minimize taxable distributions. More actively managed funds can be better suited for tax-advantaged accounts where taxes aren't an immediate concern.

5. **Emergency Fund**:
   - Maintain an adequate emergency fund outside of these accounts to avoid early withdrawals and penalties.

6. **Regular Review**:
   - Periodically reassess your asset allocation and contributions based on your financial goals, market conditions, and changes in your income.

Consulting a financial advisor may also be helpful to tailor the strategy to your specific financial situation and goals.
