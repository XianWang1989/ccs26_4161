When considering how to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, here are some strategies to optimize your investments:

1. **Maximize Contributions**:
   - **401(k)**: If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to get the full match. This is essentially "free money."
   - **IRA**: After maximizing your 401(k) contributions, consider contributing to a Traditional or Roth IRA, depending on your tax situation.

2. **Tax Considerations**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are pre-tax, which can lower your taxable income. Growth is tax-deferred until withdrawal.
   - **IRA**: A Traditional IRA offers tax-deferred growth, while a Roth IRA allows for tax-free withdrawals in retirement if certain conditions are met.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Investments here are subject to capital gains taxes on earnings, so prioritize holding investments you plan to keep for the long term to minimize taxes.

3. **Diversity of Investments**:
   - Use your 401(k) and IRA for long-term, growth-oriented investments like stocks or mutual funds.
   - Consider more liquid or varied investments in your taxable account, including lower-risk assets or funds that may offer tax advantages.

4. **Balance Risk and Accessibility**:
   - A mix of aggressive (stocks) and conservative (bonds) investments should reflect your risk tolerance and time horizon.
   - Keep some cash or easily liquidated assets in your taxable account for immediate needs.

5. **Rebalance Regularly**: Periodically review your allocations to ensure they still align with your financial goals and risk tolerance.

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: For personalized strategies, it may be beneficial to consult a financial professional, especially regarding tax implications and investment goals.

By considering these strategies, you can create a well-balanced investment approach that maximizes growth while minimizing tax liabilities.
