Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeframe. Here's a general approach:

1. **Maximize Contributions**: 
   - Start by maximizing your 401(k) contributions, especially if your employer offers a match. This is essentially "free money."
   - Next, contribute to an IRA (Traditional or Roth) depending on your eligibility and tax preferences.

2. **Tax Considerations**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are pre-tax, which can lower your taxable income. Withdrawals are taxed as ordinary income.
   - **IRA**: Traditional IRAs have tax-deductible contributions, while Roth IRAs are funded with after-tax dollars, allowing tax-free withdrawals in retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Offer flexibility for withdrawals but are subject to capital gains tax.

3. **Investment Goals**:
   - For long-term growth, allocate more towards retirement accounts, which can benefit from compound growth and tax advantages.
   - Use taxable accounts for short- to medium-term goals, as they provide greater liquidity.

4. **Age and Risk Tolerance**:
   - Younger investors may prioritize growth in tax-advantaged accounts, while those nearing retirement might lean towards capital preservation.
   - Adjust allocation between stocks and bonds based on your risk tolerance and investment horizon.

5. **Diversification**: 
   - Ensure that your investments are diversified across different asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) regardless of the account type.

6. **Regular Rebalancing**: 
   - Periodically review and rebalance your portfolio to maintain your desired asset allocation as market conditions change.

Consulting a financial advisor can provide personalized advice tailored to your unique situation.
