To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **401(k) Contributions**: Maximize contributions to your 401(k) to take advantage of employer match programs, if available. This is often considered "free money."

2. **IRA Contributions**: If you're eligible, contribute to a traditional or Roth IRA. Traditional IRAs may offer tax advantages, while Roth IRAs allow for tax-free withdrawals in retirement.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: After maximizing tax-advantaged accounts, use taxable accounts for additional investments. These accounts offer more flexibility for withdrawals and investment choices, but be mindful of capital gains taxes on any profits.

4. **Investment Mix**: Adjust your investment allocation based on your risk tolerance and timeline. A more aggressive approach may be suitable for long-term growth, while a conservative strategy might be better for short-term needs.

5. **Diversification**: Ensure your portfolio is diversified across different asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) to mitigate risks.

6. **Rebalancing**: Periodically review and rebalance your portfolio to align with your goals and market changes.

7. **Seek Professional Advice**: Consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized guidance.
