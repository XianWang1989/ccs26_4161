The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, risk tolerance, and tax situation. Here’s a general approach you might consider:

1. **Maximize Contributions**:
   - **401(k)**: If your employer offers a matching contribution, aim to contribute at least enough to get the full match. This is essentially "free money."
   - **IRA**: After maximizing the 401(k) match, consider contributing to an IRA (Traditional or Roth, depending on your tax situation and future income expectations).

2. **Consider Tax Implications**:
   - **401(k) and Traditional IRA**: Contributions are made pre-tax, reducing your taxable income for the year, but you’ll pay taxes on withdrawals in retirement. Good for high-income years.
   - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are made after-tax, but qualified withdrawals are tax-free in retirement. Beneficial if you’re in a lower tax bracket now and expect to be in a higher one later.

3. **Balance Risk and Return**:
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for investments that might generate taxable income often (like bonds) and to maintain liquidity for short-term goals. Consider tax-efficient investments to minimize tax impact.

4. **Diversification and Asset Allocation**:
   - Ensure your overall portfolio is well-diversified across different asset classes (stocks, bonds, cash). Adjust the mix based on your risk tolerance and investment horizon.

5. **Regular Rebalancing**:
   - Periodically review and adjust your asset allocation based on performance and changes in financial goals or market conditions.

6. **Emergency Fund**:
   - Before aggressively funding these accounts, ensure you have an adequate emergency fund in place (typically 3-6 months of expenses).

7. **Consult a Financial Advisor**:
   - For personalized advice tailored to your unique financial situation, consider consulting with a financial advisor.

Ultimately, a balanced approach that prioritizes maximizing tax advantages while accommodating your personal financial goals is key.
