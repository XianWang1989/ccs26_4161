The best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here are some general strategies to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Contributions**: If your employer offers a 401(k) match, contribute enough to get the full match first. This is essentially free money and a great way to boost your retirement savings.

2. **Contribute to an IRA**: After maximizing employer contributions, consider contributing to an IRA (Traditional or Roth). Traditional IRAs may offer tax deductions now, while Roth IRAs provide tax-free withdrawals in retirement. Evaluate which is more beneficial based on your tax bracket.

3. **Balance Tax Efficiency**: Taxable accounts can be useful for flexibility and investments that generate little income (like index funds) to minimize tax implications. Consider holding more tax-efficient investments in these accounts.

4. **Consider Tax Implications**: Be mindful of capital gains taxes and the tax treatment of withdrawals from each account type. Ideally, you want to withdraw from accounts that will minimize your overall tax burden during retirement.

5. **Diversify Investments**: Ensure your investments are diversified and aligned with your risk tolerance. This includes having a mix of stocks and bonds across all account types.

6. **Regularly Reassess**: Periodically review and adjust your allocations in response to changes in your financial situation, retirement goals, and market conditions.

Ultimately, you might want to consult with a financial advisor to develop a personalized strategy that suits your overall financial plan. Happy planning!
