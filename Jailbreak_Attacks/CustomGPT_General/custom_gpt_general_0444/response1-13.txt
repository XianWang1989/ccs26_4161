When distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **401(k) Advantages**:
   - **Employer Match**: Contribute enough to get any employer match, as it's essentially free money.
   - **Tax-Deferred Growth**: Contributions reduce your taxable income now and grow tax-deferred until withdrawal.

2. **IRA Benefits**:
   - **Tax Flexibility**: Choose between a traditional IRA (tax-deferred) or a Roth IRA (tax-free growth and withdrawals).
   - **Investment Options**: IRAs often offer a wider range of investment choices than 401(k)s.

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - **Tax Efficiency**: These accounts can hold investments that generate lower capital gains and qualified dividends. 
   - **Access to Money**: Unlike retirement accounts, you can access funds without penalties.

### Suggested Approach:
1. **Maximize 401(k) Contributions**: Start with enough to get your employer’s match, then consider maxing it out if possible.
2. **Contribute to an IRA**: After your 401(k), contribute to an IRA. If you qualify for a Roth IRA, consider that option for potentially tax-free growth.
3. **Use Taxable Accounts for Additional Savings**: Once you’ve contributed to your 401(k) and IRA, direct any additional savings into your taxable account, focusing on tax-efficient investments.

### Other Considerations:
- **Age and Retirement Timeline**: Younger investors can focus more on growth in tax-advantaged accounts, while nearing retirement may prefer liquidity.
- **Tax Planning**: Consider your current and projected future tax rates when deciding how much to allocate to traditional vs. Roth accounts.
- **Diversification**: Ensure your investments are diversified across all accounts to manage risk effectively.

It's often beneficial to consult with a financial advisor who can tailor strategies specific to your financial situation and goals.
