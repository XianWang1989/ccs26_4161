Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various factors including your financial goals, tax situation, investment timeline, and risk tolerance. Here are some general guidelines:

### 1. **401(k) Accounts:**
   - **Employer Match:** Contribute enough to get any employer match, as it's essentially free money.
   - **Tax Benefits:** Contributions are often pre-tax, lowering your taxable income.
   - **Investment Options:** Typically limited compared to IRAs; review the options available.

### 2. **IRA Accounts:**
   - **Roth vs. Traditional:** Consider whether to use a Traditional or Roth IRA. A Roth offers tax-free withdrawals in retirement, while a Traditional IRA may provide a tax deduction now.
   - **Contribution Limits:** Be mindful of annual contribution limits.
   - **Investment Flexibility:** Generally, IRAs have more investment options than 401(k)s.

### 3. **Taxable Accounts:**
   - **Flexible Access:** Money can be withdrawn at any time without penalties.
   - **Capital Gains Tax:** Consider the impact of taxes on any capital gains; long-term investments (held over a year) are taxed at a lower rate.
   - **Diversified Investments:** You can hold diverse investments, including stocks, bonds, and mutual funds.

### **Overall Strategy:**
- **Emergency Savings:** Ensure you have an emergency fund in a high-yield savings account before aggressively funding retirement accounts.
- **Balance Contributions:** Aim for a mix based on your age and retirement timeline. Younger investors may benefit more from growth-oriented accounts (like IRAs and taxable accounts), while those closer to retirement may want stability (like 401(k) funds).
- **Tax Considerations:** Utilize tax-advantaged accounts first for higher-return investments and keep more conservative investments in taxable accounts.
  
### **Regular Review:**
- **Rebalance Regularly:** Assess your portfolio at least annually to ensure it aligns with your goals and adjust contributions as needed.

Consulting with a financial advisor can provide personalized advice tailored to your specific situation.
