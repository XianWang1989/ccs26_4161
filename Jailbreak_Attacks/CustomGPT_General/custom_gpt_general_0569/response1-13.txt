To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match**
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match first. This is essentially free money.

### 2. **Consider Tax Implications**
   - **Tax-Deferred Accounts (401(k), Traditional IRA)**: Contributions may lower your taxable income now. Balance these with your future tax expectations.
   - **Roth Accounts (Roth IRA)**: Contributions are made with after-tax income but withdrawals are tax-free in retirement. Great for those anticipating higher tax rates later.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Use for investments with lower turnover to minimize capital gains taxes. Good for emergency savings or short-term goals.

### 3. **Diversification Strategy**
   - Allocate investments based on your risk tolerance across these accounts. For instance, keep higher-risk investments in tax-advantaged accounts to defer taxes on gains.

### 4. **Emergency Fund**
   - Before aggressively funding retirement accounts, ensure you have an emergency fund to cover 3-6 months of expenses in a high-yield savings account.

### 5. **Long-Term Growth vs. Short-Term Needs**
   - Fund long-term goals in tax-advantaged accounts. Use taxable accounts for more accessible funds, ensuring liquidity where necessary.

### 6. **Consult with a Financial Advisor**
   - Personal circumstances vary, and a financial advisor can tailor a strategy based on your goals, timeline, and tax situation.

Regularly review and adjust your allocations as your financial situation and market conditions change.
