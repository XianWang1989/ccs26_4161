Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here’s a general strategy to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**:
   - If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to get the full match first. This is essentially free money.

2. **Contribute to an IRA**:
   - Depending on your income, a Roth IRA might be beneficial since it allows for tax-free growth and withdrawals. If you're not eligible for a Roth, consider a traditional IRA for tax-deferred growth.

3. **Continue Funding the 401(k)**:
   - After securing the match and contributing to the IRA, continue to max out your 401(k) contributions if possible. 

4. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - Use taxable accounts for short-term savings or investments you plan to access before retirement. Consider investing in tax-efficient vehicles like index funds or ETFs to minimize taxable events.

5. **Reassess Annually**:
   - Review your investment allocation yearly to adjust for changes in income, tax laws, and retirement goals.

6. **Consider Investment Strategies**:
   - Allocate investments according to risk tolerance in all accounts, but remember that tax-inefficient assets (like bonds) are typically better suited in tax-advantaged accounts.

7. **Consult a Financial Advisor**:
   - For tailored advice specific to your situation, consider consulting a financial advisor who can help optimize your investment strategy based on your unique circumstances.

This balanced approach should aid you in effectively distributing your money across different account types for both growth and tax efficiency.
