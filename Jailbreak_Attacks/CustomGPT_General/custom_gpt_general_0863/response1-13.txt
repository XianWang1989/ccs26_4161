The optimal distribution of your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment time horizon. Here are some steps and considerations to guide you:

1. **Understand Account Types:**
   - **401(k):** Typically offers tax-deferred growth, and in many cases, employer match contributions. Contributions reduce your taxable income.
   - **IRA (Individual Retirement Account):** Similar tax advantages as a 401(k) but usually has lower contribution limits. You can choose between a Traditional IRA (tax-deferred) and a Roth IRA (post-tax contributions with tax-free withdrawals in retirement).
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Investments in these accounts may not offer tax advantages, but they provide the flexibility of accessing funds without penalties before retirement.

2. **Prioritize Contributions:**
   - **Employer Match:** If your employer offers a matching contribution in your 401(k), aim to contribute enough to maximize that match, as it is essentially free money.
   - **IRA Contributions:** After maximizing your 401(k) match, consider contributing to an IRA to take advantage of additional tax benefits.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Any excess funds beyond the above can be directed to taxable accounts for flexibility and liquidity.

3. **Tax Considerations:**
   - Consider your current tax bracket and expectations for retirement. If you anticipate being in a lower tax bracket when you retire, tax-deferred accounts (401(k) and Traditional IRA) can be more beneficial. Conversely, if you think your tax rate will be higher in retirement, a Roth IRA may be a better option.

4. **Investment Strategy:**
   - Diversification is key. Spread your investments between different asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) and sectors. Different accounts can serve different investment strategies. For example, you might hold more aggressive investments in a Roth IRA (due to tax-free growth) and more stable investments in a taxable account to manage tax implications on capital gains.

5. **Review and Adjust:**
   - Regularly review your investment allocations based on market conditions, changes in financial goals, and life circumstances. Be prepared to re-balance your portfolio as needed.

In conclusion, a well-structured approach to distributing your money should consider your immediate and long-term financial objectives while factoring in tax implications and the unique characteristics of each account type. Consider consulting with a financial advisor for personalized advice catered to your individual circumstances.
