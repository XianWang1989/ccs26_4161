Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here are some strategies to consider:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Contributions:**
   - **401(k):** If your employer offers a matching contribution, prioritize contributing enough to your 401(k) to receive the full match. This is essentially "free money."

### 2. **Tax Considerations:**
   - **Traditional IRA vs. Roth IRA:** If you qualify, consider a Roth IRA for tax-free withdrawals in retirement. If you’re in a higher income bracket and expect to be in a lower bracket at retirement, a traditional IRA could be more advantageous since contributions are tax-deductible.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Use these for investments you may need to access before retirement. Consider tax-efficient investments here (e.g., index funds, ETFs) since they are subject to capital gains taxes.

### 3. **Liquidity Needs:**
   - Allocate a portion to taxable accounts if you anticipate needing funds before retirement. Keep an emergency fund in cash or a high-yield savings account.

### 4. **Diversification:**
   - Ensure a diversified mix of assets (stocks, bonds) in all accounts to align with your risk tolerance. Consider your overall asset allocation across all accounts, not just within each.

### 5. **Retirement Timeline:**
   - If you're younger, prioritize growth (more stocks in your 401(k) and IRA). As you approach retirement, shift toward more stable investments (bonds and cash).

### 6. **Regular Review:**
   - Rebalance your portfolio periodically to maintain your desired asset allocation and adjust based on changes in your financial situation or market conditions.

### Example Allocation:
- **401(k):** 15% of salary (up to match)
- **IRA (Roth or Traditional):** Max contribution ($6,500 or $7,500 if over 50)
- **Taxable Investment Account:** Any remaining savings (e.g., 10-20% of income)

### Conclusion
Always consult with a financial advisor to tailor a strategy to your unique circumstances, especially considering the impact of taxes and changes in financial goals.
