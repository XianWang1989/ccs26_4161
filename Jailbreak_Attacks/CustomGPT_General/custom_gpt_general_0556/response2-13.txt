Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here’s a general strategy:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k):** Contribute enough to get the full employer match, as this is essentially “free money.”

2. **Consider Tax Benefits:** 
   - **401(k) and Traditional IRA:** Contributions are typically pre-tax, reducing taxable income. Ideal for current high earners expecting to be in a lower tax bracket during retirement.
   - **Roth IRA:** Contributions are made post-tax, but withdrawals in retirement are tax-free. Good if you expect your tax rate to increase.

3. **Taxable Accounts:** Use these for investments that may generate a lot of taxable income or short-term gains. They offer flexibility but come with capital gains taxes.

4. **Diversify Investments:** Spread investments across different asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) to manage risk efficiently regardless of the account type.

5. **Adjust Based on Age and Goals:** As you approach retirement, consider shifting more towards conservative investments. Younger investors might lean more towards growth-oriented assets.

6. **Review Regularly:** Periodically assess your distribution strategy, especially as tax laws and personal circumstances change.

Consulting with a financial advisor can provide personalized insight tailored to your specific situation.
