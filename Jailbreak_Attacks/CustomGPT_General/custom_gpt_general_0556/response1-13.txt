Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment preferences. Here are some general guidelines to consider:

### 1. **401(k) Accounts:**
   - **Employer Matching:** Maximize contributions to your 401(k) to take advantage of any employer match, as this is free money.
   - **Contribution Limits:** For 2024, the contribution limit is $23,000 (or $30,500 for those aged 50 and over). Contribute as much as you can within these limits, especially if you're in a higher tax bracket.

### 2. **IRA Accounts:**
   - **Roth vs. Traditional:** Determine which type of IRA suits you best. A Roth IRA allows for tax-free withdrawals in retirement, while contributions to a Traditional IRA may be tax-deductible.
   - **Contribution Limits:** For 2024, the limit is $6,500 (or $7,500 for age 50 and over). If you are eligible, consider fully funding your IRA after maxing out your 401(k) matching contributions.

### 3. **Taxable Accounts:**
   - **Flexibility:** Taxable accounts provide liquidity and flexibility for short-term financial goals. Invest here after maximizing tax-advantaged accounts.
   - **Tax Efficiency:** Focus on tax-efficient investments, such as index funds or ETFs, to minimize capital gains taxes.

### 4. **General Strategy:**
   - **Prioritize Contributions:** Start with the 401(k) up to the employer match, then contribute to an IRA, and finally invest in taxable accounts as needed.
   - **Diversification:** Ensure your investments across all accounts are diversified to align with your risk tolerance and time horizon.

### 5. **Review Regularly:**
   - Periodically assess your allocations and adjust based on changes in income, goals, and market conditions.

### Conclusion:
Start by maximizing contributions to tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) and IRA) before investing in taxable accounts. The right mix will depend on your individual circumstances, so consider consulting a financial advisor for tailored advice.
