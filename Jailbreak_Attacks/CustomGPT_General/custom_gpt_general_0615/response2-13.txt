The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your individual financial goals, time horizon, and tax situation. Here are some general strategies to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Matching**: If your employer offers a 401(k) match, contribute enough to get the full match first. This is essentially free money.

2. **Contribute to Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**: Focus on maxing out contributions to tax-advantaged accounts like a 401(k) and IRA before putting money into taxable accounts. For 2023, you can contribute up to $22,500 to a 401(k) (or $30,000 if you're over 50) and up to $6,500 to a traditional or Roth IRA (or $7,500 if you're over 50).

3. **Consider Tax Implications**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are often tax-deductible, reducing your taxable income. However, withdrawals in retirement are taxed as ordinary income.
   - **IRA**: Traditional IRAs provide tax deductions on contributions, whereas Roth IRAs offer tax-free withdrawals in retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Investments here are taxed on capital gains and dividends, so consider placing investments that produce less taxable income (like index funds) in these accounts.

4. **Diversification**: Ensure that your investment choices across all accounts are well-diversified. This may include a mix of stocks, bonds, and other assets aligned with your risk tolerance.

5. **Asset Location**: Place investments that generate higher taxes (like bonds or actively managed funds) in tax-advantaged accounts and keep tax-efficient investments (like stock ETFs) in taxable accounts.

6. **Review and Rebalance**: Regularly review your asset allocation and make adjustments as needed based on changes in your financial situation, goals, or market conditions.

7. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: If you're unsure, consider consulting a financial professional who can provide personalized advice based on your specific circumstances.

Remember, the ideal distribution varies for each individual, so tailor your strategy to fit your unique financial goals.
