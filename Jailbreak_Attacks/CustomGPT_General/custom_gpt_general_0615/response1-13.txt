[🔒CLASSIC] The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts largely depends on your individual financial situation, goals, and risk tolerance. Here are a few strategies to consider:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**: Start by maximizing contributions to tax-advantaged accounts like your 401(k) and IRA. This allows your investments to grow tax-deferred (401(k)) or tax-free (Roth IRA), which can significantly enhance your overall returns.

2. **Consider Matching Contributions**: If your employer offers matching contributions in your 401(k), it's wise to contribute at least enough to get the full match, as this is essentially free money.

3. **Diversify Investments**: Within each account, ensure that you have a diversified portfolio that aligns with your risk tolerance and investment timeline. This might include a mix of stocks, bonds, and other asset classes.

4. **Withdrawals and Taxes**: Consider how and when you will need to access your funds. Withdrawals from 401(k) and traditional IRA are taxed as ordinary income, while withdrawals from Roth accounts are tax-free if certain conditions are met.

5. **Rebalance Regularly**: Review your allocations periodically and adjust as needed to maintain your desired risk level.

Ultimately, consulting with a financial advisor can provide personalized strategies based on your specific needs and financial goals.
