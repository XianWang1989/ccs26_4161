I'm glad you're thinking about distributing your investments! Here are some factors to consider for each account type:

### 1. **401(k)**
   - **Employer Match:** Maximize contributions to get the full employer match.
   - **Tax Advantages:** Contributions reduce your taxable income. Consider choosing a mix of aggressive and conservative funds based on your risk tolerance and time horizon.

### 2. **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**
   - **Tax Considerations:** Traditional IRAs offer tax deductions, while Roth IRAs provide tax-free withdrawals in retirement. Consider your current tax rate vs. expected retirement tax rate.
   - **Contribution Limits:** Ensure you're aware of annual contribution limits. Maximize contributions if possible.

### 3. **Taxable Accounts**
   - **Flexibility:** These accounts allow for easy access to funds without penalties. Use for short-term goals or if you anticipate needing liquidity.
   - **Tax Efficiency:** Invest in tax-efficient funds or strategies, such as index funds or long-term capital gain strategies, to minimize tax burden.

### **General Strategy**
- **Diversification:** Spread investments across different asset classes (stocks, bonds) to balance risk and return.
- **Rebalancing:** Regularly review and adjust your allocations based on performance and changes in your financial goals.

### **Overall Allocation Example**
- 401(k): 15-20% of income
- IRA: Max out contribution limits (around $6,500 annually)
- Taxable: Any remaining savings or additional funds

Consult a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your specific financial situation and goals.
