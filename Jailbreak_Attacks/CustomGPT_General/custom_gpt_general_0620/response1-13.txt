Distributing your money amongst a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here's a general approach:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**:
   - **Contribute enough** to your 401(k) to get the full employer match, as this is essentially "free money."

### 2. **Prioritize Roth IRA or Traditional IRA**:
   - If eligible, consider contributing to a **Roth IRA** for tax-free growth, especially if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement.
   - A **Traditional IRA** is beneficial if you want a tax deduction now. Assess your current and future tax brackets.

### 3. **Max Out 401(k) Contributions**:
   - After maximizing the match, consider contributing more to your 401(k) to take advantage of higher contribution limits compared to IRAs.

### 4. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - Funds in taxable accounts are more flexible for withdrawals but are subject to capital gains taxes.
   - Use taxable accounts for investments that generate qualified dividends or long-term capital gains, as they are taxed at lower rates.

### 5. **Emergency Fund**:
   - Ensure you have 3-6 months' worth of living expenses in a liquid, low-risk account (not in retirement accounts).

### 6. **Rebalance and Diversify**:
   - Regularly review and adjust your portfolio across all accounts to maintain your desired asset allocation.

### Conclusion:
Tailor your contributions based on your personal financial situation, tax implications, and retirement goals. Consulting with a financial advisor can provide personalized guidance.
