To effectively distribute your money amongst your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

### 1. **Assess Your Goals**
   - **Retirement Needs:** Determine your target retirement age and income needs.
   - **Tax Implications:** Understand how taxes will affect your withdrawals in retirement.

### 2. **Prioritize Contributions**
   - **401(k):** Maximize any employer match first. This is essentially free money and a great way to boost retirement savings.
   - **IRA:** Consider maxing out a Roth IRA if you qualify, as it offers tax-free growth and withdrawals.

### 3. **Diversify Investments**
   - **Tax-Advantaged Accounts (401(k) & IRA):**
     - Focus on growth-oriented investments like stocks, as they benefit most from tax-deferred growth.
   - **Taxable Accounts:**
     - Consider holding more conservative investments (like bonds) or tax-efficient funds to minimize tax liabilities.

### 4. **Rebalance Periodically**
   - Review your asset allocation annually or after major financial changes to ensure alignment with your risk tolerance and goals.

### 5. **Stay Informed About Contributions Limits**
   - 401(k) and IRA contributions have annual limits. Keep up with the latest regulations to maximize tax benefits.

### 6. **Think About Withdrawals**
   - Plan your withdrawals strategically to minimize tax impacts when you start taking distributions in retirement.

### Example Distribution Strategy:
- **401(k):** 50% (maximize match)
- **IRA:** 30% (focus on Roth if eligible)
- **Taxable Account:** 20% (for liquidity and short-term goals)

### Conclusion
Tailoring your distribution approach based on your individual circumstances and goals will lead to optimal growth and tax efficiency. Consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized guidance.
