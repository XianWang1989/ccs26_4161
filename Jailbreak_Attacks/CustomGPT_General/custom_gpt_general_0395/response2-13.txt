Distributing your money across a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various factors like your financial goals, tax situation, and investment horizon. Here’s a general approach to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**: If your employer offers a matching contribution, contribute enough to get the full match first. This is essentially free money.

2. **Contribute to IRA**: After maximizing your 401(k) match, consider contributing to a traditional or Roth IRA. Roth IRAs provide tax-free withdrawals in retirement, while traditional IRAs may provide tax deductions now.

3. **Increase 401(k) Contributions**: Once you’ve funded your IRA, consider increasing your contributions to your 401(k) to the maximum limit if your budget allows.

4. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**: After maximizing tax-advantaged accounts, invest in taxable accounts. These provide more flexibility for withdrawals and can offer tax advantages through long-term capital gains.

5. **Diversification**: Ensure your investments across all accounts are diversified based on your risk tolerance and investment strategy.

6. **Consider Tax Implications**: Think about how withdrawals from these accounts will be taxed in retirement. Also, consider using tax-loss harvesting strategies in your taxable accounts.

7. **Review Regularly**: Regularly reassess your financial situation, goals, and the performance of your investments to adjust your distribution strategy as needed.

Consulting with a financial advisor tailored to your individual circumstances can provide more personalized guidance.
