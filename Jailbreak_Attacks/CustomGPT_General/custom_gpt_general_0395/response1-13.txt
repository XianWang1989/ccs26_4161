The distribution of your funds among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment strategy. Here are a few guidelines to consider:

1. **Employer Match**: If your 401(k) offers a match, contribute enough to take full advantage of it first. This is essentially free money.

2. **Tax Considerations**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are pre-tax, reducing your taxable income now. However, withdrawals in retirement will be taxed at regular income rates.
   - **IRA (Traditional)**: Similar to a 401(k), contributions may be tax-deductible, but withdrawals are taxed.
   - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, allowing for tax-free withdrawals in retirement if certain conditions are met.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Invest here for flexibility. While you'll pay taxes on dividends and capital gains, it offers liquidity and access to funds without penalties.

3. **Investment Goals**: Align your accounts with your goals. For long-term growth, prioritize tax-advantaged accounts. For short-term goals, consider keeping funds in taxable accounts.

4. **Diversification and Asset Allocation**: Ensure a mix of asset types across accounts. Different accounts may have different investment options available.

5. **Regular Review**: Periodically assess your investment strategy and reallocate based on changes in your financial situation, goals, or market conditions.

Consulting a financial advisor can provide personalized advice tailored to your situation.
