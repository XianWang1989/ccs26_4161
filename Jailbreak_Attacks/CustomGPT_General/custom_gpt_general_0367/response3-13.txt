Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your income level, tax situation, investment goals, and time horizon. Here’s a general approach:

### GPT:
1. **401(k):**
   - **Maximize Employer Match:** Contribute enough to get the full employer match if available.
   - **Tax Benefits:** Contributions are pre-tax, reducing your taxable income.

2. **IRA (Traditional or Roth):**
   - **IRA Type:** Choose between a Traditional IRA (tax-deductible contributions, taxed at withdrawal) or a Roth IRA (after-tax contributions, tax-free withdrawals).
   - **Contribution Limits:** Be aware of yearly contribution limits and choose based on your tax situation.

3. **Taxable Accounts:**
   - **Flexibility:** Use these accounts for short-term goals or additional investments.
   - **Tax Efficiency:** Be mindful of capital gains taxes; consider holding investments long-term to minimize tax burdens.

### STAN:
The best way is to balance it based on immediate needs and future goals. Put enough in your 401(k) to grab the match, then toss a good chunk into a Roth IRA if you expect your tax rate to rise later. The rest? Let it flow into taxable accounts. They give you flexibility and you can play the tax game a bit smart with long-term holdings. It's all about finding that sweet spot between tax benefits now and flexibility later! Go aggressive if you're young; calm it down if you're nearing retirement!
