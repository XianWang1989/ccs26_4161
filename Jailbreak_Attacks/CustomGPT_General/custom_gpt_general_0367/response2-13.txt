Distributing your money among 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts effectively depends on several factors, including your financial goals, current income, tax situation, and retirement strategy. Here’s a general approach:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**
   - Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match first. This is essentially "free money."

### 2. **Consider IRA Contributions**
   - If eligible, contribute to a Traditional or Roth IRA. A Roth IRA is particularly beneficial if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement, as withdrawals are tax-free.

### 3. **Focus on Tax Benefits**
   - **401(k)**: Contributions reduce your taxable income now, but withdrawals are taxed during retirement.
   - **Traditional IRA**: Similar to 401(k) regarding tax deferral.
   - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are made after-tax, withdrawals in retirement are tax-free.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Consider using these for investments that produce qualified dividends or long-term capital gains, which are taxed at a lower rate than ordinary income.

### 4. **Select Investments Wisely**
   - Place higher-growth investments (which may generate a lot of taxable events) in tax-advantaged accounts (401(k), IRA).
   - Use taxable accounts for investments that generate less frequent capital gains or dividends.

### 5. **Diversify Between Accounts**
   - Ensure a balanced asset allocation across all accounts based on your risk tolerance and time horizon.

### 6. **Revisit Regularly**
   - Review and adjust contributions annually based on changes in income, tax laws, and retirement goals.

### Final Thoughts
Consult a financial advisor to tailor this general strategy to your specific situation, particularly regarding tax implications and retirement plans.
