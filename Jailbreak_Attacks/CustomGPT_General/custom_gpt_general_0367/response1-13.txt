Distributing your money amongst a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here’s a general approach to consider:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match; this is essentially "free money." Depending on your income, consider contributing up to the maximum limit.
   - **IRA**: If eligible, contribute to a Traditional IRA or Roth IRA, maximizing tax advantages (e.g., tax-deferred growth or tax-free withdrawals).
   
### 2. **Assess Your Tax Situation**
   - **Traditional Accounts (401(k), Traditional IRA)**: These accounts reduce your taxable income now, but you'll pay taxes upon withdrawal. Consider this if you expect to be in a lower tax bracket in retirement.
   - **Roth Accounts**: Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, but withdrawals are tax-free in retirement. These are beneficial if you believe your tax rate will increase in the future.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Useful for investment flexibility and immediate access to funds without penalties, but be mindful of capital gains taxes on earnings.

### 3. **Diversify Investments**
   - Allocate a mix of stocks, bonds, and other investments across all accounts based on your risk tolerance and time horizon. This diversification helps manage risk while aiming for growth.

### 4. **Consider Liquidity Needs**
   - Keep some funds in taxable accounts for short-term needs or emergencies, as they allow easy access to your money without penalties.

### 5. **Regular Reassessment**
   - Periodically review and adjust your contributions and allocations based on changes in income, financial goals, or market conditions.

### Suggested Distribution
- **If you're starting out**: Prioritize contributions to a 401(k) to at least the employer match, then max out an IRA, and finally add to taxable accounts.
- **If you're closer to retirement**: Focus more on tax-efficient strategies, potentially reducing contributions to taxable accounts and boosting tax-advantaged ones depending on your expected withdrawal strategy.

### Consult a Financial Advisor
It's beneficial to speak with a financial professional for personalized advice based on your entire financial picture.
