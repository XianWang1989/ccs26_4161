Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including tax implications, your retirement goals, and investment time horizon. Here’s a general strategy:

### 1. Maximize Employer Match
- **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get the full employer match. This is essentially "free money."

### 2. Fund Tax-Advantaged Accounts
- **IRA**: Contribute to a traditional or Roth IRA, depending on your tax situation. Roth IRAs offer tax-free withdrawals in retirement, while traditional IRAs provide upfront tax deductions.

### 3. Taxable Accounts
- After maximizing retirement accounts, consider taxable accounts for additional investments. These allow for flexibility in withdrawals, but dividends and capital gains are taxed.

### 4. Asset Allocation
- Adjust your investment strategy based on account type:
  - Place high-growth investments in tax-advantaged accounts to defer taxes.
  - Use taxable accounts for investments that yield lower taxes (like municipal bonds).

### 5. Consider Your Tax Bracket
- Be mindful of how contributions will impact your current and future tax brackets. Balancing tax exposure now and in retirement is key.

### 6. Regular Reassessment
- Periodically evaluate your distribution strategy as financial situations and goals evolve.
