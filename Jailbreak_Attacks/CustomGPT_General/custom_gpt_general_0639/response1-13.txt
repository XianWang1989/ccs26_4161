Distributing money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your retirement goals, tax situation, and investment options. Here are some general guidelines:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match, as this is free money.

2. **Contribute to IRA**: If you qualify, consider contributing to a traditional or Roth IRA. Roth IRAs offer tax-free withdrawals in retirement, while traditional IRAs provide tax deductions now.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: Invest in taxable accounts for more flexible access to funds. Use it for emergency savings or short-term goals.

4. **Asset Allocation**: Within each account type, diversify your investments according to your risk tolerance and timeline. Typically, you might hold more aggressive investments in tax-advantaged accounts and more stable investments in taxable accounts to manage taxes effectively.

5. **Tax Considerations**: Consider tax implications when withdrawing funds. Typically, it's advisable to withdraw from taxable accounts first, then tax-deferred accounts, and lastly tax-free accounts (like Roth IRAs) in retirement.

6. **Rebalance Regularly**: Ensure that your overall asset allocation aligns with your investment strategy and risk tolerance over time.
