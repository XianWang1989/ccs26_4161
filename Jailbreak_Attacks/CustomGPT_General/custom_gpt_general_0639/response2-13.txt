Distributing your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various factors, such as your financial goals, tax situation, and investment strategy. Here are some guidelines to consider:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match first; it's free money.
   - **IRA**: Consider maxing out your IRA next, especially if it's a Roth IRA, which offers tax-free growth.

2. **Assess Tax Implications**:
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for investments that generate lower taxable income (like index funds) and to access funds without penalties.

3. **Diversify Investments**:
   - Balance your portfolio across these accounts, but consider holding higher-growth investments in tax-advantaged accounts to minimize taxes on gains.

4. **Consider Your Time Horizon**:
   - If you’re younger and are investing for the long term, lean more towards growth-oriented investments in 401(k) and IRA.

5. **Evaluate Fees and Expenses**:
   - Pay attention to investment fees, especially in your 401(k), as they can eat into returns.

6. **Revisit Regularly**:
   - Review your allocations annually or when your financial situation changes.
