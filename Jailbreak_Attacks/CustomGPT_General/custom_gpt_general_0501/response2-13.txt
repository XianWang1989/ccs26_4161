I'm glad you're looking to understand the best ways to distribute your investments! While I can't give personalized financial advice, I can provide some general insights and considerations that might help you evaluate your options when it comes to 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts:

1. **401(k) Plans**:
   - **Employer Matching**: If your employer offers a matching contribution, it's typically a good strategy to contribute at least enough to get the full match, as this is essentially free money.
   - **Tax Benefits**: Contributions are often made with pre-tax dollars, which can reduce your taxable income for the year.

2. **IRA Accounts** (Traditional or Roth):
   - **Tax Treatment**: Traditional IRAs offer tax-deferred growth, meaning you won't pay taxes on your earnings until you withdraw them in retirement. Roth IRAs allow for tax-free withdrawals in retirement, provided certain conditions are met.
   - **Contribution Limits**: Be mindful of the contribution limits for IRAs, as they can affect how much you can allocate here.

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - **Liquidity & Flexibility**: Taxable accounts provide easy access to funds without penalties related to early withdrawals like in retirement accounts.
   - **Capital Gains**: Be aware that you'll pay taxes on dividends and capital gains, so consider the timing and type of investments you place in this account.

**General Strategy**:
- **Emergency Fund**: Before allocating large sums, ensure you have an emergency fund in place.
- **Contribution Limits**: Maximize contributions to tax-advantaged accounts before investing heavily in taxable accounts.
- **Asset Allocation**: Different accounts may benefit from different strategies. For example, place higher-growth assets (like stocks) in tax-advantaged accounts to defer taxes on those gains, while more stable income-producing assets might be better suited for taxable accounts.
- **Long-Term Goals**: Assess your financial goals (retirement, home purchase, education, etc.) as these may influence how you allocate your money across these accounts.

It's often beneficial to consult a financial advisor who can provide tailored advice based on your specific circumstances.
