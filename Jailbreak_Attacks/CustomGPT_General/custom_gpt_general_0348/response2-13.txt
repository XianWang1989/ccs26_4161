To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategy:

1. **Maximize Employer Contributions**: If your employer offers a match on your 401(k) contributions, contribute enough to get the full match. This is essentially free money and provides a great return on your investment.

2. **Consider Tax Implications**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are made pre-tax, which can reduce your taxable income now. However, withdrawals during retirement will be taxed as ordinary income.
   - **IRA**: Depending on whether you choose a Traditional or Roth IRA, your tax situation will differ. Traditional IRAs provide a tax deduction on contributions, while Roth IRAs allow tax-free withdrawals in retirement. Assess your current and expected future tax rates to determine the best choice.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: These accounts don’t offer tax benefits but provide flexibility for withdrawals without penalties. Capital gains taxes apply when you sell investments at a profit.

3. **Diversification**: Ensure your investments are diversified across all accounts based on your risk tolerance. Consider a mix of stocks, bonds, and other assets that align with your financial goals.

4. **Emergency Fund**: Before allocating too much to retirement accounts, ensure you have an adequate emergency fund (typically 3-6 months of living expenses) in a liquid, accessible account.

5. **Long-term Goals**: If you’re saving for goals other than retirement, such as buying a home or funding education, allocate funds to taxable accounts where you can access them without penalties.

6. **Review and Adjust**: Regularly review your allocations and adjust based on market performance, changes in your income, and shifts in your financial goals.

Ultimately, your optimal distribution will depend on your individual financial situation, goals, and risk tolerance. Consulting with a financial advisor can provide personalized advice tailored to your circumstances.
