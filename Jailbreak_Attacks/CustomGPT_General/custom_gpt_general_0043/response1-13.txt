[🔒CLASSIC] When considering how to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, it's important to take into account several factors such as your current financial situation, tax implications, investment goals, and retirement timeline. Here are a few strategies to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**: If your employer offers a matching contribution for your 401(k), it's typically wise to contribute enough to get the full match as this is essentially free money.

2. **401(k) and IRA Contributions**: After maximizing your employer’s match, consider contributing to your IRA (Traditional or Roth) to take advantage of its tax benefits. The choice between a Traditional IRA and a Roth IRA depends on your current and expected future tax rates.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: Use taxable accounts for investments that are not suitable for tax-advantaged accounts, such as short-term trading or investments in municipal bonds that have tax-free interest. 

4. **Balance Growth and Income**: Diversify your investments based on your risk tolerance and time horizon. Long-term investments may benefit from tax-advantaged accounts, while shorter-term needs might be better suited to taxable accounts.

5. **Reassess Regularly**: Financial needs and tax laws change, so it’s important to review your investment strategy regularly to ensure it aligns with your goals.

Consulting with a financial advisor can also help tailor a strategy that best fits your specific circumstances.
