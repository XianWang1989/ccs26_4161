The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts can depend on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and time horizon. Here are some general strategies to consider:

1. **Maximize Contributions to Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - Prioritize contributing to your 401(k) if your employer offers a match. This is essentially "free money" that can significantly boost your retirement savings.
   - If you're eligible, contribute to an IRA (either traditional or Roth) as they offer tax advantages that can enhance your savings growth.

2. **Consider Tax Implications**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are often made pre-tax, reducing your taxable income in the year you contribute. Withdrawals in retirement are taxed as ordinary income.
   - **Traditional IRA**: Similar to the 401(k), contributions may be tax-deductible, but withdrawals are taxed.
   - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, meaning withdrawals (including earnings) are tax-free in retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Earnings from investments in taxable accounts are subject to capital gains tax. Consider placing investments with high expected returns (which might lead to higher capital gains) in tax-advantaged accounts instead.

3. **Asset Allocation**:
   - Different asset types might be more suitable for specific accounts. For instance, consider housing bonds in tax-advantaged accounts and stocks in taxable accounts to minimize tax liabilities on your more volatile investments.

4. **Manage Withdrawal Strategy**:
   - Plan for how you’ll withdraw from these accounts in retirement, balancing withdrawals from taxable and tax-deferred accounts to manage your tax bracket. 

5. **Rebalance Your Portfolio**:
   - Regularly review and adjust your asset allocation based on your risk tolerance, retirement timeline, and market conditions.

It's always a good idea to consult with a financial advisor who can provide personalized guidance based on your specific situation and goals.
