Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts can optimize your investment strategy and tax efficiency. Here’s a general guideline:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get any employer match—this is essentially "free money."

2. **Contribute to IRA**: If eligible, max out contributions to a traditional or Roth IRA, depending on your tax situation and retirement goals. This offers tax advantages, either now or in retirement.

3. **Back to 401(k)**: After maxing your IRA, contribute more to your 401(k), especially if you have a high-income years or expect to be in a lower tax bracket in retirement.

4. **Taxable Accounts**: Use taxable accounts for investments that have a lower potential tax impact, like long-term capital gains and qualified dividends. This can be a good space for extra savings and investments.

5. **Diversification**: Diversify your asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) across all accounts to manage risk and returns.

6. **Rebalance Regularly**: Keep an eye on your asset allocation and rebalance as needed to maintain your investment strategy.

7. **Consider Taxes**: Be mindful of how withdrawals from each account will be taxed in retirement. Plan your withdrawals strategically to minimize taxes.

Always consider consulting with a financial advisor for personalized advice based on your specific situation.
