Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your individual financial goals, tax situation, and time horizon. Here are some general strategies:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**
   - **401(k):** Contribute enough to get any employer match, as it's essentially free money. Aim to reach the annual contribution limit if possible.
   - **IRA:** After maximizing your 401(k) match, consider contributing to a traditional or Roth IRA, depending on your income level and tax situation.

### 2. **Consider Tax Implications**
   - **Traditional 401(k) and IRA:** Contributions are tax-deferred; you pay taxes on withdrawals during retirement. Ideal for higher-income earners who expect to be in a lower tax bracket in retirement.
   - **Roth 401(k) and IRA:** Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, but withdrawals are tax-free in retirement. Good for younger investors or those expecting higher tax rates in the future.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Suitable for short-term goals or if you've maxed out tax-advantaged accounts. Be mindful of capital gains taxes on investments.

### 3. **Investment Strategy**
   - **401(k) and IRA:** Focus on long-term growth investments, like stocks or mutual funds, since you won't need access to these funds for many years.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Consider a mix of lower-turnover investments, like index funds, to minimize capital gains taxes.

### 4. **Rebalancing**
   - Regularly review and rebalance your portfolio to maintain your desired asset allocation across all accounts.

### 5. **Emergency Fund**
   - Ensure you have a separate emergency fund before heavily investing in these accounts. This fund should cover 3-6 months of expenses and be easily accessible.

### Summary
Maximize contributions to tax-advantaged accounts based on your match and income level, consider tax implications, invest appropriately based on your time horizon, and ensure you have an emergency fund in place. Adjust your strategy as your financial situation and goals evolve.
