Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts requires a strategic approach based on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are the best practices:

### 1. **Prioritize Contributions:**
   - **401(k)**: If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to get the full match first. This is essentially "free money."
   - **IRA**: If you can, max out contributions to a traditional or Roth IRA, depending on your tax situation and eligibility.

### 2. **Consider Tax Implications:**
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are often pre-tax, lowering your taxable income. Withdrawals in retirement are taxed as ordinary income.
   - **Traditional IRA**: Similar tax treatment as 401(k).
   - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, but withdrawals in retirement are tax-free.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Ideal for funds you might need before retirement, but be mindful of capital gains taxes.

### 3. **Asset Allocation:**
   - Focus on growth-oriented investments (stocks) in your tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) and IRA) since they benefit most from tax deferral.
   - Use more conservative investments (bonds) in taxable accounts to minimize capital gains.

### 4. **Emergency Fund & Short-Term Goals:**
   - Maintain an emergency fund in a high-yield savings account.
   - If saving for short-term goals (within 5 years), focus on low or no-risk investments in taxable accounts.

### 5. **Review Regularly:**
   - Periodically reassess your allocation based on performance and any changes in your financial situation or goals.

By prioritizing contributions, considering tax implications, optimizing asset allocation, and regularly reviewing your strategy, you can effectively distribute your money among these accounts to meet your long-term financial goals.
