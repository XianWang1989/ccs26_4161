Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement plans. Here’s a general approach:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**:
   - Contribute enough to receive any employer match, as this is essentially free money.

### 2. **Contribute to an IRA**:
   - If eligible, consider contributing to a Traditional IRA (tax-deductible) or Roth IRA (tax-free growth).

### 3. **Increase 401(k) Contributions**:
   - After maxing out the employer match, increase your contributions to the 401(k) to its limit if possible.

### 4. **Consider Taxable Accounts**:
   - Use taxable accounts for investments not suited for retirement accounts (e.g., short-term goals or high-growth stocks).

### 5. **Diversify Investments**:
   - Ensure a diversified portfolio across all accounts, balancing between stocks, bonds, and other assets.

### 6. **Tax Efficiency**:
   - Place tax-efficient investments (like index funds) in taxable accounts, and consider tax-inefficient investments (like bonds) in tax-advantaged accounts.

### 7. **Adjust Based on Age**:
   - As you age, shift towards more conservative investments, especially in accounts intended for retirement.

### 8. **Review Regularly**:
   - Reassess your allocations periodically to ensure they align with your goals and market conditions.

Consulting a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your situation is also a good idea.
