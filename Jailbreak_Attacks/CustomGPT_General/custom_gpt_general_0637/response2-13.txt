To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider these strategies:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to take full advantage of any employer match. This is essentially free money.
   - **IRA**: Consider using a traditional IRA for tax-deferred growth or a Roth IRA for tax-free withdrawals in retirement, depending on your current tax situation.

2. **Balance Contributions**:
   - After maximizing the employer match in your 401(k), split contributions between your IRA and your 401(k) based on limits and your tax strategy.

3. **Investment Strategy**:
   - Use more aggressive investments in tax-advantaged accounts (like stocks) for long-term growth, while placing more conservative investments (like bonds) in taxable accounts to manage tax implications.

4. **Tax Efficiency**:
   - In taxable accounts, focus on tax-efficient investments (like index funds or ETFs) that generate less taxable income.
   - Consider holding tax-inefficient assets, like REITs or actively managed funds, in your tax-advantaged accounts.

5. **Withdrawal Strategy**:
   - Plan your withdrawals strategically in retirement to minimize taxes. Generally, withdrawing from taxable accounts first can help prolong tax-deferred growth in retirement accounts.

6. **Rebalancing**:
   - Regularly review and rebalance your asset allocation to maintain your risk tolerance and investment goals.

Consulting with a financial advisor can also provide personalized guidance based on your specific financial situation and goals.
