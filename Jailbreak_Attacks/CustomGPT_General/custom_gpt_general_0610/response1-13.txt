Developing a strategy for distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts involves considering several factors, including your financial goals, tax implications, and retirement timeline. Here’s a general approach:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your 401(k) offers an employer match, contribute enough to get the full match first. This is essentially "free money."

2. **Fund IRAs**: After taking advantage of the 401(k) match, consider contributing to a Roth or traditional IRA. Roth IRAs can offer tax-free withdrawals in retirement, while traditional IRAs provide tax deductions upfront.

3. **Continue 401(k) Contributions**: If you have additional funds, continue contributing to your 401(k) up to the annual limit. This helps you save for retirement with tax-deferred growth.

4. **Taxable Accounts**: Use taxable accounts for investments that are more tax-efficient, such as stocks or mutual funds that pay qualified dividends. This helps minimize your tax burden.

5. **Diversify and Rebalance**: Maintain a diversified portfolio across all accounts. Regularly rebalance based on your risk tolerance and time horizon.

6. **Consider Tax Implications**: Be mindful of how withdrawals from each account type will affect your tax situation in retirement.

7. **Emergency Fund**: Ensure you have an adequate emergency fund outside of these accounts for unexpected expenses.

Always consider consulting with a financial advisor to tailor your investments to your specific situation.
