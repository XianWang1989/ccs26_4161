Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeframe. Here are some general guidelines:

### 1. **401(k) Plan**
- **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to get the full employer match, as this is essentially free money.
- **Pre-Tax Benefits**: Contributions reduce your taxable income, which is beneficial if you're in a higher tax bracket.

### 2. **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**
- **Tax Considerations**:
  - **Traditional IRA**: Contributions may be tax-deductible; taxes are deferred until withdrawal.
  - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are made after-tax, but withdrawals are tax-free in retirement.
- **Contribution Limits**: Consider maxing out contributions, especially if you qualify for deductions.

### 3. **Taxable Accounts**
- **Flexibility**: These accounts allow easy access to funds without penalties, making them suitable for short-term goals or emergencies.
- **Tax Efficiency**: Invest in tax-efficient funds (like index funds) to minimize taxable distributions, and consider capital gains implications when selling investments.

### **General Strategy**
1. **Diversify Across Accounts**: Maintain a balanced asset allocation in all accounts, taking advantage of tax advantages where appropriate.
2. **Fund Growth in Roth Accounts**: If you expect high growth, consider placing those investments in Roth accounts for tax-free growth.
3. **Bond Investments in Tax-Deferred Accounts**: Bonds can generate interest income, which is taxable; placing them in tax-deferred accounts might be beneficial.
4. **Rebalance Periodically**: Regularly assess and adjust your portfolio to maintain your desired asset allocation.

### **Consult a Financial Advisor**
Consider speaking with a financial advisor to tailor the strategy to your specific circumstances, risk tolerance, and retirement timeline.
