Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here’s a general strategy you might consider:

### 1. Maximize Employer Match
- **401(k):** Contribute enough to get the full employer match, if available, as this is essentially free money.

### 2. Consider Tax Advantages
- **IRA:** If eligible, contribute to a Roth IRA for tax-free growth and tax-free withdrawals in retirement, or a Traditional IRA for tax-deductible contributions.

### 3. Assess Your Tax Situation
- **Taxable Accounts:** After maxing out tax-advantaged accounts, consider a taxable account for additional investing, particularly for short- to medium-term goals.

### 4. Balance Contributions
- Prioritize contributions based on:
  - **Interest Rates:** If your 401(k) has high fees or limited investment options, consider directing more to an IRA or taxable account.
  - **Withdrawal Flexibility:** Taxable accounts offer more flexibility for accessing funds without penalties.

### 5. Rebalance Regularly
- Periodically assess and adjust your investments based on market performance and changing goals.

### 6. Consult a Financial Advisor
- For personalized advice, consider speaking with a financial advisor who can tailor recommendations based on your specific situation.

### General Allocation Example
- **401(k):** 10-15% (enough for the match)
- **IRA:** Max out contributions (currently $6,500 or $7,500 if over 50)
- **Taxable Accounts:** 10-20% (after maxing retirement accounts)

Adjust based on your individual circumstances!
