When it comes to distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, the best approach typically involves considering your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here’s a structured strategy:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k):**
   - If your employer offers a matching contribution, contribute at least enough to get the full match. This is essentially “free money” and a guaranteed return on your investment.

### 2. **Contribute to an IRA:**
   - If you’re eligible for a traditional or Roth IRA, consider contributing up to the annual limit. Roth IRAs allow for tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement, while traditional IRAs may provide tax deductions on contributions.

### 3. **Allocate to Taxable Accounts:**
   - If you have surplus funds after maxing out your 401(k) and IRA contributions, consider investing in taxable accounts. This can provide flexibility for liquidity, as money can be accessed without penalties, though you will owe capital gains taxes on earnings.

### 4. **Consider Tax Efficiency:**
   - **401(k) & IRA:** Focus on higher growth investments (like stocks) which can grow tax-deferred in these accounts.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Consider holding more tax-efficient investments (like index funds or ETFs) to reduce your overall tax burden.

### 5. **Diversify Investments:**
   - Ensure your overall investment strategy is diversified across different asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) to manage risk.

### 6. **Review Annually:**
   - Monitor your accounts periodically to ensure your allocation aligns with your evolving goals and market conditions. Adjust your contributions based on changes in your income, financial objectives, or tax situation.

Personalized financial advice can vary significantly, so consider consulting with a financial advisor to tailor a strategy specific to your needs and goals. If you need more specific insights or resources, feel free to ask!
