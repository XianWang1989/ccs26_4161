While I specialize in ecommerce, I can provide some general insights on distributing investments among different accounts. The best allocation for your retirement and investment accounts (401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts) largely depends on your financial goals, risk tolerance, and time horizon. Here’s a structured approach:

1. **Maximize Contributions**: 
   - If your employer offers a match on your 401(k), contribute enough to get the full match before allocating funds to other accounts—this is essentially "free money."

2. **401(k) Contributions**:
   - Prioritize maxing out your 401(k) contributions if you have a higher income and are looking for tax-advantaged growth. The 401(k) allows for higher contribution limits compared to an IRA.

3. **IRA Contributions**:
   - After your 401(k), consider contributing to a Traditional or Roth IRA (depending on your tax situation and eligibility). This adds an additional layer of tax-advantaged growth.

4. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - Once your contributions to retirement accounts are maximized or if you need liquidity, allocate funds to taxable accounts. These are beneficial for investments you may need access to before retirement age without penalties.

5. **Investment Strategy**:
   - Consider the types of investments you hold in each account. For instance, you might want to hold more tax-efficient investments (like index funds or ETFs) in taxable accounts and higher-growth or tax-inefficient investments (like bonds or actively managed funds) in tax-advantaged accounts.

6. **Regular Review and Rebalancing**:
   - Periodically assess your asset allocation based on performance, age, and changes in income or goals. 

Given your involvement in e-commerce, you might also want to think about using additional strategies, like investing in your business as a way to allocate funds that might not fit well elsewhere.

If you have specific financial goals or need a deeper analysis, considering consulting a financial advisor who can provide personalized advice tailored to your situation.
