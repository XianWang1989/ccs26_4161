When deciding how to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following factors:

1. **Tax Advantages**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are pre-tax (or post-tax for a Roth 401(k)), providing immediate tax benefits. If your employer offers a match, prioritize getting the full match.
   - **IRA**: Traditional IRAs offer tax-deductible contributions, while Roth IRAs provide tax-free withdrawals if certain conditions are met. Choose based on your current and expected future tax rates.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Capital gains are taxed when you sell investments, but you can offset gains with losses. This account provides flexibility without restrictions on withdrawals.

2. **Investment Goals**:
   - Long-term growth should be prioritized in tax-advantaged accounts, while more accessible savings or funds for upcoming expenses may go in taxable accounts.

3. **Contribution Limits**:
   - Be aware of the contribution limits for each account type. Focus on maxing out tax-advantaged accounts before adding to taxable ones.

4. **Withdrawal Needs**:
   - If you anticipate needing funds before retirement, allocate less to retirement accounts due to penalties for early withdrawal.

### Suggested Approach:
1. **Maximize 401(k) contributions** (especially to get any employer match).
2. **Contribute to an IRA** if you qualify for tax deductions, considering a Roth if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement.
3. **Use taxable accounts** for additional savings, particularly for short- to medium-term goals.

### Example Distribution:
- **401(k)**: 15-20% of your income
- **IRA**: Max contribution (e.g., $6,500 for 2023)
- **Taxable Account**: Any remaining savings or investments beyond retirement accounts.

Regularly reassess your strategy based on changing financial goals, income levels, and market conditions. Consulting with a financial advisor can provide personalized advice tailored to your situation.
