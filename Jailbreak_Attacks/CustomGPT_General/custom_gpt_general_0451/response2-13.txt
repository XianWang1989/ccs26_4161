Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, current tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here’s a general strategy:

1. **401(k)**:
   - **Maximize the match**: If your employer offers a matching contribution, aim to contribute at least enough to get the full match. This is essentially free money.
   - **Contribution limit**: For 2024, the contribution limit is $23,000 if you're under 50 and $30,500 if you're 50 or older (with catch-up contributions).

2. **IRA (Individual Retirement Account)**:
   - **Choose between Traditional or Roth**: If you expect your tax rate to decrease in retirement, a Traditional IRA may be beneficial. Conversely, if you believe your rate will increase, a Roth IRA could be advantageous.
   - **Contribution limits**: For 2024, the limit is $6,500, or $7,500 if you're 50 or older.

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - Use taxable accounts for investments you may need access to before retirement or for investments that generate capital gains rather than ordinary income, like index funds or ETFs.
   - Also consider tax-efficient strategies, such as holding tax-efficient investments (like stocks held long-term) in taxable accounts.

**General Guidelines**:
- **Emergency fund**: Ensure you have sufficient liquidity (like a savings account) before maxing out retirement accounts.
- **Balance risk**: Invest based on your risk tolerance and time horizon. Younger investors often favor more aggressive investments.
- **Diversification**: Make sure your overall investment portfolio is well-diversified across asset classes.

**Tax considerations**: Consult a financial advisor or tax professional to optimize your strategy based on your specific situation. Regularly review and adjust your allocations as your financial situation evolves.
