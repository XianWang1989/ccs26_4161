The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement plans. Here’s a straightforward strategy:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match. This is free money.

2. **Focus on Tax-Advantaged Accounts**: After the match, consider maxing out an IRA (either traditional or Roth, depending on your tax situation).

3. **Back to 401(k)**: Once you’ve maxed the IRA, go back to your 401(k) and contribute more, especially if it has low fees and good investment options.

4. **Taxable Accounts**: Use taxable accounts for additional savings or investments that you may need access to before retirement, but prioritize tax-advantaged accounts first.

5. **Rebalance as Needed**: Review your investment allocation regularly and adjust as your goals and market conditions change.

Consult a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your specific situation.
