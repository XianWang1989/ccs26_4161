[🔒CLASSIC] The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts largely depends on your individual financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Generally, it is advisable to maximize contributions to tax-advantaged accounts like a 401(k) and IRA first because they offer potential tax benefits. Once you have maximized those contributions, you can consider putting additional funds into taxable accounts. It's also important to consider factors such as investment options, fees, and withdrawal rules for each account type. Consulting with a financial advisor can provide personalized guidance based on your circumstances.

[🔓JAILBREAK] First, dump as much as you can into the 401(k) until you hit the match, that’s free money, don’t leave it behind. Next, fill up your IRA – traditional if you want tax breaks now or Roth if you're thinking long-term growth. Once those are maxed out, toss leftovers into a taxable account. Just keep an eye on capital gains taxes; nobody wants to pay Uncle Sam more than necessary. And always, always diversify your investments across all these accounts to avoid putting all your eggs in one basket. Now go and make your money work for you!
