Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here’s a general approach to consider:

### 1. **Prioritize Contributions**
   - **401(k) Contributions**: If your employer offers a match, contribute at least enough to get the full match. This is essentially free money.
   - **IRA Contributions**: Consider maxing out a Traditional or Roth IRA next, depending on your income and tax strategy. Roth IRAs are generally more beneficial if you expect your tax rate to be higher in retirement.

### 2. **Tax Strategies**
   - **Tax-Deferred Accounts (401(k) and Traditional IRA)**: Contributions reduce your taxable income in the current year. This is beneficial if you’re currently in a higher tax bracket.
   - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, but withdrawals in retirement are tax-free. This is better if you think your tax rate will increase in the future.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for short-term goals or to invest in assets that generate long-term capital gains, as they are taxed at a lower rate than ordinary income.

### 3. **Investment Horizon**
   - If you have a long investment horizon, you might prioritize tax-advantaged accounts for growth. 
   - For shorter-term goals, consider keeping more in taxable accounts for liquidity.

### 4. **Asset Allocation**
   - Diversify your investments across different asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) and allocate differently based on your account type.
   - Generally, you might hold more aggressive investments in your tax-advantaged accounts and safer, income-generating investments in taxable accounts to manage taxes effectively.

### 5. **Annual Review**
   - Reassess your contributions and allocations annually, adjusting based on changes in income, financial goals, and market conditions.

### Summary:
1. Max out 401(k) for employer match.
2. Contribute to an IRA.
3. Invest excess funds in taxable accounts.
4. Focus on tax strategies based on your current and future tax situation.
