Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, risk tolerance, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here are some strategies to consider:

### 1. **Prioritize Employer Match**
   - **401(k)**: If your employer offers a match, prioritize contributing enough to your 401(k) to maximize that match. It's essentially "free money."

### 2. **Consider Tax Advantages**
   - **Tax-Deferred Accounts**: 401(k) and traditional IRA contributions reduce your taxable income. Maximize contributions to these accounts based on your tax situation.
   - **After-Tax Accounts**: Roth IRAs are funded with after-tax income but offer tax-free withdrawals in retirement. If you expect to be in a higher tax bracket when you retire, consider prioritizing Roth contributions.

### 3. **Account Contribution Limits**
   - Be aware of annual contribution limits for each account:
     - **401(k)**: $22,500 for individuals under 50 (2024 limit; check for any updates).
     - **IRA**: $6,500 for individuals under 50 (2024 limit).
   - If you're 50 or older, catch-up contributions are allowed.

### 4. **Diversification and Risk Tolerance**
   - Spread investments across asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) to manage risk. Consider your age, retirement date, and market conditions when deciding the asset allocation in each account.

### 5. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**
   - **Taxable Accounts**: These can be useful for short-term goals or emergency funds since they provide flexibility without penalties for early withdrawals. Be mindful of tax implications on capital gains and dividends.

### 6. **Withdrawal Strategy**
   - Plan for retirement withdrawals strategically:
     - Withdraw from taxable accounts first to allow tax-advantaged accounts to grow longer.
     - Consider tax implications when:
       - Tapping into traditional IRAs or 401(k)s (taxed as ordinary income).
       - Withdrawing from a Roth IRA (contributions can be withdrawn tax-free).

### 7. **Review Regularly**
   - Regularly assess your financial situation and make adjustments based on changes in income, expenses, and tax laws.

### Personalized Approach
Tailor your strategy based on your financial situation and goals, possibly seeking advice from a financial advisor for a customized plan.

This balanced approach helps ensure you maximize growth while managing risk and taxation effectively.
