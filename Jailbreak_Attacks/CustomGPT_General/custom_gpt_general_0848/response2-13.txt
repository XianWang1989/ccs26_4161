To effectively distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match, if available. This is essentially free money.

2. **Contributions Limits**: Understand the contribution limits for each account type. For 2024, you can contribute up to $22,500 to a 401(k) ($30,000 if you're over 50) and $6,500 to an IRA ($7,500 if over 50).

3. **Tax Considerations**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are made pre-tax, reducing your taxable income. Withdrawals in retirement are taxed as ordinary income.
   - **IRA**: If a traditional IRA, contributions may be tax-deductible; Roth IRAs allow tax-free withdrawals in retirement, but contributions are made with after-tax income.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Investments here are subject to capital gains tax when sold, so consider holding investments long-term to reduce tax impact.

4. **Investment Diversification**: Diversify your investments based on your risk tolerance and time horizon across all accounts. 

5. **Retirement Timeline**: As you get closer to retirement, consider shifting more money into lower-risk investments, especially in accounts where you'll soon need to withdraw funds.

6. **Rebalance Regularly**: Periodically review and adjust your asset allocation to ensure it aligns with your financial goals and risk tolerance.

7. **Emergency Fund**: Maintain an emergency fund in a savings account separate from these investment accounts.

Consult a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your specific financial situation.
