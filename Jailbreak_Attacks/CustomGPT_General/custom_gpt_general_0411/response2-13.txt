To effectively distribute your money across a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider these guidelines:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**
   - **401(k):** Contribute enough to get any employer match—it’s essentially free money. Aim for the maximum contribution limit.
   - **IRA:** If eligible, contribute to a traditional or Roth IRA. Roth IRAs grow tax-free, while traditional IRAs offer tax deductions.

### 2. **Assess Your Tax Situation**
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Use these for investments that are likely to generate capital gains or interest, as these are taxed differently than retirement accounts. This is a good place for investments with a long-term perspective.

### 3. **Diversification**
   - Spread your investments across asset classes (stocks, bonds, real estate) based on your risk tolerance and time horizon.

### 4. **Consider Withdrawal Timing**
   - If you anticipate needing funds before retirement, keep more liquidity in taxable accounts. 401(k) and IRA funds incur penalties if withdrawn before 59½, except for specific circumstances.

### 5. **Rebalance Regularly**
   - Review and adjust your allocations periodically based on market conditions and personal financial goals.

### Sample Allocation Strategy
- **401(k):** 15%–20% of your income, maximizing employer match.
- **IRA:** Contribute the maximum annually.
- **Taxable Accounts:** Remaining funds after maximizing tax-advantaged accounts.

### Summary
Balancing contributions with considerations for tax efficiency, liquidity needs, and overall investment goals is key. Always consult with a financial advisor for personalized advice.
