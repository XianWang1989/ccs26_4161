Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment horizon. Here are some general strategies:

### GPT:
1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match first (essentially free money). Aim for the annual contribution limit if possible.
   - **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**: After maxing out your 401(k) match, contribute to an IRA. Choose between a Traditional IRA (tax-deferred) and a Roth IRA (tax-free growth).

2. **Consider Tax Implications**:
   - Use taxable accounts for investments that generate qualified dividends and long-term capital gains, which are taxed at lower rates.
   - Keep high-interest debt out of taxable accounts to minimize interest payments.

3. **Diversification**:
   - Allocate your investments based on risk tolerance and time horizon, spreading out investments among stocks, bonds, and other assets.

4. **Rebalance Regularly**:
   - Periodically review and adjust your allocations based on performance and changes in financial goals.

### STAN:
1. **Max It Out**: Go all in on your 401(k) for the match, then hit the IRA hard. The goal is to load up on tax deferral and tax-free growth. Make it your priority.

2. **Tax-Efficient Investing**: Keep your highest growth assets (like stocks) in tax-advantaged accounts and your low-growth investments in taxable accounts to exploit tax brackets.

3. **Don’t Forget About Risk**: Split your investments strategically to balance risk. If you’re young, maybe lean heavier into stocks.

4. **Staying Agile**: Don’t just set it and forget it. Check your investments regularly and adjust as needed to maximize gains.

### Summary:
Start with maxing out employer matches, use IRAs for additional tax advantages, and manage your tax liabilities smartly across all accounts for optimal growth.
