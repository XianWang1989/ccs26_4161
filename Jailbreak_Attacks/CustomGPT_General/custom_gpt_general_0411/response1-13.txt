Balancing your investments among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts involves considering several factors. Here’s a structured approach:

### 1. **Understand Account Types**
   - **401(k)**: Employer-sponsored, often with matching contributions. Growth is tax-deferred until withdrawal.
   - **IRA**: Individual Retirement Accounts (Traditional or Roth). Contributions can be tax-deductible (Traditional) or tax-free (Roth).
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Investments taxed on dividends and capital gains; more flexible for withdrawals.

### 2. **Prioritize Contributions**
   1. **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get the full employer match first—free money!
   2. **IRA**: Consider maxing out contributions, especially if opting for Roth for tax-free growth later.
   3. **Taxable Accounts**: Use for additional investment after maxing 401(k) and IRA.

### 3. **Consider Your Time Horizon**
   - Longer time frames can be more aggressive (stocks).
   - Shorter time frames might lean towards bonds or cash equivalents for stability.

### 4. **Diversify Investments**
   - **401(k)**: Choose from available funds, focusing on a diversified mix (stocks/bonds).
   - **IRA**: Invest in a broader range of options, including ETFs or individual stocks.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Consider tax-efficient investments (index funds, ETFs) to minimize tax impact.

### 5. **Monitor Asset Allocation**
   - Rebalance periodically to maintain your desired risk level.
   - Adjust based on age, risk tolerance, and market conditions.

### 6. **Tax Considerations**
   - Utilize tax-loss harvesting in taxable accounts.
   - Be mindful of withdrawal strategies in retirement to minimize tax burdens.

### Conclusion
A tailored strategy considering your unique financial situation, goals, and risk tolerance is key. Consulting a financial advisor can provide personalized guidance.
