The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various factors including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment horizon. Here are some general guidelines to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**: If your employer offers a matching contribution, try to contribute enough to your 401(k) to receive the full match. This is essentially free money and provides a strong foundation for your retirement savings.

2. **Consider IRA Contributions**: If you are eligible, consider maxing out contributions to a Traditional or Roth IRA next. Roth IRAs offer tax-free withdrawals in retirement, whereas Traditional IRAs provide tax-deferred growth.

3. **Evaluate Tax Implications**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are often pre-tax, reducing your taxable income for the year. Withdrawals in retirement are taxed as regular income.
   - **IRA**: Contributions to a Traditional IRA may be tax-deductible, while Roth contributions are made with after-tax dollars.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Interest, dividends, and capital gains in these accounts are subject to taxes, so consider using them for investments that generate minimal taxable income.

4. **Asset Allocation**: Different accounts may have different investment strategies. For example, you might want to hold more tax-efficient investments (like index funds) in taxable accounts while reserving bonds or higher-turnover investments for tax-advantaged accounts.

5. **Maintain Liquidity**: Ensure you have enough liquidity for short-term expenses and emergencies. It may be prudent to keep a portion of your assets in a taxable account or cash reserves.

6. **Regular Review and Rebalance**: Your financial situation may change, so it’s essential to review and adjust your allocations periodically. 

7. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: Personal circumstances can vary widely, so consulting with a financial advisor tailored to your unique situation can provide valuable insights.

By considering these factors, you can create a balanced approach to distributing your funds across your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts.
