The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and time horizon. Here are some general guidelines:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**: If your employer offers a match on your 401(k) contributions, contribute enough to take full advantage of this match. This is essentially "free money."

2. **Contribute to IRA**: Depending on your income and eligibility, consider contributing to a Roth IRA or a Traditional IRA. Roth IRAs allow for tax-free withdrawals in retirement, while Traditional IRAs can offer you tax deductions.

3. **Continue 401(k) Contributions**: After maximizing your employer match and contributing to your IRA, continue to contribute to your 401(k) up to the annual limit. This is beneficial for tax-deferred growth.

4. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**: Once you have maximized contributions to your tax-advantaged accounts, consider investing in taxable accounts. These accounts provide more flexibility for withdrawals and investment choices.

5. **Consider Tax Implications**: Keep in mind the tax treatment of each account. Withdrawals from Traditional IRAs and 401(k)s are taxed as ordinary income, while long-term capital gains in taxable accounts may be taxed at a lower rate.

6. **Emergency Fund**: Ensure you have an emergency fund separate from these accounts. This helps avoid the need to tap into retirement savings in case of unforeseen expenses.

7. **Diversification**: Finally, regardless of the account type, ensure your investments are diversified to mitigate risk.

It's always wise to consult with a financial advisor to tailor these strategies to your specific situation, as they can provide more personalized insights based on your entire financial picture.
