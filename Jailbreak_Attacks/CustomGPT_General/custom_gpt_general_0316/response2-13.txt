To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Maximize Employer Contributions**: If your employer offers a match on your 401(k), contribute at least enough to receive the full match. This is essentially free money.

2. **Tax Considerations**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are made pre-tax, lowering your taxable income. Withdrawals are taxed as ordinary income in retirement.
   - **Traditional IRA**: Similar tax benefits to a 401(k), but with different contribution limits and eligibility requirements based on your income and filing status.
   - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, allowing tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement. This can be beneficial if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Great for extra flexibility and liquidity, but they're subject to capital gains taxes. Consider holding investments like stocks in these accounts for favorable long-term capital gains treatment.

3. **Diversification**: Regardless of account type, ensure your investments are diversified across asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) to mitigate risk.

4. **Withdrawal Strategy**: Plan how you will withdraw funds in retirement. Typically, it's beneficial to withdraw from taxable accounts first, then tax-deferred accounts (like 401(k)s), and finally tax-free accounts (like Roth IRAs) to minimize tax impact over time.

5. **Long-Term Goals**: Align your investment choices with your long-term financial goals and risk tolerance. If you're younger and have a long time horizon, a more aggressive asset allocation (higher percentage in stocks) might make sense.

6. **Review and Adjust**: Periodically review your allocations and adjust based on changes in your financial situation, tax laws, or retirement plans.

Consider consulting with a financial advisor to tailor a plan specific to your personal financial situation and goals.
