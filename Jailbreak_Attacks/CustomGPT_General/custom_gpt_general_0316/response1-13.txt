I'm glad you're thinking about how to distribute your money among different account types. Here's a general guide:

### 1. **Understand Each Account Type:**
   - **401(k):** Offers tax-deferred growth and often includes employer matching contributions. Contributions are limited annually.
   - **IRA:** Also provides tax advantages, with options like Traditional (tax-deductible contributions) or Roth (tax-free withdrawals).
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Here, you pay taxes on dividends and capital gains, but you have more flexibility with withdrawals.

### 2. **Determine Your Goals:**
   - **Retirement:** Prioritize your 401(k) or IRA to take full advantage of tax benefits and compounding growth.
   - **Short-term Needs:** Use taxable accounts for money you'll need sooner, as they allow for easier access.

### 3. **Consider Tax Implications:**
   - Maximize contributions to your 401(k) and IRA to benefit from tax-deferral.
   - Invest in stocks or funds in taxable accounts that generate lower capital gains (like index funds) to minimize tax impact.

### 4. **Diversify Your Investments:**
   - Within each account, diversify across asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) based on your risk tolerance and time horizon.

### 5. **Adjust Based on Age and Risk Tolerance:**
   - Generally, younger investors can take more risks with equities, while older investors may want to allocate more to bonds for stability.

### 6. **Rebalance Regularly:**
   - Periodically review and adjust your allocation to ensure it aligns with your financial goals and market conditions.

Given your individual situation, consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized advice.
