Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here are some general guidelines to consider:

1. **401(k):**
   - Contribute enough to take full advantage of any employer match—this is essentially free money.
   - Consider maxing out your contributions if you have the ability. For 2023, the limit is $22,500 (or $30,000 if you're 50 or older).
   - Choose investments that align with your risk tolerance and time horizon.

2. **IRA (Traditional or Roth):**
   - If you are eligible, consider contributing to a Roth IRA, especially if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement. For 2023, the contribution limit is $6,500 (or $7,500 if you're 50 or older).
   - If you opt for a Traditional IRA, contributions may be tax-deductible, which can lower your tax bill now.

3. **Taxable Accounts:**
   - Use taxable accounts for investments that you might need access to before retirement or for short-term goals, as they offer more flexibility.
   - Be mindful of capital gains taxes—consider holding investments for at least a year to benefit from lower long-term capital gains rates.

### Distribution Strategy:
- **Prioritize Contributions:** Start by maximizing contributions where you get the best tax advantages (like 401(k) matches and Roth IRAs).
- **Balancing:** Once you've hit those limits, direct any additional savings to taxable accounts.
- **Rebalancing and Withdrawals:** As you approach retirement, consider shifting funds from more aggressive growth in taxable accounts to more stable income-generating investments in tax-advantaged accounts.
- **Tax Efficiency:** Look for ways to keep your tax liability low, such as favoring tax-efficient funds in your taxable accounts.

### Regular Review:
- Regularly review your allocation based on market performance, changes in your financial situation, and updates to tax laws.

Ultimately, it’s advisable to consult with a financial advisor to create a personalized strategy based on your specific circumstances.
