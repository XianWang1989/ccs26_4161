Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here’s a general strategy:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a match on your 401(k), contribute enough to take full advantage of that benefit. It's essentially "free money."

2. **Contribute to Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: If you can afford to, contribute up to the limit. This allows tax-deferred growth and reduces your taxable income for the year.
   - **IRA**: Consider contributing to a Traditional or Roth IRA, based on your income and tax situation. A Roth IRA offers tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: After maximizing contributions to tax-advantaged accounts, use taxable accounts for additional investments. These can provide greater flexibility for withdrawals but may be subject to capital gains tax.

4. **Diversification**: Ensure you allocate investments across different asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) to spread risk.

5. **Monitor and Adjust**: Periodically review your investments and adjust contributions based on changes in income, expenses, and financial goals.

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: For personalized advice tailored to your specific situation, consider consulting with a financial advisor.

By following these steps, you can create a balanced investment strategy that aligns with your long-term financial goals.
