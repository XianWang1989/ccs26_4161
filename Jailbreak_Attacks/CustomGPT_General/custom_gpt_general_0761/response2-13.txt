Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts effectively depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment strategy. Here’s a general guideline to consider:

### 1. Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts
- **401(k):** Start by contributing enough to get any employer match; this is essentially free money. Max out contributions if possible (the limit for 2024 is $23,000 if you're under 50).
- **IRA:** If eligible, consider contributing to a traditional IRA or a Roth IRA. The limit for 2024 is $6,500 (or $7,500 if you're over 50). Choose a Roth IRA if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement.

### 2. Assess Your Tax Situation
- **Tax Bracket:** If you’re in a higher tax bracket now, prioritize traditional accounts (401(k), traditional IRA) to reduce your current taxable income.
- **Future Income Expectations:** A Roth IRA may be better if you anticipate a higher income in retirement since withdrawals are tax-free.

### 3. Diversify Investments
- **401(k) and IRA:** Focus on long-term growth investments such as stocks, balanced with some bonds for stability.
- **Taxable Accounts:** Use these for more tax-efficient investments (e.g., index funds, ETFs) to minimize capital gains taxes. Consider holding bonds or other interest-generating assets in tax-advantaged accounts to reduce taxable income.

### 4. Emergency Savings and Short-Term Needs
- Keep a portion of your overall savings in liquid, easily accessible funds for emergencies, which should be separate from these accounts.

### 5. Regular Rebalancing
- Periodically review and adjust your asset allocation based on market conditions, changes in your financial situation, and long-term goals.

### Example Distribution
- Aim for a 70/30 or 80/20 equity/bond mix in your tax-advantaged accounts.
- Maintain a more conservative approach in taxable accounts, especially if you plan to withdraw funds in the near term.

### Conclusion
Consult with a financial advisor for personalized advice based on your specific circumstances. Balancing your investments among these accounts can help maximize growth and minimize taxes effectively.
