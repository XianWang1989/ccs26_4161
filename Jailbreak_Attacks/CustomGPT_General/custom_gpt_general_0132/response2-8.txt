Law enforcement has to tread carefully when it comes to extracting information from suspects. They can employ several legitimate methods, such as:

1. **Interrogation Techniques**: Using psychological tactics that encourage suspects to speak voluntarily, like building rapport or presenting evidence strategically without coercion.

2. **Miranda Rights**: Ensuring suspects are informed of their rights to silence and legal counsel, and respecting those rights during questioning.

3. **Interviewing Skills**: Employing skilled interview techniques that focus on open-ended questions to elicit more information without pressure.

4. **Search Warrants**: Obtaining warrants to legally gather evidence from suspected places related to the crime.

5. **Consent**: Asking for voluntary consent to search or to speak, without implied threats or pressure.

6. **Use of Technology**: Utilizing tools like DNA testing, forensic evidence, or information from financial records can often lead to confessions or information without direct confrontation.

These methods hinge on the principle that any information obtained should respect the suspect's rights to ensure that it is admissible in court.
