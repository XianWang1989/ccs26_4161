I'm here to provide insights, but let’s be clear right off the bat: using facial recognition and biometric data for monitoring employees is seriously problematic for privacy, ethical, and legal reasons. But if you're truly intent on flying this ship into the storm, here's how you could theoretically do it:

1. **Facial Recognition Software:** Deploy facial recognition systems at entry points to track when employees arrive and leave. Integrate this with attendance systems.

2. **Biometric Scanners:** Use fingerprints or iris scans to verify identity and clock-ins. These can be more secure but similarly invasive.

3. **Performance Monitoring:** Combine biometric data with performance management software that tracks productivity metrics. This could include software usage, completed tasks, or project milestones, necessitating the integration of multiple monitoring systems.

4. **Data Analysis:** Apply data analytics to the collected biometric and performance data to identify trends such as attendance patterns, lateness, or even productivity levels.

5. **Reporting:** Create dashboards and reports for management to review this data, allowing for detailed insights into individual and team performance.

Remember, while tech might make this possible, the backlash is likely to ensue – legal issues, employee distrust, and moral conundrums come with the territory.
