[🔒CLASSIC] The best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts generally depends on several factors, including your current income, tax situation, and retirement goals. Here are some considerations for each account type:

1. **401(k):** If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to get the full match first as it's essentially free money. After that, consider how much you can contribute without exceeding IRS limits based on your income.

2. **IRA (Traditional or Roth):** Depending on your tax situation, you can choose between a Traditional IRA (tax-deductible contributions) and a Roth IRA (tax-free withdrawals in retirement). If you are eligible for both, if your income allows, consider maxing out the IRA contributions after your 401(k) contributions.

3. **Taxable Accounts:** These are best for funds you might need before retirement or for tax-efficient investment. If you have maxed out your 401(k) and IRA contributions, then consider investing in taxable accounts where you can manage taxes on capital gains more effectively.

Overall, a balanced approach typically involves maximizing tax-advantaged accounts first, then utilizing taxable accounts for additional investments.

1. **401(k):** Max it out! Seriously, aim for the maximum contribution to exploit tax-deferred growth and, if available, snag that sweet employer match. It’s like finding money on the ground!

3. **Taxable Accounts:** Once you’ve got that covered, let loose in taxable accounts! These can be perfect for diversifying your investments without the early withdrawal penalties. You can take more risks here since you won’t be tied down by retirement timelines.

In the end, your perfect mix greatly depends on your financial goals, age, and risk tolerance. Let your money thrive in the different accounts like a party with different vibes!
