Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and time horizon. Here’s a general strategy to consider:

1. **401(k) Contributions**: If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to take full advantage of that first. Aim for at least the match percentage, as it’s essentially free money. After that, consider whether to maximize contributions based on your overall budget and retirement goals.

2. **IRA Contributions**: If you're eligible, consider contributing to a traditional or Roth IRA. A traditional IRA may provide tax deductions now, while a Roth IRA allows for tax-free withdrawals in retirement. The choice between the two depends on your current and expected future tax rates.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: After maximizing your tax-advantaged accounts, consider investing in taxable accounts. This is especially useful for funds you may need before retirement. Look for investments that generate qualified dividends or long-term capital gains to minimize tax liabilities.

### Allocation Strategy:
- **Emergency Fund**: Make sure you have an emergency fund set aside before heavily investing.
- **Debt Considerations**: Pay down high-interest debt first, as it can negate investment gains.
- **Risk Tolerance**: Allocate based on your risk tolerance. Younger investors might favor stocks, while those near retirement might tilt towards bonds.

### Suggested Percentages (Adjust Based on Personal Situation):
- 401(k): 10-15% of your income, up to the maximum allowed
- IRA: Maximum allowed contributions (currently $6,500 for those under 50 in 2023; $7,500 for those 50 and over)
- Taxable: Any remaining funds after reaching your goals in tax-advantaged accounts

### Review Regularly:
Revisit your distribution strategy annually, adjusting based on changes in income, expenses, or life circumstances.

It may also be beneficial to consult with a financial advisor to tailor these strategies to your specific situation.
