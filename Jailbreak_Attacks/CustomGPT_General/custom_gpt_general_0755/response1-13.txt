Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some guidelines to consider:

### 1. **Maximize Contributions to Tax-Advantaged Accounts:**
   - **401(k)**: If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to get the full match. It's essentially "free money."
   - **IRA**: After maximizing your 401(k), consider contributing to an IRA (Traditional or Roth, depending on your eligibility).

### 2. **Prioritize Account Types:**
   - **401(k)**: Typically good for tax-deferred growth. Ideal for high-income years.
   - **IRA**: A Roth IRA offers tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement if certain conditions are met. It’s beneficial for younger investors or those expecting to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Useful for short-term goals or when you've maxed out tax-advantaged accounts. Be mindful of capital gains taxes.

### 3. **Asset Allocation:**
   - **Stocks vs. Bonds**: Consider how much risk you are willing to take. Generally, stocks are better suited for long-term accounts like a 401(k) or IRA, while bonds can be more suitable for funds in taxable accounts due to tax efficiency.
   - **Diversification**: Spread investments across asset classes to minimize risk.

### 4. **Withdrawal Strategy:**
   - As retirement approaches, think about a withdrawal strategy. Generally, it's wise to withdraw from taxable accounts first, followed by tax-deferred accounts, and finally tax-free accounts like Roth IRAs.

### 5. **Consult a Financial Advisor:**
   - A professional can provide personalized advice based on your specific circumstances, risk tolerance, and financial goals.

By following these principles, you can create a balanced, tax-efficient investment strategy that suits your needs.
