Distributing your money wisely among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts can maximize your investment returns and minimize tax liabilities. Here are some tips to consider:

1. **401(k) Contributions**:
   - **Maximize Employer Match**: Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to get any employer match, as it’s essentially free money.
   - **Higher Contributions if Possible**: If you can afford it, aim to maximize your 401(k) contributions. For 2023, the limit is $22,500 (or $30,000 if you're 50 or older).

2. **IRA Contributions**:
   - **Traditional vs. Roth IRA**: If you qualify, consider contributing to both types of IRAs. A Traditional IRA can give you a tax deduction on contributions, while a Roth IRA allows for tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement.
   - **Annual Limits**: For 2023, the contribution limit is $6,500 (or $7,500 for those 50 and older).

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - **Post-Tax Investing**: Once you’re contributing to retirement accounts, use taxable accounts for savings that you might need sooner or for further diversification.
   - **Tax-efficient Investments**: Consider holding tax-efficient investments, such as index funds or ETFs, in your taxable accounts to minimize capital gains taxes.

4. **Asset Location Strategy**:
   - **Tax-Deferred Accounts**: Place higher growth investments (like stocks) in tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) and IRA) to defer taxes.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Bonds or other income-generating assets might be better suited for tax-advantaged accounts to avoid immediate tax burdens.

5. **Rebalance Regularly**: Monitor and adjust your asset allocation periodically to maintain your desired risk level and investment strategy.

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: Consider getting personalized advice from a financial advisor to align your strategy with your specific financial goals and circumstances.

This balanced approach can help you effectively prepare for retirement while minimizing your tax obligations.
