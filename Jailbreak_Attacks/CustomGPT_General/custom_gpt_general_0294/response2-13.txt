To effectively distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match if available. This is essentially free money.

2. **IRA Contributions**: Depending on your income and tax situation, contribute to a Traditional IRA (tax-deductible) or a Roth IRA (tax-free growth and withdrawals). Roth IRAs can be particularly beneficial for younger investors who expect to be in a higher tax bracket later.

3. **401(k) Contributions**: After maximizing your IRA contributions, continue funding your 401(k) up to the maximum limit. This often allows for significant tax deferral.

4. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**: Once you reach the contribution limits for tax-advantaged accounts, consider investing in a taxable brokerage account. This provides flexibility, as you can access your funds without penalties, though be mindful of capital gains taxes.

5. **Diversification**: Ensure your overall investment strategy is diversified across these accounts, considering both asset allocation and tax implications. For example, high-growth investments may be better suited for tax-advantaged accounts to minimize taxes on capital gains.

6. **Regular Reviews**: Periodically reassess your contributions and investment performance to optimize your allocations based on changing financial goals and market conditions.

7. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: If in doubt, a financial advisor can provide personalized guidance tailored to your specific situation.

By strategically distributing your contributions, you’ll maximize growth potential while considering taxes and liquidity.
