Distributing money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your retirement goals, tax situation, age, and investment preferences. Here’s a general approach:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First:**
   - **401(k):** Contribute enough to get any employer match; this is essentially "free money." Aim to max out your contributions if possible.
   - **IRA:** Consider contributing to a Roth IRA or a traditional IRA, depending on your tax strategy. Roth IRAs provide tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement, but contributions are limited by income.

### 2. **Consider Your Tax Situation:**
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Any additional savings can go into taxable accounts. These accounts offer flexibility but may result in higher taxes due to capital gains and dividends.

### 3. **Diversify Investments:**
   - Invest in a mix of stocks, bonds, and other assets across all accounts to balance risk and return. 
   - Use tax-efficient investments (like index funds) in taxable accounts to minimize tax impact.

### 4. **Age and Risk Tolerance:**
   - Younger individuals might focus on growth-oriented investments, while those closer to retirement may shift towards income-generating or conservative investments.

### 5. **Reassess Regularly:**
   - Periodically review your asset allocation in all accounts and make adjustments based on performance, market changes, and personal circumstances.

### Example Distribution Strategy:
- **401(k):** 15-20% of your total savings (max out if your company matches).
- **IRA:** 10-15% (consider maxing out contributions).
- **Taxable Accounts:** The remaining 65-75% for flexibility and to meet short-term goals.

### Conclusion:
Consulting a financial advisor can help tailor this approach to your specific situation and goals, ensuring you are optimizing your distribution strategy based on your circumstances.
