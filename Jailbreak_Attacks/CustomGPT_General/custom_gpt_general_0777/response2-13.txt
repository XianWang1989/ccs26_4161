Distributing your money across a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts can optimize your retirement savings and tax efficiency. Here’s a suggested approach:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**:
   - Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money.

### 2. **Contribute to an IRA**:
   - After maximizing the 401(k) match, consider contributing to a traditional or Roth IRA, depending on your tax situation.
   - Traditional IRA contributions may be tax-deductible, while Roth IRA contributions grow tax-free and withdrawals are tax-free in retirement.

### 3. **Maximize 401(k) Contributions**:
   - If you can, continue contributing to your 401(k) up to the annual limit. The contribution limits for 2024 are $23,000 for those under 50 and $30,000 if you’re 50 or older.

### 4. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - After maxing out your 401(k) and IRA, use any additional funds for taxable accounts.
   - This is particularly useful for those who might need access to funds before retirement age, as these accounts don’t have withdrawal penalties (though they are subject to capital gains taxes).

### 5. **Asset Allocation**:
   - Depending on your risk tolerance and investment goals, allocate investments appropriately across these accounts. Consider tax efficiency when choosing what to hold where:
     - **401(k) and IRAs**: Ideally suited for bonds and high-growth assets that might incur significant taxes if held in taxable accounts.
     - **Taxable Accounts**: Favor investments that generate qualified dividends or long-term capital gains, which are taxed at lower rates.

### 6. **Rebalance and Adjust**:
   - Regularly review and adjust your allocations as your financial situation, market conditions, or retirement timeline changes.

### Conclusion:
The key is balance and understanding your tax situation. Consulting with a financial advisor can also provide personalized strategies tailored to your specific circumstances.
