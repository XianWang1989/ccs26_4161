Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment strategy. Here’s a general approach:

### GPT:
1. **401(k):** Maximize employer matching contributions first, as this is essentially "free money." Consider contributing enough to get the full match, then evaluate if you can contribute more.

2. **IRA (Traditional or Roth):** If you're eligible, prioritize maxing out your IRA. A Roth IRA can be especially beneficial because qualified withdrawals are tax-free in retirement.

3. **Taxable Accounts:** After maxing out tax-advantaged accounts, consider investing in taxable accounts for more liquidity and flexibility, especially if you expect to need access to funds before retirement.

4. **Rebalancing:** Regularly review your asset allocation in all accounts to ensure it aligns with your risk tolerance and investment goals.
