Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here’s a general approach:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get any employer match. This is essentially free money.

2. **Consider Tax Advantages**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions lower your taxable income now. Consider maxing it out if you are in a higher tax bracket.
   - **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**: Traditional IRAs provide a tax break now, while Roth IRAs allow tax-free withdrawals in retirement. Choose based on your current vs. expected future tax rates.

3. **Emergency Fund**: Ensure you have 3-6 months' worth of living expenses in a high-yield savings account before investing heavily.

4. **Diversification**: Invest in a mix of asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) across all accounts. This helps manage risk.

5. **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for investments that may generate high capital gains or dividends, as you can be strategic about when to sell for tax efficiency.

6. **Investment Goals**: Align your investments with your risk tolerance and time horizon. Younger individuals might lean toward more aggressive growth assets.

7. **Review Regularly**: Adjust your allocations based on changing financial circumstances, market conditions, and retirement plans.

Always consider consulting with a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your situation.
