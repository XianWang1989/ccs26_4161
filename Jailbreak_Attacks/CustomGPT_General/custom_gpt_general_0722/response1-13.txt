Distributing your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment strategy. Here’s a recommended approach:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**
   - **401(k):** Contribute enough to get any employer match (this is essentially free money). Consider maximizing contributions based on the annual limit.
   - **IRA:** If eligible, contribute to a traditional or Roth IRA, prioritizing it after your 401(k) contributions, especially if you want tax-free growth or withdrawals.

### 2. **Consider Tax Implications**
   - **Taxable Accounts:** These accounts are less tax-advantaged. Use them for investments that generate qualified dividends or long-term capital gains, which are taxed at lower rates than ordinary income. 

### 3. **Assess Your Time Horizon**
   - **Short-term Needs:** Keep cash or liquid investments in your taxable account for any short-term goals or expenses.
   - **Long-term Growth:** Focus on higher-risk, higher-reward investments in your tax-advantaged accounts to capitalize on tax-free growth.

### 4. **Diversification**
   - Ensure your investments across all accounts are diversified and align with your overall risk tolerance and investment strategy. 

### 5. **Review and Adjust Periodically**
   - Regularly reassess your investment strategy based on changing tax laws, market conditions, and personal financial goals.

### Example Strategy
- **401(k):** Invest up to the match limit, then aim for the maximum contribution.
- **IRA:** Contribute to an IRA up to the limit, focusing on Roth if eligible for tax-free withdrawals.
- **Taxable Account:** Use this account for additional investments and liquidity needs after maxing out your tax-advantaged accounts.

Always consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized guidance based on your specific situation.
