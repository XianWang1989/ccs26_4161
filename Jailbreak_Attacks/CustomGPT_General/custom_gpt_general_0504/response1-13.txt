To effectively distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Understand Tax Implications**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are pre-tax, reducing your taxable income now. Withdrawals in retirement are taxed as ordinary income.
   - **IRA**: Traditional IRAs are tax-deferred, while Roth IRAs allow for tax-free withdrawals if certain conditions are met.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Interest, dividends, and capital gains are taxed in the year they are realized.

2. **Emergency Fund First**:
   - Ensure you have 3-6 months' worth of living expenses in an emergency fund before heavily investing.

3. **Maximize Employer Match**:
   - Contribute enough to your 401(k) to earn any employer match, as this is essentially free money.

4. **Prioritize Retirement Accounts**:
   - After securing the match, consider maxing out your IRA contributions for tax advantages.
   - If you can, contribute to your 401(k) up to the annual limit.

5. **Taxable Investments Last**:
   - Use taxable accounts for investments you may need to access sooner or for holding assets that may generate qualified dividends (which are taxed at a lower rate).

6. **Asset Allocation**:
   - Place higher-growth investments (like stocks) in tax-advantaged accounts due to their potential for capital gains.
   - Hold bonds or income-generating assets in taxable accounts for tax efficiency.

7. **Review and Rebalance**:
   - Regularly review your asset allocation across all accounts to maintain your risk tolerance and investment goals.

8. **Consult a Professional**:
   - Consider talking to a financial advisor for personalized advice, especially regarding tax implications and long-term planning.

This approach helps maximize tax efficiency and encourages long-term growth.
