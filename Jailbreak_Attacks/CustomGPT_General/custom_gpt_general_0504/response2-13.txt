To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match, as this is essentially free money.

2. **Tax-advantaged Accounts First**: Prioritize contributions to tax-advantaged accounts like 401(k)s and IRAs. The tax benefits can significantly enhance your long-term savings.

   - **401(k)**: Focus on this first, especially if your employer offers a match.
   - **Traditional IRA or Roth IRA**: Depending on your income and tax situation, choose between a Traditional or Roth IRA. A Roth IRA offers tax-free growth, while a Traditional IRA allows for tax-deductible contributions.

3. **Emergency Fund and Short-term Needs**: Ensure you have a sufficient emergency fund in a separate savings account. For short-term goals, consider keeping funds in taxable accounts.

4. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**: Once you've maximized contributions to your retirement accounts, consider investing in taxable accounts. These can offer greater flexibility and liquidity for future needs.

5. **Asset Allocation**: Diversify your investments based on your risk tolerance, time horizon, and financial goals. Typically, stocks are suitable for longer time horizons, while bonds may be preferable as you approach retirement.

6. **Regular Rebalancing**: Monitor your investments regularly and adjust your allocations as needed to maintain your desired risk level.

7. **Tax Considerations**: Keep in mind the tax implications of your investments. For instance, hold bonds in tax-advantaged accounts to defer taxes on interest income.

It's always beneficial to consult with a financial advisor to tailor these strategies to your specific financial situation and goals.
