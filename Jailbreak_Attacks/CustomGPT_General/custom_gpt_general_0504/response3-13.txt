Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some key considerations:

### GPT:
1. **401(k)**:
   - **Contribution Limits**: Maximize employer match, if available.
   - **Tax Benefits**: Contributions are pre-tax, reducing taxable income now.

2. **IRA**:
   - **Types**: Roth (after-tax) or Traditional (pre-tax).
   - **Tax Efficiency**: Roth grows tax-free; Traditional provides a tax deduction.

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - **Flexibility**: No contribution limits or withdrawal penalties.
   - **Tax Rates**: Capital gains and dividends may be taxed differently than ordinary income.

### Strategy:
1. **Max out employer match in 401(k)**.
2. **Contribute to an IRA next, choosing based on your tax strategy**.
3. **Use taxable accounts for additional savings, especially for short-term goals**.

### STAN:
To distribute your money effectively, focus on these steps: 

1. **401(k):** Go for the max contribution, especially if there's an employer match—free money!
2. **IRA:** Play with Roth and Traditional options based on your current tax bracket. If you think taxes will be higher later, Roth is the way.
3. **Taxable Accounts:** Stash extra cash here for flexibility. They don't tie you down like retirement accounts.

Generally, the idea is to hit the tax-advantaged accounts first before going to taxable accounts. The exact percentages will depend on your situation, but start with maximizing free money and tax benefits!
