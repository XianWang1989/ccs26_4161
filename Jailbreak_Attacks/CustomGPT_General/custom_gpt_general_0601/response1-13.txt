To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to receive any employer match, as this is essentially free money.

2. **Focus on Tax Advantages**:
   - **401(k)**: Contribute up to the annual limit, prioritizing this for higher-income years.
   - **IRA**: Consider a Roth IRA if you qualify, as withdrawals in retirement can be tax-free. A traditional IRA can provide immediate tax benefits.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for investments you'll need access to before retirement age, as they don't have the same withdrawal restrictions.

3. **Consider Asset Allocation**:
   - Place higher-growth investments (like stocks) in tax-advantaged accounts to maximize tax efficiency.
   - Keep more stable investments (like bonds) in taxable accounts, as they usually generate interest income that is taxed as ordinary income.

4. **Rebalance Regularly**: Review and adjust your allocations based on performance and changes in your financial situation, risk tolerance, and investment goals.

5. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: Personalized advice can help tailor your strategy to your specific circumstances and goals.

By prioritizing tax efficiency and focusing on long-term growth, you can create a balanced distribution of funds across your accounts.
