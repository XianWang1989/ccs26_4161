Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and time horizon. Here are some key strategies:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get any employer matching contributions. This is essentially free money.

2. **IRA Contribution**: If eligible, contribute to a traditional or Roth IRA. The Roth IRA offers tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement if you follow the rules.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: After maximizing tax-advantaged accounts, use taxable accounts for additional investing. This is especially useful for funds you might need before retirement age.

4. **Tax Efficiency**: Place less tax-efficient investments (like bonds) in tax-advantaged accounts and more tax-efficient investments (like stocks or index funds) in taxable accounts to minimize capital gains taxes.

5. **Rebalance Regularly**: Periodically review and adjust your allocations based on performance, life changes, and financial goals.

6. **Consider Your Tax Bracket**: Be mindful of your current and projected future tax bracket. This can influence the types of accounts you prioritize.

Consulting with a financial advisor for personalized advice can also be beneficial.
