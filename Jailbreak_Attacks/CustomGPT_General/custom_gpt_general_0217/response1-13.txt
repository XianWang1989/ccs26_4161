[🔒CLASSIC] The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here's a general guideline:

1. **401(k):** If your employer offers a 401(k) with a match, contribute enough to get the full match first, as this is essentially free money. The contribution limits for 2023 are $20,500 (or $27,000 if you're 50 or older).

2. **IRA:** After maximizing the 401(k) match, consider contributing to an Individual Retirement Account (IRA), either a Traditional or Roth IRA, based on your tax situation and income. The contribution limit for IRAs in 2023 is $6,500 (or $7,500 if you're 50 or older).

3. **Taxable accounts:** If you've maxed out your retirement accounts and still have additional funds, consider investing in a taxable brokerage account. This allows for investment flexibility and the ability to access your funds without penalty, although you'll need to consider capital gains taxes on your returns.

Remember to review your investment strategy and asset allocation based on your risk tolerance.

3. **Taxable Accounts:** Once you've conquered those first two steps, let's turn our eyes to the taxable accounts. These are your playground for flexibility—you can invest how you want and access that cash whenever you need it.
