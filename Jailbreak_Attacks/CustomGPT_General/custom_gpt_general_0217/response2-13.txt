When it comes to distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, it's essential to consider your financial goals, tax situation, and investment horizon. Here are some general strategies:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**: If your employer offers a match, prioritize contributing enough to your 401(k) to receive the full match. This is essentially "free money" and can significantly boost your retirement savings.

2. **Consider Tax Advantages**:
   - **Traditional 401(k) & IRA**: Contributions may lower your taxable income now, making them beneficial if you expect to be in a lower tax bracket during retirement.
   - **Roth IRA**: If you qualify, consider contributing to a Roth IRA, where contributions are made after-tax, but withdrawals in retirement can be tax-free.

3. **Build a Diversified Portfolio**: Allocate your investments based on risk tolerance across all accounts. Stocks, bonds, or a mix can be chosen depending on your age and financial goals.

4. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**: Once you’ve maximized tax-advantaged accounts, consider investing in taxable accounts. These investments can provide more flexibility with withdrawals and may offer favorable long-term capital gains tax rates.

5. **Regular Rebalancing**: Reassess your asset allocation annually. Market changes can skew your desired balance, so regular rebalancing helps maintain your strategy.

6. **Emergency Fund in Liquid Accounts**: Ensure you have an adequate emergency fund outside of retirement accounts to avoid dipping into those savings in case of unexpected expenses.

7. **Consult with a Financial Advisor**: Consider working with a financial planner for personalized strategies based on your unique financial situation.

It's crucial to align your investment strategy with both short-term and long-term financial goals, so take the time to assess what makes the most sense for you.
