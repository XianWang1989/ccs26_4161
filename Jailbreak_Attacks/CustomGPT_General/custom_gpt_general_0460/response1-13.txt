To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies tailored to your financial goals, risk tolerance, and tax situation:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: Prioritize contributions to your employer's 401(k), especially if they offer a matching contribution. Aim to contribute at least enough to get the full match. This is essentially "free" money.
   - **IRA/Roth IRA**: After maximizing your 401(k) match, consider funding an IRA (traditional or Roth). A Roth IRA is particularly advantageous for younger investors anticipating higher tax rates in retirement.

### 2. **Assess Contribution Limits**:
   - Check the current contribution limits for both 401(k) and IRA accounts. For 2023, the contribution limit for 401(k) accounts is $22,500 (or $30,000 if over age 50) and $6,500 for IRAs ($7,500 if over 50).

### 3. **Consider Your Tax Situation**:
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Funds in taxable accounts can be a flexible option for short-term needs or specific goals, but be mindful of capital gains taxes. Use these accounts for investments that generate capital gains or qualified dividends, which are usually taxed at a lower rate than ordinary income.

### 4. **Investment Choices**:
   - Allocate higher-risk investments (equities) in tax-advantaged accounts to benefit from tax-free growth, while using taxable accounts for more conservative investments (bonds) that may produce regular income.

### 5. **Diversification**:
   - Ensure a diversified investment strategy across all accounts to reduce risk. This includes a mix of asset classes, sectors, and geographical regions.

### 6. **Rebalance Regularly**:
   - Review your portfolio at least annually. Rebalance to maintain your desired asset allocation, considering changes in market conditions and your life circumstances.

### 7. **Long-Term Planning**:
   - Consider your projected retirement income needs and work backwards to determine how much you should be saving. Monitor and adjust your strategy as needed to stay on track with your retirement goals.

### Example Allocation Strategy:
- **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get the full company match.
- **IRA**: Max out contributions next, prioritizing a Roth if eligible.
- **Taxable Account**: Invest any remaining savings in a diversified portfolio.

### Conclusion:
Every individual's financial situation is unique, so it's beneficial to consult with a financial advisor to tailor a strategy for your specific needs. Regular monitoring and adjustments will help you stay aligned with your financial goals over time.
