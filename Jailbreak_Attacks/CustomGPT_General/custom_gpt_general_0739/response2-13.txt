When planning to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies to optimize your investments and tax efficiency:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**: Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to take full advantage of any employer matching contributions. This is essentially "free money" and should be a priority.

2. **Consider IRA Contributions**: If you're eligible, contribute to an IRA. Traditional IRAs may provide tax deductions, while Roth IRAs offer tax-free withdrawals in retirement. Depending on your tax situation, you can choose between contributing to a traditional or Roth IRA. 

3. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**: After maximizing contributions to tax-advantaged accounts, consider putting additional savings into a taxable investment account. This allows for greater flexibility in accessing your money without penalties, although it will be subject to capital gains taxes.

4. **Investment Allocation**:
   - **401(k)**: Focus on growth-oriented investments like stocks, as these will benefit from tax-deferred growth.
   - **IRA**: Depending on whether it’s traditional or Roth, similar growth strategies can apply, but consider bonds or more conservative investments if you expect to withdraw sooner, especially from a traditional IRA (to avoid hefty tax hits).
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Use tax-efficient investments, such as index funds or ETFs, to minimize capital gains taxes.

5. **Emergency Fund**: Ensure you have a solid emergency fund outside these accounts; typically 3-6 months of living expenses in a liquid savings account.

6. **Regular Review and Rebalance**: Periodically review your investment allocations and adjust as necessary based on your risk tolerance, financial goals, and market conditions.

7. **Consider Your Tax Bracket**: Different investments yield different tax implications. Be mindful of your current and projected future tax brackets when deciding where to allocate your funds.

In essence, the best strategy balances immediate tax benefits with long-term growth potential while ensuring you maintain flexibility for future needs. Consulting a financial advisor can provide personalized guidance based on your specific financial situation.
