Distributing your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts should align with your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some key considerations for each type of account:

### 1. **401(k)**
- **Advantages**: Tax-deferred growth, potential employer matching contributions.
- **Strategy**: Maximize employer matching first, as it’s free money. Contribute enough to reach the match, then consider additional contributions based on your overall cash flow.

### 2. **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**
- **Advantages**: Tax advantages (deductions for Traditional IRA; tax-free growth and withdrawals for Roth IRA).
- **Strategy**: If you're eligible, consider maxing out your Roth IRA if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket during retirement. If you need tax deductions, prioritize your Traditional IRA. 

### 3. **Taxable Accounts**
- **Advantages**: Flexibility in withdrawals and investment choices; capital gains tax rates are typically lower than income tax rates.
- **Strategy**: Use these accounts for goals that are shorter-term or if you've already maxed out retirement accounts. Consider investing in tax-efficient funds or strategies, such as index funds, to minimize capital gains taxes.

### **General Tips for Distribution**:
- **Emergency Fund**: Ensure you have an emergency fund in place that is separate from these investment accounts.
- **Diversification**: Diversify investments across accounts to manage risk. For instance, consider stocks in taxable accounts (to take advantage of lower capital gains taxes) and fixed income in tax-advantaged accounts (to shield interest income from taxes).
- **Withdrawals in Retirement**: In retirement, strategically withdraw funds from these accounts to minimize tax impact. For example, start with taxable accounts to allow tax-advantaged accounts to grow longer.

### **Final Considerations**:
Consult with a financial advisor to create a personalized strategy based on your specific financial situation and goals. They can help you navigate complex tax implications and ensure your distribution aligns with your long-term objectives.
