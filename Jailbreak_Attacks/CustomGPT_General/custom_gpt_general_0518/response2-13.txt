Distributing money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts effectively depends on your financial goals, risk tolerance, and current tax situation. Here are some key considerations and strategies to guide your distribution:

1. **401(k) Contributions**:
   - Maximize employer match if available. This is effectively "free money."
   - Contribute enough to reduce your taxable income. The contribution limit for 2023 is $22,500 ($30,000 if you're over 50).
   - Invest in a diversified mix of assets, focusing on long-term growth.

2. **IRA Contributions**:
   - Consider a traditional IRA if you expect to be in a lower tax bracket during retirement, as contributions may be tax-deductible.
   - A Roth IRA can be beneficial if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement since contributions are made with after-tax dollars, and qualified withdrawals are tax-free.
   - Contribution limits for 2023 are $6,500 ($7,500 if over 50).

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - Use these accounts for flexibility. You can access your money anytime without penalties, but you will pay taxes on any investment gains.
   - Focus on investments with lower turnover and tax efficiency, such as index funds or ETFs, to minimize capital gains taxes.
   - Consider tax-loss harvesting to offset gains with losses, thereby reducing your overall tax burden.

4. **General Strategies**:
   - Diversify your investments across all accounts to maintain a balanced portfolio.
   - Review your asset allocation regularly, adjusting as needed based on your risk tolerance and market conditions.
   - Consider your withdrawal strategy for retirement. Prioritize low-tax accounts first (like Roth IRA) to maximize tax efficiency.
   - Keep an emergency fund in a readily accessible account, separate from your retirement and taxable investments.

5. **Consult a Financial Advisor**:
   - Each individual’s situation is unique. A financial advisor can provide personalized advice based on your financial goals, life situation, and tax implications.

Remember, the right mix for you will depend on your overall financial situation and goals!
