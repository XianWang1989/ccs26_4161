Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts is an important aspect of a comprehensive retirement and investment strategy. The best approach depends on several factors, including your current financial situation, retirement goals, tax considerations, and risk tolerance. Here are some general guidelines:

### 1. Maximize Employer Match in 401(k):
- **Prioritize Contributions**: If your employer offers a matching contribution for your 401(k), contribute enough to take full advantage of this match. It’s essentially free money.

### 2. Contribute to a Traditional or Roth IRA:
- **Tax Benefits**: After maximizing your 401(k) match, consider contributing to an IRA. If you expect to be in a lower tax bracket in retirement, a Traditional IRA may be beneficial due to tax-deferred growth. A Roth IRA could be advantageous if you believe you’ll be in a higher tax bracket, as it allows for tax-free withdrawals in retirement.
- **Contribution Limits**: Keep in mind the annual contribution limits for IRAs and eligibility if you have high income.

### 3. Continue Funding Your 401(k):
- **Contribution Limits**: After funding your IRA, continue to contribute to your 401(k) until you reach the annual limit. The higher contribution limits for 401(k)s compared to IRAs can significantly boost your retirement savings.

### 4. Allocate Money to Taxable Accounts:
- **Flexibility**: Any savings beyond your retirement accounts can be invested in taxable accounts. These accounts provide flexibility for short-term needs and emergencies, as you can access funds without penalties.
- **Investment Strategy**: Focus on tax-efficient investments in your taxable accounts, such as index funds or ETFs, to minimize capital gains taxes.

### 5. Diversify Investments:
- **Asset Allocation**: Within each account type, diversify your investments based on your risk tolerance and time horizon. A mix of stocks, bonds, and other assets can help mitigate risk.

### 6. Monitor Tax Implications:
- **Withdrawal Strategies**: Be mindful of the tax implications of withdrawing funds from each account during retirement. A strategic withdrawal approach can minimize taxes over the long term.

### 7. Reassess Regularly:
- **Life Changes**: Periodically re-evaluate your financial situation, investment performance, and retirement goals. Adjust your contributions and asset allocations as necessary.

It's always wise to consult with a financial advisor to tailor your strategy to your specific needs and circumstances. They can help you navigate the complexities of retirement planning and investment distribution based on your individual financial situation.
