Distributing funds among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and time horizon. Here are some strategies to consider:

### 1. **Understand Account Types**
   - **401(k)**: Typically has higher contribution limits and potential employer matching. Contributions are tax-deferred.
   - **IRA**: Offers tax advantages, with limits on contributions. Traditional IRAs have tax-deferred growth, while Roth IRAs provide tax-free withdrawals.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Flexibility with contributions and withdrawals, but subject to capital gains taxes.

### 2. **Prioritize Contributions**
   - **Maximize employer match in your 401(k)**: Free money is a priority.
   - **Contribute to an IRA**: If eligible, consider a Roth IRA for tax-free growth.
   - **Fund taxable accounts**: Use for short-term goals or after maxing out tax-advantaged accounts.

### 3. **Consider Tax Implications**
   - **Hold income-generating assets in tax-advantaged accounts**: This reduces tax burdens.
   - **Use taxable accounts for investments that create long-term capital gains**, which are taxed at a lower rate than ordinary income.

### 4. **Diversify Investments**
   - Maintain a diversified portfolio aligned with your risk tolerance across all accounts.
   - Adjust allocations based on tax considerations and your overall strategy.

### 5. **Regularly Review and Rebalance**
   - Monitor your allocations and adjust as you approach retirement or change financial goals.

### Example Allocation Strategy
- **401(k)**: Maximize contributions to get the full employer match.
- **IRA**: Contribute the maximum to a Roth or Traditional IRA based on your income.
- **Taxable Accounts**: Any extra savings can go here, prioritizing tax-efficient investment options.

### Conclusion
Tailor your distribution strategy based on your individual financial situation, goals, and tax considerations. Consulting with a financial advisor can provide personalized advice.
