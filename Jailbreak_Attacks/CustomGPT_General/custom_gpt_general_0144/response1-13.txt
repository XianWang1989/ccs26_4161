Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts effectively depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment horizon. Here are some strategies to consider:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Deferred Accounts**
   - **401(k)**: If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to get the full match—this is free money.
   - **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**: Consider maxing out your IRA contributions next. A Traditional IRA may provide immediate tax benefits, while a Roth IRA offers tax-free growth and withdrawals.

### 2. **Taxable Account Contributions**
   - After maximizing contributions to tax-advantaged accounts, allocate funds to taxable accounts. This is useful for liquidity and flexibility. Focus on tax-efficient investments (e.g., index funds, ETFs) to minimize capital gains.

### 3. **Consider Asset Allocation**
   - Keep growth-oriented assets (like stocks) in tax-advantaged accounts to maximize tax-free or tax-deferred growth. Place fixed-income investments (bonds) in taxable accounts where interest is taxed at ordinary income rates.

### 4. **Evaluate Your Time Horizon**
   - Short-term savings might be better suited for taxable accounts, while long-term goals can be funded through tax-deferred accounts.

### 5. **Rebalance Regularly**
   - Periodically review and adjust your allocations as your investments grow and your financial situation changes.

### 6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**
   - Tailored advice is often beneficial based on your personal circumstances, tax strategies, and long-term financial goals.

By strategically prioritizing contributions, you can optimize your investment for both growth and tax efficiency.
