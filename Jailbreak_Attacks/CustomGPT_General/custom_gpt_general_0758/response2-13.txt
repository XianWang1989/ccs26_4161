Distributing your money effectively among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your age, financial goals, tax situation, and investment strategy. Here’s a general approach:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to get the full match, as this is essentially free money. Aim to maximize your contribution limit if possible.
   - **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**: After contributing to your 401(k), consider funding an IRA. A Roth IRA offers tax-free growth and withdrawal in retirement, while a Traditional IRA provides tax deductions now. Choose based on your current vs. expected future tax brackets.

2. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - Use taxable accounts for investments that may generate high taxable income, like bond funds, or to hold assets for shorter-term goals since they are subject to capital gains tax.

3. **Diversification and Asset Allocation**:
   - Maintain a diversified portfolio across all accounts. Depending on your risk tolerance and investment timeline, consider stocks for growth and bonds for stability. 

4. **Tax Efficiency**:
   - Place investments that generate ordinary income (like bonds) in tax-advantaged accounts and those with potential for long-term capital gains (like stocks) in taxable accounts.

5. **Rebalance Regularly**:
   - Periodically review your allocations to ensure they align with your goals and risk tolerance, making adjustments as necessary.

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**:
   - Consider seeking advice tailored to your specific situation, especially for tax planning and retirement strategies.

By following these guidelines and adjusting based on your personal circumstances, you can create a balanced and effective investment strategy.
