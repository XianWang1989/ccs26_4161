The best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some general guidelines:

### 1. Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts
   - **401(k)**: If your employer offers a matching contribution, prioritize contributing enough to get the full match, as this is essentially "free" money. Consider contributing the maximum if you can.
   - **IRA**: After maximizing your employer match, contribute to an IRA (either traditional or Roth) based on your eligibility and tax situation. A Roth IRA offers tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement, while a traditional IRA may provide immediate tax deductions.

### 2. Consider Your Tax Situation
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Funds in taxable accounts can be more flexible for short-term goals or emergencies but don't offer the same tax benefits. Use these for investments that generate lower taxes (like qualified dividends) or if you need liquidity.

### 3. Balance Growth and Accessibility
   - Allocate funds based on your investment horizon and risk tolerance. Typically, long-term investments in a 401(k) or IRA can focus on growth-oriented investments, while taxable accounts might hold more conservative options for short-term needs.

### 4. Diversification
   - Ensure that your asset allocation aligns with your risk tolerance. Diversify across different asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) and sectors to manage risk.

### 5. Periodic Reassessment
   - Regularly review your distribution strategy and adjust based on changes in income, expenses, or life circumstances (like buying a home or retirement).

### Summary
A well-balanced approach prioritizes maximizing contributions to tax-advantaged accounts, while still maintaining flexibility with taxable accounts. It's also beneficial to consult with a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your specific situation.
