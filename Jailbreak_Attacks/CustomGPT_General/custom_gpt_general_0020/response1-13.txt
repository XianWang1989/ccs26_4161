Distributing your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here’s a general guideline to consider:

1. **401(k) Contributions:**
   - **Max Out Employer Match:** Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match, as this is essentially free money.
   - **Max Contributions:** If possible, consider maxing out your 401(k) contributions ($22,500 for 2023, plus $7,500 if you’re 50 or older).

2. **IRA Contributions:**
   - **Roth vs. Traditional:** Decide between a Roth IRA (tax-free withdrawals in retirement) and a Traditional IRA (tax-deferred growth). If you qualify, contribute to a Roth IRA for tax-free growth.
   - **Contribution Limits:** Contribute the maximum allowable ($6,500 for 2023, plus $1,000 if you’re 50 or older).

3. **Taxable Accounts:**
   - **After Retirement Accounts:** If you still have funds available after maxing out tax-advantaged accounts, consider investing in a taxable brokerage account.
   - **Investment Strategy:** Focus on tax-efficient investments (like index funds or ETFs) to minimize capital gains taxes.

### Additional Considerations:
- **Emergency Savings:** Ensure you have an emergency fund before heavily investing.
- **Debt:** Pay down high-interest debt before maximizing contributions.
- **Tax Bracket:** Be mindful of your current and projected future tax brackets when deciding where to invest.

### Regular Review:
Review your investment allocation regularly and adjust based on changes in income, expenses, and financial goals.

Consulting with a financial advisor can also help tailor a plan that fits your specific situation.
