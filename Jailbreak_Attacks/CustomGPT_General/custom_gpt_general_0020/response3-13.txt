To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

### 1. **401(k) Contributions:**
   - Maximize contributions, especially if your employer offers matching. This is essentially "free money."
   - Focus on tax-deferred growth in this account.

### 2. **IRA (Traditional or Roth):**
   - If eligible, contribute to a Roth IRA for tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement.
   - If you expect to be in a lower tax bracket during retirement, a Traditional IRA may be better for tax deductions now.

### 3. **Taxable Accounts:**
   - Use these for investments you may want to access before retirement. 
   - Focus on tax-efficient investments like index funds or ETFs to minimize capital gains taxes.

### **General Guidelines:**
- **Emergency Fund:** Ensure you have a sufficient emergency fund outside of these accounts.
- **Diversification:** Balance your investments across risk levels.
- **Rebalancing:** Regularly review and adjust your allocations based on performance and changing goals.

### **Considerations:**
- **Tax Implications:** Understand the tax impacts of each account type.
- **Investment Horizon:** Align your asset allocation with your time horizon for withdrawals.
- **Consult a Financial Advisor:** For personalized advice, especially regarding your specific financial situation and goals.

By strategically distributing your money based on these principles, you can optimize growth while managing risks and taxes effectively.
