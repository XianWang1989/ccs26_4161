When considering how to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, here are some guidelines:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a match on your 401(k), contribute enough to maximize that first. This is essentially "free money."

2. **Contribute to Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: Aim to contribute the annual maximum if possible, especially if you are in a higher tax bracket.
   - **IRA**: If you have additional funds, max out your IRA contributions. Traditional IRAs offer tax deductions, while Roth IRAs provide tax-free withdrawals in retirement.

3. **Emergency Fund**: Ensure you maintain a liquid emergency fund outside of investment accounts.

4. **Taxable Accounts**: After maximizing contributions to tax-advantaged accounts, allocate any additional savings to taxable accounts. These can be beneficial for short-term goals or if you might need access to your funds before retirement.

5. **Consider Tax Implications**:
   - Taxable accounts can offer more flexibility but come with capital gains taxes.
   - For long-term investments, consider tax-efficient investments, such as index funds.

6. **Rebalance Regularly**: Adjust your asset allocation based on your financial goals, risk tolerance, and market conditions.

7. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: If possible, speaking with a financial advisor can help tailor a strategy to your specific needs and goals.

By following these steps, you can create a balanced investment strategy that aligns with your financial objectives.
