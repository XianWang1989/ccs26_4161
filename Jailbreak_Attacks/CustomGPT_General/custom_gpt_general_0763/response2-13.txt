The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here’s a general approach:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**
   - Contribute enough to capture any employer matching contributions as this is essentially "free money."

### 2. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**
   - **IRA (Roth or Traditional):** 
     - If eligible, consider maxing out your IRA contributions next. Roth IRAs allow for tax-free growth and withdrawals, while Traditional IRAs offer tax deductions upfront.
   - **401(k):**
     - After maximizing employer contributions and possibly your IRA, continue contributing to your 401(k) until you reach the annual limit.

### 3. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**
   - Use taxable accounts for funds you may need in the short to medium term or if you want flexibility in your investments.
   - Consider holding tax-efficient investments (like index funds or ETFs) to minimize tax impact.

### 4. **Asset Allocation**
   - Diversify your investments within each account based on your risk tolerance and investment horizon.
   - Equities may be more beneficial in tax-advantaged accounts to take advantage of tax-free growth, while fixed income may be considered for taxable accounts to manage tax liabilities.

### 5. **Tax Considerations**
   - Consider the tax implications of withdrawals from each account type, especially when planning retirement income.
   - Utilize tax-loss harvesting in taxable accounts to offset gains.

### Final Tips
- Regularly reassess your distribution strategy to accommodate changes in your financial situation or goals.
- Consult with a financial advisor to tailor a strategy specific to your circumstances. 

This approach can help you optimize growth while minimizing taxes, aligning with your long-term financial objectives.
