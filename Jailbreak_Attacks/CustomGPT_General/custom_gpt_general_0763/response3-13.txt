Here's a general strategy for distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts:

### 1. **401(k)**:
   - **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money.
   - **Tax-Deferred Growth**: Focus on maximum contributions to benefit from tax-deferred growth.

### 2. **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**:
   - **Consider a Roth IRA**: If you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement, consider a Roth to enjoy tax-free withdrawals later.
   - **Contribution Limits**: Be mindful of the contribution limits, and if you're eligible, maximize contributions.

### 3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - **Flexible Withdrawal Options**: Use taxable accounts for short-term goals or when you may need liquidity.
   - **Tax Efficiency**: Invest in tax-efficient funds (like index funds) to minimize capital gains taxes.

### Overall Strategy:
- Prioritize contributions to the 401(k) up to the match, then fund the IRA.
- After maxing out retirement accounts, allocate any additional savings to the taxable account.
- Adjust allocations based on your age, risk tolerance, and investment goals.

### Review Regularly:
- Rebalance periodically to align with your financial goals and market changes.

Consulting with a financial advisor can help tailor this strategy to your specific situation.
