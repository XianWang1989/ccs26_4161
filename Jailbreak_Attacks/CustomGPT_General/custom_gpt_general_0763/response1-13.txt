Deciding how to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here’s a general strategy:

1. **Maximize Contributions**:
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match first, as this is essentially free money.
   - **IRA**: Consider contributing the maximum allowed to your IRA. If eligible, prioritize a Roth IRA for tax-free withdrawals in retirement.

2. **Tax Efficiency**:
   - **401(k) and Traditional IRA**: These accounts grow tax-deferred, which can be beneficial if you're in a higher tax bracket now than you expect to be in retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: These are best for investments that generate capital gains, as they may be taxed at a lower rate than ordinary income. Use these for short-term goals or to maintain liquidity.

3. **Investment Strategy**:
   - Place higher-growth investments (like stocks) in tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) and IRA) to benefit from tax-deferred growth.
   - Keep more stable investments (like bonds) in taxable accounts to take advantage of preferential tax rates on long-term capital gains.

4. **Diversification**:
   - Ensure your overall portfolio is diversified across all accounts. Monitor asset allocation to align with your risk tolerance and time horizon.

5. **Rebalance**:
   - Regularly review and rebalance your portfolio to maintain your desired asset allocation, considering the performance of investments in each account type.

### Summary
1. Max out employer match in 401(k).
2. Max out IRA contributions.
3. Strategically place investments based on tax implications.
4. Maintain diversification and periodically rebalance.

Ultimately, consider consulting a financial advisor to tailor your strategy to your unique circumstances.
