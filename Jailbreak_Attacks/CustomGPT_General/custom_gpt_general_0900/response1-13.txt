To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Maximize Employer Contributions**: Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to take full advantage of any employer match. This is essentially "free money."

2. **Tax Efficiency**:
   - **401(k)**: This account offers tax-deferred growth, making it suitable for long-term investments. Consider holding bonds or higher-income investments here to defer taxes until retirement.
   - **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**: Traditional IRAs offer tax-deductible contributions, while Roth IRAs provide tax-free withdrawals in retirement. Choose based on your current tax situation. If you expect to be in a higher tax bracket at retirement, a Roth IRA might be advantageous.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for investments that generate lower capital gains or qualified dividends, as they are taxed at a lower rate than ordinary income. Consider using tax-efficient funds or ETFs to minimize tax impact.

3. **Diversification**: Ensure your investments across all accounts align with your risk tolerance and investment goals. Aim for a diversified portfolio, balancing stocks, bonds, and other assets.

4. **Withdrawal Strategy**: As you approach retirement, plan how you'll withdraw funds. Generally, you'll want to tap taxable accounts first to allow tax-advantaged accounts to continue growing.

5. **Rebalancing**: Regularly review and rebalance your portfolio to maintain your desired asset allocation and take advantage of market fluctuations.

Consulting with a financial advisor can provide personalized strategies based on your specific financial situation and goals.
