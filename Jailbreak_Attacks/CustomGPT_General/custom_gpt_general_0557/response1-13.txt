Distributing your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some general strategies to consider:

1. **Understand Account Types**:
   - **401(k)**: Often has employer matching contributions and tax advantages. Contributions are typically pre-tax, allowing for tax-deferred growth.
   - **IRA (Roth or Traditional)**: Offers tax benefits, with Roth IRAs providing tax-free withdrawals in retirement, while Traditional IRAs provide tax deductions on contributions.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Flexible, but subject to capital gains taxes. They are useful for short-term goals or emergency funds.

2. **Prioritize Contributions**:
   - **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get any employer matching contributions. This is essentially free money.
   - **Max Out IRAs**: If eligible, try to maximize your IRA contributions after meeting the 401(k) match. The tax advantages of IRAs can significantly benefit your long-term savings.

3. **Consider Tax Efficiency**:
   - Position investments with high growth or high income in tax-advantaged accounts to reduce your taxable income (e.g., stocks in IRAs, bonds in taxable accounts).
   - Use taxable accounts for investments that generate capital gains or those you plan to sell in the short term to avoid unnecessary taxes.

4. **Diversify Investments**:
   - Ensure a balanced mix of asset classes (stocks, bonds, cash) across all accounts to achieve your risk tolerance and investment goals.

5. **Review and Rebalance**:
   - Regularly review your asset allocation and rebalance if necessary. Market fluctuations can cause your portfolio to drift from its target allocation.

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**:
   - If you're uncertain or have complex financial situations, consulting with a financial advisor can provide tailored strategies and insights.

By considering these factors and adjusting according to your personal situation, you can create an effective distribution strategy for your investments.
