To effectively distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a match on your 401(k) contributions, aim to contribute at least enough to take full advantage of this benefit. It's essentially free money.

2. **401(k) Contributions**: After maximizing the match, consider contributing more to your 401(k) if you have high contributions limits and prefer the tax deferral.

3. **IRA Contributions**: Contribute to a Traditional or Roth IRA based on your eligibility and tax circumstances. Roth IRAs allow tax-free withdrawals in retirement, while Traditional IRAs offer immediate tax deductions.

4. **Taxable Accounts**: Once you've contributed to retirement accounts, allocate excess funds to taxable accounts. These can be used for short-term goals or flexibility, although they may be subject to capital gains tax on earnings.

5. **Asset Allocation**: Diversify your investments in all accounts based on your risk tolerance and investment horizon. Consider placing high-growth assets in tax-advantaged accounts (like stocks) and more stable investments (like bonds) in taxable accounts to minimize tax implications.

6. **Tax Efficiency**: Hold tax-efficient investments in taxable accounts (like index funds) and less tax-efficient ones (like actively managed funds) in tax-advantaged accounts to optimize tax liabilities.

7. **Review and Adjust**: Regularly review your portfolio to ensure it aligns with your financial goals, risk tolerance, and changing tax laws.

Consider consulting a financial advisor to tailor these strategies to your unique financial situation.
