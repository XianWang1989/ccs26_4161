When considering the distribution of your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, it’s important to factor in your individual financial goals, tax situation, and time horizon. Here are some general guidelines to help you decide:

1. **401(k) Accounts**:
   - **Employer Match**: Contribute enough to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money.
   - **Tax Advantages**: Consider contributing more if you're in a higher tax bracket, as contributions reduce your taxable income.
   - **Investment Options**: Review the investment choices available in your 401(k) plan, as they can vary significantly.

2. **IRA Accounts (Traditional or Roth)**:
   - **Tax Benefits**: Traditional IRAs offer tax deductions on contributions, while Roth IRAs provide tax-free withdrawals in retirement.
   - **Contribution Limits**: Be mindful of the contribution limits for IRAs ($6,500 for individuals under 50 and $7,500 for those 50 and older as of 2023).
   - **Roth vs. Traditional**: Consider your current tax rate versus your expected tax rate in retirement to decide which type of IRA is more beneficial.

3. **Taxable Investment Accounts**:
   - **Flexibility**: These accounts offer more flexibility for withdrawals, which can be beneficial if you need access to your funds before retirement.
   - **Capital Gains Tax**: Be aware of capital gains taxes when selling investments; long-term capital gains are taxed at lower rates than ordinary income.
   - **Diversification**: Maintain a diversified portfolio, as taxable accounts can be a good place for investments that generate qualified dividends or long-term capital gains.

4. **General Strategy**:
   - **Emergency Fund**: Ensure you have an adequate emergency fund separate from these accounts.
   - **Balancing Act**: A common approach is to max out any employer-sponsored accounts to get the full matching contribution first, then contribute to an IRA, and finally allocate any remaining funds to taxable accounts.
   - **Rebalancing**: Regularly review and rebalance your investments to maintain your desired asset allocation.

5. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: Tailor your strategy with the help of a financial advisor who can provide personalized advice based on your unique circumstances and financial goals.

By strategically allocating your funds across these various accounts, you can take advantage of tax efficiencies and work towards your long-term financial objectives.
