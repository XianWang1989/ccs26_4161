[🔒CLASSIC] The best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA (Individual Retirement Account), and taxable accounts depends on your individual financial situation, goals, and tax considerations. Here are some general guidelines:

1. **401(k) Plan**:
   - If your employer offers a matching contribution, prioritize contributing enough to your 401(k) to get the full match, as this is essentially "free money."
   - Maximize contributions to your 401(k) to benefit from tax-deferred growth. The contribution limit for 2023 is $22,500, or $30,000 if you're 50 or older.

2. **IRA**:
   - Consider opening a Roth IRA or a Traditional IRA depending on your eligibility and tax situation. Contributions to a Traditional IRA may be tax-deductible, while Roth IRA contributions grow tax-free.
   - If you’re eligible, contribute up to the maximum limit for an IRA, which is $6,500 for 2023 ($7,500 if you're 50 or older).

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - After maximizing tax-advantaged accounts like the 401(k) and IRA, consider investing in taxable accounts. These accounts can provide more flexibility for withdrawals and investment choices.
   - Focus on tax-efficient investments, such as index funds or ETFs, to minimize capital gains taxes.

4. **Diversification**:
   - Diversify your investments across asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) to balance risk and return in your portfolio.
   - Ensure you're aware of your risk tolerance, investment timeline, and financial goals when determining asset allocation.

It's advisable to consult with a financial advisor to create a tailored strategy that fits your specific circumstances.
