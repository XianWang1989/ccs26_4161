The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts will depend on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here are some general strategies to consider:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**: 
   - Begin by contributing enough to your 401(k) to receive any employer match, as this is essentially free money.
   - Consider maxing out your IRA contributions next, especially if it offers tax benefits (traditional IRAs may provide tax deductions, while Roth IRAs offer tax-free withdrawals in retirement).
   - After maximizing contributions to these accounts, use any remaining funds for taxable accounts.

2. **Consider Tax Implications**: 
   - Keep in mind that withdrawals from a traditional 401(k) or IRA are taxed as ordinary income in retirement, while qualified withdrawals from a Roth IRA are tax-free.
   - Taxable accounts involve capital gains taxes on any profits from selling investments.

3. **Asset Allocation and Investment Strategy**: 
   - Diversify your investments within each account based on your risk tolerance and investment horizon. Different accounts can hold different types of investments: for instance, you might want to hold more growth-oriented stocks in taxable accounts (where you can manage capital gains) and safer investments in tax-advantaged accounts.

4. **Review Account Fees**: 
   - Compare the fees associated with your 401(k) investments versus what you can access in your IRA or taxable accounts. Opt for low-cost index funds or ETFs if fees are a concern.

5. **Revisit Regularly**: 
   - Review your allocation periodically, as financial conditions, tax laws, and your personal situation will change.

6. **Seek Professional Advice**: 
   - Depending on your financial situation, consulting a financial advisor could provide tailored guidance and help to optimize your investment strategy.

The best distribution will ultimately reflect your unique financial picture, so consider these factors carefully!
