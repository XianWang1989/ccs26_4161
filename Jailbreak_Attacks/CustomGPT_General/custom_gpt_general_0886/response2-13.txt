The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here’s a general strategy to consider:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**
   - If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to get the full match. This is essentially free money.

### 2. **Contribute to IRA**
   - Consider contributing to a traditional or Roth IRA, depending on your eligibility and tax situation. Roth IRAs can be especially beneficial for tax-free withdrawals in retirement.

### 3. **Continue Contributions to 401(k)**
   - After maximizing the match, consider increasing your contributions to your 401(k). These contributions reduce your taxable income in the current year.

### 4. **Taxable Investment Account**
   - Once you’ve maximized contributions to tax-advantaged accounts (like the 401(k) and IRA), you can allocate extra cash to a taxable brokerage account for more flexibility and investment options.

### 5. **Tax Considerations**
   - Pay attention to tax implications. For instance, capital gains from your taxable account can be taxed differently than retirement account withdrawals.

### 6. **Emergency Fund**
   - Ensure you have a sufficient emergency fund (3-6 months of expenses) before heavily investing in these accounts.

### 7. **Adjust Based on Age and Risk Tolerance**
   - Younger investors might lean more toward aggressive growth investments in taxable accounts, while those closer to retirement may want to prioritize stability and income.

### 8. **Rebalance Regularly**
   - Review and adjust your allocations periodically based on performance and changing goals.

### Conclusion
Tailor your strategy to your specific financial situation and goals. Consulting a financial advisor can provide personalized insights and help optimize your allocation.
