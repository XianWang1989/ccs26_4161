The best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here’s a general approach:

1. **401(k)**:
   - **Employer Match**: Contribute enough to get the full employer match, if available. This is essentially free money.
   - **Tax-Deferred Growth**: Prioritize contributions to take advantage of tax-deferral benefits.

2. **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**:
   - **Tax Benefits**: If you qualify, consider contributing to a Roth IRA for tax-free withdrawals in retirement. If you expect your tax rate to be lower in retirement, a Traditional IRA might be more beneficial.
   - **Contribution Limits**: Max out contributions if possible, as IRAs typically have lower limits than 401(k)s.

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - **Flexibility**: Use these accounts for investments with potential short-term needs or goals. They offer more liquidity but are subject to capital gains taxes.
   - **Tax Efficiency**: Consider holding tax-efficient investments (like index funds or tax-managed funds) to minimize tax burdens.

### General Guidelines:
- **Diversification**: Ensure your overall investment strategy is diversified across different asset classes.
- **Rebalance**: Regularly reassess and rebalance your accounts based on performance and changing goals.
- **Consult a Financial Advisor**: Tailor your approach to your specific financial situation, risk tolerance, and retirement plans.

### Example Strategy:
1. Max out 401(k) contributions to the employer match.
2. Contribute to an IRA, focusing on Roth if eligible.
3. Invest any remaining funds in a taxable account for flexibility and liquidity.

Adapting this strategy to your unique circumstances will yield the best outcomes.
