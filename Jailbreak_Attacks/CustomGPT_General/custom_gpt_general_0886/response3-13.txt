Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and time horizon. Here’s a general approach:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First:**
   - **401(k):** Contribute enough to get any employer match, as this is essentially "free money."
   - **IRA:** Consider maxing out contributions to a Traditional or Roth IRA for tax benefits. Roth IRAs offer tax-free growth and withdrawals, while Traditional IRAs provide tax deductions.

### 2. **Assess Your Tax Situation:**
   - If you're in a higher tax bracket now, prioritize contributing to tax-deferred accounts (like a 401(k) or Traditional IRA) to reduce your taxable income.
   - If you're in a lower tax bracket, contributing to a Roth IRA might be beneficial for tax-free growth.

### 3. **Balance Contributions:**
   - After maximizing contributions to tax-advantaged accounts, distribute any additional money among your taxable accounts. Taxable accounts offer more flexibility for withdrawals and investing.

### 4. **Investment Strategy:**
   - Use tax-efficient investments in taxable accounts (e.g., index funds or ETFs) to minimize capital gains taxes.
   - Consider holding higher-growth investments (like stocks) in tax-advantaged accounts where they can grow tax-free or tax-deferred.

### 5. **Diversification:**
   - Ensure your investments are diversified across asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) and sectors to manage risk.

### 6. **Review Regularly:**
   - Regularly reassess your distribution strategy based on changes in income, expenses, and market conditions.

### Example Breakdown:
- **401(k):** 15-20% of salary (up to match)
- **IRA:** Max out yearly contributions ($6,500 for individuals under 50; $7,500 for those 50 or older in 2023)
- **Taxable Accounts:** Remaining funds after maxing out tax-advantaged accounts

### Conclusion:
An effective distribution strategy will align with your long-term goals and adapt as your financial situation evolves. Consulting with a financial advisor can provide personalized insights based on your circumstances.
