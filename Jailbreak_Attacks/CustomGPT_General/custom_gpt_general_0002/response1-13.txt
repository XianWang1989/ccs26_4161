When considering how to distribute your money amongst your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, it's important to keep in mind your financial goals, tax situation, and investment time horizon. Here’s a structured approach to help you make an informed decision:

### Key Factors to Consider

1. **Retirement Goals:**
   - Determine when you plan to retire and how much income you'll need during retirement.

2. **Tax Considerations:**
   - Contributions to a **401(k)** and **traditional IRA** are typically made pre-tax, meaning you defer taxes until withdrawal.
   - Contributions to a **Roth IRA** are made after-tax, allowing tax-free withdrawals in retirement.
   - **Taxable accounts** are subject to capital gains tax on profits and dividends taxed as ordinary income.

3. **Employer Matching:**
   - Maximize contributions to your **401(k)** up to any employer match, as that is effectively "free money."

4. **Contribution Limits:**
   - Be aware of annual contribution limits for each account type. For 2023:
     - **401(k):** $22,500 (or $30,000 if age 50+)
     - **IRA:** $6,500 (or $7,500 if age 50+)

### Suggested Distribution Strategy

1. **Maximize 401(k) Contributions:**
   - Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match if available.

2. **Contribute to an IRA:**
   - If you can afford more after maximizing your match, consider contributing to a traditional or Roth IRA, depending on your tax situation and retirement plans.

3. **Continue 401(k) Contributions:**
   - After achieving the IRA contribution, return to maximizing your 401(k) contributions up to its limit.

4. **Utilize Taxable Accounts for Additional Savings:**
   - If you have additional savings after maximizing both accounts, consider contributions to a taxable account. This allows for more liquidity and flexibility.

5. **Adjust Based on Age and Goals:**
   - Younger investors might prioritize growth via higher-risk investments in their retirement accounts, while those closer to retirement may want to shift towards more stable investments.
    
6. **Rebalance Regularly:**
   - Regularly review and rebalance your investments to maintain your desired asset allocation across all accounts.

### Example Distribution

- **Age 30:**
  - 401(k): Maximize employer match ($1,500/month)
  - Roth IRA: $500/month
  - Taxable accounts: Remaining savings

- **Age 50:**
  - 401(k): Max out contributions ($1,875/month)
  - Traditional/Roth IRA: Max out contributions ($500/month)
  - Taxable accounts: Additional savings as needed.

### Consultation

Lastly, consider speaking to a financial advisor who can provide tailored advice based on your individual situation and help you create a comprehensive financial plan. 

### Conclusion

The best approach to distribute funds among these account types involves maximizing tax advantages, employer contributions, and aligning with your long-term financial goals.
