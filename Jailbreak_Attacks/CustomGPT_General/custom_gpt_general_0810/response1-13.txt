Distributing your money across a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some key considerations:

### 1. **Tax Advantages:**
   - **401(k):** Contributions are pre-tax, reducing your taxable income. Ideal for long-term growth.
   - **IRA (Traditional/Roth):** 
     - *Traditional IRA:* Pre-tax contributions may lower taxable income. Taxes owed on withdrawals.
     - *Roth IRA:* Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, and withdrawals are tax-free in retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** No tax advantages, but offer flexibility for withdrawals without penalties.

### 2. **Investment Goals:**
   - If your goal is retirement, max out your 401(k) and IRA first to take advantage of tax benefits.
   - Use taxable accounts for shorter-term goals, as they offer more liquidity.

### 3. **Contribution Limits:**
   - Be aware of contribution limits for each account type. Max out employer matches in your 401(k) first.

### 4. **Diversification:**
   - Maintain a diversified portfolio across all accounts to balance risk and return.

### 5. **Withdrawal Strategy:**
   - Consider how and when you plan to withdraw funds in retirement. This will influence how much you should contribute to each account type.

### **Suggested Allocation Strategy:**
1. **Max out your 401(k)** contributions, especially to take advantage of employer matching.
2. **Contribute to your IRA** (Traditional or Roth, depending on your tax situation).
3. Direct any additional savings to taxable accounts for flexibility.

### **Overall Tips:**
- Review your asset allocation regularly.
- Consult with a financial advisor for personalized advice based on your circumstances.
