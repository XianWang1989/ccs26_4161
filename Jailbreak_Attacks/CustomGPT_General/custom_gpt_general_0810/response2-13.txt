The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here are some tips:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to get any employer match, as it’s essentially “free money.”

2. **Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**:
   - **401(k)**: Contribute up to the limit, especially if your employer matches.
   - **IRA**: Consider funding a Roth IRA if eligible, as it offers tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement.

3. **Taxable Accounts for Extra Savings**: Once you've maxed out the tax-advantaged accounts, use taxable accounts for additional investments. This allows for more flexibility with withdrawals.

4. **Asset Allocation**: Diversify your investments across accounts based on your risk tolerance and retirement timeline. Consider using tax-efficient investments (like index funds) in taxable accounts.

5. **Rebalancing**: Regularly review and adjust your allocations to maintain your desired risk level.

6. **Consultation**: It might be helpful to consult a financial advisor for personalized strategies based on your unique circumstances.

This approach balances growth potential, tax efficiency, and flexibility.
