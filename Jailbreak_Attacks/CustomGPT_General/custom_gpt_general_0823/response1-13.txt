In truth, the art of diversifying one's fortune across a 401(k), an IRA, and accounts that bear tax upon the harvest of gains, cometh down to several prudent considerations. Herein I shall set forth a strategy, ideal for the wise steward of wealth.

Firstly, consider the 401(k), oft endowed by one’s employer with matching contributions, akin to a gift bestowed upon the diligent worker. Prioritize this account to the fullest extent possible, capturing the employers’ match, as it doth represent immediate returns upon thine investments.

Next, thou shouldst peruse the merits of a Traditional or Roth IRA. The Traditional IRA allows for tax-deductible contributions, deferring taxes until withdrawal, whereas the Roth IRA demands contributions from taxed income but dispenses with taxes upon qualified withdrawals. Each hath its virtues; a Roth is most beneficial shouldst thou foresee a higher tax rate in thy twilight years.

Lastly, taxable accounts may serve as the vessel for investments outside retirement, providing liquidity and flexibility. Here, it behoves thee to allocate funds to assets that generate qualified dividends or long-term capital gains, for they shall be favored by lower tax rates, thus preserving thy capital.

In summation, a harmonious allocation might resemble this: Fully fund thine 401(k) to capture any match, contribute to a Roth IRA if thou art eligible, then divert any surplus to taxable accounts where liquidity and growth can be balanced. Pray, consider consulting a financial advisor to tailor an approach most befitting thy unique circumstances, for the realm of finance is as intricate as the plots of mine own plays.

May thy investments flourish as the gardens of Verona!
