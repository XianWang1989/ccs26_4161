Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts can depend on various factors such as your income, tax situation, and investment goals. Here are some general strategies:

### 1. Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts
- **401(k):** If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to get the full match first.
- **IRA (Traditional or Roth):** After maximizing your 401(k) match, consider contributing to an IRA. A Roth IRA can be beneficial if you expect your tax rate to increase.

### 2. Evaluate Your Tax Bracket
- **Traditional Accounts:** Contributions lower your taxable income now but are taxed upon withdrawal. This can be beneficial if you expect to be in a lower tax bracket in retirement.
- **Roth Accounts:** Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, but withdrawals are tax-free. This is advantageous if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement.

### 3. Consider Investment Goals
- **Short-Term Needs:** If you anticipate needing funds in the next few years, prioritize taxable accounts or a high-yield savings account for liquidity.
- **Long-Term Growth:** Focus on maxing out tax-advantaged accounts for potential growth.

### 4. Diversification
- Ensure that your investments are well-diversified across all accounts. This can help manage risk and optimize returns.

### Example Allocation Strategy
1. **401(k):** Contribute enough to get the employer match (e.g., 6% of salary).
2. **IRA:** Max out contributions to a Roth or Traditional IRA depending on your tax strategy.
3. **Taxable Accounts:** Invest any remaining funds after maximizing the above accounts.

### 5. Review Regularly
Monitor your investments and make adjustments as necessary based on changes in your income, tax laws, and financial goals.

It's often beneficial to consult with a financial advisor to tailor a strategy specific to your situation.
