To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to take full advantage of any employer matching contributions, as this is essentially "free money."

2. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **Traditional IRA/401(k)**: These accounts offer tax-deferred growth, which can be beneficial if you expect to be in a lower tax bracket during retirement.
   - **Roth IRA/401(k)**: If you qualify, these accounts allow for tax-free withdrawals in retirement, making them advantageous if you anticipate being in a higher tax bracket later.

3. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**: Once you've maximized contributions to tax-advantaged accounts, consider investing in taxable accounts for more flexibility with withdrawals and to have access to funds for short-term goals.

4. **Asset Allocation**: Diversify investments across these accounts based on your risk tolerance and time horizon. Generally, higher-risk assets can be held in tax-advantaged accounts for growth, while more stable investments can be placed in taxable accounts to manage capital gains taxes.

5. **Rebalance Regularly**: Review and adjust your asset allocation periodically to stay aligned with your investment goals and risk tolerance.

6. **Consider Your Tax Situation**: Withdrawals from taxable accounts can trigger capital gains taxes, while tax-advantaged accounts have different tax implications, so plan withdrawals carefully in retirement.

Consulting with a financial advisor can also provide personalized strategies tailored to your specific financial situation and retirement goals.
