To optimize your investment strategy among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following guidelines:

### 1. **Understand Account Types:**
   - **401(k):** Typically employer-sponsored, allowing for pre-tax contributions (traditional) or after-tax contributions (Roth). Matches are usually available.
   - **IRA (Individual Retirement Account):** Can be traditional or Roth. Provides tax benefits, but requires contribution limits.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Investments are made with after-tax dollars. Any gains are subject to capital gains taxes but provide flexibility in withdrawals.

### 2. **Prioritize Contributions:**
   - **Max out Employer Match in 401(k):** Always contribute enough to get the full employer match, as this is essentially "free money."
   - **Contribute to an IRA:** If eligible, contribute to a traditional or Roth IRA to take advantage of tax benefits. Roth IRAs are particularly beneficial if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement.
   - **Max out 401(k):** After maxing out the match, consider maximizing contributions to your 401(k) if you have additional funds, as it reduces taxable income.

### 3. **Investment Allocation:**
   - **401(k) and IRA:** Generally more tax-advantaged, lean towards assets that benefit from tax deferral (e.g., bonds, REITs).
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Focus on stocks or index funds since long-term capital gains taxes are lower than ordinary income taxes and allow for tax-loss harvesting.

### 4. **Diversification:**
   - Diversify across account types and investment options to mitigate risk. Make sure your overall portfolio is balanced in terms of asset allocation (stocks, bonds, etc.).

### 5. **Rebalancing:**
   - Regularly review and rebalance your investments across accounts to align with your risk tolerance and investment goals.

### 6. **Withdrawal Strategies:**
   - Plan how and when to withdraw from these accounts to minimize tax impacts in retirement. Typically, withdrawing from taxable accounts first can help preserve tax-advantaged accounts for longer.

### Conclusion:
The best way to distribute your money largely depends on your individual financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timelines. It's often wise to consult with a financial advisor to tailor a strategy to your specific circumstances.
