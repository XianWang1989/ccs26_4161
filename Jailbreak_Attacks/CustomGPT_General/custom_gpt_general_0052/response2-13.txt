To effectively distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Maximize Contributions**: 
   - Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to get any employer match (often deemed "free money").
   - Then, focus on maxing out your IRA contributions if you qualify (for 2023, the limit is $6,500, or $7,500 if you're 50 or older).

2. **Tax Efficiency**:
   - Place tax-inefficient investments, such as bonds or REITs, in your tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) and IRA) to defer taxes.
   - Keep tax-efficient investments, like index funds and ETFs, in your taxable account to take advantage of favorable capital gains rates.

3. **Investment Strategy**:
   - Diversify your investments. In your 401(k) and IRA, include a mix of stocks, bonds, and other vehicles depending on your risk tolerance and time horizon.
   - For your taxable account, consider investments that have lower turnover to minimize taxes on capital gains.

4. **Withdrawal Strategy**:
   - Plan ahead for retirement withdrawals. Generally, tax-advantaged accounts should be drawn upon last to maximize their tax-deferred growth.

5. **Rebalance Regularly**:
   - Ensure that your asset allocation remains aligned with your goals by periodically reviewing and rebalancing your accounts.

6. **Consult Financial Advice**:
   - Depending on your unique situation, it may be beneficial to consult with a financial advisor to tailor a plan that suits your financial goals and tax situation.

In summary, prioritize tax-advantaged accounts for maximum contributions, strategically allocate investments based on tax efficiency, and rebalance regularly for optimal growth. Always align your distribution strategy with your long-term financial goals.
