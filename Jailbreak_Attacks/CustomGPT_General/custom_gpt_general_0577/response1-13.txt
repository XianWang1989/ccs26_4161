Distributing your money across a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts can optimize your investment strategy and tax efficiency. Here are some tips:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to get any employer match. This is essentially free money.

2. **Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**: Prioritize funding your tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) and IRA) before taxable accounts. These accounts grow tax-deferred or tax-free (in the case of Roth accounts).

3. **Understand Your IRA Options**: If you have a traditional IRA, contributions may be tax-deductible, whereas Roth IRA contributions are made with after-tax dollars but grow tax-free. Consider your future tax situation when choosing.

4. **Asset Allocation**:
   - **401(k)**: Often have limited investment options. Focus on low-cost index funds or target-date funds.
   - **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**: This account can hold a broader range of investments. Consider higher-risk, higher-growth options, like stocks, if you have a long investment horizon.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for investments that may generate more taxable income. Consider holding tax-efficient ETFs or index funds to minimize capital gains.

5. **Rebalancing**: Regularly review and rebalance your portfolios to align with your risk tolerance and financial goals.

6. **Withdrawal Strategy**: Plan how you will withdraw funds in retirement—traditional accounts are taxed as ordinary income, while Roth withdrawals are tax-free under certain conditions.

Each individual's situation is unique, so consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized guidance based on your specific financial goals and tax situation.
