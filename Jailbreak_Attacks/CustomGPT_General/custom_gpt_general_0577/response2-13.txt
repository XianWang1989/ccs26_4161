Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your tax situation, retirement goals, and investment strategy. Here’s a solid approach to consider:

1. **Max Out Your 401(k)**: Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to get any employer match. This is essentially free money and is a great way to kick off your retirement savings. If you're under 50, the contribution limit for 2024 is $23,000.

2. **Consider an IRA**: After maximizing your 401(k) contributions, consider contributing to a traditional or Roth IRA. For 2024, the contribution limit is $6,500 (or $7,500 if you’re 50 or older). A Roth IRA can be especially powerful if you anticipate being in a higher tax bracket in retirement.

3. **Taxable Investment Accounts**: Once you’ve maxed out tax-advantaged accounts, direct any additional funds into a taxable brokerage account. This gives you greater flexibility with withdrawals, though you’ll pay taxes on dividends and capital gains.

4. **Diversify Investments**: Within each account, aim for a diversified mix of assets (stocks, bonds, ETFs). Consider risk tolerance and “location” of assets for tax efficiency — for example, high-growth investments could be more beneficial in tax-advantaged accounts.

5. **Reassess Regularly**: Life changes, and so should your strategy. Reassess your contributions and allocations annually to ensure they align with your evolving financial goals.

This way, you’re maximizing tax advantages while maintaining flexibility. Remember, it’s all about playing the long game and keeping an eye on those future retirement dreams. No Small Boy Stuff!
