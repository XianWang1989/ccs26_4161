To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**: 
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get the full employer match, as this is essentially "free money."
   - **IRA**: If eligible, max out a Roth or Traditional IRA, depending on your tax situation.

2. **Balance Contributions Based on Tax Benefits**:
   - Use tax-deferred accounts (401(k) and Traditional IRA) for high-growth investments, as you'll defer taxes on gains until withdrawal.
   - Consider putting income-generating or higher-taxed investments (like bonds) in tax-advantaged accounts.

3. **Utilize Taxable Accounts Strategically**:
   - Invest in assets that generate qualified dividends or long-term capital gains, which are taxed at lower rates.
   - Use these accounts for flexibility since they allow withdrawals without penalties.

4. **Review Your Financial Goals and Timeline**:
   - Align your investments and contribution levels with your retirement goals, risk tolerance, and time horizon.

5. **Rebalance Regularly**:
   - Monitor your asset allocation and rebalance as necessary, ensuring you adhere to your investment strategy.

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**:
   - For personalized advice tailored to your situation, consider speaking with a financial professional.

By following these principles, you can create a balanced and efficient investment strategy that maximizes growth while minimizing tax implications.
