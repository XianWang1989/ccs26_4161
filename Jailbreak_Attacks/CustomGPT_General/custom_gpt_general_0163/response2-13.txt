Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts involves considering several factors like your financial goals, tax situation, and investment time horizon. Here’s a strategy to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to get any available employer match. This is essentially free money.

2. **Contribute to an IRA**: If you can, contribute to an IRA (Roth or Traditional) next. This allows for tax-advantaged growth, and with a Roth IRA, you can enjoy tax-free withdrawals in retirement.

3. **Max Out 401(k)**: Once you’ve hit your IRA contributions, go back to your 401(k) and consider maxing that out, especially if your employer offers a high match.

4. **Consider Taxable Accounts Last**: Use taxable accounts for additional savings. These are less tax-efficient compared to retirement accounts but offer flexibility for withdrawals.

5. **Asset Allocation**: Use tax-efficient investments (like index funds) in taxable accounts to minimize capital gains taxes, while holding actively managed funds or bonds in tax-advantaged accounts.

6. **Review Regularly**: Periodically assess and rebalance your investments to ensure they align with your risk tolerance and financial goals.

7. **Consult Financial Advisors**: Consider working with a financial advisor for personalized strategies.

This way, you balance tax efficiency with your retirement goals effectively!
