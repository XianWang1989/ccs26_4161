Determining the best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and time horizon for retirement. Here are some general strategies to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a match for your 401(k), try to contribute enough to take full advantage of this benefit. It's essentially free money.

2. **Consider Tax Implications**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are often pre-tax, allowing your investments to grow tax-deferred until withdrawal. However, withdrawals in retirement are taxed as ordinary income.
   - **IRA**: Depending on whether you have a Traditional or Roth IRA, contributions can be pre-tax (Traditional) or after-tax (Roth). Roth IRAs allow for tax-free withdrawals in retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Investments are subject to capital gains tax when sold. However, they offer more flexibility and access to funds without penalties.

3. **Prioritize Retirement Accounts**: Generally, it’s wise to contribute to retirement accounts first (401(k) and IRA) before investing in taxable accounts, as they offer tax advantages.

4. **Diversification of Investments**: Diversify your investments within each account type—consider stocks, bonds, and other asset classes to reduce risk.

5. **Reassess Regularly**: Your financial situation and goals may change over time, so it's essential to review and adjust your allocation regularly.

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: For personalized advice tailored to your specific financial situation, consider consulting a financial advisor.

Ultimately, the right distribution can vary significantly based on individual circumstances.
