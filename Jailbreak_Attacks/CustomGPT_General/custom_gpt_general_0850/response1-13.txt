When considering how to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, you should take into account several factors, such as your current financial situation, your retirement goals, and your tax strategy. Here are some general guidelines to help you:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**
   - **Contribute enough** to your 401(k) to take full advantage of any employer matching contributions. This is essentially free money.

### 2. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**
   - **Max out IRAs**: If eligible, consider fully funding a traditional or Roth IRA. This can provide tax-free growth or tax-deferral benefits.
   - **Contribute to a 401(k)**: After maxing out your IRA, continue to contribute to your 401(k) up to the maximum limit.

### 3. **Consider Tax Implications**
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for additional investments after maximizing tax-advantaged accounts. They can offer flexibility and a variety of investment options without penalties for early withdrawal.
   - **Asset Location**: Favor investments generating high returns or frequent trading (like stocks) inside tax-advantaged accounts and hold lower-return investments (like bonds) in taxable accounts to minimize taxes.

### 4. **Evaluate Your Time Horizon**
   - If you have a long time until retirement, you might want to take on more risk in your 401(k) and IRA. If you’re closer to retirement, prioritize preserving capital and consider safer investments.

### 5. **Review and Adjust Regularly**
   - Periodically review your asset allocation and rebalance as necessary based on your investment goals, market conditions, and changes in your financial situation.

### 6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**
   - If you're unsure, consider consulting a financial advisor to create a personalized strategy based on your financial situation.

By following these steps, you can create a balanced approach to distributing your money across your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts.
