Distributing your money effectively among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here’s a general approach:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match (a key benefit). Consider maximizing your contributions if you can afford it since this account grows tax-deferred.
   - **Traditional IRA or Roth IRA**: After maximizing your 401(k) match, consider contributing to an IRA. If you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement, a Roth IRA may be beneficial, as withdrawals in retirement are tax-free.

### 2. **Consider Your Tax Bracket**
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Use for investments that may generate a lot of short-term capital gains or high dividends, as these can be taxed at your ordinary income tax rate. 

### 3. **Invest Based on Time Horizon**
   - **Long-term**: Invest more aggressively in retirement accounts, given their tax advantages and longer time frames.
   - **Short-term needs**: Keep more liquid assets in taxable accounts.

### 4. **Rebalance Periodically**
   - Review your asset allocation across all accounts to ensure it aligns with your risk tolerance and financial goals.

### 5. **Consult a Financial Advisor**
   - For personalized advice based on your specific circumstances, consider consulting a financial advisor.

### Summary
Start by maximizing contributions to tax-advantaged accounts, consider your tax situation, and gradually allocate funds to taxable accounts as needed, focusing on your investment goals and timelines. Adjust your strategy as your financial situation evolves.
