Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your retirement goals, tax implications, and investment strategy. Here’s a general approach:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to receive any employer match. This is essentially free money.

2. **Fund an IRA**: Once you’ve secured the match, consider contributing to a traditional or Roth IRA, depending on your tax situation and whether you prefer tax-deferred growth or tax-free withdrawals in retirement.

3. **Return to 401(k)**: After maximizing your IRA contributions (if you can), return to your 401(k) to increase your contributions, especially if you’re aiming to reach the annual contribution limit.

4. **Taxable Accounts**: If you have excess funds after maximizing retirement accounts, consider investing in taxable accounts. This can provide more flexibility and access to your money, although you’ll face capital gains taxes on profits.

5. **Consider Tax Implications**: High-growth investments may be better suited for tax-advantaged accounts (like 401(k)s or IRAs) to defer taxes, whereas more stable investments might be suitable for taxable accounts to minimize taxes on interest and dividends.

6. **Rebalance Regularly**: Monitor and adjust your allocations according to your investment goals and market conditions.

Always consider consulting a financial advisor for advice tailored to your specific financial situation.
