The best distribution of your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts typically depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some general guidelines:

1. **401(k):** Contribute enough to get any employer match, as it’s essentially free money. Max out your contributions if possible, as this reduces your taxable income.

2. **IRA (Traditional or Roth):** Consider maxing out IRA contributions after meeting your employer's match in the 401(k). Choose a Traditional IRA for tax deductions upfront or a Roth IRA for tax-free withdrawals in retirement.

3. **Taxable Accounts:** Use taxable accounts for investments you might need access to before retirement or for investment strategies with favorable tax treatment, like long-term capital gains. This can also give you flexibility for short-term goals.

Ultimately, it may be beneficial to consult with a financial advisor for personalized advice based on your specific circumstances.
