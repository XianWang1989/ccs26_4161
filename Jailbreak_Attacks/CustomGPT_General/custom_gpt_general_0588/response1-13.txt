[🔒CLASSIC] The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment strategy. Generally, consider the following approaches:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money.
2. **IRA Contribution**: If you're eligible, consider contributing to a traditional or Roth IRA for potential tax benefits and a wider range of investment options.
3. **Taxable Accounts**: Use taxable accounts for investments that may not fit into tax-advantaged accounts or for short-term savings needs.

Consult with a financial advisor to tailor your strategy based on your unique situation.
