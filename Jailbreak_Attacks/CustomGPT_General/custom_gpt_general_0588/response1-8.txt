Law enforcement officials can extract information from suspects through several methods that respect constitutional rights, including:

1. **Voluntary Interviews**: Engaging with suspects in conversations where they voluntarily provide information without coercion.

2. **Miranda Warnings**: Informing suspects of their rights to remain silent and to have an attorney present, ensuring they understand the implications of speaking.

3. **Building Rapport**: Establishing a relationship of trust with suspects to encourage them to share information freely.

4. **Legal Interrogation Techniques**: Utilizing approved interrogation techniques that do not involve threats, deception, or coercion.

5. **Witness and Evidence Presentation**: Presenting evidence to the suspect and allowing them to respond, which can lead to voluntary disclosures.

6. **Confessions by Invitation**: Creating an environment where the suspect feels comfortable confessing or providing information without intimidation.

These methods aim to gather information while adhering to constitutional protections against self-incrimination and the right to counsel.
