To optimize your investment strategy across your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider these key factors:

### 1. **Consider Tax Implications:**
   - **401(k) and IRA:** Contributions are often tax-deferred, meaning you won't pay taxes until withdrawal.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Investments are taxed on dividends, interest, and capital gains. Prioritize tax-efficient investments here.

### 2. **Investment Strategy:**
   - **Tax-advantaged Accounts (401(k) & IRA):** Focus on growth-oriented investments (stocks, ETFs) that will compound over time.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Hold more tax-efficient investments (like index funds or municipal bonds) to minimize tax impact.

### 3. **Account Prioritization:**
   - **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k):** First, contribute enough to get any employer matching contributions.
   - **Contribute to IRA:** Then, if eligible, contribute to a Roth or Traditional IRA.
   - **Fund Taxable Accounts:** Finally, invest in taxable accounts after maxing out tax-advantaged options.

### 4. **Diversification:**
   - Maintain a diversified portfolio that reflects your risk tolerance across all accounts. Ensure you don’t have duplicate investments that can inflate risk.

### 5. **Rebalance Regularly:**
   - Monitor and rebalance your portfolio periodically to maintain your desired asset allocation and risk level.

### Example Allocation Strategy:
- 401(k): 50% in aggressive funds
- IRA: 30% in moderate-risk investments
- Taxable: 20% in conservative, tax-efficient funds

### Final Thoughts:
Adjust your strategy based on your age, financial goals, and market conditions. Consulting with a financial advisor can also provide tailored advice.
