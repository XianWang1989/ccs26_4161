Distributing money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors:

1. **401(k) Contributions**:
   - Maximize employer match if available.
   - Focus on tax-deferred growth.

2. **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**:
   - Consider contributing to a Roth IRA if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement.
   - Traditional IRAs offer tax deductions now but withdrawals are taxed.

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - Use for short-term goals or after you’ve maxed out retirement accounts.
   - Hold tax-efficient investments (e.g., index funds, ETFs).

### General Strategy:
- **Max out Retirement Accounts**: Prioritize 401(k) and IRA contributions.
- **Diversify Investments**: Spread investments among stocks, bonds, and cash equivalents based on risk tolerance.
- **Rebalance Regularly**: Adjust your allocation based on performance and changing goals.

### Final Tips:
- Review tax implications for each account type.
- Consider retirement timeline and financial goals.
