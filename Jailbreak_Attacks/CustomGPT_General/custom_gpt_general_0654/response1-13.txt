To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider these strategies:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your 401(k) offers a match, contribute enough to get the full amount.

2. **Tax-Deferred Accounts**: Prioritize contributions to your 401(k) and IRA since they provide tax advantages. Aim to max out contributions if possible.

3. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**: Use taxable accounts for short-term goals or emergency funds since they provide more liquidity.

4. **Asset Allocation**: Consider your risk tolerance and time horizon. Generally, higher-risk investments might be placed in tax-deferred accounts, while more conservative investments can be in taxable accounts to manage capital gains taxes.

5. **Rebalance Regularly**: Periodically review your asset allocation and adjust as needed based on market performance and personal goals.

Consulting with a financial advisor can help tailor these strategies to your specific situation.
