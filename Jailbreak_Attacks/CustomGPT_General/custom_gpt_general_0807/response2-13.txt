To effectively distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **401(k) Contributions**:
   - **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to get the full employer match, as this is essentially free money.
   - **Higher Contribution Limits**: Take advantage of the higher contribution limits compared to IRAs. 

2. **IRA Contributions**:
   - **Roth vs. Traditional**: Choose between a Roth IRA (after-tax contributions and tax-free withdrawals) or a Traditional IRA (pre-tax contributions with taxes upon withdrawal) based on your current vs. expected future tax rate.
   - **Consider Tax Diversification**: Balance contributions between traditional and Roth IRAs to leverage different tax treatments.

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - **Diversify Investments**: Use taxable accounts for investments that generate long-term capital gains or qualified dividends to take advantage of lower tax rates.
   - **Flexibility**: These accounts provide more liquidity and flexibility for withdrawals without penalties, making them suitable for short-to-medium-term savings.

### Suggested Distribution Approach:
- **Emergency Fund**: Ensure you have a solid emergency fund in liquid assets before investing heavily in retirement accounts.
- **Aggressive Savings**: Allocate a significant portion to your 401(k) until you meet the employer match, then focus on the IRA for tax advantages.
- **Taxable Accounts**: Use any remaining funds to invest in taxable accounts, focusing on long-term investments with favorable tax treatment.

### Review and Adjust:
- Regularly review your asset allocation and rebalance as necessary based on market conditions and personal financial goals. 

Consulting with a financial advisor can also help tailor this strategy to your unique financial situation and retirement goals.
