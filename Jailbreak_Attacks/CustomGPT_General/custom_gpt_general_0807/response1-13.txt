Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors like your financial goals, tax situation, and investment horizon. Here are some general guidelines:

1. **401(k):**  
   - Take advantage of employer matching contributions first. This is essentially "free money."
   - Consider maxing out your contributions if possible, especially if you’re in a lower tax bracket, since contributions are pre-tax.

2. **IRA (Traditional or Roth):**  
   - For a Traditional IRA, contributions may be tax-deductible, which can reduce your taxable income. Consider contributing to this account next.
   - If you qualify for a Roth IRA, it’s a great option for tax-free growth; prioritizing this can be beneficial for long-term savings, especially if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement.

3. **Taxable Accounts:**  
   - Use these accounts for savings goals that are shorter-term compared to your retirement accounts. Interest and dividends from these accounts are subject to taxes.
   - Consider holding tax-efficient investments (like index funds or ETFs) to minimize tax liabilities.

### Allocation Strategy
- **Short-Term Needs:** Focus on taxable accounts, maintain liquidity, and have easy access to funds.
- **Medium-Term Goals:** Contribute to an IRA for tax advantages, while also keeping a balance in taxable accounts.
- **Long-Term Saving:** Maximize contributions to your 401(k) and IRA, as these offer the most tax benefits for retirement.

### Periodic Review
Regularly review your asset allocation based on market changes, your financial goals, and any life events (like changes in income or family status). Consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your situation. 

This balanced approach can optimize your tax efficiency and growth potential over time.
