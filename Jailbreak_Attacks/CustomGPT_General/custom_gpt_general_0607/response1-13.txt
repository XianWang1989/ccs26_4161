[🔒CLASSIC] The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here are some general guidelines:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a match for your 401(k) contributions, it's usually advisable to contribute enough to receive the full match, as this is essentially free money.

2. **Contribute to an IRA**: If you qualify for tax-deductible contributions to a traditional IRA or wish to make contributions to a Roth IRA, consider maxing out these accounts after securing the match in your 401(k).

3. **Evaluate Tax Considerations**: Taxable accounts can provide flexibility but are subject to capital gains taxes. Accounts with tax advantages (like 401(k)s and IRAs) should generally be prioritized for long-term retirement savings.

4. **Assess Your Investment Strategy**: Consider your risk tolerance and investment timeline. More aggressive investments can be allocated to tax-advantaged accounts, while more conservative investments might be placed in taxable accounts.

5. **Rebalance as Needed**: Periodically review and adjust your allocations based on changes in your financial goals or market conditions.

Consulting with a financial advisor can provide personalized advice tailored to your unique situation.
