The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment strategies. Here are some general guidelines to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k):** If your employer offers a matching contribution, contribute enough to your 401(k) to take full advantage of this. It's essentially "free money."

2. **401(k) Contributions:** After maximizing the match, continue contributing to your 401(k) to reduce your taxable income, especially if you are in a higher tax bracket. Consider increasing your contributions annually.

3. **IRA Contributions:** If you haven’t maxed out your IRA, consider contributing to a Traditional or Roth IRA. Roth IRAs are beneficial if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement, as qualified withdrawals are tax-free.

4. **Taxable Accounts:** Use taxable accounts for money you might need in the short term or for investments that generate qualified dividends or long-term capital gains, which are taxed at a lower rate than ordinary income. 

5. **Consider Tax Implications:** Balance your investments for tax efficiency. Hold tax-inefficient assets (like bonds or real estate) in tax-deferred accounts (401(k), IRA) and more tax-efficient investments (like index funds) in taxable accounts.

6. **Diversification and Risk Tolerance:** Ensure your overall portfolio aligns with your risk tolerance and investment goals. Diversify your investments across various asset classes.

7. **Review and Adjust Regularly:** Periodically review your distribution and adjust based on changes in your financial situation, market conditions, and retirement goals.
