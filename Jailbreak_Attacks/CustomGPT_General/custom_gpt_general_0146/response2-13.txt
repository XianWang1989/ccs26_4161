To effectively distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following principles:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**: 
   - **401(k)**: If your employer offers a matching contribution, prioritize contributing enough to get the full match. This is essentially "free money."
   - **IRA**: After maximizing your 401(k) match, contribute to an IRA (Traditional or Roth) depending on your income level and tax situation.

2. **Evaluate Tax Benefits**:
   - **Traditional vs. Roth IRA**: Choose a Traditional IRA if you expect to be in a lower tax bracket in retirement; a Roth IRA is better if you anticipate being in a higher bracket.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for investments that generate qualified dividends or long-term capital gains to benefit from lower tax rates.

3. **Asset Allocation Strategy**:
   - **Diversify by Risk**: Spread investments across asset classes in each account type, keeping risk tolerance in mind. For example, equities for growth in your 401(k) and bonds for stability in your IRA.
   - **Place High-Growth Assets in Tax-Advantaged Accounts**: To take advantage of tax-free or tax-deferred growth.

4. **Annual Contribution Limits**: Be mindful of annual contribution limits for each account type and adjust your contributions accordingly to maximize benefits each year.

5. **Rebalance Regularly**: Periodically review and adjust your allocations based on performance and changing financial goals.

Lastly, remember to consider your overall financial plan and future needs. It’s wise to consult a financial advisor for personalized guidance.
