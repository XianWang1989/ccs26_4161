The optimal distribution of your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, timeline, and risk appetite. Here are some general strategies:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a 401(k) match, contribute enough to get the full match before allocating money elsewhere. This is essentially "free money."

2. **Tax Considerations**: 
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are pre-tax, reducing your taxable income now. However, withdrawals in retirement are taxed as ordinary income.
   - **IRA**: Depending on whether you choose a Traditional IRA (pre-tax) or a Roth IRA (after-tax), your tax implications will vary during retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Investments in taxable accounts may incur capital gains taxes when sold. However, they offer more flexibility for withdrawals without penalties.

3. **Retirement Timeline**:
   - If you're far from retirement, consider focusing on growth (e.g., stock-heavy investments) in your tax-advantaged accounts.
   - As you get closer to retirement, shift to more conservative investments (bonds, stable value funds) to preserve capital.

4. **Diversification**: Ensure a diversified investment strategy across all accounts. This can help manage risk and improve long-term returns.

5. **Contribution Limits**: Be aware of annual contribution limits for each account type to maximize your savings.

6. **Withdrawal Strategies**: Consider how you plan to withdraw funds during retirement. Taxable accounts can be more flexible, while tax-advantaged accounts might be better for long-term growth.

A common approach is to prioritize funding the 401(k) to the employer match, then max out an IRA, and finally contribute to a taxable account if you have additional savings.

**In essence, think of your investment strategy like planting a garden: different plants (accounts) grow best in certain conditions (tax situations), and with careful planning, your garden can flourish and yield great returns! 🌱**
