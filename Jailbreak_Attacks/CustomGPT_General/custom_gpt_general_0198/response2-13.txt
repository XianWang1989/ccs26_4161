The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts largely depends on your individual financial situation, retirement goals, tax considerations, and investment strategy. Here are some general guidelines that might help you:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**: If your employer offers a matching contribution, take full advantage of that first. Contributing enough to get the full match is essentially free money.

2. **Contribute to an IRA**: After maximizing your 401(k) match, consider contributing to a Traditional or Roth IRA. IRAs often provide more investment options and tax advantages. A Roth IRA offers tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement, while a Traditional IRA offers tax-deferred growth.

3. **Continue 401(k) Contributions**: If you can still contribute after maxing out your IRA, continue putting money into your 401(k) until you reach the annual contribution limit.

4. **Taxable Accounts for Additional Savings**: If you've maxed out your 401(k) and IRA, consider investing in taxable accounts for additional flexibility. Taxable accounts allow you to access your funds without penalties, unlike retirement accounts.

5. **Asset Allocation and Investment Strategy**: Within each account type, ensure your investments are diversified and aligned with your risk tolerance and time horizon. Generally, you might want to hold more tax-efficient investments (like index funds) in taxable accounts and allocate higher-growth investments (which might have less immediate tax impact) in tax-deferred accounts.

6. **Consider Tax Implications**: Be mindful of the tax implications of selling assets in your taxable accounts, and consider tax-efficient investing strategies to minimize capital gains taxes.

7. **Review Regularly**: Your financial situation and goals may change, so it’s important to review your distribution strategy regularly and adjust as necessary.

Ultimately, consulting with a financial advisor can provide personalized advice based on your unique circumstances.
