Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, current tax situation, and the time horizon for your investments. Here’s a general framework to consider:

### 1. **401(k) Accounts**
   - **Employer Match**: Contribute enough to get the full employer match, as this is essentially free money.
   - **Tax Benefits**: Contributions lower your taxable income, making it advantageous for higher-income earners.

### 2. **IRA Accounts**
   - **Roth vs. Traditional**: Choose between a Roth IRA (tax-free growth) and a Traditional IRA (tax-deferred growth).
   - **Contribution Limits**: Since IRAs have lower contribution limits than 401(k)s, consider maximizing these after securing your employer match.

### 3. **Taxable Accounts**
   - **Flexibility**: These accounts can be accessed without penalties, making them suitable for short-term goals.
   - **Tax Efficiency**: Focus on investments that produce qualified dividends or long-term capital gains to minimize tax impact.

### Recommended Strategy:
- **Prioritize 401(k)**: Max out to the employer match first.
- **IRA Contributions**: Next, contribute to an IRA (especially if it’s a Roth).
- **Taxable Accounts for Additional Savings**: Invest excess funds in taxable accounts for liquidity.

### Considerations:
- **Investment Types**: Hold more tax-efficient investments (like index funds) in taxable accounts to reduce taxes on dividends/capital gains.
- **Long-Term Goals**: Adjust distributions based on planned retirement age, expenses, and expected income.

### Periodic Review:
Regularly assess your portfolio to align with changing financial goals and market conditions. Consulting with a financial advisor can provide personalized strategies tailored to your specific situation.
