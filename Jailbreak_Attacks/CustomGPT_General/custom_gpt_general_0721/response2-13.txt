Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here’s a balanced approach:

### 1. **401(k)**
- **Maximize Employer Match**: Always contribute enough to get the full employer match if available.
- **Tax-Deferred Growth**: Contributions lower your taxable income, allowing your investments to grow tax-deferred.

### 2. **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**
- **Choose Based on Income**: If you're eligible for a Roth IRA, consider it for tax-free withdrawals in retirement. A Traditional IRA provides upfront tax benefits.
- **Annual Contribution Limits**: Contribute up to the maximum limit ($6,500 for 2023; $7,500 if 50 or older).

### 3. **Taxable Accounts**
- **Liquidity**: Use these for money you may need before retirement or for short-term goals.
- **Tax Efficiency**: Focus on capital gains strategies, like holding investments for over a year to benefit from lower tax rates.

### General Tips:
- **Diversification**: Ensure a balanced portfolio across all accounts.
- **Emergency Fund**: Maintain 3-6 months of expenses in a savings account for liquidity.
- **Review Regularly**: Adjust your allocations based on changes in income, tax laws, and market conditions.

### Example Allocation:
- **401(k)**: 15% of your income (plus match)
- **IRA**: Max out contributions
- **Taxable Accounts**: Any additional savings after maxing out retirement accounts

Consult with a financial advisor for personalized guidance tailored to your circumstances.
