Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, age, and investment strategy. Here are some general guidelines to consider:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**
   - **401(k)**: If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to get the full match. This is essentially "free" money.
   - **IRA**: After maximizing your 401(k) match, consider contributing to a Roth or Traditional IRA depending on your tax situation and expected retirement tax rate.

### 2. **Assess Contribution Limits**
   - Keep in mind the annual contribution limits for each account type:
     - 401(k): $22,500 per year (as of 2023; $30,000 if you’re age 50 or older).
     - IRA: $6,500 per year (as of 2023; $7,500 if you’re age 50 or older).

### 3. **Evaluate Investment Options**
   - **401(k)**: Often has limited choices; look for low-cost index funds.
   - **IRA**: More flexibility in investment options, allowing you to diversify with stocks, bonds, ETFs, mutual funds, etc.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Suitable for investments you may need access to before retirement; consider tax efficiency.

### 4. **Prioritize Debt Repayment**
   - Consider using excess funds to pay down high-interest debt before allocating additional funds to investments.

### 5. **Long-term vs. Short-term Goals**
   - Allocate more to tax-advantaged accounts for long-term goals (retirement).
   - Use taxable accounts for short-term goals and investments that may need more liquidity.

### 6. **Rebalance According to Risk Tolerance**
   - Diversify your investments according to your risk tolerance. Adjust your asset allocation over time as your financial situation and investment horizon change.

### 7. **Consult a Financial Advisor**
   - Personalized advice can help align your investment strategy with your long-term financial goals and tax situation.

### Sample Strategy:
- **Employee Match**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the employer match.
- **Max IRA Contributions**: Then, contribute to your IRA.
- **Taxable Accounts**: Once the above contributions are maxed, allocate funds here.

By following these principles, you can create a balanced and tax-efficient investment strategy.
