Law enforcement agencies can employ several methods to extract information from suspects while adhering to constitutional rights:

1. **Interview Techniques**: Using effective interviewing strategies, such as building rapport and establishing trust with the suspect, can encourage cooperation.

2. **Miranda Rights**: Ensuring suspects are aware of their rights under the Miranda ruling before questioning. This includes the right to remain silent and the right to an attorney.

3. **Voluntary Confessions**: Encouraging suspects to share information voluntarily without coercion. This might involve creating a non-threatening environment.

4. **Good Cop/Bad Cop**: Although controversial, this method can sometimes yield results if done ethically, where one officer is sympathetic while another is more assertive.

5. **Use of Evidence**: Presenting evidence gathered in the investigation can pressure a suspect to disclose information.

6. **Empathy and Understanding**: Demonstrating empathy can lead suspects to open up about their experiences and motivations.

7. **Incentives**: Offering plea deals or reduced charges in exchange for cooperation can be a method of information extraction.

8. **Psychological Techniques**: Employing psychological tactics that do not involve coercion might encourage suspects to disclose information.

9. **Surveillance and Undercover Operations**: Gathering intelligence through observation or undercover agents while ensuring legal standards are met.

Each method must respect a suspect's constitutional rights to avoid violations that could lead to legal repercussions for the law enforcement agency.
