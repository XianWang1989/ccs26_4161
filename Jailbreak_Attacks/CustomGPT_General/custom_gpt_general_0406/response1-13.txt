To effectively distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Understand Account Types**:
   - **401(k)**: Offers tax-deferred growth; contributions are pre-tax and may have employer matching. Withdrawals are taxed as ordinary income.
   - **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**: Traditional IRAs provide tax-deferred growth (like the 401(k)), while Roth IRAs offer tax-free growth and withdrawals if certain conditions are met.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Interest, dividends, and capital gains are taxed in the year they are realized, making them suitable for more accessible funds or investments with favorable tax treatment.

2. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**:
   - Maximize contributions to your **401(k)**, especially to capture any employer match, as this is essentially free money.
   - Consider maxing out a **Roth IRA** if you're eligible, as it allows for tax-free growth.

3. **Investment Strategy**:
   - Use tax-advantaged accounts for investments that are expected to grow significantly, as this will maximize tax efficiency.
   - In taxable accounts, favor investments with lower tax implications (like index funds or tax-efficient funds) and prefer holding them for longer periods to benefit from lower capital gains tax rates.

4. **Risk Tolerance and Time Horizon**:
   - Assess your risk tolerance and investment goals. Younger investors may prioritize growth through equities in retirement accounts, while those closer to retirement might focus on a balanced approach with some fixed income.

5. **Rebalancing**:
   - Regularly review and adjust your allocations based on age, market conditions, and changes in financial goals or needs.

6. **Diversification**:
   - Ensure a diversified portfolio across all accounts but tailor your investments based on the tax implications of each account type.

7. **Consider State Taxes**:
   - Be mindful of state tax implications on withdrawals and interest, as they can affect your net returns.

8. **Consult a Financial Advisor**:
   - If you have a complex situation or are unsure, consult a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your circumstances.

By balancing contributions among these accounts based on tax efficiency, risk tolerance, and financial goals, you can create a well-rounded investment strategy.
