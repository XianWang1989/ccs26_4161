The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts largely depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment preferences. Here are some general guidelines to consider:

1. **Understand Contribution Limits**: Be aware of the contribution limits for each account type. For 2023, the 401(k) limit is $22,500 (or $30,000 if you're over 50), while the IRA limit is $6,500 (or $7,500 if you're over 50).

2. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a matching contribution for your 401(k), aim to contribute enough to get the full match. This is essentially free money.

3. **Tax Considerations**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are made pre-tax, lowering your taxable income for the year. However, you'll pay taxes on withdrawals in retirement.
   - **Traditional IRA**: Contributions may be tax-deductible, but you’ll be taxed on withdrawals in retirement.
   - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are made after-tax, meaning you won’t pay taxes on withdrawals in retirement if certain conditions are met.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: You’ll pay taxes on any capital gains and dividend income as they are realized.

4. **Investment Goals**: Determine your investment timeline and risk tolerance. For long-term goals, you might prioritize your 401(k) and IRAs, while for short-term goals, taxable accounts might be more appropriate due to accessibility.

5. **Diversification**: Ensure your investments are diversified across different asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) to mitigate risk. 

6. **Review and Adjust**: Regularly review your investment strategy and asset allocation to ensure it aligns with your changing financial situation and goals.

It may also be beneficial to consult with a financial advisor to tailor a strategy specific to your individual circumstances.
