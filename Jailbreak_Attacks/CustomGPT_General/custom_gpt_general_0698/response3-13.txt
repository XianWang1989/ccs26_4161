Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some general guidelines:

### 1. Maximize Employer Match in 401(k):
   - Contribute enough to get the full employer match, as this is essentially "free money."

### 2. Consider Tax Advantages:
   - **401(k)**: Pre-tax contributions reduce taxable income, good for high earners.
   - **Traditional IRA**: Similar tax benefits as a 401(k).
   - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are after-tax, but withdrawals in retirement are tax-free. Great for young investors or those expecting to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Distribute funds here for short-term goals or for investments that generate lower tax burdens (like long-term capital gains).

### 3. Diversify Investments:
   - Allocate across asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) in each account type according to your risk tolerance and investment strategy.

### 4. Plan for Withdrawals:
   - In retirement, consider keeping taxable accounts to withdraw from first to allow tax-advantaged accounts to continue growing.

### 5. Review Regularly:
   - Rebalance your portfolio in each account periodically based on performance and changing financial goals.

### Example Distribution:
- **401(k)**: 15-20% (to ensure max match)
- **IRA/Roth IRA**: 10-15% (based on income level and goals)
- **Taxable Account**: Remaining funds (for flexibility and short-term needs)

### Conclusion:
Tailor your distribution based on individual circumstances, and consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized advice.
