Distributing your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some guidelines:

### 1. **Tax-Advantaged Accounts (401(k) and IRA)**
   - **Maximize Contributions**: Aim to contribute enough to your 401(k) to get any employer match. Then, contribute to your IRA (Roth or Traditional, depending on your tax situation).
   - **Prioritize Tax Brackets**: If you're in a higher tax bracket, favor pre-tax accounts (Traditional 401(k), Traditional IRA) to minimize current taxable income. If you're in a lower bracket, consider Roth options.

### 2. **Taxable Accounts**
   - **Invest for Flexibility**: Use taxable accounts for investments you may need access to before retirement. These can include stocks and ETFs for growth.
   - **Consider Capital Gains**: Focus on tax-efficient investments (long-term capital gains, qualified dividends) to manage your tax burden.

### 3. **Diversification**
   - **Broad Diversification**: Ensure your investments are diversified across different asset classes (stocks, bonds, real estate) to mitigate risk.

### 4. **Risk Tolerance and Time Horizon**
   - **Adjust Allocation**: Based on your age and risk tolerance, adjust the mix of equities and bonds. Younger investors typically can afford a higher equity allocation, while those closer to retirement may favor bonds.

### 5. **Rebalancing**
   - **Regular Reviews**: Periodically review and rebalance your portfolios to maintain your desired asset allocation as values change.

### Conclusion
Work with a financial advisor for personalized advice, especially regarding tax implications and retirement planning strategies.
