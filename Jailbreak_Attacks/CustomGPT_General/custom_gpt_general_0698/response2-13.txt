When distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider these key strategies:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get any employer matching contributions, as this is essentially free money.

2. **Use Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**:
   - **401(k)**: If you have a high salary or can contribute more for retirement, prioritize this.
   - **IRA**: Consider a Roth IRA for tax-free growth, especially if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement.

3. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**:
   - After maximizing contributions to tax-advantaged accounts, use taxable accounts for additional savings. They offer flexibility for withdrawals without penalties.

4. **Consider Tax Implications**: 
   - Focus on tax-efficient investments in taxable accounts, such as index funds or ETFs, which generally produce fewer taxable events.

5. **Balance Your Asset Allocation**: Ensure a diversified mix of investments (stocks, bonds) across all accounts based on your risk tolerance and investment timeline.

6. **Rebalance Regularly**: Review and adjust your portfolio allocation periodically to maintain your desired asset mix.

7. **Emergency Fund**: Maintain an emergency fund in a high-yield savings account to cover unexpected expenses without liquidating investments.

By combining these strategies, you can create a balanced approach that maximizes growth while managing tax implications.
