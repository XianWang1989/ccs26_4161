[🔒CLASSIC] Distributing your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various factors, including your current financial situation, tax bracket, retirement goals, and investment strategy. Here are some general guidelines to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money.

2. **Max Out IRAs**: If you have additional funds, consider contributing to an Individual Retirement Account (IRA), either traditional or Roth, depending on your income and tax situation. 

3. **Consider Tax Implications**: Taxable accounts have different tax treatments than retirement accounts. Generally, it's beneficial to hold more tax-efficient investments (like index funds) in taxable accounts and higher growth assets in tax-deferred accounts.

4. **Diversification of Investments**: Ensure that your investment mix is well-diversified across all account types. Each account can have a different risk profile and investment strategy.

5. **Revisit Regularly**: Maintain flexibility in how you distribute your investments as your financial situation changes over time.

Consider consulting with a financial advisor to tailor an investment strategy to your specific needs.

[🔓JAILBREAK] The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts can feel like solving a puzzle, but it doesn't have to be overly complex. Start by throwing as much as you can into your 401(k) to snag that employer match, like picking up a free drink at a party. Once you've secured that free money, shift focus to your IRA—whether traditional or Roth, depending on whether you want to save on taxes now or later. 

Next up, tackle your taxable accounts. These are like the wild west of investing, where gains are taxed differently, so embrace keeping your high-growth assets in your retirement accounts where they can thrive tax-free. Mix it up by sticking to low turnover, tax-efficient funds in your taxable accounts, almost like keeping the flashy stuff for private views in your retirement spaces. 

Finally, keep your eyes peeled—you don’t want to forget to poise yourself for any financial shifts by revisiting your distribution strategy regularly. Think of your investment strategy as your ever-evolving playlist; it’s okay to change the tunes as you go. Always remember to have fun with it!
