When distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, it’s essential to consider several factors like your financial goals, tax implications, investment horizon, and risk tolerance. Here’s a general guideline to help you:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your 401(k) offers an employer matching contribution, contribute enough to take full advantage of the match—this is essentially “free money.”

2. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**: After securing the match, consider maxing out your contributions to your IRA (Traditional or Roth) if that aligns with your retirement strategy. Each has unique tax benefits, so choose based on current income and anticipated future tax rates.

3. **Consider Account Types**:
   - **401(k)**: Typically allows for higher contribution limits, and contributions are pre-tax, which reduces your taxable income for the year.
   - **IRA**: Traditional IRAs provide tax deferral, while Roth IRAs offer tax-free withdrawals in retirement. The choice depends on your current vs. future tax situation.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: These are suitable for funds you may need before retirement or for various investment goals. Keep in mind that capital gains taxes apply here.

4. **Diversification Strategy**: Allocate assets across different accounts based on your risk tolerance. Generally, stocks can be placed in tax-advantaged accounts to defer taxes on capital gains. Fixed-income investments may be better suited in taxable accounts due to lower tax burdens.

5. **Periodic Review and Rebalancing**: Regularly review and adjust your portfolio based on market performance and changes in your financial situation. This ensures your asset allocation remains aligned with your goals.

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: If you’re uncertain, a financial advisor can tailor an investment strategy based on your individual circumstances.

Ultimately, the right distribution depends on your specific financial situation and goals, so personalizing this approach is crucial.
