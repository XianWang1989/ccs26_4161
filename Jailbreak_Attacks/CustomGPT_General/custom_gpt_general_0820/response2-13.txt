To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider these strategies:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**:
   - If your employer offers a match for your 401(k) contributions, prioritize contributing enough to get the full match. This is essentially "free money."

2. **Consider Tax Implications**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions lower your taxable income now, but withdrawals in retirement are taxed.
   - **IRA**: Traditional IRAs have similar tax benefits to a 401(k); Roth IRAs allow for tax-free withdrawals in retirement but contributions are not tax-deductible.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Gains are subject to capital gains tax, so use them for more liquid investments or funds you might want to access earlier.

3. **Diversify Investments**:
   - Spread your investments across different asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) tailored to your risk tolerance and retirement timeline. 

4. **Emergency Fund**:
   - Ensure you have an emergency fund outside these accounts to cover unexpected expenses without needing to withdraw from your retirement accounts early.

5. **Contribution Limits**:
   - Be aware of the annual contribution limits for each account type and try to maximize contributions where it makes the most sense tax-wise.

6. **Long-Term Growth Focus**:
   - Aim to place higher-risk, higher-reward investments in your tax-advantaged accounts (like stocks) since they won't be taxed until withdrawal, while placing more stable investments (like bonds) in a taxable account.

7. **Review and Rebalance**:
   - Regularly review your investments and asset allocations in each account and rebalance as needed to maintain your desired risk level.

Each individual's financial situation is unique, so it may also be beneficial to consult with a financial advisor to tailor these strategies to your specific needs. Happy investing!
