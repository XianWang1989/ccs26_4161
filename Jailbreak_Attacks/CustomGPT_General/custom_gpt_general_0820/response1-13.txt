It's great that you're thinking about how to allocate your investments effectively! While I can’t provide personalized financial advice, I can share some general strategies that might help you decide how to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts.

1. **401(k) Accounts:**
   - *Maximize Employer Match:* If your employer offers a match on contributions, make sure to contribute enough to receive the full match, as this is essentially free money.
   - *Tax Benefits:* Contributions are typically made pre-tax, reducing your taxable income for the year, and can grow tax-deferred.

2. **IRA Accounts (Traditional or Roth):**
   - *Traditional IRA:* Contributions can be tax-deductible, and investments grow tax-deferred until withdrawal. It may be beneficial if you anticipate being in a lower tax bracket in retirement.
   - *Roth IRA:* Contributions are made after-tax, but withdrawals during retirement are tax-free. This can be an advantage if you expect your tax rate to increase in the future.

3. **Taxable Accounts:**
   - These accounts do not offer the same tax benefits as retirement accounts but provide flexibility for withdrawals and investment choices.
   - They can be particularly useful for shorter-term goals or for tax-efficient investing strategies (such as capital gains management).

**General Allocation Strategy:**
- Consider your financial goals, time horizon, and risk tolerance.
- A common strategy is to prioritize contributions to a 401(k) up to the match, then fund a Roth or Traditional IRA, and finally allocate any additional funds to a taxable account.
- Regularly rebalance your portfolio to maintain your desired asset allocation.

It's always wise to consult with a financial advisor who can provide advice tailored to your individual financial situation. Happy investing!
