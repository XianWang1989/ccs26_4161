[🔒CLASSIC] The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, time horizon, tax situation, and risk tolerance. Generally, a good strategy is to prioritize tax-advantaged accounts first:

1. **401(k) Contributions:** If your employer offers a 401(k) match, it's often a good idea to contribute enough to get the full match, as it's essentially free money. Maximize your contributions here if possible, since 401(k) contributions are tax-deferred.

2. **IRA Contributions:** After maxing out any employer match in your 401(k), consider contributing to an IRA (either traditional or Roth, depending on your tax situation). Roth IRAs can be particularly beneficial if you anticipate being in a higher tax bracket in retirement.

3. **Taxable Accounts:** Once you’ve maxed out contributions to tax-advantaged accounts, you can invest in taxable accounts. This is useful for short- to medium-term goals or in cases where you may need access to your funds before retirement without penalties.

It's also important to consider asset allocation and regularly rebalance your investments based on changing financial goals and market conditions. Consulting with a financial advisor can help you develop a personalized plan.
