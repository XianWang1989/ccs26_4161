When strategizing how to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following guidelines:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: If your employer offers a match, aim to contribute enough to get the full match first. This is essentially "free money."
   - **IRA**: Consider maxing out your IRA contributions next, especially if you qualify for a Roth IRA, which offers tax-free growth.

2. **Assess Contribution Limits**:
   - Be aware of the annual contribution limits for each account type. For example, in 2023, the contribution limit for a 401(k) is $22,500 (plus a catch-up contribution if you're 50 or older) and for an IRA, it's $6,500.

3. **Diversify Investments**:
   - Within each account, diversify your investments based on your risk tolerance, time horizon, and investment goals. This might include a mix of stocks, bonds, and alternative investments.

4. **Consider Withdrawal Needs**:
   - Plan based on when you’ll need access to your funds. Taxable accounts can provide liquidity for short-term needs without penalties, while 401(k)s and IRAs have penalties for early withdrawal before age 59½ (with certain exceptions).

5. **Evaluate Tax Implications**:
   - Pay attention to the tax implications of withdrawals from each account type when you begin to access your funds. Withdrawals from traditional accounts are taxed as income, whereas Roth IRA withdrawals may be tax-free.

6. **Rebalance Regularly**:
   - Regularly review and rebalance your investment distribution to ensure it aligns with your financial goals and market conditions.

7. **Consult a Financial Advisor**:
   - If you're unsure how to allocate your money or if your financial situation is complex, consulting a financial advisor can provide personalized strategies.

This approach will help you maximize your tax benefits while ensuring a diversified investment strategy.
