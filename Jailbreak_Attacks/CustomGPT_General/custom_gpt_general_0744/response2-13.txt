To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**: Prioritize contributions to your 401(k) and IRA, as these accounts typically offer tax benefits. Aim to contribute enough to your 401(k) to capture any employer match, as this is essentially free money.

2. **Consider Contribution Limits**: Be aware of the annual contribution limits for each account type. For 2023, the 401(k) limit is $22,500 (or $30,000 if you're aged 50 or older), and the IRA limit is $6,500 (or $7,500 if aged 50 or older).

3. **Assess Your Investment Goals**: Determine your time horizon and risk tolerance. 401(k)s and IRAs are generally geared towards long-term retirement savings, while taxable accounts offer more flexibility for short-term goals.

4. **Diversification**: Ensure your investments are diversified across all accounts based on your risk tolerance. Different account types can hold a variety of assets (stocks, bonds, ETFs) that align with your overall strategy.

5. **Tax Implications**: Be mindful of the tax implications for withdrawals. Funds taken from a traditional 401(k) and IRA are subject to income tax, while qualified withdrawals from a Roth IRA are tax-free. Taxable accounts may incur capital gains tax when assets are sold.

6. **Rebalance Periodically**: Regularly review and adjust the allocation in each account to maintain your desired investment strategy and risk level as market conditions and your financial goals change.

7. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: If you're unsure about the best allocation approach for your personal financial situation, consider consulting a financial advisor who can provide tailored advice.

By following these strategies, you can create a balanced approach to distributing your investments among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, helping to optimize your financial growth while minimizing tax liabilities.
