The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment time horizon. Here are a few general strategies you may consider:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**: If your employer offers a matching contribution for your 401(k), prioritize contributing enough to get the full match. After that, consider maxing out your IRA contributions (either traditional or Roth, based on your tax situation).

2. **Tax Efficiency**: Hold tax-inefficient investments (like bonds or actively managed funds) in tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) and IRA) to reduce taxable income. While placing tax-efficient investments (like index funds) in taxable accounts can help optimize your tax liability.

3. **Consider Your Tax Bracket**: Understand how contributions to traditional 401(k)s or IRAs vs. Roth accounts will affect your taxable income. If you're in a lower tax bracket now, a Roth option may be beneficial. 

4. **Liquidity Needs**: Ensure you have cash available for short-term needs without having to tap into retirement accounts, which can incur penalties if withdrawn early.

5. **Rebalance Regularly**: Make adjustments based on performance and changes to your financial situation or retirement goals.

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: If you’re uncertain, working with a financial advisor can provide personalized guidance based on your unique circumstances.

It’s important to build a diversified portfolio across all account types, balancing your overall risk while keeping tax implications in mind.
