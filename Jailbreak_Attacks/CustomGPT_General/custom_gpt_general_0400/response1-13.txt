Distributing your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, risk tolerance, time horizon, and tax situation. Here’s a general strategy to consider:

### 1. **401(k) Accounts**
   - **Employer Match**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match, if available. This is essentially free money.
   - **Tax Benefits**: Contributions are pre-tax (traditional) or post-tax (Roth), which can help reduce your current taxable income.
   - **Investment Options**: Evaluate the investment options available within your 401(k) and choose a mix that aligns with your risk tolerance.

### 2. **IRA Accounts**
   - **Roth vs. Traditional**: Consider opening a Roth IRA or traditional IRA. Roth contributions are after-tax, allowing for tax-free withdrawals in retirement, while traditional IRAs provide tax deductions now.
   - **Contribution Limits**: Be aware of contribution limits and phases based on your income. Max out contributions if possible.
   - **Diversification**: Use IRAs to diversify investments, such as stocks, bonds, or index funds, based on your retirement goals.

### 3. **Taxable Accounts**
   - **Long-Term Investments**: Use taxable accounts for investments that you plan to hold long-term, such as index funds or stocks, to benefit from lower capital gains taxes.
   - **Flexibility**: Taxable accounts offer flexibility for withdrawals without penalties, unlike retirement accounts.
   - **Tax Planning**: Consider tax-efficient investment strategies and budgeting for taxes on dividends and interest earned.

### 4. **Overall Allocation Strategy**
   - **Emergency Fund**: Ensure you have an emergency fund in a savings account before heavily investing.
   - **Risk Tolerance**: Adjust your asset allocation across accounts according to your risk tolerance. Higher risk might be appropriate for long-term accounts, while more stable investments could be in taxable accounts.
   - **Rebalancing**: Regularly review and rebalance your portfolios to maintain your desired risk/return profile.

### 5. **Consult a Financial Advisor**
   - If you're unsure about your specific situation, consider consulting with a financial advisor to tailor a plan that resonates with your goals.

By considering these factors, you can create a balanced and effective distribution of your money across your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts.
