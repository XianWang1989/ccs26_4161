Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here’s a general strategy to consider:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**
   - **401(k):** If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to get the full match. This is essentially free money.
   - **IRA:** After maxing out your 401(k) match, consider contributing to a traditional or Roth IRA. A traditional IRA may provide tax deductions, while a Roth IRA offers tax-free withdrawals in retirement.

### 2. **Consider Contribution Limits**
   - For 2024, the 401(k) contribution limit is $23,000 ($30,000 if you're 50 or older).
   - The IRA contribution limit is $6,500 ($7,500 if you're 50 or older).
   - Make sure you stay within these limits when planning your contributions.

### 3. **Tax Efficiency**
   - **401(k) and IRA:** These accounts grow tax-deferred, so focus on investment growth here.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** These are best for investments that generate less taxable income (like long-term capital gains or qualified dividends). 

### 4. **Balance Growth and Risk**
   - Align your asset allocation with your risk tolerance and time horizon. Consider age-based strategies, like being more aggressive in your younger years and shifting to more conservative investments as you near retirement.

### 5. **Emergency Fund**
   - Ensure you have a sufficient emergency fund outside of these accounts. Ideally, this should cover 3-6 months of living expenses.

### 6. **Regular Review**
   - Review and adjust your allocations annually based on changes in your financial situation, investment performance, and tax laws.

### Example Allocation Strategy
- **401(k):** 10% of your salary (up to employer match)
- **Traditional or Roth IRA:** Maximum contributions based on limits
- **Taxable Account:** Any additional savings after maxing tax-advantaged accounts

### 7. **Consult a Financial Advisor**
   - Given the complexity of personal finance and tax laws, consider seeking advice from a certified financial planner to tailor a strategy specific to your situation.

This approach maximizes tax efficiency while helping you reach your financial goals effectively.
