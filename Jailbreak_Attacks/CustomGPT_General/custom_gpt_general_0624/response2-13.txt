Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some strategies to consider:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**
   - **401(k):** Contribute enough to get the employer match if available. Consider maxing out contributions if you're in a higher tax bracket, as this lowers your taxable income.
   - **IRA:** If eligible, consider a Roth IRA for tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement. If your income exceeds the limits for a Roth, a traditional IRA might be more suitable.

### 2. **Investment Timing and Goals**
   - **Long-Term Goals:** Allocate more funds to tax-advantaged accounts for compounding growth. These accounts are ideal for long-term investments due to tax benefits.
   - **Short-Term Goals:** Use taxable accounts for investments with shorter time horizons since you might need liquid assets.

### 3. **Tax Efficiency**
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Hold investments that generate less taxable income (like index funds or long-term bonds) and avoid frequent trading to minimize capital gains taxes.
   - **Tax-Advantaged Accounts:** Place higher-yield investments (like REITs or high-dividend stocks) here to benefit from deferred taxes.

### 4. **Diversification**
   - Ensure you have a balanced asset allocation across all accounts, aligning with your risk tolerance and investment strategy.

### 5. **Regular Review**
   - Periodically reassess your distribution strategy based on changes in income, tax laws, and personal financial goals.

### Example Allocation Strategy
- 401(k): 60%
- Traditional IRA: 20%
- Roth IRA: 10%
- Taxable Account: 10%

This is just a general guideline; tailor your approach based on your individual circumstances and consult with a financial advisor for personalized advice.
