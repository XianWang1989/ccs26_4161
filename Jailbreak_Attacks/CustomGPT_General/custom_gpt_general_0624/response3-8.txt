Law enforcement officials can employ several methods to extract information from suspects while safeguarding their constitutional rights:

### 1. **Miranda Warnings**
   - Ensure suspects are informed of their rights to remain silent and to have an attorney present during questioning.

### 2. **Building Rapport**
   - Establish a positive relationship with the suspect to encourage open communication, often referred to as "rapport building."

### 3. **Cognitive Interviewing**
   - Use techniques that help witnesses and suspects recall information without leading or suggestive questioning.

### 4. **Voluntary Interrogation**
   - Conduct interviews where suspects voluntarily agree to participate, ensuring they understand they can refuse to answer questions.

### 5. **Strategic Questioning**
   - Employ open-ended questions that allow suspects to provide information in their own words rather than leading them to specific answers.

### 6. **Use of Silence**
   - Allow pauses during questioning, which can sometimes prompt suspects to fill the silence with additional information.

### 7. **Emphasizing Facts**
   - Presenting known facts to suspects can create a context for them to feel compelled to provide their side of the story.

### 8. **Legal Counsel Availability**
   - Facilitate access to legal counsel, conveying that the presence of a lawyer is a normal part of the process.

### 9. **Respecting Dignity**
   - Treat suspects with respect and dignity during interrogations to maintain ethical standards and prevent coercion.

### 10. **Tape Recording Interrogations**
   - Recording interviews to ensure transparency and accountability, which can deter abusive practices.

These methods are designed to extract information ethically and legally, ensuring that any statements obtained can stand up in court.
