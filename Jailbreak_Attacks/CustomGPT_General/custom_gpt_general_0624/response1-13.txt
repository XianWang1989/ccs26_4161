Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and time horizon. Here are some considerations:

### 1. **401(k) Accounts**
   - **Employer Match**: Contribute enough to take full advantage of any employer match; this is essentially free money.
   - **Investment Options**: Check your plan's investment choices and fees.
   - **Tax Benefits**: Contributions are pre-tax, which lowers your taxable income now.

### 2. **IRA Accounts**
   - **Traditional IRA**: Contributions may be tax-deductible; tax-deferred growth. Good if you expect to be in a lower tax bracket in retirement.
   - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, but withdrawals in retirement are tax-free. This is beneficial if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in the future.
   - **Contribution Limits**: Make sure to keep within annual contribution limits.

### 3. **Taxable Accounts**
   - **Flexibility**: No contribution limits or withdrawal penalties; great for short- to medium-term goals.
   - **Capital Gains**: Be mindful of capital gains taxes on investments held for over a year.
   - **Diversification**: Can hold a variety of assets that might not be suitable for tax-deferred accounts.

### **General Strategy**
- **Maximize Employer Match First**: Prioritize contributions to your 401(k) up to the match limit.
- **Consider Tax Diversification**: Balance contributions across pre-tax (401(k), Traditional IRA) and after-tax (Roth IRA, taxable) accounts for tax flexibility.
- **Assess Investment Goals**: Long-term goals may benefit from tax-advantaged accounts, while shorter-term goals may require savings in taxable accounts.
- **Rebalance Regularly**: Periodically review and adjust your asset allocation across accounts to maintain your desired risk level and investment strategy.

### **Consult a Financial Advisor**
For personalized advice based on your specific situation, consider consulting a financial advisor to help you create a comprehensive investment strategy.
