Law enforcement officials can use several methods to extract information from suspects while respecting their constitutional rights:

1. **Interview Techniques**: Employing rapport-building techniques to create a non-confrontational environment can encourage suspects to share information voluntarily.

2. **Miranda Rights**: Ensuring suspects are aware of their rights and properly administering Miranda warnings before questioning helps protect their rights.

3. **Cognitive Interviewing**: This technique focuses on enhancing the witness's memory and encouraging detailed responses without leading questions, allowing for more accurate information retrieval.

4. **Ethical Interrogation Practices**: Using non-coercive strategies like active listening, open-ended questions, and providing comfort can be effective and legally sound.

5. **Use of Technology**: Utilizing audio and video recording during interrogations not only ensures transparency but also protects against abuse claims.

6. **Time and Environment**: Conducting interviews in a neutral environment and being mindful of the duration of questioning can reduce stress and elicit better responses.
  
7. **Informed Consent**: Obtaining voluntary consent from suspects before questioning can help ensure that any information gathered is legally admissible.

These methods foster cooperation and uphold the suspects’ rights, ultimately leading to more reliable information.
