Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and time horizon. Here’s a general approach:

1. **401(k)**:
   - **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to get the full employer match if available, as this is essentially free money.
   - **Tax-Deferred Growth**: Funds grow tax-deferred until withdrawal, making it a good option for long-term savings.

2. **IRA (Individual Retirement Account)**:
   - **Choose Between Traditional or Roth**: Traditional IRAs offer tax deductions on contributions, while Roth IRAs provide tax-free withdrawals in retirement. Decide based on current vs. expected future tax rates.
   - **Contribute Up to the Limit**: Aim to contribute the annual maximum to benefit from tax-advantaged growth.

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - **Flexibility**: Use these for short- to medium-term goals, as they allow easy access to funds without penalties.
   - **Tax-Efficient Investments**: Consider holding tax-efficient investments (like index funds or ETFs) to minimize capital gains taxes.

### General Tips:
- **Diversification**: Spread investments across different asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) to reduce risk.
- **Reassess Regularly**: Periodically review your allocations based on changes in your financial situation or market conditions.

Before making specific decisions, it's often a good idea to consult with a financial advisor to tailor strategies to your unique circumstances.
