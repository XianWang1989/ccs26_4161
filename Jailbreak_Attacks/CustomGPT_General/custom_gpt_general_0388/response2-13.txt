Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here’s a general approach:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match if available. This is essentially free money.

2. **IRAs**: Consider contributing to a Roth IRA (if eligible) for tax-free growth, especially if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket during retirement. If you prefer immediate tax benefits, a traditional IRA may be better.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for funds you might need before retirement. Invest in tax-efficient funds, like index funds or ETFs, to minimize tax liability.

4. **Diversification**: Ensure a balanced mix of stocks, bonds, and other assets across all accounts, tailored to your risk tolerance.

5. **Tax Considerations**: Withdraw from tax-advantaged accounts last, allowing them to grow longer. Plan for potential taxes on withdrawals from traditional IRAs and 401(k)s.

6. **Regular Review**: Reassess your distribution strategy periodically to align with changes in income, expenses, and tax laws.

Consulting with a financial advisor can also provide personalized strategies based on your situation!
