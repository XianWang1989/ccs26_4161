Determining the best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, current income, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here’s a general approach to consider:

### 1. **Understand Account Types**
   - **401(k):** Often offers employer matching, tax-deferred growth. Contribution limits are higher.
   - **IRA:** Can be traditional (tax-deductible contributions, taxes on withdrawal) or Roth (taxed contributions, tax-free withdrawals).
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Flexibility with withdrawals and investments, but subject to capital gains taxes.

### 2. **Prioritize Contributions**
   - **Maximize Employer Match:** Always contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match; it's essentially free money.
   - **Consider IRAs:** If eligible, contribute to an IRA for additional tax-advantaged growth, depending on whether you prefer tax-free growth (Roth) or tax deduction now (traditional).
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Use these for savings goals (like buying a home) or after-tax investment strategies.

### 3. **Balance Investments**
   - Invest in a diversified portfolio across accounts. Consider putting higher growth assets (like stocks) in tax-advantaged accounts to maximize tax benefits, while using taxable accounts for more stable investments.

### 4. **Monitor and Adjust**
   - Regularly reassess your investment strategy based on changes in income, tax laws, and personal goals.

### Example Strategy:
1. Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full match.
2. Max out your IRA contributions.
3. Allocate any remaining funds to your taxable account based on your immediate financial goals.

### Final Tip:
Consult with a financial advisor to tailor a strategy specific to your situation and to ensure you're making the most of tax advantages.
