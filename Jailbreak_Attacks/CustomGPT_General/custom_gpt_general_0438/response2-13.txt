When distributing your money amongst a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider these strategies:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match, as it’s essentially free money.

2. **Contribute to an IRA**: If you qualify, consider maximizing contributions to a traditional or Roth IRA (based on your income level) for tax advantages.

3. **Tax Efficiency**:
   - Place high-growth investments (like stocks) in tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) and IRA) to defer taxes on capital gains.
   - Use taxable accounts for investments that generate less tax, like index funds or tax-exempt bonds.

4. **Diversification**: Ensure a mix of asset types across accounts to balance risk and return.

5. **Withdrawal Strategy**: Plan how you will withdraw funds in retirement, considering the tax implications of each account type.

6. **Monitor and Adjust**: Regularly review your allocations based on market performance and your life changes (like age or income).

It’s often wise to consult a financial advisor for personalized guidance based on your specific situation.
