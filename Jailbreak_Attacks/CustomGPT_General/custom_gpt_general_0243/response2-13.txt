Determining the best way to allocate your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and time horizon. Here’s a structured approach to help you distribute your funds effectively:

### 1. **Understand Account Types:**
   - **401(k):** Typically offers tax benefits, and importantly, employer matching contributions. Contributions are made pre-tax, reducing your taxable income.
   - **IRA (Individual Retirement Account):** Traditional IRAs offer tax-deferred growth, while Roth IRAs provide tax-free withdrawals in retirement. Contribution limits and eligibility vary.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Flexibility in withdrawals, but subject to capital gains tax. Not ideal for long-term, tax-advantaged savings but useful for liquidity.

### 2. **Assessment of Goals:**
   - **Short-term Goals (1-5 years):** Consider maintaining a larger portion in taxable accounts for liquidity.
   - **Medium to Long-term Goals (5+ years):** Prioritize tax-advantaged accounts like 401(k) and IRA for their growth potential.

### 3. **Contribution Strategy:**
   - **Maximize Employer Match:** Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match; it’s essentially free money.
   - **Fund Your IRA:** If eligible, consider contributing to your IRA next, especially if you anticipate being in a higher tax bracket in retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** After maximizing tax-advantaged accounts, consider using taxable accounts for additional investments.

### 4. **Investment Allocation:**
   - Assess your risk tolerance and investment horizon. More aggressive growth assets (like stocks) might be suitable for accounts intended for retirement, while more conservative investments can be suitable for taxable accounts.

### 5. **Tax Considerations:**
   - Factor in your current tax rate and project your future tax rate in retirement. Use tax-efficient investments (like municipal bonds or index funds) in taxable accounts, while holding higher growth potential assets in tax-advantaged accounts.

### 6. **Review and Adjust:**
   - Regularly review your asset allocation and account contributions to ensure they align with evolving financial goals and market conditions.

### Conclusion:
Aim for a balanced approach, favoring tax-advantaged accounts initially while maintaining some flexibility with taxable accounts for short-term needs. It’s wise to consult with a financial advisor to personalize your strategy further based on your unique situation.
