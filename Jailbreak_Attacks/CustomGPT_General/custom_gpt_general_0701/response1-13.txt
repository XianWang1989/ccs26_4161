The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment strategy. Here's a general approach:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**: Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match, if available. This is essentially "free money" and provides an immediate return on your investment.

2. **Contribute to an IRA**: Next, consider contributing to a Traditional IRA or Roth IRA. The choice between the two depends on your current and expected future tax rates:
   - **Traditional IRA**: Contributions are tax-deductible; you'll pay taxes on withdrawals in retirement.
   - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, but withdrawals in retirement are tax-free.

3. **Max Out 401(k) Contributions**: If you have the ability, try to max out your 401(k) contributions after you’ve taken care of the employer match. The contribution limits for 401(k)s are higher than for IRAs, providing more tax-deferred growth.

4. **Invest in Taxable Accounts**: Once you’re maximizing your tax-advantaged accounts, you can invest in taxable investment accounts. These offer flexibility, as there are no contribution limits or penalties for withdrawals. Be mindful of capital gains taxes when investing in these accounts.

5. **Consider Tax Implications**: Be conscious of the tax implications for each investment type. Long-term capital gains in taxable accounts are taxed at a lower rate than ordinary income, so consider investing in tax-efficient funds or ETFs.

6. **Reassess Regularly**: Periodically review and adjust your allocations based on changes in income, tax laws, or financial goals.

7. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: If you're uncertain, consider speaking with a financial advisor who can provide personalized advice tailored to your specific situation.

This framework can help optimize your investments for growth while considering tax efficiency.
