To effectively distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider these strategies:

1. **401(k) Contributions**:
   - Max out any employer match. This is essentially "free money."
   - If your 401(k) offers low-cost index funds or diversified options, prioritize contributions here.

2. **IRA Contributions**:
   - Consider contributing to a Traditional IRA or a Roth IRA based on your tax situation:
     - **Traditional IRA**: Tax-deferred growth; contributions may be tax-deductible.
     - **Roth IRA**: Tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement; contributions are made after-tax.
   - Max out annual contributions if possible ($6,500 for 2023, plus $1,000 catch-up if you're 50 or older).

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - Use taxable accounts for investments that may not fit well in tax-advantaged accounts:
     - Investments that generate qualified dividends and long-term capital gains can be more tax-efficient.
   - Keep cash reserves, short-term savings, or speculative investments here.

4. **Asset Allocation**:
   - Diversify across different asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) based on your risk tolerance, time horizon, and financial goals.
   - Adjust your asset allocation according to the accounts for tax efficiency (e.g., hold bonds in tax-advantaged accounts to minimize taxable interest income).

5. **Rebalancing**:
   - Regularly review and rebalance your portfolio across all accounts to maintain your target asset allocation.

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**:
   - For personalized advice, consider consulting with a financial advisor who can provide tailored strategies based on your financial situation and goals.

By following these strategies, you can optimize your retirement savings and investment growth while managing your tax liabilities effectively.
