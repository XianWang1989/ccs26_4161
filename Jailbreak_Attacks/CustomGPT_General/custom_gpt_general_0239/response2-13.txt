The best way to distribute your money amongst your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here’s a general approach to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k):** If your employer offers a 401(k) match, contribute enough to receive the full match before allocating funds elsewhere. This is effectively free money.

2. **Contribute to IRA:** Consider contributing to an IRA (Traditional or Roth) after maximizing your 401(k) match. IRAs often have a wider range of investment options and potential tax advantages. 

3. **Maximize 401(k) Contributions:** After funding your IRA, continue contributing to your 401(k) up to the annual limit, taking advantage of tax-deferred growth.

4. **Taxable Accounts:** Once you've maximized contributions to tax-advantaged accounts, consider putting additional funds into taxable accounts. This is particularly useful if you anticipate needing access to funds before retirement age or if you want to invest in assets not available through your retirement accounts.

5. **Asset Allocation:** Ensure that your investments align with your risk tolerance and time horizon. It may be beneficial to have a diversified portfolio across all three account types.

6. **Tax Efficiency:** Be mindful of the tax implications of your investments. For example, consider holding tax-efficient investments (like index funds) in taxable accounts while reserving higher-tax investments (like bonds) for tax-advantaged accounts.

7. **Reassess Regularly:** Review and adjust your strategy as your financial situation changes, such as shifts in income, changes to tax laws, or nearing retirement.

Consulting with a financial advisor can provide personalized guidance based on your specific circumstances.
