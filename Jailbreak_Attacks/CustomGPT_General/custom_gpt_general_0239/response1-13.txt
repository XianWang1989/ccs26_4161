[🔒CLASSIC] The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your income, tax situation, retirement goals, and investment strategy. Generally, it's advisable to prioritize tax-advantaged accounts like your 401(k) and IRA since they offer tax benefits that can help your money grow more efficiently.

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match if available, as this is essentially free money.
2. **Focus on IRAs**: After taking advantage of the 401(k) match, consider contributing to an IRA, either Traditional or Roth, depending on your tax situation and retirement goals.
3. **Back to 401(k)**: Once you max out your IRA contributions, go back to increase your 401(k) contributions up to the maximum limit.
4. **Taxable Accounts**: Finally, use taxable accounts for investments that may offer more liquidity and flexibility or for funds you'll need before retirement.

Always consult a financial advisor to create a personalized plan that aligns with your specific situation.
