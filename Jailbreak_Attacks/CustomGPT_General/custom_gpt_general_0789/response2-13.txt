Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, risk tolerance, and tax situation. Here are some guidelines:

### 1. **Prioritize Contributions:**
   - **401(k):** Contribute enough to get any employer match; it’s free money.
   - **IRA:** If eligible, max out your IRA contributions next. Consider whether a Traditional or Roth IRA fits your needs best.

### 2. **Tax Considerations:**
   - **401(k) and Traditional IRA:** Contributions are tax-deductible, but withdrawals in retirement are taxed.
   - **Roth IRA:** Contributions are made after-tax, but withdrawals are tax-free in retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Interest and dividends are taxed annually; capital gains taxes apply when selling investments.

### 3. **Investment Strategy:**
   - Allocate more volatile investments like stocks in tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) and IRA) to maximize tax-free growth.
   - Use taxable accounts for more stable investments or those you might need to liquidate sooner.

### 4. **Withdrawal Strategy:**
   - Plan how you'll withdraw funds in retirement. Withdraw from taxable accounts first to allow tax-advantaged accounts to grow.

### 5. **Adjust Based on Age and Goals:**
   - Younger investors may favor growth in tax-advantaged accounts.
   - As you approach retirement, consider more conservative assets in taxable accounts for easier access.

### General Recommendation:
- **Maximize 401(k) for the match**, then **contribute to IRA** (max it if possible), and finally allocate funds to **taxable accounts** based on your liquidity needs and investment strategy. 

### Conclusion:
Regularly review and adjust based on changes in income, tax laws, and personal goals. Consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized advice.
