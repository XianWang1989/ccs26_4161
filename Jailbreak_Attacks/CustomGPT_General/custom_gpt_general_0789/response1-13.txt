Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts involves several key factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment horizon. Here are some guidelines:

### 1. **Understand Account Types**
   - **401(k)**: Employer-sponsored; often has matching contributions. Pre-tax contributions reduce taxable income.
   - **IRA (Traditional/Roth)**: IRAs offer tax advantages; Traditional IRAs may provide tax deductions, while Roth IRAs grow tax-free.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Flexibility for withdrawals; capital gains and dividends are taxed.

### 2. **Consider Tax Implications**
   - Prioritize tax-deferred accounts (like 401(k) and Traditional IRA) for higher-income years to reduce current taxable income.
   - Use Roth IRAs for tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement, particularly if you anticipate being in a higher tax bracket.

### 3. **Maximize Contributions**
   - Aim to contribute enough to your 401(k) to get any employer match.
   - Max out IRA contributions if possible, especially into a Roth if you qualify.

### 4. **Diversify Based on Goals**
   - **Short-term goals**: Use taxable accounts for flexibility.
   - **Long-term growth**: Focus on tax-advantaged accounts.

### 5. **Rebalance Regularly**
   - Periodically assess your asset allocation across all accounts to ensure alignment with your risk tolerance and investment objectives.

### Example Allocation Strategy
- **401(k)**: 40%
- **IRA**: 30%
- **Taxable Accounts**: 30%

### Conclusion
Tailor the distribution based on your specific financial situation, and consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized advice.
