Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment time horizon. Here’s a general approach:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money.

2. **Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **Max out your IRA**: If you qualify, consider contributing to a Roth IRA for tax-free growth and withdrawals, or a traditional IRA for tax deductions.
   - **Continue with the 401(k)**: Once your IRA is maxed, focus on maxing your 401(k) contributions up to the annual limit.

3. **Consider Taxable Accounts**: After maximizing tax-advantaged accounts, allocate funds to a taxable account for flexibility and liquidity.

4. **Asset Allocation Strategy**: Keep in mind the types of investments:
   - Place high-growth or high-yield investments in tax-advantaged accounts to maximize tax benefits.
   - Use taxable accounts for lower-yield investments or tax-efficient funds (e.g., index funds).

5. **Rebalance Periodically**: Review your distribution and investment strategy regularly to ensure it aligns with your goals and market conditions.

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: Tailor your strategy to your specific financial situation for the best results.

Make sure to consider your risk tolerance and investment timeline as you make these decisions!
