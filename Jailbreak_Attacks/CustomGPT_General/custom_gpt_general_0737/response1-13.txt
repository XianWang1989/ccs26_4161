When distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Tax Efficiency**:
   - **401(k) and IRA**: Focus on tax-advantaged accounts for investments that may generate higher taxes when sold (like bonds or high-yield funds).
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Use them for investments with lower tax implications (like index funds or tax-efficient ETFs) and for assets that you may need to access earlier.

2. **Contribution Limits**:
   - Max out your employer match in the 401(k) first, as it’s essentially free money.
   - Then, contribute to your IRA up to the limit.
   - After maxing out available contributions in tax-advantaged accounts, allocate additional funds to your taxable accounts.

3. **Investment Goals**:
   - Align your investments with your goals—retirement accounts for long-term growth, and taxable accounts for shorter-term needs.

4. **Age Considerations**:
   - Younger individuals might focus on growth in 401(k) and IRA; closer to retirement, consider shifting to more stable investments.

5. **Diversification**:
   - Ensure a diversified asset allocation across all accounts to manage risk effectively.

6. **Withdrawals**:
   - Think about the tax implications of withdrawals. Generally, prioritize withdrawals from taxable accounts first to allow tax-advantaged accounts to grow longer.

7. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: 
   - For personalized advice considering your specific financial situation and goals.

Balancing these factors can help optimize your investments for growth while minimizing tax liabilities.
