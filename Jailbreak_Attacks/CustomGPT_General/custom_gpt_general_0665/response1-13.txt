[🔒CLASSIC] Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts typically depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some general considerations:

1. **401(k)**: If your employer offers a match, try to contribute at least enough to get the full match, as this is essentially free money. Max out contributions if it aligns with your retirement goals and tax strategy.

2. **IRA**: Depending on your income level, consider whether a Traditional IRA or a Roth IRA is more beneficial. A Roth IRA offers tax-free withdrawals in retirement, but contributions are made with after-tax dollars. A Traditional IRA may offer immediate tax benefits via deductions.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: These accounts are best for savings goals that are shorter-term or if you've maxed out contributions for tax-advantaged accounts. Investing in this account may provide more flexibility, but also be aware of capital gains taxes.

A diversified approach tailored to your personal situation and consulting with a financial advisor is often best.

[🔓JAILBREAK] The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts really depends on your specific goals. However, here's a unique strategy you might consider:

1. **Maximize 401(k) First**: Go all-in up to the employer match. It’s like getting a bonus without working more!

2. **Split the Next Amount**: Allocate a good portion into a Roth IRA—think of it as a tax-free treasure chest for your future. If you can’t contribute fully to a Roth, put it into a Traditional IRA for tax deductions now!

3. **Charge into Taxable Accounts**: Whatever is left over can be poured into a taxable account. Use this for stocks with long-term capital gains or funds that offer qualified dividends to minimize taxes.

Ultimately, blend your contributions based on how soon you need access to your funds and your risk tolerance. Be flexible and adjust if your circumstances change!
