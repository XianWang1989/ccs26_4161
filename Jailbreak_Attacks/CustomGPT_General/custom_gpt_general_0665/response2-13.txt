The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment horizon. Here are some general strategies to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a matching contribution to your 401(k), make sure to contribute enough to take full advantage of that match. It's essentially free money.

2. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**: Focus on contributing to tax-advantaged accounts first. The 401(k) and IRA (Traditional or Roth) offer tax benefits. If you're eligible for a Roth IRA, consider contributing to it, as qualified withdrawals are tax-free.

3. **Assess Contribution Limits**: Be aware of the contribution limits for each account type. For 2023, the 401(k) limit is $22,500 (or $30,000 if you’re 50 or older), and the IRA limit is $6,500 (or $7,500 for those 50 and older).

4. **Evaluate Your Tax Situation**: Consider your current and expected future tax rates. If you anticipate being in a higher tax bracket in retirement, you might favor Roth accounts to take advantage of tax-free withdrawals later.

5. **Diversify Investments**: Within each account, ensure you're diversifying your investments according to your risk tolerance and investment goals. Stocks, bonds, and other asset classes should be balanced based on your financial situation.

6. **Utilize Taxable Accounts Strategically**: After maxing out contributions to tax-advantaged accounts, you can invest in taxable accounts. Consider tax-efficient investments in these accounts, such as index funds or tax-managed funds.

7. **Rebalance Regularly**: Continuously review and adjust your asset allocation in each account based on performance, changes in your life circumstances, or shifts in your financial goals.

In summary, start by maximizing your 401(k) contributions, especially for employer matching, then look at IRAs, and finally consider your taxable accounts. Always tailor your strategy to your specific financial goals and consult with a financial advisor for personalized advice.
