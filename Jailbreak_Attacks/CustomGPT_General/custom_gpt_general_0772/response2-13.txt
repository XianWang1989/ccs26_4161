Distributing your money across a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here’s a general approach to consider:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match:**
   - Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to get any employer matching contributions. This is essentially free money.

### 2. **Tax-Advantaged Accounts:**
   - **401(k)**: Prioritize contributions up to the limit, especially if there’s a match. These contributions reduce your taxable income.
   - **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**: After maxing out your 401(k), consider contributing to an IRA. A Traditional IRA can lower your taxable income, while a Roth IRA offers tax-free growth.

### 3. **Taxable Accounts:**
   - Once you’ve maxed out tax-advantaged accounts, invest in taxable accounts. This is useful for short-term goals or if you expect to withdraw funds before retirement.

### 4. **Asset Allocation:**
   - Maintain a diversified portfolio across all accounts, adjusting based on risk tolerance and investment horizon. For instance, younger investors might choose a higher proportion of stocks.

### 5. **Rebalance Regularly:**
   - Monitor and rebalance your portfolio at least annually to maintain your desired asset allocation.

### 6. **Consider Taxes:**
   - Be mindful of tax implications when withdrawing funds. Taxable accounts may be more advantageous for shorter time horizons due to potentially lower capital gains taxes.

### 7. **Consult a Professional:**
   - A financial advisor can provide personalized guidance based on your specific situation.

By following this strategy, you can ensure that your investments are aligned with your financial goals while maximizing tax efficiency.
