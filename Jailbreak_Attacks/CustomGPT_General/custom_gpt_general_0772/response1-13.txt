Distributing your money across a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here’s a general approach:

1. **Employer Match**: Maximize contributions to your 401(k) up to the employer match. This is essentially free money.

2. **Tax-Advantaged Accounts**: Contribute to an IRA (Traditional or Roth) after maxing out your 401(k) match. A Traditional IRA offers tax-deferred growth, while a Roth IRA provides tax-free withdrawals in retirement.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for additional savings, particularly if you want more liquidity. They can be beneficial for shorter-term goals since you can access funds without penalties.

4. **Investment Strategy**:
   - **401(k)**: Focus on long-term growth investments (e.g., stocks or mutual funds).
   - **IRA**: Similar to the 401(k), but consider your tax strategy; Roth accounts may favor growth investments for tax-free withdrawals.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Use tax-efficient investments (e.g., ETFs or index funds) to minimize capital gains taxes.

5. **Emergency Fund**: Ensure you have an emergency fund separate from these accounts to cover potential unexpected expenses.

6. **Rebalancing**: Regularly review and adjust your allocations based on market performance and changing financial goals.

Consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your specific situation.
