Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts effectively depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment time horizon. Here are some key considerations for each type of account:

1. **401(k)**:
   - **Employer Match**: If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to get the full match first. This is free money.
   - **Tax Advantages**: Contributions are typically pre-tax, reducing your taxable income. Consider maxing out contributions if your budget allows.

2. **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**:
   - **Tax Treatment**: Traditional IRAs offer tax-deferred growth, while Roth IRAs provide tax-free withdrawals in retirement. Choose based on your current and anticipated future tax rates.
   - **Contribution Limits**: Be aware of annual contribution limits. Prioritize maxing out Roth IRAs for tax-free growth if you're eligible.

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - **Flexibility**: These accounts allow for more accessible withdrawals without penalties. They’re suitable for short to mid-term goals.
   - **Capital Gains Considerations**: Be mindful of capital gains taxes when selling investments in taxable accounts. Prioritize holding investments longer to benefit from lower long-term capital gains rates.

### Suggested Distribution Strategy:
1. **Prioritize Accounts**: Start with employer matching 401(k) contributions, then contribute to your IRA (focusing on a Roth if eligible). Lastly, invest in taxable accounts.
2. **Annual Review**: Periodically reassess your distribution strategy based on changes in your income, tax situation, and financial goals.
3. **Asset Allocation**: Within each account, maintain a diversified portfolio aligned with your risk tolerance and investment horizon.

### Conclusion:
Every situation is unique, so it may also be beneficial to consult a financial advisor to tailor a strategy specifically for your circumstances.
