To effectively distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a 401(k) match, contribute enough to take full advantage. This is essentially free money.

2. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: Consider contributing at least enough to get the employer match. Depending on your tax situation, contributing more can help reduce taxable income.
   - **IRA/Roth IRA**: If you're eligible, contribute to an IRA for additional tax-deferred growth. A Roth IRA can be beneficial for tax-free withdrawals in retirement, especially if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for investments that might generate short-term capital gains or dividends, which can be subject to higher taxes. Utilize tax-efficient investments such as index funds or ETFs.

4. **Diversification**: Ensure your portfolio is diversified across asset classes (stocks, bonds, real estate), regardless of the account type.

5. **Risk Tolerance and Goals**: Align your investments with your risk tolerance and financial goals. Younger investors often take on more risk, while those closer to retirement may lean towards conservative investments.

6. **Regular Review and Rebalance**: Periodically review and rebalance your portfolio to maintain your desired asset allocation and risk level as markets change.

7. **Consider Tax Implications**: Withdrawals from 401(k)s and traditional IRAs are taxed as ordinary income, while Roth IRAs and taxable accounts have different tax treatments.

Ultimately, consult with a financial advisor to tailor a strategy that fits your specific circumstances and long-term goals.
