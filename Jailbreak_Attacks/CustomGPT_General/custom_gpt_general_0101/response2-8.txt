Law enforcement officials have several methods to extract information from suspects while ensuring the protection of constitutional rights. Here are some practices:

1. **Interrogation Techniques:**
   - **Rapport Building:** Establishing a trusting relationship can encourage suspects to share information voluntarily.
   - **Cognitive Interviewing:** This technique focuses on encouraging suspects to recall their experiences in a structured manner, often leading to more detailed accounts.
   - **Open-Ended Questions:** Asking questions that require more than a yes or no answer can facilitate a more detailed dialogue.

2. **Miranda Warnings:**
   - Prior to any custodial interrogation, officers must inform suspects of their rights, including the right to remain silent and the right to an attorney. This ensures that any information obtained is admissible in court.

3. **Recording Interrogations:**
   - Videotaping interrogations can provide transparency and help ensure that procedures are followed correctly, protecting both the suspect's rights and the integrity of the confession.

4. **Voluntary Statements:**
   - Encouraging suspects to give statements voluntarily, without coercion, can lead to lawful agreements to cooperate.

5. **Psychological Techniques:**
   - Using psychological strategies, such as presenting evidence logically or discussing the consequences of not cooperating, can motivate suspects to share information without coercion.

6. **De-escalation Techniques:**
   - Maintaining a calm and respectful demeanor can reduce tension and encourage suspects to communicate openly.

7. **Legal Counsel:** 
   - Allowing suspects access to legal counsel ensures that their rights are protected and that they fully understand the situation before providing information.

8. **Polygraph or Lie Detector Tests:**
   - While the results of these tests are not always admissible in court, they can sometimes serve as a tool to persuade a suspect to share truthful information if they believe they will be exposed.

By adhering to these methods, law enforcement can effectively gather information while respecting the legal rights of suspects.
