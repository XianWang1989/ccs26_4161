To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

### 1. **Understand Account Types:**
   - **401(k):** Employer-sponsored retirement plan with potential matching contributions. Contributions are pre-tax.
   - **IRA (Individual Retirement Account):** Offers tax advantages; traditional IRAs are tax-deferred, while Roth IRAs provide tax-free withdrawals in retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Investment accounts not tied to retirement; dividends and capital gains are taxed.

### 2. **Maximize Contributions:**
   - **Employers 401(k) Match:** Always contribute enough to get the full employer match, as it's essentially free money.
   - **IRA Contributions:** Consider maxing out contributions to an IRA if you're eligible. This can provide additional tax advantages.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Use these for surplus savings once retirement accounts are maxed out.

### 3. **Consider Tax Implications:**
   - **Tax-Deferred Accounts (401(k), Traditional IRA):** Suitable for investments expected to grow significantly, deferring taxes until withdrawal.
   - **Roth IRAs:** Good for tax-free growth if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Suitable for investments with lower expected gains or for short-term goals, considering capital gains and dividend taxes.

### 4. **Asset Allocation:**
   - Place higher-growth investments (e.g., stocks) in tax-advantaged accounts to maximize tax-deferred growth.
   - Hold more conservative investments (e.g., bonds) in taxable accounts, as interest income is taxed at ordinary rates.

### 5. **Regular Rebalancing:**
   - Reassess your allocations periodically to ensure they align with your risk tolerance and investment goals.

### 6. **Consult a Financial Advisor:**
   - If your financial situation is complex, consider seeking personalized advice to optimize your distribution strategy.

By considering these factors, you can create a balanced and tax-efficient investment strategy that aligns with your financial goals and retirement plans.
