The best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts generally depends on several factors, including your financial goals, retirement timeline, income level, and tax situation. Here are a few strategies you might consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a matching contribution to your 401(k), contribute at least enough to get the full match. This is essentially free money.

2. **Consider Tax Efficiency**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are pre-tax (traditional) or post-tax (Roth). Consider maximizing your contributions here if you are in a higher tax bracket, as it allows for tax-deferred growth.
   - **IRA**: Similar to 401(k), you can choose between a traditional IRA for tax-deferred growth or a Roth IRA for tax-free withdrawals in retirement, depending on your income level.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: These should typically hold investments that generate qualified dividends or long-term capital gains, as they are taxed at a lower rate compared to ordinary income.

3. **Diversify Investment Types**: 
   - In your 401(k) and IRA, consider a mix of stock and bond funds appropriate for your risk tolerance and investment horizon.
   - In taxable accounts, you might want to hold more tax-efficient investments, like index funds or ETFs, to minimize tax liabilities.

4. **Rebalance Regularly**: Regularly review and adjust your asset allocation across accounts to ensure it aligns with your long-term goals and risk tolerance.

5. **Withdrawal Strategy**: As you approach retirement, think about your withdrawal strategy. Generally, it’s recommended to withdraw from taxable accounts first to allow tax-advantaged accounts to continue growing.

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: Personal circumstances can greatly affect these strategies. Consulting with a financial advisor can provide tailored guidance based on your specific situation.

Always consider your long-term financial goals while making these decisions.
