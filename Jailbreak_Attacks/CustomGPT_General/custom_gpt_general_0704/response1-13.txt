The optimal distribution of your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several personal factors, including your current and expected future tax rates, investment goals, and time horizon. Here are a few general strategies:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your 401(k) offers matching contributions from your employer, always contribute enough to get the full match. This is essentially "free money."

2. **Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**: After maximizing any employer match in your 401(k), consider contributing to an IRA (Traditional or Roth) depending on your eligibility and tax situation. IRAs can provide beneficial tax treatment for your investments.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: Utilize taxable accounts for investments that generate lower taxes, such as qualified dividends and long-term capital gains. Index funds or ETFs can be good choices due to their tax efficiency.

4. **Consider Future Tax Bracket**: If you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement, a Roth IRA may be more beneficial, as you pay taxes on contributions now, and withdrawals in retirement are tax-free.

5. **Emergency Fund and Short-term Goals**: Keep some liquidity in a taxable account for emergencies, but prioritize using your 401(k) and IRA for long-term growth.

It's always wise to consult with a financial advisor to personalize these strategies based on your unique financial situation.
