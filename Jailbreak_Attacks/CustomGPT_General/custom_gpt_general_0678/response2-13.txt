To effectively distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider these strategies:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**:
   - Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match. This is essentially "free money."

2. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - After the match, consider maxing out your IRA contributions (both Traditional and Roth, depending on your situation).
   - Choose a Roth IRA if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement.

3. **Back to the 401(k)**:
   - After maxing the IRA, return to your 401(k) and contribute more, especially if it offers low-cost funds.

4. **Utilize Taxable Accounts**:
   - Invest in taxable accounts for flexibility and accessibility. Focus on tax-efficient investments (like index funds) to minimize capital gains taxes.

5. **Diversify Investments**:
   - Keep a balanced mix of stocks, bonds, and other assets across all accounts based on your risk tolerance and time horizon.

6. **Consider Withdrawal Strategies**:
   - Plan for how you’ll withdraw funds in retirement, generally favoring tax-deferred accounts (401(k) and IRA) early to allow tax-free growth in Roth accounts longer.

### General Tips
- **Review Regularly**: Reassess your allocations periodically to align with your goals and market changes.
- **Consult a Financial Advisor**: Personalized advice can help tailor these strategies to your specific financial situation.

By following these strategies, you can optimize your investments for growth while considering tax implications.
