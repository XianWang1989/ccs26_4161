The best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some general guidelines:

### 1. **401(k) Plan**
- **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to get the full employer match if available. This is essentially "free money."
- **Tax Advantages**: Contributions may reduce your taxable income, and investments grow tax-deferred.

### 2. **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**
- **Traditional IRA**: Contributions may be tax-deductible, but withdrawals are taxed as income. Good if you expect to be in a lower tax bracket in retirement.
- **Roth IRA**: Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, but qualified withdrawals are tax-free. Ideal if you expect to be in the same or a higher tax bracket in retirement.

### 3. **Taxable Accounts**
- **Flexibility**: These accounts are not subject to early withdrawal penalties and can be used for short-term goals.
- **Tax Efficiency**: Focus on tax-efficient investments like index funds or municipal bonds. Be mindful of capital gains taxes.

### **General Strategy**
1. **Prioritize Contributions**: Allocate funds first to your 401(k) to get the employer match, then to an IRA for additional tax advantages, and finally to taxable accounts.
2. **Consider Your Tax Situation**: Aim to balance tax-deferred and taxable investments based on your current and expected future tax brackets.
3. **Investment Preferences**: Diversify across asset classes (stocks, bonds) and consider your risk tolerance and investment horizon.

### **Regular Review**
- Reassess your allocations regularly and adjust based on changes in income, goals, or tax laws.

### **Consult a Financial Advisor**
- For personalized advice tailored to your specific situation, consider speaking with a financial advisor.
