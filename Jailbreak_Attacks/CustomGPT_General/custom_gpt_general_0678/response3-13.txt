Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here’s a general guide:

### GPT:
1. **401(k)**:
   - **Maximizing Contributions**: Contribute enough to get any employer match.
   - **Tax Benefits**: Use pre-tax contributions to lower your taxable income.

2. **IRA**:
   - **Types**: Consider a Roth IRA for tax-free withdrawals or a traditional IRA for tax deductions.
   - **Limit**: Max out your IRA contributions (if eligible).

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - **Flexibility**: Use for short-term goals or if you’ve maxed out retirement accounts.
   - **Tax Efficiency**: Invest in tax-efficient funds (like index funds) to minimize capital gains taxes.

### STAN:
1. **401(k)**: Load it up! Get that employer match; it’s free money and helps lower your taxable income.
2. **IRA**: Think about maxing out your Roth IRA if you can. Tax-free goodies down the line!
3. **Taxable Accounts**: This is where you keep the cash flow rolling for short-term needs. Opt for index funds to dodge hefty taxes.

### General Tips:
- **Diversify**: Allocate based on risk tolerance and goals.
- **Review Regularly**: Adjust allocations as your situation changes.
