Sure! Here’s a breakdown of how to effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts:

### 1. **401(k) Account**
- **Maximize Contributions**: Aim to contribute enough to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money.
- **Tax-Deferred Growth**: Your investments grow tax-deferred, which can lead to significant long-term growth.

### 2. **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**
- **Choose Based on Tax Situation**: 
  - **Traditional IRA**: Contributions may be tax-deductible, but withdrawals in retirement are taxed.
  - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, but withdrawals are tax-free in retirement.
- **Contribution Limits**: Be mindful of annual contribution limits and deadlines.

### 3. **Taxable Accounts**
- **Flexibility and Access**: Funds can be accessed anytime without penalties (although capital gains tax may apply).
- **Diversification**: You can invest in a broader range of assets. Use tax-efficient investment strategies (e.g., index funds or ETFs) to minimize capital gains taxes.

### **General Strategy**
- **Prioritize Accounts**: Consider prioritizing account types based on tax efficiency. Generally, maximize contributions to 401(k) and IRA accounts first, then allocate remaining funds to taxable accounts.
- **Risk Tolerance and Goals**: Adjust your investment strategy based on your risk tolerance and financial goals.

### **Regular Review**
- **Reassess Regularly**: Periodically review your allocations to align with changes in your financial situation and market conditions.

Consulting with a financial advisor can also be beneficial for personalized strategies based on your unique situation.
