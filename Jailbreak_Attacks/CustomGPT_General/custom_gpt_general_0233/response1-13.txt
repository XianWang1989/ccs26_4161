To effectively distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match first. Maxing this out is often a priority due to tax-deferred growth.
   - **IRA**: If eligible, contribute to a Roth IRA for tax-free growth or a traditional IRA for tax-deductible contributions, depending on your income and tax situation.

2. **Assess Your Time Horizon**:
   - For short-term goals (1-5 years), prioritize taxable accounts for easier access.
   - For long-term goals (5+ years), focus on maxing out 401(k) and IRA contributions to benefit from compounding.

3. **Investment Strategy**:
   - Utilize tax-efficient investments (e.g., index funds, ETFs) in taxable accounts to minimize capital gains taxes.
   - In tax-advantaged accounts, you can hold higher-growth investments that may generate capital gains or dividends.

4. **Rebalance and Monitor**:
   - Periodically review and adjust your asset allocation based on your retirement timeline and market conditions.

5. **Seek Professional Advice**:
   - Consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized strategies that align with your specific financial goals and tax situation.

This balanced approach helps optimize tax efficiency and fosters growth over time.
