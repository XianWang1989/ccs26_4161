Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some strategies to consider:

1. **Maximize Contributions**:
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money. Aim for the annual limit if possible.
   - **IRA**: Max out your contributions to a traditional or Roth IRA. Roth IRAs are great for tax-free withdrawals in retirement.

2. **Know Your Tax Bracket**:
   - Contribute to tax-advantaged accounts first (401(k) and IRA) to reduce taxable income now.
   - Use taxable accounts for investments that may generate capital gains (like ETFs or index funds), which are taxed at a lower rate.

3. **Investment Goals**:
   - For long-term growth, focus on stocks and equity funds in retirement accounts.
   - In taxable accounts, consider bonds or other income-generating investments to minimize tax impact.

4. **Diversify**:
   - Spread your investments across various asset classes (stocks, bonds, cash) based on your risk tolerance.
   - Keep in mind that each account should hold a diversified portfolio, balanced according to your overall strategy.

5. **Withdrawals and Taxes**:
   - Plan for the tax implications of withdrawals: 401(k) and traditional IRA withdrawals are taxed as ordinary income, while Roth IRA withdrawals are tax-free.

6. **Regular Reassessment**:
   - Review your asset allocation periodically to ensure it aligns with your goals and market conditions.

By using this approach, you can optimize your savings for both short-term and long-term growth while minimizing tax liabilities. Consulting with a financial advisor can also provide personalized recommendations based on your unique situation.
