Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your income, tax situation, retirement goals, and investment strategy. Here’s a general approach to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money.

2. **Contribute to an IRA**: If you qualify, contribute to a Traditional IRA or Roth IRA. A Roth IRA, in particular, allows for tax-free growth and tax-free withdrawals in retirement.

3. **Continue 401(k) Contributions**: After getting the match and contributing to an IRA, go back to your 401(k) to maximize contributions up to the annual limit.

4. **Taxable Accounts**: Once you've maxed out your tax-advantaged accounts, consider putting additional savings into taxable accounts. This is useful for liquidity and flexibility, especially if you might need the funds before retirement.

5. **Tax Efficiency**: In your taxable accounts, focus on tax-efficient investments (like index funds or ETFs) to minimize capital gains taxes.

6. **Asset Allocation**: Consider diversifying your investments based on risk tolerance across all accounts. Growth assets (stocks) might be suited for tax-advantaged accounts, while more conservative investments might be appropriate for taxable accounts.

7. **Rebalance Regularly**: Monitor and adjust your allocations regularly to ensure you stay aligned with your financial goals.

Consulting with a financial advisor can also help tailor your strategy to your specific situation.
