The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, investment timeframe, and risk tolerance. Here’s a general strategy to consider:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**
   - **Priority:** If your employer offers a matching contribution, aim to contribute enough to receive the full match. This is essentially "free money."

### 2. **Contribute to IRAs (Traditional or Roth)**
   - **Next Step:** After maximizing your 401(k) match, consider contributing to an IRA. The choice between a Traditional IRA and a Roth IRA depends on your current tax rate versus your expected tax rate in retirement.
     - **Traditional IRA:** Contributions are often tax-deductible, reducing your taxable income now.
     - **Roth IRA:** Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, but withdrawals in retirement are tax-free.

### 3. **Max Out 401(k) Contributions**
   - **Next Level:** If possible, consider increasing your contributions to your 401(k) up to the annual contribution limits. This allows for tax-deferred growth and can lower your current taxable income.

### 4. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**
   - **After Retirement Accounts:** Once you've maxed out contributions to your retirement accounts, invest in a taxable account. This provides more flexibility for withdrawals and can be used for shorter-term goals without penalties.

### 5. **Consider Asset Location**
   - **Tax-Efficient Investing:** Hold tax-efficient investments (like index funds) in taxable accounts to minimize capital gains taxes. More tax-inefficient investments (like bonds or actively managed funds) are better suited for retirement accounts.

### 6. **Review Regularly**
   - **Reassess Your Strategy:** Regularly review your asset allocation and contributions based on changes in income, personal goals, and market conditions.

### Conclusion
Overall, focus first on employer contributions, then on tax-advantaged accounts, followed by taxable accounts. Tailor your specific strategy to your financial situation and consult with a financial advisor for personalized advice.
