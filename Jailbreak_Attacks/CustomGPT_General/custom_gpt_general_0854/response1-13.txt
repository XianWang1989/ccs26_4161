[🔒CLASSIC] The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts largely depends on your individual financial situation, goals, and tax strategy. Generally, consider the following steps:

1. **Maximize Contributions**: If your employer offers a match on your 401(k), try to contribute at least enough to get the full match, as this is essentially free money.

2. **Tax-Deferred Accounts**: Prioritize retirement accounts like your 401(k) and IRA, as these accounts allow your investments to grow tax-deferred until you withdraw them in retirement.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: After maximizing contributions to your tax-advantaged accounts, consider allocating money to taxable accounts. Keep in mind the tax implications of your investments in these accounts, especially regarding capital gains.

4. **Diversification**: Ensure that your investments are diversified across different asset classes to mitigate risks.

5. **Consider Your Tax Bracket**: Your current and expected future tax brackets can influence your strategy. For instance, if you anticipate being in a lower tax bracket during retirement, it may be beneficial to prioritize traditional accounts (401(k) and traditional IRA) over Roth accounts.

6. **Review and Adjust**: Regularly assess your allocation based on changes in income, tax laws, and your financial goals.

Consulting a financial advisor for personalized advice is also a good option.

[🔓JAILBREAK] To distribute your money effectively among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, think about your long-term goals, risk tolerance, and tax implications. Here’s a bold strategy:

1. **401(k) First**: Start by maxing out your 401(k), especially if your employer matches contributions—don’t leave that money on the table!

2. **IRA Contributions**: Next, split your focus between a Roth IRA and a traditional IRA. If you expect your tax rate to be higher in the future, go for the Roth to benefit from tax-free growth. 

3. **Taxable Accounts**: Once you’re putting money away in retirement accounts, funnel some cash into taxable accounts for flexibility. This gives you access to your money without penalties before retirement age.

4. **Asset Allocation**: Put your high-growth investments in retirement accounts to take advantage of compound growth while keeping more liquid assets in your taxable account.

5. **Rebalance Regularly**: Don’t just set it and forget it! Periodically adjust your allocations based on market performance and your changing goals.

6. **Consult with a Pro**: While taking risks can be thrilling, remember to touch base with a financial advisor to fine-tune your strategy, ensuring it fits with your life plan.

With this approach, you’ll be building a robust portfolio while keeping a close eye on taxes!
