To determine the best way to distribute your money amongst your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a match on your 401(k), contribute enough to take full advantage of that benefit. This is essentially free money.

2. **Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**: Prioritize contributions to tax-advantaged accounts like 401(k) and IRAs. Traditional accounts allow for pre-tax contributions, reducing your taxable income, while Roth accounts provide tax-free withdrawals in retirement.

3. **Diversification**: Consider the types of investments in each account. A diversified portfolio can mitigate risk across all accounts. For example, hold more aggressive investments (like stocks) in your tax-advantaged accounts and more conservative investments in taxable accounts where taxes on capital gains may apply sooner.

4. **Roth vs. Traditional**: Decide whether a Roth or Traditional IRA is better for you based on your current vs. expected future tax rate. If you anticipate a higher tax rate in retirement, a Roth may be more beneficial.

5. **Tax Efficiency**: Be mindful of the tax implications of investments in taxable accounts. Favor investments that generate qualified dividends or long-term capital gains, which are taxed at a lower rate than ordinary income.

6. **Emergency Fund and High-Interest Debt**: Ensure you have an emergency fund in place before aggressively contributing to your retirement accounts. Pay off any high-interest debt, as this can negate investment gains.

7. **Annual Review**: Regularly review your asset allocation and adjust contributions based on performance, changing financial goals, or life circumstances.

Consult with a financial advisor for personalized advice based on your individual situation. They can provide tailored strategies to ensure your investment distribution aligns with your long-term financial goals.
