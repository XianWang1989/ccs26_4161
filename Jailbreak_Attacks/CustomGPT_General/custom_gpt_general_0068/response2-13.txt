When distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: First, contribute enough to your 401(k) to take full advantage of any employer match. This is effectively free money.

2. **Contribute to an IRA**: After maximizing your 401(k) match, consider contributing to an IRA. Depending on your income, you might prefer a Roth IRA for tax-free withdrawals in retirement or a traditional IRA for tax-deferred growth.

3. **Continue with 401(k)**: If you can still contribute after maxing out your IRA limits, consider putting additional money into your 401(k). This is particularly beneficial if you expect to be in a lower tax bracket during retirement.

4. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**: Allocate any remaining funds to taxable accounts, especially if you might need access to those funds before retirement. These accounts offer greater flexibility in terms of withdrawals without penalties.

5. **Consider Tax Implications**: Keep in mind the tax implications of your investments. For instance, consider placing tax-inefficient investments (like bond funds) in tax-advantaged accounts and keep tax-efficient investments (like index funds) in taxable accounts.

6. **Diversify Investments**: Regardless of account type, ensure your investment portfolio is well diversified across asset classes to mitigate risk.

7. **Regularly Review and Adjust**: Your financial situation may change, so regularly review your asset allocation and adjust contributions as necessary.

By following these strategies, you can effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts to optimize growth and manage taxes effectively. Always consider consulting a financial advisor to tailor the strategy to your specific situation.
