The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, time horizon, tax situation, and risk tolerance. Here are some general strategies you might consider:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to get the full match first; it’s essentially free money.
   - **IRA**: Consider contributing to a Roth IRA if you qualify, as it allows tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement.

2. **Debt Considerations**: If you have high-interest debt, prioritize paying that down over investing, as the interest on debt can outweigh investment returns.

3. **Emergency Fund**: Maintain a liquid emergency fund in a high-yield savings account to cover at least 3-6 months of expenses before heavily investing.

4. **Taxable Accounts**: Utilize taxable accounts for flexible investing. Consider tax-efficient investments like index funds or ETFs to minimize capital gains taxes.

5. **Risk Tolerance and Time Horizon**: 
   - For long-term goals (retirement), you can be more aggressive in your 401(k) and IRA.
   - For shorter-term goals, lean more towards safer options in taxable accounts or keep funds accessible.

6. **Asset Allocation**: Diversify your investments based on your risk tolerance. Stocks, bonds, and alternative investments can all play a part in your portfolio.

7. **Regular Review and Rebalancing**: Periodically reassess your investments to ensure your asset allocation aligns with your goals and make adjustments as necessary.

It's often helpful to consult with a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your specific situation.
