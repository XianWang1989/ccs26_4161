Distributing your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here’s a general approach:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: If your employer offers a match, aim to contribute enough to get the full match first. This is essentially free money.
   - **IRA**: If you are eligible, consider contributing to a Traditional or Roth IRA. The tax advantages can significantly enhance your retirement savings.

2. **Tax Considerations**:
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Invest after maximizing tax-advantaged accounts. These accounts don’t offer the same tax benefits but can provide flexibility for withdrawals.

3. **Investment Goals and Time Horizon**:
   - Allocate funds based on your risk tolerance. Typically, you may want to invest more aggressively in accounts designated for long-term retirement (like your 401(k) and IRA).
   - For shorter-term goals, keep a portion of your funds in your taxable accounts for liquidity.

4. **Rebalance Periodically**:
   - Review and adjust your allocations regularly to align with market conditions and your changing financial goals.

5. **Consider Professional Advice**:
   - Consulting with a financial advisor can help tailor your strategy to your specific circumstances.

Remember that individual circumstances vary, so it's essential to assess your overall financial picture before making decisions.
