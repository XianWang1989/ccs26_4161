Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and time horizon. Here are some general guidelines:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match first. This is essentially free money.
   - **IRA**: Consider maxing out a Traditional or Roth IRA next, depending on your eligibility and tax situation.

### 2. **Assess Tax Impact**
   - **Tax-Deferred Accounts** (e.g., 401(k), Traditional IRA): Good for long-term growth, as you won’t pay taxes on earnings until withdrawal.
   - **Roth IRA**: Great for tax-free growth if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Best for short-term goals or if you need flexibility. Be mindful of capital gains taxes.

### 3. **Evaluate Your Time Horizon**
   - **Retirement Savings**: Focus more on tax-advantaged accounts if you’re saving for retirement.
   - **Short-Term Goals**: Use taxable accounts for liquidity.

### 4. **Diversification of Investments**
   - Ensure a diversified asset allocation across all accounts to manage risk effectively.

### 5. **Review and Adjust**
   - Regularly review your allocations to align with changing financial goals and market conditions.

### Conclusion
Prioritize contributions to maximize tax advantages, then adjust based on your overall investment strategy and needs. Always consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized advice.
