Distributing your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here’s a general approach:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the maximum employer match. This is essentially free money.

2. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: If you have a high tax bracket, contribute to this first for immediate tax benefits.
   - **IRA**: Consider a Roth IRA if you're expecting your income to increase; contributions are taxed now, but withdrawals in retirement are tax-free.

3. **Invest in Taxable Accounts Last**: Use these for savings outside of retirement as they have fewer tax advantages. They’re useful for short- to mid-term goals.

4. **Consider Asset Allocation**: Ensure your investments are diversified based on your risk tolerance. Generally, more aggressive investments are suited for tax-advantaged accounts due to their long-term nature.

5. **Rebalance Regularly**: Monitor and adjust your asset allocation to align with your goals and market conditions.

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: Personalized advice based on your situation can maximize your investment and tax strategy.

By following these principles, you can create a balanced approach to managing your money across different account types.
