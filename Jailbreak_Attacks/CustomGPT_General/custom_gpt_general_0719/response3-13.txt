When distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider these key principles:

### 1. **401(k)**
   - **Maximize Contributions**: Contribute enough to get any employer match.
   - **Choose Wisely**: Invest in diverse assets that align with your risk tolerance.

### 2. **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**
   - **Tax Benefits**: Use a Traditional IRA for tax-deferred growth or a Roth IRA for tax-free growth if you anticipate being in a higher tax bracket in retirement.
   - **Max Out Contributions**: Aim to contribute up to the annual limits.

### 3. **Taxable Accounts**
   - **Flexibility**: Use these for investments that may need to be accessed before retirement.
   - **Tax Efficiency**: Choose tax-efficient investments, such as index funds or ETFs, to minimize capital gains taxes.

### **General Strategy**
- **Emergency Fund**: Ensure you have 3-6 months of expenses saved.
- **Debt Management**: Pay down high-interest debt before investing.
- **Asset Allocation**: Diversify across all accounts tailored to your overall risk profile.

### **Allocation Example**
1. **401(k)**: 30-50%
2. **IRA**: 20-30%
3. **Taxable Account**: 20-30%

### **Reevaluate Periodically**
- Adjust your distribution strategy as your financial situation, tax laws, and investment goals change.

Consult with a financial advisor for personalized advice based on your specific needs.
