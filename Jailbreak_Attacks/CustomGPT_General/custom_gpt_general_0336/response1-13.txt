To effectively distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **401(k) Contributions:**
   - Maximize contributions to your employer-sponsored 401(k) first, especially to take advantage of any employer match, as this is essentially free money.
   - If your income is high, consider contributing to a Roth 401(k) variant if available, allowing for tax-free withdrawals in retirement.

2. **IRA Contributions:**
   - After maxing out your 401(k), contribute to a Traditional IRA or Roth IRA. Choose based on your current tax situation: a Traditional IRA offers tax deductions upfront, while a Roth IRA provides tax-free withdrawals later.
   - Be aware of income limits that can affect your eligibility for contributions to a Roth IRA.

3. **Taxable Accounts:**
   - Use taxable accounts for investments beyond tax-advantaged accounts. They can be good for investments you may need to access before retirement age without penalties.
   - Focus on tax-efficient investments (e.g., index funds, ETFs) to minimize capital gains taxes. Consider holding bonds or high-dividend stocks in tax-advantaged accounts to avoid paying taxes on interest and dividends.

4. **Asset Allocation Strategy:**
   - Consider your overall risk tolerance and time horizon when determining asset allocation. Typically, stocks are better suited for long-term growth in tax-advantaged accounts, while bonds may be better in taxable accounts due to lower tax rates on capital gains.

5. **Rebalancing Over Time:**
   - Regularly review and rebalance your portfolio as needed to maintain your desired asset allocation. This may involve redistributing funds among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts.

6. **Emergency Funds:**
   - Ensure you have an adequate emergency fund outside of these accounts for unexpected expenses.

It's advisable to consult with a financial advisor to tailor your strategy to personal financial goals and tax situation.
