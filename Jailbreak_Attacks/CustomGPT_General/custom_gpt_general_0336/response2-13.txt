Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment horizon. Here’s a general guideline:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**:
   - Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match, if available. This is essentially free money.

### 2. **Contribute to an IRA**:
   - After securing your match, consider contributing to a Traditional or Roth IRA. A Roth IRA is beneficial if you expect your tax rate to be higher in retirement, while a Traditional IRA can reduce your taxable income now.

### 3. **Back to the 401(k)**:
   - Once you’ve maximized your IRA contributions, return to your 401(k) to increase your contributions. Aim to maximize your annual contribution limit if possible.

### 4. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - Any money beyond the tax-advantaged accounts can be placed in taxable accounts. These offer flexibility but may incur capital gains taxes on investment profits.

### 5. **Asset Allocation**:
   - Diversify your investments among stocks, bonds, and other assets according to your risk tolerance and time horizon. Typically, you may want to hold more aggressive investments (like stocks) in tax-advantaged accounts to defer taxes on growth.

### 6. **Consider Your Tax Situation**:
   - Pay attention to the tax implications of where your investments are held. For example, interest-bearing investments are better in tax-advantaged accounts, while stocks you plan to hold long-term can go in taxable accounts.

### 7. **Review Regularly**:
   - Regularly review your allocations (at least annually) to ensure they still align with your goals, risk tolerance, and market conditions.

### Conclusion:
In summary, start with your 401(k) for the match, invest in an IRA for tax benefits, then fully fund your 401(k). Finally, invest in taxable accounts with an eye on tax efficiency. Always consult with a financial advisor for personalized advice.
