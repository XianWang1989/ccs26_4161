Balancing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment time horizon. Here are some strategies to consider:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**
   - **401(k):** If your employer offers a matching contribution, prioritize contributing enough to get the full match. This is essentially free money and can significantly boost your retirement savings.
   - **IRA:** After maximizing your 401(k) contributions, consider funding an IRA. Depending on your income, you may qualify for a Roth IRA, which allows for tax-free growth and withdrawals.

### 2. **Consider Your Tax Bracket**
   - **401(k) and Traditional IRA:** Contributions are made pre-tax, reducing your taxable income now but taxed upon withdrawal in retirement. This might be beneficial if you expect to be in a lower tax bracket in retirement.
   - **Roth IRA:** Fund this if you anticipate being in a higher tax bracket in retirement. Withdrawals are tax-free, which can be advantageous for long-term growth.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Use these for investments that might generate capital gains or dividends. This is useful for short-term savings or if you need flexibility, as there are no penalties for early withdrawals.

### 3. **Diversify Investments**
   - Spread your investments across different asset classes in all accounts. Include stocks, bonds, and real estate to reduce risk and improve potential returns.

### 4. **Rebalance Regularly**
   - Keep track of your asset allocation across all accounts and rebalance periodically based on your goals and risk tolerance.

### 5. **Consider Withdrawal Strategies**
   - Plan for how you'll withdraw from these accounts in retirement. Generally, it’s smart to withdraw from taxable accounts first to allow tax-advantaged accounts to grow longer.

### 6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**
   - A financial advisor can help tailor a plan based on your specific financial situation, goals, and preferences.

### Summary
Start with employer matches in a 401(k), fund your IRA next (prioritizing Roth if beneficial), and use taxable accounts for additional investments. Diversify across all accounts and articulate a withdrawal plan for retirement.
