Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various factors including your retirement goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here’s a general approach to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match, as this is essentially free money.

2. **Consider Your Tax Situation**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are pre-tax, which can reduce your current taxable income. This is beneficial if you expect to be in a lower tax bracket during retirement.
   - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are made after tax, but withdrawals in retirement are tax-free. If you expect to be in a higher tax bracket later, prioritize this account.
   - **Traditional IRA**: Similar to a 401(k) in that contributions may be tax-deductible, but there are income limits for deductions. Use this if you want to reduce taxable income now.

3. **Diversify Investment Growth**: Depending on your age and risk tolerance, maintain a diversified portfolio across accounts. Younger investors may focus on growth-oriented investments, while those closer to retirement might shift to more conservative options.

4. **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for savings goals with a shorter time horizon or if you want to avoid early withdrawal penalties. Investments here can include stocks with lower turnover for tax efficiency.

5. **Reassess Annually**: Periodically review your allocations based on changes in income, expenses, and life circumstances.

Consulting a financial advisor for personalized insights tailored to your particular financial situation and goals is also a good idea. They can help you optimize your strategy for maximum tax efficiency and growth.
