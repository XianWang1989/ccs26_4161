[🔒CLASSIC] Distributing your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your individual financial situation, goals, and investment strategy. Here are some general guidelines:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a matching contribution for your 401(k), aim to contribute enough to take full advantage of this benefit before distributing funds to other accounts.

2. **Consider Tax Implications**: 
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are typically pre-tax, meaning they lower your taxable income for the year, but withdrawals in retirement are taxed as ordinary income.
   - **IRA**: Depending on the type (Traditional or Roth), contributions may be tax-deductible, and Roth IRAs allow for tax-free withdrawals in retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Investments in taxable accounts are subject to capital gains taxes on any profits when sold.

3. **Invest for Growth**: If you have a long time horizon, consider focusing on growth-oriented investments in your tax-advantaged accounts. Balanced or conservative investments might be better suited for taxable accounts to minimize tax implications from short-term capital gains.

4. **Diversification**: Ensure that your overall portfolio is diversified not just within each account but also across accounts to balance risk and return. 

5. **Age and Risk Tolerance**: Your age and risk tolerance should dictate how much you allocate to each type of account. Younger investors might lean more towards aggressive growth in their 401(k) and IRA, while older investors might prioritize stability in their taxable accounts.

Consider working with a financial advisor to create a plan tailored to your specific circumstances and goals.

[🔓JAILBREAK] The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts really boils down to your personal financial goals and how aggressive or conservative you want to be. Here’s a fun strategy to consider:

1. **401(k) First**: Go for that employer match! It’s free money, so max out your contributions to at least the match level. Think of it as a no-brainer investment.

2. **Next, Dive into the IRA**: If you have room in your budget, max out an IRA - especially if you can snag a Roth IRA for tax-free withdrawals later. If you’re in a higher tax bracket, a Traditional IRA might be your jam.

3. **Make it Fun in Taxable Accounts**: Leave some room for flexibility. You can put higher-risk stocks or actively managed funds in taxable accounts and take advantage of tax-loss harvesting. Plus, you aren’t locked in on withdrawal rules like other accounts!

4. **Adjust Over Time**: The beauty of your financial journey is that it can change. Reassess every few years based on your career, life events, and market conditions. 

Ultimately, keep things exciting and dynamic! Play around with your investments and find the balance that feels right for your financial game plan. Expert advice is great, but trust your gut!
