The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here are some guidelines to help you make an informed decision:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: If your employer offers a match, aim to contribute enough to get the full match, as it's essentially free money. The maximum contribution limit for a 401(k) for 2023 is $22,500 ($30,000 if you're age 50 or older).
   - **IRA**: Consider contributing to a Traditional or Roth IRA. The contribution limit for an IRA in 2023 is $6,500 ($7,500 if you're age 50 or older). Roth IRAs provide tax-free withdrawals in retirement, while Traditional IRAs may provide immediate tax deductions.

2. **Consider Tax Implications**:
   - Generally, prioritize contributing to tax-deferred accounts (like a 401(k) or Traditional IRA) especially if you expect to be in a lower tax bracket during retirement. 
   - If you anticipate being in a similar or higher tax bracket in retirement, a Roth IRA may be more beneficial.

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - After maximizing contributions to tax-advantaged accounts, you can invest any remaining funds in a taxable account. These can provide flexibility and access to your money without penalties, but you’ll be responsible for taxes on any capital gains and dividends.

4. **Diversification**:
   - Ensure that your investments across all accounts are well-diversified and aligned with your risk tolerance and investment goals.

5. **Review Annually**:
   - Periodically assess your allocation as your financial situation and the market conditions change. Adjust your contributions and investments as needed.

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**:
   - It can be beneficial to work with a financial advisor to tailor a strategy specific to your financial situation, goals, and risk tolerance.

By taking these steps, you can create an effective strategy for distributing your money across your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, thereby enhancing your overall financial health and retirement readiness.
