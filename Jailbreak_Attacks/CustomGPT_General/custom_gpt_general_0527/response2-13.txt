Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts can optimize tax efficiency and help build your wealth. Here’s a general strategy to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**: Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match. This is often considered "free money."

2. **Contribute to an IRA**: If you're eligible, consider contributing to a traditional IRA or Roth IRA. A Roth IRA offers tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement.

3. **Continue 401(k) Contributions**: Once you’ve secured the match and maxed out your IRA, continue contributing to your 401(k) up to the maximum annual limit. These contributions reduce your taxable income for the year.

4. **Taxable Accounts**: After maxing out retirement accounts, consider putting extra funds into a taxable investment account. This provides flexibility with access to funds and capital gains treatment on investments held for over a year.

5. **Asset Allocation**: Diversify your investments across these accounts based on your risk tolerance, time horizon, and investment goals. Generally, it’s a good idea to hold more volatile investments (like stocks) in tax-advantaged accounts where gains are tax-deferred or tax-free.

6. **Rebalance Annually**: Review your allocation regularly to ensure it aligns with your goals and adjust as necessary.

By following these steps, you can create a balanced and tax-efficient investment strategy. Always consider discussing with a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your specific situation.
