When considering how to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, it's essential to account for several factors, including your current financial situation, retirement goals, tax implications, and risk tolerance. Here’s a balanced approach:

1. **Maximize Employer Match (401(k))**:
   - If your employer offers a matching contribution on your 401(k), contribute enough to take full advantage of that match first. This is effectively free money.

2. **Contribute to an IRA**:
   - After maximizing your 401(k) match, consider contributing to an IRA (either traditional or Roth, depending on your tax situation). IRAs often have a wider investment choice compared to 401(k) plans.

3. **Thoroughly Fund Your 401(k)**:
   - Once you’ve contributed to an IRA, continue to contribute to your 401(k) up to the maximum limit. The tax advantages (pre-tax contributions for a traditional 401(k) or tax-free growth in a Roth 401(k)) can significantly impact your retirement savings.

4. **Utilize Taxable Accounts**:
   - After maxing out contributions to tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) and IRA), consider adding to taxable investment accounts. These offer flexibility and can be a source of funds for non-retirement goals without penalties for early withdrawal.

5. **Consider Tax Implications**:
   - Understand how withdrawals from each account type will be taxed in retirement. Traditional accounts are typically taxed as ordinary income, while qualified distributions from Roth accounts can be tax-free.

6. **Maintain an Emergency Fund**:
   - Ensure you have an adequate emergency fund outside of retirement accounts. This should cover 3-6 months of living expenses.

7. **Reassess Regularly**:
   - Periodically review your allocation and adjust based on changes in income, expenses, investment performance, and life goals.

Each individual's situation is unique, so consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your financial circumstances and retirement strategy.
