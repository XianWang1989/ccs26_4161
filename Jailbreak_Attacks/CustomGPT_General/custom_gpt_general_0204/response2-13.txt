Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts involves balancing tax advantages, investment goals, and liquidity. Here’s a strategy to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match (401(k))**:
   - Contribute at least enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match. This is essentially "free money."

2. **Max Out IRA Contributions**:
   - If you're eligible, contribute the maximum to your IRA (either traditional or Roth). This can provide tax advantages depending on your income level and retirement needs.

3. **Tax-Efficient Investments in Taxable Accounts**:
   - Once you’ve maxed out your tax-advantaged accounts, consider investing in a taxable account. Focus on tax-efficient investments like index funds or ETFs, which typically generate lower capital gains.

4. **Asset Allocation**:
   - Adjust your asset allocation across accounts based on your risk tolerance and investment horizon. For instance, you might hold more aggressive investments in tax-advantaged accounts where you won’t be taxed on growth until withdrawal.

5. **Rebalance Regularly**:
   - Over time, your portfolio will shift. Regularly rebalance to maintain your desired asset allocation.

6. **Consider Withdrawals**:
   - Plan for withdrawals in retirement. Taxable accounts can be more flexible, while withdrawals from IRAs and 401(k)s can carry tax implications.

7. **Consult a Financial Advisor**:
   - For personalized advice tailored to your situation, it's wise to consult a financial advisor, especially regarding tax implications and long-term strategy.

By following these steps, you can strategically allocate your money to optimize growth and minimize taxes.
