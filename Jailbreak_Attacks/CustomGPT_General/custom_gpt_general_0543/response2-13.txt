Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts involves several considerations, including your financial goals, tax situation, investment options, and risk tolerance. Here's a general approach to think about:

1. **401(k) Contributions**:
   - **Employer Match**: Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to get any employer match. This is essentially free money and should be prioritized.
   - **Contribution Limits**: Ensure you’re aware of the annual contribution limits ($22,500 for 2024, plus a $7,500 catch-up contribution if you're over 50).

2. **IRA Contributions**:
   - **Types of IRAs**: Decide between a Traditional IRA (pre-tax contributions, taxed on withdrawal) and a Roth IRA (post-tax contributions, tax-free withdrawals). A Roth can be more beneficial if you expect your tax rate to be higher in retirement.
   - **Contribution Limits**: For 2024, the limit is $6,500 ($7,500 if over 50).

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - Use these accounts for investments that may not fit well in tax-advantaged accounts, such as assets that generate capital gains or dividends.
   - Consider investing in tax-efficient options and strategies, like index funds, to minimize tax implications.

4. **Investment Strategy**:
   - **Age and Risk Tolerance**: Generally, the younger you are, the more you can invest in stocks for growth. As you approach retirement, shift towards more conservative investments (bond funds, stable value funds).
   - **Diversification**: Ensure a diversified portfolio across all accounts to manage risk.

5. **Tax Planning**:
   - Be mindful of the tax implications of withdrawals in retirement. Withdraw from tax-deferred accounts (401(k) and Traditional IRA) first, and save Roth IRA for later if possible, as it won’t count as taxable income.

### Suggested Approach:
- **Maximize Employer Match** in 401(k)
- **Max Out IRA** contributions if eligible
- Contribute additional funds to 401(k) until you reach the limit
- Invest remaining savings in taxable accounts

### Regular Review:
- Periodically revisit your distribution strategy based on changing circumstances, market conditions, and life events.

Consulting a financial advisor can provide personalized strategies tailored to your specific situation.
