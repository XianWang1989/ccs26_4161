Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and risk tolerance. Here are some general guidelines:

### 1. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts:**
   - **401(k):** If your employer offers a matching contribution, maximize that first. It's free money and a good starting point.
   - **IRA:** After maximizing your 401(k) match, consider contributing to a Traditional or Roth IRA based on your tax situation. Roth IRAs offer tax-free growth and tax-free withdrawals in retirement, which can be advantageous if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket later.

### 2. **Consider Your Tax Situation:**
   - **Taxable Accounts:** These can be beneficial for short-term goals or if you want more flexibility. However, investments in taxable accounts may be subject to capital gains taxes.

### 3. **Diversification:**
   - Spread your investments within these accounts. Utilize a mix of stocks, bonds, and other assets to diversify risk across different sectors and asset classes.

### 4. **Investment Strategy:**
   - **Long-term Growth:** Consider focusing on equity investments in tax-advantaged accounts to capitalize on their tax benefits. 
   - **Income-Generating Investments:** Bonds and dividend-paying stocks might be better suited for taxable accounts due to different tax treatments.

### 5. **Age and Retirement Timeline:**
   - Younger investors might lean heavily into equities for growth, while those closer to retirement should prioritize stability and income generation.

### 6. **Regular Contributions:**
   - Make consistent contributions to all accounts, taking advantage of dollar-cost averaging.

### 7. **Rebalance Periodically:**
   - Review and adjust your allocations based on market fluctuations and your evolving financial goals.

### Conclusion:
A balanced approach typically involves maximizing contributions to your tax-advantaged accounts first, then considering any taxable investments. Evaluating your specific financial situation with a financial advisor can also provide personalized recommendations.
