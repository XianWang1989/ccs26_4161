To effectively distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, you'll want to consider several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some general guidelines that can help you make informed decisions:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**: If your employer offers a matching contribution for your 401(k), start by contributing enough to receive the full match. This is essentially free money that can significantly boost your retirement savings.

2. **Contribute to IRA**: After maximizing your 401(k) match, consider contributing to a traditional or Roth IRA. The choice between the two depends on your income level, tax bracket, and whether you prefer to pay taxes now (Roth) or later (traditional). If you're eligible, try to contribute the maximum allowed each year.

3. **Prioritize 401(k) Contributions**: If you still have additional funds to invest after your IRA contributions, consider increasing your contributions to your 401(k) up to the annual limit. This can provide significant tax advantages, especially if you're in a higher tax bracket.

4. **Taxable Accounts**: After fully funding tax-advantage accounts (401(k) and IRA), allocate additional funds to taxable investment accounts. Use these accounts for more flexible investment options without the contribution limits or penalties associated with retirement accounts.

5. **Asset Allocation**: Regardless of the account type, ensure that your overall portfolio is diversified and properly allocated based on your risk tolerance and investment horizon. This might include a mix of stocks, bonds, and other asset classes.

6. **Reevaluate Annually**: Review your contributions and investment strategy annually to adjust for changes in income, tax laws, and personal financial goals. 

Working with a financial advisor can also provide personalized advice tailored to your specific circumstances and objectives.
