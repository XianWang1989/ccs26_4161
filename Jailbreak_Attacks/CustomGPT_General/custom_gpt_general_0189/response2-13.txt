The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various factors including your financial goals, tax situation, and time horizon. Here are some general strategies you can consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a matching contribution for your 401(k), aim to contribute at least enough to take full advantage of this match. It’s essentially free money for your retirement.

2. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**: After getting the match, consider maxing out your IRA if you’re eligible. Traditional IRAs offer potential tax deductions now, while Roth IRAs provide tax-free withdrawals in retirement.

3. **Evaluate Contribution Limits**: For the 2023 tax year, the contribution limits are $22,500 for 401(k)s (or $30,000 if you're 50 or older) and $6,500 for IRAs (or $7,500 if you're 50 or older). Be mindful of these limits as you plan your contributions.

4. **Consider Tax Implications**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are made pre-tax, reducing your taxable income. Withdrawals in retirement are taxed as regular income.
   - **Traditional IRA**: Similar tax treatment to a 401(k), but you may face income limits for contributions if you also have a workplace retirement plan.
   - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, but qualified withdrawals are tax-free.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: These accounts do not have the same tax advantages but provide flexibility for withdrawals without penalties and capital gains tax based on your investments held.

5. **Asset Allocation**: Think about how to allocate your investments across these accounts. Generally, it's better to hold stocks in tax-advantaged accounts (like your 401(k) and IRA) to benefit from tax-free growth, while bonds could be held in taxable accounts for better tax efficiency.

6. **Rebalance Regularly**: As your investments grow, periodically rebalance your portfolio to ensure that your asset allocation aligns with your risk tolerance and financial goals.

7. **Consider Your Time Horizon**: If you are far from retirement, you might lean more towards stocks for growth. As you approach retirement, you might want to shift towards more conservative investments.

8. **Consult a Professional**: Lastly, consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your individual situation.

Ultimately, the key is to create a balanced approach that aligns with your retirement goals while considering your tax situation and investment strategy.
