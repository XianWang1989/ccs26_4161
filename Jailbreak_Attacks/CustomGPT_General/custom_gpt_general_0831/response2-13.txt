The optimal distribution of your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, investment horizon, and risk tolerance. Here is a general approach to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a matching contribution for your 401(k), contribute enough to take full advantage of this benefit, as it is essentially "free money."

2. **Consider Tax Advantages**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are generally pre-tax, reducing your taxable income for the year. However, withdrawals in retirement are taxed as ordinary income.
   - **IRA (Traditional)**: Similar to a 401(k), contributions may be tax-deductible, with taxes owed upon withdrawal.
   - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, but qualified withdrawals in retirement are tax-free. This option can be beneficial if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket during retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Investments in these accounts do not have tax advantages, but you have more flexibility in terms of withdrawals and investment choices.

3. **Emergency Fund and Short-Term Needs**: Ensure you have an emergency fund set up before heavily investing in these accounts to avoid penalties for early withdrawal.

4. **Diversification and Asset Allocation**: Within each account type, diversify your investments to manage risk. Consider a mix of stocks, bonds, and other assets aligned with your risk tolerance and investment strategy.

5. **Tax Efficiency**:
   - Place tax-inefficient investments (like bonds) in tax-advantaged accounts (401(k), IRA) to defer taxes on interest income.
   - Utilize taxable accounts for tax-efficient investments (like index funds or ETFs) to minimize capital gains taxes.

6. **Plan for Withdrawals**: As you approach retirement, create a strategy for how you will withdraw funds from each account type, considering tax implications and your cash flow needs.

It's advisable to consult with a financial advisor to develop a personalized strategy tailored to your specific circumstances and goals.
