When determining the best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Maximize Employer Matching**: If your employer offers a 401(k) match, contribute enough to get the full match. This is effectively "free money" and should be a top priority.

2. **Tax Considerations**:
   - **Tax-deferred accounts (401(k) and traditional IRA)**: Contributions reduce your taxable income for the year. Consider maximizing these if you expect to be in a lower tax bracket during retirement.
   - **Tax-free accounts (Roth IRA)**: Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, but withdrawals in retirement are tax-free. These are great if you believe your tax rate will be higher in retirement.
   - **Taxable accounts**: These accounts do not have tax advantages, but they offer flexibility. Use taxable accounts for investing beyond your tax-advantaged accounts once you’ve maximized contributions to the others.

3. **Investment Goals and Time Horizon**: Align your investment strategy with your goals. Consider risk tolerance and time until you need the funds. Long-term investments might focus more on growth, while short-term goals may require more conservative investments.

4. **Diversification**: Ensure that your investments are diversified across different asset classes (stocks, bonds, real estate) to mitigate risk.

5. **Withdrawal Strategy**: Plan how you will withdraw funds in retirement. Typically, withdrawing from taxable accounts first can help you minimize taxes over time.

6. **Regular Review and Adjustment**: Periodically review your asset allocation and contributions. Adjust based on changes in your financial situation, market conditions, or retirement goals.

It's advisable to consult with a financial advisor to tailor these strategies to your specific circumstances and ensure optimal tax efficiency.
