Distributing your money across a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment horizon. Here are some general guidelines:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts:**
   - **401(k):** Contribute enough to get any employer match first; it's free money.
   - **IRA (Traditional or Roth):** After maxing out the 401(k) match, consider contributing to an IRA (especially if you qualify for a Roth IRA).

2. **Evaluate Tax Implications:**
   - **Traditional 401(k) and IRA:** Contributions are tax-deductible, but withdrawals are taxed as income.
   - **Roth IRA:** Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, but withdrawals are tax-free in retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Investment income is taxed annually, but you have more flexibility regarding withdrawals.

3. **Diversify Investments:**
   - Use tax-advantaged accounts for higher-growth assets (e.g., stocks) to benefit from tax deferral.
   - Hold more stable, income-generating assets (e.g., bonds, REITs) in taxable accounts to manage taxes effectively.

4. **Consider Your Time Horizon:**
   - Longer time horizons can tolerate more risk, so allocate more to equities in retirement accounts.
   - For shorter-term goals, prioritize liquidity and stability in taxable accounts.

5. **Regularly Rebalance:** 
   - Review your asset allocation periodically to ensure it aligns with your financial goals and risk tolerance.

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor:**
   - Consider personalized advice to better fit your individual circumstances.
  
By following this approach, you can optimize your overall investment strategy while considering tax efficiency and personal financial goals.
