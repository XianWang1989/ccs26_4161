Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here’s a general strategy:

1. **401(k) Contributions**:
   - Max out contributions, especially if your employer matches (this is free money).
   - Focus here for long-term growth, as these funds grow tax-deferred.

2. **IRA Contributions**:
   - Consider a Roth IRA if you anticipate being in a higher tax bracket when you retire, as withdrawals are tax-free.
   - A traditional IRA may be beneficial if you want to reduce taxable income now.

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - Use these for short-term goals or when you've maxed out tax-advantaged accounts.
   - Prioritize investments that generate long-term capital gains (lower tax rates) and tax-efficient funds.

### General Advice:
- **Emergency Fund**: Maintain an emergency fund outside of these accounts.
- **Diversification**: Ensure your investments are diversified across asset classes.
- **Rebalance**: Regularly review and adjust your allocations based on market conditions and your financial goals.

Consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized advice based on your specific circumstances.
