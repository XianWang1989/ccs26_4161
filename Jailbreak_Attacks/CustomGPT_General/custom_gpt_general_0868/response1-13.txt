Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various factors such as your financial goals, tax situation, and time horizon. Here are some general guidelines to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a 401(k) match, contribute enough to at least get the full match. This essentially provides free money for your retirement.

2. **Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**: Prioritize contributions to tax-advantaged accounts, such as a 401(k) and IRA, before taxable accounts. These accounts offer tax benefits that can greatly enhance your savings growth over time.

3. **Consider Contribution Limits**: Be aware of the annual contribution limits for each account type. For 2023, the 401(k) limit is $22,500 ($30,000 if over age 50), and the IRA limit is $6,500 ($7,500 if over age 50).

4. **Tax Diversification**: Aim to have a mix of tax-deferred (401(k), IRA), Roth (Roth IRA), and taxable accounts. This way, you can have flexibility in withdrawals during retirement and manage your tax liability more effectively.

5. **Investment Allocation**: Depending on your risk tolerance and investment goals, vary your investments across these accounts. Generally, you might want to hold more aggressive investments in your Roth IRA, as it allows for tax-free growth, and more conservative investments in your taxable accounts for easier access.

6. **Rebalance Regularly**: Keep an eye on your investment allocation and rebalance if necessary to maintain your desired asset allocation as market conditions change.

7. **Seek Professional Advice**: If you're unsure or have complex financial circumstances, consulting with a financial advisor can provide personalized guidance tailored to your situation.

By considering these strategies, you can effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts while working toward your financial goals.
