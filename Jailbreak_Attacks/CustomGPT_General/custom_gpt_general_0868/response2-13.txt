Distributing your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, risk tolerance, and tax situation. Here are some general guidelines to consider:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to get the full match first; it’s essentially free money. Aim to contribute the maximum if possible, as contributions reduce your taxable income.
   - **IRA**: Consider contributing to a Roth IRA if you qualify, as tax-free withdrawals in retirement can be advantageous. Traditional IRAs offer tax deductions now but tax on withdrawals later.

2. **Assess Your Time Horizon**:
   - If you're far from retirement, a more aggressive investment strategy in these accounts may be appropriate, as they benefit from compound growth and tax deferral.
   - As you approach retirement, shift towards more conservative investments to preserve capital.

3. **Consider Liquidity Needs**:
   - Keep in mind that funds in your taxable account are more accessible without penalties, making it suitable for short-term savings or emergencies.
   - Ensure you have enough in your taxable account for any immediate cash flow needs before tying up funds in retirement accounts.

4. **Diversification**:
   - Spread your investments across asset classes (stocks, bonds, real estate) within each account to reduce risk. Your asset allocation should align with your overall financial strategy.

5. **Tax Efficiency**:
   - Place higher-tax assets (like bonds or actively managed funds) in tax-advantaged accounts, while holding lower-tax assets (like index funds) in taxable accounts to minimize your tax burden.

6. **Review and Adjust**:
   - Regularly review your asset allocation and rebalance as necessary. Life changes, market performance, and tax laws might necessitate adjustments.

It’s always wise to consult a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your specific situation.
