When considering how to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, several factors come into play, including your investment goals, time horizon, and tax situation. Here are some strategies to help you decide:

### 1. **Understand the Accounts:**
   - **401(k):** Often has employer matching; contributions are pre-tax, reducing taxable income now.
   - **IRA:** Offers tax advantages (traditional IRAs are pre-tax, Roth IRAs are post-tax, allowing for tax-free withdrawals in retirement).
   - **Taxable Accounts:** No tax advantages but more flexibility; you owe taxes on dividends, interest, and capital gains.

### 2. **Prioritize Contributions:**
   - **Max Out Employer Match:** Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money.
   - **Consider IRAs:** If eligible, consider contributing to a Roth IRA for tax-free growth or a traditional IRA for tax deductions.
   - **Fund Taxable Accounts Last:** Use these for additional savings or investments after maxing out tax-advantaged accounts.

### 3. **Asset Allocation:**
   - **401(k) and IRAs:** Generally, hold investments with high growth potential (like stocks) in these accounts to defer taxes on growth.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Place lower-growth investments (like bonds or dividend-paying stocks) here to minimize tax implications.

### 4. **Consider Your Tax Situation:**
   - **Tax Brackets:** If you're in a higher tax bracket now, a traditional 401(k) or IRA may benefit you now, allowing for tax-deferred growth until withdrawal.
   - **Future Tax Plans:** If you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement, consider prioritizing Roth accounts.

### 5. **Reassess Regularly:**
   - Periodically review your investment allocation and account balances to ensure they align with your changing financial situation and goals.

### 6. **Consult a Professional:**
   - It can be beneficial to work with a financial advisor who can provide personalized advice based on your unique financial situation.

By strategically allocating your funds across these accounts, you can maximize your tax advantages, investment growth, and meet your financial goals effectively.
