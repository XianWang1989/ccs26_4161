Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and the specific investment options available. Here’s a general approach:

1. **401(k)**:
   - **Maximize Contributions**: If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to get the full match first.
   - **Investment Choices**: Choose a diversified mix of investments that align with your risk tolerance and retirement timeline.

2. **IRA** (Traditional or Roth):
   - **Tax Considerations**: If you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement, consider a Roth IRA. For current tax savings, a Traditional IRA might be better.
   - **Maximize Contributions**: Aim to max out your IRA contributions annually.

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - **Flexibility**: Use these accounts for short- to medium-term goals since they allow more flexibility without penalties for withdrawal.
   - **Tax-Efficient Investments**: Focus on tax-efficient investments like index funds or tax-managed funds to minimize capital gains taxes.

### Suggested Distribution Strategy:
- **Short-Term Goals**: Funds in taxable accounts.
- **Medium-Term Goals**: Contributions to a Roth IRA or taxable accounts.
- **Long-Term Goals**: Maximize your 401(k) and IRA contributions first.

### Annual Review:
Reassess your allocation yearly based on changes in income, financial goals, and market conditions to ensure your distribution remains aligned with your overall strategy. Consulting with a financial advisor can also provide personalized insights.
