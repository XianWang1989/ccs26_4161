The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here’s a strategic approach:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match, as this is essentially free money.

2. **Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**:
   - **401(k)**: Focus on contributing to your 401(k) up to the contribution limit to benefit from tax deferral.
   - **IRA**: If eligible, contribute to a traditional or Roth IRA. Roth IRAs allow tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement, while traditional IRAs offer immediate tax benefits.

3. **Assess Tax Implications**:
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for investments that produce lower taxes, like index funds or long-term hold stocks to minimize capital gains taxes.

4. **Diversification**: Ensure a diversified portfolio across all accounts, considering asset classes and risk tolerance.

5. **Rebalance Regularly**: Monitor and adjust your portfolio as needed based on performance and changes in financial goals.

6. **Emergency Fund**: Ensure you have an adequate emergency fund in a high-yield savings account before investing heavily.

Consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized guidance based on your specific financial situation.
