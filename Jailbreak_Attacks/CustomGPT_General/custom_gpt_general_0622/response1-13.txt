Distributing money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts requires a strategic approach. Here are some guidelines:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to receive the full employer match. This is free money.

2. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: Great for higher contribution limits and tax deferral. Consider traditional vs. Roth options based on your tax situation.
   - **IRA**: If eligible, consider a Roth IRA for tax-free growth, or a traditional IRA for tax deductions.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: Invest in taxable accounts for flexibility. Focus on tax-efficient investments like index funds or ETFs to minimize capital gains taxes.

4. **Emergency Fund**: Ensure you have a solid emergency fund before heavily investing.

5. **Asset Allocation**: Diversify by placing more volatile assets (like stocks) in tax-advantaged accounts and stable investments (like bonds) in taxable accounts for better tax efficiency.

6. **Rebalance Periodically**: Regularly review and adjust your allocations based on performance and changing goals.

Consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized advice based on your specific situation.
