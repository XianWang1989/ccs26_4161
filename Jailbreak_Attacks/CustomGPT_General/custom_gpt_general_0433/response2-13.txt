Distributing money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts effectively depends on several factors, including your financial goals, risk tolerance, and tax situation. Here are some general guidelines:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**:
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match, as it’s essentially free money. Aim for the maximum annual contribution if possible.
   - **IRA**: Consider maxing out a Traditional or Roth IRA, depending on your income and tax situation. Roth IRAs are especially advantageous if you anticipate being in a higher tax bracket in retirement.

2. **Consider Tax Implications**:
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for investments that generate capital gains or use tax-efficient funds (like index funds) to minimize taxes.
   - **Asset Location**: Place higher-tax investments (like bonds) in tax-deferred accounts (401(k), IRA) and lower-tax investments (like stocks) in taxable accounts.

3. **Diversification**:
   - Ensure a balanced portfolio across all accounts, considering your overall asset allocation (stocks, bonds, etc.).

4. **Emergency Fund**:
   - Maintain a cash reserve separate from these accounts for short-term needs.

5. **Rebalance Regularly**:
   - Adjust your allocations based on performance and changes in your financial goals.

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**:
   - If unsure, professional guidance can provide personalized strategies based on your specific circumstances.

### Example Distribution Strategy

- **401(k)**: 15-20% of your income. Max out contributions if feasible.
- **IRA**: Contribute the maximum annually based on eligibility.
- **Taxable Accounts**: Allocate any remaining funds while investing in low-turnover, tax-efficient investments.

Adjust these percentages based on personal goals, upcoming expenses, and retirement plans.
