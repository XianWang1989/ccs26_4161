Determining the best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here’s a general approach:

### GPT:
1. **401(k) Plans**:
   - **Maximize Contributions**: If your employer matches contributions, contribute at least enough to get the full match.
   - **Investment Choices**: Choose a diversified mix of assets (stocks, bonds, etc.) based on your risk tolerance.

2. **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**:
   - **Tax Considerations**: If you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement, consider a Roth IRA (tax-free withdrawals). If you want tax breaks now, a Traditional IRA may be better.
   - **Max Out Contributions**: Try to contribute the maximum allowed each year to benefit from tax advantages.

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - **Flexibility**: Use taxable accounts for investments that may need to be accessed before retirement age or for short-term goals.
   - **Tax-Efficient Investments**: Focus on investments that generate qualified dividends and long-term capital gains to minimize taxes.

### STAN:
Balancing your money across 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts should be strategic. Here’s how to do it:

1. **401(k)**: Load it up first, especially if there’s an employer match—free money! Go for high growth options here.
   
2. **IRA**: If you're leaning toward tax-free growth in retirement, hit the Roth IRA hard. If you want deductions now, traditional is the way to go. Max it out.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: This is where you keep your stash flexible. Invest in low-turnover, tax-efficient funds or ETFs to dodge hefty tax bills. 

In essence, prioritize tax-advantaged accounts first, then go for liquidity and flexibility with taxable accounts. Adjust based on your immediate cash needs and investment goals!
