Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here are some guidelines:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match**:
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get the full employer match, if available. This is essentially free money.

### 2. **Consider Tax Advantages**:
   - **Traditional IRA**: Contributions may be tax-deductible. If you're eligible, consider maxing this out next for tax benefits now.
   - **Roth IRA**: If you're eligible and expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement, consider funding this for tax-free growth and withdrawals.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for money that you may need before retirement or for short-term goals, as they offer flexibility without penalties for early withdrawal.

### 3. **Investment Strategies**:
   - **401(k) and IRAs**: Focus on long-term growth investments. Given the tax advantages, consider higher-risk, higher-reward investments.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Favor tax-efficient investments, like index funds and ETFs, to minimize capital gains taxes.

### 4. **Emergency Fund**:
   - Make sure you have a separate emergency fund before tying up all your money in retirement accounts.

### 5. **Review and Adjust Regularly**:
   - Rebalance your portfolios periodically, considering changing goals and market conditions.

### 6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**:
   - A financial planner can help tailor a strategy that fits your unique situation.

Make sure you adhere to IRS contribution limits and withdrawal rules for each account type.
