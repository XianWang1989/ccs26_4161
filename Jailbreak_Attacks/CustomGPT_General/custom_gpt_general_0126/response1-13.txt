Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your retirement goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here’s a breakdown of some strategic approaches:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match**
   - **401(k):** Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money.

### 2. **High-Interest Debt**
   - If you have high-interest debt, prioritize paying that off before investing heavily. 

### 3. **Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**
   - **IRA:** After maximizing your 401(k) match, consider contributing to an IRA (Traditional or Roth). A Roth IRA offers tax-free growth, while a Traditional IRA may provide tax deductions.
   - Consider the tax implications of each account type depending on your current income and expected retirement tax bracket.

### 4. **Continual Contributions to 401(k)**
   - Once you've contributed to an IRA, continue maximizing your 401(k) contributions, aiming for the annual limit.

### 5. **Taxable Accounts**
   - Use taxable accounts for investment flexibility and access to funds without withdrawal penalties. 
   - Consider tax-efficient investments like index funds and ETFs to minimize capital gains taxes.

### 6. **Diversification**
   - Diversify your investments across accounts to manage risk. Each account can hold various asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.).

### 7. **Review and Adjust**
   - Regularly review your portfolio and adjust based on investment performance, changes in income, and life events.

### Summary
A good strategy might be:
1. Contribute to your 401(k) up to the employer match.
2. Max out your IRA contributions.
3. Continue maximizing your 401(k).
4. Use taxable accounts for any additional savings, focusing on tax-efficient investments.

Consulting a financial advisor can also help tailor these strategies to your specific situation.
