When considering how to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, here are some strategic approaches:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a match on your 401(k) contributions, prioritize contributing enough to get the full match. This is essentially free money.

2. **Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**:
   - **401(k)**: If you have the ability to contribute the maximum to your 401(k), do so, especially if your tax rate is high. This reduces your taxable income.
   - **IRA**: Consider contributing to a Traditional or Roth IRA (depending on your income level and future tax expectations). Roth IRAs offer tax-free withdrawals in retirement, which can be beneficial.

3. **Consider Current Tax Situation**:
   - **Traditional IRA**: Contributions may be tax-deductible, which is advantageous if you're currently in a higher tax bracket.
   - **Roth IRA**: Post-tax contributions grow tax-free, making it a great option for future tax-free income.

4. **Build a Taxable Account**: Once you've maximized your contributions to tax-advantaged accounts, consider putting money into a taxable account. This gives you flexibility for withdrawals and investments but keep in mind potential capital gains taxes.

5. **Asset Allocation**: Diversify your investments across all accounts. Stocks may be better for tax-advantaged accounts, where higher capital gains taxes apply. Bonds or conservative investments can be suitable for taxable accounts due to lower tax impacts.

6. **Rebalancing**: Regularly review and adjust your portfolio to maintain your desired risk level and diversification.

7. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: If you're unsure, a professional can help tailor a strategy based on your specific financial situation, goals, and risk tolerance.

By strategically balancing contributions and considering your tax implications, you can optimize your overall investment growth.
