Distributing your money across a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and time horizon. Here are some general guidelines:

1. **401(k):**
   - **Maximize Contributions:** If your employer matches contributions, aim to contribute enough to get the full match.
   - **Pre-Tax vs. Roth:** Consider a traditional pre-tax 401(k) if you expect to be in a lower tax bracket in retirement. A Roth 401(k) may be better if you expect higher taxes later.

2. **IRA (Traditional or Roth):**
   - **Contribution Limits:** Max out your IRA contributions (for 2023, $6,500 or $7,500 if age 50+).
   - **Tax Strategy:** A traditional IRA offers tax deductions, while a Roth IRA provides tax-free withdrawals in retirement. Choose based on your current vs. expected future tax rate.

3. **Taxable Accounts:**
   - **Flexibility:** Use these for funds you might need before retirement; they offer more access without penalties.
   - **Tax Efficiency:** Invest in assets that generate qualified dividends or long-term capital gains, which are taxed at lower rates.

**Overall Strategy:**
- Start by maxing out employer matches and IRA contributions.
- Allocate remaining funds based on risk tolerance and investment goals, using tax-efficient investments in taxable accounts.

Consider consulting a financial advisor for a personalized plan!
