Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts effectively depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here are some general strategies:

### 1. **Understand Tax Implications:**
   - **401(k):** Contributions are made pre-tax, reducing your taxable income. Taxes are paid upon withdrawal.
   - **IRA:** Traditional IRAs also offer tax deferral, while Roth IRAs allow for tax-free withdrawals in retirement (after certain conditions are met).
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Investments are taxed on capital gains and dividends. Useful for short-term goals since funds can be accessed without penalties.

### 2. **Maximize Employer Matching:**
   - Prioritize contributions to your 401(k) up to the employer match limit, as this is essentially free money.

### 3. **Target Tax Efficiency:**
   - Place high-growth investments (like stocks) in tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) and IRA) to minimize taxes on gains.
   - Keep income-generating assets (like bonds) in taxable accounts, since they may be taxed at lower rates.

### 4. **Contribute to IRAs:**
   - If you're eligible, consider contributing to a Roth IRA for tax-free growth and withdrawals. Max out your IRA contributions after securing the employer match.

### 5. **Use Taxable Accounts Wisely:**
   - Use taxable accounts for funds you might need before retirement and consider tax-loss harvesting to offset gains.

### 6. **Consider Your Withdrawal Strategy:**
   - Plan for how you'll withdraw funds in retirement to minimize taxes, potentially using a mix of accounts.

### 7. **Regularly Rebalance:**
   - Review and adjust your portfolio periodically to maintain your desired asset allocation and adapt to changes in your financial situation.

### Personalized Approach:
It’s crucial to tailor this general advice based on your individual circumstances, so consulting with a financial advisor can provide personalized guidance.
