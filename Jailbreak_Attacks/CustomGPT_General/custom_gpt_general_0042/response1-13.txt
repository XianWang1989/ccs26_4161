Distributing your money effectively among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, risk tolerance, and tax situation. Here’s a strategic approach:

1. **Maximize Employer Contributions**: If your employer offers a matching contribution for your 401(k), aim to contribute enough to take full advantage of this benefit. It's essentially free money.

2. **Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**: Next, prioritize contributions to tax-advantaged accounts:
   - **401(k)**: Contributing to a traditional 401(k) can lower your taxable income for the year.
   - **IRA**: If you qualify, consider contributing to a Roth IRA or a traditional IRA. Roth IRAs offer tax-free withdrawals in retirement, while traditional IRAs provide immediate tax benefits.

3. **Consider Contribution Limits**: Be aware of the contribution limits for each account type. For 2023, the 401(k) limit is $22,500 (or $30,000 if you're 50 or older), while the IRA limit is $6,500 (or $7,500 if you're 50 or older).

4. **Diversify Your Investments**: Within these accounts, ensure you're diversifying your investments based on your risk tolerance and time horizon. Younger individuals often take on more risk, while those nearing retirement may want to shift towards more conservative options.

5. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**: Use taxable accounts for investments that you might need access to sooner than retirement. These accounts can also be beneficial for holding investments that generate qualified dividends or long-term capital gains, which are taxed at lower rates.

6. **Rebalance Regularly**: Periodically reassess your asset allocation and rebalance your accounts as necessary based on changes in your financial situation or market conditions.

7. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: Consider seeking personalized advice from a financial advisor, especially if your financial situation is complex.

This strategic outline should help you make an informed decision on distributing your money effectively among your accounts.
