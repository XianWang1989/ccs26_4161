When deciding how to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **401(k) Contributions**:
   - **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute at least enough to get any employer match, as this is essentially "free money."
   - **Tax Benefits**: Contributions are often made pre-tax, reducing your taxable income. Focus on maximizing contributions to take advantage of this benefit.

2. **IRA Contributions**:
   - **Traditional vs. Roth**: If eligible, consider contributing to both a Traditional IRA for tax-deferred growth and a Roth IRA for tax-free withdrawals in retirement. Allocate funds based on your current and expected future tax situation.
   - **Contribution Limits**: Be aware of annual contribution limits ($6,500 for 2024; $7,500 if age 50 or older).

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - **Flexibility**: Investments in taxable accounts offer more flexibility regarding withdrawals. This is useful for short- to medium-term goals.
   - **Tax Efficiency**: Focus on tax-efficient investments (like index funds and ETFs) to minimize capital gains taxes.

### Suggested Allocation Approach:
- **Emergency Fund**: Ensure you have 3-6 months of living expenses saved in a high-yield savings account before heavily investing.
- **Percentage Allocation**:
  - Start with 401(k) (up to the match)
  - Then, fund an IRA (prioritize based on Roth vs. Traditional)
  - Finally, direct any additional funds into taxable accounts

### Regular Review:
- **Annual Rebalancing**: Adjust your allocations regularly based on performance, changes in financial goals, and tax considerations.

Consider consulting with a financial advisor who can provide personalized guidance based on your unique financial situation and goals.
