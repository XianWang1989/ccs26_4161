Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, risk tolerance, and tax situation. Here’s a general approach:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your 401(k) offers a match, contribute enough to get the full match first. This is essentially free money.

2. **Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **Max Out IRA Contributions**: If you qualify, consider maximizing your IRA contributions for tax advantages.
   - **Continue 401(k) Contributions**: After your IRA, keep contributing to your 401(k) up to the annual limit, especially if you are in a high tax bracket.

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - **Invest Remaining Funds**: Use taxable accounts for flexibility. This is especially useful if you plan to access funds before retirement or if you want to invest in assets not available in tax-advantaged accounts.

4. **Consider Asset Allocation**:
   - Generally, put higher-risk investments (like stocks) in tax-advantaged accounts to defer taxes. Use taxable accounts for lower-risk investments (like bonds) to minimize tax impact on income.

5. **Review Regularly**: Reassess your strategy periodically to adjust for changes in income, tax laws, and financial goals.

Consulting a financial advisor can provide personalized strategies tailored to your situation.
