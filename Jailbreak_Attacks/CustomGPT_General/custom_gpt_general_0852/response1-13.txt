Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and time horizon. Here’s a general strategy to consider:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money. Consider maxing out contributions if you can, as contributions lower your taxable income.
   - **IRA**: Depending on your eligibility, max out your contributions to a traditional (pre-tax) or Roth IRA (post-tax, tax-free growth). Roth IRAs are particularly beneficial if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement.

### 2. **Balance Contributions**
   - If you’re reaching the contribution limits for your 401(k) and IRA, consider how to allocate excess funds between them.
   - Evaluate the tax implications based on whether you expect your income to rise in the future (favoring Roth contributions).

### 3. **Consider Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**
   - Use taxable accounts for funds you might need before retirement or for specific short-term goals. These accounts provide flexibility but are less tax-efficient.

### 4. **Diversify Your Investments**
   - Within each account type, ensure diversification across asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) and sectors to manage risk.

### 5. **Review and Adjust**
   - Regularly review your investment mix based on market conditions and your personal financial situation. Adjust contributions if necessary to align with your evolving goals.

### 6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**
   - Consider speaking with a financial professional to tailor a plan specific to your financial situation, tax bracket, and investment goals.

This approach helps maximize tax advantages while ensuring you maintain flexibility for your investment needs.
