To effectively distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider these strategies:

### 1. **Understand the Account Types:**
   - **401(k)**: Pre-tax contributions with potential employer matching. Good for tax-deferred growth.
   - **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**: Tax advantages vary; Traditional IRAs provide tax deductions, while Roth IRAs offer tax-free withdrawals in retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: No tax advantages, but offer flexibility for withdrawals and investment choices.

### 2. **Prioritize Contributions:**
   - Maximize employer matching in your 401(k) first, as it's essentially free money.
   - Next, contribute to your IRA (consider a Roth if you qualify).
   - Finally, invest in your taxable account for additional savings.

### 3. **Tax Efficiency:**
   - Hold tax-inefficient investments (like bonds) in tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) and IRA).
   - Keep tax-efficient investments (like index funds) in taxable accounts to minimize capital gains taxes.

### 4. **Diversification and Allocation:**
   - Diversify your investments to balance risk and return based on your age, risk tolerance, and financial goals.
   - Rebalance your portfolio periodically to maintain your desired asset allocation.

### 5. **Withdrawal Strategies:**
   - Plan for how and when you'll withdraw funds in retirement, considering the tax implications of each account type.
   - Generally, it may be beneficial to withdraw from taxable accounts first, tax-deferred accounts second, and Roth accounts last.

### Example Allocation:
- **401(k)**: 50%
- **IRA**: 25%
- **Taxable Account**: 25%

Adjust these percentages based on personal savings goals and investment strategy.

### 6. **Consult a Financial Advisor:**
   - If uncertain, a financial advisor can provide personalized advice based on your financial situation.

These strategies can help maximize your investments and retirement savings effectively.
