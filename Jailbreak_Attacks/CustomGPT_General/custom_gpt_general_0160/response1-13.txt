Determining the best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your age, income, investment goals, and tax situation. Here are some general guidelines to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**: If your employer offers a matching contribution, aim to contribute enough to get the full match first as it's essentially free money.

2. **Contribute to an IRA**: After maximizing the 401(k) match, consider contributing to an IRA (Traditional or Roth) depending on your income level and retirement goals. A Roth IRA offers tax-free growth on withdrawals in retirement if you meet certain conditions.

3. **Max Out 401(k) Contributions**: If you have the financial capacity, aim to maximize your 401(k) contributions next, as it offers tax-deferred growth and potentially higher contribution limits compared to an IRA.

4. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**: If you have additional funds after contributing to your 401(k) and IRA, consider investing in taxable accounts. This allows for greater liquidity and flexibility, as you can withdraw contributions without penalties or taxes.

5. **Consider Your Tax Situation**: Think about your current and expected future tax situations. For example, if you anticipate being in a higher tax bracket in retirement, Roth contributions may be more beneficial.

6. **Diversify Your Investments**: Regardless of account type, ensure your investments are well-diversified across asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) to manage risk effectively.

7. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: For personalized advice tailored to your unique financial situation, consider consulting with a financial advisor.

Remember, these are general recommendations, and individual circumstances may warrant different strategies.
