To determine the best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following guidelines:

1. **401(k) Contributions**: Max out your 401(k) contributions if your employer offers a match, as this is essentially free money. Focus on contributing enough to get the full match first.

2. **IRA Contributions**: After maximizing your 401(k) match, consider contributing to an IRA (traditional or Roth) based on your income and tax situation. Roth IRAs offer tax-free growth, while traditional IRAs provide tax deductions upfront.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: Utilize taxable accounts for investments after contributing to tax-advantaged accounts. This allows for flexibility, such as accessing funds without penalties and managing capital gains tax efficiently.

4. **Investment Strategy**: Ensure your asset allocation aligns with your risk tolerance and investment goals. Typically, retirement accounts can have a more aggressive strategy since they are long-term, while taxable accounts might incorporate a mix of safer investments for liquidity.

5. **Emergency Fund**: Keep a portion of your savings in a liquid form outside of retirement accounts as an emergency fund before heavily investing in these accounts.

6. **Review Regularly**: Periodically reassess your strategy based on changing financial circumstances and retirement goals.

Consult with a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your specific situation.
