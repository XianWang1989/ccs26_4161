Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, investment timeline, and tax situation. Here are some strategies:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a match for your 401(k), contribute enough to get the full match first. This is essentially free money.

2. **Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: Contribute to your 401(k) up to the limit, especially if you’re in a high tax bracket, as contributions reduce your taxable income.
   - **IRA**: Consider contributing to a traditional IRA for tax-deferred growth or a Roth IRA for tax-free growth in retirement, depending on your income eligibility.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: Once you’ve maximized contributions to tax-advantaged accounts, you can invest in a taxable account. This can provide more flexibility for withdrawals and investment choices.

4. **Diversification**: Diversify your investments across these accounts to balance risk and return, considering asset allocation strategies based on your age and risk tolerance.

5. **Withdrawal Strategy**: Plan for how you will withdraw from these accounts in retirement. Typically, it’s beneficial to withdraw from taxable accounts first to allow tax-advantaged accounts to continue growing.

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: Tailor your strategy to your specific financial situation by consulting with a financial planner.

Monitor your allocations annually to ensure they align with your goals and market conditions.
