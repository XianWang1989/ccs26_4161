To effectively distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get any employer match, as it’s essentially free money.

2. **Prioritize Tax Advantages**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are pre-tax, reducing your taxable income. Focus on this first to benefit from any match.
   - **IRA**: Depending on whether it’s traditional or Roth, consider your current tax bracket. A Roth IRA allows tax-free growth and withdrawals, which can be beneficial if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement.
   
3. **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for investment flexibility. Consider placing investments that generate capital gains and qualified dividends, as they are taxed at lower rates than ordinary income.

4. **Diversification**: Ensure a mix of asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) across all accounts, adapting your allocation based on your risk tolerance and time horizon.

5. **Rebalance**: Regularly review and adjust your allocations to maintain your desired risk level and investment goals.

6. **Consult a Professional**: Consider speaking with a financial advisor to tailor a strategy that fits your specific financial situation and goals.

By following these strategies, you can optimize your investments for growth, minimize taxes, and prepare for retirement effectively.
