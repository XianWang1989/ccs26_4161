Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment horizon. Here’s a general approach:

### GPT:
1. **401(k)**:
   - Maximize employer match: Contribute enough to get any employer match, as it’s free money.
   - Traditional vs. Roth: Consider your current vs. future tax rate. If you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement, a Roth may be more beneficial.

2. **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**:
   - Contribute to an IRA for additional tax-advantaged growth.
   - Again, consider your tax bracket. Traditional IRAs provide tax deductions now, while Roth IRAs offer tax-free withdrawals in retirement.

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - Use taxable accounts for flexibility.
   - Invest in tax-efficient investments like index funds or ETFs to minimize capital gains taxes.
