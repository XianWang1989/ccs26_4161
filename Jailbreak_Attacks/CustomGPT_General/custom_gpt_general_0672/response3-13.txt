Investing is a complex process that depends on various personal factors, including your risk tolerance, financial goals, age, and the specific tax implications of each account type. Here are some strategies for distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts:

1. **401(k) Accounts**: 
   - If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to get the full match before moving money anywhere else. This is essentially "free money."
   - Consider maxing out your contributions if you're in a higher tax bracket to take advantage of tax deferral.

2. **IRA Accounts**:
   - Roth IRAs are beneficial if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket during retirement. Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, but withdrawals in retirement are tax-free.
   - Traditional IRAs can reduce your current taxable income, making them beneficial if you're in a lower tax bracket now.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: 
   - These are best for funds you may need access to before retirement age.
   - Consider investing in tax-efficient funds or strategies, as you will be subject to capital gains taxes on earnings and withdrawals.

4. **General Recommendations**:
   - Maintain a balanced asset allocation that matches your risk tolerance (e.g., stocks vs. bonds).
   - Regularly rebalance your portfolio to align with your investment strategy.
   - Diversify within each account type to reduce risk.

Ultimately, consulting with a financial advisor remains a good idea to tailor your investment strategy to your unique circumstances.
