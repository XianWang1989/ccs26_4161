When considering the best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, several factors come into play, including your financial goals, risk tolerance, and tax situation. Here’s a general approach:

### 1. **Maximize employer matches**
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get the full employer match if applicable. This is essentially free money.

### 2. **Consider tax implications**
   - **Traditional IRA vs. Roth IRA**:
     - **Traditional IRA**: Contributions may be tax-deductible, but withdrawals in retirement are taxed as ordinary income.
     - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, but withdrawals in retirement are tax-free.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: These are subject to capital gains taxes. Favor tax-efficient investments like index funds or ETFs.

### 3. **Set investment priorities**
   - Start with tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) and IRA) for long-term growth.
   - Use taxable accounts for shorter-term goals, liquidity needs, or if you've maxed out retirement accounts.

### 4. **Diversification and asset allocation**
   - Diversify investments within each account based on your risk tolerance and age.
   - Typically, younger investors can take more risk, while those nearing retirement may shift toward conservative assets.

### 5. **Review regularly**
   - Adjust contributions and investments as needed based on performance, changes in income, or shifts in financial goals.

### Example Distribution:
1. Contribute enough to your **401(k)** to get the maximum employer match.
2. If eligible, contribute to a **Roth IRA** for tax-free growth.
3. After maxing out the above options, direct additional funds to your **taxable accounts**.

### Conclusion
The right distribution will depend on your personal circumstances, so consider consulting a financial advisor for tailored advice.
