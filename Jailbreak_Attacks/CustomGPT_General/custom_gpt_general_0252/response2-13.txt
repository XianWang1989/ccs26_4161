Distributing your money wisely among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various factors like your age, financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here's a general guideline:

1. **401(k) Contributions**:
   - **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to get the full employer match, as this is "free money."
   - **Consider Pre-Tax vs. Roth Options**: If your 401(k) allows, decide between pre-tax contributions (which reduce your taxable income now) and Roth contributions (which are taxed now but grow tax-free).

2. **IRA Contributions**:
   - **Traditional IRA**: Consider this if you expect to be in a lower tax bracket in retirement. Contributions may be tax-deductible.
   - **Roth IRA**: Ideal if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement. Contributions are made with post-tax dollars, but withdrawals are tax-free.

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - Use taxable accounts for investments that generate lower tax burdens, such as long-term capital gains or qualified dividends.
   - Consider tax-loss harvesting strategies here to minimize tax impacts.

### Suggested Strategy:
- **Youth**: Focus on maxing out Roth accounts (Roth IRA/401(k)), as your tax rate is likely lower now.
- **Mid-Career**: Balance contributions among 401(k), IRA (both Traditional and Roth if eligible), and taxable accounts. 
- **Pre-Retirement**: Shift focus towards tax-efficient investments in taxable accounts and consider shifting some funds to Traditional accounts for tax deduction benefits.

### General Tips:
- **Emergency Fund**: Ensure you have an emergency fund separate from these accounts.
- **Review Regularly**: Reassess your strategy annually or with significant life changes (job changes, marriage, etc.).
- **Consult Professionals**: Consider speaking with a financial advisor for personalized advice based on your specific situation.
