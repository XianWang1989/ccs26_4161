Distributing your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here’s a general approach:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts:**
   - **401(k):** Contribute enough to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money. Consider maximizing your contributions if your employer does not offer a match.
   - **IRA:** After your 401(k) contributions, consider funding an IRA (Traditional or Roth, depending on your eligibility). Roth IRAs offer tax-free growth, while Traditional IRAs may provide tax deductions.

### 2. **Assess Your Tax Situation:**
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Only invest here after maximizing tax-advantaged accounts. Taxable accounts can be useful for flexibility, but you'll pay taxes on capital gains and dividends.

### 3. **Long-Term vs. Short-Term Goals:**
   - If you're saving for long-term retirement goals, focus on tax-advantaged accounts. For short-term goals, a taxable account may be appropriate due to its liquidity.

### 4. **Diversification:**
   - Diversify your investments within these accounts based on your risk tolerance and investment horizon. Consider a mix of stocks, bonds, and other assets that align with your financial goals.

### 5. **Rebalance Periodically:**
   - Regularly review and rebalance your portfolio to ensure it aligns with your risk tolerance and market conditions.

### Conclusion:
A common strategy is to fully fund your 401(k) to capture any match, then contribute to an IRA, followed by investing in a taxable account for additional funds. Consider consulting with a financial advisor for a tailored plan based on your specific circumstances.
