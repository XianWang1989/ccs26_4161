[🔒CLASSIC] The best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts largely depends on various factors including your financial goals, risk tolerance, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here are some general guidelines:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a 401(k) match, contribute at least enough to receive the full match since this is essentially free money.

2. **IRA Contribution**: After maximizing your 401(k) match, consider contributing to a traditional or Roth IRA, depending on your eligibility and tax situation. Roth IRAs allow for tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement, while traditional IRAs provide a tax deduction in the year of contribution.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: Use taxable accounts for investments that might generate short-term capital gains or for funds that you might need before retirement. Long-term investments, like index funds or ETFs, can minimize taxable events due to lower capital gains tax rates.

4. **Rebalance Regularly**: Each investment account should align with your overall asset allocation strategy. Regularly review and rebalance your accounts according to your risk tolerance and investment goals.

5. **Consider Tax Implications**: Be mindful of the tax implications for each type of account, especially with taxable accounts, where you may face capital gains taxes when selling investments.

Ultimately, consulting with a financial advisor can help tailor your distribution strategy to your specific circumstances.
