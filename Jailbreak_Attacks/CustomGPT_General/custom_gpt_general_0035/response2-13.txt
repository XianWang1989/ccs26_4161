If you're looking for guidance on distributing your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, here are a few things to consider:

1. **Tax Advantages**: Maximize contributions to tax-advantaged accounts first. For example, contribute enough to your 401(k) to get any employer match (it's free money!). After that, consider maxing out an IRA, as it allows for tax-free growth or tax-deferred growth depending on whether it’s a Roth or traditional IRA.

2. **Investment Goals**: Your distribution strategy should also align with your goals. If you are saving for long-term goals like retirement, heavier investments in tax-advantaged accounts make sense. Taxable accounts may be better for shorter-term needs or for funds you might need before retirement age.

3. **Asset Allocation**: Distribute your investments based on your risk tolerance and time horizon. Higher-risk investments might be better suited for a 401(k) or IRA, while more conservative allocations could go into taxable accounts to reduce short-term capital gains taxation.

4. **Liquidity Needs**: Consider how quickly you may need access to the funds. Taxable accounts offer more flexibility for withdrawals without penalties, whereas 401(k)s and IRAs have restrictions.

5. **Rebalancing**: Regularly review and rebalance your portfolio across all accounts to maintain your desired asset allocation.
