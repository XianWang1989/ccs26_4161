Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts involves considering several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here’s a framework you might find useful:

### 1. **Understand Contribution Limits and Tax Implications**
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are pre-tax, reducing your taxable income for the year. For 2023, the limit is $22,500 (or $30,000 if you're over 50).
   - **IRA**: Contributions can be pre-tax (Traditional IRA) or post-tax (Roth IRA). For 2023, the limit is $6,500 (or $7,500 if you're over 50).
   - **Taxable Accounts**: No contribution limits, but you’ll pay capital gains tax on profits when you sell investments.

### 2. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**
   - Prioritize contributions to your 401(k) if your employer offers a match. This is effectively "free money."
   - Consider maxing out your IRA next, especially if you can do so in a Roth format for tax-free growth.

### 3. **Evaluate Your Time Horizon**
   - Longer time horizons (10+ years): Favor tax-advantaged accounts (like IRAs and 401(k)s) for compounding benefits.
   - Shorter time horizons: Allocate more funds to taxable accounts for flexibility and access.

### 4. **Consider Your Current and Future Tax Bracket**
   - If you anticipate being in a higher tax bracket during retirement, a Roth IRA may be beneficial. 
   - If you expect a lower tax rate, traditional IRAs or 401(k)s might be more advantageous.

### 5. **Diversify Investments Across Accounts**
   - **401(k)**: Typically offers a range of mutual funds and target-date funds.
   - **IRA**: Can be more flexible with individual stocks, ETFs, and various other investment vehicles.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Consider tax-efficient investments like index funds or ETFs due to lower capital gains taxes.

### 6. **Review and Adjust Regularly**
   - Periodically assess your asset allocation and adjust contributions based on changing goals and market conditions.

### Example Distribution Strategy:
- **401(k)**: Contribute up to the match, then evaluate maxing contributions.
- **IRA**: Fully fund either a Traditional or Roth IRA based on tax implications.
- **Taxable**: Invest in a diversified portfolio, keeping in mind potential capital gains implications.

Consulting a financial advisor can also help tailor this strategy to your specific situation and ensure compliance with regulations.
