Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here are some guidelines for effective distribution:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match:**
   - Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to get any employer match. This is essentially "free money."

### 2. **Consider Tax Implications:**
   - **401(k):** Contributions are typically pre-tax, reducing your taxable income. However, withdrawals in retirement will be taxed as ordinary income.
   - **IRA (Traditional or Roth):**
     - **Traditional IRA:** Like a 401(k), it would lower your taxable income now, with taxes paid on withdrawals later.
     - **Roth IRA:** Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, but qualified withdrawals are tax-free. This is beneficial if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Earnings are taxed annually on dividends and interest, and any capital gains are taxed when sold.

### 3. **Emergency Fund First:**
   - Ensure you have adequate emergency savings (3-6 months of living expenses) before aggressively investing.

### 4. **Investment Goals and Time Horizon:**
   - If you’re younger and won’t need the money for years, consider a more aggressive allocation (e.g., stocks).
   - If you’re closer to retirement, you might want to shift toward more conservative investments (e.g., bonds).

### 5. **Diversification:**
   - Diversify across asset classes (stocks, bonds, real estate) to manage risk.

### 6. **Annual Review:**
   - Regularly review your accounts to adjust your contributions and allocations based on performance and changing goals.

### Suggested Distribution Strategy:

- **401(k):** Contribute enough to get the match, then consider investing up to the annual limit.
- **IRA:** Max out your IRA contributions next, prioritizing Roth if you can, or traditional if you expect lower taxes in retirement.
- **Taxable Accounts:** Use these for additional savings once your tax-advantaged accounts are maximized.

### Example Allocation:

- **401(k):** 15-20% of your income until the match is met.
- **IRA:** Up to the maximum limit allowed.
- **Taxable Accounts:** Any remaining savings after maxing tax-advantaged accounts.

Always consider consulting with a financial advisor to tailor these strategies to your specific situation.
