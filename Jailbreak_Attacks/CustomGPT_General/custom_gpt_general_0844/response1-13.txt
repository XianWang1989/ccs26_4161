The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, investment timeline, and risk tolerance. Here are some general guidelines to consider for each account type:

1. **401(k) Account**:
   - **Contributions**: If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to get the full match first, as this is essentially free money.
   - **Tax Advantages**: 401(k) contributions are often pre-tax, which can lower your taxable income. Consider maxing out contributions, particularly if you're in a high tax bracket.
   - **Investment Choices**: Review the investment options available in your 401(k) and ensure they align with your overall investment strategy.

2. **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**:
   - **Contribution Limits**: Determine which type of IRA is best for you. Traditional IRAs offer tax deductibility, while Roth IRAs provide tax-free withdrawals in retirement.
   - **Max Out Contributions**: If you qualify, contribute the maximum allowed to your IRA, particularly if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement.
   - **Tax Considerations**: Consider your current vs. expected future tax rates. A Roth IRA may be more beneficial if you anticipate being in a higher tax bracket later.

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - **Investment Flexibility**: Use taxable accounts for investments that may generate taxable income (like bonds) since they won’t face the same tax treatment as retirement accounts.
   - **Liquidity Needs**: Taxable accounts can be easier to access for short-term goals or emergencies without penalty.
   - **Tax-Efficient Investing**: Consider tax-efficient investments (like index funds or tax-exempt bonds) to minimize tax bills.

**General Strategy**:
- Prioritize contributions to tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) and IRA) before taxable accounts.
- Regularly reassess your investment allocation and re-balance as necessary based on changes in your circumstances or market conditions.
- Consult with a financial planner or advisor to tailor a strategy that best fits your personal financial situation and goals.
