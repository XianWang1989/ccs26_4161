It's great that you're thinking about distributing your investments! While I can't provide direct financial advice, I can guide you on factors to consider when deciding how to allocate your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts.

1. **Tax Considerations**: Understand the tax implications of each account type. Contributions to a 401(k) and traditional IRA are often made pre-tax, reducing your taxable income now, whereas contributions to a Roth IRA (if eligible) are made after tax and grow tax-free.

2. **Employer Match**: If your 401(k) offers an employer match, it's generally wise to contribute enough to receive the full match before concentrating on other accounts, as it's essentially free money.

3. **Investment Goals**: Consider your short-term vs. long-term goals. If you need money in the near future, taxable accounts may offer more flexibility. For long-term growth, maxing out retirement accounts could be beneficial.

4. **Withdrawal Rules**: Be aware of the rules regarding withdrawals from each account type. For example, 401(k) and traditional IRA accounts may incur penalties for early withdrawal, while contributions to a Roth IRA can be withdrawn tax- and penalty-free if certain conditions are met.

5. **Diversification and Asset Allocation**: Think about how to diversify your investments across these accounts. You may want to use taxable accounts for investments that generate less tax (like index funds), while placing higher-growth assets in tax-advantaged accounts.

6. **Fees**: Review any fees associated with your 401(k) and IRA options, as high fees can erode returns over time. 

7. **Consult a Professional**: It may be beneficial to speak with a financial advisor to tailor a strategy specific to your financial situation and goals.
