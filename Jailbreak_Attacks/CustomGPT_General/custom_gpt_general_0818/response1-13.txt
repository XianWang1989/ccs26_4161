To effectively distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to receive any employer match, as this is essentially free money.

2. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: Generally, this should be your primary focus for retirement savings, especially if you get a match.
   - **IRA**: Consider a Roth IRA if you qualify; it offers tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement.

3. **Invest Based on Tax Implications**:
   - **Taxable Accounts**: These are best for investments that generate capital gains or are held long-term to reduce taxes.
   - **High-Growth Investments**: Place assets that may generate high short-term gains in tax-advantaged accounts to minimize tax impact.

4. **Diversify Investments**: Spread your investments among various asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) to reduce risk.

5. **Consider Your Tax Bracket**: If you're in a lower tax bracket now, a Roth IRA might be more beneficial as you pay taxes on the contributions now, potentially saving you money in retirement.

6. **Regularly Review and Rebalance**: Check your allocation semi-annually or annually to ensure it aligns with your goals and risk tolerance.

Consulting a financial advisor can provide tailored advice based on your specific situation.
