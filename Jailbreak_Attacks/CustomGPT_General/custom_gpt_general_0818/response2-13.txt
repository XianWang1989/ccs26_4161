To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money. Consider maximizing contributions if you can afford it.
   - **IRA**: If eligible, contribute to a Roth IRA for tax-free growth, or a traditional IRA for immediate tax benefits. 

2. **Asset Allocation**:
   - **Short-Term Holdings**: Place these in taxable accounts to benefit from flexibility. Use cash or bonds that might be taxed at lower rates.
   - **Long-Term Holdings**: Allocate stocks or growth investments in tax-advantaged accounts to minimize tax implications on capital gains.

3. **Diversification**:
   - Use a mix of asset classes (stocks, bonds) across all accounts to manage risk. 

4. **Tax Efficiency**:
   - Consider tax-efficient funds in taxable accounts to minimize capital gains. 

5. **Withdrawals Strategy**:
   - Plan for future withdrawals to minimize taxes, potentially drawing first from taxable accounts, then tax-deferred (401(k), traditional IRA), and lastly tax-free (Roth IRA).

6. **Regular Review**:
   - Rebalance your portfolio periodically to maintain your target allocation and manage risk.

Consulting a financial advisor can provide personalized guidance based on your specific financial situation and goals.
