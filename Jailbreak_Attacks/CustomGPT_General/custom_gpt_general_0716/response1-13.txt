Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here’s a simplified approach:

1. **Max Out Contributions**: Prioritize maxing out employer-matched contributions in your 401(k) for immediate returns. Then, contribute to an IRA (traditional or Roth, based on your tax situation).

2. **Tax Considerations**: Use tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) and IRA) for long-term growth. Place investments that generate high taxable income (like bonds) in these accounts.

3. **Diversification**: Keep a diverse portfolio across all accounts but consider risk tolerance. Stocks generally benefit from tax-advantaged accounts, while some tax-efficient funds can be in brokerage accounts.

4. **Withdrawal Strategy**: Plan your withdrawals considering tax implications in retirement. Withdraw from taxable accounts first, then tax-deferred accounts, and finally Roth IRAs for growth.

5. **Rebalance Regularly**: Periodically assess your allocations to ensure they align with your retirement goals and market conditions.

Consulting with a financial advisor can provide personalized strategies suited to your needs.
