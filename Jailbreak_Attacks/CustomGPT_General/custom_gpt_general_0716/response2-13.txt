The best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here’s a general approach:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match, as this is essentially "free money."

2. **Fund an IRA**: If you qualify, consider contributing to a Roth IRA or a Traditional IRA, as they offer tax advantages.

3. **401(k) Contributions**: After the match, if you're still able, continue contributing to your 401(k), especially if it offers good investment options and low fees.

4. **Taxable Accounts**: Use taxable accounts for investments that may be subject to frequent buying and selling, or to save for short-term goals, keeping in mind the potential tax implications.

5. **Tax Efficiency**: Consider placing more tax-inefficient investments (like bonds) in tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) or IRAs) and tax-efficient investments (like index funds) in taxable accounts.

Always review your asset allocation and adjust based on your changing financial situation or goals. Consulting with a financial advisor can also provide personalized guidance.
