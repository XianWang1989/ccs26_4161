To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider these factors:

### 1. **Tax Treatment of Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are pre-tax, reducing taxable income now but taxed upon withdrawal.
   - **IRA**: Traditional IRAs are similar to 401(k)s; Roth IRAs use after-tax dollars, allowing tax-free withdrawals in retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Investments are subject to capital gains tax upon selling.

### 2. **Retirement Goals**:
   - **Short-Term Needs**: Prioritize taxable accounts for liquidity.
   - **Long-Term Growth**: Use tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) and IRA) for maximum growth.

### 3. **Contribution Limits**:
   - Max out employer match in your 401(k) before contributing to other accounts.
   - Understand annual contribution limits for IRAs and 401(k)s.

### 4. **Investment Strategy**:
   - Consider higher-risk investments in accounts with the most favorable tax treatments (e.g., stocks in Roth IRAs).
   - Use fixed income in taxable accounts to manage tax implications.

### 5. **Diversification**:
   - Ensure your overall portfolio is diversified across asset classes, regardless of the account type.

### 6. **Withdrawal Strategy**:
   - Plan for tax-efficient withdrawals in retirement (e.g., consider which accounts to tap first).

### Example Distribution Strategy:
- **Max 401(k)** to take full advantage of employer match.
- **Contribute to an IRA** (preferably Roth if eligible) to maximize tax benefits.
- Use **taxable accounts** for any additional investments focusing on short-term needs or accessible funds.

### Regular Review:
- Periodically reassess your distribution and investment strategy based on changing financial circumstances and market conditions.

### Conclusion:
Balanced contributions and strategic withdrawals can optimize your investments across all account types, maximizing returns and minimizing taxes over time. Consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized advice based on your specific financial situation.
