To effectively distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider these key strategies:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**:
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money. Then, consider maxing out contributions if your budget allows.
   - **IRA**: If eligible, consider contributing to a Traditional IRA or Roth IRA based on your current and expected future tax rate. Traditional IRAs provide tax-deferred growth, while Roth IRAs offer tax-free withdrawals in retirement.

2. **Diversify Investments**:
   - Ensure that your investment choices are diversified across asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) within each account for risk management.

3. **Understand Withdrawal Rules**:
   - Taxable accounts offer more flexibility for withdrawals without penalties, especially if you might need access to funds before retirement.

4. **Consider Tax Implications**:
   - Investments generating capital gains and dividends may be more suitable for tax-advantaged accounts, while investments with lower tax burdens can reside in taxable accounts.

5. **Reevaluate Regularly**:
   - Review your allocations annually or after significant life changes to adjust for performance, changes in income, or retirement goals.

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**:
   - A professional can provide personalized advice tailored to your specific financial situation, goals, and risk tolerance.

By prioritizing contributions to tax-advantaged accounts and carefully allocating your investments, you can optimize your savings for retirement.
