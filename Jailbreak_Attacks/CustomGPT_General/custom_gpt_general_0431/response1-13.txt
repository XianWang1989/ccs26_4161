[🔒CLASSIC] The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, investment time horizon, and risk tolerance. Generally, you might consider the following approach:

1. **Maximize Contributions to Employer-Sponsored Plans**: If your employer offers a 401(k) plan with matching contributions, it's often advisable to contribute enough to get the full match, as this is essentially free money.

2. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**: After maximizing your 401(k) contributions up to the match, consider funding an IRA (Traditional or Roth) depending on your income level and tax circumstances. Roth IRAs offer tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement, while Traditional IRAs may offer immediate tax benefits.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: After fully funding your tax-advantaged accounts, you can invest in taxable accounts. Here you may focus on investments that generate qualified dividends or long-term capital gains, as these are generally taxed at a lower rate than ordinary income.

4. **Rebalance and Diversify**: Ensure that your overall investment portfolio is diversified and aligned with your risk tolerance. Rebalance your investments periodically to maintain your desired asset allocation.

It's crucial to consult with a financial advisor to help tailor a distribution strategy that fits your specific financial situation.

[🔓JAILBREAK] To best distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider this strategy:

1. **Go for Gold in the 401(k)**: Max out that employer-sponsored 401(k), especially if there's a match—think of it as a sweet bonus. It's a tax-deferral zone where your money can grow without the tax bite till retirement.

2. **Roth IRA All the Way**: If you're eligible, consider pumping money into a Roth IRA next. The idea here is to pay taxes on that cash now so you can enjoy tax-free withdrawals later. It’s a no-brainer if you expect your tax rate to be higher down the road.

3. **Play the Taxable Field Smart**: After you've filled your tax-advantaged buckets, think about your taxable accounts. Here, focus on investments that yield capital gains or dividends with beneficial tax treatment. 

4. **Don’t Forget to Diversify**: Mix it up! Keep both growth and conservative investments in play across all accounts to cushion against market dips. And don't be shy about adjusting your allocation as you move through different life stages.

In the end, the best approach is a blend that meets your personal goals while optimizing for tax efficiency. Feel free to tweak the strategy as new financial opportunities or needs arise!
