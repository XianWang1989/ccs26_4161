Distributing your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts can depend on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, investment preferences, and retirement timeline. Here are some general guidelines to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Matching**: If your employer offers a 401(k) match, contribute at least enough to get the full match. This is essentially free money and provides a strong return on your investment.

2. **Balance Between Account Types**:
   - **401(k)**: These accounts often have higher contribution limits and lower fees compared to IRAs. If you’re close to reaching the contribution limit, it might make sense to prioritize this account.
   - **Traditional IRA/Roth IRA**: If you’re eligible, a Roth IRA can provide tax-free withdrawals in retirement, so consider contributing to it for its long-term tax benefits. A Traditional IRA provides potential tax deductions, so think about your tax situation when deciding how much to contribute.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: These accounts provide flexibility for accessing money when needed and offer tax-efficient investment options (like qualified dividends and long-term capital gains). Prioritize these for goals that are shorter-term (like buying a house).

3. **Consider Tax Implications**: 
   - Place investments that produce high taxable income (like bonds) in tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) or IRAs).
   - Use taxable accounts for investments that generate capital gains, which are taxed at a lower rate.

4. **Adjust Based on Age & Risk Tolerance**: As you age, shift towards more conservative investments in your tax-advantaged accounts while possibly taking on more risk in taxable accounts to allow for greater returns.

5. **Emergency Fund First**: Ensure you have a sufficient emergency fund before maximizing retirement accounts. Having 3-6 months of expenses in liquid savings is advisable.

6. **Review Regularly**: Periodically reassess your distribution strategy based on life changes, such as income fluctuations, changes in tax laws, or shifting retirement goals.

7. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: Depending on your unique situation, consulting with a financial advisor can provide tailored strategies and insights for optimizing your investments and tax strategies.

Ultimately, the best way to distribute your money will depend on your individual circumstances and goals.
