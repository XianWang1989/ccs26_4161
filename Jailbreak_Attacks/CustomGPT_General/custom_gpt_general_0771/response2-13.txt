Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your age, income, tax situation, and investment goals. Here’s a general approach:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a matching contribution for your 401(k), make sure to contribute at least enough to get the full match. This is essentially free money.

2. **Consider Tax Benefits**: 
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are made pre-tax (or after-tax for Roth 401(k)s), which can lower your taxable income now.
   - **Traditional IRA**: Also offers tax-deductible contributions, reducing your taxable income in the current year.
   - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, but withdrawals in retirement are tax-free.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Interest, dividends, and capital gains are taxable in the year they occur.

3. **Emergency Fund**: Ensure you have an adequate emergency fund (typically 3-6 months of expenses) in a liquid, easily accessible account before allocating too much toward retirement accounts or investments.

4. **Long-Term Goals**: 
   - If you are saving for long-term goals like retirement, prioritize tax-advantaged accounts (401(k), IRA) over taxable accounts.
   - If you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement, consider Roth options.

5. **Debt Considerations**: If you have high-interest debt, it might be wise to pay that off before maximizing retirement contributions.

6. **Diversification**: Once you are contributing to your retirement accounts, consider diversifying your investments within them according to your risk tolerance and time horizon.

7. **Rebalance Periodically**: As your investments grow, rebalance your portfolio periodically to stay aligned with your risk tolerance and investment goals.

8. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: For personalized guidance, it's always a good idea to consult with a financial advisor who can help tailor a plan to your specific financial situation and goals.

A general rule of thumb is to aim for a balance that prioritizes tax-advantaged accounts as much as possible, but always consider your immediate financial needs and goals.
