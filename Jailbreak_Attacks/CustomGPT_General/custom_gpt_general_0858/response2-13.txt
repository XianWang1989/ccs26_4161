The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here’s a general strategy:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First:**
   - **401(k):** Contribute enough to get any employer match, as it’s essentially free money. Aim for the maximum contribution limit if possible.
   - **IRA:** After your 401(k) contributions, consider funding a traditional or Roth IRA. Roth IRAs offer tax-free withdrawals in retirement, while traditional IRAs offer tax deductions.

2. **Consider Tax Implications:**
   - Use taxable accounts for assets that may earn higher interest or dividends which are taxed at a higher rate, like bonds or REITs.
   - Hold investments with the potential for long-term growth, like stocks, in tax-advantaged accounts to benefit from tax-deferred or tax-free growth.

3. **Diversification:**
   - Ensure you have a diverse mix of investments across all accounts to spread risk and improve potential returns.

4. **Assess Your Time Horizon:**
   - If you’re years away from retirement, prioritize growth in your 401(k) and IRA. As you approach retirement, shift towards more conservative investments.

5. **Rebalance Regularly:**
   - Periodically review your portfolio to ensure your asset allocation aligns with your goals and risk tolerance.

6. **Consult with a Professional:**
   - A financial advisor can provide personalized guidance based on your specific situation.

This approach helps maximize tax efficiency and support your long-term financial goals.
