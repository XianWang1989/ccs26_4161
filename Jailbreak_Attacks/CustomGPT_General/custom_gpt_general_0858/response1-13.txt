Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, risk tolerance, and tax situation. Here are some guidelines:

### 1. **Consider Tax Implications:**
   - **401(k) and IRA:** Contributions are often tax-deferred. Evaluate your tax bracket now and in retirement. If you expect to be in a lower bracket, prioritizing these accounts is wise.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Investments here are subject to capital gains taxes. Consider keeping investments with high growth and low turnover in these accounts to minimize tax liabilities.

### 2. **Maximize Employer Matches:**
   - If your 401(k) offers matching contributions, aim to at least contribute enough to get the full match before directing additional funds elsewhere.

### 3. **Diversify Investments:**
   - Use your 401(k) and IRA for retirement-based investments (bonds, stocks, mutual funds) while using taxable accounts for more flexible, short-term investments.

### 4. **Consider Contribution Limits:**
   - Max out contributions to tax-advantaged accounts first (e.g., 401(k) and IRA) before putting funds into taxable accounts. Stay updated on contribution limits for each account type.

### 5. **Emergency Fund:**
   - Prioritize having an adequate emergency fund (3-6 months of expenses) in a liquid account before investing heavily in retirement accounts.

### 6. **Investment Horizon:**
   - If you’re younger and investing for the long-term, focus on growth-oriented investments in tax-deferred accounts. As you age, shift towards income-oriented investments.

### 7. **Review Regularly:**
   - Re-assess your strategy periodically based on changes in income, expenses, market conditions, and retirement goals.

### Example Allocation:
- **401(k):** 20-30%
- **IRA:** 10-20%
- **Taxable Accounts:** 10-30%
- **Emergency Fund:** 10-15%
- **Flexible allocation based on individual needs**

### Consult a Financial Advisor:
- Personalized advice based on your unique situation can be beneficial.

Consider your long-term objectives and stay informed about financial tools and strategies!
