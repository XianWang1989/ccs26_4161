The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment horizon. Here are some key considerations for each type of account:

### 1. **401(k) Accounts**
- **Employer Match**: Contribute enough to get any employer match, as this is essentially "free" money.
- **Tax Benefits**: Contributions reduce your taxable income. Consider maxing this out if you’re in a higher tax bracket.

### 2. **IRA Accounts**
- **Types**: Decide between a Traditional IRA (tax-deductible contributions) and a Roth IRA (tax-free withdrawals). A Roth IRA can be beneficial if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement.
- **Contribution Limits**: Be aware of annual contribution limits and the eligibility criteria based on income.

### 3. **Taxable Accounts**
- **Flexibility**: These accounts offer more liquidity and withdrawal flexibility but don't have the same tax advantages.
- **Tax Efficiency**: Consider investing in tax-efficient funds or strategies (like holding long-term investments) to minimize capital gains taxes.

### General Strategy:
- **Diversification**: Maintain a diversified portfolio across all accounts to manage risk effectively.
- **Asset Allocation**: Place tax-inefficient assets (like bonds or REITs) in tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) or IRA) and keep more tax-efficient assets (like stocks) in taxable accounts.
- **Regular Review**: Periodically reassess your distribution based on changes in income, tax laws, and life circumstances.

### Example Allocation:
- Max out 401(k) contributions, especially up to the match.
- Contribute to an IRA for additional tax advantages.
- Use any remaining funds for a taxable account, focusing on long-term growth investments.

### Conclusion:
It’s often beneficial to consult with a financial advisor to tailor this strategy to your specific situation and goals.
