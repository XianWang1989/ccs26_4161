Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts involves considering several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment time horizon. Here’s a general approach:

### 1. **Assess Goals and Time Horizon**
   - **Retirement**: Prioritize tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) and IRA).
   - **Short-term needs**: Consider keeping more in taxable accounts for liquidity.

### 2. **Maximize Contributions to Tax-Advantaged Accounts**
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match (free money). Consider maxing this out if you can afford it.
   - **IRA**: Contribute to a Traditional or Roth IRA depending on your income and tax situation. Maximize contributions if possible.

### 3. **Consider Tax Implications**
   - **401(k) and Traditional IRA**: Pre-tax contributions reduce taxable income now, but withdrawals in retirement will be taxed.
   - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are taxed now, but withdrawals in retirement are tax-free.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Consider capital gains tax on investments; holding investments long-term can minimize taxes.

### 4. **Use Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**
   - Invest in taxable accounts for short-term goals or to maintain liquidity.
   - Use tax-efficient investments (e.g., index funds or ETFs) to minimize capital gains.

### 5. **Diversify Investments Across Accounts**
   - Maintain a balanced allocation across all accounts (stocks, bonds, etc.) based on your risk tolerance.
   - Consider placing more tax-efficient investments (like index funds) in taxable accounts and holding less tax-efficient options (like REITs) in tax-advantaged accounts.

### 6. **Review and Adjust Regularly**
   - Periodically review your asset allocation and contributions based on changes in income, expenses, or financial goals.

### Example Allocation Strategy
- **401(k)**: 30-50% of total investments (maximize match and contributions).
- **IRA**: 20-30% (if eligible, split between Traditional and Roth based on tax strategy).
- **Taxable**: 20-50% (for flexibility and short-term needs).

### Conclusion
This strategy can vary based on individual circumstances, so consult with a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your situation.
