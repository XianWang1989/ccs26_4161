Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here’s a general strategy:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**:
   - Contribute enough to get the full employer match, as this is essentially "free money."

2. **Contribute to IRAs**:
   - If eligible, consider contributing to a Traditional or Roth IRA. Roth IRAs can be advantageous if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement, while Traditional IRAs may provide tax deductions now.

3. **Continue Contributing to 401(k)**:
   - After maximizing the match, continue contributing to your 401(k) until you reach the annual limit if your employer’s plan offers low fees and good investment options.

4. **Use Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**:
   - Invest in taxable accounts for money you might need before retirement age. Focus on tax-efficient investments, such as index funds or ETFs.

5. **Consider Tax Implications**:
   - Be mindful of the tax implications of each account type. Taxable accounts are subject to capital gains tax, while contributions to 401(k)s and IRAs often provide tax deferral.

6. **Diversify Investments**:
   - Ensure a diversified asset allocation aligned with your risk tolerance across all accounts.

7. **Review Regularly**:
   - Reassess your distribution strategy periodically, especially after major life changes (e.g., marriage, job change).

By balancing these factors, you can optimize your investment strategy for both growth and tax efficiency. Consult a financial advisor for personalized advice based on your specific situation.
