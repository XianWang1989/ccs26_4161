The best way to distribute your money amongst your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some general strategies to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**: If your employer offers a matching contribution, contribute at least enough to get the full match. This is essentially free money and provides an immediate return on your investment.

2. **Contribute to Your IRA**: Depending on your income and tax situation, consider maximizing your contributions to a Traditional IRA or Roth IRA. A Traditional IRA may offer immediate tax deductions, while a Roth IRA allows for tax-free withdrawals in retirement.

3. **Consider Tax Implications**: Taxable accounts can provide more flexibility, but they don't offer the same tax advantages as retirement accounts. Keep investments that generate taxable income (like bonds) in tax-advantaged accounts, while placing long-term investments (like stocks) in taxable accounts to take advantage of lower capital gains tax rates.

4. **Diversification Across Accounts**: Diversify your investments across all accounts to balance risk. For example, you might have more aggressive investments in your 401(k) and IRA, while keeping some safer investments in your taxable account.

5. **Monitor and Adjust Based on Life Changes**: As your financial situation, tax laws, and retirement goals change, be prepared to adjust your contributions. Regularly review and rebalance your portfolio to maintain alignment with your risk tolerance and investment objectives.

It's advisable to consult with a financial advisor to tailor these strategies to your specific situation and goals for optimal results.
