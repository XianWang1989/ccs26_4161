The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors including your financial goals, tax situation, investment strategy, and time horizon. Here are some general guidelines:

1. **401(k) Accounts**:
   - **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a matching contribution, aim to contribute at least enough to get the full match, as this is essentially free money.
   - **Tax-Deferred Growth**: 401(k) contributions are typically made pre-tax, which reduces your taxable income in the year you contribute. This can be beneficial if you expect to be in a lower tax bracket during retirement.

2. **IRA Accounts (Traditional and Roth)**:
   - **Tax Diversification**: Depending on your eligibility, consider contributing to a Roth IRA if you anticipate being in a higher tax bracket in retirement. Contributions are made after-tax, but you can withdraw them tax-free in retirement.
   - **Contribution Limits**: Be mindful of the annual contribution limits for IRAs, which can change annually. Consider funding a Traditional or Roth IRA based on your tax situation and retirement goals.

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - **Flexibility**: Unlike tax-advantaged accounts, investments in taxable accounts can be accessed without penalties or restrictions (though you may need to pay capital gains taxes). This can make them suitable for shorter-term goals or emergency funds.
   - **Tax-Efficient Investing**: Consider strategies like holding tax-efficient investments (e.g., index funds or ETFs) in taxable accounts to minimize tax liability.

**Overall Strategy**:
- **Balance**: Prioritize contributions to accounts with tax advantages first (401(k) and IRA) before allocating excess funds to taxable accounts.
- **Diversification**: Ensure your investment strategy is diversified across all accounts to optimize performance and reduce risk.
- **Rebalance as Necessary**: Periodically review and adjust your allocations to stay aligned with your financial goals, risk tolerance, and market conditions.

It may also be helpful to consult with a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your specific situation.
