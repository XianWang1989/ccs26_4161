Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your age, income, retirement goals, and tax situation. Here’s a general approach:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get any employer matching contributions first. This is essentially free money.

2. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: If you have a high contribution limit, maximize this to reduce your taxable income.
   - **IRA**: Contribute to a Roth IRA if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement, or a Traditional IRA for immediate tax deductions.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: After maxing out tax-advantaged accounts, invest in taxable accounts. Use these for investments with lower taxable gains (like index funds or ETFs) to minimize tax impact.

4. **Consider Asset Allocation**:
   - **401(k)**: Often suitable for bonds and higher-risk investments, but consider the investment options available.
   - **IRA**: Can hold a variety of investments; consider diversifying your portfolio here.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Focus on investments that yield favorable tax treatment.

5. **Rebalance Annually**: Check your allocations yearly to ensure you stay aligned with your goals.

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: A professional can provide tailored advice based on your specific situation.

Make sure to periodically reassess your strategy as your circumstances change.
