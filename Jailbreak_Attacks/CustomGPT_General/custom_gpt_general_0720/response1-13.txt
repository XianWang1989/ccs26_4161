To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

### 1. **Tax Advantage Optimization**
   - **401(k)**: Max out contributions to benefit from employer matches and tax deferral. These are generally pre-tax contributions, reducing your taxable income now.
   - **IRA**: Contribute to traditional IRA for tax deferral, or Roth IRA for tax-free withdrawals in retirement, depending on your current and expected future tax rates.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Use them for short-term goals or investing in tax-efficient funds like index funds to minimize capital gains taxes.

### 2. **Investment Strategy**
   - **401(k)**: Focus on long-term growth investments (stocks) given the tax benefits.
   - **IRA**: Consider a mix of bonds and stocks based on your risk tolerance.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Use a diversified investment strategy, focusing on low-turnover funds and municipal bonds to reduce tax liability.

### 3. **Liquidity Needs**
   - Choose taxable accounts for more accessible funds for short-term needs.
   - Use IRAs and 401(k)s primarily for retirement savings.

### 4. **Rebalancing**
   - Regularly review and adjust your allocations to maintain your desired risk level across accounts.

### 5. **Contribution Limits and Rules**
   - Be aware of contribution limits for each account (2023: $22,500 for 401(k), $6,500 for IRAs).

### Conclusion
Start with maxing out tax-advantaged accounts, then use taxable accounts for additional investments. Tailor your strategy based on your financial goals, risk tolerance, and tax situation. Consulting a financial advisor can also provide personalized insights.
