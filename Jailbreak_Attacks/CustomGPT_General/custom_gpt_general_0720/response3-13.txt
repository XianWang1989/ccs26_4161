Distributing money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment strategy. Here’s a general approach:

### 1. **Prioritize Contributions**
   - **401(k)**: If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to get the full match first. This is essentially “free money.”
   - **IRA**: After maxing out the 401(k) match, consider contributing to an IRA (Roth or traditional, depending on your income and tax situation).
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for additional savings once you've maxed out tax-advantaged accounts.

### 2. **Consider Tax Implications**
   - **Traditional 401(k) and IRA**: Contributions may be tax-deductible; withdrawals are taxed as income.
   - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, but withdrawals (if qualified) are tax-free.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Pay capital gains taxes on earnings, but you have more flexibility in accessing funds.

### 3. **Investment Strategies**
   - **Aggressive Growth**: Consider stocks in taxable accounts for long-term growth, as they can benefit from lower long-term capital gains rates.
   - **Bonds**: Keep more interest-bearing assets in tax-advantaged accounts to defer taxes.

### 4. **Rebalance Periodically**
   - Regularly assess your portfolio across all accounts to maintain your desired asset allocation.

### 5. **Consult a Financial Advisor**
   - A professional can provide tailored advice considering your specific situation.

### General Distribution Example:
- **401(k)**: 15% of salary (enough to get a match)
- **IRA**: Max out to $6,500 ($7,500 if over age 50)
- **Taxable Accounts**: Any additional savings after maxing out above.

### Conclusion
Customizing this strategy based on your situation will yield the best results.
