When it comes to distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, it's essential to consider several factors, including your financial goals, risk tolerance, tax implications, and time horizon. Here's a strategy to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**: If your employer offers a match on your 401(k) contributions, aim to contribute enough to get the full match. This is essentially free money for your retirement.

2. **Contribute to an IRA**: If you're eligible, contribute to a traditional or Roth IRA. These accounts offer tax advantages that can help your investments grow. The choice between traditional and Roth depends on your current and expected future tax rates.

3. **Increase 401(k) Contributions**: After maximizing your IRA contributions, consider increasing your 401(k) contributions. The contribution limit is typically higher than that of an IRA, allowing for more savings.

4. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**: Once you've maximized contributions to tax-advantaged accounts, consider investing in taxable accounts. These can provide more flexibility for accessing funds before retirement and can be beneficial for long-term investments.

5. **Asset Allocation**: Within each account, maintain a diversified asset allocation that aligns with your risk tolerance and investment goals. Generally, it's advisable to hold more volatile investments in tax-advantaged accounts to minimize tax implications on gains.

6. **Regularly Review and Adjust**: Periodically review your investment strategy and asset allocation, making adjustments as needed based on changes in your financial situation or market conditions.

7. **Seek Professional Guidance**: If you're uncertain, consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your financial situation.

By following these steps, you can create a balanced approach to your investments that maximizes growth while considering tax implications and your unique financial circumstances.
