When deciding how to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies based on your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a 401(k) match, contribute enough to get the full match. This is essentially free money.

2. **Prioritize Debt Repayment**: If you have high-interest debt, you may want to focus on paying that down before investing more aggressively.

3. **Contribute to Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: Contribute to your 401(k) first, taking advantage of any employer match.
   - **IRA**: If you're eligible, consider contributing to a Roth or Traditional IRA for additional tax benefits. Roth IRAs offer tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement, while Traditional IRAs provide tax-deductible contributions.

4. **Diversify Contributions**: 
   - Allocate a percentage of your overall contribution to each account type based on your current and expected tax rates. For example, if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement, prioritize the Roth IRA.
   - Consider the time horizon for each account. Longer-held investments can withstand market fluctuations, favoring stocks in IRAs for growth.

5. **Utilize Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**: After maxing out tax-advantaged retirement accounts, consider putting money into taxable accounts for flexibility, especially if you think you might need access to funds before retirement age.

6. **Annual Re-assessment**: Regularly review your investment allocations and overall financial situation, adjusting contributions as needed based on changes in your income, expenses, tax laws, and retirement goals.

7. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: Consider seeking personalized advice from a financial advisor, especially if your financial situation is complex or if you're nearing retirement.
