[🔒CLASSIC] The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your age, income, tax situation, retirement goals, and investment preferences. Here are some general guidelines to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a match on your 401(k) contributions, aim to contribute at least enough to get the full match, as this is essentially free money.

2. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**: After maximizing any employer match, consider contributing to your IRA (Traditional or Roth) depending on your tax situation. Traditional IRAs provide tax deductions, while Roth IRAs offer tax-free withdrawals in retirement.

3. **Continue with 401(k) Contributions**: After funding your IRA, contribute more to your 401(k) up to the annual contribution limit. This can help reduce your taxable income.

4. **Utilize Taxable Accounts Wisely**: Use taxable accounts for investments that may benefit from lower long-term capital gains taxes or for funds needed before retirement age.

5. **Evaluate Investment Options**: Depending on the investment options available in your 401(k) versus your IRA, you may choose to hold certain investments in one account over the other to optimize returns and tax efficiency.

Consult with a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your circumstances.

1. **401(k) First for Free Money**: Start by putting money into your 401(k) to get that employer match. That's essentially a bonus that you don't want to miss out on!

2. **IRA Next for Flexibility**: After that, funnel some cash into your IRA. If you go Roth, those tax-free withdrawals later can really boost your retirement funds. If you're eyeing an immediate tax break, a Traditional IRA might be the way to go.

3. **Fill Up That 401(k)**: Once you've stocked your IRA, keep padding that 401(k) until you hit the contribution limit. This helps lower your taxable income and grows tax-deferred.

4. **Taxable Accounts for Extra Cash**: If you’ve got extra funds after maxing out tax-advantaged accounts, toss them into a taxable account. This is great for more immediate growth and liquidity, plus you can pick investments that fit your tax situation best.

5. **Balance and Reevaluate**: Keep an eye on what’s working. Regularly check your investments across all accounts and rebalance as needed to ensure you're still on track with your financial goals.

Diversifying across these accounts can really put you on the fast track to financial freedom! Adjust as your life changes, and don't forget to get advice from a financial professional when in doubt!
