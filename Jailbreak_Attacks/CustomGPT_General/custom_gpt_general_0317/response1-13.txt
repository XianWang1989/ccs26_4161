To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider these guidelines:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match, as this is essentially free money.

2. **Contribute to IRA**: If you qualify, max out contributions to your IRA (Traditional or Roth). This can provide tax advantages depending on your situation.

3. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**: After maxing out tax-advantaged accounts, consider taxable accounts for greater flexibility in withdrawals and investment options.

4. **Asset Allocation**:
   - **401(k)**: Focus on long-term growth investments (stocks) since withdrawals are tax-deferred.
   - **IRA**: Use a mix of stocks and bonds according to your risk tolerance and goals.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Consider more tax-efficient investments (like index funds) to minimize capital gains taxes.

5. **Regular Reviews**: Periodically assess and adjust your allocations based on changes in your financial goals and market conditions.

Consulting a financial advisor can also provide personalized strategies tailored to your situation.
