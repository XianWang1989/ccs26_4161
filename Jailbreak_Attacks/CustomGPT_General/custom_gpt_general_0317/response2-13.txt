Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeframe. Here are some general strategies:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Contributions**
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match. This is essentially free money.

### 2. **Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**
   - **IRA**: After maximizing the 401(k) match, consider contributing to an IRA (Traditional or Roth, depending on your income and tax situation).
   - **Roth IRAs**: Great for tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement.

### 3. **Assess Your Tax Situation**
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for investments that are more tax-efficient (like index funds) or for short-term goals, since you'll pay capital gains tax on profits.

### 4. **Long-Term Growth Focus**
   - Prioritize accounts with tax benefits for long-term investments, such as your 401(k) and IRAs.

### 5. **Diversification**
   - Ensure your investment choices are diversified across all accounts to manage risk effectively.

### 6. **Rebalance Periodically**
   - Regularly review and adjust your allocations to stay aligned with your financial goals.

### 7. **Consider Withdrawal Strategies**
   - Plan for how and when you’ll withdraw funds in retirement from these accounts to minimize tax implications.

### Example Allocation:
- **401(k)**: 15-20% of your income (max if possible)
- **IRA**: Up to the annual contribution limit
- **Taxable Accounts**: Any remaining investment funds after maxing tax-advantaged accounts

### Conclusion
Consult with a financial advisor to tailor this strategy to your specific circumstances for optimal results.
