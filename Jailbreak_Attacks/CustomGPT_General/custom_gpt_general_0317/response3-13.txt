Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment horizon. Here’s a general approach:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts:**
   - **401(k):** Contribute enough to get any employer match first. Aim for the maximum annual limit ($22,500 for 2023, or $30,000 if over 50).
   - **IRA (Traditional or Roth):** Max out contributions next (limit is $6,500 for 2023, or $7,500 if over 50).

### 2. **Consider Tax Implications:**
   - **401(k) and Traditional IRA:** Contributions are tax-deductible, but withdrawals are taxed as income. Suitable for higher-income earners expecting to be in a lower tax bracket in retirement.
   - **Roth IRA:** Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, but withdrawals are tax-free. Best for those expecting to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Useful for funds you might need before retirement age or for specific goals (e.g., buying a home). Capital gains and dividends are taxed, so consider tax-efficient investments (like index funds).

### 3. **Diversification:**
   - Diversify across different asset classes (stocks, bonds, real estate) depending on your risk tolerance. Keep more aggressive investments in tax-advantaged accounts to delay taxes on growth.

### 4. **Regular Rebalancing:**
   - Periodically review your portfolio to ensure it aligns with your financial goals and risk tolerance.

### 5. **Emergency Fund:**
   - Ensure you have an adequate emergency fund before fully investing. Typically, aim for 3-6 months’ worth of expenses in a liquid account.

### Example Strategy:
- **401(k):** Max out contributions for employer match first.
- **IRA:** Fund either a Roth or Traditional IRA based on your tax situation.
- **Taxable Account:** Allocate any excess money here after maxing out tax-advantaged accounts, focusing on low-turnover, tax-efficient investments.

### Conclusion:
Balance contributions based on your current tax bracket, retirement timeline, and individual financial goals for optimal growth and tax efficiency. Consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized advice.
