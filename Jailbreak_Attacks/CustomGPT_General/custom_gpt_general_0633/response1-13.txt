[🔒CLASSIC] The best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your retirement goals, tax situation, and investment strategy. Here are some general guidelines to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a matching contribution to your 401(k), aim to contribute enough to take full advantage of the match, as this is essentially free money for your retirement.

2. **Contribute to Your IRA**: After maximizing the match in your 401(k), consider contributing to a traditional or Roth IRA. A Roth IRA offers tax-free growth and tax-free withdrawals in retirement if certain conditions are met, while a traditional IRA may offer tax deductions depending on your income level and whether you have access to a workplace retirement plan.

3. **Prioritize Tax Efficiency**: Generally, it’s advisable to hold more tax-efficient investments in taxable accounts (like index funds and ETFs) and more tax-inefficient investments (like bond funds) in tax-advantaged accounts (like your 401(k) and IRA).

4. **Consider Your Goals and Risk Tolerance**: Depending on your time horizon and risk tolerance, you may want to adjust the asset allocation within these accounts. Ensure you have a mix of equities and fixed-income investments that align with your overall financial goals.

5. **Review and Adjust Regularly**: Financial situations can change, so it’s important to review your allocations periodically and adjust based on changes in your income, goals, and market conditions.
