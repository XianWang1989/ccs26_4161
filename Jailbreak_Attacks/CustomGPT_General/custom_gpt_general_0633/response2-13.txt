Distributing your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts can enhance your overall investment strategy and optimize your tax situation. Here's a general approach you can consider:

1. **401(k) Contributions**:
   - **Employer Match**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to maximize any employer matching contributions. This is essentially “free money” and should be a priority.
   - **Tax Advantages**: Contributions to a traditional 401(k) are made pre-tax, reducing your taxable income for the year. If you anticipate higher taxes in retirement, a Roth 401(k) might be advantageous since withdrawals are tax-free.

2. **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**:
   - **Eligibility**: Depending on your income, you may be eligible for a traditional IRA with potential tax deductions or a Roth IRA for tax-free withdrawals.
   - **Contribution Limits**: Be aware of the annual contribution limits. As of 2023, it’s $6,500 ($7,500 if you’re aged 50 or older) for IRAs. Maximize these if possible, especially if you qualify for a Roth IRA.

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - **Flexibility**: Taxable accounts offer more flexibility for withdrawals and do not have the same restrictions as retirement accounts.
   - **Tax Considerations**: Be mindful of capital gains taxes when selling investments in taxable accounts. Holding investments for over a year to qualify for long-term capital gains rates can be beneficial.

### Suggested Strategy:
- **Start by maximizing your 401(k)** contributions up to the employer match.
- **Next, contribute to your IRA** to take advantage of tax advantages, switching between traditional and Roth based on your current tax situation and retirement strategy.
- **Finally, use taxable accounts** to hold investments you may want to access before retirement or as part of a broader investment strategy, including bonds and funds that have favorable tax treatment.

### Additional Tips:
- **Diversification**: Ensure your asset allocation reflects your risk tolerance across all accounts.
- **Regular Review**: Periodically review your contributions and adjust based on changes in income, tax laws, and financial goals.
- **Consult a Financial Advisor**: For personalized advice based on your financial situation and goals, consider consulting with a financial planner.

This strategy can help balance the benefits of tax-advantaged accounts while maintaining liquidity and investment diversity through taxable accounts.
