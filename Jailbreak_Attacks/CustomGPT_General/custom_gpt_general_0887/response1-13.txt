To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following steps:

1. **Understand Contribution Limits**:
   - **401(k)**: Check annual contribution limits (e.g., $22,500 for 2023).
   - **IRA**: Annual limit is $6,500 (or $7,500 if you're over 50).
   - **Taxable Accounts**: No limits, but consider your overall investment strategy.

2. **Tax Efficiency**:
   - **401(k) and IRA**: Use these for tax-deferred growth; prioritize higher-growth investments here.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Hold investments with lower tax impacts, like index funds or tax-efficient funds. Avoid high-dividend stocks to minimize taxes on ordinary income.

3. **Employer Match**:
   - Max out your employer match in your 401(k) first; it’s essentially free money.

4. **Diversification**:
   - Maintain a balanced portfolio across all accounts based on your risk tolerance and investment goals. Diversify with stocks, bonds, and other assets that suit your strategy.

5. **Withdrawal Strategy**:
   - Plan for tax implications when withdrawing. Generally, it's more tax-efficient to withdraw from taxable accounts first, then tax-deferred (401(k) and IRA), to reduce your tax burden in retirement.

6. **Regular Review**:
   - Reassess your allocations periodically based on market conditions, life changes, or shifts in financial goals.

7. **Consult a Financial Advisor**:
   - For personalized advice, consider speaking with a financial advisor to tailor a distribution strategy specific to your financial situation.

Following these guidelines can help you optimize your investment distribution for growth and tax efficiency.
