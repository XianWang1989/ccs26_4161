Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on factors like your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here's a general strategy:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match, as this is essentially free money.

2. **IRA Contributions**: If you qualify, consider maxing out your IRA (either traditional or Roth). A Roth IRA provides tax-free withdrawals in retirement, which can be advantageous.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: Use taxable accounts for more flexible investing. This is useful for short-term goals (less than five years) or if you exceed contribution limits for retirement accounts.

4. **Emergency Fund**: Ensure you have a sufficient emergency fund (3-6 months of expenses) before heavily investing in these accounts.

5. **Asset Allocation**: Diversify your investments based on your risk tolerance. Consider stocks for growth and bonds for stability across all accounts, but tailor it to tax implications.

6. **Tax Efficiency**: Keep high-growth investments in tax-advantaged accounts to minimize taxes. Place bonds or other interest-generating assets in taxable accounts to benefit from lower capital gains rates.

7. **Regular Review**: Reassess your allocation regularly to adjust for life changes and market conditions.

Consulting a financial advisor can provide personalized guidance based on your specific situation.
