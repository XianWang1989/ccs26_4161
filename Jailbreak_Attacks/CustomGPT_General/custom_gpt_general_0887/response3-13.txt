To effectively distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategy:

### 1. **Maximize Contributions Based on Tax Advantages:**
   - **401(k):** Contribute enough to get any employer match if available. This is essentially free money.
   - **IRA:** Consider contributing to a Roth IRA if eligible (for tax-free growth), or a traditional IRA for potential tax deductions.

### 2. **Prioritize Accounts by Tax Efficiency:**
   - **Tax-Deferred Accounts (401(k) and Traditional IRA):** These accounts grow tax-deferred. Invest long-term growth assets here.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Use for assets like bonds or dividend-paying stocks, as they are taxed annually on interest and dividends.

### 3. **Balance Asset Allocation:** 
   - Diversify investments across account types to align with your risk tolerance while keeping in mind that you can choose more aggressive investments in tax-advantaged accounts.

### 4. **Withdrawal Strategy:**
   - In retirement, consider withdrawing from taxable accounts first for lower tax implications, then tax-deferred accounts, and lastly tax-free accounts like Roth IRAs.

### 5. **Regular Review and Rebalance:**
   - Periodically review your asset allocation and adjust as needed to stay aligned with your financial goals.

### Example Distribution:
- **401(k):** 15-20% of your income if maxing out employer match.
- **IRA:** Contribute the maximum allowable amount annually.
- **Taxable Accounts:** Allocate savings after retirement accounts, focusing on short-term needs or specific goals.

### Conclusion:
Tailor your plan based on your age, retirement goals, and risk tolerance while maximizing tax benefits. Consulting a financial advisor can provide personalized guidance.
