Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment horizon. Here's a general guideline to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a 401(k) match, consider contributing enough to take full advantage of that match before putting money into other accounts.

2. **Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**: Prioritize contributions to tax-advantaged accounts like a 401(k) and IRA. These accounts provide tax benefits either at contribution or withdrawal.

3. **401(k)**: Aim to contribute at least enough to get the full employer match. After that, consider how much you want to contribute based on your overall retirement goals and tax bracket.

4. **IRA**: If eligible, consider contributing to a Traditional IRA or a Roth IRA, depending on whether you want tax deductions now or tax-free withdrawals later. The contribution limits for IRAs may be lower than those for 401(k)s, so maximize these as much as possible.

5. **Taxable Accounts**: Once you’ve maximized contributions to your 401(k) and IRA, consider investing in taxable accounts. These accounts offer flexibility but come with capital gains taxes on earnings.

6. **Diversify Investments**: Regardless of the account type, ensure your investments are diversified to manage risk effectively.

7. **Rebalance Regularly**: Periodically review and rebalance your accounts to ensure alignment with your long-term goals and risk tolerance.

Consulting with a financial advisor can also provide personalized strategies based on your specific situation.
