Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and risk tolerance. Here’s a general approach to consider:

### 1. **Maximize Contributions**
   - **401(k)**: If your employer offers matching contributions, aim to contribute at least enough to get the full match. This is essentially free money.
   - **IRA**: Consider maxing out your IRA contributions after your 401(k) contributions. Choose between a Traditional IRA (tax-deductible contributions) or Roth IRA (tax-free withdrawals) based on your income and tax situation.

### 2. **Asset Allocation**
   - **Retirement Accounts (401(k) and IRA)**: 
     - These accounts should focus on long-term growth. Consider a diversified mix of stocks and bonds according to your risk tolerance.
     - Younger investors might lean more towards equities for growth potential.
   
   - **Taxable Accounts**: 
     - Use these for short- to medium-term goals. Focus on investments with favorable tax treatment, such as index funds or ETFs, which typically generate fewer taxable events.
     - Be mindful of capital gains tax when selling.

### 3. **Tax Efficiency**
   - Hold tax-inefficient investments (like bonds) in tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) and IRA) to minimize taxable income.
   - Keep tax-efficient investments (like stock index funds) in taxable accounts to reduce tax liability.

### 4. **Emergency Fund**
   - Ensure you have a separate emergency fund (usually 3-6 months’ worth of expenses) to avoid withdrawing from your investment accounts prematurely.

### 5. **Regular Review and Rebalance**
   - Periodically review your allocations and rebalance as necessary based on market performance and personal financial changes.

### 6. **Consult a Professional**
   - Consider working with a financial advisor to tailor a plan that fits your unique situation.

### Conclusion
The optimal distribution varies based on individual circumstances, so it's crucial to adapt these guidelines to your specific financial situation and goals.
