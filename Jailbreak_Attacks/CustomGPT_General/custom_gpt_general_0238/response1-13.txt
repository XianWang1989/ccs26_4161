The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment horizon. Here are some general guidelines:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts:**
   - **401(k):** Consider maximizing employer match contributions first. This is essentially free money.
   - **IRA:** Contribute to a Traditional or Roth IRA depending on your income and tax situation. Roth IRAs provide tax-free withdrawals in retirement.

### 2. **Consider Tax Implications:**
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Use these for investments that generate qualified dividends or long-term capital gains, which are taxed at lower rates.
   - Be mindful of interest-bearing investments in taxable accounts, as they can lead to a higher tax burden.

### 3. **Diversify Investments:**
   - Keep higher-risk investments in tax-advantaged accounts to take advantage of tax-free growth (especially for Roth IRAs).
   - Use taxable accounts for more stable or conservative investments that generate income.

### 4. **Liquidity Needs:**
   - Consider maintaining some liquid assets in taxable accounts for short-term needs or emergencies.

### 5. **Long-Term Strategy:**
   - Revisit your allocations periodically. Adjust based on changes in income, tax laws, and financial goals.

### Example Allocation:
- **Max out 401(k)** up to the match.
- **Contribute to IRA** (either type based on eligibility).
- **Invest remaining funds** in taxable accounts.

### Summary:
Balance contributions across accounts with a focus on tax efficiency, long-term growth, and your personal financial situation. Consulting a financial advisor may provide additional tailored advice.
