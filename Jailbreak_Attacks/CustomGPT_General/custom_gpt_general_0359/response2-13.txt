The best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your income, tax situation, retirement goals, and risk tolerance. Here are some general strategies:

1. **Maximize Employer Match:** If your employer offers a match on your 401(k) contributions, contribute enough to get the full match first—this is essentially free money.

2. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts:**
   - **401(k):** Consider maxing out your contributions here if you have not reached the employer match limit. Contributions are tax-deferred.
   - **Traditional IRA:** If you're eligible, this account allows for tax-deductible contributions. It’s useful if you expect to be in a lower tax bracket in retirement.
   - **Roth IRA:** Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, allowing for tax-free withdrawals in retirement. It’s beneficial if you anticipate being in a higher tax bracket later.

3. **Evaluate Taxable Accounts Last:** Use these for investments that may generate taxable income, like stocks or mutual funds if you need liquidity or you’ve maxed out tax-advantaged accounts.

4. **Consider Diversification:** Diversify your investments within each account type based on your risk tolerance and investment goals.

5. **Regularly Review and Adjust:** Your circumstances and market conditions change, so reassess your strategy annually.

Consulting with a financial advisor can provide personalized guidance based on your unique situation.
