The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your current financial situation, retirement goals, tax bracket, and investment time horizon. Here are some general guidelines to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a matching contribution to your 401(k), consider contributing enough to get the full match before investing in other accounts. This is essentially "free money."

2. **IRA Contributions**: If you're eligible, contribute to a Roth IRA or traditional IRA. A Roth IRA allows for tax-free withdrawals in retirement, while a traditional IRA provides tax deductions now. Consider your current vs. expected future tax rate when choosing.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: After maximizing contributions to tax-advantaged accounts, consider putting additional funds into taxable accounts. These accounts offer more flexibility in terms of withdrawals and investment choices, though you'll pay capital gains taxes on earnings.

4. **Investment Strategy**: Take into account your risk tolerance and investment goals. Keep in mind that lower-risk investments may be better suited for accounts that you'll use later, while higher-risk investments could perform better over time.

5. **Regular Review**: Periodically review and adjust your allocations based on changes in your financial situation, market conditions, and retirement goals.

It might also be helpful to consult with a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your unique circumstances.
