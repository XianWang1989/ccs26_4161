Determining the best way to distribute your money amongst your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment strategy. Generally, here are some guidelines:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**: Before funding taxable accounts, consider maximizing contributions to your 401(k) and IRA. These accounts provide tax benefits that can significantly enhance your investment growth over time. 

2. **401(k) Contributions**: If your employer offers a 401(k) match, contribute at least enough to get the full match, as this is essentially "free money." After that, evaluate how much further you can contribute based on your budget and retirement goals.

3. **IRA Contributions**: If you qualify, consider contributing to a traditional or Roth IRA. A traditional IRA offers tax-deductible contributions (depending on your income), while a Roth IRA allows for tax-free withdrawals in retirement.

4. **Taxable Accounts**: Once you’re maximized on contributions to your retirement accounts, you can start investing in taxable accounts. These accounts provide flexibility as you can access the funds without penalties, though you'll be responsible for capital gains taxes on any profits.

5. **Investment Strategy**: Consider your investment horizon and risk tolerance. Allocate your investments across various assets (stocks, bonds, real estate, etc.) based on your financial goals and when you plan to access the funds.

6. **Rebalancing**: Regularly review and adjust your allocations as needed based on performance and changes in your financial situation.

It’s also wise to consult with a financial advisor, especially to understand the implications of your tax situation and retirement goals better. They can provide personalized guidance based on your unique circumstances.
