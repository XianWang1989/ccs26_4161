The best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some general considerations:

1. **401(k) Plan**: 
   - **Employer Match**: Always contribute enough to your 401(k) to take full advantage of any employer match, as it's essentially free money.
   - **Tax Benefits**: Contributions to a traditional 401(k) are made pre-tax, lowering your taxable income for the year, which can be beneficial if you're currently in a higher tax bracket.

2. **IRA (Individual Retirement Account)**:
   - **Roth vs. Traditional**: Decide between a Roth IRA (tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement) and a traditional IRA (tax-deductible contributions but taxed on withdrawal). A Roth may be preferable if you anticipate being in a higher tax bracket in retirement.
   - **Contribution Limits**: Be aware of annual contribution limits for IRAs, which may influence how much you can allocate to this account.

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - **Flexibility**: Taxable accounts offer more flexibility for withdrawals without penalties, making them suitable for short-term goals or emergencies.
   - **Tax Efficiency**: Consider holding investments that generate capital gains, such as stocks, in taxable accounts to benefit from lower long-term capital gains tax rates.

### Strategy:
- **Maximize Contributions**: Start by maximizing your 401(k) contributions up to the match, then consider fully funding an IRA if you're eligible, before shifting any additional savings into taxable accounts.
- **Balance**: Keep in mind your investment timeline; allocate more towards retirement accounts if you have a long time horizon, and towards taxable accounts if you need liquidity.
- **Evaluate Annually**: Revisit and adjust your allocation yearly based on changes in income, expenses, and market performance.

### Example Allocation:
- **401(k)**: 15% of income to capture the full employer match
- **IRA**: Max out contributions if eligible
- **Taxable Accounts**: Any additional savings after contributing to retirement accounts

Consulting with a financial advisor can also provide personalized guidance tailored to your specific situation.
