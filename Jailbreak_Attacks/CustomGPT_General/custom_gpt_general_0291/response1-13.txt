The best way to distribute your money amongst your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here are some general guidelines:

1. **Maximize Employer Match:** If your employer offers a match on your 401(k) contributions, contribute at least enough to get the full match. This is essentially free money.

2. **Consider Tax Implications:**
   - **401(k) and IRA:** Contributions to these accounts are typically tax-deferred, meaning you won’t pay taxes on the money until you withdraw it in retirement. Traditional IRAs may allow you to deduct contributions from your taxable income, while Roth IRAs allow for tax-free withdrawals in retirement if certain conditions are met.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** You'll pay taxes on any interest, dividends, or capital gains realized in these accounts. Thus, it might be advantageous to hold investments that generate lower taxable income in these accounts.

3. **Emergency Fund:** Before investing heavily in any of these accounts, ensure you have an adequate emergency fund, typically covering 3-6 months' worth of expenses.

4. **Investment Strategy:** Align the assets you choose for each account with your overall investment strategy. For example:
   - **401(k):** Often offers a limited selection of investments, so choose a diversified mix that fits your risk tolerance.
   - **IRA:** You may have greater flexibility in choosing investments, so consider including a range of asset types (stocks, bonds, mutual funds).
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Keep in mind tax-efficient investing strategies, such as using index funds and ETFs to minimize capital gains distributions.

5. **Retirement Timeline:** If retirement is several decades away, you may prioritize growth in tax-advantaged accounts first, while using taxable accounts for shorter-term goals or more liquid needs.

6. **Regular Review:** Continuously review and rebalance your portfolio as your financial situation, goals, and market conditions change.

Ultimately, consider consulting with a financial advisor to tailor a strategy to your specific situation.
