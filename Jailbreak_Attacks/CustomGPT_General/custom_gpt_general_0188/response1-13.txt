Distributing your money across a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts effectively depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeframe. Here are some general strategies:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money. Consider maxing out contributions if possible, especially if your tax bracket is higher.
   - **IRA**: If eligible, contribute to a Traditional IRA or Roth IRA, depending on your income and tax situation. Roth IRAs offer tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement.

### 2. **Consider Your Tax Situation**
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for funds that you may need access to before retirement or for investments that generate qualified dividends or long-term capital gains, which are taxed at lower rates.

### 3. **Diversify Investments**
   - **401(k) and IRA**: Focus on a diversified mix of stocks, bonds, and other assets according to your risk tolerance and investment horizon. Consider target-date funds or index funds.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Consider holding more tax-efficient investments (like index funds) to minimize tax impact.

### 4. **Liquidity Needs**
   - Ensure you have sufficient funds in taxable accounts for short-term needs or emergencies while maximizing long-term growth in retirement accounts.

### 5. **Regular Balancing and Review**
   - Regularly assess and rebalance your investments across all accounts to align with your financial goals and market conditions.

### 6. **Seek Professional Advice**
   - If you’re uncertain about the best strategy, consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized recommendations.

By following these guidelines, you can effectively allocate your money to optimize growth and minimize taxes over time.
