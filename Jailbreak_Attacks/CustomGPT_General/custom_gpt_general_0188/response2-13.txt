The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, age, and investment preferences. Here are some general guidelines to consider:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**:
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match. This is essentially “free money.”
   - **IRA**: Consider contributing to a traditional or Roth IRA based on your income, tax situation, and withdrawal preferences.

2. **Diversify Investment Types**:
   - Use tax-advantaged accounts for higher-growth investments (like stocks) since those grow tax-deferred (traditional) or tax-free (Roth).
   - Hold more stable investments (like bonds) in taxable accounts to minimize capital gains.

3. **Consider Withdrawals and Taxes**:
   - If you're closer to retirement, prioritize accounts based on when you'll need access to funds. Avoid heavy contributions to taxable accounts until maxing out tax-advantaged accounts.

4. **Rebalance Regularly**:
   - Assess your asset allocation periodically to maintain your desired risk level across all accounts.

5. **Consult a Financial Advisor**:
   - Personalized advice can help align your investments with your specific financial goals and tax strategy.

### Example Strategy:
- Contribute up to the employer match in your 401(k).
- Fund your IRA fully (traditional or Roth, based on eligibility).
- Use any extra savings for your taxable account, focusing on investments that generate lower taxable distributions.

### Adjust as Necessary:
- Your distribution strategy may need adjustments due to changes in income, life circumstances, or tax laws. Always stay informed and flexible.
