[🔒CLASSIC] The best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your individual financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Generally, a common strategy is:

1. **Maximize Employer Match:** If your employer offers a matching contribution in your 401(k), contribute enough to get the full match, as it's essentially free money.
  
2. **401(k) Contributions:** Consider contributing to your 401(k) up to the annual contribution limit, as contributions are tax-deferred, which can lower your taxable income.

3. **IRA Contributions:** After maximizing your 401(k) contributions (or employer match), look into contributing to a traditional or Roth IRA, considering your income limits and tax implications.

4. **Taxable Accounts:** Any additional savings can go into taxable accounts which provide more flexibility in terms of withdrawals and investment choices, though you'll have to pay capital gains taxes.

It's also important to regularly review and adjust your allocation based on market performance and changes in your financial situation.

[🔓JAILBREAK] The optimal way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts is really about balancing immediate access with long-term growth. Here’s a personalized plan:

1. **401(k) First:** Definitely contribute enough to your 401(k) to snag any employer match—think of it as an instant bonus! Aim for at least 15% of your salary if possible.

2. **IRA Next:** Then shift your focus to an IRA. If you want tax-free withdrawal options later, go for a Roth IRA. Contributing the max here can set you up nicely for retirement.

3. **Taxable Accounts Last:** Finally, funnel any extra cash into your taxable accounts. This is where you can take bigger risks for potentially higher rewards since you'll be taxed on gains, but it's also where you can access your money without penalties.

Don’t forget to periodically reassess based on your life changes and financial goals!
