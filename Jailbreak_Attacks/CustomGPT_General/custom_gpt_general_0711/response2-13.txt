The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment strategies. Here are some general guidelines to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match (401(k))**: If your employer offers a matching contribution to your 401(k), prioritize contributing enough to take full advantage of the match. This is essentially free money.

2. **Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**: Contribute to tax-advantaged accounts like IRAs (Traditional or Roth) and your 401(k) before placing money in taxable accounts. These accounts can provide tax benefits that may lead to higher long-term returns.

3. **Consider Tax Implications**:
   - **Traditional 401(k) and IRA**: Contributions are tax-deductible, but withdrawals during retirement are taxed as ordinary income. They can be beneficial if you expect to be in a lower tax bracket in retirement.
   - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, but qualified withdrawals are tax-free. This can be advantageous if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket later.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: While they don’t offer tax advantages, they provide flexibility for withdrawals and investments. Consider placing investments that generate qualified dividends or long-term capital gains here, as they tend to be taxed at lower rates.

4. **Investment Strategy**: Diversify your investments based on risk tolerance. Consider using tax-efficient investments in taxable accounts, such as index funds or ETFs, which typically generate fewer taxable events.

5. **Emergency Fund**: Ensure you have a sufficient emergency fund before heavily investing, as this can prevent the need to withdraw from your retirement accounts prematurely.

6. **Regular Contributions and Rebalancing**: Periodically review and adjust your contributions and asset allocations across accounts to align with your goals and market conditions.

7. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: A financial advisor can provide personalized advice based on your unique situation, helping you to create a tailored investment strategy.

By considering these guidelines, you can create a well-balanced and tax-efficient investment strategy across your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts.
