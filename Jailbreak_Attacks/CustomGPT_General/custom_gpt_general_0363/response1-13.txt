Distributing your money across a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your goals, tax situation, and investment preferences. Here are some strategies to consider:

### 1. **Understand Each Account Type:**
   - **401(k):** Typically offers tax-deferred growth, and many employers match contributions. Contributions reduce your taxable income.
   - **IRA (Traditional or Roth):** Traditional IRAs offer tax-deferred growth, while Roth IRAs provide tax-free withdrawals in retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Offer flexibility but are subject to capital gains taxes and taxes on dividends.

### 2. **Prioritize Accounts:**
   - **Max Out Employer Match:** Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money.
   - **Contribute to an IRA:** If you can, contribute to a Roth IRA for tax-free growth and withdrawals, given you qualify.
   - **Maximize 401(k) Contributions:** After maxing out the employer match and IRA, consider increasing your 401(k) contributions.
   - **Use Taxable Accounts for Flexibility:** Invest in taxable accounts for more immediate access to funds, knowing you’ll be taxed on gains.

### 3. **Consider Tax Implications:**
   - **Tax Bracket Considerations:** Invest more heavily in tax-deferred accounts (401(k) and Traditional IRA) if you expect to be in a lower tax bracket in retirement.
   - **Roth Conversions:** Consider making Roth IRA conversions during low-income years to take advantage of lower tax rates.

### 4. **Allocate Investments:**
   - **Diverse Asset Allocation:** Depending on your risk tolerance, allocate a mix of stocks, bonds, and other assets across all accounts. Typically, equities are held in tax-advantaged accounts for longer-term growth, while bonds might be held in taxable accounts due to their tax treatment.
   
### 5. **Rebalance Regularly:**
   - Review and adjust your allocations periodically to ensure they remain in line with your investment goals and market conditions.

### 6. **Seek Professional Advice:**
   - Consider consulting with a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your financial situation and goals.

Ultimately, the allocation should reflect your individual risk tolerance, investment horizon, and retirement objectives.
