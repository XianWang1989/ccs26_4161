Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and time horizon. Here’s a general approach:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your 401(k) offers an employer match, contribute enough to get the full match first. This is essentially free money.

2. **Contribute to an IRA**: If you're eligible, consider maxing out a traditional or Roth IRA. This can provide tax advantages depending on your income and tax filing status.

3. **Continue with 401(k) Contributions**: After maximizing the IRA, continue contributing to your 401(k) up to the maximum limit if your financial situation allows. This helps you benefit from tax-deferred growth.

4. **Taxable Accounts**: If you have additional funds after maxing out your retirement accounts, invest in taxable accounts. These can provide flexibility, as you can withdraw funds without penalty.

5. **Asset Allocation**: Within these accounts, ensure that you have a diversified mix of assets based on your risk tolerance. Consider index funds or ETFs for a broad market exposure.

6. **Rebalance Regularly**: Periodically review and rebalance your portfolio to maintain your desired asset allocation.

7. **Consider Tax Implications**: Be mindful of the tax implications of withdrawals from each account type. Taxable accounts may be subject to capital gains tax, while retirement accounts have different tax treatments based on their type.

### Example Distribution Strategy
- **401(k)**: 15% to reach your employer match, plus additional contributions based on your retirement timeline.
- **IRA**: Fully max out your IRA contributions.
- **Taxable Accounts**: Any remaining funds go here for liquidity and flexibility.

Consult with a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your specific situation.
