Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts effectively depends on your financial goals, risk tolerance, and investment timeline. Here’s a structured approach:

### 1. **Understand Each Account Type**
   - **401(k)**: Often features employer matching, pre-tax contributions (or post-tax if it's a Roth), and has high contribution limits.
   - **IRA**: Traditional IRAs offer tax-deferred growth with potential tax deductions. Roth IRAs provide tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Flexible access to funds without penalties; however, you’ll incur capital gains taxes on profits.

### 2. **Determine Your Financial Goals**
   - **Retirement**: Prioritize the 401(k) and IRA for their tax advantages.
   - **Short-term goals**: Use a taxable account for easier access to funds.
   - **Emergency Fund**: Ensure you have cash reserves before investing.

### 3. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**
   - Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match (maximize this free money).
   - If you can, contribute to an IRA, considering whether a Roth or traditional IRA aligns better with your tax situation.

### 4. **Diversify Investments**
   - **401(k)**: Choose a mix of stocks, bonds, and funds based on your risk tolerance.
   - **IRA**: Similar strategy as the 401(k), with potentially more diverse investment options. 
   - **Taxable Account**: Prioritize tax-efficient investments, like index funds or ETFs, to minimize tax impact.

### 5. **Rebalance Regularly**
   - Review and adjust your portfolio periodically to maintain your desired asset allocation across all accounts.

### 6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**
   - For personalized advice, especially if your financial situation is complex or you’re unsure about tax implications.

### Suggested Distribution Strategy Example:
1. **401(k)**: Contribute enough to receive the full employer match.
2. **IRA**: Contribute the maximum allowable amount.
3. **Taxable Accounts**: Invest remaining funds, focusing on tax-efficient investments.

This framework should help you maximize growth while considering tax efficiency and liquidity.
