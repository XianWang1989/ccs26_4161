Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment horizon. Here are some key considerations to help you determine the best approach:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**
   - **If available**, contribute enough to your 401(k) to obtain any employer match, as this is essentially "free money."

### 2. **Consider Tax Implications**
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are made pre-tax and taxes are paid upon withdrawal.
   - **IRA/Roth IRA**: Traditional IRA contributions may be tax-deductible, but distributions are taxed. Roth IRA contributions are made post-tax, but growth and withdrawals are tax-free in retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Interest, dividends, and capital gains are taxed in the year they are realized.

### 3. **Investment Goals and Time Horizon**
   - **Long-Term Goals (Retirement)**: Focus on tax-advantaged accounts (401(k), IRA) to maximize growth and defer taxes.
   - **Short-Term Goals**: Maintain liquidity in taxable accounts for easier access without penalties or restrictions.

### 4. **Diversification and Asset Allocation**
   - Use tax-advantaged accounts for investments that generate high taxable income (like bonds) to minimize taxes.
   - Consider holding growth-oriented investments (such as stocks) in taxable accounts for favorable capital gains rates.

### 5. **Contribution Limits**
   - Be aware of annual contribution limits for 401(k) and IRAs. Allocate funds to maximize these limits if possible.

### 6. **Regular Review and Rebalancing**
   - Periodically review your asset allocation and make adjustments based on changes in your financial situation, market conditions, or investment goals.

### Sample Distribution Approach:
- **Max out 401(k) contributions** (up to employer match).
- **Contribute to an IRA or Roth IRA** (if income limits allow).
- **Invest remaining funds in taxable accounts** for flexibility.

It's advisable to consult with a financial advisor to tailor a strategy specific to your individual circumstances.
