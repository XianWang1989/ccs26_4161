When distributing money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

### 1. **Maximize Retirement Accounts First**
   - **401(k):** Contribute enough to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money.
   - **Traditional IRA or Roth IRA:** After maximizing the 401(k) match, consider contributing to an IRA. Choose a Traditional IRA if you want tax deductions now or a Roth IRA for tax-free withdrawals in retirement.

### 2. **Tax Considerations**
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Aim to keep more tax-efficient investments (like index funds) in taxable accounts to minimize capital gains taxes.
   - **Retirement Accounts:** Hold assets that generate ordinary income (like bonds) in retirement accounts to defer taxes until withdrawal.

### 3. **Diversification**
   - Ensure a diverse mix of assets across all accounts, balancing stocks, bonds, and other investments based on your risk tolerance and investment horizon.

### 4. **Withdrawal Strategy**
   - Plan your withdrawals strategically in retirement. For example, consider withdrawing from taxable accounts first to allow tax-advantaged accounts to grow longer.

### 5. **Review Regularly**
   - Regularly review and adjust your contributions and allocations based on changes in income, expenses, tax laws, and your retirement goals.

### Conclusion
This balanced approach helps take advantage of tax benefits while preparing for a solid retirement. Always consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your situation.
