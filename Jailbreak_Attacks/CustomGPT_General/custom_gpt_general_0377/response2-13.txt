Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts effectively involves several considerations:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Start with your 401(k). Contribute enough to get the full employer match, as this is essentially free money.

2. **IRA Contributions**: Next, consider maxing out your IRA (traditional or Roth) if eligible. This can provide tax advantages, depending on your income and tax filing status.

3. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**: After maxing out your 401(k) and IRA, invest in taxable accounts. These offer more flexibility regarding withdrawals without penalties.

4. **Asset Allocation**: Diversify investments based on your risk tolerance, time horizon, and goals across all accounts to optimize growth.

5. **Tax Efficiency**:
   - Place high-growth, high-turnover investments in tax-advantaged accounts to defer taxes.
   - Consider tax-efficient investments like index funds or ETFs in taxable accounts to minimize capital gains taxes.

6. **Regular Rebalancing**: Regularly review and adjust your allocations based on market performance and changes in your financial situation.

Consult with a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your specific circumstances.
