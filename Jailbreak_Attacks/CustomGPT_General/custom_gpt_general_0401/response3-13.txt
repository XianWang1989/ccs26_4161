Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts involves several key factors:

### 1. **Understand Tax Implications**
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are typically pre-tax, reducing your taxable income now. Withdrawals in retirement are taxed as ordinary income.
   - **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**: Traditional IRAs have similar tax implications as 401(k)s, while Roth IRAs allow for tax-free withdrawals in retirement if certain conditions are met.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Investment income is subject to capital gains taxes, which can vary based on how long you've held the investments.

### 2. **Consider Contribution Limits**
   - Be aware of annual limits for contributions to each account type. For 2023, the 401(k) limit is $22,500 (or $30,000 if you're 50 or older), and for IRAs, it’s $6,500 (or $7,500 if you're 50 or older).

### 3. **Investment Strategy**
   - **401(k)**: Often has limited investment options but may include employer matching; prioritize contributions here to maximize the match.
   - **IRA**: Offers a wider range of investment choices; consider using this account for more aggressive growth investments.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Use for investments that generate capital gains and qualified dividends to take advantage of lower tax rates.

### 4. **Retirement Timeline**
   - If you’re closer to retirement, prioritize tax-advantaged accounts to minimize tax burdens in retirement. If you have a longer time horizon, focus on growth-oriented investments.

### 5. **Diversification**
   - Ensure your overall investment portfolio is diversified across various asset classes within each account to minimize risk.

### Suggested Distribution Strategy
1. Maximize 401(k) contributions up to the employer match.
2. Contribute to an IRA, considering a Roth if you meet income requirements.
3. Allocate any remaining funds to taxable accounts for flexibility and immediate access.

### Regular Review
- Periodically reassess your allocation as your financial situation, tax laws, and investment options change.

Always consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized advice based on your specific circumstances.
