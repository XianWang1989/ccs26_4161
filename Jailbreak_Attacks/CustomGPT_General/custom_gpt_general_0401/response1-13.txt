Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax considerations, and investment time horizon. Here are some strategies to consider:

### 1. **Prioritize Contributions**
   - **401(k) Match:** Contribute enough to your 401(k) to take full advantage of any employer match, as this is essentially free money.
   - **IRA Contributions:** Consider maxing out your IRA contributions next, especially if you can benefit from tax advantages (traditional vs. Roth).

### 2. **Consider Tax Implications**
   - **Tax-Deferred Accounts (401(k) & IRA):** Contributions are typically tax-deductible, and investments grow tax-deferred. Withdrawals are taxed as ordinary income.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Investments are subject to capital gains taxes when sold. Consider placing investments with high expected growth in tax-deferred accounts to minimize future tax liabilities.

### 3. **Diversify Investments**
   - **401(k):** Choose a mix of stocks, bonds, and mutual funds that align with your risk tolerance and investment horizon.
   - **IRA:** Use this account for long-term investments, including index funds or ETFs that can provide tax-efficient growth.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Use for investing in stocks or bonds where you anticipate needing access to funds without penalties.

### 4. **Asset Location Strategy**
   - Place higher-growth and riskier assets in tax-deferred accounts (401(k) and IRA) to maximize tax benefits.
   - Use taxable accounts for lower-growth investments, like bonds or cash equivalents, where taxes are less impactful.

### 5. **Rebalance Periodically**
   - Review and adjust your allocation periodically based on performance and changes in your financial goals or market conditions.

### 6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**
   - Consider working with a professional to tailor your strategy to your specific situation.

By aligning contributions with your financial goals and tax implications, you can create a balanced and effective investment strategy across your accounts.
