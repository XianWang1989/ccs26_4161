Distributing your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some guidelines to consider:

### 1. **Accounts Overview**
   - **401(k)**: Employer-sponsored plan with tax-deferred growth; consider contributions up to any employer match.
   - **IRA**: Individual retirement account, either Traditional (tax-deferred) or Roth (tax-free withdrawals in retirement).
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Investments outside retirement accounts, subject to capital gains tax.

### 2. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**
   - **401(k)**: Contribute at least enough to get the full employer match; this is essentially free money.
   - **IRA**: If eligible, consider maxing out your IRA (traditional or Roth) for tax advantages.

### 3. **Consider Your Goals**
   - **Retirement Focus**: Prioritize tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) and IRA) for long-term growth.
   - **Short-Term Goals**: Use taxable accounts for more liquid assets, as funds can be accessed without penalties.

### 4. **Asset Allocation**
   - **Equities vs. Bonds**: Place higher-risk investments (like stocks) in tax-advantaged accounts to defer taxes, while keeping lower-risk, interest-generating investments in taxable accounts to minimize tax implications.
   - **Tax Efficiency**: In taxable accounts, focus on tax-efficient investments like index funds or ETFs.

### 5. **Maintenance and Rebalancing**
   - Regularly review and rebalance your portfolio to maintain your desired asset allocation and respond to market changes.

### 6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**
   - Personalized advice can be invaluable, particularly concerning your specific tax situation and retirement goals.

### Summary
Start with maximizing contributions to your 401(k) for any match, then consider IRAs, and finally allocate funds to taxable accounts for liquidity and short-term needs. This strategic distribution can help you maximize growth while managing tax implications effectively.
