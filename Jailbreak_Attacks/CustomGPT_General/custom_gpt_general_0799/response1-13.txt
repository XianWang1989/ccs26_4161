Determining the best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some strategies to consider:

### 1. **Maximize Contributions**
   - **401(k)**: If your employer offers a match, contribute at least enough to take full advantage of it. This is essentially free money.
   - **IRA**: If eligible, contribute to an IRA. Depending on your income, consider a Roth IRA for tax-free growth.

### 2. **Tax Considerations**
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are tax-deferred, reducing your taxable income now, but you'll pay taxes on withdrawals in retirement.
   - **IRA**: Traditional IRAs also offer tax deferral, while Roth IRAs provide tax-free withdrawals (subject to conditions).
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Investments held here are generally taxed on capital gains and dividends, so consider this for your short-term investments or when you need more liquidity.

### 3. **Investment Timeline**
   - **Long-Term (20+ years)**: Focus on tax-advantaged accounts (401(k), IRA) since they can grow tax-free or tax-deferred.
   - **Medium-Term (5-20 years)**: Utilize taxable accounts for projects or needs that might arise sooner, while still contributing to your 401(k) and IRA.
   - **Short-Term (<5 years)**: Consider keeping cash or low-risk investments in taxable accounts to avoid penalties and taxes on early withdrawals from retirement accounts.

### 4. **Asset Location**
   - Place tax-inefficient investments (like bonds or REITs) in tax-advantaged accounts to reduce taxable income. 
   - Keep tax-efficient investments (like index funds) in taxable accounts.

### 5. **Diversification**
   - Ensure you have a diversified portfolio across all accounts to manage risk effectively.

### 6. **Regular Rebalancing**
   - Periodically reassess and rebalance your portfolio to maintain your desired asset allocation.

### Conclusion
Consulting with a financial advisor can provide personalized recommendations based on your situation. Always consider your risk tolerance, investment goals, and tax implications when allocating funds across these account types.
