Determining the best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here are some strategies to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a 401(k) match, contribute enough to get the full match. This is essentially free money.

2. **Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**:
   - **401(k)**: If you have a high tax rate, prioritize contributions here for tax deferral.
   - **IRA**: After maxing out the 401(k) match and if you're eligible, contribute to a traditional or Roth IRA. Roth IRAs allow for tax-free growth, which can be beneficial long-term.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for funds needed in the short term or for more flexible investing. They are useful for expenses not covered by retirement accounts.

4. **Consider Tax Implications**: 
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Investments held long-term are taxed at lower capital gains rates. Consider investing in tax-efficient funds.
   - **IRA**: Contributions may be tax-deductible, and growth is tax-deferred.

5. **Emergency Fund**: Ensure you have liquid savings for emergencies before committing all your funds to retirement accounts.

6. **Diversify Investments**: Regardless of account type, diversify your investments to manage risk effectively.

7. **Reassess Regularly**: Review your asset allocation and contributions periodically to align with your financial goals and market conditions.

Consulting with a financial advisor can also help tailor a strategy to your specific needs.
