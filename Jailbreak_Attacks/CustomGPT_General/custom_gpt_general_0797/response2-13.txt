Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts involves considering several factors:

1. **401(k) Contributions**:
   - Max out any employer match, as it's essentially free money.
   - Consider increasing contributions over time, especially if you get a salary raise.

2. **IRA Accounts**:
   - Depending on your income, you might opt for a Traditional or Roth IRA. 
   - Roth IRAs offer tax-free withdrawals in retirement, while Traditional IRAs provide tax deduction now.

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - Use these for short-term goals or investments that don't qualify for tax-advantaged accounts.
   - Consider tax-efficient investments, such as index funds or tax-managed funds, to minimize capital gains taxes.

### General Guidelines:
- **Emergency Fund**: Ensure you have 3-6 months of expenses set aside.
- **Diverse Investments**: Diversify across asset classes within each account.
- **Tax Efficiency**: Place tax-inefficient investments (like bonds) in tax-advantaged accounts and tax-efficient investments (like stocks) in taxable accounts.

### Strategy:
1. **Prioritize contributions to retirement accounts** (401(k) and IRA) for tax benefits.
2. **Invest within each account** based on your risk tolerance and timeline.
3. **Rebalance periodically** to maintain your desired asset allocation.

Consulting with a financial advisor for personalized assistance based on your specific situation is also advisable.
