Distributing your money effectively among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your individual financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here are some general strategies to consider:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match:**
   - **401(k):** Contribute at least enough to get the full employer match if available. This is essentially free money.

### 2. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts:**
   - **IRA (Traditional or Roth):** If you can, max out your IRA contributions after your 401(k) match. 
     - **Roth IRA** is beneficial if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement since withdrawals are tax-free.
     - **Traditional IRA** allows for tax-deferred growth but taxes will apply upon withdrawal.

### 3. **Consider Taxable Accounts:**
   - Use taxable accounts for investments that generate less taxable income (like index funds or long-term investments) to minimize tax liability. 
   - It can also serve your short-term liquidity needs without penalties.

### 4. **Asset Location:**
   - Place high-growth and higher-risk investments (like stocks) in tax-advantaged accounts to maximize tax-deferred or tax-free growth.
   - Keep income-generating assets (like bonds) in taxable accounts to take advantage of lower capital gains tax rates.

### 5. **Monitor Your Contributions:**
   - As your salary increases or if you receive bonuses, consider re-evaluating your contributions to enhance retirement savings.

### 6. **Review Periodically:**
   - Regularly assess your investment allocation and adjust based on performance, changing financial goals, and market conditions.

### 7. **Consult a Financial Advisor:**
   - Personalized advice can help align your strategy with your unique circumstances.

By taking a balanced approach and considering your immediate needs versus long-term growth, you can create a distribution strategy that enhances your financial security both now and in retirement.
