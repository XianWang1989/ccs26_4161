The best way to distribute your money amongst your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors including your financial goals, time horizon, tax situation, and risk tolerance. Here are some general guidelines:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**: 
   - **401(k)**: If your employer offers a matching contribution, aim to contribute enough to get the full match first, as this is essentially free money.
   - **IRA**: Consider contributing to a Traditional IRA or Roth IRA based on your tax situation. A Roth IRA is beneficial if you expect your tax rate to be higher in retirement.

2. **Diversification**: Allocate your investments across different asset classes (stocks, bonds, real estate) within each account to help manage risk and achieve growth.

3. **Consider Withdrawal Rules**: Remember that withdrawals from 401(k)s and Traditional IRAs may be taxed as ordinary income, while qualified distributions from Roth IRAs are tax-free.

4. **Keep an Emergency Fund**: Ensure you have a sufficient emergency fund in your taxable account to cover unexpected expenses without tapping into retirement accounts.

5. **Tax Efficiency**: Use your taxable accounts for investments that generate lower capital gains or qualified dividends, as these may be taxed at lower rates than ordinary income.

6. **Review and Rebalance Regularly**: Periodically assess your investment performance and make adjustments as needed to stay aligned with your goals.

7. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: For personalized advice tailored to your specific situation, consider speaking with a financial advisor.

It's important to consider your unique circumstances and financial goals when making these decisions.
