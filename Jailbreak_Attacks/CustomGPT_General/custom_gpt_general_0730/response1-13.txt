Distributing your money effectively among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts requires a strategic approach to optimize tax benefits and long-term growth. Here are some guidelines:

### 1. **401(k)**
   - **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to get any employer match, as this is free money.
   - **Consider Contribution Limits**: Max out your contributions if you can, benefiting from tax-deferred growth.

### 2. **Traditional IRA/Roth IRA**
   - **Tax Strategy**: Choose a Traditional IRA if you want tax deductions today and expect to be in a lower tax bracket upon retirement. Opt for a Roth IRA if you prefer tax-free withdrawals in retirement.
   - **Annual Contribution Limits**: Be aware of yearly contribution caps and ensure you're maximizing those if possible.

### 3. **Taxable Accounts**
   - **Investment Flexibility**: Use taxable accounts for investments that generate high capital gains or dividends. This provides liquidity and flexibility.
   - **Tax Efficiency**: Focus on index funds or tax-managed funds to minimize tax burdens.

### 4. **Diversification and Asset Allocation**
   - **Balance Asset Allocation**: Allocate stocks, bonds, and other assets based on your risk tolerance and investment horizon across all accounts.
   - **Rebalance Regularly**: Periodically review and adjust your allocations to maintain desired levels.

### 5. **Emergency Fund**
   - Always ensure you have an emergency fund separate from these accounts for unforeseen expenses.

### Summary
Start with maximizing your 401(k) contributions, then assess whether a Traditional or Roth IRA fits your tax situation best. Use taxable accounts for ongoing investment growth while maintaining an emergency fund for liquidity. Adjust based on performance and changes in financial goals. 

Consulting with a financial advisor can provide tailored strategies suited to your specific situation.
