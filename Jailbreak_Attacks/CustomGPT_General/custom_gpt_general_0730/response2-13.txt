Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some strategies:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money.

2. **Utilize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: Consider maxing out contributions if you anticipate being in a lower tax bracket in retirement.
   - **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**: IRAs offer tax advantages; consider a Roth IRA if you expect your income to rise significantly.

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - Use these for short-term goals or if you need liquidity. Investments here can be subject to capital gains tax, so focus on tax-efficient investments (like index funds).

4. **Asset Allocation**:
   - Match your risk tolerance and investment horizon with an appropriate asset mix. Higher risk (stocks) can be favored in tax-advantaged accounts, while more stable investments (bonds) might be better suited for taxable accounts.

5. **Tax Considerations**:
   - Regularly evaluate the tax implications of your investments and adjust your strategy to optimize tax efficiency.

Consider consulting with a financial advisor to tailor a specific plan to your situation.
