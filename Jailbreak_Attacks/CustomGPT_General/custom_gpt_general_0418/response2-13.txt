When considering how to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, it's essential to think about your financial goals, tax implications, and investment strategy. Here are some general guidelines:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**: If your employer offers a matching contribution, start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to get the full match. This is essentially free money.

2. **Contribute to an IRA**: After maximizing the employer match, consider contributing to an IRA (Traditional or Roth) depending on your eligibility and tax situation. IRAs can offer tax advantages that may be beneficial for retirement savings.

3. **Continue Contributing to 401(k)**: If you can, continue contributing to your 401(k) up to the annual limit. This allows for a higher contribution and potential tax-deferred growth.

4. **Fund Taxable Accounts Last**: Taxable investment accounts are typically funded last since they do not offer the same tax advantages as retirement accounts. However, they do provide flexibility in access and investment choices.

5. **Diversification**: Regardless of the account type, ensure your investments are well-diversified to manage risk and return effectively.

6. **Consider Future Tax Implications**: Think about your expected tax rate in retirement compared to now. If you expect to be in a lower tax bracket in retirement, a Traditional 401(k) or IRA may be more advantageous. Conversely, if you anticipate being in a higher tax bracket, a Roth option might be better.

7. **Revisit Your Plan Regularly**: As your income, expenses, and investment goals change, revisit and adjust your distribution strategy accordingly.

Consult with a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your specific situation.
