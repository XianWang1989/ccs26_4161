The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here's a general strategy to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**: If your employer offers a matching contribution, it's usually a good idea to contribute enough to your 401(k) to take full advantage of this match. This is essentially free money.

2. **Consider Tax Implications**:
   - **Traditional 401(k) and IRA**: Contributions are often made pre-tax, reducing your taxable income now, but you'll pay taxes on withdrawals in retirement. These accounts can be beneficial if you expect to be in a lower tax bracket when you retire.
   - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, and qualified withdrawals are tax-free. If you expect your tax rate to increase in the future or you want to avoid taxes in retirement, consider contributing to a Roth IRA.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: These are useful for short to medium-term investments and provide flexibility. Investment gains may be taxed at capital gains rates, which can be lower than ordinary income tax rates for long-term holdings.

3. **Diversification of Investments**: Ensure your investments are diversified to manage risk. This includes different asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) and investment types across all accounts.

4. **Retirement Timeline**: If you’re young and have a long time until retirement, a high allocation to equities might make sense. As retirement nears, consider shifting toward safer, income-generating investments.

5. **Withdrawal Strategy**: Consider your plans for withdrawals in retirement. Different accounts have different rules for withdrawals and penalties, so plan accordingly to minimize taxes.

6. **Continuous Review**: Periodically review your asset allocation and distribution strategy as your financial situation, market conditions, and retirement goals evolve.

It's often beneficial to consult with a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your individual circumstances.
