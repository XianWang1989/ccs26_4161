When deciding how to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Tax Advantages**: 
   - **401(k) and IRA**: These accounts offer tax-deferred growth. A 401(k) may provide employer matching, so contribute enough to maximize this benefit first. Use a Traditional IRA for tax deductions or a Roth IRA for tax-free withdrawals in retirement, depending on your income and tax strategy.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: These are subject to capital gains taxes but offer flexibility for withdrawals. Consider allocating investments with higher turnover (which generate short-term capital gains) here.

2. **Contribution Limits**:
   - **401(k)**: For 2023, the limit is $22,500 (or $30,000 if you're 50 or older). Maximize this if possible.
   - **IRA**: The contribution limit is $6,500 (or $7,500 if you're 50 or older). Consider contributing to both Traditional and Roth IRAs based on your eligibility.

3. **Investment Goals**:
   - Prioritize retirement savings in tax-advantaged accounts. Use taxable accounts for shorter-term goals or if you anticipate needing access to funds before retirement.

4. **Risk Tolerance**: 
   - Depending on how close you are to retirement, adjust the risk level in each account. Generally, you can take more risks in your 401(k) and IRA since they are for long-term growth.

5. **Withdrawal Strategy**:
   - Plan how and when you will withdraw funds. For example, it may be advantageous to withdraw from taxable accounts first during retirement to let tax-advantaged accounts grow longer.

6. **Rebalance Regularly**: 
   - Set an annual or semi-annual review to rebalance your portfolio to maintain your desired asset allocation across all accounts.

By balancing contributions and taking advantage of tax benefits effectively, you can enhance your overall investment strategy. Consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your specific financial situation.
