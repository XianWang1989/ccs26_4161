Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and time horizon. Here’s an overview to help you strategize:

### 1. **401(k) Accounts**
   - **Employer Match**: If your employer offers a match, prioritize contributing enough to receive the full match. This is essentially free money.
   - **Pre-Tax vs. Roth**: Consider whether to contribute to a traditional (pre-tax) 401(k) or a Roth (after-tax). Pre-tax contributions reduce your taxable income now, while Roth contributions grow tax-free.

### 2. **IRA Accounts**
   - **Traditional vs. Roth**: Similar to the 401(k), a traditional IRA can lower your taxable income, while a Roth IRA offers tax-free withdrawals in retirement if certain conditions are met.
   - **Contribution Limits**: Be aware of annual contribution limits ($6,500 for 2023, or $7,500 if you’re age 50 or older).

### 3. **Taxable Accounts**
   - **Flexibility**: Taxable accounts offer more flexibility for withdrawals without penalties, but you’ll pay capital gains taxes on profits.
   - **Investment Choices**: These accounts can be ideal for investments you expect to hold for the long term, as they may receive favorable capital gains treatment.

### **Overall Strategy**
1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Ensure you contribute enough to your 401(k) to get any matching contributions.
2. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**: After the match, consider maxing out your IRA contributions next, especially if you can take advantage of a Roth IRA.
3. **Fill Up Your 401(k)**: If you still have funds available, go back to your 401(k) to reach the annual contribution limit.
4. **Invest in Taxable Accounts**: Finally, use taxable accounts for additional savings and investments, focusing on long-term growth.

### **Considerations**
- **Emergency Fund**: Ensure you have an adequate emergency fund before investing too much.
- **Tax Implications**: Consult with a tax advisor to ensure you’re optimizing your tax situation.
- **Investment Strategy**: Diversify your investments across asset classes in all accounts, keeping in mind the various tax treatments.

Adjust your contributions based on changes in income, expenses, and financial goals, and revisit your strategy regularly.
