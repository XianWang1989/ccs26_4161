Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, risk tolerance, and tax situation. Here’s a general approach:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k):**
   - Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match, as it's essentially "free money."

### 2. **Prioritize Roth or Traditional IRA:**
   - If eligible, consider contributing to a Roth IRA for tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement. If you expect to be in a higher tax bracket during retirement, a Roth may be more beneficial.
   - Conversely, if you anticipate a lower tax bracket in retirement, a Traditional IRA can provide tax-deferred growth.

### 3. **Contribute Beyond the Match:**
   - After maximizing the employer match in your 401(k), consider putting additional funds into your IRA. If you reach the IRA maximum, consider returning to the 401(k).

### 4. **Taxable Accounts:**
   - Use taxable accounts for investments that could face high tax rates, like bonds or actively managed funds, to keep you within the tax-efficient environments of your IRAs or 401(k).
   - This is also where you can hold investments for shorter-term goals.

### 5. **Asset Allocation:**
   - Diversify holdings across accounts. For example, you might hold more aggressive investments in tax-deferred accounts (401(k) and IRA) and more conservative ones in taxable accounts to manage risk and tax implications.

### 6. **Periodic Review:**
   - Regularly review your asset allocation and adjust contributions based on performance, changes in financial goals, or tax laws.

### 7. **Consult a Financial Advisor:**
   - Seeking professional advice can help tailor a strategy specific to your financial situation.

This balanced approach allows you to take advantage of tax-deferred growth while managing your overall investment risk.
