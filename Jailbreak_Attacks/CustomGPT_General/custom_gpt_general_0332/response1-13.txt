I'm not a financial advisor, but I can offer some general tips on how to think about distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts:

1. **401(k)**:
   - **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to get the full amount.
   - **Tax Benefits**: Contributions are often pre-tax, which lowers your taxable income now.

2. **IRA**:
   - **Consider Roth vs. Traditional**: Choose based on your current tax situation and future expectations. Roth IRAs allow for tax-free growth and withdrawals, while Traditional IRAs offer tax deductions now.
   - **Contribute the Maximum**: Aim for the yearly limit if possible.

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - **Invest for Flexibility**: These accounts offer more liquidity and flexibility regarding withdrawals without penalties.
   - **Tax Efficiency**: Focus on growth investments (like stocks) that are subject to lower capital gains taxes over the long term.

### General Strategy:
- **Diversification**: Balance assets across these accounts to mitigate risks.
- **Asset Allocation**: Place more aggressive investments in tax-advantaged accounts (like stocks in a 401(k) or IRA) and use taxable accounts for more conservative investments that generate income.
- **Review Regularly**: Reassess your distribution based on changing life circumstances, market conditions, and retirement goals.

Consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your specific situation!
