To effectively distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match. This is essentially free money.

2. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: If your employer offers a match, max it out first.
   - **IRA**: If eligible, also contribute to a Traditional or Roth IRA, depending on your income and tax situation. Roth IRAs provide tax-free withdrawals in retirement.

3. **Emergency Fund**: Ensure you have 3-6 months of living expenses in a liquid account before heavily investing.

4. **Taxable Accounts**: After maximizing contributions to tax-advantaged accounts, consider taxable accounts for flexibility and access. They can be beneficial for short-term goals or if you anticipate needing funds before retirement.

5. **Investment Strategy**: Diversify your investments across all accounts, considering asset allocation based on your risk tolerance and timeline.

6. **Rebalance Regularly**: Review and adjust your portfolio periodically to maintain your desired asset allocation.

Consult with a financial advisor for personalized recommendations that reflect your unique circumstances and goals.
