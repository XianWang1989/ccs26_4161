Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and time horizon. Here’s a general approach:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get any employer match — it’s essentially free money.

2. **Contribute to IRA**: If eligible, contribute to a Roth IRA (for tax-free growth) or a traditional IRA (to potentially lower taxable income).

3. **Maximize 401(k) Contributions**: After your IRA contributions, go back to your 401(k) to maximize contributions.

4. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**: If you have extra money after maxing out tax-advantaged accounts, invest in taxable accounts for more liquidity and flexibility.

5. **Asset Allocation**: Within each account, diversify your investments according to your risk tolerance and investment goals, keeping higher-risk investments in tax-advantaged accounts if possible.

6. **Rebalance Annually**: Regularly review and adjust your allocations to align with your financial goals and market conditions.

Consider consulting a financial advisor to tailor this strategy to your specific situation.
