Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts can depend on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some tips:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match, then consider maxing it out if possible. This account grows tax-deferred.
   - **IRA**: If eligible, consider a Roth IRA for tax-free growth and withdrawals, or a traditional IRA for tax-deductible contributions.

2. **Diversify Investments**:
   - In your 401(k) and IRA, focus on long-term growth investments (e.g., stocks, index funds).
   - Use taxable accounts for assets that generate lower tax burdens, like municipal bonds or long-term capital gains investments.

3. **Liquidity Needs**:
   - Keep sufficient funds in taxable accounts for short-term needs or emergencies since these accounts allow for easier access without penalties.

4. **Consider Your Tax Bracket**:
   - If you're in a higher tax bracket now, you may prefer Roth IRA contributions for tax-free growth. If you anticipate being in a lower bracket in retirement, traditional accounts may be more beneficial.

5. **Rebalance Regularly**:
   - Adjust your allocations periodically based on performance and changes in your financial goals.

By tailoring your contributions based on these factors, you can effectively manage your investments and optimize for growth while minimizing taxes.
