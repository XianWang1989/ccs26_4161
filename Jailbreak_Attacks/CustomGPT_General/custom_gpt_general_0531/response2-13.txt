Distributing your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some general principles to consider:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**
   - **Contribution**: Aim to contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match. This is essentially "free money" and a great return on investment.

### 2. **Consider Tax Advantages**
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are pre-tax, reducing your taxable income now. Withdrawals in retirement are taxed as ordinary income.
   - **IRA**: Traditional IRAs also allow for tax-deductible contributions, while Roth IRAs offer tax-free growth and withdrawals. Consider your current vs. future tax rates.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Investments here are subject to capital gains taxes. However, they offer more flexibility and access without penalties.

### 3. **Diversify Investments**
   - **401(k) and IRA**: Focus on a mix of stocks and bonds aligned with your risk tolerance and time horizon.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Consider tax-efficient investments, such as index funds or ETFs, to minimize capital gains taxes.

### 4. **Adjust Based on Age and Goals**
   - **Younger Investors**: May prioritize aggressive growth with a heavier allocation in stocks within tax-advantaged accounts.
   - **Closer to Retirement**: Shift to more conservative investments, focusing on income and capital preservation.

### 5. **Rebalance Regularly**
   - Periodically review and adjust your allocations to maintain your target asset mix, especially after significant market movements.

### 6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**
   - A professional can provide personalized advice based on your unique situation and help you navigate complex tax implications.

By strategically distributing your investments across these accounts, you can optimize growth potential while managing tax implications effectively.
