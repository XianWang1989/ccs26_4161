Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts can depend on several factors, including your age, retirement goals, tax situation, and current financial needs. Here are some strategies to consider:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match:**
   - **401(k):** If your employer offers a match, contribute at least enough to take full advantage of it. This is essentially free money.

### 2. **Focus on Tax-Advantaged Accounts First:**
   - **IRA:** Consider maxing out your IRA contributions before going too heavily into taxable accounts. IRAs can provide tax benefits that can enhance your retirement savings.
   - **401(k):** Continue contributing to your 401(k) if it offers tax deferral and potential tax benefits, particularly if you're in a higher tax bracket.

### 3. **Consider Tax Implications:**
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Use these for investments that generate lower tax burdens, like long-term capital gains or qualified dividends. However, avoid holding investments in taxable accounts that produce significant short-term gains or interest income, which are taxed at higher ordinary income rates.

### 4. **Balance for Flexibility:**
   - **Emergency Savings:** Ensure you have sufficient liquidity for emergencies. This might mean keeping a portion in taxable accounts that can be easily accessed without penalty.
   
### 5. **Age and Risk Tolerance:**
   - If you're younger, you might want to skew more towards equities in your retirement accounts for growth potential. As you age, gradually shift towards more conservative investments.

### 6. **Rebalance Regularly:**
   - Adjust allocations periodically based on changes in your financial situation, market performance, or retirement goals.

### Sample Distribution Strategy:
- **Younger Saver (20s-30s):** 60% 401(k), 30% IRA, 10% Taxable.
- **Mid-Career (40s-50s):** 50% 401(k), 30% IRA, 20% Taxable.
- **Closer to Retirement (60s):** 40% 401(k), 40% IRA, 20% Taxable.

### Consult a Financial Advisor:
It's often beneficial to work with a financial advisor to create a personalized strategy based on your unique situation.

By following these guidelines, you can create a balanced approach to saving and investing that aligns with your financial goals.
