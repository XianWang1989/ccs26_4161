To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Understand Account Types**:
   - **401(k)**: Typically tax-deferred, meaning you won’t pay taxes on your contributions or earnings until withdrawal. Many employers match contributions, which is essentially free money.
   - **IRA**: Individual Retirement Accounts can be traditional (tax-deferred) or Roth (tax-free growth). Contributions to a traditional IRA may be tax-deductible based on your income and other factors.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: These accounts are subject to capital gains taxes but offer more flexibility for withdrawals without penalties.

2. **Maximize Employer Match**: Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match. This is often the best way to leverage your retirement savings.

3. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - Once you’ve maximized your 401(k) match, consider contributing to an IRA, particularly if you qualify for a Roth IRA, which allows for tax-free withdrawals in retirement.

4. **Diversify Investments**: Ensure a well-diversified portfolio across all accounts. Consider factors like your risk tolerance, age, and investment goals.

5. **Weigh Contribution Limits**: Be aware of the annual contribution limits for each account type. In 2023, for example, the 401(k) limit is $22,500 (plus $7,500 if you're over 50), and the IRA limit is $6,500 (plus $1,000 if you're over 50).

6. **Tax Considerations**: 
   - Use tax-efficient investments in taxable accounts to minimize capital gains taxes, like index funds or ETFs.
   - Consider placing higher-tax investments (like bonds) in tax-advantaged accounts and lower-tax investments (like stocks) in taxable accounts.

7. **Rebalancing and Review**: Regularly review your portfolio to maintain your desired asset allocation, rebalance when necessary, and adjust your contributions based on changes in income or tax laws.

8. **Consult a Financial Professional**: Given the complexities in tax implications and investment strategies, it may be beneficial to consult a financial advisor to tailor your plan to your specific situation.

By following these strategies, you can create a balanced approach to your retirement savings while maximizing potential growth and minimizing tax liabilities.
