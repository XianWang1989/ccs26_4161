To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

### 1. **Assess Your Retirement Goals**
   - Determine your target retirement age and lifestyle. This will influence how aggressively you invest.

### 2. **Understand Account Types**
   - **401(k)**: Often has employer matching contributions and tax advantages for higher contribution limits.
   - **IRA (Traditional/Roth)**: Provides tax benefits; Roth IRAs offer tax-free withdrawals in retirement, while Traditional IRAs allow pre-tax contributions.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: More flexible but subject to capital gains tax on profits.

### 3. **Prioritize Contributions**
   - **Max Out Employer Match**: First, aim to contribute enough to your 401(k) to receive any employer match—this is free money.
   - **Fund a Roth IRA or Traditional IRA**: Depending on your income level and tax situation, contribute to an IRA, aiming for the max annual limit ($6,500 for those under 50; $7,500 for those 50+ in 2023).
   - **Return to 401(k)**: After maxing out employer match and any IRA contributions, return to contribute more to your 401(k).

### 4. **Diversify Investments**
   - Within each account, diversify your investments across stocks, bonds, and other vehicles based on your risk tolerance and time horizon.

### 5. **Consider Withdrawal Strategies**
   - Plan withdrawals during retirement strategically. Generally, it may be tax-efficient to withdraw from taxable accounts first, then from tax-deferred accounts (401(k) and Traditional IRA), and leave Roth IRAs to grow longer.

### 6. **Review Regularly**
   - Periodically review and adjust your allocations as your financial situation, tax laws, and market conditions change.

### 7. **Consult a Financial Advisor**
   - If you’re uncertain, working with a financial advisor can help tailor a strategy that meets your specific needs.

This approach balances tax efficiency, growth potential, and flexibility, ensuring you maximize your investments for retirement.
