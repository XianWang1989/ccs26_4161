Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment strategy. Here are some general guidelines:

### 1. **401(k) Accounts:**
   - **Employer Match:** If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to your 401(k) to receive the full match; this is essentially free money.
   - **Tax Advantages:** Contributions are pre-tax, reducing your taxable income now. Consider maximizing this option if you’re in a higher tax bracket.

### 2. **IRA Accounts (Traditional and Roth):**
   - **Tax Benefits:** Contributions to a Traditional IRA may be tax-deductible, while Roth IRA contributions are made with after-tax dollars, allowing tax-free withdrawals in retirement.
   - **Contribution Limits:** Be aware of the annual contribution limits ($6,500 for individuals under 50, $7,500 for those 50 and older in 2023).
   - **Roth Option:** If you anticipate being in a higher tax bracket in retirement, prioritize a Roth IRA for its tax-free withdrawals.

### 3. **Taxable Accounts:**
   - **Flexibility:** These accounts provide flexibility for withdrawals without penalties, making them suitable for short-term goals or emergencies.
   - **Tax Considerations:** Be mindful of capital gains taxes when buying and selling investments. Favor long-term investments to minimize tax exposure.

### 4. **General Strategy:**
   - **Emergency Fund:** Ensure you have an emergency fund before heavily investing. Aim for 3-6 months’ worth of living expenses.
   - **Investment Allocation:** Diversify your investments across all accounts to balance risk and return, considering your overall asset allocation strategy.
   - **Rebalance Regularly:** Review your allocations periodically to ensure they align with your goals, risk tolerance, and market changes.

### 5. **Consider Your Goals:**
   - **Retirement Timing:** Tailor your contributions based on when you plan to retire. If retirement is far off, higher risk investments may be suitable for growth.
   - **Tax Impact:** Analyze how contributions to each account will impact your current and future tax situations.

### Consultation:
It’s often beneficial to consult a financial advisor to personalize your strategy based on your unique circumstances and goals.
