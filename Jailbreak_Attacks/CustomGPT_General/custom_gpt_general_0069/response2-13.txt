When it comes to distributing your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, it's essential to consider several factors such as your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some strategies to consider:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**: Start by maximizing contributions to tax-advantaged accounts like 401(k) and IRAs. This helps reduce your taxable income and grow your investments tax-deferred.

   - **401(k)**: If your employer offers matching contributions, try to contribute enough to get the full match. This is essentially "free money." 

   - **IRA**: Depending on your eligibility, you may consider both a Traditional IRA (tax-deductible contributions) and a Roth IRA (tax-free withdrawals in retirement). 

2. **Diversify Investments**: Within each account type, ensure your investments are diversified. This reduces risk and can improve returns over time. Consider a mix of stocks, bonds, and other assets according to your risk tolerance.

3. **Tax Considerations**: Be aware of the tax implications of each account type. 

   - **401(k) and Traditional IRA**: Withdrawals are taxed as ordinary income, so consider minimizing withdrawals until you're in a lower tax bracket (typically in retirement).

   - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, but qualified withdrawals are tax-free. This can be advantageous in retirement for managing tax liabilities.

   - **Taxable Accounts**: Investments in these accounts can lead to capital gains taxes, so consider keeping high-growth investments here for long-term capital gains rates, while placing income-generating assets (like bonds) in tax-advantaged accounts to defer taxes.

4. **Consider Your Age and Timeline**: Your investment strategy should change as you approach retirement. Younger investors might have a more aggressive approach, focusing on growth, while those nearing retirement should prioritize capital preservation.

5. **Review Regularly**: Your financial situation, investment performance, and tax laws change over time. Regularly review and adjust your allocations to ensure they align with your goals.

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: If you're unsure, consulting with a financial advisor can provide personalized guidance tailored to your specific situation.

This balanced approach can help optimize your tax situation while working toward your long-term financial goals.
