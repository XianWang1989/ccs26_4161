Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here’s a general strategy:

1. **Maximize Employer Match (401(k))**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match, if available. This is essentially free money.

2. **Contribute to an IRA**: After securing the match, consider contributing to a Traditional or Roth IRA. Roth IRAs provide tax-free growth, while Traditional IRAs offer tax-deferred growth, which can be beneficial based on your expected future income.

3. **Increase 401(k) Contributions**: Once you've maxed out your IRA contributions, increase your 401(k) contributions, especially if you can still benefit from tax deferral.

4. **Taxable Accounts**: Use taxable accounts for additional investments once you've maximized tax-advantaged accounts. This can be suitable for funds you'll need in the short term or for specific financial goals, as it provides flexibility.

5. **Diversification**: Ensure your investments within these accounts are well-diversified and aligned with your risk tolerance and time horizon.

6. **Rebalance Regularly**: Periodically review and adjust your allocations to stay in line with your investment goals.

7. **Consider Taxes**: Be mindful of the tax implications of selling assets in your taxable account, and try to hold onto investments long enough to benefit from long-term capital gains rates.

It’s wise to consult with a financial advisor to tailor a strategy that suits your unique situation.
