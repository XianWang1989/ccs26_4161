Distributing your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts can optimize your investments for growth and tax efficiency. Here’s a strategic approach:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to receive the full employer match. This is essentially free money.

2. **Prioritize Retirement Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: If you haven’t maxed your 401(k) (the limit for 2023 is $22,500, or $30,000 if you're over 50), focus on this next.
   - **IRA**: Consider a traditional or Roth IRA (limit is $6,500, or $7,500 if over 50). Roth IRAs offer tax-free growth, while traditional IRAs offer upfront tax deductions.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: After maxing out retirement accounts, you can put excess funds in taxable accounts. These are useful for flexibility and accessing funds without penalties.

4. **Asset Allocation**:
   - Use tax-inefficient investments (like bonds) in tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) and IRA).
   - Place tax-efficient investments (like ETFs and index funds) in taxable accounts to minimize capital gains taxes.

5. **Emergency Fund**: Maintain an emergency fund (3-6 months of expenses) in a high-yield savings account or similar, before heavily investing.

6. **Rebalance Regularly**: Review and adjust your allocation annually based on performance and changing goals.

Consult with a financial advisor to tailor these strategies to your specific financial situation and goals.
