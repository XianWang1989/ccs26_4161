The best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here’s a general approach to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k):** If your employer offers a 401(k) match, contribute at least enough to get the full match, as this is essentially free money.

2. **Contribute to an IRA:** After maximizing your 401(k) match, consider contributing to an IRA (either a Traditional or Roth IRA, depending on your eligibility and tax situation). This can provide tax advantages and diversify your retirement funds.

3. **Continue to Max Out the 401(k):** If you can afford to, continue contributing to your 401(k) up to the annual contribution limit. This will help you build a significant nest egg for retirement, while also potentially giving you tax breaks.

4. **Taxable Accounts:** Once you've maxed out contributions to tax-advantaged accounts, any remaining funds can be allocated to taxable accounts. These accounts offer more flexibility regarding withdrawals and investment choices, although you will have to pay taxes on capital gains and dividends.

5. **Rebalance Your Portfolio:** Regardless of where you allocate your money, regularly review and rebalance your portfolio to ensure it aligns with your risk tolerance, investment goals, and market conditions.

6. **Consult with a Financial Advisor:** Consider speaking with a financial advisor to create a personalized strategy that reflects your individual circumstances and goals.

Each individual's financial situation is unique, so tailoring this general guidance to fit your specific needs is essential.
