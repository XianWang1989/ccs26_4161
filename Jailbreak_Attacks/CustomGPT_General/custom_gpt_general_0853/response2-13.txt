The best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here’s a general guideline to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match (401(k)):** If your employer offers a matching contribution for your 401(k), contribute enough to get the full match. This is essentially "free money."

2. **Consider Tax Advantages:**
   - **401(k):** Contributions are typically made pre-tax, which reduces your taxable income. However, taxes will be owed upon withdrawal during retirement.
   - **IRA (Traditional):** Similar tax treatment as a 401(k), with potential for tax-deductible contributions, depending on your income and participation in other retirement plans.
   - **Roth IRA:** Contributions are made post-tax, but qualified withdrawals in retirement are tax-free. This can be beneficial if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket later.
   
3. **Taxable Accounts:** These are more flexible but can be less tax-efficient due to capital gains taxes on investments. They are suitable for goals outside of retirement, as you can access the funds anytime without penalties.

4. **Diversification:** Ensure you have a mix of asset types (stocks, bonds, etc.) across all accounts to spread risk. Consider different investment strategies based on the account type.

5. **Age and Risk Tolerance:** Younger investors might opt for more aggressive growth strategies in their 401(k) and IRA, while those nearing retirement may want to shift toward more conservative investments.

6. **Investment Timeline:** If you have short-term goals (e.g., purchasing a home), prioritize liquid assets in taxable accounts. For long-term retirement goals, focus on maximizing tax-advantaged accounts.

7. **Consult a Financial Advisor:** They can provide personalized advice tailored to your specific financial situation, helping you develop a comprehensive strategy.

In summary, start by maximizing your employer 401(k) match, then evaluate the tax advantages of IRAs versus taxable accounts while considering your individual investment goals and timeline. Distributing your investments this way can optimize your tax efficiency and help you grow your wealth effectively.
