To effectively distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following steps:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get any employer match first. This is essentially free money.

2. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: Contribute to the maximum limit ($22,500 for 2024, or $30,000 if you're 50 or older).
   - **IRA**: After the 401(k), consider contributing to a traditional or Roth IRA (up to $6,500 for 2024, or $7,500 if you're 50 or older).

3. **Optimize Investment Types**:
   - For tax-advantaged accounts (401(k), IRA), focus on growth-oriented investments since these accounts benefit from tax-deferred growth.
   - For taxable accounts, consider holding investments that generate qualified dividends or long-term capital gains, as they are taxed at lower rates.

4. **Emergency Fund**: Ensure you have an emergency fund in a high-yield savings account or similar, separate from these investment accounts.

5. **Diversification**: Ensure your portfolio is well-diversified across different asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) to manage risk effectively.

6. **Tax Considerations**: Be mindful of the tax implications of withdrawing funds from each account type, especially in retirement.

7. **Review and Adjust**: Regularly review your asset allocation and adjust based on changes in your financial goals, market conditions, and life events.

By following these guidelines, you can optimize your investment strategy while considering both growth potential and tax efficiency.
