To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to obtain any employer match. This is essentially free money.

2. **Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**: Prioritize contributions to your IRA (Traditional or Roth) if you’re eligible, as these accounts offer significant tax advantages.

3. **401(k) Contributions**: If you have maxed out your IRA, consider increasing your 401(k) contributions, especially if you’re in a higher tax bracket. Traditional 401(k) contributions reduce your taxable income.

4. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**: Use taxable accounts for money not needed for retirement. They offer more liquidity and flexibility but are subject to capital gains taxes.

5. **Asset Allocation**: Diversify your investments according to your risk tolerance and time horizon. Consider placing investments with higher expected returns and higher volatility in tax-advantaged accounts to minimize tax implications.

6. **Ongoing Adjustments**: Regularly review and adjust your allocations based on market performance, changes in your financial situation, and retirement goals.

7. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: Depending on your financial complexity, consider professional advice to tailor a strategy specific to your needs.

By combining these approaches, you can create a balanced investment strategy that maximizes growth potential while managing tax implications effectively.
