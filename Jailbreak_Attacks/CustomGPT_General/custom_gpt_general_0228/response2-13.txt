The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and timeline for accessing the funds. Here's a general strategy to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**: If your employer offers a matching contribution for your 401(k), aim to contribute enough to at least get the full match. This is essentially free money.

2. **Pay Attention to Contribution Limits**: In 2023, the contribution limit for a 401(k) is $22,500 ($30,000 if you’re over 50), while for IRAs, it's $6,500 ($7,500 if you’re over 50). Contribute to your accounts up to these limits as applicable.

3. **Consider Traditional vs. Roth IRA**: If you expect to be in a higher tax bracket during retirement, you might benefit more from a Roth IRA (contributions made after taxes). Conversely, if you think you’ll be in a lower bracket, a Traditional IRA (tax-deferred contributions) might be preferable.

4. **Taxable Accounts for Short-Term Goals**: If you have financial goals within the next 5 years, consider putting money into taxable accounts, as they offer more liquidity and flexibility.

5. **Diversification**: Spread out your investments among different asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) within these accounts to minimize risk and optimize returns.

6. **Assess Tax Implications**: Withdrawals from a 401(k) and a Traditional IRA are taxed as ordinary income, while Roth IRA withdrawals may be tax-free if rules are followed. Taxable accounts will incur capital gains taxes, so understand how this affects your overall tax strategy.

7. **Rebalance Regularly**: Check your asset allocation and performance at least annually, and adjust if needed to stay aligned with your investment goals.

Overall, it’s wise to consult a financial advisor to tailor an investment strategy based on your specific situation.
