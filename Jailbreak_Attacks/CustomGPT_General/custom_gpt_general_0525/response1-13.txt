Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on a few key factors: your age, investment goals, risk tolerance, and tax situation. Here’s a general strategy:

1. **401(k):** Maximize your contributions, especially if your employer offers matching. This account typically grows tax-deferred until withdrawal.

2. **IRA:** Consider funding a Roth IRA if eligible, as it allows for tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement. If you're looking for tax deductions now, a traditional IRA can be beneficial.

3. **Taxable Accounts:** Use these for investments that may generate capital gains or dividends, as they'll be taxed differently than 401(k) or IRA distributions.

### Suggested Distribution Approach:
- **Max out 401(k) contributions** (especially match).
- **Contribute to an IRA** (prioritize Roth for tax-free growth).
- **Invest remaining funds in taxable accounts**, focusing on long-term investments to minimize tax impact.

### Review Regularly:
Reassess your strategy annually based on changes in income, taxes, and life goals.
