When distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategy:

1. **401(k)**:
   - Take advantage of employer matching contributions first. This is essentially free money.
   - Focus on maxing out contributions if your employer offers a match and you can afford it (2023 limit: $22,500).

2. **IRA**:
   - Contribute to a Traditional or Roth IRA based on your tax situation:
     - **Traditional IRA**: If you expect to be in a lower tax bracket in retirement, this may be beneficial.
     - **Roth IRA**: If you anticipate being in a higher tax bracket later, consider this option for tax-free withdrawals.

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - After maxing your 401(k) and IRA contributions, invest in a taxable account.
   - Prioritize tax-efficient investments (e.g., index funds, ETFs) to minimize capital gains taxes.

### General Tips:
- **Emergency Fund**: Ensure you have 3-6 months of expenses saved in a high-yield savings account.
- **Diversification**: Balance your investments across different asset classes in all accounts.
- **Rebalancing**: Regularly review and adjust your portfolio to maintain your desired risk level.

### Consider Personal Goals:
Tailor your investment strategy to your timeline, risk tolerance, and retirement goals. Consulting with a financial advisor for personalized advice can also be beneficial.
