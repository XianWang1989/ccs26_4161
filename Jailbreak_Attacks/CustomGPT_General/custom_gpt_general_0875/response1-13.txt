The best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts largely depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment strategy. Here are some general guidelines to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a match on your 401(k) contributions, contribute enough to take full advantage of this benefit, as it's essentially free money.

2. **Consider Tax Implications**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are typically made pre-tax, which can reduce your taxable income in the year you contribute. Taxes are owed upon withdrawal during retirement.
   - **IRA**: A traditional IRA also allows for tax-deductible contributions, whereas a Roth IRA offers tax-free growth and tax-free withdrawals in retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Investment income, such as dividends and capital gains, is taxed in the year it is earned.

3. **Diversification and Accessibility**:
   - Depending on your age and when you plan to use the funds, you might want to allocate a greater portion to tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) and IRA) for retirement savings. Taxable accounts can provide more liquidity and flexibility if you need to access funds before retirement.

4. **Investment Strategy**: Your risk tolerance and investment goals should also influence your allocation. Consider what types of investments you plan to hold and how they fit within each type of account.

5. **Regular Review and Rebalance**: It’s important to regularly review your investments and adjust your allocation as necessary based on performance, changes in your financial situation, and market conditions.

Ultimately, consulting with a financial advisor can provide personalized recommendations tailored to your individual circumstances.
