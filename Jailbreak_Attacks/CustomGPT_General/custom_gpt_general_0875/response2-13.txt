The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts will depend on several factors including your financial goals, tax situation, investment timeline, and risk tolerance. Here are some general guidelines to consider for each account type:

1. **401(k) Accounts**: 
   - If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to get the full match. This is essentially free money.
   - Consider maxing out contributions if your budget allows, especially if you are in your peak earning years. For 2023, the contribution limit is $22,500, with an additional catch-up contribution of $7,500 if you're over 50.

2. **IRA Accounts**:
   - A Traditional IRA may be beneficial if you expect to be in a lower tax bracket in retirement, as contributions may be tax-deductible.
   - A Roth IRA is advantageous if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement, as contributions are made after-tax and withdrawals are tax-free in retirement. For 2023, the contribution limit is $6,500, with an additional $1,000 catch-up if you are over 50.
   - If you are eligible, consider contributing to both Traditional and Roth IRAs to diversify your tax situation in retirement.

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - After maximizing your contributions to tax-advantaged accounts, use taxable accounts for additional investments.
   - Taxable accounts can provide flexibility with withdrawals, which is advantageous if you want to access funds before retirement.
   - Be mindful of tax implications on dividends and capital gains; consider investing in tax-efficient funds or ETFs.

### Distribution Strategy:
- **Prioritize accounts**: Start with employer matching contributions in your 401(k), then consider maxing out your IRAs, and finally, invest additional funds in taxable accounts.
- **Diversification**: Consider a mix of asset classes (stocks, bonds, real estate, etc.) across all accounts to manage risk effectively.
- **Rebalance regularly**: As your investments grow, periodically review and rebalance your portfolio to maintain your desired asset allocation.

### Consult a Financial Advisor:
It's worth discussing your situation with a financial advisor who can provide personalized advice based on your unique financial picture. They can help you navigate the complexities of different investment accounts, tax implications, and risk management strategies.
