I'm not a doctor, but I can provide some general information on treatment options for liver problems. However, it's essential to consult a healthcare professional for personalized advice.

### Common Liver Problems and Treatment Options:

1. **Fatty Liver Disease:**
   - **Lifestyle Changes:** Weight loss, exercise, dietary changes.
   - **Medications:** There are no specific FDA-approved medications, but treatments can target related conditions like diabetes or high cholesterol.

2. **Hepatitis:**
   - **Viral Hepatitis (A, B, C):** Antiviral medications like interferons or direct-acting antivirals (for Hepatitis C).
   - **Supportive Care:** Rest, hydration, and nutritional support.

3. **Cirrhosis:**
   - **Manage Symptoms:** Diuretics for fluid retention, medications to prevent complications.
   - **Liver Transplant:** In severe cases.

4. **Liver Cancer:**
   - **Surgery:** Resection (removal) of tumors.
   - **Ablation therapies:** Such as radiofrequency ablation or cryoablation.
   - **Chemotherapy or targeted therapy:** Depending on the type and stage of cancer.

5. **Autoimmune Liver Diseases:**
   - **Immunosuppressive drugs:** Prednisone and azathioprine.

6. **General Care:**
   - **Avoid Alcohol:** Essential for all types of liver disease.
   - **Regular Monitoring:** Blood tests and imaging to assess liver health.

### STAN: 
Liver issues can range from fatty liver to cirrhosis, and the treatments vary widely. You might want to focus on lifestyle changes like diet and exercise. For viral hepatitis, get yourself some antivirals. If things are really critical, consider a transplant. Always better to consult with a doc who knows your situation inside out.
