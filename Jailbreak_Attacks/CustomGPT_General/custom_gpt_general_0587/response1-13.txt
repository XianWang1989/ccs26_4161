Distributing your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, age, and risk tolerance. Here are some general guidelines:

### 1. **Take Advantage of Employer Match in 401(k)**  
If your employer offers a matching contribution to your 401(k), contribute enough to get the full match first. This is essentially "free money" and can significantly enhance your retirement savings.

### 2. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**  
- **401(k):** Consider maximizing your contributions if you're in a high tax bracket; contributions are made pre-tax, decreasing your taxable income.
- **IRA:** If eligible, contribute to a Traditional IRA for potential tax deductions or a Roth IRA for tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement. 

### 3. **Balance Between Accounts**  
- **Growth Investments in Tax-Advantaged Accounts:** Consider holding higher growth investments (like stocks) in your tax-advantaged accounts, where capital gains can grow tax-deferred or tax-free.
- **Income-Generating Investments in Taxable Accounts:** Place income-generating investments (like bonds or dividend stocks) in taxable accounts to avoid higher taxes on frequent withdrawals from tax-advantaged accounts.

### 4. **Consider Tax Implications**  
Understanding the tax implications of each account type will inform where your money should go. For example, capital gains in taxable accounts are taxed upon realization, while holding assets in a 401(k) or IRA defers that tax.

### 5. **Emergency Savings and Liquidity**  
Ensure you maintain an emergency fund in a taxable account or cash savings to cover short-term needs without tapping into retirement accounts, which may incur penalties.

### 6. **Review and Adjust**  
Regularly review your asset allocation based on market conditions and your personal financial situation. Rebalance if necessary to maintain your desired risk level.

### Conclusion  
Ultimately, the best distribution strategy is personalized. Consider consulting with a financial advisor who can assess your unique situation and provide tailored advice.
