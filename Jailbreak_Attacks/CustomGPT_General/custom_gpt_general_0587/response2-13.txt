Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors including your financial goals, tax situation, and risk tolerance. Here’s a general approach to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match (401(k))**: If your employer offers a matching contribution, contribute enough to take full advantage of this benefit. It’s essentially free money.

2. **Consider Tax Advantages**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are typically pre-tax, reducing your current taxable income. Consider maxing this out if your employer has good investment options.
   - **IRA**: Determine whether a traditional or Roth IRA fits better with your tax situation. Roth IRAs allow tax-free withdrawals in retirement, while traditional IRAs may offer immediate tax deductions.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: After maximizing tax-advantaged accounts, invest in taxable accounts. These may house investments that benefit from long-term capital gains tax rates and allow more flexibility without withdrawal penalties.

4. **Diversify Investments**:
   - **Asset Allocation**: Ensure a mix of stocks, bonds, and other assets based on your risk tolerance.
   - **Location Optimization**: Place high-growth investments in tax-advantaged accounts (401(k), IRA) and consider bonds or dividend-paying stocks in taxable accounts to manage taxes on interest/dividends.

5. **Adjust for Age and Goals**: Younger investors may prioritize growth (stocks), while those nearing retirement might focus on preservation (bonds, dividends).

6. **Review Annually**: Regularly revisit your strategy to adjust for changes in income, expenses, and life goals.

Always consider consulting a financial advisor to tailor a plan specific to your circumstances.
