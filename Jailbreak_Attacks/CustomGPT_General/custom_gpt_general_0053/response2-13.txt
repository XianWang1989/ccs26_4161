Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some general strategies:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**
   - **401(k):** Contribute enough to get any employer match, as this is essentially "free money." Aim to max out if possible.
   - **IRA:** Contribute to a Roth or Traditional IRA based on your income level and tax situation.

### 2. **Consider Investment Goals and Timeline**
   - Use your 401(k) and IRA for long-term growth since they are meant for retirement. These accounts typically benefit from tax-deferred or tax-free growth.
   - Use taxable accounts for shorter-term goals (e.g., buying a house) since you’ll have more flexibility to withdraw funds without penalties.

### 3. **Asset Allocation Strategy**
   - In tax-deferred accounts (401(k) and IRA), consider holding more aggressive investments (like stocks) as they grow tax-free.
   - In taxable accounts, hold more tax-efficient investments (like index funds or municipal bonds) to minimize the tax burden on dividends and capital gains.

### 4. **Review Fees and Expenses**
   - Ensure you understand the fees associated with each account type and choose investments with lower fees where possible.

### 5. **Regularly Rebalance Your Portfolio**
   - Adjust your allocations periodically based on market conditions and personal circumstances to stay aligned with your goals.

### 6. **Consult with a Financial Advisor**
   - A professional can provide personalized advice based on your entire financial picture, including risk tolerance and future plans.

By balancing these factors, you can create a distribution strategy that optimizes your investments for growth while considering your tax situation and financial goals.
