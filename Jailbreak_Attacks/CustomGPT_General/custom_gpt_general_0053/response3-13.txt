Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your tax situation, retirement goals, age, and risk tolerance. Here’s a general approach to consider:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match**
   - **401(k)**: If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to get the full match first. This is essentially free money.

### 2. **Tax Considerations**
   - **Contributions**:
     - **401(k)**: Traditional contributions reduce your taxable income. If you expect to be in a lower tax bracket in retirement, this is beneficial.
     - **IRA**: Consider a Roth IRA for tax-free withdrawals if you expect higher taxes in retirement.
   - **Withdrawals**:
     - **Taxable Accounts**: Be aware that capital gains are taxed when you sell investments.

### 3. **Emergency Fund**
   - Before heavily investing, ensure you have a sufficient emergency fund in a separate, accessible account.

### 4. **Diversification**
   - Within each account type, diversify your investments across asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) to manage risk.

### 5. **Withdrawal Strategy**
   - Plan for retirement withdrawals strategically. Generally, taxable accounts are tapped first to allow tax-advantaged accounts to grow longer.

### Sample Allocation Strategy:
- **401(k)**: Maximize to employer match, then contribute enough to reach the annual limit if feasible.
- **IRA**: Contribute to a Roth or Traditional IRA based on your tax situation, aiming for max contribution.
- **Taxable Accounts**: Invest in these for flexibility, especially if nearing retirement.

### Regularly Review
   - Revisit your strategy annually or when significant life changes occur to adjust based on market performance and changes in your financial situation or goals.

Consulting with a financial advisor can also help tailor a distribution strategy specific to your needs.
