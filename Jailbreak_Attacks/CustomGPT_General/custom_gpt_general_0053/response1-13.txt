Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts involves considering several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment time horizon. Here’s a general approach:

### 1. **Maximize Contributions:**
   - **401(k):** Contribute enough to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money. Aim for the maximum contribution limit if possible ($22,500 for 2023, plus an additional $7,500 if you're over 50).
   - **IRA:** Consider contributing to a traditional IRA or Roth IRA, depending on your eligibility and tax situation. The contribution limit is $6,500 (or $7,500 if over 50) in 2023.

### 2. **Consider Tax Implications:**
   - **Tax-Deferred Accounts (401(k) and Traditional IRA):** These accounts grow tax-deferred, meaning you won’t pay taxes on growth until withdrawal. This is beneficial if you expect to be in a lower tax bracket during retirement.
   - **Roth IRA:** Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, but withdrawals in retirement are tax-free. Consider this if you anticipate being in a higher tax bracket later.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** You will pay taxes on any realized gains or dividends. These accounts offer more flexibility for withdrawals but can be less tax-efficient for long-term growth.

### 3. **Investment Strategy:**
   - **Risk Tolerance and Time Horizon:** Consider allocating more aggressive investments (e.g., stocks) in accounts where tax efficiency is less of a concern (like Roth IRAs or taxable accounts). For tax-advantaged accounts, ensure a diversified mix suitable for your risk tolerance.

### 4. **Withdrawal Strategy:**
   - Plan how you will withdraw funds in retirement. Often, withdrawing from taxable accounts first can be beneficial, allowing tax-deferred accounts to continue growing.

### 5. **Review Regularly:**
   - Regularly assess your investment performance and goals, adjusting your contributions and allocations as needed based on life changes and market conditions.

### 6. **Seek Professional Advice:**
   - Consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your specific situation, especially regarding tax strategies and retirement planning.

This strategy can help optimize your investment growth while managing taxes effectively.
