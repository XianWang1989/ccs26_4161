The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here are some general strategies:

1. **Maximize Employer Matches in 401(k)**: If your employer offers a 401(k) match, contribute enough to receive the full match. This is essentially "free money" and should generally be a priority.

2. **Consider Tax Advantages**: 
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are made pre-tax, which lowers your taxable income for the year, but you will pay taxes on withdrawals in retirement.
   - **IRA (Traditional)**: Similar to 401(k); contributions may be tax-deductible, and taxes are paid on withdrawals.
   - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, but withdrawals in retirement (including earnings) are tax-free, which may be advantageous if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket later.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Investments are subject to capital gains taxes when sold, but they offer more flexibility and accessibility than retirement accounts.

3. **Diversification**: Ensure a mix of asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) across all accounts to optimize growth and risk management. You may want to hold more aggressive investments (like stocks) in tax-advantaged accounts and more conservative investments (like bonds) in taxable accounts to manage tax implications.

4. **Understand Withdrawal Rules**: Keep in mind age requirements and penalties for early withdrawals from 401(k)s and IRAs. Having a taxable account can provide the flexibility needed for unexpected expenses without penalties.

5. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: A tax professional or financial advisor can provide personalized insights based on your specific financial situation and goals.

6. **Review and Adjust Regularly**: As your situation or the market changes, reassess your distribution strategy to ensure it aligns with your current goals and risk tolerance.

This approach is meant to balance growth, tax efficiency, and liquidity as you work toward your long-term financial objectives.
