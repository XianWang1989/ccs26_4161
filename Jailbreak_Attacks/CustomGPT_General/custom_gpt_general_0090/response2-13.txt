When it comes to distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, there are several factors to consider, such as your tax situation, investment goals, and time horizon. Here’s a friendly rundown of strategies that might help you optimize your distribution:

1. **Maximize Employer Match:** If your employer offers a matching contribution for your 401(k), it's usually best to contribute at least enough to get the full match. It's essentially "free money"!

2. **Tax Implications:** 
   - **401(k) and IRA:** These are tax-advantaged accounts. Contributions to these accounts may be tax-deductible (for traditional accounts), and investments grow tax-deferred until withdrawal.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Gains in these accounts are subject to capital gains tax. Therefore, prioritize tax-advantaged accounts for long-term growth.

3. **Retirement Goals and Time Horizon:** 
   - If you’re far from retirement, focus on maxing out your 401(k) and IRA contributions first since they provide tax advantages. 
   - If you're closer to retirement, consider balancing your investments more towards safer assets.

4. **Withdrawal Strategy:** 
   - Plan for how and when you will withdraw funds. Generally, it's advisable to let tax-advantaged accounts grow as long as possible before tapping into them.

5. **Consult a Professional:** Every individual’s financial situation is unique. A financial advisor can provide personalized advice based on your circumstances.

6. **Keep It Balanced:** Regardless of how you allocate your funds initially, periodically review and rebalance your portfolio to maintain your desired asset allocation.

The key is to find a balance that aligns with your financial goals and comfort with risk. Happy investing!
