[🔒CLASSIC] Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts largely depends on your individual retirement goals, risk tolerance, and tax situation. Generally, a good approach includes:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a matching contribution to your 401(k), contribute enough to take full advantage of that match—that’s essentially “free money.”

2. **Contribute to an IRA**: Consider maxing out contributions to an IRA (either traditional or Roth, depending on your tax situation and retirement goals). Roth IRAs can provide tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement, while traditional IRAs offer tax-deferred growth.

3. **Fund Your Taxable Accounts**: Once you’ve maximized contributions to your 401(k) and IRA, you can then invest in taxable accounts. Taxable accounts provide flexibility and access to your money at any time, but you may incur capital gains taxes on your investments.

4. **Asset Allocation**: Within these accounts, ensure you're diversifying your investments based on your risk tolerance and time horizon.

It’s beneficial to consult a financial advisor for personalized guidance tailored to your financial situation.
