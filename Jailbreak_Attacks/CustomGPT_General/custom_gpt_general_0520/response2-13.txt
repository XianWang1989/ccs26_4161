To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Understand Tax Advantages:**
   - **401(k):** Contributions are often pre-tax, reducing your taxable income. Many employers offer matching contributions, which is essentially free money.
   - **IRA (Traditional or Roth):** Traditional IRAs provide tax-deferred growth, while Roth IRAs offer tax-free withdrawals in retirement. Choose based on your current versus expected future tax bracket.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** These are not tax-advantaged, but they provide flexibility in withdrawals and can be leveraged for short-term goals.

2. **Investment Goals and Time Horizon:**
   - Align your investment strategy with your short-term and long-term goals. Use your taxable account for short-term investments and your retirement accounts for long-term growth.

3. **Asset Allocation:**
   - Diversify your investments across asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) based on your risk tolerance and time horizon. Consider holding more volatile assets in tax-advantaged accounts to defer taxes on higher growth.

4. **Maximize Contributions:**
   - Aim to maximize contributions to your 401(k) and IRAs first, especially to get the employer match in your 401(k) if available. For 2024, the contribution limits are $23,000 for 401(k)s (plus $7,500 catch-up if you’re 50 or older) and $6,500 for IRAs (plus $1,000 catch-up if applicable).

5. **Tax Considerations:**
   - Keep tax-efficient assets in your taxable account (like index funds) to minimize capital gains tax. Let your high-growth potential investments grow tax-deferred in retirement accounts.

6. **Review and Adjust:**
   - Regularly review your asset allocations and re-balance as necessary, taking into account any changes in your financial situation or the market.

Consider consulting with a financial advisor to tailor strategies specific to your financial situation and goals.
