To effectively distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies based on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment horizon:

### 1. **401(k) Contributions**
   - **Maximize Employer Match:** Contribute enough to your 401(k) to capture any employer match, as this is essentially free money.
   - **Tax Deferral Benefits:** This account defers taxes until withdrawal, which is beneficial if you anticipate being in a lower tax bracket during retirement.
   - **Higher Contribution Limits:** If you're close to retirement, prioritize maxing out contributions to benefit from the higher limits compared to IRAs.

### 2. **IRA Contributions**
   - **Traditional vs. Roth IRA:**
     - **Traditional IRA:** Contributions may be tax-deductible, and taxes are paid on withdrawals, which can be advantageous if you expect to be in a lower tax bracket in retirement.
     - **Roth IRA:** Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, but withdrawals are tax-free. This is beneficial if you expect higher tax rates in the future.
   - **Diversification of Tax Treatment:** Having both types allows for tax diversification in retirement years.

### 3. **Taxable Accounts**
   - **Flexibility:** These accounts offer the most flexibility for withdrawals without penalties. This could be advantageous for short-term goals.
   - **Tax-Efficient Investments:** Consider holding investments that generate qualified dividends or long-term capital gains, which are taxed at lower rates compared to ordinary income.
   - **Avoiding Losses:** Be mindful of wash sale rules when realizing losses to offset gains.

### **General Allocation Strategy**
1. **Prioritize Contributions:**
   - Fund 401(k) up to the match.
   - Contribute to an IRA (either type, depending on your eligibility and tax situation).
   - Fund additional 401(k) or taxable accounts as needed.

2. **Assess Overall Balance:**
   - Diversify investments across accounts to manage risk and return.
   - Adjust contributions based on your overall portfolio performance and tax implications.

3. **Consult with a Financial Advisor:**
   - Consult a professional to tailor a specific strategy based on your individual financial situation.

### Conclusion
Your strategy should balance immediate tax benefits with long-term growth potential and withdrawal flexibility. Periodic review of your allocation and adjustments as your financial situation or goals change is essential. 

Seek advice tailored to your personal circumstances for the most effective approach.
