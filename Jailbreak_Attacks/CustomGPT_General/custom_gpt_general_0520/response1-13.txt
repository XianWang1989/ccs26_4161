Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and time horizon. Here are some general guidelines to consider:

1. **401(k) Contributions**:
   - **Maximize Employer Match**: Aim to contribute enough to get the full employer match if available, as this is essentially free money.
   - **Contribution Limits**: For 2023, the limit is $22,500 (or $30,000 if you're 50 or older). Make sure to utilize these limits if possible.

2. **IRA Contributions**:
   - **Tax Benefits**: Consider a Traditional IRA for tax-deductible contributions or a Roth IRA for tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement. The contribution limit for IRAs is $6,500 (or $7,500 if 50 or older) for 2023.
   - **Income Limits**: Be aware of the income thresholds for tax-deductible contributions to a Traditional IRA or contributions to a Roth IRA.

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - **Flexibility**: Invest in taxable accounts for short-term goals or if you want liquidity. They provide more flexibility compared to retirement accounts.
   - **Tax Efficiency**: Use tax-efficient investment strategies, such as holding index funds or tax-exempt bonds to minimize tax liabilities on capital gains and dividends.

4. **Asset Allocation**:
   - **Risk Tolerance**: Adjust your asset allocation according to your risk tolerance and investment horizon. Younger investors might prefer more equities, while those closer to retirement may prioritize bonds.
   - **Rebalancing**: Regularly rebalance your portfolio to maintain your desired asset allocation and manage risk.

5. **Tax Considerations**:
   - **Withdrawal Strategy**: Plan your withdrawals carefully in retirement to minimize taxes. Typically, withdrawing from taxable accounts first can reduce the tax burden on your retirement accounts.

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: Given the complexities of personal finance, consider working with a financial advisor to tailor a strategy specific to your situation.

Make sure your strategy aligns with your long-term financial goals and adjust it as your situation changes.
