The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment strategy. Here are some tips to consider:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts:**
   - **401(k):** If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to get the full match. It's free money and a great way to increase your retirement savings.
   - **IRA:** Consider contributing to a Traditional IRA or Roth IRA, depending on your income level and tax situation. Roth IRAs provide tax-free withdrawals in retirement, while Traditional IRAs may offer a tax deduction upfront.

2. **Asset Allocation:**
   - Consider your age, risk tolerance, and investment timeline. Generally, younger investors may prefer a more aggressive allocation (more stocks), while those closer to retirement might opt for a conservative approach (more bonds).
   - Diversify within each account to spread risk across different asset classes (stocks, bonds, real estate, etc.).

3. **Tax Implications:**
   - Understand how withdrawals from each account may affect your tax situation. Withdrawals from Traditional accounts are taxed as ordinary income, while qualified withdrawals from Roth accounts are tax-free.
   - Hold investments with high growth potential (like stocks) in tax-advantaged accounts and more tax-efficient investments (like bonds or index funds) in taxable accounts to maximize your overall tax efficiency.

4. **Reassess Regularly:**
   - Periodically review your allocations and make adjustments based on changes in your financial situation, market conditions, and goals.

5. **Consult a Professional:**
   - Consider talking to a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your specific situation and objectives.

By evaluating these factors, you can create a strategic distribution that aligns with your long-term financial goals.
