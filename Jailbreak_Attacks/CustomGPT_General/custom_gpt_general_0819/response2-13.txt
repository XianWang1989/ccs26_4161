The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, risk tolerance, and tax situation. Here are some general strategies:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**: 
   - Prioritize contributing to your 401(k) up to any company match, as this is free money.
   - Next, consider maximizing your IRA contributions (traditional or Roth, depending on your eligibility and retirement strategy).

2. **Consider Tax Implications**:
   - Taxable accounts can be used for investments that generate capital gains, which may be taxed at a lower rate than ordinary income.
   - Hold tax-efficient investments in taxable accounts (like index funds or ETFs) to minimize tax burden.

3. **Balance Risk and Growth**:
   - Allocate your more aggressive investments (e.g., stocks) in tax-advantaged accounts, where they can grow without immediate tax consequences.
   - Use safer investments (like bonds) in taxable accounts to avoid high tax implications on interest income.

4. **Rebalance Regularly**: 
   - Monitor and adjust your asset allocation based on market conditions and your changing goals.

5. **Consider Your Time Horizon**:
   - If retirement is far off, you can afford to take more risks in your retirement accounts.
   - For shorter-term goals, keep funds in safer, more liquid accounts.

Consulting with a financial advisor can provide personalized recommendations tailored to your specific situation.
