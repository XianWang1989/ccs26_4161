The best way to distribute your money amongst your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts largely depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment strategy. Here are some strategies to consider:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**: Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money. Then, consider maxing out your IRA contributions. The tax benefits from these accounts can significantly enhance your investments over time.

2. **Consider Tax Implications**: 401(k) and IRA accounts are tax-deferred, meaning you won't pay taxes on the money until you withdraw it in retirement. Taxable accounts don't offer these tax advantages, so be mindful of capital gains taxes when investing. 

3. **Diversify Investments**: Use your tax-advantaged accounts for higher growth investments (like stocks), as you won't be taxed on gains until withdrawal. For a taxable account, consider holding more conservative investments or income-generating assets, which could incur taxes.

4. **Balance Contributions Based on Life Stage**: Younger investors might focus more on growth-oriented investments and take full advantage of tax-advantaged accounts. As you get closer to retirement, consider shifting towards safer investments and withdrawing from taxable accounts to manage taxes.

5. **Regular Review and Adjustments**: Keep an eye on your asset allocation and make adjustments based on market conditions and your own financial situation.

Consulting with a financial advisor can provide personalized insights that align with your overall financial objectives.
