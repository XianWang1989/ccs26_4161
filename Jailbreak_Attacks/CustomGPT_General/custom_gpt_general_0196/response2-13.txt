Distributing your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some general strategies:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**
   - Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match. This is essentially free money.

### 2. **Fund IRA Contributions**
   - Consider contributing to a Traditional or Roth IRA. Roth IRAs offer tax-free growth and withdrawals if you meet certain conditions.

### 3. **Prioritize Tax Efficiency**
   - **Tax-Deferred Accounts (401(k), Traditional IRA):** Good for long-term growth; taxes are paid upon withdrawal.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Better for funds you may need sooner. Opt for investments with lower turnover to minimize capital gains taxes.
   - **Roth Accounts:** Ideal for long-term, high-growth investments due to tax-free growth.

### 4. **Asset Allocation**
   - Allocate more aggressive investments (like stocks) in tax-advantaged accounts (401(k), IRA) and more tax-efficient investments (like ETFs or index funds) in taxable accounts.

### 5. **Withdrawal Strategy**
   - As you approach retirement, plan your withdrawal strategy to minimize taxes while meeting your income needs.

### 6. **Regular Review and Rebalance**
   - Regularly assess your investments and rebalance to stay aligned with your risk tolerance and goals.

### Conclusion
Consult with a financial advisor for personalized advice that considers your unique situation, including income, age, and retirement goals.
