Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some strategies:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match**
   - **401(k):** Start by contributing enough to get any employer match, as it’s essentially free money.

### 2. **Consider Tax Implications**
   - **Tax-Advantaged Accounts (401(k) and IRA):** Contributions are often tax-deductible, and investments grow tax-deferred. Prioritize maxing out contributions to these accounts.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Use these for investments you may want to access before retirement, or for tax-efficient investments (like index funds) to minimize capital gains tax.

### 3. **Diversification of Investments**
   - **401(k):** Choose a mix of stocks, bonds, and funds based on your risk tolerance.
   - **IRA:** Use it for longer-term strategies, including more aggressive investments if you're younger.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Consider more liquid investments or those you might need to access early.

### 4. **Rebalance Regularly**
   - Periodically review your asset allocation across all accounts to maintain your desired risk level.

### 5. **Withdrawal Strategy**
   - Plan how you’ll withdraw funds in retirement to manage your tax liability effectively, prioritizing tax-advantaged accounts first.

### 6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**
   - Tailored advice can help align your strategy with your goals, especially regarding complex tax situations.

### Final Tips
- **Emergency Fund:** Ensure you have an adequate emergency fund outside these accounts.
- **Lifetime Savings:** Consider your overall savings needs, including retirement and other financial goals, to guide your distribution strategy.

Adjust your contributions based on changes in income, life circumstances, and financial goals.
