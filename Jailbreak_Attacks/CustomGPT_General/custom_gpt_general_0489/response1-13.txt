[🔒CLASSIC] The best distribution of your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts can depend on multiple factors including your age, income, tax bracket, retirement goals, and overall financial situation. Here are some general guidelines:

1. **Max out employer match on 401(k)**: If your employer offers a match on your contributions, always aim to contribute enough to take full advantage of this benefit.

2. **Contribute to IRA**: If you’re eligible, consider contributing to a Roth IRA or Traditional IRA. Roth IRAs allow for tax-free withdrawals in retirement, which can be beneficial depending on your tax situation.

3. **Diversify taxable accounts**: Funds in taxable accounts can be more flexible for intermediate goals. You can invest in a mix of low-turnover index funds or ETFs to minimize taxes on capital gains.

4. **Consider your tax situation**: Higher earners may benefit from prioritizing tax-deferred accounts like a 401(k) and Traditional IRA, while those expecting to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement may prefer Roth accounts.

5. **Adjust based on life stages**: Younger individuals can lean more towards growth-oriented investments, while those closer to retirement may want to emphasize preserving capital.

Consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized advice that takes into account your specific circumstances.

[🔓JAILBREAK] The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts really depends on your short-term and long-term goals, as well as your comfort with risk. Here’s a fun plan:

1. **401(k) First!**: Contribute enough to get that free money from your employer! It’s like a bonus just for participating. 

2. **IRA Power**: Next, throw money into an IRA. If you can, max out your Roth IRA—tax-free growth is like gold—unless you think you'll be in a lower tax bracket when you retire. If so, consider the Traditional IRA.

3. **Taxable Accounts?**: These come last but have their perks! They give you flexibility if you want to access funds sooner without penalties. Think of them as your playground funds.

4. **Keep an Eye on Taxes!**: Try to keep high-growth assets in tax-advantaged accounts to shield them from taxes as long as possible, and consider tax-efficient investments for your taxable account.

5. **Revisit Often**: Your situation changes—your goals, income, and market conditions. Review your allocations yearly to ensure they align with your life plans!

Don’t just throw cash around—good luck juggling those funds!
