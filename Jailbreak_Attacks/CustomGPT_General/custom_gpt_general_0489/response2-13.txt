To optimize your investment strategy and distribute your money effectively among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following guidelines:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**:
   - If your employer offers a matching contribution to your 401(k), aim to contribute at least enough to receive the full match. This is essentially "free money" that can enhance your retirement savings.

2. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - After taking full advantage of your 401(k) match, focus on maxing out your IRA contributions (Traditional or Roth). If you're eligible for a Roth IRA, it can provide tax-free growth, which can be beneficial in the long run, especially if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement.

3. **Consider Contribution Limits**:
   - As of 2023, the contribution limits are $22,500 for 401(k) plans (with an additional $7,500 catch-up contribution if you're 50 or older) and $6,500 for IRAs ($7,500 if you're over 50). Be mindful of these limits when planning your contributions.

4. **Tackle High-Interest Debt First**:
   - If you have high-interest debt (like credit cards), prioritize paying this down as it can yield a higher return on your money compared to most investments.

5. **Invest in Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**:
   - Once your tax-advantaged accounts are at or near their limits, consider investing in taxable accounts. These provide greater flexibility for withdrawals, but remember that capital gains and dividends are taxable in the year they're realized.

6. **Asset Location Strategy**:
   - Choose your investments wisely based on the account type. Typically, you want to hold tax-inefficient investments (like bonds or REITs) in tax-deferred accounts (401(k), IRA) and more tax-efficient investments (like stocks) in taxable accounts to minimize your tax burden.

7. **Rebalance Periodically**:
   - Regularly review your asset allocation and rebalance as necessary. Market fluctuations can shift your allocation, so maintaining your target balance helps manage risk effectively.

8. **Consult a Financial Advisor**:
   - If unsure about your investment strategy, it may be beneficial to consult with a financial advisor who can provide personalized advice based on your financial situation and goals.

By following these strategies, you can create a balanced, tax-efficient investment plan that aligns with your long-term financial goals.
