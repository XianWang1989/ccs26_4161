The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your current financial situation, future goals, tax considerations, and investment strategy. Here are some general guidelines to consider when making your distribution decision:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a matching contribution for your 401(k), prioritize contributing enough to get the full match as it’s essentially free money.

2. **Tax Considerations**: 
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are typically pre-tax, which reduces your taxable income for the year. However, withdrawals in retirement are taxed as ordinary income.
   - **IRA (Traditional vs. Roth)**: Traditional IRAs have tax-deductible contributions but are taxed upon withdrawal. Roth IRAs allow after-tax contributions, but withdrawals in retirement are tax-free.
   - **Taxable accounts**: Investment income such as dividends and capital gains in taxable accounts may be subject to taxes, so it’s important to consider the implications of holding various assets in this account.

3. **Investment Strategy**: Consider your risk tolerance and investment goals.
   - **Long-Term Growth**: If you have a long time horizon, you might prefer higher-risk investments in your tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) and IRA) for potential growth, while keeping more stable investments in your taxable account.
   - **Short-Term Goals**: If you need access to funds within a few years, consider keeping cash or stable investments in a taxable account.

4. **Diversification**: Ensure your overall portfolio is well-diversified across different asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) and sectors. This could influence how you allocate your funds among the different accounts.

5. **Withdrawal Strategy**: As you approach retirement, consider how you will withdraw from these accounts and develop a strategy that minimizes taxes and maximizes your income.

6. **Consulting with a Financial Advisor**: Given the complexity of financial planning, speaking with a financial advisor can provide personalized guidance tailored to your specific circumstances.

Ultimately, the ideal distribution will reflect your personal financial goals, risk tolerance, and tax situation. It might be beneficial to revisit and adjust your allocations over time as your financial situation and market conditions change.
