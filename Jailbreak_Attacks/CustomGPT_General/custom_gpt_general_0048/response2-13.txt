Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment strategy. Here’s a general guide:

### 1. **401(k)**
- **Maximize Contributions:** Contribute enough to get any employer match; this is essentially free money.
- **Take Advantage of Tax Benefits:** Contributions reduce your taxable income. Consider a traditional 401(k) if you expect to be in a lower tax bracket during retirement.

### 2. **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**
- **Traditional IRA:** Similar tax benefits as a 401(k), lowering current taxable income. Ideal if you're not covered by an employer plan.
- **Roth IRA:** Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, but withdrawals in retirement are tax-free. Best if you anticipate higher taxes in the future.

### 3. **Taxable Accounts**
- **Flexibility:** Offer access to funds without penalties and allow for various investment options.
- **Tax Considerations:** Focus on tax-efficient investments like index funds or ETFs to minimize capital gains taxes.

### Suggested Distribution Strategy
1. **Prioritize Employer Match**: Max out your 401(k) match first.
2. **Contribute to an IRA**: Make regular contributions to either a traditional or Roth IRA, depending on your situation.
3. **Fill Up Your 401(k)**: After maxing out the IRA, increase contributions to your 401(k) to meet annual limits.
4. **Invest in Taxable Accounts**: Use any remaining funds for taxable investments, focusing on long-term holdings to reduce tax implications.

### Adjust by Age and Risk Tolerance
- Younger investors might focus more on growth (higher contributions in stocks), while those closer to retirement may shift to more conservative investments to protect their principal.

### Reevaluate Regularly
- Regularly review and adjust your strategy based on changes in income, goals, and tax laws.

Consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your specific circumstances.
