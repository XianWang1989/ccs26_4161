To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider these key strategies:

### 1. **Understand the Purpose of Each Account:**
   - **401(k):** Employer-sponsored, often with matching contributions. Ideal for long-term growth with tax advantages.
   - **IRA:** Offers tax-deferred growth (traditional) or tax-free withdrawals (Roth). Great for retirement savings.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Flexible withdrawals, but subject to capital gains taxes. Best for short- to medium-term goals.

### 2. **Maximize Contributions:**
   - **401(k):** Aim to contribute enough to get any employer match. This is essentially "free money."
   - **IRA:** Contribute the maximum allowed, especially if you qualify for tax deductions.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Use these for any excess funds after maximizing your tax-advantaged accounts or for specific financial goals.

### 3. **Asset Allocation:**
   - Diversify your investments by balancing between stocks, bonds, and cash across all accounts. 
   - Place high-growth investments like stocks in tax-advantaged accounts to benefit from tax-free growth.
   - Use bonds or other income-generating investments in taxable accounts to manage tax implications.

### 4. **Tax Efficiency:**
   - Consider the tax implications of each account. For example, hold tax-inefficient investments (high dividend or bond funds) in tax-advantaged accounts, while keeping tax-efficient ones (like stock ETFs) in taxable accounts.

### 5. **Withdrawal Strategy:**
   - Plan for retirement withdrawals strategically to minimize taxes. Withdraw from taxable accounts first, then tax-deferred accounts, and leave Roth accounts for last.

### 6. **Regular Review and Rebalance:**
   - Periodically review and adjust your investments to align with your risk tolerance and financial goals.

### Conclusion
Ultimately, the best distribution depends on your age, income, retirement goals, and risk tolerance. Consulting a financial advisor can help tailor the strategy to your specific situation.
