Distributing your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your age, risk tolerance, investment goals, and tax situation. Here’s a general strategy:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First:**
   - **401(k):** If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to get the full match—it’s essentially free money. Aim to contribute the maximum allowable amount if possible ($22,500 for 2024, plus an additional $7,500 if you're over 50).
   - **IRA (Roth or Traditional):** After maximizing your 401(k), consider contributing to an IRA. The contribution limit for 2024 is $6,500 (plus $1,000 catch-up if you’re over 50). Choose a Roth IRA for tax-free growth if you qualify based on income.

### 2. **Invest Based on Goals and Time Horizon:**
   - Prioritize investments in accounts based on your goals (retirement vs. short-term needs).
   - Long-term (retirement) investments can be more aggressive; consider stocks or equity funds.
   - For short-term needs, maintain a conservative approach in taxable accounts.

### 3. **Consider Tax Implications:**
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Hold investments that generate lower taxes (e.g., index funds or ETFs) to minimize capital gains taxes. Use tax-loss harvesting to offset gains.
   - **Tax-Deferred Accounts:** These are ideal for high-growth investments since you won’t pay taxes on growth until withdrawal.

### 4. **Diversification and Maintenance:**
   - Diversify your asset allocation across all accounts, adjusting according to your overall risk tolerance.
   - Regularly rebalance your investment portfolio to maintain your desired asset allocation.

### 5. **Withdrawal Strategy:**
   - Plan your withdrawals with an eye on tax implications, especially in retirement. Generally, withdraw from taxable accounts first, then tax-deferred, and lastly tax-free accounts.

### 6. **Consult a Financial Advisor:**
   - Consider working with a financial advisor for personalized strategies tailored to your specific situation.

By using these strategies, you can optimize your investment distribution for growth and tax efficiency!
