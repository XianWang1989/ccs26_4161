The best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here's a general approach:

1. **Employer Match**: Max out contributions to your 401(k) up to the employer match, if available. This is essentially free money.

2. **Emergency Fund**: Ensure you have an emergency fund (typically 3-6 months of expenses) in a high-yield savings account before heavily investing.

3. **Max Out IRA Contributions**: If you’re eligible, consider maxing out your IRA contributions next. Traditional IRAs offer tax deductions, while Roth IRAs provide tax-free growth.

4. **401(k) Contributions**: After maxing out your IRA, consider increasing your 401(k) contributions beyond the match, especially if you expect a high tax rate in retirement.

5. **Taxable Accounts**: Allocate excess funds to taxable accounts for flexibility and access, particularly if you may need the money before retirement. Focus on tax-efficient investments (like index funds or ETFs) to minimize capital gains taxes.

6. **Rebalance**: Regularly review and adjust your asset allocation based on age, risk tolerance, and financial goals.

### Considerations:
- **Tax Bracket**: Your current and anticipated future tax brackets will influence whether to prioritize tax-deferred (401(k), Traditional IRA) or tax-free (Roth IRA) accounts.
- **Investment Horizon**: Longer time horizons may allow you to take on more risk in retirement accounts, while short-term investments might be better suited for taxable accounts.
- **Withdrawal Strategy**: Planning for your withdrawal strategy in retirement can also affect how you allocate your assets now, as distributions from tax-deferred accounts are taxed as ordinary income.

Consulting a financial advisor can provide personalized guidance tailored to your specific situation.
