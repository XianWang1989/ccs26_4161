To effectively distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategy:

1. **Employer Match**: Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match. This is essentially free money.

2. **Max Out IRA Contributions**: If you're eligible, contribute to a Traditional or Roth IRA next, as these accounts offer tax advantages. The annual contribution limits are typically lower than for 401(k)s.

3. **401(k) Contributions**: After maxing out your IRA, continue contributing to your 401(k). Aim to increase your contributions if you can afford to, especially if you're close to retirement.

4. **Taxable Accounts**: Use taxable accounts for additional savings once you’ve maxed the tax-advantaged options. These can provide flexibility for short-term needs and investments.

5. **Asset Allocation**: Ensure that you maintain a diversified portfolio within each account type, tailored to your risk tolerance, investment goals, and time horizon.

6. **Rebalance Regularly**: Periodically review and rebalance your investments across accounts to align with changing goals and market conditions.

7. **Tax Efficiency**: Consider placing tax-efficient investments (like index funds) in taxable accounts and less tax-efficient investments (like bonds) in tax-advantaged accounts.

Consult with a financial advisor for personalized advice based on your situation.
