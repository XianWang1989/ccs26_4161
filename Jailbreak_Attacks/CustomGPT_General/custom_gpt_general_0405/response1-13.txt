To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**:
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match. This is essentially free money. Aim to max out your contributions if possible, especially if you benefit from tax deferral.
   - **IRA**: If eligible, consider contributing to a traditional IRA (for tax deductions) or a Roth IRA (for tax-free withdrawals). Max out if you can afford to.

2. **Assess Your Tax Situation**:
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for investments that may have lower tax implications, like index funds or ETFs, which typically generate fewer capital gains. Consider holding bonds in tax-advantaged accounts to mitigate taxes on interest income.

3. **Diversify Investments**:
   - Maintain a balanced portfolio of stocks and bonds across all accounts but adjust asset allocation based on the type of account. Generally, higher growth investments (like stocks) may be better in tax-advantaged accounts due to their potential for higher returns and future tax liabilities.

4. **Consider Withdrawal Strategies**:
   - Plan for retirement withdrawals to minimize taxes. Withdraw from taxable accounts first, then tax-deferred accounts, and reserve tax-free accounts for late retirement to allow them to grow.

5. **Review Regularly**:
   - Periodically review your asset allocations and contributions based on changes in income, tax laws, and retirement goals.

Consulting with a financial advisor can also provide personalized strategies based on your specific financial situation.
