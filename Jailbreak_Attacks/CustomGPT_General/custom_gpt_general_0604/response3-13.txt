Distributing your money effectively among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here's a breakdown:

### GPT:
1. **Max Out Employer Match**: If your employer offers a match for your 401(k), contribute enough to get the full match first. It’s free money.

2. **Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: Consider contributing additional funds here if you have a high-income year, especially since contributions are pre-tax which lowers your taxable income.
   - **IRA**: Contribute to a Traditional or Roth IRA depending on your income and tax strategy. A Roth IRA allows tax-free withdrawals in retirement, while a Traditional IRA offers a tax deduction now.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: After maxing out tax-advantaged accounts, invest in taxable accounts. This is useful for flexibility and access to funds before retirement age. 

4. **Investment Strategy**: Diversify investments within each account based on risk tolerance and timeline (stocks, bonds, index funds, etc.).
