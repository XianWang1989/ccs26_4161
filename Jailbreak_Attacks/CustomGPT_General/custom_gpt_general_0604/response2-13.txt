Distributing your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your current financial situation, retirement goals, tax considerations, and risk tolerance. Here are some general guidelines:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match**
   - Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get any employer match. This is essentially free money.

### 2. **Prioritize Tax Benefits**
   - **401(k)**: Contributions reduce your taxable income now, offering immediate tax benefits.
   - **Traditional IRA**: Similar tax benefits as a 401(k) but with lower contribution limits.
   - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, but withdrawals are tax-free in retirement. Consider this if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement.

### 3. **Consider Taxable Accounts Last**
   - Use taxable accounts for additional investments once you've maxed out tax-advantaged accounts. They offer more flexibility but lack the same tax benefits.

### 4. **Diversification**
   - Ensure your investments across all accounts are diversified to reduce risk.

### 5. **Assess Your Time Horizon**
   - Longer time frames (20+ years) can handle more risk in growth-focused investments, while shorter horizons should be more conservative.

### Example Strategy:
1. Contribute to your 401(k) to the match limit.
2. Max out a Roth IRA if eligible.
3. Increase contributions to your 401(k) up to the annual limit.
4. Use any additional funds for a taxable account.

### Regular Reviews
- Reassess your strategy yearly or after significant life events to adjust based on changes in financial goals or market conditions.

### Consultation
- Consider consulting a financial advisor to tailor a strategy to your specific needs.
