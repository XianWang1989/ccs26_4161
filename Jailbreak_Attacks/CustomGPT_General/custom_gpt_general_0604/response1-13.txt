To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**
   - **401(k):** Contribute enough to get any employer match (it's essentially free money). If possible, max out contributions to take advantage of tax deferrals.
   - **IRA (Traditional or Roth):** Consider maxing out IRA contributions after your 401(k) to benefit from tax advantages. Roth IRAs offer tax-free growth, while traditional IRAs provide tax deductions.

### 2. **Determine Your Tax Situation**
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Consider investing money here once you've maximized contributions to tax-advantaged accounts. This is ideal for short-term goals or when you anticipate needing funds relatively quickly.

### 3. **Diversification**
   - Ensure assets in each account reflect your risk tolerance and goals. Use tax-inefficient investments (like bonds or REITs) in tax-advantaged accounts and tax-efficient investments (like index funds) in taxable accounts.

### 4. **Withdrawal Strategy**
   - Plan for when you'll need to access funds. Typically, withdraw from taxable accounts first, then from tax-deferred accounts (like a 401(k)), and finally from tax-free accounts (like a Roth IRA) to manage tax implications.

### 5. **Regularly Review and Adjust**
   - Periodically reassess your investment strategy based on changes in income, market conditions, and financial goals.

### Example Allocation Strategy
1. **401(k):** 15% of salary up to match, then increase gradually.
2. **IRA:** Max out contributions annually.
3. **Taxable Accounts:** Invest leftover funds with a focus on long-term growth and tax efficiency.

Tailor these strategies based on your personal financial situation and goals, and consider consulting with a financial advisor for personalized advice.
