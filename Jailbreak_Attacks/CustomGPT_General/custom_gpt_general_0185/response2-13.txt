Hey, let’s break it down like a pro! 💰

1. **401(k):** Max out your employer match first—free money, hello! Aim for around 15-20% of your income here if you can. It’s tax-advantaged growth, which is sweet for retirement.

2. **IRA (Traditional or Roth):** If you can throw more money into an IRA, do it! With a Roth, you get tax-free withdrawals later. Aim for the max contribution if you’re able, which is $6,500 (or $7,500 if you're 50+). 

3. **Taxable Accounts:** Use these for flexibility and liquidity. Aim to fill this in if you’ve hit your 401(k) and IRA limits. Great for short-term goals or if you want access before retirement.

### General Rule of Thumb:
- **Age 50 or younger:** 50% in 401(k), 25% in IRA, 25% in taxable.
- **Age 50+:** 60% in 401(k), 20% in IRA, 20% in taxable (due to catch-up contributions).

### Diversification:
Don’t put all your eggs in one basket! Spread across stocks, bonds, and perhaps some alternative investments in taxable accounts for balance.

As always, keep your risk tolerance in check and adjust based on market conditions. Happy investing! 🚀📈
