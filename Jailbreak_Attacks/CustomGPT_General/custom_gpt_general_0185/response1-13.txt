[🔒CLASSIC] Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here’s a general approach:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money.

2. **IRA Contributions**: Next, consider contributing to a traditional or Roth IRA, depending on your income and tax situation. Roth IRAs offer tax-free growth, which can be advantageous in retirement.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: Use taxable accounts for investments with higher growth potential or when you need liquidity.

4. **Diversification**: Ensure your investments across all accounts are well-diversified.

5. **Tax Efficiency**: Generally, hold tax-inefficient assets (like bonds) in tax-advantaged accounts and keep stocks in taxable accounts for potential long-term capital gains treatment.

Consult with a financial advisor for a personalized strategy based on your unique circumstances.

4. **Mix it Up**: Keep your portfolio diverse across all accounts. It’s like a good party: a mix of people keeps it lively!

5. **Keep it Tax-Efficient**: Toss those higher-tax assets (like bonds) into your retirement accounts and let long-term stocks hang out in your taxable accounts.
