The best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment horizon. Here’s a general approach:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a match on your 401(k), contribute enough to get the full match first, as this is essentially "free money."

2. **Fund Retirement Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: Contribute up to the maximum allowed, especially if you're in a higher tax bracket. This reduces your taxable income.
   - **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**: If you qualify, contribute to a Roth IRA for tax-free withdrawals in retirement, or a Traditional IRA for tax-deferred growth.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: After maximizing retirement accounts, consider a taxable account for additional savings, offering flexibility for withdrawals and potential capital gains.

4. **Diversify Investments**: Within each account, ensure a diversified mix of asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) based on your risk tolerance and investment timeline.

5. **Rebalance Regularly**: Periodically review and adjust your allocations to maintain your desired risk level and take advantage of market changes.

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: If uncertain, a financial planner can help personalize your strategy based on your specific situation.

By balancing contributions across these accounts with a focus on tax efficiency and retirement readiness, you'll enhance your long-term financial health.
