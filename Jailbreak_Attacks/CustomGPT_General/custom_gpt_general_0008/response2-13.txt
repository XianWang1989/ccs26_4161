Distributing your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts requires a strategic approach based on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some key considerations to help you decide:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a match on your 401(k), contribute enough to get the full match first. It's essentially free money.

2. **Tax Considerations**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are pre-tax, reducing your taxable income. Consider maxing out if you're in a high tax bracket.
   - **IRA**: Traditional IRAs offer similar tax benefits as 401(k)s, whereas Roth IRAs allow for tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement (if certain conditions are met).
   - **Taxable Accounts**: These are taxed on dividends and capital gains. Use these for savings goals that are shorter-term or for investments that are more tax-efficient.

3. **Diversification of Tax Treatment**: Aim for a balance between tax-deferred (401(k) and traditional IRA) and tax-free (Roth IRA) growth. This can provide more flexibility in retirement.

4. **Investment Goals**: 
   - If you're planning for long-term growth, tax-advantaged accounts are typically more beneficial due to their tax benefits.
   - For short-term goals, prioritize taxable accounts for easier access to funds.

5. **Withdrawal Strategy**: Consider your expected retirement income needs. Having funds in taxable accounts can provide liquidity without penalties, while tax-deferred accounts can provide income later when you may be in a lower tax bracket.

6. **Review Regularly**: Your personal financial situation and tax laws can change, so it’s important to review your allocations regularly and adjust as needed.

### Suggested Distribution:
1. **Maximize 401(k)**: Up to the employer match.
2. **Contribute to IRA**: Max out if eligible, particularly a Roth if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement.
3. **Allocate Remaining Funds**: Use taxable accounts for ongoing investments, emergency funds, or short-term goals.

### Example Allocation:
- 401(k): 15%
- IRA (Traditional/Roth): 10%
- Taxable Accounts: 5%

Tailor this according to personal circumstances, risk tolerance, and financial goals. Consulting a financial advisor for personalized advice can also be beneficial.
