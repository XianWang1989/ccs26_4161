Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment strategy. Here are some general guidelines to consider:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match (401(k))**
   - If your employer offers a match for your 401(k) contributions, aim to contribute enough to get the full match. This is essentially free money and a great return on your investment.

### 2. **Contribute to IRA**
   - If you’re eligible, consider contributing to an IRA (Traditional or Roth). These accounts have tax advantages that can significantly benefit your long-term savings. A Roth IRA offers tax-free growth and tax-free withdrawals in retirement, while a Traditional IRA allows for tax-deferred growth.

### 3. **Balance Contributions**
   - Once you’ve maximized your employer match in your 401(k) and contributed to an IRA, consider how much you can contribute to your taxable account. Taxable accounts offer more flexibility in accessing funds but do not have the same tax advantages as retirement accounts.

### 4. **Consider Tax Implications**
   - Taxable accounts may be subject to capital gains tax upon selling investments. Invest more in tax-efficient funds (like index funds) in these accounts.
   - Hold assets that generate high dividends or interest in tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) and IRA) to minimize tax liability.

### 5. **Diversification and Asset Allocation**
   - Diversify your investments across your accounts based on your risk tolerance and goals. Consider a mix of stocks, bonds, and other assets that align with your investment strategy.

### 6. **Regularly Review and Rebalance**
   - Periodically assess your asset allocation and make adjustments as needed. Rebalancing can help maintain your desired risk level and investment strategy.

### 7. **Understand Withdrawal Rules**
   - Be mindful of withdrawal rules for each account type, particularly 401(k) and IRA, which have specific regulations regarding early withdrawals and required minimum distributions (RMDs).

### Example Distribution Strategy:
- **401(k)**: 15-20% of your income (to maximize employer match and growth)
- **IRA**: Contribute the maximum allowed (e.g., $6,500 or $7,500 if age 50+ for 2023)
- **Taxable Accounts**: Use any remaining funds for investing, focusing on tax-efficient assets.

### Conclusion
Ultimately, the best distribution strategy will depend on your individual financial situation. Consulting a financial advisor can provide personalized recommendations based on your specific circumstances and goals.
