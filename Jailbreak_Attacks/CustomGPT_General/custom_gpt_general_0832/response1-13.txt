[🔒CLASSIC] The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, investment timeline, and risk tolerance. Here are some general guidelines:

1. **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money. The contribution limit for 2023 is $22,500 (or $30,000 if you’re 50 or older). Focus on maximizing this account, especially if your employer offers a matching contribution.

2. **IRA**: Consider contributing to a Traditional IRA or Roth IRA depending on your income level, tax situation, and retirement strategy. The contribution limit for IRAs in 2023 is $6,500 (or $7,500 if you’re 50 or older). 

3. **Taxable Accounts**: After maximizing your pre-tax and tax-advantaged accounts, consider investing in a taxable account. This can provide more flexibility with withdrawals and investment choices. Municipal bonds or tax-efficient index funds can be good options for these accounts.

Overall, prioritize contributing to your 401(k) (especially for matches), then consider your IRA for tax advantages, and finally, use taxable accounts for flexibility and additional investments.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: Once you’ve dominated the retirement realms, let your money breathe in a taxable account. This is where you can play a little with investments like stocks, ETFs, or high-dividend yield investments. Just be mindful of the capital gains tax!
