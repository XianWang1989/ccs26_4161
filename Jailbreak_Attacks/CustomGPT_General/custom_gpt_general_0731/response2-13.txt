Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some strategies:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**:
   - Begin by contributing enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match. This is essentially free money.

2. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **IRA/401(k)**: Focus on maximizing contributions to your tax-advantaged accounts. In 2023, you can contribute up to $22,500 to a 401(k) and $6,500 to an IRA, with catch-up contributions available if you're over 50. This helps reduce your taxable income.

3. **Consider Taxation**:
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Invest in them for funds you may need in the short term. Keep in mind that capital gains and dividends are taxed annually, whereas income in tax-advantaged accounts grows tax-deferred.

4. **Diversify Investments**:
   - Within each account type, diversify your investments (stocks, bonds, ETFs) according to your risk tolerance and timeline.

5. **Rebalance Regularly**:
   - Monitor and adjust your asset allocation periodically to ensure it aligns with your investment goals.

6. **Plan Withdrawals Strategically**:
   - As you approach retirement, plan which accounts to withdraw from first to minimize taxes and sustain your retirement income.

Consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized advice based on your specific situation.
