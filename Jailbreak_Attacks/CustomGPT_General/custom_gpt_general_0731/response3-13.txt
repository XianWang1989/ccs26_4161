To effectively distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

### 1. **Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**:
   - **Max Out 401(k)**: Contribute enough to your employer's 401(k) to get any available match. This is essentially free money.
   - **Max Out IRA**: Consider contributing to a Traditional or Roth IRA based on your income level and tax situation.

### 2. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - **Allocate Remaining Funds**: Once you've maximized contributions to tax-advantaged accounts, use taxable accounts for additional investments.
   - **Focus on Tax Efficiency**: Invest in funds with low turnover and consider municipal bonds for tax-free income.

### 3. **Investment Strategy**:
   - **Asset Allocation**: Diversify your investments across stocks, bonds, and other assets based on your risk tolerance and goals.
   - **Rebalance Regularly**: Adjust your allocations periodically to maintain your desired risk level.

### 4. **Long-Term Considerations**:
   - **Withdrawal Strategy**: Plan how and when you will withdraw from these accounts in retirement to minimize tax impact.
   - **Consult a Financial Advisor**: Personalized advice can help tailor your strategy to your specific financial situation.

By following these guidelines, you can optimize your investment distribution for growth and tax efficiency.
