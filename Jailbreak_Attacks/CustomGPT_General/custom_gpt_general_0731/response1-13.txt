Distributing your money effectively among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment horizon. Here are some considerations:

### 1. **401(k) Contributions**
- **Employer Match**: Always contribute enough to get any employer match, as it's essentially free money.
- **Tax Advantages**: Contributions are pre-tax or Roth (after-tax), which can lower your taxable income.

### 2. **IRA Contributions**
- **Roth vs. Traditional**: Decide based on your current vs. future tax rate. Roth IRAs allow tax-free withdrawals in retirement, while traditional IRAs provide tax deductions now.
- **Contribution Limits**: Keep in mind annual contribution limits ($6,500 per year as of 2023, plus $1,000 catch-up if 50 or older).

### 3. **Taxable Accounts**
- **Flexibility**: These offer more liquidity and access to funds without penalties, but they’re taxed on capital gains and dividends.
- **Invest for the Long Term**: Consider holding investments for over a year to benefit from lower long-term capital gains tax rates.

### General Strategy
- **Prioritize Accounts**: Max out the 401(k) match first, then focus on IRA contributions, and lastly contribute to taxable accounts as necessary.
- **Asset Allocation**: Invest based on your risk tolerance across all accounts, adjusting for tax implications (e.g., place bonds in tax-advantaged accounts to minimize taxable interest).

### Final Tips
- **Regular Rebalancing**: Review your allocations periodically to ensure they align with your goals.
- **Consult a Financial Advisor**: A professional can provide personalized advice based on your specific situation.

### Conclusion
A balanced approach that considers tax implications, contribution limits, and your financial goals will help optimize your investment strategy among these account types.
