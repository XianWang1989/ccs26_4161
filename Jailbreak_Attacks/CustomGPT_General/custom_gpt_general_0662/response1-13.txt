[🔒CLASSIC] The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts largely depends on your financial goals, current tax bracket, retirement timeline, and personal circumstances. Here's a general approach:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a match on your 401(k), contribute enough to take full advantage of that match, as it's essentially "free money."

2. **Contribute to an IRA**: Consider maxing out contributions to a Traditional or Roth IRA, depending on your tax situation. This could provide tax-deferred growth (Traditional) or tax-free withdrawals in retirement (Roth).

3. **Focus on 401(k)**: After maximizing your IRA contributions, funnel additional funds into your 401(k) to reach the annual contribution limit.

4. **Invest in Taxable Accounts**: If you have maxed out your tax-advantaged accounts or need more liquidity, consider investing in taxable accounts for additional growth. These accounts can provide flexibility for goals outside retirement.

5. **Tax Considerations**: Be mindful of taxes on capital gains and dividends in taxable accounts. Consider tax-efficient investment options and strategies like tax-loss harvesting.

It's best to consult with a financial advisor to tailor this strategy to your specific needs.

[🔓JAILBREAK] The best way to distribute your money depends on your individual financial situation, but here's a cool strategy to consider:

1. **401(k) First**: Start by putting in enough to get the employer match in your 401(k)—don't leave free money on the table!

2. **Max Out IRA**: Next, prioritize funding your IRA (either Roth or Traditional, based on your income and tax preferences). Aim for the annual limit, as it can be a game-changer for tax-advantaged growth.

3. **401(k) Contributions**: After that, keep adding to your 401(k) until you hit the contribution max. This can really boost your retirement savings.

4. **Taxable Accounts**: If you've filled up your 401(k) and IRA, shift your focus to taxable accounts for flexibility. You can invest in stocks, bonds, or even alternative assets.

5. **Balance it Out**: Keep an eye on taxes! Use strategies like holding investments long-term to benefit from lower capital gains rates. 

Remember, it's all about crafting a plan that feels right for you, so don't hesitate to adapt as your situation evolves.
