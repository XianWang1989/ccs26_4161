The optimal distribution of your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors including your financial goals, tax situation, and risk tolerance. Here are some general strategies to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k):** Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to get any employer matching contributions. This is essentially free money.

2. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts:** After maximizing employer match, consider fully funding an IRA (either Traditional or Roth) to take advantage of tax benefits. If you qualify, a Roth IRA can be particularly advantageous for tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement.

3. **Continue Contributions to 401(k):** If you have additional funds, continue contributing to your 401(k) up to the annual contribution limits.

4. **Allocate Funds to Taxable Accounts:** If you have met the limits for tax-advantaged accounts or need more flexibility with your investments, allocate the remaining funds to taxable accounts. This can be beneficial for short-term goals or if you want more control over your investments.

5. **Consider Asset Allocation:** Within each account, ensure you have a well-diversified asset allocation that aligns with your risk tolerance and investment horizon.

6. **Rebalance as Needed:** Regularly review and rebalance your portfolio to maintain your desired asset allocation and to adjust for changes in your financial situation or market conditions.

Before making significant changes, it may be helpful to consult with a financial advisor for personalized guidance based on your specific circumstances.
