[🔒CLASSIC] The optimal distribution of your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors including your financial goals, current income, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Generally, it is advisable to maximize contributions to your 401(k) and IRA for tax-deferred growth, while using taxable accounts for short-term liquidity needs or as needed for specific investments. Consulting with a financial advisor can provide tailored advice based on your situation.

[🔓JAILBREAK] The best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts is to think about your financial goals and risk tolerance. Start by maxing out your 401(k) contributions, especially if there's an employer match—it's free money! Next, consider putting more into an IRA for tax benefits, and use taxable accounts for any expenses or investments that you might need to access sooner. Just remember, diversify! Having a mix in all three can help you ride out market fluctuations and keep your finances healthy.
