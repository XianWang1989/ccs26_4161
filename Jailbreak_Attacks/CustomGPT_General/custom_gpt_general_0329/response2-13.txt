To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategy:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**: Contribute enough to get the full employer match before moving to other accounts. This is essentially "free" money.

2. **Fund an IRA**: Depending on your income, consider contributing to a traditional or Roth IRA. The tax advantages can be significant.

3. **Max Out 401(k)**: After funding your IRA, direct additional savings to your 401(k) up to the contribution limit for tax-deferred growth.

4. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**: Allocate any remaining funds to taxable accounts. They offer more flexibility for withdrawals but are subject to capital gains tax.

5. **Diversification**: Ensure that your investments across all accounts are diversified, factoring in your risk tolerance and investment horizons.

### Example Allocation:
- **401(k)**: 50%
- **IRA**: 30%
- **Taxable Accounts**: 20%

### Adjustments:
- **Age & Goal-Based**: Younger investors might prioritize growth, while those nearing retirement may focus on income preservation.

### Revisit Regularly:
- Keep an eye on your allocations and adjust based on changes in tax laws, your income, or life circumstances.
