It's essential to balance your investments based on your financial goals, tax situation, and risk tolerance. Here's a strategic approach:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match; ideally max out contributions.
   - **IRA**: If eligible, contribute to a Roth or traditional IRA for tax advantages.

2. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - After maximizing retirement accounts, allocate extra savings to taxable accounts for flexibility and liquidity.

3. **Diversification**:
   - Ensure a mix of stocks, bonds, and other assets across all accounts, tailored to your risk tolerance.

4. **Investment Strategies**:
   - Use tax-efficient investments in taxable accounts (e.g., index funds, ETFs) to minimize capital gains taxes.

5. **Rebalance Regularly**:
   - Monitor your allocations annually to adjust for changes in market conditions and personal goals.

This approach helps optimize growth while managing tax implications. Always consult with a financial advisor for personalized guidance.
