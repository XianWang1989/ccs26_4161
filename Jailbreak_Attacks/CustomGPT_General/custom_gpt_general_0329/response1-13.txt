To effectively distribute your money between a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider these principles:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match first. This is essentially free money.
   - **IRA**: After maxing the 401(k) match, consider contributing to an IRA (Roth or Traditional) based on your tax situation.

2. **Tax Treatment**:
   - **Roth Accounts**: Contributions are made after-tax, but withdrawals in retirement are tax-free. Good if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket later.
   - **Traditional Accounts**: Contributions reduce taxable income now, but withdrawals are taxed later. Favorable if you expect to be in a lower tax bracket in retirement.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: 
   - Use these for short-term goals or emergencies due to their flexibility. Be mindful of capital gains taxes on investments held for less than a year.

4. **Asset Allocation**: 
   - Consider your risk tolerance and investment timeline. Generally, higher-risk investments (like stocks) should be in tax-advantaged accounts to avoid taxes on capital gains.

5. **Rebalance Regularly**: Adjust allocations based on performance and changing financial goals.

**Sample Allocation Strategy**:
- **401(k)**: 15-20% of your income (maximize employer match).
- **IRA**: Contribute the maximum allowable each year.
- **Taxable Accounts**: Use excess funds after maxing tax-advantaged accounts for additional investments.

Consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your specific situation.
