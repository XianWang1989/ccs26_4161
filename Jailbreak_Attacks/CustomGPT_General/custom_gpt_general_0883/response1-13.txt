The best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some general strategies to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a matching contribution for your 401(k), try to contribute enough to get the full match. This is essentially "free money."

2. **Consider Tax Implications**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are often pre-tax, reducing your taxable income, but withdrawals in retirement are taxed as ordinary income.
   - **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**:
     - **Traditional IRA**: Similar to a 401(k), you may get a tax deduction on contributions, but withdrawals in retirement will be taxed.
     - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, but qualified withdrawals are tax-free in retirement.
   
3. **Emergency Fund**: Ensure you have sufficient savings in a liquid account (not tied up in retirement accounts) to cover at least 3-6 months of expenses.

4. **Allocate Funds Based on Goals**: If you have short-term goals (e.g., buying a house), consider taxable accounts for easier access. For long-term goals (like retirement), focus on tax-advantaged accounts.

5. **Diversification**: Spread investments across different asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) and ensure your overall portfolio is well-diversified to manage risk effectively.

6. **Revisit Regularly**: Investment needs can change, so review and adjust your allocations periodically based on age, income changes, and financial goals.

If you're uncertain, consider consulting a financial advisor who can provide personalized guidance based on your unique situation.
