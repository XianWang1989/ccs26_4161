The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some general strategies to consider:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**: 
   - **401(k)**: If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to get the full match first. This is essentially free money.
   - **IRA**: Once you’re maximizing your 401(k) match, consider contributing to an IRA (either traditional or Roth) depending on your income and tax situation.

2. **Consider Contribution Limits**: 
   - Be aware of the contribution limits for each account type. As of 2023, individuals can contribute up to $22,500 to their 401(k) (or $30,000 if age 50 or older) and $6,500 to an IRA (or $7,500 if age 50 or older).

3. **Tax Implications**: 
   - Traditional 401(k) and IRA contributions are tax-deductible now but taxed upon withdrawal. Roth accounts involve after-tax contributions, allowing for tax-free withdrawals in retirement.
   - Taxable accounts can incur capital gains taxes when you sell investments, so it may be beneficial to hold more tax-efficient investments there.

4. **Investment Goals & Timeline**:
   - If your goals are long-term, lean heavily into your retirement accounts. If you have short-term goals (like buying a house in a few years), consider keeping funds in a taxable account for easier access.

5. **Asset Allocation**:
   - Consider how you want to allocate your investments across these accounts. Stocks might be better in retirement accounts where they can grow tax-deferred, while bonds could be placed in taxable accounts to minimize tax impacts.

6. **Rebalance Regularly**: 
   - Over time, your asset allocation may drift from your goals. Regularly review and rebalance your accounts to maintain your desired investment strategy.

Consulting with a financial advisor can also provide personalized advice tailored to your specific financial situation.
