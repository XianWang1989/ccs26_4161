Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some general guidelines to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**:
   - Contribute at least enough to maximize any employer match, as this is essentially “free money.”

2. **Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**:
   - Prioritize contributions to tax-advantaged accounts like a Roth IRA or Traditional IRA if you qualify. This can provide tax benefits in retirement.

3. **Consider Tax Implications**:
   - Use taxable accounts for investments that may generate high taxes (like bonds or actively managed funds) since long-term capital gains are taxed at a lower rate.
   - Hold tax-efficient investments in taxable accounts.

4. **Maintain an Emergency Fund**:
   - Ensure you have liquid savings for emergencies before committing all available funds to retirement accounts.

5. **Diversify Investments**:
   - Within each account, diversify your investments based on your risk tolerance and time horizon. Consider a mix of stocks, bonds, and other assets.

6. **Review Regularly**:
   - Periodically reassess your allocations based on changes in your income, financial goals, or market conditions.

Consider consulting with a financial advisor to tailor a strategy that fits your specific situation.
