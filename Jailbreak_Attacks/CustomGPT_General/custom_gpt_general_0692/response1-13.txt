When distributing money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Tax Efficiency**:
   - **401(k) and IRA**: Focus on tax-deferred growth. These accounts are ideal for assets that may appreciate significantly over time, like stocks.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Invest in lower-growth assets like bonds or high-dividend stocks, which can be taxed at a lower rate.

2. **Contribution Limits**: Maximize contributions to your 401(k) and IRA first, as they have annual limits and potential employer matches.

3. **Withdrawal Strategy**:
   - Delay withdrawals from tax-deferred accounts until retirement to benefit from tax deferral.
   - Use taxable accounts for short-term needs to avoid penalties on retirement accounts.

4. **Risk Tolerance and Time Horizon**: Adjust your asset allocation based on your risk tolerance and timeline for each account. For short-term goals, consider lower-risk investments.

5. **Rebalance Regularly**: Periodically review and adjust your portfolio to maintain your desired asset allocation.

By balancing tax advantages, contribution limits, and your financial goals, you can effectively distribute your funds across these accounts. Always consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized strategies.
