The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment horizon. Here are some general guidelines to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your 401(k) offers an employer match, contribute enough to receive the full match. This is essentially free money.

2. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: Contribute up to the limit if your employer provides a match and consider maximizing this account for its tax deferral benefits. It may have limited investment options.
   - **IRA (Traditional/Roth)**: Depending on your income level, you may want to contribute to a Roth IRA (for tax-free growth) or a Traditional IRA (for tax-deferred growth). These accounts often have more investment options than 401(k)s.

3. **Invest in Taxable Accounts Last**: Use taxable accounts for additional investments after maximizing contributions to your tax-advantaged accounts. Be mindful of capital gains taxes, especially if you’re investing in growth-oriented securities.

4. **Consider Asset Allocation**: Depending on your risk tolerance, you might want to hold more aggressive investments in tax-advantaged accounts where they can grow tax-free or tax-deferred. Conversely, more stable investments, which may generate taxable income, could be better suited for taxable accounts.

5. **Review Regularly**: Periodically reassess your investment strategy and asset allocation in all accounts, especially as your financial situation and goals change.

It’s often convenient to consult with a financial advisor to tailor a plan specific to your situation for optimal results.
