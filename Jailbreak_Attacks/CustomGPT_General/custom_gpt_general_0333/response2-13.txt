Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts can optimize your tax efficiency and help you achieve your investment goals. Here are some key strategies to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a 401(k) match, contribute enough to receive the full match first. This is essentially "free money."

2. **Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**: After securing the match, prioritize contributions to tax-advantaged accounts:
   - **401(k)**: If you have a high income or expect to be in a lower tax bracket during retirement, consider maxing this out. Traditional contributions reduce your taxable income.
   - **IRA**: Depending on your income, you can choose between a Traditional IRA (tax-deductible now, taxed later) and a Roth IRA (contributions taxed now, withdrawals tax-free).

3. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**: Once you reach the limits of your tax-advantaged accounts, invest in taxable accounts. They offer more flexibility for withdrawals and are suitable for short-term goals.

4. **Asset Location Strategy**: Place investments based on their tax efficiency:
   - **Tax-inefficient investments** (like bond funds and REITs) are best held in tax-advantaged accounts.
   - **Tax-efficient investments** (like index funds) can go into taxable accounts, as they typically incur lower capital gains taxes.

5. **Regularly Review and Rebalance**: Keep an eye on your asset allocation and rebalance as needed to stay aligned with your risk tolerance and investment goals.

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: A professional can help tailor your strategy based on your specific financial situation, goals, and retirement plans.

By following these steps, you can effectively manage your investments across different account types to maximize your returns and minimize your tax burden.
