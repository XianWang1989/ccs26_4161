Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and time horizon. Here are some considerations for each account type:

### 1. **401(k)**
- **Benefits**: Employer match (if available), tax-deferred growth.
- **Allocation Strategy**: Contribute enough to get the full employer match before allocating funds elsewhere. Max out contributions if possible, especially if you are in a high tax bracket.

### 2. **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**
- **Benefits**: Tax advantages (traditional provides tax-deferred growth, Roth allows for tax-free withdrawals in retirement).
- **Allocation Strategy**: If eligible, consider a Roth IRA for tax-free growth and withdrawals, especially if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement. Use a traditional IRA to reduce current taxable income if you’re in a higher bracket.

### 3. **Taxable Accounts**
- **Benefits**: Flexibility (access funds anytime without penalties), control over investment choices.
- **Allocation Strategy**: Use these for short- to medium-term goals. Focus on tax-efficient investments (like index funds or ETFs) to minimize capital gains taxes.

### General Tips:
- **Diversification**: Ensure your overall portfolio is diversified across asset classes and sectors.
- **Rebalancing**: Regularly review and adjust allocations based on changes in your financial goals or market conditions.
- **Tax Considerations**: Be mindful of the tax implications for withdrawals, especially in retirement.

### Suggested Approach:
1. Max out employer match in your 401(k).
2. Contribute to an IRA to enhance tax-advantaged growth.
3. Invest extra savings in a taxable account for flexibility.

Assess your situation periodically and adjust your strategy based on changes in income, expenses, and financial goals. Consulting a financial advisor for personalized advice can also be beneficial.
