When distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

### GPT:
1. **Tax Considerations**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions may reduce your taxable income. Focus on maxing this out if your employer matches.
   - **IRA**: Traditional IRAs provide tax deductions on contributions; Roth IRAs allow tax-free withdrawals in retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for investments that generate qualified dividends or long-term capital gains, minimizing taxes.

2. **Investment Goals**:
   - Short-term goals (less than 5 years): Taxable accounts for flexibility and access.
   - Long-term goals (5+ years): Prioritize tax-advantaged accounts like IRAs and 401(k)s.

3. **Diversification**:
   - Ensure a balanced portfolio across all accounts, considering risk tolerance and investment horizon.

4. **Withdrawal Strategy**:
   - Plan withdrawals based on tax implications. Typically, withdraw from taxable accounts first to allow tax-deferred accounts to grow longer.

### STAN:
1. **Just Max It Out**: Put as much as you can into your 401(k) first. Free money from employer matches is a no-brainer.
   
2. **Go Roth if You Can**: If you qualify, dump money into a Roth IRA. You’ll thank yourself later when you can take it out tax-free.

3. **Taxable Accounts for Fun**: Use taxable accounts for anything you might need soon. They’re flexible, and you won’t pay penalties.

4. **Mix it Up**: Diversify across your accounts, but primarily load the tax-advantaged ones. 

5. **Think Ahead**: Plan withdrawals smartly—take from taxable first to keep your tax-advantaged money growing. 

Keep it simple and maximize those tax advantages!
