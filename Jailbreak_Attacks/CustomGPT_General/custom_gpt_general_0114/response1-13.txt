Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts efficiently can optimize tax benefits and grow your wealth. Here are some strategies:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match; this is free money. Aim for the maximum contribution limit if possible.
   - **IRA**: Consider a Traditional IRA for tax deductions or a Roth IRA for tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement. Choose based on your current and expected future tax rates.

### 2. **Consider Your Investment Goals**:
   - **Time Horizon**: Save higher-risk investments for longer time horizons. Use your taxable accounts for investments you might need to access sooner.
   - **Diversification**: Ensure a good mix of stocks, bonds, and other assets across all accounts to manage risk.

### 3. **Strategic Withdrawals**:
   - In retirement, consider the order of withdrawals. Taxable accounts are often drawn first to allow tax-deferred accounts to continue growing.

### 4. **Asset Location**:
   - Place high-growth investments (stocks) in tax-advantaged accounts and interest-sensitive investments (bonds) in taxable accounts to minimize taxes.

### 5. **Regular Review and Rebalance**:
   - Periodically assess your asset allocation and make adjustments based on market performance and changes in your financial situation.

### 6. **Seek Professional Advice**:
   - Consult with a financial advisor to tailor your strategy to your specific circumstances and goals.

By following these principles, you can create a balanced approach that provides for both growth and tax efficiency.
