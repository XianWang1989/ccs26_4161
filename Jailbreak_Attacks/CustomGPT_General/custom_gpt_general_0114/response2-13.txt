Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts can optimize your tax situation and grow your retirement savings. Here are some strategies:

1. **Maximize Employer Matches**: Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match. This is essentially free money.

2. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: Contribute up to the matched amount, then consider increasing contributions based on your financial goals.
   - **IRA**: If you qualify for a Roth IRA, consider contributing there as withdrawals in retirement can be tax-free.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for investments that may be less tax-efficient, like stocks held long-term to benefit from lower capital gains tax rates. 

4. **Asset Allocation**:
   - **Tax-Deferred Accounts (401(k) and IRA)**: Focus on growth-oriented investments (stocks) since you won’t pay taxes until withdrawal.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Consider including tax-efficient investments like index funds or ETFs, which can minimize taxable events.

5. **Consider Age and Risk Tolerance**: Tailor your allocation based on how many years you have until retirement and your comfort with market fluctuations.

6. **Regularly Reassess**: Review and adjust your allocations regularly based on changes in income, expenses, and market conditions.

Consult with a financial advisor for a personalized plan that aligns with your specific situation.
