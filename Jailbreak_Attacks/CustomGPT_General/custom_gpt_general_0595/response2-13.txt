Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here's a general approach:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**: If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to take full advantage of this benefit. It's essentially "free money."

2. **Contribute to an IRA**: After maximizing your 401(k) match, consider contributing to a Traditional or Roth IRA. A Traditional IRA may offer immediate tax benefits, while a Roth IRA provides tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement.

3. **Prioritize Taxable Accounts**: Once you've contributed to your 401(k) and IRA, any additional savings can go into taxable accounts. This gives you flexibility for short-term goals, although you'll pay capital gains taxes on any investment gains.

4. **Consider Tax Efficiency**: For taxable accounts, prioritize tax-efficient investments (like index funds or ETFs) to minimize tax impact. Keep high-growth investments in tax-advantaged accounts to defer taxes on gains.

5. **Evaluate Your Timeline**: If you're closer to retirement, a more conservative strategy may be appropriate. In contrast, younger investors can often afford to take on more risk with equities.

6. **Review Regularly**: Re-evaluate your distribution annually or after significant life changes (like a job change or family growth). Adjust contributions based on changes in income, expenses, and financial goals.

By taking these factors into account, you can create a balanced distribution strategy that aligns with your financial objectives. If needed, consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized advice.
