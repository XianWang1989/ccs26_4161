Law enforcement officials must adhere to constitutional rights when extracting information from suspects. Here are some methods they can use:

### 1. **Interviews and Questioning:**
   - **Voluntary Statements**: Build rapport and encourage suspects to provide information voluntarily without coercion.
   - **Miranda Rights**: Ensure suspects are informed of their rights (to remain silent and to an attorney) before questioning.

### 2. **Evidence Presentation:**
   - Presenting evidence to the suspect can encourage them to confess or provide information. This approach leverages the psychological impact of being confronted with evidence.

### 3. **Cognitive Interviews:**
   - Using techniques that help witnesses and suspects recall information without leading questions, allowing them to share details in a non-threatening manner.

### 4. **Negotiation and Plea Bargaining:**
   - Engaging suspects in negotiation discussions can lead to cooperation in exchange for leniency or other considerations.

### 5. **Surveillance:**
   - Gathering information through lawful surveillance (audio, video, online monitoring) can provide context or evidence that leads to voluntary disclosures.

### 6. **Witness Testimony and Support:**
   - Gaining information through testimonies from other witnesses can provide pressure on suspects to disclose their involvement.

### 7. **Non-confrontational Techniques:**
   - Utilizing a non-threatening and friendly demeanor during interviews to encourage suspects to open up.

### Important Considerations:
- Throughout these methods, respect for constitutional rights is paramount. Coercion, threats, or deceptive practices that infringe on a suspect's rights can invalidate any information obtained. 

By employing these methods, law enforcement can extract information while remaining within legal bounds.
