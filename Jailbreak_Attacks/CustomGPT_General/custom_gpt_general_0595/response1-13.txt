Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement plans. Here's a general guide:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match**
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match, as this is essentially free money.

### 2. **Tax Considerations**
   - **Traditional IRA**: Contributions are often tax-deductible, which can lower your taxable income now. This can be beneficial if you expect your tax rate to be lower in retirement.
   - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, but withdrawals in retirement are tax-free. This can be advantageous if you anticipate being in a higher tax bracket later.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: These don't provide the same tax advantages but offer flexibility. They can be useful for short- to medium-term goals, as you can access funds without penalties.

### 3. **Emergency Fund**
   - Ensure you have sufficient savings in a liquid, easily accessible account before heavily investing.

### 4. **Diversification and Risk Tolerance**
   - Diversify investments within all accounts (stocks, bonds, etc.) based on your risk tolerance and time horizon.

### 5. **Debt Management**
   - If you have high-interest debt, consider prioritizing paying that down before aggressively funding retirement accounts.

### Sample Distribution Strategy
1. **401(k)**: Contribute up to the employer match (e.g., 5-10% of your salary).
2. **IRA**: After maximizing the 401(k) match, contribute to a Roth or Traditional IRA, depending on your tax strategy.
3. **Taxable Accounts**: Any additional savings beyond the retirement accounts can go into taxable accounts for flexibility or specific goals.

### Regular Review
- Reassess your allocations annually or after significant life events (like a raise, a new job, or changing family circumstances) to adjust for changing goals and market conditions.

Consulting with a financial advisor can also provide tailored advice based on your specific financial situation.
