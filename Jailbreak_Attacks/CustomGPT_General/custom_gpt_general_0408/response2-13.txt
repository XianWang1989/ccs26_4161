When considering how to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, here are some general guidelines:

### 1. **Max Out Employer Match (401(k))**
   - Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match, as this is essentially free money.

### 2. **Prioritize High-Interest Debts**
   - Pay off any high-interest debts before contributing heavily to retirement accounts.

### 3. **Max Out IRA Contributions**
   - If you can afford to, max out your IRA contributions (Traditional or Roth) next. These accounts offer tax advantages that can be beneficial for long-term growth.

### 4. **Continue Contributing to Your 401(k)**
   - After maximizing your IRA, continue contributing to your 401(k) up to the annual limit.

### 5. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**
   - Allocate funds to taxable accounts for more flexibility and liquidity. These accounts can be beneficial for shorter-term goals or emergencies.

### 6. **Consider Asset Allocation**
   - Diversify your investments between stocks, bonds, and other assets based on your risk tolerance and time horizon.

### 7. **Rebalance Regularly**
   - Review and adjust your allocations periodically to ensure they align with your financial goals.

### 8. **Tax Considerations**
   - Keep in mind how taxes impact your investments in taxable accounts versus tax-deferred accounts.

### Summary
- **401(k)**: Get the match, then contribute heavily.
- **IRA**: Max out if possible.
- **Taxable**: Use for flexibility after retirement accounts are funded.

Tailor these strategies based on your specific financial situation, goals, and timeline. Consulting with a financial advisor can also provide personalized guidance.
