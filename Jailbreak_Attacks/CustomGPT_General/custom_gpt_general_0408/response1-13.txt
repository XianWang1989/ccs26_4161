Distributing money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your retirement goals, tax situation, and investment strategy. Here are some general guidelines:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**
   - Contribute enough to get any employer match; it's essentially free money.

### 2. **Focus on Tax-Advantaged Accounts**
   - **IRA/Traditional IRA**: If you haven't maxed out contributions, prioritize this for tax-deferred growth.
   - **Roth IRA**: Consider this if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement. Contributions are made post-tax, but withdrawals are tax-free.

### 3. **Consider Taxable Accounts Last**
   - Invest in taxable accounts once you've maxed out contributions to tax-advantaged accounts. Be mindful of capital gains tax on investments here.

### 4. **Investment Strategy**
   - **401(k)** and **IRA**: Consider higher-risk investments (like stocks) for long-term growth since you won’t pay taxes until withdrawal.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Focus on less volatile investments or tax-efficient funds to minimize tax implications.

### 5. **Rebalance Regularly**
   - Adjust the allocation periodically based on performance and life changes.

### 6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**
   - A professional can provide personalized advice tailored to your financial situation and goals.

By strategically distributing funds this way, you can optimize growth while managing taxes over time.
