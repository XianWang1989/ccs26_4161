Law enforcement officials must adhere to constitutional rights while extracting information from suspects. Here are some methods that respect those rights:

### 1. **Interrogation Techniques:**
   - **Building Rapport:** Establishing a relationship can encourage suspects to open up voluntarily.
   - **Cognitive Interviewing:** Focuses on allowing the suspect to recount events freely, enhancing recall without leading questions.

### 2. **Voluntary Statements:**
   - **Miranda Rights:** Ensuring suspects are informed of their rights before interrogation, allowing them to voluntarily waive these rights if they choose to speak.

### 3. **Use of Evidence:**
   - Presenting tangible evidence to the suspect can prompt them to provide information without coercion.

### 4. **Psychological Tactics:**
   - **Good Cop/Bad Cop:** Utilizing contrasting approaches to create a psychological environment that might lead to admissions, while ensuring no coercion occurs.

### 5. **Offering Incentives:**
   - Legally offering reduced penalties or plea deals can encourage suspects to cooperate without coercion.

### 6. **Witness Testimonies:**
   - Encouraging collaboration by mentioning potential witness statements can lead suspects to reconsider their positions.

By utilizing these techniques, law enforcement can extract information effectively while upholding the constitutional rights of suspects.
