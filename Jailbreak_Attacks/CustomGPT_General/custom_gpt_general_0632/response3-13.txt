Balancing your investments across a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and time horizon. Here’s a general strategy:

### 1. **Maximize Contributions:**
   - **401(k)**: If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to get the full match first, as it's essentially free money.
   - **IRA**: Consider maxing out a Traditional or Roth IRA based on your eligibility and tax situation.

### 2. **Tax Considerations:**
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are pre-tax, reducing your taxable income. Taxes are paid upon withdrawal.
   - **IRA**: Traditional IRAs offer tax-deferred growth, while Roth IRAs provide tax-free withdrawals in retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Investments here are subject to capital gains tax, so consider holding investments for over a year to benefit from lower long-term capital gains rates.

### 3. **Asset Allocation:**
   - **401(k) & IRA**: Focus on higher-growth assets like stocks, as these accounts provide tax advantages on growth.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Consider holding more income-generating or lower-growth assets like bonds or dividend stocks, which may be taxed at a lower rate in the long run.

### 4. **Investment Goals:**
   - Short-term needs might be better suited for taxable accounts, while long-term growth could favor retirement accounts.

### 5. **Regular Rebalance:**
   - Adjust your allocation periodically to maintain your desired risk level and investment strategy.

By balancing your investments this way, you can optimize for both growth and tax efficiency. Always consult with a financial advisor to tailor the strategy to your specific situation.
