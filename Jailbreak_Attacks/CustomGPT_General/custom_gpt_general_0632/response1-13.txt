When distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider these key factors:

1. **Tax Advantages**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are tax-deferred, reducing taxable income now. Choose this for higher contributions if your employer offers matching.
   - **IRA**: Traditional IRAs provide tax deductions, while Roth IRAs allow for tax-free withdrawals in retirement. Choose based on your current vs. expected future tax rate.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: These do not have tax advantages but offer flexibility. Capital gains and dividends are taxed in the year they’re realized.

2. **Investment Goals**:
   - Consider your time horizon and risk tolerance. Long-term goals may favor higher-risk investments in tax-advantaged accounts, while shorter-term needs may be better suited for taxable accounts.

3. **Withdrawal Strategy**:
   - Plan for when and how you'll withdraw funds. Typically, you should withdraw from taxable accounts first, then tax-deferred accounts, reserving tax-free accounts like Roth IRAs for later to maximize growth.

4. **Diversification**:
   - Ensure your investments across all accounts are diversified. This helps manage risk and optimize returns.

5. **Regular Review**:
   - Adjust your allocation as your financial situation changes, such as income changes or retirement planning stages.

### Example Allocation Strategy:
- **401(k)**: Max out contributions up to employer match.
- **IRA**: Contribute to a Roth or Traditional IRA, depending on tax situation.
- **Taxable Account**: Invest any additional savings in a mix of stocks, bonds, or mutual funds.

Consult a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your specific situation and goals.
