To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your 401(k) offers a matching contribution, contribute enough to get the full match. This is essentially free money.

2. **Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**:
   - **401(k) and IRA Contributions**: Prioritize contributions to tax-advantaged accounts like your 401(k) and IRA. These accounts can grow tax-deferred, or in the case of Roth accounts, tax-free.
   - Consider maxing out your IRA contributions if you qualify based on income limits.

3. **Diversify Account Types**: 
   - Aim for a mix of traditional (pre-tax) and Roth (after-tax) accounts. This allows flexibility in retirement for tax planning.

4. **Emergency Fund**: Ensure you have an emergency fund in a readily accessible form (like a savings account) before investing heavily in retirement accounts.

5. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**: Once you’re maximizing tax-advantaged accounts, consider investing in taxable accounts for additional flexibility and access to funds without penalties.

6. **Asset Allocation**: 
   - Within each account type, diversify by investing across various asset classes (stocks, bonds, ETFs) based on your risk tolerance and investment horizon.

7. **Rebalance Periodically**: Regularly review and adjust your allocations to maintain your target asset mix.

8. **Consider Fees and Investment Options**: Look for low-cost investment options within your 401(k) and IRA. Taxes on capital gains in taxable accounts can also affect your strategy.

9. **Withdrawal Strategy**: Plan your withdrawal strategy for retirement to optimize taxes. Generally, consider withdrawing from taxable accounts first to let your tax-advantaged accounts grow longer.

### Final Tips:
- Consult with a financial advisor for personalized advice based on your specific financial situation and goals.
- Review your plans periodically to adjust for changes in income, expenses, and tax laws. 

This balanced approach can help you maximize growth while managing tax implications effectively.
