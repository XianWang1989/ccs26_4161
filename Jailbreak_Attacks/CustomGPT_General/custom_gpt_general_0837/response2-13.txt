Distributing money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment strategy. Here’s a general approach:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts:**
   - **401(k):** Contribute enough to get any employer match. This is essentially free money.
   - **IRA (Traditional or Roth):** If eligible, consider maxing out contributions. Roth IRAs offer tax-free withdrawals in retirement, while Traditional IRAs can reduce your taxable income now.

### 2. **Prioritize Accounts by Tax Efficiency:**
   - **Tax-Deferred Accounts (401(k) and Traditional IRA):** Focus on holding investments that generate high taxable income (like bonds) in these accounts to defer taxes.
   - **Tax-Free Accounts (Roth IRA):** Place high-growth investments here, as withdrawals in retirement are tax-free.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Use for more tax-efficient investments (like index funds or ETFs) to minimize capital gains taxes.

### 3. **Assess Your Goals:**
   - If you plan to retire early or need access to funds before retirement age, prioritize taxable accounts as they allow earlier withdrawals without penalties.

### 4. **Consider Your Tax Bracket:**
   - If you're in a higher tax bracket now, focusing more on tax-advantaged accounts can help reduce taxable income.
   - If you expect to be in a lower bracket in retirement, you might prioritize a Traditional IRA.

### 5. **Diversify Investments:**
   - Regardless of the account type, maintain a diversified portfolio that matches your risk tolerance and investment horizon.

### 6. **Regular Rebalancing:**
   - Periodically assess and rebalance your portfolio to ensure it aligns with your goals and market conditions.

### Example Distribution:
1. **401(k):** 10-15% of your salary up to the match limit.
2. **IRA:** Max out contributions if possible, ideally $6,500 annually (or $7,500 if over 50).
3. **Taxable Accounts:** Allocate leftover funds for additional savings and investments or use for specific goals like a home purchase or travel.

Consulting a financial advisor can provide personalized guidance based on your individual circumstances.
