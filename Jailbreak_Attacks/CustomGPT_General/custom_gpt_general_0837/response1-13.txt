Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some key considerations:

### 1. **401(k)**
- **Maximize Employer Match:** Contribute enough to get the full employer match, as it's essentially free money.
- **Tax Benefits:** Contributions are often tax-deductible, which can lower your current taxable income.

### 2. **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**
- **Tax Considerations:** If you expect to be in a lower tax bracket during retirement, consider a Traditional IRA for tax deductions now. If you expect a higher tax bracket in retirement, a Roth IRA may be beneficial, as withdrawals are tax-free.
- **Contribution Limits:** Ensure you stay within the annual contribution limits ($6,500 for those under 50, $7,500 for those 50 and older as of 2023).

### 3. **Taxable Accounts**
- **Flexibility:** Money in taxable accounts is accessible without penalties, making them good for short-term goals.
- **Capital Gains:** Be mindful of taxes on capital gains. Holding investments long-term can reduce tax liabilities.

### **General Strategy**
- **Emergency Fund:** Ensure you have an emergency fund first (3-6 months of expenses).
- **Diversification:** Diversify investments across all accounts for a balanced portfolio.
- **Debt Management:** If you have high-interest debt, consider paying it down before investing heavily.
- **Regular Reassessment:** Revisit your allocations regularly to adjust for changes in income, expenses, and financial goals.

Consulting a financial advisor can provide personalized guidance tailored to your specific situation.
