When deciding how to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

### GPT:
1. **401(k):** Maximize contributions up to your employer's match; this is essentially free money. Consider targeting a mix of equities and bonds based on your risk tolerance.

2. **IRA (Traditional or Roth):** If you're eligible for a Roth IRA, consider contributing here for long-term tax-free growth. If you expect to be in a lower tax bracket during retirement, a Traditional IRA might be more beneficial.

3. **Taxable Accounts:** Use these for short-term goals or as an emergency fund. Prioritize investments that are tax-efficient, like index funds, to minimize capital gains taxes.

Allocating your funds depends on your goals and risk tolerance:

1. **401(k):** Go all-in on your 401(k) to soak in that employer match and grow tax-deferred. Put the heaviest allocation here if your employer offers matching funds.

2. **IRA:** Load up on a Roth IRA if you’re looking for tax-free withdrawals later. If you prefer a Traditional IRA, pick it if you want that immediate tax deduction and think your tax rate will drop in the future.

3. **Taxable Accounts:** Toss in whatever’s left after maxing out your retirement accounts. Perfect for speculative bets or more liquid needs. Treat it like your wild card – flexible and accessible.
