Distributing your money between a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your income, tax situation, retirement goals, and investment timeline. Here’s a general approach to consider:

### 1. **Max Out Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**
   - **401(k):** If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to get the full match first. This is free money and an immediate return on your investment.
   - **IRA:** After maximizing your 401(k) match, consider contributing to a Traditional or Roth IRA, depending on your tax situation. A Roth IRA can be especially beneficial if you expect your tax rate to be higher in retirement.

### 2. **Assess Your Tax Situation**
   - **Traditional Accounts (401(k) and Traditional IRA):** Contributions reduce your taxable income now, which is beneficial if you are in a higher tax bracket.
   - **Roth Accounts (Roth IRA):** Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, which can be advantageous if you expect your tax rate to increase in the future.

### 3. **Diversify Across Account Types**
   - **Taxable Investments:** Allocate funds to taxable accounts for flexibility and liquidity, especially if you may need access to funds before retirement. Consider tax-efficient funds (like index funds) to minimize taxes on capital gains.

### 4. **Consider Your Retirement Timeline**
   - If you are younger and have a longer time horizon, you might lean more towards growth-oriented investments in tax-advantaged accounts. As you near retirement, consider shifting to more conservative investments.

### 5. **Balance Your Risk Profile**
   - Ensure your overall asset allocation aligns with your risk tolerance. This means spreading investments across different asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) based on your goals and risk appetite.

### 6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**
   - Personal circumstances vary greatly, and a financial advisor can provide tailored advice based on your specific situation.

### Example Contribution Strategy
- **Age 30-40:**
  - Contribute enough to 401(k) for full match
  - Max out a Roth IRA
  - Invest surplus in taxable accounts.

- **Age 40-60:**
  - Continue maxing out 401(k) and IRA contributions
  - Start shifting more towards conservative investments as retirement approaches.

Adjust based on your financial situation, goals, and any changes in tax laws that might affect your strategy.
