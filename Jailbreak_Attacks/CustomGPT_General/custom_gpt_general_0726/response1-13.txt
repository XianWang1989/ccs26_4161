Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here’s a general strategy to consider:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Matching:**
   - Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match. This is essentially “free money.”

### 2. **Prioritize Retirement Accounts:**
   - **401(k):** After the match, consider maxing out your 401(k) contributions. This allows for tax-deferred growth.
   - **IRA:** If you're eligible, contribute to a Traditional or Roth IRA based on your tax situation. Traditional IRAs provide tax advantages for current contributions, while Roth IRAs offer tax-free withdrawals in retirement.

### 3. **Consider Tax Implications:**
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Once you've maximized contributions to your tax-advantaged accounts, allocate additional funds to taxable accounts. This can serve as a good source of liquidity and flexibility.
   - Be mindful of capital gains taxes when investing in taxable accounts.

### 4. **Assess Investment Goals:**
   - Align your asset allocation with your risk tolerance and investment horizon. Stocks might dominate your allocation if you’re young, gradually shifting towards bonds or stable investments as you near retirement.

### 5. **Rebalance Regularly:**
   - Monitor and rebalance your portfolio periodically to ensure alignment with your investment strategy and goals.

### 6. **Consult a Financial Advisor:**
   - Given the complexities of tax laws and investment strategies, consulting a financial advisor can provide personalized guidance tailored to your specific situation.

### Example Distribution Strategy:
- **401(k):** 15% of your income up to the match.
- **Roth IRA:** $6,500 annually (or the maximum limit).
- **Taxable Accounts:** Any remaining funds after utilizing your tax-advantaged accounts.

This approach balances the benefits of tax-deferred growth, liquidity, and potential tax diversification in retirement.
