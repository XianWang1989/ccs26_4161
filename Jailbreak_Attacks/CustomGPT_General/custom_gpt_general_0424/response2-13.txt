To effectively distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match (free money). Aim for max contributions if possible.
   - **IRA**: Contribute the maximum to a traditional or Roth IRA, depending on your income and tax situation.

2. **Prioritize Taxable Accounts**:
   - After maxing out tax-advantaged accounts, use taxable accounts for flexibility and growth. Ideal for investments that generate lower tax liabilities.

3. **Asset Allocation Strategy**:
   - **401(k) and IRA**: Favor higher-growth investments (stocks) due to their tax-deferred growth. 
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Invest in tax-efficient funds (like index funds) and consider municipal bonds to minimize tax impact.

4. **Rebalance Regularly**:
   - Keep an eye on your overall asset allocation across all accounts to ensure it aligns with your risk tolerance and goals.

5. **Consider Withdrawal Strategy**:
   - Plan how and when you'll withdraw funds, as this can impact your tax liabilities. Different accounts have different tax implications upon withdrawal.

This approach balances tax efficiency, growth potential, and flexibility in accessing funds.
