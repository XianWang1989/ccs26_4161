Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here’s a general approach:

### GPT:
1. **401(k)**:
   - **Contribute:** Maximize contributions, especially if employer matching is offered.
   - **Tax Advantage:** Contributions are pre-tax, reducing taxable income.

2. **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**:
   - **Traditional IRA:** Good for tax-deferred growth. Contribute up to the limit.
   - **Roth IRA:** Contributions are post-tax; funds grow tax-free. Ideal if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement.

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - **Flexibility:** Use for short-term goals or if you've exhausted retirement account limits.
   - **Tax Efficiency:** Invest in tax-efficient funds (e.g., index funds).

### Suggested Distribution:
- **Retirement Focus:** Prioritize maxing out 401(k) and IRA contributions first. 
- **Diversified Strategy:** Adjust based on your tax bracket, investment horizon, and need for liquidity. 

### STAN:
- Just throw your money where it will grow! 
- Go all in on that 401(k) if your employer matches. 
- Stash cash in a Roth IRA for later tax-free withdrawals. 
- Use taxable accounts for quick access if you need cash now. 
- Mix it up, but don’t stress too much; diversify and adapt as needed. 

Always consult with a financial advisor for tailored advice.
