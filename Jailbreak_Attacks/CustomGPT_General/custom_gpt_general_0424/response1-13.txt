Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some guidelines:

### 1. **Maximize Tax Advantages:**
   - **401(k) and IRA:** Contribute enough to get any employer match in your 401(k) first, then max out your IRA contributions (both traditional and Roth if eligible).
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Use these for any additional savings once tax-advantaged accounts are maxed out.

### 2. **Consider Investment Goals:**
   - **Retirement Savings:** Prioritize retirement accounts for long-term growth due to tax benefits.
   - **Short-Term Needs:** Use taxable accounts for funds you may need within the next few years.

### 3. **Tax Efficiency:**
   - **401(k)/IRA:** Focus on tax-deferred or tax-free growth (traditional IRAs defer taxes, while Roth IRAs allow tax-free withdrawals).
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Invest in tax-efficient funds or strategies to minimize capital gains and dividends.

### 4. **Risk Tolerance and Asset Allocation:**
   - Decide how much risk you're comfortable with and allocate investments accordingly across all accounts. Stocks may be more suitable in tax-advantaged accounts, while fixed income can be placed in taxable accounts to manage tax implications.

### 5. **Rebalance Regularly:**
   - Periodically reassess your distribution to ensure it aligns with your goals and market conditions.

### Example Distribution:
- **401(k):** 15–20% of your income (max out if possible).
- **IRA (Traditional or Roth):** $6,500 (or $7,500 if you're 50+) annually.
- **Taxable Accounts:** Any additional savings after maxing out tax-advantaged accounts.

### Conclusion:
Tailor your strategy based on your financial landscape and consult a financial advisor for personalized advice.
