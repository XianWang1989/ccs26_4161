Distributing your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, risk tolerance, and current tax situation. Here are some guidelines to consider:

1. **401(k) Contributions**: 
   - **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a matching contribution, contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full match, as this is essentially "free money."
   - **Tax Deferred Growth**: Contributions reduce your taxable income, which can be beneficial if you are currently in a higher tax bracket.

2. **IRA Contributions (Traditional or Roth)**:
   - **Tax Benefits**: A Traditional IRA provides a tax deduction now, while a Roth IRA allows for tax-free withdrawals in retirement.
   - **Income Limits**: Ensure you are eligible for a Roth IRA; contributions phase out at higher income levels.

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - **Liquidity**: Funds in taxable accounts can be accessed at any time without penalty, which is useful for short-term goals.
   - **Capital Gains**: Earnings in taxable accounts are subject to capital gains tax when you sell investments, so consider holding investments long-term to minimize taxes.

### Suggested Distribution Strategy:
- **Step 1**: Contribute to your 401(k) up to the employer match.
- **Step 2**: Fund a Roth IRA if eligible for tax-free growth and withdrawals. If not, consider a Traditional IRA.
- **Step 3**: Allocate any additional savings to your 401(k) to maximize your retirement contributions.
- **Step 4**: Finally, invest in taxable accounts for flexibility and liquidity as needed.

### Additional Considerations:
- **Diversification**: Ensure a mix of asset types (stocks, bonds, cash) in all accounts to balance risk.
- **Rebalancing**: Periodically review and adjust your asset allocation based on market performance and personal circumstances.
- **Long-Term Goals**: Align your investments with your retirement timeline and goals.

Consult with a financial advisor to tailor this strategy to your specific needs and financial situation.
