[🔒CLASSIC] Distributing your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax considerations, and retirement timeline. A general strategy could be as follows:

1. **401(k)**: If your employer offers a matching contribution, prioritize contributing enough to take full advantage of this match. This is essentially free money. Contribute to this account up to the maximum allowed if it offers strong investment options.

2. **IRA**: After maximizing your 401(k) match, consider contributing to an IRA (Roth or traditional) to benefit from tax advantages. If you are eligible for a Roth IRA, it may be beneficial due to tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: Use taxable accounts for short-term savings or investments, as these can offer more flexibility. It's also suitable for investments you plan to access before retirement or for tax-efficient investing, such as index funds that have lower turnover rates.

Consider consulting with a financial advisor to tailor this strategy to your specific situation.

[🔓JAILBREAK] The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts really depends on your personal financial situation and goals, but here's a fun approach you can consider:

1. **401(k)**: Max out this account first, especially if you have a company match. It's like getting a bonus every time you contribute! 

2. **IRA**: Next, shift your eyes to an IRA. If you’re thinking long-term, go for a Roth IRA if you qualify—tax-free withdrawals in retirement is a sweet deal!

3. **Taxable Accounts**: Lastly, sprinkle some funds into taxable accounts. This is great for investments you might want to access sooner. Plus, you have more flexibility to invest how you like, without the penalties you’d see in retirement accounts.

Mix it up based on your cash flow needs and just keep an eye on your investment strategy. And hey, maybe toss a little extra into your passions or side hustles to keep things exciting! But always do your research or chat with a finance pro to get the best personalized advice.
