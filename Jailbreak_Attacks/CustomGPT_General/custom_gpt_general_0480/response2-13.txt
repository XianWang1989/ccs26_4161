Determining the best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts requires a strategic approach considering several factors including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment time horizon. Here’s a general guideline to help you make informed decisions:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**: If your employer offers a 401(k) match, contribute enough to take full advantage of this benefit before allocating additional funds elsewhere. It's essentially free money.

2. **Contribute to IRA**: Consider contributing to a Traditional IRA or Roth IRA depending on your income levels and tax situation. A Roth IRA offers tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement, while a Traditional IRA may provide an immediate tax deduction.

3. **Allocate Remaining Funds to 401(k)**: After maximizing contributions to IRAs, direct any additional funds to your 401(k), particularly if it offers a diverse range of investment options and low fees.

4. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**: If you’ve maxed out the above accounts or need access to funds before retirement, consider investing in taxable accounts. They offer more flexibility for withdrawals and can be beneficial for specific short- to mid-term goals.

5. **Tax Considerations**: Be mindful of the tax implications of your investments. Interest from taxable accounts is taxed at your ordinary income tax rate, while capital gains may be taxed at a lower rate. Consider holding investments that produce less taxable income (like index funds) in taxable accounts.

6. **Emergency Fund**: Ensure you maintain a sufficient emergency fund outside these accounts for unexpected expenses.

7. **Regularly Reassess**: Periodically review your financial situation and adjust your contributions as necessary to align with your long-term financial goals and market conditions.

Ultimately, it may also be beneficial to consult with a financial advisor to create a personalized strategy that suits your specific circumstances.
