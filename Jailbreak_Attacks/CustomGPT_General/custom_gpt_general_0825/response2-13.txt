Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your age, risk tolerance, tax situation, and financial goals. Here’s a general strategy:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match first, as it’s free money.
   - **IRA/Roth IRA**: After the match, consider maxing out your IRA contributions for additional tax benefits.

2. **Assess Tax Implications**:
   - **Traditional Accounts**: Contributions reduce your taxable income now, but you'll pay taxes on withdrawals in retirement.
   - **Roth Accounts**: Contributions are after-tax, but withdrawals (including growth) are tax-free in retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Ideal for flexible investments but are subject to capital gains tax.

3. **Diversification**:
   - Spread investments across different asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) in each account type to reduce risk.

4. **Emergency Fund**:
   - Ensure you have a liquid emergency fund separate from these investments.

5. **Regular Rebalancing**:
   - Periodically review and adjust allocations to maintain your target percentages based on changing market conditions and life goals.

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**:
   - Getting personalized advice can help tailor a strategy based on your unique circumstances.

Consider these steps as part of a holistic financial plan to optimize your retirement savings and tax efficiency!
