The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and time horizon. Here are some general guidelines:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First:**
   - **401(k)**: If your employer offers a matching contribution, prioritize contributing enough to get the full match. This is essentially free money.
   - **IRA**: After maximizing your 401(k) match, consider contributing to an IRA (Traditional or Roth) to benefit from tax advantages. Roth IRAs allow tax-free withdrawals in retirement, while Traditional IRAs offer tax deductions on contributions.

### 2. **Consider Account Taxation:**
   - **401(k) and IRA**: These accounts grow tax-deferred (or tax-free for Roth). Utilize these for long-term investments or assets that are expected to appreciate significantly.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: These should primarily hold investments that are more tax-efficient, like index funds or ETFs, due to capital gains tax. Consider including bonds or other income-generating assets which could incur high tax liabilities.

### 3. **Adjust Based on Financial Goals:**
   - **Short-term Needs**: If you anticipate needing funds in the short term, it may be best to keep money in taxable accounts, as withdrawing from 401(k) or IRA can incur penalties.
   - **Long-term Growth**: Focus on growing your nest egg within tax-advantaged accounts, as they provide the best growth potential over time.

### 4. **Maintain an Asset Allocation Strategy:**
   - Diversify investments across all accounts to align with your risk tolerance and investment strategy. Ensure that you are not overly concentrated in one asset class.

### 5. **Reassess Regularly:**
   - Periodically review your allocations as your situation changes (e.g., marriage, job changes, market conditions). Rebalance as needed to maintain your desired asset allocation.

### Conclusion:
Combining contributions with strategic asset placement across your accounts can optimize your overall investment growth and tax efficiency. Consulting a financial advisor for personalized advice can also be beneficial.
