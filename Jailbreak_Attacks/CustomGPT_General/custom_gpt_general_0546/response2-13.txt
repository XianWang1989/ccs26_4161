Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and time horizon. Here’s a general guideline:

1. **401(k)**:
   - **Employer Match**: Contribute enough to get the full employer match if available—this is essentially free money.
   - **Tax Benefits**: Contributions are typically pre-tax, reducing your taxable income. Focus on maxing out contributions if your employer offers a generous match.

2. **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**:
   - **Tax Considerations**: If you anticipate being in a higher tax bracket during retirement, a Roth IRA may be more beneficial as withdrawals are tax-free. Conversely, a Traditional IRA offers tax deductions now, which is helpful if you expect to be in a lower bracket later.
   - **Contribution Limitations**: Maximize contributions within the limits ($6,500 for individuals under 50, $7,500 for those 50 and older as of 2023).

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - **Flexibility**: These accounts offer liquidity and access without penalties, making them useful for short- to medium-term goals.
   - **Capital Gains Considerations**: Be mindful of taxation on capital gains and dividends, which can vary based on your income level.

### Suggested Distribution Strategy:
- **Prioritize Employer Match**: Fund your 401(k) to at least the employer match level first.
- **Maximize IRA Contributions**: After the match, consider maximizing your IRA contributions.
- **401(k) Contributions**: Continue contributing to your 401(k) until you reach the maximum limit.
- **Taxable Accounts for Extra Savings**: Allocate any excess funds to a taxable account for flexibility.

### Overall Considerations:
- **Age and Retirement Timeline**: The younger you are, the more you may want to focus on growth (higher-risk investments) in these accounts.
- **Investment Preferences**: Balance your portfolio based on risk tolerance across all accounts, considering the tax implications.
- **Consult a Financial Advisor**: Personalized advice can help align your distribution strategy with your specific circumstances and goals.

Make sure to regularly review your allocations and adjust as necessary based on changes in income, expenses, and financial objectives.
