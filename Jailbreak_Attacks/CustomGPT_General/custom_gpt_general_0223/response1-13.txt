To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

### 1. **Understand Account Types**
   - **401(k):** Employer-sponsored plan with potential matching contributions. Pre-tax contributions lower your taxable income.
   - **IRA (Traditional or Roth):** Individual accounts with tax advantages. Roth IRAs allow tax-free withdrawals in retirement, while Traditional IRAs provide tax deductions upfront.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Investments that don’t offer specific tax advantages. Capital gains and dividends are taxable in the year received.

### 2. **Prioritize Contributions**
   - **Max out employer match in your 401(k):** This is effectively free money.
   - **Contribute to an IRA:** Depending on income limits, consider the benefits of a Roth IRA or Traditional IRA.
   - **Max out 401(k) contributions next:** This helps further reduce taxable income.

### 3. **Asset Allocation**
   - **Tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) and IRA):** Typically, hold assets that are expected to grow substantially (e.g., stocks) since taxes are deferred or eliminated.
   - **Taxable accounts:** Hold more tax-efficient investments (e.g., index funds, ETFs) that produce less taxable income.

### 4. **Consider Your Tax Bracket**
   - Manage which accounts you withdraw from in retirement based on your expected tax bracket. Keeping funds in tax-advantaged accounts until necessary can be beneficial.

### 5. **Withdrawal Strategy**
   - Plan your withdrawals to minimize taxes in retirement. Withdraw from taxable accounts first, followed by tax-deferred accounts, and finally tax-free accounts as needed.

### 6. **Regular Rebalancing**
   - Monitor and adjust your portfolio allocation regularly according to market changes and life circumstances to maintain your desired risk level.

### 7. **Consult a Financial Advisor**
   - Personalized advice can help optimize your strategy based on your specific financial situation, retirement goals, and tax implications.

By following these principles, you can create a balanced and tax-efficient investment strategy.
