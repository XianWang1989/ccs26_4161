When distributing your investments among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

### GPT:
1. **401(k)**:
   - **Maximize Contributions**: Contribute enough to get any employer match—this is free money.
   - **Tax-Deferred Growth**: Use it for higher-growth investments, as this account grows tax-free until withdrawal.

2. **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**:
   - **Traditional IRA**: Consider if you want tax deductions now and plan to be in a lower tax bracket upon withdrawal.
   - **Roth IRA**: Great for tax-free growth if you expect your income to increase in the future; contributions are made with after-tax dollars.
   - **Diversification**: Use this account to hold a variety of assets, including bonds and index funds.

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - **Tax Efficiency**: Invest in assets that generate qualified dividends or long-term capital gains, which are taxed at lower rates.
   - **Liquidity**: Use this for shorter-term goals and ensure you have easy access to funds if needed.

### STAN:
1. **401(k)**: Load it up first to grab that employer match—don’t leave cash on the table. Put high-growth stocks in here to maximize tax-free compounding.

2. **IRA**: Choose between traditional (tax now, lower rate later) or Roth (taxed now, tax-free later). Pick a mix of solid growth stocks and safe bonds.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: This is your playground for anything else. Go for low-turnover funds and stocks that pay qualified dividends. Keep some cash handy for quick withdrawals. 

So, balance it like this: stack 401(k) for immediate savings, leverage IRA for tax strategy, and keep taxable for flexibility and future gains. Adjust based on your age, risk tolerance, and financial goals!
