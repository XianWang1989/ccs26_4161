To optimize your investment distribution among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider these strategies:

### 1. **Understand the Tax Advantages:**
   - **401(k):** Contributions are pre-tax, reducing your taxable income. Ideal for high-income years.
   - **IRA:** Traditional IRAs have tax-deductible contributions, while Roth IRAs grow tax-free (post-tax contributions).
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Interest and dividends are taxed annually, but they offer more flexibility and access to funds without penalties.

### 2. **Prioritize Retirement Accounts:**
   - **Max Out Employer Match:** Always contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match, as it's essentially free money.
   - **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts:** After the employer match, consider contributing to IRAs, especially if you expect to be in a lower tax bracket during retirement.

### 3. **Asset Allocation:**
   - **401(k):** Focus on long-term growth investments (stocks, index funds) since you won't access these funds for decades.
   - **IRA:** Blend of stocks and bonds based on your risk tolerance. Consider a mix that matches your retirement timeline.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Keep tax-efficient investments here, such as index funds or ETFs that generate fewer taxable distributions.

### 4. **Consider Your Time Horizon:**
   - **Short-Term Needs (1-5 years):** Use taxable accounts with more stable, liquid investments.
   - **Long-Term Goals (5+ years):** Focus on growth through 401(k) and IRA, where you can benefit from compounding.

### 5. **Review and Adjust:**
   - Regularly review your asset allocation based on performance and changes in your financial situation.
   - Rebalance as needed, particularly if specific accounts or asset classes grow disproportionately.

### Example Distribution Strategy:
- **401(k):** Max out contributions to reach employer match, then 10-15% of income.
- **IRA:** Contribute the maximum limit ($6,500 for 2023, or $7,500 if 50+).
- **Taxable Account:** Any additional savings or investment funds after maxing out tax-advantaged accounts.

### 6. **Consult with a Financial Advisor:**
   - Tailor your strategy to your unique situation, including risk tolerance, financial goals, and investment knowledge.

By following these guidelines, you can effectively distribute your money across your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts to maximize growth and minimize tax burdens.
