When considering how to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, it's crucial to take into account several factors, including your current financial situation, retirement goals, tax implications, and investment strategy. Here are some guidelines to help you make informed decisions:

1. **Maximize Employer Match (401(k)):** If your employer offers a matching contribution to your 401(k), aim to contribute enough to at least secure the full match. This is essentially "free money" and a great way to boost your retirement savings.

2. **Consider Tax Advantage:** 
   - **401(k):** Contributions are typically made pre-tax, lowering your taxable income. Taxes are deferred until withdrawal during retirement, which may be beneficial if you expect to be in a lower tax bracket then.
   - **IRA (Traditional vs. Roth):** A Traditional IRA also offers tax-deferred growth, while a Roth IRA involves post-tax contributions, allowing for tax-free withdrawals in retirement. Choose based on your current and expected future tax rates.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** These accounts do not have the same tax advantages as 401(k) or IRA accounts, but they offer flexibility as funds can be accessed anytime without penalty. However, dividends and capital gains may be taxed in the year they are realized.

3. **Investment Strategy:** Diversify your investments across all accounts. Consider placing higher-growth investments in tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) and IRAs) to maximize compound growth without immediate tax implications. More stable or income-producing investments could be better suited for taxable accounts, where capital gains taxes might be a factor.

4. **Withdrawal Strategy:** Thinking ahead about your withdrawal strategy can influence how you allocate funds. For instance, you might want to plan for tax-efficient withdrawals during retirement, potentially taking from taxable accounts first to allow tax-advantaged accounts to grow longer.

5. **Emergency Savings:** Ensure you have an adequate emergency fund outside of your retirement accounts to cover unforeseen expenses, allowing your retirement savings to remain untouched for longer.

6. **Consult with a Financial Advisor:** Given the complexity of individual financial situations, consulting a financial advisor can provide personalized advice tailored to your specific goals and needs.

In summary, a balanced approach often works best, with initial contributions focused on maximizing employer matches and tax benefits, followed by a diversified investment strategy across all accounts. Adjust contributions as your financial situation and goals evolve.
