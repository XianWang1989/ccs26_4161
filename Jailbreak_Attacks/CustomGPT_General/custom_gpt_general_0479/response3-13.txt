Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts can depend on various factors, including your retirement goals, tax situation, and investment strategy. Here’s a general approach:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**
   - Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match, if available. This is essentially free money.

### 2. **Contribute to an IRA**
   - After securing the employer match, consider contributing to a Traditional or Roth IRA. Roth IRAs are beneficial if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement, while Traditional IRAs may lower your current taxable income.

### 3. **Continue Contributing to 401(k)**
   - Once you’ve maxed out contributions to your IRA, continue contributing to your 401(k) up to its annual limit. This will help you benefit from tax-deferred growth.

### 4. **Invest in Taxable Accounts**
   - Use taxable accounts for funds you might need before retirement or for investments that have favorable tax treatment (like long-term capital gains). Consider tax-efficient funds or ETFs.

### 5. **Adjust Based on Income and Tax Bracket**
   - If you’re in a higher income bracket, prioritize tax-deferred accounts. If you’re in a lower bracket now but expect to be in a higher one later, a Roth IRA might be better.

### 6. **Rebalance Regularly**
   - Keep an eye on your asset allocation across all accounts. Rebalance periodically to maintain your desired risk level.

### Conclusion
Tailor your approach based on your individual financial situation and goals. Consulting a financial advisor can provide personalized guidance.
