When considering how to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, here are some key points to guide your strategy:

### 1. **Prioritize Contributions**
   - **401(k):** If your employer offers a matching contribution, contribute enough to capture that match first—it’s essentially free money.
   - **IRA:** After meeting the 401(k) match, consider maximum contributions to a traditional or Roth IRA based on your income and tax situation.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Use these for additional investments after maxing out your retirement accounts.

### 2. **Tax Considerations**
   - **401(k) and Traditional IRA:** Contributions reduce your taxable income now but taxes will be due upon withdrawal during retirement.
   - **Roth IRA:** Contributions are made after-tax, but withdrawals in retirement can be tax-free, making it a good option for tax diversification.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** You'll owe taxes on dividends and capital gains, so consider holding more tax-efficient investments (like ETFs) here.

### 3. **Investment Strategy**
   - Align your investments based on the time horizon and risk tolerance. Typically, longer-term investments (like those in retirement accounts) can be more aggressive.

### 4. **Rebalance Regularly**
   - Monitor your asset allocation and adjust as needed, considering any changes in your financial situation or market conditions.

### 5. **Consult a Financial Advisor**
   - Given the complexities of personal finance, seeking tailored advice can help optimize your strategy based on your specific goals and circumstances.

### Example Distribution Strategy:
- **401(k):** 6% of salary to get full employer match.
- **IRA:** Max out the contribution limit.
- **Taxable Accounts:** Allocate any remaining funds here, focusing on long-term growth investments.

This approach helps maximize growth potential while managing risk and tax implications effectively.
