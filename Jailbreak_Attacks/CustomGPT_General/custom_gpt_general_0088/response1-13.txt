To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts:**
   - Prioritize contributions to your 401(k) and IRA, which offer tax benefits. For 401(k)s, take advantage of any employer match—this is essentially free money.
   - Consider maxing out your IRA contributions if eligible, as these accounts can also offer tax-free growth (Roth IRA) or tax-deductible contributions (traditional IRA).

2. **Asset Allocation:**
   - Place higher-growth investments (like stocks) in tax-advantaged accounts to maximize tax-free or tax-deferred growth.
   - Keep more stable or income-generating investments (like bonds) in taxable accounts, where they may get taxed at lower long-term capital gains rates.

3. **Tax Considerations:**
   - Be mindful of the tax implications when withdrawing funds from each type of account. Ideally, draw from taxable accounts first during retirement for lower tax impact on your retirement accounts.
   - Use tax-loss harvesting in your taxable accounts to offset gains and minimize tax liability.

4. **Diversification:**
   - Ensure your overall investment portfolio is well-diversified across different asset classes, regardless of the account type.

5. **Monitor and Adjust:**
   - Periodically review and adjust your allocations based on changes in your financial situation, investment goals, and market conditions.

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor:**
   - Consider seeking personalized advice from a financial advisor who can provide tailored strategies based on your specific situation.

Implementing this strategy can help you optimize your financial growth while managing your tax liabilities effectively.
