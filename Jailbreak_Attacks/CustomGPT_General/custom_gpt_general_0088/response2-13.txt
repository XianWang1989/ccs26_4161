Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some guidelines to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Contributions**: Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to get any employer match. This is essentially free money.

2. **Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**:
   - **401(k)**: If your employer offers a match, prioritize it. Contribute up to the match limit.
   - **IRA**: If eligible, consider contributing to a Roth IRA for tax-free growth or a Traditional IRA for tax-deferred growth.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: After maximizing contributions to your 401(k) and IRA, allocate funds to a taxable account for flexibility and access to funds without penalties.

4. **Consider Asset Allocation**: Diversify your investments based on your risk tolerance and time horizon. Typically, stocks are better suited for long-term accounts due to their growth potential, while bonds may fit well in taxable accounts for generating income.

5. **Tax Efficiency**: Place tax-inefficient investments (like bonds and real estate) in tax-advantaged accounts and keep tax-efficient investments (like index funds) in taxable accounts to minimize taxes.

6. **Regular Review and Rebalance**: Periodically reassess your distribution strategy based on life changes, market performance, and retirement goals.

It's always wise to consult a financial advisor to tailor the strategy to your specific situation.
