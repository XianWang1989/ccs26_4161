Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and the types of investments you prefer. Here are some strategies to consider:

### 1. **Understand Account Types**

- **401(k)**: This account is employer-sponsored, allowing for pre-tax contributions, which can reduce your taxable income. Some employers may offer matching contributions.

- **IRA (Individual Retirement Account)**: You can choose between a Traditional IRA (tax-deductible contributions) and a Roth IRA (tax-free withdrawals in retirement). Roth IRAs have income limits.

- **Taxable Accounts**: These accounts do not have the tax advantages of retirement accounts but offer flexibility in terms of contributions and withdrawals. They are subject to capital gains taxes when you sell investments.

### 2. **Set Financial Goals**

- **Retirement Age**: Consider how far you are from retirement. Younger individuals might prioritize growth, while those nearing retirement might focus on preserving capital.

- **Liquidity Needs**: Assess how much money you may need access to before retirement. Taxable accounts offer more liquidity.

### 3. **Tax Considerations**

- **Tax-Deferred Growth**: Max out contributions to your 401(k) and IRA before investing in taxable accounts to benefit from tax-deferred growth.

- **Tax Efficiency**: Place tax-inefficient investments (like bonds or high-yield stocks) in tax-advantaged accounts and keep tax-efficient investments (like index funds) in taxable accounts to minimize tax liability.

### 4. **Diversification**

- Spread your investments across different asset classes (stocks, bonds, real estate) and sectors to manage risk and enhance potential returns.

### 5. **Regular Contributions**

- Set up automatic contributions to each account. Prioritize employer matches in your 401(k) first, then max out your IRA, and allocate additional funds to taxable accounts.

### 6. **Review Periodically**

- Reassess your allocations regularly, especially after significant life events (like marriage, a new job, or having children), and adjust based on changes in your financial situation or goals.

### Example Distribution Strategy:

1. **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get the full employer match.
2. **IRA**: Max out contributions to a Traditional or Roth IRA.
3. **Taxable Account**: Allocate additional funds based on your liquidity needs and investment goals.

### Conclusion

Consulting with a financial advisor can provide personalized advice tailored to your unique situation, ensuring you're making the best choices for your financial future.
