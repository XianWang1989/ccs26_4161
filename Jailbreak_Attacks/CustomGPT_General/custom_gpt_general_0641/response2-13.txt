Distributing your money across a 401(k), IRA, and taxable account can optimize your retirement savings while managing your tax implications effectively. Here’s a general approach:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money. Consider maximizing contributions if you can. The 2023 limit for employee contributions is $22,500 (or $30,000 if you're aged 50 or older).
   - **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**: If you qualify, contribute to a Roth IRA for tax-free withdrawals in retirement or a Traditional IRA for tax-deductible contributions. The limit is $6,500 (or $7,500 if you're 50 or older). 

### 2. **Debt Management and Emergency Fund**
   - Before investing in taxable accounts, ensure you have manageable debt and an emergency fund (3-6 months of expenses).

### 3. **Invest in a Taxable Account**
   - After maximizing retirement contributions, consider taxable accounts for additional investing. These provide flexibility, and you can access your funds without penalties, unlike IRAs and 401(k)s.

### 4. **Asset Allocation Strategy**
   - **Tax-Deferred Accounts (401(k), Traditional IRA)**: Consider holding bonds or high-yield investments, as these can be taxed at ordinary income rates upon withdrawal.
   - **Tax-Free Accounts (Roth IRA)**: Consider holding stocks or high-growth investments for the tax-free growth potential.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Focus on tax-efficient investments, like index funds or ETFs, that minimize capital gains distribution.

### 5. **Reassess Investment Choices Periodically**
   - Regularly review your asset allocation and contributions based on market performance and your financial goals.

### Final Tip
Consult with a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your specific situation, income level, and financial goals.
