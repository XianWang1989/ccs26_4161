Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment horizon. Here’s a general strategy to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match, as this is essentially free money.

2. **Individual Retirement Accounts (IRA)**: If eligible, consider contributing to a Traditional or Roth IRA. A Roth IRA offers tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement, while a Traditional IRA provides immediate tax deductions.

3. **401(k) Contributions**: After maximizing the match and IRA contributions, continue contributing to your 401(k). Focus on tax-deferred growth if you're in a higher tax bracket.

4. **Taxable Accounts**: Funds not needed for retirement can go into a taxable account. Use these for investments with favorable tax treatments, like long-term capital gains or tax-efficient funds.

5. **Diversification**: Maintain a diversified portfolio across all accounts to manage risk effectively.

6. **Consider Withdrawals**: Think about how you’ll withdraw funds in retirement. IRAs and 401(k)s have required minimum distributions (RMDs) at age 73, while taxable accounts don’t.

7. **Rebalance Regularly**: Review and adjust your allocations periodically based on performance and changing goals.

Consulting with a financial advisor can provide you with personalized advice based on your specific circumstances.
