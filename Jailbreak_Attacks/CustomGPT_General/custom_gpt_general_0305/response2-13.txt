Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts can optimize your retirement savings and tax efficiency. Here are some strategies to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match, as this is free money.

2. **Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**:
   - **401(k)**: Focus on your workplace retirement plan for tax-deferred growth. Aim to max out your contributions.
   - **IRA**: If you qualify for a Roth IRA, consider contributing here for tax-free growth on withdrawals in retirement.
  
3. **Invest in Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**:
   - After maxing out tax-advantaged accounts, use taxable accounts for additional investments. This offers more flexibility for withdrawals.

4. **Asset Location Strategy**:
   - Place high-growth investments (like stocks) in tax-advantaged accounts to benefit from tax-free growth.
   - Keep income-generating assets (like bonds) in taxable accounts to manage tax implications effectively.

5. **Regular Reassessment**: Review your allocations periodically to adjust for changes in income, expenses, and market performance.

Consulting with a financial advisor can also help tailor these strategies to your specific situation.
