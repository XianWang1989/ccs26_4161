To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following approach:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**:
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match, as it’s essentially free money. Aim to reach the maximum contribution limit if feasible.
   - **IRA**: Depending on your income, contribute to a Traditional or Roth IRA. Roth IRAs can provide tax-free growth and withdrawals, while Traditional IRAs may offer upfront tax benefits.

2. **Assess Your Tax Situation**:
   - Prioritize contributions based on your current and expected future tax rates. If you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement, focus more on Roth accounts.

3. **Allocate for Flexibility**:
   - After maximizing your tax-advantaged accounts, allocate remaining funds to taxable accounts. This provides flexibility for withdrawals without penalties and allows for tax-loss harvesting.

4. **Diversify Investments**:
   - Maintain a balanced asset allocation across all accounts, considering your risk tolerance, investment time horizon, and the specific tax implications of each account type.

5. **Regularly Review Your Strategy**:
   - Periodically reassess your financial situation, investment performance, and tax policies to adjust your contributions as needed.

Always consider consulting with a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your specific circumstances.
