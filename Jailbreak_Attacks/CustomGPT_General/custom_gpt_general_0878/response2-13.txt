When considering the best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, several factors come into play, including your financial goals, risk tolerance, and tax situation. Here are some general guidelines to help you make an informed decision:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a match on your 401(k) contributions, aim to contribute at least enough to take full advantage of that match. It's essentially free money.

2. **Consider Tax Implications**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are typically made pre-tax, reducing your taxable income for the year. Taxes are due on withdrawals during retirement.
   - **IRA**: You can choose between a Traditional IRA (tax-deductible contributions, tax on withdrawals) or a Roth IRA (contributions are made post-tax, and withdrawals are tax-free in retirement).
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Investments in taxable accounts are subject to capital gains tax and income tax on dividends, but they offer more flexibility with withdrawals.

3. **Establish an Emergency Fund**: Before heavily investing, ensure you have a liquid emergency fund with 3-6 months’ worth of expenses.

4. **Distribute Based on Goals and Time Horizon**:
   - If retirement is far off, prioritize investments in tax-advantaged accounts like a 401(k) and Roth IRA, which allow your investments to grow tax-free.
   - As you approach retirement, consider shifting some investments to taxable accounts to maintain flexibility.

5. **Diversification**: Regardless of the account type, ensure you diversify your investments across various asset classes (stocks, bonds, ETFs) to manage risk effectively.

6. **Rebalance Periodically**: Regularly review and adjust your portfolio to maintain your desired asset allocation.

7. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: It can be beneficial to seek advice tailored to your individual situation, especially for complex tax implications and investment strategies.

By taking these factors into account and forming a balanced distribution strategy, you can work toward optimizing your investments across your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts.
