Distributing money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment time horizon. Here are some key considerations to help guide your decision:

### 1. **Understand Account Types:**
   - **401(k):**
     - Contributions are made pre-tax, which can lower your taxable income.
     - Employers may offer matching contributions; take full advantage of this.
     - Withdrawals in retirement are taxed as ordinary income.

   - **IRA (Traditional and Roth):**
     - Traditional IRAs work similarly to 401(k)s for tax benefits.
     - Roth IRAs allow for tax-free withdrawals if certain conditions are met.
     - Choose based on your current vs. expected future tax rates.

   - **Taxable Accounts:**
     - Investments taxed annually on interest, dividends, and capital gains.
     - More flexible in terms of withdrawals and investment choices.
     - Consider strategies to minimize taxes (e.g., tax-loss harvesting).

### 2. **Goals and Time Horizon:**
   - Identify your short-term vs. long-term goals.
   - Long-term investments (retirement savings) typically go into retirement accounts.
   - Short-term savings might be better in a taxable account for liquidity.

### 3. **Tax Considerations:**
   - Maximize tax-advantaged accounts first (401(k), IRAs).
   - Pay attention to tax implications for each account type in terms of withdrawals and growth.

### 4. **Diversification and Risk Tolerance:**
   - Ensure a well-diversified portfolio across all accounts, considering your risk tolerance.
   - Different accounts may allow for different investment strategies based on tax situations.

### 5. **Contribution Limits:**
   - Stay informed about contribution limits for retirement accounts.
   - Allocate funds accordingly, ensuring you maximize employer matches and contribution limits.

### Suggested Strategy:
1. **Max Out Employer Match in 401(k):**
   - Contribute enough to receive the full match, as this is essentially “free money.”

2. **Consider Roth IRA Contributions:**
   - If eligible, contribute to a Roth IRA for tax-free growth and withdrawals.

3. **Continue to Max Out 401(k):**
   - After the match, contribute as much as you can to your 401(k) or max it out if feasible.

4. **Fund a Taxable Account:**
   - Once you've optimized retirement accounts, consider using a taxable account for flexibility.

5. **Rebalance Regularly:**
   - Monitor and rebalance your investments periodically to maintain your desired asset allocation.

### Conclusion:
Consult with a financial planner to tailor your strategy based on personal circumstances and goals. Adjust your allocations as your life situation and financial landscape change.
