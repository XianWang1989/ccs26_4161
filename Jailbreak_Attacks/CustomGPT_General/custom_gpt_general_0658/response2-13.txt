Distributing your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment strategy. Here’s a general approach to consider:

### 1. **Understand Account Types**
   - **401(k):** Employer-sponsored retirement plan with tax-deferred growth. Contributions may reduce taxable income.
   - **IRA (Traditional/Roth):** Individual retirement accounts. Traditional IRAs offer tax-deferred growth; Roth IRAs offer tax-free growth with after-tax contributions.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Regular brokerage accounts where investments are subject to capital gains tax.

### 2. **Prioritize Contributions**
   - **Employer Match:** Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get any employer match; it's essentially free money.
   - **IRA Contributions:** If you qualify, contribute to an IRA (preferably a Roth if you anticipate being in a higher tax bracket in retirement).
   - **Maximize 401(k):** After securing the match and maxing out your IRA, increase contributions to your 401(k).
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Use these for additional savings or investments, especially if you need liquidity.

### 3. **Investment Strategy**
   - **401(k):** Focus on low-cost index funds or target-date funds that align with your retirement timeline.
   - **IRA:** Diversify your investments. Consider more aggressive options if you have a long time horizon.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Invest in tax-efficient funds (like index funds or ETFs) to minimize tax impacts on gains.

### 4. **Consider Tax Implications**
   - Keep higher-growth investments in tax-advantaged accounts to defer or avoid taxes.
   - Use taxable accounts for investments that may produce lower tax burdens, like municipal bonds or those with lower turnover.

### 5. **Monitor and Rebalance**
   - Regularly review your asset allocation and rebalance as needed based on performance and changes in your financial goals or risk tolerance.

### 6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**
   - Personalized advice can help you tailor strategies to your specific circumstances, taking into account your entire financial picture.

By following these guidelines, you can create a balanced approach to managing your retirement and investment accounts for optimal growth and tax efficiency.
