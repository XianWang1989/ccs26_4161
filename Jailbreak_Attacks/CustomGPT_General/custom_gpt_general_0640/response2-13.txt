When considering how to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, it's essential to take into account your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here’s a general strategy to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Contributions**: If your 401(k) offers a company match, contribute enough to get the full match first. This is essentially free money and provides a solid return on investment.

2. **Fund Your IRA**: After taking advantage of your employer's match, consider funding your IRA (either traditional or Roth) depending on your income and tax situation. Roth IRAs are generally more advantageous if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement.

3. **Continue Contributing to 401(k)**: If you can, continue to contribute to your 401(k) up to the annual contribution limits. This allows you to benefit from tax-deferred growth.

4. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**: If you have additional funds after maxing out your retirement accounts, consider investing in taxable accounts. This gives you more flexibility for withdrawals before retirement age without penalties.

5. **Investment Strategy**: Ensure that your investments are diversified and aligned with your risk tolerance and timeline. Generally, younger investors can afford to take more risks, while those closer to retirement may need a more conservative approach.

6. **Revisit Annually**: Your asset allocation should be reviewed regularly, especially as financial conditions and personal circumstances change.

Additionally, consulting with a financial advisor can provide personalized insights based on your unique situation. Remember to factor in the impact of taxes, required minimum distributions, and fees when making your decisions.
