Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, risk tolerance, and time horizon. Here are some guidelines to consider:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**
   - **401(k)**: If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to receive the full match. This is essentially free money.
   - **IRA**: Consider maxing out your IRA contributions (either traditional or Roth, depending on your eligibility and tax situation).

### 2. **Assess Contribution Limits**
   - **401(k)**: For 2024, the contribution limit is $23,000 (plus catch-up contributions for those 50+).
   - **IRA**: The limit is $6,500 (with a catch-up of $1,000 for those 50+).

### 3. **Evaluate Tax Implications**
   - **Traditional 401(k) and IRA**: Contributions may reduce your taxable income, but withdrawals in retirement will be taxed.
   - **Roth Accounts**: Contributions are made after-tax, but qualified withdrawals are tax-free.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Investments don’t provide immediate tax benefits, but you can control when to realize gains or losses.

### 4. **Consider Risk Tolerance and Time Horizon**
   - **Long-term Goals**: Prioritize tax-advantaged accounts for long-term growth (e.g., retirement).
   - **Short-term Goals**: Funds needed sooner should be more accessible, so utilize taxable accounts as necessary.

### 5. **Diversification**
   - Ensure that your investments across all accounts are well-diversified to manage risk effectively.

### 6. **Rebalance Regularly**
   - Periodically review and adjust your allocations based on your changing financial situation and market conditions.

### Example Distribution Strategy
1. Max out your 401(k) up to the employer match.
2. Contribute to an IRA.
3. Use any remaining funds to invest in a taxable account.

### Conclusion
Tailor your strategy based on personal factors, and consider consulting a financial advisor for a plan suited to your specific circumstances.
