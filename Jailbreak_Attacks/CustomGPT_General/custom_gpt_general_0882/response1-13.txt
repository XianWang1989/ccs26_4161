Balancing your investments between a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts is crucial for a well-rounded financial strategy. Here’s how to approach it:

### 1. **Maximize Contributions:**
   - **401(k):** Contribute enough to get any employer match first, as it’s essentially free money.
   - **IRA:** Max out contributions if you can, as these accounts often provide valuable tax advantages.

### 2. **Investment Types:**
   - **401(k) and IRA:** Focus on long-term growth with stocks. Use tax-advantaged accounts for higher-growth investments since you won’t pay capital gains taxes until withdrawal.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Consider holding bonds or dividend-paying stocks here, which might be taxed at a lower rate or where you can manage tax implications better.

### 3. **Tax Considerations:**
   - **Tax-Deferred Accounts:** (401(k) and IRA) are great for assets that may generate high taxable income or gains.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Use for investments that generate lower taxes or have strategic tax use, such as harvesting tax losses.

### 4. **Withdrawal Strategy:**
   - Plan how you will withdraw funds in retirement to minimize taxes (e.g., withdrawing from taxable accounts first).

### 5. **Rebalance Regularly:**
   - Periodically review your asset allocation based on your goals, market performance, and changes in your financial situation.

### Example Allocation Strategy:
- **401(k):** 40% (including employer match)
- **IRA:** 30%
- **Taxable Accounts:** 30%

This division can vary based on your age, risk tolerance, and retirement timeline. Consulting with a financial advisor can also provide personalized insights based on your specific circumstances.
