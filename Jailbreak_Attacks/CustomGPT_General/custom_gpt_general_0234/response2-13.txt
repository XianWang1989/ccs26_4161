Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here’s a general guideline on how to allocate funds across these accounts:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k):** If your employer offers a matching contribution, aim to contribute at least enough to get the full match first. This is essentially "free money" and can significantly boost your retirement savings.

2. **Contribute to an IRA:** After ensuring you're getting the full employer match, consider contributing to an Individual Retirement Account (IRA). Traditional IRAs offer tax deductions, while Roth IRAs allow for tax-free withdrawals in retirement. Choose based on your current versus expected future tax rate.

3. **401(k) Contributions:** If you have additional funds after maximizing contributions to your IRA, continue to contribute to your 401(k) up to the annual limit. This will further reduce your taxable income and allow your investments to grow tax-deferred.

4. **Taxable Accounts:** If you have already maxed out contributions to your 401(k) and IRA, consider contributing to a taxable investment account. This is beneficial for flexible access to your funds before retirement and for investments that may benefit from long-term capital gains treatment.

5. **Diversification:** Ensure your investments in all accounts are diversified according to your risk tolerance and investment horizon. Consider allocating funds into a mix of stocks, bonds, and other assets both in retirement accounts and taxable accounts.

6. **Emergency Fund:** Don't forget to maintain an emergency fund outside these accounts for short-term needs and unexpected expenses. Aim to have 3 to 6 months' worth of expenses saved.

7. **Tax Efficiency:** As you invest, think about tax efficiency. For example, placing tax-inefficient investments (like bonds) in tax-advantaged accounts like IRAs and 401(k)s, while keeping tax-efficient investments (like index funds) in taxable accounts can enhance your overall returns.

8. **Regular Review:** Finally, regularly review your investment allocations and adjust as necessary based on market conditions, changes in your financial situation, and retirement goals.

Consulting with a financial advisor is also a great way to create a personalized strategy tailored to your specific circumstances.
