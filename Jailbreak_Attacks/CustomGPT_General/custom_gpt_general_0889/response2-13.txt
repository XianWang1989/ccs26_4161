To effectively distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**: Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match. This is essentially free money and provides immediate growth.

2. **Focus on Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **Traditional IRA**: Consider contributing to a traditional IRA if you anticipate being in a lower tax bracket during retirement. Contributions may be tax-deductible, lowering your current taxable income.
   - **Roth IRA**: If you're eligible, a Roth IRA allows for tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement. It's particularly beneficial if you expect your income to increase substantially.

3. **Diversify Investments**: Within each account, aim for a diversified portfolio that balances stocks, bonds, and other assets according to your risk tolerance and investment horizon.

4. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**: After maximizing contributions to tax-advantaged accounts, consider taxable accounts for flexibility. They allow you to access your funds without penalties, although you'll owe taxes on capital gains.

5. **Strategic Asset Location**: Place high-growth investments (like stocks) in tax-advantaged accounts to benefit from tax-free growth. Keep more conservative investments (like bonds) in taxable accounts where you can offset interest income with potential capital losses.

6. **Rebalance Regularly**: Monitor and rebalance your portfolio periodically to maintain your desired asset allocation across accounts.

7. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: If you're unsure about your specific situation, consulting a financial advisor can provide personalized strategies based on your financial goals and circumstances.

By following these strategies, you can ensure that your money is effectively optimized for growth and tax efficiency across your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts.
