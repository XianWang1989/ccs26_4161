To effectively distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Tax Advantages**:
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money. Max out if possible since contributions are pre-tax, reducing your taxable income.
   - **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**: Choose based on your current tax rate and retirement expectations. Traditional IRAs offer tax-deferred growth, while Roth IRAs allow tax-free withdrawals in retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for investments that may generate immediate income or capital gains, as they are subject to different tax rules compared to retirement accounts.

2. **Investment Strategy**:
   - Place more aggressive investments (like stocks) in your 401(k) and IRA, as these are long-term accounts with the potential for higher growth.
   - Use taxable accounts for more conservative investments or for those that may frequently trade, to avoid triggering short-term capital gains taxes.

3. **Liquidity Needs**:
   - Keep funds in taxable accounts if you anticipate needing money before retirement, since accessing funds from a 401(k) or IRA may come with penalties if withdrawn early.

4. **Diversification**:
   - Spread your investments across different asset classes and sectors within each account to mitigate risk.

5. **Regular Rebalance**:
   - Periodically review and adjust your investment allocation as your financial situation and market conditions change.

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**:
   - Tailor your strategy to your individual financial situation and retirement goals with a professional's help.

By balancing contributions and investments across these account types, you can optimize your growth potential and tax efficiency.
