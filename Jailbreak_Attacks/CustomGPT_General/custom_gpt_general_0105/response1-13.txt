Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors such as your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here’s a general approach to consider:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match, as it’s essentially free money. The contribution limit is typically higher than IRAs.
   - **IRA**: After maximizing your 401(k) match, consider contributing to a Traditional or Roth IRA (depending on your eligibility and tax strategy).

### 2. **Allocate Based on Tax Efficiency**
   - **Tax-Deferred Accounts (401(k) and Traditional IRA)**: Ideal for high-growth investments since you won’t pay taxes on gains until withdrawal.
   - **Roth IRA**: Great for tax-free growth; consider holding investments expected to appreciate significantly here.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Suitable for less volatile investments. Use these for short-term goals or to generate income with lower capital gains tax implications.

### 3. **Consider Your Time Horizon**
   - Long-term investments should be placed in tax-advantaged accounts to maximize growth.
   - For short-term needs, keep funds in taxable accounts for easier access.

### 4. **Diversify Investments**
   - Ensure a balanced portfolio across all accounts that aligns with your risk tolerance, investing across stocks, bonds, and other asset classes.

### 5. **Monitor and Adjust**
   - Regularly review your allocations and adjust as your financial situation, goals, or market conditions change.

### Conclusion
Customize your strategy according to your personal financial situation, and consider consulting a financial advisor for tailored advice. This approach generally helps maximize tax efficiency and growth potential.
