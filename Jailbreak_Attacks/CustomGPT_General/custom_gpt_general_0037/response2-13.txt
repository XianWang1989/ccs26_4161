To effectively distribute your money among a 401(k), Individual Retirement Account (IRA), and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to receive the full employer match, as this is essentially free money.

2. **Contribution Limits**: Be aware of annual contribution limits for each account type. For 2023, the limit for 401(k) contributions is $22,500 (or $30,000 if you’re aged 50 or older), while the IRA limit is $6,500 (or $7,500 if you're 50 or older).

3. **Tax Considerations**:
   - **401(k) and Traditional IRA**: Contributions are tax-deferred, reducing taxable income in the year you contribute. However, withdrawals are taxed as ordinary income during retirement.
   - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, allowing for tax-free withdrawals in retirement. If eligible, consider maximizing contributions to a Roth IRA.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Suitable for more flexible access to funds. They don’t have contribution limits but may incur capital gains tax on earnings when assets are sold.

4. **Asset Allocation**: Consider risk tolerance and investment goals. Generally, place higher-risk investments in tax-advantaged accounts (401(k), IRA) to benefit from tax deferment, while allocating more conservative investments to taxable accounts to minimize tax liabilities.

5. **Rebalancing and Withdrawal Strategy**: Regularly review and adjust your portfolio to maintain your desired asset allocation. During retirement, consider withdrawing from taxable accounts first to allow tax-advantaged accounts to grow longer.

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: For personalized advice tailored to your specific financial situation, a qualified financial advisor can provide insights based on your unique goals.

The ideal distribution will depend on individual circumstances, including income, tax bracket, and retirement goals.
