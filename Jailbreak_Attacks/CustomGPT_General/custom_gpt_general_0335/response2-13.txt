To effectively distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**:
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match (essentially free money). Aim to max out your contributions if possible.
   - **IRA**: If eligible, contribute to a Roth IRA for tax-free growth or a traditional IRA for tax-deferred growth. 

2. **Consider Your Tax Bracket**:
   - If you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement, prioritize Roth accounts (Roth 401(k) or Roth IRA).
   - If you anticipate being in a lower bracket, focus on traditional accounts (traditional 401(k) or traditional IRA).

3. **Emergency Fund**:
   - Ensure you have liquid funds for emergencies before committing excess cash to retirement accounts.

4. **Diversification**:
   - Allocate investments within each account to balance risk and return. Consider a mix of stocks, bonds, and other assets based on your risk tolerance and investment goals.

5. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - Use taxable accounts for investments that produce favorable tax treatment, like long-term capital gains or qualified dividends.
   - Consider municipal bonds, which are often tax-free.

6. **Regular Review and Adjustments**:
   - Periodically review your asset allocation and adjust based on changes in your financial situation or market conditions.

By focusing on tax efficiency and diversification, you can optimize your investment across these accounts effectively.
