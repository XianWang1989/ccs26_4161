Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here’s a general approach:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match**
   - Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match, as this is essentially "free money."

### 2. **Consider Tax Advantages**
   - **401(k)**: Pre-tax contributions lower your taxable income now. Great for high earners.
   - **IRA**: Choose between traditional (tax-deductible contributions) or Roth IRA (tax-free withdrawals in retirement).
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Best for short-term goals or flexibility; keep in mind capital gains tax.

### 3. **Increase Contributions Gradually**
   - Aim to max out your contributions to retirement accounts once you're stable with the employer match. The annual limits vary, so keep updated.

### 4. **Diversify Investments**
   - Spread investments across asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) based on your risk tolerance and investment horizon, adjusting as needed.

### 5. **Monitor and Adjust**
   - Regularly review your allocations based on performance and life changes (e.g., major purchases or changes in income).

### 6. **Tax Considerations**
   - Withdrawals from a traditional 401(k) and IRA are taxable; Roth withdrawals are tax-free. Plan your withdrawals strategically to minimize tax impact during retirement.

### 7. **Withdrawal Strategy**
   - Consider your withdrawal strategy for retirement—utilizing taxable accounts first can help reduce tax liability on 401(k) and IRA withdrawals later.

### Conclusion
Start with a solid emergency fund, then focus on maximizing tax-advantaged accounts. Regularly reassess your strategy based on financial goals and changing circumstances. It's also wise to consult with a financial advisor for personalized guidance.
