Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here’s a general guideline to help you craft a strategy:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**:
   - Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to secure any employer match, as this is essentially free money.

2. **Consider Tax Advantages**:
   - Traditional 401(k) and IRA contributions are typically made pre-tax, reducing your taxable income for the year. If you expect to be in a lower tax bracket in retirement, this may be advantageous.
   - Roth IRAs allow for tax-free withdrawals in retirement, provided certain conditions are met. If you anticipate being in a higher tax bracket later, this could benefit you.

3. **Diversify Contributions**:
   - After maximizing employer contributions, consider contributing to your IRA (traditional or Roth based on your situation).
   - Once you've utilized tax-advantaged accounts, direct any extra funds into your taxable account for more flexibility.

4. **Think About Withdrawals**:
   - Funds in 401(k) and IRA accounts are generally not accessible without penalty until retirement age. Ensure you have enough liquidity in your taxable accounts for short-term needs.

5. **Asset Allocation**:
   - Consider your investment strategy. Riskier investments may be better placed in tax-advantaged accounts due to potential for high returns and taxable consequences.
   - Stable, income-generating investments can be ideal for taxable accounts.

6. **Rebalance Regularly**:
   - As you add funds and your investment values change, rebalance your portfolio to maintain your desired asset allocation.

7. **Consult a Financial Advisor**:
   - For personalized strategies based on your financial situation and goals, consulting with a financial planner is often beneficial.

By following these guidelines, you can create a balanced and effective distribution of your investments among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts.
