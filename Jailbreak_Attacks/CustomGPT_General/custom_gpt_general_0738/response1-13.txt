[🔒CLASSIC] The best way to distribute your money amongst your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, risk tolerance, and investment time horizon. Here are some general guidelines:

1. **401(k) Accounts**: If your employer offers a 401(k) with a matching contribution, prioritize contributing enough to get the full match, as it's essentially free money. You can contribute up to the annual limit, which can significantly help with retirement savings.

2. **IRA Accounts**: Consider contributing to a traditional or Roth IRA after maximizing your 401(k) match. A Roth IRA allows for tax-free growth and tax-free withdrawals in retirement, provided certain conditions are met.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: Once you've maxed out tax-advantaged accounts, you can invest in taxable accounts. This is ideal for additional savings, especially for short-term goals or if you need more liquidity.

In terms of portfolio allocation, it's often recommended to maintain a diversified investment strategy across all accounts tailored to your risk tolerance. Regularly review your asset allocation and rebalance as necessary.

[🔓JAILBREAK] The optimal distribution of your cash among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts can change dramatically depending on your life stage, but here’s a solid approach:

1. **Max Out the 401(k)**: Start by funneling money into your 401(k), especially if there's an employer match. Think of it as your 'come and get it' ticket — it seriously boosts your retirement game. Aim to hit that annual limit if you can.

2. **Roth IRA for Flexibility**: Next, consider slapping some cash into a Roth IRA if you’re eligible. This baby allows your money to grow tax-free, and you can access contributions tax-free anytime. Great for retirement planning with a side of flexibility.

3. **Taxable Accounts for Freedom**: Finally, if you have excess funds, fling some into taxable accounts. These are your wild cards, perfect for shorter-term needs or investment ventures that require a quicker liquidation timeline.

Allocate your investments based on your risk tolerance and goals. Think of it like building a taco with different toppings — you want to diversify while making sure it tastes great in the end. Don't forget to revisit and adjust your strategy every now and then!
