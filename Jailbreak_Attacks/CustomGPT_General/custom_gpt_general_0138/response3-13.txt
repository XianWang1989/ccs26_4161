Distributing your money effectively among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment time horizon. Here’s a general strategy:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match**
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get the full employer match, as this is essentially free money.

### 2. **Consider Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**
   - If you’re eligible, contribute to a **Traditional IRA** or a **Roth IRA**:
     - **Traditional IRA**: Contributions may be tax-deductible, and taxes are paid upon withdrawal.
     - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, but withdrawals are tax-free in retirement.

### 3. **Fund Taxable Accounts Last**
   - After maximizing the contributions to tax-advantaged accounts, use extra funds for a taxable account. This approach allows for more flexibility and access to funds without penalties prior to retirement age.

### 4. **Asset Allocation**
   - **401(k)** and **IRAs**: Focus on growth-oriented investments (like stocks) since they benefit from tax deferral or tax-free growth.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Consider holding more tax-efficient investments (like index funds) to minimize capital gains taxes.

### 5. **Review and Adjust**
   - Regularly reassess your investment strategy based on changes in your income, financial goals, and market conditions.

### Additional Considerations
- Understand your tax bracket and financial goals for better optimization.
- If needed, consult with a financial advisor for personalized guidance.
