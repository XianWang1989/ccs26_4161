Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts requires considering several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment strategy. Here’s a general approach:

1. **401(k) Contributions**:
   - **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to get any employer match, as it’s essentially free money.
   - **Pre-Tax vs. Roth**: Decide between traditional (pre-tax) and Roth (after-tax) options based on your current vs. expected future tax situation.

2. **IRA Accounts**:
   - **Contribute to an IRA**: Consider both Traditional and Roth IRAs. Roth IRAs are beneficial for tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement, especially if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket later.
   - **Tax Advantages**: Take advantage of the tax deductions available for Traditional IRA contributions if eligible.

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - **Invest for Short-Term Goals**: Use these for funds you might need in the near future or for goals like buying a house.
   - **Tax-Efficient Investments**: Consider index funds or tax-efficient ETFs to minimize capital gains taxes.

4. **General Strategy**:
   - **Emergency Fund**: Ensure you have 3-6 months of living expenses saved in a high-interest savings account before maximizing retirement contributions.
   - **Diversification**: Maintain a diversified portfolio across all accounts, adjusting asset allocation based on your risk tolerance and time horizon.
   - **Rebalance Regularly**: Periodically review and rebalance your investments to stay aligned with your goals.

5. **Consult a Professional**: Consider working with a financial advisor for personalized guidance based on your specific financial situation.

This framework will help you create a structured investment strategy tailored to your needs.
