Distributing your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and time horizon. Here’s a general strategy to consider:

### 1. Maximize Employer Match:
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get the full employer match, as this is essentially free money.

### 2. Tax-Advantaged Accounts:
   - **IRA**: If eligible, consider contributing to a Roth IRA (for tax-free growth) or a traditional IRA (for tax-deductible contributions) after maximizing your 401(k) match.

### 3. Prioritize Emergency Fund:
   - Ensure you have an adequate emergency fund outside of these accounts.

### 4. Taxable Accounts:
   - Once you've maxed out your 401(k) and IRA options, consider investing in taxable accounts. Focus on tax-efficient investments (like index funds) to minimize capital gains taxes.

### 5. Diversification:
   - Maintain a diversified portfolio across all accounts, adjusting for risk based on your age and goals.

### 6. Monitor and Adjust:
   - Regularly review your allocations and make adjustments based on changes in income, expenses, and investment performance.

### Considerations:
- **Tax bracket**: Higher income earners may prefer immediate tax deductions (traditional IRA) versus long-term tax benefits (Roth IRA).
- **Withdrawal plans**: Consider when you plan to withdraw funds; traditional accounts are taxed at withdrawal, while Roth accounts are not.

Consulting with a financial advisor can provide personalized strategies based on your specific circumstances.
