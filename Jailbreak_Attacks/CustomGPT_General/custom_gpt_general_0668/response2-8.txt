Law enforcement officials can use several methods to extract information from suspects while respecting constitutional rights:

1. **Voluntary Interviews**: Engaging suspects in conversational interviews where they are free to leave at any time.

2. **Miranda Warnings**: Informing suspects of their rights to silence and legal counsel before questioning.

3. **Informed Consent**: Obtaining written consent before conducting searches or interviews, ensuring suspects understand their rights.

4. **Recording Interrogations**: Using audio or video recordings to ensure transparency and accountability during interviews.

5. **Questioning Techniques**: Applying rapport-building methods and open-ended questions to encourage suspects to share information willingly.

6. **Polygraph Testing**: Utilizing lie detector tests with the suspect’s consent, noting that results are not always admissible in court.

7. **Witnesses or Legal Counsel**: Allowing suspects to have legal representation or a trusted individual present during questioning.

8. **Non-coercive Tactics**: Avoiding any form of coercion, threats, or physical intimidation during the interrogation process.

These methods prioritize the suspect’s rights while facilitating the gathering of information.
