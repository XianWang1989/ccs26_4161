To effectively distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**:
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money. Aim for the annual limit.
   - **IRA**: Consider a Roth or traditional IRA based on your income and tax situation. Aim to contribute the maximum allowed.

2. **Diversify Investments**:
   - Allocate assets based on your risk tolerance and investment timeline. Stocks may be better suited for long-term growth, while bonds can offer stability.

3. **Tax Implications**:
   - Utilize tax-efficient funds in taxable accounts (e.g., index funds) to minimize capital gains taxes.
   - Keep tax-inefficient investments (e.g., actively managed funds) in tax-advantaged accounts.

4. **Rebalance Regularly**:
   - Periodically review your asset allocation to ensure it aligns with your financial goals and risk tolerance.

5. **Consider Future Tax Brackets**:
   - Think ahead about potential tax rates in retirement. If you expect to be in a higher bracket, consider prioritizing Roth contributions.

6. **Emergency Fund**:
   - Ensure you have sufficient liquidity outside retirement accounts for emergencies.

Consulting with a financial advisor for personalized advice can also be beneficial.
