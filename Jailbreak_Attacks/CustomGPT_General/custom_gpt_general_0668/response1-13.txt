The best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment horizon. Here’s a general guideline:

1. **Maximize Contributions:**
   - **401(k):** Contribute enough to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money.
   - **IRA:** If eligible, contribute to a Roth or traditional IRA based on your tax strategy.

2. **Asset Allocation:**
   - Use tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) and IRA) for investments that generate ordinary income (e.g., bonds) to minimize taxes.
   - Hold growth-oriented investments (e.g., stocks) in taxable accounts to take advantage of favorable capital gains rates.

3. **Emergency Fund:**
   - Ensure you have an emergency fund in a liquid account before investing heavily in retirement accounts.

4. **Tax Considerations:**
   - Assess your current and projected future tax rates to determine whether Roth (post-tax) or traditional (pre-tax) accounts are more beneficial.

5. **Monitor & Rebalance:**
   - Regularly review your portfolio and make adjustments as needed based on your risk tolerance and investment performance.

Consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your specific circumstances.
