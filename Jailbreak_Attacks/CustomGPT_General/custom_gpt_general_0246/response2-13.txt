When considering how to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, it's essential to take into account several factors, including your financial goals, risk tolerance, tax implications, and investment time horizon. Here’s a general approach you might consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**: If your employer offers a match for your 401(k) contributions, prioritize this to take full advantage of free money. Contribute enough to get the maximum match before diverting funds elsewhere.

2. **Consider Tax Implications**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are often made pre-tax, and you pay taxes upon withdrawal during retirement. This can be beneficial if you expect to be in a lower tax bracket in retirement.
   - **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**: Traditional IRAs can also provide tax-deferred growth, while Roth IRAs allow for tax-free withdrawals in retirement if certain conditions are met. Choose based on your current and expected future tax situation.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Investments here do not have tax advantages but can offer flexibility. You will pay capital gains taxes on profits. Consider placing investments that generate higher taxable income (like bonds) in tax-advantaged accounts.

3. **Diversification**: Ensure your overall portfolio across all accounts is well-diversified. This may include a mixture of stocks, bonds, and other investments suitable for your risk tolerance and investment goals.

4. **Investment Horizon and Risk Tolerance**: If you're young and have a long time until retirement, you might lean more toward stocks for growth. If you're closer to retirement, consider a more balanced approach with bonds to preserve capital.

5. **Periodic Review and Rebalancing**: Regularly assess your asset allocation across all accounts to ensure it aligns with your goals. Life changes and market fluctuations may require adjustments.

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: For personalized advice tailored to your specific situation, consider seeking guidance from a financial advisor who can provide insights based on your total financial picture.

Ultimately, the best distribution strategy is one that aligns with your individual financial needs and retirement goals.
