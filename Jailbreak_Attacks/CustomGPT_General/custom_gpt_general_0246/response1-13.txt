[🔒CLASSIC] The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several individual factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, investment horizon, and risk tolerance. Here’s a general strategy:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**: If your employer offers a match on your 401(k) contributions, it's wise to contribute at least enough to take full advantage of this benefit, as it’s essentially free money.

2. **Tax-Advantaged Accounts**: After maximizing the employer match, consider contributing to an IRA (Traditional or Roth, depending on your income level and tax situation). Traditional IRAs can reduce your taxable income now, while Roth IRAs offer tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement.

3. **Fund Remaining 401(k)**: Continue to contribute to your 401(k) up to the annual contribution limit if you're still able, as this account also offers tax-deferred growth.

4. **Taxable Accounts**: Once you've maximized contributions to your tax-advantaged accounts, you can allocate funds to your taxable accounts. These can provide more liquidity and flexibility but are subject to capital gains taxes on profits.

5. **Consider Asset Allocation**: Within each account, develop an appropriate asset allocation strategy that aligns with your risk tolerance and investment goals (e.g., stocks vs. bonds).

It's advisable to consult with a financial advisor who can provide personalized guidance based on your specific financial situation.
