When deciding how to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Tax Advantages**:
   - **Maximize Employer Match**: If your 401(k) offers a match, contribute at least enough to get the full match first.
   - **Consider Tax Bracket**: If you're in a higher tax bracket now, prioritize pre-tax accounts (401(k) or traditional IRA) to reduce current taxable income. If you expect to be in a lower bracket during retirement, consider post-tax accounts like a Roth IRA.

2. **Contribution Limits**:
   - Ensure you're aware of the annual contribution limits for each type of account. For 2023, the limits are:
     - 401(k): $22,500 (or $30,000 if age 50+).
     - IRA: $6,500 (or $7,500 if age 50+).

3. **Emergency Savings**:
   - Maintain enough liquidity in taxable accounts for emergencies, as retirement accounts can impose penalties for early withdrawals.

4. **Long-Term Growth**:
   - Consider placing higher-growth investments (like stocks) in accounts meant for long-term growth (401(k) and IRAs) due to tax-deferred benefits. Reserve stable investments for taxable accounts to minimize taxes on dividends and capital gains.

5. **Diversification**:
   - Diversify your investments across all accounts to spread risk and enhance growth potential.

6. **Withdrawal Strategy**:
   - Plan for how you'll withdraw funds in retirement; typically, you might withdraw from taxable accounts first, followed by tax-deferred accounts, to manage tax liabilities effectively.

7. **Seek Professional Advice**:
   - Depending on your financial situation, consulting a financial advisor can help tailor a strategy to your specific needs.

This balanced approach can help optimize your money allocation for both current and future financial goals.
