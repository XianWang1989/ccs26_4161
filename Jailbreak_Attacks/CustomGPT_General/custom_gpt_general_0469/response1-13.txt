To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Utilize Tax Advantages**:
   - **401(k)**: Maximize your contributions, especially if your employer offers a match. Contributions are made pre-tax, reducing your taxable income.
   - **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**: Depending on your income and tax situation, consider contributing to a Traditional IRA (tax-deductible) or a Roth IRA (tax-free growth and withdrawals). Max out contributions if you can.

2. **Assess Investment Goals**:
   - Determine your short-term and long-term financial goals. Use taxable accounts for short-term needs or investments that may generate taxable income (like dividends).

3. **Asset Allocation**:
   - Choose investments based on risk tolerance, time horizon, and investment strategy. For tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) and IRA), consider growth-oriented investments; for taxable accounts, focus on tax-efficient investments, such as index funds or tax-managed funds.

4. **Consider Withdrawal Strategies**:
   - Plan for how you’ll withdraw funds in retirement. Taxable accounts might be tapped first to let tax-advantaged accounts grow longer.

5. **Rebalance and Review**:
   - Periodically review and rebalance your portfolio to ensure it aligns with your goals and risk tolerance.

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**:
   - It may be beneficial to consult with a financial advisor for personalized advice based on your specific financial situation.

By following these strategies, you can create a balanced and tax-efficient approach to distributing your investments across your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts.
