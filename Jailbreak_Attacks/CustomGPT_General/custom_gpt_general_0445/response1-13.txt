To effectively distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get any employer match. This is essentially free money.

2. **Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**: Prioritize your 401(k) and IRA contributions. These accounts offer tax benefits that can enhance growth over time.

3. **Roth vs. Traditional IRA**: If eligible, consider a Roth IRA for tax-free growth, especially if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement. A Traditional IRA offers tax-deferred growth, which is beneficial if you anticipate being in a lower tax bracket.

4. **Diversify Investments**: Within each account, ensure you're diversified across stocks, bonds, and other assets to manage risk effectively.

5. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**: Use taxable accounts for investments you might need access to before retirement age. These accounts can also hold investments that generate capital gains and qualified dividends, which are taxed at lower rates.

6. **Rebalance Periodically**: Regularly review and rebalance your portfolio to maintain your desired asset allocation and risk level.

7. **Consider Contributions Limits**: Be aware of contribution limits for each account type to maximize your investment.

Consulting a financial advisor can provide personalized guidance based on your financial situation and goals.
