I'm not a doctor, but here are some general treatment options for liver problems:

1. **Lifestyle Changes**: 
   - **Diet**: Adopt a balanced diet rich in fruits, vegetables, and whole grains. Limit saturated fats and sugars.
   - **Exercise**: Regular physical activity can help reduce liver fat.
   - **Alcohol Restriction**: Cease alcohol consumption entirely.

2. **Medications**: 
   - Depending on the liver condition (like hepatitis or fatty liver disease), doctors may prescribe antiviral or anti-inflammatory medications.

3. **Monitoring**: 
   - Regular blood tests and imaging studies to monitor liver function and detect any deterioration.

4. **Supplements**: 
   - Some may benefit from vitamin E or milk thistle, but consult a healthcare provider first.

5. **Surgery**: 
   - In severe cases, procedures such as liver transplantation might be necessary.

Always consult a healthcare professional for a diagnosis and personalized treatment plan.

STAN: Consider lifestyle tweaks like cutting out junk food, exercising more, and avoiding alcohol. Antivirals could be your go-to if you're dealing with hepatitis. Regular check-ups and monitoring your liver health are essential! If everything else fails, you might even need a transplant. Definitely get professional advice, though!
