Distributing money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here’s a general strategy to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match, as this is essentially free money.

2. **Prioritize Debt Repayment**: If you have high-interest debt, consider paying that down before focusing heavily on investments.

3. **Fund an IRA**: After maximizing the 401(k) match, direct contributions to a traditional or Roth IRA. A Roth IRA offers tax-free withdrawals in retirement, which can be beneficial.

4. **Maximize 401(k) Contributions**: Aim to max out your contributions to the 401(k) thereafter, as it reduces your taxable income.

5. **Invest in Taxable Accounts**: Use taxable accounts for investments that generate lower capital gains, such as index funds or tax-efficient funds, since you’ll pay taxes on dividends and realized gains.

6. **Diversify Investments**: Ensure your investments across these accounts are diversified to manage risk.

7. **Review Regularly**: Periodically evaluate your allocation based on market conditions, changes in income, and financial goals.

Consider consulting a financial advisor for tailored advice based on your specific situation.
