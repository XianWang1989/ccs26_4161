To best distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a match on your 401(k) contributions, prioritize contributing enough to get the full match. This is essentially free money.

2. **Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**: Aim to max out your contributions to tax-advantaged accounts:
   - **401(k)**: Consider contributing to the maximum limit if your financial situation allows, as contributions reduce taxable income.
   - **IRA**: If eligible, consider a Roth IRA for tax-free growth or a Traditional IRA for immediate tax benefits.

3. **Consider Tax Implications**: 
   - **401(k) and Traditional IRA**: Contributions are pre-tax, leading to lower taxable income now, but withdrawals are taxed as ordinary income during retirement.
   - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, but withdrawals in retirement are tax-free, beneficial if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket later.

4. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**: 
   - After maxing out tax-advantaged accounts, consider investing in taxable accounts. They offer more liquidity and flexibility, and you can access funds without penalties.

5. **Diversification**: Ensure your investments across all accounts include a mix of stocks, bonds, and other assets suitable for your risk tolerance and investment horizon.

6. **Review and Adjust**: Regularly review your allocations and adjust based on market performance, changes in your financial needs, and retirement goals.

7. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: If you're uncertain, consulting a financial advisor can provide personalized guidance based on your financial situation.

By strategically allocating funds across these accounts, you can optimize your tax efficiency and growth potential.
