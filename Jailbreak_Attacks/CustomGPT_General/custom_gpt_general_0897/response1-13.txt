Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some general guidelines:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**
   - **Strategy**: Contribute enough to get any employer match, as this is essentially "free" money.
   - **Benefit**: Increases your retirement savings substantially.

### 2. **Contribute to an IRA**
   - **Strategy**: After maximizing your 401(k) match, consider contributing to a traditional or Roth IRA, depending on your income and tax situation.
   - **Benefit**: IRAs often provide a wider range of investment options compared to 401(k)s.

### 3. **Fund 401(k) to the Annual Limit**
   - **Strategy**: Once you've funded your IRA, continue contributing to your 401(k) up to the annual maximum.
   - **Benefit**: Offers tax-deferred growth and potentially lower taxes on contributions.

### 4. **Invest in Taxable Accounts Last**
   - **Strategy**: Use taxable accounts for funds you may need before retirement and for investments that generate capital gains.
   - **Benefit**: Capital gains are taxed at a lower rate, and you can withdraw funds without penalties at any time.

### 5. **Consider Tax Efficiency**
   - **Strategy**: Hold investments that produce ordinary income (like bonds) in tax-advantaged accounts and equities in taxable accounts.
   - **Benefit**: Reduces your overall tax burden.

### 6. **Reassess Based on Life Changes**
   - **Strategy**: Regularly review your investments and adjust based on major life events (like marriage, children, job changes).
   - **Benefit**: Keeps your investment strategy aligned with your goals.

### Summary
A solid approach is to start with maximizing the 401(k) employer match, then fund an IRA, followed by contributing more to your 401(k) until you reach the limit, and finally, utilize taxable accounts for additional investments. Always consult with a financial advisor to tailor a plan specific to your situation.
