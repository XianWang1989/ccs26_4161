When deciding how to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider several factors like your financial goals, tax situation, retirement timeline, and risk tolerance. Here’s a general guideline for each type of account:

1. **401(k)**: 
   - **Employer Match**: Prioritize contributing enough to receive any employer match, as it’s essentially “free money.”
   - **Tax Advantages**: Contributions may be made with pre-tax dollars (traditional) or after-tax dollars (Roth); choose based on your current vs. expected future tax rates.

2. **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**: 
   - **Contribution Limits**: In 2023, you can contribute up to $6,500 ($7,500 if age 50 or older). Roth IRAs offer tax-free withdrawals in retirement, while traditional IRAs provide tax deductions now.
   - **Investment Choices**: IRAs often have more investment options than 401(k)s, allowing for diversification.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: 
   - **Flexibility**: These accounts lack the tax advantages of the first two, but they offer flexibility for withdrawals and investment choices.
   - **Capital Gains Tax**: Be mindful of taxes on capital gains; long-term investments can be taxed at a lower rate.

### Suggested Distribution Strategy:
- **Emergency Fund First**: Ensure you have an emergency fund covering 3-6 months' expenses before investing heavily.
- **Maximize 401(k)**: Especially if there’s an employer match. Aim for at least this amount.
- **Contribute to IRA**: Once your 401(k) is maxed out (up to the match), contribute to an IRA based on your tax strategy (traditional vs. Roth).
- **Invest in Taxable Accounts**: Use these for longer-term goals (such as buying a home or other investments) after maximizing tax-advantaged accounts.

### Final Considerations:
- **Rebalance Regularly**: Revisit your asset allocation annually or after significant life changes.
- **Consult a Financial Advisor**: For personalized advice, consider speaking with a financial planner who can assess your unique situation.

This approach combines growth potential with tax efficiency while allowing access to funds when needed.
