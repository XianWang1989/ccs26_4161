Determining the best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeframe. Here are some general guidelines to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Matches**: If your employer offers a match on your 401(k) contributions, try to contribute enough to get the full match. This is essentially "free money" that can significantly boost your retirement savings.

2. **Contribute to Tax-Advantaged Accounts**: After maximizing any employer match, consider contributing to an IRA (either traditional or Roth, depending on your eligibility and tax situation). These accounts provide tax advantages that can help your savings grow more efficiently.

3. **Consider Tax Implications**: Taxable accounts may offer more flexibility for withdrawals but do not provide the same tax advantages as retirement accounts. Be mindful of capital gains taxes when investing in taxable accounts. 

4. **Balance Your Contributions**: Depending on your situation, you may want to balance your contributions among these accounts. For instance, if you're in a high tax bracket now, a traditional IRA may be beneficial more than a taxable account.

5. **Diversification**: Regardless of account type, ensure you are diversifying your investments according to your risk tolerance and investment horizon.

6. **Review Regularly**: Your financial situation and tax laws may change, so it's essential to review your allocations regularly.

Consider consulting with a financial advisor to create a personalized strategy based on your individual situation.
