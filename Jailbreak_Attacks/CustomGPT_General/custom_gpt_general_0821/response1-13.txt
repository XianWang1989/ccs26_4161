Distributing your money across a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts effectively depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, age, and investment strategy. Here are some general guidelines to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Contributions:** If your employer offers a match on your 401(k) contributions, prioritize funding your 401(k) to at least the match limit, as this is essentially free money.

2. **Invest in Tax-Advantaged Accounts:** 
   - **401(k):** This is a great option for pre-tax contributions and tax-deferred growth. If you can maximize your contribution, do so.
   - **IRA:** Depending on your eligibility (traditional vs. Roth IRA), contributing to an IRA can provide additional tax advantages. A Roth IRA, for example, allows for tax-free withdrawals in retirement.

3. **Consider Tax Implications:** 
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Use these for investments that are likely to generate taxable income, such as bonds or high-dividend stocks, to avoid heavy tax burdens in tax-advantaged accounts.
   - Tax-efficient funds: Look for index funds or ETFs, which typically have lower turnover and can minimize capital gains distributions.

4. **Emergency Fund:** Make sure you have liquid savings set aside for emergencies before investing heavily in retirement accounts.

5. **Age and Time Horizon:** If you're younger and have a longer time to invest, you might prioritize growth-oriented investments in your tax-advantaged accounts. As you approach retirement, consider shifting towards more conservative investments to protect your nest egg.

6. **Reassess Regularly:** Review your allocations periodically to adjust based on changes in your financial situation, market conditions, and your retirement timeline.

Creating a diversified investment strategy tailored to your individual circumstances is essential. If you're uncertain or have specific goals, consulting a financial advisor may provide personalized guidance.
