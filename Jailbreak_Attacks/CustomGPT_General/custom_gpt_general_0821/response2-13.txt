It's great that you're thinking ahead about your financial strategy! Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts can significantly impact your long-term wealth. Here are some general guidelines to consider:

1. **401(k)**:
   - **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a match, contribute at least enough to get the full match—it’s free money!
   - **Contribution Limits**: For 2024, the contribution limit is $23,000 (or $30,000 if you’re 50 or older). Try to maximize contributions if possible.

2. **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**:
   - **Consider Your Tax Situation**: If you expect to be in a lower tax bracket during retirement, a Traditional IRA might be better for you since contributions are tax-deductible. If you expect to be in the same or higher bracket, consider a Roth IRA for tax-free withdrawals in retirement.
   - **Annual Contribution Limits**: The total contribution limit for 2024 is $6,500 (or $7,500 if you’re 50 or older).

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - **Flexible Access**: Use taxable accounts for investments you may need access to before retirement, since there are no penalties for early withdrawal.
   - **Tax Efficiency**: Focus on tax-efficient investments, like index funds or ETFs, to minimize capital gains taxes.

### Suggested Distribution Strategy:

- **Emergency Fund First**: Ensure you have 3-6 months’ worth of living expenses saved in a high-yield savings account.
- **Employer 401(k) Match**: Contribute enough to get your employer's full match.
- **Max Out IRA Contributions**: Prioritize maxing out your IRA contributions next.
- **Max Your 401(k)**: If you can, continue to increase contributions to your 401(k) up to the limit.
- **Invest in Taxable Accounts**: Once you've maximized your 401(k) and IRA contributions, you can allocate extra funds into a taxable account.

This strategy balances tax efficiency, growth potential, and liquidity. You may also want to consult with a financial advisor to tailor these recommendations to your specific situation.
