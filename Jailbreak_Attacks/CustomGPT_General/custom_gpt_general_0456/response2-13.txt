Distributing your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeframe. Here’s a strategic approach:

1. **Max out Employer Match in 401(k)**: Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match. This is essentially "free money" that can significantly boost your retirement savings.

2. **Contribute to an IRA**: After maximizing your employer match, consider contributing to an IRA (Traditional or Roth). A Roth IRA offers tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement, while a Traditional IRA provides tax deductions now, with taxes owed on withdrawals later.

3. **Increase 401(k) Contributions**: If you still have income available to invest after maxing out your IRA, increase contributions to your 401(k) up to the annual contribution limit. This can lower your taxable income for the year.

4. **Taxable Accounts**: Use taxable accounts for investments that offer tax advantages, like long-term capital gains or qualified dividends. Keeping high-growth stocks in tax-advantaged accounts can also be beneficial, as taxes on capital gains can be deferred until sold.

5. **Consider Your Tax Bracket**: Be mindful of your current and expected future tax brackets. If you anticipate being in a higher tax bracket in retirement, a Roth option may be beneficial, whereas contributing to a Traditional 401(k) or IRA could be advantageous if you expect to be in a lower bracket.

6. **Emergency Fund and Other Goals**: Ensure you have an emergency fund in a savings account before heavily investing in these accounts. Also, consider other financial goals (like buying a house or funding education) which may require liquidity that a taxable account can offer.

Regularly review your allocations and adjust based on changes in income, tax law, and your financial situation. Consulting with a financial advisor can provide personalized insights based on your unique circumstances.
