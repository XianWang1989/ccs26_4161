Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some general strategies:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**
   - **401(k)**: If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to get the full match. This is essentially “free money.”
   - **IRA**: Consider funding a Roth IRA if your income qualifies, as it offers tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement.

### 2. **Consider Your Tax Situation**
   - **Tax-Deferred Accounts** (401(k), Traditional IRA): Good for high-income years to reduce taxable income.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Useful for flexibility and access to funds before retirement age, but be mindful of capital gains taxes.

### 3. **Investment Goals and Time Horizon**
   - If your goal is retirement, prioritize 401(k) and IRAs.
   - For shorter-term goals (like buying a house), consider keeping more in taxable accounts for liquidity.

### 4. **Diversification and Risk Tolerance**
   - Ensure your investments in each account align with your overall risk tolerance. Consider a mix of stocks, bonds, and other assets.

### 5. **Regular Rebalancing**
   - Periodically review and rebalance your portfolio to maintain your desired asset allocation across all accounts.

### Example Distribution
- **401(k)**: 15-20% of annual income (or up to the max contribution limit)
- **IRA/Roth IRA**: $6,500 per year (if under 50; $7,500 if 50+)
- **Taxable Account**: Any additional savings after maxing out tax-advantaged accounts

### Final Thoughts
Consulting with a financial advisor can provide tailored advice based on your specific circumstances.
