Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, risk tolerance, and timeline. Here’s a general approach:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match (it's essentially free money). Aim to maximize contributions if possible, especially if you're in a high tax bracket.
   - **IRA**: Consider a traditional IRA for tax-deferred growth or a Roth IRA for tax-free withdrawals in retirement. Again, max out contributions if you can.

### 2. **Evaluate Tax Implications**
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for investments that generate capital gains or dividends, which can be more tax-efficient when held long-term.

### 3. **Diversification Strategy**
   - Maintain a balanced portfolio across all accounts. Consider your risk tolerance and time horizon for each. Equities might be more suitable for long-term growth, while bonds could provide stability.

### 4. **Emergency Fund**
   - Before heavily investing, ensure you have an emergency fund in a savings account to cover 3-6 months of expenses.

### 5. **Regular Review**
   - Periodically reassess your allocations based on changes in income, expenses, market conditions, and retirement goals.

### Example Allocation:
- **401(k)**: Up to employer match, then max contributions if possible.
- **IRA**: Max contributions.
- **Taxable Account**: Any extra funds, focusing on growth-oriented investments.

### Consult a Financial Advisor
Consider speaking with a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your specific situation.

Happy investing!
