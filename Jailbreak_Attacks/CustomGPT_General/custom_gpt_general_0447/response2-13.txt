To effectively distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Understand Tax Implications**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are typically pre-tax, reducing your taxable income now, but withdrawals in retirement are taxed as ordinary income.
   - **IRA (Traditional)**: Similar to a 401(k), contributions may be tax-deductible, and taxes are due upon withdrawal.
   - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, but withdrawals in retirement are tax-free.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Investments here are subject to capital gains taxes, both short-term and long-term, based on how long you hold them.

2. **Maximize Employer Match**:
   - Contribute enough to your 401(k) to take full advantage of any employer matching. This is essentially free money and can significantly boost your retirement savings.

3. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - If you haven't maxed out your contributions to your 401(k) or IRA (Traditional or Roth), prioritize these accounts for their tax benefits.

4. **Consider Your Retirement Timeline**:
   - If you are far from retirement, you may opt for a more aggressive investment strategy in growth-oriented assets like stocks in your 401(k) and IRA.
   - As you approach retirement, consider shifting towards more conservative investments to preserve capital.

5. **Diversification**:
   - Ensure your investments are diversified across different asset classes (stocks, bonds, real estate) to mitigate risk.

6. **Rebalance Regularly**:
   - Check your allocations periodically and rebalance as needed to maintain your desired risk level and investment strategy.

7. **Emergency Fund**:
   - Keep a portion of your savings in easily accessible and liquid accounts (like a high-yield savings account) for emergencies or immediate needs.

8. **Tax Efficiency**:
   - Consider placing tax-inefficient investments (like bonds) in tax-advantaged accounts and tax-efficient investments (like index funds) in taxable accounts to optimize your overall tax situation.

9. **Consult a Financial Advisor**:
   - Personal circumstances vary widely, so working with a financial advisor can help tailor a strategy specific to your goals, risk tolerance, and financial situation.

Ultimately, the appropriate distribution will depend on your individual financial goals, risk tolerance, and timeline for accessing your funds.
