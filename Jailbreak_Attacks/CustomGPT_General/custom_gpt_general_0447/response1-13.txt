Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts effectively depends on your financial goals, risk tolerance, and timeline for withdrawals. Here are some general strategies:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First:**
   - **401(k):** If your employer offers matching contributions, contribute enough to get the full match. This is essentially free money.
   - **IRA:** After maximizing the 401(k) match, consider contributing to a traditional or Roth IRA. Roth IRAs provide tax-free growth, while traditional IRAs offer tax-deferred growth.

### 2. **Consider Your Tax Situation:**
   - **Traditionally Taxed Accounts:** Use taxable accounts for investments that generate capital gains or dividends, as they may be taxed at a lower rate than ordinary income.
   - **Roth IRA:** Invest in higher-growth potential assets in a Roth IRA, since qualified withdrawals are tax-free.

### 3. **Diversify Your Investments:**
   - Within all account types, ensure you have a well-diversified portfolio. This can involve a mix of stocks, bonds, and other assets based on your risk tolerance.

### 4. **Balance Contributions Based on Age and Goals:**
   - Younger investors may prioritize growth and contribute more to growth-oriented assets (like stocks) in their IRAs and 401(k)s. 
   - As you approach retirement, shift towards preserving capital, which may mean increasing bonds and lower-risk investments.

### 5. **Review and Rebalance Periodically:**
   - Regularly review your allocations and rebalance your portfolio as needed to maintain your desired asset allocation between the accounts.

### 6. **Factor in Withdrawal Strategies:**
   - Plan how you will withdraw funds in retirement. Consider that withdrawals from tax-advantaged accounts (like traditional IRAs) will be taxed as ordinary income.

### Summary:
Start by maximizing contributions to tax-advantaged accounts, considering employer matches, and then invest in taxable accounts while diversifying your portfolio aligned with your timelines and risk.

It's often beneficial to consult with a financial advisor for personalized strategies tailored to your specific financial situation.
