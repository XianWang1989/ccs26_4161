To effectively distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to take full advantage of any employer match. This is essentially free money.

2. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: Focus on tax-deferred growth. If your employer offers a match, prioritize contributing to this first.
   - **IRA**: If eligible, consider a Roth IRA for tax-free growth, particularly if you expect your tax rate to increase in retirement.

3. **Diversify Your Investments**: Choose a mix of asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) based on your risk tolerance and time horizon across all accounts.

4. **Consider Tax Implications**: 
   - Keep high-growth investments in tax-advantaged accounts to defer taxes.
   - Hold bonds in tax-advantaged accounts to avoid the higher taxes associated with_interest income in taxable accounts.

5. **Assess Withdrawal Strategies**: Plan for future withdrawals from these accounts, considering factors like age, tax rates, and required minimum distributions (RMDs).

6. **Reassess Annually**: Review your allocation and adjust based on changes in income, tax laws, and financial goals.

Consulting a financial advisor for personalized advice based on your specific circumstances is also highly recommended.
