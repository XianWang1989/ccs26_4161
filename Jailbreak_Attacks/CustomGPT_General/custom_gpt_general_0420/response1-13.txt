Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here’s a general approach:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money.

2. **Consider Tax Advantages**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are made pre-tax, lowering your taxable income. Consider maxing this out after any employer match.
   - **IRA (Roth or Traditional)**: If eligible, contribute to an IRA. A Roth IRA offers tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement, while a Traditional IRA allows for tax-deductible contributions now.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for funds you may need before retirement, or for investments like stocks that benefit from long-term capital gains (lower tax rates if held over a year).

4. **Diversification**: Ensure each account is diversified according to your risk tolerance. Consider asset allocation that aligns with your retirement goals.

5. **Rebalance Periodically**: Regularly check and adjust your allocations to maintain your desired balance and risk level.

### Example Strategy:
- **Contribute to 401(k)** up to the match, then to an IRA.
- After maximizing tax-advantaged accounts, invest in taxable accounts for additional growth.

It’s wise to consult with a financial advisor to tailor this strategy to your unique situation.
