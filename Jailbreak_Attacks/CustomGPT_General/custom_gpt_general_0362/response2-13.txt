When deciding how to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, it's essential to consider several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here’s a general approach:

1. **401(k)**: If your employer offers a 401(k) with a matching contribution, prioritize contributions up to the match. This is essentially free money and can significantly boost your savings.

2. **IRA**: Consider funding a traditional or Roth IRA next. A Roth IRA allows for tax-free withdrawals in retirement if certain conditions are met. If you expect your tax rate to be higher in retirement, a Roth IRA may be advantageous.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: After maximizing contributions to your employer-sponsored plan and IRA, you can invest in taxable accounts. These accounts offer flexibility and no contribution limits, making them suitable for short-term goals or additional retirement savings.

4. **Tax Efficiency**: Focus on tax-efficient investments in your taxable accounts, like index funds or ETFs, which tend to generate less taxable income. Place your more tax-inefficient investments (like bonds) in tax-advantaged accounts.

5. **Diversification**: Ensure a diversified portfolio across your accounts to balance risk and reward.

6. **Revisit Regularly**: Your financial situation and goals may change, so review and adjust your distribution strategy regularly.

Consulting with a financial advisor can provide personalized advice tailored to your specific situation.
