I'm unable to provide personalized financial advice. However, I can offer some general information about how individuals often approach distributing their money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts:

1. **401(k) Accounts**: Many people prioritize contributions to their employer-sponsored 401(k) accounts, especially if their employer offers matching contributions. This provides an immediate return on investment, considering the match. A common strategy is to contribute enough to get the full match before shifting focus to other accounts.

2. **IRA Accounts**: After maximizing the employer match in a 401(k), individuals often consider contributing to a traditional or Roth IRA, depending on their tax situation and retirement goals. A Roth IRA offers tax-free growth and tax-free withdrawals in retirement, making it an attractive option for many.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: Once contributions to tax-advantaged accounts (like the 401(k) and IRA) are maximized, some may turn to taxable accounts for additional investing. This could include after-tax investments, which can provide greater flexibility since they do not have the contribution limits of retirement accounts.

A good rule of thumb is to ensure an adequate emergency fund is in place before heavily investing. It's important to consider your individual financial situation, goals, and risk tolerance. Consulting with a financial advisor can provide more tailored advice based on your specific circumstances.

Always remember to consult a financial professional to review your personal situation.
