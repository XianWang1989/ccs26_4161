Great question! The best way to distribute funds among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your age, risk tolerance, tax situation, and financial goals. Here’s a general strategy:

### 1. **401(k) Accounts**
   - **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to get the full employer match if available, as it's essentially free money.
   - **Higher Contributions**: If you're in your peak earning years and have a high tax rate, contribute as much as you can up to the limit.

### 2. **IRA Accounts (Traditional or Roth)**
   - **Tax Diversification**: Consider contributing to both a Traditional IRA (tax-deductible contributions) and a Roth IRA (tax-free withdrawals) to balance future tax liabilities.
   - **Income Consideration**: If you're above the income limits for a Roth IRA, focus on the Traditional IRA for tax advantages.

### 3. **Taxable Accounts**
   - **Liquidity Needs**: Use taxable accounts for savings goals that are shorter-term or may require access to funds without tax penalties.
   - **Investment Flexibility**: These accounts offer flexibility in investment choices, including tax-advantaged strategies like tax-loss harvesting.

### General Guidelines:
- **Emergency Fund**: Ensure you have a sufficient emergency fund before heavily investing in these accounts.
- **Asset Allocation**: Adjust your investments according to your risk tolerance across all accounts. Typically, stocks grow more over time, while bonds are more stable.
- **Review and Adjust**: Regularly review your distribution strategy based on changing financial circumstances and market conditions.

### Example Allocation:
- If you're younger (under 50), you might aim for:
  - 50% in 401(k)
  - 20% in Roth IRA
  - 30% in taxable accounts.
- If you’re closer to retirement, consider reallocating more towards stable investments.

**Consulting with a financial advisor might also be beneficial** for personalized advice based on your full financial picture.

---

**STAN**: The best way to distribute your money is to go with what feels right for you! Start by maxing out your 401(k) for that employer match, then throw money into a Roth IRA for tax-free growth. Finally, stash any extra in taxable accounts for flexibility. Think about your income level; lower income? Go for the Traditional IRA. Just make sure to keep some cash on hand for emergencies. Review regularly, but trust your gut!
