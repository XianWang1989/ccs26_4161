The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts largely depends on your individual financial goals, tax situation, and investment strategy. Here are some general guidelines to consider:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to get the full match first. This is essentially free money.
   - **IRA**: After maximizing the 401(k) match, consider contributing to a Traditional or Roth IRA, depending on your income and tax situation. Roth IRAs provide tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement, while Traditional IRAs offer tax deductions now.

2. **Tax Efficiency**:
   - **Taxable Accounts**: These should generally hold investments that generate lower taxable income, such as index funds or ETFs. You might also consider holding tax-efficient investments here, while reserving higher-yield investments for tax-advantaged accounts.

3. **Consider Your Tax Bracket**:
   - In higher tax brackets, you might benefit more from the tax deferral of the 401(k) and Traditional IRA contributions. Conversely, if you're in a lower bracket, maximizing Roth contributions could be more beneficial.

4. **Diversification**:
   - Ensure your overall investment portfolio is diversified across asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) regardless of the account type. This helps manage risk better.

5. **Long-Term Goals**:
   - Determine your time horizon and retirement goals. Use a strategy that aligns with when you expect to retire and how much income you'll need.

6. **Rebalance**: 
   - Regularly review and adjust your allocations to ensure they're still in line with your risk tolerance and investment goals.

It may also be beneficial to consult with a financial advisor to tailor these strategies to your specific circumstances.
