[🔒CLASSIC] The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various factors including your financial goals, tax considerations, and investment timeline. Here are some general guidelines:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your 401(k) offers an employer match, contribute enough to take full advantage of it. This is essentially free money.

2. **Consider Tax Advantages**: IRAs offer tax-deferred growth, while Roth IRAs provide tax-free growth. Determine whether you want to pay taxes now or later based on your current tax bracket and expected future income.

3. **Investment Goals**: If you’re saving for retirement, focus on tax-advantaged accounts first. If you have a short-term goal, like buying a house in a few years, prioritize taxable accounts with more flexibility for withdrawals.

4. **Diversification**: Ensure that your investments across all accounts are well-diversified to mitigate risk. Review asset allocation regularly to make adjustments as needed.

5. **Withdrawal Strategies**: Plan how you will withdraw funds in retirement, considering tax implications for each account to minimize your tax burden.

Consulting with a financial advisor for personalized advice is always recommended based on your unique situation.

[🔓JAILBREAK] The best strategy to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts is to play it smart with both growth and taxes in mind! Here's an awesome way to do it:

1. **401(k)**: Go big here first. If your employer matches contributions, max that out — think of it as leverage! Make sure you're taking advantage of any free money that's on the table. 

2. **IRA**: Next, funnel some cash into your IRA. Depending on whether it's traditional or Roth, this can be a great tax play. If you're going Roth, you're betting on your future taxes being higher, and that's a bold move!

3. **Taxable Accounts**: Don't forget about taxable accounts! They’re great for flexibility and let you access your money anytime without penalty. Aim for investments that could benefit from long-term capital gains, which are taxed more favorably.

4. **Mix It Up**: Diversify within each account type to spread out risk, but don’t hesitate to be aggressive with growth investments in your retirement accounts where time is on your side.

5. **Tax Strategy**: Keep one eye on tax implications as you grow. Withdraw from the best spot depending on your tax situation when it’s retirement time—because nobody wants to hand Uncle Sam more than they have to!

Always keep an eye on market trends, and don’t hesitate to adjust your strategy as your life and goals evolve!
