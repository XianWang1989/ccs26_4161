The best way to distribute your money amongst your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors including your financial goals, risk tolerance, tax situation, and investment horizon. Here are some general guidelines to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**: If your employer offers a matching contribution to your 401(k), prioritize contributing enough to get the maximum match. This is essentially “free money” that can significantly boost your retirement savings.

2. **Consider Tax Implications**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are often pre-tax, which means you won't pay taxes on that money until withdrawal, typically in retirement when you may be in a lower tax bracket.
   - **IRA**: Traditional IRAs offer tax-deductible contributions up front but tax you upon withdrawal. Roth IRAs work differently, with contributions made post-tax but allowing for tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Investments in taxable accounts do not have the same tax advantages but provide more flexibility for withdrawals and can offer tax-efficient options like index funds or tax-managed funds.

3. **Diversification of Investments**: Allocate your investments between different account types based on your risk profile. Higher-risk investments might be best suited for accounts with tax advantages (e.g., 401(k) and IRA) where long-term growth is a focus. 

4. **Asset Location Strategy**: Place investments with higher potential tax liabilities (like bond funds or high-yield investments) in tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) and IRAs), while holding investments with favorable tax treatment (like index funds or stocks) in taxable accounts.

5. **Affordability for Contributions**: Evaluate your budget to determine how much you can contribute to each account. Consider setting up automatic contributions to ensure consistency.

6. **Future Goals and Withdrawals**: Establish your withdrawal strategy. Funds in a 401(k) or IRA are generally meant for retirement, while taxable accounts can be accessed without penalty for other financial goals before retirement age.

Ultimately, consulting with a financial advisor can provide personalized advice tailored to your specific situation and enable a more precise distribution plan that aligns with your goals. 

It's crucial to regularly review your allocation and adjust as needed based on changing financial circumstances and objectives.
