Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various factors, including your current financial situation, retirement goals, risk tolerance, and tax considerations. Here's a general strategy to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**: Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to take full advantage of any employer match. This is essentially free money and should be prioritized.

2. **Contribute to IRA**: Once you've secured the employer match, consider contributing to a traditional IRA or Roth IRA. A traditional IRA can provide tax-deductible contributions, while a Roth IRA offers tax-free withdrawals in retirement. Choose based on your current vs. expected future tax rates.

3. **Increase 401(k) Contributions**: After maxing out your IRA contributions (if possible), continue to increase your contributions to your 401(k) up to the maximum allowable limit. This will help you further reduce your taxable income.

4. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**: Once you've contributed to tax-advantaged accounts, consider investing in taxable accounts. This approach offers greater flexibility and access to funds without age restrictions, although you may face capital gains taxes.

5. **Diversification**: Ensure that your investments are diversified across asset classes (stocks, bonds, real estate) to mitigate risk.

6. **Review and Adjust**: Regularly review your investment strategy and adjust your contributions based on changes in income, tax laws, and your overall financialGoals. 

Remember, consulting with a financial advisor can provide tailored advice that considers your specific situation and goals.
