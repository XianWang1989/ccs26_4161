Distributing your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts can depend on several factors including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment strategy. Here are some general guidelines that might help you make decisions about how to allocate your funds:

1. **Employer Match**: If your employer offers a 401(k) match, prioritize contributing enough to your 401(k) to receive the full match first. This is essentially “free money”.

2. **Tax Considerations**: Contribute to tax-advantaged accounts like your 401(k) and IRA first, as they can defer taxes on contributions and growth. A Traditional IRA and 401(k) allow for pre-tax contributions, reducing your taxable income. Conversely, a Roth IRA and Roth 401(k) use after-tax contributions but provide tax-free withdrawals in retirement.

3. **Emergency Fund**: Before heavily investing, ensure you have an adequate emergency fund (typically 3-6 months of expenses). This fund should be held in a readily accessible account, such as a savings account, not in a retirement account.

4. **Investment Goals and Timeline**: If your goals are long-term (such as retirement), focus on funding your 401(k) and IRAs. If you have shorter-term goals, like buying a house, consider allocating more funds into a taxable account given the liquidity.

5. **Diversification**: Maintain a diversified portfolio across all accounts to manage risk. Ensure that your investment choices align with your risk tolerance and investment time horizon across each type of account.

6. **Periodic Review**: Regularly assess your allocation based on changes in your financial situation, market conditions, and investment performance.

Ultimately, a well-structured strategy takes into account your unique financial landscape, so consider consulting with a financial advisor for personalized advice.
