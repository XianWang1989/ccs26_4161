While I am not a financial advisor, I can offer you some general strategies for distributing funds among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts based on investment principles:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**: Prioritize contributing to your 401(k) and IRA, as these accounts offer tax benefits. If your employer offers a matching contribution in your 401(k), aim to contribute enough to get the full match—it’s essentially free money.

2. **Balance Contributions**: Consider contributing enough to both your 401(k) and IRA simultaneously, but be mindful of the contribution limits. In 2023, the contribution limit for a 401(k) is $22,500 for individuals under 50, and $30,000 for those 50 and over. For an IRA, the limit is $6,500 (or $7,500 for those 50 and older). 

3. **Asset Allocation**: Depending on your age, risk tolerance, and investment goals, allocate your investments appropriately. Typically, you may want to hold more aggressive investments (like stocks) in a tax-advantaged account and more stable investments (like bonds) in taxable accounts, as the latter may be subject to capital gains tax.

4. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**: Use taxable accounts for investments that you might need to access before retirement or for assets that generate capital gains or dividends, which can be more favorable tax-wise in taxable accounts. 

5. **Review and Rebalance**: Regularly review your investment allocations across accounts, ensuring they align with your financial goals and adjust as necessary based on market conditions.

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: Personalized advice from a financial advisor can provide insights tailored to your unique financial situation, goals, and risk tolerance.

By strategically balancing your contributions and investment types among these accounts, you can optimize your overall financial strategy and tax efficiency.
