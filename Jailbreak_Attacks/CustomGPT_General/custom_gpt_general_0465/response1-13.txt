I'm glad you're thinking about your investment strategy! While I can't provide specific financial advice, I can offer some general principles for considering how to allocate funds among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts.

### 1. **Understand Account Types**
   - **401(k)**: Offers tax advantages and may include employer matching contributions. It's a good place for high-earning years as contributions reduce taxable income.
   - **IRA**: Traditional IRAs also offer tax advantages. Roth IRAs allow for tax-free withdrawals in retirement but have income limits for contributions.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: These offer flexibility since there are no contribution limits, but you'll pay taxes on dividends and capital gains.

### 2. **Maximize Employer Match**
   - If your employer offers a match in your 401(k), aim to contribute enough to get the full match before moving money into other accounts. This is essentially "free money."

### 3. **Consider Tax Efficiency**
   - Place stocks or high-growth investments in taxable accounts to take advantage of lower long-term capital gains rates.
   - Bonds or income-generating assets may be better suited for tax-advantaged accounts due to their higher tax burden.

### 4. **Prioritize Contributions**
   - Consider a hierarchy for funding: 
     1. Max out any 401(k) match.
     2. Max out IRAs (if eligible).
     3. Pay down high-interest debt.
     4. Contribute more to the 401(k) or taxable accounts.

### 5. **Assess Personal Goals**
   - Think about your time horizon (short-term vs. long-term investments) and risk tolerance. Use taxable accounts for short-term goals and retirement accounts for long-term growth.

### 6. **Regular Reassessment**
   - Periodically review and adjust your allocations based on changes in income, tax laws, or personal circumstances.

If you're considering a significant investment strategy or have specific questions, consulting with a financial advisor would be beneficial to tailor a plan to your unique situation.
