To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Employer Match First**: If your 401(k) offers a match, contribute enough to maximize that feature first. This is essentially free money.

2. **Emergency Fund**: Ensure you have a sufficient emergency fund before committing a large portion to retirement accounts.

3. **Tax Considerations**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are tax-deferred, which could reduce your taxable income now. However, withdrawals in retirement will be taxed as ordinary income.
   - **Traditional IRA**: Similar tax treatment as a 401(k), but may have income limits for tax-deductible contributions.
   - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, but withdrawals are tax-free in retirement. It's particularly beneficial if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in the future.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Best for short-term goals, as they offer flexibility and access to funds without penalties. However, you'll pay taxes on dividends and capital gains.

4. **Investment Strategy**:
   - **401(k)** and **IRAs**: Focus on long-term growth with a diversified portfolio including stocks and bonds.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Consider tax-efficient investments (like index funds or ETFs) to minimize tax impact.

5. **Rebalancing**: Regularly review and adjust your allocations based on your age, risk tolerance, and investment goals.

It's often advisable to consult a financial advisor to tailor a strategy to your specific financial situation and goals.
