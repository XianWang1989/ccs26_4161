Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some guidelines to consider:

### 1. **401(k) Contribution**
   - **Max Out Employer Match**: Aim to contribute enough to get the full employer match, as this is essentially free money.
   - **Contribution Limits**: For 2024, the limit is $23,000 for those under 50 and $30,500 for those 50 and older.
   - **Investment Options**: Check the fund options available in your 401(k) and select a diversified mix.

### 2. **IRA (Traditional/Roth)**
   - **Contribution Limits**: For 2024, the limit is $6,500 ($7,500 if age 50 or older).
   - **Choose Between Traditional and Roth**:
     - **Traditional IRA**: Contributions may be tax-deductible, but withdrawals in retirement are taxed.
     - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, allowing for tax-free withdrawals in retirement.
   - **Maximize Contributions**: If you have additional funds after your 401(k) contributions, consider funding your IRA.

### 3. **Taxable Accounts**
   - **Flexibility and Accessibility**: These accounts provide flexibility for withdrawals without penalties and can be useful for short- to medium-term goals.
   - **Tax Considerations**: Be mindful of capital gains taxes and consider holding lower-turnover investments to minimize tax impact.

### 4. **General Strategy**
   - **Emergency Fund**: Ensure you have an emergency fund (3-6 months of expenses) in a liquid account.
   - **Debt Management**: Pay down high-interest debt before aggressively investing.
   - **Balanced Portfolio**: Maintain a diversified investment portfolio across all accounts, tailored to your risk tolerance and timeline.

### 5. **Review and Adjust**
   - **Regular Check-ins**: Review your allocations annually or after major life changes to adjust based on your goals, market performance, and changes in tax laws.

### Conclusion
A balanced approach may involve prioritizing contributions to the 401(k) for the match, funding an IRA for tax advantages, and using taxable accounts for flexibility. Consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your specific financial situation.
