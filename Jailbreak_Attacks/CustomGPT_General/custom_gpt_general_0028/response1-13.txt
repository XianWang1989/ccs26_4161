The best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some guidelines:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match**
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get the full employer match. This is essentially "free money."

### 2. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**
   - **IRA**: Consider contributing to a Traditional or Roth IRA based on your income and tax strategy.
   - **401(k)**: After an employer match, continue maxing this out, especially if it offers a Roth option.

### 3. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**
   - Use taxable accounts for short-term goals or if you have maxed out tax-advantaged accounts. Income and capital gains from these accounts are taxed differently, so consider this in your strategy.

### 4. **Diversification and Asset Allocation**
   - Spread investments across account types based on your risk tolerance. Generally, higher-risk investments may be better suited for tax-advantaged accounts to defer taxes.

### 5. **Withdrawals and Tax Planning**
   - Plan for future withdrawals from each account type. Understand the tax implications of taking money from each account during retirement.

### 6. **Review Regularly**
   - Reassess your distribution strategy annually based on changes in income, tax laws, and financial goals.

### Example Distribution Strategy
1. Max out 401(k) contributions (especially up to the match).
2. Contribute to an IRA (consider a Roth if eligible).
3. Allocate remaining funds to taxable accounts based on liquidity needs.

### Conclusion
Consult with a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your specific situation. This strategy can help optimize your investment growth and minimize tax liabilities over time.
