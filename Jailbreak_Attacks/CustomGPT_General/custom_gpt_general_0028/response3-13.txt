The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your age, income level, tax situation, and financial goals. Here's a general approach to consider:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**
   - If your employer offers a matching contribution, prioritize contributing enough to your 401(k) to get the full match. It’s free money!

### 2. **Contribute to IRA**
   - If you're eligible, consider contributing to an IRA (Traditional or Roth). Roth IRAs are especially advantageous if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket during retirement.

### 3. **Balance Between Accounts**
   - After maximizing your 401(k) match, balance contributions among accounts based on tax considerations:
     - **Tax-Deferred Accounts** (401(k), Traditional IRA): Good for high-income years since you reduce your taxable income now.
     - **Roth Accounts** (Roth IRA): Beneficial if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket later or want tax-free withdrawals in retirement.
     - **Taxable Accounts**: Useful for investing with more flexibility and taking advantage of lower long-term capital gains tax rates.

### 4. **Consider Tax Implications**
   - Withdrawals from tax-deferred accounts are taxed as ordinary income, while qualified withdrawals from Roth IRAs and long-term capital gains in taxable accounts are taxed at lower rates.

### 5. **Diversify Investments**
   - Regardless of the account type, ensure your investments are diversified across asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) and sectors to manage risk.

### 6. **Rebalance as Needed**
   - Regularly review and adjust your portfolio as life circumstances, tax laws, and market conditions change.

### 7. **Consult a Financial Advisor**
   - For personalized advice tailored to your specific situation, consider consulting a financial advisor.

### Final Thought
Balancing your contributions should consider both current tax benefits and long-term growth potential to build wealth effectively over time.
