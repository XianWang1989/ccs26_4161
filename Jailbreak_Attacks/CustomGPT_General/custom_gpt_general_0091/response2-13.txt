It sounds like you're looking for advice on how to allocate your investments across different types of accounts. To provide a tailored strategy, it would be helpful to know more about your financial goals, risk tolerance, and current financial situation. However, I can outline some general principles that may assist you:

1. **401(k) Accounts**: These plans often come with employer matching, so maximizing your contributions to take advantage of that match should be a priority. Generally, it’s advised to contribute enough to get the full match.

2. **IRAs**: Depending on whether you choose a Traditional IRA or Roth IRA, your tax situation may differ. A Traditional IRA can provide tax deductions on contributions, while a Roth IRA allows for tax-free withdrawals in retirement. Consider your current tax rate versus your expected tax rate in retirement to decide which type might serve you better.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: These accounts can provide more flexibility in accessing funds. They are typically used for goals that occur before retirement and can be useful for investments that involve tax-loss harvesting.

**General Allocation Strategy**:
- Start with maximizing contributions to your 401(k) to get employer matching.
- Next, contribute to an IRA (choose the type based on your tax situation).
- Use taxable accounts for additional savings, focusing on investments that are more tax-efficient (like index funds or ETFs).

This approach helps benefit from tax advantages while also maintaining the flexibility to access funds when needed. Always consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your specific situation.
