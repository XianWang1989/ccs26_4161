The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some general guidelines:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**: If your employer offers a matching contribution, aim to contribute enough to get the full match. This is essentially free money.

2. **Consider Tax Implications**:
   - **401(k) and IRA**: Contributions to these accounts are often tax-deferred (traditional accounts) or tax-free (Roth accounts). Prioritize these accounts for long-term growth, especially if you're in a higher tax bracket now.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: These accounts can be effective for investments you may need to access in the short to medium term. Be mindful of capital gains taxes when withdrawing funds.

3. **Build an Emergency Fund**: Before distributing your money, ensure you have an adequate emergency fund—generally 3 to 6 months' worth of living expenses.

4. **Investment Strategy**:
   - **401(k)**: Focus on a diversified mix of funds that align with your risk tolerance and investment horizon.
   - **IRA**: Similar to your 401(k), but consider adding different asset classes or investment options that may not be available in your 401(k).
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Invest in tax-efficient funds, such as index funds or ETFs, to minimize capital gains taxes.

5. **Adjust for Time Horizon**: If you're closer to retirement, you may want to be more conservative in your 401(k) and IRA. In contrast, you might hold more aggressive investments in your taxable account if you have a longer time horizon.

6. **Regular Review and Rebalancing**: Periodically review your asset allocation and rebalance as necessary to maintain your desired risk level.

It's wise to consult with a financial advisor to tailor this strategy to your specific situation.
