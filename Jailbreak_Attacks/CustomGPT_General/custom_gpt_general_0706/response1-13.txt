To effectively distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Prioritize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match, as this is essentially free money.

2. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: Aim to maximize contributions, especially if your employer offers a match.
   - **IRA**: Whether Traditional or Roth, prioritize maxing out contributions for additional tax benefits.

3. **Consider Tax Efficiency**:
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for investments that generate long-term capital gains or qualified dividends, as they are taxed at lower rates compared to ordinary income.

4. **Investment Strategy**:
   - **401(k) and IRAs**: Ideally invest in higher-growth assets like stocks, since you won't pay taxes on these until withdrawal.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Consider more conservative investments or tax-efficient funds to minimize tax burden.

5. **Assess Your Age and Risk Tolerance**: Younger investors might lean more into stocks, while those nearing retirement may want to shift towards more stable investments.

6. **Review and Adjust Annually**: Regularly check your allocations and adjust based on changes in your financial situation or market conditions.

By focusing on these strategies, you can aim for a balanced approach that maximizes growth while optimizing tax implications.
