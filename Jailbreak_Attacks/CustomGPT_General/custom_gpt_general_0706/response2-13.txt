Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment strategy. Here are some general guidelines:

1. **401(k)**: 
   - **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to get the full employer match, as this is essentially free money.
   - **Target Percentage**: Aim for 15-20% of your income if possible.

2. **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**:
   - **Contribute Next**: After maximizing the 401(k) match, contribute to an IRA. A Roth IRA is beneficial for tax-free growth if you anticipate being in a higher tax bracket in retirement.
   - **Annual Limits**: Stay within annual contribution limits (e.g., $6,500 for individuals under 50 in 2023).

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - **Invest Additional Savings**: After maxing out contributions to retirement accounts, use taxable accounts for additional investments. This provides flexibility for withdrawals without penalties.

### Considerations:
- **Emergency Fund**: Ensure you have an emergency fund (3-6 months of expenses) before heavily investing.
- **Tax Efficiency**: Place lower-risk investments (like bonds) in tax-advantaged accounts and higher-risk stocks in taxable accounts for potentially better tax treatment.
- **Diversification**: Diversify your portfolio across all accounts for risk management.

Review your distribution strategy regularly based on changes in income, expenses, and financial goals. Consulting a financial advisor can also provide personalized recommendations.
