Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts requires a balanced approach based on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment horizon. Here’s a general strategy:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match. This is free money and an excellent first step.

2. **Fund IRA Contributions**: If eligible, contribute to a traditional IRA or Roth IRA. Roth IRAs are beneficial for tax-free growth, while traditional IRAs may offer tax deductions.

3. **Consider Tax Efficiency**:
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Invest in tax-efficient assets like index funds or ETFs, which have lower turnover and capital gains distributions.
   - **Tax-Deferred Accounts (401(k) & IRA)**: Hold assets that might generate higher short-term gains or interest here, as they defer taxes until withdrawal.

4. **Emergency Fund**: Before heavily investing, ensure you have an emergency fund (3-6 months’ worth of expenses) in a liquid account.

5. **Adjust Based on Goals**: 
   - For short-term goals (buying a house), focus on taxable accounts for liquidity.
   - For long-term growth (retirement), prioritize 401(k) and IRA contributions.

6. **Review Tax Impact**: Consider your current tax bracket to maximize tax benefits. Withdrawals from traditional IRAs and 401(k)s are taxed as income.

7. **Diversification**: Ensure your investment mix is diversified across different asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) to mitigate risk.

Regularly review and adjust your strategy as your financial situation and goals evolve. Consulting a financial advisor can provide personalized insights based on your individual circumstances.
