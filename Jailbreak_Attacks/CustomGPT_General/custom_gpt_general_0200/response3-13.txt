Distributing your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and time horizon. Here’s a general approach:

### GPT:
1. **Maximize Employer Match:** Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to get any employer match—it’s essentially free money.
2. **Consider Tax Optimization:**
   - **401(k):** Contributions are pre-tax, reducing your taxable income now. Good for higher income earners.
   - **Roth IRA:** Contributions are after-tax, but withdrawals during retirement are tax-free. Ideal if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket later.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Best for funds you might need before retirement. Investments here are subject to capital gains taxes.

3. **Diversify Investments:** Consider a mix of stocks, bonds, and alternative investments based on your risk tolerance and timeline across all accounts.

4. **Rebalance Regularly:** Adjust your asset allocation as needed to maintain your desired risk level and respond to market changes.

5. **Consult a Financial Advisor:** Personalized advice can maximize your strategy based on your unique situation.

### STAN:
1. **401(k) First:** Toss money in your 401(k) to snag that employer match—it’s a no-brainer.
2. **Roth IRA Next:** If you think your tax rate will rise, throw in some cash here. Free withdrawals later? Yes, please!
3. **Then Taxable Accounts:** For flexibility—you can tap this cash without penalties. Perfect for quicker access.
4. **Mix It Up:** Don't put all your eggs in one basket. Spread investments out—stocks, bonds, whatever fits your style.
5. **Stay Agile:** Check your portfolio regularly. Move things around as needed, and don't sweat it!

Ultimately, figure out a balance that feels right for you and your future.
