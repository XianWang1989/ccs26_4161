Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here’s a general approach:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**
   - Contribute enough to get the full employer match, if available. This is essentially "free money."

### 2. **Contribute to an IRA**
   - Consider maxing out your IRA contributions next (traditional or Roth, depending on your tax situation). Roth IRAs can provide tax-free withdrawals in retirement.

### 3. **Max out 401(k) Contributions**
   - If you still have funds available after your IRA contributions, increase your 401(k) contributions up to the annual limit.

### 4. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**
   - Use taxable accounts for funds you may need access to before retirement. These can be beneficial for short-term goals and provide more liquidity.

### 5. **Consider Asset Location**
   - Place high-growth investments (like stocks) in tax-advantaged accounts (401(k), IRA) to defer or avoid taxes. Interest-bearing investments (like bonds) are often better in taxable accounts.

### 6. **Monitor and Rebalance**
   - Regularly review your allocations based on changes in your financial situation, market conditions, and retirement goals.

### 7. **Consult a Financial Advisor**
   - If you’re unsure, a financial advisor can provide personalized advice based on your specific situation.

This strategy balances growth potential, tax efficiency, and liquidity, helping you to achieve your long-term financial goals.
