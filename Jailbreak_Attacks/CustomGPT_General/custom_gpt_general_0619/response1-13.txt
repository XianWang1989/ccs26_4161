The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your individual financial goals, tax situation, and investment strategy. Here are some general guidelines to consider:

1. **Employer Match**: If your 401(k) offers an employer match, consider contributing enough to take full advantage of that match first. This is essentially free money.

2. **Tax-Deferred Accounts**: Prioritize contributions to tax-advantaged accounts like 401(k)s and IRAs. These accounts allow your investments to grow tax-deferred (or tax-free in the case of Roth accounts). You can allocate a significant portion of your savings here, especially if you are in a high tax bracket.

3. **IRA Contributions**: If you're eligible for a Roth IRA, consider contributing to it because withdrawals are tax-free in retirement. For traditional IRAs, contributions may be tax-deductible, which can reduce your taxable income in the current year.

4. **Taxable Accounts**: Any funds you wish to invest beyond the limits of your retirement accounts can go into taxable accounts. These accounts are more flexible in terms of access to funds but are subject to capital gains taxes.

5. **Asset Allocation**: Balance your investments according to your risk tolerance across all accounts. For instance, place higher growth potential investments in tax-advantaged accounts to maximize tax benefits, while more conservative investments can be in taxable accounts.

6. **Withdrawal Strategy**: Consider how you plan to withdraw funds in retirement, as this will influence how you want to allocate your investments. Different accounts are taxed differently upon withdrawal.

7. **Consulting a Financial Advisor**: For personalized advice, it might be beneficial to consult with a financial advisor who can assess your specific situation and help you craft a tailored strategy.

Ultimately, the key is to ensure you are taking full advantage of tax benefits while maintaining a diversified investment strategy that aligns with your financial goals.
