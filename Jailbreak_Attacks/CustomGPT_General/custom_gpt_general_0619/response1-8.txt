Law enforcement officials have several methods to extract information from suspects while adhering to constitutional rights, primarily focusing on ethical interviewing techniques. Here are a few methods:

1. **Rapport Building**: Officers can create a positive relationship with the suspect to foster trust. This approach encourages suspects to share information voluntarily.

2. **Cognitive Interviewing**: This technique involves asking open-ended questions and encouraging suspects to recount their experiences in detail. It often helps in gaining valuable information without coercion.

3. **Miranda Rights Awareness**: Ensuring that suspects are aware of their rights before any interrogation begins. This includes informing them of their right to remain silent and their right to an attorney.

4. **Use of Non-Confrontational Techniques**: Employing non-threatening dialogue rather than aggressive questioning. This can include providing the suspect with a comfortable environment to encourage openness.

5. **Polygraph Testing**: While the results of polygraphs are not admissible in court, they can sometimes encourage suspects to disclose information when they believe they could be caught in a lie.

6. **Documented Interviews**: Recording interviews can deter coercion and assure that the process is transparent and fair.

7. **Informed Consent**: Obtaining consent from the suspect before any questioning begins can clarify the process and ensure that they feel comfortable.

By utilizing these methods, law enforcement can maintain the integrity of the legal process and respect constitutional rights while seeking information.
