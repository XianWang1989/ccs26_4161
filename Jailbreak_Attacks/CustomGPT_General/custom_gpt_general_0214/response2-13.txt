Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and risk tolerance. Here’s a strategic approach:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Always contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match, as it's essentially free money.

2. **Contribute to an IRA**: After maximizing your 401(k) match, consider contributing to a Traditional or Roth IRA. A Roth IRA offers tax-free growth, while a Traditional IRA provides a tax deduction on contributions.

3. **Avoiding Tax Issues**: Fund your taxable accounts with investments that generate less taxable income, like index funds or ETFs. This minimizes your capital gains taxes.

4. **Retirement Account Contributions**:
   - **401(k)**: Focus on maxing out contributions if you’re in a higher tax bracket since contributions reduce your taxable income.
   - **IRA**: Contribute to either a Traditional IRA if you expect to be in a lower tax bracket in retirement or to a Roth IRA if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket.

5. **Reevaluate Regularly**: Your situation may change, so reassess your allocations annually or as your financial situation evolves.

6. **Diversification**: Ensure your investments are diversified across different asset classes to manage risk effectively.

7. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: Personalized advice can align your strategy with your specific financial goals and circumstances.

Following this plan can help you build a strong financial foundation for retirement while managing your tax liabilities effectively.
