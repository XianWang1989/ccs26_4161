Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts can optimize your savings for retirement and manage tax implications. Here’s a strategic approach:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match**
   - **401(k) First:** Contribute enough to get the full employer match if available. This is essentially free money.

### 2. **Fund Tax-Advantaged Accounts**
   - **IRA Contributions:** If you’re eligible, consider maxing out a Roth IRA or Traditional IRA next, depending on your tax situation and retirement income expectations.

### 3. **Continue with 401(k)**
   - After maxing your IRA, increase your contributions to the 401(k) up to the annual limit.

### 4. **Use Taxable Accounts Wisely**
   - **Taxable Accounts:** After maxing tax-advantaged accounts, consider investing in taxable accounts for additional flexibility, especially if you need access to funds before retirement age.

### 5. **Investment Strategy**
   - **Asset Allocation:** Diversify your investments across these accounts based on your risk tolerance and investment horizon. Generally, higher-risk investments can be placed in tax-advantaged accounts, while more stable investments may go into taxable accounts to manage tax liability on capital gains.

### 6. **Consider Future Tax Implications**
   - Think about your expected tax bracket in retirement. Roth accounts may be more beneficial if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket later.

### 7. **Regular Review and Adjustments**
   - Periodically review your allocations and adjust based on changes in financial situations, market conditions, and retirement goals.

This framework can help optimize your savings and provide a balanced approach to retirement planning. Always consider consulting a financial advisor to tailor strategies to your specific circumstances.
