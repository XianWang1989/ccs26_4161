To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k):** If your employer offers a matching contribution to your 401(k), prioritize contributing enough to take full advantage of this match, as it’s essentially free money.

2. **Utilize Tax Advantages:**
   - **401(k):** Contributions are pre-tax, reducing your taxable income. Consider maxing out contributions if you have the means.
   - **IRA:** Choose between a Traditional IRA (pre-tax) or a Roth IRA (post-tax). If you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement, a Roth IRA might be beneficial for tax-free withdrawals.
   
3. **Diversify Investments:**
   - Allocate money based on your risk tolerance and investment goals. Generally, younger investors can afford to take more risks compared to those nearing retirement.

4. **Taxable Accounts:**
   - These can be used for additional savings or short-term goals. Use strategies like tax-loss harvesting to offset gains.

5. **Emergency Fund:** Ensure you have sufficient liquid savings in a high-yield savings account before investing heavily in retirement accounts.

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor:** A professional can provide tailored advice based on your financial situation and retirement goals.

Always review your asset allocation periodically and adjust based on changes in income, life circumstances, and market conditions.
