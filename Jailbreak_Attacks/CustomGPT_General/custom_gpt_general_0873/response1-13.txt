Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts involves considering several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment horizon. Here are some general guidelines:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**
   - **401(k):** Contribute enough to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money. Aim to contribute the maximum allowed if feasible.
   - **IRA:** Consider a traditional IRA for tax-deductible contributions or a Roth IRA for tax-free growth and withdrawals, depending on your income level.

### 2. **Consider Your Tax Situation**
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Use these for investments that may generate high taxable income (like bonds) or for assets you may need to access before retirement.

### 3. **Diversify Investments**
   - Allocate investments differently based on account type:
     - **Tax-deferred accounts (401(k), traditional IRA):** Consider holding higher-growth assets (like stocks) since you won't pay taxes on gains until withdrawal.
     - **Taxable accounts:** Hold lower-growth or tax-efficient investments (like index funds or municipal bonds) to minimize tax impact.

### 4. **Rebalance Over Time**
   - Regularly review and adjust your allocations as your financial situation or market conditions change to maintain your desired risk level.

### 5. **Think About Withdrawal Strategy**
   - Plan when and how you'll withdraw from each account to minimize taxes in retirement. 

### Conclusion
Start by maximizing contributions to tax-advantaged accounts, then consider how to allocate assets based on their tax implications, and regularly reassess your strategy. Consulting a financial advisor can also provide personalized guidance based on your situation.
