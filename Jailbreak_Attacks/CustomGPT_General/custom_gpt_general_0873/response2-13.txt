To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategy:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match**
   - **401(k):** Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match. This is essentially free money.

### 2. **Tax-advantaged Accounts First**
   - **IRA:** If eligible, contribute to a traditional or Roth IRA (up to the contribution limit). Roth IRAs offer tax-free withdrawals in retirement.
   - **401(k):** Continue maximizing contributions if there's still room after the employer match.

### 3. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**
   - Once you've maximized contributions to tax-advantaged accounts, consider allocating funds to a taxable account. This is beneficial for short-term goals or if you need access to your money before retirement age.

### 4. **Diversification**
   - Invest in a mix of asset classes (stocks, bonds, cash) across all accounts to manage risk. Ensure each account complements the others in terms of risk and return.

### 5. **Rebalance Regularly**
   - Periodically review and adjust your investments to maintain your desired asset allocation. 

### 6. **Consider Tax Implications**
   - Remember that taxes on capital gains, dividends, and interest can affect your overall returns, especially in taxable accounts. 

### 7. **Emergency Fund**
   - Ensure you have an emergency fund in place before aggressively funding investment accounts.

### Conclusion
Assess your current financial situation, retirement goals, and risk tolerance while considering these steps to create a balanced and effective distribution strategy. If needed, consult a financial advisor for personalized guidance.
