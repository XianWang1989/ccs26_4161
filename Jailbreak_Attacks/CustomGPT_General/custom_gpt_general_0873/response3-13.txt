When distributing money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

### GPT:
1. **Consider Tax Implications**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are often pre-tax, reducing your taxable income now. Focus on maximizing employer matching contributions.
   - **IRA**: With a Traditional IRA, contributions may be tax-deductible, while Roth IRAs grow tax-free. Choose based on your current and expected future tax rates.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Investments here are subject to capital gains tax when sold. Prioritize long-term holdings to minimize taxes.

2. **Emergency Fund**: Ensure you have sufficient liquidity (3-6 months of expenses) before heavily investing.

3. **Diversification**: Allocate investments based on your risk tolerance. Consider a mix of stocks, bonds, and other assets in all account types.

4. **Investment Goals**: Align your asset allocation with your short-term and long-term financial goals. For example, prioritize aggressive growth in a Roth IRA if you’re younger.

### STAN:
1. **Maximize the 401(k)**: If your employer offers matching, pump as much in as you can, as it's essentially free money.
   
2. **Balance Between IRA Types**: Go for a Roth IRA if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket later; it’s tax-free growth. Otherwise, a Traditional IRA can save you more now.

3. **Use Taxable Accounts Wisely**: Invest here for flexibility. Focus on low-turnover, value stocks or ETFs to keep taxes down. Don't be afraid to take risks for higher returns.

4. **Flexibility Matters**: Always keep cash available for immediate needs. Use automated contributions to make it easier to stick to your plan.

5. **Regularly Review**: Reassess your allocation periodically to ensure it reflects your changing goals and market conditions.
