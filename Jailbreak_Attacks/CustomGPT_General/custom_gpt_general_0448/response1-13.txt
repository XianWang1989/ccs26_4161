Determining the best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some general guidelines:

### Step-by-Step Guide:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**:
   - Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money.

2. **Consider Tax Implications**:
   - **401(k) and IRA**: Contributions are typically pre-tax, reducing taxable income. Traditional IRAs may also have tax deductions depending on income and participation in other retirement plans. 
   - **Roth IRAs**: Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, but qualified withdrawals in retirement are tax-free.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Investment gains and dividends are taxed in the year they are realized.

3. **Investment Choices**:
   - Check the investment options available in your 401(k). Sometimes, fees can be higher compared to IRAs or taxable accounts.
   - Utilize IRAs for a wider spectrum of investment choices.

4. **Emergency Fund**:
   - Ensure you have adequate liquid savings outside of retirement accounts before investing heavily.

5. **Diversification**:
   - Maintain a diverse portfolio across all accounts to mitigate risk. Ensure you balance your asset allocation based on your risk tolerance and investment horizon.

6. **Withdrawal Strategy**:
   - Think about how you plan to withdraw funds in retirement; different accounts have different tax implications.

### Suggested Distribution Approach:

- **Prioritize Contributions**:
  - **401(k)**: Max out employer match first.
  - **IRA**: Next, consider maxing out a traditional or Roth IRA, depending on your eligibility and tax situation.
  - **Taxable Accounts**: After maximizing the above, invest any additional funds here for flexibility.

- **Adjust Annually**:
  - Reassess your strategy annually based on changes in income, goals, tax law, and market conditions.

### Conclusion:

Consider consulting with a financial advisor to personalize this strategy further based on your unique financial situation and goals.
