Distributing your money across a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment horizon. Here’s a general approach:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**:
   - Contribute enough to get the full employer match. This is free money!

### 2. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **Traditional IRA or Roth IRA**: Contribute to an IRA for tax advantages. A Roth IRA allows tax-free withdrawals in retirement.
   - **401(k)**: If you have maxed out your IRA contributions, continue with your 401(k) to leverage tax-deferred growth.

### 3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - Use these for investments that may generate taxable income, like stocks or ETFs that you plan to hold long-term. Focus on tax-efficient investments to minimize capital gains taxes.

### 4. **Consider Your Goals**:
   - Short-term needs might prioritize taxable accounts for liquidity.
   - Long-term retirement savings should emphasize tax-advantaged accounts.

### 5. **Diversify Investments**:
   - Across all accounts, maintain a diversified portfolio tailored to your risk tolerance.

### 6. **Reevaluate Periodically**:
   - Adjust your allocations based on changes in income, market conditions, and personal goals.

### Example Allocation:
- **401(k)**: 15% of income
- **IRA**: Max out contributions ($6,500 or $7,500 if over 50 for 2023)
- **Taxable Account**: Remaining savings after completing 401(k) and IRA contributions.

Consult a financial advisor to tailor this strategy to your specific circumstances!
