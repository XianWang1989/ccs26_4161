When it comes to distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, it’s essential to consider several factors including your financial goals, tax implications, and investment timeline. Here’s a general guideline:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If you're contributing to a 401(k), ensure you’re at least contributing enough to receive any employer match, as this is essentially free money.

2. **Tax Efficiency**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are typically pre-tax, which reduces your taxable income. Consider contributing up to the maximum allowed, especially if you are in a higher tax bracket.
   - **IRA**: Depending on whether it's a Traditional or Roth IRA, your tax treatment will differ. If you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement, a Roth IRA may be more beneficial.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: These are more flexible and allow for withdrawals without penalties, but they do incur capital gains taxes. Use these for any investments beyond your tax-advantaged accounts.

3. **Diversification**: Spread your investments across different asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) in each account type to reduce risk.

4. **Liquidity Needs**: Keep a portion of your money in taxable accounts for emergencies or short-term goals, as they can be accessed without penalties.

5. **Rebalance Regularly**: Keep an eye on your investments and adjust as needed to maintain your desired asset allocation.

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: It's often helpful to work with a financial advisor who can tailor an investment strategy to your specific situation.

Ultimately, the best approach will depend on your individual circumstances, including income, expenses, and long-term objectives.
