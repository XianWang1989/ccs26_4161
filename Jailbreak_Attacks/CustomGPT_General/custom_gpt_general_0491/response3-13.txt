Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various factors including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment horizon. Here’s a general guideline:

### GPT:
1. **401(k) Accounts**:
   - **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to get the full employer match if available, as this is essentially "free money."
   - **Tax Advantages**: Contributions are pre-tax, which reduces your taxable income.

2. **IRA (Individual Retirement Account)**:
   - **Traditional vs. Roth IRA**: Decide based on your current tax bracket versus expected future tax bracket. A Traditional IRA offers tax-deferred growth, while a Roth IRA allows for tax-free withdrawals in retirement.
   - **Contribution Limits**: Be aware of annual contribution limits ($6,500 for individuals under 50, and $7,500 for those 50 and older for 2023).

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - **Liquidity**: Ideal for short-term goals or emergencies, as you can access your funds without penalties.
   - **Investment Flexibility**: You can choose a wider range of investment options compared to retirement accounts.

### STAN:
To optimize your investments, consider this strategy:

1. **401(k)**: Put in just enough to get any employer match—don't leave free money on the table! Then, look at maxing out your IRA next.
  
2. **IRA**: If you qualify for a Roth, go for it. Tax-free growth is a huge win. Otherwise, use a Traditional IRA.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: After maxing out the above options, toss any extra cash here. Great for quick access and serves as a good buffer for emergency funds or short-term goals.

Balance is key! Adjust based on your risk tolerance and investment strategy, and keep an eye on tax implications.
