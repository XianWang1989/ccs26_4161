Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and timeline for withdrawals. Here are some key considerations:

### 1. **401(k) Contributions**
   - **Employer Match**: Start by contributing enough to get the full employer match, as this is essentially free money.
   - **Tax Benefits**: Contributions are typically made pre-tax, reducing your taxable income. Consider maximizing contributions if you are in a higher tax bracket.

### 2. **IRA Contributions**
   - **Types of IRAs**: Decide between a Traditional IRA (tax-deductible contributions) and a Roth IRA (tax-free withdrawals).
   - **Tax Considerations**: A Roth IRA may be more beneficial if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket during retirement.

### 3. **Taxable Accounts**
   - **Flexibility**: These accounts provide more access and flexibility compared to retirement accounts.
   - **Investment Options**: You can invest in a variety of assets without the same restrictions as retirement accounts.

### 4. **Overall Strategy**
   - **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**: Prioritize maxing out your 401(k) and IRA contributions before putting significant amounts into taxable accounts.
   - **Diversification**: Maintain a balanced investment strategy across all accounts to mitigate risk.
   - **Withdrawal Strategy**: Plan your withdrawals from taxable accounts first, followed by tax-deferred accounts (401(k) and Traditional IRA), to minimize tax liabilities in retirement.

### 5. **Consider Individual Goals**
   - Assess your retirement goals, risk tolerance, and investment horizon.
   - Rebalance periodically to maintain your desired asset allocation.

### Conclusion
A common strategy is to first take advantage of tax-deferred growth (401(k) and IRA) and then utilize taxable accounts for additional flexibility and access. Consulting with a financial advisor can also help tailor a plan suited to your specific situation.
