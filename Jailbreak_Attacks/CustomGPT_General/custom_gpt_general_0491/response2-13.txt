Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts involves considering several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here’s a general strategy to optimize your distribution:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a match in the 401(k), contribute enough to take full advantage of this benefit first. It's essentially free money.

2. **Contribute to Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: Contribute the maximum allowed, especially if your employer offers a match. Focus on pre-tax contributions to reduce your taxable income.
   - **IRA**: Depending on your income level, consider contributing to a Traditional IRA (pre-tax) or a Roth IRA (post-tax). A Roth IRA can be advantageous if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: After maximizing contributions to your 401(k) and IRA, consider investing in taxable accounts. These can help you maintain liquidity and flexibility while being mindful of tax implications.

4. **Asset Allocation**: Diversify your investments based on your risk tolerance and time horizon. Typically, you may want a mix of stocks, bonds, and other assets:
   - Long-term growth (stocks) in tax-advantaged accounts.
   - More conservative investments (bonds) that might generate interest in taxable accounts to manage taxes.

5. **Focus on Tax Efficiency**:
   - Place high-growth investments in tax-advantaged accounts to defer taxes.
   - Use taxable accounts for investments with lower tax burdens, such as index funds or municipal bonds.

6. **Review Regularly**: Adjust your allocations as your goals, market conditions, and tax laws change. Periodically review your investments to ensure they align with your long-term objectives.

### Conclusion
Tailor this general strategy to fit your unique circumstances. Consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized advice based on your specific financial situation and goals.
