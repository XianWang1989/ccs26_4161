To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Understand Tax Implications**: 
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are pre-tax (traditional) or post-tax (Roth). Investment growth is tax-deferred until withdrawal (traditional).
   - **IRA**: Similar tax structure to 401(k), with potential tax benefits depending on traditional vs. Roth.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Interest, dividends, and capital gains are taxed in the year earned, making tax efficiency crucial.

2. **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to receive any employer match, as this is essentially free money.

3. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**: 
   - If you can, max out contributions to your IRA and 401(k) before funding a taxable account.
   - Focus on pre-tax options (traditional accounts) for money you'll need in retirement, as you'll defer taxes until withdrawal.

4. **Diversify Investments**: 
   - Balance high-growth investments in tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) and IRA) to benefit from tax deferral.
   - Use taxable accounts for more stable investments (like bonds) which may generate interest income that's taxed at a higher rate.

5. **Consider Withdrawal Strategies**: 
   - When you retire, plan withdrawals strategically to minimize tax liabilities, drawing first from taxable accounts, then tax-deferred (401(k), IRA), and finally from Roth accounts.

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: Tailor your distribution strategy based on your individual financial situation, tax bracket, and retirement goals.

By diversifying and prioritizing tax-advantaged strategies, you can optimize your investment growth and tax efficiency over time.
