To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Maximize Contributions**:
   - Ensure you are contributing enough to your 401(k) to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money.
   - Contribute to your IRA, aiming to reach the annual limit. If eligible, consider a Roth IRA for tax-free growth.

2. **Account Type Considerations**:
   - **401(k)**: Typically offers higher contribution limits and possibly employer matching. Suitable for long-term growth.
   - **IRA**: Provides tax advantages; traditional IRAs offer tax-deferred growth. Roth IRAs allow for tax-free withdrawals in retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: These provide flexibility and access to funds without penalties but are subject to capital gains taxes on profits.

3. **Asset Allocation Strategy**:
   - Consider holding high-growth investments in tax-advantaged accounts (like stocks in your 401(k) or IRA) to benefit from tax deferral or tax-free growth.
   - Keep more conservative investments (like bonds) in taxable accounts to minimize capital gains tax implications.

4. **Tax Considerations**:
   - Withdrawals from 401(k) and traditional IRA are taxed as ordinary income, while Roth IRA withdrawals are tax-free.
   - Prioritize tax-efficient funds (index funds or ETFs) in taxable accounts to minimize tax liabilities.

5. **Review and Adjust Regularly**:
   - Regularly assess your asset allocation and investment performance. Adjust contributions based on performance, life changes, or market conditions.

6. **Seek Professional Advice**:
   - Consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized strategies based on your specific financial goals, risk tolerance, and retirement timeline.

By employing these strategies, you can optimize your investment distribution for long-term financial health.
