Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts involves considering factors like your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here's a general strategy you might consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**: If your employer offers a matching contribution, try to contribute enough to your 401(k) to receive the full match, as this is essentially "free money."

2. **Contribute to IRA**: After maximizing your 401(k) match, consider contributing to an IRA (Traditional or Roth), depending on your eligibility and tax situation. A Traditional IRA may offer tax-deductible contributions, while a Roth IRA allows for tax-free withdrawals in retirement.

3. **Maximize 401(k) Contributions**: Once you've funded your IRA, go back to your 401(k) to maximize annual contributions. The contribution limits for 401(k)s are generally higher than those for IRAs.

4. **Taxable Accounts**: After maximizing tax-advantaged accounts, you can invest in taxable accounts. This allows for greater flexibility and access to your funds, albeit with tax implications on capital gains and dividends.

5. **Consider Asset Allocation**: Ensure that the overall allocation between stocks, bonds, and other assets aligns with your risk tolerance and investment goals. You might want to hold more aggressive investments in tax-advantaged accounts and use taxable accounts for more tax-efficient investments.

6. **Review and Adjust**: Regularly assess your portfolio and rebalance as necessary to ensure it continues to meet your financial objectives.

It's advisable to consult with a financial advisor to tailor a strategy that works best for your specific financial situation and goals.
