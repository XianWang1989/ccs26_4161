[🔒CLASSIC] The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts largely depends on your individual financial goals, tax situation, and investment strategy. Here are some general guidelines:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a match on your 401(k) contributions, aim to contribute at least enough to get the full match, as this is essentially free money.

2. **Contribute to Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**: After maximizing the employer match, consider contributing to your IRS-backed retirement accounts like a Traditional or Roth IRA. These accounts offer tax benefits that can enhance your long-term growth.

3. **Diversify Investments**: Allocate your investments according to your risk tolerance and investment timeline. Higher risk investments may be more suitable in tax-advantaged accounts, where withdrawals will be taxed at a potentially lower rate later.

4. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**: Funds in taxable accounts can be accessed without penalties and can also provide tax-loss harvesting opportunities. This flexibility can be advantageous in managing your overall tax liability.

5. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: If you're unsure how to best allocate your funds, consider seeking advice from a financial advisor who can provide personalized strategies based on your financial situation.
