Distributing your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts can depend on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and time horizon. Here are some general guidelines that can help you structure your investments effectively:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**:
   - **401(k)**: If your employer offers a matching contribution, aim to contribute at least enough to get the full match first. This is essentially free money! 
   - **IRA**: Consider making contributions to an IRA (either traditional or Roth) if you qualify. Roth IRAs can offer tax-free withdrawals in retirement, which can be beneficial.

2. **Consider Your Tax Bracket**:
   - If you're in a higher tax bracket, traditional 401(k) and IRA contributions can help reduce your taxable income now. 
   - If you expect to be in a lower tax bracket in retirement, a Roth option may be more advantageous.

3. **Investment Strategy**:
   - Use tax-efficient investments within taxable accounts, such as index funds or ETFs, which typically generate lower taxable distributions.
   - Higher-risk or higher-yield investments, like bonds or REITs, might be better suited for tax-advantaged accounts due to their tax implications.

4. **Diversification**:
   - Ensure that your overall portfolio remains diversified across different asset classes. Allocate funds based on your risk tolerance and investment timeline.

5. **Rebalance Regularly**:
   - Monitor your allocations periodically to stay aligned with your investment goals. Rebalance as necessary to maintain your desired asset allocation.

6. **Emergency Fund**:
   - Ensure you have an emergency fund set aside in a liquid savings account before aggressively investing in your retirement accounts.

7. **Consult a Financial Advisor**:
   - Consider seeking professional advice tailored to your specific financial situation, as they can provide personalized strategies based on your goals and market conditions.

Always remember that your individual circumstances may vary, so tailor these guidelines to fit your specific financial situation and goals.
