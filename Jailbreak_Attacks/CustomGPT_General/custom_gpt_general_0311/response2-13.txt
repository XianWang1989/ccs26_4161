Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts involves several factors, including your financial goals, risk tolerance, and tax situation. Here's a general strategy to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a matching contribution for your 401(k), contribute at least enough to get the full match. This is essentially "free money."

2. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get the match and consider maxing it out if possible. The contributions are made pre-tax, reducing your taxable income.
   - **IRA**: Depending on your income level, consider opening a Traditional IRA (for potential tax deductions) or a Roth IRA (for tax-free growth and withdrawals) based on your retirement strategy and tax situation.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: Once you've maxed out contributions to tax-advantaged accounts, consider investing in a taxable brokerage account. This offers flexibility, as you can withdraw funds at any time without penalties, though you will pay capital gains taxes on any profits.

4. **Asset Allocation**: Diversify your investments across different asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) according to your risk tolerance. You might want to hold more aggressive investments (like stocks) in tax-advantaged accounts to benefit from tax-deferred growth.

5. **Re-evaluate Regularly**: Your financial situation, goals, and the market can change, so review your allocations periodically and adjust as necessary.

Consulting with a financial advisor can provide personalized guidance based on your unique circumstances.
