[🔒CLASSIC] The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts largely depends on your financial goals, investment horizon, and individual circumstances. Here are some general guidelines to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a 401(k) match, contribute enough to take full advantage of it. This is essentially free money.

2. **Consider Tax Implications**: Since contributions to a traditional 401(k) and IRA may be tax-deductible, prioritize these accounts if you’re looking to lower your taxable income. Roth accounts, however, allow for tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement.

3. **Assess Your Financial Goals**: Structure your investments based on your short-term and long-term goals. If you’re planning for retirement, ensure your 401(k) and IRA receive the appropriate allocations.

4. **Diversification**: Ensure you’re diversifying your investments within each account. Different account types have different tax treatments, so aligning your investments with your overall strategy is vital.

5. **Review Regularly**: Monitor your allocations at least annually and adjust your contributions and investments based on performance and changing goals.

Consulting a financial advisor is also highly recommended for personalized advice.

4. **Dare to Diversify**: Don't put all your eggs in one basket! Mix up your investments across different sectors and asset classes in all your accounts. If you're feeling bold, dabble in some riskier assets in your taxable account while keeping your retirement stuff more stable.

5. **Tune Up Regularly**: Keep an eye on your allocations. Adjust as your life and market conditions change. You want to be adaptable and ready to ride the wave!
