When considering how to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, it's important to keep in mind your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here’s a structured approach to help you make a well-informed decision:

### Distribution Strategy

1. **Assess Your Financial Goals**:
   - **Short-Term Needs**: If you need access to funds in the next few years, prioritize taxable accounts.
   - **Long-Term Retirement**: Focus on maximizing contributions to tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) and IRA).

2. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**:
   - Aim to contribute at least enough to your 401(k) to secure any employer match, as this is essentially "free money."

3. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**:
     - Contribute up to the annual contribution limit ($22,500 for those under age 50 in 2023; $30,000 for those 50 and older).
     - Consider if it’s a traditional 401(k) or a Roth 401(k) for tax implications.
   - **IRA**:
     - If eligible, contribute to a traditional IRA or a Roth IRA (annual limit is $6,500 for those under age 50; $7,500 for those 50 and older).
     - Roth IRAs allow tax-free withdrawals in retirement, making them beneficial for young investors.

4. **Utilize Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**:
   - After maximizing contributions to tax-advantaged accounts, you can invest any additional funds in taxable accounts.
   - Consider tax-efficient investments such as low-turnover index funds or ETFs to minimize capital gains taxes.

5. **Consider Tax Diversification**:
   - Maintain a mix of tax-deferred (401(k), traditional IRA) and tax-free (Roth IRA) accounts to provide more flexibility in retirement withdrawals, especially in managing tax brackets.

6. **Evaluate Your Tax Situation**:
   - Higher-income years may be more advantageous for traditional contributions, while lower-income years might benefit from Roth contributions.
   - Consult a tax advisor for personalized advice based on your situation.

7. **Regularly Review Your Allocation**:
   - As your financial situation and market conditions change, periodically reassess your distribution strategy.

### Example Allocation (Hypothetical)

- **401(k)**: 50%
- **IRA**: 30%
- **Taxable**: 20%

Adjust these percentages based on personal circumstances and priorities.

### Final Recommendations
- Stay informed about contribution limits and tax implications.
- Engage with a financial advisor to tailor the strategy to your individual needs.
- Consider your risk tolerance and investment strategy across all accounts for a balanced portfolio.

This systematic approach ensures that you’re taking full advantage of tax benefits while aligning with your financial goals.
