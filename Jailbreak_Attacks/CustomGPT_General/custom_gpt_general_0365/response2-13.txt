Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors including your age, risk tolerance, tax situation, and retirement goals. Here are some general guidelines:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to get the full match first. This is essentially free money.
   - **IRA (Roth or Traditional)**: If you qualify, contribute to an IRA after maximizing your 401(k) match. Roth IRAs offer tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement, while Traditional IRAs provide tax deductions on contributions.

2. **Assess Contribution Limits**:
   - For 2024, the contribution limit for a 401(k) is $23,000 (or $30,000 if you’re 50 or older).
   - The IRA contribution limit is $6,500 (or $7,500 if you’re 50 or older).

3. **Diversify Investments**:
   - Within these accounts, diversify your investments to balance risk and return. Consider a mix of stocks, bonds, and other assets based on your risk tolerance.

4. **Tax Considerations**:
   - **Taxable Accounts**: These accounts are best for investments that generate lower tax burdens (like qualified dividends or long-term capital gains) or for funds you may need to access before retirement.
   - Avoid placing high-turnover or high-income-generating investments in taxable accounts to minimize tax burdens.

5. **Evaluate Withdrawal Needs**:
   - Consider when you will need access to your funds. Since 401(k) and IRA funds are typically not accessible without penalties until retirement age (59½), ensure you have enough liquidity in taxable accounts for short-term needs.

6. **Rebalance Regularly**:
   - Periodically review and adjust your asset allocation based on your changing financial situation, market performance, and retirement timeline.

Ultimately, consulting with a financial advisor can provide personalized recommendations tailored to your unique circumstances.
