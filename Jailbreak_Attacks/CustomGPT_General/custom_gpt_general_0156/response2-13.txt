To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following factors and strategies:

1. **Understand the Types of Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: Employer-sponsored retirement account, often with matching contributions. Contributions are pre-tax, which means you reduce your taxable income now but will pay taxes upon withdrawal.
   - **IRA (Individual Retirement Account)**: Can be traditional (pre-tax contributions) or Roth (post-tax contributions). Traditional IRAs follow the same tax structure as 401(k)s, while Roth IRAs allow tax-free withdrawals in retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Investments outside of retirement accounts where you'll pay capital gains taxes on profits. These accounts provide more liquidity and flexibility for withdrawals.

2. **Prioritize Contributions**:
   - **401(k) Match**: Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money.
   - **Maximize IRA**: If you can contribute more after maximizing the match, consider funding your IRA next, particularly if you are eligible for a Roth IRA.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Once you've fully funded your tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) and IRA), direct additional savings to your taxable account for more accessibility.

3. **Consider Tax Implications**:
   - High-income earners may benefit from maxing out tax-deferred accounts (like 401(k) and traditional IRA) to lower their taxable income now.
   - If you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement, contributing to a Roth IRA might be advantageous, allowing for tax-free growth.

4. **Investment Choices**:
   - Diversify your investments according to each account's purpose; higher-risk investments may be more suitable for retirement accounts where you have a longer time horizon.
   - Use tax-efficient funds in taxable accounts to minimize your annual tax burden (e.g., index funds, ETFs).

5. **Review and Adjust**:
   - Regularly review your allocations and adjust as needed based on changes in income, tax laws, and your retirement timeline.

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: For personalized advice tailored to your financial situation, consider speaking with a financial advisor who can help you create a specific plan based on your goals.

By understanding the characteristics of each account and following these strategies, you can effectively distribute your money to optimize growth and manage taxes efficiently.
