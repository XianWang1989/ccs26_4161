Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, investment horizon, and tax situation. Here’s a general approach:

### 1. **Assess Your Investment Goals**
   - **Retirement**: Prioritize tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) and IRA).
   - **Short-Term Needs**: Use taxable accounts for liquidity.

### 2. **Maximize Employer Match**
   - Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match, as this is essentially "free money."

### 3. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**
   - **401(k)**: Maximize contributions (up to $22,500 in 2023, or $30,000 if aged 50+).
   - **IRA**: Contribute to a Traditional or Roth IRA (max $6,500 in 2023, or $7,500 if aged 50+).

### 4. **Consider Tax Implications**
   - **Traditional Accounts**: Contributions are tax-deductible; withdrawals are taxed.
   - **Roth Accounts**: Contributions are made with after-tax dollars; withdrawals are tax-free in retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Realize capital gains and dividends can be taxed.

### 5. **Diversify Investments**
   - Spread investments across stocks, bonds, and other assets in all accounts for balanced risk.

### 6. **Rebalance Regularly**
   - Adjust allocations based on market performance and changes in your risk tolerance.

### 7. **Consult a Financial Advisor**
   - Personalized advice can help align your investments with your specific financial situation and goals.

By following this structured approach, you can effectively distribute your money across these accounts to optimize growth and minimize taxes.
