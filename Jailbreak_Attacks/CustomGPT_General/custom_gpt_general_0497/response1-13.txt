Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here’s a general approach to consider:

### 1. **Prioritize Contributions**
   - **Employer Match**: Maximize contributions to your 401(k) to capture any employer match, as this is essentially free money.
   - **Max Out IRA**: If eligible, consider contributing to a traditional or Roth IRA for tax advantages. Roth IRAs provide tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement.

### 2. **Consider Tax Implications**
   - **401(k) and Traditional IRAs**: Contributions are typically tax-deductible, which can reduce your tax burden now. However, taxes are paid on withdrawals in retirement.
   - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, allowing for tax-free growth and withdrawals.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Invest after-tax dollars; be mindful of capital gains taxes when selling investments.

### 3. **Investment Choices**
   - **401(k)**: Often has limited investment options. Focus on low-cost index funds or target-date funds based on your retirement timeline.
   - **IRA**: More flexible in terms of investment choices. Consider diversifying with stocks, bonds, ETFs, and mutual funds.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Utilize for investments that generate lower short-term capital gains or tax-efficient funds, such as index funds or ETFs.

### 4. **Rebalance and Adjust**
   - Regularly review your allocations to ensure they align with your risk tolerance and financial goals. Adjust based on changes in your life situation (e.g., marriage, children, retirement).

### 5. **Emergency Fund**
   - Ensure you have an emergency fund in place before heavily investing. This can be in a high-yield savings account.

### 6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**
   - Consider working with a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your specific circumstances and retirement goals.

### Example Allocation Strategy:
- **401(k)**: Max out up to employer match and contribute 10-15% of your income.
- **IRA**: Contribute the maximum allowed (currently $6,500 or $7,500 if over 50).
- **Taxable Account**: After maxing out retirement accounts, invest any additional savings here for flexibility.

This strategy helps optimize your tax situation while preparing for long-term growth. Always adjust based on personal goals and changing circumstances.
