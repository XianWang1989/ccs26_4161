When considering the best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, think about a few key factors like your tax situation, investment goals, retirement timeline, and any employer matching contributions. Here are some general guidelines:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a matching contribution on your 401(k), contribute at least enough to get the full match, as this is essentially “free money.”

2. **Prioritize Retirement Accounts**: After maximizing your employer match, consider maxing out your IRA (if eligible) before contributing more to your 401(k). IRAs often have more investment options compared to a 401(k).

3. **Tax Considerations**: 
   - Traditional 401(k) and IRA contributions are pre-tax, reducing your taxable income, but you'll pay taxes on withdrawals in retirement.
   - Roth IRA contributions are after-tax, allowing for tax-free withdrawals in retirement if certain conditions are met.
   - Taxable accounts allow for more flexibility but may have capital gains tax implications.

4. **Emergency Fund**: Ensure that you have a sufficient emergency savings fund before heavily investing. Aim for 3-6 months of expenses.

5. **Diversification and Risk Tolerance**: Your allocation should also reflect your risk tolerance and investment horizon. A balanced mix of stocks, bonds, and other assets can help you manage risk effectively.

6. **Periodic Reassessment**: Your needs and the market conditions change, so regularly review and adjust your allocation as necessary.
