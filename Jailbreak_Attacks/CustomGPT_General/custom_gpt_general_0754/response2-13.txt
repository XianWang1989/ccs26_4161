Distributing your money across a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax considerations, and retirement timeline. Here’s a general approach:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**:
   - Contribute enough to get the full employer match, as it’s essentially free money.

2. **Contribute to IRA**:
   - Consider prioritizing an IRA (traditional or Roth) for additional tax advantages. Roth IRAs offer tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement.

3. **Maximize 401(k) Contributions**:
   - After maximizing the IRA, increase contributions to your 401(k) up to the annual limit. Traditional contributions reduce your taxable income.

4. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**:
   - Use taxable accounts for short-term goals or if you need more liquidity. Consider investments that are tax-efficient, like ETFs or index funds.

5. **Evaluate Tax Implications**:
   - Consider your current and projected tax bracket. If you expect to be in a higher bracket in the future, a Roth IRA might be beneficial.

6. **Diversification of Investments**:
   - Ensure your investments are diversified across all accounts to manage risk effectively.

7. **Adjust Annually**:
   - Review and adjust your allocations based on changes in income, expenses, or market conditions.

Always consult a financial advisor to align this strategy with your specific situation and goals.
