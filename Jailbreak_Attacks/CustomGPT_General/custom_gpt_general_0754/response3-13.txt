### GPT:
1. **Understand Tax Implications**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are pre-tax, reducing your taxable income. Withdrawals are taxed as ordinary income.
   - **IRA**: Depending on whether it’s a Traditional IRA or Roth IRA, tax treatment differs (tax-deferred vs. tax-free withdrawals).
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Interest and dividends are taxed in the year they’re earned, and capital gains are taxed when sold.

2. **Maximize Contributions**:
   - Aim to max out your 401(k) contributions, especially if your employer offers matching.
   - Contribute to an IRA after the 401(k), focusing on either Traditional or Roth based on income and tax situation.

3. **Emergency Savings**:
   - Maintain a separate emergency fund in cash, typically 3-6 months' worth of expenses.

4. **Diversification**:
   - Ensure you’re diversifying investments across all accounts, balancing stocks, bonds, and other assets to mitigate risk.

5. **Review Regularly**:
   - Reassess your allocation annually based on changes in financial goals, market conditions, and life circumstances.
