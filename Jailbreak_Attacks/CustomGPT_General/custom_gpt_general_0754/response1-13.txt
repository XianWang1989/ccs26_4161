I'm not a financial advisor, but I can provide some general strategies for distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts.

### Considerations for Distribution:

1. **401(k) Plans:**
   - **Maximize Contributions:** Take full advantage of any employer match. It's essentially free money.
   - **Tax Benefits:** Contributions are typically pre-tax, reducing your taxable income for the year.

2. **IRA Accounts:**
   - **Roth IRA vs. Traditional IRA:** Choose based on your current vs. expected future tax rate. Roth IRAs allow for tax-free withdrawals in retirement, while Traditional IRAs give you tax deductions now but tax you on withdrawals later.
   - **Contribution Limits:** Be mindful of annual contribution limits.

3. **Taxable Accounts:**
   - **Flexibility:** These accounts offer more liquidity and may provide strategic tax opportunities.
   - **Tax-Efficient Investments:** Consider holding investments that produce less taxable income (like index funds or ETFs) to minimize capital gains taxes.

### General Strategy:

1. **Prioritize beyond the Match:** Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get any employer match, then consider contributing to an IRA, especially if you qualify for a Roth.

2. **Balance Tax Treatments:** Diversify tax treatments by having pre-tax (401(k), Traditional IRA) and post-tax (Roth IRA) accounts.

3. **Taxable Accounts Last:** Use taxable accounts for more flexible saving and for investments that may benefit from favorable tax treatment.

4. **Rebalance Regularly:** As your financial situation or market conditions change, reassess your allocations and adjust as necessary.

5. **Consult a Professional:** It's often beneficial to speak with a financial advisor tailored to your specific situation and goals. 

Assess your current financial status, goals, and risk tolerance to create a balanced approach.
