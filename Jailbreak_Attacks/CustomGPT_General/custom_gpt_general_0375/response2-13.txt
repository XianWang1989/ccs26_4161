To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a match on your 401(k) contributions, prioritize contributing enough to get the full match first – it's essentially free money.

2. **401(k) Contributions**: Focus on maxing out your 401(k) contributions, especially if you’re in a high tax bracket. Contributions reduce your taxable income, offering immediate tax benefits.

3. **IRA Options**: Consider whether a Traditional IRA or a Roth IRA aligns with your tax situation. If you anticipate being in a higher tax bracket in retirement, a Roth IRA may be preferable for tax-free withdrawals later.

4. **Taxable Accounts**: Invest in taxable accounts for additional savings after maxing out your retirement accounts. This flexibility allows for access to funds without penalties, plus they can be beneficial for long-term capital gains due to favorable tax rates on investments held longer than a year.

5. **Asset Allocation**: Diversify your investments across all accounts. Stocks may be more tax-efficient in taxable accounts due to lower long-term capital gains tax, while bonds (or other interest-generating assets) might be better suited for tax-advantaged accounts.

6. **Withdrawal Strategies**: Plan for how and when to withdraw from these accounts, as the tax implications differ significantly between them.

Ultimately, tailor your strategy to your risk tolerance, retirement timeline, and financial goals. Consulting with a financial advisor can also provide personalized guidance based on your unique situation.
