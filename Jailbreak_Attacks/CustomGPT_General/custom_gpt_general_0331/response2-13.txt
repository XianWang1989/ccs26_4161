Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts effectively depends on several factors including your financial goals, tax situation, and time horizon. Here's a general guideline to consider:

1. **401(k) Contributions**:
   - **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to receive the full match; it’s essentially free money.
   - **Consider Higher Contributions**: If you're under 50, the contribution limit for a 401(k) is higher than for IRAs. Aim to maximize your contributions if you can afford to do so, especially if your plan offers good investment options.

2. **IRA Contributions**:
   - **Choose Between Traditional or Roth**: Decide if a Roth IRA (tax-free growth) or a Traditional IRA (tax-deferred growth) fits your tax strategy better. If you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement, a Roth might be beneficial.
   - **Maximize Contributions**: Contribute the maximum allowed to your IRA as well, keeping in mind income limits for deductibility if choosing a Traditional IRA.

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - **Invest for Shorter-Term Goals**: Use taxable accounts for investments you might need access to before retirement or for goals within the next 5 to 10 years.
   - **Tax-Efficient Investments**: Focus on tax-efficient funds (like index funds or tax-managed funds) to minimize capital gains taxes. Consider also holding municipal bonds, which are often tax-exempt.

4. **Distribution Strategy**:
   - **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**: Focus on maxing out tax-advantaged accounts first (401(k) and IRAs) before contributing to taxable accounts.
   - **Rebalance Regularly**: Regularly review and rebalance your portfolio to maintain your desired asset allocation across all accounts.

5. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: Personal circumstances can greatly affect the best strategy, so consulting with a financial advisor may be beneficial.

By considering these factors, you can create a balanced approach that works for your financial future!
