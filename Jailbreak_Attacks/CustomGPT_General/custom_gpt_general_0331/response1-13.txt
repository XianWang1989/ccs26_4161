Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various factors, including your financial goals, current income, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here’s a general approach to consider:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**:
   - **401(k)**: If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to get the full match. This is essentially free money.
   - **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**: After maximizing your 401(k) match, consider contributing to an IRA. A Roth IRA allows tax-free withdrawals in retirement, while a Traditional IRA may offer immediate tax deductions.

2. **Assess Contribution Limits**:
   - Keep in mind the annual contribution limits for both 401(k) and IRA accounts. For 2023, the limit is $22,500 for a 401(k) and $6,500 for IRAs (with an additional catch-up contribution if you're over 50).

3. **Evaluate Your Tax Situation**:
   - **Taxable Accounts**: These accounts can be useful for investments that may generate taxable income (like bonds), or if you anticipate needing access to funds before retirement age without penalties. 
   - Consider capital gains tax implications and the tax efficiency of your investments.

4. **Long-Term Growth vs. Accessibility**:
   - Prioritize long-term growth in tax-advantaged accounts. Use taxable accounts for more liquid investments that might be accessed sooner.

5. **Diversify Investments**:
   - Across all accounts, ensure diversification based on your risk tolerance and investment goals.

6. **Adjust Regularly**:
   - Regularly review and adjust your allocations based on market conditions, your age, and financial goals to maintain a balanced portfolio.

7. **Consult a Financial Advisor**:
   - Consider seeking personalized advice from a financial advisor to tailor a strategy that suits your situation.

By following these guidelines, you can create a balanced approach to investing that maximizes tax advantages and aligns with your overall financial goals.
