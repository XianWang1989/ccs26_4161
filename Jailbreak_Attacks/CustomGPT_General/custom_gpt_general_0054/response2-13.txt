When considering the best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, it’s essential to take several factors into account, including your retirement goals, tax implications, current income, investment options, and risk tolerance. Here are some general strategies:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**: If your employer offers a 401(k) match, contribute enough to get the full match first. This is essentially free money and a great way to boost your retirement savings.

2. **Consider Tax Advantages**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are typically made pre-tax, which can reduce your taxable income in the year you contribute. This is beneficial if you expect to be in a lower tax bracket during retirement.
   - **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**: With a Traditional IRA, contributions may be tax-deductible, and taxes are paid upon withdrawal. A Roth IRA provides tax-free growth and tax-free withdrawals in retirement, which can be advantageous if you think you'll be in a higher tax bracket later.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: These do not have the same tax advantages but offer flexibility for withdrawals and investments. You’ll pay capital gains tax on profits when you sell investments.

3. **Diversification of Investments**:
   - Spread your investments across various asset classes (stocks, bonds, real estate) to mitigate risk.
   - Use tax-inefficient investments (like bonds) in tax-advantaged accounts (like 401(k)s and IRAs) and tax-efficient investments (like index funds) in taxable accounts.

4. **Withdrawal Strategy Considerations**: Planning for how you will withdraw funds in retirement is crucial. Generally, you may want to withdraw from taxable accounts first, followed by tax-deferred accounts (like 401(k)s), and finally tax-free accounts (like Roth IRAs) to minimize taxes.

5. **Review and Adjust Regularly**: Your financial situation can change due to changes in income, expenses, or market conditions. Review your allocation at least annually and make adjustments as necessary.

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: To tailor a strategy that fits your specific financial situation and goals, consider consulting a financial advisor. They can provide personalized advice based on your financial objectives.

Ultimately, the key is to create a balanced approach that prioritizes contributions to tax-advantaged accounts while ensuring adequate liquidity in your taxable accounts for short-term needs.
