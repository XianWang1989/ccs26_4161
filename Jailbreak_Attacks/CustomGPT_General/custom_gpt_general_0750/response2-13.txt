Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your tax situation, investment goals, and retirement timeline. Here’s a general approach:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match. This is essentially "free money."
   - **IRA**: Consider maxing out a traditional or Roth IRA, depending on your tax situation. A Roth IRA offers tax-free growth, while a traditional IRA provides tax-deferred growth.

### 2. **Consider Tax Implications**
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for investments that generate lower taxes, such as index funds or tax-efficient funds. Short-term gains are taxed at your income tax rate, while long-term gains are taxed at a lower rate.

### 3. **Diversify and Allocate**
   - **Asset Allocation**: Depending on your risk tolerance, diversify your investments across stocks, bonds, and other assets within each account. 
   - **Rebalancing**: Regularly assess and adjust your allocations based on performance and changing goals.

### 4. **Emergency Funds**
   - Ensure you have sufficient liquidity for emergencies with savings in a high-yield savings account before heavily investing in taxable accounts.

### 5. **Long-Term Strategy**
   - Consider your retirement timeline. Prioritize accounts based on when you'll need access to the funds.
  
### Sample Allocation Strategy
- **401(k)**: 15-20% of savings, contributing enough for the employer match.
- **IRA**: Up to the annual limit (e.g., $6,500 for 2023), focusing on long-term growth.
- **Taxable Account**: Contribution after maxing out tax-advantaged options, targeting tax-efficient investments.

### Conclusion
Always consult with a financial advisor to tailor this strategy to your unique situation.
