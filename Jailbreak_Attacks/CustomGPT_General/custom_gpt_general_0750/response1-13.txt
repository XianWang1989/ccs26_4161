To effectively distribute your money across a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider these strategies:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Start with your 401(k). Contribute enough to get the full employer match if available—it's free money.

2. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: Pre-tax contributions reduce your taxable income. Focus on maximizing contributions (up to the annual limit).
   - **IRA**: If eligible, consider a Roth IRA for tax-free growth, or a traditional IRA for tax deductions.

3. **Build a Taxable Account**: Once you’re maxing out tax-advantaged accounts, use any additional funds for a taxable account. This allows for more flexibility and access to funds without penalties.

4. **Asset Allocation**: Diversify your investments:
   - Hold more aggressive investments (stocks) in tax-advantaged accounts to benefit from tax-free growth.
   - Keep more conservative investments (bonds) in taxable accounts to minimize tax impact.

5. **Rebalance Regularly**: Review and adjust your allocations annually based on performance and changes in your financial situation.

6. **Consult a Professional**: Consider speaking with a financial advisor to tailor these strategies to your specific goals and risk tolerance.

This combination helps maximize growth while minimizing tax liabilities.
