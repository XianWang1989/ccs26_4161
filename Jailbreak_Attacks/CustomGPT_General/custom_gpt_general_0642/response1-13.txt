The best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your age, financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some strategies to consider:

### 1. **Prioritize Contributions**:
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match, as it's essentially free money.
   - **IRA**: Max out your IRA contributions next, focusing on either a traditional or Roth IRA based on your tax situation. Roth IRAs are often beneficial for younger savers expecting higher future taxes.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for additional savings once you've maximized tax-advantaged accounts.

### 2. **Consider Tax Implications**:
   - **Tax Deferred**: Contributions to 401(k) and traditional IRAs grow tax-deferred until withdrawal.
   - **Tax-Free Growth**: Roth IRAs provide tax-free growth, making them ideal if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket during retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Be mindful of capital gains tax on investments sold for profit.

### 3. **Asset Allocation**:
   - Diversify your investments across accounts based on your risk tolerance and financial goals.
   - Maintain a consistent asset allocation strategy across all accounts.

### 4. **Withdrawal Strategy**:
   - As retirement approaches, consider which accounts to draw from first based on tax implications. Typically, it may be advantageous to withdraw from taxable accounts first to allow tax-advantaged accounts to continue growing.

### 5. **Re-Evaluate Regularly**:
   - Periodically review your asset allocation and contribution levels to ensure they align with changing financial goals and market conditions.

### Example Distribution Strategy:
- **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get matching.
- **IRA**: Max out contributions after your 401(k).
- **Taxable**: Allocate the remainder of your savings here, focusing on investments that generate tax-efficient income.

### Conclusion:
Consulting with a financial advisor can also help tailor a strategy specific to your situation, as they can provide insights into tax-efficient investment strategies and retirement planning.
