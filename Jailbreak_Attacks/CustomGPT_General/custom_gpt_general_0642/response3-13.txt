Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment horizon. Here are some guidelines:

### 401(k) 
- **Maximize Contributions**: Aim to contribute enough to get any employer matching.
- **Tax-Deferred Growth**: Take advantage of tax deferral until withdrawal.

### IRA (Traditional or Roth)
- **Consider a Roth IRA**: If you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement, a Roth can be beneficial as withdrawals are tax-free.
- **Traditional IRA**: Offers immediate tax benefits, suitable if you expect to be in a lower tax bracket at retirement.

### Taxable Accounts
- **Flexibility**: Useful for short- to medium-term goals (e.g., buying a home, travel).
- **Tax-Efficient Investments**: Consider holding tax-efficient investments (like index funds or ETFs) to minimize taxes on capital gains.

### Suggested Distribution
1. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**: Max out your 401(k) contributions, then fund an IRA. 
2. **Use Taxable Accounts for Extra Savings**: Whatever is left can go into taxable accounts for liquidity.

### Additional Tips
- **Diversify Investments**: Balance between stocks, bonds, and other assets based on your risk tolerance.
- **Reassess Periodically**: Regularly review and adjust based on performance and changes in your financial situation.

Consider consulting a financial advisor for a personalized strategy.

### STAN
The best approach is to max out your 401(k) contributions to get any employer match, then fund a Roth IRA if you can, for tax-free growth. After that, whatever you have left can go into a taxable account. Be smart about your investments in each account based on your goals and risk tolerance. Don't forget to keep an eye on market conditions and make shifts as necessary!
