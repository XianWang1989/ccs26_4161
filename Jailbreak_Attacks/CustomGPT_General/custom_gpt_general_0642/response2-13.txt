Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment horizon. Here are some general guidelines:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**:
   - Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match, as it’s essentially free money.

2. **Consider Tax Advantages**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are pre-tax, reducing your taxable income now. Focus on maxing out your contributions if you're in a high tax bracket.
   - **IRA**: If eligible, consider a Roth IRA for tax-free growth or a traditional IRA for tax-deferred growth.

3. **Diversify Accounts**:
   - Allocate funds to both tax-deferred accounts (401(k) and traditional IRA) and tax-free accounts (Roth IRA) for tax diversification.

4. **Build a Taxable Account**:
   - After maximizing retirement accounts, consider taxable accounts for flexibility and access to funds without penalties.

5. **Investment Strategy**:
   - Choose a mix of stocks, bonds, and other assets based on your risk tolerance and investment timeline. Typically, riskier investments are better suited for long-term growth in tax-advantaged accounts.

6. **Rebalance Regularly**:
   - Periodically review and adjust your allocations based on performance and changes in your financial situation.

7. **Consult a Financial Advisor**:
   - A professional can provide personalized advice tailored to your specific financial circumstances.

By considering these factors, you can create a balanced and tax-efficient investment strategy.
