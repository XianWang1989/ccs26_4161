Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors including your income, tax situation, and retirement goals. Here’s a general strategy:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match. This is essentially "free money."

2. **Consider an IRA**: If you're eligible, consider maxing out your IRA (either traditional or Roth) next. This can provide tax advantages depending on your situation.

3. **Continue with the 401(k)**: After your IRA, direct any additional savings towards your 401(k) until you've met the annual contribution limit.

4. **Taxable Accounts**: Finally, allocate funds to taxable accounts. These accounts are flexible and allow you to access your investments without penalty, but they come with different tax implications.

5. **Tax Efficiency**: Keep tax-efficient investments (like index funds) in taxable accounts and high-growth investments in tax-advantaged accounts to minimize tax liabilities.

6. **Adjust According to Goals**: Consider your retirement timeline, risk tolerance, and investment goals to adjust contributions. If you're closer to retirement, you might prioritize more stable investments.

Consult with a financial advisor to tailor these suggestions to your individual situation for the best outcomes.
