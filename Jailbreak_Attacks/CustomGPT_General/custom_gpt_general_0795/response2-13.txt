Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment horizon. Here are some general strategies:

### 1. **Prioritize Contributions**
   - **401(k) Up to Employer Match**: Aim to contribute enough to get the full employer match—this is free money.
   - **IRA Contributions**: After the match, consider maxing out your IRA (traditional or Roth) for potential tax advantages.

### 2. **Consider Tax Implications**
   - **Tax-Deferred Accounts (401(k) and Traditional IRA)**: Contributions reduce taxable income now, but withdrawals are taxed in retirement.
   - **Roth Accounts (Roth IRA and Roth 401(k))**: Contributions are after-tax, but withdrawals are tax-free if certain conditions are met.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Good for flexibility but subject to capital gains taxes.

### 3. **Asset Allocation**
   - **Growth Investments in Tax-Advantaged Accounts**: Since these accounts grow tax-deferred, consider holding higher-growth investments (e.g., stocks).
   - **Income Investments in Taxable Accounts**: Holding bonds or income-generating assets in taxable accounts can minimize tax liabilities from interest.

### 4. **Emergency Fund**
   - Ensure you have a sufficient emergency fund in a accessible account apart from your investment accounts before aggressively investing.

### 5. **Review Regularly**
   - Revisit your allocations at least annually to adjust for changes in income, expenses, and financial goals.

### Example Distribution Strategy
- **401(k)**: 15% of salary up to match, then 10% max contribution.
- **IRA**: Max out contributions ($6,500 for under age 50 or $7,500 for 50+).
- **Taxable Account**: Contribute any remaining funds after hitting those targets.

### Final Thoughts
Consult a financial advisor to tailor strategies to your personal situation, especially concerning tax implications and retirement planning.
