To optimize your money distribution among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider these key principles:

1. **Tax Efficiency**:
   - **401(k) and IRA**: These are tax-advantaged accounts. Prioritize maxing out contributions to these accounts, especially if your employer offers a match in your 401(k).
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for investments that generate lower taxes, such as index funds or ETFs. Avoid holding high-dividend stocks or actively managed funds here, as they generate more taxable income.

2. **Investment Horizon**:
   - **Long-Term**: Focus on equities in tax-advantaged accounts like your 401(k) and IRA due to their potential for higher growth over time.
   - **Shorter-Term Goals**: Use taxable accounts for goals within a 1-5 year horizon, as you'll need access to the funds without penalties.

3. **Withdrawal Strategies**:
   - In retirement, consider withdrawing from taxable accounts first to allow tax-advantaged accounts to grow tax-free longer.
   - Utilize tax-loss harvesting in your taxable accounts to offset gains.

4. **Contribution Limits**:
   - Keep in mind contribution limits for 401(k) and IRA accounts. Aim to max out these contributions to take full advantage of tax benefits.

5. **Diversification**:
   - Diversify across all accounts to spread risk and achieve a balanced portfolio.

By combining tax considerations, investment strategies, and your financial goals, you can create an effective distribution plan. Consulting a financial advisor can also provide personalized guidance based on your situation.
