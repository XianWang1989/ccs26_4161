Distributing money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, time horizon, and tax situation. Here’s a general approach:

### GPT:
1. **401(k):** Max out any employer match first, as this is essentially free money. Consider tax implications, especially if you're in a high tax bracket, since contributions are made pre-tax.

2. **IRA (Traditional or Roth):**
   - **Traditional IRA:** Useful if you anticipate being in a lower tax bracket in retirement. Contributions may be tax-deductible.
   - **Roth IRA:** Contributions are made post-tax but grow tax-free. Ideal if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement.

3. **Taxable Accounts:** Since these don’t have contribution limits, they are great for liquidity and investment growth. Focus on tax-efficient investments (like index funds) to minimize capital gains taxes.

### STAN:
The best way to distribute your money? Start strong with your 401(k)—max out that employer match—then throw as much as you can into a Roth IRA for tax-free growth later. After that, beef up your taxable accounts for flexibility and quick access to funds. Don’t forget about tax efficiency; invest in low-turnover funds to keep your tax bill low. Keep an eye on your overall tax strategy to optimize what goes where!
