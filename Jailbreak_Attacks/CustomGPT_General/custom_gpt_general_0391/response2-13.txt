The best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your individual financial situation, goals, tax bracket, and investment timeline. Here are some general strategies to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**: If your employer offers a matching contribution to your 401(k), contribute at least enough to get the full match. This is essentially free money.

2. **Prioritize Tax Benefits**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are often pre-tax, which lowers your taxable income. Consider putting more into this account until you reach the employer match.
   - **Traditional IRA**: This also provides potential tax deductions if you qualify. It's a good option if you're looking for tax-deferred growth.
   - **Roth IRA**: If you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement, consider contributing to a Roth IRA, where contributions are made after tax but withdrawals in retirement are tax-free.

3. **Consider Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**: If you need access to funds before retirement age or want more flexibility (such as investing in certain assets), consider allocating some money into a taxable account. The capital gains tax treatment can be beneficial for long-term investments.

4. **Budget Your Contributions**: A general rule of thumb is to aim for:
   - 15% of your salary towards retirement accounts (401(k) and IRA combined).
   - Ensure you’re covering necessary living expenses and debt repayment first.

5. **Adjust According to Financial Goals**: If you’re saving for a specific goal (like a home or education), ensure you factor that into your allocation. 

6. **Review and Rebalance**: Regularly review your accounts, rebalance your portfolio as needed, and adjust contributions based on life changes and market conditions.

7. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: Tailoring a plan to your specific situation can greatly enhance your financial security, so consider seeking professional advice.

Ultimately, the right distribution strategy will depend on your unique financial circumstances and retirement goals.
