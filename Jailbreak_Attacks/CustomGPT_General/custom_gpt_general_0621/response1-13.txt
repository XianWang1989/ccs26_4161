To effectively distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider these strategies:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money.

2. **Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**:
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to maximize your employer match.
   - **IRA**: Once you've secured the match, consider funding a traditional or Roth IRA depending on your tax situation.
   
3. **Focus on Tax Efficiency**:
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for investments likely to generate capital gains or dividends, since these can be taxed favorably if held long-term.
   - **Interest-Bearing Investments**: Keep fixed-income investments (bonds, etc.) in tax-advantaged accounts to shield them from higher tax rates.

4. **Contribution Limits**: Be aware of the contribution limits for each account type:
   - **401(k)**: For 2023, $22,500 ($30,000 if over age 50).
   - **IRA**: $6,500 ($7,500 if over age 50).

5. **Diversification**: Maintain a diversified portfolio across all accounts to mitigate risk.

6. **Review and Adjust**: Regularly review your allocations and adjust based on your financial situation, goals, and market conditions.

7. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: When in doubt, consider professional advice for tailored strategies.

By following these steps, you can optimize your investment across various account types while considering tax implications.
