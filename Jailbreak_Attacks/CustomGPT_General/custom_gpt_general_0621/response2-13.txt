To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategy:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match. This is essentially free money.

2. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: Focus on this account if your employer offers a match and if you're in a higher tax bracket, as contributions lower your taxable income.
   - **Traditional IRA**: Consider contributing here if you're eligible for tax deductions. This helps with tax-deferred growth.
   - **Roth IRA**: If you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement, prioritize contributions to a Roth IRA since withdrawals are tax-free.

3. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**: After maxing out tax-advantaged accounts, allocate funds to taxable accounts. These provide liquidity for short-term needs and allow for tax-loss harvesting.

4. **Investment Allocation**: Choose a diversified mix of stocks, bonds, and other assets based on your risk tolerance and time horizon within each account type.

5. **Rebalance Regularly**: Monitor your investments and rebalance to maintain your target allocation.

Consult a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your specific situation.
