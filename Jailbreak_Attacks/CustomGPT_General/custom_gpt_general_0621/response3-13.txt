Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your tax situation, retirement goals, and risk tolerance. Here’s a breakdown:

2. **IRA (Traditional or Roth):** Depending on your tax bracket, invest in a Traditional IRA for tax deduction or a Roth IRA for tax-free withdrawals in retirement. Max out contributions if possible.

3. **Taxable Accounts:** Use these for money you might need before retirement or for investments that could benefit from favorable capital gains tax rates. They're good for flexibility and liquidity.
