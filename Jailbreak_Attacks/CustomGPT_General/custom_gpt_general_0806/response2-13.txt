When considering how to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, it's essential to think about your financial goals, tax implications, and investment strategy. Here are some general guidelines to help you make an informed decision:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in Your 401(k):** If your employer offers a matching contribution, prioritize contributing enough to your 401(k) to take full advantage of the match. This is essentially "free money" and can significantly boost your retirement savings.

2. **Contribute to an IRA:** If you’re eligible, consider contributing to a Traditional IRA or Roth IRA. A Traditional IRA provides tax-deferred growth, while a Roth IRA offers tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement. Choose based on your current tax situation and expected tax bracket in retirement.

3. **Consider Taxable Accounts for Flexibility:** If you’ve maxed out contributions to your 401(k) and IRA, consider investing in a taxable account. This offers more flexibility in accessing funds without penalties and can be beneficial for mid-term goals.

4. **Tax Efficiency:** Be mindful of the types of investments in each account. Generally, it’s wise to hold tax-inefficient investments (like bonds or real estate) in tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) or IRA) and tax-efficient investments (like index funds or ETFs) in taxable accounts.

5. **Diversification and Risk Tolerance:** Ensure your overall portfolio is diversified according to your risk tolerance and investment timeline. Adjust your allocations based on whether you're saving for retirement (long-term) or other short-term goals.

6. **Regular Reviews and Adjustments:** Periodically review your allocations and make adjustments based on performance, changes in your financial situation, or life events.

7. **Consult a Financial Advisor:** If you’re uncertain about your strategy, consider consulting a financial advisor who can help tailor a plan specific to your needs.

Ultimately, the best distribution will depend on your individual financial situation, goals, and the specific offerings of your 401(k) and IRA accounts.
