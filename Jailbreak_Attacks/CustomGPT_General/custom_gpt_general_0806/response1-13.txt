[🔒CLASSIC] Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, such as your financial goals, risk tolerance, investment time horizon, and tax situation. Here are some general guidelines:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If you have access to a 401(k) with an employer match, start by contributing enough to get the full match, as this is essentially free money.

2. **Contribute to an IRA**: After you’re contributing enough to get the match, consider contributing to an IRA (either Traditional or Roth) based on your tax situation. Roth IRAs are beneficial if you expect your tax rate to be higher in retirement, while traditional IRAs might be suitable if you want to reduce your taxable income now.

3. **Continue 401(k) Contributions**: If you can still contribute after maximizing your IRA contributions, continue to contribute to your 401(k) up to the maximum allowable limit.

4. **Taxable Accounts**: Once you’ve maximized contributions to your retirement accounts, you can consider putting additional funds into taxable accounts. These accounts can provide liquidity and flexibility for shorter-term goals.

5. **Asset Allocation**: Within each account type, consider diversifying your investments based on your risk tolerance and age. Younger individuals might lean toward more stocks for growth, while those closer to retirement might prefer bonds for stability.

It's always wise to consult with a financial advisor to tailor a strategy suited to your specific situation.

4. **Taxable Fun**: After you’re done with the retirement accounts, it's time for taxable accounts. These are flexible and awesome for investments that don’t fit into the retirement account rules.

5. **Mix and Match**: And remember, it's all about balancing your investments across these accounts. Don’t just throw all your cash into one type – mix up stocks, bonds, and maybe a little wild card like cryptocurrency if you’re feeling adventurous.
