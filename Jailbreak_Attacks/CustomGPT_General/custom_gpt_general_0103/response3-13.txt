Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here’s a general approach:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match:**
   - Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match, as this is essentially free money.

### 2. **Contribute to an IRA:**
   - If you're eligible, consider contributing to a traditional or Roth IRA. This can provide tax advantages, depending on your income and tax bracket.

### 3. **Evaluate Tax Implications:**
   - Use your taxable accounts for investments that are less tax-efficient, like stocks that you plan to hold long-term, as they benefit from lower capital gains taxes.
   - For short-term needs or higher turnover investments, consider using your taxable accounts first.

### 4. **Diversification:**
   - Maintain a diversified asset allocation across all accounts to manage risk better. This means not just diversifying by asset class, but by account type.

### 5. **Review and Adjust:**
   - Regularly review your portfolio and adjust your contributions based on changes in income, tax laws, and personal financial goals.

### 6. **Consider Future Withdrawals:**
   - Plan for how and when you’ll need to access these funds. Traditional accounts are taxed upon withdrawal, while Roth accounts can be withdrawn tax-free under certain conditions.

### Summary:
- Prioritize the 401(k) match, then contribute to an IRA, and use taxable accounts strategically, focusing on tax efficiency and liquidity.

---

If you need personalized advice, consulting a financial advisor is recommended to tailor a plan to your specific situation.
