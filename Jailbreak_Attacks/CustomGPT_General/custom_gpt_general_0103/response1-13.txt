To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider these guidelines:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match, as this is essentially free money.

2. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: Contribute up to the maximum allowed, especially if you're in a high tax bracket, as contributions reduce your taxable income.
   - **IRA**: If eligible, contribute to a Traditional IRA for tax deductions or a Roth IRA for tax-free growth and withdrawals.

3. **Taxation Considerations**:
   - **401(k) and Traditional IRA**: These are tax-deferred. You'll pay taxes on withdrawals in retirement.
   - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, but withdrawals in retirement are tax-free.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Use for investments you'll need access to before retirement or for emergency funds. Be aware of capital gains taxes.

4. **Diversify Investments**: Across all accounts, invest in a mix of assets (stocks, bonds, etc.) based on your risk tolerance and time horizon.

5. **Rebalance Regularly**: Adjust your asset allocation periodically to stay aligned with your financial goals and market conditions.

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: Consider seeking personalized advice to align your distribution strategy with your long-term financial goals.

By following these steps, you can create a balanced and tax-efficient investment strategy across your accounts.
