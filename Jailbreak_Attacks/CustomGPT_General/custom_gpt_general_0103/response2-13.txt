Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your specific financial goals, risk tolerance, and timeline. Here’s a general approach to consider:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k):**
   - Contribute enough to get the full employer match if available. This is effectively free money.

### 2. **Focus on Tax-Advantaged Accounts:**
   - **IRA (Traditional or Roth):**
     - If eligible, consider maxing out a Roth IRA for tax-free growth, especially if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement.
     - If you're in a higher tax bracket now, a Traditional IRA can offer tax deductions.

### 3. **Contribute to Taxable Accounts:**
   - After maxing out contributions to your 401(k) and IRA, use taxable accounts for additional investments.
   - This provides flexibility for withdrawals without penalties.

### 4. **Consider Investment Strategies:**
   - **401(k):** Focus on a mix of stocks and bonds based on your risk tolerance.
   - **IRA:** Similar strategy as the 401(k), but consider a wider range of investment options.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Use tax-efficient funds or strategies, like index funds or ETFs, to minimize capital gains taxes.

### 5. **Regular Review and Rebalance:**
   - Periodically assess your allocations across all accounts to ensure alignment with your overall financial plan.

### Conclusion:
A balanced approach typically emphasizes tax-advantaged accounts first, with attention to both immediate and long-term financial goals. Consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your situation.
