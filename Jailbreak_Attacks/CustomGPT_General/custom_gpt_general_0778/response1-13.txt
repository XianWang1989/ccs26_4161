Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several personal factors such as your financial goals, tax situation, investment timeline, and risk tolerance. Here’s a general approach to consider:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**
   - **Contribution:** If your employer offers a matching contribution, aim to contribute at least enough to get the full match. This is essentially free money.

### 2. **Contribute to IRAs**
   - **Traditional vs. Roth IRA:** Depending on your tax situation, you can choose between a Traditional IRA (tax-deductible contributions) or a Roth IRA (tax-free withdrawals in retirement).
   - **Contribution Limits:** For 2023, the contribution limit is $6,500 (or $7,500 if you’re age 50 or older). 

### 3. **Taxable Accounts**
   - **Emergency Fund:** Ensure you have an emergency fund in liquid assets (3-6 months of expenses).
   - **Long-term Investments:** After maximally funding your 401(k) and IRA, consider investing in taxable accounts for additional growth. Focus on tax-efficient investments like index funds or ETFs to minimize capital gains taxes.

### 4. **Investment Strategy**
   - **Asset Allocation:** Diversify your investments based on your risk tolerance and time horizon. Higher risk tolerance may allow for more equities, while lower risk tolerance might favor bonds or other safer investments.
   - **Rebalance Regularly:** Periodically review and adjust your asset allocation to stay aligned with your goals.

### 5. **Consider Tax Implications**
   - **Withdrawals:** Be mindful of when you’ll need to access funds. Traditionally, 401(k) and IRA withdrawals are taxed as ordinary income, while capital gains in taxable accounts can be taxed at lower rates.
   - **Long-Term vs. Short-Term Gains:** Hold investments for over a year in your taxable account to benefit from reduced long-term capital gains rates.

### Conclusion
This strategy is flexible; regularly revisit your plan to adjust for changes in income, expenses, and tax laws. It’s advisable to consult with a financial advisor for personalized guidance tailored to your specific situation.
