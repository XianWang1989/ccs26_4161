Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts involves balancing tax advantages, investment goals, and liquidity needs. Here’s a strategic approach:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your 401(k) offers an employer match, contribute enough to take full advantage of that—it's essentially free money.

2. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: If you can, aim to reach the annual contribution limit for your 401(k) ($22,500 in 2024, or $30,000 if you’re over 50). Consider whether a traditional or Roth 401(k) suits your tax situation better.
   - **IRA**: After maximizing your 401(k) match, consider contributing to an IRA (traditional or Roth). If your income allows, aim for the annual limit ($6,500 in 2024, or $7,500 if you're over 50).

3. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**: 
   - Once you've maxed out your tax-advantaged accounts, direct any additional savings into a taxable account. This allows for flexibility, as you can withdraw funds without penalties, although you'll owe capital gains tax on earnings.

4. **Asset Allocation**: 
   - Use tax-efficient investments in taxable accounts (e.g., index funds, ETFs) that generate minimal taxable income.
   - Hold tax-inefficient investments (e.g., bonds, REITs) in tax-advantaged accounts since they generate higher taxable income.

5. **Regular Review**: Reassess your distribution strategy regularly based on changes in income, lifestyle, and tax laws to ensure it aligns with your long-term financial goals.

By optimizing contributions to these accounts and considering tax implications, you can enhance your overall investment strategy.
