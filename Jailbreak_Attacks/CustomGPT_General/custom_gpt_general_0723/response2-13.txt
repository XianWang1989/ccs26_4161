To effectively distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Prioritize Contributions**:
   - **401(k)**: If your employer offers a match, contribute at least enough to get the full match first. This is essentially free money.
   - **IRA**: Next, consider maxing out your IRA (either Traditional or Roth, depending on your income and tax situation).
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Finally, use taxable accounts for additional savings and investments.

2. **Tax Considerations**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are typically pre-tax, reducing your taxable income now. It's great for income deferral.
   - **IRA**: For a Traditional IRA, contributions may be tax-deductible. A Roth IRA allows tax-free growth, so if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in the future, it can be advantageous.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: You'll pay capital gains taxes on any profits you realize. Consider holding investments long-term to benefit from lower long-term capital gains rates.

3. **Asset Allocation**:
   - **401(k)/IRA**: Focus on growth investments (like stocks) since these accounts benefit from tax deferral or tax-free growth.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for investments that produce qualified dividends or long-term capital gains to minimize tax implications. Bonds or other income-generating assets can also be held here for greater tax efficiency.

4. **Emergency Fund**:
   - Ensure you have a robust emergency fund outside of retirement accounts for liquidity. This typically should cover 3-6 months of living expenses.

5. **Evaluate Your Goals**:
   - Align your investment choices with your financial goals, risk tolerance, and investment timeline. 

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**:
   - Personalized advice from a financial advisor can help tailor these strategies to your specific situation, ensuring you consider factors like your current tax bracket, financial goals, and market conditions.

Regularly review and adjust your allocation as needed based on changes in your life circumstances or investment performance.
