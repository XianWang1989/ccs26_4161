Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts effectively depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and time horizon. Here are key considerations for each type of account:

### 1. **401(k) Plans**
- **Employer Matches**: Maximize contributions to your 401(k) up to any employer match. This is essentially “free money.”
- **Contribution Limits**: For 2023, the limit is $22,500 (or $30,000 for those 50 and older). 
- **Tax Advantages**: Contributions are typically pre-tax, lowering your taxable income now, or you might choose a Roth option for tax-free withdrawals in retirement.

### 2. **IRA (Individual Retirement Account)**
- **Contribution Limits**: The limit for 2023 is $6,500 (or $7,500 for those 50 and older).
- **Tax Benefits**: Conventional IRAs may offer tax-deductible contributions, while Roth IRAs allow for tax-free withdrawals. Choose based on your current vs. expected future tax rate.
- **Flexibility**: If you're maxed out on your 401(k), contributing to an IRA is beneficial.

### 3. **Taxable Accounts**
- **Flexibility**: These accounts offer greater liquidity and can be tapped for expenses before retirement without penalties.
- **Investment Choices**: You can invest in a wider range of assets without the restrictions of retirement accounts.
- **Tax Considerations**: Be mindful of capital gains taxes; long-term gains are typically taxed at a lower rate than short-term gains. Consider holding investments for over a year if possible.

### **Overall Strategy**
1. **Emergency Fund**: Ensure you have an emergency fund before heavily investing.
2. **Prioritize Accounts**: Start with the 401(k) (up to the match), then fund the IRA, and lastly, allocate to taxable accounts.
3. **Invest Based on Tax Implications**: Consider placing less tax-efficient investments (like bonds) in tax-deferred accounts and more tax-efficient investments (like index funds or ETFs) in taxable accounts.
4. **Diversification**: Ensure a diversified investment strategy across all accounts.
5. **Review and Adjust**: Re-evaluate your allocation periodically based on changing financial circumstances or goals.

### Example Distribution
- **401(k)**: 25%-50% of your total investment amount.
- **IRA**: 10%-15% of your total.
- **Taxable**: 10%-30%, adjusted based on liquidity needs and investment strategy.

### Conclusion
Ultimately, consult with a financial advisor to tailor your strategy according to your unique financial situation, goals, and risk tolerance.
