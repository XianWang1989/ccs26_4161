To effectively distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies based on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment horizon:

1. **401(k) Contributions**:
   - If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to maximize that match first. It’s essentially free money.
   - Prioritize contributions to the 401(k) if you are in a higher tax bracket, as contributions are pre-tax and can help reduce your taxable income.

2. **IRA Contributions**:
   - Consider contributing to a Roth IRA if you qualify, as it allows for tax-free growth and tax-free withdrawals in retirement.
   - If you anticipate being in a higher tax bracket in retirement, focus on a traditional IRA, where contributions may be tax-deductible.

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - Use taxable accounts for investments that might generate capital gains and dividends, which can be taxed at a lower rate than ordinary income.
   - This is also a good place for more flexible investments that you may need to access before retirement age.

4. **Emergency Fund**:
   - Ensure you have a sufficient emergency fund in a savings or money market account before aggressively investing.

5. **Overall Allocation and Diversification**:
   - Diversify across asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) in all accounts based on your risk tolerance.
   - Use tax-efficient funds or ETFs in taxable accounts to minimize tax liabilities.

6. **Regular Review and Rebalance**:
   - Periodically review your allocations and rebalance as needed to stay aligned with your investment goals and risk tolerance.

7. **Consult a Financial Advisor**:
   - For personalized advice tailored to your individual financial situation, consulting a financial advisor can be beneficial.

By following these strategies, you can optimize your investment across your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts for potential growth while considering tax implications.
