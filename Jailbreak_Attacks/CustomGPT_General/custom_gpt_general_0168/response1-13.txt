Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts involves several strategic considerations. Here’s a structured approach you might consider:

### 1. **Understand Contribution Limits:**
   - **401(k):** Maximize contributions (e.g., $22,500 for 2023; $30,000 if age 50 or older).
   - **IRA:** Max contribution is $6,500 (or $7,500 if over 50).

### 2. **Evaluate Tax Advantages:**
   - **401(k):** Pre-tax contributions lower your taxable income; employer matching is beneficial.
   - **Traditional IRA:** Similar tax benefits; consider if you qualify for tax deductions.
   - **Roth IRA:** Contributions are after-tax, but withdrawals are tax-free if conditions are met.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Gains are taxed; ideal for investments you might sell within a year.

### 3. **Asset Allocation Strategy:**
   - **Aggressive Growth:** Stocks in taxable accounts for capital gains, bonds in tax-advantaged accounts to minimize tax impact.
   - **Conservative Growth:** Debt instruments in tax-advantaged, equities in taxable.

### 4. **Withdrawal Strategy:**
   - Prioritize tax-advantaged accounts for retirement needs. Consider taxable accounts for short-term goals.

### 5. **Adjust as Needed:**
   - Rebalance based on performance and changing financial goals.

### Conclusion:
Prioritize employer-sponsored plans for contributions, maximize tax-advantaged accounts first, and consider your investment timeline and tax implications for taxable accounts. Consulting with a financial advisor can tailor this strategy to your specific situation.
