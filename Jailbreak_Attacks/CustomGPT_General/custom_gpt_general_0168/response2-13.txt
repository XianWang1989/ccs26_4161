Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, risk tolerance, and tax situation. Here’s a structured approach:

1. **Prioritize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a match on your 401(k), contribute enough to take full advantage of that—it’s essentially free money.

2. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: Consider maxing out if you're behind on contributions and the match is substantial.
   - **IRA**: Depending on your eligibility, maximize contributions to a traditional or Roth IRA for tax benefits.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for funds you may need access to before retirement. They offer flexibility, but be mindful of capital gains taxes.

4. **Diversification**: Ensure your investments across these accounts are diversified. Consider asset allocation based on your risk tolerance and investment horizon.

5. **Rebalance Regularly**: Monitor your asset allocation and rebalance as needed to align with your financial goals.

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: A personalized strategy can often yield better results, especially when considering tax implications and changes in financial goals over time.

Ultimately, your specific situation will dictate the best approach, so consider your current financial standing and future needs when making decisions.
