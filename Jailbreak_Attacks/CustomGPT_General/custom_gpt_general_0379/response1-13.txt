To distribute your money effectively among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider these steps:

1. **Emergency Fund**: Make sure you have a sufficient emergency fund (3-6 months' worth of expenses) before investing.

2. **Maximize Employer Match**: Prioritize contributing enough to your 401(k) to take full advantage of any employer match, as this is free money.

3. **IRA Contributions**: Next, consider maxing out your IRA (Traditional or Roth). IRAs often have tax advantages that can benefit your long-term growth.

4. **Taxable Accounts**: If you’ve optimized contributions to tax-advantaged accounts, you can allocate additional funds to taxable accounts. Consider using these for investments in ETFs or individual stocks, keeping in mind tax implications.

5. **Investment Strategy**: Tailor your investment strategy based on your risk tolerance, investment horizon, and retirement goals. High-growth investments might be better suited for tax-advantaged accounts, while more stable returns can be allocated to taxable accounts.

6. **Regular Reassessment**: Periodically reassess your allocations based on changes in income, financial goals, or market conditions.

Consulting with a financial advisor can provide personalized guidance tailored to your specific situation.
