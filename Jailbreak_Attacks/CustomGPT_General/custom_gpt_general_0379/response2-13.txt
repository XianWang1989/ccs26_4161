When deciding how to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**: Prioritize contributions to your 401(k) and IRA, as they provide tax benefits. Aim to contribute enough to your 401(k) to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money.

2. **Understand Contribution Limits**: For 2023, the maximum contribution to a 401(k) is $22,500 (or $30,000 if over 50). For IRAs, it's $6,500 (or $7,500 if over 50). Make sure to consider these limits when allocating funds.

3. **Consider Tax Implications**: Withdrawals from a 401(k) and traditional IRA (after retirement) are taxed as ordinary income. In contrast, contributions to Roth IRAs are taxed upfront, but qualified withdrawals are tax-free. Use taxable accounts for funds you may need in the short term, where capital gains taxes apply.

4. **Plan for Liquidity**: Keep some money in taxable accounts for short-term needs or emergencies, as these are more accessible without penalties.

5. **Diversify Investments**: Regardless of the account type, ensure your investment portfolio is diversified to spread risk.

6. **Review Your Goals**: Your distribution should align with your financial goals, risk tolerance, and investment time horizon. 

7. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: A professional can provide personalized advice tailored to your unique financial situation.

By evaluating these factors, you can create a balanced and effective distribution plan for your contributions.
