Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here's a general guide:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match if available. This is essentially free money.

2. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get the match, then consider maxing this out first for tax deferral.
   - **IRA**: If you're eligible, contribute to a traditional or Roth IRA next, as these accounts also offer tax advantages (deductions for traditional IRAs, tax-free growth for Roth).

3. **Consider Tax Implications**:
   - **Roth vs. Traditional**: If you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement, a Roth IRA might be beneficial. If not, a traditional IRA might make more sense.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for investments that may generate capital gains or dividends, which are taxed at potentially lower rates than ordinary income.

4. **Emergency Fund**: Ensure you have an adequate emergency fund outside of these accounts to cover unexpected expenses.

5. **Diversification**: Within each account, diversify your investments to balance risk and returns. Consider a mix of stocks, bonds, and other assets based on your risk tolerance.

6. **Regular Reassessment**: Review your asset allocation and contributions periodically, adjusting as needed based on your changing goals, performance, and tax laws.

Consult with a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your specific situation.
