To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

### 1. **Maximize Contributions to Tax-Advantaged Accounts**
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to take full advantage of any employer match (if available). This is essentially free money.
   - **IRA**: Contribute the maximum allowed, especially if you're eligible for a Roth IRA, as withdrawals in retirement can be tax-free.

### 2. **Prioritize Account Types**
   - **Tax-Deferred Accounts (401(k) and Traditional IRA)**: Focus on holding investments that generate high income or short-term gains (like bonds or high-yield dividend stocks) in these accounts. This defers taxes until retirement.
   - **Tax-Free Accounts (Roth IRA)**: Good for investments with high growth potential (like stocks) since you won't pay taxes on qualified withdrawals.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Use for investments that generate capital gains or dividends that are taxed at a lower rate than ordinary income (like long-term stock holdings). This can help manage your tax burden.

### 3. **Consider Asset Allocation**
   - Diversify across asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) in each account while being mindful of your overall portfolio's risk based on your age, goals, and risk tolerance.

### 4. **Rebalancing**
   - Regularly assess and rebalance your portfolio to maintain your desired asset allocation and adjust for changes in your financial situation or market conditions.

### 5. **Seek Professional Advice**
   - Consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized guidance based on your specific financial situation and retirement goals.

By implementing these strategies, you can optimize your investments across your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts for both growth and tax efficiency.
