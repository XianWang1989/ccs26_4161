Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here’s a structured approach:

### 1. **Understand Each Account Type:**
   - **401(k)**: Employer-sponsored retirement plan with potential company matching. Growth is tax-deferred until withdrawal.
   - **IRA (Traditional/Roth)**: Individual retirement accounts offering tax advantages. Contributions can be tax-deductible (Traditional) or made post-tax for tax-free growth (Roth).
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Non-retirement accounts with no tax advantages. Capital gains and interest are taxed annually.

### 2. **Prioritize Contributions Based on Matches:**
   - Maximize contributions to your 401(k) to secure any employer matching funds, as this is "free money."

### 3. **Consider Tax Implications:**
   - **Short-Term Needs**: Invest in taxable accounts for liquidity.
   - **Long-Term Growth**: Utilize tax-deferred accounts (401(k) and IRAs) to maximize growth without immediate tax burdens.

### 4. **Age and Risk Tolerance:**
   - Younger investors might prefer higher-risk, growth-oriented investments in tax-advantaged accounts.
   - As retirement nears, consider shifting to safer, income-generating investments in taxable accounts.

### 5. **Rebalance Regularly:**
   - Review and adjust allocations annually based on performance and changing goals.

### 6. **Investment Strategy:**
   - Diversify investments across all accounts to mitigate risk.
   - Consider placing high-growth assets in tax-advantaged accounts to maximize tax efficiency.

### Example Allocation:
- **401(k)**: 15-20% of your salary (maximize employer match)
- **IRA**: Up to the annual contribution limit ($6,500 or $7,500 if age 50+)
- **Taxable Account**: Remainder of savings, adjusted based on liquidity needs

### Final Thoughts:
Consulting with a financial advisor can provide personalized strategies based on your specific situation. 

This balanced approach ensures you are taking advantage of the benefits each account provides while considering your unique circumstances.
