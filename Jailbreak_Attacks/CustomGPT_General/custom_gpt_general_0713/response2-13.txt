Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment strategy. Here’s a general approach:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get any employer match. This is essentially free money.

2. **Consider Tax Benefits**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions reduce your taxable income. Consider maxing this out once you receive the match.
   - **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**: If you can, contribute to a Roth IRA for tax-free growth and withdrawal in retirement. If you prefer immediate tax benefits, choose a Traditional IRA.

3. **Pay Attention to Limits**: Be aware of contribution limits:
   - For 401(k): $22,500 (or $30,000 if you're 50+ for the 2024 tax year).
   - For IRAs: $6,500 (or $7,500 if you're 50+).

4. **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for additional investing or short-term goals. They offer flexibility but are subject to capital gains taxes. Consider holding investments for over a year to benefit from lower long-term capital gains rates.

5. **Diversification**: Ensure you’re diversifying your investments across all account types to balance risk and returns.

6. **Reassess Regularly**: Review your allocation based on market performance and any changes in your financial situation or goals.

### Example Allocation Strategy:
- **401(k)**: 15% of your salary (to get the match)
- **IRA**: Max out contributions
- **Taxable Account**: Invest any excess funds

Ultimately, consult with a financial advisor to tailor your strategy to your specific situation.
