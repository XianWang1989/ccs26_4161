Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and time horizon. Here’s a general strategy:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Contributions:**
   - **401(k):** Contribute enough to get the full employer match if available, as this is essentially free money.

### 2. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts:**
   - **IRA (Traditional or Roth):** After maxing out any employer match, contribute to an IRA. Choose a Roth IRA if you anticipate being in a higher tax bracket in retirement; otherwise, a Traditional IRA may offer immediate tax benefits.

### 3. **Consider Contribution Limits:**
   - Keep in mind the annual contribution limits for each account type to maximize tax advantages.

### 4. **Evaluate Investment Options:**
   - Assess the investment choices available in your 401(k) and respective IRAs. Choose those with low fees and good growth potential.

### 5. **Use Taxable Accounts for Flexibility:**
   - If you have maxed out tax-advantaged accounts, consider investing in taxable accounts. This provides more liquidity and flexibility in accessing funds without penalties.

### 6. **Tax-Efficient Investing:**
   - Place tax-inefficient investments (like bonds or REITs) in tax-advantaged accounts, while holding more tax-efficient investments (like index funds) in taxable accounts to minimize tax liabilities.

### 7. **Rebalance Regularly:**
   - Review and adjust your allocations periodically based on performance, changes in financial goals, or shifts in your investment strategy.

### Conclusion:
A common approach could be to focus on maxing out contributions to your 401(k) first, followed by funding an IRA, and then using taxable accounts for additional investments. Tailor this strategy based on your risk tolerance, investment knowledge, and specific financial situation. Consider consulting with a financial advisor for personalized guidance.
