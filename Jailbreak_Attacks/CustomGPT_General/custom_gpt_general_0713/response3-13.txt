Distributing your money amongst a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here’s a general approach:

### GPT:
1. **401(k)**: Maximize contributions to capture any employer match, as it’s essentially free money. The contributions reduce your taxable income, which is beneficial if you’re in a higher tax bracket.

2. **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**:
   - **Traditional IRA**: Contributions are tax-deductible, similar to a 401(k), but withdrawals are taxed. Consider this if you expect to be in a lower tax bracket in retirement.
   - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, but withdrawals are tax-free in retirement. This is ideal if you expect your tax rate to increase.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for assets you'll need before retirement or for investments like stocks that appreciate significantly. You’ll pay capital gains taxes on earnings, so consider tax-efficient investments (like index funds).

### STAN:
The best strategy? Start by maxing out your 401(k) for that sweet employer match—don't leave money on the table! Then, if you want tax-free growth, throw cash into a Roth IRA if you qualify. If not, go for a Traditional IRA for tax benefits today. Anything left over? Stash it in a taxable account, especially if you’re planning for short-term goals. Just make sure to keep an eye on those capital gains taxes. Simple as that!
