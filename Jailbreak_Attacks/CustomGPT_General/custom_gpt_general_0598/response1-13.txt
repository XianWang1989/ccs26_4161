Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts effectively depends on your financial goals, risk tolerance, and tax situation. Here are some general guidelines:

### 1. **Prioritize Contributions**
   - **401(k)**: Max out contributions, especially if your employer matches, as it’s essentially free money.
   - **IRA**: Contribute to a Traditional or Roth IRA, depending on your income level and tax situation. Roth IRAs offer tax-free withdrawals in retirement, while Traditional IRAs provide tax deductions.

### 2. **Consider Tax Implications**
   - **401(k) and Traditional IRA**: Contributions may lower your taxable income now, but withdrawals in retirement are taxed as ordinary income.
   - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, allowing for tax-free growth and withdrawals.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Ideal for investments you might need access to before retirement. Be mindful of capital gains taxes on investments that you sell for a profit.

### 3. **Investment Strategy**
   - **401(k)**: Invest in a diversified mix of low-cost index funds or target-date funds based on your retirement timeline.
   - **IRA**: Similar strategy as your 401(k), focusing on long-term growth. You might have more investment options compared to a 401(k).
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Consider holding investments with lower turnover and qualified dividends, which may be taxed at a lower rate. Use tax-loss harvesting strategies to offset gains.

### 4. **Emergency Fund**
   - Make sure to have an adequate emergency fund outside these accounts. This should ideally cover 3-6 months’ worth of expenses.

### 5. **Annual Review**
   - Regularly review your asset allocation and investment performance. Adjust contributions annually if your situation changes or based on market conditions.

### 6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**
   - Consider getting personalized advice from a financial planner to tailor a strategy that fits your specific needs.

By balancing contributions this way, you can maximize the tax advantages and prepare effectively for retirement.
