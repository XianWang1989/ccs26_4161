Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here are some general guidelines:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to get the full match; it’s essentially free money. Aim to contribute the annual maximum if you can.
   - **IRA**: Consider maximizing your IRA contributions as well. Depending on your income, choose between a Traditional IRA (tax-deductible contributions) and a Roth IRA (tax-free withdrawals in retirement).

2. **Assess Tax Implications**:
   - **Taxable Accounts**: These are best for short- to medium-term goals or if you’ve maxed out tax-advantaged accounts. Capital gains and dividends can be taxable, so consider tax-efficient investments.

3. **Diversification**:
   - Ensure that your investments across all accounts are diversified. This mitigates risk and can help with returns.

4. **Rebalance Regularly**:
   - Periodically review and adjust your asset allocation to maintain your desired risk level and investment goals.

5. **Consider Withdrawals**:
   - Plan for the types of accounts you'll withdraw from first in retirement. Generally, it's favorable to withdraw from taxable accounts first, then tax-deferred accounts (like 401(k) or Traditional IRA), after that, tax-free accounts (like Roth IRAs).

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**:
   - Personal circumstances can vastly change the best approach. An advisor can help tailor a strategy to your specific needs.

By strategically allocating your investments across these accounts, you can maximize growth potential and minimize taxes.
