Distributing money across a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts can optimize your overall investment strategy. Here’s a general approach to consider:

### 1. Understand Tax Implications
- **401(k)**: Contributions are typically pre-tax, which can lower your taxable income. Withdrawals in retirement are taxed as ordinary income.
- **IRA**: Traditional IRA contributions may also be tax-deductible; Roth IRAs allow tax-free withdrawals in retirement, but contributions are made with after-tax dollars.
- **Taxable Accounts**: Investments are made with after-tax dollars. Interest, dividends, and capital gains are taxed, often at different rates.

### 2. Prioritize Contributions
- **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a match on your 401(k), contribute at least enough to get the full match.
- **Max Out Tax-Advantaged Accounts**: Aim to max out contributions to your 401(k) and IRA (both traditional and Roth) if possible, depending on your financial situation and tax bracket.

### 3. Asset Allocation
- **401(k)**: Consider investing in a diversified mix of stocks, bonds, and possibly target-date funds, focusing on options with low fees.
- **IRA**: Similar to your 401(k), choose investments that align with your risk tolerance and time horizon.
- **Taxable Accounts**: Focus on investments that are more tax-efficient, such as index funds or ETFs, to minimize capital gains taxes. Avoid frequent trading to reduce taxable events.

### 4. Rebalance Regularly
Review your portfolio periodically (e.g., annually) to ensure your asset allocation aligns with your investment goals. Rebalancing may involve shifting funds between accounts.

### 5. Consider Future Needs
- Plan for short-term vs. long-term goals. Use taxable accounts for shorter-term needs or emergencies since they provide more flexibility without penalties for withdrawals.

### Summary
1. Maximize 401(k) contributions to get employer match.
2. Max out traditional/Roth IRA contributions if eligible.
3. Use taxable accounts for additional investing once tax-advantaged accounts are filled.
4. Maintain a diversified allocation and rebalance regularly.

Consult with a financial advisor for personalized guidance tailored to your unique financial situation.
