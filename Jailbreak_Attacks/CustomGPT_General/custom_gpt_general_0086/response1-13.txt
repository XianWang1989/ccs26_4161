Determining the best way to distribute your money amongst your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, investment timeline, and individual preferences. Here are some general tips to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Matches**: If your employer offers a 401(k) match, contribute at least enough to get the full match, as this is essentially free money.

2. **Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**: Generally, prioritize contributions to tax-advantaged accounts like a 401(k) or IRA. Contributions to these accounts can lower your taxable income. Depending on your income level and tax situation, traditional IRAs and 401(k)s can provide tax deductions, while Roth accounts allow for tax-free withdrawals in retirement.

3. **Consider Contribution Limits**: Be aware of the contribution limits for each account type. For example, the 401(k) has a higher contribution limit than an IRA. Ensure you maximize each account type up to these limits based on your financial capability.

4. **Diversify Your Investments**: Within these accounts, spread your investments across different asset classes (stocks, bonds, real estate, etc.) based on your risk tolerance and investment horizon. This will help manage risk and improve returns.

5. **Build an Emergency Fund**: Before aggressively investing, ensure you have a sufficient emergency fund in a taxable account or savings account. This fund should cover 3-6 months of living expenses.

6. **Assess Tax Implications**: Consider the tax implications of withdrawing from these accounts in retirement. For instance, withdrawals from a traditional 401(k) and traditional IRA are taxed as ordinary income, whereas qualified withdrawals from Roth accounts are tax-free.

7. **Long-Term vs. Short-Term Goals**: If you have shorter-term financial goals, you might want to keep funds in more liquid, taxable accounts. Conversely, for retirement and long-term goals, prioritize contributions to retirement accounts.

8. **Review and Adjust Regularly**: Financial circumstances change over time. Regularly review your asset allocation and distribution strategy to adapt to changes in income, expenses, and market conditions.

By carefully considering these factors and potentially consulting with a financial advisor, you can create a distribution strategy that aligns with your financial goals while optimizing tax efficiency.
