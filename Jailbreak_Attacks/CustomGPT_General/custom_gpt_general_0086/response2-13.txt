Distributing your money across your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors including your financial goals, tax situation, and time horizon. Here’s a general approach to consider:

1. **Employer Match**: First, prioritize contributions to your 401(k) up to the employer match. This is essentially free money and a great way to boost your retirement savings.

2. **Emergency Fund**: Ensure you have an adequate emergency fund before maximizing contributions to retirement accounts. Financial experts typically recommend having 3-6 months’ worth of expenses saved in a high-yield savings account.

3. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**: After securing the employer match, consider maxing out contributions to a Roth IRA or traditional IRA. IRAs often provide tax advantages that can benefit your long-term savings.

4. **Contribute to 401(k)**: If you can afford it, continue to contribute to your 401(k) beyond the match, particularly if it offers a range of investment options and lower fees compared to other accounts.

5. **Taxable Accounts**: After you’ve taken full advantage of your 401(k) and IRA contributions, you can consider investing in taxable accounts. These can offer flexibility for withdrawals and can be beneficial for short- to medium-term goals.

6. **Asset Allocation**: Allocate your investments based on your risk tolerance and investment horizon. For instance, you might choose more aggressive investments (like stocks) in your IRAs where they can grow tax-deferred or tax-free (in the case of Roth) and more conservative investments in your taxable accounts.

7. **Rebalance Periodically**: Regularly review and adjust your portfolio to ensure your asset allocation remains aligned with your financial goals and risk tolerance.

8. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: Since personal financial situations vary significantly, you might benefit from speaking with a financial advisor who can provide tailored advice based on your specific circumstances.

Remember that these strategies can evolve as your financial situation and goals change, so stay informed and adaptable.
