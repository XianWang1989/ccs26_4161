The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment horizon. Here are some general guidelines to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a match on your 401(k) contributions, try to contribute enough to take full advantage of that match. It’s essentially “free money.”

2. **Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**: After taking full advantage of your employer match, consider maxing out your IRA contributions, especially if it’s a Roth IRA, which allows for tax-free withdrawals in retirement.

3. **Debt Management**: If you have high-interest debt (like credit cards), you might want to pay that down before investing too heavily in retirement accounts.

4. **Diversification of Accounts**: Keep in mind the tax implications of each account type. Generally, it's beneficial to hold investments with the highest expected growth in tax-advantaged accounts (like stocks or mutual funds) and more stable, lower-growth investments (like bonds) in taxable accounts.

5. **Rebalance Periodically**: Make sure to periodically review and adjust your allocation to align with your risk tolerance and investment goals.

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: If you're unsure, it may be worth consulting with a financial advisor who can provide personalized advice based on your unique situation.

By following these steps, you can create a balanced approach to distributing your money across your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts.
