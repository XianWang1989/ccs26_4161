To optimize your investment strategy among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following guidelines:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**:
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match, as it’s essentially free money. Consider maxing out if your budget allows.
   - **IRA**: After your 401(k), contribute to a traditional or Roth IRA, depending on your tax situation.

2. **Asset Location Strategy**:
   - Place high-growth and high-volatility assets (like stocks) in tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) and IRA) to benefit from tax-deferral or tax-free growth.
   - Keep stable income-generating assets (like bonds) in taxable accounts to use your tax bracket advantageously.

3. **Tax Considerations**:
   - In taxable accounts, be mindful of capital gains taxes. Favor long-term investments to benefit from lower capital gains tax rates.
   - Use tax-loss harvesting to offset gains in taxable accounts when possible.

4. **Diversification**:
   - Ensure your overall investment portfolio is diversified in terms of asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) and sectors regardless of the account type.

5. **Reassessment and Rebalancing**:
   - Periodically review your asset allocation and make adjustments based on your risk tolerance, financial goals, and changing market conditions.

6. **Withdrawal Strategy**:
   - As you approach retirement, plan your withdrawal strategy to minimize tax implications and ensure a sustainable income.

7. **Consult a Financial Advisor**:
   - For personalized advice tailored to your specific financial situation, consulting with a financial advisor can provide valuable insights.

This approach will help you maximize returns while minimizing tax liabilities over time.
