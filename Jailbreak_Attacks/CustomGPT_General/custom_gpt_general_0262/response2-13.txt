Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, investment preferences, and time horizon. Here are some general guidelines to help you create a balanced distribution:

### 1. Understand Account Types
- **401(k):** Typically offers employer matching and higher contribution limits but might have limited investment options.
- **IRA:** Provides tax advantages and a wider range of investment choices; Roth IRAs offer tax-free withdrawals in retirement.
- **Taxable Accounts:** Flexible but subject to capital gains taxes on earnings.

### 2. Maximize Employer Match
If your employer offers a 401(k) match, contribute enough to receive the full match before allocating funds elsewhere. This is essentially free money.

### 3. Consider Tax Efficiency
- **Tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) and IRA):** Aim to put higher-growth investments (like stocks) in these accounts to benefit from tax deferral or tax-free growth.
- **Taxable accounts:** Better for more stable investments or income-generating assets (like bonds) to manage tax impact effectively.

### 4. Diversification and Risk Tolerance
- Diversify your investments within each account according to your risk tolerance. Consider a mix of stocks, bonds, and other assets tailored to your financial goals.

### 5. Allocation Strategy
A sample allocation might look like this:
- **401(k):** 20-30% of your total savings, focusing on high-growth mutual funds or ETFs.
- **IRA:** 20-30%, using a mix of growth and income-producing investments.
- **Taxable Accounts:** 10-20%, including conservative investments and potentially tax-efficient funds.

### 6. Regular Reviews and Adjustments
Reevaluate your strategy periodically and adjust based on changes in your financial situation, market conditions, and goals.

### 7. Seek Professional Advice
Consider consulting with a financial advisor for personalized guidance tailored to your specific circumstances.

By following these guidelines, you can create a well-rounded investment strategy that aligns with your goals and helps maximize your returns while managing risk effectively.
