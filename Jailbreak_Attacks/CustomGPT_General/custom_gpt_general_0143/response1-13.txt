Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and timeline. Here’s a structured approach:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get any employer match first. This is essentially free money.

2. **Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: Focus on this to reduce taxable income if you have a high tax rate.
   - **IRA**: Consider a Roth IRA if you expect your tax rate to be higher in retirement; contributions are made with after-tax dollars, but withdrawals are tax-free.

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - Once you maximize contributions to retirement accounts, use taxable accounts for flexibility.
   - Choose tax-efficient investments (like index funds or ETFs) to minimize capital gains taxes.

4. **Diversification**: Ensure your investments are diversified across asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.).

5. **Goals and Timelines**: Adjust allocations based on when you plan to access funds (short-term vs. long-term).

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: Personal circumstances can impact the best strategy for you, so professional advice tailored to your situation can be beneficial.

This balanced approach helps optimize growth while managing tax implications and retirement readiness.
