Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here’s a general approach to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: First, contribute enough to your 401(k) to get any employer match. This is essentially free money.

2. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: Contribute the maximum you can, especially if you have a match.
   - **IRA**: Consider a Roth IRA if you qualify; it offers tax-free growth and withdrawals.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: After maximizing contributions to retirement accounts, use taxable accounts for additional savings and investments. These accounts offer flexibility for withdrawals.

4. **Asset Allocation**: Diversify your investments based on your risk tolerance across all accounts:
   - **Higher Growth Investments**: Keep these in tax-advantaged accounts where they can grow without immediate tax implications.
   - **Income-Producing Investments**: Consider placing these in taxable accounts to take advantage of lower capital gains rates.

5. **Regular Rebalancing**: Reassess your allocation periodically to ensure it aligns with your goals and risk tolerance.

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: Personal circumstances can vary greatly, so consider professional advice tailored to your situation.

By following these steps, you can efficiently grow your investments while minimizing your tax burden.
