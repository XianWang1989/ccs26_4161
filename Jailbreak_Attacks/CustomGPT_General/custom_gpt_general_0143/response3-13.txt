Balancing your investments across a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various factors, including your age, tax bracket, and long-term goals. Here’s a breakdown of an effective strategy:

### 1. **401(k)**
   - **Maximize Contributions**: Aim to contribute enough to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money.
   - **Investment Choices**: Look for low-fee index funds or target-date funds.

### 2. **IRA**
   - **Choose Traditional vs. Roth**: If you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement, a Roth IRA might be better. If you seek immediate tax breaks, consider a Traditional IRA.
   - **Diversification**: Invest in a mix of assets similar to your 401(k), but consider adding options not available in your 401(k) (like certain ETFs or mutual funds).

### 3. **Taxable Accounts**
   - **Tax-Efficient Investments**: Focus on index funds and ETFs to minimize capital gains taxes. Municipal bonds can also be beneficial in a taxable account.
   - **Withdrawal Strategy**: Use taxable accounts for funding short-term goals, allowing tax-advantaged accounts to grow.

### Suggested Allocation
- If you’re younger (under 40): Prioritize maxing out your 401(k) and then your IRA.
- If you’re closer to retirement, focus on a mix that considers your immediate cash needs alongside long-term growth.

### Review Regularly
Reassess your portfolio regularly and adjust your contributions based on changes in income, expenses, and market performance.

### Additional Tips
- **Diversification**: Spread investments across different asset classes to reduce risk.
- **Consult a Financial Advisor**: For personalized advice based on your situation.

This balanced approach helps optimize tax efficiency and aligns investments with your retirement goals.
