Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors including your retirement goals, tax situation, and time horizon. Here are some guidelines to consider:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**:
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money. Aim for the maximum contribution limit if possible.
   - **IRA**: After the 401(k), consider contributing to a Traditional or Roth IRA. A Roth IRA offers tax-free withdrawals in retirement if certain conditions are met.

2. **Evaluate Tax Implications**:
   - **401(k) and Traditional IRA**: Contributions are pre-tax, reducing your taxable income now, but withdrawals in retirement are taxed as ordinary income.
   - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are post-tax, but qualified withdrawals are tax-free in retirement. Consider this if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket later.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: These are best for short- to mid-term goals, as they offer flexibility without withdrawal penalties. Keep in mind capital gains taxes on investments that you sell.

3. **Diversify Investments**:
   - Ensure a diversified investment strategy across all accounts. This includes a mix of stocks, bonds, and other assets tailored to your risk tolerance and investment timeline.

4. **Consider Your Time Horizon**:
   - The longer your time until retirement, the more risk you might be willing to take in your investment strategy, potentially leaning more towards stocks in your 401(k) and IRAs.

5. **Rebalance Regularly**:
   - Periodically assess and rebalance your allocation across all accounts based on performance and changing financial goals.

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**:
   - It's advisable to get personalized guidance based on your specific financial situation, goals, and tax implications.

By following these strategies, you can create a balanced and effective distribution of your funds across your retirement and taxable accounts.
