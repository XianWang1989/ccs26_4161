Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment horizon. Here are some general guidelines:

### 1. **401(k) Accounts**
   - **Employer Match**: Contribute enough to get any employer match first, as this is essentially "free money."
   - **Tax Benefits**: Traditional 401(k) contributions are made pre-tax, reducing your taxable income. Consider maxing out if you’re in a higher tax bracket now.

### 2. **IRA Accounts**
   - **Roth vs. Traditional**: If you expect to be in a higher tax bracket during retirement, a Roth IRA (after-tax contributions) may be advantageous. If you need tax deductions now, a Traditional IRA may be better.
   - **Limits**: Be aware of contribution limits and phase-out thresholds based on income.

### 3. **Taxable Accounts**
   - **Flexibility**: These accounts offer more flexibility for withdrawals. They are ideal for mid-term goals (buying a house, etc.).
   - **Capital Gains Tax Management**: Be mindful of tax implications on dividends and capital gains. Holding investments longer than a year can reduce taxes.

### Recommended Approach:
1. **Maximize 401(k) Contributions** up to the employer's match.
2. **Contribute to an IRA**, favoring a Roth IRA if eligible and you expect to be in a higher tax bracket later.
3. **Any remaining funds** can go into taxable accounts for added flexibility.

### Additional Considerations:
- **Emergency Fund**: Ensure you have savings for emergencies before fully committing.
- **Investment Strategy**: Diversify your investments among different asset classes according to your risk tolerance.
- **Review Regularly**: Reassess your distribution strategy annually or as your financial situation changes.

Consulting a financial advisor can provide personalized insights based on your circumstances.
