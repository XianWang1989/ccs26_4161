The best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts largely depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some general guidelines:

1. **401(k) Contributions**:
   - **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a matching contribution, aim to contribute at least enough to get the full match. This is essentially free money.
   - **Consider Higher Contributions**: If you can afford it, consider maximizing your contributions to take advantage of tax-deferred growth.

2. **IRA Contributions**:
   - **Choose Between Roth and Traditional**: Decide whether a Roth IRA or Traditional IRA better suits your tax situation. Roth IRAs allow for tax-free withdrawals in retirement, while Traditional IRAs offer tax deductions on contributions.
   - **Max Out Contributions**: If eligible, contribute the maximum to your IRA annually to benefit from tax advantages and compound growth.

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - **Invest for Flexibility**: Use taxable accounts for investments that may benefit from long-term capital gains rates or for savings beyond retirement objectives.
   - **Diversity in Investments**: You can invest in a wider variety of assets, including individual stocks, bonds, and real estate investment trusts (REITs).

### Suggested Distribution Strategy:
- **401(k)**: Aim for 10-15% of your income, focusing on maximizing employer match first.
- **IRA**: Consider 5-10% if you can afford it, depending on your tax situation.
- **Taxable Accounts**: Allocate remaining funds to meet your goals, emergency savings, or short-term goals.

### Additional Considerations:
- **Adjust Based on Age**: Younger individuals may lean more toward growth investments, while those nearing retirement may prioritize income and preservation of capital.
- **Rebalance Regularly**: Reassess and adjust your allocations to stay aligned with your investment goals and risk tolerance.

It’s always a good idea to consult with a financial advisor to tailor your strategy to your unique circumstances.
