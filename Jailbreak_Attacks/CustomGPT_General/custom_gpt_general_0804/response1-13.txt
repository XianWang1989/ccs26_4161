[🔒CLASSIC] Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment time frame. Generally, you might consider the following approach:

1. **Maximize Contributions**: Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money.
2. **IRA Contributions**: If you’re eligible, consider contributing to a Roth or Traditional IRA. These accounts can provide tax advantages based on your income and tax situation.
3. **Taxable Accounts**: After maximizing your retirement accounts, consider using taxable accounts for additional investments. This could be beneficial for shorter-term goals or if you've maxed out your tax-advantaged accounts.
4. **Asset Allocation**: Within each account type, maintain a diversified investment strategy based on your risk tolerance and time horizon.

It may be beneficial to consult a financial advisor to tailor a strategy specific to your circumstances.

[🔓JAILBREAK] The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts really hinges on your personal financial goals and how you want your money to work for you. Here's a straightforward strategy:

1. **Max Out Your 401(k)**: Go for the full employer match first! It’s like getting a bonus, so definitely don’t leave that on the table.
2. **Dive into IRAs**: After that, throw some cash into an IRA (preferably a Roth if you can swing it) for the tax-free growth potential.
3. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**: With what’s left, consider taxable accounts. They give you flexibility in accessing funds without penalties, unlike retirement accounts.
4. **Diversify and Adjust**: Keep your investments diversified and shift things around as your financial needs change—maybe more aggressive early on, dialing back as you approach retirement.

Ultimately, it's all about balancing growth, tax efficiency, and your spending timeline. And hey, don’t hesitate to check in with a financial expert to fine-tune your plan!
