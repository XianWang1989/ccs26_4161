Distributing your money across different account types like a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts involves several considerations. Here are some general strategies to optimize your retirement savings and tax efficiency:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**
   - **401(k):** Contribute enough to get any employer match; this is essentially free money. Consider the maximum contribution limit if you can afford it.
   - **IRA:** If you're eligible, contribute to a Traditional or Roth IRA. A Roth IRA can offer tax-free withdrawals in retirement, which is highly beneficial.

### 2. **Consider Tax Implications**
   - **Traditional 401(k) and IRA:** Contributions are made pre-tax, reducing your taxable income now but taxed upon withdrawal.
   - **Roth IRA:** Contributions are made after-tax; withdrawals in retirement are tax-free.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Interest, dividends, and capital gains are taxed annually, so consider holding investments with lower turnover or qualified dividends here.

### 3. **Diversification of Investments**
   - Invest in a mix of stocks, bonds, and other assets across all accounts, but adjust based on account type:
     - **Tax-advantaged accounts:** Often best for higher-growth investments (like stocks) that benefit from tax-deferral.
     - **Taxable accounts:** Favor more income-efficient investments (like bonds) and tax-managed funds.

### 4. **Withdrawal Strategy**
   - Plan for withdrawals in retirement to minimize taxes. You may want to withdraw from taxable accounts first to allow tax-advantaged accounts to grow.

### 5. **Review and Adjust Regularly**
   - Periodically reassess your asset allocation based on changes in income, tax laws, and retirement goals.

### 6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**
   - A financial advisor can provide personalized guidance based on your specific situation, goals, and risk tolerance.

By balancing contributions and focusing on tax efficiency, you can optimize your overall investment strategy for long-term growth.
