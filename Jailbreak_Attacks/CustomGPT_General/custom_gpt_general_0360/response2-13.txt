Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax considerations, and investment horizons. Here’s a general approach to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to receive any employer match. This is often considered "free money."

2. **Contribute to an IRA**: If you’re eligible, contribute to a traditional or Roth IRA next. A Roth IRA allows for tax-free withdrawals in retirement, while traditional IRAs provide tax-deductible contributions.

3. **Debt Reduction and Emergency Fund**: Ensure you have a solid emergency fund (3-6 months of expenses) and consider paying down high-interest debt. 

4. **Taxable Accounts**: Use taxable accounts for investments that may be subject to lower capital gains taxes (like long-term investments or municipal bonds).

5. **Asset Allocation**: 
   - In tax-deferred accounts (401(k) and IRA), consider holding higher-growth investments (stocks) to benefit from tax compounding.
   - In taxable accounts, favor tax-efficient investments (bonds or index funds) to minimize tax liability.

6. **Rebalancing**: Regularly review and rebalance your portfolio to maintain your target asset allocation based on changing market conditions.

7. **Consider Tax Implications**: Be aware of the tax implications of withdrawals and capital gains. Prioritize tax-deferred accounts for growth strategies and consider assets with higher tax liabilities for taxable accounts.

8. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: It's beneficial to discuss your specific situation with a financial advisor for personalized recommendations.

This balanced approach can help optimize your investment strategy while considering both current needs and future growth.
