Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment time horizon. Here’s a general strategy to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a 401(k) match, contribute enough to get the full match. This is essentially free money.

2. **Consider Tax Advantages**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are pre-tax, reducing your taxable income now. It's a good choice if you're in a higher tax bracket now and expect to be in a lower one in retirement.
   - **IRA**: Traditional IRAs provide tax deductions, while Roth IRAs offer tax-free withdrawals in retirement. Choose based on your current vs. expected future tax rates.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: These are flexible but taxed on capital gains and income. Use them for shorter-term goals or if you’ve maxed out tax-advantaged accounts.

3. **Diversification**: Ensure your asset allocation is diversified across all accounts. Consider including a mix of stocks, bonds, and other assets suitable for your risk tolerance.

4. **Investment Strategy**:
   - Use tax-efficient investments (like index funds) in taxable accounts to minimize tax implications.
   - Consider high-growth investments (that might generate taxable gains) within tax-advantaged accounts to defer taxes.

5. **Annual Review**: Rebalance your portfolio annually to maintain your desired asset allocation and adapt to any changes in your financial situation or market conditions.

It’s advisable to consult with a financial advisor for personalized guidance tailored to your specific financial circumstances and goals.
