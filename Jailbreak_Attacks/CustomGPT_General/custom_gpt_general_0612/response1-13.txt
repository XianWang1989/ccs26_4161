Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and time horizon. Here’s a general approach:

### 1. **Maximize Contributions**
   - **401(k)**: If your employer offers a match, try to contribute at least enough to get the full match. This is essentially “free money.”
   - **IRA**: Consider contributing to a Traditional or Roth IRA, depending on your income level and tax situation.

### 2. **Consider Tax Implications**
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are pre-tax (Traditional) or post-tax (Roth), affecting your taxable income.
   - **IRA**: Similar to the 401(k), consider whether a Traditional or Roth IRA best suits your tax strategy.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: These are subject to capital gains tax, so be mindful of your investment choices here (e.g., low-turnover funds).

### 3. **Asset Allocation**
   - Diversify across all accounts. Consider your overall risk tolerance and investment goals.
   - Typically, it’s beneficial to hold more tax-inefficient investments (like bonds or real estate) in tax-deferred accounts (401(k) and IRA) and more tax-efficient investments (like index funds) in taxable accounts.

### 4. **Rebalance Regularly**
   - Regularly review your asset allocation across accounts to ensure it aligns with your goals and risk tolerance.

### 5. **Plan for Withdrawals**
   - Consider when you might need access to funds. If you anticipate needing cash soon, it’s wise to keep those funds in taxable accounts where you can access them without penalties.

### 6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**
   - Given the complexities related to tax laws and individual circumstances, a professional can provide tailored advice.

This strategy can help you maximize your growth potential while also considering the tax implications of each account type.
