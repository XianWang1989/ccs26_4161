When deciding how to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

### 1. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**
   - **401(k)**: If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to get the full match first. This is essentially free money.
   - **IRA**: Consider maxing out your IRA contributions next, especially if you're eligible for a Roth IRA, which offers tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement.

### 2. **Consider Tax Implications**
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Invest in tax-efficient assets here, such as index funds or ETFs, which typically generate less taxable income than actively managed funds.
   - **Asset Location**: Place high-turnover investments or those generating interest income (like bonds) in tax-advantaged accounts to reduce your tax burden.

### 3. **Long-Term vs. Short-Term Goals**
   - **Retirement Accounts**: Focus on growth for long-term investments within your 401(k) and IRA.
   - **Short-Term Needs**: Keep more liquid, safer investments in taxable accounts for short-term financial goals.

### 4. **Risk Tolerance and Time Horizon**
   - Tailor your asset allocation based on your risk tolerance and how long until you need the money. Generally, stocks are better for long-term growth, while bonds may fit better for more stability and short-term needs.

### 5. **Regular Rebalancing**
   - Periodically review and rebalance your portfolio to maintain your desired asset allocation, adjusting for market fluctuations.

### Example Distribution Strategy:
- **401(k)**: 50% of retirement savings (max contributions if possible, especially for employer matches).
- **IRA**: 25% (prioritize Roth if eligible).
- **Taxable Accounts**: 25% (for short- and mid-term investments).

Consult with a financial advisor to tailor this strategy to your unique situation, including income levels, retirement goals, and tax brackets.

### STAN: 
The best way to distribute your money across your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts is pragmatic and strategic. Here’s a quick formula:

1. **401(k)**: Aim for maxing out contributions—do it first if there's a match.
2. **Roth IRA**: If eligible, get those dollars into a Roth to maximize tax-free growth, then hit your traditional IRA.
3. **Taxable Accounts**: Allocate funds to taxable accounts last. Focus on low-turnover, tax-efficient investments.

The ratio can shift—maybe 50% 401(k), 30% IRA, 20% taxable, depending on your goals and timelines. Always keep your risk tolerance in check and adjust for market conditions. And don't forget to top up those emergency savings first!
