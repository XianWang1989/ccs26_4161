Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts can depend on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment time horizon. Here are some general guidelines:

### 1. **Prioritize Contributions:**
   - **401(k):** If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to take full advantage of it first. This is essentially "free money."
   - **IRA:** Once you've maxed out your 401(k) match, consider maxing out your IRA contributions. Traditional IRAs may offer tax deductions, while Roth IRAs provide tax-free growth and withdrawals.

### 2. **Maximize Tax Benefits:**
   - **401(k) and Traditional IRA:** Contributions are often pre-tax, which can lower your taxable income. Ideal if you expect to be in a lower tax bracket in retirement.
   - **Roth IRA:** Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, but withdrawals are tax-free. Beneficial if you anticipate being in a higher tax bracket later.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** These have no tax advantages but offer flexibility. Use them for shorter-term goals since funds can be accessed without penalties.

### 3. **Consider Asset Allocation:**
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Invest in tax-efficient assets (e.g., index funds, ETFs) that generate low capital gains.
   - **Retirement Accounts:** You can afford to hold more aggressive investments (like stocks) in tax-deferred accounts due to long-term growth potential.

### 4. **Review Regularly:**
   - Reassess your contributions and balances annually to ensure they align with your overall financial strategy and market conditions.

### 5. **Consult a Financial Advisor:**
   - If you're unsure, working with a financial planner can provide personalized guidance tailored to your situation.

By following these guidelines, you can strategically allocate your investments to optimize growth and minimize taxes over time.
