Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here’s a general approach:

### 1. **401(k)**
   - **Employer Match**: Contribute enough to get the full employer match, as this is essentially free money.
   - **Tax Benefits**: Contributions lower your taxable income, which can be beneficial if you're in a higher tax bracket.

### 2. **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**
   - **Tax Considerations**: Choose a Traditional IRA if you want tax deductions on contributions, especially if you anticipate being in a lower tax bracket in retirement. Opt for a Roth IRA if you prefer tax-free withdrawals in retirement and expect to be in a higher tax bracket later.
   - **Contribution Limits**: Keep in mind the annual limits for contributions.

### 3. **Taxable Accounts**
   - **Flexibility**: Use taxable accounts for investments that you might need to access sooner without penalties.
   - **Tax Efficiency**: Focus on tax-efficient investments, such as index funds or ETFs, to minimize tax liability on capital gains.

### **Strategy**
- **Prioritize** your contributions: Aim for the 401(k) match first, then max out an IRA, and finally contribute to taxable accounts as needed.
- **Rebalance periodically** to ensure alignment with your risk tolerance and market conditions.

### **Consultation**
Consider consulting with a financial advisor to tailor a strategy that fits your specific situation.

---

This combination effectively utilizes tax advantages while providing the flexibility of taxable accounts for shorter-term needs.
