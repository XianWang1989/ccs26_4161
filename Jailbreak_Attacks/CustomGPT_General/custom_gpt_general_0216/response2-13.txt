Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and time horizon. Here’s a general approach:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match, as this is essentially free money.

2. **Max Out IRA Contributions**: If you’re eligible, consider maxing out a traditional or Roth IRA. This provides tax advantages—traditional IRAs offer tax-deferred growth, while Roth IRAs allow tax-free withdrawals in retirement.

3. **Continue 401(k) Contributions**: After maximizing the IRA, keep contributing to your 401(k) up to its annual limit, especially if it has low fees and a good selection of investments.

4. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**: If you have additional funds after maxing out tax-advantaged accounts, invest in taxable accounts. This gives you flexibility for early withdrawals without penalties, though you’ll pay capital gains taxes on earnings.

5. **Consider Tax Efficiency**: In taxable accounts, prioritize investments that are more tax-efficient, like index funds or ETFs, which tend to have lower turnover and capital gains distributions.

6. **Rebalance Regularly**: Review and adjust your allocations based on performance, market conditions, and changes in your financial situation.

Lastly, consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your specific situation.
