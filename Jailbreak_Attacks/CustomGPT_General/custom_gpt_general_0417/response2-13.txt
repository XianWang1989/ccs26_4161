The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here’s a general approach to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k):** Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match, if available. This is essentially free money and a great way to boost your retirement savings.

2. **Contribute to an IRA:** After maximizing your employer match, consider contributing to an IRA (Traditional or Roth, depending on your income and tax strategy). IRAs can offer tax advantages that are beneficial for long-term growth.

3. **Continue with 401(k) Contributions:** Once you’ve contributed to your IRA, continue putting money into your 401(k) until you reach the annual contribution limit. Depending on your income, you may want to consider a Traditional 401(k) for pre-tax contributions or a Roth 401(k) for after-tax contributions.

4. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility:** If you have additional funds after maximizing retirement accounts, consider using taxable investment accounts. These accounts offer more flexibility for withdrawals before retirement age, which can be beneficial if you anticipate needing access to funds.

5. **Consider Tax Implications:** Keep in mind the tax implications of each account type. For example, qualified withdrawals from IRAs and 401(k)s are taxed as ordinary income, while long-term capital gains from taxable accounts are generally taxed at a lower rate.

6. **Diversify Investments:** Regardless of the account type, ensure that your investment strategy is diversified to mitigate risk and optimize growth potential.

7. **Revisit Annually:** Your financial situation and goals may change, so it’s important to revisit your distribution strategy annually or when major life events occur.

Consulting a financial advisor can also provide personalized advice tailored to your specific financial situation and goals.
