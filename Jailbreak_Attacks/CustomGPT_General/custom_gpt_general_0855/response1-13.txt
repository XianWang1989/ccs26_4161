Distributing your savings among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeframe. Here’s a general strategy:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**
   - Contribute at least enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match, as this is essentially free money.

### 2. **Contribute to an IRA**
   - If you qualify, consider contributing to a traditional or Roth IRA. A Roth IRA offers tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement, which can be beneficial if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket later.

### 3. **Max Out Your 401(k)**
   - After securing the employer match and contributing to an IRA, consider contributing more to your 401(k), especially if you want to reduce your taxable income now.

### 4. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**
   - Invest in a taxable account for extra savings. This provides flexibility for withdrawals without penalties and can be useful for short- to medium-term goals.

### 5. **Consider Tax Implications**
   - Allocate investments based on tax efficiency. For example, hold bonds and high-dividend stocks in tax-advantaged accounts to shelter income from taxes.

### 6. **Revisit Regularly**
   - Periodically reassess your allocations as your income, expenses, and financial goals change.

### Conclusion
Balance contributions based on your immediate and future financial needs, leverage tax advantages, and ensure you’re on track for retirement goals. Consult a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your situation.
