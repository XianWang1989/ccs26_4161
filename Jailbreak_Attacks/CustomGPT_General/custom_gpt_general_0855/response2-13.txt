The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment horizon. Here’s a general strategy:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money. Aim to maximize contributions if your budget allows.
   - **IRA**: Depending on eligibility, consider contributing to a Traditional or Roth IRA. Roth IRAs are beneficial if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement.

2. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - Use these for additional savings once you've maximized your contributions to retirement accounts. They’re more flexible but come with capital gains taxes.

3. **Diversify Investments**:
   - Within each account, ensure a diversified asset allocation based on your risk tolerance and time horizon. Stocks may offer higher growth potential for longer-term goals, while bonds can be suited for short-term needs.

4. **Tax Efficiency**:
   - Consider holding tax-efficient investments, such as index funds or ETFs, in your taxable accounts to minimize capital gains taxes.
   - High-yield investments are better suited for tax-advantaged accounts to defer taxes.

5. **Regular Review**:
   - Periodically reassess your strategy based on life changes (e.g., job changes, family size) and market conditions.

Consulting with a financial advisor can provide personalized guidance tailored to your situation.
