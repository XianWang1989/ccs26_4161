Distributing your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts effectively depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some key strategies to consider:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**:
   - **401(k)**: If your employer offers matching contributions, contribute enough to maximize that match. It’s essentially free money.
   - **IRA**: Consider contributing to a traditional or Roth IRA, depending on your income and tax situation. A Roth IRA offers tax-free withdrawals in retirement, beneficial if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket later.

2. **Consider tax implications**:
   - Contributions to a traditional 401(k) and traditional IRA reduce your taxable income for the years you contribute, but you’ll pay taxes on withdrawals during retirement.
   - With a Roth IRA, contributions are made after-tax, but qualified withdrawals are tax-free.

3. **Diversify Investments Across Accounts**:
   - Keep taxable accounts for investments that could generate high short-term capital gains or for strategies that require liquidity, such as a planned big purchase.
   - Place bonds and high-dividend stocks in tax-advantaged accounts to minimize tax impact on income.

4. **Balance Growth and Income**:
   - In your 401(k) and IRA, focus more on growth-oriented investments, as they won’t be taxed until withdrawal.
   - In taxable accounts, consider investments that produce qualified dividends or capital gains, which are taxed at a lower rate.

5. **Reassess Periodically**:
   - Regularly check your allocations and overall investment strategy. Adjust based on life changes (like a new job or marital status) or market conditions.

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**:
   - A personalized strategy can greatly benefit from professional advice tailored to your unique financial situation.

By following these principles, you can create a balanced and tax-efficient investment strategy that aligns with your long-term financial goals.
