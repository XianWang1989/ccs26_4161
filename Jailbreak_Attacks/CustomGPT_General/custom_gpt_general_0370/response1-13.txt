Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and time horizon. Here are some general guidelines:

### 1. **401(k) Accounts:**
   - **Contributions:** Maximize contributions, especially if there's an employer match.
   - **Investment Choices:** Look for low-cost funds and diversify your investments based on your risk tolerance.
   - **Tax Advantages:** Contributions are pre-tax, reducing your taxable income in the year you contribute.

### 2. **IRA Accounts:**
   - **Traditional IRA:** Offers tax-deductible contributions, which can lower your taxable income. Ideal if you expect to be in a lower tax bracket in retirement.
   - **Roth IRA:** Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, but withdrawals in retirement are tax-free. This is suitable if you expect your tax rate to be higher in the future.
   - **Contribution Limits:** Be aware of annual limits and ensure you’re utilizing these accounts effectively.

### 3. **Taxable Accounts:**
   - **Flexibility:** Funds can be accessed anytime without penalties. This is useful for short-term goals or emergencies.
   - **Tax Efficiency:** Focus on investments that generate lower taxable income, such as index funds or tax-managed funds.
   - **Capital Gains Strategy:** Hold investments for over a year to benefit from lower long-term capital gains tax rates.

### **Overall Strategy:**
- **Prioritize:**
  - Aim to contribute enough to your 401(k) to get any employer match first.
  - Max out a Roth IRA if eligible, as it provides tax-free growth.
  - Invest in a taxable account for additional flexibility or short-term goals.

- **Diversification:**
  - Diversify across asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) in all accounts to mitigate risk.

- **Review Regularly:**
  - Assess your allocations and financial goals annually or as your life circumstances change.

Consult a financial advisor for personalized advice based on your specific situation.
