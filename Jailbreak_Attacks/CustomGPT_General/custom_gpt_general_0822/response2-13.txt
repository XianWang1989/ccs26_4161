Distributing your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here's a breakdown to consider:

1. **401(k) Contributions**:
   - If your employer offers a matching contribution, contribute at least enough to get the full match. This is essentially “free money.”
   - Consider maxing out your 401(k) if you can. For 2023, the contribution limit is $22,500 (or $30,000 if you're age 50 or older).

2. **IRA Contributions**:
   - Depending on your income level, you may qualify for a Roth IRA, which allows for tax-free withdrawals in retirement. If eligible, consider contributing to a Roth IRA after maxing out any 401(k) match.
   - Traditional IRAs can also provide tax deductions, depending on your income and other factors. The 2023 contribution limit is $6,500 (or $7,500 if you're age 50 or older).

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - After maximizing contributions to tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) and IRA), consider investing in taxable accounts for greater liquidity and flexibility.
   - Use these accounts for investments that generate capital gains, as they are generally taxed at a lower rate than ordinary income.

4. **Investment Strategy**:
   - Diversify your investments across these accounts to balance risk. Consider your risk tolerance and investment horizon when choosing specific assets.
   - Think about asset location: place higher-tax investments (like bonds or REITs) in tax-advantaged accounts and lower-tax investments (like stocks) in taxable accounts.
