Distributing your money across your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various factors like your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here are some strategies to consider:

### 1. **Understand Account Types**
   - **401(k):** Often has employer matching, tax-deferred growth, and higher contribution limits.
   - **IRA (Traditional or Roth):** Offers tax advantages; Roth IRAs provide tax-free withdrawals in retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Flexible access to funds but subject to capital gains taxes.

### 2. **Maximize Employer Match**
   - Contribute enough to your 401(k) to receive the full employer match if available. This is essentially free money.

### 3. **Consider Tax Implications**
   - **Traditional 401(k) and IRA:** Contributions are tax-deductible, but withdrawals are taxed as ordinary income.
   - **Roth IRA:** Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, so withdrawals in retirement are tax-free.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** You’ll pay capital gains taxes on any profits when you sell investments.

### 4. **Prioritize Contributions**
   - Start with the 401(k) to the match, then consider maxing out an IRA (especially Roth, if eligible).
   - After those, if you have additional funds, contribute to the 401(k) up to the max limit and then to taxable accounts.

### 5. **Investment Strategy**
   - Use tax-advantaged accounts for high-growth investments that can benefit from tax deferral.
   - In taxable accounts, consider holding more tax-efficient investments (like index funds or ETFs) to minimize tax burdens.

### 6. **Assess Your Retirement Timeline**
   - The closer you are to retirement, the more you might want to allocate towards less volatile investments, especially in your 401(k) and IRA.

### 7. **Regularly Rebalance**
   - Review and adjust your asset allocation periodically based on market performance and changes in your financial goals.

### 8. **Consult a Financial Advisor**
   - Personal circumstances vary widely, so consider seeking personalized advice from a financial planner for tailored strategies.

Implementing a strategy like this can help you optimize your overall investment growth while accounting for tax implications and risk tolerance.
