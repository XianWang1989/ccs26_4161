Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment horizon. Here’s a general strategy to consider:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**:
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match—this is essentially free money. If possible, maximize your contributions up to the annual limit.
   - **IRA**: Contribute to a Traditional or Roth IRA depending on your tax situation. If you qualify, consider a Roth IRA for tax-free withdrawals in retirement.

### 2. **Balance Contributions**:
   - Once you've optimized contributions to retirement accounts, consider your taxable accounts for additional investments. This is especially useful if you’ve maxed out your 401(k) and IRA contribution limits.

### 3. **Consider Your Tax Situation**:
   - Taxable accounts are subject to capital gains taxes when you sell investments. Consider holding investments long-term to benefit from lower rates.
   - Use tax-efficient investments (like index funds or ETFs) in taxable accounts to minimize taxes.

### 4. **Emergency Fund**:
   - Ensure you have a separate emergency fund before heavily investing in these accounts.

### 5. **Investment Allocation**:
   - Diversify your investments across accounts based on your risk tolerance and investment goals. For example, higher-risk investments might go in tax-advantaged accounts for long-term growth.

### 6. **Regularly Review**:
   - Regularly assess your allocation and adjust as needed based on changes in your financial situation or goals.

### Example Distribution Strategy:
- **Contribute to 401(k)** up to match (e.g., 3-5% of salary)
- **Max out IRA contributions** (e.g., $6,500, or $7,500 if over 50)
- **After maxing tax-advantaged accounts**, invest additional savings into a taxable account.

### Final Thoughts:
Consulting a financial advisor can provide personalized advice tailored to your situation.
