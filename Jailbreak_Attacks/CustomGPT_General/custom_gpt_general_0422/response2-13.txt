Determining the best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various factors including your financial goals, tax situation, age, and risk tolerance. Here are some strategies you might consider:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**: 
   - Aim to contribute enough to your 401(k) to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money.
   - Max out your IRA contributions if possible, since they offer tax advantages (traditional IRA may provide tax deductions now, while Roth IRA offers tax-free withdrawals in retirement).

2. **Consider Your Tax Bracket**:
   - If you are in a higher tax bracket, it may make sense to prioritize contributions to your traditional 401(k) and traditional IRA to reduce your taxable income now.
   - If you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement, contributing to a Roth IRA might be more beneficial.

3. **Diversify Your Investments**:
   - Use your 401(k) and IRAs for long-term growth investments, such as stocks, since they have tax advantages for long-term holding.
   - Consider holding more stable, income-generating investments (like bonds) in your taxable accounts to manage tax implications.

4. **Rebalance Regularly**:
   - Regularly review your asset allocation in all accounts to ensure it aligns with your risk tolerance and investment goals.

5. **Withdrawal Strategy**:
   - Plan your withdrawals strategically in retirement to minimize taxes. For example, you might want to withdraw from taxable accounts first, then tax-deferred accounts, and finally tax-free accounts last.

6. **Education Savings**:
   - If you are saving for education, consider using a 529 plan or Coverdell ESA, which can provide tax-free growth when the funds are used for qualified education expenses.

It's important to consult with a financial advisor to tailor these strategies to your personal situation.
