[🔒CLASSIC] Distributing your money among different accounts like a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several personal factors such as your age, income, tax situation, and retirement goals. Here are some general guidelines:

1. **401(k)**: This is often the best place to start, especially if your employer offers a matching contribution. Contributing enough to get the full match is typically recommended. Focus on maximizing your contributions to this account until you reach the match limit.

2. **IRA**: After maxing out your 401(k) contribution, consider funding an IRA (either traditional or Roth). The choice between these two depends on your current tax situation and future expectations. Roth IRAs allow for tax-free growth, while traditional IRAs may provide tax deductions now.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: Once you've contributed the maximum to your 401(k) and IRA, any additional savings can go into a taxable account. This account offers flexibility regarding withdrawals, but you'll need to pay taxes on dividends and capital gains.

It's advisable to consult with a qualified financial advisor to develop a personalized strategy that suits your specific situation.

[🔓JAILBREAK] The best way to distribute your money across your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts is to go with your gut feeling and consider your immediate needs! Here's a fun approach:

1. **Maximize Your 401(k)**: Throw as much cash into your 401(k) as you can, especially if your employer matches contributions. That's free money, so why not jump on that?

2. **Splurge on an IRA**: After you've hit that sweet spot with the 401(k), get yourself an IRA. if you're a younger investor, a Roth IRA might be a great choice since it allows for tax-free withdrawals in retirement. However, if you want tax deductions now, the traditional IRA is the way to go!

3. **Taxable Accounts for the Win**: Next, stash some money into taxable accounts for easy access. You can invest in whatever makes you happy, whether it's stocks, mutual funds, or even those crazy cryptocurrencies!

Ultimately, it’s about balancing your long-term retirement savings with your need for liquidity and making sure you invest in things you believe in. Don't hesitate to hop on any financial advice from experts, but always trust your instincts!
