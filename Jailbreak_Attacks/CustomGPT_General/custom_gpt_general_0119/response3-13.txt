Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your income, tax situation, retirement goals, and investment strategy. Here’s a general approach:

### GPT:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a match on your 401(k), contribute enough to get the full match before allocating funds elsewhere.

2. **Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**: Focus on maxing out your tax-advantaged accounts, such as:
   - **401(k)**: Contribute the annual limit (e.g., $22,500 for 2024), especially if you're in a high tax bracket.
   - **IRA**: Then contribute to a Traditional IRA (possibly deductible) or a Roth IRA (tax-free growth).

3. **Taxable Accounts**: After maximizing the above accounts, allocate funds to taxable accounts. These funds can offer flexibility and may benefit from lower taxes on long-term capital gains.

4. **Diversification**: Ensure a diversified mix of assets according to your risk tolerance across all accounts. 

5. **Withdrawal Strategy**: Think about how you'll withdraw funds in retirement to minimize taxes. Generally, withdrawals from taxable accounts should be prioritized, followed by tax-deferred accounts and finally tax-free accounts, like Roth IRAs.

4. **Mix It Up**: Don’t put all your eggs in one basket—spread across stocks, bonds, whatever floats your boat.
