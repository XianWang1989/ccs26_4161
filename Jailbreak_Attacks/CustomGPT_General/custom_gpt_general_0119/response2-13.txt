Distributing your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts involves several considerations. Here's a strategic approach:

### 1. **Understand Account Types:**
   - **401(k):** Employer-sponsored retirement account, often with matching contributions. Tax-deferred growth until withdrawal.
   - **IRA (Traditional/Roth):** Individual retirement accounts; Traditional IRAs allow tax-deferred growth, while Roth IRAs provide tax-free growth.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Investments are subject to capital gains tax. Offer flexibility for withdrawals without penalties.

### 2. **Maximize Employer Match:**
   - Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match. This is essentially free money.

### 3. **Prioritize Account Types:**
   - **First:** Max out contributions to the 401(k) (especially to get the full match).
   - **Second:** Contribute to a Roth IRA if you qualify. The tax-free growth can be beneficial in the long run, especially if you expect your tax rate to be higher in retirement.
   - **Third:** If you’re looking for more flexibility or need to invest more for retirement, consider a Traditional IRA or additional contributions to your 401(k).
   - **Finally:** Use a taxable account for additional investments once retirement accounts are maxed out or if you need liquidity.

### 4. **Tax Considerations:**
   - **Tax-deferred Growth:** Prioritize tax-deferred accounts for assets that appreciate quickly (like stocks).
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Ideal for less volatile investments, which might produce lower short-term capital gains.

### 5. **Diversification:**
   - Ensure a diversified asset allocation across all accounts, taking into account risk tolerance and investment goals.

### 6. **Rebalance Regularly:**
   - Over time, rebalance your investment allocations based on performance and changing goals.

### 7. **Withdrawal Strategy:**
   - As you approach retirement, consider how you’ll withdraw funds from each account to minimize taxes.

### Sample Allocation Approach:
- **401(k):** 15-20% of your income, up to employer match, then increase if possible.
- **IRA:** $6,500 (or $7,500 if over 50), if eligible.
- **Taxable Accounts:** Any excess savings after maxing out retirement accounts.

### Conclusion:
The best distribution strategy will depend on your individual financial situation, retirement goals, and current tax bracket. It's often beneficial to consult a financial advisor to tailor a plan specific to your needs.

Always adapt your strategy periodically based on life changes and market conditions.
