Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts involves careful consideration of several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment strategy. Here are some general guidelines:

### 1. **Understand Account Types**

- **401(k)**: Often has employer matching contributions and tax-deferred growth. Contributions are pre-tax (traditional) or after-tax (Roth).
- **IRA**: Offers tax-deferred growth (traditional) or tax-free growth (Roth), with limits on contributions.
- **Taxable Accounts**: Flexibility in withdrawals and investments, but subject to capital gains taxes on profits.

### 2. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**

- **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get the full employer match. This is essentially "free money."
- **IRA**: After maximizing your 401(k) match, consider contributing to an IRA. If you’re eligible, a Roth IRA can be beneficial for tax-free growth.

### 3. **Consider Tax Implications**

- **Tax-Deferred Growth**: Prioritize contributions to tax-deferred accounts (401(k) and traditional IRA) if you're in a higher tax bracket now than you expect to be in retirement.
- **Taxable Accounts**: Use for income-producing investments to manage tax liabilities more effectively.

### 4. **Allocate Investments Wisely**

- **401(k)**: Diversify with a mix of stocks, bonds, and funds based on your risk tolerance and time horizon.
- **IRA**: Similar diversification strategy; consider higher-growth investments if the time horizon is long.
- **Taxable Accounts**: Focus on tax-efficient investments, like index funds or municipal bonds, to minimize tax impact.

### 5. **Adjust as Needed**

- Regularly review and rebalance your portfolio in all accounts. Change your contributions based on life events, tax law changes, or shifts in financial goals.

### Conclusion

A common strategy is to max out your 401(k) to get the employer match, then fund a Roth IRA or a traditional IRA based on your eligibility and tax situation. After that, use taxable accounts for additional investments, focusing on tax efficiency.
