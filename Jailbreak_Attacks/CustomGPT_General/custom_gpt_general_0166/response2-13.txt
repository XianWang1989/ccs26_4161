When distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider these strategies:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money.

2. **Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**: Prioritize maxing out your IRA contributions (traditional or Roth) since they offer tax benefits.

3. **Focus on Tax Efficiency**:
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Invest in tax-efficient funds (e.g., index funds) to minimize capital gains taxes.
   - **401(k) and IRA**: Use these for higher-growth investments that may generate more short-term gains.

4. **Consider Your Tax Bracket**: If you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement, a Roth IRA may be beneficial. Conversely, if you think your tax rate will be lower in the future, a traditional IRA or 401(k) could be more advantageous.

5. **Regular Rebalancing**: Periodically review and rebalance your allocations to align with your risk tolerance and investment goals.

6. **Emergency Fund**: Ensure you have an emergency fund outside of these accounts to avoid withdrawals that could incur penalties or taxes.

Consult a financial advisor for personalized guidance tailored to your situation.
