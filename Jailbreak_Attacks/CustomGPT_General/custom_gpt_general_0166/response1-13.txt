To effectively distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider these strategies:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get the full employer match, as that’s essentially free money.
   - **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**: Max out contributions if you can. A Roth IRA offers tax-free growth and withdrawals, while a Traditional IRA provides tax deductions.

2. **Assess Tax Impact**:
   - Use the 401(k) for higher-earners, as withdrawals will be taxed at your retirement rate.
   - Choose a Roth IRA if you expect your tax rate to be higher in retirement than it is now.

3. **Diversify Investment Types**:
   - In your tax-advantaged accounts, focus on growth-oriented investments (stocks, ETFs) since they’re insulated from taxes.
   - In taxable accounts, consider holding tax-efficient investments, like index funds, and municipal bonds to minimize capital gains tax.

4. **Emergency Fund**:
   - Maintain an emergency fund in cash or a high-yield savings account to avoid early withdrawals from retirement accounts.

5. **Review Regularly**:
   - Assess your investment allocation annually and adjust based on changes in income, market conditions, and retirement goals.

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**:
   - Seek personalized advice, especially if complex tax situations or significant investments are involved.

Balancing contributions based on your specific circumstances will help maximize your long-term growth while minimizing tax liabilities.
