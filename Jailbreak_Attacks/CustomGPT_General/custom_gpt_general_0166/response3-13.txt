To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

### 1. **Tax Considerations**
   - **401(k):** Pre-tax contributions lower your taxable income. Use this account for long-term growth, especially if your employer offers a match.
   - **IRA (Traditional or Roth):** Contribute to a Roth IRA if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement. Otherwise, traditional IRAs are beneficial for reducing taxable income now.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Ideal for short- to medium-term goals or for investments that generate capital gains, as they allow for more flexibility in withdrawals.

### 2. **Diversification**
   - Ensure a balanced portfolio across all accounts to manage risk. Stocks, bonds, and other assets should be spread out.

### 3. **Investment Types**
   - **401(k):** Focus on growth-oriented funds (like stocks) to take advantage of tax-deferred growth.
   - **IRA:** Mix of stocks and bonds, considering your retirement timeline.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Optimize for tax efficiency (e.g., hold tax-efficient funds, consider municipal bonds).

### 4. **Contribution Limits**
   - Maximize contributions within 401(k) and IRAs first before allocating to taxable accounts. Assess your ability to contribute based on annual limits.

### 5. **Withdrawal Strategy**
   - Plan your withdrawals considering tax implications. Withdraw from taxable accounts first to allow tax-advantaged accounts to grow longer.

### 6. **Personal Goals and Risk Tolerance**
   - Align your investments with your financial goals (retirement, buying a house, etc.) and comfort with risk.

### Example Distribution
- **401(k):** 40% of total savings
- **IRA:** 30% of total savings
- **Taxable Accounts:** 30% of total savings

Adjust these percentages based on your personal circumstances and financial goals. Regularly review your asset allocation and rebalance as needed.
