Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts effectively depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment horizon. Here are some strategies to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to secure any employer match, as it’s essentially free money.

2. **Consider Tax Advantages**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are tax-deferred; great for lowering current taxable income.
   - **Traditional IRA**: Also tax-deferred, good for those who qualify for tax deductions.
   - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, allowing tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement. Ideal if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket later.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Useful for flexibility; invest after-tax income but be mindful of capital gains taxes.

3. **Investment Time Horizon**:
   - Longer-term investments should be prioritized in tax-advantaged accounts (401(k), IRAs).
   - If you need access to funds in the next few years, consider allocating more to taxable accounts.

4. **Diversification**: Diversify within each account type. Allocate based on risk tolerance—stocks for growth, bonds for stability.

5. **Evaluate Fees**: Compare fees in your 401(k) with investment options in your IRA or taxable accounts. Lower fees can lead to better long-term performance.

6. **Roth Conversion**: If you anticipate higher tax rates in the future, consider converting some traditional IRA or 401(k) funds to a Roth IRA.

7. **Annual Review**: Regularly review and rebalance your allocations based on changing financial goals and market conditions.

Consulting a financial advisor can provide tailored advice based on your personal situation.
