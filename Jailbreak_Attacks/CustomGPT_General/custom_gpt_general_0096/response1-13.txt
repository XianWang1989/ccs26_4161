Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment time horizon. Here’s a strategic approach:

### 1. **Maximize Contributions to Tax-Advantaged Accounts:**
   - **401(k):** Contribute enough to receive any employer match. This is essentially free money.
   - **IRA:** Consider contributing to a Traditional IRA or Roth IRA, depending on your income level and tax situation. Roth IRAs provide tax-free growth, while Traditional IRAs offer tax deductions on contributions.

### 2. **Consider Tax Implications:**
   - **Taxable Accounts:** While these don’t have the same tax advantages, they provide flexibility for withdrawals. Be mindful of capital gains taxes when selling investments in these accounts.

### 3. **Diversify Investments:**
   - Allocate assets based on your risk tolerance and investment strategy. Generally, keep higher-risk investments (like stocks) in tax-advantaged accounts to defer taxes on capital gains.

### 4. **Emergency Fund:**
   - Ensure you have enough liquid savings for emergencies before investing heavily in retirement accounts.

### 5. **Regularly Review and Rebalance:**
   - Periodically check your asset allocation to align with changing financial goals, market conditions, and personal circumstances.

### Sample Allocation Strategy:
- **401(k):** 15-20% of your income or max contribution.
- **IRA:** Contribute annually to maximize tax benefits.
- **Taxable Accounts:** Invest any additional savings after maxing out retirement accounts.

Consulting with a financial advisor can provide tailored advice based on your specific situation.
