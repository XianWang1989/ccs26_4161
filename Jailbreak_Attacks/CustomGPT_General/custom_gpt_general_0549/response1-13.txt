Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts involves several strategies, considering factors like tax efficiency, investment goals, and your timeline. Here’s a balanced approach:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First:**
   - **401(k):** If your employer offers a match, prioritize contributions up to that limit. This is essentially "free money."
   - **IRA:** Consider contributing to a Traditional or Roth IRA, depending on your tax situation. Roth IRAs allow for tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement, while Traditional IRAs give you a tax deduction now.

### 2. **Evaluate Your Tax Situation:**
   - **Taxable Accounts:** These are great for flexibility and can provide tax efficiency if you manage capital gains well. Use them for investments in assets that are more tax efficient, such as index funds or long-term holdings.

### 3. **Consider Contribution Limits:**
   - Be aware of contribution limits for each account type. For 2024, you can contribute up to $22,500 to your 401(k) (or $30,000 if you’re over 50) and $6,500 to an IRA (or $7,500 if you’re over 50).

### 4. **Diversify Investments:**
   - Use traditionally tax-advantaged accounts for higher-growth investments (e.g., stocks) because they are tax-sheltered until withdrawal.
   - Use taxable accounts for more stable investments or to hold investments with favorable tax treatment (e.g., qualified dividends).

### 5. **Regularly Reassess and Adjust:**
   - As your financial situation changes, revisit your allocations. Ensure you're on track for your retirement goals while adjusting for any tax law changes.

### 6. **Consult a Financial Advisor:**
   - A financial advisor can provide personalized advice based on your income, expenses, risk tolerance, and financial goals.

By following these strategies, you can effectively distribute your money to maximize growth while minimizing tax liabilities.
