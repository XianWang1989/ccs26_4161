Distributing money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on a few factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here's a general strategy to consider:

1. **Max Out Retirement Accounts First**:
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money. If possible, max out your contributions.
   - **IRA**: After funding your 401(k), consider a Roth IRA for tax-free growth, or a traditional IRA for tax-deferred growth, depending on your tax strategy.

2. **Tax Efficiency**:
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for investments that are taxed at a lower rate, like index funds or long-term capital gains. Keep high-growth or interest-generating investments in retirement accounts to defer or minimize taxes.

3. **Asset Allocation**:
   - Diversify your investments based on risk tolerance. Generally, you might allocate stocks in your taxable account for long-term growth and bonds in tax-advantaged accounts to avoid paying tax on interest income.

4. **Withdrawal Strategy**:
   - Plan your withdrawals based on tax implications. Withdraw from taxable accounts first to allow your tax-advantaged accounts to grow longer.

5. **Review and Adjust**: Regularly review your allocations based on changes in your life circumstances, market conditions, or retirement goals.

It’s wise to consult a financial advisor to create a personalized plan based on your individual circumstances.
