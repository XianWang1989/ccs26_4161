Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your individual financial goals, time horizon, and tax situation. Here’s a general approach to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money.

2. **Emergency Fund**: Ensure you have a sufficient emergency fund (3-6 months of expenses) in a high-yield savings account before heavily investing.

3. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: Fill this up to the annual contribution limit if your employer offers matching.
   - **IRA**: Contribute to a traditional or Roth IRA (depending on your eligibility and tax situation).

4. **Taxable Accounts**: After maximizing contributions to tax-advantaged accounts, invest in taxable accounts. This provides flexibility for withdrawals without penalties.

5. **Asset Allocation**: Adjust your investment strategy based on account type:
   - **401(k) and IRA**: Consider holding more aggressive investments (e.g., stocks) as these grow tax-deferred.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Focus on tax-efficient investments (e.g., ETFs, municipal bonds) to minimize capital gains taxes.

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: A professional can tailor a strategy to your specific situation.

This balanced approach helps leverage tax advantages while ensuring liquidity for financial goals.
