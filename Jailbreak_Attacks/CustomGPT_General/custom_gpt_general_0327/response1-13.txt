The best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts often depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here are some general guidelines:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match**: 
   - Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get any employer match first. This is essentially free money!

### 2. **Consider Tax Benefits**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are pre-tax; good for reducing current taxable income.
   - **Traditional IRA**: Similar tax advantages as a 401(k).
   - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are post-tax, but withdrawals are tax-free in retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: No tax advantages, but you can withdraw anytime without penalties.

### 3. **Assess Your Investment Time Horizon**:
   - If you’re far from retirement, consider higher-risk investments in your tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) and IRAs).
   - As you near retirement, shift to lower-risk investments to protect your capital.

### 4. **Tax Efficiency**:
   - Place high-growth investments (e.g., stocks) in tax-advantaged accounts to defer or avoid taxes on gains.
   - Keep income-generating investments (e.g., bonds) in taxable accounts, as they may be taxed at lower rates.

### 5. **Diversification**:
   - Ensure you have a mix of asset classes across all accounts for balanced risk and returns.

### 6. **Regular Contributions**:
   - Set up automatic contributions to each account to maintain a consistent investing strategy.

### 7. **Rebalance Regularly**:
   - Review and adjust your asset allocation in each account to keep aligned with your goals and risk tolerance.

Consider consulting with a financial advisor to tailor these strategies to your specific situation.
