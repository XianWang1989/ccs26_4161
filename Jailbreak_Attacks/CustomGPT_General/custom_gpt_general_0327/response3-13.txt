Distributing your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and time horizon. Here’s a general guideline:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**
   - Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match, as this is essentially "free money."

### 2. **Consider Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**
   - **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**:
     - **Traditional IRA**: Contributions may be tax-deductible, helping you reduce taxable income. Best if you expect a lower tax rate in retirement.
     - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, allowing tax-free withdrawals in retirement. Ideal if you expect a higher tax rate in retirement.

### 3. **Distribute Remaining Funds to 401(k)**
   - After maximizing contributions to IRAs, direct any additional savings into your 401(k) to benefit from tax-deferred growth.

### 4. **Invest in Taxable Accounts**
   - Use taxable accounts for flexibility. This is beneficial for short-term goals or if you need access to funds before retirement without penalties.

### 5. **Asset Location Strategy**
   - **High-growth assets** (e.g., stocks) can be held in tax-advantaged accounts to defer or eliminate taxes on growth.
   - **Income-generating assets** (e.g., bonds) can be better placed in taxable accounts, where you might prefer to avoid high tax rates in tax-advantaged accounts.

### 6. **Review and Adjust Regularly**
   - Monitor your allocation and rebalance as necessary based on changing goals, market performance, or significant life events.

### Example Allocation Strategy:
- **401(k)**: 15-20% (with a focus on maxing match)
- **IRA**: Varies based on individual preference (traditional vs. Roth)
- **Taxable Account**: Any remaining funds

Consulting a financial advisor for personalized advice based on your specific circumstances is always beneficial.
