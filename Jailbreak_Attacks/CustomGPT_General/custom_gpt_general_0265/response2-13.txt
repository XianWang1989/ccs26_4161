When deciding how to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Maximize Employer Contributions**: If your employer offers a 401(k) match, contribute enough to receive the full match as this is essentially free money.

2. **Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**: Prioritize contributions to your 401(k) and IRA due to their tax benefits. For 401(k)s, especially if you are in a high tax bracket, contributions reduce your taxable income. With IRAs, depending on whether it’s a Traditional or Roth IRA, you can either defer taxes now or enjoy tax-free withdrawals in retirement.

3. **Contribution Limits**: Be aware of the annual contribution limits. In 2023, the 401(k) limit is $22,500 ($30,000 if you're 50 or older), and the IRA limit is $6,500 ($7,500 if you're 50 or older). Make sure to not exceed these limits.

4. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**: Once you’ve maximized your tax-advantaged accounts, consider contributing to a taxable investment account. This can provide more flexibility for withdrawals and investment choices without penalty.

5. **Investment Strategy**: Your investment strategy should reflect your risk tolerance and time horizon. Typically, it’s advisable to hold more aggressive investments in tax-deferred accounts (like stocks) and safer investments (like bonds) in taxable accounts to minimize tax implications on interest and dividends.

6. **Rebalancing**: Regularly review and rebalance your portfolio to ensure it aligns with your investment goals and risk tolerance.

It may also be helpful to consult with a financial advisor for personalized advice based on your individual circumstances.
