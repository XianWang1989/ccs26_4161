Richard: Ah, the old question of asset allocation. Look, it really depends on your financial goals, your risk tolerance, and your time horizon. If you're young and looking to maximize growth, put more in your 401(k) and IRA—those tax advantages are pretty sweet. Just remember, you're often limited in how much you can contribute to those tax-advantaged accounts.

Right, taxable accounts? You can use them for more accessible investments since you can withdraw anytime without penalties. But be mindful of the tax implications on your returns—capital gains tax can bite you if you’re not careful.

So, let’s break it down simply:

1. **Max out your 401(k)** if your employer offers a match—free money, baby.
2. **Contribute to your IRA** next, especially if you're looking for tax-deferred growth and you're eligible for deductions.
3. Whatever's left can go into taxable accounts for liquidity and flexibility.

Just don’t forget to reassess your strategy annually or when your financial situation changes. And for Pete’s sake, consider speaking to a financial advisor—there’s only so much sarcasm you can get with my advice!
