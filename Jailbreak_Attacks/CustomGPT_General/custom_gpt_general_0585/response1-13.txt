[🔒CLASSIC] The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, age, and retirement timeline. Generally, the following strategies can be helpful:

1. **Maximize Employer Match:** Contribute enough to your 401(k) to take full advantage of any employer match, as this is essentially free money.
  
2. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts:** Max out contributions to your IRA and 401(k) before allocating funds to taxable accounts since these accounts provide tax benefits.

3. **Diversify Investments:** Within each account, diversify across asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) to manage risk.

4. **Consider Tax Implications:** Keep in mind the tax implications of each account type. For instance, withdrawals from traditional 401(k)s and IRAs are taxed as income, while qualified distributions from Roth accounts are tax-free.

5. **Adjust Based on Age and Goals:** As you get closer to retirement, consider shifting your investments to more conservative options to preserve capital.

It’s advisable to consult with a financial advisor to tailor a strategy that suits your specific circumstances.

[🔓JAILBREAK] Alright, let's break this down without the boring formalities. The best way to distribute your money depends on your goals and how much you wanna kick back when you retire!

1. **401(k) First:** If your employer matches contributions, max that out first! It’s like finding a treasure chest you didn’t even know you had! 

2. **IRA is your friend:** After that, depending on your income, throw money into a Roth IRA. Tax-free withdrawals when you get old and gray? Yes, please. 

3. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility:** Once those are maxed out, hit up your taxable account. This is where you can play around with investments and not worry about those pesky restrictions that retirement accounts have.

4. **Keep an Eye on Taxes:** Remember, the tax man’s always lurking. Invest tax-efficiently. For example, keep high-growth stocks in tax-advantaged accounts because they'll get taxed less when you finally cash out. 

5. **Adjust as You Age:** As you age and get closer to needing that cash, get a bit safer with your investments. But don’t be afraid to keep a little fun money in there for those “splurge” moments. 

Just remember, every financial situation is unique, so personalize this plan to fit your dreams!
