To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider these key factors:

1. **Tax Efficiency**:
   - **401(k) and IRA**: These accounts offer tax-deferred growth, meaning you won't pay taxes on gains until withdrawal. Prioritize these for investments that are likely to grow significantly, as you can maximize tax benefits.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for investments that generate taxable income, like bonds or certain mutual funds, and consider tax-efficient strategies like holding investments for longer than a year to benefit from lower long-term capital gains rates.

2. **Contribution Limits**:
   - Ensure you are maximizing contributions to your 401(k) and IRA, especially if your employer offers matching contributions in the 401(k). For 2024, the IRA limit is $6,500 ($7,500 if you're 50 or older), and the 401(k) limit is $23,000 ($30,000 for those 50 and older).

3. **Investment Horizon**:
   - If your timeline for needing the funds is long (10+ years), lean more heavily on growth-oriented investments like stocks in tax-advantaged accounts. For shorter timelines, consider more conservative investments in taxable accounts.

4. **Withdrawal Strategy**:
   - Plan for how and when you’ll need to access these funds. IRAs and 401(k)s have penalties for early withdrawal, which could affect your distribution strategy.

5. **Diversification**:
   - Keep a diversified portfolio across all accounts to manage risk effectively. Typically, you’ll want a mix of stocks, bonds, and other assets.

6. **Regular Review**:
   - Reassess your allocation annually or whenever significant life changes occur (such as a change in income or retirement plans).

**Example Distribution Strategy**:
- Max out your 401(k) to take advantage of employer matching.
- Contribute to an IRA up to the limit.
- Any additional savings can go into taxable accounts, focused on growth or tax-efficient investments.

Using a financial advisor can also provide personalized guidance based on your specific circumstances and goals.
