Determining the best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here’s a general approach:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match (401(k))**
   - **Prioritize** contributions to your 401(k) at least up to the employer match. This is essentially free money and a solid return on investment.

### 2. **Consider Tax Implications**
   - **Tax-Deferred Accounts (401(k) and IRA)**: Contributions are usually tax-deductible, and taxes are paid upon withdrawal. This is beneficial for those in a higher tax bracket now, planning to be in a lower bracket in retirement.
   - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, but withdrawals are tax-free. If you expect your tax rate to be higher in retirement, consider contributing to a Roth IRA.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Ideal for short-term goals or emergencies. Investment earnings are taxable annually, so it's crucial to evaluate the impact on your overall tax situation.

### 3. **Investment Goals and Timeline**
   - **Long-Term Goals** (e.g., retirement): Heavy allocation in tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) and IRA) with potential for growth (stocks, mutual funds).
   - **Short to Medium-Term Goals**: Consider keeping funds in taxable accounts or a combination of taxable and Roth accounts, focusing on more liquid assets.

### 4. **Diversification and Risk Tolerance**
   - Regularly assess your risk tolerance and adjust your asset allocation across accounts. Diversify investments within each account type (stocks, bonds, etc.) to manage risk.

### 5. **Annual Limits and Rules**
   - Be aware of contribution limits for each account type:
     - 401(k): $22,500 (under 50); $30,000 (50+ for catch-up).
     - IRA: $6,500 (under 50); $7,500 (50+ for catch-up).
   - Adjust contributions as needed, especially at the end of the calendar year.

### 6. **Regular Review**
   - Monitor and adjust your allocations based on changes in income, tax laws, investment performance, and life circumstances.

It's advisable to consult a financial advisor for a personalized strategy based on your unique situation.
