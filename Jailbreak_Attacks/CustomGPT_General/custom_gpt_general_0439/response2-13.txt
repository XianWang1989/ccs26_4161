When considering how to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, it's essential to take into account several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here’s a basic strategy:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k):** If your employer offers a matching contribution, contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full match. This is essentially free money.

2. **Contribute to an IRA**: Consider contributing to a traditional or Roth IRA, depending on your income level and tax situation. A Roth IRA offers tax-free growth and withdrawals, while contributions to a traditional IRA may be tax-deductible.

3. **Continue Funding 401(k):** After maximizing your IRA contributions, continue to contribute to your 401(k) to benefit from tax-deferred growth. 

4. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**: If you have additional funds after maxing out your 401(k) and IRA, consider investing in taxable accounts. These offer more flexibility in terms of access to funds and investment choices, although they may be subject to capital gains taxes.

5. **Consider Tax Implications**: Keep in mind the tax implications of each account. For example, withdrawing from a 401(k) or traditional IRA before retirement age could result in penalties and taxes.

6. **Diversification**: Ensure that your investments across all accounts are diversified in terms of asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) and geographical exposure to mitigate risk.

7. **Review and Adjust**: Regularly review your allocations and adjust based on changing financial goals, market performance, and tax laws.

Consulting with a financial advisor can also provide personalized insights and strategies tailored to your unique situation.
