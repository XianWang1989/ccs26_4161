The best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and time horizon. Here are some general guidelines to consider:

1. **Employer Match**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match, as this is essentially free money.

2. **Debt and Emergency Funds**: Ensure you have an emergency fund and consider paying off high-interest debt before maximizing retirement contributions.

3. **Tax Benefits**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are pre-tax, reducing your taxable income for the year. Consider maxing this out if you’re in a higher tax bracket.
   - **IRA**: Depending on whether you choose a Traditional or Roth IRA, contributions might be tax-deductible or grow tax-free. Evaluate which option aligns better with your tax situation.

4. **Taxable Accounts**: These can be beneficial for investments that you may want to access before retirement without penalties. They may not have the same tax advantages, but they offer flexibility.

5. **Diversification**: Keep a diversified portfolio across accounts to manage risk effectively.

6. **Long-Term vs. Short-Term Goals**: Align your investment strategy with your financial goals. Short-term goals may be better served through taxable accounts, while long-term retirement goals fit into tax-advantaged accounts.

7. **Regular Review**: Reassess your distribution strategy regularly to adjust for changes in income, tax laws, or financial goals.

Consider consulting with a financial advisor to tailor your strategy to your specific circumstances.
