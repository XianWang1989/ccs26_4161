Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts can depend on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here’s a general strategy to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**: If your employer offers a matching contribution, aim to contribute enough to get the full match. This is essentially "free money."

2. **Contribute to IRA**: After maximizing your 401(k) match, consider contributing to a Traditional or Roth IRA. Roth IRAs provide tax-free growth, ideal if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement.

3. **Return to 401(k)**: Once you’ve maxed out your IRA contributions, return to your 401(k) to increase your contributions, especially if you can contribute up to the annual limit.

4. **Taxable Accounts**: Use taxable accounts for investments that might generate higher tax bills, like bonds or actively managed funds, as these can lead to short-term capital gains. This accounts can also serve as an emergency fund or for short-term goals.

5. **Consider Tax Implications**: Be mindful of the tax implications for each account. For instance, keep high-growth investments in tax-advantaged accounts to avoid capital gains taxes.

6. **Diversify Investments**: Ensure your investments across all accounts are appropriately diversified according to your risk tolerance and time horizon.

7. **Reassess Regularly**: Regularly review your asset allocation and make adjustments as needed based on performance and life changes.

Consulting a financial advisor can provide personalized advice tailored to your specific situation.
