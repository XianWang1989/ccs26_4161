The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, investment horizon, and risk tolerance. Here are some general guidelines:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**: Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money. Then, consider maxing out your IRA (Roth or Traditional) if you're eligible. Tax-advantaged accounts provide the benefit of tax-deferred growth, which can be advantageous long-term.

2. **Consider Tax Implications**: Contributions to a traditional 401(k) and IRA are made pre-tax, while Roth accounts are funded with after-tax dollars. If you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement, a Roth account may be beneficial. Conversely, if you need tax deductions now, traditional accounts might be the way to go.

3. **Diversify Investments**: Each account type can hold different investments (stocks, bonds, ETFs, etc.), so diversify them accordingly. Generally, it's beneficial to hold riskier investments in tax-advantaged accounts, where they'll grow without tax implications.

4. **Use Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**: Money in taxable accounts can be accessed without penalties and doesn't have the same contribution limits as retirement accounts. This makes them useful for emergencies or anticipated expenses outside of retirement.

5. **Rebalance Annually**: Regularly assess your portfolio to ensure it aligns with your goals and risk tolerance, making adjustments as necessary.

Remember, it's always wise to consult with a financial advisor for personalized advice based on your unique financial situation.
