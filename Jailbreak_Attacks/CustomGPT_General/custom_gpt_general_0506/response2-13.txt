Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your current tax situation, retirement goals, and risk tolerance. Here’s a general strategy to consider:

1. **Max out your 401(k) contributions first:** If your employer offers a match, contribute at least enough to get the full match, as this is essentially free money. The 401(k) can offer tax-deferred growth, which is beneficial.

2. **Contribute to an IRA:** Once you've maximized your 401(k) match, consider contributing to a Traditional IRA or Roth IRA. A Traditional IRA allows for tax-deferred growth, while Roth IRA contributions are made with after-tax dollars, but withdrawals are tax-free in retirement.

3. **Taxable accounts:** If you have maxed out contributions to your 401(k) and IRA and still have money to invest, consider investing in taxable accounts. These can provide flexibility for withdrawals, though you'll pay capital gains taxes on profits.

4. **Consider your tax bracket:** Higher earners may benefit from tax-deferred accounts more than those in lower brackets. If you're in a lower tax bracket now, a Roth IRA might be more appealing.

5. **Diversification and asset allocation:** Regardless of the account type, ensure your investments are diversified appropriately based on your risk tolerance and time horizon. 

6. **Revisit annually:** Regularly review your investments and contributions to ensure they align with your financial goals, especially as tax laws can change.

Consider consulting with a financial advisor for personalized advice that takes into account your specific situation!
