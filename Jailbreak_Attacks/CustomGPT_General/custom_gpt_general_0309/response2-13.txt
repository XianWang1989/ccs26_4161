Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts effectively depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here’s a general strategy you might consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to get any employer match. This is essentially free money.

2. **IRA Contributions**: Consider contributing to a traditional or Roth IRA next, as these accounts provide tax advantages. A Roth IRA is beneficial if you anticipate being in a higher tax bracket in retirement.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: After maximizing contributions to your tax-advantaged accounts, use taxable accounts for additional investing. These accounts offer more flexibility for withdrawals, though they may incur capital gains taxes.

4. **Diversification**: Allocate your investments across different asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) in each account to manage risk effectively.

5. **Rebalance Regularly**: Monitor and adjust your allocations as necessary based on performance and changes in your life circumstances.

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: Personal circumstances vary, so consider professional advice to tailor a strategy that suits your specific needs.

By following this approach, you can work towards optimizing your retirement savings and overall financial health.
