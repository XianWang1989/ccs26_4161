Distributing your money across a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment time horizon. Here are some general guidelines:

1. **Maximize Contributions**:
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match, as it’s essentially free money.
   - **IRA**: Consider contributing the maximum allowed ($6,500 for individuals under 50, $7,500 for those 50 and older for 2023).

2. **Tax Considerations**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are pre-tax, which may lower your taxable income now. However, withdrawals in retirement will be taxed.
   - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, allowing for tax-free withdrawals in retirement. Consider this if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket later.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Ideally for funds you might need in the short term, or for investments that generate favorable long-term capital gains.

3. **Investment Time Horizon**:
   - If you’re younger and have a longer time horizon, higher-risk, higher-reward investments might be suitable. Conversely, if you’re closer to retirement, consider more conservative investments.

4. **Diversification**:
   - Ensure a mix of asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) in each account. Given the different tax treatments, you might place higher-growth investments in tax-advantaged accounts and income-generating assets (like bonds) in taxable accounts.

5. **Regularly Review and Adjust**:
   - As your financial situation changes or after significant life events, review and adjust your contributions and allocations accordingly.

### Example Allocation Strategy:
- **401(k)**: Max out to the employer match, then contribute up to the annual limit if feasible.
- **IRA**: Contribute regularly, possibly prioritizing a Roth IRA if eligible.
- **Taxable Accounts**: Use this for excess savings and investing any leftover cash flow after maxing out tax-advantaged accounts.

Consulting a financial advisor can provide personalized advice tailored to your unique situation.
