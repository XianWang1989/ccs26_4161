Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here’s a general strategy to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match (401(k))**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match, as this is essentially “free money.”

2. **Contribute to an IRA**: If eligible, contribute to a traditional IRA or Roth IRA. Traditional IRAs can offer tax deductions, while Roth IRAs allow for tax-free growth.

3. **Flexible Contributions (Taxable Accounts)**: Once you’ve maximized contributions to the above, consider taxable accounts for more flexibility in withdrawals and investment choices.

4. **Consider Tax Implications**:
   - **401(k) and Traditional IRA**: Tax-deferred growth, but withdrawals are taxed as income.
   - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are after-tax, but withdrawals are tax-free.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Taxes on dividends and capital gains apply, so consider investments with lower turnover (like index funds).

5. **Invest Based on Risk Tolerance and Time Horizon**: Allocate your investments appropriately, considering factors such as your age, risk tolerance, and retirement timeline.

6. **Rebalance Regularly**: Periodically review and adjust your asset allocation based on performance and changes in your financial situation.

Consulting with a financial advisor can help tailor this strategy to your specific needs.
