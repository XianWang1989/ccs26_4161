Determining the best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and time horizon. Here are some guidelines to consider:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**
   - If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full match. This is essentially free money.

### 2. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**
   - After maximizing the employer match, consider maxing out contributions to an IRA (Traditional or Roth) based on your eligibility and current tax situation.
   - **Traditional IRA**: Contributions may be tax-deductible, lowering your taxable income.
   - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, allowing for tax-free withdrawals in retirement.

### 3. **Consider Investment Options and Fees**
   - Compare the investment options and fees in your 401(k) versus your IRA. Some 401(k) plans have limited choices or higher fees.

### 4. **Taxable Accounts for Additional Goals**
   - Use taxable accounts for money that you might need in the near term or for specific goals (e.g., buying a house, travel).
   - Be aware of capital gains taxes when investing in taxable accounts.

### 5. **Emergency Fund**
   - Ensure you have an adequate emergency fund before heavily investing in these accounts. A common recommendation is 3-6 months' worth of living expenses.

### 6. **Diversification and Asset Allocation**
   - Diversify your investments across accounts based on risk tolerance, time horizon, and investment strategy.

### 7. **Withdrawals and RMDs**
   - Consider the implications of required minimum distributions (RMDs) from traditional accounts starting at age 73.

### Conclusion
A balanced approach involving maximizing employer contributions, utilizing tax-advantaged accounts, and maintaining liquidity through taxable accounts generally offers the best strategy. It’s often wise to consult with a financial advisor to tailor this approach to your unique situation.
