When it comes to distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, it's important to consider several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and time horizon. Here's a general approach to help you optimize your contributions across these account types:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a matching contribution for your 401(k), prioritize contributions up to the match limit. This is essentially free money.

2. **Consider Tax Implications**: 
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are made pre-tax, reducing your taxable income now, but you'll pay taxes when you withdraw in retirement. If your tax rate is lower in retirement, this can be advantageous.
   - **Traditional IRA**: Similar benefits as a 401(k), with potential tax deductibility based on income and other factors.
   - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are made after-tax, allowing for tax-free withdrawals in retirement. This could be beneficial if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket later.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: These are subject to capital gains taxes upon sale of investments. They offer flexibility and can be useful for short- to medium-term goals.

3. **Diversify Investments**: Regardless of account type, diversify your investments to mitigate risk. Consider a mix of stocks, bonds, and other asset classes based on your risk tolerance and time horizon.

4. **Create a Balanced Strategy**:
   - **Short-Term Goals**: Fund your taxable accounts for shorter-term needs (like a house down payment) since these accounts are more accessible without penalties.
   - **Medium-Term Goals**: Continue prioritizing your 401(k) and IRA, particularly if you’re aiming for retirement.
   - **Long-Term Goals**: Maximize contributions to tax-advantage accounts like your 401(k) and IRA, and consider using a Roth IRA for tax-free growth.

5. **Reassess Regularly**: Your financial situation and goals may change. Regularly review and adjust your contributions to ensure alignment with your current objectives.

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: While these tips provide a solid foundation, a professional can offer personalized advice tailored to your specific situation.

Ultimately, the best approach is one that balances immediate needs with long-term goals while considering tax implications.
