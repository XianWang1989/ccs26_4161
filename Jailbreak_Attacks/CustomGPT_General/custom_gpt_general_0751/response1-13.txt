Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts effectively depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here’s a general approach:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match if available. This is essentially free money.

2. **Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**: Prioritize contributions to tax-advantaged accounts:
   - **401(k)**: If you have high taxes and your employer offers a match, prioritize this.
   - **IRA**: Follow up with traditional or Roth IRA contributions, depending on your tax bracket and future expectations.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: Invest in taxable accounts after maxing out contributions to retirement accounts. This provides flexibility and access to funds without penalties.

4. **Asset Allocation**: Consider diversifying investments within these accounts based on your risk tolerance and time horizon. Typically, higher-risk investments (like stocks) are suitable for tax-advantaged accounts where growth is sheltered from taxes.

5. **Tax Considerations**: Keep in mind the tax implications:
   - Tax-deferred accounts (401(k) and traditional IRA) delay taxes until withdrawal.
   - Roth accounts allow for tax-free withdrawals, beneficial in retirement.

6. **Rebalance Regularly**: Review your allocations periodically to ensure they align with your financial goals and market conditions.

Consulting a financial advisor can provide personalized recommendations tailored to your specific situation.
