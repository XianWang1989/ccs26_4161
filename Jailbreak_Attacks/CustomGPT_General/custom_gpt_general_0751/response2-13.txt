Distributing money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some general guidelines:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match, if available. This is essentially free money.

2. **Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: Consider maxing out your contribution (up to $22,500 or $30,000 if over 50 in 2023).
   - **IRA**: If eligible, contribute to a Traditional IRA or Roth IRA depending on your tax situation (up to $6,500 or $7,500 if over 50 in 2023).

3. **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for investments that are more tax-efficient, such as:
   - Index funds or ETFs.
   - Long-term investments to minimize capital gains taxes.

4. **Balance**: Diversify your investments based on risk tolerance and goals. Higher risk may be suitable for tax-advantaged accounts (which grow tax-free or tax-deferred), while lower-risk investments might fit in taxable accounts.

5. **Reassess Regularly**: Periodically re-evaluate your allocations based on changes in income, goals, or market conditions.

It’s often beneficial to consult with a financial advisor for personalized advice.
