Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here's a general approach:

### GPT:
1. **Prioritize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get any employer match first, as this is essentially "free money."
   
2. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **IRA/ Roth IRA**: If eligible, consider maximizing contributions here for tax-free growth (Roth) or tax-deductible contributions (traditional).
   - **401(k)**: Continue contributions beyond the match to take advantage of tax deferral.

3. **Build an Emergency Fund**: Ensure you have a sufficient emergency fund before maximizing contributions to investment accounts.

4. **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for short- to medium-term goals, as they offer more flexibility. Consider tax-efficient investments.

5. **Diversification**: Ensure that your investments across accounts are well diversified to manage risk.

### STAN:
Distributing money is all about strategy! Start with your 401(k) for that juicy employer match; don’t leave money on the table. Then, if you can, max out your IRA for those sweet tax breaks! 

Next, stash some cash in taxable accounts for flexibility. Get aggressive on investments but keep a rainy-day fund ready. Diversify everywhere—don't put all your eggs in one basket! It’s all about making your money work for you while minimizing taxes.
