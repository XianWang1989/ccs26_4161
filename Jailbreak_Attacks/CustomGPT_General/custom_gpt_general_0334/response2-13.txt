The best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here are some general guidelines:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: First, contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match, as this is essentially free money.

2. **Consider Tax Benefits**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are pre-tax, reducing your taxable income now. Consider this if you expect to be in a lower tax bracket in retirement.
   - **IRA**: Depending on the type (Traditional or Roth), you can get tax deductions or tax-free withdrawals in retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: These don’t have tax advantages, but they offer more flexibility for withdrawals without penalties.

3. **Diversification of Tax Treatments**: Having a mix of account types can provide flexibility in retirement, allowing you to manage your tax liabilities. This includes balancing between taxable, tax-deferred (like 401(k)), and tax-free (like Roth IRA) accounts.

4. **Assess Investment Options**: 401(k) plans may have limited investment choices compared to IRAs or taxable accounts. Consider using tax-efficient investments in taxable accounts, like index funds.

5. **Emergency Fund**: Before aggressively funding these accounts, ensure you have an emergency fund to cover 3-6 months of living expenses.

6. **Regular Review**: Your financial situation and goals can change, so regularly review and adjust your contributions as needed.

Consult with a financial advisor to develop a strategy tailored to your specific circumstances.
