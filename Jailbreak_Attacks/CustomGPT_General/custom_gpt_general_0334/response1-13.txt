The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, investment strategy, and time horizon. Here are some general guidelines to consider:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**: 
   - **401(k)**: If your employer offers a 401(k) match, contribute at least enough to get the full match, as this is essentially "free money."
   - **IRA**: Consider maxing out your contributions to an IRA (Traditional or Roth) next, depending on your income level and eligibility.

2. **Evaluate Your Tax Situation**:
   - A **Traditional 401(k)** and **Traditional IRA** allow for tax-deferred growth, but you'll pay taxes on withdrawals in retirement.
   - A **Roth IRA** allows for tax-free growth and tax-free withdrawals in retirement, which can be advantageous if you expect your tax rate to be higher in the future.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for investments that may yield capital gains or dividends, as these are taxed differently than ordinary income.

3. **Consider Your Time Horizon**:
   - If you have a long time until retirement, you may prefer to invest more heavily in stocks for growth, primarily in tax-advantaged accounts where you won't be taxed until withdrawal.
   - As you get closer to retirement, you may want to shift some investments into more stable assets, potentially using your taxable account for immediate liquidity needs.

4. **Diversify Investments**:
   - Ensure that your investments are diversified across asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) and sectors, regardless of the account type.

5. **Reassess Regularly**:
   - Regularly review and adjust your contributions and investment allocations based on changes in your financial situation, market conditions, and retirement goals.

It’s often wise to consult with a financial advisor to create a tailored strategy that best fits your unique financial situation and goals.
