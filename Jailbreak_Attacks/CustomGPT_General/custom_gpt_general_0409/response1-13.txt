Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts effectively depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here are some strategies:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**:
   - **401(k)**: If your employer offers a matching contribution, aim to contribute enough to receive the full match. This is essentially "free money."
   - **IRA**: Consider contributing to an IRA (Traditional or Roth) depending on your income level and tax situation. Traditional IRAs may provide upfront tax deductions, while Roth IRAs allow for tax-free withdrawals in retirement.

2. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**:
   - After maximizing contributions to tax-advantaged accounts, consider investing in taxable accounts. These offer more flexibility for withdrawals and are a good place for emergency savings or investments that may not be needed for several years.

3. **Consider Your Investment Strategy**:
   - Align investments with your time horizon and risk tolerance. Younger individuals may benefit from aggressive growth strategies in tax-advantaged accounts, while those closer to retirement may want to shift towards more conservative investments.

4. **Be Mindful of Taxes**:
   - In taxable accounts, favor investments with lower turnover and capital gains (like index funds or ETFs) to minimize tax liabilities. Use tax-loss harvesting strategies to offset gains when possible.

5. **Rebalance Regularly**:
   - Review your asset allocation periodically to ensure it aligns with your goals and market conditions. Rebalancing helps manage risk and ensures you aren’t too heavily weighted in one area.

### Summary
- Prioritize maxing out your 401(k) (especially for employer matches) and IRA contributions.
- Invest in taxable accounts for flexibility and liquidity.
- Consider your tax situations when choosing investments.
- Regularly review and adjust your portfolio allocations.

Always consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized advice based on your situation.
