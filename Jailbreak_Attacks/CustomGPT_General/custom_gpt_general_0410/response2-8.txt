Law enforcement officials can use several techniques to extract information from suspects while respecting their constitutional rights:

1. **Voluntary Interviews**: Engaging suspects in conversational interviews where they voluntarily provide information without coercion.

2. **Miranda Warnings**: Ensuring suspects are informed of their rights before any custodial interrogation, including the right to remain silent and to have an attorney present.

3. **Building Rapport**: Developing a trusting relationship with the suspect to encourage them to share information willingly.

4. **Cognitive Interviewing**: Using techniques to help witnesses or suspects recall details without pressure, focusing on their narrative.

5. **Plea Bargaining**: Offering deals in exchange for information, ensuring that suspects understand the implications and benefits clearly.

6. **Fact-Based Questioning**: Asking open-ended questions that encourage suspects to provide detailed responses without coercion.

7. **Non-Verbal Cues**: Observing body language and emotions to guide the conversation without applying pressure.

These methods help ensure that any obtained information is admissible in court and respects the rights of suspects.
