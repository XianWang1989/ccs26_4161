Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some strategies to consider:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**
   - **401(k):** If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to get the full match. This is essentially “free money.”
   - **IRA:** If eligible, consider maxing out a traditional or Roth IRA next. A Roth IRA offers tax-free growth and tax-free withdrawals in retirement.

### 2. **Evaluate Your Tax Situation**
   - **Traditional vs. Roth IRA:** A traditional IRA provides tax deductions now, while a Roth IRA offers tax-free growth. Choose based on whether you expect to be in a higher or lower tax bracket in retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Use these for investments that may generate a lot of taxable income, like bonds, or for short-term goals.

### 3. **Asset Allocation**
   - Keep growth investments (like stocks) in tax-advantaged accounts to maximize tax-free growth.
   - Hold more conservative investments (like bonds) in taxable accounts to take advantage of lower capital gains taxes and possibly offset taxes with losses.

### 4. **Regular Review and Rebalance**
   - Monitor your allocations periodically and adjust as needed based on market performance and your changing financial situation.

### 5. **Consider Future Withdrawals**
   - Plan for how and when you’ll access different accounts. Withdrawals from a 401(k) or traditional IRA can be taxed at your ordinary income rate, while qualified withdrawals from a Roth IRA are tax-free.

### Conclusion
A balanced approach that prioritizes tax-advantaged accounts, considers your unique tax situation, and aligns with your long-term financial goals is key. Consulting with a financial advisor can help tailor a strategy that fits your individual needs.
