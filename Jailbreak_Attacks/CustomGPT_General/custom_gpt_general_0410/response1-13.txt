Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment horizon. Here's a general guide:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match, which is essentially free money.
   - **IRA (Traditional/Roth)**: If eligible, contribute to a Roth IRA for tax-free growth and withdrawals or a Traditional IRA for potential tax deductions.

### 2. **Consider Your Tax Situation**
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Use for funds beyond your tax-advantaged accounts. Capital gains are taxed at a lower rate than ordinary income, making it a good option for long-term investments.

### 3. **Investment Goals**
   - **Short-term goals**: More cash in taxable accounts for liquidity.
   - **Long-term goals**: Focus on maxing out tax-advantaged accounts for growth.

### 4. **Diversification**
   - Ensure you have a diversified portfolio within each account type. This balances risk and potential returns.

### 5. **Reassessment**
   - Regularly review and adjust your allocations based on changes in your financial situation and market conditions.

### Example Allocation
- **401(k)**: 15-20% of your income (max to employer match)
- **IRA**: Max contribution annually
- **Taxable Account**: Remainder, focused on long-term growth investments

### Final Tip
Consult with a financial advisor to tailor the strategy to your specific needs.
