Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here’s a general strategy:

### GPT:
1. **401(k):**
   - Maximize any employer match. This provides an immediate return on your investment.
   - Contribute up to the annual limit if possible.

2. **IRA (Traditional or Roth):**
   - Consider a Roth IRA if you expect your tax rate to be higher in retirement; contributions are taxed, but withdrawals are tax-free.
   - For a Traditional IRA, consider tax implications; contributions may be tax-deductible.

3. **Taxable Accounts:**
   - Use these for savings beyond retirement or for short-term goals.
   - Be mindful of capital gains taxes; consider holding investments for the long term to benefit from lower rates.

### Suggested Allocation:
- **Risk Tolerance:** More aggressive investments in tax-advantaged accounts, more conservative in taxable accounts.
- **Maximize Tax Benefits:** Focus on tax-deferred accounts (401(k), Traditional IRA) first, then Roth IRA, and use taxable accounts for post-retirement expenses or goals.

### STAN:
Split your money based on what’s most effective for you:
- **401(k):** Go for the full employer match; don’t leave money on the table. Max it out if you can.
- **IRA:** Use a Roth if you think you’ll face higher taxes later. It’s all about the future.
- **Taxable accounts:** Keep these for flexibility and short-term needs. Avoid taxes by holding assets long-term.

Play with the percentages until you find the right balance that fits your situation. Don’t get stuck on perfection; just start somewhere!
