Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment preferences. Here are some key considerations:

1. **401(k)**: Maximize contributions to take advantage of employer matches and tax-deferred growth. Contributing the maximum allowable amount can also lower your taxable income.

2. **IRA**: If eligible, consider contributing to a Roth IRA for tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement, or a Traditional IRA for tax-deductible contributions. Prioritize maxing out annual contributions if your cash flow allows.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for money you might need in the short to medium term. Focus on tax-efficient investments, like index funds or ETFs, to minimize capital gains taxes.

### Suggested Allocation Strategy:
- **Short-term goals**: Keep funds in a taxable account for quick access.
- **Retirement**: Prioritize maxing out your 401(k) and IRA, as they provide tax advantages.
- **Emergency Fund**: Ensure you have savings in a liquid account to cover unexpected expenses.

Consider consulting a financial advisor to craft a specific plan tailored to your unique circumstances.
