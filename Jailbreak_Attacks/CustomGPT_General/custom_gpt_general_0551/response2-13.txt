To effectively distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Begin by contributing enough to your 401(k) to receive any employer match, as this is essentially free money.

2. **Increase IRA Contributions**: If you're eligible, prioritize contributing to a Roth or traditional IRA, especially if you want tax-free growth or expect to be in a higher tax bracket in the future.

3. **Focus on Taxable Accounts**: After maxing out retirement accounts, use taxable accounts for additional savings. They offer flexibility in accessing funds without penalties.

4. **Asset Allocation**: Place more tax-efficient investments (like index funds or ETFs) in taxable accounts and consider tax-inefficient funds (like bond funds or REITs) for tax-deferred accounts.

5. **Review and Adjust**: Reassess your distribution annually based on changes in income, expenses, and financial goals.

This balanced approach can help optimize your tax situation while building wealth for retirement.
