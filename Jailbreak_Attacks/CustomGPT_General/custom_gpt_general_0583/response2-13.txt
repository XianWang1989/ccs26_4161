The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here are some strategies to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Contributions**: If your employer offers a match on your 401(k), contribute enough to get the full match first. This is essentially "free money" and provides an immediate return on your investment.

2. **Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**: Focus on maxing out tax-advantaged accounts like your 401(k) and IRA. Both offer tax benefits that can enhance your long-term growth:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions reduce your taxable income in the year you make them, and growth is tax-deferred until withdrawal.
   - **Traditional IRA**: Similar tax benefits as a 401(k), but with different contribution limits and rules regarding withdrawals.
   - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, but withdrawals in retirement are tax-free. This can be beneficial if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket later.

3. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**: Once you've maximized your contributions to tax-advantaged accounts, you can invest additional funds in taxable accounts. These accounts provide more flexibility in terms of withdrawals and access to funds before retirement, though investment gains may be taxed annually.

4. **Asset Allocation**: Consider how to allocate your investments among stocks, bonds, and other assets based on your risk tolerance and investment horizon. Generally, you might want to keep more aggressive investments (like stocks) in your tax-advantaged accounts where you won't pay taxes on gains until withdrawal.

5. **Strategic Withdrawals**: Be mindful of your withdrawal strategy in retirement. Lower your tax burden by planning to withdraw funds from your accounts in a way that minimizes taxes. Withdraw from your taxable accounts first, then tax-deferred accounts, and lastly from Roth accounts if possible.

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: Given the complexity of personal finance and tax laws, consider consulting a financial advisor who can provide personalized advice based on your specific situation.

By following these strategies, you can create a balanced and tax-efficient distribution of your funds that aligns with your long-term financial goals.
