Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals and situation. Here’s a general approach:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer matching contributions, as this is effectively free money.

2. **Contribute to IRA**: If you’re eligible, contribute to a traditional or Roth IRA, depending on your tax situation. Roth IRAs are advantageous if you expect your tax rate to be higher in retirement.

3. **Continue 401(k) Contributions**: After maximizing your IRA contributions, continue adding to your 401(k) until you reach the annual limit if you have additional funds.

4. **Taxable Accounts**: After maxing out tax-advantaged accounts, consider putting any additional savings into taxable accounts. This is particularly useful for investments that are more tax-efficient.

5. **Diversification**: Ensure that your investments in all accounts are diversified according to your risk tolerance and investment timeline.

### Considerations:
- **Tax Implications**: Understand the tax implications of withdrawals from these accounts.
- **Withdrawal Strategy**: Plan for how and when you will withdraw funds in retirement.
- **Consult a Professional**: A financial advisor can help tailor these strategies to your specific needs.
