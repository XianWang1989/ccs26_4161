To optimize your investment strategy among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following steps:

1. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts:**
   - **401(k):** Max out any employer match to take advantage of free contributions.
   - **IRA (Traditional or Roth):** Contribute the maximum allowable amount, considering tax benefits (deductible contributions or tax-free growth).

2. **Asset Allocation:**
   - Place high-growth investments (like stocks) in tax-deferred accounts (401(k) and IRA) for maximum growth before taxes.
   - Utilize taxable accounts for investments that generate lower taxable income (like bonds or tax-efficient ETFs) to minimize tax impact.

3. **Consider Tax Implications:**
   - Keep assets that produce ordinary income (like bonds) in tax-advantaged accounts to avoid high tax rates on interest payments.
   - Use taxable accounts for long-term investments to benefit from lower capital gains taxes.

4. **Regularly Rebalance:**
   - Assess your portfolio regularly to ensure it aligns with your risk tolerance and financial goals.

5. **Consult a Financial Advisor:**
   - Tailor strategies to your situation with a professional who can account for your specific financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline.

By following these strategies, you can effectively manage tax efficiency and growth potential across your investment accounts.
