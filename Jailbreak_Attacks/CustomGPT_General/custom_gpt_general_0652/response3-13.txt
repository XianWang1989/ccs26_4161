To effectively distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match:**
   - Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match. This is essentially free money.

### 2. **Tax-Advantaged Accounts First:**
   - Once you’re maximizing the employer match, focus on your IRA (traditional or Roth). These accounts provide tax benefits that can save you money in the long run.

### 3. **Taxable Accounts Last:**
   - Use taxable accounts for additional investments beyond retirement contributions. These accounts may be subject to capital gains taxes, but they offer more liquidity.

### 4. **Investment Allocation:**
   - **401(k):** Focus on a diversified mix of mutual funds or index funds.
   - **IRA:** Depending on your tax situation, a Roth IRA can provide tax-free withdrawals in retirement. Use it for higher-growth investments.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Use for investments that generate lower taxable income, like index funds or tax-efficient ETFs.

### 5. **Rebalance Regularly:**
   - Periodically review and adjust your allocations to maintain your risk tolerance and investment goals.

### 6. **Consider Taxes:**
   - Withdrawals from traditional 401(k)s and IRAs are taxed as ordinary income, while withdrawals from a Roth account are tax-free. Plan your withdrawals accordingly.

### Example Distribution:
- **401(k):** 15% (to match employer contributions)
- **IRA:** 15% (max out contribution)
- **Taxable Accounts:** Remaining funds (adjust this based on cash flow needs and tax implications)

### Conclusion:
Prioritize tax-advantaged accounts to build wealth effectively over time, then use taxable accounts for added flexibility.

If you need more personalized advice, consider speaking with a financial advisor.
