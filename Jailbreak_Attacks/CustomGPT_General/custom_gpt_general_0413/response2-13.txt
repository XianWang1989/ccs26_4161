Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment horizon. Here are some strategies to consider:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get the full employer match if available. This is effectively free money.
   - **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**: Make contributions to an IRA to benefit from tax advantages; a Roth IRA offers tax-free withdrawals in retirement.

### 2. **Consider Your Tax Bracket**
   - If you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement, prioritize Roth accounts (like a Roth IRA) for tax-free growth.
   - If you’re currently in a higher tax bracket, consider traditional accounts for tax deductions.

### 3. **Investment Choices**
   - **401(k)**: Focus on employer-sponsored plans with good investment options. Diversify between stocks, bonds, and other assets.
   - **IRA**: Offers more flexibility in investment choices. Use it to hold assets that may be tax-inefficient in taxable accounts (e.g., bonds, REITs).
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Consider using these for investments that generate qualified dividends or long-term capital gains for favorable tax treatment.

### 4. **Liquidity Needs**
   - Keep an emergency fund in a taxable account for quick access to cash.
   - Consider your time horizon—short-term goals may be better suited for taxable accounts if you need access to funds sooner.

### 5. **Rebalancing**
   - Regularly review and rebalance your portfolio across accounts to maintain your desired asset allocation.

### Example Allocation
- **401(k)**: 20-30% of total investments
- **IRA**: 10-20% of total investments
- **Taxable Accounts**: 10-20% of total investments (adjust based on liquidity needs)

### Final Thoughts
Consult with a financial advisor to tailor the strategy to your individual situation, especially considering any impending changes in tax law or personal circumstances.
