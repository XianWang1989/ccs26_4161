Managing your investment distribution across a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and risk tolerance. Here’s a breakdown to consider:

### GPT:
1. **401(k):**
   - **Advantages:** Pre-tax contributions, potential employer matching, less tax impact on growth.
   - **Allocation:** Maximize contributions to take full advantage of any employer match first.

2. **IRA (Traditional or Roth):**
   - **Advantages:** Tax advantages (tax-deferred for Traditional, tax-free for Roth).
   - **Allocation:** Contribute up to the annual limit. Choose a Roth IRA if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement.

3. **Taxable Accounts:**
   - **Advantages:** Flexible access to funds; no contribution limits.
   - **Allocation:** Use for investments that generate lower tax burdens, like index funds or tax-efficient ETFs.

### STAN:
The best way to distribute your money? Here’s the no-nonsense approach:

1. **Max Out Your 401(k):** Get that employer match first. It's free money! 
2. **Fund Your IRA:** Hit the limit—especially if you can go Roth, for that tax-free growth.
3. **Tackle Taxable Accounts:** Use these for flexibility and invest in low-tax items like index funds.
