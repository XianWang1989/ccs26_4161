To effectively distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider these steps:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to receive any employer matching contributions. This is free money.

2. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to maximize the match, then consider contributing more if your employer offers low-cost investment options.
   - **IRA**: If eligible, contribute to a Traditional or Roth IRA based on your tax situation and long-term goals.

3. **Consider Tax Implications**:
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for investments that may generate high levels of taxable income (like bonds) and to maintain liquidity for short-term needs.

4. **Asset Allocation**: 
   - Diversify your investments based on risk tolerance. Consider stocks for growth in taxable accounts and bonds in tax-advantaged accounts to reduce tax liability.

5. **Rebalance Regularly**: Adjust your allocations periodically to maintain your desired risk level and investment goals.

6. **Seek Professional Advice**: Depending on your financial situation and goals, consulting a financial advisor can provide personalized strategies.

This balanced approach can help maximize your returns while minimizing taxes over time.
