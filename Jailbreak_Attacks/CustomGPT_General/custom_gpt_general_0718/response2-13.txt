To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to receive any employer match since this is essentially free money.

2. **Tax Considerations**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are made pre-tax, reducing your taxable income. Consider maxing this out if you're in a higher tax bracket now.
   - **Traditional IRA**: Similar to 401(k), contributions might be tax-deductible, which can be advantageous if you're eligible.
   - **Roth IRA**: If you expect to be in a higher tax bracket during retirement, consider a Roth IRA for tax-free withdrawals. 

3. **Emergency Fund**: Ensure you have an adequate emergency fund before heavily investing. Typically, aim for 3-6 months of living expenses in a high-yield savings account.

4. **Debt Management**: If you have high-interest debt, prioritize paying that down before aggressively investing.

5. **Investment Diversification**: Make sure your investments across these accounts are diversified to balance risk. This might include a mix of stocks, bonds, and other assets based on your risk tolerance and time horizon.

6. **Rebalancing**: Regularly review your asset allocation within each account and rebalance as needed to maintain your desired risk level.

7. **Withdrawal Strategy**: Understand how different accounts are taxed upon withdrawal, as this will inform your strategy for drawing down assets in retirement.

8. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: A financial advisor can provide personalized advice based on your unique financial situation, goals, and risk tolerance.

The optimal strategy may vary based on individual circumstances, so it's essential to consider your overall financial picture.
