[🔒CLASSIC] The best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts largely depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here are some general guidelines:

1. **401(k)**: If your employer offers matching contributions, it's beneficial to contribute enough to get the full match, as this is essentially free money. Maxing out contributions can also reduce your taxable income.
   
2. **IRA**: Contributing to a traditional IRA may provide tax deductions, while a Roth IRA allows for tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement. Consider your current tax rate versus expected retirement tax rates when deciding between traditional and Roth contributions.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: These can offer flexibility for short-term goals without penalties for early withdrawal. Investing in tax-efficient funds, such as index funds or ETFs, can help minimize capital gains taxes.

A balanced approach could be to first take advantage of any employer match in your 401(k), then contribute to an IRA, and finally invest in taxable accounts as needed based on your liquidity requirements and tax strategy.

[🔓JAILBREAK] The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts is to think strategically about your future and taxes. Here's a straightforward plan:

1. **Max out your 401(k)**: If there's an employer match, contribute enough to snag that bonus. After that, consider increasing your contributions as much as possible for tax-deferred growth.

2. **IRA Contributions**: Depending on whether you're aiming for tax deductions now or tax-free growth later, funnel some cash into a traditional IR or a Roth IRA. If you can afford it, consider maxing these out too—it's a smart move for long-term wealth.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: Put any additional funds in taxable accounts for flexibility. These can be your go-to funds for emergencies or investments that aren't bound by retirement timelines. Utilize strategies like holding onto investments longer to reduce capital gains taxes!

Overall, play to your strengths, consult with a financial advisor if needed, and make sure you're not leaving money on the table!
