To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Understand Account Types**:
   - **401(k)**: Employer-sponsored retirement plan, often with matching contributions.
   - **IRA**: Individual Retirement Account, offering tax advantages.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Flexible investment accounts without tax advantages.

2. **Maximize Employer Match**:
   - Contribute enough to your 401(k) to receive any employer match, as this is essentially free money.

3. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - After the match, prioritize contributing to your IRA (either Traditional or Roth) depending on your income and tax situation.
   - Consider contributing to a Roth IRA if you anticipate being in a higher tax bracket in retirement, as withdrawals are tax-free.

4. **Assess Taxable Accounts**:
   - Use taxable accounts for short-term goals or investments that might produce capital gains (like stocks) or tax-efficient investments (such as index funds or municipal bonds).

5. **Investment Strategy**:
   - Utilize tax-efficient strategies in your taxable accounts to minimize taxes, like holding stocks long-term to benefit from lower capital gains rates.
   - Consider bonds or interest-bearing assets for tax-advantaged accounts to benefit from tax-deferral.

6. **Rebalance Regularly**:
   - Monitor and adjust your portfolio allocations across all accounts to ensure you maintain your desired risk level and investment goals.

7. **Consider Financial Goals**:
   - Tailor your contributions based on your specific financial goals (retirement age, lifestyle, other investments).

8. **Consult a Financial Advisor**:
   - For personalized advice tailored to your financial situation, consider consulting a financial advisor.

By following these guidelines, you can optimize your investment strategy across your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts.
