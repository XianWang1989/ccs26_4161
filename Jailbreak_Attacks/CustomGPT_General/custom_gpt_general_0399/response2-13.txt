Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various personal factors like your financial goals, tax situation, and time horizon. Here are some general guidelines:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**
   - If your employer offers a match, contribute at least enough to get the full match. It’s essentially free money.

### 2. **Contribute to IRA**
   - After maximizing your 401(k) match, consider contributing to a Traditional IRA or Roth IRA. Roth IRAs allow for tax-free withdrawals in retirement, while Traditional IRAs provide upfront tax benefits.

### 3. **Continue 401(k) Contributions**
   - If you have additional funds to invest after maxing out your IRA, keep contributing to your 401(k) until you reach the annual limit.

### 4. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**
   - Use taxable accounts for investments that you might need to access before retirement. These accounts can be more flexible in terms of withdrawals.

### 5. **Consider Asset Location**
   - Place tax-efficient investments (like index funds or ETFs) in taxable accounts and higher-tax investments (like bonds or REITs) in tax-advantaged accounts.

### 6. **Rebalance Periodically**
   - Regularly review and adjust your allocations to ensure they align with your risk tolerance and financial goals.

### 7. **Consult a Financial Advisor**
   - A financial advisor can help tailor your strategy to your specific situation, including retirement goals and spending needs.

By following this structured approach, you can create a balanced investment strategy that maximizes growth while managing tax implications.
