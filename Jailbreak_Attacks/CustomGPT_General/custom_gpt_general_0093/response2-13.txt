When distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategic approach:

1. **Maximize Employer Match (401(k))**: If your employer offers a matching contribution, prioritize contributing enough to your 401(k) to take full advantage of the match. This is essentially free money.

2. **Contribute to Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: After the match, consider maximizing your annual contribution limits based on your financial capability and retirement goals.
   - **IRA**: Next, consider contributing to a Traditional IRA or Roth IRA. Roth IRAs provide tax-free growth, whereas Traditional IRAs may offer immediate tax benefits.

3. **Prioritize Taxable Accounts**: Once you’re maximizing contributions to your tax-advantaged accounts, you can begin to invest in taxable accounts. This is especially important for short-term goals, as these funds can be accessed without penalties.

4. **Consider Asset Location**: Place your growth-oriented investments (like stocks) in tax-advantaged accounts to benefit from tax-free growth. Conversely, consider placing income-generating assets (like bonds) in taxable accounts to mitigate taxes on dividends and interest.

5. **Adjust for Tax Bracket**: Depending on your current and expected future tax brackets, you may want to adjust how much you contribute to each account type. If you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement, prioritize Roth contributions.

6. **Review Regularly**: Reassess your distribution strategy regularly—at least annually—to ensure it aligns with your financial goals, risk tolerance, and market conditions.

7. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: If you're uncertain about your distribution strategy, consulting a financial advisor can provide personalized guidance tailored to your specific financial situation.

By taking these factors into account, you can create a balanced approach that maximizes your retirement savings and minimizes tax impacts.
