Determining the best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment strategy. Here's a general approach to consider:

### 1. **Understand Account Types**
   - **401(k):** Employer-sponsored retirement account that often includes matching contributions. Contributions are pre-tax, reducing your taxable income. Withdrawals are taxed as ordinary income.
   - **IRA (Individual Retirement Account):** Can be traditional (pre-tax contributions, taxed upon withdrawal) or Roth (after-tax contributions, tax-free withdrawals). Consider the type based on your current and future tax situation.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Investments not tied to retirement benefits. You pay capital gains tax on profits when you sell assets, and you may also incur taxes on dividends and interest.

### 2. **Evaluate Tax Implications**
   - Prioritize tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) and IRA) for long-term growth and to minimize taxes on compound growth.
   - Maximize contributions to your 401(k) to get any employer match and consider maxing your IRA contributions as well.

### 3. **Set Goals and Time Horizons**
   - **Short-Term Goals:** Use taxable accounts for money needed soon, as they offer more flexibility without penalties for early withdrawals.
   - **Long-Term Goals:** Focus on 401(k) and IRAs for retirement savings, which benefit from tax deferral or tax-free growth.

### 4. **Diversification and Asset Allocation**
   - Diversify investments across all accounts, maintaining a balanced approach based on your risk tolerance and investment goals.
   - Consider holding tax-efficient investments (like index funds) in your taxable account to minimize tax impact.

### 5. **Regularly Review and Rebalance**
   - Periodically review your asset allocation in all accounts and adjust as necessary based on performance, changing goals, or market conditions.

### Example Distribution Strategy
- **401(k):** Contribute enough to get the full employer match, then consider maxing it out.
- **IRA:** Max out traditional or Roth IRA depending on your tax situation.
- **Taxable Accounts:** Use these for additional investments once contributions to retirement accounts are maximized.

### Conclusion
Consulting with a financial advisor can provide personalized recommendations based on your specific financial situation and goals.
