Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here’s a general strategy to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a matching contribution for your 401(k), contribute enough to get the full match first. This is essentially "free money."

2. **Contribute to an IRA**: After maximizing your employer match, consider contributing to a Roth or traditional IRA. Roth IRAs are beneficial if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket during retirement, while traditional IRAs may be advantageous if you want to reduce your current taxable income.

3. **Back to the 401(k)**: If you still have funds available, go back to your 401(k) to further boost your retirement savings, especially if your high-income limit allows for it.

4. **Taxable Accounts**: Once you’ve maxed out tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) and IRA), consider opening a taxable account. This is useful for short- to medium-term goals and offers flexibility for withdrawals without penalties.

5. **Asset Allocation**: Ensure that your asset allocation is aligned with your risk tolerance and investment horizon. Generally, equities are more suitable for long-term growth in tax-advantaged accounts, while bonds and cash-like investments can be better suited for taxable accounts to manage taxes on interest income.

6. **Tax Considerations**: Keep in mind the tax implications of different accounts. Tax-deferred accounts can grow without immediate tax consequences, while taxable accounts may incur capital gains tax.

7. **Regular Review**: Regularly review and adjust your investment allocations based on market conditions, changes in income, and shifts in your financial goals.

Tailoring this strategy to your specific situation with the help of a financial advisor can further optimize your approach.
