Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts effectively involves several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment horizon. Here's a general strategy:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**
   - **Contribute enough to get the full employer match.** This is essentially "free money" and should be prioritized.

### 2. **Consider IRA Contributions**
   - **Traditional IRA vs. Roth IRA:** Depending on your tax situation, you might prefer a Traditional IRA for tax-deferred growth or a Roth IRA for tax-free withdrawals in retirement.
   - **Max Contribution:** Aim to contribute the maximum allowable amount if possible.

### 3. **Utilize 401(k) for Higher Limits**
   - After maximizing the match and IRA contributions, consider increasing your contributions to your 401(k) to benefit from the higher contribution limits.

### 4. **Assess Taxable Accounts**
   - **Invest in Tax-Efficient Funds:** Use taxable accounts for investments that are less tax-efficient, such as stocks, which are generally taxed at lower capital gains rates when held long-term.
   - **Cash for Short-Term Needs:** Keep enough liquidity in taxable accounts for emergencies or short-term goals.

### 5. **Debt Repayment**
   - If you carry high-interest debt, consider allocating some funds toward paying it off alongside your investment contributions.

### 6. **Periodic Review**
   - Regularly review and adjust your allocations based on performance, changes in your financial situation, and retirement goals.

### Conclusion
A general progression could be:
1. Contribute to 401(k) to receive full match.
2. Max out IRA contributions.
3. Increase 401(k) contributions up to the limit.
4. Invest in taxable accounts as needed.

Always consider consulting with a financial advisor to tailor advice to your specific situation.
