Investing strategically in your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts can optimize your savings. Here’s an approach to consider:

### GPT
1. **401(k) Contributions**:
   - Max out any employer match first; this is free money.
   - Focus on tax-advantaged growth; consider a mix of stocks and bonds based on your risk tolerance.

2. **IRA Contributions**:
   - Choose between Traditional and Roth IRA based on your current and future tax bracket.
   - Again, diversify your investments—stocks for growth, bonds for stability.

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - Invest in tax-efficient funds (e.g., index funds or ETFs).
   - Consider holding investments long-term to minimize capital gains taxes.
   - Use tax-loss harvesting strategies to offset gains.

### STAN
For distributing your money, think of it this way:  
1. **401(k)**: Dive in and max it out if your employer matches—get that free cash. Play with a balanced portfolio but lean into growth-heavy stocks for long-term gains.

2. **IRA**: Decide if you want deductions now (Traditional) or tax-free growth later (Roth). Go aggressive with stocks since you’ll have years to recover from any dips.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: Load these up with ETFs or index funds that don’t make your taxes a nightmare. Keep your gains and trades long-term to dodge those pesky capital gains. Maybe even play around with some individual stocks, but be smart about it.

Balance is key, but aim for growth where you can because time is on your side!
