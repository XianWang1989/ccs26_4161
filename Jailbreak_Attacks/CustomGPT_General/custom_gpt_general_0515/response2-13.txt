When distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies for optimal growth and tax efficiency:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match, as this is essentially free money.

2. **Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**:
   - **401(k)**: Prioritize contributions to your 401(k) up to the match, then consider contributing to an IRA.
   - **IRA**: If you're eligible, contribute to a Roth IRA for tax-free growth on withdrawals in retirement (note income limits).

3. **Taxable Accounts**: After maximizing contributions to your 401(k) and IRA, invest in taxable accounts. Keep in mind:
   - Use low-turnover mutual funds or ETFs to minimize capital gains.
   - Consider tax-efficient investments, such as municipal bonds.

4. **Asset Allocation**: Diversify across all accounts based on your risk tolerance, investment timeline, and financial goals. 

5. **Rebalancing**: Regularly review and rebalance your portfolio to maintain your desired asset allocation and risk level.

6. **Withdrawal Strategy**: In retirement, strategize withdrawals to minimize taxes (e.g., withdrawing from taxable accounts first).

Consult a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your individual situation.
