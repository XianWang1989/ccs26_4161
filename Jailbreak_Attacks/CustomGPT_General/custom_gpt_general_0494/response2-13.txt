When distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Maximize Employer Match:** If your employer offers a 401(k) match, prioritize contributing enough to get the full match, as this is essentially free money.

2. **Tax-Advantaged Accounts First:** Focus on funding tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) and IRA) before taxable accounts. These accounts offer tax benefits that can enhance your savings growth.

3. **401(k) Contributions:** Contribute to your 401(k) up to the annual contribution limit, considering both traditional and Roth options if available.

4. **IRA Contributions:** After maxing out your 401(k) match, consider contributing to an IRA (traditional or Roth). The contribution limits for IRAs are generally lower than for 401(k)s, so plan accordingly.

5. **Taxable Accounts:** Once you've maximized contributions to your 401(k) and IRA, allocate funds to taxable accounts for flexibility. Taxable accounts can be useful for shorter-term goals.

6. **Investment Strategy:** Diversify your investments across all accounts based on your risk tolerance, liquidity needs, and investment horizon. Keep tax implications in mind, as different investments may be more or less tax-efficient.

7. **Rebalance Regularly:** Periodically review and adjust your asset allocation to ensure it aligns with your goals and risk profile.

8. **Consult a Financial Advisor:** If you're unsure about your strategy, consider consulting a financial advisor to tailor a plan specific to your financial situation and goals. 

By strategically allocating funds and considering tax implications, you can optimize your overall investment growth.
