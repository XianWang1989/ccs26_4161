Distributing your money effectively among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some general guidelines:

### 1. **Prioritize Contributions**
   - **401(k)**: If your employer offers a match, prioritize contributions to your 401(k) up to the match limit. This is effectively "free money."
   - **IRA**: After maximizing the 401(k) match, consider contributing to a traditional or Roth IRA, depending on your income and tax situation.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for funds you may need access to before retirement or for investments that aren't suitable for tax-advantaged accounts.

### 2. **Consider Tax Implications**
   - **401(k)** and **Traditional IRA**: Contributions are often pre-tax, deferring tax payments until withdrawal during retirement, which can be advantageous if you expect to be in a lower tax bracket then.
   - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, but withdrawals are tax-free in retirement. This can be beneficial if you expect tax rates to rise.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Be mindful of capital gains taxes and interest income.

### 3. **Investment Strategy**
   - **401(k) and IRA**: These accounts typically benefit from a long-term investment strategy due to tax advantages. Consider a diversified portfolio with a mix of stocks, bonds, and other assets suited to your risk tolerance.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Consider investments that are tax-efficient, such as index funds or tax-managed funds, to minimize tax liability.

### 4. **Maintain Flexibility**
   - Reserve some liquidity in your taxable accounts for short-term needs and emergencies.
   - Reassess your distribution strategy periodically based on changes in your financial situation, goals, and market conditions.

### 5. **Consult with a Financial Advisor**
   - Personalized advice from a financial advisor can help tailor your strategy to your specific situation, including projected retirement income needs and tax implications.

This balanced approach can help optimize your overall investment strategy while considering tax efficiency and liquidity needs.
