Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors such as your current financial situation, retirement goals, tax considerations, and investment time horizon. Here’s a general guideline to consider:

1. **401(k) Contributions**:
   - **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match, if available. This is essentially free money.
   - **Consider Contribution Limits**: For 2023, the limit is $22,500 (or $30,000 if you're over 50). Aim to maximize this if possible, especially given the tax advantages.

2. **IRA Contributions**:
   - **Roth vs. Traditional**: Decide between a Roth IRA (tax-free withdrawals in retirement) and a Traditional IRA (tax-deductible contributions). 
   - **Consider Contribution Limits**: For 2023, the limit is $6,500 (or $7,500 if 50 or older). If you qualify, consider maximizing these contributions as well.

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - **Post-Retirement Savings**: Use taxable accounts for funds you may need before retirement age or for flexibility.
   - **Tax-efficient Investments**: Consider stocks or index funds that minimize dividends and capital gains taxes in these accounts. 

4. **Diversification**:
   - Ensure you have a diversified investment strategy across all three accounts. Asset allocation can vary based on your overall investment horizon and risk tolerance.

5. **Emergency Fund**: 
   - Before heavily investing in retirement accounts, ensure you have an emergency fund covering 3-6 months of expenses.

6. **Evaluate Periodically**:
   - Circumstances change, so regularly review and adjust your allocation based on your financial goals and market conditions.

Ultimately, it may be beneficial to consult with a financial advisor who can provide personalized advice based on your specific circumstances.
