Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and time horizon. Here are some strategies:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts:**
   - **401(k):** Contribute enough to get any employer match, as it's essentially free money.
   - **IRA:** If eligible, contribute to a traditional or Roth IRA. Roth IRAs are great for tax-free growth in retirement.

### 2. **Diversify Investment Types:**
   - **401(k):** Consider funds with lower fees and good diversification.
   - **IRA:** Use it for investments that may generate higher taxable income, like bonds or dividend stocks, particularly in a traditional IRA.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Focus on long-term investments like index funds or ETFs to minimize taxes on capital gains.

### 3. **Consider Your Tax Bracket:**
   - If you're in a lower tax bracket now, prioritize Roth accounts (Roth IRA and Roth 401(k)) for tax-free growth.
   - In a higher tax bracket, traditional accounts may be more beneficial for current tax savings.

### 4. **Adjust for Age and Goals:**
   - Younger investors might lean into riskier assets in tax-advantaged accounts for growth.
   - As you approach retirement, shift towards more stable investments to preserve capital.

### 5. **Emergency Funds and Short-Term Needs:**
   - Keep enough liquid assets in your taxable account for emergencies, rather than locking it all in retirement accounts.

### Conclusion:
Consider consulting a financial advisor to tailor a strategy to your specific circumstances, ensuring you maximize growth and minimize taxes over time.
