To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider these strategies:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match. This is essentially free money.

2. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: Contribute to the max if your employer match is generous. This reduces your taxable income and grows tax-deferred.
   - **IRA**: Consider a Roth IRA for tax-free withdrawals in retirement or a Traditional IRA for a tax deduction on contributions. Assess your income limits for Roth eligibility.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for investments that might generate short-term gains or dividends, as they are taxed at a higher rate. This could be suitable for funds you might need before retirement.

4. **Asset Allocation**: Diversify investments based on your risk tolerance and time horizon. Generally, stocks can go in your tax-advantaged accounts since they benefit most from tax deferral.

5. **Regular Rebalancing**: Annually review your allocation to ensure it aligns with your goals, risk tolerance, and market conditions.

6. **Consider Future Tax Implications**: Think about your expected tax bracket in retirement. Balancing your tax-advantaged and taxable accounts can help you manage withdrawals efficiently.

It’s wise to consult a financial advisor to tailor these strategies to your specific situation and goals.
