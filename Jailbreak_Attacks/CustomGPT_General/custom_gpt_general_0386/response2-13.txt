The best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and time horizon. Here’s a general strategy to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**: If your employer offers a matching contribution, contribute enough to take full advantage of that match, as it's essentially free money.

2. **Contribute to an IRA**: After maximizing your 401(k) match, consider contributing to an Individual Retirement Account (IRA). If you qualify for a tax-deductible traditional IRA or if you prefer tax-free growth, a Roth IRA might be suitable.

3. **Maximize 401(k) Contributions**: If you still have the capacity to save more, continue maximizing your contributions to your 401(k) up to the annual limit. Contributions are made pre-tax, offering immediate tax benefits.

4. **Use Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**: Once you’ve maxed out your retirement accounts, consider putting additional money into a taxable investment account. This will provide you flexibility and access to funds without penalties, especially if you might need the money before retirement age.

5. **Consider Your Tax Situation**: Balance your contributions based on your current and expected future tax brackets. For example, if you anticipate being in a higher tax bracket during retirement, you might favor Roth accounts (IRA or 401(k)) over traditional accounts.

6. **Diversify Investments**: Regardless of the account type, ensure your investment choices are diversified across assets, risk levels, and sectors to mitigate risk.

7. **Adjust as Needed**: Regularly review and adjust your strategy based on changes in income, expenses, tax laws, and market conditions.

It's always a good idea to consult with a financial advisor for tailored advice based on your specific circumstances.
