When considering how to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, here are some key strategies:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match—this is free money.

2. **Consider Roth IRAs**: If eligible, consider contributing to a Roth IRA for tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement. Prioritize this if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket later.

3. **Tax Efficiency**: Place tax-inefficient investments (like bonds) in tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) or IRA) to minimize taxes. Keep tax-efficient investments (like index funds or stocks) in taxable accounts.

4. **Diversification**: Ensure a well-diversified portfolio across all accounts, balancing between stocks, bonds, and other assets according to your risk tolerance.

5. **Regular Contributions**: Aim to consistently contribute to all accounts, adjusting the balance based on market conditions, your income, and changing financial goals.

6. **Withdrawal Strategy**: Plan for how and when you’ll withdraw money in retirement to minimize taxes and maximize your income.

Consult a financial advisor to personalize these strategies based on your specific situation, goals, and risk tolerance.
