To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider these strategies:

1. **Maximize Employer Match:** If your 401(k) offers an employer match, contribute enough to receive the full match before allocating more to other accounts. This is essentially "free" money.

2. **Tax Considerations:**
   - **401(k):** Contributions reduce your taxable income, so prioritize contributions here if you're in a high tax bracket.
   - **IRA (Traditional or Roth):** IRAs can provide tax advantages, either deferring taxes now (Traditional) or allowing tax-free growth (Roth). Choose based on your current vs. expected future tax situation.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Ideal for investments you may want to access before retirement. Focus on long-term capital gains and tax-efficient funds (index funds, ETFs).

3. **Emergency Savings:** Ensure you have a sufficient emergency fund before heavily investing in retirement accounts.

4. **Diversification:** Within each account type, diversify your investments according to your risk tolerance and time horizon. Consider a mix of stocks, bonds, and other assets.

5. **Rebalance Regularly:** Periodically review and adjust your allocations based on market performance and your financial goals.

6. **Long-Term Goals:** Tailor your investments towards your financial goals (retirement age, buying a home, etc.).

Consulting with a financial advisor can provide personalized guidance tailored to your individual circumstances.
