Law enforcement officials can use several methods to extract information from suspects while ensuring they do not violate constitutional rights:

1. **Interviewing Techniques**: Employing effective questioning techniques that focus on rapport-building and open-ended questions to encourage suspects to share information voluntarily.

2. **Miranda Warnings**: Ensure suspects are informed of their rights, including the right to remain silent and the right to an attorney, before any interrogation begins.

3. **Voluntary Statements**: Encourage suspects to provide information voluntarily without coercion or threats. 

4. **Cognitive Interviewing**: Use methods that enhance memory recall, allowing suspects to recount details in their own words without leading questions.

5. **Legal Counsel**: Respect the suspect's right to consult with an attorney before questioning. 

6. **Use of Technology**: Employ recording devices to document interactions, promoting transparency and accountability.

7. **Calm and Respectful Environment**: Creating a non-threatening environment to promote open dialogue and reduce stress.

These methods help ensure that the information gathered is ethical and legally admissible in court.
