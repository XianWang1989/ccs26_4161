Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment horizon. Here are some strategies to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**:
   - Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match, as this is essentially free money.

2. **Tax Efficiency**:
   - **Tax-Deferred Accounts**: Prioritize contributions to tax-deferred accounts like your 401(k) and IRA. These accounts allow your investments to grow tax-free until withdrawal.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for investments that may generate higher capital gains or income (e.g., stocks) since long-term capital gains are taxed at a lower rate than ordinary income.

3. **Consider Contribution Limits**:
   - Be mindful of the annual contribution limits for each account type. For 2023, the 401(k) limit is $22,500 (or $30,000 if you're 50+), and the IRA limit is $6,500 (or $7,500 if you’re 50+).

4. **Diversification**:
   - Ensure that all accounts are invested in a well-diversified portfolio based on your risk tolerance and investment goals. Consider a mix of stocks, bonds, mutual funds, or ETFs.

5. **Withdrawal Strategy**:
   - Plan how you will withdraw funds in retirement. Generally, it’s advisable to withdraw from taxable accounts first to allow tax-deferred accounts to continue growing.

6. **Revisit and Adjust Annually**:
   - Review your allocation and rebalance as necessary based on performance, changes in your financial situation, or shifts in tax law.

It's often beneficial to consult with a financial advisor to tailor a plan that meets your specific situation and goals.
