The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timelines. Here are some general strategies:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First:**
   - **401(k):** If your employer offers a match, contribute up to at least that amount to take full advantage of the free money.
   - **IRA:** Consider traditional or Roth IRAs based on your income and tax situation. Roth IRAs are beneficial for tax-free withdrawals in retirement.

### 2. **Tax Efficiency:**
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Use these for investments that generate lower taxable income, such as index funds or ETFs. This can help minimize capital gains taxes.

### 3. **Investment Goals:**
   - **Short-Term Goals:** Consider allocating more to taxable accounts for liquidity.
   - **Long-Term Goals:** Favor tax-advantaged accounts for banking on potential growth.

### 4. **Adjust Based on Income:**
   - Monitor your income and adjust contributions accordingly to maximize tax benefits from different accounts.

### Example Allocation:
- **401(k):** 10-15% of your income, especially to get the full employer match.
- **IRA:** Contribute the allowed maximum annually.
- **Taxable Accounts:** Use any surplus funds after maxing out tax-advantaged accounts.

### 5. **Regular Review:**
   - Periodically reassess and adjust based on performance and changes in your financial situation.

Consulting with a financial advisor can provide personalized guidance tailored to your individual circumstances.
