Distributing your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax considerations, and time horizon. Here are some general guidelines:

1. **401(k):** Maximize employer match first. Contribute enough to take advantage of this benefit, as it’s essentially free money.

2. **IRA:** If you can contribute to both a traditional and a Roth IRA, consider your current tax rate. Traditional IRAs provide tax deductions now, while Roth IRAs allow tax-free growth and withdrawals. Diversifying between both can provide tax flexibility in retirement.

3. **Taxable Accounts:** Once you’ve maximized contributions to your retirement accounts, consider putting extra funds into taxable accounts. This may offer more liquidity and flexibility for short-term goals, but keep an eye on taxes on dividends and capital gains.

### General Strategy
- **Emergency Fund:** Ensure you have a sufficient emergency fund before investing heavily.
- **Risk Tolerance:** Adjust the allocation based on your risk tolerance. More conservative investors might prioritize safer investments in retirement accounts.
- **Long-term vs. Short-term:** Focus on long-term growth in retirement accounts and allocate for shorter-term needs in taxable accounts.

### Example Allocation
- **401(k):** 20-25%
- **IRA:** 15-20%
- **Taxable Accounts:** 55-65%

Reassess your strategy periodically to account for changes in income, goals, or market conditions. Consulting a financial advisor for personalized advice can also be beneficial.
