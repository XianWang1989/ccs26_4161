Distributing your money across a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some key considerations for an effective strategy:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match, as this is essentially "free money."
   - **IRA**: Consider maxing out your contributions to either a Traditional or Roth IRA, depending on your tax situation and retirement goals.

2. **Consider Tax Implications**:
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Invest in assets that generate qualified dividends or long-term capital gains, which are taxed at lower rates than ordinary income.

3. **Rebalance Regularly**:
   - Assess and adjust your asset allocation to maintain your desired risk level across all accounts.

4. **Investment Goals and Timeline**:
   - If you have short-term goals, prioritize taxable accounts for flexibility.
   - For long-term retirement goals, focus more on retirement accounts where the money can grow tax-deferred.

5. **Diversification**:
   - Maintain a diversified portfolio across all accounts to mitigate risk.

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**:
   - A professional can provide tailored advice based on your individual circumstances.

Ultimately, a balanced approach that takes advantage of the benefits of each account type can help you optimize your overall investment strategy.
