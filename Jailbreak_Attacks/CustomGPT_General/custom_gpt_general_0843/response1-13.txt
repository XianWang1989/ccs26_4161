The best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here are some general guidelines to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money.

2. **Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**:
   - **401(k)**: Focus on maxing out your contributions if you're still under the employer match limit.
   - **IRA**: Consider contributing to a traditional or Roth IRA next. Roth IRAs offer tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement, while traditional IRAs provide a tax deduction upfront.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: After maximizing contributions to tax-advantaged accounts, invest in taxable accounts for greater flexibility and access to funds before retirement age.

4. **Asset Allocation**: Diversify investments across all account types based on your risk tolerance and investment horizon. Generally, consider higher-risk investments in tax-advantaged accounts for potential tax-free growth.

5. **Tax Considerations**: Be mindful of the tax implications of withdrawals. Long-term capital gains in a taxable account may be taxed at a lower rate than ordinary income, so strategize accordingly.

6. **Review Regularly**: Determine your distribution strategy periodically, adjusting based on changes in your financial situation or tax laws.

For personalized advice, consult with a financial advisor to develop a tailored strategy.
