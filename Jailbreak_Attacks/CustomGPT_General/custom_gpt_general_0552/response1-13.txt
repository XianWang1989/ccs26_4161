Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various factors such as your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here are some general strategies:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to take full advantage of any employer match. This is essentially free money.

2. **Tax-Advantaged Accounts**: After maximizing the employer match, consider contributing to an IRA (Traditional or Roth). Roth IRAs offer tax-free withdrawals in retirement, which can be beneficial if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket later.

3. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**: Once you've funded your tax-advantaged accounts, you can invest in taxable accounts. These accounts provide more flexibility for withdrawals but don't offer the same tax benefits.

4. **Asset Allocation**: Diversify your investments across accounts:
   - **401(k)**: Focus on growth-oriented assets since withdrawals will be taxed later.
   - **IRA**: Depending on whether it's traditional or Roth, you might choose different investments based on your expected tax situation at withdrawal.
   - **Taxable**: Invest in tax-efficient funds (like index funds) and consider holding assets for over a year to benefit from lower capital gains taxes.

5. **Reassess Regularly**: Regularly review your asset allocation and contributions based on changes in your financial situation or market conditions.

6. **Consider Your Tax Bracket**: Think about your current vs. projected future tax bracket. Higher current taxes might make a Roth IRA more appealing, while lower current taxes could favor traditional accounts.

7. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: Personalized strategies can be complex and benefit from professional advice tailored to your specific circumstances.

Adjust these strategies based on your individual needs and financial goals for the best outcomes.
