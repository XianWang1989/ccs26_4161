Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and time horizon. Here’s a general approach:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a 401(k) match, contribute enough to qualify for the full match. This is essentially "free money" and should be a priority.

2. **Contribute to a Traditional or Roth IRA**: Next, consider funding an IRA. If you qualify, a Roth IRA can provide tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement, while a Traditional IRA allows for tax-deferred growth.

3. **Continue with 401(k) Contributions**: After maxing out the IRA, continue contributing to your 401(k) up to the annual limit. Remember that 401(k) contributions reduce your taxable income.

4. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**: Any additional savings can go into taxable accounts, which offer liquidity and flexibility but do not provide the same tax advantages as retirement accounts.

5. **Diversification and Investment Strategy**: Ensure a diversified investment mix based on your risk tolerance and time horizon across all accounts. Generally, you might want to hold more conservative investments in taxable accounts to minimize tax impacts from capital gains.

6. **Reevaluate Regularly**: As your financial situation and goals change, regularly reassess how you distribute your funds among these accounts.

7. **Consider Professional Advice**: For personalized strategies, consult with a financial advisor, particularly to navigate complex tax situations.

This approach helps balance immediate tax benefits with long-term growth while maintaining flexibility for future needs.
