To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k):** If your employer offers a matching contribution, contribute enough to get the full match first. This is essentially free money and an immediate return on your investment.

2. **Contribute to Tax-Advantaged Accounts:**
   - **401(k):** Focus on maximizing your 401(k) contributions, especially if you have a high income. The contribution limit for 2023 is $22,500 (or $30,000 if you're over 50).
   - **IRA (Traditional or Roth):** Consider maxing out your IRA contributions next. The limit for 2023 is $6,500 (or $7,500 if you're over 50). A Roth IRA can be beneficial, especially if you expect your tax rate to be higher in retirement.

3. **Assess Your Tax Situation:**
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Once you’ve maximized contributions to your 401(k) and IRA, any additional funds can be directed to a taxable account. This is useful for investments that you may want to access before retirement age without penalties.
   - Consider holding investments that generate long-term capital gains and qualified dividends in your taxable accounts to minimize tax liability.

4. **Diversification of Investments:** Regardless of the account, aim for a diversified portfolio that aligns with your risk tolerance and investment goals. 

5. **Review Regularly:** Make it a habit to review your allocations regularly (at least annually) and adjust based on market conditions and changes in your financial situation.

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor:** If you're unsure, a financial advisor can help tailor a plan based on your specific situation and goals.

By following these strategies, you can create a balanced investment approach that capitalizes on both tax advantages and liquidity needs.
