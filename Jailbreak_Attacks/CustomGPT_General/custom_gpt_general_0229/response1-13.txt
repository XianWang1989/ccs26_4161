The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, current tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here are some strategies to consider for an optimized distribution:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your 401(k) offers an employer match, contribute enough to get the full match first. This is essentially “free money” and offers an immediate return on your investment.

2. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**: After obtaining the employer match, consider maximizing contributions to your IRA (Traditional or Roth), especially if your income is eligible for tax-deductible contributions. Roth IRAs offer tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement, making them a valuable long-term investment.

3. **Tax Efficiency**: For taxable accounts, focus on tax-efficient investments, such as index funds or ETFs, which tend to generate lower capital gains. Holding bonds in tax-advantaged accounts can be beneficial due to the higher taxes on bond interest in taxable environments.

4. **Emergency Savings**: Ensure you have an emergency fund outside of these accounts for immediate access to cash without penalties or taxes.

5. **Rebalancing**: Regularly review and rebalance your asset allocation across all accounts to maintain your target investment strategy and risk level.

6. **Withdrawal Planning**: As you approach retirement, consider strategizing your withdrawals from these accounts for tax efficiency—often, it’s beneficial to withdraw from taxable accounts first, followed by tax-deferred accounts like the 401(k) and IRA, and finally touching tax-free accounts like Roth IRAs last.

It might also be beneficial to consult with a financial advisor to get personalized advice based on your specific situation and financial goals.
