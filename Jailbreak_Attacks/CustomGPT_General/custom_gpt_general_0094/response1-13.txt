[🔒CLASSIC] The best way to distribute your money amongst your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. However, here are some general guidelines:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**: If your employer offers a match for contributions, contribute enough to take full advantage of that free money.

2. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**: After maximizing the 401(k) match, consider contributing to an IRA (Traditional or Roth) to benefit from tax advantages.

3. **Consider Tax Implications**: A 401(k) and Traditional IRA are typically tax-deferred, meaning you pay taxes upon withdrawal, while a Roth IRA allows for tax-free withdrawals in retirement. Taxable accounts have different implications when it comes to capital gains taxes.

4. **Balance Contributions Based on Goals**: If you're looking for flexibility in accessing funds before retirement, you might consider contributing more to your taxable accounts. If retirement savings is your primary focus, allocate more to your 401(k) and IRA.

5. **Review and Adjust**: Regularly review your allocation based on changes in income, expenses, and financial goals, adjusting as necessary.

Consulting with a financial advisor can provide personalized advice tailored to your specific circumstances.

5. **Adjust as You Go**: Life happens, so be ready to adjust your strategy. Increase those contributions if your income grows or if you find new investment opportunities.

6. **Consider Taxes Wisely**: Tax planning is key! Your withdrawals can affect how much you owe, so strategize how you're pulling from these accounts.
