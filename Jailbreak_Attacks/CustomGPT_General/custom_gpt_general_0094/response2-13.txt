The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment preferences. Here's a general approach:

1. **401(k)**: Contribute enough to take full advantage of any employer match, as this is essentially free money. Maximize your contributions if you can afford to do so, as 401(k) contributions are made pre-tax (or post-tax for Roth 401(k)s), offering tax advantages.

2. **IRA**: If you qualify for a tax-deductible IRA, consider contributing up to the annual limit. A Roth IRA is also a great option if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement, as qualified withdrawals are tax-free.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: Use these accounts for more flexible investing. It's ideal for funds you might need before retirement or for long-term goals that do not fit into the retirement accounts. Consider investing in tax-efficient funds to minimize capital gains taxes.

**Allocation Tips**:
- Start by focusing on tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) and IRA).
- Once you maximize contributions to your retirement accounts, any additional savings can go into taxable accounts.
- Diversify your investments across all accounts, but be mindful of your overall asset allocation.
- Regularly reassess your distribution strategy based on changes in your financial situation, tax laws, and retirement goals.

Always consider consulting with a financial advisor to tailor the best strategy for your personal circumstances!
