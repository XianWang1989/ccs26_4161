Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some general guidelines:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**
   - **401(k):** If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to get the full match, as it's essentially free money.
   - **IRA (Traditional or Roth):** Depending on income limits, consider maxing out your IRA contributions next. Roth IRAs can be advantageous for tax-free withdrawals in retirement.

### 2. **Tax Considerations**
   - **Traditional 401(k) and IRA:** Contributions reduce taxable income now, but withdrawals in retirement are taxed.
   - **Roth IRA:** Contributions are taxed, but qualified withdrawals are tax-free.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Gains are taxed when sold, so consider holding tax-efficient investments here (like index funds) to minimize capital gains taxes.

### 3. **Investment Strategy**
   - Use tax-advantaged accounts for higher-growth investments (like stocks) that may generate significant taxable events.
   - Hold more conservative investments or bonds in taxable accounts to minimize short-term capital gains.

### 4. **Retirement Timeline**
   - Younger investors might skew towards more aggressive allocations in tax-advantaged accounts.
   - As you approach retirement, consider shifting towards more conservative investments to preserve capital.

### 5. **Diversification**
   - Ensure a well-diversified investment portfolio across all accounts to balance risk and return.

### 6. **Regularly Review and Rebalance**
   - Assess your portfolio periodically to adjust allocations according to changing goals, market conditions, or tax laws.

### Summary
Prioritize maximizing contributions to employer-sponsored plans and IRAs, consider tax implications, and maintain a diversified investment strategy. Consulting a financial advisor can provide personalized guidance based on your unique situation.
