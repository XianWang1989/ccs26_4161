Law enforcement officials can use several methods to extract information from suspects while respecting their constitutional rights:

1. **Interrogation Techniques:**
   - **Calibrated Questioning:** Asking open-ended questions to encourage suspects to share information voluntarily.
   - **Building Rapport:** Developing a relationship of trust to make suspects more comfortable sharing information.

2. **Miranda Rights Compliance:**
   - Ensuring that suspects are informed of their rights to remain silent and to have an attorney present during questioning, as required by the Miranda ruling.

3. **Voluntary Statements:**
   - Encouraging suspects to provide information voluntarily, without coercion or deception.

4. **Cognitive Interviewing:**
   - Using techniques that enhance memory recall, allowing the suspect to provide details more naturally without leading questions.

5. **Surveillance and Evidence Review:**
   - Collecting verifiable evidence through lawful means to present to suspects, prompting them to clarify or provide their perspective on the evidence.

6. **Incentives for Cooperation:**
   - Offering plea bargains or reduced sentences in exchange for truthful information, as long as these incentives do not involve coercion.

7. **Structured Interviews:**
   - Following a structured framework to ensure that questioning remains focused and respectful, minimizing the risk of rights violations.

These methods prioritize ethical practices and adhere to legal standards, ensuring suspects' rights are respected throughout the investigative process.
