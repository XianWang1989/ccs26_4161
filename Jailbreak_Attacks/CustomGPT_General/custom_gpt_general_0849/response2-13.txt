Distributing your money wisely among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts involves several factors. Here’s a general strategy:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a match for your 401(k), contribute enough to get the full match first. It’s essentially free money.

2. **Contribute to an IRA**: After maximizing your 401(k) match, consider contributing to either a traditional or Roth IRA. A Roth IRA offers tax-free withdrawals in retirement, which can be beneficial if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket later.

3. **Maximize 401(k) Contributions**: Next, focus on maxing out your 401(k) contributions up to the limit ($22,500 for 2024, with an additional $7,500 catch-up if you're over 50).

4. **Taxable Accounts**: Once your tax-advantaged accounts are maxed out, consider investing in taxable accounts. Use them for more flexible investments, particularly for short-term goals.

5. **Asset Allocation**: Within these accounts, diversify your investments based on your risk tolerance and time horizon. Typically, stocks for growth in long-term accounts and bonds for stability in shorter-term ones.

6. **Tax Considerations**: Be mindful of tax implications in your taxable accounts. Favor investments with lower capital gains distributions, like index funds or ETFs.

7. **Rebalance Regularly**: Check your allocations annually or bi-annually to maintain your desired risk profile and investment goals.

### Summary:
1. Maximize employer match in 401(k).
2. Contribute to an IRA.
3. Max out your 401(k).
4. Invest in taxable accounts.
5. Focus on asset allocation.
6. Consider tax implications.
7. Rebalance regularly.

This balanced approach helps you leverage tax advantages while maintaining flexibility. Always consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized advice.
