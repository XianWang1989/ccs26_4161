Determining the best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and timeline for needing the funds. Here are some guidelines to consider:

### General Guidelines:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First:**
   - **401(k):** If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to get the full match first. It's essentially free money.
   - **IRA:** After maximizing contributions to your 401(k) (up to the annual limit), consider contributing to a Traditional or Roth IRA. The choice between them depends on your current vs. expected retirement tax rate.

2. **Evaluate Tax Implications:**
   - **Taxable Accounts:** While these accounts don’t offer the same tax advantages, they provide flexibility in accessing your funds without penalties. You can manage capital gains and losses here, which may benefit your tax situation.

3. **Consider Asset Location:**
   - **Tax-Deferred Accounts (401(k) and IRA):** Suitable for holding assets that generate high taxable income (like bonds or real estate) because you defer taxes until withdrawal.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Best for low-growth investments or investments you may need to sell in the short term.

4. **Emergency Fund:**
   - Ensure you have an emergency fund in a separate account to avoid early withdrawals from your retirement accounts, which may incur penalties.

5. **Diversify Investments:**
   - Regardless of account type, diversify your investments to balance risk and return. Consider stocks for growth in tax-advantaged accounts and bonds in taxable accounts.

### Example Strategy:

1. **401(k):** Contribute enough to get the full match. If your employer allows, consider higher-risk investments for growth.
2. **IRA:** Max out your IRA contributions next. Choose between a Traditional IRA (for tax deductions now) or a Roth IRA (for tax-free withdrawals in retirement).
3. **Taxable Accounts:** Once you've maximized retirement contributions, use any surplus funds for taxable investments, focusing on low-turnover investments to minimize taxable events.

### Periodic Review:

Reassess your strategy annually or when your financial situation changes (like job changes, income increases, or changes in tax law). 

### Professional Advice:

Consider speaking with a financial advisor for personalized guidance based on your specific circumstances and goals.
