The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts largely depends on your financial goals, risk tolerance, and tax situation. Here are some general guidelines to consider:

1. **Maximize Contributions to Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money.
   - Then, consider maximizing contributions to your IRA (Traditional or Roth) for additional tax advantages.

2. **Understand the Tax Implications**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are tax-deferred, and you’ll pay taxes on withdrawals in retirement. This is great for lowering your taxable income in your working years.
   - **IRA**: A Traditional IRA also offers tax-deferred growth, while Roth IRAs allow for tax-free withdrawals in retirement. Choose based on your current and future tax expectations.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Investment growth is subject to capital gains taxes, but they offer more flexibility in terms of accessing your funds without penalties.

3. **Diversification**: 
   - Ensure a well-diversified portfolio across all accounts to mitigate risk, taking into account the assets' growth potential and your overall risk tolerance.

4. **Liquidity Needs**: 
   - If you anticipate needing funds before retirement, keep a portion in a taxable account, as these offer more liquidity without early withdrawal penalties.

5. **Rebalancing and Annual Review**: 
   - Regularly review your allocations, rebalance your portfolio as necessary, and adjust contributions based on your financial situation and goals.

6. **Consider Consulting a Financial Advisor**: 
   - If your situation is complex or you're unsure, consulting with a financial advisor can provide personalized insights.

Adjust the percentages of your total investment based on these factors to create a balanced and strategic approach for your financial future.
