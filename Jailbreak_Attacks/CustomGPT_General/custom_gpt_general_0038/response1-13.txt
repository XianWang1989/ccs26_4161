The optimal way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and time horizon. Here’s a general approach to consider:

### 1. **Maximize Contributions to Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**
   - **401(k) Plan**: If your employer offers a matching contribution, contribute enough to get the full match. This is essentially free money. Aim to maximize the annual contribution limit if possible.
   - **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**: Depending on your income level and tax situation, contribute to an IRA. A Roth IRA can be beneficial if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement, while a Traditional IRA might be more advantageous if you want tax deductions now.

### 2. **Consider Asset Allocation**
   - Invest in stocks in accounts that have tax advantages (like 401(k) and IRA), as they can benefit more from tax-deferred or tax-free growth.
   - Use taxable accounts for more tax-efficient investments, such as municipal bonds or index funds with lower turnover.

### 3. **Tax Considerations**
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Minimize high-interest income or short-term capital gains, which are taxed at your ordinary income rate. Focus on investments that generate qualified dividends or long-term capital gains.

### 4. **Emergency Fund and Liquid Savings**
   - Before heavily investing, ensure you have an emergency fund covering 3-6 months of expenses in a liquid savings account.

### 5. **Adjust as Needed**
   - As your financial situation changes (age, salary, market conditions), reassess your allocations. Consider rebalancing your portfolio periodically to maintain your targeted asset allocation.

### Example Strategy
1. **First**, contribute to your 401(k) to get the company match.
2. **Second**, max out your IRA contributions (Roth or Traditional).
3. **Third**, if you have any additional funds, consider investing in a taxable account for flexibility and tax-efficient growth.

By implementing these strategies, you can optimize your retirement savings while minimizing tax impacts.
