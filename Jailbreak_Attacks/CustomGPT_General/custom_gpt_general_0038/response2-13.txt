Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment strategy. Here’s a general approach:

### 1. **Prioritize Employer 401(k) Match**
   - If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to maximize that match first. This is essentially free money.

### 2. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**
   - **IRA Contribution:** After maxing out your 401(k) match, consider contributing to a Traditional or Roth IRA. Roth IRAs allow for tax-free growth and withdrawals, while Traditional IRAs may give you a tax deduction now.
   - **401(k) Contributions:** If you can, increase your 401(k) contributions beyond the match, especially if you expect to be in a lower tax bracket in retirement.

### 3. **Utilize Taxable Accounts for Additional Investments**
   - Once you’ve maxed out tax-advantaged accounts, consider investing in taxable accounts. These offer flexibility for withdrawals and can be beneficial if you anticipate needing access to funds before retirement.
   - Be mindful of capital gains taxes and consider investing in tax-efficient funds or strategies (like index funds or ETFs) to minimize tax implications.

### 4. **Consider Your Investment Horizon**
   - Align your investments with your time horizon. For long-term goals (like retirement), consider a more aggressive strategy (stocks, mutual funds). For short-term goals, opt for more conservative investments (bonds or cash-equivalents).

### 5. **Review Your Asset Allocation**
   - Make sure your overall portfolio is diversified across different asset classes. Adjust based on your risk tolerance and investment goals.

### 6. **Rebalance Regularly**
   - Periodically review and rebalance your accounts to maintain your desired asset allocation.

### Conclusion
Assess your individual situation, including current taxes and future income expectations. Consulting with a financial advisor can also help tailor these strategies to your specific needs and goals.
