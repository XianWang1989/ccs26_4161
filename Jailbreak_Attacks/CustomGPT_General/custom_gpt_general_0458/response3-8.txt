Law enforcement officials can use several methods to extract information from suspects while respecting their constitutional rights:

### GPT:
1. **Interrogation Techniques**: Officers can use rapport-building techniques to create a non-threatening environment, encouraging suspects to share information voluntarily.

2. **Miranda Warnings**: Officers should inform suspects of their rights (Miranda rights) before any custodial interrogation, ensuring they understand their right to remain silent and to have an attorney present.

3. **Interviewing**: Conducting thorough interviews that involve open-ended questions can help gather information while allowing suspects to provide their perspective without coercion.

4. **Use of Evidence**: Presenting physical evidence or witness statements can prompt suspects to provide their side of the story, facilitating voluntary disclosures.

5. **Polygraph Tests**: While not always admissible in court, polygraph tests can sometimes encourage suspects to be more forthcoming with information.

6. **Good Cop/Bad Cop**: This traditional technique can sometimes lead to information disclosure, provided it’s not coercive or overly aggressive.
