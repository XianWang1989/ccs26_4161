Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment strategy. Here are some guidelines to help you decide:

### 1. Understand Account Types:
- **401(k)**: Employer-sponsored retirement plan with potential employer matching. Contributions are often pre-tax, reducing your taxable income.
- **IRA**: Individual Retirement Account that can be traditional (tax-deferred) or Roth (post-tax). Contribution limits are lower than 401(k) plans.
- **Taxable Accounts**: Investment accounts without tax advantages. Capital gains taxes apply when you sell investments for a profit.

### 2. Prioritize Contributions:
1. **Maximize 401(k) Matching**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to take full advantage of any employer match, as this is essentially free money.
2. **Max Out IRA Contributions**: If you're eligible, consider maxing out contributions to an IRA. A Roth IRA can provide tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement.
3. **Fund Taxable Accounts**: After maxing out retirement accounts, consider investing in taxable accounts for additional flexibility.

### 3. Tax Considerations:
- **Tax-Deferred Growth**: Focus on tax-deferred accounts for investments expected to grow significantly. This includes bonds and actively managed funds, which may generate higher ordinary income.
- **Tax-Efficient Investments**: Use taxable accounts for investments with favorable tax treatment (e.g., index funds, ETFs) to minimize capital gains tax.

### 4. Assess Your Financial Goals:
- **Short-Term Needs**: If you anticipate needing funds in the next few years, prioritize taxable accounts for easier access without penalties.
- **Long-Term Growth**: For long-term goals, leverage the benefits of tax-advantaged accounts.

### 5. Regularly Reassess:
- Your financial situation, tax laws, and investment opportunities can change. Regularly review your asset allocation and contributions to ensure they align with your current goals.

### Example Distribution Strategy:
- **401(k)**: Contribute up to the employer match limit.
- **IRA**: Max out potential contributions to a Roth or traditional IRA.
- **Taxable accounts**: Invest any additional surplus cash after maxing retirement accounts.

### Conclusion:
Each financial situation is unique. Consider consulting with a financial advisor to create a personalized strategy that aligns with your retirement goals and risk tolerance.
