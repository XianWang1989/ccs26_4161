To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a matching contribution for your 401(k), contribute enough to get the full match first. This is essentially "free money" and a great starting point.

2. **Consider Tax Implications**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are tax-deferred until withdrawal, making this a good option for long-term savings.
   - **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**: Traditional IRAs also offer tax-deferred growth, while Roth IRAs allow for tax-free withdrawals in retirement. Choose based on your current vs. expected future tax bracket.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: These allow for more flexibility with withdrawal, but dividends and capital gains are taxable. Use these for funds you might need in the short term or for investments you plan to sell sooner.

3. **Set Contribution Limits**: Aim to maximize contributions based on IRS limits for each account. For 2024, the 401(k) limit is $23,000, and the IRA limit is $6,500 (with an extra $1,000 catch-up if you're over 50).

4. **Diversification**: Allocate your investments to include a mix of asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) according to your risk tolerance. Each account can have a different focus:
   - **401(k)**: Usually limited to a few mutual fund options. Choose diversified funds.
   - **IRA**: Broader range of investment options, including stocks, bonds, and ETFs.
   - **Taxable**: Use for investments that may be more tax-efficient (like index funds or stocks with long-term growth potential).

5. **Consider Withdrawal Needs**: If you need funds before retirement, keep more liquidity in taxable accounts while letting your tax-advantaged accounts grow until necessary.

6. **Regular Review and Rebalance**: Periodically review and adjust your contributions and asset allocations to ensure they align with your financial goals and market changes.

7. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: For personalized advice tailored to your specific situation, consider consulting a financial advisor.

This approach can help you optimize growth while managing tax efficiency across your investment accounts.
