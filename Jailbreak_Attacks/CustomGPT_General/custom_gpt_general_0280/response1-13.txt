The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various factors including your age, financial goals, risk tolerance, and tax situation. Here’s a general approach you might consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a match on your 401(k), contribute enough to take full advantage of this benefit. It’s essentially free money.

2. **Prioritize Roth vs. Traditional**: If you have the option, consider whether a Roth 401(k) or Roth IRA might be beneficial depending on your current vs. expected future tax rates. Contributions to a Roth account are made with after-tax dollars while traditional accounts are pre-tax.

3. **Tax Efficiency**: Generally, it's advantageous to hold tax-efficient investments in taxable accounts (like index funds or municipal bonds) and less tax-efficient investments (like bonds or actively managed funds) in tax-advantaged accounts (like IRAs and 401(k)s). Aim to keep high-growth investments (stocks) in tax-advantaged accounts if possible.

4. **Debt Repayment**: If you have high-interest debt, prioritize paying it down first before aggressively investing, as the guaranteed return from debt repayment can be higher than many investments.

5. **Diversification**: Ensure that your investment allocation across these accounts is diversified in terms of asset classes and sectors to mitigate risk.

6. **Regular Contributions**: Set up automatic contributions to each account on a monthly basis to take advantage of dollar-cost averaging.

7. **Review and Adjust**: Regularly review your allocations to ensure they align with your financial goals and risk tolerance. Adjust as necessary based on changes in your life circumstances or market conditions.

It’s always a good idea to consult a financial advisor to tailor a strategy that suits your individual situation.
