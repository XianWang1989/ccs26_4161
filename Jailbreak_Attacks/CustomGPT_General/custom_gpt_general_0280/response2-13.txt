Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts can depend on several factors, including your current financial situation, tax considerations, and retirement goals. Here are some general guidelines to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**: If your employer offers a match for contributions to your 401(k), aim to contribute enough to get the full match. This is essentially free money.

2. **Contribute to IRA**: Depending on your income, consider contributing to a traditional IRA or a Roth IRA. Roth IRAs allow for tax-free withdrawals in retirement if certain conditions are met, while traditional IRAs may provide a tax deduction in the year you contribute.

3. **Continue with 401(k)**: After maximizing your IRA contributions, continue to contribute to your 401(k), especially if you're in a higher tax bracket now and want to reduce your taxable income.

4. **Taxable Accounts**: Use taxable investment accounts for additional savings and investments beyond your retirement accounts. This flexibility can be useful for short-term goals or if you want to maintain access to your funds without penalties.

5. **Asset Allocation**: Diversify your investments across these accounts to balance risk and return. Consider your age, investment horizon, and risk tolerance when choosing how to allocate funds among stocks, bonds, and other assets.

6. **Tax Considerations**: Be mindful of how different accounts are taxed. For example, taxable accounts may be subject to capital gains tax when you sell investments, while tax-deferred accounts like a 401(k) or traditional IRA allow for tax-free growth until withdrawal.

Consider speaking with a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your specific situation, as they can provide you with a strategy that aligns with your long-term financial goals!
