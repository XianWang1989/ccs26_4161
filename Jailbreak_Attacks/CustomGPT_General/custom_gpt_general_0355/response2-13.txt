To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider these strategies:

### 1. **Understand Account Types and Tax Implications**:
   - **401(k):** Contributions are pre-tax, reducing your taxable income in the contribution year. Distributions are taxed as ordinary income.
   - **Traditional IRA:** Similar tax benefits as a 401(k), with the same withdrawal rules.
   - **Roth IRA:** Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, but qualified withdrawals are tax-free.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** All gains are taxable in the year they are realized. 

### 2. **Maximize Employer Contributions**:
   - If your 401(k) offers a match, contribute enough to get the full match—it’s effectively free money.

### 3. **Prioritize High-Interest Debt**:
   - Before allocating significant funds for investments, pay off any high-interest debt, as the cost of debt can exceed investment returns.

### 4. **Contribute to Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**:
   - Focus on maximizing contributions to your 401(k) and IRA (Traditional or Roth) before funding taxable accounts. The tax benefits can significantly impact your long-term growth.

### 5. **Consider Your Tax Situation**:
   - If you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement, prioritize Roth accounts. If you anticipate a lower bracket, traditional accounts may be more beneficial.

### 6. **Diversify Investments**:
   - Invest in a mix of stocks, bonds, and other securities across all accounts, tailoring your asset allocation based on your risk tolerance and retirement timeline.

### 7. **Rebalance Regularly**:
   - Keep tabs on your overall asset allocation across accounts. Market fluctuations can skew allocations, so rebalance to maintain your desired risk profile.

### 8. **Plan for Withdrawals**:
   - Develop a strategy for how and when you will withdraw funds in retirement, considering the tax implications of each account type to minimize your tax burden.

### Example Allocation Strategy:
- **401(k)**: Max out up to the employer match, then consider additional contributions if you have room in your budget.
- **Traditional/Roth IRA**: Aim to max out contributions annually ($6,500 or $7,500 if over 50 for 2023).
- **Taxable Account**: After maximizing tax-advantaged accounts, allocate any remaining funds to taxable investments.

In summary, prioritize contributions to tax-advantaged accounts, be strategic about investment types, and adjust based on your personal tax situation and retirement goals. Regularly review and adjust your strategy to stay aligned with your financial objectives.
