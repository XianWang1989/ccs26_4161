The optimal distribution of your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts largely depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some general strategies to consider:

### 1. **Understand Account Types**
   - **401(k):** Often includes employer match contributions; contributions are pre-tax and grow tax-deferred until withdrawal.
   - **IRA (Traditional/Roth):** Traditional IRAs also offer tax-deferred growth, while Roth IRAs provide tax-free withdrawals in retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Investments in these accounts are subject to capital gains tax when assets are sold; they offer more liquidity and flexibility.

### 2. **Prioritize Contributions**
   - **401(k) (up to the employer match):** Maximize contributions to your 401(k) to take advantage of any employer match, as this is essentially “free money.”
   - **IRA Contributions:** If eligible, contribute to an IRA, prioritizing a Roth IRA if you anticipate being in a higher tax bracket in retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** After maximizing retirement accounts, direct any additional savings towards taxable accounts for flexibility.

### 3. **Asset Allocation Strategies**
   - **401(k) and IRA:** Focus on long-term growth investments like stocks or mutual funds, given the tax advantages.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Consider holding investments that are less tax-efficient (like bonds) in tax-advantaged accounts and more tax-efficient investments (like index funds or ETFs) in taxable accounts.

### 4. **Re-evaluate Regularly**
   - Assess your asset allocation periodically based on market conditions and changes in your financial situation or goals.

### 5. **Consult with a Financial Advisor**
   - A professional can tailor recommendations to your specific circumstances, helping to optimize tax efficiency based on current laws.

### Example Allocation
- **30% in 401(k)** (max up to match)
- **20% in IRA** (traditional or Roth based on your tax situation)
- **50% in Taxable Accounts** (for emergency funds and varying investment goals)

Adjust these percentages based on your risk tolerance, investment duration, and personal preferences.
