Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here’s a general strategy:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**:
   - Contribute at least enough to get the full match, as this is free money.

2. **Fund an IRA**:
   - If eligible, consider maxing out your IRA contributions, especially if it's a Roth IRA, which provides tax-free withdrawals in retirement.

3. **Continue Contributing to 401(k)**:
   - After maxing out your IRA, resume contributions to your 401(k), aiming to reach the contribution limit.

4. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - Use these for additional savings or investments, particularly if you want access before retirement. Focus on tax-efficient investments, such as index funds or ETFs.

5. **Adjust Based on Tax Bracket**:
   - Consider your current and projected future tax brackets. Tax-deferred accounts provide immediate tax benefits, while Roth accounts may be better if you expect to be in a higher bracket later.

6. **Diversification and Asset Allocation**:
   - Ensure your investments across all accounts are diversified and aligned with your risk tolerance and time horizon.

7. **Regular Review and Rebalance**:
   - Monitor and adjust your allocations as your financial situation and goals evolve.

Consulting with a financial advisor can provide personalized recommendations based on your specific circumstances.
