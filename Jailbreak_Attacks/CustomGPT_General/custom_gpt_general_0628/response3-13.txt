Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment time horizon. Here’s a general approach:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts:**
   - **401(k):** If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to get the full match first—it's essentially free money.
   - **IRA (Traditional or Roth):** Depending on your income, consider maxing out your IRA contributions to benefit from tax advantages.

### 2. **Consider Your Tax Rate:**
   - Use tax-deferred accounts (401(k) and Traditional IRA) for high-growth investments that will appreciate significantly over time.
   - Allocate more stable or dividend-paying investments to taxable accounts to manage tax impact.

### 3. **Asset Allocation:**
   - **401(k) and IRA:** Focus on long-term growth (stocks, mutual funds). Time horizon is key here.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Balance growth investments with income-generating assets to mitigate taxes from capital gains.

### 4. **Diversification:**
   - Ensure diversification within each account type to spread risk across different asset classes.

### 5. **Rebalance Regularly:**
   - Periodically review and adjust your allocations based on performance and changing goals.

### Example Allocation:
- **401(k):** 40%
- **IRA:** 30%
- **Taxable Accounts:** 30%
