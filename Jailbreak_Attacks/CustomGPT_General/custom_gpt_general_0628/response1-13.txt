To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Tax Optimization**:
   - **401(k)**: Prioritize contributions to your employer's plan, especially if they offer matching. These contributions are pre-tax, reducing your taxable income.
   - **IRA**: Depending on your income, a Roth IRA can provide tax-free growth for retirement. Traditional IRAs offer tax deductions but withdrawals are taxed as income.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for funds you might need before retirement. Focus on investments that generate long-term capital gains for favorable tax treatment.

2. **Investment Goals**:
   - **Long-Term Growth**: Use tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) and IRA) for higher-risk investments since taxes are deferred or eliminated on growth.
   - **Short-Term Needs**: Keep more liquid, less volatile investments in taxable accounts.

3. **Diversification**:
   - Ensure you diversify investments within each account type to manage risk effectively.

4. **Income Needs**:
   - If you anticipate needing funds for specific goals (like buying a house), prioritize accessibility in taxable accounts.

5. **Contribution Limits**:
   - Be aware of annual contribution limits for 401(k)s and IRAs. Maximize these accounts before considering taxable accounts.

6. **Regular Review**:
   - Periodically reassess your allocations to align with changing goals and market conditions.

Consult with a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your specific financial situation and goals.
