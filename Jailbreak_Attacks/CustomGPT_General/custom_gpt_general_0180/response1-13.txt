To effectively distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First:**
   - **401(k):** Contribute enough to take full advantage of your employer's match first, as this is essentially free money.
   - **IRA (Traditional or Roth):** After maximizing your 401(k) match, contribute to an IRA. If eligible, consider a Roth IRA for tax-free growth.

2. **Prioritize Based on Tax Implications:**
   - **Traditional 401(k) and IRA:** Contributions reduce your taxable income, suitable for those expecting to be in a lower tax bracket in retirement.
   - **Roth Accounts:** Ideal for those who expect to be in a higher tax bracket during retirement, as qualified withdrawals are tax-free.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** After maximizing tax-advantaged contributions, use taxable accounts for investments that might require liquidity or for investing legacy funds.

3. **Asset Allocation:**
   - Consider your investment mix (stocks, bonds, etc.) based on your risk tolerance and time horizon. Growth-oriented investments can go in tax-advantaged accounts, while more stable investments may be better suited for taxable accounts to minimize capital gains taxes.

4. **Rebalance Regularly:**
   - Keep an eye on your overall asset allocation across all accounts. Rebalancing is essential to align with your risk tolerance and investment goals.

5. **Withdrawal Strategy:**
   - As you approach retirement, plan your withdrawals tax-efficiently by considering which account to draw from first to minimize tax exposure.

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor:**
   - Personalized advice tailored to your specific financial situation can be invaluable, especially for tax planning and investment strategy.

By combining these strategies, you'll create a well-structured approach to managing your retirement and investment accounts effectively.
