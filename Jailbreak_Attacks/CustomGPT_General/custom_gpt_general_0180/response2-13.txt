When considering the best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, several key factors come into play, including your financial goals, timeline, tax situation, and investment choices. Here's a general approach to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**: If your employer offers a matching contribution, aim to contribute enough to get the full match. This is essentially "free money" and a great way to boost your retirement savings.

2. **Consider IRA Contributions**: If you’re eligible, consider contributing to an IRA (Traditional or Roth). A Traditional IRA may provide tax-deductible contributions, while a Roth IRA allows for tax-free withdrawals in retirement. The choice depends on your current versus expected future tax rates.

3. **Assess Your Taxable Account**: Once you’re maximizing contributions to retirement accounts, consider using a taxable account for additional investments. This can provide liquidity and flexibility but comes with tax implications on capital gains and dividends.

4. **Diversify Investments**: Depending on your age and risk tolerance, allocate funds across different asset classes (stocks, bonds, real estate, etc.) in all accounts. Younger investors may lean more toward growth investments, while those closer to retirement may prefer more conservative allocations.

5. **Evaluate Tax Implications**: Keep in mind the tax implications of each account type. Withdrawals from a 401(k) or Traditional IRA are taxed as ordinary income, while capital gains from a taxable account may be taxed at a lower rate.

6. **Rebalance Regularly**: Periodically review and adjust your investment allocations to ensure they align with your goals and risk tolerance.

7. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: Given the complexity of tax laws and investment strategies, consulting a financial advisor can provide personalized insights based on your specific situation.

Consider your individual financial circumstances, including your income level, expenses, investment horizon, and retirement goals when making these decisions.
