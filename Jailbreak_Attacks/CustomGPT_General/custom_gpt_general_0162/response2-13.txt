The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts really depends on your financial goals, risk tolerance, and tax situation. Here’s a general approach to consider:

1. **Maximize Retirement Accounts First**: Aim to contribute enough to your 401(k) to take full advantage of any employer match, as this is essentially free money. After that, consider maximizing contributions to your IRA, especially if it offers tax advantages (Traditional IRA for tax-deferred growth or Roth IRA for tax-free withdrawals in retirement).

2. **Understand Tax Implications**: 
   - **401(k)**: Contributions reduce your taxable income now, but withdrawals in retirement are taxed as ordinary income.
   - **Traditional IRA**: Similar tax benefits as a 401(k).
   - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are made after-tax, allowing for tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Income and capital gains are taxed in the year they’re realized, so they often aren't the first choice for retirement savings.

3. **Balance Investment Strategies**: Place more conservative investments in tax-advantaged accounts (like bonds or dividend stocks in a 401(k) or IRA) to minimize tax liabilities, and keep more aggressive investments (like growth stocks) in taxable accounts, where long-term capital gains taxes may apply.

4. **Adjust for Liquidity Needs**: If you anticipate needing access to funds before retirement, allocate more to taxable accounts, as these provide greater liquidity without penalties.

5. **Regularly Reassess**: Periodically review your asset distribution based on changes in your financial situation, market conditions, and retirement goals.

It's often beneficial to consult with a financial advisor to tailor the strategy to your specific situation.
