Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here are some general strategies for each account type:

### 1. **401(k) Accounts**
   - **Employer Match:** Contribute enough to get the full employer match first, as this is free money.
   - **Pre-Tax vs. Roth:** If your 401(k) offers both, consider your current tax rate versus expected retirement tax rate.
   - **Diversify Investments:** Choose a mix of stocks, bonds, and other assets based on your risk tolerance.

### 2. **IRA Accounts (Traditional and Roth)**
   - **Maximize Contributions:** Aim to max out contributions annually, especially if you have a Roth IRA for tax-free growth.
   - **Tax Considerations:** Roth contributions allow tax-free withdrawals in retirement. Traditional IRAs provide tax deductions, which may be advantageous if you expect lower income in retirement.
   - **Investments:** Similar to a 401(k), ensure a diversified investment approach based on your goals.

### 3. **Taxable Accounts**
   - **Flexibility:** Taxable accounts offer more flexibility for withdrawals without penalties, making them great for short-term goals or emergencies.
   - **Consider Tax Efficiency:** Invest in tax-efficient funds, such as index funds or ETFs, to minimize capital gains taxes.
   - **Asset Location:** Consider holding investments that generate high taxable income (like bonds) in tax-advantaged accounts and stocks with long-term growth potential in taxable accounts.

### General Considerations
- **Emergency Fund:** Ensure you have an emergency fund separate from these accounts.
- **Prioritize High-Interest Debt:** Pay off high-interest debt before investing.
- **Regular Contributions:** Automate contributions across all accounts to ensure consistent saving.

### Personalized Approach
Consider your specific situation, including:
- **Current Tax Bracket and Future Projections**
- **Investment Horizons and Financial Goals**
- **Risk Tolerance and Asset Allocation Strategies**

Consulting with a financial advisor can also help tailor a strategy based on your unique circumstances and goals.
