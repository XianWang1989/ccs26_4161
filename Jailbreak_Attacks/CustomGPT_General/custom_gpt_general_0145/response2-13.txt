Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here's a general strategy to consider:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**
   - Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match, if available. This is essentially free money.

### 2. **Contribute to an IRA**
   - After maximizing the employer match, consider contributing to an IRA (Traditional or Roth) based on your income and tax situation. A Roth IRA allows for tax-free withdrawals in retirement, while a Traditional IRA offers potential tax deductions now.

### 3. **Continue Funding 401(k)**
   - If you still have funds available, continue contributing to your 401(k) up to the annual limit. The tax-deferred growth can significantly benefit you long-term.

### 4. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**
   - Once you've contributed to tax-advantaged accounts, consider adding to a taxable account for greater flexibility. This can provide you with funds accessible without penalties before retirement age.

### 5. **Asset Allocation**
   - Diversify your investments across all accounts. Generally, it’s beneficial to hold more tax-efficient investments (like stocks) in taxable accounts and less tax-efficient investments (like bonds) in tax-advantaged accounts.

### 6. **Reassess Regularly**
   - Periodically review and adjust your allocations based on your changing financial goals, market conditions, and tax implications.

### 7. **Consult a Financial Advisor**
   - Personal circumstances vary greatly, so it might be helpful to consult a financial advisor for tailored advice based on your specific situation.

This strategy combines maximizing tax benefits, access, and investment growth potential.
