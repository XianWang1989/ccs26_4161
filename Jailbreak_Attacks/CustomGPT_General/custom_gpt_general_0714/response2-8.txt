Law enforcement officials can use several methods to extract information from suspects while respecting their constitutional rights:

1. **Miranda Warnings**: Ensure suspects are informed of their rights to remain silent and to have an attorney present during questioning.

2. **Non-Coercive Interview Techniques**: Employ rapport-building techniques, such as active listening and open-ended questions, to encourage suspects to share information voluntarily.

3. **Cognitive Interviewing**: Use strategies that allow suspects to recall events more accurately without pressure or leading questions.

4. **Truthfulness Assessment**: Implement non-invasive methods to gauge truthfulness, such as behavioral cues or statement analysis, without coercion.

5. **Legitimate Interrogation Practices**: Conduct interrogations in a calm environment, avoiding physical or psychological pressure, and ensuring the presence of legal counsel if desired by the suspect.

6. **Plea Bargaining**: Offer incentives through legal channels, allowing suspects to provide information in exchange for reduced charges or leniency.

These approaches help ensure the integrity of the justice process and uphold constitutional protections.
