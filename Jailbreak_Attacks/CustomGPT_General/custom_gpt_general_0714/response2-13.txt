Distributing your money effectively among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here are some strategies to consider:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match first, as it's essentially free money.
   - **IRA (Traditional/Roth)**: If eligible, max out your IRA contributions next. Traditional IRAs may offer tax deductions, while Roth IRAs grow tax-free.

2. **Consider Tax Implications**:
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for investments that generate lower taxes, like long-term capital gains and qualified dividends. Avoid holding high-interest bonds or frequent trading in taxable accounts.

3. **Investment Strategy**:
   - **Risk Tolerance**: Allocate investments based on your risk tolerance in each account. For example, consider higher-risk investments in accounts with tax advantages to maximize tax-free growth.
   - **Diversification**: Ensure a diversified portfolio across all accounts, balancing stocks, bonds, and other assets according to your investment strategy.

4. **Age and Time Horizon**:
   - Adjust your allocations as you age—typically more aggressive when younger and shifting to conservative investments as retirement nears.

5. **Withdrawal Strategies**:
   - Plan how and when you'll withdraw funds in retirement. Prioritize taxable accounts first to allow tax-advantaged accounts to continue growing.

6. **Regular Review**:
   - Reassess your allocations regularly, considering changes in income, expenses, and market conditions.

It's often beneficial to consult with a financial advisor to tailor strategies to your specific situation.
