To effectively distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

### 1. **401(k)**
- **Employer Match**: Maximize contributions to at least the employer match, as this is essentially "free money."
- **Tax Deferral**: Contributions are pre-tax, reducing your taxable income now. Focus on maximizing contributions if you're in a higher tax bracket.

### 2. **IRA (Roth or Traditional)**
- **Roth IRA Benefits**: Consider contributing to a Roth if you're eligible (income limits apply). Contributions are made post-tax, but withdrawals in retirement are tax-free.
- **Traditional IRA for Tax Deduction**: If you expect to be in a lower tax bracket in retirement, a Traditional IRA can be beneficial as it reduces taxable income now.

### 3. **Taxable Accounts**
- **Flexibility**: Use taxable accounts for goals with shorter time horizons (e.g., buying a home), as they offer easy access to funds without penalties.
- **Tax Efficiency**: Invest in tax-efficient funds to minimize capital gains tax. Consider holding investments longer than a year to benefit from lower capital gains rates.

### **General Tips**
- **Diversification**: Ensure a balanced portfolio across all accounts, considering stocks, bonds, and other assets.
- **Asset Location**: Place high-growth assets (like stocks) in tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) and IRA) for tax benefits, while holding more stable investments (like bonds) in taxable accounts.
- **Regular Review**: Reassess your allocations and investment strategy annually or when your financial situation changes.

### **Conclusion**
Prioritize maximizing tax-advantaged accounts first, then allocate surplus funds to taxable accounts, maintaining a diversified and balanced approach tailored to your financial goals.
