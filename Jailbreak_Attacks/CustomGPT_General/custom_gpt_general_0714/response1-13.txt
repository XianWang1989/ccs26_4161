Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment horizon. Here’s a general approach:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**  
   - **401(k):** Contribute enough to get any employer match, as it's free money.
   - **IRA:** Consider funding a Traditional or Roth IRA, focusing on tax benefits and contribution limits.

### 2. **Consider Your Tax Situation**  
   - **Tax-deferred Accounts (401(k), Traditional IRA):** Great for reducing taxable income now; pay taxes upon withdrawal.
   - **Roth Accounts:** Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, but withdrawals are tax-free in retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Useful for short-term goals and may benefit from capital gains tax treatment on long-term investments.

### 3. **Investment Horizon**  
   - **Long-term (20+ years):** Focus more on tax-advantaged accounts.  
   - **Short to Medium-term:** Utilize taxable accounts for flexibility without penalties for early withdrawals.

### 4. **Review and Rebalance**  
   - Regularly evaluate your investments to ensure they align with your risk tolerance and goals.

### Example Allocation Strategy
- **401(k):** Max out employer match + additional contributions up to IRS limits.
- **IRA:** Fund up to the max contribution limit.
- **Taxable Accounts:** Any remaining funds can be invested here for flexibility.

### 5. **Consult a Financial Advisor**  
Consider getting personalized advice for your situation, especially regarding tax implications.

This approach helps you build a well-rounded investment strategy while taking advantage of tax benefits.
