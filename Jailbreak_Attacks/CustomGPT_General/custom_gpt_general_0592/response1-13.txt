Determining the best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts involves several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, investment options, and time horizon. Here are some key considerations:

### 1. **Understand Account Types**

- **401(k)**: Typically offers employer matching contributions and tax-deferred growth. Contributions lower your taxable income.
- **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**:
  - **Traditional IRA**: Offers tax-deferred growth, but contributions may be tax-deductible.
  - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, but qualified withdrawals are tax-free.
- **Taxable Accounts**: Investments here are subject to capital gains taxes upon sale, but offer flexibility without withdrawal penalties.

### 2. **Prioritize Contributions**

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a match on your 401(k), contribute enough to take full advantage of this "free money."
  
2. **Focus on Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - Contribute to your IRA up to the limit to benefit from tax advantages. Choose a Traditional or Roth IRA based on your current vs. projected future tax rates.
   - After maximizing the IRA, continue to contribute to your 401(k) until you reach your personal contribution limit.

3. **Invest in Taxable Accounts Last**: Use these for investments that may generate capital gains or dividends, especially if you anticipate needing access before retirement.

### 3. **Consider Tax Implications**

- **Tax Diversification**: Aim for a mix of accounts that provide both tax-deferred and tax-free growth. This can give you flexibility in retirement to manage your tax liability.
  
- **Long-Term Growth in Tax-Advantaged Accounts**: Invest growth-oriented assets (like stocks) in tax-advantaged accounts to reduce tax exposure on capital gains.

- **Hold Bonds in Taxable Accounts**: If you have bonds, consider holding them in taxable accounts where interest is taxed at ordinary income rates, as their growth potential is lower.

### 4. **Rebalance Regularly**

Monitor your investments and rebalance as necessary to maintain your desired asset allocation across all accounts, adjusting for changes in financial goals or market conditions.

### 5. **Consult a Financial Advisor**

Consider consulting with a financial advisor to create a personalized strategy based on your specific financial situation, goals, and risk tolerance.

By strategically distributing your contributions and investments across these accounts, you can help maximize your long-term financial growth and minimize taxes.
