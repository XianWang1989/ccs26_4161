To effectively distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following approach:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First:**
   - **401(k):** Contribute enough to receive any employer match, as this is essentially free money. Aim for the annual contribution limit if you're able.
   - **IRA:** Contribute to a traditional or Roth IRA based on your eligibility and tax situation. Maximize this if your 401(k) has already received the company match.

### 2. **Emergency Fund:**
   - Before distributing excess funds, ensure you have a sufficient emergency fund (typically 3-6 months of expenses).

### 3. **Taxable Accounts:**
   - After maximizing contributions to your 401(k) and IRA, consider investing additional funds in a taxable account. This is particularly useful for short-term goals or for flexibility with withdrawals.

### 4. **Investment Strategy:**
   - **Diversify Investments:** Spread investments across various assets (stocks, bonds, mutual funds) according to your risk tolerance and time horizon.
   - **Rebalance:** Regularly review and adjust your allocations based on performance and changes in your life circumstances.

### 5. **Tax Implications:**
   - Be mindful of tax implications for withdrawals from different accounts. Taxable accounts can be more flexible but may incur capital gains taxes.

### 6. **Consult a Financial Advisor:**
   - If you're unsure about the best strategy, consider consulting with a financial advisor for personalized advice.

By following this structured approach, you can optimize your growth potential while managing risk and tax implications effectively.
