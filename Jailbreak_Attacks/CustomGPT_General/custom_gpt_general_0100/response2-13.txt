Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here’s a general approach:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match first, as this is essentially free money.

2. **Contribute to IRA**: If eligible, contribute to an IRA (Traditional or Roth) next. A Roth IRA offers tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement, while a Traditional IRA gives you tax deductions now.

3. **Return to 401(k)**: After maximizing your IRA contributions, increase your 401(k) contributions, especially if you're in a high tax bracket.

4. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**: If you’ve met retirement account limits or want more liquidity, invest in taxable accounts. This is useful for mid-term goals or emergencies.

5. **Asset Allocation**: Each account type can hold different investments. Consider a more aggressive allocation in tax-advantaged accounts, while keeping more conservative investments in taxable accounts due to potential capital gains taxes.

6. **Review Regularly**: Reassess your investment strategy annually to adjust based on performance and changing life circumstances.

Consult a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your unique situation.
