Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment horizon. Here’s a general strategy:

1. **Maximize Employer Contributions**: Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to receive any employer match, as this is essentially "free money."

2. **Contribute to IRAs**: If you’re eligible, consider maximizing contributions to a Traditional or Roth IRA. Roth IRAs offer tax-free withdrawals in retirement, which can be beneficial if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket later.

3. **401(k) Contributions**: After maxing out employer matching and IRAs, increase your contributions to your 401(k) up to the annual limit. Focus on pre-tax contributions to reduce taxable income.

4. **Taxable Accounts**: Invest in taxable accounts if you still have funds after maxing tax-advantaged options. Use them for shorter-term goals or to maintain liquidity.

5. **Asset Allocation**: 
   - **401(k) and IRA**: Typically better for long-term investments (stocks, mutual funds).
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Favor more tax-efficient investments (index funds, ETFs) to minimize capital gains taxes.

6. **Monitor and Adjust**: Regularly review your allocations based on performance, market conditions, and changes in your financial situation.

Consulting a financial advisor can provide personalized guidance tailored to your specific needs.
