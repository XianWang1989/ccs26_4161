Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including tax implications, time horizon, and investment goals. Here’s a general approach:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts:**
   - **401(k):** Contribute enough to get any employer match first. This is essentially free money.
   - **IRA:** Consider maxing out traditional or Roth IRA contributions, depending on your income level and tax situation.

### 2. **Consider Tax Implications:**
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Use these for investments that generate lower tax liabilities (like long-term capital gains or qualified dividends). This is where you can hold investments you might sell in the near future.

### 3. **Diversification:**
   - Ensure a diversified portfolio across all accounts to mitigate risk. Consider asset allocation based on your investment strategy.

### 4. **Withdrawal Strategy:**
   - Plan how your funds will be withdrawn during retirement. Generally, you might want to withdraw from taxable accounts first to let tax-advantaged accounts grow.

### 5. **Rebalance Regularly:**
   - Adjust your allocations based on market performance and your changing goals over time to maintain your desired risk level.

### Sample Distribution Strategy:
- **50% in 401(k)**
- **30% in IRA**
- **20% in Taxable Accounts**

### Adjusting Based on Life Stage:
- Young investors might lean more into growth strategies in tax-advantaged accounts, while those nearing retirement might shift towards more conservative investments.

Consulting a financial advisor for personalized advice based on your specific situation can also be very helpful.
