Distributing your money across a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment strategy. Here’s a general guide:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your 401(k) offers an employer match, contribute enough to get the full match first. It’s essentially free money.

2. **Prioritize Tax Advantage**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are tax-deferred, which helps reduce taxable income. Consider maxing out contributions if your employer offers a match.
   - **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**: If you qualify, a Roth IRA allows for tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement. A Traditional IRA offers tax deductions on contributions (subject to income limits). Prioritize either based on your current versus expected future tax rates.

3. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**: After maximizing tax-advantaged accounts, invest in taxable accounts. These are flexible for withdrawals and may be beneficial for shorter-term goals.

4. **Asset Allocation**: Within each account, maintain a diversified asset allocation based on your risk tolerance and time horizon. Stocks may be best in tax-advantaged accounts due to potential for higher growth (and thus higher taxes if sold in taxable accounts).

5. **Consider Tax Implications**: Be mindful of capital gains taxes in taxable accounts. Long-term investments (held for more than a year) are typically taxed at a lower rate than short-term investments.

6. **Rebalance Regularly**: Review and adjust your portfolio periodically to ensure it aligns with your goals and risk tolerance.

7. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: Tailoring this strategy to your unique financial situation can yield the best results, especially considering tax laws and personal circumstances.

This approach will help maximize growth while managing risks and tax impacts over time.
