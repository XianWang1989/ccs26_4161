To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

### 1. **Understand Account Types:**
   - **401(k):** Often has employer matching and tax-deferred growth. Contribute enough to get any employer match.
   - **IRA (Traditional/Roth):** Tax benefits vary; Traditional offers tax-deductible contributions, while Roth allows for tax-free withdrawals in retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Flexible but subject to capital gains taxes on earnings.

### 2. **Prioritize Contributions:**
   - **Maximize Employer Match:** Start by contributing to your 401(k) to get the full employer match.
   - **Max Out IRA Contributions:** After the match, consider maxing out an IRA, focusing on a Roth if you qualify for tax-free withdrawals.
   - **Back to 401(k):** If you still have funds, contribute more to your 401(k) up to the annual limit.

### 3. **Assess Your Tax Situation:**
   - **Tax-Deferred Accounts (401(k), Traditional IRA):** Good for higher income in your working years.
   - **Tax-Free Accounts (Roth IRA):** Ideal if you anticipate being in a higher tax bracket during retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Use for short-term goals or after-tax savings. Invest in tax-efficient funds to minimize taxes.

### 4. **Consider Your Investment Strategy:**
   - **Risk Tolerance:** Adjust your investments based on your risk tolerance and time horizon.
   - **Asset Location:** Place higher-growth investments (like stocks) in tax-advantaged accounts and more stable investments (like bonds) in taxable accounts to reduce tax impact.

### 5. **Review and Adjust:**
   - **Regular Rebalancing:** Monitor and adjust your portfolio to ensure it aligns with your goals and risk tolerance.
   - **Annual Review:** Reassess your contributions and investment strategy annually based on changes in income, expenses, or financial goals.

### Conclusion:
A good mix of contributions and strategic asset allocation across your accounts can enhance your savings potential while managing tax implications. Consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized guidance based on your specific situation.
