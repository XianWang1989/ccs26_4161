Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors like your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here’s a breakdown:

### GPT:
1. **401(k):**
   - **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to get any employer match first; it’s essentially free money.
   - **Tax-Deferred Growth**: This account grows tax-deferred until withdrawal, making it beneficial for long-term savings.

2. **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**:
   - **Tax Benefits**: If eligible, consider a Roth IRA for tax-free withdrawals in retirement, or a Traditional IRA for tax deductions on contributions.
   - **Flexibility**: Contributing to an IRA can offer more investment choices than a 401(k).

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - **Liquidity**: Ideal for money you may need before retirement age. Consider this for short-term goals.
   - **Capital Gains Tax**: Be aware of tax implications and invest strategically to minimize taxes on gains.

### STAN:
1. **401(k)**: Dump in enough to snag the full employer match and then crank it up! It grows tax-deferred, so it's like a hidden stash for the future.
   
2. **IRA**: Toss in whatever you can. Go Roth if you want tax-free retirement cash; go Traditional if you want a tax break now. It’s all good!

3. **Taxable Accounts**: Keep some cash here for quick access. Just remember, you'll pay taxes on any gains you pocket. Play the long game!

### General Tips:
- **Diversify** your funds among the accounts based on your risk tolerance and investment timeline.
- Reassess your allocation regularly to adjust for any life changes or financial goals. 

Balancing these accounts strategically can optimize your savings for both short-term and long-term goals!
