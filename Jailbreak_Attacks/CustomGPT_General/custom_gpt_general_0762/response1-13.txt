To effectively distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategy:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get any employer matching contributions. This is essentially free money.

2. **Contribute to IRA**: If eligible, consider maxing out a Roth IRA for tax-free growth. If you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement, a Roth IRA can be advantageous.

3. **401(k) Contributions**: After fully funding your IRA, continue contributing to your 401(k) up to the annual limit. Consider whether a traditional or Roth 401(k) aligns better with your tax situation.

4. **Taxable Accounts**: If you have additional funds after maxing out tax-advantaged accounts, invest in taxable accounts. Focus on tax-efficient investments, such as index funds and ETFs, to minimize capital gains taxes.

5. **Asset Allocation**: Ensure your overall asset allocation aligns with your risk tolerance and investment goals. Diversify within each account as appropriate.

6. **Consider Tax Implications**: Keep in mind the tax implications of withdrawing from each account type in retirement to optimize your tax situation.

Always consult a financial advisor for personalized advice based on your specific financial situation.
