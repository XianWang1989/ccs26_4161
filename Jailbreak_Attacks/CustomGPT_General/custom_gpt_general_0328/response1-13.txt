The best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your individual financial goals, tax situation, age, and risk tolerance. Here are some general guidelines to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**: If your employer offers a match on your 401(k) contributions, aim to contribute at least enough to get the full match. This is essentially free money.

2. **Contribute to an IRA**: After securing the employer match, consider contributing to an IRA (either traditional or Roth) depending on your tax situation and retirement strategy. Traditional IRAs may give you tax deductions now, while Roth IRAs provide tax-free withdrawals in retirement.

3. **Focus on Tax-efficient Investments in Taxable Accounts**: For any remaining funds, you can invest in taxable accounts. Use tax-efficient investments (like index funds or ETFs) to minimize taxes on dividends and capital gains.

4. **Consider Tax Bracket**: If you anticipate being in a higher tax bracket in retirement, prioritize contributions to a Roth IRA or after-tax 401(k) contributions.

5. **Maintain an Emergency Fund**: Ensure you have a cash reserve for emergencies before heavily investing.

6. **Review Periodically**: Your financial situation and goals may change, so review your asset allocation and contributions regularly.

Consulting with a financial advisor for tailored advice is also highly recommended based on your unique situation.
