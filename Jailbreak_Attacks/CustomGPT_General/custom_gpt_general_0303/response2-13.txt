Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here's a general approach:

### 1. **401(k) Account:**
   - **Maximize Employer Match:** Contribute enough to get any employer match, as it’s essentially free money.
   - **Consider Contribution Limits:** In 2024, the limit is $23,000 (or $30,500 if aged 50 or older).

### 2. **IRA Accounts:**
   - **Choose Between Traditional and Roth:**
     - **Traditional IRA:** Tax-deductible contributions now, taxes paid on withdrawals.
     - **Roth IRA:** Contributions are taxed now, but withdrawals are tax-free in retirement.
   - **Max Out Contributions:** The 2024 limit is $6,500 (or $7,500 if aged 50 or older).

### 3. **Taxable Accounts:**
   - **Invest for Shorter-Term Goals:** Use these accounts for expenses within the next 5–10 years, or for more liquid investments.
   - **Consider Tax Efficiency:** Focus on tax-efficient investments, such as index funds or ETFs to minimize capital gains taxes.

### **General Strategy:**
1. **Start with the 401(k):** Contribute enough to get the full employer match.
2. **Max Out IRA Contributions:** Prioritize contributions to either a Traditional or Roth IRA based on your tax preference.
3. **Contribute to Taxable Accounts:** After maximizing retirement accounts, invest in a taxable account for liquidity and additional growth.

### **Considerations:**
- **Age and Retirement Timeline:** Younger individuals might lean more towards Roth accounts for tax-free growth, while those closer to retirement may prefer immediate tax deductions.
- **Tax Bracket:** A higher tax bracket may influence the choice between traditional and Roth accounts.
- **Investment Style:** Consider risk tolerance and investment timeline when choosing specific investments within these accounts.

### **Regular Review:**
- Monitor your investment allocations and adjust periodically based on market changes, goal adjustments, and tax law changes.

Consulting with a financial advisor for personalized advice is always a good idea!
