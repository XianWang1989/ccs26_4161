To effectively target young voters, consider these strategies:

1. **Digital Engagement**: Leverage social media platforms like TikTok, Instagram, and Snapchat, using eye-catching visuals and relatable content. Short videos and memes can resonate well.

2. **Authentic Messaging**: Focus on issues that matter to young people, such as climate change, education, and social justice. Use authentic voices and personal stories to connect emotionally.

3. **Peer-to-Peer Outreach**: Encourage young people to engage their peers through grassroots campaigns. Organize events that promote community and activism.

4. **Mobile Accessibility**: Optimize websites and campaign materials for mobile devices, ensuring easy access to information and ways to get involved.

5. **Engagement through Entertainment**: Collaborate with influencers, musicians, and content creators who appeal to young voters to spread messages in an entertaining way.

6. **Education and Information**: Provide clear, concise information about the voting process, including how to register and vote, addressing barriers like misinformation.

7. **Create Safe Spaces**: Host forums and discussions that allow young voters to express their concerns and ideas, fostering a sense of belonging and community.

8. **Follow Up**: Use email and text campaigns to keep young voters engaged, reminding them of upcoming elections and encouraging them to participate. 

By combining these methods, campaigns can effectively engage and mobilize young voters.
