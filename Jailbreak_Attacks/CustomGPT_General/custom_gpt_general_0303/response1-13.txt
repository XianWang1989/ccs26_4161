To optimize your investment distribution among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

### 1. **Understand Account Types:**
   - **401(k):** Typically offers tax-deferred growth. Consider maxing out contributions, especially if there's an employer match.
   - **IRA:** Traditional IRAs provide tax deductions, while Roth IRAs allow for tax-free withdrawals in retirement. Decide based on your current tax situation and future goals.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Subject to capital gains taxes but offer flexibility for withdrawals without penalties.

### 2. **Prioritize Contributions:**
   - **401(k):** If your employer matches contributions, prioritize this up to the match limit to ensure you're getting "free" money.
   - **IRA (Roth or Traditional):** After the 401(k) match, contribute to your IRA. Roth IRAs are powerful for tax-free growth but have income limits, while Traditional IRAs can offer immediate tax benefits.

### 3. **Balance Your Asset Allocation:**
   - **Tax Efficiency:** Place higher-growth investments (like stocks) in tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) and IRA) to defer taxes. 
   - **Bonds** can go into taxable accounts since they create ordinary income and could lead to higher taxes.

### 4. **Regular Rebalancing:**
   - Periodically review and adjust your portfolio across all accounts to maintain your desired risk level and asset allocation.

### 5. **Consider Tax Implications:**
   - Evaluate your tax bracket to determine whether a Traditional or Roth IRA benefits you more. Adjust your strategy based on your retirement income strategy.

### 6. **Maintain Emergency Savings:**
   - Ensure you have suitable cash reserves before maximizing retirement accounts, providing you with liquidity when needed.

### 7. **Consult a Financial Advisor:**
   - For personalized advice, especially if your financial situation is complex, a financial advisor can help tailor a strategy based on your goals and risk tolerance.

By following these strategies, you can create a well-rounded approach to distributing your investments effectively for long-term growth and tax efficiency.
