Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here's a practical approach:

### 1. Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First
- **401(k):** Contribute enough to get any employer match. This is essentially free money.
- **IRA:** If eligible, consider maxing out your IRA contributions (Traditional or Roth) next for additional tax benefits.

### 2. Tax Considerations
- **401(k) and IRA:** Contributions may be tax-deductible or tax-free (Roth), and withdrawals in retirement are typically taxed at your ordinary income rate.
- **Taxable Accounts:** Long-term capital gains (holding assets for over a year) are taxed at a lower rate. Diversifying your investments in this account can help manage tax impact.

### 3. Emergency Fund
- Ensure you maintain an emergency fund in a liquid account, separate from these investment accounts.

### 4. Diversification Strategy
- Distribute investments within these accounts based on your risk tolerance, investment horizon, and market conditions. Generally:
  - **Stock Funds:** Higher growth potential, suitable for long-term growth.
  - **Bond Funds:** Generally lower risk, providing steady income.

### 5. Review and Adjust
- Regularly review your asset allocation. As you approach retirement, gradually shift to more conservative investments.

### Example Strategy
- **401(k):** Contribute up to the match, then max out your IRA.
- If funds are still available, continue contributing to your 401(k).
- Use any extra money for taxable accounts, focusing on low-turnover, tax-efficient investments.

### Final Thoughts
Consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your specific financial situation.
