Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here’s a general guide to help you decide:

### 1. **Maximize Contributions to Tax-Advantaged Accounts:**
   - **401(k)**: If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to get the full match first. This is essentially free money.
   - **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**: After maxing out your 401(k) match, consider contributing to an IRA. The choice between a Traditional IRA and Roth IRA depends on your current vs. expected future tax rates.

### 2. **Tax Considerations:**
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Capital gains and dividends from these accounts are taxed at a lower rate than ordinary income. Holding investments long-term can minimize these taxes.
   - **Tax-Deferred Growth**: Funds in 401(k)s and IRAs grow tax-deferred (or tax-free in the case of Roth IRAs), making them advantageous for long-term growth.

### 3. **Emergency Fund and Short-Term Needs:**
   - Ensure you have an adequate emergency fund, typically 3-6 months of living expenses, in a high-yield savings account.
   - Keep short-term savings in taxable accounts for easy access.

### 4. **Investment Strategy:**
   - **401(k) and IRA**: Focus on a diversified portfolio based on your risk tolerance. Generally, you can take more risks in these accounts since they’re for long-term growth.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for investments that may generate taxable income or those you may need to sell in the short term.

### 5. **Monitor and Adjust:**
   - Regularly review your asset allocation and rebalance as needed. Consider your changing financial situation and market conditions.

### Sample Distribution Strategy:
- **Max out your 401(k) match first.**
- **Contribute to an IRA until you reach the annual limit.**
- **Put any additional savings into a taxable account.**

### Consult a Financial Advisor:
Each situation is unique, so consider discussing your strategy with a financial advisor to tailor it to your specific circumstances.

By thinking through these factors, you can create a balanced approach to funding your accounts effectively.
