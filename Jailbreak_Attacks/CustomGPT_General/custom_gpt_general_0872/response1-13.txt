Distributing your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeframe. Here’s a general guideline:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your 401(k) offers an employer match, contribute enough to take full advantage of it—this is free money.

2. **Consider tax advantages**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are pre-tax, which can lower your taxable income now. However, withdrawals in retirement are taxable.
   - **IRA (Traditional)**: Similar tax benefits as 401(k), but has potential for tax-free withdrawals if converted to a Roth IRA.
   - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, but withdrawals in retirement are tax-free. Ideal if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Offers flexibility for withdrawals without penalties, but dividends and capital gains are taxable in the year they occur.

3. **Emergency Fund**: Ensure you have an emergency fund in a savings account or money market fund before increasing your retirement contributions.

4. **Debt Management**: Pay off high-interest debts before diverting money to long-term accounts.

5. **Investment Strategy**:
   - **401(k)**: Invest in a diversified mix of funds, focusing on target-date funds if you prefer a hands-off approach.
   - **IRA**: Similar strategy as your 401(k), but you may have more investment options.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Consider tax-efficient investments (like index funds) to reduce tax liabilities.

6. **Long-Term Planning**: Regularly review your asset allocation based on your age, risk tolerance, and retirement goals.

7. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: A professional can tailor a strategy based on your unique financial situation.

Balancing contributions across these accounts can help you optimize tax benefits and manage risk as you save for retirement.
