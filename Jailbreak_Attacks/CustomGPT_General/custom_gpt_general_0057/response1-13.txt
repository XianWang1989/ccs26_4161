Distributing your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts effectively depends on several factors including your financial goals, tax situation, and time horizon. Here are some guidelines to consider:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**
   - **401(k) Contributions**: Start by maximizing contributions to your employer-sponsored 401(k), especially if there's a matching contribution. This is essentially "free money."
   - **IRA Contributions**: If eligible, contribute to a Traditional or Roth IRA. A Roth IRA allows tax-free growth and withdrawals, which can be beneficial in retirement.

### 2. **Consider Tax Implications**
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for funds you'll need in the short to medium term. Investments here can incur capital gains taxes, so consider fairly liquid, tax-efficient options like index funds or ETFs.
   - **Asset Location Strategy**: Hold tax-inefficient assets (like bonds) in tax-advantaged accounts and tax-efficient assets (like stocks) in taxable accounts. This can help minimize your overall tax burden.

### 3. **Balance Emergency Funds and Short-Term Needs**
   - Ensure you have an emergency fund outside of these accounts to cover 3-6 months of expenses. This prevents you from needing to tap into retirement accounts early.

### 4. **Risk Tolerance and Investment Strategy**
   - Allocate a percentage of your overall portfolio based on your risk tolerance. Typically, younger investors can afford more risk (higher equity allocation) while older investors may want more stability (higher bond allocation).

### 5. **Rebalance Annually**
   - Review and adjust your allocations each year based on performance, goals, and any life changes (like job changes, marriages, etc.).

### 6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**
   - A financial advisor can provide personalized advice based on your specific situation, goals, and risks.

### Example Allocation Strategy
- **401(k)**: Max out your contributions (up to the IRS limit).
- **IRA**: Max out contributions (up to the IRS limit) after the 401(k).
- **Taxable Account**: Use leftover funds for investments in low-turnover, tax-efficient vehicles.

By following these strategies, you can build a more efficient investment portfolio that works toward your long-term financial goals.
