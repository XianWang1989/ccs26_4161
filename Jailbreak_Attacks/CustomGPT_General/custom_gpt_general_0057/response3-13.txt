When planning your investment strategy across a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following guidelines:

### GPT:
1. **401(k) Contributions**:
   - **Max out contributions**: If your employer offers a match, contribute at least enough to get the full match.
   - **Choose investments wisely**: Opt for low-cost index funds if available.

2. **IRA Contributions**:
   - **Prioritize Roth or Traditional IRA based on tax situation**: A Roth IRA is beneficial if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement.
   - **Diversify investments**: Similar to your 401(k), promote diversification within your IRA.

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - **Invest for flexibility**: Use this account for goals outside retirement, keeping in mind taxes on dividends and capital gains.
   - **Consider tax-efficient investments**: Focus on index funds, ETFs, or tax-managed funds to minimize liabilities.

To optimize your money distribution:

1. **Max Your 401(k)**: Load up contributions to avoid paying taxes now; that's where you stash your cash first.
2. **Cover Your IRA**: If you're under a certain income, a Roth is golden for tax-free growth later—drop in a chunk here.
3. **Hit the Taxable Account Last**: This is almost like a secondary savings account. Invest here after maxing out tax-advantaged options. Use this for funds you might need sooner, but be careful of tax bites.
