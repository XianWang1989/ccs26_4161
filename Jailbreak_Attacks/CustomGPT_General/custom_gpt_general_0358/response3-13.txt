Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment horizon. Here’s a general approach:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match. This is essentially “free money.”
   - **IRA**: Max out contributions to a traditional or Roth IRA, depending on your tax situation. Roth IRAs are beneficial if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement.

### 2. **Consider Your Tax Situation**
   - **401(k) and Traditional IRA**: Contributions are tax-deductible now but taxed upon withdrawal.
   - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, but withdrawals are tax-free in retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: These are taxed annually on dividends and capital gains, so use these for investments that you might want to access before retirement.

### 3. **Investment Time Horizon**
   - **Long-Term Goals**: Focus on growth investments (stocks) in tax-advantaged accounts to maximize tax benefits.
   - **Shorter-Term Goals**: Use taxable accounts for more conservative investments to avoid early withdrawal penalties from retirement accounts.

### 4. **Diversify Your Investments**
   - Ensure a mix of asset types (stocks, bonds, etc.) across all accounts, tailored to your risk tolerance.

### 5. **Rebalance Periodically**
   - Regularly review and adjust your allocations to maintain your desired risk level.

**Example Distribution:**
1. Max out your 401(k) up to the employer match.
2. Contribute to your IRA next.
3. Invest any excess funds in taxable accounts.

### Conclusion
Your specific distribution should align with your financial goals, time frame, and risk tolerance. Consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized guidance.
