Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts can optimize your investment strategy. Here are some general guidelines:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match first, as this is essentially "free money." Aim to contribute the maximum allowable if you're financially comfortable.
   - **IRA**: Consider contributing to a Roth IRA if you qualify, as withdrawals are tax-free in retirement. If you expect your tax rate to be higher in retirement, this could be beneficial.

2. **Emergency Fund**:
   - Ensure you have an adequate emergency fund in a separate account before investing heavily.

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - Use taxable accounts for investments that generate qualified dividends or long-term capital gains, which are taxed at lower rates than ordinary income. Consider using these accounts for investments with a longer time horizon.

4. **Asset Allocation**:
   - Each account can hold different types of investments based on your risk tolerance and time horizon. For example, you might hold more stocks in your tax-advantaged accounts and bonds in taxable accounts for more tax-efficient growth.

5. **Rebalance Regularly**:
   - Periodically review and adjust your allocations to ensure they remain aligned with your financial goals and risk tolerance.

6. **Seek Professional Advice**:
   - Consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized strategies based on your financial situation.

This approach can help balance growth, tax efficiency, and risk management in your overall investment strategy.
