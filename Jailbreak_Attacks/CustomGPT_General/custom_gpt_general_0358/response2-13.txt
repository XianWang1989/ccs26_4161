Distributing your money across a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here’s a general strategy:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money.

2. **Contribute to an IRA**: If eligible, contribute to a traditional or Roth IRA. The tax advantages can enhance your long-term growth.

3. **Fund 401(k) Contributions**: After maximizing the match and IRA contributions, continue to contribute to your 401(k) up to the annual limit.

4. **Taxable Accounts**: Use taxable accounts for funds you may need access to before retirement or for investment flexibility. Focus on tax-efficient investments (e.g., index funds) to minimize capital gains taxes.

5. **Consider Tax Implications**: Generally, you want to prioritize tax-advantaged accounts first. As you get closer to retirement, you might shift your focus based on your expected tax bracket.

6. **Diversification**: Ensure that your overall portfolio is diversified across asset classes, regardless of the account type.

7. **Regular Review**: Periodically review and adjust your allocations based on life changes, market conditions, and your financial goals.

Consult a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your unique situation.
