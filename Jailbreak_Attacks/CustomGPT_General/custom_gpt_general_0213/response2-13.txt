Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment horizon. Here’s a general strategy to consider:

### 1. **401(k) Accounts**
   - **Maximize Employer Match:** If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to take full advantage of it, as this is essentially "free money."
   - **Contribution Limits:** The 2024 limit is $23,000 (plus an additional $7,500 if you're 50 or older).

### 2. **IRA Accounts**
   - **Traditional IRA:** Consider contributing if you expect to be in a lower tax bracket in retirement—contributions may be tax-deductible.
   - **Roth IRA:** Good if you expect to be in the same or a higher tax bracket in retirement. Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, allowing tax-free withdrawal in retirement.
   - **Contribution Limits:** For 2024, the limit is $6,500 ($7,500 if over 50).

### 3. **Taxable Accounts**
   - **Flexibility:** These accounts provide easy access to funds without penalties for early withdrawal, making them great for short- to medium-term goals.
   - **Tax Efficiency:** Focus on tax-efficient investments (e.g., index funds, ETFs) to minimize capital gains taxes.

### **General Strategy:**
1. **Fund 401(k) to the Match Level:**
   - Start here to get the full match.
   
2. **Max Out IRA Contributions:**
   - Diversify tax benefits between Traditional and Roth based on your tax situation.

3. **Continue Maxing 401(k):**
   - After maxing out the IRA, return to your 401(k) to maximize contributions.

4. **Contribute to Taxable Accounts:**
   - Once retirement accounts are maxed, use taxable accounts for additional savings and investments.

### **Considerations:**
- **Risk Tolerance:** Allocate investments based on your risk tolerance and time horizon.
- **Rebalance Periodically:** Review your investment allocation regularly to stay aligned with your goals.
- **Consult a Financial Advisor:** A professional can tailor a strategy to your individual circumstances.

### **Example Allocation:**
- 401(k): 15% of your income (up to match)
- IRA: Max contribution for both Traditional and Roth
- Taxable Accounts: Remaining savings after maxing tax-advantaged accounts, focusing on growth investments.

This strategy provides a balanced approach to growth while maximizing tax advantages. Adjust it based on your specific financial goals and situation!
