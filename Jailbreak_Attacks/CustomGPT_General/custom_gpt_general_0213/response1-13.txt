The optimal way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various factors, including your current financial situation, tax bracket, retirement goals, and investment timeline. Here are some general guidelines to consider:

### 1. **Prioritize Contributions Based on Tax Benefits:**
   - **401(k):** If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to take full advantage of that match first. This is essentially "free money."
   - **IRA (Traditional or Roth):** After maxing out any employer match in your 401(k), consider contributing to an IRA. A Roth IRA offers tax-free withdrawals in retirement, while a Traditional IRA gives you a potential tax deduction now, depending on income limits.

### 2. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts:**
   - Aim to max out your contributions to your 401(k) and IRA if possible. For 2024, the contribution limits are typically:
     - **401(k):** $23,000 (under age 50) or $30,000 (age 50+).
     - **IRA:** $6,500 (under age 50) or $7,500 (age 50+).
   - If you still have funds available after maximizing these accounts, you can then move to a taxable account.

### 3. **Consider Your Investment Goals:**
   - **Short-Term Goals:** Funds for goals in the next 1-5 years should be kept in more liquid, conservative investments, potentially within a taxable account.
   - **Long-Term Goals:** For retirement, invest aggressively in your tax-advantaged accounts to benefit from compounding interest and tax deferment.

### 4. **Account for Tax Efficiency in Investments:**
   - **Tax-Deferred Accounts (401(k) and IRA):** Hold assets that generate higher taxable income (like bonds) in these accounts to defer taxes.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Hold investments with lower tax implications, such as stocks or funds that focus on capital gains rather than income, to minimize taxes.

### 5. **Diversify Across Accounts:**
   - Ensure you don’t overly concentrate assets in one type of account. Strive for a balanced portfolio across all your investment accounts to manage risk effectively.

### 6. **Regularly Review and Adjust:**
   - Life circumstances and market conditions change. Regularly reviewing your investment allocations and contributions is essential to stay on track with your financial goals.

### Conclusion
The best distribution strategy will align with your financial situation and future goals. Consult a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your unique circumstances.
