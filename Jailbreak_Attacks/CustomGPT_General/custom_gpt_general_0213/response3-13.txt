Distributing money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts is essential for optimizing tax efficiency and achieving your financial goals. Here’s a general approach:

### GPT:
1. **Maximize Employer Contributions**: Start with your 401(k) by contributing enough to get any employer match. This is essentially free money.
   
2. **Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**: After the match, prioritize contributions to your IRA (either Roth or traditional) for its tax benefits. If your income is too high for a Roth, consider a traditional IRA.

3. **Tax Diversification**: Contribute to both pre-tax (traditional 401(k) and IRA) and post-tax (Roth IRA) accounts if eligible. This provides flexibility in retirement.

4. **Funding Taxable Accounts**: Use taxable accounts for additional savings once you’re maximizing contributions in the above tax-advantaged accounts. These can be beneficial for short-term goals as you can access them without penalties.

5. **Asset Allocation**: Within each account, consider keeping more aggressive investments (like stocks) in taxable accounts to benefit from capital gains rates, while placing stable investments (like bonds) in tax-advantaged accounts.

6. **Consider Your Tax Bracket**: Take into account your current and expected future tax rates to determine which accounts to prioritize.
