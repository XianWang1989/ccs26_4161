To effectively distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following steps:

1. **Understand Account Types**:
   - **401(k)**: Typically funded with pre-tax dollars; grows tax-deferred until retirement. Check if your employer offers matching contributions and aim to contribute at least enough to get the full match.
   - **IRA (Individual Retirement Account)**: Can be a Traditional IRA (pre-tax) or Roth IRA (post-tax). Decide based on your tax situation and retirement goals.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: These accounts don't have the same tax advantages but offer more flexibility for withdrawals and investment choices. 

2. **Evaluate Contribution Limits**:
   - Max out contributions to your 401(k) if your employer matches contributions. For 2023, the limit is $22,500 (or $30,000 if age 50 or older).
   - For IRAs, the contribution limit is $6,500 (or $7,500 if age 50 or older) in 2023.

3. **Consider Your Tax Situation**:
   - A traditional 401(k) and IRA can lower your taxable income now, helpful if you expect to be in a lower tax bracket in retirement.
   - A Roth IRA is beneficial if you anticipate being in a higher tax bracket in the future, as withdrawals in retirement will be tax-free.

4. **Diversify Investments**:
   - Spread investments across various asset classes (stocks, bonds, mutual funds) in each account type based on your risk tolerance and time horizon.

5. **Withdrawal Strategy**:
   - Plan how you will withdraw funds in retirement. Typically, people withdraw from taxable accounts first, followed by tax-deferred accounts, and finally tax-free accounts, to manage tax liabilities effectively.

6. **Reassess Periodically**:
   - Regularly review your investment strategy and asset allocation based on market changes, your financial goals, and life circumstances.

7. **Consult a Financial Advisor**:
   - If uncertain, consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your specific situation and goals.

In summary, prioritize contributions to tax-advantaged accounts, balance your investment strategy, and remain flexible to adjust as your financial landscape changes.
