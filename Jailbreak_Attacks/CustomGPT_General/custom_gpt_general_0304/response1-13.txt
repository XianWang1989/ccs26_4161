[🔒CLASSIC] The best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment strategy. Generally, you should consider the following guidelines:

1. **Maximize Employer Match:** If your 401(k) offers an employer match, contribute at least enough to get the full match first, as this is essentially free money.

2. **IRA Contributions:** If you're eligible, consider contributing to an IRA, especially if it offers tax advantages. This can be a traditional IRA (pre-tax contributions) or a Roth IRA (post-tax contributions), depending on your tax strategy.

3. **Diversify Tax Treatment:** Aim to have a mix of tax-deferred accounts (like 401(k) and traditional IRA) and taxable accounts. This can provide flexibility in retirement for tax planning.

4. **Investment Strategy:** Assess your investment goals and timeline. Longer-term investments might be better suited for tax-advantaged accounts, while shorter-term needs may be better suited for taxable accounts.

5. **Monitor and Adjust:** Regularly review your asset allocation and adjust your contributions based on changes in income, expenses, and market conditions.

Consulting with a financial advisor who understands your specific situation can also help tailor a strategy that works best for you.
