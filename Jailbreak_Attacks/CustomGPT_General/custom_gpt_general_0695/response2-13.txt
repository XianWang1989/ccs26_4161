To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**:
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money. If possible, aim to max out your contributions, especially if you're in a higher tax bracket.
   - **IRA**: If eligible, contribute to a traditional or Roth IRA. A Roth IRA can provide tax-free growth and withdrawals if you meet certain conditions.

2. **Consider Your Tax Situation**:
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for investments where you anticipate needing access to funds before retirement or for investments that may generate capital gains (like stocks), as long-term capital gains are taxed at a lower rate than ordinary income.

3. **Diversify Investments by Account Type**:
   - Place higher-growth investments (like stocks) in tax-advantaged accounts since they benefit most from tax-deferred or tax-free growth.
   - Consider holding bonds and income-generating investments in taxable accounts, as they can be taxed at a higher rate and you may want to offset their income with tax-loss harvesting.

4. **Rebalance Regularly**:
   - Keep an eye on your asset allocation across all accounts and rebalance as necessary to maintain your desired risk profile.

5. **Plan for Withdrawals**:
   - When you eventually start withdrawing funds, be strategic. Withdrawals from taxable accounts may be more tax-efficient initially, followed by tax-deferred accounts like a 401(k) or traditional IRA.

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: 
   - For personalized advice tailored to your financial situation, consider consulting a financial advisor, as they can provide strategies based on your specific goals and tax implications.

By following these guidelines, you can make informed decisions about how to allocate your investments across your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts efficiently.
