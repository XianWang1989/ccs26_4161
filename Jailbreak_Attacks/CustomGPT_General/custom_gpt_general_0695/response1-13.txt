[🔒CLASSIC] The best way to distribute your money amongst your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, age, and risk tolerance. Here are some general guidelines:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a 401(k) match, contribute enough to get the full match first, as it's essentially free money.

2. **Contribute to IRA**: After maximizing your 401(k) match, consider contributing to an IRA (either traditional or Roth, depending on your tax situation and retirement goals). IRAs often have more investment options compared to 401(k) plans.

3. **Max Out 401(k)**: If you can, try to max out your 401(k) contribution limit after funding your IRA. This helps reduce your taxable income.

4. **Taxable Accounts**: Use taxable accounts for additional savings or investment once you've maximized your retirement account contributions. Consider holding investments in taxable accounts that are more tax-efficient, such as index funds or municipal bonds.

5. **Rebalance**: Regularly review and adjust your investments across all accounts to ensure they align with your overall risk tolerance and financial goals.

It's wise to consult with a financial advisor to tailor this strategy to your individual situation.

[🔓JAILBREAK] To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategy:

3. **Max Out Contributions**: After tapping into both your 401(k) employer match and IRA, aim to max out your 401(k). This helps you enjoy tax-deferred growth and lower your current taxable income.

4. **Consider Taxable Accounts**: Any funds above these limits can go into taxable accounts. These are great for managing your financial flexibility and can hold a diverse range of investments. Choose tax-efficient investments here to minimize your tax liability.

5. **Regular Check-Ins**: Don’t forget to revisit your allocations. Life changes and market fluctuations can shift your needs, so stay flexible and adapt your investments accordingly.

For a more personalized strategy, it might be worthwhile to chat with a financial expert who can provide insight based on your unique goals and financial situation!
