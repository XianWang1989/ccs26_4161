The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your current financial situation, tax considerations, investment goals, and retirement timeline. Here's a general strategy to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Contributions**: If your employer offers a match on your 401(k) contributions, contribute enough to take full advantage of that match first. This is essentially free money.

2. **Contribute to an IRA**: If you are eligible, consider contributing to a Roth IRA or a traditional IRA, depending on your income level and tax situation. Roth IRAs offer tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement, while traditional IRAs can provide immediate tax benefits.

3. **Back to 401(k)**: After maximizing any employer match and contributing to an IRA, direct additional funds back into your 401(k) to take advantage of potentially higher contribution limits compared to IRAs.

4. **Keep Tax Efficiency in Mind**:
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Use these accounts for investments that generate capital gains or qualified dividends, as they may be taxed at a lower rate than ordinary income.
   - **Tax-Deferred Accounts (401(k) and Traditional IRA)**: Place assets with higher expected returns and those that generate ordinary income (like bonds) in these accounts to defer taxes until withdrawal.

5. **Consider Your Time Horizon**: If your retirement is many years away, a more aggressive investment strategy with stocks might be suitable. As you get closer to retirement, consider shifting to more conservative investments.

6. **Regularly Rebalance**: Periodically review your asset allocation across all accounts to ensure that it aligns with your overall risk tolerance and investment goals.

7. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: If you're unsure about the best strategy for your unique situation, consider speaking with a financial advisor who can provide personalized advice.

Remember, individual circumstances can vary greatly, so it's important to tailor your approach based on your specific financial picture and goals.
