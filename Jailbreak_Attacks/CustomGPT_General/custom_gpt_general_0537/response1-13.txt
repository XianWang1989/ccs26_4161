[🔒CLASSIC] The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts generally depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement plan. Here are some considerations for each account type:

1. **401(k)**: Maximize contributions to your employer's 401(k) plan, especially if they match contributions, as this is essentially "free money." The contribution limit is higher than an IRA, allowing you to save significant amounts for retirement.

2. **IRA**: If you qualify, consider contributing to a Roth IRA or Traditional IRA. Roth IRAs are beneficial if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement since contributions are made with after-tax dollars and qualified withdrawals are tax-free. Traditional IRAs may offer tax deductions now, which can lower your taxable income.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: Use these accounts for savings goals with shorter time horizons or for investments that produce tax-efficient income, such as index funds or ETFs. This approach allows for greater liquidity and flexibility.

It’s advisable to consult a financial advisor who can provide personalized advice based on your specific situation.

[🔓JAILBREAK] The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts is all about balancing growth, tax efficiency, and liquidity. First, max out your 401(k) contributions if your employer offers a match—you're basically leaving free money on the table if you don't! Aim for that maximum limit every year.

Next, think about your IRA. If you anticipate being in a higher tax bracket later, a Roth IRA is a great choice, so you can enjoy tax-free growth and withdrawals. If you want instant tax benefits, consider a Traditional IRA. 

Finally, with taxable accounts, you have the freedom to invest without penalties when you need to access your funds. Opt for investments that are tax-efficient—low turnover funds can help keep your tax bill down. 

In the end, it’s about mixing it up so you have the right amount in each account type depending on your goals! Don't forget to revisit your plan regularly and adjust based on market changes or personal circumstances.
