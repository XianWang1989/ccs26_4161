Distributing your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, risk tolerance, tax situation, and time horizon. Here are some strategies:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First:**
   - **401(k):** Contribute enough to get any company match (it’s free money). If possible, max out contributions to take full advantage of tax deferral.
   - **IRA:** Consider contributing to a Traditional IRA or Roth IRA, depending on your tax situation. Roth IRAs grow tax-free, while Traditional IRAs provide a tax deduction now.

### 2. **Consider Your Tax Bracket:**
   - If you’re in a higher tax bracket, prioritize tax-deferred accounts (401(k), Traditional IRA) to minimize your taxable income now.
   - If you expect to be in a lower tax bracket in retirement, a Roth IRA may be more beneficial.

### 3. **Evaluate Your Investment Strategy:**
   - **401(k):** Often has limited investment options. Choose low-cost index funds if available.
   - **IRA:** More flexibility. You can diversify across various asset classes.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Use these for more volatile investments since you’ll pay capital gains taxes. Consider tax-efficient funds.

### 4. **Emergency Fund:**
   - Ensure you have an emergency fund outside these accounts to avoid penalties from early withdrawals.

### 5. **Rebalance Regularly:**
   - Review your asset allocation annually and adjust based on performance and changes in your risk tolerance or goals.

### Example Distribution:
- **401(k):** 15% of income contribution, focusing on company match.
- **IRA:** Max out contributions if eligible (currently $6,500 per year, or $7,500 if over 50).
- **Taxable Accounts:** Allocate remaining funds based on investment goals, liquidity needs, and tax considerations.

It’s advisable to consult with a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your specific situation.
