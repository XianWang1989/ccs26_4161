Determining the best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and the types of investments you plan to hold. Here are some key considerations:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Matching**
   - **401(k)**: If your employer offers a matching contribution, prioritize contributing enough to get the full match. This is essentially free money.

### 2. **Consider Tax Advantages**
   - **Tax-Deferred Accounts (401(k) and IRA)**: Contributions are often made pre-tax, reducing your taxable income now and allowing investments to grow tax-deferred.
   - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are made after-tax, but withdrawals in retirement are tax-free, which may be beneficial if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket later.

### 3. **Evaluate Contribution Limits**
   - Be aware of annual contribution limits for each account type. For 2023, the limits are:
     - **401(k)**: $22,500 (or $30,000 if over age 50)
     - **IRA**: $6,500 (or $7,500 if over age 50)

### 4. **Taxable Accounts**
   - After maximizing tax-advantaged accounts, consider investing in taxable accounts. These provide flexibility for withdrawals and investment choices.
   - Keep in mind capital gains taxes, so focus on tax-efficient investments (like index funds).

### 5. **Diversification of Investments**
   - Ensure your overall investment portfolio is diversified, spreading money across different asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) to manage risk.

### 6. **Withdrawal Strategy**
   - Think about how you'll withdraw funds in retirement. Generally, it’s wise to withdraw from taxable accounts first to allow tax-deferred accounts to continue growing.

### 7. **Adjust for Personal Goals**
   - Tailor your distribution based on your retirement timeline, risk tolerance, and other financial goals (like buying a home, funding education).

### Example Distribution Strategy
1. Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match.
2. If you still have funds after that, contribute to a Roth or traditional IRA up to the limit.
3. Use any remaining funds for a taxable account, focusing on long-term, tax-efficient investments.

### Regular Reviews
- Reassess your allocations annually or as your financial situation changes to ensure they align with your goals.

Consulting a financial advisor can also provide personalized guidance based on your specific financial situation and goals.
