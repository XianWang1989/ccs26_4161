To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Tax Efficiency**:
   - Invest high-growth assets (like stocks) in tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) and IRA) since they can benefit from tax-free growth or tax deferral.
   - Use taxable accounts for investments that generate regular income (like bonds or dividend-paying stocks), which may be taxed at a lower rate.

2. **Maximize Employer Matches**:
   - Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money. 

3. **Diversify Contributions**:
   - Consider a balanced approach by contributing to all three types of accounts, adjusting based on your tax situation and retirement goals.
   - For higher income years, max out tax-advantaged accounts before using taxable accounts.

4. **Emergency Fund**:
   - Ensure you have a sufficient emergency fund in cash or liquid assets before heavily investing.

5. **Consult a Professional**:
   - Speak with a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your specific financial situation.

By considering these strategies, you can optimize your investments for growth while managing tax implications effectively.
