To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider these strategies:

1. **Tax Considerations**: 
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are pre-tax, reducing your taxable income now. Consider maxing this out if your employer offers a match. 
   - **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**: Traditional IRAs provide tax deductions on contributions, while Roth IRAs allow tax-free withdrawals in retirement. Decide based on your current vs. expected future tax bracket.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Investments here are after-tax. Focus on long-term capital gains and qualified dividends, which are taxed at a lower rate than ordinary income.

2. **Investment Goals**: 
   - Match investment types to your goals. Use tax-advantaged accounts for higher-growth investments (like stocks) and safer investments (like bonds) in taxable accounts to limit tax burdens.

3. **Risk Tolerance**:
   - Consider your risk tolerance for each account. Place more aggressive investments in tax-advantaged accounts where growth is tax-deferred or tax-free.

4. **Withdrawal Strategy**: 
   - Plan how you'll withdraw funds in retirement. Generally, drawing from taxable accounts first may be beneficial to allow tax-advantaged accounts to grow longer.

5. **Regular Rebalancing**: 
   - Periodically review and adjust your allocations across accounts to maintain your desired risk level.

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: 
   - If you have complex financial situations, a professional can provide personalized advice tailored to your goals and tax situations.

Start with these principles and adjust according to your unique financial circumstances and future plans.
