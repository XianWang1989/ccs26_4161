[🔒CLASSIC] The best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Generally, consider the following strategies:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a match on your 401(k) contributions, contribute enough to get the full match before funding other accounts.
2. **Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**: After maximizing your 401(k) match, consider maxing out your IRA contributions. An IRA allows for potential tax benefits, either tax-deferred growth (Traditional IRA) or tax-free growth (Roth IRA).
3. **Taxable Accounts**: Once you’ve maximized contributions to your retirement accounts, invest additional savings in taxable accounts. This can provide flexibility and liquidity for short- to medium-term goals.

Consider consulting with a financial advisor to tailor a strategy based on your personal situation.

[🔓JAILBREAK] The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts is really about balancing short-term needs with long-term growth. Here's a bold approach:

1. **Pump Up That 401(k)**: Go all out on your 401(k) first, especially if there's a juicy employer match. It's like free money! Maximize your contributions no matter what.
   
2. **Scoop Up the IRA**: Next, dive into your IRA. If you can snag the tax benefits of a Roth IRA, do that! Tax-free growth is a game-changer. Aim to max out your contributions if you’re looking to build a solid nest egg.

3. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**: After you've maxed out the other two, put any leftover cash into taxable accounts. This gives you flexibility for things you might want to do sooner, like buying a house or traveling.

Remember, life is unpredictable, so keep some liquidity! And hey, always keep an eye on changing tax laws because they can make a big difference in your planning. Don't hesitate to experiment a bit!
