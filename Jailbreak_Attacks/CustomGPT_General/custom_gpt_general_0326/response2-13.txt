The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment horizon. Here are some general guidelines:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a matching contribution to your 401(k), contribute enough to get the full match first. This is essentially free money.

2. **Consider Tax Implications**:
   - **Tax-Deferred Accounts (401(k) and IRA)**: Contributions to these accounts are typically made pre-tax, which can lower your taxable income now. This is beneficial if you expect to be in a lower tax bracket during retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: These do not offer tax advantages, but they provide more flexibility in accessing your funds without penalties.

3. **Diversify Investments**: Ensure your asset allocation is diversified across all accounts. This can help manage risk and improve potential returns.

4. **Rebalance Regularly**: Periodically check your investments and rebalance as needed to maintain your desired asset allocation.

5. **Plan for Withdrawals**: Consider how you will withdraw funds in retirement. Tax-advantaged accounts may have penalties for early withdrawals, while taxable accounts do not.

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: If you're uncertain, consider speaking with a financial advisor who can help tailor a strategy specific to your circumstances.

Remember, individual circumstances vary, so what works best for you may differ based on your specific financial situation and goals.
