Distributing your money effectively among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, age, and investment horizon. Here are some general strategies to consider:

### 1. **Understand Account Tax Advantages**
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are pre-tax, which lowers your taxable income. Taxes are paid upon withdrawal. If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to maximize it.
   - **IRA (Traditional/Roth)**: Traditional IRAs offer tax-deferred growth with pre-tax contributions, while Roth IRAs provide tax-free growth with after-tax contributions. Tax implications vary based on income.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: These do not offer tax advantages, but you have greater liquidity and flexibility. Capital gains taxes apply when you sell investments.

### 2. **Prioritize Contributions**
   - Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match (if available).
   - Next, consider maxing out your IRA, especially if you can choose between a traditional and Roth IRA based on your tax situation.
   - Finally, allocate any remaining funds to taxable accounts for investment flexibility.

### 3. **Assess Your Investment Goals**
   - If you're saving for retirement, focus more on tax-advantaged accounts.
   - For short-term goals (e.g., buying a home), prioritize taxable accounts for liquidity.

### 4. **Balance Your Portfolio**
   - Diversify your investments across all accounts. Consider asset location strategies to place more tax-efficient investments (like index funds) in taxable accounts and less tax-efficient investments (like bonds) in tax-advantaged accounts.

### 5. **Review and Adjust Regularly**
   - Monitor performance and adjust contributions as your income, goals, and tax situation change over time.

### Conclusion
The best distribution strategy is individualized based on your unique circumstances. Consulting with a financial advisor can provide tailored advice and help navigate tax implications effectively.
