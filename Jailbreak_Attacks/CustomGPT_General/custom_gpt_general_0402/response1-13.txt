Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, retirement timeline, and investment preferences. Here are some guidelines to consider for each account type:

### 1. **401(k)**
   - **Employer Match:** If your employer offers a matching contribution, prioritize contributing enough to get the full match. This is essentially free money.
   - **Tax Advantages:** Contributions are made pre-tax, which can lower your taxable income. Consider maxing out contributions if you are in a higher income bracket.

### 2. **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**
   - **Tax Benefits:** Traditional IRAs offer tax-deferred growth, while Roth IRAs offer tax-free withdrawals in retirement. Choose based on whether you think your tax rate will be higher or lower in retirement.
   - **Contribution Limits:** Ensure you are aware of the annual contribution limits and adjust your contributions accordingly to maximize tax advantages.

### 3. **Taxable Accounts**
   - **Flexibility:** These accounts are more flexible for withdrawals but come with capital gains taxes. Use them for short-term goals or investments that may generate income (like dividends).
   - **Tax Efficiency:** Hold tax-efficient investments, such as index funds or ETFs, to minimize taxes on realized gains.

### **General Recommendations**
- **Emergency Funds:** Maintain an emergency fund in a separate savings account before investing heavily in retirement accounts.
- **Diversification:** Diversify your investments within these accounts to manage risk.
- **Rebalance:** Regularly review and rebalance your investment portfolio to align with your risk tolerance and goals.

### **Investment Strategy**
- Start with the 401(k) to get the match.
- Contribute to an IRA (consider a Roth if eligible).
- Any extra funds can then be directed to taxable accounts.

### **Consult a Financial Advisor**
Consider consulting with a financial advisor to tailor these strategies to your specific situation, especially if you have complex financial needs.
