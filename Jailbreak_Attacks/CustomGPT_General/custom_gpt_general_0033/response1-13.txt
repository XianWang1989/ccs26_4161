The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment horizon. Here’s a general strategy:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match (often considered "free money"). Aim for the maximum contribution limit if possible.
   - **IRA**: If eligible, contribute to a traditional IRA for tax deductions or a Roth IRA for tax-free growth, depending on your situation.

### 2. **Consider Tax Implications**
   - **Tax Efficiency**: Hold tax-efficient investments (like index funds) in taxable accounts to minimize capital gains taxes.
   - **Tax-Deferred Accounts**: Suitable for higher-yield investments that may generate ordinary income, which would be taxed at your ordinary rate in the future.

### 3. **Emergency Fund and Liquid Savings**
   - Keep enough cash in taxable accounts to cover emergencies or short-term needs—typically 3-6 months' worth of expenses.

### 4. **Diversification Across Accounts**
   - Ensure your investments are diversified across all accounts. This can help balance risk and return based on your overall portfolio.

### 5. **Review and Adjust**
   - Regularly assess your asset allocation based on changing goals, market conditions, and tax laws.

### Example Distribution:
- **401(k)**: 15-20% of your total savings
- **IRA**: 10-15%
- **Taxable**: Remaining funds, focusing on growth-oriented investments

### Conclusion
Tailor the percentages based on your individual risk tolerance, retirement timeline, and tax position. Consulting with a financial advisor can provide personalized recommendations tailored to your circumstances.
