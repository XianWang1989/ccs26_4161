Distributing your money across a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts wisely can have a significant impact on your long-term financial health. Here are some strategies to consider:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**
   - **401(k)**: If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to get the full match. It’s essentially free money. Aim to contribute the maximum allowed if you can.
   - **IRA**: Consider contributing to a Roth IRA if you qualify, as withdrawals during retirement can be tax-free. If you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement, this can be beneficial.

### 2. **Allocate Funds Based on Tax Efficiency**
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Use these accounts for investments that generate lower taxes, such as index funds or tax-managed funds, to minimize capital gains taxes. Consider holding bonds in tax-advantaged accounts because they generate interest income, which is taxed at ordinary income rates.

### 3. **Diversify Your Investments**
   - Within each account type, diversify across asset classes (stocks, bonds, real estate). Use a mix of domestic and international investments to balance risks and rewards.

### 4. **Consider Your Time Horizon**
   - If you’re younger, you can afford to take more risks in your 401(k) and IRA. As you approach retirement, gradually shift towards more conservative investments.

### 5. **Withdrawal Strategy Planning**
   - Plan how you’ll draw from each account in retirement. Generally, withdraw from taxable accounts first to allow tax-advantaged accounts to continue growing, while being mindful of required minimum distributions (RMDs) applicable to 401(k) and traditional IRA accounts.

### 6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**
   - A financial advisor can provide personalized advice tailored to your specific circumstances, goals, and tax situation.

### Conclusion
A balanced approach, prioritizing tax-advantaged accounts while managing an efficient allocation strategy across all account types, will help you grow your wealth and prepare for retirement successfully.
