Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your overall financial goals, tax considerations, and time horizon. Here’s a general approach to consider:

### 1. **Assess Your Goals**
   - **Retirement Goals**: If retirement is your primary focus, prioritize tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) and IRA).
   - **Short-Term Needs**: If you have goals in the near term (like buying a home or funding education), consider taxable accounts.

### 2. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match—this is essentially free money. Aim for the annual maximum contribution if possible.
   - **IRA/Roth IRA**: Depending on your eligibility and tax situation, consider maxing out your contributions to an IRA or a Roth IRA. Roth IRAs are particularly beneficial if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement.

### 3. **Consider Tax Implications**
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are made pre-tax, reducing your taxable income now but taxed upon withdrawal.
   - **Traditional IRA**: Similar to a 401(k) in terms of taxation on withdrawals.
   - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are after-tax, but withdrawals in retirement are tax-free.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Capital gains, interest, and dividends are taxed annually. Consider holding investments with lower turnover or qualifying for long-term capital gains treatment.

### 4. **Diversify Investments**
   - Within each account type, aim for a diverse portfolio that aligns with your risk tolerance and investment time horizon. This could include:
     - **Stocks**: Higher growth potential, suitable for long-term.
     - **Bonds**: Stability and income, can balance risk.
     - **Funds/ETFs**: For diversification without needing to pick individual stocks.

### 5. **Rebalance Regularly**
   - Maintain your desired asset allocation by periodically rebalancing your portfolio. This helps to manage risk and ensures you are on track with your investment goals.

### 6. **Seek Professional Advice**
   - If uncertain, consider consulting with a financial advisor who can provide tailored advice based on your specific situation.

### Example Distribution Strategy
- **401(k)**: Contribute up to the employer match, then max out contributions if feasible.
- **IRA/Roth IRA**: Prioritize contributions based on your tax situation.
- **Taxable Accounts**: Allocate remaining funds here, focusing on long-term capital growth investments.

By balancing contributions and considering tax implications, you can create a distribution strategy that supports your financial goals effectively.
