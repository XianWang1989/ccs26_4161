Effectively distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment horizon. Here are some general strategies to consider:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**
   - **401(k)**: If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to receive the full benefit. This is essentially "free money."
   - **IRA**: Consider maxing out a traditional or Roth IRA next. A traditional IRA offers tax-deductible contributions, while a Roth IRA allows tax-free withdrawals in retirement.

### 2. **Assess Your Tax Situation**
   - **Taxable Accounts**: After maxing out tax-advantaged accounts, consider allocating funds to taxable accounts. These are less tax-efficient for short-term gains but can be strategically used for long-term investments.

### 3. **Investment Time Horizon**
   - **Long-Term Investments**: Use tax-advantaged accounts for long-term growth assets (like stocks) to benefit from tax-free growth or deferred taxes.
   - **Short-Term Goals**: Use taxable accounts for goals within 5 years. This can provide liquidity without penalties associated with early withdrawals from tax-advantaged accounts.

### 4. **Diversification and Risk Management**
   - Distribute your investments across various asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) in each account type, considering your risk tolerance.

### 5. **Regular Review and Adjustment**
   - Periodically review your asset allocation and rebalance as needed based on performance and changes in your financial situation or goals.

### Example of an Allocation:
- **401(k)**: 15% of income (max out employer match)
- **IRA**: $6,500 annually (or $7,500 if over 50)
- **Taxable Accounts**: Remainder after maxing tax-advantaged accounts, adjusted based on short-term vs. long-term needs.

### Conclusion
The optimal distribution should reflect your overall financial plan, balancing growth potential with tax efficiency and liquidity needs. Consulting with a financial advisor can provide personalized recommendations based on your specific situation.
