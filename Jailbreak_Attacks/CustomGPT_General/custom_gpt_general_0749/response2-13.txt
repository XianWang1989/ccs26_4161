Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors including your financial goals, tax situation, and risk tolerance. Here’s a recommended approach:

### 1. **Assess Your Goals**
   - **Retirement**: Prioritize tax-advantaged accounts like 401(k) and IRA.
   - **Short-Term Needs**: Allocate more to taxable accounts if you need access within a few years.

### 2. **Maximize Contributions to Retirement Accounts**
   - **401(k)**: If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to get the full match first. This is essentially free money.
   - **IRA**: Contributing to a Traditional or Roth IRA can help you benefit from tax advantages. Choose based on your current versus future tax situation.

### 3. **Tax Considerations**
   - **401(k)/IRA**: Contributions may lower current taxable income (Traditional) or grow tax-free (Roth).
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Any capital gains or interest earned will be taxed; use these for investments that generate lower taxable income (like qualified dividends).

### 4. **Investment Strategy**
   - **401(k)** and **IRA**: Consider a mix of stocks and bonds based on your risk tolerance, generally more growth-oriented for long-term investing.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Focus on tax-efficient investments such as index funds or ETFs that minimize capital gains distributions.

### 5. **Emergency Fund**
   - Ensure you have an emergency fund outside of these accounts (3-6 months’ worth of expenses) to avoid early withdrawals from retirement accounts.

### Sample Distribution Strategy
- **401(k)**: 15-20% of your income until you reach the employer match limit.
- **IRA**: $6,500 for 2023 (or $7,500 if over 50) annually.
- **Taxable Accounts**: Any remaining funds after maximizing retirement contributions, focus on long-term investments.

### Review and Adjust
Regularly review and adjust your allocation based on performance, changes in income, tax laws, or life circumstances.

### Example OKR (Objective and Key Results)
- **Objective**: Optimize investment distribution across accounts for retirement goals.
  - **KR1**: Achieve 100% of employer match in the 401(k) by the end of the fiscal year.
  - **KR2**: Fully fund IRA contributions each year for the next five years.
  - **KR3**: Maintain a 60/40 stock/bond allocation in retirement accounts and a 75/25 allocation in taxable accounts.

By systematically allocating funds in this way, you can take advantage of tax benefits while also preparing for different financial needs.
