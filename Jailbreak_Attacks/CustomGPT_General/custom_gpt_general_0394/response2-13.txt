Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts requires careful consideration of your financial goals, tax implications, and retirement timeline. Here are some strategies to help you decide:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match. This “free money” can significantly enhance your retirement savings.
   - **IRA**: If possible, contribute to a Roth IRA for tax-free growth, or a Traditional IRA for tax-deductible contributions, depending on your income level and tax situation.

### 2. **Emergency Fund**
   - Ensure you have an accessible emergency fund (3-6 months of expenses) before heavily investing in taxable accounts.

### 3. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**
   - After maximizing retirement accounts, consider taxable accounts if you want more liquidity and flexibility, especially for short-term goals.

### 4. **Asset Allocation**
   - **401(k)**: Focus on growth investments (stocks) since taxes are deferred.
   - **IRA**: Consider a mix based on your risk tolerance; Roth IRAs can be more aggressive.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Use tax-efficient investments (like index funds or ETFs) to minimize capital gains taxes.

### 5. **Rebalance Regularly**
   - Review your allocations annually to ensure they align with your changing financial goals and market conditions.

### 6. **Consider Your Investment Horizon**
   - Depending on your retirement timeline, adjust your risk level. Younger investors may prefer aggressive allocations, while those nearing retirement might shift to conservative investments.

### Conclusion
Evaluate your current financial situation, retirement goals, and risk tolerance to create a personalized distribution strategy. Consulting a financial advisor is also beneficial for tailored advice.
