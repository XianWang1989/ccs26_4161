Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment horizon. Here’s a general guideline to consider:

### 1. Understand Account Types:
- **401(k)**: Offers tax-deferred growth, often with employer matching. Good for long-term retirement savings.
- **IRA**: Traditional IRAs offer tax-deferred growth, while Roth IRAs provide tax-free withdrawals in retirement.
- **Taxable Accounts**: Flexible for withdrawals and investment choices, but subject to capital gains taxes.

### 2. Prioritize Contributions:
1. **401(k) Up to Employer Match**: Contribute enough to get any employer match, as it’s essentially free money.
2. **IRA Contributions**: Fund a Traditional or Roth IRA to benefit from tax advantages. The limit is lower, but it’s helpful for retirement diversification.
3. **Max Out 401(k)**: If possible, contribute the maximum allowed to your 401(k) after hitting the employer match.
4. **Taxable Accounts**: Use for additional savings, especially if you have short-term goals or want more liquidity.

### 3. Consider Tax Implications:
- Use tax-advantaged accounts for assets that generate high tax liabilities (like bonds).
- Hold low-turnover or tax-efficient funds in taxable accounts.

### 4. Diversification & Risk Management:
Diversify your investments across all accounts to manage risk and optimize returns according to your risk tolerance.

### 5. Review and Adjust:
Regularly assess your allocation as your financial situation and goals evolve. Rebalance your portfolio periodically to maintain your desired risk level.

### Conclusion:
A balanced approach tailored to your specific circumstances and goals will help you maximize your investment potential while minimizing taxes. Consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized advice.
