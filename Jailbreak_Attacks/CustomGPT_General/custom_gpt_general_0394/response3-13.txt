Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here’s a breakdown of strategies:

### GPT:

2. **Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: Prioritize contributions to your 401(k) if your employer offers a matching contribution.
   - **IRA**: If you're eligible, max out contributions to a traditional or Roth IRA, especially if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: After maximizing tax-advantaged accounts, allocate additional funds to taxable accounts. These can be flexible for withdrawals and investments.

4. **Investment Strategy**: Consider asset allocation for each account based on your risk tolerance and investment horizon. 

5. **Tax Efficiency**: Place tax-inefficient investments (like bonds) in tax-advantaged accounts and keep tax-efficient investments (like stocks) in taxable accounts.
