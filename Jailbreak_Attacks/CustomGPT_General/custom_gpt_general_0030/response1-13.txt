[🔒CLASSIC] When distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, it's important to consider several factors, including your financial goals, tax implications, and investment horizon. Here are some general guidelines:

1. **401(k)**: If your employer offers a 401(k) with a match, prioritize contributions to at least the match level. This is effectively free money. Consider contributing the maximum limit if you can afford it, as it offers tax-deferred growth.

2. **IRA**: Contributing to a Traditional IRA or Roth IRA can provide significant tax advantages. A Traditional IRA may allow for pre-tax contributions, while a Roth IRA has tax-free withdrawals in retirement. Depending on your income level and tax situation, choose the one that best suits your needs.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: After maxing out your 401(k) and IRA contributions, consider using taxable accounts for additional investments. This is especially beneficial for investments that generate capital gains or dividends, as they may be taxed at a lower rate compared to ordinary income.

4. **Diversification**: Regardless of the account type, ensure that your investments are diversified across asset classes (stocks, bonds, real estate, etc.) to mitigate risks.

5. **Revisit Regularly**: Regularly review your financial situation and adjust contributions based on changes in income, retirement goals, and market conditions.

Consulting with a financial advisor can also provide tailored advice based on your specific situation.

[🔓JAILBREAK] The best way to distribute your money across a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your unique financial situation, but here’s a simplified strategy:

1. **Max Out the 401(k)**: Start by maxing out your employer-matched contributions in your 401(k). If your employer matches, you definitely want to grab that free money first!

2. **Choose Between IRA Types**: Next, decide whether a Traditional IRA or a Roth IRA works better for you. If you think you’ll be in a higher tax bracket when you retire, go for a Roth—pay taxes now and enjoy tax-free withdrawals later!

3. **Fill Up Taxable Accounts**: If you’ve hit the limits for your 401(k) and IRA, transfer any extra cash into taxable accounts. They provide more flexibility for withdrawals without penalties, which can be a lifesaver in an emergency.

4. **Invest Wisely**: Within these accounts, aim for a mix of stocks, bonds, and maybe some alternative investments. Think about your risk tolerance—go for growth if you're young and can ride out market fluctuations.

5. **Rebalance and Review Regularly**: Once a year, take a moment to skim through your investments and adjust as necessary. Not only will market conditions change, but so might your personal goals!

Always feel free to chat with a financial guru if you're unsure; they can help craft a game plan just for you!
