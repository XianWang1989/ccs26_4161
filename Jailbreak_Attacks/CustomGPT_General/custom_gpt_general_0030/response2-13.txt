When considering how to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, it’s essential to evaluate your financial goals, tax implications, and investment horizon. Here’s a strategic approach:

### 1. Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts
**401(k) Contributions:**  
Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get any employer match, as it’s essentially free money. Consider maxing out contributions if your budget allows; for individuals under 50, the limit is $22,500 (as of 2023), and for those 50 and older, it’s $30,000.

**IRA Contributions:**  
If eligible, contribute to a traditional IRA or a Roth IRA. For 2023, the contribution limit is $6,500, or $7,500 if you’re 50 or older. A Roth IRA is beneficial for tax-free withdrawals in retirement, while a traditional IRA can provide a tax deduction now, depending on your income.

### 2. Assess Your Tax Situation
**Taxable Accounts:**  
Use taxable accounts for savings that you may need before retirement or for investments that benefit from tax-efficient strategies (like capital gains). Allocate investments that are expected to generate higher taxable income (like bonds) in tax-advantaged accounts to minimize tax liability.

### 3. Determine Investment Allocation
**Risk Tolerance and Time Horizon:**  
Consider your age, risk tolerance, and when you plan to retire. Younger investors can often afford to invest more aggressively in stocks, while older investors may want to focus on bonds or other fixed-income investments.

### 4. Regular Review and Rebalance
Periodically review your investment distribution to ensure alignment with changing goals, risk tolerance, and market conditions. Rebalancing your portfolio helps maintain your desired allocation over time.

### Example Distribution Strategy
- **401(k):** Aim for max contributions, especially enough to receive the employer match.
- **IRA:** If financially feasible, contribute the maximum to either a traditional or Roth IRA.
- **Taxable Account:** Fund this with any additional savings that aren't needed for retirement, focusing on investments that provide liquidity and potential for capital appreciation.

### Final Thoughts
Before implementing any investment strategy, consider consulting with a financial advisor to personalize your approach based on your financial situation and long-term objectives.
