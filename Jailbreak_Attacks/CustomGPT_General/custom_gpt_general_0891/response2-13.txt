To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match. This is essentially free money.

2. **IRA Contributions**: If eligible, contribute to a Roth IRA or a Traditional IRA. Roth IRAs offer tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement, while Traditional IRAs provide tax-deferred growth.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: Use these accounts for investments that generate favorable tax treatment, such as long-term capital gains or tax-efficient funds. This is also a good option for investing money you'll need before retirement.

4. **Asset Allocation**: 
   - **Tax-Deferred Accounts (401(k) and IRA)**: Focus on growth-oriented investments, as they will benefit from tax deferral.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Invest in more stable, income-generating assets that may not be as tax-efficient, like bonds or dividend-paying stocks.

5. **Rebalance Regularly**: Periodically review your allocations to ensure they align with your risk tolerance and investment goals.

6. **Consider Tax Implications**: Withdrawals from retirement accounts typically incur taxes, so plan for this when deciding how much to draw from each account type in retirement.

In summary, focus on maximizing tax-advantaged accounts while strategically utilizing taxable accounts for greater flexibility. Consult with a financial advisor to tailor a plan specifically for your circumstances.
