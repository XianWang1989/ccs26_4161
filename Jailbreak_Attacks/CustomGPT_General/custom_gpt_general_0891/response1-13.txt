Distributing your money effectively among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some guidelines:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**:
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match (free money). Aim for the maximum contribution limit if possible.
   - **IRA**: If eligible, contribute to a traditional or Roth IRA, as they offer tax advantages.

### 2. **Consider Tax Implications**:
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for investments that could generate capital gains or interest income, as they don't benefit from tax-deferred growth.
   - **Roth IRA**: Good for tax-free withdrawals in retirement, making it ideal for high-growth investments.

### 3. **Investment Strategy**:
   - Place higher-risk investments in tax-advantaged accounts to defer taxes on potential gains.
   - Keep more stable, income-generating investments in taxable accounts to manage tax liability better.

### 4. **Emergency Fund**:
   - Ensure you have a sufficient emergency fund in cash or liquid assets outside these accounts.

### 5. **Regular Rebalancing**:
   - Periodically review and adjust your allocations to maintain your desired risk tolerance and investment strategy.

### Example Allocation Strategy (for a hypothetical scenario):
- **401(k)**: 15% of income (to max out employer match)
- **IRA**: $6,000/year (if under 50, or $7,000 if over)
- **Taxable Account**: Remaining income after maxing out retirement contributions

### Conclusion:
Tailor this strategy according to your specific financial situation, time horizon, and goals. Consulting a financial advisor can also provide personalized advice.
