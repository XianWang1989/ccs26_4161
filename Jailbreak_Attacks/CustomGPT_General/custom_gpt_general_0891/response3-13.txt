Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts effectively depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment time horizon. Here’s a general strategy:

### 1. **Prioritize Contributions:**
   - **401(k):** If your employer offers a matching contribution, contribute at least enough to get the full match—this is essentially free money.
   - **IRA:** Consider maxing out a Roth or traditional IRA. Roth IRAs offer tax-free growth and withdrawals if you're eligible.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Use these for investments that may generate capital gains or have short-term needs, as they are less tax-advantaged.

### 2. **Consider Tax Implications:**
   - **Tax-Deferred Accounts (401(k) and Traditional IRA):** Contributions reduce your taxable income now, but withdrawals are taxed as ordinary income.
   - **Roth IRA:** Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, but qualified withdrawals are tax-free.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** You're taxed on dividends, interest, and realized capital gains, so consider tax-efficient investments.

### 3. **Think About Investment Goals:**
   - **Long-Term Goals (retirement):** Prioritize tax-advantaged accounts for growth.
   - **Short-Term Goals (buying a home, starting a business):** Use taxable accounts where you can access funds without penalties.

### 4. **Diversification:**
   - Invest in a mix of stocks, bonds, and other assets across all accounts, considering the tax implications of where you hold each type of investment.

### Example Allocation:
- **401(k):** 15-20% of income (especially to get the match)
- **IRA:** Max out contributions if possible
- **Taxable Account:** Allocate remaining funds, focusing on growth and tax efficiency

Adjust this strategy based on your specific circumstances, financial goals, and tax situation. Consulting with a financial advisor can also provide personalized advice.
