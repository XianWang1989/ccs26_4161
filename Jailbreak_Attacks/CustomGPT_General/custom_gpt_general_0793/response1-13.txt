Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts involves a strategic approach to balance tax advantages, liquidity needs, and investment goals. Here are some tips to consider:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**
   - **401(k):** If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to get the full match. This is essentially free money.
   - **IRA (Traditional or Roth):** If eligible, contribute to an IRA. Roth IRAs are particularly beneficial for tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement.

### 2. **Diversify Investments**
   - **401(k):** Typically offers limited investment choices. Focus on a mix of stocks, bonds, and potentially target-date funds.
   - **IRA:** Can provide broader investment options. Consider having a balance of equities and fixed income based on your risk tolerance.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Utilize for investing in high-growth stocks or funds; tax losses can offset gains, which is advantageous.

### 3. **Consider Tax Implications**
   - **Withdrawals:** Traditional 401(k) and IRA withdrawals are taxed as ordinary income. Plan your withdrawals to minimize tax burden in retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Investments here can incur capital gains taxes, so consider holding investments for over a year to qualify for lower long-term rates.

### 4. **Balance Accessibility and Growth**
   - Keep a portion of your assets in taxable accounts for flexibility and access to funds without penalties.
   - Prioritize growth-oriented investments in your tax-deferred accounts to take advantage of compound growth.

### 5. **Reassess Regularly**
   - As your financial situation or tax laws change, revisit your allocation to ensure it continues to meet your goals.

### Example Allocation Strategy
- **Max out 401(k) match:** 6% of salary
- **Contribute to IRA:** $6,500 annual max (or $7,500 if over 50)
- **Allocate remaining savings:** 60% to taxable accounts for flexibility, 40% to retirement accounts.

### Conclusion
Creating a balanced distribution among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts can optimize tax efficiency and growth potential. Consulting with a financial advisor can also provide personalized strategies tailored to your individual circumstances.
