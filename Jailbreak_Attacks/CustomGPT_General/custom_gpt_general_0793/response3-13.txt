Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on factors like your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here’s a general approach:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**
   - **401(k):** Contribute up to the employer match first, as it’s essentially free money. If possible, aim to maximize your contributions (2023 limit is $22,500, or $30,000 if over 50).
   - **IRA (Traditional or Roth):** If you have additional funds, consider contributing to an IRA. The 2023 limit is $6,500 (or $7,500 if over 50). Choose between a Traditional IRA (tax-deferred) or Roth IRA (tax-free growth).

### 2. **Start with the Taxable Account**
   - After maximizing tax-advantaged accounts, allocate funds to a taxable brokerage account for flexibility. This allows you to access funds without early withdrawal penalties.

### 3. **Consider Your Investment Strategy**
   - **401(k) and IRA:** Focus on long-term growth investments (e.g., stocks, mutual funds) since you won't need these funds for years.
   - **Taxable Account:** Use this for more liquid, lower-risk investments or funds you'll need sooner. Consider tax-efficient investments, such as index funds or ETFs.

### 4. **Evaluate Your Tax Situation**
   - Higher-income years may benefit from tax-deferred vehicles. In lower-income years, a Roth might be more beneficial for its tax-free growth.

### 5. **Review and Adjust Regularly**
   - Periodically reassess your strategy based on changes in income, expenses, financial goals, and tax laws.

### Example Distribution Strategy
- **Initially Funding:**
  - Max out 401(k) to employer match
  - Contribute to IRA (if eligible)
- **Ongoing Contributions:**
  - Continue maximizing 401(k) and IRA
  - Allocate any surplus to a taxable account

### Key Considerations
- **Retirement Age:** Your timeline influences risk tolerance.
- **Tax Implications:** Understand how withdrawals will be taxed when you retire.

### Conclusion
A balanced approach, prioritizing tax-advantaged accounts while maintaining some liquidity in taxable accounts, often yields the best long-term results. Consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized guidance!
