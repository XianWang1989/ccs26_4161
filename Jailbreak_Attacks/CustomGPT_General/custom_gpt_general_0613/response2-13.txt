Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here’s a structured approach to consider:

### 1. **Understand Account Types:**
   - **401(k):** This is typically employer-sponsored, offering tax-deferred growth. Contributions are often made pre-tax, reducing your taxable income.
   - **IRA (Traditional or Roth):** IRAs provide tax advantages. With a traditional IRA, contributions may be tax-deductible and grow tax-deferred; with a Roth IRA, contributions are made with after-tax money, but withdrawals in retirement are tax-free.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** These allow for more flexible access and no contribution limits, but you will pay taxes on dividends and capital gains each year.

### 2. **Prioritize Retirement Accounts:**
   - **Max Out 401(k) Contributions:** If your employer offers a match, contribute at least enough to get the full match—a vital step for maximizing retirement savings.
   - **Roth vs. Traditional IRA:** Depending on your current tax rate vs. expected retirement tax rate, choose between a Roth (tax-free growth) or traditional IRA (tax-deferred growth). If you qualify, consider maxing out contributions to both types.

### 3. **Balance Your Tax Exposure:**
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Allocate funds that you might need before retirement or that exceed limits for your tax-advantaged accounts. Use these for investments that generate long-term capital gains, which are taxed at a lower rate.
   - **Tax-efficient Investments:** Focus on tax-efficient mutual funds or ETFs in taxable accounts to minimize tax burdens.

### 4. **Consider Your Financial Goals:**
   - **Emergency Fund:** Ensure you have a sufficient emergency fund before committing large sums to investments.
   - **Time Horizon:** The longer your investment horizon, the more you can typically afford to invest in equities for growth potential.

### 5. **Regularly Review and Adjust:**
   - **Rebalancing:** Regularly review your allocations based on market conditions, changes in financial situation, and approaching retirement.
   - **Tax Strategy Changes:** Stay informed about tax law changes and adjust contributions accordingly.

### Final Recommendations:
- **Start with the 401(k),** then assess the potential of the IRA, and finally plan your contributions to taxable accounts.
- Consult with a financial advisor to create a personalized strategy based on your unique circumstances.

By understanding the strengths and implications of each account type, you can strategically allocate your funds for both growth and tax efficiency.
