When considering how to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, here are some general strategies to optimize your investments:

### 1. **Understand Account Types and Tax Implications**
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are pre-tax, reducing your taxable income for the year, but withdrawals in retirement are taxed as ordinary income. Often, there’s a company match to consider.
   - **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**: Traditional IRAs are tax-deferred, while Roth IRAs grow tax-free. Contributions may reduce taxable income (for Traditional), whereas Roth contributions are made with after-tax dollars.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Investments are taxed on capital gains and dividends, so they can be less tax-efficient but provide flexibility for withdrawals.

### 2. **Maximize Employer Match**
   - Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to get any employer matching contributions. This is essentially free money.

### 3. **Contribute to IRAs**
   - Max out your IRA contributions after securing the 401(k) match. If you have the option, consider a Roth IRA for tax-free growth, especially if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement.

### 4. **Consider Tax Efficiency**
   - Place less tax-efficient investments (like bonds or REITs) in tax-advantaged accounts (401(k), IRA) to benefit from tax deferral.
   - Keep tax-efficient investments (like index funds or certain ETFs) in taxable accounts, as they typically generate less taxable income.

### 5. **Balance Contributions**
   - A general guideline might be to prioritize your 401(k) up to the match, contribute to an IRA, then return to maximize your 401(k). Adjust based on income and specific tax situations.

### 6. **Review Regularly**
   - Adjust contributions based on your tax situation, investment performance, and retirement goals.

### 7. **Consider Financial Goals**
   - Evaluate your short-term vs. long-term goals. If you need flexibility, prioritize taxable accounts. For long-term growth, focus on tax-advantaged accounts.

### Conclusion
It’s often beneficial to use a combination of these accounts to maximize your retirement savings while considering tax implications. Consulting with a financial advisor may also help tailor a strategy specific to your situation.
