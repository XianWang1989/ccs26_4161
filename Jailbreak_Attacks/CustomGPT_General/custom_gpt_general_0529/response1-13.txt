Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some guidelines to help you make your decision:

1. **401(k) Contributions**:
   - **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money.
   - **Tax Advantages**: Contributions are pre-tax, reducing your taxable income. If you have a Roth 401(k) option, consider it for tax-free withdrawals in retirement.
  
2. **IRA Contributions**:
   - **Traditional vs. Roth**:
     - **Traditional IRA**: Offers tax-deductible contributions (depending on income) and tax-deferred growth.
     - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are made with after-tax money, allowing tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement.
   - **Consider the Contribution Limits**: For 2023, the limit is $6,500 (or $7,500 if you’re 50 or older).

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - **For Flexibility**: These accounts offer more flexibility for withdrawals and investment choices.
   - **Tax Efficiency**: Focus on tax-efficient investments (like index funds) to minimize capital gains taxes.
   - **Short-Term Goals**: Good for savings or investments you need to access before retirement.

### Suggested Distribution Strategy:

1. **Start with 401(k)**: Contribute enough to get the full employer match.
2. **Max Out IRA**: Depending on your tax situation, prioritize either Traditional or Roth IRA contributions.
3. **Contribute to Taxable Account**: Once you’ve maximized the benefits from the 401(k) and IRA, direct additional funds here, focusing on long-term investments with favorable tax treatment.

### General Tips:
- **Diversification**: Maintain a diversified portfolio across all accounts.
- **Rebalance Regularly**: Keep your investment allocations aligned with your risk tolerance and goals.
- **Consult a Financial Advisor**: A personalized plan that considers your unique situation can be very beneficial.

This approach helps balance tax benefits, growth potential, and accessibility based on your specific needs.
