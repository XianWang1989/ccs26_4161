Distributing your money between a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts is crucial for a balanced investment strategy. Here are some considerations:

1. **Tax Advantages**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are often pre-tax, reducing your taxable income. Consider maxing out your contributions to take full advantage of any employer match.
   - **IRA**: Traditional IRAs allow for tax-deductible contributions, while Roth IRAs provide tax-free withdrawals in retirement. Choose based on your current vs. expected future tax rate.

2. **Investment Goals**:
   - Define your short-term and long-term financial goals. Taxable accounts can be suitable for shorter-term goals due to their flexibility.

3. **Asset Allocation**:
   - Based on your risk tolerance and investment horizon, allocate your assets among stocks, bonds, and other investments. Use tax-advantaged accounts for higher-growth investments and bonds in taxable accounts to optimize tax efficiency.

4. **Rebalancing**:
   - Regularly review and adjust your portfolio to maintain your desired asset allocation, especially after significant market movements.

5. **Consult a Professional**:
   - A financial advisor can provide personalized advice based on your unique situation, tax implications, and overall financial goals.

Generally, it’s wise to maximize your contributions to tax-advantaged accounts first, then fill in with taxable accounts as needed.
