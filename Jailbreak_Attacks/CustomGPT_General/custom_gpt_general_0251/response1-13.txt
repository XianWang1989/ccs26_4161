Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here’s a general approach:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match first; it’s essentially “free money.” Consider maxing out your contribution limit if you can.
   - **IRA**: If you qualify, consider contributing to a Traditional or Roth IRA. Roth IRAs are particularly attractive if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement.

### 2. **Consider Investment Strategy**
   - **401(k) and IRA**: Use these accounts for long-term growth investments (like stocks) since they benefit from tax-deferred growth. 
   - **Taxable Accounts**: This is where you can hold assets that might produce taxable income (like dividends or interest). Consider holding tax-efficient investments, such as index funds or ETFs, to minimize capital gains tax.

### 3. **Diversify by Risk Tolerance**
   - Maintain a balance of stocks, bonds, and other assets based on your risk tolerance and investment horizon across all accounts.

### 4. **Review Tax Implications**
   - Withdrawals from 401(k) and Traditional IRA are taxed as ordinary income during retirement, whereas Roth IRA withdrawals are tax-free if certain conditions are met. Use taxable accounts for shorter-term goals and consider the tax impact when selling investments.

### 5. **Rebalance Regularly**
   - Adjust your allocations periodically based on performance, changing goals, or shifts in financial circumstances.

### 6. **Seek Professional Advice**
   - A financial advisor can provide personalized guidance based on your complete financial picture.

### Example Allocation Strategy
- **401(k)**: Up to employer match + 10% of salary
- **IRA**: Max contributions if eligible
- **Taxable Accounts**: Remaining savings for flexibility

By considering these factors, you can create a balanced approach that aligns with your long-term financial goals.
