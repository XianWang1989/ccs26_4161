Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, investment timeline, and risk tolerance. Here’s a general approach:

### 1. **Prioritize Accounts**

- **401(k) Contributions**:
  - Contribute enough to get any employer match; this is essentially "free money."
  - Consider maxing out contributions if you have a high salary and can afford it.

- **IRA Contributions**:
  - If you’re eligible, also contribute to a traditional or Roth IRA.
  - Determine your eligibility based on income for Roth contributions and tax benefits for traditional IRA contributions.

- **Taxable Accounts**:
  - After maximizing tax-advantaged accounts, consider investing in a taxable account for additional savings and flexibility.

### 2. **Assess Investment Types**

- **Tax-Deferred vs. Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
  - Use 401(k) and traditional IRA for bonds and income-producing assets, as income is taxed at withdrawal.
  - Use Roth IRA for growth-oriented assets (stocks) since withdrawals are tax-free.
  - Use taxable accounts for investments with favorable capital gains treatment.

### 3. **Consider Your Tax Situation**

- Monitor your tax bracket and invest in tax-efficient funds or strategies in taxable accounts. 
- Evaluate the benefits of tax-loss harvesting to offset gains.

### 4. **Account Growth Potential**

- Balance your portfolio for growth (stocks) versus income (bonds/cash). Focus on higher growth in tax-advantaged accounts, as they compound without immediate tax liabilities.

### 5. **Rebalancing and Adjustments**

- Regularly review and adjust your allocations based on life changes (jobs, retirement age), market conditions, and changing financial goals.

### Example Allocation Strategy

1. **401(k)**: Contribute enough to receive the full employer match, then consider maxing it out.
2. **IRA**: Max out if possible based on income limits.
3. **Taxable Accounts**: Allocate any remaining funds here for flexibility and additional growth.

### Key Points

- Always consider fees, investment options, and your specific situation.
- Consult a financial advisor for personalized guidance tailored to your circumstances.

By following these strategies, you can help ensure that your money is distributed efficiently to meet your long-term financial goals.
