To effectively distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider these key factors:

1. **Tax Implications**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are made pre-tax, which can lower your taxable income. Withdrawals in retirement are taxed as ordinary income.
   - **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**: Traditional IRAs offer tax-deductible contributions (taxed upon withdrawal), while Roth IRAs provide tax-free growth and withdrawals (after age 59½ and if the account has been open for at least five years).
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Investments in these accounts are subject to capital gains tax when sold and dividends are taxed as ordinary income.

2. **Contribution Limits**:
   - Maximize contributions to your 401(k) if your employer offers a match, as this is essentially “free money.”
   - Contribute to your IRA (Traditional or Roth) to take advantage of additional tax benefits.

3. **Investment Goals**:
   - Determine your time horizon and risk tolerance. For long-term goals, consider a more aggressive allocation in tax-advantaged accounts, while keeping more conservative assets in taxable accounts to reduce potential capital gains taxes.

4. **Diversification**:
   - Ensure your investments are diversified across different asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) regardless of the account type.

5. **Routine Rebalancing**:
   - Regularly review and adjust your allocations to maintain your desired risk profile and investment strategy.

### Suggested Allocation Strategy:
- **401(k)**: Max out contributions and focus on growth-oriented investments (e.g., stocks or mutual funds).
- **IRA**: If eligible for a Roth, consider contributions here for tax-free growth; otherwise, prioritize a Traditional IRA for deductions.
- **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for investments that generate lower capital gains and income, such as index funds or tax-efficient ETFs.

### Example Distribution:
- **401(k)**: 40% 
- **IRA**: 30% 
- **Taxable Account**: 30%

Consult a financial advisor for personalized advice, especially based on your specific financial situation and retirement goals.
