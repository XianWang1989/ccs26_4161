Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here are some general guidelines:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a matching contribution for your 401(k), contribute enough to get the full match, as this is essentially free money.

2. **Contribute to an IRA**: After maximizing your 401(k) match, consider contributing to a traditional or Roth IRA. If you qualify for a Roth IRA, it offers tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement.

3. **Max Out 401(k)**: If you can afford it, aim to max out your 401(k) contributions after the IRA, as this account often offers higher contribution limits and tax deferral.

4. **Taxable Accounts**: Consider investing in taxable accounts for flexibility. These accounts allow you to access funds without penalties or taxes on withdrawals (though capital gains taxes may apply).

5. **Consider Tax Implications**: Be mindful of the tax implications of each account type. Traditional accounts provide tax deferral, while Roth accounts offer tax-free withdrawals.

6. **Diversify Investments**: Regardless of the account type, diversify your investments across assets like stocks, bonds, and real estate to balance risk and return.

7. **Review and Adjust**: Regularly review your allocations and adjust based on market performance, life changes, or shifts in your financial goals.

It's always advisable to consult with a financial advisor to tailor a plan to your specific circumstances.
