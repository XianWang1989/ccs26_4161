Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here’s a general strategy:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a matching contribution to your 401(k), prioritize contributing enough to get the full match. This is free money and a great start.

2. **401(k) Contributions**: After securing the match, consider maximizing your 401(k) contributions if you're in a high tax bracket. Contributions are pre-tax, which lowers your taxable income.

3. **IRA Contributions**: Utilize an IRA (Traditional or Roth) next. A Traditional IRA may give you a tax deduction, while a Roth IRA allows for tax-free withdrawals in retirement.

4. **Taxable Accounts**: After maximizing contributions to tax-advantaged accounts, consider investing in taxable accounts. These offer more flexibility for withdrawals and may include investments that generate favorable capital gains tax treatments.

5. **Diversification**: Ensure your investments across all accounts are diversified. This can help manage risk and maximize returns.

6. **Reassess Regularly**: Periodically review your allocations based on changes in income, expenses, or tax legislation, ensuring your strategy aligns with your long-term goals.

Consulting a financial advisor can also help tailor this strategy to your individual circumstances.
