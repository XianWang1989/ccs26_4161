Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts involves considering several factors like tax implications, investment goals, and financial needs. Here’s a general strategy:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money. If you can afford to, aim to contribute the maximum allowable amount ($22,500 for 2024, or $30,000 if you're 50 or older).
   - **IRA**: Contribute to an IRA (traditional or Roth) if you qualify. For 2024, the contribution limit is $6,500 (or $7,500 if you're 50 or older).

2. **Consider Tax Implications**:
   - **401(k) and Traditional IRA**: Contributions are typically made pre-tax, which reduces your taxable income in the year you contribute. Taxes are paid upon withdrawal in retirement.
   - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, but distributions in retirement are tax-free.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: This is where you'll pay taxes on any capital gains, dividends, or interest earned. Use this for funds that you expect to need in the short to medium term.

3. **Debt Considerations**:
   - If you have high-interest debt, consider using some funds to pay that off before maximizing contributions to these accounts.

4. **Diversify Investments**:
   - Within each account, diversify your investments across asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) based on your risk tolerance and time horizon.

5. **Reevaluate Regularly**:
   - Review your distribution strategy annually or after significant life changes (like a raise, job change, or family additions).

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**:
   - Given individual circumstances can vary widely, working with a financial advisor can help create a tailored strategy based on your unique financial situation.

By following these guidelines, you can create a balanced approach to effectively distribute your money among your retirement accounts and taxable investments.
