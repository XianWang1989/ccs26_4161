Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement plans. Here’s a general strategy:

1. **401(k) Contributions**:
   - **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to get the full employer match, if available.
   - **Consider Contribution Limits**: For 2024, the contribution limit is $22,500 (or $30,000 if you're 50 or older).

2. **IRA Contributions**:
   - **Choose Roth or Traditional**: Decide between a Roth IRA (tax-free withdrawals in retirement) and a Traditional IRA (tax-deductible contributions). Your current and expected future tax rates will influence this choice.
   - **Max Out Contributions**: The limit for 2024 is $6,500 (or $7,500 if 50 or older).

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - Use taxable accounts for investments that could have higher tax implications (like bonds or stocks expected to generate short-term capital gains), or for short-term goals (like saving for a house).
   - Consider tax-efficient funds (like index funds or ETFs) to minimize taxes on capital gains.

### Suggested Order:
1. Contribute to your 401(k) up to the employer match.
2. Max out your IRA.
3. Continue contributing to your 401(k) until you reach the annual limit.
4. Invest in taxable accounts for any additional savings.

### Other Considerations:
- **Emergency Fund**: Maintain a cash reserve for unexpected expenses.
- **Investment Strategy**: Ensure your investment choices align with your risk tolerance and time horizon. 
- **Review Regularly**: Reassess your strategy annually or when your financial situation changes.

Consulting a financial advisor can provide personalized recommendations based on your specific situation.
