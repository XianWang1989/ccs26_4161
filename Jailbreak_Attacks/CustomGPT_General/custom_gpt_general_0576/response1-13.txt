Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment horizon. Here’s a general guideline to consider:

### 1. **Prioritize Employer Match in 401(k)**
   - **Maximize Contributions:** Aim to contribute enough to get the full employer match if available. This is essentially "free money."

### 2. **Contribute to an IRA**
   - **Tax Advantages:** Consider contributing to a traditional IRA or Roth IRA (depending on your eligibility and tax situation). Roth IRAs provide tax-free withdrawals in retirement, while traditional IRAs give you a tax deduction now.

### 3. **Max Out Your 401(k)**
   - **Continued Contributions:** After capturing the employer match and contributing to an IRA, if funds allow, maximize your 401(k) contributions. It has a higher contribution limit compared to IRAs.

### 4. **Invest in Taxable Accounts**
   - **Flexibility:** Use taxable accounts for investing after maximizing tax-advantaged accounts. These accounts offer flexibility for withdrawals without penalties and can also be a strategic place for tax-efficient investments.

### 5. **Assess Asset Allocation**
   - **Diversification Strategy:** Consider how to allocate assets (stocks, bonds, etc.) based on your risk tolerance. Tax-inefficient investments (like bonds) may be better in tax-advantaged accounts, while tax-efficient ones (like index funds) can go in taxable accounts.

### 6. **Monitor and Adjust**
   - **Regular Reviews:** Reassess your investment strategy and allocations regularly to ensure they align with your financial goals as life changes.

### Considerations:
- **Short-Term vs. Long-Term:** If you need access to funds before retirement, prioritize your taxable accounts.
- **Tax Bracket:** Your current and expected future tax brackets can influence where to allocate your investments.

### Conclusion
Consulting with a financial advisor can provide personalized advice tailored to your unique situation.
