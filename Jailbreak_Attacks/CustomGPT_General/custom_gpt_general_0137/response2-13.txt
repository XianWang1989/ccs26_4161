To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match, as it's free money.

2. **Tax Considerations**:
   - **401(k)**: These are pre-tax contributions, reducing your taxable income now. They grow tax-deferred.
   - **IRA**: Choose between Traditional (pre-tax) and Roth (post-tax). Roth IRAs grow tax-free, advantageous if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Best for short-term goals or liquidity. Interest and dividends may be taxed at your ordinary rate, while long-term capital gains are taxed at a lower rate.

3. **Emergency Fund**: Ensure you have savings outside these accounts for emergencies, typically 3-6 months' worth of expenses.

4. **Investment Goals**: Align investments with your risk tolerance and time horizon. Stocks, bonds, ETFs, or mutual funds can be suitable options.

5. **Diversification**: Spread investments across different assets to manage risk effectively.

6. **Review and Adjust**: Regularly assess and rebalance your portfolio based on changes in your financial situation and goals.

Consulting a financial advisor for personalized advice is also beneficial.
