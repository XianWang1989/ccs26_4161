Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here’s a general approach:

### 1. **Maximize Contributions**
   - **401(k):** Contribute enough to get any employer match; it’s free money.
   - **IRA:** If eligible, contribute to a Roth or traditional IRA, prioritizing a Roth for tax-free withdrawals in retirement.

### 2. **Consider Tax Implications**
   - **401(k) and Traditional IRA:** Contributions are typically tax-deferred, meaning you pay taxes upon withdrawal.
   - **Roth IRA:** Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, and withdrawals in retirement are tax-free.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Interest, dividends, and capital gains are taxed in the year they are realized.

### 3. **Investment Goals and Time Horizon**
   - Allocate more to accounts with longer investment horizons (like a 401(k) or IRA) to benefit from compounding and tax advantages.
   - Use taxable accounts for shorter-term goals, keeping in mind potential capital gains taxes.

### 4. **Diversification**
   - Ensure a balanced allocation across all accounts. This can include equities, bonds, and alternative investments depending on your risk tolerance.

### 5. **Rebalance and Adjust**
   - Regularly review and adjust your allocations based on performance, market conditions, and changes in your financial situation.

### Example Allocation Strategy
- **Age and Risk Tolerance:**
  - **Younger (20-30s):** Higher equity in 401(k) and IRAs, moderate cash in taxable accounts.
  - **Middle Age (40-50s):** Balanced equities and fixed income in retirement accounts, shorter-term bonds/cash in taxable accounts.
  - **Near Retirement (60+):** Focus on preserving capital; more fixed income in all accounts.

### Consultation
- Consider working with a financial advisor for personalized strategies to reflect your specific circumstances and goals.
