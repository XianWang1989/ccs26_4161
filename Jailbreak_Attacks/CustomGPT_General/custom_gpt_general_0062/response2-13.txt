When distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies for optimal growth and tax efficiency:

### 1. **Maximize Contribution Limits:**
   - **401(k):** Contribute enough to get any employer match, as this is essentially "free money."
   - **IRA:** If eligible, contribute the maximum allowed (traditional or Roth, depending on your tax situation).

### 2. **Tax Efficiency:**
   - Place **higher-growth investments** (like stocks) in your tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) and IRA) to take advantage of tax-deferred growth.
   - Use your **taxable account** for more tax-efficient investments, like index funds or municipal bonds, which may incur lower capital gains taxes.

### 3. **Consider Your Time Horizon:**
   - For long-term goals (retirement), focus on maxing out your 401(k) and IRA first.
   - For shorter-term goals, maintain a more liquid investment in your taxable account.

### 4. **Diversification:**
   - Ensure a balanced mix of asset classes across all accounts to manage risk effectively.

### 5. **Withdrawal Strategy:**
   - When approaching retirement, consider which accounts to withdraw from first, often starting with taxable accounts to allow tax-advantaged accounts to grow longer.

### 6. **Costs and Fees:**
   - Be mindful of the fees associated with your investment choices in each account type. Aim for low-fee diversification.

### 7. **Regular Rebalancing:**
   - Periodically review and adjust your allocations to maintain your desired asset mix.

### Example Example Strategy:
- Max out your 401(k) contributions, ensuring you get the employer match.
- Contribute to an IRA (Roth if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket later).
- Any additional investments can go into a taxable account.

By blending these strategies, you can effectively manage your investments for both growth and tax efficiency. Consider consulting with a financial advisor for personalized advice based on your situation.
