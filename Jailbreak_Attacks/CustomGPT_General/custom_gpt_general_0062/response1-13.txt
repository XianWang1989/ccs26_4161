Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and time horizon. Here are some considerations for each account type to help you decide:

### 1. **401(k) Accounts**
   - **Employer Match:** Contribute enough to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money.
   - **Tax-Deferred Growth:** Investments grow tax-deferred until withdrawal in retirement. Prioritize funding this account to maximize tax benefits.

### 2. **IRA Accounts (Traditional or Roth)**
   - **Tax Considerations:**
     - **Traditional IRA:** Contributions may be tax-deductible, and withdrawals are taxed as income in retirement.
     - **Roth IRA:** Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, but qualified withdrawals are tax-free in retirement.
   - **Availability:** Max out contributions if eligible, especially with a Roth IRA to benefit from tax-free growth.

### 3. **Taxable Accounts**
   - **Flexibility:** Money can be withdrawn anytime without penalties, making these accounts useful for shorter-term goals.
   - **Tax Implications:** Be aware of capital gains taxes on investment returns, which can be higher than ordinary income tax rates for traditional accounts.

### **General Distribution Strategy**
- **Short-Term Goals:** Prioritize taxable accounts for flexibility.
- **Medium to Long-Term Goals:** Focus on maxing out your 401(k) and IRA contributions.
- **Tax Efficiency:** Consider holding tax-efficient investments (like index funds) in taxable accounts to minimize tax liability.

### **Final Thoughts**
- Assess your retirement timeline, tax bracket, and tolerance for risk.
- Consulting with a financial advisor can help tailor a strategy that fits your unique situation.
