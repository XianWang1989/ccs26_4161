Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various factors such as your financial goals, tax situation, and investment horizon. Here are some general guidelines:

1. **Maximize Employer Contributions**: If your employer offers a 401(k) match, contribute enough to get the full match first. This is essentially free money.

2. **Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: Consider maxing out contributions here, especially if you benefit from tax deferrals and employer matching. The limit for 2023 is $22,500 (or $30,000 if you're over 50).
   - **IRA**: After contributing to your 401(k), consider funding a Roth or Traditional IRA. For 2023, the contribution limit is $6,500 (or $7,500 if over 50). A Roth IRA offers tax-free growth, while Traditional IRAs may give you tax deductions upfront.
   
3. **Taxable Accounts**: Use taxable accounts for money you may need in the short term or for investments that generate capital gains not subject to taxation until sold. They are also useful for holding investments that benefit from long-term capital gains rates.

4. **Investment Strategy**: Allocate funds based on your risk tolerance and investment strategy. Typically, riskier assets (like stocks) might go into tax-advantaged accounts to benefit from tax-free growth, while more stable assets (like bonds) could be in taxable accounts for their predictable income.

5. **Rebalance Regularly**: Monitor your portfolio and rebalance as needed to maintain your target allocation.

6. **Consider Future Tax Implications**: Think about your expected tax bracket in retirement. If you anticipate being in a higher tax bracket, prioritize tax-deferred accounts; if lower, Roth options may be better.

7. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: A professional can tailor advice based on your specific situation and help with complex tax issues.

## Summary
Start with employer matching in your 401(k), then maximize retirement accounts, and finally use taxable accounts strategically. Always consider tax implications and seek professional guidance for personalized advice.
