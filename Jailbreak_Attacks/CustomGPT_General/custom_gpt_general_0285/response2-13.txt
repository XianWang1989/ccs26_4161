Distributing your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here are some general guidelines:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match if available. This is essentially free money.

2. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**: 
   - Max out your IRA contributions. Traditional IRAs can provide tax deductions, while Roth IRAs offer tax-free withdrawals in retirement.
   - If you have additional funds after maxing out the IRA, consider contributing more to your 401(k).

3. **Consider Tax Implications**:
   - Keep investments that generate high taxable income (like bonds or REITs) in tax-deferred accounts (401(k) or IRA) to avoid annual tax burdens.
   - Place investments with the potential for long-term capital gains (like stocks) in taxable accounts since long-term gains are taxed at a lower rate.

4. **Maintain Liquidity**: Ensure you have some cash in taxable accounts for short-term needs or emergencies, as access to these funds is easier without penalties.

5. **Balance Assets for Growth**: Allocate a diversified mix of assets based on your risk tolerance and investment horizon across all accounts.

6. **Reassess Regularly**: Review and adjust your allocations periodically based on market performance, changes in your financial situation, or retirement goals.

Consulting with a financial advisor can also provide personalized strategies tailored to your specific circumstances.
