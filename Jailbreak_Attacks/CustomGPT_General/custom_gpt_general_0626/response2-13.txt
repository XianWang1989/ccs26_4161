Distributing your money across a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here’s a general approach to consider:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match in Your 401(k):**
   - **Contribution Priority:** Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match. This is essentially free money and a strong return on your investment.

### 2. **Contribute to an IRA:**
   - **Roth vs. Traditional:** Depending on your income, consider contributing to a Roth IRA (after-tax contributions with tax-free withdrawals) or a Traditional IRA (tax-deferred growth). 
   - **Tax Situations:** If you anticipate being in a higher tax bracket in retirement, a Roth might be more beneficial.

### 3. **Max Out 401(k) Contributions:**
   - **Tax-Deferred Growth:** Once you’re maximizing your IRA contributions, focus on maximizing your 401(k) contributions, especially if your income allows.

### 4. **Invest in Taxable Accounts:**
   - **Flexibility and Accessibility:** Use taxable accounts for shorter-term goals or emergencies where you might need access to funds without penalties.
   - **Tax-Efficient Investments:** Consider tax-efficient investments (like index funds or ETFs) to minimize capital gains tax.

### 5. **Consider Your Asset Allocation:**
   - **Diverse Investments:** Ensure you have a mix of stocks, bonds, and other assets across all accounts that aligns with your risk tolerance and investment timeline.
   - **Rebalance:** Regularly rebalance your portfolio to maintain your desired asset allocation.

### 6. **Evaluate Your Tax Strategy:**
   - **Withdrawals:** Plan for tax implications when withdrawing from each account type in retirement.
   - **Tax-Loss Harvesting:** Use strategies in your taxable accounts to offset gains with losses, reducing your tax burden.

### Summary:
1. Maximize 401(k) employer match.
2. Contribute to an IRA.
3. Max out your 401(k).
4. Then invest in taxable accounts.
5. Ensure a balanced asset allocation across all accounts.

Consulting a financial advisor can provide tailored advice based on your specific circumstances.
