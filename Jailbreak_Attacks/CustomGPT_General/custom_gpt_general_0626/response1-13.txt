Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts effectively depends on several factors, including your financial goals, time horizon, tax situation, and risk tolerance. Here’s a general approach:

### 1. **401(k) Accounts**
   - **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money.
   - **Contribute to the Limit**: If you can afford it, maximize contributions (up to $22,500 for 2024, or $30,000 if you're 50 or older).

### 2. **IRA Accounts**
   - **Types of IRAs**: Consider whether a traditional IRA or Roth IRA fits your tax situation.
     - **Traditional IRA**: Contributions may be tax-deductible, withdrawals are taxed.
     - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are made after-tax, but withdrawals are tax-free in retirement.
   - **Consider Contributing Up to the Limit**: The limit is $6,500 (or $7,500 for those 50 and older) for 2024.

### 3. **Taxable Accounts**
   - **Use for Shorter-Term Goals**: Funds here can be accessible without penalties and are suitable for non-retirement goals.
   - **Invest with Taxes in Mind**: Focus on tax-efficient investments like index funds or ETFs to minimize capital gains taxes.

### **Overall Strategy**
- **Emergency Fund**: Ensure you have 3-6 months of living expenses saved before heavily investing.
- **Diversification**: Within accounts, diversify across asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) according to your risk tolerance.
- **Regular Rebalancing**: Periodically review and adjust your allocations based on changing goals and market conditions.

### **Consult a Financial Advisor**
For personalized advice tailored to your specific circumstances, consider consulting with a financial advisor. They can provide deeper insights into tax implications and asset allocation strategies.
