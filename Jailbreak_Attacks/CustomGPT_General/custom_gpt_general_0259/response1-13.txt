When deciding how to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

### 1. **Prioritize Tax Benefits**
   - **401(k)**: If your employer offers a match, prioritize contributing enough to get the full match. This is essentially "free money."
   - **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**: Contribute to an IRA next to take advantage of tax benefits. A Traditional IRA offers tax-deferred growth, while a Roth IRA provides tax-free withdrawals in retirement.

### 2. **Assess Your Goals and Time Horizon**
   - **Retirement Savings**: Focus on tax-advantaged accounts first. Use the 401(k) and IRA for long-term savings.
   - **Short-term Goals**: Use taxable accounts if you have medium- to short-term goals (e.g., buying a house), as these offer flexibility for withdrawals.

### 3. **Consider Your Tax Situation**
   - In high-income years, focus on pre-tax contributions (401(k) and Traditional IRA) to lower your taxable income. In lower-income years, consider Roth IRA contributions for tax-free growth.

### 4. **Diversify Investments Within Accounts**
   - Consider different investments based on the account type. For example, put higher-growth investments (like stocks) in taxable accounts, where you may incur capital gains taxes, and more stable investments (like bonds) or income-producing assets in tax-advantaged accounts.

### 5. **Regularly Review and Adjust**
   - Reassess your allocation periodically based on changes in income, tax laws, and investment performance.

### 6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**
   - A tailored strategy based on individual circumstances can maximize benefits, so getting professional advice is often beneficial.

By balancing contributions based on tax advantages, investment goals, and time horizons, you can optimize your savings across these accounts.
