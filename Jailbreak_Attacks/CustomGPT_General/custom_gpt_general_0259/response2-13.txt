Distributing your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here’s a general strategy to consider:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts:**
   - **401(k):** Aim to contribute enough to get any employer match. This is essentially “free money.” Max out contributions if possible, as contributions are pre-tax and can grow tax-deferred.
   - **IRA (Traditional or Roth):** Max out your IRA contributions. If you qualify, a Roth IRA can be especially beneficial for tax-free growth and withdrawals.

### 2. **Tax Efficiency:**
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Use these for investments that generate qualified dividends and long-term capital gains, which are taxed at lower rates. It’s also a good place for funds that you might need before retirement, as withdrawals are not penalized.

### 3. **Asset Allocation:**
   - **Growth Investments:** Place growth-oriented investments (like stocks) in tax-advantaged accounts to benefit from tax-deferred growth.
   - **Income-Generating Investments:** Hold dividend-paying stocks and bonds in taxable accounts to take advantage of lower long-term capital gains tax rates.

### 4. **Emergency Fund:**
   - Keep some liquidity in your taxable accounts for emergencies, rather than tying up all funds in retirement accounts where early withdrawals incur penalties.

### 5. **Regular Rebalancing:**
   - Monitor and adjust your allocation annually or semi-annually to align with your financial goals and risk tolerance.

### 6. **Consider Future Tax Bracket:**
   - If you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement, prioritize Roth accounts. If you anticipate being in a lower bracket, traditional accounts may be more beneficial.

### 7. **Consult a Financial Advisor:**
   - Personalized advice tailored to your specific situation can help optimize your strategy.

By strategically distributing your investments across these accounts, you can maximize both growth potential and tax efficiency over time.
