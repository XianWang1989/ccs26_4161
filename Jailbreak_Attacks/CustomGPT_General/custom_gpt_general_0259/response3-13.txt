Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts should be based on several factors including your financial goals, tax situation, and time horizon. Here are some strategies to consider:

### 1. **Understand Tax Implications**
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are typically pre-tax, reducing your taxable income now, but withdrawals during retirement will be taxed as ordinary income.
   - **IRA (Traditional and Roth)**: Traditional IRAs provide tax-deferred growth, while Roth IRAs offer tax-free withdrawals in retirement. Choose based on your expected tax rate during retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Earnings and capital gains are subject to taxes in the year they are realized. These accounts are great for short-term goals or flexibility.

### 2. **Consider Your Goals**
   - **Retirement**: Max out contributions to your 401(k) and IRA if your goal is long-term growth. The 401(k) often has higher contribution limits.
   - **Short- to Medium-Term Goals**: Use taxable accounts for funds you might need sooner. They provide more liquidity and flexibility.

### 3. **Prioritize Contributions**
   - **Employer Match**: Always contribute enough to your 401(k) to get any employer match—this is essentially free money.
   - **Max Out IRAs**: After contributing enough to get the match, prioritize maxing out your IRA contributions (both traditional and Roth).

### 4. **Asset Allocation**
   - Diversify your investments across all accounts. Consider your risk tolerance and age when determining the mix of equities, bonds, and other assets. 

### 5. **Review and Adjust**
   - Review your portfolio regularly. As you approach retirement, shift towards more conservative investments to protect your capital.

### Sample Distribution Strategy
- **401(k)**: 50% (max contributions)
- **IRA (Traditional/Roth)**: 30% (max contributions)
- **Taxable Account**: 20% (for flexibility and emergencies)

This can vary significantly based on personal circumstances, so adjusting the percentages based on your financial situation and goals is crucial.

### Conclusion
Evaluate your current financial situation, retirement goals, and investment strategy to determine the best allocation for your money across these accounts. Consulting with a financial advisor might also be a good step to tailor a specific plan for you.
