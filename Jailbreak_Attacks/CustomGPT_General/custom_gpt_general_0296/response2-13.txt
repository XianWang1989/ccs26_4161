To effectively distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider these strategies:

- **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**: 
  - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match; aim for the maximum contribution limit if financially feasible.
  - **IRA**: Contribute to a Traditional or Roth IRA based on eligibility and tax implications.

- **Assess Your Financial Goals**: 
  - Define short-term vs. long-term goals. 
  - Use taxable accounts for goals within the next 5 years, as they offer more liquidity.

- **Evaluate Your Tax Situation**: 
  - Traditional 401(k) and IRA contributions may reduce current tax burdens. 
  - Roth accounts provide tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement.

- **Diversify Investment Choices**: 
  - Consider asset allocation based on risk tolerance and investment horizon across all accounts.
  - Utilize a mix of stocks, bonds, and mutual funds to blend growth and stability.

- **Reassess Regularly**: 
  - Review your investment distribution annually or as your financial situation changes.
  - Adjust contributions based on performance and changes in tax laws.

- **Consult a Financial Advisor**: 
  - Seek professional advice tailored to your specific financial situation and goals.

Following these guiding principles can help optimize your investment strategy across different account types.
