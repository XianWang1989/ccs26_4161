Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts involves considering several factors to optimize tax efficiency and long-term growth. Here are some best practices:

### Optimizing Your Distribution Strategy

- **Maximize Employer Match**:  
  Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match. This is essentially "free money."

- **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:  
  After the match, allocate funds to a Traditional IRA or Roth IRA depending on your tax situation and eligibility. Roth IRAs offer tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement.

- **Assess Your Tax Bracket**:  
  Understand your current and expected future tax brackets. Taxable accounts may be more beneficial if you anticipate being in a lower tax bracket during retirement.

- **Consider Withdrawal Strategy**:  
  Plan your investments based on the order of withdrawals during retirement. Tax-deferred accounts can be tapped later, while taxable accounts may offer capital gains taxes but provide liquidity.

- **Diversify Investments**:  
  Distribute risk by diversifying among asset classes across all accounts. Consider how each account fits into your overall risk tolerance and investment horizon.

- **Rebalance Regularly**:  
  Monitor the performance of your investments and rebalance your portfolio periodically to maintain your desired asset allocation.

### Tailored Approach

- **Evaluate Contribution Limits**:  
  Be aware of annual contribution limits for 401(k)s and IRAs, and maximize contributions if financially feasible.

- **Utilize Tax-Loss Harvesting**:  
  In taxable accounts, take advantage of tax-loss harvesting strategies to offset capital gains.

This tailored approach will help you create a balanced and tax-efficient distribution of your investments. Always consider consulting with a financial advisor for personalized advice.
