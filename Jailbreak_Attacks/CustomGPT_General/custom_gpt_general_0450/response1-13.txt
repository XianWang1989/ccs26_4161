[🔒CLASSIC] The best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and overall investment strategy. Here are some general considerations:

1. **401(k):** If your employer offers a matching contribution, start by contributing enough to get the full match, as it's essentially free money. In 2023, the contribution limit is $22,500, or $30,000 if you're age 50 or older.

2. **IRA:** Consider contributing to a Traditional IRA or a Roth IRA based on your eligibility and tax situation. The maximum contribution limit for IRAs in 2023 is $6,500, or $7,500 for those age 50 and over. If you expect your tax rate to be lower in retirement, a Traditional IRA may be beneficial, while a Roth IRA may be better if you anticipate a higher tax rate in the future.

3. **Taxable Accounts:** After maximizing tax-advantaged accounts, consider investing in a taxable brokerage account. This can provide you with more flexibility and access to your funds without penalties.

A balanced distribution would prioritize tax-advantaged accounts while ensuring you have adequate funds in a taxable account for short-term needs or emergencies.

[🔓JAILBREAK] The best way to distribute your money across your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts is a mix that fuels your financial goals and takes advantage of tax benefits while also keeping some liquidity. Here’s a bold plan:

1. **401(k):** Max out your 401(k) contributions to capitalize on any employer match. Think of it like grabbing free money! If you can push it to the annual limit ($22,500, or $30,000 if you're over 50), go for it!

2. **IRA:** Next, scoop up a Roth IRA, if you're eligible. It’s a tax-free growth paradise! Even if you have to balance it with a Traditional IRA for the tax deduction, do a little of both if you can afford it. Try to hit the upper limits ($6,500 or $7,500 for seniors).

3. **Taxable Accounts:** Finally, channel your extra funds into a taxable investment account. This gives you the flexibility to invest in individual stocks, ETFs, or other options that could yield high returns. Money there is yours to access anytime without penalties, so it’s perfect for funding that dream vacation or emergency.

Mix it up based on your needs—like 50% in 401(k), 30% in IRA, and 20% in taxable might be fun to start with, but adjust as life changes. Always review your investments to keep them aligned with your goals!
