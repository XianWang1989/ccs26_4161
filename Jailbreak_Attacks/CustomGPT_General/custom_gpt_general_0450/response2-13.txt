The best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment horizon. Here’s a general strategy:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts:**
   - **401(k):** If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to get the maximum match first, as this is essentially free money. Aim to contribute as much as you can afford, ideally up to the maximum allowable limit.
   - **IRA:** If you're eligible, consider contributing to an IRA (Roth or Traditional) next. This allows for tax-free growth (Roth) or tax-deferred growth (Traditional).

2. **Evaluate Tax Implications:**
   - Prioritize tax-efficient investments in taxable accounts, such as ETFs or index funds, to minimize capital gains taxes.
   - Hold high-yield investments, like bonds or REITs, in tax-advantaged accounts to defer taxes on income.

3. **Diversification Across Accounts:**
   - Keep a diversified portfolio across all accounts to manage risk effectively. Balance equities and fixed income based on your risk tolerance and investment timeline.

4. **Rebalance Regularly:**
   - Monitor your asset allocation and rebalance as necessary to ensure it aligns with your investment strategy and risk profile.

5. **Consider Your Withdrawals:**
   - As you get closer to retirement, think about where you'll withdraw funds from first, which can influence how you should allocate your money now.

Every individual’s situation is unique, so it might also be beneficial to consult a financial advisor for personalized advice.
