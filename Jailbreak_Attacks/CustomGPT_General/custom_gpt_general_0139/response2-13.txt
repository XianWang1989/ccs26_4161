Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment time horizon. Here are some general strategies:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**:
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match. This is essentially "free money."
   - **IRA**: After maximizing your 401(k) contributions (up to the limit), consider contributing to a Roth or Traditional IRA for additional tax benefits.

2. **Tax Considerations**:
   - **Tax-Deferred Accounts (401(k) & Traditional IRA)**: Contributions lower your taxable income now, but withdrawals in retirement are taxed.
   - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, but withdrawals in retirement are tax-free.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Ideal for investments that generate long-term capital gains, which are taxed at a lower rate than ordinary income.

3. **Emergency Fund**:
   - Before heavily investing, ensure you have 3-6 months of living expenses in a liquid, accessible fund.

4. **Diversification**:
   - Within each account type, aim for a balanced mixture of stocks, bonds, and other assets based on your risk tolerance.

5. **Rebalance Regularly**:
   - Periodically review your asset allocation to ensure it aligns with your goals and risk tolerance.

6. **Withdrawal Strategy**:
   - In retirement, consider withdrawing from taxable accounts first to allow tax-advantaged accounts to continue growing.

Consulting with a financial advisor can provide personalized strategies based on your unique situation.
