Determining the best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and time horizon. Here’s a general approach:

### 1. **401(k) Contributions**:
   - **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to get the full employer match, as this is essentially "free money."
   - **Tax Benefits**: Contributions are often pre-tax, reducing your taxable income now.

### 2. **IRA Contributions**:
   - **Consider a Roth IRA**: If you're eligible, consider a Roth IRA for tax-free withdrawals in retirement.
   - **Tax Diversification**: Having tax-deferred and tax-free accounts can help manage your tax burden in retirement.

### 3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - **Supplement Retirement Contributions**: Once you maximize retirement accounts, use taxable accounts for additional investments.
   - **Tax Efficiency**: Focus on assets with lower turnover or tax obligations (like index funds or tax-efficient ETFs).

### **General Strategy**:
1. **Emergency Fund**: Ensure you have an emergency fund (3-6 months of expenses) before heavy investing.
2. **Asset Allocation**: Diversify within each account based on your risk tolerance and investment horizon.
3. **Rebalancing**: Regularly reassess your portfolio and rebalance as needed.

### **Consult a Financial Advisor**:
For personalized advice tailored to your specific financial situation, it may be beneficial to consult a certified financial planner.
