Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here’s a general approach:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Contributions**
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get the full employer match, if available. This is effectively free money.

### 2. **Consider Tax Implications**
   - **Traditional IRA/401(k)**: Contributions may be tax-deductible. Ideal if you expect to be in a lower tax bracket in retirement.
   - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are made with after-tax money, but withdrawals are tax-free. Consider if you anticipate being in a higher tax bracket later.

### 3. **Investment Growth Potential**
   - **401(k)**: Often includes limited investment options. Choose low-cost index funds if available.
   - **IRA**: Offers more flexibility. Diversify your investments across stocks, bonds, and other assets based on your risk tolerance.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Suitable for investments you may want to access before retirement age (without penalties). Be mindful of capital gains taxes.

### 4. **Liquidity Needs**
   - Keep a portion in taxable accounts for short-term needs or emergencies. Aim for an emergency fund that covers 3-6 months of expenses.

### 5. **Debt Considerations**
   - If you have high-interest debt, consider prioritizing paying that down before heavily investing.

### 6. **Regular Rebalancing**
   - Periodically review your asset allocation and performance. Adjust contributions based on changing goals, market conditions, and your age.

### Example Distribution Strategy:
- **401(k)**: 10%-15% (up to employer match)
- **Traditional/Roth IRA**: 10%-15% (max out contributions if possible)
- **Taxable Accounts**: Remaining funds based on your liquidity needs and goals.

### Conclusion
Consulting with a financial advisor can help tailor a strategy to your specific situation, ensuring it aligns with your long-term goals and risk tolerance.
