Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts involves several factors like your retirement goals, tax situation, investment horizon, and risk tolerance. Here are some general guidelines:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a 401(k) match, contribute enough to get the full match. This is essentially free money.

2. **Tax-Advantaged Accounts**: 
   - **401(k)**: Prioritize contributions here for tax-deferred growth. Consider traditional if you expect to be in a lower tax bracket in retirement, or a Roth 401(k) if you think you'll be in a higher bracket.
   - **IRA**: If you are eligible, contribute to a traditional or Roth IRA after maximizing your 401(k) match. A Roth IRA offers tax-free withdrawals in retirement, while a traditional IRA gives you tax deductions now.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for investments that might generate taxable income. Consider holding tax-efficient investments like index funds or ETFs to minimize capital gains taxes.

4. **Diversification**: Ensure your investments are diversified across asset classes (stocks, bonds) and sectors to spread risk.

5. **Emergency Fund**: Before heavily investing, have an emergency fund covering 3-6 months of expenses.

6. **Regular Contributions**: Consistently contribute to each account, adjusting as your financial situation changes.

7. **Rebalance Periodically**: Review your asset allocation and rebalance to maintain your desired risk level.

Consulting with a financial advisor can also provide tailored strategies based on your individual circumstances.
