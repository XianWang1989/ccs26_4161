Distributing your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some general guidelines:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get any employer match first, as this is essentially “free money.”

2. **Consider Tax Benefits**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are pre-tax, reducing your taxable income for the year. However, withdrawals will be taxed as ordinary income.
   - **Traditional IRA**: Similar tax benefits as a 401(k), but ensure you adhere to contribution limits and income restrictions.
   - **Roth IRA**: Provides tax-free withdrawals in retirement, which might be beneficial if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket later.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Ideal for more flexible investing and access to funds without penalties; however, you will owe capital gains tax on investments.

3. **Emergency Fund**: Ensure you have an adequate emergency fund in a readily accessible account before heavily investing.

4. **Investment Goals and Timeline**:
   - Long-term savings (retirement) should be prioritized in tax-advantaged accounts (401(k), IRAs).
   - Shorter-term goals may be better suited for taxable accounts where access to funds isn’t penalized.

5. **Asset Allocation**: 
   - Consider your risk tolerance and diversify your investments accordingly. Typically, higher-risk investments can be placed in tax-advantaged accounts to minimize tax impact on gains.

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: Personal financial situations vary widely; consulting a professional can provide tailored advice.

By thoughtfully distributing your contributions and considering the associated tax implications, you can optimize your investment strategy for both short-term and long-term goals.
