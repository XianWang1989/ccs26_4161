Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here’s a general approach:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your 401(k) offers an employer matching contribution, aim to contribute at least enough to get the full match, as this is essentially free money.

2. **Consider Tax Advantages**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are often pre-tax, reducing your taxable income. Ideal if you expect to be in a lower tax bracket in retirement.
   - **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**: Traditional IRAs may provide tax deductions, while Roth IRAs offer tax-free withdrawals in retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Best for goals outside retirement or if you’ve maxed out tax-advantaged accounts. Be mindful of capital gains taxes.

3. **Diversify Investments**: Ensure that you have a mix of asset types (stocks, bonds, etc.) across all accounts to manage risk and optimize growth.

4. **Assess Liquidity Needs**: Taxable accounts provide more flexibility for withdrawals without penalties compared to retirement accounts.

5. **Long-Term Goals**: Align your distribution strategy with your retirement timeline and other financial objectives. For example, if retirement is far off, prioritize growth in your 401(k) and IRA.

6. **Get Professional Advice**: If unsure, consider consulting a financial advisor to tailor a specific strategy to your situation.

Ultimately, the right distribution will depend on your personal financial picture.
