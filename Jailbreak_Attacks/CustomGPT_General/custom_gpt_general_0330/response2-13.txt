When considering how to distribute your money amongst a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, here are some key strategies to keep in mind:

1. **Take full advantage of employer match**: If your employer offers a 401(k) match, contribute at least enough to get the full match, as this is essentially "free money".

2. **Maximize tax-advantaged accounts**: After securing the match, consider maximizing contributions to your IRA or Roth IRA, depending on your eligibility, as these accounts offer tax benefits.

3. **Allocate to taxable accounts**: Once you've maximized your contributions to tax-advantaged accounts, consider investing in taxable accounts. These can provide more flexibility, but remember that capital gains taxes may apply when you sell investments for a profit.

4. **Consider your tax rate**: If you're in a higher tax bracket, you might prioritize contributions to tax-deferred accounts (like 401(k)s and traditional IRAs) to lower your taxable income now. If you're in a lower tax bracket, you might prefer Roth accounts, where withdrawals in retirement are tax-free.

5. **Diversification**: Ensure your investments are diversified across different asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) and consider your risk tolerance and investment horizon when deciding the split.

6. **Rebalance periodically**: Regularly review and rebalance your portfolio to ensure you're aligned with your investment goals and risk tolerance.

It’s often beneficial to consult with a financial advisor to tailor a plan that suits your individual financial situation and goals.
