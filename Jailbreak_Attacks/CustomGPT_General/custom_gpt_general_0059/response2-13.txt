Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment horizon. Here’s a strategic approach to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match, if available. This is essentially free money.

2. **Contribute to an IRA**: If you qualify, contribute to an IRA (Traditional or Roth). A Roth IRA is beneficial if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement.

3. **Additional 401(k) Contributions**: After maximizing your IRA contributions, return to your 401(k) to maximize contributions. Higher contribution limits can help you save more for retirement.

4. **Taxable Accounts**: If you have extra money after maxing out retirement accounts, invest in taxable accounts. Use tax-efficient investments like index funds or ETFs to minimize tax impact.

5. **Consider Asset Location**: Keep investments that generate high taxable income (like bonds) in tax-advantaged accounts and let tax-efficient investments (like stocks) reside in taxable accounts for potential long-term capital gains treatment.

6. **Regular Reassessment**: Revisit your allocation regularly based on changes in income, expenses, and financial goals.

7. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: Consider speaking with a financial advisor to tailor a plan specific to your situation.

This balanced approach helps maximize tax advantages while meeting your long-term financial goals.
