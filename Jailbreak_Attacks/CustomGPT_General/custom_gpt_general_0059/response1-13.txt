Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment horizon. Here’s a general approach:

### 1. **Prioritize Your Accounts**
   - **401(k):** Often, this is where you should start, especially if your employer offers matching contributions. Aim to at least contribute enough to get the full match.
   - **IRA/Roth IRA:** Consider maxing out an IRA, especially if you’re eligible for tax deductions (traditional IRA) or tax-free growth (Roth IRA). The choice between these depends on your current and expected future tax rates.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Use this for flexibility and when you've maximized tax-advantaged accounts. It’s also good for short-term goals or emergency funds.

### 2. **Assess Your Tax Situation**
   - **Tax-Deferred Growth:** Contributions to a 401(k) and traditional IRA reduce your taxable income now, growing tax-deferred.
   - **Tax-Free Growth:** Roth IRAs provide tax-free growth, ideal for those expecting to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement.

### 3. **Investment Strategy**
   - **Risk Tolerance:** Match your investments to your risk tolerance across all accounts. Generally, higher-risk investments can fit into tax-advantaged accounts for long-term growth.
   - **Asset Location:** Place income-generating assets (like bonds) in tax-advantaged accounts and high-growth stocks in taxable accounts to minimize tax impact.

### 4. **Regular Review**
   - Adjust contributions based on changes in income, expenses, or financial goals. Rebalance your portfolio as needed.

### Example Distribution
- **401(k):** 15% of income until match is maxed.
- **IRA:** Max out contributions annually.
- **Taxable Accounts:** Invest remaining funds for flexibility.

Always consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized advice based on your specific situation.
