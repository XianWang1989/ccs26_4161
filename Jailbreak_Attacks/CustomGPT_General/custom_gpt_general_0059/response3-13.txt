Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and time horizon. Here’s a general approach:

### 1. **Maximize Retirement Accounts First**:
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money. Aim to max out if possible, especially if you're in a high tax bracket.
   - **IRA**: Consider contributing to a Traditional or Roth IRA. A Traditional IRA can offer tax deductions, while a Roth IRA allows for tax-free withdrawals in retirement.

### 2. **Assess Tax Implications**:
   - **Tax-Deferred Accounts**: 401(k) and Traditional IRA grow tax-deferred, lowering your tax burden now.
   - **Roth IRA**: Offers tax-free growth and withdrawals, which can be beneficial later.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Useful for liquidity and flexibility but involve capital gains taxes on earnings.

### 3. **Allocate Based on Goals**:
   - **Short-Term Goals**: Focus on taxable accounts for easy access.
   - **Long-Term Goals**: Allocate more to tax-advantaged accounts, allowing for compound growth over time.

### 4. **Diversify Investments**:
   - Maintain a diversified portfolio across all accounts, considering risk tolerance and asset allocation.

### 5. **Rebalance Regularly**:
   - Review and adjust your allocations periodically to stay aligned with your goals and market conditions.

### Sample Allocation Strategy:
- **401(k)**: 15% of your income (max out if possible)
- **IRA**: 10% of your income
- **Taxable Account**: Remaining funds, focusing on investment types that are tax-efficient (e.g., index funds).

### Conclusion:
Optimize contributions based on tax benefits and your retirement timeline. Regularly revisit your strategy to adapt to changes in financial situation or market conditions. Consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized advice.
