Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment horizon. Here’s a general strategy:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match (this is effectively free money).
   - **IRA**: Contribute to a traditional or Roth IRA depending on your income level and tax situation. 

### 2. **Consider Tax Implications**:
   - **Traditional 401(k) and IRA**: Contributions are tax-deductible, but withdrawals in retirement are taxed as income.
   - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, but withdrawals are tax-free in retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Investments here are subject to capital gains taxes; consider holding tax-efficient investments like index funds.

### 3. **Investment Horizon**:
   - Long-term goals (like retirement) should typically be funded first with tax-advantaged accounts.
   - Short-term goals can be funded through taxable accounts.

### 4. **Diversification**:
   - Spread your investments across asset classes (stocks, bonds) for risk management.
   - Consider your risk tolerance when determining the allocation.

### 5. **Rebalance Regularly**:
   - Monitor and adjust your allocations based on performance and changing financial goals.

### Example Distribution Strategy:
- **401(k)**: 15-20% of income (or max up to employer match)
- **IRA**: Max contribution (currently $6,500 for individuals under 50, $7,500 for those 50 and over)
- **Taxable Accounts**: Remainder of savings after fully funding tax-advantaged accounts

It's advisable to consult a financial advisor for personalized guidance based on your specific situation.
