Distributing your money effectively among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors: your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here’s a general strategy:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to get the full match—it’s essentially free money. Consider contributing up to the annual limit if feasible.
   - **IRA**: Contribute to a Traditional IRA if you qualify for tax deductions or a Roth IRA for tax-free withdrawals in retirement. Prioritize maxing out contributions if you can.

### 2. **Consider Tax Implications**:
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for investments that generate lower tax implications or for money you might need before retirement age. They offer flexibility for withdrawals but will be subject to capital gains taxes.

### 3. **Investment Strategy**:
   - **Index Funds or ETFs**: Consider low-cost index funds in all accounts for long-term growth.
   - **Bonds**: Consider holding bonds in tax-advantaged accounts to shelter income from taxation.

### 4. **Balance and Rebalance**:
   - Regularly review and adjust your asset allocation across accounts to align with your changing goals and market conditions.

### 5. **Emergency Fund**:
   - Ensure you have an adequate emergency fund outside these accounts before investing heavily.

### Example Allocation**:
- **401(k)**: 20-30% of your total savings, especially if you're receiving an employer match.
- **IRA**: 10-15% of your total annual savings.
- **Taxable Accounts**: The remainder, focusing on investments that align with your risk tolerance and investment horizon.

### Consult a Financial Advisor:
For personalized advice tailored to your specific situation, consider speaking with a financial advisor. They can help navigate complex rules and optimize your strategy.
