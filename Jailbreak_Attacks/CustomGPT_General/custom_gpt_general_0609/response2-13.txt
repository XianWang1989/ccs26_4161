Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts involves balancing tax advantages, investment goals, and liquidity needs. Here are some strategies:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to take full advantage of any employer matching.

2. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**: Aim to max out your IRA (if eligible) and contribute to your 401(k) up to the annual contribution limit. These accounts typically offer tax-deferred growth.

3. **Consider Tax Implications**:
   - **401(k)**: Generally pre-tax contributions reduce your taxable income.
   - **IRA**: Traditional IRAs may allow for pre-tax contributions, while Roth IRAs provide tax-free growth but contributions are made with after-tax dollars.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Interest and dividends are taxed in the year received, so consider using these for investments that generate qualified dividends or long-term capital gains to minimize taxes.

4. **Emergency Fund**: Ensure you have an adequate emergency fund in cash or near-cash investments, which typically should not be in retirement accounts.

5. **Asset Allocation**: Diversify investments across accounts based on your risk tolerance and time horizon. Growth-oriented investments may be better suited for tax-advantaged accounts while bonds or income-producing investments may fit taxable accounts due to different tax treatments.

6. **Review and Adjust**: Periodically reassess your allocation based on changes in income, tax laws, and life circumstances.

This approach helps balance long-term growth and liquidity while optimizing tax efficiency.
