The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment strategy. Here’s a general approach:

### Step-by-Step Distribution Strategy

1. **Maximize Employer Match**:
   - Contribute enough to your 401(k) to receive any employer matching contributions. This is essentially "free money."

2. **Consider Tax Advantages**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are pre-tax, lowering your taxable income now. Good for higher earners.
   - **Traditional IRA**: Also tax-deductible, but may have income limits for contributions.
   - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, allowing tax-free growth and withdrawals.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: No tax advantages, but offer flexibility and access without penalties.

3. **Prioritize Tax-Deferred Accounts**:
   - Focus on 401(k) and IRA contributions to benefit from tax deferral. Aim to maximize contributions to these accounts.

4. **Diversify Tax Treatment**:
   - Aim for a mix of tax-deferred (401(k), Traditional IRA) and tax-free (Roth IRA) accounts to manage taxes in retirement effectively.

5. **Emergency Funds**:
   - Ensure you have sufficient liquid savings in a taxable account or high-yield savings account for emergencies.

6. **Long-Term Growth**:
   - Invest for growth in tax-advantaged accounts. Consider stocks, mutual funds, or ETFs in these accounts.

7. **Review and Adjust**:
   - Regularly assess your portfolio and rebalance as needed based on performance, risk tolerance, and changing financial goals.

### Conclusion
This distribution strategy ensures you take advantage of tax benefits while maintaining liquidity and growth potential. Adjust your contributions based on your specific financial situation and goals.
