To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategy:

### 1. **Understand Account Types:**
   - **401(k):** Typically offers tax-deferred growth; employer matching could be available.
   - **IRA (Traditional/Roth):** 
     - **Traditional IRA:** Tax-deferred; taxes owed on withdrawals.
     - **Roth IRA:** Contributions are post-tax, but withdrawals are tax-free.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Investments are taxed annually on dividends and capital gains.

### 2. **Contribution Prioritization:**
   - **Employer Match:** Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match—this is essentially free money.
   - **Maximize Roth IRA:** If eligible, contribute to a Roth IRA for tax-free growth.
   - **Maximum 401(k) Contributions:** After maximizing the match and Roth IRA, contribute to your 401(k) up to the limit.

### 3. **Investment Allocation:**
   - **Risk Tolerance:** Match your investments to your risk tolerance and time horizon across all accounts.
   - **Diversification:** Ensure diversification of assets (stocks, bonds, etc.) within accounts for balanced growth.

### 4. **Tax Efficiency:**
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Consider holding tax-efficient investments (e.g., index funds) that generate fewer taxable events.
   - **Bonds in Tax-Deferred Accounts:** Place interest-generating investments, like bonds, in tax-deferred accounts.

### 5. **Review and Adjust:**
   - Regularly review your asset allocation and re-balance based on changes in market conditions or life circumstances.

### Summary: 
Start with employer match in 401(k) → max out Roth IRA → then maximize 401(k) contributions → focus on tax efficiency in taxable accounts.
