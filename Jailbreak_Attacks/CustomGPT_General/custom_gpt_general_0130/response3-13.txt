To effectively distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategy:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match (this is essentially free money).
   - **IRA**: If eligible, contribute to a Roth or Traditional IRA (consider tax implications).

### 2. **Understand Contribution Limits**
   - Stay within annual contribution limits to maximize tax benefits:
     - **401(k)**: $22,500 (or $30,000 if you're over 50 for 2023).
     - **IRA**: $6,500 (or $7,500 if over 50).

### 3. **Tax Efficiency**
   - Allocate investments based on tax efficiency:
     - **Taxable Accounts**: Hold investments that are tax-efficient, such as index funds or ETFs. Consider tax-loss harvesting strategies.
     - **401(k) and IRA**: These accounts can hold less tax-efficient investments, like bonds or actively managed funds.

### 4. **Diversify Investments**
   - Ensure a diversified portfolio across all accounts, adjusting based on risk tolerance and investment horizon.

### 5. **Rebalance Regularly**
   - Review and adjust your allocations periodically to maintain your desired investment strategy.

### 6. **Prepare for Withdrawals**
   - Plan for how you’ll withdraw funds in retirement, considering the tax implications of withdrawing from each account type.

### 7. **Consider Professional Advice**
   - Consult a financial advisor for personalized strategies based on your financial situation and goals.

### Summary
Prioritize contributions to tax-advantaged accounts, maintain tax efficiency in taxable accounts, diversify your portfolio, and review your allocations regularly.
