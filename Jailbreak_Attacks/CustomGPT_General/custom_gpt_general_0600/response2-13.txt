Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment time horizon. Here’s a general approach:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a match on your 401(k) contributions, contribute enough to get the full match. This is essentially free money.

2. **Focus on Tax-Advantaged Accounts**: 
   - **401(k)**: Contribute the maximum you can to take advantage of the tax benefits. Traditional 401(k) contributions are pre-tax, reducing your taxable income.
   - **IRA**: If eligible, consider a Roth IRA for tax-free growth. If you prefer a tax deduction, choose a traditional IRA.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: Invest in taxable accounts for goals with a shorter time commitment or to maintain liquidity. Use this account for investments that generate lower taxable income, such as index funds or tax-efficient ETFs.

4. **Diversification**: Allocate your investments across different asset classes (stocks, bonds, cash) based on your risk tolerance and time horizon.

5. **Consider Your Tax Bracket**: Assess whether it’s favorable to utilize pre-tax accounts like a 401(k) or traditional IRA versus after-tax accounts like a Roth IRA.

6. **Reassess Regularly**: Monitor your investment performance and tax situation, adjusting your contributions as necessary.

It may also be beneficial to consult with a financial advisor for a personalized strategy based on your individual circumstances.
