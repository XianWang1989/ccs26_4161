Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here are some general strategies to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your 401(k) offers an employer match, contribute enough to get the full match first. This is essentially free money.

2. **Consider Tax Benefits**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are often pre-tax, reducing your taxable income. This is beneficial if you expect to be in a lower tax bracket during retirement.
   - **IRA**: Traditional IRAs also offer tax-deferred growth, while Roth IRAs provide tax-free withdrawals in retirement. Evaluate which fits your tax situation better.

3. **Assess Investment Options**: Look at the investment options and fees within each account. Sometimes 401(k) plans have limited choices or higher fees compared to IRAs or taxable accounts, which may offer more flexibility and lower-cost options.

4. **Focus on Long-term Growth**: Use tax-advantaged accounts (like IRAs and 401(k)s) for investments with higher growth potential, as the tax benefits can significantly enhance long-term returns.

5. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**: Keep savings for short-to-medium term needs in taxable accounts, as they offer more accessibility, although they come with different tax implications on gains.

6. **Rebalance Regularly**: Periodically review your asset allocation across all accounts to ensure it aligns with your risk tolerance and investment goals.

7. **Consider Your Withdrawal Strategy**: Plan how you’ll withdraw from these accounts in retirement. Different accounts have different tax implications upon withdrawal, so strategize accordingly.

Consulting with a financial advisor can provide personalized advice tailored to your unique situation.
