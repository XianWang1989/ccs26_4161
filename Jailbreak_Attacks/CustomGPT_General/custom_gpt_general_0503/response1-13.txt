Distributing your money effectively across a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and time horizon. Here’s a general approach:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your 401(k) offers an employer match, contribute enough to receive the full match first. This is essentially free money.

2. **Tax-Advantaged Accounts**: Consider prioritizing contributions to tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) and IRA) before taxable accounts. These accounts typically offer tax benefits that can enhance growth over time. The choice between a 401(k) or IRA can depend on factors like your income and access to a Roth option.

3. **Diversify Contributions**:
   - **401(k)**: Focus on maximizing contributions, especially if there’s a match. If your employer offers a Roth option, decide if that aligns with your tax strategy.
   - **IRA**: If eligible, consider a Roth IRA for tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement. If you expect to be in a lower tax bracket in retirement, traditional IRAs might be more beneficial.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for short-term goals or to invest in assets that generate qualified dividends or long-term capital gains, which are taxed at a lower rate.

4. **Plan for Withdrawals**: Think about how you will withdraw these funds in retirement. Taxable accounts can provide more flexibility, while traditional IRA and 401(k) withdrawals will be taxed at your ordinary income rate.

5. **Rebalance Regularly**: Monitor your asset allocation across all accounts and rebalance as necessary to maintain your desired investment strategy.

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: A professional can help tailor a distribution strategy specific to your situation, goals, and tax implications.

By considering these factors, you can create a balanced and tax-efficient distribution strategy.
