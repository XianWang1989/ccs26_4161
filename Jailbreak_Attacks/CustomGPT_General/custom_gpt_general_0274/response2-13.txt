The best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts generally depends on several factors, including your current financial situation, tax bracket, retirement goals, and investment strategy. Here are some guidelines to consider:

1. **401(k):**
   - **Employer Match:** Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match if available. This is essentially free money.
   - **Pre-Tax vs. Roth:** If your plan offers a Roth option, consider your current tax rate versus expected retirement tax rate. Pre-tax contributions reduce your taxable income now, while Roth contributions may offer tax-free withdrawals later.

2. **IRA (Traditional vs. Roth):**
   - **Tax Benefits:** Irregular contributions can be made, but try to maximize if within income limits. Traditional IRAs may provide tax deductions, while Roth offers tax-free growth.
   - **Income Limits:** Be aware of income limitations on Roth contributions and deductibility for traditional IRAs.

3. **Taxable Accounts:**
   - **Flexibility:** These accounts offer the most flexibility for withdrawals and investment choices, but they come with tax implications for capital gains and dividends.
   - **Long-Term Investments:** Consider using these accounts for investments that you plan to hold long-term to take advantage of lower capital gains tax rates.

### Suggested Distribution Strategy:
- **Maximize Employer Match:** Contribute at least enough to your 401(k) to match any employer contributions.
- **IRA Contributions:** Once you’re contributing enough to your 401(k), consider maxing out your IRA contributions, choosing between a Traditional or Roth based on your tax situation.
- **Taxable Accounts:** Use these for any excess savings, especially if you’re looking for more liquidity or have already contributed the max to your retirement accounts.

### General Tips:
- Review your investment allocation in each account; aim for diversification.
- Rebalance periodically to maintain your target allocation.
- Consult with a financial advisor to personalize your strategy based on your specific goals and situation.

This strategy helps you save for retirement while optimizing for tax efficiency and liquidity.
