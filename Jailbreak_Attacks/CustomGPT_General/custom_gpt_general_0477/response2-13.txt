Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and time horizon. Here’s a general strategy:

1. **Max out employer-sponsored 401(k)**: If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to get the full match first. This is essentially free money.

2. **Contribute to an IRA**: If eligible, consider contributing to a Roth IRA for tax-free growth or a Traditional IRA for tax-deferred growth. The choice here depends on your current tax rate versus your expected tax rate in retirement.

3. **401(k) contributions**: Continue contributing to your 401(k) until you reach the maximum contribution limit, especially if you’re in a high-income bracket. This offers tax deferral and often additional employer contributions.

4. **Taxable accounts**: With any additional savings, consider taxable accounts. These give you more flexibility for withdrawals without penalties, and you can take advantage of lower capital gains tax rates.

5. **Diversification**: Ensure diversification across all accounts, investing in a mix of stocks, bonds, and other assets.

6. **Emergency fund**: Before allocating all funds into these accounts, ensure you have an adequate emergency fund (usually 3-6 months of expenses).

7. **Consult a financial advisor**: Consider speaking with a financial advisor for personalized strategies that cater to your specific situation and goals.

Regularly review and adjust your allocations as your financial situation and goals evolve.
