Distributing your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some key considerations for each account type:

### 1. **401(k)**
   - **Contribution Limits**: Take advantage of employer matches if available. This is essentially "free money."
   - **Tax Benefits**: Contributions are made pre-tax, reducing your taxable income for the year. Consider maximizing your contributions, especially if you're in a higher tax bracket.
   - **Investment Options**: Assess the investment options available in your 401(k); they can sometimes be limited compared to IRAs.

### 2. **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**
   - **Tax Treatment**: Traditional IRAs may offer immediate tax benefits but tax you on withdrawals. Roth IRAs allow tax-free withdrawals in retirement.
   - **Diversification**: Use IRAs to invest in a broader range of assets, including stocks, bonds, ETFs, and mutual funds.
   - **Contribution Limits**: Keep in mind the annual contribution limits, which might be lower than those for a 401(k).

### 3. **Taxable Accounts**
   - **Liquidity Needs**: Taxable accounts can provide quicker access to funds without penalties, useful for short-term goals.
   - **Tax Considerations**: Be mindful of capital gains tax on investments; holding assets long-term can be beneficial for tax efficiency.
   - **Investment Flexibility**: You can invest in a wider array of investment options without contribution limits.

### **General Strategies**
- **Priorities**: Start with contributions to your 401(k) up to the employer match, then max out your IRA contributions if eligible. After these, consider increasing your taxable account contributions.
- **Tax Strategy**: Balance tax-deferred accounts (401(k), Traditional IRA) with tax-free accounts (Roth IRA) and taxable accounts to optimize your overall tax strategy.
- **Rebalancing**: Regularly review and rebalance your portfolio across these accounts based on your investment goals and changes in your life circumstances.

### **Consult a Financial Advisor**
Consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your specific situation, investment goals, and risk tolerance. They can provide insights into how to best allocate your money across these accounts based on your overall financial picture.
