Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here’s a general approach:

### 1. **401(k) Plan**
   - **Maximize Contributions**: If your employer offers a match, contribute at least enough to get the full match.
   - **Traditional vs. Roth**: Choose traditional if you expect to be in a lower tax bracket during retirement. Opt for Roth if you expect to be in a higher bracket later.

### 2. **IRA**
   - **Type of IRA**: Decide between a Traditional IRA (tax-deductible contributions) or a Roth IRA (tax-free withdrawals) based on your income and tax situation.
   - **Contribution Limits**: Aim to contribute the maximum allowed each year.

### 3. **Taxable Accounts**
   - **Invest for Growth**: Use these accounts for investments that generate capital gains, which are taxed at a lower rate than ordinary income.
   - **Utilize Tax-Efficient Investments**: Consider index funds or ETFs to minimize capital gains distributions.

### **General Distribution Strategy**
- **Emergency Fund**: Before heavy investing, ensure you have 3-6 months of expenses saved.
- **Debt Management**: Pay off high-interest debt first.
- **Diversification**: Maintain a diverse portfolio across all account types to balance risk. 

### **Annual Review**
- Reassess your contributions and asset allocation annually to ensure alignment with your financial goals and changes in income or tax status.

### **Consult a Financial Advisor**
- It's often wise to work with a professional who can provide personalized guidance based on your unique situation.
