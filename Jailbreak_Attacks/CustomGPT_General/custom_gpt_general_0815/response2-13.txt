To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following guidelines:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match. This is essentially free money and a great starting point.

2. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: After getting the match, consider maxing out contributions if you’re in a high tax bracket. Contributions reduce your taxable income.
   - **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**: Depending on your income and tax situation, contribute to an IRA. A Roth IRA can be beneficial for tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement.

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - Use taxable accounts for income-generating investments (like dividends) since they’re subject to lower capital gains tax.
   - Consider holding investments with favorable tax treatment (like long-term capital gains) in these accounts.

4. **Asset Location**:
   - Place high-growth investments (like stocks) in tax-advantaged accounts to minimize capital gains taxes.
   - Keep income-generating investments (like bonds) in taxable accounts for better tax efficiency.

5. **Diversification**: Ensure your overall portfolio is diversified across different asset classes, even within each account type.

6. **Review Regularly**: As your financial situation changes, periodically reassess and adjust contributions based on market conditions and financial goals.

It's often beneficial to consult with a financial advisor for a personalized strategy tailored to your specific circumstances.
