To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following guidelines:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k):**
   - Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match, as this is essentially free money.

2. **Contribute to IRAs:**
   - If eligible, contribute to a traditional or Roth IRA. The tax advantages can significantly enhance your savings. Consider a Roth IRA for tax-free withdrawals in retirement.

3. **Fill the 401(k) Contribution Limit:**
   - After securing the match, prioritize maxing out your 401(k) contributions, especially if you’re in a high tax bracket. This reduces your taxable income.

4. **Invest in Taxable Accounts:**
   - Use taxable accounts for investments that may have lower tax implications (like index funds or ETFs). This is a good option for funds you may need before retirement age.

5. **Consider Your Tax Situation:**
   - Allocate more to pre-tax accounts (401(k), traditional IRA) if you expect to be in a lower tax bracket in retirement. Conversely, prioritize Roth accounts if you anticipate a higher tax bracket.

6. **Diversify Investments:**
   - Within each account type, aim for a diversified portfolio that aligns with your risk tolerance and investment horizon.

7. **Rebalance Regularly:**
   - Monitor your investments and rebalance as necessary to maintain your desired asset allocation.

8. **Consult a Financial Advisor:**
   - For personalized strategies tailored to your specific financial situation, consider consulting a professional.

By following these guidelines, you can create a balanced approach that leverages the unique benefits of each account type while considering your overall financial goals.
