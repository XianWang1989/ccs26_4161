Distributing money across your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment time horizon. Here’s a general approach:

1. **401(k)**: 
   - **Maximize Contributions**: Contribute enough to get any employer match first, as this is essentially free money.
   - **Investment Choices**: Choose low-cost index funds or target-date funds.

2. **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**:
   - **Consider Tax Implications**: If you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement, a Roth IRA might be advantageous. With a Traditional IRA, contributions may be tax-deductible now, but you’ll pay taxes upon withdrawal.
   - **Max Contributions**: Aim to contribute the maximum allowed ($6,500 for individuals under 50, $7,500 for those 50 and older in 2023).

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - **Flexibility**: Use these for short-term goals or if you need accessible funds without penalties.
   - **Tax-Efficient Investments**: Consider tax-efficient funds, like index funds or ETFs, to minimize capital gains taxes.

### Sample Distribution Strategy:
- **401(k)**: Contribute up to the employer match.
- **IRA**: Contribute the max to Roth/Traditional based on your tax profile.
- **Taxable Accounts**: Invest any remaining funds, focusing on longer-term growth.

### Monitoring:
- Regularly review and adjust your portfolios according to market conditions and personal financial changes.

### Consult a Financial Advisor:
- For personalized advice, especially considering tax implications and future goals, consulting a financial advisor could be beneficial.
