To effectively distribute your money amongst a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Maximize Employer Contributions**: If your employer offers a 401(k) match, contribute enough to receive the full match first. This is essentially free money.

2. **Focus on Tax Advantages**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are pre-tax, which reduces your taxable income now. Consider contributing enough to maximize your benefits.
   - **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**: If eligible, consider a Roth IRA for tax-free withdrawals in retirement. For a Traditional IRA, contributions may be tax-deductible depending on your income and whether you have a 401(k).

3. **Separate Your Goals**:
   - **Short-term needs**: Use taxable accounts for savings you may need access to before retirement.
   - **Long-term growth**: Utilize your 401(k) and IRA for retirement savings, taking advantage of compound growth and tax benefits.

4. **Diversification**: Spread investments across various asset classes (stocks, bonds, ETFs) within all accounts to mitigate risk.

5. **Consider Your Tax Bracket**: If you're in a higher tax bracket now, lean towards the tax-deferred accounts (401(k), Traditional IRA). If you anticipate being in a lower tax bracket in retirement, consider more contributions to Roth accounts.

6. **Rebalancing**: Regularly review and adjust your investments based on performance and changes in your financial situation.

7. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: Personalized advice can help tailor your strategy based on your unique situation, risk tolerance, and financial goals.

This balanced approach incorporates immediate tax savings while preparing for future financial stability.
