Distributing your money across a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, current situation, and tax considerations. Here’s a practical approach:

### 1. **Assess Your Financial Goals**
   - **Retirement Needs**: Determine how much you need for retirement and at what age you plan to retire.
   - **Short-Term Goals**: Identify any upcoming expenses (e.g., buying a home, education).

### 2. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**
   - **401(k)**:
     - **Match Contributions**: Contribute enough to get the full employer match (if available), as this is essentially free money.
     - **Annual Limit**: For 2023, max out contributions ($22,500; $30,000 if age 50+). 

   - **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**:
     - **Choose Based on Income**: If you fall under the income limits, consider a Roth IRA for tax-free withdrawals in retirement. If you're above those limits, a Traditional IRA could be beneficial for tax deductions.
     - **Annual Limit**: Contribute up to $6,500 ($7,500 if age 50+).

### 3. **Invest in Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**
   - Once you maximize contributions to retirement accounts, direct additional savings to taxable accounts.
   - **Flexibility**: Taxable accounts give you more liquidity for emergencies and short-term goals.
   - **Tax Efficiency**: Consider tax-efficient investment strategies (like index funds or municipal bonds) to minimize capital gains taxes.

### 4. **Maintain a Diverse Investment Portfolio**
   - **Diversification**: Spread investments across various asset classes (stocks, bonds, real estate).
   - **Adjust Based on Risk Tolerance**: Your asset allocation should reflect your risk tolerance and investment horizon.

### 5. **Review and Adjust Regularly**
   - **Annual Review**: Revisit your allocations and adjust based on performance, changing financial goals, and market conditions.

### Example Allocation Strategy
- **If just starting out**:
  - 401(k): Max employer match
  - IRA: Max annual limit
  - Taxable: Any excess savings after above contributions

- **As you accumulate savings**:
  - 401(k): Continue max contributions
  - IRA: Fully utilize annual limits
  - Taxable: Invest any additional savings.

### Conclusion
Prioritize tax-advantaged accounts first, followed by taxable accounts for liquidity and flexibility. Tailor your approach based on your evolving financial situation and goals. Consider consulting with a financial advisor for personalized guidance!
