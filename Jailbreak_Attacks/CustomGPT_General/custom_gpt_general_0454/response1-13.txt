Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors including your current financial situation, retirement goals, and tax considerations. Here are some strategies to consider:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**
   - **401(k)**: If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to get the full match. This is essentially free money. 
   - **IRA**: If you’re eligible, consider contributing to a traditional or Roth IRA next. Traditional IRAs offer tax-deductible contributions whereas Roth IRAs offer tax-free withdrawals in retirement.

### 2. **Consider Contribution Limits**
   - Monitor the annual contribution limits for each account type. For 2024, the contribution limits are generally $22,500 for a 401(k) and $6,500 for IRAs (with a catch-up contribution option for those aged 50 and over).

### 3. **Diversification Within Accounts**
   - Maintain a diversified portfolio within each account type based on your risk tolerance. Consider a mix of stocks, bonds, and other assets. Generally, you might want higher-risk investments in your taxable accounts (since you can more easily take gains without penalties) and more conservative investments in retirement accounts.

### 4. **Tax Considerations**
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Invest in tax-efficient funds (like index funds or ETFs) to minimize capital gains taxes. Use these accounts for assets you'll need access to before retirement.
   - **401(k) and IRA**: Focus on higher-return investments in these accounts, as taxes are deferred until withdrawal.

### 5. **Withdrawal Strategy Planning**
   - As you approach retirement, consider how you will withdraw funds based on tax implications. Typically, it can be beneficial to withdraw from taxable accounts first to allow tax-advantaged accounts to grow longer.

### 6. **Monitor and Adjust**
   - Regularly review and adjust your contribution strategy based on changes in income, expenses, and market conditions.

### Conclusion
A balanced approach that maximizes employer matches, tax-advantaged growth, and diversification while considering your personal financial situation is key. If you’re uncertain, consulting with a financial advisor can help tailor a plan to your specific needs and goals.
