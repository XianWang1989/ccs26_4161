Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various factors, including your retirement goals, tax situation, and investment preferences. Here’s a structured approach:

### 1. **Understand Contribution Limits and Benefits**
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are pre-tax (or after-tax for Roth 401(k)), with a limit of $22,500 for 2023 (or $30,000 if age 50+). Employer matching can significantly boost your savings.
   - **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**: The limit is $6,500 for 2023 (or $7,500 if age 50+). Roth IRAs provide tax-free withdrawals in retirement, while Traditional IRAs offer a tax deduction now.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: No contribution limits, but you pay taxes on dividends, interest, and capital gains.

### 2. **Prioritize Retirement Accounts**
   - **Maximize Employer Match**: Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to receive any employer match; it’s essentially free money.
   - **Consider Traditional vs. Roth Accounts**: Choose between Traditional and Roth IRAs based on your current and expected future tax rates. If you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement, a Roth IRA might be preferable.

### 3. **Create a Balanced Allocation Strategy**
   - **401(k)**: Focus on growth-oriented investments (stocks, ETFs) but consider your risk tolerance and time horizon.
   - **IRA**: Similar to your 401(k), with the option to invest in a broader range of assets. You might include bonds for more stability.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Focus on tax-efficient investments, such as index funds or tax-managed funds. Avoid frequent trading to minimize capital gains taxes.

### 4. **Adjust Based on Tax Implications**
   - **Withdrawals**: Plan your withdrawals strategically in retirement to minimize taxes. Generally, it’s wise to withdraw from taxable accounts first, followed by tax-deferred accounts, and lastly, tax-free accounts.
   - **Tax Location**: Place higher-growth assets in tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) and IRA) and keep more stable, income-generating assets in taxable accounts.

### 5. **Rebalance Regularly**
   - Monitor and adjust your allocations periodically based on performance, changes in the market, and your risk tolerance.

### 6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**
   - For personalized advice considering your entire financial situation, consulting a certified financial planner can be very beneficial.

By carefully considering these factors, you can create a balanced and effective investment strategy that aligns with your financial goals.
