Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts requires a strategic approach based on your financial goals, tax situation, and time horizon. Here are some key considerations for optimizing your distributions:

### 1. **Start with Employer Match**
   - **401(k)**: If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to get the full match first. This is essentially free money.

### 2. **Consider Tax Implications**
   - **Tax-Deferred Accounts (401(k) and IRA)**: Contributions reduce your taxable income now, but withdrawals in retirement are taxed as ordinary income. Prioritize maxing out these accounts in early stages, especially if you're in a higher tax bracket.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Investments here are subject to capital gains tax. Use these accounts for investments that may generate income or high turnover which can trigger taxes.

### 3. **Long-Term Growth vs. Short-Term Needs**
   - **401(k) and IRA**: Ideal for long-term growth. Focus on higher-risk, higher-return investments here if you don’t need immediate access to funds.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Best for short to medium-term goals. Keep more liquid and less volatile investments for access without penalties.

### 4. **Adjust with Age and Goals**
   - As you approach retirement, shift towards more conservative investments in your tax-deferred accounts to protect your nest egg.
   - Maintain a growth strategy in your taxable accounts if you have long-term goals.

### 5. **Diversify Investments**
   - Balance your investments across all accounts based on your overall risk tolerance, aligning with your financial goals.

### 6. **Review Regularly**
   - Annually reassess your distribution strategy to adapt to changes in income, expenses, life events, and tax law.

### Suggested Distribution Strategy
Here’s a basic framework to consider:
- **Max out 401(k) contributions** to benefit from company match and tax deferral.
- **Contribute to an IRA** (traditional or Roth, depending on your eligibility and tax situation).
- **Invest additional funds** in taxable accounts for flexibility and after maximizing tax-advantaged options.

### Example Allocation
- **401(k)**: 15-20% of salary up to the company match.
- **IRA**: Dedicate an additional $6,500 (or $7,500 if over 50).
- **Taxable Accounts**: Invest any surplus funds here after maxing tax-advantaged accounts.

### Final Thoughts
It's wise to consult a financial advisor to tailor your strategy based on your specific circumstances. They can provide personalized recommendations to align with your unique financial situation and retirement goals.
