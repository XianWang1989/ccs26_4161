Distributing your money across a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts can help optimize your retirement savings and tax efficiency. Here’s a strategy to consider:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**
   - **401(k):** If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to get the full match first. It’s essentially free money.
   - **IRA:** After maximizing your 401(k) contributions, consider funding a traditional or Roth IRA. If eligible, Roth IRAs can provide tax-free withdrawals in retirement, making them a great choice for younger savers or those expecting to be in a higher tax bracket later.

### 2. **Consider Contribution Limits**
   - Make sure to stay within the annual contribution limits for each account type. For 2023, the limits are:
     - **401(k)**: $22,500 (or $30,000 if you’re over 50)
     - **IRA**: $6,500 (or $7,500 if over 50)

### 3. **Balance Based on Tax Implications**
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Use these for investments that generate lower capital gains (e.g., index funds) or tax-efficient investments (like municipal bonds). This can minimize the tax impact of dividends and interest income.
   - Invest in tax-inefficient assets (like actively managed mutual funds) in tax-advantaged accounts.

### 4. **Diversify Investments**
   - Use different investment strategies across accounts:
     - High-growth investments in taxable accounts for long-term appreciation.
     - More stable, income-generating investments in tax-deferred accounts to avoid immediate taxation.

### 5. **Rebalance Regularly**
   - Review your portfolio at least annually to ensure your asset allocation aligns with your retirement goals and risk tolerance.

### 6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**
   - A financial planner can help tailor your distribution strategy based on your specific financial situation and goals.

### Example Allocation Strategy
- **401(k):** Max out contributions, especially if a company match is available.
- **IRA:** Contribute the maximum annually to an IRA of your choice (traditional or Roth) based on your tax situation.
- **Taxable Account:** Any additional savings can go here, focusing on investments that are tax-efficient.

By following these steps, you’ll be well-positioned to grow your wealth while managing taxes effectively.
