Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts largely depends on your individual financial goals, tax situation, and investment horizon. Here’s a general approach:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match**
   - **401(k)**: If your employer offers a matching contribution, contribute enough to get the maximum match. This is essentially free money and an immediate return on your investment.

### 2. **Understand Contribution Limits**
   - **401(k)**: The contribution limit for 2023 is $22,500 (or $30,000 if age 50 or older).
   - **IRA**: The limit is $6,500 (or $7,500 if age 50 or older).
   - **Taxable Accounts**: There are no contribution limits, but be mindful of capital gains taxes.

### 3. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**
   - After maximizing the 401(k) match, consider maxing out your IRA contributions next. This allows for tax-deferred growth (traditional IRA) or tax-free growth (Roth IRA).
   - Return to your 401(k) after funding the IRA to maximize contributions.

### 4. **Diversification and Asset Allocation**
   - Assess your investment strategy (risk tolerance and time horizon) and diversify your investments accordingly across all accounts. Generally, equities may be allocated in tax-advantaged accounts, while bonds or more conservative investments might be in taxable accounts to manage tax implications.

### 5. **Consider Tax Implications**
   - Taxable accounts can trigger capital gains when selling investments, so be mindful of how and when you withdraw funds.
   - Long-term investments in taxable accounts usually incur lower capital gains tax rates (0%, 15%, or 20% based on your income), while short-term gains are taxed as ordinary income.

### 6. **Review Regularly**
   - Periodically reassess your contributions and asset allocation based on your evolving financial goals, market conditions, and life events.

### Example Distribution Strategy:
- **401(k)**: 10% until the match is maximized.
- **IRA**: 15% after maximizing the match.
- **Taxable**: 5% or more, depending on your overall savings goal.

Always consult with a financial advisor for personalized strategies, as they can consider your specific financial situation and objectives.
