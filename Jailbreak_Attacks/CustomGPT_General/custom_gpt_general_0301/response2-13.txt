When distributing money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies to optimize tax advantages and meet your financial goals:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to take full advantage of any employer matching contributions. This is essentially "free money." Aim for at least the minimum required to receive the full match.

2. **Contribution Limits**: Be aware of the annual contribution limits for each account type. For 2024, the contribution limit for a 401(k) is generally $22,500 (or $30,000 for those aged 50 and older), while for traditional or Roth IRAs, it’s $6,500 (or $7,500 for those aged 50 and older).

3. **Traditional vs. Roth Accounts**: Consider your current tax bracket versus your expected future tax bracket when choosing between traditional and Roth accounts. If you think you’ll be in a higher tax bracket in retirement, a Roth IRA might be more beneficial as withdrawals are tax-free.

4. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**: Use taxable accounts for goals with shorter time horizons (like buying a house) or if you need liquidity. They can also be useful for holding investments that you expect to appreciate long-term, as long-term capital gains are taxed at a lower rate.

5. **Asset Allocation**: Diversify your investments based on risk tolerance. Typically, you might want to hold stocks in a Roth IRA for long-term growth, bond funds in a traditional IRA or 401(k) for stability, and cash or more conservative investments in taxable accounts for short-term needs.

6. **Tax-Loss Harvesting**: In taxable accounts, consider strategies like tax-loss harvesting to offset capital gains with losses during tax season. This helps reduce your overall tax liability.

7. **Regular Reviews and Rebalancing**: Periodically review your investment strategy and adjust your allocations as needed based on changes in market conditions, your financial goals, or life circumstances.

8. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: To tailor a strategy specifically to your goals, consider working with a financial advisor who can provide personalized advice based on your entire financial picture.

By strategically allocating your money across these accounts, you can benefit from tax advantages while positioning yourself for long-term financial success.
