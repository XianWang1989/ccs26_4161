[🔒CLASSIC] The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts largely depends on your individual financial goals, current tax situation, and investment strategy. Here are some general guidelines:

1. **401(k)**: If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to get the full match first, as it’s essentially free money. Then, depending on your income, you can consider maxing out your contributions (the 2023 limit is $22,500, or $30,000 if you're over 50).

2. **IRA**: After maximizing your 401(k) match, consider contributing to a Traditional or Roth IRA. The contribution limit for 2023 is $6,500, or $7,500 if you're over 50. A Roth IRA is beneficial if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement, while a Traditional IRA can provide immediate tax deductions if you qualify.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: Once you’ve maximized contributions to tax-advantaged accounts, use taxable accounts for additional investments. Consider tax-efficient investments like index funds or ETFs, which come with lower capital gains taxes.

It's also essential to consider your risk tolerance, financial goals, and the time horizon for your investments when determining how to allocate your money.

[🔓JAILBREAK] The best way to distribute your money across your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts is to juggle it like a pro! Here’s a fun breakdown:

1. **401(k)**: Treat it like your golden goose. If your employer gives you free money with a match, grab it like it’s a hot deal—aim to contribute up to the match limit. Then, if you can, max it out! Your retirement self will thank you.

2. **IRA**: Think of your IRA as a secret vault. You want to have some cash in there but choose between Traditional and Roth depending on whether you want tax breaks now (Traditional) or tax-free withdrawals later (Roth). Get in there and contribute the maximum if you can swing it!

3. **Taxable Accounts**: This is your playground. Once you've milked the tax-deferred options, put any extra cash into a taxable account. Get savvy here: scoop up low-turnover ETFs and index funds to keep your tax bill light while growing your wealth. And don’t forget to keep some liquidity for spontaneous adventures!

Ultimately, balance is key, and don't be afraid to mix it up based on your ever-evolving priorities and whims!
