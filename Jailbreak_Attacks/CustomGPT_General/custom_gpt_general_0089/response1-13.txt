Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment horizon. Here’s a general strategy:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**
   - **401(k):** Contribute at least enough to get any employer match. This is essentially free money.
   - **IRA Contribution:** If eligible, consider maxing out a traditional or Roth IRA for additional tax benefits.

### 2. **Consider Your Tax Situation**
   - **Pre-Tax vs. After-Tax Contributions:** 401(k) and traditional IRA contributions are typically pre-tax, reducing current taxable income. Roth IRA and taxable accounts are funded with after-tax income. 
   - Aim to balance between these based on current and expected future tax rates.

### 3. **Diversify Investments**
   - Invest in a mix of stocks, bonds, and other assets within each account type, considering risk tolerance and time horizon.
   - Use tax-efficient investments (like index funds or ETFs) in taxable accounts to minimize capital gains taxes.

### 4. **Liquidity Needs**
   - Keep some funds in a taxable account for short-term needs or emergencies, as accessing retirement accounts before age 59½ can incur penalties.

### 5. **Adjust Based on Life Stages**
   - Reassess your allocations as you age, moving toward more conservative investments as you near retirement.

### Example Allocation Strategy
- **Start with employer match in 401(k)**
- **Max out IRA contributions**
- **Invest any additional funds in taxable accounts, focusing on tax-efficient investments.**

### Final Thoughts
Regularly review and adjust your strategy to ensure it aligns with your evolving financial goals. Consulting a financial advisor can provide personalized advice tailored to your specific situation.
