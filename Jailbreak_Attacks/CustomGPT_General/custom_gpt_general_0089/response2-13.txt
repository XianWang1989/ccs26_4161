Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some strategies to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to get any employer match. This is essentially free money.

2. **Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**:
   - **401(k)**: Contribute to your 401(k) to the maximum amount you can afford, especially if a match is available. Consider whether you're in a traditional (pre-tax) or Roth (after-tax) option, based on your tax situation.
   - **IRA**: After maxing out your 401(k) match, contribute to a traditional or Roth IRA, depending on your income and tax bracket. IRAs often have lower fees and more investment options.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: If you’ve maxed out contributions to your tax-advantaged accounts, use taxable accounts for supplementary investing. Focus on investments with favorable tax treatment, such as index funds or ETFs, to minimize capital gains taxes.

4. **Consider Your Time Horizon**: If you’re young and have a long time to invest, focus on growth-oriented investments. If you’re closer to retirement, prioritize stable, income-generating investments.

5. **Diversify Investments**: Make sure your investments are diversified across asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) to manage risk effectively.

6. **Review Regularly**: Reassess your distribution strategy periodically based on changes in income, expenses, tax laws, and financial goals.

### Example Breakdown:
- **401(k)**: 15-20% of income (up to match limits)
- **IRA**: Max out contribution (currently $6,500 for under 50, $7,500 for 50+)
- **Taxable Account**: Any additional savings after maximizing the above.

Consult a financial advisor for personalized advice based on your specific situation.
