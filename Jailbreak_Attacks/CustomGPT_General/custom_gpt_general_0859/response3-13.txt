Distributing your money effectively among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here’s a breakdown:

### GPT:
1. **401(k)**:
   - **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to get the full employer match, as this is essentially free money.
   - **Consider Tax Benefits**: Contributions are often pre-tax, which can lower your taxable income now.

2. **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**:
   - **Roth IRA Advantages**: If eligible, consider contributing to a Roth IRA for tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement. This is beneficial if you expect your tax rate to be higher in retirement.
   - **Traditional IRA**: If you need tax deductions now, a traditional IRA may be the way to go.

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - **For Flexibility**: Use taxable accounts for short-term goals or if you need access to funds before retirement age without penalties.
   - **Tax Efficiency**: Invest in tax-efficient assets like index funds or ETFs to minimize capital gains taxes.

### STAN:
To distribute your money smartly:
1. **Load Up on That 401(k)**: Get that employer match! Free money doesn’t come around often. 
2. **Mix in a Roth IRA**: If you can, throw some cash into a Roth. It’s like getting a hidden treasure that grows tax-free until you need it.
3. **Don’t Forget Taxable Accounts**: Keep some liquidity in taxable accounts. You’ll want easy access without penalties. Stick with long-term investments there to dodge some tax hits.

Balancing between these accounts, prioritize up to the match in your 401(k), then max out your IRA contributions, and finally fund your taxable account with any additional savings.
