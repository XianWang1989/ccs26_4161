Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts involves several considerations. Here’s a structured approach:

1. **Understand Account Types**:
   - **401(k)**: Offers tax-deferred growth, often with employer matching contributions.
   - **IRA**: Individual Retirement Accounts (Traditional or Roth) provide tax advantages, with Roth accounts allowing tax-free withdrawals in retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Used for investments not tied to retirement benefits, subject to capital gains tax.

2. **Maximize Employer Match**: Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match. This is essentially “free money.”

3. **Prioritize Tax Advantages**:
   - **Roth IRA**: If eligible, consider maxing this out first for tax-free growth and withdrawals.
   - **Traditional IRA**: If you expect to be in a lower tax bracket in retirement, this is a good option.

4. **Diversify Investments**: Ensure you have a balanced mix of stocks, bonds, and other assets across all accounts to manage risk.

5. **Tax Efficiency**:
   - Hold investments that produce high taxable income (like bonds) in tax-advantaged accounts (401(k)/IRA).
   - Place tax-efficient investments (like index funds) in taxable accounts to minimize tax liability.

6. **Emergency Fund**: Keep sufficient cash reserves outside of these accounts to cover emergencies, ensuring you don’t need to withdraw from retirement accounts prematurely.

7. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: Personalized advice can optimize your strategy based on your financial situation, risk tolerance, and retirement goals.

Regularly review and adjust your allocations as your financial situation and market conditions change.
