Distributing your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts involves several considerations, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment options. Here’s a breakdown:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match—this is essentially free money.
   - **IRA**: Contribute up to the limit (consider a Roth IRA if eligible for tax-free withdrawals in retirement).

### 2. **Consider Investment Options**
   - **401(k)**: Look for low-cost funds or index funds. Check the fees and investment choices available.
   - **IRA**: Offers broader investment options; consider a mix of stocks, bonds, and ETFs that align with your risk tolerance.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Ideal for more flexible and accessible funds. Use tax-efficient investments, such as index funds or tax-exempt bonds.

### 3. **Understand Tax Implications**
   - **Pre-Tax Contributions (401k, Traditional IRA)**: You'll pay taxes on withdrawals in retirement.
   - **Roth Accounts (Roth IRA)**: Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, and withdrawals are tax-free in retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Interest and short-term capital gains are taxed at ordinary income rates; long-term capital gains have lower rates.

### 4. **Emergency Fund Considerations**
   - Ensure you have an emergency fund before investing heavily in retirement accounts. Typically, 3-6 months' worth of expenses is recommended.

### 5. **Regularly Review and Rebalance**
   - Monitor your investment allocations and adjust as needed based on market performance and changes in your personal situation.

### Sample Allocation Strategy
- **401(k)**: Max out to employer match, then consider contributing 15-20% of your salary if feasible.
- **IRA**: Max out contributions if possible ($6,500 per year, with an additional $1,000 if you're over 50).
- **Taxable Account**: Allocate any additional savings here, focusing on tax-efficient investments.

### Conclusion
The best distribution often depends on individual circumstances. Assess your situation, retirement goals, and risk tolerance, and consider consulting with a financial advisor for personalized guidance.
