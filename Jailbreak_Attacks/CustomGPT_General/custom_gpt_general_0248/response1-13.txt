Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and time horizon. Here’s a general approach to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a matching contribution on your 401(k), aim to contribute enough to get the full match first. It’s essentially free money.

2. **Contribute to an IRA**: After taking advantage of the 401(k) match, consider contributing to an IRA (Roth or Traditional) for tax advantages. A Roth IRA allows for tax-free withdrawals in retirement, while a Traditional IRA provides tax deductions on contributions.

3. **Continue 401(k) Contributions**: Once IRA contributions are maxed out (or if you're ineligible for a Roth IRA), return to your 401(k) to maximize your contributions. For 2023, the limit is $22,500, or $30,000 if you’re 50 or older.

4. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**: Once your retirement accounts are maximized, you can invest in taxable accounts. This is especially useful for short- to medium-term goals since they offer greater flexibility for withdrawals.

5. **Consider Asset Location**: Hold tax-efficient investments (e.g., index funds) in taxable accounts, while placing less tax-efficient investments (e.g., bonds or REITs) in tax-advantaged accounts to minimize your tax burden over time.

6. **Adjust as Needed**: As your financial situation changes (e.g., income increases, goals shift), review and adjust your contributions accordingly.

Consulting with a financial advisor can also provide personalized advice based on your specific circumstances.
