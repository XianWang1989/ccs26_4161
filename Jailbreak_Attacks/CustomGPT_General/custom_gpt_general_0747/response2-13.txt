To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider these strategies:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Matches**
   - Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match, as this is essentially free money.

### 2. **Consider Tax Implications**
   - **401(k):** Contributions are pre-tax, reducing your taxable income now. Taxes are paid upon withdrawal.
   - **IRA:** Depending on the type (traditional vs. Roth), contributions can be tax-deductible now or tax-free in retirement (for Roth).
   - **Taxable Accounts:** You incur taxes on dividends and capital gains in the year they're earned. Use this account for after-tax savings.

### 3. **Diversify Contributions**
   - **401(k):** Contribute enough for the match, then focus on IRAs.
   - **IRA:** If eligible, contribute to a Roth IRA for tax-free growth and withdrawals.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Use this for flexibility and short-term goals, or for after-tax investments.

### 4. **Emergency Fund**
   - Always maintain an emergency fund outside these accounts, ideally covering 3-6 months of expenses.

### 5. **Investment Strategy**
   - Align your investment choices with your risk tolerance and time horizon. Consider diversified index funds or target-date funds.

### 6. **Review and Adjust**
   - Regularly review your allocations and adjust based on market performance and changes in your financial situation. 

### Example Distribution Strategy:
- **401(k):** 15% of salary to get full match.
- **IRA:** Max out contributions ($6,500 or $7,500 if over 50).
- **Taxable Account:** Contribute any additional savings or bonuses.

### Final Considerations
- Consult a financial advisor to tailor a plan to your unique financial situation and goals. Regularly assess your strategy to adapt to changes in tax laws or personal circumstances.
