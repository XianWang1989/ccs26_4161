To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategic approach:

1. **401(k) Contributions**: If your employer offers a matching contribution, prioritize contributing enough to maximize that match. This is essentially “free money” and can significantly enhance your retirement savings.

2. **Tax-Advantaged Accounts (IRA)**: After maximizing your 401(k) match, assess whether to contribute to a Traditional IRA or a Roth IRA. A Traditional IRA allows for tax-deferred growth, and contributions may be tax-deductible, while a Roth IRA offers tax-free withdrawals in retirement. Choose based on your current tax bracket and your expected tax situation in retirement.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: Once you’ve maximized contributions to your tax-advantaged accounts, you can distribute any additional savings into taxable brokerage accounts. This allows for more flexibility in terms of withdrawals and investment choices, but be mindful of capital gains taxes on earnings.

4. **Asset Allocation**: Within each account type, aim for a diversified portfolio that suits your risk tolerance, investment horizon, and financial goals. Generally, you may want to hold more aggressive investments (like stocks) in your tax-advantaged accounts, where growth is tax-deferred, while placing more income-generating assets (like bonds) in taxable accounts to benefit from favorable tax treatment.

5. **Rebalancing**: Regularly review and rebalance your investments to align with your risk tolerance and investment goals, taking into account tax implications for taxable accounts.

In summary, prioritize contributions to employer-sponsored plans for matching, then consider IRAs based on tax implications, and use taxable accounts primarily for flexibility and investment diversity. Always consult with a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your specific financial situation.
