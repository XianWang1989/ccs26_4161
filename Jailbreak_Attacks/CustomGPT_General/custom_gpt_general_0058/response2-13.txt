The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and risk tolerance. Here are some general guidelines to consider:

1. **Maximize 401(k) Contributions**: If your employer offers a matching contribution, aim to contribute enough to get the full match. This is essentially free money and should be a priority.

2. **Contribute to an IRA**: After maximizing any employer match, consider contributing to a traditional or Roth IRA. A traditional IRA may offer tax deductions, while a Roth IRA provides tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement.

3. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**: If you have additional funds after contributing to your retirement accounts, consider investing in taxable accounts. These offer more flexibility for accessing funds without penalties and can be a good option for short-term goals.

4. **Asset Allocation**: Ensure that your investments align with your risk tolerance and time horizon across all accounts. For example, younger investors might lean more towards equities, while those nearing retirement might prefer bonds.

5. **Tax Efficiency**: Place tax-efficient investments (like index funds or tax-free municipal bonds) in taxable accounts to minimize tax impact, while holding tax-inefficient investments (like actively managed funds or REITs) in tax-deferred accounts.

6. **Review Regularly**: Periodically review your asset allocation and make adjustments as needed based on market conditions and changes in your financial situation.

Consulting with a financial advisor can also help tailor a strategy that meets your specific needs and goals.
