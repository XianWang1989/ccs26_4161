Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts involves understanding your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some general guidelines:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a 401(k) match, contribute enough to get the full match first. This is essentially "free money."

2. **Consider Tax Benefits**: 
   - **401(k)**: Contributions reduce your taxable income, and taxes are deferred until withdrawal.
   - **IRA (Traditional)**: Offers similar tax benefits as a 401(k), but typically has lower contribution limits.
   - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are made after-tax, but withdrawals are tax-free in retirement. Consider this for tax diversification.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Suitable for short-term goals or if you’ve maxed out tax-advantaged accounts. Be mindful of capital gains taxes.

3. **Diversify Investments**: Allocate assets according to your risk tolerance, investment horizon, and financial goals, regardless of the account type.

4. **Withdrawal Strategies**: Plan for withdrawals based on your retirement timeline. Generally, it's advisable to withdraw from taxable accounts first to allow tax-advantaged accounts to grow.

5. **Regularly Reassess**: Your financial situation and goals may change over time, so periodically review and adjust your asset allocation.

It's advisable to consult with a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your specific situation.
