When distributing money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Prioritize Contributions**:
   - Max out your 401(k) contributions, especially if your employer offers a match. This is essentially free money.
   - After your 401(k) is maxed out (or if you’re not eligible for a match), focus on contributing to an IRA (Traditional or Roth) based on your income and tax situation.

2. **Understand Tax Implications**:
   - Retirement accounts like a 401(k) and Traditional IRA offer tax-deferred growth, meaning you won’t pay taxes on earnings until withdrawal.
   - Roth IRAs offer tax-free growth since contributions are made after taxes, which can be beneficial if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement.
   - Taxable accounts do not offer tax advantages, but they provide flexibility with withdrawals, which can be useful for mid-term needs.

3. **Diversify Investments**:
   - Use tax-advantaged accounts for investments likely to experience higher growth (like stocks) because the tax benefits can amplify your returns over time.
   - Consider holding more stable investments (like bonds or cash) in your taxable accounts, as they may generate interest or dividends that are taxed at a higher rate.

4. **Monitor and Adjust**:
   - Regularly review your asset allocation and contributions. Life changes like marriage, children, or job changes can impact your financial strategy.
   - Rebalance your portfolio at least annually to maintain your desired investment mix.

5. **Consider Withdrawal Strategies**:
   - Think ahead to retirement and how you'll withdrawal funds. Plan for tax implications at that time, considering which accounts to draw from first.

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**:
   - If you're unsure, consulting a financial advisor can provide personalized advice tailored to your financial situation, goals, and risk tolerance.

By following these steps, you can strategically distribute your funds to maximize growth and minimize taxes, aligning your investment strategy with your long-term financial goals.
