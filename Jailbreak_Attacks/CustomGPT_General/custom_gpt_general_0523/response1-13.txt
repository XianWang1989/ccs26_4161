Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various factors such as your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some general guidelines:

1. **401(k) Accounts**:
   - **Maximize Contributions**: If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to get the full match first, as this is essentially free money.
   - **Consider Tax Benefits**: Traditional 401(k) contributions are pre-tax, reducing your taxable income now, while Roth 401(k) contributions are made with after-tax dollars and can grow tax-free.

2. **IRA Accounts**:
   - **Choose the Right Type**: Decide between a Traditional IRA (tax-deductible contributions, taxable withdrawals) and a Roth IRA (after-tax contributions, tax-free withdrawals). If you anticipate being in a higher tax bracket in retirement, a Roth IRA might be more beneficial.
   - **Max Out Contributions**: Aim to contribute the maximum allowed each year to take advantage of tax benefits and compounding growth.

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - **Flexibility and Access**: Use taxable accounts for investments you may want to access before retirement. They allow for more liquidity but come with capital gains tax on earnings when you sell.
   - **Tax-Efficient Investments**: Consider holding tax-efficient investments like index funds or ETFs in taxable accounts to minimize tax liabilities.

4. **Consider Your Goals**:  
   - For long-term growth (retirement), prioritize tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) and IRA).
   - For shorter-term goals, use taxable accounts for more liquidity.

5. **Diversification**: Ensure your overall portfolio is diversified across various asset classes (stocks, bonds, cash) to balance potential risks and returns.

6. **Review Regularly**: Periodically review your asset allocation and re-balance as necessary based on changes in your financial situation or investment goals.

It is often advisable to consult with a financial advisor to tailor a strategy that best meets your unique circumstances and objectives.
