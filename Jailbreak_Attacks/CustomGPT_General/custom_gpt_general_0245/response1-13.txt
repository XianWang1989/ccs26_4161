Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various factors including your financial goals, current income, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here’s a general approach you can consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Contributions**: If your employer offers a 401(k) match, contribute enough to get the full match. This is essentially free money.

2. **Tax-Advantaged Accounts**: After maximizing the match, consider contributing to an IRA (Traditional or Roth) depending on your income level and tax strategy. Roth IRAs are beneficial for tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement, while Traditional IRAs may provide immediate tax deductions.

3. **Continue Funding 401(k)**: Once you’ve contributed to your IRA, go back to your 401(k). If your 401(k) has good investment options and low fees, it’s a sensible choice for additional contributions.

4. **Taxable Accounts**: Use these accounts for investments that are less tax-efficient, such as stocks or ETFs, which can take advantage of long-term capital gains rates. 

5. **Emergency Fund**: Ensure you have a sufficient emergency fund before heavily investing in these accounts. Typically, 3-6 months' worth of expenses is advisable.

6. **Monitor and Adjust**: Regularly review your allocations and adjust based on market conditions, changes in income, and your retirement goals.

7. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: If you have specific circumstances or are unsure, consider speaking to a financial advisor for personalized advice.
