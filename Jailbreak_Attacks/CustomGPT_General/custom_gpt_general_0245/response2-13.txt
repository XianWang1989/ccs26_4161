Distributing your money effectively among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment strategy. Here are some general guidelines:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**:
   - **401(k)**: If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to get the full match. This is essentially free money.
   - **IRA**: Consider maxing out a Roth IRA or a traditional IRA, depending on your eligibility and tax situation. Roth IRAs are funded with after-tax dollars, allowing for tax-free growth and withdrawals.

2. **Assess Your Tax Situation**:
   - **Tax-Deferred Accounts (401(k) and Traditional IRA)**: These accounts are beneficial for high earners who expect to be in a lower tax bracket during retirement.
   - **Roth Accounts**: Beneficial for those who expect higher income in the future, as withdrawals in retirement are tax-free.

3. **Consider Your Time Horizon**:
   - If you’re younger and have a long time until retirement, you may favor riskier investments in taxable accounts, as you have more time to recover from market downturns.
   - For shorter-term savings goals, use more conservative investments in taxable accounts to preserve capital.

4. **Diversification**:
   - Ensure diversified investments across all accounts. Consider the overall allocation based on risk tolerance rather than limiting diversification to individual accounts.

5. **Investment Strategy**:
   - Some investors prefer to hold bonds in tax-advantaged accounts (to avoid taxes on interest income) while keeping equities in taxable accounts for favorable long-term capital gains tax rates.

6. **Rebalancing**:
   - Regularly review and rebalance your portfolio across all accounts to maintain your desired asset allocation.

It’s always a good idea to consult a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your specific circumstances.
