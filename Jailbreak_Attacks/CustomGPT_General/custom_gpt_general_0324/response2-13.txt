Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment time horizon. Here are some general strategies:

1. **Maximize Employer Match (401(k))**:
   - Contribute enough to your 401(k) to receive any employer match. This is essentially free money.

2. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - After securing the match, consider maxing out an IRA (Traditional or Roth) depending on your income and tax situation. Roth IRAs provide tax-free growth and withdrawals, while Traditional IRAs may offer immediate tax deductions.

3. **Consider Tax Implications**:
   - Taxable accounts can be beneficial for flexibility and liquidity. However, they incur taxes on dividends and capital gains. Hold investments that generate qualified dividends or long-term capital gains for more favorable tax treatment.

4. **Diversify Investments**:
   - Use the 401(k) and IRA for more aggressive investments that benefit from tax deferral. In taxable accounts, consider more stable investments or tax-efficient funds.

5. **Rebalance Regularly**:
   - Periodically check your asset allocation across all accounts to ensure your investments align with your risk tolerance and goals.

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**:
   - Personalized advice can help you navigate specific tax implications and optimize your strategy based on your unique situation.

By following these guidelines, you can create an effective investment strategy tailored to your financial goals.
