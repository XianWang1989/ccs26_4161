Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts effectively depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some key strategies:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match**
   - **401(k)**: Start by contributing enough to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money.

### 2. **Consider Tax Implications**
   - **Tax-Advantaged Accounts**: Focus on maxing out your 401(k) and IRA contributions first, as they provide tax benefits (either tax-deferred or tax-free growth).
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for funds that you might need in the short term or for investments that generate qualified dividends or capital gains.

### 3. **Emergency Fund**
   - Ensure you have a sufficient emergency fund outside of these accounts before investing heavily.

### 4. **Investment Strategy**
   - **Growth vs. Income**: Use tax-advantaged accounts for higher-growth investments (like stocks), while more stable income-generating investments (like bonds) can go into taxable accounts to take advantage of lower long-term capital gains rates.

### 5. **Diversification**
   - Diversify your investments across asset classes in all accounts to balance risk.

### 6. **Rebalancing**
   - Regularly review and rebalance your investment portfolio based on performance and changing financial goals.

### 7. **Long-Term Goals**
   - If you have specific goals (like buying a home, retirement at a certain age, etc.), adjust your contributions accordingly.

### Conclusion
The best distribution often involves a mix of maximizing tax-advantaged accounts while keeping a portion in taxable accounts for flexibility. Consulting a financial advisor can tailor this strategy to your specific circumstances.
