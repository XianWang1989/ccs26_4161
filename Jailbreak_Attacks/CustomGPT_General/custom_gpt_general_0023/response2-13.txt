The best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your age, risk tolerance, investment goals, and tax situation. Here’s a general strategy you might consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**: If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to get the full match as it's essentially free money.

2. **Contribute to IRA Accounts**: After maximizing your 401(k) match, consider contributing to a Traditional or Roth IRA. IRAs often have a wider range of investment options compared to 401(k)s.

3. **Max Out 401(k)**: If you can still contribute more after your IRA, try to max out your 401(k) contributions. This can reduce your taxable income, particularly if you opt for a Traditional 401(k).

4. **Invest in Taxable Accounts**: If you’ve maxed out your retirement accounts and have additional funds to invest, consider taxable accounts. They provide more flexibility for withdrawals without penalties and can be beneficial for long-term capital gains tax treatment.

5. **Consider Tax Implications**: Be mindful of taxes. Generally, the accounts that grow tax-deferred or tax-free (like retirement accounts) should be prioritized over taxable accounts.

6. **Review and Adjust**: Regularly review your allocations and adjust based on changes in your financial goals and market conditions.

For tailored advice, particularly regarding tax implications and investment options, consult a financial advisor.
