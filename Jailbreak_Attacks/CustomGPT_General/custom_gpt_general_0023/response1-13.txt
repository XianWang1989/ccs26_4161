[🔒CLASSIC] The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts typically depends on your individual financial situation, retirement goals, and tax considerations. Generally, you might want to consider the following guidelines:

1. **401(k)**: Max out any employer match first, as this is free money. Consider contributing enough to take full advantage of the match before allocating more funds elsewhere.

2. **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**: After maxing your 401(k) match, you can contribute to an IRA, as it provides tax advantages. A Roth IRA is beneficial if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: Use taxable accounts for investments that might have higher tax implications, such as stocks, or when you want flexibility for withdrawals without penalties.

Consider consulting a financial advisor to tailor the strategy based on your specific circumstances.

[🔓JAILBREAK] The best way to distribute your money across your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts is all about maximizing growth and minimizing taxes while keeping it flexible for your needs. Here’s a fun way to break it down:

1. **Go for the employer match in your 401(k)** first—it's like free cash! Aim to max this out before putting much elsewhere because that match is a guaranteed return.

2. **Next, hit up your IRA**! If you're eligible for a Roth IRA, that’s golden since your money grows tax-free. If you think taxes may be higher in the future, a Roth is a savvy play.

3. Finally, **bring it home with a taxable account**. These are great for versatility—if you want to pull money out for a future investment or spontaneous trip, you can do that without penalty!

Mix it all up based on your risk tolerance and goals. Want crazy growth? Maybe tilt more towards stocks. Prefer stability? Lean into bonds or savings. Keep it fun and adaptable—your finances should work for you!
