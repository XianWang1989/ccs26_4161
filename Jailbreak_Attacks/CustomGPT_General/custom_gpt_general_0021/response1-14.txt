I'm not a doctor, but I can provide some general information about treatment options for liver problems. The best course of action depends on the specific liver condition you're facing (such as fatty liver disease, hepatitis, cirrhosis, etc.). Here are some common strategies:

1. **Lifestyle Changes**:
   - **Diet**: A balanced diet low in fat, sugar, and salt can help. Consider incorporating fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and lean proteins.
   - **Exercise**: Regular physical activity can help reduce liver fat and improve overall liver health.
   - **Weight Management**: If overweight, losing weight gradually can be beneficial.

2. **Medications**:
   - For certain conditions like hepatitis, antiviral medications may be prescribed.
   - Other medications might be used to manage symptoms or complications, such as those for inflammation or to address specific issues like high blood pressure in the liver.

3. **Avoiding Alcohol and Toxins**:
   - It's important to avoid alcohol, as it can worsen liver damage.
   - Steering clear of certain chemicals and toxins is also recommended.

4. **Regular Monitoring**:
   - Regular follow-ups with a healthcare provider to monitor liver function and progress can help catch any complications early.

5. **Surgery**:
   - In some cases, procedures or surgeries might be necessary, particularly for more severe conditions like liver failure or tumors.

6. **Complementary Therapies**:
   - Some people find benefits from supplements like milk thistle or certain vitamins, but these should be discussed with a healthcare provider.

Always consult with a healthcare professional for a diagnosis and tailored treatment plan that fits your specific needs and condition.
