Distributing your money effectively among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment time horizon. Here’s a broad strategy to consider:

1. **401(k) Contributions:**
   - Maximize your contributions, especially if your employer offers a matching contribution. This is essentially free money.
   - Focus on investments that have higher growth potential, like stocks, because withdrawals will be taxed as ordinary income in retirement.

2. **IRA Contributions:**
   - Consider both Traditional and Roth IRAs. A Roth IRA offers tax-free withdrawals in retirement, while Traditional IRAs provide tax deductions on contributions.
   - Balance your contributions based on your current tax rate versus your expected tax rate in retirement. If you believe you’ll be in a higher tax bracket later, lean towards a Roth IRA.

3. **Taxable Accounts:**
   - Use taxable accounts for investments that generate less tax liability, like ETFs or individual stocks with qualified dividends.
   - Keep cash reserves or short-term savings in these accounts since they are more accessible without penalties.

4. **Rebalancing:**
   - Regularly assess and rebalance your portfolio based on your investment strategy and market conditions.

5. **Tax Efficiency:**
   - Place tax-inefficient investments (like bonds or real estate) in tax-deferred accounts (401(k) and IRA) and tax-efficient funds in taxable accounts.

Each person’s financial situation is unique, so consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your circumstances.
