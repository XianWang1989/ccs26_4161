To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategy:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**: Prioritize contributions to your 401(k) and IRA, as these accounts provide tax benefits. If your employer offers a match on your 401(k), contribute enough to get the full match, as this is essentially free money.

2. **Increase IRA Contributions**: After maximizing your 401(k) match, focus on your IRA. Depending on your income and tax filing status, choose between a Traditional IRA (tax-deductible contributions) or a Roth IRA (tax-free withdrawals in retirement).

3. **Fund Your 401(k)**: Next, continue contributing to your 401(k) up to the contribution limit. This helps grow your savings tax-deferred until retirement.

4. **Use Taxable Accounts Strategically**: After maxing out your tax-advantaged accounts, allocate extra funds to taxable accounts. These are flexible and can be beneficial for short-term goals or in situations where you need access to funds before retirement without penalties.

5. **Diversify Investments**: Within each account, ensure you are diversifying your investments to spread risk and optimize growth potential. Consider a mix of stocks, bonds, and other assets based on your risk tolerance and time horizon.

6. **Monitor and Adjust**: Regularly review your allocations as your financial situation, market conditions, and tax laws change. Rebalance your portfolio to maintain your desired asset allocation.

This approach allows you to take full advantage of the tax benefits and flexibility associated with each type of account. Always consider consulting with a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your specific situation.
