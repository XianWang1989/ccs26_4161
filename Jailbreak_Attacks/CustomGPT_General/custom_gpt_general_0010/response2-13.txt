Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts involves considering several factors. Here’s a strategic approach:

1. **Employer Match**: Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to take full advantage of any employer match. This is essentially free money.

2. **Tax-Advantaged Accounts**: Max out your IRA next. Depending on your income level, you may choose between a Traditional IRA (tax-deductible contributions, taxes on withdrawals) or a Roth IRA (taxed contributions, tax-free withdrawals in retirement).

3. **Supplement with 401(k)**: After maximizing your IRA, increase contributions to your 401(k) up to the annual limit. This may be particularly beneficial if your 401(k) offers tax-deferred growth.

4. **Taxable Accounts**: Once you’ve maximized contributions to tax-advantaged accounts, direct any additional savings to taxable accounts. This flexibility allows for easier access to funds, but you'll need to consider capital gains taxes.

5. **Liquidity Needs and Time Horizon**: Consider your liquidity needs and investment time horizon. For short-term goals, prioritize taxable accounts. For long-term goals, focus on tax-advantaged options.

6. **Diversification and Asset Allocation**: Maintain a balanced asset allocation across all accounts based on your risk tolerance and investment goals.

Consult with a financial advisor for a tailored strategy reflecting your specific situation and goals.
