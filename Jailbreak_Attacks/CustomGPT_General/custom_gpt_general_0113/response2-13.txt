Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment strategy. Here are some key considerations for each account type:

1. **401(k)**:
   - **Employer Match**: If your employer offers a match, contribute at least enough to get the full match first. This is essentially free money.
   - **Tax-Deferred Growth**: Contributions reduce your taxable income and grow tax-deferred. Consider maxing out your contributions if possible, especially if you are in your peak earning years.

2. **IRA (Traditional/Roth)**:
   - **Tax Benefits**: Traditional IRAs offer tax-deferred growth, while Roth IRAs provide tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement. Depending on your current tax bracket and expected future bracket, choose based on your needs.
   - **Contribution Limits**: Be aware of annual contribution limits; prioritize your IRA contributions after maximizing your 401(k) match if you're eligible.

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - **Flexibility**: These accounts provide flexibility for withdrawals and investments. Use them for short-term goals or emergency funds.
   - **Capital Gains Tax**: Investment returns are subject to capital gains tax, so consider holding investments long-term to take advantage of lower rates.

### Recommended Distribution Strategy:
- **Prioritize 401(k) contributions up to the match**: This maximizes employer contributions.
- **Next, fund a Roth IRA if eligible**, for tax-free growth and flexibility.
- **After utilizing tax-advantaged accounts**, allocate any additional funds to a taxable account, considering tax implications.

### General Allocation Tips:
- **Risk Tolerance**: Your distribution should align with your risk tolerance; generally, higher-risk investments can be placed in tax-advantaged accounts to maximize tax benefits.
- **Diversification**: Ensure a diversified portfolio across all accounts to spread risk.
- **Regular Review**: Reassess your allocation annually to account for changes in income, tax laws, and personal circumstances.

Consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your specific financial situation and goals.
