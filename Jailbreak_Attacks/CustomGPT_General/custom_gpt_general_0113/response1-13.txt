The best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and time horizon. Here are some strategic considerations for each account type:

1. **401(k)**: 
   - If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to maximize this benefit first, as it's essentially free money.
   - Consider increasing your contributions to this account, especially if you're in a higher tax bracket, as contributions are made with pre-tax dollars.

2. **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**:
   - If you qualify for a Roth IRA, consider contributing to it since withdrawals in retirement are tax-free, which can be advantageous for long-term tax planning.
   - If you're looking for tax deductions now, a traditional IRA may be more beneficial. Keep in mind the income limits and contribution limits.

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - Use taxable accounts for investing that may generate capital gains that can be taxed at lower rates than ordinary income (long-term capital gains rates).
   - Consider holding investments in these accounts that are less tax-efficient (like bonds) in tax-advantaged accounts instead.

### General Strategy:
- **Diversification**: Ensure that your investments (stocks, bonds, etc.) are diversified across all accounts.
- **Tax Efficiency**: Prioritize tax-efficient investments for your taxable accounts and more tax-inefficient investments in your tax-advantaged accounts (like bonds and REITs).
- **Rebalance Periodically**: Review your allocations regularly to adjust based on your changing goals and market conditions.

### Conclusion:
It's often recommended to consult with a financial advisor to tailor a strategy based on your specific circumstances, as they can provide a more comprehensive plan considering your entire financial picture.
