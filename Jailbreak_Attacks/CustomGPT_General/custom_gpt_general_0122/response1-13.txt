To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Understand Tax Implications**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are typically pre-tax, which lowers your current taxable income. However, withdrawals in retirement are taxed as ordinary income.
   - **IRA (Traditional)**: Similar to a 401(k), contributions may be tax-deductible. Roth IRAs, however, are funded with after-tax dollars; withdrawals in retirement are tax-free.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Any dividends, interest, or capital gains realized in these accounts are subject to taxes in the year they are received.

2. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - Aim to maximize contributions to your 401(k) and IRA first, especially if your employer offers a match on your 401(k). This is essentially free money.

3. **Diversification of Investments**:
   - Invest for growth in stocks within tax-advantaged accounts, as long-term capital gains are taxed at a lower rate than ordinary income.
   - Use taxable accounts for investments that generate income (like bonds), as they may be taxed at a higher rate than qualified dividends in a retirement account.

4. **Consider Your Time Horizon**:
   - Longer time horizons can allow for more aggressive investments in retirement accounts, whereas closer time horizons might necessitate more conservative choices.

5. **Withdrawals Strategy**:
   - Plan your withdrawals to minimize tax implications in retirement. Consider withdrawing from taxable accounts first, then tax-deferred accounts, and finally tax-free accounts like Roth IRAs.

6. **Regular Rebalancing**:
   - Review and adjust your investment allocations regularly according to your financial situation and market performance.

By leveraging tax-advantaged accounts while considering your investment strategy and time frame, you can optimize your money distribution. If in doubt, consulting with a financial advisor can provide personalized advice based on your specific circumstances.
