Distributing your money between a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts can help optimize your tax situation and investment growth. Here are some general strategies to consider:

1. **Employer Match**: Maximize your contributions to your 401(k) up to the employer match limit first. This "free money" can significantly boost your retirement savings.

2. **401(k) Contributions**: After securing the match, consider contributing the maximum limit to your 401(k) if you have no other pressing financial needs. This account provides tax-deferred growth.

3. **IRA Contributions**: If you're eligible, contribute to a traditional or Roth IRA. A Roth IRA allows for tax-free withdrawals in retirement, while a traditional IRA offers tax-deferred growth.

4. **Taxable Accounts**: Once you've maximized your tax-advantaged options, consider investing additional funds in a taxable account. This is advantageous for liquidity and flexibility, but keep in mind that you’ll pay capital gains tax on profits.

5. **Investment Strategy**: Diversify your investments across all accounts according to your risk tolerance and goals. You might want a more aggressive strategy in taxable accounts and a conservative approach in retirement accounts, depending on your timeline.

6. **Tax Considerations**: Be mindful of the tax implications. Interest income and short-term capital gains are taxed at higher rates in taxable accounts, while qualified dividends and long-term capital gains are taxed more favorably.

7. **Rebalance**: Regularly review and rebalance your portfolio to ensure it aligns with your long-term goals and risk tolerance.

Consider consulting with a financial planner to tailor these strategies to your unique financial situation.
