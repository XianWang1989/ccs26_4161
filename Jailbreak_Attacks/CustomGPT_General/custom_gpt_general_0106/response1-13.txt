While I can provide general advice on financial matters, it's always best to consult with a financial advisor for personalized recommendations. Here's a typical approach to distributing money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts:

1. **401(k) Accounts**:
   - **Employer Match**: Contribute enough to maximize any employer match. This is essentially "free money" and should be a priority.
   - **Contribution Limit**: Take advantage of the tax deferral and contribute up to the annual limit ($19,500 for those under 50, and $26,000 for those 50 and over for 2021 and similar limits adjusted for inflation in subsequent years).

2. **IRA Accounts**:
   - **Types of IRAs**: Depending on your eligibility, consider a Traditional IRA for pre-tax contributions or a Roth IRA for tax-free withdrawals in retirement.
   - **Contribution Limits**: Know the annual limits (typically $6,000 for those under 50, $7,000 for those 50 and over as of 2021).

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - **Flexible Access**: Once you've maxed out tax-advantaged accounts, you can allocate additional funds to a taxable brokerage account for more flexibility and liquidity.
   - **Investment Strategy**: These accounts can hold a mix of stocks, bonds, and other assets. Consider holding investments that generate qualified dividends or long-term capital gains to minimize taxes.

### General Strategy:
- **Maximize Contributions to Tax-Advantaged Accounts**: Ensure you're taking full advantage of 401(k) contributions with an employer match and consider IRA options based on your tax situation.
- **Diversify Investments**: Spread investments across different assets in all accounts to manage risk effectively.
- **Consider Taxes**: Be mindful of the tax implications of your investment choices, especially in taxable accounts, to maximize after-tax returns.

### Important Note:
Consider your financial goals, risk tolerance, and tax situation when distributing assets among these accounts. Regularly review and adjust your allocation as needed based on your life changes. A financial advisor can provide valuable personalized assistance tailored to your specific situation.
