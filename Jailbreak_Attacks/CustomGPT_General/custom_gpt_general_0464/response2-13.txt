When it comes to distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Tax Efficiency**:
   - **401(k)**: Since contributions are typically pre-tax, prioritize contributing enough to get any employer match before allocating funds elsewhere. This is essentially “free money.”
   - **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**: If eligible, consider a Roth IRA for tax-free growth and withdrawals, especially if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement. Traditional IRAs may be better if you need the immediate tax deduction.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for investments that generate lower capital gains or qualify for favorable tax treatment, like qualified dividends.

2. **Contribution Limits**: 
   - Max out contributions to tax-advantaged accounts first if possible. For 2023, the contribution limits are:
     - 401(k): $22,500 (plus $7,500 catch-up if you're over 50).
     - IRA: $6,500 (plus $1,000 catch-up if you're over 50).

3. **Investment Types**:
   - **401(k) and IRA**: Suitable for growth-oriented investments (stocks, mutual funds) since taxes are deferred or exempt.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Consider bonds or funds that yield qualified dividends to maximize tax efficiency.

4. **Rebalancing and Diversification**:
   - Ensure your total investment portfolio is diverse across different asset classes. Periodically review and rebalance to align with your risk tolerance and investment goals.

5. **Emergency Fund**: 
   - Maintain an emergency fund outside of these accounts, typically in a high-yield savings account, to avoid having to dip into retirement accounts or incur penalties.

6. **Long-Term Goals**:
   - Assess your retirement timeline, risk tolerance, and financial goals. This will guide how aggressively you invest in each account type.

Always consider consulting with a financial advisor to tailor a strategy that fits your specific situation and goals.
