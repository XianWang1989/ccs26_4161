The best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment time horizon. Here are some general strategies:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**
   - **401(k)**: If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to receive the full match first; it’s essentially free money.
   - **IRA**: Consider contributing to a Roth IRA if you qualify, as it offers tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement. If you prefer upfront tax deductions, consider a traditional IRA.

### 2. **Assess Contribution Limits**
   - Ensure you understand the contribution limits for each account type:
     - **401(k)**: Generally, you can contribute up to $22,500 (or $30,000 if you're over 50) in 2023.
     - **IRA**: The limit is $6,500 (or $7,500 if over 50) in 2023.

### 3. **Diversification Strategy**
   - Balance your asset allocation across accounts based on risk tolerance and investment goals. Use tax-efficient investments (like index funds or ETFs) in taxable accounts, while holding higher-yield or less tax-efficient investments (like bonds) in tax-advantaged accounts.

### 4. **Consider Your Tax Bracket**
   - Invest in tax-deferred accounts (401(k) and traditional IRA) if you're currently in a higher tax bracket and expect to be in a lower bracket in retirement. Taxable accounts may be better for investments you plan to hold long-term to minimize capital gains tax.

### 5. **Emergency Funds and Short-Term Needs**
   - Ensure you maintain sufficient liquidity in taxable accounts for emergencies and short-term needs, while reserving tax-advantaged accounts for long-term growth.

### 6. **Rebalance Regularly**
   - Review your investment distribution periodically based on changes in your financial situation, goals, and market conditions. 

### 7. **Seek Professional Advice**
   - Consider consulting a financial advisor to create a personalized strategy that aligns with your overall financial plan.

### Example Distribution Strategy
- If your goal is retirement savings and you’re on a solid growth path:
  - Max out your 401(k) contributions to get the employer match first.
  - Fund a Roth IRA if you qualify.
  - Use the remaining funds for a taxable account, prioritizing low-cost index funds or ETFs to minimize tax impact.

By following these strategies, you can optimize your investments for growth while managing tax implications.
