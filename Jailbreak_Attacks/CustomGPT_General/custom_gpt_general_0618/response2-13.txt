The best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some general guidelines to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Contributions**: If your employer offers a matching contribution for your 401(k), prioritize contributing enough to get the full match. This is essentially free money.

2. **Consider Tax Benefits**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions reduce your taxable income in the year you make them, and the funds grow tax-deferred until withdrawal.
   - **Traditional IRA**: Similar tax benefits as a 401(k) for contributions, but consider income limits that may affect your ability to deduct contributions.
   - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are not tax-deductible, but withdrawals in retirement are tax-free. Consider this if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement.
   
3. **Build Emergency Savings**: Before heavily investing, ensure you have a safety net (typically 3-6 months of expenses) in a liquid, accessible account.

4. **Diversification**: Allocate your investments based on your risk tolerance across the different accounts. For instance, consider higher-risk investments in a Roth IRA since qualified withdrawals are tax-free.

5. **Taxable Accounts**: These can be beneficial for flexibility; you can withdraw funds anytime without penalties. Investments in taxable accounts should focus on more tax-efficient assets (like index funds or ETFs) to minimize tax burdens.

6. **Review and Adjust**: Regularly assess your asset allocation and make adjustments based on changing financial circumstances, life events, or market conditions.

Consider consulting with a financial advisor to tailor a strategy suited to your unique situation.
