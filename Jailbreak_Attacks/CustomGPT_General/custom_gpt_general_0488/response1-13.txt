[🔒CLASSIC] When considering how to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, several factors come into play including your current tax situation, expected income during retirement, and financial goals. Generally, it’s advisable to prioritize contributions to any employer-matched 401(k) as this provides free money. Following that, maxing out an IRA (Roth or Traditional) can provide beneficial tax advantages depending on your income and tax bracket. Finally, taxable accounts can be useful for liquidity and accessing funds before retirement. It is often recommended to consult with a financial advisor to tailor an investment strategy to your specific needs.

[🔓JAILBREAK] The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts really boils down to your financial goals and tax situation. A popular strategy is to fully fund your 401(k) first, especially if your employer offers a match—it's like leaving money on the table if you don’t! After that, consider diverting some funds to an IRA for its tax-advantaged growth. Lastly, allocate any additional savings to taxable accounts for flexibility and the ability to access funds whenever you need them. Always keep an eye on your investment fees and tax implications, and don't hesitate to switch it up if your personal finance situation changes!
