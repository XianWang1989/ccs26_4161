When it comes to distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, several factors should be considered, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment horizon. Here are some general strategies:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money. Then aim to max out your IRA contributions if you can (either a Traditional or Roth IRA depending on your eligibility and tax situation).

2. **Consider Tax Implications**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are usually tax-deductible, but withdrawals are taxed as ordinary income.
   - **IRA**: Traditional IRA contributions may also be tax-deductible, and withdrawals are taxed; Roth IRA contributions are made with after-tax dollars, but qualified withdrawals are tax-free.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Investment income (interest, dividends, and capital gains) is subject to taxes in the year it's earned, so these accounts should be used for more tax-efficient investments.

3. **Emergency Fund and Short-Term Goals**: Ensure you have an adequate emergency fund in a savings account or low-risk investment before aggressively investing in retirement accounts.

4. **Asset Allocation**: Once your contributions are allocated, think about how to diversify your investments within each account based on your risk tolerance and investment goals.

5. **Periodic Review and Adjustment**: Regularly review your investment allocation and rebalance as needed based on changes in your goals, market conditions, or tax laws.

It's always advisable to consult with a financial advisor who can provide personalized advice based on your specific circumstances and objectives.
