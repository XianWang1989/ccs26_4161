Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment horizon. Here's a general strategy:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**  
   - Contribute enough to get the full employer match, as it's essentially free money.

### 2. **Contribute to IRA**  
   - If eligible, consider contributing to a Roth IRA for tax-free withdrawals in retirement, or a Traditional IRA for potential tax deductions.

### 3. **Maximize 401(k) Contributions**  
   - After your IRA contributions, increase your 401(k) contributions to the maximum allowable limit.

### 4. **Taxable Accounts**  
   - Use taxable accounts for investments that may grow rapidly (like stocks) since they qualify for lower capital gains taxes if held long-term. Additionally, consider investing in tax-efficient funds or strategies.

### 5. **Diversification and Risk Tolerance**  
   - Ensure your overall asset allocation aligns with your risk tolerance and investment horizon. Diversify across different asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) to mitigate risk.

### 6. **Regular Rebalancing**  
   - Periodically review and adjust your allocations based on performance and changing financial goals.

Consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized guidance tailored to your specific situation.
