Distributing your money amongst a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment horizon. Here are some general guidelines to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match, as this is essentially free money.

2. **Focus on Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: Contribute pre-tax dollars, which reduces your taxable income and allows your investments to grow tax-deferred.
   - **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**: If eligible, consider maxing out an IRA for additional tax advantages. Traditional IRAs provide tax-deductible contributions, while Roth IRAs offer tax-free withdrawals in retirement.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: After maxing out your tax-advantaged accounts, consider funding a taxable account for flexibility. These accounts can provide access to funds without penalties, though they’re subject to capital gains taxes.

4. **Diversification**: Ensure your investments are diversified across all accounts to manage risk effectively.

5. **Rebalance Regularly**: Assess your allocations periodically and rebalance them according to your risk tolerance and market conditions.

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: A professional can offer personalized advice tailored to your specific situation.

By prioritizing tax-advantaged accounts while ensuring long-term flexibility, you can create an effective investment strategy.
