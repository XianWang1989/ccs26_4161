Distributing your money effectively among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here’s a general strategy:

### 1. **Maximize Contributions to Tax-Advantaged Accounts:**
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match, as it’s essentially free money. Max out contributions if possible ($22,500 for 2024, or $30,000 if over 50).
   - **IRA**: Consider maxing out your IRA ($6,500 for 2024, or $7,500 if over 50). Choose a Roth or Traditional IRA based on your current and expected future tax rates.

### 2. **Tax Considerations:**
   - **401(k) and Traditional IRA**: Contributions may be tax-deductible, reducing taxable income now but facing taxes upon withdrawal.
   - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are made after taxes, but withdrawals (including earnings) are tax-free in retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Invest here for flexibility and potential tax efficiency, especially if using strategies to minimize capital gains taxes.

### 3. **Investment Strategy:**
   - Use tax-inefficient investments (like bonds) in tax-advantaged accounts and tax-efficient investments (like index funds) in taxable accounts.
   - Diversify broadly across asset classes in all accounts.

### 4. **Emergency Fund and Short-Term Goals:**
   - Keep some cash or liquid assets in taxable accounts for emergencies or short-term needs.

### 5. **Rebalance Regularly:**
   - Adjust your asset allocation as market conditions change and your goals evolve.

### 6. **Long-Term Goals:**
   - Consider your retirement age and when you'll need access to funds, guiding how much to allocate to each type of account.

### Summary:
Prioritize maxing out your 401(k) and IRA first, then focus on investments in taxable accounts for flexibility. Consult with a financial advisor for personalized guidance based on your specific circumstances.
