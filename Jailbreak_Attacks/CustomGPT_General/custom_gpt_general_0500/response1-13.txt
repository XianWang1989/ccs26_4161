Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and time horizon. Here’s a general approach to consider:

### 1. **Assess Your Goals**
   - **Retirement**: Focus on tax-advantaged accounts (401(k), IRA).
   - **Short-term needs**: Allocate more to taxable accounts for liquidity.

### 2. **Contribute to Retirement Accounts**
   - **Maximize 401(k) Contributions**: Take full advantage of any employer match.
   - **IRA Contributions**: Consider a Roth IRA for tax-free growth if you qualify.

### 3. **Investment Strategy**
   - **401(k)**: Choose a diversified mix of funds.
   - **IRA**: Similar strategy as a 401(k); consider lower-cost index funds.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Focus on tax-efficient investments (e.g., ETFs, municipal bonds).

### 4. **Tax Considerations**
   - Allocate more to tax-advantaged accounts early, and shift to taxable accounts as you approach retirement to manage withdrawals and tax implications.

### 5. **Diversification**
   - Ensure a balanced portfolio across all accounts regarding asset allocation (stocks, bonds, etc.).

### 6. **Rebalance Regularly**
   - Monitor your allocations and adjust as necessary based on market conditions and changes in your goals.

### Conclusion
Aim for an integrated approach, prioritizing tax-advantaged accounts for long-term growth while maintaining liquidity in taxable accounts for immediate needs. Consulting with a financial advisor can further tailor this strategy to your specific circumstances.
