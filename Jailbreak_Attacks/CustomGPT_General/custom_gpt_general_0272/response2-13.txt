Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts effectively depends on several factors, including your overall financial goals, tax situation, and investment horizon. Here’s a general strategy you might consider:

1. **401(k) Contributions**:
   - **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money.
   - **Tax Advantages**: Since contributions are made pre-tax, it’s beneficial to maximize these contributions if you’re in a higher tax bracket.

2. **IRA Contributions**:
   - **Roth vs. Traditional IRA**: If you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement, consider contributing to a Roth IRA for tax-free withdrawals. If you want immediate tax benefits, then a traditional IRA might be better.
   - **Max Out Contributions**: If you can afford it, aim to max out IRA contributions after handling your 401(k) match.

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - **Flexible Access**: Use taxable accounts for money that you may need sooner, as they provide more liquidity without penalties.
   - **Tax-Efficient Investments**: Focus on low-turnover index funds or ETFs to minimize capital gains distributions. Tax-exempt bonds can also be a good choice here.

### Suggested Allocation Strategy:
- **Start with 401(k)**: Contribute up to the match threshold.
- **Next, fund an IRA**: Max out if possible, especially if you qualify for a Roth.
- **Lastly, allocate to Taxable Accounts**: Use this for additional savings or investments, focusing on after-tax strategies.

### Factors to Consider:
- **Time Horizon**: The longer your investment horizon, the more you may want to focus on tax-advantaged accounts.
- **Current vs. Future Tax Rates**: Consider whether your current tax rate is higher or lower than what you expect in retirement.
- **Investment Goals**: Different accounts can serve different purposes, so align your contributions with your specific financial goals.

Consult with a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your individual situation.
