### 1. **401(k) Accounts:**
   - **Employer Match:** If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to get the full match first. It's free money!
   - **Contribution Limits:** Max out your contributions if possible, especially if you're at or near retirement age. For 2023, the limit is $22,500 (or $30,000 if you’re over 50).

### 2. **IRAs (Traditional and Roth):**
   - **Tax Considerations:** If you expect to be in a lower tax bracket in retirement, a Traditional IRA might be more beneficial. If you’re in a lower bracket now and expect to be higher later, consider a Roth IRA.
   - **Contribution Limits:** In 2023, the limit is $6,500 (or $7,500 if you’re over 50). Consider maxing out your IRA after securing any employer match in your 401(k).

### 3. **Taxable Accounts:**
   - **Liquidity:** These accounts provide flexibility for withdrawals without penalties, making them suitable for goals that are closer in time.
   - **Diversification:** You can invest in a broader range of assets, which might not be available in tax-advantaged accounts. Be mindful of tax implications on capital gains and dividends.

### Strategy: 
1. **Prioritize 401(k)** contributions to take advantage of the employer match.
2. **Consider Roth IRA** contributions if eligible after maxing out a 401(k).
3. **Use taxable accounts** for additional savings once you've maxed out the other options.

### Additional Tips:
- **Rebalance Annually:** Ensure your asset allocation reflects your risk tolerance and investment horizon.
- **Taxes:** Pay attention to the tax implications of your investments, especially in taxable accounts.
- **Consult a Financial Advisor:** Personal advice tailored to your situation can be invaluable.

Balancing your investments in this way can help you maximize growth while managing risk effectively.
