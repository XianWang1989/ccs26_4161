When considering how to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, here are a few strategies to keep in mind:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First:**
   - **401(k):** Contribute enough to get any employer match (usually free money). If you're under 50, aim for the annual limit.
   - **IRA:** If you're eligible, consider contributing to a Traditional IRA (tax-deductible) or a Roth IRA (tax-free withdrawals in retirement). The limits are typically lower than for a 401(k), so maximize this after your 401(k) contributions.

2. **Consider Your Tax Situation:**
   - **Tax-Deferred Accounts (401(k), Traditional IRA):** These are beneficial for higher earners, as they lower your current taxable income.
   - **Tax-Free Accounts (Roth IRA):** Great if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in the future. Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, but withdrawals are tax-free in retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Useful for funds you may need before retirement or for investments in assets that generate little to no taxable income (like long-term capital gains).

3. **Asset Location Strategy:**
   - Place high-growth investments (which may be taxed at higher rates) in tax-advantaged accounts. 
   - Hold bonds or other income-generating assets in taxable accounts to take advantage of lower tax rates on qualified dividends or capital gains.

4. **Liquidity Needs:**
   - Consider how soon you’ll need access to the funds. Taxable accounts offer more flexibility for early withdrawals compared to retirement accounts.

5. **Diversification:**
   - Maintain a diversified portfolio across all account types to manage risk effectively.

6. **Revisit Your Strategy:**
   - Regularly reassess your distribution based on changes in your income, tax laws, and retirement goals.

Consulting with a financial advisor can also help tailor a strategy based on your unique situation.
