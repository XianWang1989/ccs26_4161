To optimize your investment strategy across a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following guidelines:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**:
   - **401(k)**: If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to get the full match first. This is essentially "free money." Aim to contribute the maximum allowable limit.
   - **IRA**: Depending on your income, consider funding a Traditional IRA or a Roth IRA. Roth IRAs grow tax-free, making them beneficial if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement.

2. **Asset Allocation**:
   - **401(k)/IRA**: Use these accounts for assets that may incur higher taxes if sold in a taxable account, such as bonds or high-dividend stocks, due to tax deferral.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Place lower-tax assets here, like index funds or ETFs, which tend to have lower turnover and can benefit from lower capital gains taxes.

3. **Consider Investment Goals and Time Horizon**:
   - Younger investors can afford higher-risk investments in their retirement accounts since they have time to recover from market downturns.
   - Diversify within each account, using a mix of stocks, bonds, and other assets appropriate for your risk tolerance.

4. **Regular Rebalancing**:
   - Periodically review your asset allocation to maintain your desired risk level. This involves selling overperforming assets and buying underperforming ones to keep your portfolio balanced.

5. **Tax Considerations**:
   - Be mindful of the tax implications when withdrawing from these accounts. Generally, take distributions from taxable accounts first in retirement to allow tax-advantaged accounts to grow longer.

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**:
   - For personalized advice, especially considering your financial situation, goals, and market conditions, consulting a financial advisor can provide tailored strategies.

By coordinating contributions and investments across these accounts, you can maximize growth potential while managing tax liabilities effectively.
