Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts involves balancing growth potential, tax efficiency, and your overall financial goals. Here are some guidelines to consider:

### 1. **Understand Account Types:**
   - **401(k):** Often has employer matching; contributions reduce taxable income.
   - **IRA (Traditional or Roth):** Offers tax benefits; Roth IRAs provide tax-free growth and withdrawals.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Invested funds are subject to capital gains taxes; good for flexibility and withdrawals anytime without penalties.

### 2. **Prioritize Contributions:**
   - **Maximize 401(k):** If your employer matches contributions, aim to contribute at least enough to get the full match.
   - **Contribute to IRA:** After the 401(k), contribute to an IRA for additional tax-advantaged growth.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Use for investments beyond retirement needs or for short-term goals.

### 3. **Investment Allocation:**
   - **Risk Tolerance:** Assess your comfort level with market volatility to determine asset allocation (stocks, bonds, etc.).
   - **Time Horizon:** Longer time frames can tolerate more risk; adjust allocations as retirement approaches.

### 4. **Tax Efficiency Strategies:**
   - **Hold Bonds and REITs in Tax-Advantaged Accounts:** Since they generate ordinary income, it's beneficial to keep them in tax-deferred accounts like 401(k) or IRA.
   - **Invest in Stocks in Taxable Accounts:** Long-term capital gains from stocks are taxed at a lower rate, which can be advantageous.

### 5. **Regular Review and Adjustment:**
   - Reassess your asset allocation and contributions regularly (at least annually) based on changes in income, tax laws, and personal goals.

### Example Distribution Strategy:
- Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full match.
- Max out your IRA contributions.
- Allocate any remaining funds into taxable accounts based on your investment strategy and liquidity needs.

### 6. **Consult a Financial Advisor:**
   - For personalized advice tailored to your specific financial situation, consider speaking with a financial advisor.

Following these principles can help you effectively manage your retirement and investment accounts while minimizing taxes and maximizing growth potential.
