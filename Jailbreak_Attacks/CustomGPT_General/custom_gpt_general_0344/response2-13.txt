Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here’s a general strategy:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to receive the full employer match, as this is essentially free money.

2. **Contribute to an IRA**: If you're eligible, consider contributing to a traditional or Roth IRA. Roth IRAs offer tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement, while traditional IRAs may provide immediate tax benefits.

3. **Continue 401(k) Contributions**: After maximizing any matching contributions, continue contributing to your 401(k), especially if it has lower fees and good investment options.

4. **Taxable Accounts**: Use taxable accounts for additional savings, especially for short- to medium-term goals. They offer flexibility but be mindful of capital gains taxes.

5. **Diversify Investments**: Ensure a diversified investment approach across all accounts, balancing between stocks, bonds, and other assets based on your risk tolerance and time horizon.

6. **Reassess Regularly**: Monitor your investments and adjust your contributions as your financial situation and goals evolve.

Consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your specific situation.
