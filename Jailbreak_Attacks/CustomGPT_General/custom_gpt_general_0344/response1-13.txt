Distributing your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here’s a general strategy:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First:**
   - **401(k):** Contribute enough to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money. If possible, aim to max out your contributions annually.
   - **IRA:** Once you've contributed to your 401(k), consider funding a traditional or Roth IRA. Roth IRAs offer tax-free growth, while traditional IRAs can provide immediate tax benefits.

### 2. **Assess Your Tax Situation:**
   - If you're in a higher tax bracket now, leaning towards pre-tax accounts (like a traditional 401(k) or IRA) might be beneficial.
   - If you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement, prioritize Roth accounts.

### 3. **Allocate to Taxable Accounts for Flexibility:**
   - Use taxable accounts for investments that generate little taxable income or capital gains, such as index funds or ETFs. This provides flexibility for withdrawals without penalties.
   - Consider using these accounts for short-term goals or emergency funds.

### 4. **Diversification Strategy:**
   - Diversify your investments based on risk tolerance across all accounts. Utilize the benefits of each account type:
     - **Growth investments** in tax-advantaged accounts (where capital gains can grow tax-deferred).
     - **Income-generating** investments in taxable accounts, as they may incur taxes annually.

### 5. **Rebalance Regularly:**
   - Monitor and adjust your allocations as your goals and risk tolerance change.

### 6. **Consider Fees and Investment Options:**
   - Evaluate the fees associated with your 401(k) and the investment options available. If there’s a high fee, it might make sense to prioritize other accounts.

Overall, it's wise to consult a financial advisor to tailor a plan specific to your situation.
