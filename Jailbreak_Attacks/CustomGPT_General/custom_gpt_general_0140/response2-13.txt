Distributing your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors. Here’s a strategic approach:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**:
   - Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to receive any employer match. This is effectively free money.

2. **Contribute to an IRA**:
   - After maximizing your 401(k) match, consider contributing to a traditional or Roth IRA for additional tax advantages. Roth IRAs allow tax-free growth, useful if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement.

3. **Maximize 401(k) Contributions**:
   - If you can, increase your 401(k) contributions up to the annual limit. This can provide tax-deferred growth.

4. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - Once you’ve utilized tax-advantaged accounts, direct any additional savings to taxable accounts. These offer flexibility but are subject to capital gains tax.

5. **Consider Investment Strategy**:
   - Place higher-growth investments (like stocks) in tax-advantaged accounts due to their longer tax deferral. Consider bonds or income-generating assets in taxable accounts to manage taxes more efficiently.

6. **Rebalance Regularly**:
   - Review your allocations periodically to ensure they align with your goals and risk tolerance.

### Additional Tips:
- **Consult a Financial Advisor**: Tailored advice can cater to your unique situation.
- **Consider Your Tax Situation**: Evaluate how withdrawals will be taxed in retirement.

This balanced approach helps optimize tax efficiency while building long-term wealth!
