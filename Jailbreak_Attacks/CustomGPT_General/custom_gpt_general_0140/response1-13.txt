Determining the best way to allocate your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts involves several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment strategy. Here are some key considerations:

### 1. **Tax Efficiency**
   - **401(k) and IRA (Traditional or Roth)**: Contributions may be tax-deferred (Traditional) or after-tax (Roth), affecting your tax liabilities. Maximize contributions to these accounts first for tax advantages.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Capital gains are taxed when you sell investments. Use these for investments that generate qualified dividends or long-term capital gains to minimize taxes.

### 2. **Contribution Limits**
   - **401(k)**: Check annual contribution limits and employer matches. Aim to max out employer match first.
   - **IRA**: Be aware of annual contribution limits. Consider a Roth IRA for tax-free growth if you qualify.

### 3. **Investment Strategy**
   - **Retirement Accounts**: Focus on long-term growth. Consider higher-risk investments, like stocks, due to the time horizon.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Maintain more liquidity. Consider a mix of equities and fixed income based on your risk tolerance.

### 4. **Financial Goals**
   - **Short-term vs. Long-term**: If funds are needed sooner, prioritize taxable accounts for easier access without penalties.
   - **Retirement**: Prioritize accounts with tax benefits for your long-term retirement savings.

### 5. **Withdrawal Strategy**
   - Plan for how and when you will withdraw funds in retirement. Understand tax implications of withdrawals from each account type.

### Example Allocation Strategy
- Max out your employer match in the 401(k).
- Contribute to a Roth IRA up to the limit if eligible.
- Invest additional savings in a taxable account for flexibility.

### Final Thoughts
Consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized guidance based on your financial situation and goals. Diversification across account types can help optimize tax efficiency and investment returns.
