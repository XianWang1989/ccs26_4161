Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here’s a structured approach to consider:

### 1. **Understand the Accounts**
   - **401(k)**: Typically offers employer matching, pre-tax or Roth options, and higher contribution limits. Good for tax-deferred growth.
   - **IRA**: Individual Retirement Account with pre-tax (Traditional) or post-tax (Roth) options. Lower contribution limits than a 401(k).
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Flexibility for withdrawals without penalties but subject to capital gains taxes on earnings.

### 2. **Prioritize Contributions**
   - **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match as it’s essentially “free money.”
   - **Contribute to IRA**: If eligible, consider contributing to a Roth IRA for tax-free growth or a Traditional IRA for potential tax deductions.
   - **Continue 401(k) Contributions**: After maxing the employer match, consider increasing your 401(k) contributions.

### 3. **Consider Tax Implications**
   - **Tax-Deferred Growth**: Use 401(k) and Traditional IRA for tax-deferred growth. This is beneficial if you expect to be in a lower tax bracket in retirement.
   - **Tax-Free Growth**: Utilize Roth IRAs for tax-free withdrawals in retirement, especially if you anticipate being in a higher tax bracket later.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for goals with shorter time horizons since gains are taxed at capital gains rates.

### 4. **Diversification of Investments**
   - Diversify your investments across all accounts to reduce risk, but consider asset location:
     - **Tax-inefficient assets** (like bonds and REITs) are better held in tax-advantaged accounts (401(k), IRA).
     - **Tax-efficient assets** (like index funds) can be held in taxable accounts to minimize tax impact.

### 5. **Regularly Reassess**
   - Review your allocation at least annually and adjust based on changing financial situations, market conditions, and retirement goals.

### Summary
1. Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the employer match.
2. Max out an IRA (preferably a Roth if eligible).
3. Continue contributing to your 401(k).
4. Use taxable accounts for flexibility and liquidity.

Consult with a financial advisor to tailor this approach to your specific situation.
