Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment horizon. Here’s a general approach:

### 1. **Maximize Retirement Accounts First**
   - **401(k):** If your employer offers a match, contribute at least enough to get the full match. This is essentially free money.
   - **IRA (Traditional or Roth):** After maximizing employer match, consider contributing to an IRA. Roth IRAs offer tax-free growth and withdrawals, while Traditional IRAs may provide tax deductions.

### 2. **Evaluate Tax Implications**
   - **Tax-Deferred Accounts:** 401(k) and Traditional IRA contributions reduce your taxable income. However, withdrawals in retirement are taxed as ordinary income.
   - **Roth IRA:** Contributions are made with after-tax income, but withdrawals (including earnings) are tax-free in retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Investments here are subject to capital gains tax when sold. They offer flexibility but are less tax-efficient for long-term growth.

### 3. **Build a Diversified Portfolio**
   - Regardless of the account type, ensure your investments are diversified across asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) based on your risk tolerance and goals.

### 4. **Consider Your Time Horizon**
   - For long-term goals (e.g., retirement), prioritize tax-advantaged accounts.
   - For shorter-term goals (e.g., buying a house), focus on taxable accounts and use them for flexibility.

### 5. **Regularly Review and Adjust**
   - Monitor your investment performance and adjust allocations as needed based on market conditions and changes in your financial situation.

### Recommendations:
- **Emergency Savings:** Ensure you have sufficient emergency savings before heavily investing.
- **Stay Within Contribution Limits:** Be aware of annual contribution limits for 401(k)s and IRAs.
- **Consult a Financial Advisor:** Tailored advice specific to your situation can optimize your strategy.

This balanced approach can help you optimize growth while managing tax implications over time.
