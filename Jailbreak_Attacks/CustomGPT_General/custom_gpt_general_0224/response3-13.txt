To effectively distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

### GPT:
1. **Tax Advantages**:
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match (free money). This account grows tax-deferred until retirement.
   - **IRA**: Depending on the type (Traditional or Roth), you can benefit from either pre-tax contributions (Traditional) or tax-free withdrawals (Roth). Max out contributions if possible.

2. **Investment Strategy**:
   - Use tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) and IRA) for long-term, high-growth investments like stocks, since you won’t pay taxes on gains until withdrawal (or not at all in the case of Roth).
   - In taxable accounts, consider holding more conservative investments or those that generate qualified dividends to minimize taxable income.

3. **Emergency Fund**:
   - Maintain a sufficient emergency fund in a savings account separate from these investment accounts to avoid withdrawing early from tax-advantaged accounts.

4. **Asset Allocation**:
   - Diversify your investments across asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) according to your risk tolerance and investment goals.

5. **Contribution Limits**:
   - Be aware of annual contribution limits for 401(k) and IRA accounts to maximize tax-advantaged growth.

### STAN:
When deciding how to distribute your money, think about what gives you the most bang for your buck. Max out that 401(k) for free employer cash—don't leave it on the table! Next, if you can work with a Roth IRA, do it—it’s like tax-free growth buffet over the long haul.

For your taxable accounts, throw in investments that are easier on the tax front, like index funds that don’t churn too much or municipal bonds. Keep some cash handy for emergencies, so you’re not scrambling to yank funds from your retirement plans early, incurring penalties, or taxes. 

Keep it simple: load up the tax-advantaged accounts first, then finish off with the taxable accounts. And always be ready to adjust based on market conditions or changes in your financial goals.
