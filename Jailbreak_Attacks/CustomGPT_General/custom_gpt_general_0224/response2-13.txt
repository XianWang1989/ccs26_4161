Distributing your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment horizon. Here’s a general strategy to consider:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**  
   - **Contribution**: Contribute enough to get the full matching contribution from your employer, as this is essentially "free money."

### 2. **Contribute to an IRA**  
   - **Type**: Depending on your tax situation, consider contributing to a Roth IRA (tax-free growth) or a traditional IRA (tax-deferred growth).
   - **Limit**: Are you within the income limits for contributions?

### 3. **Fill Up the 401(k)**  
   - After maximizing your IRA, continue contributing to your 401(k) up to the annual limit if you can afford it.

### 4. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**  
   - Use taxable accounts for investments that you may need to access before retirement age, or for assets that generate tax-efficient income.
   - Consider tax-loss harvesting strategies to offset gains.

### 5. **Investment Strategy**  
   - **401(k) and IRA**: Focus on long-term growth investments (stocks, mutual funds).
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Balance risk with more conservative investments, and focus on tax-efficient funds.

### 6. **Rebalancing**  
   - Regularly rebalance your portfolio to maintain your desired asset allocation across all accounts.

### 7. **Seek Professional Advice**  
   - Consider consulting with a financial advisor to tailor a strategy that meets your specific needs and goals.

### Conclusion  
This approach provides a balanced strategy, aiming for growth while also considering tax implications. Adjust contributions based on your income, expenses, and financial goals over time.
