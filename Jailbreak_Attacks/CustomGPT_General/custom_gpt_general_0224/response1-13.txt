Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here’s a structured approach to help you allocate your funds effectively:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**
   - **401(k)**: If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to get the full match. This is effectively free money.
   - **IRA**: Consider maxing out a Traditional or Roth IRA (depending on tax situation) after your 401(k). Roth IRAs offer tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement.

### 2. **Assess Your Tax Situation**
   - **Tax-Deferred Accounts (401(k), Traditional IRA)**: Contributions lower your taxable income now but will be taxed upon withdrawal.
   - **Roth Accounts**: Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, providing tax-free withdrawals in retirement, which can be beneficial if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket later.

### 3. **Fund Taxable Accounts Last**
   - These accounts are subject to capital gains taxes and do not offer the same tax advantages, but they provide flexibility and liquidity.
   - Use taxable accounts for short-term goals or investments that generate income, like bonds or certain ETFs.

### 4. **Diversification and Investment Strategy**
   - Allocate investments based on your risk tolerance and timeline:
     - **Stocks** for long-term growth (suitable for tax-advantaged accounts to minimize tax impact).
     - **Bonds** for stability and income (consider placing these in tax-advantaged accounts).
     - **Dividend-paying stocks or REITs** in taxable accounts for potential tax advantages on qualified dividends.

### 5. **Regular Review and Adjustment**
   - Reassess your allocations periodically based on changing financial goals, market conditions, or tax laws.

### Example Allocation:
- **401(k)**: 15% of income (max to employer match).
- **Traditional IRA**: 10% of income.
- **Roth IRA**: 10% of income (if eligible).
- **Taxable Account**: Remaining funds after maxing tax-advantaged accounts.

### Conclusion
The optimal allocation varies based on personal goals and financial situations. Consider consulting a financial advisor for tailored advice.
