Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts effectively depends on several factors including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment horizon. Here are some strategies to consider:

### 1. **Understand Each Account's Benefits**

- **401(k)**:
  - **Employer Match**: Contribute enough to get the full employer match if available, as this is essentially free money.
  - **Tax Advantages**: Contributions are often made pre-tax, lowering your taxable income.

- **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**:
  - **Tax Benefits**: Traditional IRAs may give you a tax deduction now, while Roth IRAs offer tax-free withdrawals in retirement. Choose based on your current vs. future tax expectations.
  
- **Taxable Accounts**:
  - **Flexibility**: These funds can be accessed anytime without penalties, but they don’t have the same tax advantages. They are best for short-term goals.

### 2. **Assess Your Goals**

- **Retirement**: Prioritize tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) and IRA) for long-term growth.
- **Short-term Needs**: Allocate more funds to taxable accounts if you need flexibility.

### 3. **Consider Tax Implications**

- **Tax-Deferred Growth**: Maximize contributions to 401(k) and IRA for tax-deferred or tax-free growth. This is particularly beneficial if you're in a higher tax bracket now.
- **Taxable Investments**: Favor ETFs or index funds in taxable accounts to minimize capital gains taxes.

### 4. **Create a Balanced Strategy**

- **Contribution Priorities**:
  1. **Maximize 401(k)** (especially if there's an employer match).
  2. **Max out IRA contributions** next (determine if Traditional or Roth suits you better).
  3. **Contribute to a Taxable Account** for additional savings or if you're already at retirement contribution limits.

### 5. **Regularly Reevaluate Your Strategy**

- Adjust your contributions based on changes in income, expenses, and financial goals.
- Monitor investment performance and tax implications yearly.

### 6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**

- A professional can provide personalized advice, helping you navigate complex tax laws and investment strategies tailored to your situation.

### Conclusion

By prioritizing contributions to tax-advantaged accounts and evaluating your financial goals, you can create a distribution strategy that optimizes your investments for growth while minimizing tax liabilities.
