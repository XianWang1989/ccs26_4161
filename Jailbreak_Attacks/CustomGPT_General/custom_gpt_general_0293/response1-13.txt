Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts effectively depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment strategy. Here’s a step-by-step approach:

### 1. **Assess Your Financial Goals**
   - **Retirement**: Focus on maximizing tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) and IRA).
   - **Short-Term Needs**: Prioritize liquidity, often found in taxable accounts.

### 2. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match. This is essentially free money.
   - **IRA**: After the 401(k), consider contributing to a traditional or Roth IRA, depending on your tax situation.

### 3. **Consider Tax Implications**
   - **Traditional Accounts**: Contributions are often tax-deductible, but withdrawals are taxed as ordinary income.
   - **Roth Accounts**: Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, but withdrawals are tax-free in retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Interest and dividends may incur annual taxes. Consider holding tax-efficient investments here.

### 4. **Determine Your Investment Strategy**
   - **Risk Tolerance**: Allocate investments according to your comfort with risk. Consider bonds, stocks, mutual funds, or ETFs based on your time horizon.
   - **Diversification**: Spread your investments across different asset classes in all accounts for risk management.

### 5. **Regularly Review and Rebalance**
   - **Revisit Allocation**: At least annually, review your investment allocation and rebalance as necessary to align with your goals and risk tolerance.

### 6. **Emergency Fund Consideration**
   - Ensure you have a solid emergency fund outside of these accounts to avoid early withdrawals, which can incur penalties.

### Sample Allocation Strategy
- **401(k)**: Maximize employer match + 15% of your salary (if possible).
- **IRA**: Contribute up to the annual limit (currently $6,500 for individuals under 50, $7,500 for those over 50).
- **Taxable Accounts**: Direct leftover funds here, focusing on lower-turnover and tax-efficient investments.

### Conclusion
Tailor your distribution based on personal circumstances and financial goals. Consulting with a financial advisor can provide personalized insights.
