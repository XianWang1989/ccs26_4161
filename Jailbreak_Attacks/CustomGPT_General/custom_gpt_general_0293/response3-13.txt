Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here’s a strategy to consider:

### 1. **Maximize Contributions to Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**
   - **401(k)**: If your employer offers a matching contribution, aim to contribute enough to at least get the full match. This is essentially free money.
   - **IRA**: If you can, contribute to a Traditional or Roth IRA. Roth IRAs are particularly beneficial if you expect your tax rate to be higher in retirement since withdrawals are tax-free.

### 2. **Consider Tax Implications**
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are pre-tax, reducing your current taxable income. However, withdrawals are taxed as ordinary income.
   - **Traditional IRA**: Similar to a 401(k) for contributions.
   - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, but qualified withdrawals are tax-free.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Investments here incur capital gains taxes on profits when sold. Consider holding investments long-term in these accounts to benefit from lower capital gains rates.

### 3. **Diversify Investments Across Accounts**
   - Use different accounts for various investment types:
     - **Retirement Accounts (401(k), IRA)**: Suitable for long-term, growth-oriented investments (stocks, mutual funds).
     - **Taxable Account**: Might be better for more liquid investments or less tax-efficient investments like bonds that produce regular income.

### 4. **Rebalance Regularly**
   - Regularly assess and rebalance your portfolio to ensure alignment with your risk tolerance and investment goals.

### 5. **Emergency Fund and Short-Term Goals**
   - Before heavily investing, ensure you have a sufficient emergency fund (3-6 months of living expenses) in cash or cash equivalents.

### 6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**
   - A certified financial planner can provide personalized advice based on your specific situation.

### Example Allocation Strategy
1. Fund the 401(k) up to the employer match.
2. Contribute to an IRA (maximize if eligible).
3. Invest extra funds in a taxable account while keeping an eye on tax implications.

### Conclusion
Tailoring your contributions based on tax advantages and investment goals can lead to better long-term returns. Always consider your personal circumstances in financial planning.
