Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here’s a general approach to consider:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Contributions**
   - **401(k)**: If your employer matches contributions, prioritize funding your 401(k) up to the match limit. This is essentially free money.

### 2. **Contribute to an IRA**
   - **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**: After maximizing your 401(k) match, consider contributing to an IRA. A Traditional IRA offers tax-deductible contributions that may lower your taxable income now, whereas a Roth IRA allows for tax-free withdrawals in retirement.

### 3. **Fund Your 401(k)**
   - Once you’ve contributed to your IRA, go back to your 401(k) to maximize yearly limits. This offers tax-deferred growth.

### 4. **Consider Taxable Accounts**
   - If you have additional funds after maxing out retirement accounts, consider taxable accounts for flexibility. Investments here can offer capital gains advantages, especially if you hold them for more than a year.

### 5. **Asset Allocation**
   - Diversify investments across all accounts based on your risk tolerance and investment timeline. Stocks might be preferable for growth in tax-advantaged accounts, while bonds or income-generating assets are often better suited for taxable accounts.

### 6. **Monitor and Adjust**
   - Regularly review your distribution strategy based on changes in income, goals, and tax laws.

### Summary
- **Max out 401(k) up to employer match**
- **Contribute to IRA**
- **Further fund 401(k) to maximum limits**
- **Invest in taxable accounts for flexibility**
