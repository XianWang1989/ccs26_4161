Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts is a key part of a solid investment strategy. Here are some best practices to consider:

### 1. **Understand Each Account Type:**
   - **401(k):** Often has employer matching contributions and tax advantages; contributions are pre-tax (traditional) or post-tax (Roth).
   - **IRA:** Individual Retirement Accounts (traditional and Roth) also offer tax advantages. Traditional IRAs may have tax-deductible contributions, while Roth IRAs allow tax-free withdrawals in retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** These provide flexibility, as you can withdraw funds anytime, but you’ll owe taxes on any capital gains or interest earned.

### 2. **Maximize Contributions:**
   - Aim to **max out your 401(k)** contributions, especially if your employer matches. This is essentially free money.
   - For 2023, the maximum contribution limit for a 401(k) is $22,500 (or $30,000 if you’re age 50 or older). The IRA limit is $6,500 (or $7,500 if you’re 50 or older).

### 3. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts:**
   - Focus on contributions to **tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) and IRA)** first. These accounts can grow tax-deferred or tax-free, which is beneficial in the long run.

### 4. **Balance Risk and Time Horizon:**
   - Consider your investment time horizon and risk tolerance. Younger investors might prioritize growth in equities, while those closer to retirement might allocate more to bonds or stable investments.

### 5. **Diversify Investments:**
   - Within each account, ensure a diverse mix of assets (stocks, bonds, real estate) to spread risk and enhance returns.

### 6. **Monitor and Adjust Periodically:**
   - Regularly review your allocations and rebalance as needed, especially as you approach retirement.

### 7. **Consult a Financial Advisor:**
   - If you're unsure about the best distribution for your personal situation, working with a financial advisor can provide tailored advice based on your specific financial goals and circumstances.

By following these steps, you can create a strategy that maximizes your retirement savings and helps you achieve your long-term financial goals.
