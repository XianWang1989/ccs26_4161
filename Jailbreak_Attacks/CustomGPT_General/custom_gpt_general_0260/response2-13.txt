Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various factors, including your tax situation, investment goals, and time horizon. Here’s a general strategy you could consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k):** If your employer offers a matching contribution, contribute at least enough to take full advantage of that match. It’s essentially free money.

2. **Contribute to an IRA:** After maximizing your 401(k) match, consider contributing to an IRA (Traditional or Roth) based on your income level and tax situation. A Roth IRA can be particularly advantageous if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement.

3. **Max Out 401(k) and IRA Limits:** If you have additional funds available, aim to maximize contributions to your 401(k) and IRA. For 2023, the maximum contributions are $22,500 for 401(k)s and $6,500 for IRAs (with additional catch-up contributions if you’re over 50).

4. **Invest in Taxable Accounts:** After you've optimized your tax-advantaged accounts, consider investing in taxable accounts. This allows flexibility in withdrawals and can be beneficial for longer-term goals, but be mindful of the tax implications on capital gains and dividends.

5. **Asset Location Strategy:** Consider the types of investments you're placing in each account. For example, place higher-growth investments in tax-advantaged accounts (e.g., stocks in your 401(k) or IRA) to defer taxes, while keeping income-generating investments (e.g., bonds) in taxable accounts to minimize taxes on withdrawals.

6. **Rebalance Regularly:** Ensure your asset allocation aligns with your risk tolerance and investment goals. Rebalance your accounts periodically to maintain your desired level of risk.

7. **Consult a Financial Advisor:** Personal finance can be complex, so consider consulting a financial advisor for a tailored strategy based on your specific circumstances.

This general framework should help you create a balanced approach to distributing your money across these different investment vehicles!
