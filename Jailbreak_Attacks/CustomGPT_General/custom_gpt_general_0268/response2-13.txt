The best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment strategy. Here are some general guidelines to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match:** If your employer offers a 401(k) match, contribute enough to take full advantage of that match first. It’s essentially free money.

2. **Traditional vs. Roth Account Decisions:** If you have both a Traditional and Roth IRA option, consider your current tax rate and expected future tax rate. Contributing to a Roth IRA could be beneficial if you anticipate being in a higher tax bracket during retirement.

3. **Investment Diversification:** Allocate your investments based on your risk tolerance and time horizon. Stocks can be beneficial for growth, while bonds may provide stability. Some investors choose to hold more aggressive investments in tax-advantaged accounts like a 401(k) or IRA, where capital gains tax doesn’t apply until withdrawal.

4. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility:** If you need access to your funds before retirement age, savings in a taxable account are more flexible. You can withdraw funds without penalties, but keep in mind you'll pay capital gains tax on earnings.

5. **Consider Tax Efficiency:** Focus on placing more tax-efficient investments (like index funds or ETFs) in taxable accounts, while holding higher yields or actively managed funds in tax-advantaged accounts.

6. **Regularly Reassess and Rebalance:** Your financial situation and market conditions may change, so regularly review and adjust your allocations to ensure they align with your goals.

It's always advisable to consult with a financial advisor to create a plan tailored to your individual situation.
