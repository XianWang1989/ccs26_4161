To effectively distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider these guidelines:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match. This is essentially "free money."

2. **Contribute to IRA**: If you're eligible, contribute to a Traditional or Roth IRA. Roth IRAs are beneficial for tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket later.

3. **Utilize Taxable Accounts**: Once you’ve maximized tax-advantaged accounts, use taxable accounts for additional investments. Consider investments with favorable tax treatment, like index funds or municipal bonds.

4. **Balance Asset Allocation**: Diversify your investments across all accounts based on your risk tolerance. Consider your time horizon—longer terms can tolerate more risk.

5. **Tax Efficiency**: Place tax-efficient investments (like ETFs or stocks) in taxable accounts, while less tax-efficient investments (like bonds) can go into tax-advantaged accounts to minimize tax liability.

6. **Review Regularly**: Reassess your allocation annually to adjust to changing personal circumstances and market conditions.

Consult a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your specific goals and situation.
