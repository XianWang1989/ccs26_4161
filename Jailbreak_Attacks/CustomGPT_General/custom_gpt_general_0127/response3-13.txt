Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including tax implications, investment goals, and risk tolerance. Here’s a strategic approach:

### 1. Maximize Employer Match
- **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get the full employer match. This is essentially "free money."

### 2. Focus on Tax-Advantaged Accounts First
- **IRA**: If you qualify, consider maxing out a Roth IRA for tax-free growth. If you're in a higher tax bracket now, a traditional IRA may be better for tax deductions.
- **401(k)**: After maximizing the match, contribute to your 401(k) up to the contribution limit.

### 3. Taxable Accounts
- After maximizing contributions in tax-advantaged accounts, use taxable accounts for investments. This allows for flexibility and access without penalties before retirement age.

### 4. Asset Allocation
- **Tax-efficient Investments**: Hold bonds or high-yield investments in tax-advantaged accounts (like 401(k) or IRA) to defer taxes.
- **Stocks**: Consider holding growth stocks or ETFs in taxable accounts, as they often generate lower capital gains.

### 5. Rebalancing and Review
- Regularly review and rebalance your portfolio across these accounts to optimize for performance and risk.

### 6. Consult a Financial Advisor
- Individual circumstances can vary drastically. A financial advisor can provide tailored advice based on your specific situation.

This strategy helps optimize your investments while minimizing tax liabilities over time.
