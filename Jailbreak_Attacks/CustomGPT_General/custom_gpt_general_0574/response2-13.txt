Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment time horizon. Here are some guidelines to consider:

1. **401(k) Contributions:**
   - **Employer Match:** If your employer offers a match, contribute at least enough to get the full match before investing elsewhere. This is essentially "free money."
   - **Tax Advantages:** Contributions are made pre-tax (traditional) or post-tax (Roth), impacting your current taxable income.

2. **IRA Investments:**
   - **Tax Flexibility:** Consider maximizing your IRA, especially if it offers tax-free growth (Roth) or tax-deferred growth (traditional). 
   - **Investment Options:** IRAs typically provide a wider range of investment choices compared to 401(k)s.

3. **Taxable Accounts:**
   - **Flexibility:** Taxable accounts offer more liquidity and flexibility for withdrawals without penalties.
   - **Tax Efficiency:** Be mindful of capital gains taxes. Favor tax-efficient investments (e.g., index funds, municipal bonds) to minimize tax liability.

4. **General Strategy:**
   - **Short-Term vs. Long-Term Goals:** Use taxable accounts for short-term savings (e.g., emergencies) and tax-advantaged accounts for long-term growth.
   - **Rebalance Annually:** Review your allocations regularly to ensure they align with your risk tolerance and investment goals.

5. **Consult a Financial Advisor:** A professional can provide personalized advice based on your specific financial situation and goals.

In summary, prioritize your 401(k) contributions to maximize employer matches, balance IRA contributions for tax advantages, and use taxable accounts for flexibility and short-term needs.
