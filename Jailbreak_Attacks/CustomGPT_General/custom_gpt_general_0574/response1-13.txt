Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax considerations, and investment horizon. Here are some key strategies for each account type:

### 1. **Prioritize Contributions**
   - **Employer Match**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match, as this is essentially free money.
   - **Debt & Emergency Fund**: Ensure high-interest debt is managed and maintain an emergency fund before maximizing retirement accounts.

### 2. **Consider Tax Implications**
   - **401(k)**: Contributions reduce taxable income in the year they are made, and taxes are deferred until withdrawal. Ideal for high-income earners in their peak earning years.
   - **IRA**: Traditional IRAs also provide tax advantages, but contributions may not be deductible depending on your income. Roth IRAs offer tax-free growth and withdrawals if certain conditions are met.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Suitable for short-term goals; you pay taxes on dividends and capital gains here. Consider using this account for more liquid investments.

### 3. **Asset Allocation**
   - **401(k) and IRA**: Generally, allocate growth-oriented investments like stocks to your tax-advantaged accounts for maximum compounding without immediate tax liabilities. Bonds and fixed-income investments can go here as well.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Invest in tax-efficient funds (like index funds) to minimize capital gains distributions. Consider holding bonds or less tax-efficient investments 

### 4. **Withdrawal Strategy**
   - **Tax Bracket Management**: When you retire, strategize withdrawals to manage your tax bracket effectively. Withdraw from taxable accounts first, then tax-deferred accounts, and finally tax-free accounts like Roth IRAs.

### 5. **Regular Reassessment**
   - Regularly reassess your portfolio and contributions based on changes in income, expenses, and financial goals.

### Example Distribution Approach
1. Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full match.
2. Max out your IRA contributions if possible.
3. Allocate any additional funds to your taxable accounts.

Consulting a financial advisor for personalized advice can also be beneficial.
