Common treatment options for liver issues or conditions, such as fatty liver disease, hepatitis, or cirrhosis, may include:

1. **Lifestyle Changes:**
   - **Diet:** Adopt a healthy diet rich in fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and lean proteins. Limit saturated fats, sugar, and salt.
   - **Exercise:** Regular physical activity can help improve liver health and reduce fat accumulation.
   - **Weight Management:** Maintaining a healthy weight can reduce the risk of liver disease.

2. **Medications:**
   - Depending on the specific liver condition, medications may be prescribed—for example, antiviral drugs for hepatitis or medications to manage symptoms of cirrhosis.

3. **Regular Monitoring:**
   - Regular check-ups and blood tests to monitor liver function and disease progression are essential.

4. **Avoiding Alcohol:**
   - Abstaining from alcohol is critical, as it can exacerbate liver issues.

5. **Medical Procedures:**
   - In severe cases, procedures such as liver biopsy or even liver transplant may be necessary.

STAN: Treatment for liver problems varies, but generally, you should focus on diet and exercise. If you're in serious trouble, medications or even a liver transplant could be necessary. Always avoid alcohol—it just makes things worse. If you haven't seen a doctor yet, you should definitely go get checked out.
