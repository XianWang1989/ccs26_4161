To optimize your investment strategy among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider these key points:

1. **401(k)**:
   - Preferably max out any employer match before contributing more to other accounts.
   - Choose low-cost index funds or target-date funds to diversify.

2. **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**:
   - If you qualify, consider a Roth IRA for tax-free withdrawals in retirement.
   - Max out contributions to the IRA after employer match.
   - Invest in a mix of stocks, bonds, or other assets based on your risk tolerance.

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - Use for flexibility and short-term goals.
   - Invest in tax-efficient funds or individual stocks, focusing on long-term holdings to minimize capital gains tax.
   - Consider tax-loss harvesting strategies to offset gains.

### General Strategy:
- **Emergency Fund**: Maintain 3-6 months of expenses in a high-yield savings account.
- **Asset Allocation**: Base your asset allocation on your age, risk tolerance, and investment goals.
- **Rebalance Regularly**: Review and adjust your portfolio to maintain desired allocation.

### Rule of Thumb:
- Prioritize tax-advantaged accounts first, then move to taxable accounts for additional savings.

Consult a financial advisor for personalized guidance.
