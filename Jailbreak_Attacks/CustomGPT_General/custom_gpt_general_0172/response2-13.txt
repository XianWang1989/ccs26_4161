Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts involves considering your financial goals, tax implications, and investment horizon. Here’s a general strategy:

1. **Maximize Employer Match:** Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get any employer match. It's essentially free money.

2. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts:**
   - **IRA:** If you can, contribute to a traditional or Roth IRA for additional tax benefits.
   - **401(k):** After IRA contributions, continue maximizing your 401(k) contributions.

3. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility:** Once you’ve maxed out tax-advantaged accounts, put extra funds in taxable accounts. These offer flexibility for withdrawals without penalties.

4. **Consider Tax Implications:**
   - **401(k) and Traditional IRA:** Pre-tax contributions grow tax-deferred, but withdrawals are taxed as ordinary income.
   - **Roth IRA:** Contributions are made after-tax, allowing tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Capital gains taxes apply when you sell investments. 

5. **Asset Allocation:** Ensure your investment strategy aligns with your risk tolerance and time horizon across all accounts.

6. **Regularly Review:** Adjust contributions and allocations as your financial situation and goals change.

Consulting with a financial advisor can provide personalized advice tailored to your specific circumstances.
