Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here’s a general approach:

### 1. Maximize Employer Match
- **401(k)**: Contribute at least enough to get the full employer match, as this is essentially free money.

### 2. Focus on Tax-Advantaged Accounts
- **IRA**: Consider maxing out contributions to a traditional or Roth IRA based on eligibility. Traditional IRAs offer tax deductions, while Roth IRAs provide tax-free withdrawals in retirement.

### 3. Allocate Remaining Funds
- **401(k)**: If you have more to invest after hitting IRA limits, contribute more to your 401(k).
- **Taxable Accounts**: Use taxable accounts for investments that may generate short-term capital gains or for funds you might need in the near term.

### 4. Consider Investment Types
- Put tax-efficient investments (like index funds) in taxable accounts to minimize tax impact.
- Hold tax-inefficient investments (like bonds) in tax-advantaged accounts.

### 5. Regular Rebalancing
- Regularly review and rebalance your portfolio to align with your risk tolerance and market changes.

### Conclusion
This diversified approach helps maximize growth potential while managing tax implications. Consulting with a financial advisor can also provide tailored advice based on your personal circumstances.
