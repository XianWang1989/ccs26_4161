Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various factors such as your financial goals, tax situation, and investment strategy. Here are some general guidelines to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a match on your 401(k) contributions, try to contribute enough to take full advantage of this benefit. It’s essentially "free money."

2. **Evaluate Tax Treatments**: 
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are typically pre-tax, which reduces your taxable income. Withdrawals in retirement are taxed as ordinary income.
   - **IRA (Traditional)**: Similar to a 401(k), contributions may be tax-deductible, and withdrawals are taxed in retirement. Consider a Roth IRA if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement; contributions are made with after-tax dollars, but withdrawals are tax-free.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: These are more flexible for withdrawals but come with capital gains taxes. They’re best for short- to medium-term goals or if you’ve maxed out tax-advantaged accounts.

3. **Diversification**: Make sure your investments are diversified across these accounts to balance risk and reward. Consider asset allocation based on your retirement timeline and risk tolerance.

4. **Liquidity Needs**: Determine how much money you might need in the short term and keep sufficient funds in your taxable accounts to cover those expenses without penalties.

5. **Future Tax Implications**: Consider how withdrawals from these accounts will affect your tax situation in retirement. Having a mix allows for flexibility in managing your tax bracket during retirement.

6. **Long-Term Growth**: Generally, prioritize accounts meant for long-term growth in tax-advantaged accounts to maximize their benefits.

7. **Review Regularly**: Periodically review your asset allocation and make adjustments based on your age, income changes, and market conditions.

Always consult with a financial advisor to tailor a strategy that suits your individual circumstances, as they can provide personalized advice based on your entire financial situation.
