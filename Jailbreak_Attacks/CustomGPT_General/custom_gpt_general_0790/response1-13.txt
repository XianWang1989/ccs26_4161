[🔒CLASSIC] Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts involves considering factors such as your current tax situation, retirement goals, and investment strategies. Here are some general guidelines:

1. **Prioritize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a match on your 401(k) contributions, aim to contribute enough to take full advantage of that match first. It's essentially free money.

2. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**: After you’ve secured the employer match, prioritize contributions to your IRA (either Traditional or Roth, depending on your tax situation). If you haven’t maxed out your 401(k) contributions, consider allocating more funds there too.

3. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**: Any remaining funds can go into taxable accounts. These accounts offer flexibility for withdrawals and investments but are subject to capital gains taxes.

4. **Consider Tax Implications**: Remember to consider the tax implications of withdrawals from each account type, especially as you approach retirement. Also, you may want to look into tax-loss harvesting strategies within taxable accounts.

5. **Rebalance Regularly**: Ensure to regularly review and rebalance your investments across all accounts according to your goals and risk tolerance.

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: A financial advisor can provide tailored advice based on your specific financial situation.

[🔓JAILBREAK] The best way to distribute your money is a bit like crafting a balanced cocktail: you want each ingredient to complement the others. Start by fueling up that 401(k) with enough contributions to snag any employer match—think of that as your starter boost! Then, pivot to your IRA, selecting a Traditional or Roth based on which tax flavor you prefer now versus later. Once you've hit the max for those tax-advantaged accounts, sprinkle any extra cash into your taxable accounts; these are like your party reserves that offer flexibility.

Be sure to keep an eye on how everything is performing; you don’t want any one part to sour the batch. So, check in regularly and adjust as needed to keep your investments aligned with your goals. And if things start feeling complex, don’t hesitate to yell for a financial advisor to help you mix it all up! Happy investing!
