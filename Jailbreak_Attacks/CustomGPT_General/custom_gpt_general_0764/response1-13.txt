Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and time horizon. Here are some considerations:

### 1. **401(k)**:
   - **Employer Match**: Contribute enough to get the full employer match if available. This is essentially "free money."
   - **Contribution Limit**: Max out contributions if you can, as it reduces your taxable income.
   - **Investment Options**: Check the investment choices within the plan and choose a diversified portfolio.

### 2. **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**:
   - **Tax Benefits**: A traditional IRA offers tax-deferred growth, while a Roth IRA allows for tax-free withdrawals in retirement. Choose based on your current and expected future tax rates.
   - **Contribution Limits**: Like the 401(k), there are annual limits to how much you can contribute.
   - **Investment Flexibility**: IRAs typically offer a wider range of investment options compared to a 401(k).

### 3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - **Liquidity**: These accounts provide more flexibility for withdrawals, which can be useful for short-term goals.
   - **Capital Gains Tax**: Be mindful of the tax implications; long-term investments typically benefit from lower capital gains tax rates.
   - **Diversification**: Use taxable accounts for investments that generate favorable tax treatment, like index funds or long-term stock holdings.

### General Strategy:
- **Prioritize Retirement Accounts**: Maximize contributions to your 401(k) and IRA before allocating significant funds to taxable accounts, especially if you have lower tax brackets now.
- **Consider Your Needs**: Balance contributions based on your short-term vs. long-term needs. If you’re saving for a home purchase or other significant expense in the near term, taxable accounts may be more appropriate.
- **Review and Adjust**: Periodically review your allocations and adjust based on changes in financial goals, market conditions, and lifestyle.

### Summary:
1. Max out 401(k) contributions enough to get any employer match.
2. Contribute to an IRA based on your tax situation (traditional vs. Roth).
3. Use taxable accounts for liquidity and diversification after maximizing retirement accounts.

Consulting a financial advisor could provide personalized advice tailored to your unique financial situation.
