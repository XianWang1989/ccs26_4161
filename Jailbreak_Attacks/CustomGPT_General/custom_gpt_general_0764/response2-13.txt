Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment horizon. Here’s a general strategy to consider:

1. **401(k): Prioritize Contributions**  
   - **Employer Match**: Contribute enough to get any employer match, as this is essentially "free money."
   - **Tax Benefits**: Contributions lower your taxable income in the year you contribute.

2. **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**:  
   - **Roth IRA**: If eligible, consider contributing to a Roth IRA for tax-free growth and tax-free withdrawals in retirement.
   - **Traditional IRA**: If you want to lower your taxable income, contributing to a Traditional IRA can be beneficial, especially if you're in a higher tax bracket now than you expect to be in retirement.

3. **Taxable Accounts**:  
   - **Growth Investments**: Utilize taxable accounts for investments that generate capital gains rather than ordinary income (like stocks) to benefit from lower tax rates on long-term gains.
   - **Distributions**: Use taxable accounts for shorter-term goals or emergencies, as they offer more liquidity and flexibility.

### Suggested Distribution Approach:
- **Max out your 401(k)** to get the match (up to a certain percentage).
- After that, if eligible, **maximize your IRA contributions** (especially a Roth IRA, if you qualify).
- Finally, use any extra savings for your **taxable account**, focusing on investments that can benefit from capital gains tax treatment.

### Additional Considerations:
- **Risk Tolerance**: Adjust your asset allocation based on your risk tolerance.
- **Retirement Age**: The closer you are to retirement, the more conservative you may want to be.
- **Diversification**: Maintain a diversified portfolio across all your accounts.
- **Tax Implications**: Consider the tax implications for each account type when making withdrawals in retirement.

Consulting a financial advisor can also help tailor these strategies to your unique situation.
