Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here’s a general strategy to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match (401(k))**: If your employer offers a matching contribution for your 401(k), contribute enough to get the full match. This is essentially free money.

2. **Tax-Advantaged Accounts**: After maximizing the match, consider contributing to an IRA (traditional or Roth, depending on your eligibility and tax situation):
   - **Traditional IRA**: Contributions may be tax-deductible, lowering your taxable income.
   - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, but withdrawals in retirement are tax-free.

3. **Contribute to 401(k)**: If you have more room in your budget after the employer match and IRA contributions, increase your contributions to your 401(k), especially if it has a wide range of investment options.

4. **Taxable Accounts**: If you’ve maximized contributions to your 401(k) and IRA, consider investing in a taxable brokerage account. This can provide flexibility for future expenses and allows for more immediate access to your funds without penalties.

5. **Asset Allocation**: Within each account type, ensure you have a diversified mix of investments aligned with your risk tolerance and time horizon. Typically, younger investors may opt for a more aggressive approach, while those nearing retirement might prioritize stability.

6. **Tax Efficiency**: Consider placing less tax-efficient investments (like bonds) in tax-advantaged accounts and more tax-efficient investments (like stocks) in taxable accounts to minimize tax impacts.

7. **Review Regularly**: Periodically reassess your allocations and contributions based on life changes, market conditions, and retirement goals.

It's often beneficial to consult with a financial advisor to tailor this approach to your specific situation.
