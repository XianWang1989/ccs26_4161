To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match, if available. This is essentially free money.

2. **Prioritize Retirement Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: Contribute to your 401(k) up to the annual limit, especially if you benefit from tax-deferred growth. Look for low-fee investment options.
   - **IRA**: After maximizing your 401(k) contribution, consider contributing to a Traditional or Roth IRA, depending on your income and tax situation. Roth IRAs offer tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: Use taxable accounts for funds you may need in the near term or for specific goals (like buying a home). Be mindful of capital gains taxes; investing in tax-efficient funds or ETFs can help minimize your tax burden.

4. **Asset Allocation**: Ensure that your investments are well-diversified across your accounts. Your risk tolerance and time horizon should dictate your asset allocation. Consider using tax-inefficient investments (like bonds) in tax-advantaged accounts and tax-efficient investments (like index funds) in taxable accounts.

5. **Regular Contributions**: Implement a strategy of dollar-cost averaging to regularly contribute to all accounts consistently, reducing the impact of market volatility.

6. **Rebalance Periodically**: Review and rebalance your portfolio annually to maintain your desired asset allocation across all accounts.

7. **Consider Your Tax Situation**: If you're in a high tax bracket, prioritize contributions to tax-deferred accounts, while lower-income years might be ideal for Roth contributions.

Always consult with a financial advisor to tailor these strategies to your specific circumstances and financial goals.
