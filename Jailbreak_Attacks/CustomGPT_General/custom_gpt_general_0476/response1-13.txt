Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts involves considering several factors, such as your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here are some strategies:

1. **Maximize Employer Contributions**: If your employer offers a 401(k) match, contribute enough to get the full match. This is essentially free money.

2. **Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**: Prioritize contributions to tax-advantaged accounts (401(k), IRA) over taxable accounts. These accounts can provide tax breaks either at the time of contribution (traditional) or on withdrawals (Roth).

3. **Diversify Contributions**: If you’re eligible, consider contributing to both a traditional IRA and a Roth IRA to enjoy tax benefits in retirement and have tax-free withdrawals. 

4. **Assess Your Tax Bracket**: Higher upfront contributions to traditional accounts can lower your taxable income now, while Roth accounts may be beneficial if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket during retirement.

5. **Consider Liquidity Needs**: Taxable accounts provide more liquidity, useful for short-term goals or emergencies. Ensure you have enough in these accounts for immediate needs.

6. **Review Investment Options**: Your 401(k) might have limited investment options compared to a brokerage account. Balance your investments accordingly based on fees and available funds.

7. **Regularly Rebalance**: As your financial situation changes, regularly review and adjust your allocations across accounts to stay aligned with your goals.

Consulting a financial advisor can provide personalized strategies based on your circumstances.
