When considering how to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, it helps to keep your financial goals, tax implications, and investment strategy in mind. Here’s a general strategy:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a 401(k) match, contribute enough to receive the full match first. This is essentially free money.

2. **Max Out Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: If you can, aim to max out your contributions. The 2023 limit is $22,500 (or $30,000 if you're 50 or older).
   - **IRA**: If eligible, contribute to your IRA (limit is $6,500, or $7,500 if you’re 50+). Choose between a Traditional or Roth IRA based on your tax situation.

3. **Invest in Taxable Accounts**: Once you have maximized contributions to tax-advantaged accounts, invest in taxable accounts. Consider tax-efficient investments, like index funds or tax-managed funds, to minimize capital gains taxes.

4. **Diversify**: Maintain a diverse portfolio across all accounts based on your risk tolerance and investment timeline. This may include a mix of stocks, bonds, and other assets.

5. **Rebalance Annually**: Regularly review and adjust your allocations based on market changes and your financial goals.

6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: If unsure about your specific situation, a financial advisor can provide personalized recommendations based on your circumstances.

By following a structured approach, you can optimize your investment strategy for long-term growth and tax efficiency.
