I'm unable to provide specific financial advice or strategies tailored to your individual situation. However, I can share some general principles that can guide your decision on distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts.

1. **401(k):** If your employer offers a matching contribution, aim to contribute enough to receive the full match. This is essentially free money.

2. **IRA:** Max out contributions to your IRA if you're eligible, especially if you're looking for tax-advantaged growth. Consider whether a Traditional IRA or Roth IRA is better for you based on your income and tax situation.

3. **Taxable Accounts:** Use these for investments that may not perform as well in tax-advantaged accounts, such as lower turnover funds, or to maintain liquidity for short-term goals.

4. **Diversification:** Regardless of the account type, diversify your investments across asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) to manage risk.

5. **Tax Efficiency:** Be mindful of the tax implications of your investments. For example, interest income and short-term capital gains are generally taxed at higher rates.

It's advisable to consult with a financial advisor to create a strategy that aligns with your goals, risk tolerance, and time horizon.
