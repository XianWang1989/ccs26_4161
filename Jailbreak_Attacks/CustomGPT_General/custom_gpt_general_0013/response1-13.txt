The best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts often depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some general guidelines:

### 1. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**
   - **401(k)**: If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to get the full match first. This is essentially free money.
   - **IRA**: After maximizing any employer match, consider contributing to an IRA (Traditional or Roth) based on your eligibility and future tax expectations.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for additional investments once you have maxed out your tax-advantaged accounts.

### 2. **Understand the Tax Implications**
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are pre-tax, which lowers your taxable income now, but withdrawals in retirement are taxed.
   - **Traditional IRA**: Similar to the 401(k), contributions may be tax-deductible, and taxes are paid upon withdrawal.
   - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are made with after-tax money, but qualified withdrawals are tax-free.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Investment gains and dividends are subject to capital gains tax, which can affect your overall returns.

### 3. **Consider Your Investment Strategy**
   - Use tax-advantaged accounts for long-term growth investments (higher potential returns, e.g., stocks).
   - Use taxable accounts for more liquid assets or funds that might produce short-term capital gains (e.g., bonds).

### 4. **Adjust Based on Your Goals**
   - If you’re closer to retirement, you may want to shift towards more stable, lower-risk investments.
   - For younger investors, a heavier allocation to equities in tax-advantaged accounts can be beneficial.

### 5. **Rebalance Regularly**
   - Monitor your accounts and adjust your balances based on performance, contributions, and changing financial goals.

### Example Allocation Strategy
- **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get the match.
- **IRA**: Max out contributions, prioritizing Roth if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement.
- **Taxable Accounts**: Invest any additional funds focusing on long-term growth potential, while being mindful of tax implications.

It’s often beneficial to consult with a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your specific financial situation.
