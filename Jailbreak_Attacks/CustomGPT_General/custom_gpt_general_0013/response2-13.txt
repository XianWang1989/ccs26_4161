Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment horizon. Here’s a general strategy to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match. This is essentially free money.

2. **Contribute to an IRA**: After maxing out any match from your 401(k), consider contributing to an IRA (traditional or Roth, depending on your income and tax situation). Roth IRAs offer tax-free growth and withdrawals, which can be very beneficial if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement.

3. **Increase 401(k) Contributions**: If possible, continue maxing out contributions to your 401(k). The tax-deferred growth can lead to significant accumulations over time.

4. **Consider Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**: Funds in taxable accounts can be accessed at any time without penalty, making them useful for medium-term goals or emergencies. However, be mindful of capital gains tax on investment profits.

5. **Diversify Investments**: Ensure that your investments across these accounts are diversified. Consider a mix of stocks, bonds, and other assets to balance risk and return based on your risk tolerance.

6. **Tax-Efficiency**: Place tax-inefficient investments (like bond funds) in tax-advantaged accounts (401(k), IRA) and keep more tax-efficient investments (like stocks or index funds) in taxable accounts.

7. **Rebalance Annually**: Regularly review and adjust your asset allocation based on market fluctuations and changes in your financial goals.

It's always wise to consult with a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your situation.
