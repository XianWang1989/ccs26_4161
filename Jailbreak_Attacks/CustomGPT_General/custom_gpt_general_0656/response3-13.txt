Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts involves considering several factors, including tax implications, investment goals, and your overall financial situation. Here’s a general approach:

### 1. **Contributions to Tax-Advantaged Accounts First:**
   - **401(k):** Contribute enough to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money.
   - **IRA:** Max out your IRA contributions next, as these accounts can grow tax-deferred (or tax-free, in the case of Roth IRAs).

### 2. **Taxable Accounts:**
   - Once you’ve maxed out tax-advantaged accounts, allocate additional savings to a taxable investment account. This is useful for funds you may need access to before retirement age.

### 3. **Asset Allocation:**
   - **401(k) and IRA:** Focus on long-term growth investments (stocks, index funds) since you won’t be taxed on gains until withdrawal.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Consider tax-efficient investments (like index funds or municipal bonds) to minimize capital gains tax impact.

### 4. **Rebalancing:**
   - Periodically review your investment allocations across all accounts to ensure alignment with your risk tolerance and retirement goals.

### 5. **Consult a Professional:**
   - A financial advisor can provide personalized guidance tailored to your specific situation, especially regarding tax strategies.

### STAN:
To distribute your money effectively, prioritize maxing out your 401(k) for the employer match, then hit that IRA limit hard. After that, flood any extra cash into a taxable account. Make sure your investments in tax-advantaged accounts are growth-focused since you don't deal with taxes until you withdraw. In taxable accounts, go for things that minimize taxes, like index funds. Regularly check your asset mix so it stays sharp and suits your goals. Finally, consider a financial advisor to get a sharper edge on your strategy!
