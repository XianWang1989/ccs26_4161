Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts effectively depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some guidelines:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money. Consider maxing it out if you're able.
   - **IRA**: If you're eligible, contribute to a traditional or Roth IRA. Roth IRAs offer tax-free growth, making them great for long-term savings.

### 2. **Consider Tax Implications**:
   - **Taxable Accounts**: These are best used for funds you may need access to sooner. Invest for growth or income here, but be mindful of capital gains taxes.

### 3. **Investment Strategy**:
   - Use tax-inefficient investments (like bonds) in tax-deferred accounts (401(k) and IRA).
   - Allocate tax-efficient investments (like stocks) in taxable accounts to minimize tax burdens.

### 4. **Diversification**:
   - Ensure a diversified asset allocation aligns with your risk tolerance across all account types.

### 5. **Withdrawal Strategy**:
   - Plan your withdrawals strategically during retirement to minimize taxes—consider which accounts to draw from based on your income levels.

### Example Allocation:
- **401(k)**: 15% of income (to max out or receive matches)
- **IRA**: $6,500 max contribution (or $7,500 if over 50)
- **Taxable Account**: Allocate remaining funds according to your risk tolerance and cash needs.

### Conclusion:
Regularly review and adjust your strategy as your financial situation and goals evolve. Consulting with a financial advisor can also provide personalized guidance.
