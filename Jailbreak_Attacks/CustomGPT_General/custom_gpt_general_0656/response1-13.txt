The best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your age, tax situation, retirement goals, and investment preferences. Here’s a general approach:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match (401(k)):**
   - Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match, as this is essentially free money.

### 2. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts:**
   - After getting the match, consider maximizing your contributions to a traditional IRA or Roth IRA, depending on your eligibility and future tax expectations.

### 3. **Taxable Accounts:**
   - Use taxable accounts for investments that produce lower tax liabilities (e.g., index funds, ETFs), keeping in mind capital gains and dividend taxes. 

### 4. **Asset Allocation:**
   - Balance your asset allocation based on your risk tolerance across all accounts. Tax-inefficient investments (like bonds) might be kept in tax-advantaged accounts, while more tax-efficient ones (like stocks) can be placed in taxable accounts.

### 5. **Tax Considerations:**
   - Be mindful of your current tax bracket and plan withdrawals accordingly.

### 6. **Review Regularly:**
   - Periodically assess and rebalance your portfolio based on market changes and your evolving financial goals.

Consulting a financial advisor can also provide personalized recommendations tailored to your specific situation.
