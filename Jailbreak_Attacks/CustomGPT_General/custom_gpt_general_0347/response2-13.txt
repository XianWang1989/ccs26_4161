When distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider these key strategies:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**
   - **401(k):** Contribute enough to get any employer match; this is essentially free money. Consider maxing out your contributions if possible.
   - **IRA:** If eligible, consider contributing to a Roth IRA for tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement. A traditional IRA is also beneficial for immediate tax deductions.

### 2. **Asset Allocation**
   - **401(k) & IRA:** Focus on growth-oriented investments like stocks, especially if you are younger. Tax-deferred accounts allow you to benefit from compound growth without immediate tax implications.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Consider holding less tax-efficient investments here, such as bonds or real estate investment trusts (REITs), since you'll pay taxes on dividends and capital gains.

### 3. **Emergency Fund**
   - Ensure you have a sufficient emergency fund outside these accounts, ideally covering 3-6 months of expenses, to avoid early withdrawals.

### 4. **Investment Strategies**
   - **Tax-Loss Harvesting:** In taxable accounts, you can offset gains by selling losing investments.
   - **Diversification:** Ensure you have a good mixture of asset classes across all accounts.

### 5. **Regular Rebalancing**
   - Periodically review your account allocations to ensure they align with your risk tolerance and investment goals.

### Conclusion
Prioritize contributions to tax-advantaged accounts, then allocate funds based on investment goals and tax efficiency. Adjust your strategy based on your age, risk tolerance, and retirement timeline.
