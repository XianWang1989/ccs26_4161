To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

### 1. **Tax Efficiency**
   - **401(k)**: Utilize for high contributions and employer matches. It's pre-tax, so it reduces taxable income now.
   - **IRA**: Choose a Roth IRA if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket during retirement; contributions are taxed now, but withdrawals are tax-free.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Invest in tax-efficient funds or ETFs to minimize capital gains taxes.

### 2. **Investment Goals**
   - Align your investment type with your time horizon. Use the 401(k) for long-term growth and keep more volatile investments here. For taxable accounts, consider more conservative, income-generating investments.

### 3. **Contribution Limits**
   - Max out contributions to your 401(k) and IRA first, as these accounts offer tax benefits and growth potential.

### 4. **Asset Allocation**
   - Diversify your investments based on risk tolerance. For example, you might want to hold stocks in your 401(k) and bonds in your taxable account for balance.

### 5. **Withdrawal Strategy**
   - Plan how and when you’ll withdraw from each account. Withdrawals from 401(k) and IRAs are typically taxed as income, while taxable accounts might incur capital gains taxes based on how long you've held the investments.

### Example Distribution
- **401(k)**: 50% of retirement savings
- **IRA**: 20% (Roth or Traditional based on tax expectations)
- **Taxable Accounts**: 30% for liquidity and flexibility

### Conclusion
Review and adjust your strategy annually based on changes in income, tax laws, or personal circumstances. Consulting a financial advisor can provide tailored advice specific to your situation.
