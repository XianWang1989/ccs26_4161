Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your current financial situation, tax bracket, retirement goals, and investment preferences. Here are some guidelines:

1. **401(k)**:
   - **Employer Match**: Prioritize contributions to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money.
   - **Tax Benefits**: Consider pre-tax contributions for tax deduction benefits if you're in a high tax bracket.

2. **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**:
   - **Tax Strategy**: If you expect to be in a higher tax bracket during retirement, prioritize a Roth IRA for tax-free withdrawals later. Otherwise, a Traditional IRA is beneficial for current tax savings.
   - **Contribution Limits**: Be aware of annual contribution limits and adjust your contributions accordingly.

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - **Flexibility**: Use these for investments you may need access to before retirement. They offer more flexibility without penalties for early withdrawals.
   - **Tax Efficiency**: Invest in tax-efficient assets like index funds or ETFs to minimize capital gains taxes.

### Suggested Approach:
- **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**.
- **Contribute to an IRA**, focusing on the type that aligns with your tax strategy.
- **Invest any remaining funds in taxable accounts**, especially if you have met the limits for retirement accounts.

### Regular Review:
- Assess your investment allocation periodically and adjust according to changing financial goals, tax situations, and market conditions.

Consult with a financial advisor for personalized advice based on your specific circumstances.
