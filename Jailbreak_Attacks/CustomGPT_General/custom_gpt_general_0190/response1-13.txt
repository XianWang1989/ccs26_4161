The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts generally depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment horizon. Here are some guidelines to consider:

### 1. **Understand Account Types**:
   - **401(k)**: Typically offers tax advantages and sometimes employer matching. Limits on contributions are higher than IRAs.
   - **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**: Traditional IRAs offer tax-deferred growth, while Roth IRAs allow tax-free withdrawals in retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: No tax advantages, but they provide liquidity and flexibility. Taxes apply on dividends, interest, and capital gains.

### 2. **Prioritize Contributions**:
   - **Employer Match**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to take full advantage of any employer match—it’s essentially free money.
   - **Maximize IRA Contributions**: If you can, maximize contributions to an IRA (especially a Roth, if eligible) after meeting your 401(k) match.
   - **Additional 401(k) Contributions**: If you still have funds available, contribute more to your 401(k) to reach the annual limit.

### 3. **Consider Tax Implications**:
   - **Tax-Deferred Growth**: Prioritize tax-deferred accounts for assets expected to grow significantly, as this helps postpone taxes until retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts for Short-Term Goals**: If you need access to funds in the short term (education, purchase), consider putting money in taxable accounts.

### 4. **Investment Strategy**:
   - **Risk Tolerance and Time Horizon**: Allocate investments based on how long you plan to hold them. Longer horizons generally allow for more aggressive investments. 
   - **Diversification**: Ensure that your investments within these accounts are diversified to reduce risk.

### 5. **Reassess Regularly**:
   - Regularly review and adjust your portfolio based on performance and changes in your financial situation or goals.

### Example Distribution Strategy
- **Max out 401(k) up to match**: 50%
- **Max out IRA contributions**: 30%
- **Fill taxable accounts for short-term goals or emergencies**: 20%

### Final Thoughts
Consult with a financial advisor for personalized advice that considers your specific situation. They can help create a tailored strategy that incorporates your overall financial plan.
