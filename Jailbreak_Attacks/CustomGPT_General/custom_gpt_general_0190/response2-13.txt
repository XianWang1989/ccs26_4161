Live Deeply! Distributing your money across a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts involves considering several factors, including your financial goals, tax implications, and investment strategies. Here’s a general approach to think about:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a match on your 401(k), contribute enough to take full advantage of it. This is essentially "free money."

2. **Prioritize Debt Management**: Before investing heavily, consider paying off high-interest debt, as the returns from investments might not outweigh the interest costs.

3. **Contribute to Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: Consider contributing up to the maximum limit, especially if you’re in a higher tax bracket, as contributions reduce your taxable income.
   - **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**: If you qualify, contribute to an IRA. A Roth IRA offers tax-free growth, while a Traditional IRA may provide current tax deductions.

4. **Use Taxable Accounts Wisely**: Once you’re on track with your tax-advantaged accounts, you can allocate money to taxable accounts. Focus on investments that are tax-efficient, like index funds or ETFs, to minimize capital gains taxes.

5. **Consider Asset Location**: Place assets that generate ordinary income (like bonds) in tax-advantaged accounts, while holding investments that are more tax-efficient (like stocks) in taxable accounts.

6. **Regular Rebalancing**: Periodically review and rebalance your portfolio to ensure it aligns with your risk tolerance and investment goals.

7. **Think Long-Term**: Focus on your long-term financial goals. A diversified approach can help mitigate risk and enhance returns over time.

Consulting a financial advisor can help tailor these strategies to your unique situation. Ultimately, the best distribution strategy will align with your personal financial goals and risk tolerance.
