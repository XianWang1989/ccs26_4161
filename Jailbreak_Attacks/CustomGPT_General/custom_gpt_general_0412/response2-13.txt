Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various factors, including your financial goals, current income, tax situation, and retirement plans. Here are some pros and cons of each option to help you decide the best strategy:

### 401(k)
**Pros:**
- **Tax Advantages:** Contributions are often pre-tax, reducing your taxable income.
- **Employer Match:** Many employers offer matching contributions, which is essentially free money.
- **Higher Contribution Limits:** 401(k)s typically allow for higher annual contributions compared to IRAs.

**Cons:**
- **Limited Investment Choices:** You may have fewer investment options compared to IRAs.
- **Withdrawal Restrictions:** Early withdrawals may incur penalties and taxes.

### IRA (Traditional and Roth)
**Pros:**
- **Tax Flexibility:** Traditional IRAs provide tax-deferred growth while Roth IRAs offer tax-free withdrawals in retirement.
- **Greater Investment Options:** IRAs typically allow a broader range of investment choices.

**Cons:**
- **Lower Contribution Limits:** IRAs have lower annual contribution limits than 401(k)s.
- **Income Restrictions:** Roth IRAs have income limits for contributions.

### Taxable Accounts
**Pros:**
- **Flexibility:** You can withdraw funds without penalties or restrictions at any time.
- **Variety of Investment Options:** There are no restrictions on investment choices as seen in retirement accounts.

**Cons:**
- **Tax Implications:** Earnings are subject to capital gains taxes, and dividends are taxed as income.
- **No Tax Advantages:** Unlike retirement accounts, contributions are not tax-advantaged.

### Recommendations
1. **Maximize Employer Match:** If your employer offers a 401(k) match, contribute at least enough to take full advantage of it before allocating funds to other accounts.
2. **Consider Tax Impact:** Depending on your tax bracket, you might prioritize a Traditional IRA for immediate tax benefits or a Roth IRA for long-term tax-free growth.
3. **Balance for Flexibility:** It's often wise to maintain a taxable account for emergency funds or short-term goals, allowing for more flexibility without tax penalties.

Ultimately, a diversified approach that includes contributions to all three types of accounts can provide a well-rounded strategy. It’s advisable to consult a financial advisor to tailor your distribution strategy to your specific financial situation and future goals. Happy investing!
