It's great that you're thinking about how to allocate your investments! However, investment strategy can vary significantly depending on individual circumstances like your age, risk tolerance, financial goals, tax situation, and more. Here are some general considerations:

1. **401(k) Accounts:**
   - **Pros:**
     - Often have employer matching contributions, effectively giving you "free money."
     - Contributions reduce your taxable income for the year.
     - Higher contribution limits (up to $22,500 in 2023, or $30,000 if you're over 50).
   - **Cons:**
     - Limited investment choices compared to IRAs or taxable accounts.
     - Withdrawals are taxed and penalties apply if taken before age 59½.

2. **IRA Accounts:**
   - **Pros:**
     - More investment options compared to a 401(k).
     - Traditional IRAs may be tax-deductible, while Roth IRAs allow for tax-free withdrawals in retirement.
   - **Cons:**
     - Contribution limits are lower (up to $6,500 in 2023, or $7,500 if you're over 50).
     - There may be income restrictions for Roth IRA contributions.

3. **Taxable Accounts:**
   - **Pros:**
     - No contribution limits and easy access to funds without penalties.
     - Flexibility to invest in a variety of assets.
     - Potential tax benefits from long-term capital gains and qualified dividends.
   - **Cons:**
     - Investment income is taxed in the year it is realized.
     - No tax advantages compared to retirement accounts.

### Strategy Recommendations:
- **Maximize 401(k) Contributions:** If your employer offers a match, contribute at least enough to get the full match, as this is essentially free money.
- **Contribute to an IRA:** After securing your 401(k) match, consider maxing out an IRA account. Depending on your tax situation, choose between Traditional and Roth IRA.
- **Utilize Taxable Accounts for Flexibility:** Once you’ve maxed out your retirement accounts, consider putting additional funds into taxable accounts for flexibility, especially if you plan to access those funds before retirement.

### Suggested Allocation:
1. **401(k):** Aim for contributing enough to get the maximum employer match.
2. **IRA:** Contribute up to the maximum limit.
3. **Taxable Account:** Any additional savings can go here for flexibility.

### Example:
- If you can contribute $20,000 in total towards these accounts, consider allocating:
  - $10,000 to your 401(k) (taking full advantage of your employer match).
  - $6,500 to your IRA.
  - $3,500 to a taxable account.

Ultimately, it’s best to consult with a financial advisor who can tailor a strategy to your personal financial picture. Happy investing!
