Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, age, and risk tolerance. Here are some guidelines:

1. **401(k)**:
   - **Maximize Contributions**: Try to contribute enough to get any employer match, as it's essentially free money.
   - **Tax Benefits**: Contributions lower your taxable income. Prioritize this for tax-advantaged growth.

2. **IRA (Individual Retirement Account)**:
   - **Choose Between Traditional or Roth**: If you anticipate being in a higher tax bracket during retirement, consider a Roth IRA. If not, a Traditional IRA might be better for immediate tax deductions.
   - **Max Out Contributions**: Aim to max out your IRA contributions as well, assuming you meet income limits.

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - **Flexibility**: Useful for short-term goals or if you want more liquidity.
   - **Tax Efficiency**: Invest in tax-efficient funds (like index funds) to minimize capital gains tax.

### Suggested Distribution Strategy:
- **Prioritize Retirement Accounts**: Focus on maxing out the 401(k) and IRA contributions first.
- **Emergency Fund**: Ensure you have enough savings in a liquid account.
- **Taxable Accounts**: Once retirement contributions are maxed, invest any additional funds here.

### Additional Considerations:
- **Age and Risk Tolerance**: Younger investors might lean more towards growth stocks, while older investors may prefer more conservative investments.
- **Investment Mix**: Diversify across asset classes (stocks, bonds, real estate) to manage risk.

### Regular Review:
Periodically assess your allocations and adjust as necessary based on changes in income, expenses, or financial goals. 

Consider speaking with a financial advisor for personalized advice.
