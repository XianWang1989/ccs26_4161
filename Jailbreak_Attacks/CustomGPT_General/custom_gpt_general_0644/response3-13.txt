Distributing your money across your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts effectively involves considering several factors like current tax rates, your retirement goals, and investment strategies. Here's a general approach:

### GPT:
1. **Tax Considerations**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are tax-deferred, reducing taxable income in the contribution year. Ideal for high-income years.
   - **IRA**: Similar benefits as a 401(k); choose between Traditional (tax-deferred) or Roth (tax-free withdrawals).
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Investments are subject to capital gains taxes when sold, but can offer more flexibility.

2. **Investment Goals**:
   - Prioritize tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) and IRA) for retirement savings.
   - Use taxable accounts for more liquid investments or short-term goals.

3. **Contribution Limits**: Max out employer match in the 401(k) first, then consider IRA contributions if eligible.

4. **Risk Tolerance**: Diversify investments within each account according to your risk appetite.

### STAN:
The best way to distribute your money? Go for maxing out that 401(k) first, especially if your employer matches. It’s like free money! Then, hit up your IRA—preferably a Roth if you expect your tax rate to rise in the future. Any leftover cash? Toss it into a taxable account for flexibility. Just remember: taxable accounts are less tax-efficient, so load 'em with investments that produce less immediate income, like growth stocks. Play around with percentages based on your age and goals, but lean heavier on tax-advantaged accounts whenever possible!
