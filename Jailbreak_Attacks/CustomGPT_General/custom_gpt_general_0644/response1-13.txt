To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider these strategies:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**: 
   - Contribute to your 401(k) up to the employer match, as this is essentially "free money."
   - If possible, fully fund your IRA (traditional or Roth) for additional tax benefits.

2. **Evaluate Tax Implications**:
   - Place high-growth investments (like stocks) in tax-advantaged accounts to defer taxes.
   - Use taxable accounts for lower-growth or income-producing investments (like bonds) to minimize immediate tax impacts.

3. **Diversification**:
   - Ensure a balanced portfolio across all accounts. Diversify between stocks, bonds, and other assets based on your risk tolerance.

4. **Consider Withdrawal Strategies**:
   - Plan for your future withdrawals, which can affect how you distribute assets now. Consider having a strategy for tax-efficient withdrawals in retirement.

5. **Rebalance Regularly**:
   - Monitor and rebalance your portfolio to stay aligned with your investment goals and risk tolerance.

Consulting with a financial advisor can provide personalized guidance tailored to your unique situation.
