Certainly! Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here’s a general guideline:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**
   - **401(k):** Contribute enough to get any employer match. This is essentially free money.
   - **IRA:** If eligible, consider contributing to a Roth or Traditional IRA. A Roth allows for tax-free withdrawals in retirement, while a Traditional IRA may offer tax deductions upfront.

### 2. **Consider Tax Implications**
   - **Taxable Accounts:** After maximizing retirement accounts, use taxable accounts for investments that generate lower capital gains and qualify for lower long-term rates.
   - **Asset Location Strategy:** Hold more tax-efficient investments (like index funds) in taxable accounts, while placing higher-growth (and potentially higher tax) assets in tax-deferred accounts.

### 3. **Diversification and Balance**
   - Maintain a diversified portfolio across all accounts to mitigate risk, adapting to your risk tolerance and investment horizon.

### 4. **Review Regularly**
   - Regularly revisit your allocations to adjust for changes in goals, market conditions, or life events.

### 5. **Consult a Financial Advisor**
   - Consider speaking with a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your specific circumstances.

This framework should help you create a balanced distribution strategy for your investments!
