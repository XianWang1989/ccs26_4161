To effectively distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to take full advantage of any employer match. This is essentially free money and should be a priority.

2. **IRA Contributions**: If you're eligible, contribute to a traditional or Roth IRA. A Roth IRA offers tax-free withdrawals in retirement, while a traditional IRA might provide upfront tax deductions.

3. **Balance Contributions**: After maximizing the 401(k) match and contributing to an IRA, consider additional contributors to your 401(k). Depending on your income, you may want to focus on a Roth IRA or traditional IRA.

4. **Tax Efficiency**: Generally, keep more tax-efficient investments (like stocks) in taxable accounts, as they can benefit from lower capital gains tax rates. Place less tax-efficient investments (like bonds) in tax-advantaged accounts.

5. **Emergency Fund**: Ensure you have a sufficient emergency fund (typically 3-6 months of expenses) in a savings account before heavily investing in these accounts.

6. **Diversification**: Diversify your investments across all accounts to mitigate risk and enhance potential returns.

7. **Rebalance Regularly**: Periodically review and adjust your investments to align with your risk tolerance and investment goals.

Consulting with a financial advisor can further tailor these strategies to your specific situation.
