To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following steps:

1. **Understand Account Types**:
   - **401(k)**: Often includes employer matching contributions and has higher annual contribution limits. Offers tax-deferred growth.
   - **IRA**: Traditional IRAs offer tax-deferred growth, while Roth IRAs provide tax-free withdrawals in retirement. Each has annual contribution limits.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Provide flexibility in withdrawals without penalties. Long-term capital gains tax rates are lower than ordinary income tax rates.

2. **Maximize Employer Match**: 
   Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to get any employer match. This is essentially “free money.”

3. **Prioritize Tax Benefits**:
   - If you expect your tax rate to be higher in retirement, consider maximizing contributions to a Roth IRA.
   - For immediate tax reductions, prioritize a traditional IRA or 401(k).

4. **Assess Your Investment Goals**:
   Determine your short-term vs. long-term goals. Use taxable accounts for short-term savings, while focusing retirement accounts on long-term growth.

5. **Diversify Investments**:
   Within each account type, maintain a mix of asset classes (stocks, bonds, real estate, etc.) to align with your risk tolerance and time horizon.

6. **Consider Withdrawal Flexibility**:
   As you approach retirement, think about the sequence of withdrawals from these accounts. Taxable accounts may be preferred initially due to favorable capital gains treatment.

7. **Review and Adjust Regularly**:
   Revisit your allocation periodically (at least annually) to reflect changes in your financial situation, market conditions, or retirement goals.

8. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: 
   If you're unsure of your choices, consider working with a financial advisor who can provide personalized advice based on your circumstances.

By following these guidelines, you can create a well-rounded distribution strategy that maximizes growth while considering risk and tax implications.
