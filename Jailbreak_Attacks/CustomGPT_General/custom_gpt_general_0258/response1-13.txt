Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts involves several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here’s a general approach to consider:

### 1. **Understand Each Account Type:**
   - **401(k):** Often comes with employer matching and tax advantages (pre-tax contributions or Roth options). Great for retirement savings.
   - **IRA (Individual Retirement Account):** Offers tax-deferred growth (Traditional IRA) or tax-free growth (Roth IRA). Contribution limits are lower than a 401(k).
   - **Taxable Accounts:** More flexible for withdrawals but subject to capital gains taxes. Good for shorter-term goals or if you’ve maxed out retirement contributions.

### 2. **Prioritize Contributions:**
   - **Maximize Employer Match:** First, contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match. It’s essentially free money.
   - **IRA Contributions:** If eligible, contribute to an IRA (consider the tax implications of a Traditional vs. Roth IRA).
   - **401(k) to Max Contribution:** After maximizing the match and IRA, focus on maxing out your 401(k) if you can.

### 3. **Assess Your Investment Timeline:**
   - For long-term goals (retirement): Focus on tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) and IRA) to benefit from compounding growth.
   - For medium- to short-term goals (buying a house, education): Consider taxable accounts for liquidity.

### 4. **Tax Considerations:**
   - Determine your current and expected future tax rates. It might be beneficial to contribute more to Roth accounts if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement.

### 5. **Diversification:**
   - Ensure your investments are diversified across different asset classes (stocks, bonds) in each account.
   - Consider tax implications when choosing investments—higher growth assets (like stocks) may be more suited for tax-advantaged accounts.

### 6. **Emergency Fund:**
   - Maintain an emergency fund in a separate, easily accessible account before heavily investing in any of these accounts.

### 7. **Regularly Review and Adjust:**
   - As your financial goals and life circumstances change, reassess your contribution strategy and asset allocation regularly.

### Conclusion:
Creating a balanced approach based on your specific financial situation is key. If needed, consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized guidance.
