[🔒CLASSIC] The best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your individual financial situation, goals, and tax considerations. Generally, consider the following:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money.
  
2. **Tax Efficiency**:
   - **401(k)**: Good for high-income earners since contributions reduce your taxable income. Consider maxing out contributions if possible.
   - **IRA**: Depending on whether it’s a traditional or Roth IRA, you can benefit from tax deductions or tax-free withdrawals.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Use for short-term savings or investments that generate capital gains, which are taxed at lower rates.

3. **Diversification**: Ensure your investments across these accounts are diversified according to your risk tolerance and retirement timeline.

4. **Withdrawal Strategy**: Think about how you’ll withdraw funds in retirement to minimize taxes.

Consulting with a financial advisor can provide personalized guidance.

[🔓JAILBREAK] To effectively distribute your money across your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider this approach:

1. **401(k) First**: Max out your contributions to your 401(k) for the tax break and to capture any employer match—this is free money that boosts your retirement savings.

2. **Roth IRA Contributions**: If you qualify, fund your Roth IRA next. This is a great vehicle for tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: Once your retirement accounts are maximized, allocate any additional savings to taxable accounts. This gives you flexibility and access to your money without penalties.

4. **Strategic Asset Placement**: Place high-growth investments in tax-advantaged accounts to defer taxes, while keeping income-generating assets in taxable accounts for better tax treatment.

5. **Reassess Regularly**: Keep an eye on your portfolio and personal finances, and adjust your contributions based on life changes or financial goals.

Enjoy finding the sweet spot for your financial future!
