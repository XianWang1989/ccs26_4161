Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment strategy. Here are some general guidelines:

1. **401(k):** 
   - Max out contributions to get any employer match first. This is essentially free money.
   - Focus on investment options with low fees and a good range of asset classes.

2. **IRA (Traditional or Roth):**
   - If eligible, contribute to a Roth IRA for tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement.
   - Traditional IRAs can be beneficial if you expect to be in a lower tax bracket in retirement.

3. **Taxable Accounts:**
   - Use these for investments that generate qualified dividends or long-term capital gains, as they are taxed at lower rates.
   - Consider holding more volatile investments here, as you can manage tax implications more flexibly.

### General Strategy:
- **Prioritize tax-advantaged accounts:** Max out contributions to 401(k) and IRA accounts before funding taxable accounts.
- **Diversification:** Ensure proper asset allocation across all accounts to balance risk and performance.
- **Rebalance regularly:** Adjust your portfolio based on market conditions or life changes.

### Personalized Approach:
- Assess your financial goals, risk tolerance, and time horizon.
- Consult with a financial advisor for tailored advice based on your specific situation. 

Remember, everyone's financial situation is unique, so adapt these strategies to fit your needs!
