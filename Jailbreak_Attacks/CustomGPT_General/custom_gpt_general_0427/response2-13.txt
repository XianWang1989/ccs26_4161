To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategy:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to take full advantage of any employer match. This is essentially free money and should be prioritized.

2. **IRA Contributions**: After maximizing your 401(k) match, consider contributing to an IRA. Depending on your income, you may choose a traditional IRA (tax-deductible contributions) or a Roth IRA (tax-free withdrawals in retirement). Aim for the maximum limit for IRA contributions.

3. **Maximize 401(k) Contributions**: If you have additional funds after fully funding your IRA, increase your 401(k) contributions up to the annual limit. This allows for greater tax deferral and can be beneficial for high-income earners.

4. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**: If you’ve maxed out your retirement accounts but still have excess funds to invest, direct them to taxable accounts. This provides liquidity and the ability to access funds without penalties, though keep in mind the tax implications on capital gains.

5. **Asset Allocation**: Ensure that your investment choices reflect your risk tolerance and retirement timeline across all accounts. Diversifying investments can help balance risk and potential returns.

6. **Review and Adjust**: Regularly review your asset allocation and contributions as your financial situation or tax laws change. Adjust your strategy to meet your evolving retirement goals.

7. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: If unsure, consider consulting a financial advisor to tailor the best plan for your specific situation, especially for tax implications.

By following this structured approach, you can optimize your investment across different account types, balancing tax benefits and growth potential effectively.
