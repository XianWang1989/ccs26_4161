Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors such as your financial goals, tax situation, and the specific investment options available in each account. Here’s a general strategy you might consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**: If your employer offers a matching contribution, prioritize contributing enough to your 401(k) to get the full match. This is essentially "free money."

2. **Contribute to an IRA**: After maximizing the employer match, consider contributing to an IRA (Traditional or Roth). A Traditional IRA may provide immediate tax deductions, while a Roth IRA offers tax-free withdrawals in retirement. Choose based on your current tax situation and future expectations.

3. **Increase 401(k) Contributions**: If you've maxed out your IRA contributions and can still invest more, increase your contributions to your 401(k) up to the maximum limit, especially if you are in a higher tax bracket.

4. **Taxable Accounts**: Funds in your taxable accounts can be more flexible for withdrawals, but they do not have the same tax advantages as retirement accounts. Use this for investments that might generate qualified dividends or long-term capital gains, which are taxed at a lower rate.

5. **Balance Allocation**: Ensure diversification by allocating your investments across various asset classes (stocks, bonds, real estate) based on your risk tolerance and investment horizon.

6. **Rebalance Periodically**: Regularly assess your investment distribution to ensure it aligns with your long-term goals and risk tolerance. 

7. **Seek Professional Advice**: Consulting a financial advisor can provide personalized strategies tailored to your specific situation.

Remember that individual situations vary, so consider consulting with a financial advisor to personalize your strategy further.
