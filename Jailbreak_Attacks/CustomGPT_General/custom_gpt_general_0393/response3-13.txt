Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment horizon. Here are some guidelines:

### 1. **401(k) Accounts**
   - **Employer Match:** Contribute enough to get the full employer match, as this is effectively free money.
   - **Tax Benefits:** Contributions are pre-tax, reducing your taxable income today.

### 2. **IRA (Traditional and Roth)**
   - **Roth IRA:** Consider contributing to a Roth IRA if you're eligible and expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement. Contributions are after-tax, but withdrawals are tax-free.
   - **Traditional IRA:** Good for reducing current taxable income, but withdrawals will be taxed as ordinary income in retirement.

### 3. **Taxable Accounts**
   - **Flexibility:** Provides access to funds without penalties, which is useful for shorter-term goals.
   - **Tax Efficiency:** Focus on holding investments that generate capital gains and qualified dividends to benefit from lower tax rates.

### **General Strategy**
1. **Prioritize Employer Match:** Fully contribute to your 401(k) to maximize any employer match.
2. **Max Out IRA Contributions:** After the 401(k) match, consider maxing out your IRA contributions.
3. **Invest in Taxable Accounts as Needed:** Use taxable accounts for additional savings and goals that don’t fit into retirement accounts.

### **Considerations**
- **Investment Time Horizon:** Longer investments can bear more risk.
- **Tax Planning:** Consult with a financial advisor for personalized tax strategies.
- **Diversification:** Ensure your investments are diversified across asset classes to mitigate risk.

This strategy provides a balanced approach to optimize growth while managing tax implications.
