Distributing your money effectively among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some guidelines:

### 1. **Prioritize Contributions:**
   - **401(k) Match:** If your employer offers matching contributions, prioritize maximizing this benefit first. It's essentially free money.
   - **IRA Contributions:** After maximizing any match, consider contributing to a Traditional or Roth IRA, depending on your income level and tax situation.

### 2. **Account Types:**
   - **Tax-Advantaged Accounts (401(k) and IRA):** These accounts grow tax-deferred (Traditional) or tax-free (Roth). 
   - **Taxable Accounts:** These accounts do not have tax advantages, so consider holding assets that generate lower capital gains or qualify for long-term capital gains rates, such as index funds or ETFs.

### 3. **Diversification:**
   - Ensure your investments across all accounts are diversified. You may choose to hold different asset classes in each account, but aim for an overall balanced portfolio.

### 4. **Tax Planning:**
   - Keep higher-growth assets in your tax-advantaged accounts to maximize tax efficiency.
   - Consider the tax implications of withdrawals from your Traditional IRA and 401(k) in retirement.

### 5. **Assess Your Goals:**
   - Factor in your retirement age, risk tolerance, and financial goals to determine how aggressively to invest and balance your allocations.

### 6. **Rebalance Regularly:**
   - Periodically review and rebalance your portfolio to ensure it aligns with your risk tolerance and investment goals.

### Conclusion:
It's often wise to consult a financial advisor for a personalized strategy based on your specific financial situation. This way, you can make informed decisions about how much to allocate to each account type.
