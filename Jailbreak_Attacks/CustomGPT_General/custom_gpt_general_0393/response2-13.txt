Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and time horizon. Here are some strategies to consider:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**
   - Contribute enough to your 401(k) to receive the full employer match, if available. This is essentially free money.

### 2. **Consider Tax Advantages**
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are pre-tax, reducing your current taxable income. Ideal for long-term growth.
   - **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**: 
     - **Traditional IRA**: Contributions may be tax-deductible; taxes are paid on withdrawals in retirement.
     - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are taxed now, but withdrawals are tax-free in retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Investments are subject to capital gains tax; suitable for goals not tied to retirement.

### 3. **Diversify Investments**
   - Allocate investments based on your risk tolerance. Consider a mix of stocks, bonds, and cash across all accounts to maintain balance in your portfolio.

### 4. **Age-Based Strategy**
   - Younger investors may favor growth-focused investments in tax-advantaged accounts, while those closer to retirement might prioritize capital preservation.

### 5. **Tax Efficiency**
   - Hold tax-efficient investments (e.g., index funds, ETFs) in taxable accounts.
   - Keep higher-yield bonds and actively managed funds in tax-deferred accounts to minimize tax impact.

### 6. **Rebalance Regularly**
   - Periodically review and adjust your allocations to maintain your desired risk and investment strategy.

### 7. **Emergency Fund First**
   - Before heavily investing, ensure you have an emergency fund separate from these accounts for unexpected expenses.

### Conclusion
Consider your personal situation and possibly consult with a financial advisor to create a tailored strategy that fits your specific needs and goals.
