The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment strategy. Here are some general guidelines to consider:

1. **401(k)**: 
   - **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to get the full employer match if available; this is essentially free money.
   - **Tax Advantages**: Contributions are usually pre-tax, meaning you can reduce your taxable income. Depending on your situation, consider maximizing contributions, especially if you're in a higher tax bracket now.

2. **IRA (Traditional/Roth)**:
   - **Tax Implications**: Traditional IRAs may provide tax deductions, while Roth IRAs offer tax-free withdrawals in retirement.
   - **Contribution Limits**: Pay attention to annual contribution limits and consider a Roth IRA if you anticipate being in a higher tax bracket in retirement or want tax-free income.

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - **Liquidity and Flexibility**: Taxable accounts are more flexible for withdrawals and can be used for short-term goals. 
   - **Capital Gains**: Consider the tax implications of investments in a taxable account; long-term capital gains are taxed at a lower rate than ordinary income.

### Recommendation Strategy:
- **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**: Start by maximizing contributions to your 401(k) and IRAs (especially to receive employer matches and tax benefits).
- **Diversify Investments**: Within these accounts, maintain a diversified portfolio that aligns with your risk tolerance and investment timeline.
- **Use Taxable Accounts for Additional Savings**: Once you've maximized tax-advantaged accounts, consider investing in taxable accounts, focusing on tax-efficient investments such as index funds or ETFs.

### Additional Considerations:
- Assess your overall financial plan: Ensure that your distribution strategy aligns with your short-term and long-term financial goals.
- Consult a financial advisor: For personalized advice tailored to your specific financial situation, it might be beneficial to consult with a financial advisor.

This approach helps minimize your tax burden while effectively growing your investments over time.
