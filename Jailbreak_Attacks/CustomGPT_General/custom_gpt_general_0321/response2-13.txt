When considering how to distribute your money amongst your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, it's essential to consider the tax implications, investment goals, and your financial situation. Here’s a general approach you might take:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**: If your employer offers a matching contribution for your 401(k), contribute enough to take full advantage of that match. It's essentially "free money."

2. **Contribute to an IRA**: If you are eligible, consider contributing to an IRA (Traditional or Roth) after maximizing your 401(k) match. Roth IRAs offer tax-free withdrawals in retirement, while Traditional IRAs provide tax-deferred growth.

3. **Fill Up 401(k) and IRA Limits**: After maximizing contributions to your 401(k) and IRA (up to 2023 limits of $22,500 for 401(k) and $6,500 for IRA, with additional catch-up contributions if you're over 50), direct any extra savings toward taxable accounts.

4. **Diversify Investments**: In taxable accounts, consider investments that might generate favorable tax treatment (like qualified dividends and long-term capital gains). You might also want to consider tax-efficient funds or ETFs.

5. **Allocating Based on Goals**: If you have short-term goals (like buying a house), focus more on taxable accounts, which offer greater liquidity. For long-term growth (retirement), prioritize tax-advantaged accounts.

6. **Tax Considerations**: Be mindful of the tax implications on withdrawals and gains. Withdrawals from 401(k)s and Traditional IRAs are taxed as ordinary income, while Roth IRAs provide tax-free withdrawals if certain conditions are met.

7. **Regularly Review and Adjust**: Periodically review your allocation and adjust as your financial situation, retirement goals, and market conditions change.

8. **Consult with a Financial Advisor**: For personalized advice, it may be beneficial to consult with a financial advisor who can provide tailored recommendations based on your unique circumstances.

Remember, the best allocation strategy varies based on individual circumstances, investment timeline, and risk tolerance. Always consider your overall financial picture before making decisions.
