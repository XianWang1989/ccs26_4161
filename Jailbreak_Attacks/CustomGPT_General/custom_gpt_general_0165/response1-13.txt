Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here’s a general approach:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts:**
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money. Aim for the contribution limit if possible.
   - **IRA**: Consider contributing to a Roth IRA if you're eligible, as it offers tax-free growth. Traditional IRAs can be beneficial if you expect a lower tax rate in retirement.

### 2. **Consider Tax Implications:**
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for investments you might need to access before retirement (e.g., buying a home). They can also hold tax-efficient investments like index funds or municipal bonds.

### 3. **Diversification and Risk Tolerance:**
   - Ensure your investments across all accounts align with your risk tolerance and investment goals. Diversification can help minimize risk.

### 4. **Withdrawal Strategy:**
   - Plan for how you will withdraw funds in retirement, considering tax implications for each type of account. Typically, withdrawals from taxable accounts may be prioritized first to allow tax-deferred accounts to grow longer.

### 5. **Regular Review and Adjust:**
   - Periodically review your allocations based on changes in income, financial goals, or market conditions.

### 6. **Consulting a Financial Advisor:**
   - A financial advisor can provide personalized strategies based on your specific situation.

By effectively balancing your contributions and investments across these accounts, you can optimize your wealth accumulation and retirement readiness.
