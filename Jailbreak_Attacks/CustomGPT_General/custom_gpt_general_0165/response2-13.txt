Distributing your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your age, financial goals, tax situation, and investment preferences. Here’s a general strategy:

1. **Maximize 401(k) Contributions**: If your employer offers a matching contribution, aim to contribute enough to capture the full match first. This is essentially free money.

2. **Fund an IRA**: Consider contributing to an IRA (Traditional or Roth) next. A Roth IRA offers tax-free growth and withdrawals, which can be beneficial if you expect your tax rate to be higher in retirement.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: After maximizing your contributions to tax-advantaged accounts, consider using taxable accounts for additional investments. This can provide more flexibility with withdrawals and no contribution limits.

4. **Asset Allocation**: Keep in mind the types of investments in each account. For instance, put higher-growth investments (like stocks) in tax-advantaged accounts to maximize tax efficiency, while placing income-generating assets (like bonds) in taxable accounts.

5. **Reevaluate Regularly**: Periodically review your accounts and rebalance your portfolio based on changes in your goals, financial situation, or market conditions.

It's wise to consult with a financial advisor to tailor this strategy to your specific circumstances.
