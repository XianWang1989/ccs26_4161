Distributing your money across a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax considerations, and investment preferences. Here’s a strategic approach:

1. **Maximize Employer Matching**: Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to receive any available employer match. This is essentially free money.

2. **Max Out Tax-Advantaged Accounts**: Once you’re getting the full match, consider maxing out contributions to an IRA (Traditional or Roth, depending on your eligibility and long-term tax strategy). For 2024, the IRA limit is $6,500 ($7,500 if you're over 50).

3. **Focus on 401(k) Contributions**: After funding your IRA, continue contributing to your 401(k) up to its annual limit ($23,000 for 2024, $30,500 if age 50 or older).

4. **Invest in Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**: Any extra funds can be placed in taxable accounts. This gives you flexibility and access without penalties, but you’ll face capital gains taxes when you sell.

5. **Asset Allocation**: Consider placing high-growth investments (like stocks) in tax-advantaged accounts to take advantage of tax-deferred or tax-free growth. Use taxable accounts for investments that generate regular income (like bonds) to manage tax impacts.

6. **Review Tax Implications**: Monitor your tax situation annually. Taxable accounts are subject to capital gains tax, while retirement accounts grow tax-deferred or tax-free.

7. **Consider Your Risk Tolerance and Time Horizon**: Adjust your investment choices based on how long you have until retirement and your risk tolerance.

8. **Rebalance Regularly**: Periodically reassess and rebalance your portfolio to maintain your desired asset allocation and account for market changes.

This strategy provides a balanced approach to growth and tax efficiency while keeping your financial goals in mind. Always consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized advice.
