Distributing your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and time horizon. Here are some general guidelines to consider:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**
   - **Contribution**: If your employer offers a 401(k) match, aim to contribute enough to get the full match. This is essentially free money.

### 2. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**
   - **IRA (Traditional/Roth)**: If you're eligible, consider maxing out your IRA contributions next. Traditional IRAs offer tax deductions, while Roth IRAs provide tax-free withdrawals in retirement.
   - **401(k) Contributions**: After the employer match, continue to contribute to your 401(k) up to the annual limit.

### 3. **Taxable Accounts**
   - **Invest Wisely**: If you have maxed out contributions in tax-advantaged accounts, you can invest in taxable accounts. Be mindful of capital gains taxes; consider using tax-efficient funds or strategies (e.g., tax-loss harvesting).

### 4. **Consider Your Tax Bracket**
   - Higher earners may benefit more from tax-deferred accounts, while lower earners might prioritize Roth accounts for tax-free growth.

### 5. **Assess Liquidity Needs**
   - Taxable accounts can provide more liquidity since withdrawals from retirement accounts often come with penalties if accessed early. Ensure you maintain an emergency fund outside these accounts.

### 6. **Diversify Investments**
   - Ensure your investment choices across accounts are diversified according to your risk tolerance and goals. For instance, you might choose more aggressive investments in tax-advantaged accounts.

### 7. **Review and Adjust Regularly**
   - Financial situations change, so regularly review your distribution strategy to align with life changes, economic conditions, and shifts in your financial goals.

### Example Strategy:
- First, contribute enough to your 401(k) to receive the employer match.
- Next, contribute to your IRA (maximize if you can).
- Then, focus on maxing out your 401(k) contributions.
- Finally, allocate funds to taxable accounts for additional investment.

### Consultation
Consider working with a financial advisor for personalized strategies based on your unique situation.
