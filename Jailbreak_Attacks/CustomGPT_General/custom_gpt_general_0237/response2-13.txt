When it comes to distributing your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, the best approach often depends on several factors, including your financial goals, risk tolerance, tax situation, and time horizon. Here are some guidelines to consider for each account type:

1. **401(k)**:
   - **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a matching contribution, try to contribute at least enough to get the full match; this is essentially free money.
   - **Consider Contribution Limits**: For 2023, the contribution limit is $22,500 (or $30,000 if you're age 50 or older). Maxing out your contributions can provide significant tax benefits.

2. **IRA (Traditional and Roth)**:
   - **Determine Eligibility**: Depending on your income, you may be eligible for a tax-deductible contribution to a Traditional IRA or a tax-free withdrawal from a Roth IRA. 
   - **Max Out Contributions**: For 2023, the limit is $6,500 (or $7,500 for those 50 and older). Try to max out your contributions if possible.
   - **Choose the Right Type**: A Traditional IRA can reduce taxable income now, while a Roth IRA offers tax-free growth. The choice depends on your current and expected future tax rates.

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - **Invest for Flexibility**: Taxable accounts provide liquidity and flexibility so that you can access funds without penalties. 
   - **Tax-Effected Investments**: Consider using tax-efficient investments like index funds or ETFs that generate fewer capital gains distributions. Long-term capital gains are taxed at lower rates than ordinary income, so holding investments for over a year can be beneficial.

**General Strategy**:
- Allocate funds based on your time horizon: Longer-term investments can go into tax-advantaged accounts (like 401(k) and IRA), while shorter-term funds may be better suited for taxable accounts.
- Diversify across both types of accounts: Maintaining a mix of investments (stocks, bonds, etc.) can help manage risk and volatility.
- Review and Adjust: Regularly review your portfolio to ensure it aligns with changes in financial goals, market conditions, and tax laws.

It's often beneficial to consult with a financial advisor to create a personalized strategy tailored to your individual circumstances.
