Distributing your money across a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, risk tolerance, and tax situation. Here are some strategies to consider:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**:
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match. This is free money.
   - **IRA**: Consider maximizing contributions to an IRA (Traditional or Roth) after your 401(k) match. 

2. **Evaluate Tax Implications**:
   - **401(k)** and **Traditional IRA** generally offer tax deductions upfront, but withdrawals in retirement are taxed as ordinary income.
   - **Roth IRA** contributions are made with after-tax money, but withdrawals are tax-free in retirement.

3. **Diversify Your Investments**:
   - Use your taxable accounts for investments that may generate taxable income, such as stocks or index funds with low turnover.
   - Keep bonds and real estate in tax-advantaged accounts to minimize the impact of interest income and capital gains.

4. **Consider Your Time Horizon**:
   - If you have a long time until retirement, consider more aggressive, growth-oriented investments in your tax-advantaged accounts.
   - For shorter-term needs, keep more liquid assets in taxable accounts.

5. **Monitor and Adjust**:
   - Regularly review your asset allocation and adjust contributions based on performance, market conditions, and changes in your financial goals.

Consulting with a financial advisor can help tailor this strategy to your specific situation.
