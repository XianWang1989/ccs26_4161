Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment horizon. Here’s a general approach:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a match on your 401(k), contribute enough to at least receive the full match. This is essentially free money.

2. **Contribute to an IRA**: After maximizing your 401(k) match, consider contributing to a Traditional or Roth IRA. A Roth IRA offers tax-free growth and tax-free withdrawals in retirement, but contributions are made with after-tax dollars. A Traditional IRA provides tax-deferred growth, with contributions potentially tax-deductible.

3. **Maximize 401(k) Contributions**: Once you've contributed to an IRA, continue to contribute to your 401(k) up to the annual limit if your cash flow allows. This provides tax-deferred growth.

4. **Taxable Accounts**: Use taxable accounts for investments that may have short-term goals or for additional savings outside of tax-advantaged accounts. Consider tax-efficient investments (like index funds or ETFs) to minimize capital gains taxes.

5. **Diversification and Asset Allocation**: Ensure your investments are diversified across asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) to match your risk tolerance and investment goals. Maintain a consistent asset allocation strategy across all accounts.

6. **Rebalance Regularly**: Monitor and rebalance your portfolio periodically to maintain your desired asset allocation.

### Conclusion

Ultimately, the best distribution will vary based on your specific financial situation, so consider consulting with a financial advisor for tailored advice.
