The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some general strategies to consider:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**:
   - **401(k)**: If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to get the full match before allocating funds elsewhere. This is essentially “free money.”
   - **IRA**: Consider contributing to a traditional or Roth IRA. A Roth IRA offers tax-free withdrawals in retirement, while a traditional IRA provides tax deductions now.

### 2. **Understand Tax Implications**:
   - **Tax-Deferred Accounts (401(k), Traditional IRA)**: Contributions reduce your taxable income now, but withdrawals in retirement are taxed as ordinary income.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Investments in taxable accounts allow for more flexibility and can be taxed at a lower capital gains rate for long-term investments.

### 3. **Consider Your Retirement Timeline**:
   - If you are years away from retirement, focus on maximizing growth in tax-advantaged accounts.
   - As you approach retirement, consider reallocating to more conservative investments.

### 4. **Diversification and Risk Management**:
   - Ensure you have a diversified investment strategy across all accounts according to your risk tolerance.
   - Different accounts may benefit from different asset allocations.

### 5. **Emergency Fund and Liquid Assets**:
   - Maintain an emergency fund in a high-yield savings account for immediate needs, separate from retirement accounts.

### Sample Distribution Approach:
- **401(k)**: Up to the match.
- **IRA**: Max out contributions.
- **Taxable Account**: Allocate remaining funds after maxing out retirement accounts.

### Long-Term Planning:
- Regularly review and adjust your contributions based on changes in income, financial goals, and market conditions.

Consulting with a financial advisor can help tailor a strategy specific to your situation.
