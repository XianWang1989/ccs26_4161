Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment horizon. Here’s a general guideline:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your 401(k) offers an employer match, contribute at least enough to get the full match, as this is essentially free money.

2. **Tax Considerations**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are pre-tax, reducing your taxable income. Taxes are due upon withdrawal. Consider this account if you expect to be in a lower tax bracket during retirement.
   - **Traditional IRA**: Similar to a 401(k) for tax benefits. If you qualify, contribute to this account as well, especially if you have higher income and want the tax deduction.
   - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, allowing for tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement. If you anticipate being in a higher tax bracket later, prioritize this option.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for short-term goals or if you’ve maxed out tax-advantaged accounts. Investments here are subject to capital gains tax on profits.

3. **Investment Horizon**:
   - For long-term goals (retirement), prioritize tax-advantaged accounts (401(k), IRA).
   - For medium-term goals (house down payment, etc.), consider a mix of taxable and tax-advantaged accounts.

4. **Asset Allocation**: Diversify within each account based on risk tolerance and time frame. Stocks may be more suitable for long-term accounts, while bonds might be better for short-term needs.

5. **Review Regularly**: Regularly assess and rebalance your portfolio to adjust for market changes and ensure alignment with your goals.

Consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your specific circumstances.
