The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here's a general approach to consider:

### 1. **Maximize Retirement Accounts First**
   - **401(k):** If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to get the full match. It's essentially "free money."
   - **IRA:** Contribute to a Traditional or Roth IRA. A Roth IRA offers tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement, while Traditional IRAs provide tax deductions upfront.

### 2. **Consider Tax Implications**
   - **Tax-Deferred Accounts:** 401(k) and Traditional IRA allow your money to grow tax-deferred, which is beneficial if you expect to be in a lower tax bracket in retirement.
   - **Tax-Free Accounts:** Roth IRAs provide tax-free growth and withdrawals, which can be beneficial for tax management in retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Investments in taxable accounts can provide liquidity and flexibility but may incur taxes on capital gains and dividends.

### 3. **Diversification of Investments**
   - **Asset Allocation:** Diversify your investments across different asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) based on your risk tolerance and investment horizon.
   - **Real Estate/Other Investments:** Consider including alternative investments for additional diversification if appropriate for your financial situation.

### 4. **Regular Review and Adjustments**
   - Assess your investment performance periodically and adjust contributions or asset allocations as needed based on changes in goals, market conditions, or life circumstances.

### 5. **Consult a Financial Advisor**
   - Depending on your circumstances, a professional can provide personalized advice tailored to your specific financial situation and goals.

### Suggested Contribution Strategy
- **Max out employer match in 401(k)**
- **Contribute to IRA (up to the annual limit)**
- **Invest in taxable accounts as necessary for flexibility**

This structure helps you benefit from tax advantages while also maintaining access to capital when needed.
