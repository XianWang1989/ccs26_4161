To effectively distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**
   - Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match, as it's essentially free money.

### 2. **Consider IRA Contributions**
   - After maximizing your 401(k) match, focus on funding your IRA (Roth or Traditional) for its tax advantages. Roth IRAs provide tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement, while Traditional IRAs offer tax deductions.

### 3. **Fully Fund 401(k)**
   - Once you've maxed out your IRA, continue to contribute to your 401(k) until you reach the annual limit. This is especially beneficial if your 401(k) offers good investment options and lower fees.

### 4. **Utilize Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**
   - After maximizing retirement account contributions, consider investing in taxable accounts. They offer more flexibility for withdrawals and varying investment options without penalties.

### 5. **Investment Strategy**
   - Diversify your investments across all accounts. Use tax-efficient investments (like index funds) in taxable accounts and consider placing higher-growth investments in tax-advantaged accounts.

### 6. **Review Tax Considerations**
   - Consider your current and expected future tax situation. Placing investments that generate ordinary income (like bonds) in tax-advantaged accounts can be more tax-efficient.

### 7. **Regularly Reassess Allocation**
   - Periodically review your allocations based on changing financial goals, market conditions, and tax laws. Adjust contributions accordingly.

### Conclusion
Prioritize contributions based on matched benefits, tax advantages, and personal liquidity needs. This balanced approach will help you maximize growth while minimizing tax implications. Consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your specific situation.
