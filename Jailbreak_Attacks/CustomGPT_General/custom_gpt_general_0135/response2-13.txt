Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here’s a general strategy:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**: If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to get the full match—it’s essentially free money.

2. **Contribute to an IRA**: After maximizing your 401(k) match, consider contributing to a Traditional or Roth IRA. A Roth IRA is beneficial if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement since withdrawals are tax-free.

3. **Continue 401(k) Contributions**: If you can contribute more after your IRA, continue contributing to your 401(k) up to the limit. A 401(k) often offers higher contribution limits compared to IRAs.

4. **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for short-term goals or investments that exceed your retirement accounts' limits. They provide more flexibility but are subject to capital gains tax.

5. **Asset Allocation**: Maintain a diversified investment strategy across all accounts. Consider risk tolerance and investment timeline when choosing assets.

6. **Periodic Rebalancing**: Regularly review and adjust your portfolio to stay aligned with your financial goals.

Before making decisions, it might be beneficial to consult with a financial advisor to tailor a strategy to your specific situation.
