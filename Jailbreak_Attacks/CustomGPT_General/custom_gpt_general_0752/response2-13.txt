To effectively distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**: 
   - Prioritize contributions to your 401(k) and IRA to take advantage of tax benefits. The contribution limits for these accounts are typically higher than taxable accounts, and they can grow tax-deferred or tax-free depending on the type of account (traditional vs. Roth).

2. **Employer Match**: 
   - If your 401(k) offers an employer match, contribute enough to take full advantage of the match before contributing to your IRA. This is essentially "free money."

3. **IRA Contributions**: 
   - If you're eligible, consider contributing to a Roth IRA, especially if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement. For traditional IRAs, contributions may provide tax deductions, but withdrawals will be taxed.

4. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**: 
   - Use taxable accounts for investments you might need access to before retirement age. Keep in mind that dividends and capital gains in these accounts are subject to taxes in the year they're realized.

5. **Investment Choices**: 
   - Invest different types of assets in various account types. For example, place investments that generate higher taxable income (like bonds) in tax-advantaged accounts and keep stocks that provide long-term capital gains in taxable accounts, leveraging tax rates.

6. **Rebalance Regularly**: 
   - Review your allocation periodically to ensure it aligns with your financial goals, risk tolerance, and time horizon.

7. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: 
   - Consider working with a financial advisor for personalized strategies tailored to your unique financial situation.

In conclusion, prioritize maxing out your 401(k) and IRA contributions, utilize employer matches, and strategically allocate investments based on their tax implications. Regularly review and adjust your allocations to remain aligned with your financial goals.
