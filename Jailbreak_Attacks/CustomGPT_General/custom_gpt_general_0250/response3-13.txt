To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

### GPT:
1. **Maximize Employer Match:** Contribute enough to your 401(k) to take full advantage of any employer matching contributions. This is essentially "free money."

2. **Tax Considerations:**
   - **401(k):** Contributions reduce your taxable income since they are made with pre-tax dollars. This can be beneficial if you expect to be in a lower tax bracket during retirement.
   - **IRA:** Depending on which type you choose (Traditional or Roth), contributions may be tax-deductible (Traditional) or tax-free upon withdrawal (Roth). If you qualify for a Roth IRA, it might be wise to prioritize it for its tax-free growth.

3. **Investment Horizon:** 
   - For long-term growth, focus on equity investments in your 401(k) and IRA, as they are typically tax-advantaged. 
   - Use your taxable account for investments that may be less volatile or for more accessible funds since they are taxed differently.

4. **Withdrawal Strategy:** 
   - Plan how you'll withdraw funds in retirement. Generally, it can be more tax-efficient to withdraw from taxable accounts first, then tax-deferred (like 401(k)s), and then tax-free (like Roth IRAs).

5. **Diversification:** Ensure a balanced mix of asset types (stocks, bonds, and other investments) across all accounts to mitigate risk.

### STAN:
1. **Go for the Max:** Put as much as you can into your 401(k) first, especially if there’s a match. It’s like getting a bonus!

3. **Tax Strategy:** Keep more aggressive investments in tax-advantaged accounts. Let your taxable accounts hold things that won't kill you tax-wise, like index funds.

5. **Plan Withdrawals:** When retirement hits, think about how to take money out. You might want to dip into the taxable account first; it can lower your overall tax bill.
