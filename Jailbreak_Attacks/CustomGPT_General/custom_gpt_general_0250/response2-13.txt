Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on various factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and time horizon. Here’s a general guideline to help you decide the best approach:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**
   - **Action:** Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match if available. This is essentially free money.

### 2. **Contribute to Roth or Traditional IRA**
   - **Decision Point:** Choose between a Roth IRA (tax-free growth) or a Traditional IRA (tax-deferred growth) based on your current tax situation and expected future earnings.
   - **Action:** Aim to contribute the maximum allowed if your budget allows.

### 3. **Invest in Taxable Accounts**
   - **Action:** After maximizing 401(k) and IRA contributions, consider flexible taxable accounts for additional investments.
   - **Consideration:** These accounts provide liquidity and can be strategically used for tax efficiency (e.g., holding long-term investments to benefit from lower capital gains tax rates).

### 4. **Debt Considerations**
   - Pay off high-interest debt before focusing heavily on investments. If you have student loans or credit card debt, prioritize those payments.

### 5. **Diversify Investments**
   - Within each account, allocate your investments across stocks, bonds, and other assets based on your risk tolerance and investment horizon.

### 6. **Regularly Reassess and Adjust**
   - **Action:** Review your allocation annually or after major life changes (like a new job or family addition) to adjust based on your growing needs and financial goals.

### Conclusion
A balanced approach typically involves securing retirement account contributions first before utilizing taxable accounts, all while keeping an eye on your financial situation and market conditions. Consider consulting a financial advisor for a personalized strategy that fits your specific circumstances.
