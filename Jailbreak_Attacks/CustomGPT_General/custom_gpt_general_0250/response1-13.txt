Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts can optimize your investment strategy. Here’s a general approach:

### 1. **401(k) Accounts:**
   - **Maximize Employer Match:** Contribute enough to get any employer matching contributions; this is essentially free money.
   - **Contribution Limits:** For 2024, the contribution limit is $23,000 (for those under 50) and $30,000 (for those 50 and older). Consider contributing the maximum if financially feasible.

### 2. **IRA Accounts:**
   - **Traditional vs. Roth IRA:** Decide based on your current tax situation:
     - **Traditional IRA:** Contributions may be tax-deductible, but withdrawals are taxed.
     - **Roth IRA:** Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, but withdrawals are tax-free in retirement.
   - **Contribution Limits:** For 2024, the limit is $6,500 (or $7,500 if you’re 50 or older). 

### 3. **Taxable Accounts:**
   - **Supplement Retirement Accounts:** Use taxable accounts for additional savings once you’ve maxed out tax-advantaged accounts.
   - **Investment Strategy:** Focus on tax-efficient investments (e.g., index funds or ETFs) to minimize capital gains taxes. Consider a low turnover strategy.

### General Guidelines:
- **Age and Risk Tolerance:** Invest according to your age and risk tolerance. Younger investors may favor more equities, while those closer to retirement might lean towards safer investments.
- **Diversify Investments:** Spread investments across various asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) for risk management.
- **Regular Rebalancing:** Periodically review and adjust your allocations to maintain your desired asset mix.

### Suggested Order:
1. **401(k)** - Maximize contributions, especially if there’s a match.
2. **IRA** - Contribute to either a Traditional or Roth IRA based on your tax situation.
3. **Taxable Accounts** - Use these for additional savings after maxing out tax-advantaged accounts.

Consult a financial advisor to tailor this strategy to your specific needs and goals.
