To effectively distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**: Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to take advantage of any employer match, as this is essentially free money.

2. **IRA Contributions**: If you're eligible, contribute to a Traditional or Roth IRA, depending on your tax situation. A Roth IRA allows tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement, while a Traditional IRA gives you potential tax deductions now.

3. **Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**: After maximizing contributions to tax-advantaged accounts, consider putting additional funds into a taxable brokerage account. This offers more flexibility for withdrawals and investment choices.

4. **Asset Allocation**: Consider the nature of investments in each account. For instance, hold more tax-efficient investments (like index funds or ETFs) in your taxable accounts and tax-inefficient investments (like high-yield bonds or REITs) in your retirement accounts.

5. **Regular Review**: Regularly review your investment performance and adjust your contributions accordingly. Rebalancing between accounts can help maintain your desired asset allocation.

6. **Long-Term Growth**: Focus on long-term growth in your accounts, taking on appropriate risk for your age and retirement timeline.

Always consult with a financial advisor to tailor these strategies to your personal circumstances and goals.
