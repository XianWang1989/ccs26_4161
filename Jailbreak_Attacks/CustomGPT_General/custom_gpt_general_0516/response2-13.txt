Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your current financial situation, retirement goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here’s a general strategy to consider:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Contributions**
   - **401(k)**: If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to get the full match. This is essentially free money and should be a priority.

### 2. **Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**
   - **IRA**: After maximizing your 401(k) match, consider contributing to an IRA. Traditional IRAs may provide a tax deduction, while Roth IRAs offer tax-free withdrawals in retirement. Choose based on your current vs. projected future tax situation.
   - Contribution Limits: Keep in mind annual limits (e.g., $6,500 for IRAs in 2023, $7,500 if you’re 50 or older).

### 3. **Continue with 401(k) Contributions**
   - **Max Out**: Aim to contribute as much as you can to your 401(k) after funding your IRA. The 2023 limit is $22,500 ($30,000 if over 50).

### 4. **Taxable Accounts for Additional Flexibility**
   - If you've maxed out your tax-advantaged accounts and have extra funds, consider a taxable account for additional investments. These can provide flexibility for short-term goals and are taxed differently.
   - **Tax Management**: Be mindful of capital gains tax. Consider tax-efficient investments like index funds or ETFs to minimize tax impact.

### 5. **Consider Your Investment Strategy**
   - **Asset Allocation**: Diversify your investments across your accounts based on your risk tolerance, investment goals, and timeline. Generally, higher-risk investments can be placed in tax-advantaged accounts to maximize tax benefits.

### 6. **Evaluate Regularly**
   - Reassess your portfolio regularly to ensure it aligns with your goals. Adjust contributions as your financial situation changes.

### Summary
1. Get the full 401(k) match.
2. Fund your IRA (consider Traditional vs. Roth).
3. Maximize your 401(k) contributions.
4. Use taxable accounts for additional investments.
5. Regularly evaluate your investment strategy.

Consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your unique financial situation.
