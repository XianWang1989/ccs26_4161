Distributing your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts is crucial for maximizing growth and minimizing taxes. Here’s a general guideline to help you decide:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If you have a 401(k) with an employer match, contribute enough to get the full match first. This is essentially free money.

2. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**: 
   - **401(k)**: If you have a good plan with low fees and desirable investment choices, continue contributing. Consider traditional or Roth options depending on your tax situation.
   - **IRA**: After maxing out the employer match, consider contributing to an IRA. A Roth IRA offers tax-free growth, but may have income limits. A traditional IRA offers tax-deductible contributions, depending on your income.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: Funds in taxable accounts can be used for short-term goals or emergencies. However, they are subject to capital gains taxes. Focus on low-turnover investments (like index funds) to minimize tax exposure.

4. **Emergency Fund**: Ensure you have an emergency fund (3-6 months of living expenses) outside of these accounts for unexpected expenses before heavily investing.

5. **Asset Allocation**: Within these accounts, ensure your investments align with your risk tolerance, time horizon, and goals. Diversify your investments across stocks, bonds, and other assets.

6. **Rebalance**: Regularly review and adjust your contributions and investments to maintain your desired asset allocation.

### Sample Allocation Strategy:

- **401(k)**: 15%-20% of your salary until maximum match (then evaluate).
- **IRA**: Max out the contribution limit ($6,500 for 2023, or $7,500 if over 50).
- **Taxable Accounts**: Use any additional savings for tax-efficient investments.

### Seek Professional Advice:

Consider consulting a financial advisor for a personalized strategy tailored to your specific financial situation.
