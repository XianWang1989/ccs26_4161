Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts effectively depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some strategies to consider:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:  
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get the full employer match, as it's essentially "free money." Aim to max out contributions if possible (up to $22,500 for 2023, or $30,000 if you're over 50).
   - **IRA**: If you're eligible, consider contributing to a Traditional IRA or Roth IRA, depending on your tax situation. The contribution limit for 2023 is $6,500, or $7,500 if you’re over 50.

### 2. **Assess Your Tax Situation**:  
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for investments that may generate taxable income (like dividend stocks or bonds) if you're in a lower tax bracket or need access to funds without penalties.
   - **Roth IRA**: If you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement, prioritizing Roth contributions might be beneficial since withdrawals are tax-free in retirement.

### 3. **Prioritize Emergency Savings**:  
   Ensure you have an emergency fund in a high-yield savings account or similar before investing heavily in other accounts.

### 4. **Consider Investment Goals and Timeline**:  
   - Short-term goals might be better served by taxable accounts, whereas long-term goals can benefit from the compounded growth in tax-advantaged accounts.
  
### 5. **Maintain a Balanced Portfolio**:  
   Regardless of which account you’re investing in, diversify your investments across asset classes to manage risk.

### Suggested Approach:  
1. **Max out the employer match in your 401(k)**.  
2. **Contribute to an IRA** (preferably Roth if eligible).  
3. **Increase contributions to your 401(k)** to the maximum.  
4. **Invest in taxable accounts for additional flexibility** and diversification, especially for shorter-term goals.
