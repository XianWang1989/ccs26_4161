Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here’s a general approach:

### 1. **Maximize Employer Match**
   - **401(k)**: Start by contributing enough to get the full employer match, as it's essentially free money.

### 2. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**
   - **Traditional IRA/Roth IRA**: Depending on your income, contribute to a Roth IRA for tax-free growth (if eligible) or a Traditional IRA for tax-deductible contributions.

### 3. **Contribute to 401(k)**
   - After maximizing the IRA contributions, increase your 401(k) contributions to the annual limit.

### 4. **Consider Taxable Accounts**
   - Invest in taxable accounts for liquidity and flexibility, especially if you've met your contribution limits in tax-advantaged accounts.

### 5. **Asset Allocation**
   - **Tax-Deferred Accounts (401(k), Traditional IRA)**: Consider holding higher-growth assets here since you won't pay taxes on gains until withdrawal.
   - **Roth IRA**: This is ideal for assets expected to appreciate significantly.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Focus on more stable, dividend-paying investments to manage tax implications better.

### 6. **Regular Review**
   - Reassess your asset allocation and contributions regularly, adjusting based on changes in income, tax laws, or market conditions.

### Consult with a Financial Advisor
If you're unsure, a financial advisor can provide personalized advice tailored to your situation.
