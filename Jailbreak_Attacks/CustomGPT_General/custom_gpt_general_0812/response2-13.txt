Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here’s a strategy to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match, as this is essentially free money.

2. **Prioritize High-Interest Debt**: Pay off any high-interest debt before investing.

3. **Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: Maximize contributions, especially if your employer matches.
   - **IRA**: Contribute to a Traditional or Roth IRA based on your income tax situation (Roth for tax-free growth; Traditional for immediate tax deduction).

4. **Taxable Accounts**: Invest any additional funds here. This provides flexibility for withdrawals without penalties and can be helpful for shorter-term goals.

5. **Asset Allocation**: Diversify investments within each account based on your risk tolerance and time horizon. Consider stocks for growth and bonds for stability.

6. **Rebalance Regularly**: Review your portfolio periodically to ensure it aligns with your goals and risk tolerance.

Consult a financial advisor for personalized guidance based on your specific circumstances.
