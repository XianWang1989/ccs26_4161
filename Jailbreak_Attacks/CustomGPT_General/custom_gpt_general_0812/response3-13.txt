Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here’s a general guide:

### 1. **401(k)**
   - **Maximize Contributions**: Aim to contribute enough to get any company match, as this is essentially free money.
   - **High Contribution Limits**: Take advantage of higher contribution limits (up to $22,500 for 2023, plus a catch-up of $7,500 if you're over 50).
   - **Tax Benefits**: Contributions reduce your taxable income; taxes are paid upon withdrawal.

### 2. **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**
   - **Choose Based on Income**: If eligible, contribute to a Roth IRA for tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement. Otherwise, consider a Traditional IRA for tax deductions.
   - **Contributions Limits**: Contribute up to $6,500 annually ($7,500 if over 50).
   - **Time Horizon**: Start contributing early for compounding benefits.

### 3. **Taxable Accounts**
   - **Flexible Access**: Use for goals not tied to retirement, as funds can be accessed any time without penalties.
   - **Investment Options**: Focus on tax-efficient investments, like index funds or ETFs, to minimize capital gains taxes.
   - **Tax Management**: Be mindful of buying and selling to avoid significant tax liabilities.

### **Overall Strategy**
- **Emergency Fund**: Ensure you have an emergency fund before investing heavily.
- **Diversification**: Allocate assets across different account types to balance risks and tax efficiencies.
- **Rebalance Regularly**: Monitor your allocations and adjust based on performance and life changes.

### **General Recommendation**
- *Prioritize contributions in this order*: 
   1. 401(k) up to the match.
   2. IRA (Roth or Traditional).
   3. 401(k) up to max limit.
   4. Taxable accounts for additional investments.

Consider consulting a financial advisor for a tailored plan.
