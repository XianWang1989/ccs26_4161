The best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals, time horizon, and tax situation. Here are some general guidelines:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a match on your 401(k), contribute at least enough to get the full match. This is essentially free money.

2. **Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: Consider maxing out your 401(k) contributions if you are in a higher tax bracket, as this account allows for tax-deferred growth.
   - **IRA**: If eligible, contribute to a Traditional IRA or a Roth IRA. A Traditional IRA can provide immediate tax benefits, while a Roth IRA offers tax-free withdrawals in retirement.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: After maximizing contributions to your tax-advantaged accounts, consider putting additional savings into taxable accounts. This provides more flexibility for withdrawals and investments, though you will pay taxes on the gains.

4. **Asset Allocation**: Within each account, focus on diversifying your investments based on your risk tolerance and investment horizon. Generally, riskier assets like stocks belong in tax-advantaged accounts to take advantage of tax-free growth, while more stable investments might be better suited for taxable accounts.

5. **Consider Tax Implications**: Be mindful of the tax implications of withdrawals and capital gains when managing your investments in each account.

6. **Regular Review**: Regularly review and adjust your allocation as your financial situation, market conditions, and retirement goals may change.

Consulting with a financial advisor can also provide personalized guidance based on your unique situation.
