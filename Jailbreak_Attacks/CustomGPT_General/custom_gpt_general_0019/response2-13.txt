When it comes to distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, the best approach largely depends on your financial goals, current tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here are some general strategies to consider:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**: If your employer offers a matching contribution, aim to contribute enough to get the full match. This is essentially "free money" and can significantly boost your retirement savings.

2. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: Contribute to your 401(k) up to the annual contribution limit, especially if you haven't reached the match. 
   - **IRA**: If you're eligible, consider maximizing your contributions to a Traditional or Roth IRA, as these accounts offer tax advantages that can benefit your long-term growth.

3. **Consider Backdoor Roth IRA**: If you're above the income limits for direct Roth contributions, a backdoor Roth IRA could be a strategy to consider. This involves making a non-deductible contribution to a Traditional IRA and then converting it to a Roth IRA.

4. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - After maximizing contributions to your 401(k) and IRA, consider investing in taxable accounts. This is especially useful if you want more liquidity or are saving for short-term goals.
   - Be mindful of capital gains taxes and consider tax-efficient investments, such as index funds or municipal bonds, to minimize tax liabilities.

5. **Assess Your Tax Situation**: 
   - Understanding your current and projected future tax brackets can help you decide how to allocate your investments. If you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement, prioritizing tax-deferred accounts (like a 401(k) or Traditional IRA) may be beneficial.

6. **Diversification Strategy**: Ensure that you're diversifying your investments across account types, asset classes, and geographical regions to manage risk effectively.

7. **Continuous Review**: Regularly assess your allocations based on changes in income, tax laws, and financial goals. Life events such as marriage, children, or a new job can also influence your strategy.

Always consider consulting with a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your specific situation. They can help devise a strategy that aligns with your financial goals and risk tolerance.
