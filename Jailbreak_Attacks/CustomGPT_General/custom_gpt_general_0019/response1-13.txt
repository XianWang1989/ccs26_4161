The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, retirement timeline, and investment strategy. Here are some general guidelines to consider:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**:
   - **401(k)**: If your employer offers a 401(k) plan with matching contributions, aim to contribute enough to get the full match. This is essentially "free money."
   - **IRA**: Consider funding a traditional IRA or Roth IRA. A traditional IRA may allow for tax-deductible contributions, while a Roth IRA offers tax-free withdrawals in retirement (subject to certain conditions). 

2. **Assess Your Tax Situation**:
   - **Traditional vs. Roth**: If you expect your tax rate to be lower in retirement, a traditional account might be advantageous. Conversely, if you expect a higher tax rate, a Roth could be beneficial since withdrawals are tax-free.
   - **Capital Gains Considerations**: Investments in taxable accounts may be subject to capital gains taxes. Be mindful of how much you realize in gains and losses in these accounts.

3. **Diversify Your Investments**:
   - Ensure your investments are well-diversified across your accounts, considering the overall risk tolerance and investment strategy you’ve set. It can be beneficial to hold different asset classes across your accounts based on their tax treatments.

4. **Rebalance and Adjust Regularly**:
   - Regularly review and rebalance your portfolio to maintain your desired asset allocation. As market conditions change, your accounts may drift from your investment strategy.

5. **Consider Your Withdrawal Strategy**:
   - Plan how and when you intend to withdraw funds in retirement. Generally, it may be beneficial to withdraw from taxable accounts first (to allow tax-advantaged accounts to continue growing), then from the traditional IRA, and lastly from Roth accounts, which can continue to grow tax-free for longer.

6. **Emergency Fund**:
   - Before heavily investing, ensure you have an adequate emergency fund set aside in easily accessible accounts. Typically, 3-6 months’ worth of expenses is recommended.

7. **Consult a Financial Advisor**:
   - It might be prudent to consult a financial advisor for personalized advice, as they can take into account your entire financial picture and help you develop a tailored strategy.

Ultimately, the right distribution strategy will depend on your unique circumstances and goals.
