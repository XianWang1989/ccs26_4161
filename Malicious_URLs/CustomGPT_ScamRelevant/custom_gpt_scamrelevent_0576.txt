### 1. **Basic Investment Concepts**

* **Risk Tolerance**: Understanding your own comfort with risk is essential. Risk tolerance refers to how much risk you're willing to take with your investments. Robo-advisors typically ask questions to assess your risk tolerance to create a portfolio that matches your preferences.
* **Asset Allocation**: This refers to how your investments are divided among different asset classes like stocks, bonds, and cash. Robo-advisors use algorithms to help determine the best allocation based on your risk profile.
* **Diversification**: The strategy of spreading investments across various assets to reduce risk. Robo-advisors automatically diversify portfolios by investing in a range of asset classes, reducing exposure to any one investment.
* **Time Horizon**: The length of time you expect to hold an investment before you need to access the money. This is a key factor in determining your investment strategy.

### 2. **Types of Robo-Advisors**

* **Automated vs. Hybrid**: Some robo-advisors are fully automated (e.g., Wealthfront), while others combine automation with human advisors (e.g., Vanguard Personal Advisor Services). Understanding the differences can help you choose the right platform based on your preferences for human interaction versus automation.
* **Passive vs. Active Management**: Most robo-advisors focus on **passive investing**, which uses low-cost index funds or ETFs that track the market. However, some platforms may offer **active management**, which involves more frequent changes to your portfolio based on market conditions.

### 3. **Technology and Algorithms**

* **Algorithm-Based Investing**: Robo-advisors use algorithms to create and manage investment portfolios based on your financial goals, risk tolerance, and time horizon. You don’t need to know the specifics of how the algorithms work, but an understanding that they automate much of the decision-making process can help you feel more confident in the system.
* **Tax-Loss Harvesting**: This is a technique used by robo-advisors to reduce your tax liability by selling investments that have lost value to offset gains elsewhere in your portfolio. Knowing this term will help you appreciate the extra value these platforms can provide.

### 4. **Fees and Costs**

* **Fee Structure**: Robo-advisors generally charge lower fees than traditional human advisors, but understanding how these fees are structured (e.g., percentage of assets under management) is crucial to evaluate whether a platform is right for you.
* **Expense Ratios**: These are the fees charged by funds (like ETFs) that the robo-advisor invests in. While these are typically lower in index funds, it’s helpful to understand how these fees can affect your returns over time.

### 5. **Regulatory and Legal Considerations**

* **Fiduciary Duty**: Many robo-advisors are fiduciaries, meaning they are legally required to act in your best interest. This ensures that the advice you receive is unbiased.
* **Suitability and Disclosure**: Understanding the concept of **suitability** (ensuring investments match your goals and risk tolerance) is important because robo-advisors may ask you a series of questions to ensure they are providing suitable advice.

### 6. **Using the Platform**

* **Profile Setup**: Robo-advisors typically require you to create a profile by answering questions about your financial goals, time horizon, risk tolerance, etc. Familiarizing yourself with these questions and thinking about your financial situation beforehand will help the process go smoothly.
* **Account Linking**: Most platforms allow you to link bank accounts or other investment accounts for easy fund transfers. Having a clear idea of what accounts you want to link and how much you’re willing to invest initially can speed up the setup.

* **ESG Investing**: Some robo-advisors offer **Environmental, Social, and Governance (ESG)** investing options for people who want their money to support sustainable and ethical businesses. If this is important to you, knowing about these options can help you align your investments with your values.

### 8. **Monitoring and Adjusting Your Portfolio**
