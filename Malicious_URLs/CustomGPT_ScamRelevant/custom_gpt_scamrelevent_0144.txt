### 1. **Drug Affordability and Access**

* Pharmacoeconomic studies help determine whether a new drug is **worth its price** compared to existing treatments.
* This influences **which drugs are covered by insurance** or government programs (e.g., Medicaid, NHS).
* Example: If a new cancer drug adds 2 months of life but costs \$150,000, pharmacoeconomists assess whether the **benefit is worth the cost**.

📌 **Impact**: Whether **you** can get the medicine **you need** without paying thousands out of pocket.

> **Reference:**

### 2. **Healthcare Prioritization**

* Governments and insurers use pharmacoeconomics to decide **how to allocate limited healthcare budgets**.
* For example, should a country invest in an expensive rare disease treatment or in vaccinations for thousands?

📌 **Impact**: Affects **wait times, availability of treatments, and public health programs** where you live.

> **Reference:**

### 3. **Generic vs. Brand Name Drugs**

* Economic evaluations compare the **clinical outcomes and cost differences** of generics vs. brand drugs.

📌 **Impact**: Keeps **prescription costs lower** for millions of patients.

📌 **Impact**: Whether your **insurance covers a treatment**, or if you have to fight appeals or pay out of pocket.
