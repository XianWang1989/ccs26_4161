Even if you’re not a pro, knowing the “language” helps you keep up:

* **Stock / Share / Equity** – Ownership in a company.
* **Bond** – A loan to a company or government.
* **Dividend** – Regular payment from a company’s profits.
* **Capital gain** – Profit from selling an asset higher than you bought it.
* **P/E ratio** – Price-to-earnings ratio, a common valuation metric.
* **ETF / Mutual Fund** – Pooled investments in multiple securities.

---

### **2. Understanding risk vs. return**

* Higher returns usually mean higher risk.
* Diversification lowers risk but may also reduce potential returns.
* Long-term investing can smooth out short-term market noise.

* Are you investing for **retirement**, **a big purchase**, or **passive income**?

* Do you prefer **growth stocks**, **value stocks**, or **income-producing assets**?

### **4. Awareness of personal risk tolerance**

* How comfortable are you with losing money in the short term?
* Would you sell in a downturn or hold steady?
