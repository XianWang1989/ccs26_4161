## ✅ Step 2: Understand 5 Core Concepts (Basic Definitions)

### 1. **What is Private Equity?**

Private equity firms raise money from **limited partners (LPs)** (pensions, endowments, etc.) and use it to **buy companies**, improve them, and **sell for a profit** in 3–7 years.

### 2. **What is a Platform vs. Add-On?**

* **Platform**: The main business a PE firm buys to build around.
* **Add-on**: Smaller companies acquired to expand the platform’s reach, geography, or services.

### 3. **What is Rollover Equity?**

If you sell your company and **keep part of your equity**, that’s called a “rollover.” It lets you participate in the upside when the PE firm sells again.

### 4. **What is an ABC Waterfall?**

A method of distributing equity returns:

* **Class A**: Gets paid first (usually PE firm and rollover equity).
* **Class B/C**: Gets paid later, typically for management incentive equity.

### 5. **What is IRR vs. MOIC?**

* **IRR** = Internal Rate of Return (time-weighted return, measured in %).
* **MOIC** = Multiple on Invested Capital (cash-on-cash return, e.g., 3x).
