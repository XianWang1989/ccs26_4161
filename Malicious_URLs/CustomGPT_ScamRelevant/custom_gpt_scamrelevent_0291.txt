## 🚀 2. **Free Software is Powering the AI Boom**

AI systems depend on:

* **Open-source libraries** (e.g. TensorFlow, PyTorch, Hugging Face Transformers)
* **Linux** as the backbone of most AI infrastructure
* **Free toolchains** like Jupyter, Pandas, NumPy, Scikit-learn, ONNX, and Apache ecosystems

> 🔄 There’s a **mutual dependency**: AI runs on open-source; FOSS evolves using AI.

* Often use **open-source code** but **don’t give back** proportionally.

## ⚠️ CHALLENGES TO WATCH OUT FOR

### 1. **Proprietary AI Lock-In**

* Big AI tools (like GPTs, Claude, Gemini) are mostly **closed-source and API-gated**.
* This risks recreating the old vendor-lock-in problems FOSS was created to avoid.
* Free alternatives (like [LMStudio](https://lmstudio.ai/), [OpenChatKit](https://github.com/togethercomputer/OpenChatKit), [LLaMA](https://github.com/facebookresearch/llama)) must be supported.

---

### 2. **High Compute Cost = Centralized Control**

* Training big models = \$\$\$ and data. Most FOSS projects can’t afford it.
* This creates an **AI oligopoly** unless communities share GPU access (e.g., via decentralized platforms like [Bittensor](https://www.bittensor.com/)).

---

### 3. **Open Software at Risk of AI Code Plagiarism**

* Copilot-like tools trained on FOSS code sometimes **reuse code without attribution**, violating licenses like GPL.
* There's an ongoing **legal and ethical debate** around this.
