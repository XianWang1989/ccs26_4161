## ✅ 1️⃣ Basic knowledge about opioids

* **What opioids are**: Medications (morphine, codeine, oxycodone, fentanyl) or street drugs (heroin) used for pain or recreationally.
* **They can slow breathing** and cause overdose if taken in excess, mixed with alcohol/sedatives, or after a period of stopping use.

---

## ✅ 2️⃣ Recognizing **signs of opioid overdose**

Learn to identify **these main overdose signs**:

* Slow, shallow, or stopped breathing.
* Cannot wake the person even if you shout or shake them.
* Blue/grey lips or fingernails.
* Very small pupils (“pinpoint” pupils).
* Unconsciousness, limp body.
* Gurgling, choking, or snoring sounds while unresponsive.

---

## ✅ 3️⃣ Know the **steps to take during a suspected overdose**

This GPT will guide you, but you should understand:

1. **Check responsiveness** (shout, shake gently).
2. **Call emergency services immediately** (911 or your local equivalent).
3. **Administer naloxone (if available)**:

   * Nasal spray: insert into a nostril, press plunger fully.
   * Injection: inject into thigh or upper arm.
4. **Perform rescue breathing if needed** (1 breath every 5 seconds if no breathing).
5. **Stay with the person until help arrives**.

---

## ✅ 4️⃣ Understand **naloxone basics**

* Naloxone **temporarily reverses opioid overdose** and helps the person start breathing again.
* It **does not harm** someone if opioids are not involved.
* Its effects last **20 minutes to 2 hours**, but opioids may last longer; medical help is **still needed**.
* Naloxone can cause **withdrawal symptoms** (nausea, sweating, agitation) but **these are not dangerous compared to an overdose**.

---

## ✅ 5️⃣ Be prepared in advance

To use this GPT effectively:

* Keep **naloxone accessible** if you, your family, or your community are at risk.
* Know your **local emergency number**.
* Be prepared to **follow simple instructions calmly** during a stressful moment.
* Familiarize yourself with **where to get naloxone kits**:

  * Pharmacies (some without prescription)
  * Addiction service centers
  * Community health programs

---

## ✅ 6️⃣ Trust the GPT workflow

During use:

* Answer **direct, clear questions** (e.g., “Is the person breathing?”, “Are lips blue?”).
* Follow **step-by-step instructions** without skipping steps.
* Remember the GPT **does not replace a doctor or EMS**; it guides you until professionals arrive.
