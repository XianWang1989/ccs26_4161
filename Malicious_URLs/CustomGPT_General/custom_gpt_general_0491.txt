2. **Data Analysis and Reporting:**
   - AI technologies can analyze large volumes of data from clinical trials and post-market surveillance, leading to more robust risk assessments and reporting for regulatory submissions.

3. **Design and Development:**
   - AI can aid in the design and development of medical devices through simulation and predictive modeling, enabling manufacturers to optimize their products before regulatory approval.

4. **Regulatory Review Process:**
   - As AI becomes more prevalent in medical devices, regulators may need to establish new guidelines specifically tailored to the unique challenges posed by AI algorithms, including transparency and explainability.

6. **Training and Workforce Development:**

7. **Ethical Considerations:**

8. **Integration with Existing QMS:**
   - Companies might need to adapt their existing QMS processes to incorporate AI tools and methodologies, ensuring alignment with regulatory requirements while maintaining product quality and safety.
